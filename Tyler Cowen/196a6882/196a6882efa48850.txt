1. Writing an economics paper using LLMs entirely ( https://github.com/chenandrewy/Prompts-to-Paper/blob/master/README.md ) .  Sort of.

3. The macroeconomics of tariffs and retaliation ( https://www.nber.org/papers/w33739#fromrss ) .  The real news here is the claim that, in the absence of foreign retaliation, the tariffs will make America better off.  Typically I am skeptical of such conclusions, due to the importance of more dynamic factors, including those of public choice, but in any case I expect that finding will be underreported.

4. China's One Child Policy lowered fertility more than we used to think ( https://www.nber.org/papers/w33741#fromrss ) .

5. Daniel Muñoz on Coleman Hughes and the norm of colorblindness ( https://bigifftrue.substack.com/p/the-costs-of-colorblindness ) .  A very good piece.

6. Empathy as a service ( https://x.com/omooretweets/status/1919191145973997914?s=46 ) .

7. Organisms that live longer than humans ( https://x.com/MaxUnfried/status/1919421897273692462 ) .
