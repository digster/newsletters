That is the new William Easterly book, and the subtitle is The West's Conquest of the Rest ( https://www.amazon.com/Violent-Saviors-Wests-Conquest-Rest/dp/1541675754/ref=sr_1_1?crid=25K9F83MMMSBC&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.TJHjBndsVM2wlTQRIMm_ImP9q886FomHCUTjKBBG5dekwJl15g0jrsyQ555VTZZl8xg_GkaullPf20SkuwxaZqUXkVV8CGfDkG_arupYOMXgjgtumb5Q1bdlhWYDEK0XE4eom4-BZ_E42Yj-gDO9ojYH-jaokY3OfTSXMRS-UmMdD9srFNo0x7JcXCoy0Fmw.m9XYIVw_qcfsuXppOi37dSyJ2BQQ1cZNEch5zJmLglc&dib_tag=se&keywords=violent+saviors&qid=1762701759&sprefix=violent+saviors%2Caps%2C120&sr=8-1 ) .  I liked this book very much, but found the title and also book jacket and descriptions misleading.  I think of this work as a full-throated examination and study of the classical liberal anti-imperialist tradition.  We have been needing such a thing for a long time.  It is not that I expected Easterly to be poorly informed, but it amazes me how well he knows this material from a historical point of view.  A lengthy (and good) discussion of E.D. Morel!

So recommended, and here's hoping these traditions find some new legs and less crazy adherents.
