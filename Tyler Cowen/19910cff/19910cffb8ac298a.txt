Post       : What should I ask Jonny Steinberg?

Posted     : September 3, 2025 at 2:19 pm
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Books, Current Affairs, History, Uncategorized

Yes I will be doing a Conversation with him.  From Wikipedia ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jonny_Steinberg ) :

> Steinberg was born and raised in the northern suburbs of Johannesburg ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johannesburg ) , South Africa. He was educated at Wits University ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wits_University )  in Johannesburg, and at the University of Oxford ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Oxford ) , where he was a Rhodes Scholar ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhodes_Scholar )  and earned a doctorate in political theory. He taught for nine years at Oxford ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oxford_University ) , where he was Professor of African Studies. He currently teaches at Yale University ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yale_University ) 's Council on African Studies.
>
> Three of Steinberg's books – Midlands (2002), about the murder of a white South African farmer, The Number ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Number_(book) )  (2004), a biography of a prison gangster, and Winnie & Nelson (2023) – won South Africa's premier non-fiction prize, the Sunday Times CNA Literary Awards ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunday_Times_CNA_Literary_Awards )  making him the first writer to win it three ties.

I am a special fan of Winnie & Nelson, which I consider to be one of the best books of the last ten years.  He is currently working on a biography of Cecil Rhodes ( https://politicalscience.yale.edu/people/jonny-steinberg ) .  So what should I ask him?
