Yes, I will be doing a Conversation with her.  Here is Wikipedia ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alison_Gopnik ) :

> Alison Gopnik (born June 16, 1955) is an American professor of psychology and affiliate professor of philosophy at the University of California, Berkeley ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_California,_Berkeley ) . She is known for her work in the areas of cognitive ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_development )  and language development ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_development ) , specializing in the effect of language on thought, the development of a theory of mind ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_of_mind ) , and causal learning. Her writing on psychology and cognitive science has appeared in Science ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_(magazine) ) , Scientific American ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientific_American ) , The Times Literary Supplement ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Times_Literary_Supplement ) , The New York Review of Books ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Review_of_Books ) , The New York Times ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times ) , New Scientist ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Scientist ) , Slate ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slate_(magazine) )  and others. Her body of work also includes four books and over 100 journal articles...
>
> Gopnik has carried out extensive work in applying Bayesian networks ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bayesian_networks )  to human learning and has published and presented numerous papers on the topic...Gopnik was one of the first psychologists to note that the mathematical models also resemble how children learn.
>
> Gopnik is known for advocating the "theory theory ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theory_theory ) " which postulates that the same mechanisms used by scientists to develop scientific theories are used by children to develop causal models of their environment.

Here is her home page ( https://www.alisongopnik.com/ ) .  So what should I ask her?
