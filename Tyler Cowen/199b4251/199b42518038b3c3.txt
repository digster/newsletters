> Virginia saw one of the smallest increases in electricity costs in the nation this past year, per new federal data ( https://www.eia.gov/electricity/data/browser/ ) , but it could get even higher as data centers proliferate statewide...
>
> By the numbers: Between May 2024 and May 2025, the average cost of electricity for residential customers in Virginia rose about 3%, from 14.95 cents to 15.41 cents, according to the U.S. Energy Information Administration.

The negative media bias aside, I find this encouraging.  OK, "...could get even higher," but the nationwide average was 6.5%.  Here is the full story ( https://www.axios.com/local/richmond/2025/08/06/electricity-costs-bills-data-centers-ai-virginia ) .  Via Andy Masley ( https://x.com/AndyMasley/status/1974702025222095028 ) .
