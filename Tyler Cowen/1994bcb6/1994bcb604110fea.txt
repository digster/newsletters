Post       : What I&#8217;ve been reading
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/09/what-ive-been-reading-276.html
Posted     : September 15, 2025 at 1:10 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Books, Uncategorized

1. Mark Gilbert, Italy Reborn: From Fascism to Democracy ( https://www.amazon.com/Italy-Reborn-Democracy-Mark-Gilbert/dp/0393867080/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3NEHQAEKT8FN3&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.fdVlJ-z8UdljqCNSPr5r7XdRbLPTXGpJxpzqecFlqHw.4R8ASXAtma1txdpKv-OtFlgZrb55jBS5YVtpDEV5Z8c&dib_tag=se&keywords=Italy+Reborn%3A+From+Fascism+to+Democracy&qid=1757869828&sprefix=italy+reborn+from+fascism+to+democracy%2Caps%2C70&sr=8-1 ) .  How Italy built a democracy after WWII, more or less out of nothing.  An optimistic and good book.  De Gasperi was a great man, and essential to the building out of a democratic Italy, yet today his name is hardly known.

2. Dave Edmonds, Death in a Shallow Pond: A Philosopher, A Drowning Child, and Strangers in Need ( https://www.amazon.com/Death-Shallow-Pond-Philosopher-Strangers/dp/0691254028/ref=sr_1_1?crid=KXZBCGVS4QQ4&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.ReVPX1adw5CsWSUwgmfdvQ.yJ-qLnS4CEiEll_GYA6JwdFvyMokjbcmvATiCcsx6Zo&dib_tag=se&keywords=Death+in+a+Shallow+Pond%3A+A+Philosopher%2C+A+Drowning+Child%2C+and+Strangers+in+Need&qid=1757869753&sprefix=death+in+a+shallow+pond+a+philosopher%2C+a+drowning+child%2C+and+strangers+in+need%2Caps%2C109&sr=8-1 ) .  An engaging but also intellectually serious history of some strands of utilitarianism and effective altruism.  I was happy to blurb this book, you may recall Edmonds also wrote the excellent biography of Derek Parfit.

3. Arundhati Roy, Mother Mary Comes to Me ( https://www.amazon.com/Mother-Mary-Comes-Arundhati-Roy/dp/1668094711/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3HPF8M03279SN&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.Dk7GPaQ_IA82qTrfuuNw1om-oEbK83cwySj3VA2oJc6LaX_-q6PL1BkVlLweQ-ruZME9Kr4jMRvjo-uAuvObC-0ga_ggUWRo6km4ZaG236VnDqVq8y7D_Wca41xOucyHH5rZBuicGif0v4pa4BMcStsRxjYXKifKZYLEoFs2OBzxkuMP5QUJ_mXgzMEq0gKIcRa-mqrftR1gMQFbYu6_7xrgSOjRbjmLxqchBxQ2_Cg.7A6anMD3GFjeAFAV-0vrEI6X8dPAhIi9r9grdmYmoVQ&dib_tag=se&keywords=Mother+Mary+Comes+to+Me&qid=1757869723&sprefix=mother+mary+comes+to+me%2Caps%2C95&sr=8-1 ) .  Well-written, but at some point I started wondering why I should care.  OK, Mama was a pain in the ass, but then what?  You had to take care of her when she was old.  I guess I prefer whaling tales?

4. Chuck Klosterman, Football ( https://www.amazon.com/Football-Chuck-Klosterman/dp/0593490649/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2RI5QBQ2FS75K&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.BtPJlxbCdKYSdl8jD492SMzhN7lEEPbf2JPM_5sd8z7y-G89e77UXCHAbjtjC2xj9bqzca00uMazq5HUgv1CD5YxfIlgwWzMD0lY5fZEV3RqhZY1wqlC4pHby2cqjnWSZDqjT6XszpsldJ06BEe2DI1nua3Mfs0vO2JyzECnTwGGee_UozqKlL6lj0JoOcVTLcaKiUC9c9M9pk_qLUSLimJ-GUXO3d9yB8ce3E3rErU.aFaTg8JNFrsLmLOPrEQ1gyKI4JmBtzzOPxKlyXpVifU&dib_tag=se&keywords=chuck+klosterman+football&qid=1757869682&sprefix=chuck+klosterma%2Caps%2C95&sr=8-1 ) .  An excellent and highly conceptual book about America's favorite sport.  Could this be the best book on (American) football ever?

5. Jordana Pomeroy, Daring: The Life and Art of Elisabeth Vigée Le Brun ( https://www.amazon.com/Daring-Life-Elisabeth-Vig%C3%A9e-Brun/dp/1947440101/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1Y5UA5EBX07BP&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.kApjuruouM5wZkK4EvzdjQ.LsvgxfXxzHM4X8ouzSqr2oX5VOe4BFwzPyhN8GAQQmM&dib_tag=se&keywords=Daring%3A+The+Life+and+Art+of+Elisabeth+Vig%C3%A9e+Le+Brun&qid=1757869648&sprefix=daring+the+life+and+art+of+elisabeth+vig%C3%A9e+le+brun%2Caps%2C93&sr=8-1 ) .  Both a book and a picture book rolled into one.  This new release is a very good introduction to her life, her art, and her role as semi-official court painter for Marie de Antoinette.  She remains an underrated artist.

6. Michael Lentz, Schattenfroh ( https://www.amazon.com/Schattenfroh-Michael-Lentz/dp/1646053826/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3S7L87DC5KBK8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.yPvLyZc7flf9n0jCUVCcIvcOv8PMj87_aPn5AZobj3UL62ZD9L4BNObanU84tOQio90C01BKL3On1zywhtbsZg.EMygyKzaYSivehb8iislc5uQA46L0daulGDUuDEySgA&dib_tag=se&keywords=schattenfroh+by+michael+lentz&qid=1757869973&sprefix=schattenfr%2Caps%2C101&sr=8-1 ) .  About one thousand pages, it is receiving buzz as a new novel to master, some are calling it "the new Solenoid" (not a positive for everyone, I do understand).  I have tried parts in English and parts in German, but still I do not get it.  Does it have a plot?  Any humor?  For purposes of norming, I am a big fan of James Joyce's Ulysses.  I will try it again, however.  At least the author was not complacent.
