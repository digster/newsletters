Post       : Top Posts of 2025
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/12/top-posts-of-2025.html
Posted     : December 29, 2025 at 7:16 am
Author     : Alex Tabarrok
Categories : Current Affairs, Economics, Uncategorized

Here are the top MR posts of 2025 as measured by page views. Number one post goes to Tyler:

* Trumpian policy as cultural policy ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/02/trumpian-policy-as-cultural-policy.html ) .

An excellent post that pairs well with another Tyler post, also in the top ten, A median voter theory of right-wing populism ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/08/a-median-voter-theory-of-right-wing-populism.html )  which has the punchline:

> The right-wing populists are gaining ground in so many countries because the cultural liberals in various parliaments and congresses are extremely reluctant to meet the preferences of their median voters.

Number two was also a Tyler post. Why I think AI take-off is relatively slow ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/02/why-i-think-ai-take-off-is-relatively-slow.html ) , an excellent accounting of AI economic and institutional bottlenecks. This pairs well with another top-ten post in which Tyler announces that AGI is already here ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/04/o3-and-agi-is-april-16th-agi-day.html ) . Both posts are correct. An interesting conundrum.

Third and fourth are two of my posts:

3. UCSD Faculty Sound Alarm on Declining Student Skills ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/11/ucsd-faculty-sound-alarm-on-declining-student-skills.html )

4. One-Third of US Families Earn Over $150,000 ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/11/one-third-of-us-families-earn-over-150000.html )

Next is Tyler's rundown of non-fiction books. Well worth re-reading.

5. Best non-fiction books of 2025 ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/11/best-non-fiction-books-of-2025.html )  with one late addition ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/12/38-londres-street.html ) .

Next I was pleased to see my post in which I explain some standard economics but in a deeper, more fundamental way than is usually done: One of my favorite posts of the year:

Why Do Domestic Prices Rise With Tariffs? ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/04/why-do-domestic-prices-rise-with-tarriffs.html )

Zephyr Teachout's op-ed wasn't fun to read but I admit I did have some fun writing a response

Gross(ery) Confusion ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/07/gossery-confusion.html )

Here's another issue which makes me mad. The destruction of boarding houses, a perfectly reasonable housing form that reduces homelessness. Or to put it more simply, why is sharing a house illegal? Outrageous.

The War on Roommates: Why Is Sharing a House Illegal? ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/08/the-war-on-roommates-why-is-sharing-a-house-illegal.html )

I am all for American greatness but the approach of the Trump administration is often backwards. I pointed out the big differences between the Sputnik moment and what I called the DeepSeek Moment in two posts.

The Sputnik vs. DeepSeek Moment: Why the Difference?  ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/07/the-sputnik-vs-deepseek-moment-why-the-difference.html ) and The Sputnik vs. Deep Seek Moment: The Answers. ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/07/the-sputnik-vs-deep-seek-moment-the-answers.html )

I was pleased that David Brooks picked up on my framing ( https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/17/opinion/trump-america-china.html )  in the NYTimes.

Finally my post The Library Burned Slowly ( https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/04/the-library-burned-slowly.html )  sparked a brief spat with Chris Rufo. Rufo's ability to turn the tools of the left on them is impressive but I haven't changed my mind that "Bludgeoning your enemies is fun while it lasts but you can’t bludgeon your way to a civilization."

What were your favorite posts of the year, either at MR or elsewhere?
