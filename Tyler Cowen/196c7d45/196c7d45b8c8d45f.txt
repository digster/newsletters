Post       : Mississippi schools are pretty good
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/05/mississippi-schools-are-pretty-good.html
Posted     : May 13, 2025 at 12:05 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Data Source, Education, Uncategorized

> ...in recent years...Mississippi has become the fastest-improving school system in the country.
>
> You read that right. Mississippi is taking names.
>
> In 2003, only the District of Columbia had more fourth graders in the lowest achievement level on our national reading test (NAEP ( https://nces.ed.gov/nationsreportcard/about/ ) ) than Mississippi. By 2024, only four states had fewer.
>
> When the Urban Institute adjusted national test results ( https://www.urban.org/research/publication/states-demographically-adjusted-performance-2024-national-assessment )  for student demographics, this is where Mississippi ranked:

* Fourth grade math: 1st
* Fourth grade reading: 1st
* Eighth grade math: 1st
* Eighth grade reading: 4th

> (Here is a great rundown ( https://www.the74million.org/article/there-really-was-a-mississippi-miracle-in-reading-states-should-learn-from-it/ )  of how the remarkable turnaround was achieved.)
>
> ...Black students in Mississippi posted the third-highest fourth grade reading scores in the nation. They walloped their counterparts in better-funded states. The average black student in Mississippi performed about 1.5 grade levels ahead of the average black student in Wisconsin. Just think about that for a moment. Wisconsin spends about 35 percent more per pupil ( https://educationdata.org/public-education-spending-statistics )  to achieve worse results.

That is from Tim Daly at The Free Press ( https://www.thefp.com/p/mississippi-cant-possibly-have-good?utm_campaign=trueanthem&utm_medium=organic-social&utm_source=twitter ) .
