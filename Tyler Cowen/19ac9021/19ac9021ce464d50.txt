Post       : What I&#8217;ve been reading

Categories : Books, Uncategorized

1. Thomas Meyer-Wieser, Cairo: Architectural Guide ( https://www.amazon.com/Cairo-Architectural-Guide-Thomas-Meyer-Wieser/dp/386922813X/ref=sr_1_2?crid=VS517POS4PY1&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.fO5PiGlG4wnW_XTmS_jfMLYoATmCPxOstBicrshYhEyVPX2gpRQsf96Yc0slac52sFrBotOc26X4RwFCOhDWfyPVbFec9aSMVERxIKzljqYR9Fb4KMw2_HL3lA4ceZe-LpMlK8CcSR5uEu2eFSKh--ZwL5DfbQa6SaUTpDmjaeS6eV4b-sJyMPMCcyUA3CbvrBo5AIY7t1j2tPUydnkTzGd1OypMr8r1jXZ8ptxDm1c.xJ2I9AfdSbnFnRbkejhBcIP19FpPXOF90V7HsWOictU&dib_tag=se&keywords=Architectural+Guide+Cairo&qid=1764238602&sprefix=architectural+guide+cairo%2Caps%2C117&sr=8-2 ) .  A picture book, sort of.  Reading a book on the architectural history of a place, while intrinsically interesting, is also usually the best way to learn the non-architectural history of that same place.  Recommended.

2. Mary Hays, Memoirs of Emma Courtney ( https://www.amazon.com/Memoirs-Emma-Courtney-Mary-Hays-ebook/dp/B00AQML1N2/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3P930015F9AIC&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.pwJ1m3br54MpDWkweLe4Cp7Lk_Ubr_b-B9Nl4pcn1Gz5O79tnRPV4qnZDXYviFzJ452LWGoa75HTadgZ4GWhTbfBUxIEbyxyWspLRc-k31A.-uYoWieiCoNHLYxCACf9W4Zt2K7I7gCQR4GMobHOTzA&dib_tag=se&keywords=Memoirs+of+Emma+Courtney&qid=1764247804&sprefix=memoirs+of+emma+courtney%2Caps%2C124&sr=8-1 ) .  A late 18th English fictional memoir, still underrated and fairly short to boot.  Very interesting on Enlightenment culture, what it meant to grow up in a reading culture, and the power of early feminism.

3. Sylvia Plath, The Unabridged Journals of Sylvia Plath ( https://www.amazon.com/Unabridged-Journals-Sylvia-Plath/dp/0385720254/ref=sr_1_1?crid=32GNE3UWLJZ4X&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.mOEn6dVU5nxtYiGHFsDfPeXeBZ7AM1yDqs_pi3Z5kgHvjfPOrDyH2PhJ1TuVgNF7f_OmZF0NFwdiFLamjhZC3izw9DrU665QXpQvgfmJ2rf3-fuJFCtkUio0p1Tm4DDmSRU7A9izKFcSxaD8rgtkBn1PPQgHSiXtcR-gb5TmgEtjyw0N_OeEaCVz0H4eVVoQcW0XTxeHNkRm7qKtmC3CTNE3FJXNism387Ff0DJQr7o.rEm2h6o2p4g6kF7AP_YURmblU1aFdy9G1EH7AGiHt7U&dib_tag=se&keywords=sylvia+plath+journals+unabridged&qid=1764238478&sprefix=sylvia+plath+jour%2Caps%2C103&sr=8-1 ) .  Usually journals bore me after the first fifty pages.  But this lengthy volume is fascinating throughout, and arguably her greatest achievement?  At the very least worth a try.  She maintained an impossibly high level of writing across these years, plus you see (close up) the shifts in how her life was going, electroshock therapy and all.  Recommended.

4. Somerset Maugham, Up at the Villa ( https://www.amazon.com/UP-at-Villa-Somerset-Maugham/dp/0375724621/ref=sr_1_1?crid=7VD7ZISEZHVA&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.zbqJr06fvOyRc1d8y3cEgZooVISHsVPMyvk6nExGoVQ6bmsbs0EBKTuG0q6LXuyv8kAJlN_uzyE6chYRJ_uqaYHSo77zgLoq1peuxHRFMg96sLU__4qnUbwtPPtuEMPDqdzFzbxOOeb5pOiSC6zbKFMKqJxKTtIzw3y62OdFNLhl8KZfEWbc2Q8_DZvEvYLGyk7lo0UFefUWJKaX0Rwhr48xotB0LWIQZDg_oTPxk9k.q9c5nCxUYErrvSpZJXKFCbUy9BflvJKz6UZkbL1trxY&dib_tag=se&keywords=up+at+the+villa+by+somerset+maugham&qid=1764238437&sprefix=somerset+maugham+villa%2Caps%2C99&sr=8-1 ) .  Great fun at first, and very short.  It ends up "overinvesting" in plot, but still for me a worthwhile read.  It is best when at its most psychological.

5. Joel J. Miller, The Idea Machine: How Books Built Our World and Shape Out Future ( https://www.amazon.com/Idea-Machine-Books-Built-Future/dp/1493088939 ) .  A paean to reading and its importance, comprised of many historical anecdotes.  I wish each part went into more detail, nonetheless this is an important book about a cultural transmission method that is in some unfortunate ways diminishing in its cultural centrality.

6. Virginia Woolf, Three Guineas ( https://www.amazon.com/Three-Guineas-Virginia-Woolf/dp/0156901773/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1F3PLTMK0HLZ3&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.E-xZOWgmEVY46ck3dcu9hqvFSuFDuReH8vf5t0Rvxt3__uBk-VOzwSjmjlQeDvgqX-C5Yy4d7J79u6-WlrbS3k3vResNYYeUl7GSstv-WIIGQl_GFrT1piC0QKxm5WfjgfmxJAushTUEg4VimMKTUIOORDTpzQo9lp0lPrV7MI7GI3TYaosymJG23U01vxoJkOFxD9Isa2J1pg_Na1p72Bq6iGRP9a_viA-VItjysMw.8QxImTug5SOJSX8kLu1PaL2FIT_xzhfvWV6-7c2BAAE&dib_tag=se&keywords=virginia+woolf+Three+Guineas&qid=1764247569&sprefix=virginia+woolf+three+guineas%2Caps%2C127&sr=8-1 ) .  Why do so few people talk about this piece?  It is Woolf writing on feminization and the prevention of war.  The argument is dense, and I will give it a reread.  She seems to attributing some of the worst aspects of militarized society to the approbational propensities of educated women?  She also considers -- well ahead of her time -- how male and female philanthropy are likely to differ.  In any case, there is more here than at first meets the eye.

There is also Keija Wu's A Modern History of China's Art Market ( https://www.amazon.com/Modern-History-Chinas-Art-Market/dp/1032287969/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2QQSAHBPRHWOD&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.YtBsI2eYHnh3--04CsMtGKaHQLpY9THjzGhZyLShognWDFTGJ2KBUjnuuGbU8a4mpXpJIT0jCiQKEfSpDOIz9LQdej2LIEM9T1epRyiQ-Bnkty0v0RdyX3goFQLkIDykEQ4bVnQC8yY-vseKWXH1xwpSkIIRXcH8tR6Lhvc9Odr9o67G3ssmc3HabEQ-hW_oFB2KMULnPQCVF3ZKkb70OKPbrFg32EjMWWfMRIpepj0.wRe20SQBfmFtqRK93fBbJPMEFguEz3L-BnlQRN0SZfY&dib_tag=se&keywords=A+Modern+History+of+China%27s+Art+Market&qid=1764247634&sprefix=a+modern+history+of+china%27s+art+market%2Caps%2C110&sr=8-1 ) .
