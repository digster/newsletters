Post       : What has gone wrong with tourism to Las Vegas?
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/12/what-has-gone-wrong-with-tourism-to-las-vegas.html
Posted     : December 8, 2025 at 3:07 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Current Affairs, Travel, Uncategorized

> Agitators in the city have attempted to document the deterioration by posting ominous images of barren casinos ( https://x.com/FoxBusiness/status/1977033822383620149/photo/1 ) , conjuring the perception of a place hollowed out by economic armageddon. The reality is more nuanced, but it is true that practically every conceivable indicator tracking tourism to Las Vegas is flashing warning signs. Hotel occupancy has cratered. Rooms were only 66.7 percent full in July ( https://www.travelweekly.com/Travel-News/Hotel-News/Las-Vegas-hotels-grapple-with-steep-occupancy-decline ) , down by 16.8 percent from the previous year. The number of travelers passing through Harry Reid International Airport also declined by 4.5 percent in 2025 ( https://www.fox5vegas.com/2025/09/24/las-vegas-airport-sees-august-traffic-decline/ )  during an ongoing ebb of foreign tourists, for familiar reasons ( https://www.wsj.com/business/its-summer-vacation-time-tourists-are-saying-no-to-america-753e1ddf?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqeZ4rsXtSlR7GK7ElcwlmPVmtNYwpaWCOux4aMSyPLqqFSjDBo5_2Efp6rfAJg%3D&gaa_ts=69156517&gaa_sig=z0YWG7_2Ca5sdJ689m5jL-Jnzm895lv8N6J6ENcpkR1tQhirIuk03x_VIzv5yughLRu5qlrdk68r7B3UJ0adJw%3D%3D ) . Canadians, historically one of the city’s most reliable sources of degenerates, have effectively vanished. Ticket sales for Air Canada jets flying to Las Vegas have slipped by 33 percent ( https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/las-vegas-tourism-canadian-slump-1.7607707 ) , while the Edmonton-based low-cost carrier Flair has reported a 62 percent drop-off.

Here is the full story ( https://slate.com/business/2025/11/las-vegas-travel-sphere-hotel-donald-trump.html?utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=traffic&utm_source=article&utm_content=copy_link ) , which shows it is by no means an exclusively Canadian phenomenon.  Overall, I am happy to see a shift away from gambling, drinking, and "shows for wealthy old people"?
