Post       : That was then, this is now
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/03/that-was-then-this-is-now-51.html
Posted     : March 27, 2025 at 4:47 pm
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Uncategorized, Web/Tech

> This year is likely to be remembered for the Covid-19 pandemic and for a significant presidential election, but there is a new contender for the most spectacularly newsworthy happening of 2020: the unveiling of GPT-3 ( https://arxiv.org/pdf/2005.14165.pdf ) . As a very rough description, think of GPT-3 as giving computers a facility with words that they have had with numbers for a long time, and with images since about 2012...
>
> The eventual uses of GPT-3 are hard to predict, but it is easy to see the potential. GPT-3 can converse at a conceptual level, translate language, answer email, perform (some) programming tasks ( https://twitter.com/sharifshameem/status/1282676454690451457 ) , help with medical diagnoses ( https://twitter.com/QasimMunye/status/1278750809094750211 )  and, perhaps someday, serve as a therapist. It can write ( https://www.gwern.net/GPT-3 )  poetry, dialogue and stories with a surprising degree of sophistication, and it is generally good at common sense ( http://lacker.io/ai/2020/07/06/giving-gpt-3-a-turing-test.html )  — a typical failing for many automated response systems. You can even ask it ( https://twitter.com/Merzmensch/status/1283419366143524866 )  questions about God.
>
> ...It also has the potential to outperform ( https://twitter.com/paraschopra/status/1284801028676653060 )  Google for many search queries, which could give rise to a highly profitable company.
>
> ...It is not difficult to imagine a wide variety of GPT-3 spinoffs, or companies built around auxiliary services, or industry task forces to improve the less accurate aspects of GPT-3. Unlike some innovations, it could conceivably generate an entire ecosystem.

That was the opening paragraph of my 2020 Bloomberg column on GPT-3 ( https://www.bloomberg.com/view/articles/2020-07-21/artificial-intelligence-offers-the-hope-2020-needs?cmpid%3D=socialflow-twitter-view&utm_content=view&utm_source=twitter&utm_campaign=socialflow-organic&utm_medium=social&sref=htOHjx5Y ) .
