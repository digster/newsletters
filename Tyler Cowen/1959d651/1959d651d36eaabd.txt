Post       : Was our universe born inside a black hole?
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/03/was-our-universe-born-inside-a-black-hole.html
Posted     : March 16, 2025 at 1:16 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : History, Science, Uncategorized

> Without a doubt, since its launch, the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) has revolutionized our view of the early universe, but its new findings could put astronomers in a spin. In fact, it could tell us something profound about the birth of the universe by possibly hinting that everything we see around us is sealed within a black hole.
>
> The $10 billion telescope ( https://www.space.com/21925-james-webb-space-telescope-jwst.html ) , which began observing the cosmos in the Summer of 2022, has found that the vast majority of deep space and, thus the early galaxies ( https://www.space.com/james-webb-space-telescope-early-galaxies-cosmic-rulebook )  it has so far observed, are rotating in the same direction. While around two-thirds of galaxies spin clockwise, the other third rotates counter-clockwise.
>
> In a random universe, scientists would expect to find 50% of galaxies rotating one way, while the other 50% rotate the other way. This new research suggests there is a preferred direction for galactic rotation... ( https://www.space.com/dark-matter-slows-milky-way-rotation )
>
> "It is still not clear what causes this to happen, but there are two primary possible explanations," team leader Lior Shamir, associate professor of computer science at the Carl R. Ice College of Engineering, said in a statement. ( https://www.k-state.edu/media/newsreleases/2025/03/lior-shamir-james-webb-space-telescope-spinning-galaxies.html )  "One explanation is that the universe was born rotating. That explanation agrees with theories such as black hole cosmology ( https://www.space.com/8293-universe-born-black-hole-theory.html ) , which postulates that the entire universe is the interior of a black hole.
>
> "But if the universe was indeed born rotating, it means that the existing theories about the cosmos are incomplete."
>
> ...This has another implication; each and every black hole in our universe could be the doorway to another "baby universe." These universes would be unobservable to us because they are also behind an event horizon, a one-way light-trapping point of no return from which light cannot escape, meaning information can never travel from the interior of a black hole to an external observer.
