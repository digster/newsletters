Post       : What I&#8217;ve been reading
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/10/what-ive-been-reading-277.html
Posted     : October 3, 2025 at 1:20 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Books, Uncategorized

Marcus Willaschek, Kant: A Revolution in Thinking ( https://www.amazon.com/Kant-Revolution-Thinking-Marcus-Willaschek/dp/0674296109/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2KM22PTJCYK0H&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.LBLvRDUBhi66-X5R94702mcXzmGeIAeUyw7qZC3bEM6k91nNaODGdbT9dwTV5JA_nBx4sDZnfA0EE1L0hB2igU0IWD2e-YjcG4bFaC-f5h7S5Ht0zVIXp2b_pQhGrnf6.e0upJxaxb0VQxlCA1n2GnlN7QGWf1kxKavLMRevOlkI&dib_tag=se&keywords=Kant%3A+A+Revolution+in+Thinking&qid=1759277986&sprefix=kant+a+revolution+in+thinking%2Caps%2C64&sr=8-1 ) .  A very good book, perhaps the best introduction to Kant?  Though for me it is mostly interior to my current knowledge set.

Matthew Bell, Goethe: A Life in Ideas ( https://www.amazon.com/Goeths-8211-Life-Ideas/dp/0691153957/ref=sr_1_1?crid=372UNMAM11EGS&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.9h9rc1iMq_xjEng4SuAr6L0TqYYor7KUAZFJM7ltAahe14mGyHBOWHFcGxC-cXwwGffe-Iqihg47QZY-5sFWxw.J4YkvDxBaq_0XRx2qnyXtWlDXQOWvoyWP8mY9uQ1GHw&dib_tag=se&keywords=Goethe%3A+A+Life+in+Ideas&qid=1759278052&sprefix=goethe+a+life+in+ideas%2Caps%2C104&sr=8-1 ) .  A beautiful book, now in English we have Nicholas Boyle's work and also this.  Bell is wise enough to understand and value Iphigenia auf Tauris, a good test for Goethe appeciation.  Although I had a library copy out to read, I went ahead and bought a copy of this one to own.

Benjamin Wilson, Strange Stability: How Cold War Scientists Set Out to Control the Arms Race and Ended up Serving the Military-Industrial Complex ( https://www.amazon.com/Strange-Stability-Scientists-Control-Military-Industrial/dp/0674976088/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2DMSSKMETUA3Z&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.MYmf4H8xZvQI8OtLVIHCNw.IByGmRvI0TSv7BBv-UtBVidKDuQhtSGAaoybaL7vW7A&dib_tag=se&keywords=Strange+Stability%3A+How+Cold+War+Scientists+Set+Out+to+Control+the+Arms+Race+and+Ended+up+Serving+the+Military-Industrial+Complex&qid=1759278088&sprefix=strange+stability+how+cold+war+scientists+set+out+to+control+the+arms+race+and+ended+up+serving+the+military-industrial+complex%2Caps%2C107&sr=8-1 )  is both interesting and has plenty of information on early Thomas Schelling and his precursors.

Very well researched is The Highest Exam: How the Gaokao Shapes China ( https://www.amazon.com/Highest-Exam-Gaokao-Shapes-China/dp/0674295390/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3SJIITI3TSPS8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.Vjz6OHdP0kgITvahjC8GJg.pSpwZtSeXo2q8HZ0N2000GmH01A7DoltinzLMUHyxrE&dib_tag=se&keywords=The+Highest+Exam%3A+How+the+Gaokao+Shapes+China&qid=1759278125&sprefix=the+highest+exam+how+the+gaokao+shapes+china%2Caps%2C74&sr=8-1 ) , by Ruixue Jia and Hongbin Li, with Claire Cousineau.

Peter Baxter, Rhodesia: A Complete History 1890-1980 ( https://www.amazon.com/Rhodesia-Complete-1890-1980-Peter-Baxter/dp/1726710629/ref=sr_1_1?crid=YBEOHZDV4S00&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.ggan9dblFKquXOBwfT42fA.F4Aws7j_eGzkLAugWtKzsVz5Cm9E3nPLUW3Kv5pmMGA&dib_tag=se&keywords=Rhodesia%3A+A+Complete+History+1890-1980&qid=1759277950&sprefix=rhodesia+a+complete+history+1890-1980%2Caps%2C58&sr=8-1 ) .  The most complete history of the country I have been able to find.  Many of the other books contain a few dominant, non-false narratives, but one gets tired of that?  I say LLMs come especially in handy for learning this history.

Luka Ivan Jukic, Central Europe: The Death of a Civilization and the Life of an Idea ( https://www.amazon.com/Central-Europe-Death-Civilization-Life/dp/1911723987/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3G55WTXSUR0G8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.DNoyseekFYJhaWHbWfzy8A.2-MJq-6YLWZGLR0KKXMeiPKqP61BW_j3RlUnmb7H4Ko&dib_tag=se&keywords=Central+Europe%3A+The+Death+of+a+Civilization+and+the+Life+of+an+Idea&qid=1759277885&sprefix=central+europe+the+death+of+a+civilization+and+the+life+of+an+idea%2Caps%2C89&sr=8-1 ) .  I took this sentence to encapsulate the main lesson of the book, namely that this does not usually work: "Central Europeans were, as ever, masterfully adept at rearranging polities into new configurations."

I enjoyed Maxim Samson, Earth Shapers: How We Mapped and Mastered the World, From the Panama Canal to the Baltic Way ( https://www.amazon.com/Earth-Shapers-Mapped-Mastered-Panama/dp/0226844749/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3376OGXZ1IW08&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.xY94trVzgBUqCcmTAs2eBw.PAoA1kI3tZTemS0qO2JBro1wpU4X9OQLjFOkOux4B2I&dib_tag=se&keywords=Earth+Shapers%3A+How+We+Mapped+and+Mastered+the+World%2C+From+the+Panama+Canal+to+the+Baltic+Way&qid=1759277918&sprefix=earth+shapers+how+we+mapped+and+mastered+the+world%2C+from+the+panama+canal+to+the+baltic+way%2Caps%2C72&sr=8-1 ) .
