Yes, I will be doing a Conversation with him.  Here is Wikipedia on John ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Amaechi ) :

> John Uzoma Ekwugha Amaechi // ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English )  ⓘ ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:Ig-John_Uzoma_Ekwugha_Amaechi.ogg ) , OBE ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire )  (/əˈmeɪtʃi/ ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English ) ; born 26 November 1970) is an English psychologist ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Psychologist ) , consultant ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consultant )  and former professional basketball ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basketball )  player. He played college basketball ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/College_basketball )  for the Vanderbilt Commodores ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanderbilt_Commodores_men%27s_basketball )  and Penn State Nittany Lions ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penn_State_Nittany_Lions_men%27s_basketball ) , and professional basketball in the National Basketball Association ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Basketball_Association )  (NBA). Amaechi also played in France, Greece, Italy, and the United Kingdom. Since retiring from basketball, Amaechi has worked as a psychologist and consultant, establishing his company Amaechi Performance Systems.
>
> In February 2007, Amaechi became the first former NBA player to publicly come out ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coming_out )  as gay after doing so in his memoir Man in the Middle.

John has a new book coming out, namely It's Not Magic: The Ordinary Skills of Exceptional Leaders ( https://www.amazon.com/Its-Not-Magic-Ordinary-Exceptional/dp/1394338279/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2QKC2B8RWDKFQ&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.G5cey2u7wsv_XmEE9w8Bnirupyd_FNRiqauR9OP_xBFZ-GR7rXoI9wvfC3XjUJaziC0iWhEmq1-GG4VNjsfwnfzMweTBI8DNypO10mpjekIkgmGfq19hFkYHwLFFpIPnyXe1SVE9QDzR82Suth3kXdJ9a6op_vBZnDtETaWUHki-x19otyC9JfThVvfPjG76RfYh_4xYT5gXWeSO01LhTFZql7hNKPbMgvs5k1KaqN0.xfph0XftkT9RjkXreGOqt-nA9_anVjir9mk4A2h_Kh0&dib_tag=se&keywords=john+amaechi&qid=1756035336&sprefix=john+amaechi%2Caps%2C86&sr=8-1 ) .  So what should I ask him?
