Yes I will be doing a Conversation with her.  She is the author of a forthcoming book on Weimar, namely Weimar: Life on the Edge of Catastrophe ( https://www.amazon.com/Weimar-Life-Catastrophe-Katja-Hoyer/dp/1541605799/ref=sr_1_1?crid=FFNC9FV1FRE8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.qWcjf-taTF6F6JFmbg-Qmz1Ttriw6MvoeXVYVnQjIE-NjEXEZU-hNquntqp259TwWfeAj7Fp14miWK6yteogbg.LJ6ZO80aU2L-SKUIboCnsIpQBz-MrJLNLMy9WSm6HSI&dib_tag=se&keywords=katja+hoyer+weimar&qid=1772312908&sprefix=katja+hoyer%2Caps%2C218&sr=8-1 ) .  Note that much of the book considers the city of Weimar, mostly in Nazi times, and not just the Weimar era.  She also has published Beyond the Wall: A History of East Germany ( https://www.amazon.com/Beyond-Wall-History-East-Germany/dp/1541606361/ref=tmm_pap_swatch_0?_encoding=UTF8&dib_tag=AUTHOR&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.DXDex5aPztmRcM0kq461Rmjxj41KzZkziKuGrAN8XgafxTJRsJkL2upU07Xrz5iM-8bPV2xcs3IIEk4DPzHoiyuwqP_1I1_mThqI5ZiA5zY7r8-1QaMi-M-tLXwMEoHvxzNcv2VW3z-oW8g9f4ll7w.MeKIYjLbmYuKgRAIzA-YER1QdzNYMp4B-aUNXgrluak ) , and Blood and Iron: The Rise and Fall of the German Empire 1871-1918 ( https://www.amazon.com/Blood-Iron-Rise-German-Empire/dp/1639362975/ref=tmm_pap_swatch_0 ) .  She is active in journalism, podcasting, and is a visiting research fellow at King's College London.  She was born in East Germany and is both British and German.

So what should I ask her?
