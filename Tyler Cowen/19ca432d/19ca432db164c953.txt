Post       : If you have the right to die, you should have the right to try!

Author     : Alex Tabarrok
Categories : Law, Medicine

Ruxandra Teslo asks a good question ( https://x.com/JerusalemDemsas/status/2027041390903877940?s=20 ) :

> I have a curiosity: why is it the case that it is easier to get MAID in Canada than it is to access experimental treatments which carry a higher risk? In the past, I used to think ppl do not like "deaths caused by the medical system", but for MAID the prob of death is 100%...

The Canadians maybe somewhat inconsistent on this point. Unfortunately, the Supreme Court has been consistent and has rejected medical self-defense arguments for physician assisted suicide ( http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_v._Glucksberg )  and let stand an appeals court ruling that patients do not have a right to access drugs  ( http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abigail_Alliance_v._von_Eschenbach ) which have not yet been permitted for sale by the FDA (fyi, I was part of an Amici Curiae brief ( https://web.archive.org/web/20100203052700/https://www.overlawyered.com/files/abigail.pdf/BRIEF%20FOR%20ECONOMISTS%20JOHN%20E.CALFEE.pdf )  for this case).

Hat tip for the post title to Jason Crawford.
