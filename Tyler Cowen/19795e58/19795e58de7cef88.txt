Categories : Books, Uncategorized

Francesca Wade, Gertrude Stein: An Afterlife ( https://www.amazon.com/Gertrude-Stein-Afterlife-Francesca-Wade/dp/1982186011/ref=sr_1_1?crid=12RZ2H9XFGZN8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.3uPro9uFWwhKb7wv9Yg3NdflkzAcQhFQzo-KjXbRUxwZ7IPNyTdOnPm3-qW5MRqXPkQsL15q1pw02pYc59EwiAxtZ5pO6Uv4eACgvNfJ69xW6OEExxFFsAjq0eK_JN241h2OIPwhxJCYqTII-MrHtw.4TNmmZEVuSMRF5tjSVVchuSllSf2BKhWg0FKoloR7D0&dib_tag=se&keywords=Gertrude+Stein%3A+An+Afterlife&qid=1750522481&sprefix=gertrude+stein+an+afterlife%2Caps%2C223&sr=8-1 ) .  One of the best-written biographies I have read in years.  I would not say it is close to my core interests, but if you think you might like it you will.

Jacqueline Harpman, I Who Never Known Men ( https://www.amazon.com/Who-Have-Never-Known-Men/dp/1945492600/ref=sr_1_1?crid=38FFF76AB7K7C&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.47NVAvScu6ZdgBqCTs_2y_6g16BjQCpbMCEongpDRmoedfpDJom4Gg92JcpEhS2Ivvo_JQcFQwK48fpF_Q3KRg.WLtjLMJ5BKruRLSakTz8WIXtR8kd2Ululjxh7uH22ms&dib_tag=se&keywords=I+Who+Never+Known+Men&qid=1750522528&sprefix=i+who+never+known+men%2Caps%2C242&sr=8-1 ) .  I enjoyed this novel: "Deep Underground, thirty-nine women live imprisoned in a cage.  Watched over by guards, the women have no memory of how they got there, no notion of time, and only a vague recollection of their lives before."

J.P. Mallory, The Indo-Europeans Rediscovered: How a Scientific Revolution is Rewriting Their Story ( https://www.amazon.com/Indo-Europeans-Rediscovered-Scientific-Revolution-Rewriting/dp/050002863X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1APV1B5059B5A&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.lD2E84h1BVqY1_vaoby19A.o0hVNaliPQYA_M88Y_euy34gpLJ6bYb9JLOti5uKh78&dib_tag=se&keywords=The+Indo-Europeans+Rediscovered%3A+How+a+Scientific+Revolution+is+Rewriting+Their+Story&qid=1750522601&sprefix=the+indo-europeans+rediscovered+how+a+scientific+revolution+is+rewriting+their+story%2Caps%2C136&sr=8-1 ) .  The best book I know of on the origins and unities of Indo-European languages.  I had not known Edward Sapir was born in Pomerania.  And "...the Keres people who occupy seven pueblos (villages) in New Mexico speak a language totally unrelated to any of their neighbours and their origins have been frequently disputed."

Anthony Trollope, Phineas Finn ( https://www.amazon.com/Phineas-Penguin-Classics-Anthony-Trollope/dp/0140430857/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3F63IQAO0RVGB&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.pMZX2_pH2Ejq973XIiU3YryCIC0m7QugSGK38sECprP7Oc2okz0dpTHS408mw99pbWVtaWg2woRDGpm6Q1zvhPMyednH8wXMX8Vf_H3x-D659AVomeX5vXTkyLSNVrTk-HuKs2t3YsEeEYFeWOjt_bcB0M9pnQylGwiAGZjeOKba8xzS5P5xWZmvgQhipIfHsUnDRJW3cTPT39Oca9cV7BSQb4wdIQoduOgBZa2dPlY.meNqX6r5m0bGeqwoGOSJHXkrfbdKmYlRqsE6CkXdX8w&dib_tag=se&keywords=phineas+finn&qid=1750522901&sprefix=phineas+finn%2Caps%2C161&sr=8-1 ) .  One of the best Trollope novels it seems, even though it is not (yet?) clear what the plot actually consists of.  Currency decimalization is also one of the side plots ( https://chatgpt.com/share/6853ddb2-8394-8010-8694-f04204fe8c71 ) , who can argue with that?

Manu S. Pillai, Gods, Guns and Missionaries: The Making of the Modern Hindu Identity ( https://www.amazon.com/Gods-Guns-Missionaries-Making-Identity/dp/0670093653/ref=sr_1_1?crid=G7WO7N6WICBO&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.0pp1NoE9AbVqQW1yuZziAw.nH1FWQ3-8WQIGWuucren1lTu9gnGDIMYpICalP1v_os&dib_tag=se&keywords=Gods%2C+Guns+and+Missionaries%3A+The+Making+of+the+Modern+Hindu+Identity&qid=1750523161&sprefix=gods%2C+guns+and+missionaries+the+making+of+the+modern+hindu+identity%2Caps%2C269&sr=8-1 ) .  It may be fruitless to argue about this topic on-line, but almost all Westerners under-read when the topic is Hinduism.
