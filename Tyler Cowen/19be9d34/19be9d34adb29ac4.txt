Yes, I will be doing a Conversation with her.  She has a new and very good book out, namely Motherland: A Feminist History of Modern Russia ( https://www.amazon.com/Motherland-Feminist-History-Revolution-Autocracy/dp/006287912X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=S9GLH9IPSQVK&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.qO4IodUXqzaSW2VUO-EkCWuymuKFB8KXva9TVtIjsooD7Y_T-I5QE8Evn-7rKi06o03O83vYwls7hi8DdxbCAw.sZjK8GO7-wiJuuNUDGFLlEYPQe4tOwMcSKvK0CVP1-g&dib_tag=se&keywords=julia+ioffe+motherland&qid=1769092819&sprefix=julia+ioffe%2Caps%2C140&sr=8-1 ) .  I will focus on that topic, but she has done much else as well.  From Wikipedia ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julia_Ioffe ) :

> ...a Russian-born American journalist. Her articles have appeared in The Washington Post ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Washington_Post ) , The New York Times ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times ) , The New Yorker ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_Yorker ) , Foreign Policy ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_Policy_Magazine ) , Forbes ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbes ) , Bloomberg Businessweek ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomberg_Businessweek ) , The New Republic ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_Republic ) , Politico ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politico ) , and The Atlantic ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Atlantic ) . Ioffe has appeared on television programs on MSNBC ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MSNBC ) , CBS ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CBS ) , PBS ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PBS ) , and other news channels as a Russia expert. She is the Washington correspondent for the website Puck ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puck_(media_company) ) .

And here is Julia on Twitter ( https://x.com/juliaioffe?lang=en ) .  So what should I ask her?
