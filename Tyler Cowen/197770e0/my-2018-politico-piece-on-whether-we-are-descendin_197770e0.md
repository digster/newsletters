In 2018 I published [an article in Politico](http://marginalrevolution.com?action=user_content_redirect&uuid=16b161a98c5e6ee0311c1cb8a91747506724be2162cb60d2e87de892656bc727&blog_id=42693868&post_id=90753&user_id=262258391&subs_id=225460721&signature=7db1291a415986aaf4ffba6621424b27&email_name=new-post&user_email=ishan.mail@gmail.com&encoded_url=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucG9saXRpY28uY29tL21hZ2F6aW5lL3N0b3J5LzIwMTgvMDMvMDEvbm8tZmFzY2lzbS1jYW50LWhhcHBlbi1oZXJlLTIxNzA5Mi8&email_id=73c921435325d8a5c5240e02a8774833), arguing that fascism would not come to America. In part that is because of our very long democratic traditions (much longer than Weimar!), and in part because American bureaucracy has become unmanageable, thus limiting the power of the executive. DOGE in particular has been a quite vivid representation of the latter point, as I made it in 2018 -- "The net result is they simply can’t control enough of the modern state to steer it in a fascist direction."
A few people have asked me to revisit that prediction, and frankly I think it is looking great. (I do not doubt, however, that the Trump administration represents a major increase in blatant corruption, and a deterioration of norms of governance, most of all in the areas of public health and science but not only. I think of America as evolving back to some of its 19th century norms, and often bad ones, not fascism.)
