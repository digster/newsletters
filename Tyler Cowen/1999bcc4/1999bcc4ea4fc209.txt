Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Current Affairs, Uncategorized

> London has slipped out of the world’s top 20 initial public offering markets as the third quarter ends, overtaken by Mexico and Singapore in a fresh blow to its standing as a global finance hub.
>
> The UK exchange has slipped three places to 23rd in a Bloomberg ranking of the world’s busiest IPO destinations, placing it behind the frontier market of Oman. Volume this year dropped 69% to $248 million, the weakest haul in more than 35 years.
>
> This year’s largest ( https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2025-07-21/london-s-biggest-2025-ipo-rallies-33-after-braving-tariff-storm )  London IPO — an April offering from accountancy MHA Plc ( https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/MHA:LN )  — raised £98 million ($132 million). No deals have involved a major Wall Street bank; they were instead arranged by small local outfits like Cavendish Plc ( https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/CAV:LN )  and Singer Capital Markets ( https://www.bloomberg.com/quote/0618485D:LN ) . The third-quarter picture is even starker with just $42 million of deal volume, down 85% from the same period last year, according to data compiled by Bloomberg.
>
