Post       : Reims and Amiens
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/06/reims-and-amiens.html
Posted     : June 21, 2025 at 12:28 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : History, Religion, The Arts, Travel, Travels, Uncategorized

Both cities have significant war histories, but they are very different to visit, even though they are only two hours apart by car.

Reims ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reims )  was largely destroyed in World War I, and so the central core was rebuilt in the 1920s, with a partial Art Deco look ( https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=f7661461505a9ca6&q=reims+art+deco&udm=2&fbs=AIIjpHxU7SXXniUZfeShr2fp4giZ1Y6MJ25_tmWITc7uy4KIemkjk18Cn72Gp24fGkjjh6xBoibEaR8uzvPi9MPqTLIFFRz8Nrq6LqOK7ioaVKk7Pt4hQmHoQggRFoCqaJYQKIR-UPOO0v_oJBCfBk1TonINX1AWeVVt_FczIWpWaBeM2SWo1H4Ih69bNSWssixzZjhPuWKf&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjfk-XI24COAxUxTqQEHeFxCRkQtKgLegQIExAB&biw=1280&bih=585&dpr=1.5 ) .  The downtown is attractive and prosperous, the people look sharp and happy, and it is a university town.  You arrive and feel the place is a wonderful success.  If you had to live in a mid-sized French city, you might choose this one.

The main cathedral ( https://www.google.com/search?q=reims+cathedral&sca_esv=f7661461505a9ca6&udm=2&biw=1280&bih=585&ei=9rNVaOHeKaCskdUP6eKEoAo&ved=0ahUKEwjhsZvK24COAxUgVqQEHWkxAaQQ4dUDCBE&uact=5&oq=reims+cathedral&gs_lp=EgNpbWciD3JlaW1zIGNhdGhlZHJhbDIFEAAYgAQyBRAAGIAEMgUQABiABDIFEAAYgAQyBRAAGIAEMgUQABiABDIFEAAYgAQyBRAAGIAEMgUQABiABDIFEAAYgARInCVQ_QVY1B5wAXgAkAEBmAGQAaABpAuqAQQxMS42uAEDyAEA-AEBmAINoALWCMICBhAAGAcYHsICBhAAGAgYHsICCBAAGAgYChgewgIGEAAYBRgemAMAiAYBkgcDNy42oAegWrIHAzYuNrgHzQjCBwQyLTEzyAcz&sclient=img )  is one of the best in France, and arguably in the world.  The lesser-known basilica ( https://www.google.com/search?q=reims+basilica+interior&sca_esv=f7661461505a9ca6&udm=2&biw=1280&bih=585&ei=MbRVaPD-A87_nsEP8-bF4AE&ved=0ahUKEwiw24bm24COAxXOvycCHXNzERwQ4dUDCBE&uact=5&oq=reims+basilica+interior&gs_lp=EgNpbWciF3JlaW1zIGJhc2lsaWNhIGludGVyaW9ySMgsUOUFWKwncAF4AJABAZgBkAKgAecUqgEGMTUuOS4yuAEDyAEA-AEBmAIMoALpCsICChAAGIAEGEMYigXCAgYQABgHGB7CAgUQABiABMICBhAAGAoYHsICBBAAGB7CAgYQABgFGB7CAgYQABgIGB7CAggQABgIGAoYHpgDAIgGAZIHBTcuMi4zoAeTXbIHBTYuMi4zuAfgCsIHBjAuMS4xMcgHLg&sclient=img )  also is top tier.  There are scattered Roman ruins.  French kings were coronated in Reims from early on, all the way up through 1825.

Amiens is on the Somme, and the 1918 Battle of the Somme ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Amiens_(1918) )  was a turning point in WWI history.  The town is a melange of architectural styles, with many half-timbered homes ( https://www.amiens-tourisme.com/en/incontournables/saint-leu-quarter/ )  but also scattered works from different centuries.  The town also has France's "first skyscraper," ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tour_Perret_(Amiens) )  renowned in its time but now a rather short and out of place embarrassment.  The main Amiens cathedral ( https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=f7661461505a9ca6&q=amiens+cathedral&udm=2&fbs=AIIjpHxU7SXXniUZfeShr2fp4giZ1Y6MJ25_tmWITc7uy4KIeuYzzFkfneXafNx6OMdA4MQRJc_t_TQjwHYrzlkIauOKiWCnruNfHTRmzt0RxosbrOMpYTFDxPGyD8T2B3g2diIu3ngkbY9zBvCWUufXsTFwoiRfzCo6PN174qxy5RWVWKD-YXE&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwjk6-qh2oCOAxVQUqQEHZhBGAQQtKgLegQIERAB&biw=1280&bih=585&dpr=1.5 ) , however, is perhaps the best in all of France.

The town itself feels like visiting a banlieu, with large numbers of African and Muslim immigrants.  It is lively, and it feels as if a revitalization is underway, though I do understand opinions on these matters differ.  Real estate prices are at about 3x their 1990s levels ( https://chatgpt.com/share/6855b59b-ae18-8010-bf41-912b173df77e ) .  That to me is strong evidence that things are going well.

Restaurant Momos Tibetian ( https://www.google.com/search?q=Restaurant+Momos+Tibetian+amiens&sca_esv=f7661461505a9ca6&ei=3rVVaMibFoCHkdUP0Zy_4AM&ved=0ahUKEwjIguGy3YCOAxWAQ6QEHVHODzwQ4dUDCBA&uact=5&oq=Restaurant+Momos+Tibetian+amiens&gs_lp=Egxnd3Mtd2l6LXNlcnAiIFJlc3RhdXJhbnQgTW9tb3MgVGliZXRpYW4gYW1pZW5zMgoQABiwAxjWBBhHMgoQABiwAxjWBBhHMgoQABiwAxjWBBhHMgoQABiwAxjWBBhHMgoQABiwAxjWBBhHSKgQULkFWKEKcAF4AZABAJgBAKABAKoBALgBA8gBAPgBAZgCAaACBpgDAOIDBRIBMSBAiAYBkAYFkgcBMaAHALIHALgHAMIHAzItMcgHBA&sclient=gws-wiz-serp )  has excellent Chinese and Tibetan food.  The Picardy museum has some very good works by Boucher, Balthus, Picabia, El Greco, and Chavannes.

Both cities are radically undervisisted.  They do attract some tourists, but for the most part you feel you have them to yourself.
