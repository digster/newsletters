Yes, I will be doing a Conversation with him.  From Wikipedia ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andrew_Ross_Sorkin ) :

> Andrew Ross Sorkin (born February 19, 1977) is an American journalist ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Journalist )  and author. He is a financial columnist ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columnist )  for The New York Times ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times )  and a co-anchor of CNBC's Squawk Box ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squawk_Box ) . He is also the founder and editor of DealBook, a financial news service published by The New York Times. He wrote the bestselling book Too Big to Fail ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Too_Big_to_Fail_(book) )  and co-produced a movie adaptation ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Too_Big_to_Fail_(film) )  of the book for HBO Films. He is also a co-creator of the Showtime ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Showtime_(TV_network) )  series Billions ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Billions_(TV_series) ) .
>
> In October 2025, Sorkin published 1929: Inside the Greatest Crash in Wall Street History--and How It Shattered a Nation, a new history of the Crash ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wall_Street_crash_of_1929 )  based on hundreds of documents, many unpublished.

Most of all I am interested in his new book, but not only.  So what should I ask him?
