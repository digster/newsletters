Post       : Congress is reversing Trump&#8217;s budget cuts to science
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2026/01/congress-is-reversing-trumps-budget-cuts-to-science.html
Posted     : January 10, 2026 at 7:20 pm
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Uncategorized

> Surprisingly, analysts foresee a possible rise of more than 2 percent ( https://www.aaas.org/sites/default/files/2026-01/Senate%20Report%202026.pdf )  in the budget category known as basic research — the blue-sky variety that produces fundamental strides and spinoffs in fields such as health care and artificial intelligence. Last year, the Trump administration called for a cut in federal basic research ( https://www.nytimes.com/2025/07/10/science/trump-science-budget-cuts.html )  of more than one-third.
>
> Mr. Trump sought even larger cuts for the National Science Foundation, which sponsors much of the nation’s basic research. He proposed that its budget be slashed ( https://www.nytimes.com/live/2025/05/02/us/trump-budget-2026#science-backers-say-proposed-science-cuts-pose-dire-risks )  to $3.9 billion from $8.8 billion, a drop of 56 percent. The Senate package countered ( https://www.appropriations.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/fy26_cjs_bill_summary_and_highlights.pdf )  with a reduction to $8.75 billion, or less than 1 percent.
>
> The bipartisan accord on funding science, Ms. Zimmermann said, stands in sharp contrast with the congressional impasse ( https://www.nytimes.com/2025/09/24/health/obamacare-cost-aca-health-insurance.html )  that shut down the government last fall as Democrats and Republicans clashed over the renewal of subsidies for the Affordable Care Act.
>
> “They’re working together now,” she said. “It’s a return to normalcy.” The new cooperation, Ms. Zimmermann added, is “promising for the eventual passage of the bills.”
>
