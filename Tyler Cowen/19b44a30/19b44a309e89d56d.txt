Post       : What I&#8217;ve been reading

Posted     : December 22, 2025 at 12:55 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Books, Uncategorized

1. I have been reading in the history of archaeology, and have profited from Eric H. Cline, Three Stones Make a Wall: The Story of Archaeology ( https://www.amazon.com/Three-Stones-Make-Wall-Archaeology/dp/0691183236/ref=sr_1_1?crid=2R5N8MM1XPIVY&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.7jyMOW0Dc-JN8wVRMLdXWMyD-Tn9x0RS_Ivew9GwWwHxqmNPc1AWZ_mAkRbCkGny.lltqj1rxmc4tTbpk3_RENTCLgPmKknIEgdhqhfWQrlY&dib_tag=se&keywords=Three+Stones+Make+a+Wall%3A+The+Story+of+Archaeology&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766361158&sprefix=three+stones+make+a+wall+the+story+of+archaeology%2Caps%2C82&sr=8-1 ) , which is a very good introduction to what the subtitle claims.  There is also Toby Wilkinson, A World Beneath the Sands: The Golden Age of Egyptology ( https://www.amazon.com/World-Beneath-Sands-Golden-Egyptology/dp/0393882403/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1H55AD0XBXJMX&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.lATEz-SWLNOz7TQlhhg6yA.l7uKuX4eau3QQdEN5dFLY2iIEtmd3S9Bjhv5SDDoU_4&dib_tag=se&keywords=A+World+Beneath+the+Sands%3A+The+Golden+Age+of+Egyptology&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766361194&sprefix=a+world+beneath+the+sands+the+golden+age+of+egyptology%2Caps%2C93&sr=8-1 ) , and Jason Thompson, Wonderful Things: A History of Egyptology, volume 2, The Golden Age: 1881-1914 ( https://www.amazon.com/Wonderful-Things-History-Egyptology-1881-1914/dp/9774169948/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3CO1XV8M6TGK2&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.TIW6ES25BSTzPPQcKbh-SA.zM5Q0Wq1Cp9P66cFLwrDl7uvOo702P6j2GGg9DSTTzo&dib_tag=se&keywords=Wonderful+Things%3A+A+History+of+Egyptology%2C+volume+2%2C+The+Golden+Age%3A+1881-1914&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766361228&sprefix=wonderful+things+a+history+of+egyptology%2C+volume+2%2C+the+golden+age+1881-1914%2Caps%2C75&sr=8-1 ) .

2. Elizabeth Alker, Everything We Do is Music: How 20th-Century Classical Music Shaped Pop ( https://www.amazon.com/Everything-We-Music-20th-Century-Classical-ebook/dp/B0F8BVPFMN ) .  A very good and readable book on this interaction, with excellent discussions of Donna Summer, Stevie Wonder, La Monte Young, and Penderecki, among many others.

3. Arindrajit Dube, The Wage Standard: What's Wrong in the Labor Market and How to Fix It ( https://www.amazon.com/Wage-Standard-Whats-Wrong-Market/dp/0593471415/ref=sr_1_1?crid=PKEPWWUSO3WI&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.ei0X9H3G9L_J0oc94U7PXQ.lRw03-Lhu-SO648GKurtpaY-LDtTFELVeaL74Y-W3F8&dib_tag=se&keywords=The+Wage+Standard%3A+What%27s+Wrong+in+the+Labor+Market+and+How+to+Fix+It&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766360512&sprefix=the+wage+standard+what%27s+wrong+in+the+labor+market+and+how+to+fix+it%2Caps%2C93&sr=8-1 ) .  Dube notes his main theme is that employers have discretion in setting the real wage. A good overview of his work in labor economics.  I would stress that if you think "tight labor markets" are good for workers, you should be obsessed with doing lots to favor capital.

4. Richard H. Davis, Religions of Early India: A Cultural History ( https://www.amazon.com/Religions-Early-India-Cultural-History/dp/0691199264/ref=sr_1_1?crid=21A3S578UOXC8&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.nXBZrqFYE8kJ58HohGxExls42B-dB10zO2U23v0AGpOtv-1sxL_m_sSMO_vszmGXQCGZaw8U2vdwDLZU5GJcBjrJGg5fpFxCPe3bCcwtHm7CsP7Ldy0WEgWfVArbjNKEnKnDvzo8N0Qtsr53z26N5eymXP5iF3CBW5xSD2ie-ImxI8VxJs1MLJq4iIRnF4w-zRT2v7LlEWOp1U_DyJLFYHxf-qmlplZ2av8e6h1oZBw.WN_hR45dnD4YE3fhb-WaQ7RvIbl5RFYaBWEd2qDZtmk&dib_tag=se&keywords=Religions+of+Early+India%3A+A+Cultural+History&qid=1766360570&sprefix=religions+of+early+india+a+cultural+history%2Caps%2C71&sr=8-1 ) .  A very useful background read for understanding later Indian history and religions, as well as the more general spread of religion throughout the southern regions of Asia.  Avoids the common mistake of becoming too obscure on these topics.

For those interested in the longer term, there is Hilary Greaves, Jacob Barrett, and David Thorstad, editors, Essays on Longtermism: Present Action for the Distant Future ( https://www.amazon.com/Essays-Longtermism-Present-Action-Distant/dp/0192883852/ref=sr_1_1?crid=3FYTKCWI2MTK0&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.ZCqAJv6_m_shki8JsgAc0g.2Cf_Zvw4eboQh61GstWC_Z8XZd25CIgids1pZ-KSR9M&dib_tag=se&keywords=Essays+on+Longtermism%3A+Present+Action+for+the+Distant+Future&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766360619&sprefix=essays+on+longtermism+present+action+for+the+distant+future%2Caps%2C99&sr=8-1 ) .

There is Kevin Kelly, Colors of Asia: A Visual Journey, Photos and Design ( https://www.amazon.com/Colors-Asia-Journey-Kevin-Kelly/dp/B0FGJ18PG5?&linkCode=sl1&tag=kevinkelly-20&linkId=054187194119d6a09f57a7ed732a6e0e&language=en_US&ref_=as_li_ss_tl ) .

Alvaro Rivas, Marx in the Age of AI: How Artificial Intelligence Reshapes Value, Class, and Ideology ( https://www.amazon.com/Marx-Age-AI-Artificial-Intelligence/dp/B0FWY9YW1V/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1P6HL0GCHVLQ5&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.fYyD8ae9QKMj--dLeAoA2FWJs6DerUpqU4EnGksOk6TYME85NNI1sYoTni3CZq1AkkxPHEz9hk6qZ-UdlrKuFwT9Wz8K0MEf5dY_BBthR6c.QQivtPIXuMeKle_jY-LrgwbYG80crdCG6ksSusL_k7Q&dib_tag=se&keywords=Marx+in+the+Age+of+AI&qid=1766360727&s=books&sprefix=marx+in+the+age+of+ai%2Cstripbooks%2C75&sr=1-1 )  is a short but serious look as to how Marxian concepts might apply to AI, for instance whether surplus value will be earned on the AIs, or for that matter on non-human animals.

You will find a different intersection of topic areas in Dominic Roser, David Zhang, and J.D. Bauman, All the Lives You Can Change: Effective Altruism for Christians ( https://www.amazon.com/All-Lives-You-Can-Change/dp/0802885136/ref=sr_1_1?crid=LEARBH9KFYJE&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.A0Ol15Tdz5yH3i937T6mDw.G4Rr9-TZNE3ueoRW9fpFl2qelCRsjDHfQ0jp2PJroAk&dib_tag=se&keywords=All+the+Lives+You+Can+Change%3A+Effective+Altruism+for+Christians&nsdOptOutParam=true&qid=1766361382&s=books&sprefix=all+the+lives+you+can+change+effective+altruism+for+christians%2Cstripbooks%2C115&sr=1-1 ) .
