The most important factors behind trade balances include savings decisions, fiscal policy, economic growth rates, wealth levels and demographic characteristics such as the age of the population. As economist Joseph Gagnon

Nor is currency manipulation a problem with Canada, Mexico or the European Union, other targets of Trump’s tariffs.Insofar as currencies do matter, currency appreciation is one very direct mechanism that limits the potential for tariffs to improve the trade balance. If a country slaps tariffs on imports, that does make those imports more expensive and thus lowers the demand for them. But then the value of the domestic currency will rise, which in turn makes it harder for domestic exporters. There is no guarantee that these effects will cancel each other out exactly, but it is difficult to get much of a trade balance boost through this mechanism, given these offsetting effects.
