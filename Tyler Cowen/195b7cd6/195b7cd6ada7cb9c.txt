Yes I will be doing a Conversation with her.  Here is Wikipedia ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helen_Castor ) :

> Helen Ruth Castor FRSL ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society_of_Literature )  (born 4 August 1968) is a British historian of the medieval and Tudor ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Tudor )  period and a BBC broadcaster. She taught history at the University of Cambridge ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Cambridge )  and is the author of books including Blood and Roses ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blood_and_Roses_(book) )  (2004) and She-Wolves: The Women Who Ruled England Before Elizabeth (2010). Programmes she has presented include BBC Radio 4 ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_4 ) 's Making History and She-Wolves on BBC Four ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Four ) . Her most recent book is The Eagle and the Hart: The Tragedy of Richard II and Henry IV ( https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=The_Eagle_and_the_Hart:_The_Tragedy_of_Richard_II_and_Henry_IV&action=edit&redlink=1 )  (2024).

I very much liked her last book in particular.  And here is a good interview with her ( https://www.theguardian.com/books/2024/oct/12/historian-helen-castor-id-rather-not-live-in-such-interesting-times-the-eagle-and-hart-tragedy-of-richard-ii-and-henry-iv ) .  So what should I ask her?
