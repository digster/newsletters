Author     : Tyler Cowen

I liked it very much, noting it is not one for the purists.  The visuals and soundtrack added to the general passionate feel.  I can recommend the Jonathan Bate review ( https://jonathanbate.substack.com/p/wuthering-heights?utm_source=post-email-title&publication_id=3588492&post_id=188231746&utm_campaign=email-post-title&isFreemail=true&r=3o9&triedRedirect=true&utm_medium=email )  and the Louise Perry review ( https://www.wsj.com/opinion/free-expression/wuthering-heights-is-a-millennial-fantasy-73af9b23?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqccnfPy2KRR7RYEplACMYXfvw3VxDDq6ttz19NX_aObRAKfczKGEh-eo4Y5T5Q%3D&gaa_ts=6997c361&gaa_sig=i33WI19QlY1Ms-GiP79ob01CQ89QSX4O6yI4DZ7xqfm4wj8NqqSt5Ox_L9y-FNk17odQYAshVbvzhxrSjtQG5A%3D%3D )  (WSJ).  The other version of this movie I can recommend is the Luis Buñuel Mexican interpretation ( https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wuthering_Heights_(1954_film) ) , also full of passion and that poor pig.  At its heart, this is a very Mexican story and no way should it be done in a Masterpiece Theater kind of style.
