A new app offering to record your phone calls and pay you for the audio so it can sell the data to AI companies is, unbelievably, the No. 2 app in Apple’s U.S. App Store’s Social Networking section.
The app,
[Neon Mobile], pitches itself as a moneymaking tool offering “hundreds or even thousands of dollars per year” for access to your audio conversations.Neon’s website says the company pays 30¢ per minute when you call other Neon users and up to $30 per day maximum for making calls to anyone else. The app also pays for referrals. The app first ranked No. 476 in the Social Networking category of the U.S. App Store on September 18 but jumped to No. 10 at the end of yesterday, according to data from app intelligence firm
[Appfigures].On Wednesday, Neon was spotted in the No. 2 position on the iPhone’s top free charts for social apps...
However, Neon’s
[marketing]claims to only record your side of the call unless it’s with another Neon user.
