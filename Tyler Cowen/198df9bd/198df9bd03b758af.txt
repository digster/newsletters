Post       : What I&#8217;ve been reading
URL        : https://marginalrevolution.com/marginalrevolution/2025/08/what-ive-been-reading-275.html
Posted     : August 25, 2025 at 1:00 am
Author     : Tyler Cowen
Categories : Books, Uncategorized

David Woodman, The First King of England: Aethelstan and the Birth of a Kingdom ( https://www.amazon.com/First-King-England-%C3%86thelstan-Kingdom/dp/0691249490/ref=sr_1_1?crid=RQAN9JTB6PTX&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.pDM2V3S4CEs9X6eEmR8jiLed_J54hEk7XkqbF-ckI98.pPwhTMUL3hC5kdqYYiphJsvQijlcnvNH8eGmoo4eL3s&dib_tag=se&keywords=The+First+King+of+England%3A+Aethelstan+and+the+Birth+of+a+Kingdom&qid=1756069224&sprefix=the+first+king+of+england+aethelstan+and+the+birth+of+a+kingdom%2Caps%2C96&sr=8-1 ) .  An excellent work.  One of the best books on early English history, and also one of the best books on how the Dark Ages morphed into early Medieval times.  Usually I find treatments in both areas difficult to follow, but this one produces a coherent and also non-exaggerated narrative.  It also will make you want to visit Northumbria.

Edmund Phelps, My Journeys in Economic Theory ( https://www.amazon.com/Journeys-Economic-Theory-Edmund-Phelps/dp/0231207301/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1UZGOOBS1EOT2&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.Gdd8FUdtp4oSellJVdWSIfcMeEHFNp3SwotgW-O_Wk6-w4warLG2WLWF9q4Kjao4k6P26gQs3Dlil7ekLh80F5bSTGCKLfsH6lyh5ba-Ly0.Li4uDNNS7dH-iI6DmyvUM08A08T3BaIGpVjggylqHKw&dib_tag=se&keywords=My+Journeys+in+Economic+Theory&qid=1756069165&sprefix=my+journeys+in+economic+theory%2Caps%2C75&sr=8-1 ) .  A fascinating memoir, I had not known he was so obsessed with Rawls and Nagel.  He also loved the tenor Franco Corelli, and was a Birgit Nilsson fan too.  Recommended, for those who like this sort of thing, and who already are familiar with the cast of characters.

Bench Ansfield, Born in Flames: The Business of Arson and the Remaking of the American City ( https://www.amazon.com/Born-Flames-Business-Remaking-American/dp/132409351X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=35MV53F5LIKOC&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.rXM021HmPDlVuK1lHPV2rw.lXdyPHRBLKeyT8oS_QP2M-LODucgaPJBNDatNAoHin4&dib_tag=se&keywords=Born+in+Flames%3A+The+Business+of+Arson+and+the+Remaking+of+the+American+City&qid=1756069116&sprefix=born+in+flames+the+business+of+arson+and+the+remaking+of+the+american+city%2Caps%2C66&sr=8-1 ) .  Whenever a book demonstrates what people in New Jersey have known for decades, usually it is a good book.

Andrew Sean Greer, Less: A Novel ( https://www.amazon.com/Less-Winner-Pulitzer-Prize-Novel/dp/031631613X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=1IAAIFUMMQZPN&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.wieSPJQyjNy5GOFnQKlqv7SW4I_jeJYkj4xpo0Lg9cSrHAmfO0v1kquXQAg3yFAu0eYHfpmGnx7bTTIFJb2ek_7-dqF-v_0tpoxYppWZ1_GkPZMSBS60SOFm8vUvoYjDIDxblRRmZlBffVLwgMb5OcBePKp8wXhDhf8YaruHuYxhWQRJWjV_lMp-rVc3NpGQ29HsIAtJNzWQ1B5qs6I5FQzo8kjN_bNtpUL72gt82JM.TZAcbCtGztxvSeM8TyduBgD2G0CD4FvZCPDF5wXAvBk&dib_tag=se&keywords=less+andrew+sean+greer&qid=1756069256&sprefix=andrew+sean+greer%2Caps%2C90&sr=8-1 ) .  I do not like much in contemporary American fiction, but so far I am quite enjoying this one.

Bernd Roeck, The World at First Light: A New History of the Renaissance ( https://www.amazon.com/World-First-Light-History-Renaissance/dp/069118383X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=139HWTI8ACNL7&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.h5r6y7D_Cl0cOLnGGub-h3pGtmsA0e9LSHI3pdvYvkM.1E7IaiDoiBsGiqQm6fe_XvV5pepNX4nae2deVMEWuhs&dib_tag=se&keywords=The+World+at+First+Light%3A+A+New+History+of+the+Renaissance&qid=1756069083&sprefix=the+world+at+first+light+a+new+history+of+the+renaissance%2Caps%2C75&sr=8-1 ) .  934 pp. of text, covers too many topics in too desultory a fashion?

Pablo A. Pena, Human Capital for Humans: An Accessible Introduction to the Economic Science of the People ( https://www.amazon.com/Human-Capital-Humans-Accessible-Introduction/dp/022684143X/ref=sr_1_1?crid=36DY11M268IBQ&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.JMwsPMav8ZkwWPusqHJwiA.q8VSw3XwlJWbeoDTkBz-_UFmpz_nXkA9Xx4RFPtwORY&dib_tag=se&keywords=Human+Capital+for+Humans%3A+An+Accessible+Introduction+to+the+Economic+Science+of+the+People&qid=1756069031&sprefix=human+capital+for+humans+an+accessible+introduction+to+the+economic+science+of+the+people%2Caps%2C75&sr=8-1 ) , is a good popular-level introduction to human capital theory.

There is Carl Benedikt Frey, How Progress Ends: Technology, Innovation, and the Fate of Nations ( https://www.amazon.com/How-Progress-Ends-Technology-Innovation/dp/0691233071/ref=sr_1_1?crid=K36AECJ1K64C&dib=eyJ2IjoiMSJ9.fyPnQQqXumNYRqlAiUWOeg.-NThroNHtht6ggaHXuJb_uL6V3MiS8mSKYub5_bym9s&dib_tag=se&keywords=How+Progress+Ends%3A+Technology%2C+Innovation%2C+and+the+Fate+of+Nations&qid=1756068997&sprefix=how+progress+ends+technology%2C+innovation%2C+and+the+fate+of+nations%2Caps%2C77&sr=8-1 ) .
