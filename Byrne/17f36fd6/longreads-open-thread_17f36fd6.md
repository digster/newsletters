One possibility is that since Russia has lots of currency reserves, a not especially liquid market, and a powerful business community, the government was keeping prices elevated until the war started. It’s a lot easier to criticize a prospective war than to criticize your country for a war that’s being actively fought (even if most people oppose it, wars seem to make politicians temporarily more popular and then continuously less popular over time). This is a speculative theory that has only circumstantial evidence, and it’s most likely that the price changes were pretty organic—sometimes a market-moving surprise is just a surprise.
