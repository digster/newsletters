Whether or not you like the music of U2, you should watch the video memoir of its lead singer Bono. The format of [Bono: Stories of Surrender](https://substack.com/redirect/f2c98550-9b54-4ce2-b406-8d35ca6180a9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o), streaming on Apple+, is peculiar. Like an autobiography, this is an auto-documentary, a documentary made by Bono about himself. It begins with spoken-word monologues by Bono, mixes in music, dips in and out of him telling his Irish family story on stage, personal confessions of his own journey, punctuated by lyrics of well-known songs to fill out his biography. It is a performance, but also a record, an oral history. It’s a film version of his mammoth book autobiography, Surrender, but I appreciated this cinematic novella for its innovative approach to a memoir. — KK

Richard Sharpe Shaver (1907–1975) was a pulp science fiction writer, best known for his articles that ran in [Amazing Stories](https://substack.com/redirect/e5e9625d-5f3b-462a-ae3c-1c792bfec27d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) in the 1940s about an evil race of humanoids living beneath the Earth’s surface. Shaver insisted the stories were non-fiction. Later in life, Shaver came to realize that the patterns in rocks were messages written by intelligent beings of antiquity, and he devoted the remainder of his life to sharing his discovery with his small cadre of admirers. [Richard Sharpe Shaver: Some Stones Are Ancient Books](https://substack.com/redirect/2b1a8b76-74fd-4ab3-af3a-2d7ad7e892ee?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) is a book of Shaver’s “rokfogos” research, complete with photos and typewritten rock-book translations. It was published by a delightful small press called The Further Reading Library. — MF
