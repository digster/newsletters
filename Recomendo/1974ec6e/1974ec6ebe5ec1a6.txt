Calendar of festivals
I try to coordinate my travel to exotic places with local festivals that occur at the same time. Trouble is, there’s been no easy way to find out which festivals are happening where. For example many traditional celebrations run on a lunar cycle. So I built a calendar that will show me all the festivals in Asia that are happening on a particular day. Or I can look at the map and see what festivals occur nearby and when. My site is called Festivo [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb6c494a-4174-4c72-856e-c7513befcabd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. It provides the local festivals of Asia—which are crammed with color, costumes, and traditions—in calendar format. It’s open and free to all, no ads. If enough people (besides myself) find it useful I will expand it to Europe, Africa, and the rest of the world. — KK
The Sound of Love
The website The Sound of Love [ https://substack.com/redirect/def9d911-32f4-42c3-a091-13e21577ea19?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] offers a beautiful way to experience love songs. For four years, the creator collected comments found beneath love songs on YouTube, carefully selecting nostalgic and touching stories about longing, love, and loss. You can read these personal stories and memories here while you listen. There’s also a Spotify playlist [ https://substack.com/redirect/491ccd1e-f9e9-4772-a209-32380abef18d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] featuring all the songs from The Sound of Love. Wide zester
While I like my thin microplane [ https://substack.com/redirect/3f8a7247-e7cc-4435-a5fd-95dce3d8dcdf?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] for super-fine zesting, I use this wider grater from Allwin [ https://substack.com/redirect/ef4244be-cf55-4b55-a65f-755dec23584d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] a lot more. The key difference is its curved blade profile — it really bites into whatever you're grating. The wider surface area also means you can get through a block of parmesan or a big knob of ginger much faster than with a traditional narrow microplane. — MF
Contain your mess
My husband is the gardener of the family, and this Repotting Mat [ https://substack.com/redirect/3d4f9c4a-a09b-490a-b460-faf580b105e8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is his favorite gardening tool. It’s quick to snap together and contains all the soil mess when potting plants. He has two in different sizes. I imagine it would work well for keeping track of small parts too, if you’re working on other projects. — CD
Leak detectors
Three separate acquaintances of mine recently suffered significant, expensive flood damage in their homes as a result of water leaks while they were away. It’s not an uncommon disaster. After some research I settled on the best recommended solution: a set of wireless water sensors from GoveeLife [ https://substack.com/redirect/2fef874a-98c9-4b14-9da2-ca5df7a316dc?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] ($100) that emit a loud alarm and send a text/email to my phone if they detect water leaking. I placed the 6 small wireless units below sinks, near toilets and water heaters, etc.—the most likely places to leak. They were very easy to pair with my home wifi and phone app. Downside is that in a few years their batteries will need to be changed. Upside is they really work and in my testing, just a small drip or a millimeter of water will elicit an immediate alarm and text/email message. — KK
Peanut butter hack — use a massage gun
After seeing a video of someone using a massage gun to force every last drop of mayonnaise out of a bottle, I decided to try in on a plastic jar of unmixed natural peanut butter. I pushed the business end of the gun against the side of the jar and marveled at how quickly the vibrations mixed the separated oil with the solids. A week later, the peanut butter remains perfectly blended. Note: I've only tested this on plastic jars; use caution with glass containers. — MF
