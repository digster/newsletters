Like the Grand Canyon, or the Pyramids, I believe Burning Man [ https://substack.com/redirect/b301e471-0838-4e55-bc10-0f071acde50b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is something you should experience at least once in your life, no matter who you are. Clever Word Game: Bracket City
I discovered a delightful five-minute word puzzle game called Bracket City [ https://substack.com/redirect/bb7c77eb-b343-4cf0-8806-884b363d111f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. The gameplay is simple — you just start typing words to solve crossword-like clues in brackets that are nested within other clues. What makes it addictive is how the clues build on each other, with each solution revealing new parts of connected puzzles. Here's the tutorial example: [where [opposite of clean] dishes pile up] or [exercise in a [game played with a cue ball]]. You can peek at the first letter of any clue if you're stuck. I’ll bet the NY Times will end up buying it. — MF

Electrician’s magic wand
I noticed several professional electricians using this thing: The Klein Non-Contact Voltage Tester [ https://substack.com/redirect/ddc8c7d7-632d-433d-8146-f266f7dc630a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is a small magic wand that beeps when it detects a live current in a wire, or a switch or a cable or anything electronic. The cool thing is that you don’t need to bare the wire or contact, you just wave this near the wire. That is both much safer and way quicker. It can detect voltages between 12 and 1,000 which will be enough for most uses. For me it has replaced several devices in my toolbox I used to use to detect a live current. — KK
