After one too many frustrating calls trying to spell out airline confirmation codes ("No, B as in Boy, not D!"), I built this [simple web tool](https://substack.com/redirect/b844e7e4-26a3-4ed6-8b69-1d1a5736dac1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o). Try it for clearly communicating flight bookings, WiFi passwords, or any string of letters and numbers over the phone. — MF

My daughter's college friends are obsessed with this simple dice game called [LCR Wild](https://substack.com/redirect/52275da1-4c98-40dd-b524-4fe4139e89e0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o). Each turn, you roll up to three dice marked L, C, R or Wild - passing chips left, right, to a center pot, or using Wild to steal from anyone. As she explains: "Chips that go to the center are eliminated from play, so the game gets more intense as the supply dwindles. You can theoretically win the center pot with three wild rolls, but I've never seen it happen!" The last player with chips wins. Perfect balance of luck and light strategy that works for 3 or 30 people. — MF

The dollar-to-yen exchange rate continues to make now a fabulous time to visit Japan. The main trick is to get out of Tokyo, but Tokyo is a good place to start for first time visitors. My favorite guide to Tokyo is one aimed at kids, but also one that anyone young at heart will benefit from: [Tokyo, City Trails](https://substack.com/redirect/d3f60d29-df1f-44ae-8f5b-e187fc5e6d84?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Lonely Planet Kids. It points you to all the actual fun things to do, not just endless food and temples. How about a toilet showroom, a chocolate bath, or a sleep capsule? In the same vein, for your guidance to cosplay, manga, anime, otaku activities, I recommend [Tokyo Geek’s Guide](https://substack.com/redirect/f19de1cb-0124-45f7-8ae1-c94c5c063730?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o). It’s a great way to experience the city and modern Japanese culture. — KK
