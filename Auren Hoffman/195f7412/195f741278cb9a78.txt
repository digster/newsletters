Here are five links worth reading…
Inside the Interconnection Queue [ https://substack.com/redirect/d8210ecf-2576-4b37-a291-190d7aadf5fd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Brian Potter  
Everyone talks about what “the grid” can and can’t do, its vulnerabilities, etc. Brian has concrete numbers: Today there are 11,000 electrical generation projects under review that could generate nearly twice the US’s current capacity.
The End of Children [ https://substack.com/redirect/4ea63edf-2323-4110-9393-b90bf563471e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Gideon Lewis-Kraus  
A deep look at fertility rates around the world with special emphasis on Korea, where strollers for dogs outsold strollers for children last year. The US is currently matching Korea’s 1980s fertility rates.
GOD Mode [ https://substack.com/redirect/8412d024-14bd-448a-8eb0-3039785f94a4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Sahil Lavingia 
In response to Paul Graham's Founder Mode [ https://substack.com/redirect/d287b834-1c80-4d8b-a7b7-af73b9b51d05?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] essay, Sahil proposes a more specific framework called "GOD mode." (He also argues true innovation requires solo founders, not the equal co-founders YC recommends.)
Bonus (Listen): Rob Henderson - luxury beliefs: how elites exploit the poor [ https://substack.com/redirect/dd786e3a-e3bb-4891-ab1c-4d0728bd5e49?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (World of Daas)
Rob Henderson, author of the bestselling memoir Troubled [ https://substack.com/redirect/c98dff57-647c-499b-a2e9-b7751e0a4cc0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (which was my favorite book I read in 2024), talks status vs money, defining elites, and how the military and marriage could encourage social mobility.
The Longevity Business Is Booming—and Its Scientists Are Clashing [ https://substack.com/redirect/dc0091cd-f250-4b68-9f47-f4b02803fbc0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Amy Dockser Marcus  ($WSJ)
As anti-aging science has gone mainstream, more and more companies have been founded with competing scientific claims.
How artificial intelligence can make board games better [ https://substack.com/redirect/93d02b48-a3c6-4c7c-a178-1e59bcb3b59e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by the $Economist
One of the best AI use cases I have seen: ironing out glitches in board game rules before they go to market.
Bonus (Preppers): Corporations dig deeper: using bunkers to secure data (and their CEOs) [ https://substack.com/redirect/06bf2c8f-83b4-4766-9697-45c4abff07b8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Rachyl Jones
Many of the early adopters of the apocalypse shelter/data center concept are crypto firms.
Bonus (Extreme): This Is Everything I’ll Eat as I Race the Iditarod [ https://substack.com/redirect/8d663170-00c5-4225-9cea-a8a0e579b9a2?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Quince Mountain 
Nearly 2000 lbs of chicken legs, raw bacon, gatorade and cat food.
