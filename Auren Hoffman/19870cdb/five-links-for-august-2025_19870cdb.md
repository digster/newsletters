Here are five links worth reading…
[The Story of Emily and Control](https://substack.com/redirect/0e29d9d7-77e0-4b7b-9b63-ce1c7c5a17b4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Scott Alexander
a livejournal classic about two identical twins who run an escalating series of experiments on themselves.
[Data is actually not a great VC-backed business](https://substack.com/redirect/191ad9b7-8eb2-4fb9-abfb-c860da9110e4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Auren Hoffman](https://open.substack.com/users/841447-auren-hoffman?utm_source=mentions)
data businesses are good businesses. They make good profits. They grow (albeit slowly) well. But they are not great businesses – and most should not take venture capital funding.
[The Consulting Crash Is Coming](https://substack.com/redirect/aff1ded0-781c-4665-b399-cf5bfd241367?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Joe Nocera](https://open.substack.com/users/235404248-joe-nocera?utm_source=mentions)
the consulting industry added value in the 1980s when companies needed help with M&A and layoffs, but now they're "bloated, overpaid, and outpaced by AI" - making big consulting firms ripe for disruption as their traditional “efficiency advice” becomes obsolete.
[The Man Who Beat IBM](https://substack.com/redirect/be21ad28-2937-4b95-b15b-096dbd62a333?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Gareth Edwards
when IBM called Compaq CEO Rod Canion in 1988 offering to split the PC market between them, Canion had two choices - say yes and become incredibly rich, or say no and bet everything on an open standard that IBM was trying to kill. he chose to go to war with a company that “couldn’t be beat.”
Bonus (Listen) [FJ Labs Founder Fabrice Grinda](https://substack.com/redirect/388ce2c2-f92d-416a-a5b1-b9e2bf3a1e0b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) on [World of DaaS](https://substack.com/redirect/dbfe7571-cecc-46a9-a3c8-a4f843766c25?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) pod
Fabrice runs one of the world's most active VC firms with investments in 1,200+ startups, using a hyper-diversified approach driven by intellectual curiosity rather than ownership targets .
[Can Sunlight Cure Disease?](https://substack.com/redirect/dd2ab42c-d438-4c13-9642-a8e39440b586?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Rowan Jacobsen
scientists are discovering that UV light triggers reactions in the skin that can calm autoimmune diseases like multiple sclerosis, with early trials showing dramatic improvements in patients using simple light boxes.
Bonus (Mysteries): [Why Hal Finney Is Not Satoshi Nakamoto](https://substack.com/redirect/bcc1a44c-9adf-422f-8d02-6641c7a9bb38?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Dominic Frisby](https://open.substack.com/users/2808281-dominic-frisby?utm_source=mentions)
a deep dive into the compelling evidence (similar writing style, cryptographic expertise, first Bitcoin responder and receiver) that software developer Hal Finney was the creator of Bitcoin.
Bonus (Life lesson) [Priced‑In Flaws: from cars to colleagues to couples](https://substack.com/redirect/ba4f77f9-f492-4852-9738-f8b1436109c1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Auren Hoffman](https://open.substack.com/users/841447-auren-hoffman?utm_source=mentions)
complaining about known flaws (whether products, employees, or spouses) is unfair. If you hired someone knowing they miss deadlines, that weaknesses should already be priced into your decision.
