Here are five links worth reading…
The dawn of the post-literate society [ https://substack.com/redirect/1b69e82d-c785-4b70-886a-6e9726d29ab0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by James Marriott  
The revolution in mass literacy reshaped the 18th century and gave rise to democracy. We’re in the middle of a “screen revolution” which could be even more consequential.
The Right to Bear Technology [ https://substack.com/redirect/26b708d1-8785-4c1f-b0ac-11cbe3584cc5?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Katherine Boyle  
Silicon Valley’s origin story as the innovation lab for midcentury defense contractors is deeply tied to the Second Amendment, the only constitutional protection for a specific technology.
The Lesson of 1929 [ https://substack.com/redirect/deb2bcf2-5f40-4c10-9bbe-f4591d7eb090?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Andrew Ross Sorkin
“Debt is the almost singular through line behind every major financial crisis,” and debt is starting to look dangerous.
Venturehouse Group Found Mark Ein [ https://substack.com/redirect/a1d9341c-3ca6-47b7-8cc1-427ef0525118?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on World of DaaS [ https://substack.com/redirect/a1d9341c-3ca6-47b7-8cc1-427ef0525118?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] pod
Mark Ein, who helped build six billion-dollar companies, argues that investing across different industries creates advantages because lessons from one sector can unlock hidden value in another.
Malaysia: Truly Asia [ https://substack.com/redirect/13d7ec64-b828-4b4e-baba-73213d68f739?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Noah Smith  
Malaysia quietly became a semiconductor powerhouse exporting $74 billion in chips annually (nearly double the U.S. despite having just 35 million people) while successfully creating a peaceful, diverse society by constructing an entirely new racial identity to unify multiple ethnic groups.
What I’ve Learned About Toddlers [ https://substack.com/redirect/11193298-6897-4467-9f06-4dc3607e0ba4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Tim Urban
Two years after his first child, Tim shares some reflections on life with a toddler. “You can be simultaneously completely obsessed with and dramatically bored by the same person.”
Bonus (Optimistic): Sharpie Found a Way to Make Pens More Cheaply—By Manufacturing Them in the U.S. [ https://substack.com/redirect/9e2bdd9a-0292-4f01-85c5-7f224197dca3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Natasha Kahn
How Newell Brands onshored the manufacturing of Sharpie pens to a 37-year-old factory in Tennessee, while enhancing quality and without raising prices.
Bonus (Useful): 21 Facts About Throwing Good Parties [ https://substack.com/redirect/566f8b99-3b6c-4ccc-9408-8bfe85e6aca0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Angela Chen
A straightforward tactical manual for the vital public service of throwing parties. If you’ve never thrown a true party (or a good one), this is a great place to start.
