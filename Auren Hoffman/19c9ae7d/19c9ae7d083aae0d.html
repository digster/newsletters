here are five links worth reading in March…

Going Founder Mode on Cancer by Elliot Hershberg

GitLab founder Sid Sijbrandij refused clinical trials, built a 1,000-page Google Doc to manage his own cancer treatment, and is now in full remission.

The Last Human Edge by Dom Cooke

A detailed profile on Henry Ellenbogen -- the most successful tech investor you’ve never heard of, who turned T. Rowe Price’s lowest ranked small-cap fund into its best performing one.

Why I Run by Nicholas Thompson

The CEO of The Atlantic holds the American record for men 45+ in the mile -- this is his memoir on running, fatherhood, and what happens when the person you’re racing against is gone.

The untold story of what fuels Michael Jordan’s legendary fire by Wright Thompson

Tracing six generations of Jordans in rural North Carolina to find the source code behind the most competitive person in sports history.

End Game Play by Will Manidis

Chess is boring because computers “solved” the opening and the middle, so pros just memorize moves to reach the end. We’re doing the same in war and tech.

Bonus (optimistic): How will the miracle happen today by Kevin Kelly

Kevin Kelly hitchhiked to work every day and was never late. Someone always picked him up.

Bonus (listen): Nirav Tolia on Summation (formerly the World of DaaS pod)

Nirav co-founded Nextdoor, grew it to 100 million users across 345,000 neighborhoods, left, watched the stock drop 80%, and came back to fix it.

Bonus (warning): Founders, you’re quietly being screwed by VCs

There’s a clause in your term sheet that can silently strip your voting rights even if you own a majority of the company.
