Here are five links worth reading…
[Face it: you’re a crazy person](https://substack.com/redirect/bd6e9d74-8bb6-46f8-a9ab-0c4ebc978657?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Adam Mastroianni](https://open.substack.com/users/69354522-adam-mastroianni?utm_source=mentions)
A simple guide to unpacking any dream with ruthless specificity before chasing it. Adam gives some of the clearest, most straightforward career advice that anyone, in just about any field, can use.
[How Pickleball Explains American Culture](https://substack.com/redirect/fe9cdcb5-b548-42cc-b0ef-d1e7e4f92502?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Derek Thompson](https://open.substack.com/users/157561-derek-thompson?utm_source=mentions)
Pickleball, the fastest growing sport in America, is now more popular than baseball. A case study in how American fitness fads follow fashion cycles and “familiar surprise.”
[How I taught my 3-year-old to read like a 9-year-old](https://substack.com/redirect/63ef98b1-d44c-4ff3-a05c-9fb8ddfb146a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Erik Hoel](https://open.substack.com/users/9379583-erik-hoel?utm_source=mentions)
Practical methods + data to get your kid to “the only literacy milestone worth caring about,” reading for pleasure.
[The Only Right Way to Ask for an Intro (Everything Else Is Wrong)](https://substack.com/redirect/e4da37a2-f684-4bb3-8df5-4e42de9bc039?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o)
My definitive, battle-tested, no-BS guide to asking for introductions, complete with email templates.
Bonus (Listen): [OpenAI CSO Jason Kwon: Power, Policy and the Race to Scale](https://substack.com/redirect/c23f95b9-f877-4403-8bfb-0af77b6e5dcd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) on [World of Daas](https://substack.com/redirect/c23f95b9-f877-4403-8bfb-0af77b6e5dcd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) pod
Jason Kwon lays out the real AI bottlenecks (compute and power at the industry level, bet selection and org design at the company level) plus why U.S. policy should focus on energy, faster adoption, and attracting top talent.
[Should We Have Kept The American Empire?](https://substack.com/redirect/8d558a93-cba0-45bb-b481-d7c301b72f11?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Maxwell Tabarrok](https://open.substack.com/users/18317550-maxwell-tabarrok?utm_source=mentions)
If the U.S. had retained more of its 1918 holdings, would economic and political outcomes have been better for those territories and for America?
[How to Be Great? Just Be Good, Repeatably](https://substack.com/redirect/5cc29dd0-2506-4042-8a6f-84034079b413?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Steph Smith
Greatness is about consistency over time, where small repeatable actions compound into major gains, though most people quit at the inevitable local minimums before reaching the next peak.
Bonus (travel): [Tracking Down the ‘Real’ Orient Express Is Complicated](https://substack.com/redirect/019b1308-502a-4594-b419-50eb109ed281?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Laura Dannen Redman
Exploring the three different trains and one yacht that call themselves the Orient Express (none of which are Hercule’s Poirot’s original Orient Express)
Bonus (high speed travel): [Meet The Guy Who Drove Across The U.S. In A Record 28 Hours 50 Minutes](https://substack.com/redirect/b367d2fa-d911-42b2-9c70-63c2c1f59465?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by Doug Demuro
Details on the real Cannonball run: how Ed Bolian shattered a decades-old cross country speed record— an obsessive masterclass in planning, risk, and optimization.
HT: Theo Baker
|Bonus: [The Chess Cheating Epidemic](https://substack.com/redirect/35da909d-40c6-437f-9841-14c2ec52f2b5?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) by [Danny Rensch](https://open.substack.com/users/124682782-danny-rensch?utm_source=mentions)
Cheating has become rampant in elite chess since the rise of AI. The Chief Chess Officer of Chess.com shares the story of the algorithm they developed to catch cheaters, and how it was used against an American grandmaster.
