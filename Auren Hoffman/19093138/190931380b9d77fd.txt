Here are five links worth reading…
Why we stopped making Einsteins [ https://substack.com/redirect/9a31baad-5e20-44ae-ae9e-f4bd9b267806?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Erik Hoel
Many have observed that we don’t have stand-out intellectuals (like Beethoven, Einstein, Tolstoy and Darwin) in any field today. Erik Hoel makes an interesting case that the culprit is the decline of one-on-one tutoring, which was the common method of educating elites for thousands of years. 
The B Lane Swimmer [ https://substack.com/redirect/e3bc259c-2a90-4ad1-9394-e3b85ad6cc83?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Holly Witteman
On status, sorting and prestige in sports (and everywhere else). The people you might expect to be the most competitive are often the most welcoming, and vice versa. 
Bumble’s Whitney Wolfe Herd says your dating ‘AI concierge’ will soon date hundreds of other people’s ‘concierges’ for you [ https://substack.com/redirect/b1d286d6-b2f6-480a-91ef-6e35138fcc4d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Eleanor Pringle
There are already AI dating simulators and chat assistants to help with dating app messaging. The founder of Bumble thinks the next step could be outsourcing the early stages of dating to AI.  Imagine on going on 3 AI dates with someone all before you go on your first date. 
Bonus (Listen): Shopify President Harley Finkelstein - The Ecommerce Entrepreneur Class [ https://substack.com/redirect/5727928e-2d82-4c46-9ca2-0179c8f43248?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on World of DaaS podcast
Breaking down the “golden age of entrepreneurship” with someone who has thought more about it than just about anyone: ecommerce trends, modern brands, and innovation in the 2010s.

The West Coast’s Fanciest Stolen Bikes Are Getting Trafficked by One Mastermind in Jalisco, Mexico [ https://substack.com/redirect/41a1f4a7-9ca3-4ad7-9826-2dcc81b6bf7b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Christopher Solomon
As bikes have become fancier and more expensive, bike theft has professionalized. How a simple crime of opportunity became a cross-border racket running bikes 2,000+ miles from California to Mexico.  
Bonus (Interesting): China Is Mixed on Elon Musk—but They Sure Love His Mom [ https://substack.com/redirect/588d5509-18b6-48c9-b26f-600b4259c798?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Selina Cheng
Maye Musk gets plenty of attention in her own right as a model and dietician with 2 million instagram followers. But in a culture deeply focused on the success of their children, she’s a “perfect idol.”
Bonus (Hopeful):  [ https://substack.com/redirect/3e72f3fa-4959-4fa8-ab04-96164c3771eb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]Baby born deaf can hear after breakthrough gene therapy [ https://substack.com/redirect/3e72f3fa-4959-4fa8-ab04-96164c3771eb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by University of Cambridge
Opal Sandy from Oxfordshire was born deaf, but after a global gene therapy trial, she has “near normal” hearing in her treated ear and can respond to her parents’ voices.
