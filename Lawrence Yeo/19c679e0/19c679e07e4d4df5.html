Learn how to tell better stories.

You might think of the word "storytelling" and think it doesn't apply to you. That it's only for novelists who want to write about magical beasts or hot romances.

But that's because you think storytelling is about complex plot lines, 3-act structures, and Hero's Journeys. The truth is that storytelling is much simpler than that, and by applying it to your work, you will have all the tools you need to draw people to your ideas.

If your writing isn't resonating (whether with yourself or with others), it's a skill issue. The skill, however, isn't to learn how to write flowery sentences or to copy your favorite author. Rather, it's to learn how to take your own ideas, to connect them in novel ways, and to present them in a compelling manner.

Everyone has a wealth of knowledge that can benefit so many people. The key is to know how to present it so that the benefit is made obviously known.
