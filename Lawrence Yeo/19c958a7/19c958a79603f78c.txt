a lot of direction to produce something worth caring about.

People can smell AI slop from a mile away because its output
converges upon the mean. If you ask it to draft an article for
you on a given topic, it will be the most average thing you’ve
ever read. And these days, average is just another word for
slop.

tell stories. I view storytelling as the meta-skill, or the

limited by your ability to clearly articulate what you want, and
why others should be interested in that as well.

It’s why every product requires marketing. Or why companies must
have a clear mission. Or why any book must have a theme.

Any tool we’ve created - no matter how profound it is - needs a
story that people can grasp and remember. Only then will people
be curious enough to venture into the specifics.
