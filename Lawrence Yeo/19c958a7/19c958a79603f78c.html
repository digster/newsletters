A Thought I’ve Been Pondering

As AI continues to change the creative landscape, my position has been to embrace and harness its power. I’ve been using it to build tools that help with my business, to act as a thought partner, to build apps for my household, and a lot more.

But one thing I’ve found - time and time again - is that it needs a lot of direction to produce something worth caring about.

People can smell AI slop from a mile away because its output converges upon the mean. If you ask it to draft an article for you on a given topic, it will be the most average thing you’ve ever read. And these days, average is just another word for slop.

The only way to unlock AI’s true power is to understand how to tell stories. I view storytelling as the meta-skill, or the skill that sits above all other skills. Your usage of AI will be limited by your ability to clearly articulate what you want, and why others should be interested in that as well.

It’s why every product requires marketing. Or why companies must have a clear mission. Or why any book must have a theme.

Any tool we’ve created - no matter how profound it is - needs a story that people can grasp and remember. Only then will people be curious enough to venture into the specifics.

The best part is that storytelling principles are timeless, as the tapestry of human nature weaves its way through every technology.

Always has been the case, and always will.
