A Thought I’ve Been Pondering
The end of the year is always a time for reflection, whether it takes an active form (through journaling) or a passive one (through resting). There’s a collective period of slowing down and taking inventory of one’s life, which sets the intention for how you’d like to proceed once the new year begins.
What I’ve realized is that this period is a worldwide recalibration of [our inner compasses](https://b2cc74dc.click.convertkit-mail4.com/qdunqv78mqb7h4q9mw8ilh820mwkkb4hp5q7q/48hvhehmk0lkz5iq/aHR0cHM6Ly9nZW5pLnVzL2lubmVyY29tcGFzcw==). It’s one of the few stretches in the year where you’re not just encouraged to direct your attention inward, but are expected to do so. The expectation of a quick reply fades, the temptation to put in overtime subsides, and the noise of the world loses its luster.
What remains is just you, free from the conditioning that usually feels all-encompassing. The amazing thing is that this dynamic is occurring at scale, so this is a shared state of mind across many timezones and cultures. It’s not just you that can hit pause on the relentless pursuit of your goals, but any number of people in any given environment as well.
This moment doesn’t last long, which is what makes it special. So I encourage you to make the most of it while we all have it together.
