Reflections on The Inner Compass

About 6 months ago, I released my first book, The Inner Compass. By all external measures, the book has been a success (especially as a self-published book). It’s sold thousands of copies, received hundreds of reviews, and is being translated into multiple languages.

But all of that seems secondary when I think about the kinds of messages people have been sending me. I consistently receive messages from real people telling me about real, heavy problems that my book has helped them with.

Consider this email I got a few days ago:

What metric captures the essence of this emotion? Trying to use as a 5-star Amazon review as a surrogate for it is just laughable.

I’ve been reflecting on this because prior to publishing the book, I was spiraling with self-doubt. I thought the book was good, but that it wasn’t ready to be out yet. I thought it needed more refinement, more stories, more everything. But the truth is, that desire for it to be perfect was just fear in disguise. It felt safe to be in my own little corner working on it, but safety isn’t what gets your creation out into the world.

It all comes down to the courage to trust yourself. To trust that you’ve done what you can, and that now it’s time for your imperfections to come into contact with others. And because everyone contains imperfections of their own, they will relate to the fact that you have them too.

I felt this before publishing my book, before starting my Substack newsletter, before any creative project, really. My hope is that you allow a similar texture of courage to carry you through 2026 as well.
