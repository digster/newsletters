Just a little reminder about our paywall...

 

Cates Holderness

 
 
 

Hi, Managing Editor Cates here,

We recently announced some changes to Garbage Day — most importantly, that we’re shifting from two free issues a week to one, the Monday issue. We’re not going to flood your inbox about this, but given that the change happened this week we figured a reminder would be nice. Hope you don’t mind that we bug you about throwing us a few bucks from time to time.

We’re putting more behind the paywall for a few reasons, but a big one is that we don’t want to rely upon advertisers whose values don’t align with ours or yours, our readers. Garbage Day has also expanded (thanks for the paycheck Ryan!) and we have a lot of ideas for how we continue to grow, and it’s only possible thanks to y’all. So consider upgrading if you want more Garbage Day in your inbox and access to the Discord where a delightful group of people hang out and share, well, a lot. It’s a fun community that I’ve gotten to know well over the last few months, even if there are far too many requests to reveal Ryan’s shoe size.

It’s been a busy couple of weeks for us. We wanted to show you the kind of work that a paid subscription supports.

Garbage Day and Panic World’s coverage of the murder of Charlie Kirk was shared widely: from The Telegraph, The Guardian, and The Financial Times, to r/technology. Ryan also went on The Bulwark podcast, PBS News Hour, and NPR’s On The Media, the last of which was very exciting for me personally, as the number of NPR pledge drive tote bags hanging on my door can attest. The coverage also made its way to Croatian, German, and Spanish outlets. Garbage Day is in a particularly unique position to contextualize and make sense of the current news, and we couldn’t get it out there without the support of our readers and paid subscribers. So thank you!
