## How Far Will The Trump Administration Go?

Alex Pretti, a nurse and VA worker, was shot and killed by Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents on Saturday. According to [multiple videos](https://www.bellingcat.com/news/2026/01/25/alex-pretti-analysing-footage-of-minneapolis-cbp-shooting/) filmed by [bystanders](https://www.dropsitenews.com/p/customs-border-patrol-agent-kills-minneapolis-nurse-video-analysis), Pretti was trying to protect a woman from ICE agents when they tackled him to the ground and shot him nearly a dozen times.

Not that it actually matters — no one deserves to be extrajudicially murdered in the street — Pretti [was known](https://www.cbsnews.com/minnesota/news/alex-pretti-veteran-final-salute-video/) as a kind and decent man. Yes, he did have a gun on him, which was holstered at the time ICE tackled him to the ground. He also had a permit for it.

According [to an affidavit](https://storage.courtlistener.com/recap/gov.uscourts.mnd.229758/gov.uscourts.mnd.229758.109.0.pdf) filed over the weekend, agents didn’t administer CPR to Pretti and, instead, were audibly counting the bullet holes. (And [it looks like](https://x.com/pamphletsy/status/2015120379896058326?s=12) at least one of the agents was doing a fucked-up little jig in the street, as well.) After celebrating Pretti’s murder, the agents arrested as many witnesses as they could. One witness [told CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/minnesota/news/minneapolis-alex-pretti-shooting-witness-arrested/) that he was detained for hours and was labeled a “USC,” or “United States citizens in custody.”

Not only have residents have barricaded the site of Pretti’s murder and turned it into another living memorial/autonomous zone, as they did with the neighborhood where Renee Good was killed. But there are now residents [patrolling the area](https://x.com/ScooterCasterNY/status/2015227055806288302) with assault weapons. There was also a brief showdown between ICE agents and local police, after Minneapolis law enforcement was asked to leave the scene. The city’s police chief, Brian O’Hara, [ordered officers](https://www.startribune.com/ice-raids-minnesota/601546426) to stay put. Meanwhile, Minnesota Gov. Tim Walz finally sent the National Guard into the Twin Cities. They’re wearing reflective vests to differentiate themselves from ICE agents and were [handing out](https://x.com/anthonycabassa/status/2015515107363549485?s=12) coffee and donuts at the Whipple Federal Building yesterday. To say nothing of the one-man army that is X/Twitter/Bluesky superposter Will Stancil, who has been stalking ICE agents all week, while also [begging ICE online](https://bsky.app/profile/whstancil.bsky.social/post/3mcng64fycc27) to arrest him for filming them to prove that it’s actually illegal.

View image: (https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/39bc2065-0f65-4027-ad40-5d3d25b05731/GettyImages-2257539872.jpg?t=1769445698)
Caption: (Photo by Madison Thorn/Anadolu via Getty Images)

The Trump administration’s response to Pretti’s death has evolved considerably since Saturday. Immediately after his death, Vice President JD Vance, in a [post on X](https://x.com/jdvance/status/2015285455940399296?s=12), tried to blame the “chaos” in Minnesota on “far-left agitators.” And Homeland Security Advisor Stephen Miller [wrote on X](https://x.com/StephenM/status/2015132322840850461), “A would-be assassin tried to murder federal law enforcement and the official Democrat account sides with the terrorists.” There’s a Community Note underneath that post with links to all the videos that prove that’s complete and total horse shit.

Attorney General Pam Bondi [wrote](https://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2026/01/24/us/pam-bondi-walz-doc.html) to Walz that all of this will stop if the state hands over its voter rolls. As Democratic strategist Matt McDermott [wrote on X](https://x.com/mattmfm/status/2015218874111639976?s=12) over the weekend, “They’re openly using state violence as a bargaining chip to seize election infrastructure.” And as NPR’s Stephen Fowler pointed out [in an X post](https://x.com/stphnfwlr/status/2015241244436320514?s=12), the Department of Government Efficiency [was reportedly](https://www.npr.org/2026/01/23/nx-s1-5684185/doge-data-social-security-privacy) very interested in collecting voter rolls as a way to overturn election results. Bondi’s letter [is currently being](https://www.ms.now/rachel-maddow-show/maddowblog/minnesota-quickly-rejects-ag-pam-bondis-request-for-the-states-voter-rolls) torn apart in Minnesota courts right now.

For a brief moment, it seemed as if President Donald Trump was going to invoke the Insurrection Act, [writing](https://truthsocial.com/@realDonaldTrump/posts/115951636521315703) on Truth Social, “Much of what you’re witnessing is a COVER UP for this Theft and Fraud. The Mayor and the Governor are inciting Insurrection, with their pompous, dangerous, and arrogant rhetoric!” But the administration [signaled](https://www.wsj.com/politics/policy/trump-says-administration-is-reviewing-everything-about-minneapolis-shooting-a501f48e?gaa_at=eafs&gaa_n=AWEtsqfC605c1CWWpDa-ctuhC5fxZ5i44pCU75PGtApPQB38M-zJqK20lWNnG7rpV4k%3D&gaa_ts=69779275&gaa_sig=PjaeNM4zbHnCe3vVVROAa_ALjgEGvdkOf-ugzxGT2Iad3SEIsHfbJ2WWWIzVuBgXCzbZh36HbfHTlBcQXiiDdA%3D%3D) to _The Wall Street Journal_ that ICE agents will leave the state at some point. And this morning Trump [announced](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/26/trump-tom-homan-minnesota-alex-pretti-ice.html) that he’s sending Border Czar Tom Homan to the state, hopefully to manage some kind of end to the occupation. Walz and Trump also [reportedly had a call](https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/donald-trump/trump-positive-tone-tim-walz-phone-minnesota-governor-rcna255967) today that was “positive.”

It wasn’t just the Republicans who seemed unable to figure out exactly what to say about Pretti’s death. The Democrats had a fairly chaotic weekend, as well. Connecticut Sen. Chris Murphy [wrote on X](https://x.com/ChrisMurphyCT/status/2015108433322594571) that “Congress should not fund this version of ICE — that is seeking confirmation, chaos and dystopia.” Oh, interesting, Chris, what “version” of ICE should they fund?

Meanwhile, [pathologically millennial](https://www.politico.com/news/magazine/2024/07/01/marie-gluesenkamp-perez-washington-democrats-profile-00164188) Washington Rep. Marie Gluesenkamp Perez was one of seven House Democrats left holding the hot potato this weekend. These seven absolute losers [voted](https://newrepublic.com/post/205604/seven-democrats-voted-keep-funding-ice) to increase the Department of Homeland Security’s funding literally last Thursday. So, needless to say, [her statement](https://gluesenkampperez.house.gov/posts/rep-marie-gluesenkamp-perez-statement-on-second-fatal-shooting-in-minneapolis) on Pretti’s death [hasn’t been](https://x.com/RepMGP/status/2015239064195145808) well-received. One X user [called her](https://x.com/MashaParty/status/2015481648528179404), “a wolf in meep's clothing.” Another one of the seven ICE Dems, New York Rep. Tom Suozzi, [apologized](https://x.com/migold/status/2015804708707795339) to his constituents in an email this morning. I honestly hope the Democratic “rotating villain” theory [is correct](https://jacobin.com/2021/11/conservative-democrats-block-biden-agenda-corporate-lobbying-careers), because if it’s not these guys are either really stupid or just think we are. 

Thankfully, Senate Democrats [are saying](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/25/minneapolis-shooting-government-shutdown.html) that they plan to vote no on the bill. And Republicans refuse to take out the Department of Homeland Security funding. Which means it’s increasingly likely we’re heading into another government shutdown by the end of the week.

If only the Democrats were as clear on this issue as the, uh, Libertarian Party? In one of those big dumb long posts [on X](https://x.com/lpnational/status/2015198712423981183?s=12) this weekend, Party Chair Steven Nekhaila wrote, “What we are seeing in the United States is the rise of the warrior cop. Masked federal agents donning multicam and plate carriers, injected into civilian life with a GWOT mentality. This is no routine law enforcement. This is occupation-style policing.” Concluding the statement with, “Abolish ICE. Reform the broken immigration system. Do not give the police state an inch.” I’m glad libertarians could take a break from their busy schedule of arguing about age of consent laws to weigh in.

As I wrote [on Friday](https://www.garbageday.email/p/the-memes-will-continue), Springfield, Ohio, was the prototype for Minneapolis, not some stupid right-wing misadventure like the Cracker Barrel logo or Sydney Sweeney’s jeans. The Trump campaign’s swarm in 2024 around the conspiracy theory that Haitian immigrants were eating cats and dogs was disorganized, but clearly an early experiment. To see what it would take to engineer a moral panic, how far you would need to push a community before you can fully dehumanize them through political violence and propaganda. A hazy first attempt that they are now following up with the full force of the federal government, moving at the speed of the internet. But Springfield didn’t back down, and, now, neither has Minneapolis. The only question that remains is whether the Trump administration will or not.

## There’s A Big Clawdbot Hack Coming

There’s a hot new AI tool out there called Clawdbot. It’s basically [an open source wrapper](https://www.macstories.net/stories/clawdbot-showed-me-what-the-future-of-personal-ai-assistants-looks-like/) for generative AI that let’s you build an AI agent into whatever chat app you use most. ## Kanye West Apologizes, Is A Redditor Now

_—by Adam Bumas_

Kanye West took out a [full-page ad](https://www.vanityfair.com/style/story/kanye-west-antisemitism-apology-wsj-ad) in the _Wall Street Journal _today. The ad is an apology addressed “To Those I’ve Hurt,” specifically his use of Nazi symbols and imagery. West has been making amends for a while, [meeting](https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/kanye-west-apology-jewish-rabbi-antisemitic-comments-1236107138/) with a major Orthodox rabbi last November. This is a more direct apology, but it’s also an ad — West has a new album out on Friday. And that’s not the only factor here.

As we [reported](https://www.garbageday.email/p/am-i-too-stupid-to-vibe-code), West’s song “Heil Hitler” has reentered the [cultural conversation](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/01/20/us/tate-brothers-miami-heil-hitler-kanye-west.html). Earlier this month, a Miami nightclub played it at the request of Nick Fuentes, Andrew Tate, and Clavicular (more on that last one below). Whether it’s for moral or financial reasons, West is clearly trying to distance himself from the hate-filled sewer carnival he’s performed in regularly for years. Which is why it’s a sober, thoughtful piece in the _Wall Street Journal _instead of his first stab at this from last year: an X post saying he’s “[done with antisemitism](https://www.billboard.com/music/rb-hip-hop/kanye-west-done-with-antisemitism-1235978704/).”

West was more or less the first person at the apex of mainstream pop culture to be [terminally online](https://www.reddit.com/r/Kanye/comments/kt00y0/i_know_this_is_an_old_tweet_but_i_decided_to/). We talked about it on [Panic World](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=n_-CcJn2yNg), but not long ago it was sort of a cultural oddity that he would choose to apologize for interrupting Taylor Swift at the Grammys with a [blog post](https://ew.com/article/2009/09/13/kanye-taylor-swift-apology/), or release songs sampling [obscure YouTube memes](https://www.distractify.com/p/globglogabgalab-kanye). Now that we all live online, it’s interesting to see where he’s decided to retreat. In his apology, the only online reference is to “Reddit forums,” where he says he got help coming to terms with his bipolar diagnosis.

It’s definitely better for everyone than [pirating OnlyFans](https://www.rap-up.com/article/2021/11/12/kanye-west-bootlegs-onlyfans), the only other thing we know West has used Reddit for.
