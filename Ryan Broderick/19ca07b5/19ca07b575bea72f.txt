## Here’s A Big List Of Indie Media For You

Last night, Netflix officially [withdrew](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/26/media/netflix-paramount-wbd) their bid for Warner Bros Discovery. This was probably the right decision, financially, for the streamer. But it is, without question, a disaster for the country. Not that Netflix would have been a much better owner for WBD, seeing as how they still want to kill movie theaters. At the very least, though, they wouldn't be directly in the pocket of the Trump administration.

WBD’s likely new owner, Paramount Skydance, owned by David Ellison, failson of Oracle Chief Technology Officer Larry Ellison, has made [no qualms](https://www.forbes.com/sites/saradorn/2026/02/24/paramounts-david-ellison-tapped-as-state-of-the-union-guest-after-trump-wades-into-warner-bros-battle/) about his allegiance to Trump World. To say nothing of the Ellisons’ [connections](https://puck.news/newsletter_content/epstein-bombshells-marc-rowans-daf-an-ellison-update/) to the Epstein network. Oh, also, the elder Ellison [now controls](https://www.npr.org/2025/12/21/nx-s1-5649856/the-ellisons-add-tiktoks-u-s-business-to-their-entertainment-empire) the US version of TikTok. In the words of one CBS News staffer [quoted](https://www.theguardian.com/media/2026/feb/27/cbs-news-cnn-paramount-warner-bros-employees) by _The Guardian _this morning, “Fuck, fuck, fuck.”

It’s not just the news that’s under attack, but the entire American media industry. And the only way we can really fight back is with our attention — or lack of it. Last night, on Bluesky, _The Onion_’s Ben Collins [asked folks](https://bsky.app/profile/bencollins.bsky.social/post/3mfsexbsnes2u) what indie media they were supporting these days. I asked my team the same question. Here is, in no particular order, some great newsletters, websites, podcasts, and video teams we’re all following that you should check out. It’s not exhaustive — we’d love to hear about your favorites — but it’s a good starting point for anyone who wants to get out from under the thumb of the new state media machine.

[_Aftermath_](https://aftermath.site/)_ _— An excellent worker-owned gaming site founded by former _Kotaku_ writers.

[_Today In Tabs_](https://www.todayintabs.com/)_ _— Newsletter long-hauler (and National Magazine Award [nominee](https://bsky.app/profile/greenwell.bsky.social/post/3mfs3m435hs25)) Rusty Foster's unmissable daily round up of, well, various tabs on his computer.

[_Platformer_](https://www.platformer.news/)_ _— You all probably already read it, but it’s an essential tech newsletter founded by _The Verge'_s Casey Newton. 

[_Citation Needed_](https://www.citationneeded.news/) — A deeply skeptical and analytical newsletter from Molly White covering the murky world of cryptocurrency.

[_Hell Gate_](https://hellgatenyc.com/)_ _— A fantastic worker-owned local New York City newsroom.

[_Link In Bio_](https://www.milkkarten.net/)_ _— A terrific newsletter from Rachel Karten covering the world of social media marketing and digital advertising.

[_User Mag_](https://www.usermag.co/)_ _— Taylor Lorenz's newsletter and podcast, which has recently started a great series on the death of Section 230. 

[_Status_](https://www.status.news/)_ _— Unmissable media reporting from CNN reporter Oliver Darcy.

[_Deez Links_](https://www.deezlinks.com/)_ _— The newsletter that, in many ways, started all of this. Delightful and incisive link roundups from Vanity Fair correspondent Delia Cai.

[_404 Media_](https://www.404media.co/) — The best tech newsroom in the world right now. We are jealous of everything they do lol.

[_Search Engine_](https://www.searchengine.show/)_ _— Podcaster PJ Vogt’s super fun followup to _Reply All_.

[_Read Max_](https://maxread.substack.com/)_ _— _Gawker'_s Max Read. Read Max Read. 

[_Never Post_](https://www.neverpo.st/)_ _— A terrific independent podcast (and production company) specializing in all things extremely online. 

[_1-900-HOTDOG_](https://1900hotdog.com)_ _— For anyone who still misses _Cracked _(it’s OK, you can admit it). 

[_More Perfect Union_](https://perfectunion.us/)_ _— Emmy-winning, nonprofit newsroom focusing on labor that goes for the jugular in every video.

[_Howtown_](https://www.youtube.com/@Howtown)_ _— Truly delightful science journalism from former _Vox _and NPR journalists._ _

[_The Handbasket_](https://www.thehandbasket.co/)_ — _Marisa Kabas has broken too many stories about Trump administration skullduggery to list here.

[_65,000_](https://65000blog.substack.com) — A mix of serious and completely silly news about niche online creative communities, from one of Adam's old _Know Your Meme _colleagues.

[_Ken Klippenstein_](https://www.kenklippenstein.com/) — Ken is one of the few independents with the cred and connections to regularly get scoops about what governments are doing behind the scenes.

[_Defector_](https://defector.com) — The flagship of our weird little flotilla. Amazing writing about sports and more, with genuine community among both the writers and readers (you have to pay to comment).

[_Drop Site News_](https://www.dropsitenews.com/)_ _— Journalist Ryan Grim’s new nonprofit investigative outlet that has run circles around the big boys digging into the Epstein files.

[Cool Zone Media](https://www.coolzonemedia.com/) — Podcast network founded by _Behind The Bastards’ _Robert Evans, including shows from Jamie Loftus, Bridget Todd, and more.

[_Some More News_](https://www.youtube.com/@SMN)_ _— Comedian and former _Cracked_ contributor Cody Johnston's delivers very funny and very insightful tear downs of the week’s news.

[_Tech Dirt_](https://techdirt.com)_ _— Maybe the longest-serving Big Tech watchdog. News about the politics and policies surrounding new technology from people who understand how it works.

[_The Present Age_](https://www.readtpa.com/)_ — _Insightful analysis on news and politics from Parker Molloy.

[_Spitfire News_](https://spitfirenews.com/) — Former NBC News reporter Kat Tenbarge has gone independent, continuing her incredible coverage of the post-Me Too era.

[_Local News International _](https://lni.media/)— A newsletter and video series from _The Washington Post_’s former video guy, Dave Jorgenson. (And most of his team from _WaPo_, as well!)

[Dropout](https://www.dropout.tv/) — This subscriber-based comedy streaming platform started by former _College Humor_ alums is one of the only bright spots in the world of entertainment right now.

[Nebula](https://nebula.tv/) — What started as a platform for YouTubers to paywall content has recently expanded into original titles like their excellent series _Abolish Everything! _and, like Dropout, could very well be the studio system of the future.
