[Google is teaching robots to think for themselves (4 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.fastcompany.com%2F90779334%2Fgoogle-robot-research-everyday-robots-palm-saycan%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000182ab4fe7f0-1f2041ca-6dc5-4f7b-b106-fb5677c45fc2-000000/P8W5JWsUwvwfrAzHKDfwfO3vzXFmZ00is0f6ff1T4i8=262)

[Microsoft Dev Box now ready for developers to try cloud-powered workstations (2 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.theverge.com%2F2022%2F8%2F16%2F23307742%2Fmicrosoft-dev-box-preview-cloud-workstations%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000182ab4fe7f0-1f2041ca-6dc5-4f7b-b106-fb5677c45fc2-000000/LUrlpoSE4g6QUrpBq3U2kzYjcYrcdgktABx0bt3OVgk=262)
Microsoft has opened up a preview version of its Microsoft Dev Box cloud-powered workstations to developers. Microsoft Dev Box is a service that allows developers to spin up powerful cloud PCs for testing applications. Its workstations can be configured to have up to 32 virtual CPUs and 128GB of RAM. They can be accessed from anywhere using a modern browser. Microsoft plans to charge users on a per-hour basis for compute and storage. More details about Microsoft Dev Box are available in the article.

[Researchers change blood type of kidney in transplant breakthrough (3 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.theguardian.com%2Fsociety%2F2022%2Faug%2F15%2Fresearchers-change-blood-type-of-kidney-in-transplant-breakthrough%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000182ab4fe7f0-1f2041ca-6dc5-4f7b-b106-fb5677c45fc2-000000/_KMe6p8zBXgSFOcii5R62ZP7vz_bywXZ_U6PE-ad90A=262)
Researchers from the University of Cambridge have successfully altered the blood type of three donor kidneys. They flushed the kidneys with blood infused with an enzyme that removed blood type markers from the blood vessels of the kidneys, converting them to blood type O. The technology could significantly increase the number of kidneys available for patients who need them. One of the biggest restrictions to kidney transplantations is blood-type compatibility.

[De-extinction company sets its next (first?) target: The thylacine (11 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Farstechnica.com%2Fscience%2F2022%2F08%2Fde-extinction-company-sets-its-next-first-target-the-thylacine%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000182ab4fe7f0-1f2041ca-6dc5-4f7b-b106-fb5677c45fc2-000000/Bi1FsgDgVL9o3uHT3csH0ntJzqeris1KajP36oo3X30=262)
A company called Colossal has partnered with an Australian lab to de-extinct the thylacine, also known as the Tasmanian Tiger. The plan involves obtaining thylacine genomes, identifying key differences between those genomes and related lineages, and then editing those differences into marsupial stem cells and using those stem cells for IVF. The project faces significant hurdles, but many factors make thylacine de-extinction more likely to be successful than mammoth de-extinction.
