SYNTHETIC MOUSE EMBRYOS CREATED FROM STEM CELLS — WITHOUT SPERM,
EGGS, OR A UTERUS (3 MINUTE READ)
[https://www.statnews.com/2022/08/01/synthetic-mouse-embryos-created-from-stem-cells-without-sperm-eggs-uterus/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter]


Israeli scientists have developed mice embryos made up entirely of
stem cells and grown inside a bioreactor. The embryos lived for up to
8.5 days, long enough to develop the beginnings of a brain, a neural
tube, and an intestinal tract, as well as a beating heart. While the
technique has many limitations, it is an important step in the
development of technology that can generate an embryo from scratch.
