[Linux Foundation announces the OpenWallet Foundation to develop interoperable digital wallets (3 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2022%2F09%2F13%2Flinux-foundation-announces-the-openwallet-foundation-to-develop-interoperable-digital-wallets%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/010001833b87495c-5e7f8f3e-7da1-401f-b86e-2e8f6abf1129-000000/2AEDkG1kyYlmGXtGLgnrcMHh0E4_6oARrWAJSkoY4M4=266)
The Linux Foundation has announced a new effort called the OpenWallet Foundation that aims to enable interoperability across digital wallets. A universal digital wallet infrastructure will allow people to carry tokenized identity, money, and objects from place to place in the digital world. The OWF aims to foster an effort to build a secure, multi-purpose open source engine that developers can use to build wallets that are interoperable with other wallets.

[Chinese Researchers Test Cars That Hover Over Road Using Magnets (2 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Ffuturism.com%2Fthe-byte%2Fchinese-researchers-car-hovers-magnets%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/010001833b87495c-5e7f8f3e-7da1-401f-b86e-2e8f6abf1129-000000/fs5udIlaJ8NXIrPBBag1SHxhOBrRpoHgCEU5Kfk5tDQ=266)
Chinese researchers are testing modified cars that can magnetically levitate over a stretch of specially modified highway. The researchers modified a roughly five mile stretch of highway and added an array of permanent magnets to the floor of eight vehicles for the test. The cars could reach a velocity of 143 miles per hour on the track. A link to a short video from the test is available in the article.

[Fast charging over 10,000 cycles (4 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.seas.harvard.edu%2Fnews%2F2022%2F09%2Ffast-charging-over-10000-cycles-future-electric-vehicles-harvard-engineers-solid-state%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/010001833b87495c-5e7f8f3e-7da1-401f-b86e-2e8f6abf1129-000000/htudf0YqnHwnXefXxc_Kg0D6UJ8QBt4fxPlzs2i7J3E=266)
Harvard's Office of Technology Development has granted an exclusive technology license to Adden Energy to scale a solid-state lithium-metal battery for commercial deployment. The battery prototype can charge in three minutes and last over 10,000 charge cycles. Adden aims to create a full-scale vehicle battery within the next five years. Enabling electric vehicles to charge within minutes with batteries that last for many years will make EV technology accessible to many more people.

[Futurist prediction methods and accuracy (2 hour read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fdanluu.com%2Ffuturist-predictions%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/010001833b87495c-5e7f8f3e-7da1-401f-b86e-2e8f6abf1129-000000/T1K78R0mdOrw7MTe868OC-Arv8DGQIPTqXy-GRUBc9U=266)
This post looks at past predictions of the future to evaluate which prediction techniques worked and which didn't. Most predictors and futurists have a fairly poor track record of predictions. People with big ideas and a shallow understanding of their subject generally fared poorly. Those who had a deep understanding of the problem and had a record of learning lessons from past predictive errors had relatively accurate predictions.
