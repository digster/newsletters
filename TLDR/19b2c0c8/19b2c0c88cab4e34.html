OpenAI's ChatGPT Updated to Make Images Better and Faster (2 minute read)

The new version of ChatGPT Image is designed to make and edit images more precisely. It can generate pictures up to four times faster than the previous image-generation model. OpenAI is creating a new section within ChatGPT specifically for making images. The company is aiming to cater to a mix of personal and professional uses.
Warner Bros. Plans to Reject Paramount Bid on Funding, Terms (4 minute read)

Warner Bros. plans to reject Paramount's hostile takeover bid due to concerns about financing and other terms. The board will urge shareholders to reject the tender offer. It views the company's existing agreement with Netflix as offering greater value, certainty, and terms than what Paramount has proposed. While Warner Bros.' agreement with Netflix bars it from soliciting proposals from other bidders, it is allowed to entertain incoming proposals.

Science & Futuristic Technology

If AI could interact and learn from the physical world, could it make more scientific advances? (3 minute read)

OpenAI partnered with Red Queen Bio to introduce a new evolutionary framework that uses GPT-5 to optimize cloning protocols. The project involved the pilot of an autonomous robot to carry out the protocols. GPT-achieved a 79x cloning efficiency gain and introduced a new enzyme-based approach. While this is not a bio breakthrough, the novel optimization shows that GPT-5 is at the level of a competent PhD student at this task.
Sawing a Dam in Half (on Purpose) (18 minute read)

Fontana Dam started showing cracks in 1972, caused by concrete expansion. Engineers decided to give the concrete more room to grow rather than patching cracks as they appeared. They cut a relief slot all through the dam without draining the reservoir or causing any disruptions to the hydropower plant. This post explains how this was achieved. A video showing footage of the procedure is available.

Instagram Reels is coming to a TV near you (1 minute read)

Meta has unveiled a dedicated Instagram Reels TV app. The app will debut on Amazon's Fire TV before expanding to other platforms. It will allow users to watch Instagram Reels' short-form, user-generated videos on bigger screens. Messaging is a Moat, not a Business (5 minute read)

Messaging platforms command astronomical acquisition prices, but they often flounder when left to stand alone in the public markets. Messaging is perhaps the most valuable loss leader in digital history. It creates massive utility, commands unparalleled user attention, and offers high retention, but captures almost no direct financial value of its own.
