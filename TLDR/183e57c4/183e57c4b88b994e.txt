MALE BIRTH CONTROL OPTIONS ARE IN DEVELOPMENT, BUT A NUMBER OF
BARRIERS STILL STAND IN THE WAY (4 MINUTE READ)
[https://www.freethink.com/health/male-birth-control?utm_source=tldrnewsletter]


There haven't been any new male birth control methods since the '80s.
Male contraception research has primarily been supported by agencies
that don't have the drug development infrastructure comparable to
pharmaceutical companies. Pharmaceutical companies have shown a lack
of interest in developing male contraceptives due to the cost,
uncertainties with the market, and unclear regulatory requirements.
There are several methods currently in development and a few that are
working towards first-stage clinical trials. 

EXPERIMENTAL DRUG REVERSES DAMAGE FROM CONCUSSION (3 MINUTE READ)
[https://newatlas.com/science/experimental-drug-traumatic-brain-injury-concussion-cognition-memory/?utm_source=tldrnewsletter]


An experimental drug called ISRIB can reverse some of the neural
damage associated with traumatic brain injury. The drug has previously
proved successful in improving cognition across several animal models
of brain diseases. It is still unclear how the drug improves behavior
and cognition. Human safety trials of the drug are already underway.
