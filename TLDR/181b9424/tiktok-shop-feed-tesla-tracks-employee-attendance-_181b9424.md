[Amazon’s new physical retail analytics service gives brands insights about product and ad performance (3 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Ftechcrunch.com%2F2022%2F06%2F29%2Famazons-physical-retail-analytics-service-gives-brands-shopper-data-ad-product-performance%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000181b942460e-0c405c3f-2942-44cf-a9d1-d52a3371981d-000000/xoLkOkFnipd2IHAfHMORCpcnR7CCKcgxSpCf_z1LF9k=255)
Amazon is launching a new physical retail store analytics service that will give brands anonymized insights about their products in stores that use Just Walk Out and Dash Cart technologies. Brands will have access to information about how their products are discovered, considered, and purchased, as well as data on how their products rank and perform. The data is aggregated and anonymized, and it doesn't contain any personal information. Shoppers can opt-out of data collection while using Just Walk Out or Dash Cart through Amazon's Store Analytics website.

[Raspberry Pi announces the Pico W, a $6 microcontroller equipped with Wi-Fi (3 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.theverge.com%2F2022%2F6%2F30%2F23189994%2Fraspberry-pi-pico-w-wi-fi-microcontroller-6%3Fscrolla=5eb6d68b7fedc32c19ef33b4%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000181b942460e-0c405c3f-2942-44cf-a9d1-d52a3371981d-000000/6baS3z2TEtwttvmsGQYuLrqDrOf_Bwl8EMTW9hZovzs=255)
Raspberry Pi has announced the Pico W, Pico H, and Pico WH. The Pico W is a version of the Pico with a wireless antenna. Its wireless chip is capable of Bluetooth, but Bluetooth is not currently enabled. The Pico H and Pico WH are versions of the Pico and Pico W with pre-attached pinheaders and debug connectors. The Pico W and H are available now and the Pico WH will be available in August. More details about the hardware are available in the article.

[Scientists develop first-of-its-kind implant that relieves pain without drugs (4 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Finterestingengineering.com%2Ffirst-of-its-kind-implant-pain-drugs%3Futm_source=rss%26utm_medium=article%26utm_content=30062022%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000181b942460e-0c405c3f-2942-44cf-a9d1-d52a3371981d-000000/wzcQMzCY_VbGEc0JxVIF6GmSBpwnasBzlt_-6dNZ95o=255)
Researchers have developed an implant that can relieve pain on demand without the use of drugs. The device works by wrapping around nerves and delivering targeted cooling via evaporation. Cooling nerves numbs them and blocks pain signals to the brain. A sensor ensures that the cooling doesn't damage any tissue. The device can be remotely activated and users can control its intensity. It is water-soluble and the device is naturally absorbed by the body after use.

[Improve Git monorepo performance with a file system monitor (22 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fgithub.blog%2F2022-06-29-improve-git-monorepo-performance-with-a-file-system-monitor%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000181b942460e-0c405c3f-2942-44cf-a9d1-d52a3371981d-000000/TZb-Q3NaI_6MF2Ra7bn_3CC3f8-5vj_MHoRfgSSRDbo=255)
This article discusses the new built-in FSMonitor added in Git version 2.37.0 and how it improves performance on very large worktrees. The Git file system monitor (FSMonitor) feature speeds up commands like git status and git add by reducing the size of the search. These commands are slow as they are searching the entire worktree looking for changes, which can be a problem for very large worktrees. FSMonitor continually watches worktrees to have a complete view of all file system changes.

[Samsung beats TSMC to production of 3nm chips (2 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.theverge.com%2F2022%2F6%2F30%2F23189362%2Fsamsung-3nm-chips%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/01000181b942460e-0c405c3f-2942-44cf-a9d1-d52a3371981d-000000/fugOdQW9S5mUQ8-f-sY56Bhf5DyjRLJTxXyK8LrUnro=255)
Samsung has started producing 3nm chips. Its new process is 45% more power-efficient than its previous 5nm process and it produces chips with 23% higher performance and a 16% smaller surface area. TSMC's 3nm process isn't expected to go into mass production until the second half of 2022. Samsung's chips will be manufactured in South Korea for now. Its Texas plant is not scheduled to start manufacturing until 2024.
