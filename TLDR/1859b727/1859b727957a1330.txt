THIS BIOTECH STARTUP SAYS MICE LIVE LONGER AFTER GENETIC REPROGRAMMING
(5 MINUTE READ) [https://archive.ph/eYEVE?utm_source=tldrnewsletter] 

Rejuvenate Bio, a San Diego biotech company, claims that it has used a
technique called reprogramming to rejuvenate old mice and extend their
lives. Reprogramming works by exposing cells to a set of three or four
proteins that are typically active in early-stage embryos. The mice
who received treatment in Rejuvenate's studies lived 7% longer overall
compared to control mice. While the study's results are promising,
there are issues with reprogramming, such as an increased risk of
cancer.
