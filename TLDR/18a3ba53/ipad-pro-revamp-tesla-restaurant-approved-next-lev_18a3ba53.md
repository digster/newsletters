[IBM’s AI Chip May Find Use in Generative AI (3 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fspectrum.ieee.org%2Fanalog-ai-ibm%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100018a3ba5342d-ed426048-a4d4-4786-b0fe-9c57ee0c386d-000000/SnoLTR5WHzX6n2k344bJI46fzEX3dGDBbt7H1cBTZdw=315)
IBM's latest AI chip is more than a dozen times as energy efficient as conventional microchips on automated transcription tasks. It is capable of 12.4 trillion operations per second per watt. When tested on speech recognition tasks, the chip was able to perform as accurately as neural networks run on conventional hardware while completing the job seven times as fast and up to 14 more efficiently. The chip can support transformers and may be beneficial for many types of AI systems. |
[We are not empty (20 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Faeon.co%2Fessays%2Fwhy-the-empty-atom-picture-misunderstands-quantum-theory%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100018a3ba5342d-ed426048-a4d4-4786-b0fe-9c57ee0c386d-000000/0MwllPbqiE2nbaxY7m_X252FpajopYWhfbRwbXwUsC4=315)
There are no empty spaces within an atom. The empty atom picture often shown in popular science is incorrect. It is unclear who created the myth, but it was popularized in Carl Sagan's Cosmos TV series. This essay explains why the idea of an empty atom is wrong by carefully interpreting quantum theory. |

[The strange, secretive world of North Korean science fiction (6 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Farstechnica.com%2Fculture%2F2023%2F08%2Fthe-strange-secretive-world-of-north-korean-science-fiction%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100018a3ba5342d-ed426048-a4d4-4786-b0fe-9c57ee0c386d-000000/73qX-B_vSx6RLhbRyxs4yMycxu_FsMF6cr5yyogclgo=315)
This article looks at the history of North Korean Sci-Fi and how it changed throughout the decades. North Korean Sci-Fi has its roots in Soviet literature. Kim Il-sung, the founder of North Korea, thought that literature could be used to push propaganda and recommended that authors translate Soviet works. They were also told to write their own stories depicting a thriving communist society. |
[WordPress Now Offers a 100-Year Plan for Domains (1 minute read)](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.pcmag.com%2Fnews%2Fwordpress-100-year-plan%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100018a3ba5342d-ed426048-a4d4-4786-b0fe-9c57ee0c386d-000000/l8WXi4g6L8GslOf18Z1zv05BeD4E2MMOPvdlBoAta7U=315)
