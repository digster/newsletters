Tiny, 45 base long RNA can make copies of itself (8 minute read)
](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Farstechnica.com%2Fscience%2F2026%2F02%2Fresearchers-find-small-rnas-that-can-make-copies-of-themselves%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100019c663369c4-990b47f7-64ff-4368-9831-8e50536679ea-000000/vuhHMhTIpx2O5_rRhU6ApdsR-rIRDdVGxjmGC_B02Sg=444)
One of the key steps in the origin of life is the emergence of an RNA molecule that could replicate itself. RNA can carry genetic information and also fold into three-dimensional structures that act as catalysts. This suggests that early life was protein-free, with RNA handling heredity and catalyzing a simple metabolism. A team of scientists has discovered a 45-base-long piece of RNA that can make copies of itself, adding to our knowledge of the conditions in which life emerged.

On cognitive debt (6 minute read)
](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Fwww.natemeyvis.com%2Fon-cognitive-debt%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100019c663369c4-990b47f7-64ff-4368-9831-8e50536679ea-000000/oaG0X7nG_RIB3h1kbvTVlZxc38FbeY26_jZ_eXp0ksw=444)
Cognitive debt is the idea that AI-generated codebases are at risk of falling into a state where nobody knows how they work, leaving them inextensible, unobservable, and hard to debug. This is already happening in AI-driven codebases. However, when controlling for the size and scope of a project, cognitive debt tends to be at least as bad, if not worse, in pre-AI code bases than in AI code bases. This fact is obscured because a lot of traditional engineering work is either managing crippling cognitive debt or avoiding it at enormous cost. The best AI users are already pretty good at avoiding cognitive debt, and they're only going to get better at it.

Bias Toward Action (17 minute read)
](https://tracking.tldrnewsletter.com/CL0/https:%2F%2Faddyosmani.com%2Fblog%2Fbias-towards-action%2F%3Futm_source=tldrnewsletter/1/0100019c663369c4-990b47f7-64ff-4368-9831-8e50536679ea-000000/iZ5Z5hCV8CBm2HktwJfXkUlfjjj0tpSzOeRy9wBqkdw=444)
Bias toward action is defaulting to the smallest responsible step that produces real feedback while pre-committing to guardrails. It helps companies learn faster while making sure that being wrong doesn't kill them. It's about optimizing for learning speed while investing in foundations that make you faster. Fast teams use a lot of information, but they don't wait for perfect information. Their tight feedback loops allow them to learn quickly.
