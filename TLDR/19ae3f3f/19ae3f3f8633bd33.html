Big Tech & Startups

OpenAI Declares ‘Code Red' as Google Threatens AI Lead (3 minute read)

OpenAI CEO Sam Altman has declared a 'code red' effort to improve the quality of ChatGPT. Other products will be delayed as a result. OpenAI is facing immense pressure from competitors, who have narrowed the gap in the AI race. Google Gemini last month surpassed OpenAI's models in industry benchmark tests. OpenAI is also facing pressure from Anthropic, which is becoming popular among business customers.
Anthropic reportedly preparing for one of the largest IPOs ever in race with OpenAI (3 minute read)

Anthropic is in early talks to launch an IPO next year. It has engaged a law firm that previously worked on high-profile tech IPOs such as Google, LinkedIn, and Lyft. The company has also discussed a potential IPO with major investment banks, but the discussions are in an informal preliminary stage. The potential listing will test investors' appetite for loss-making AI startups amid growing fears of an AI bubble.

How Blue Origin Plans to Beat SpaceX to the Moon (4 minute read)

Blue Origin sees the Moon as an opportunity to gain an edge over SpaceX. It is currently working on a bigger, more powerful version of its New Glenn rocket. The formerly slow-moving company is now transitioning into a production organization. It has planned several orbital missions, including an early 2026 cargo flight to the Moon.
Waymo's Self-Driving Cars Are Suddenly Behaving Like New York Cabbies (8 minute read)

Waymo has been trying to make its cars more confidently assertive. The move is necessary to scale up in some cities because of how busy they are. Passive vehicles can be disruptive. The vehicles are now programmed to make common-sense decisions, which can involve trade-offs.

Miscellaneous

Why local tech scenes have changed (19 minute read)

AI has changed the relationship between local tech scenes and San Francisco. The opportunity cost of being anywhere other than San Francisco is now at an all-time high. However, it is also much easier for players who want to stay local to start a one-person business and grow it themselves. AI will enable local tech scenes to produce promising startups and select for promising talent in very different ways than they used to.
The Junior Hiring Crisis (12 minute read)

AI is accelerating the erosion of the traditional apprenticeship model in tech. This puts the 10- to 20-year talent pipeline at risk. Students and early-career professionals need to start building relational intelligence now. Relationship skills have always been a differentiator, but now they're a necessity.
