Today’s issue is a little different than usual. Holiday season is just around the corner, meaning it’s time to think about gifts – which can be a non-trivial challenge in itself. With so many choices, what are the best presents for people working in tech? I’ve put together a list for the upcoming holiday season to make this process a little easier.
For this list, I crowdsourced recommendations from Twitter [ https://substack.com/redirect/43813f59-ff6f-4613-9c91-4b56b061539b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and Linkedin [ https://substack.com/redirect/7e6866e3-b9c5-44ae-9213-7370488756f1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and also added some suggestions of my own. If you’re stuck on what to get for your nearest and dearest in tech, maybe this article can serve up some ideas…
We cover:

Gadgets and tinkering

Office equipment

1. Books and creative thinking
Books are a tried and tested – and increasingly underrated – way to learn something new. See my newly updated list of software engineering, engineering management, and product strategy books recommendations [ https://substack.com/redirect/801150cc-0054-4d74-8145-515ceb557ff7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
While nonfiction books can teach you something new, fiction books are an excellent way to unwind by escaping into new worlds. Websites like Goodreads [ https://substack.com/redirect/62bf92dd-97c7-4c42-8447-ed42d9d80587?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] offer recommendations, as do lists like the most anticipated adult fiction books for 2023 [ https://substack.com/redirect/7f575918-e8f6-4126-b5f8-22930387351c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], or Amazon’s best books of 2023 list [ https://substack.com/redirect/f3505a0b-9ff3-4361-bd40-637951b1074d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
Breaking creative blocks: Oblique Strategies is a set of 100 cards, each with a suggestion for how to break creative block. For example:

Work at a different speed
Try faking it!
These cards are popular in the creative professions and it’s a gift that could help software engineers get unstuck by prompting some outside-the-box thinking. Get it here [ https://substack.com/redirect/64249614-d003-4eef-9085-ffd9cc3c2d2e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
2. Gadgets and tinkering
Flipper Zero. The single most mentioned, most recommended gift idea this year. A multi-tool for security folks and those loving to hack around with signals and hardware.
You can use it to hack around with digital stuff like hardware, radio protocols or access control systems. It’s open source, accessible, and a lot of fun! It features:
A sub-1 GHz antenna with a 50m range which can trigger things like garage doors, IoT sensors, and keyless systems
NFC module supporting all major standards
An RFID chip which can be used to emulate simple IDs for access control
Bluetooth to connect to smartphones and third-party devices
Infrared transmitter to control items like TVs, air conditioners, and stereo systems
MicroSD card for external storage
… and a bunch more [ https://substack.com/redirect/fced486c-f105-4fb9-8e72-ebe40ad83967?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] neat features to hack around with!

3. Wellbeing
Massage or spa voucher/booking. Gift someone special a massage or spa coupon to revitalize with. Gifting a pass to a salon or spa you can personally recommend based on your own experience makes it more personal.
Most of us software engineers spend far too much time sitting at desks, so a professional massage can help mitigate the effects of a sedentary job.
A back, neck or foot massager. A massage in the comfort of your own home can be a great gift, and there are plenty of gadgets that provide a surprisingly good experience. Search for electric back massagers [ https://substack.com/redirect/bba1370e-ec0b-4ba2-a844-564a895352a4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], neck massagers [ https://substack.com/redirect/92a6cd74-9842-443b-9d1e-0eb9ebb69093?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and foot massagers [ https://substack.com/redirect/c0057962-a779-4c67-af14-96f58e669aa3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].

4. Office equipment
A rubber duck. “Rubber ducking” is a popular concept among software engineers, where you talk through a problem to a rubber duck, and through this process of explanation, you discover the solution to the problem (sometimes.) There’s no shortage of rubber ducks to choose from, and there’s also dedicated online rubber duck stores offering personalized ducks in Amsterdam [ https://substack.com/redirect/9a020a03-22b6-40f3-902e-64c49c9e0aef?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Barcelona [ https://substack.com/redirect/8c379b35-a83b-472a-87b4-84e7df52b978?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and Paris [ https://substack.com/redirect/3631aae9-6a1c-4f96-978e-32c93c8f9d9c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. 6. Toys for adults
Fidget toys. From staff engineer Lee McKeeman [ https://substack.com/redirect/33f514da-1730-407d-8a0d-0df7ee5c211a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]:

Check out the LEGO 18+ section [ https://substack.com/redirect/e7a7a0d5-4624-4dbd-8f87-e26911c7c353?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] for more ideas.
7. Board games
There’s plenty to choose from, including evergreen games that are great fun with friends and family. A few lists for ideas:
The best adult board games [ https://substack.com/redirect/03ccc91a-e1d2-42b6-9766-35df8573254f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by The Strategist (2023)
11 board games we love [ https://substack.com/redirect/988d656d-a1f8-4456-b28e-faac9b18ad96?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by The Wirecutter (2023)
The top 25 board games [ https://substack.com/redirect/7ba82525-e9dc-45aa-88ac-bfb8b50867ab?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], by The Board Game Geek (2020)
Board games for software engineers are a bit harder to come by. However, the Revelry team has put together a list [ https://substack.com/redirect/733230e1-3c02-4b19-a695-dc8e14a96444?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of ones which developers may enjoy even more:
Dominion. “The game lends itself to a lot of strategy centered around the correct order of chaining different cards.”
Fields of Arle. “The branching decision trees spanning multiple rounds when planning your future turns can get very complex and confusing.”
RoboRally. “It can teach us to plan for and deal with errors.”
Tzolk’in. “Challenges our ability to plan ahead and manage resources.”
Burke’s Gambit. “It not only improves deduction skills, but also social skills.”
On a Reddit thread [ https://substack.com/redirect/3c650a4e-5139-4f8d-87a4-714f9e684db4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] discussing these suggestions, users added more recommendations for games techies may enjoy:
Zendo: “a game of inductive logic, where players work to ‘reverse engineer’ a moderator-designed construct to discover the rule.”
Epic Spell Wars: “another good one for this list because it has pre-programmed actions and lots of chaining. Plus, it's just goofy fun!”
AquaSphere: “an eurogame with a cool underwater theme. You play as programming bots and program other bots in your resource chain.”
8. Non-tech gifts
Music and the arts. Indulge the senses by gifting an experience. Don’t forget to take into account who an experience is for, to avoid disappointment:
Musical show
Theater tickets
Light show
Concert
Food and drink. There are plenty of options for gifting a tasty, culinary experience:
Cookery class
Restaurant vouchers
Wine tasting
Home cooking kits, or vouchers for services like HelloFresh or Blue Apron
Special or interesting food or drink
Homemade food or drink as gifts
A cookbook
When gifting food and drink, you could consider going local and sourcing from small vendors. For example, in The Netherlands, The Good Box [ https://substack.com/redirect/b42429d6-2fb3-48b5-8d5b-1fd5fe51b5e7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is a gift box with products from local startups and scaleups. Look for similar initiatives in your region, or just seek out local producers.
A plant. Plants are less conventional gifts, but in my view, they’re underrated. I once received one from a friend; and although at first I was surprised, I found it therapeutic to have to keep it alive. It was a nice, big, green plant which lasted a few years, and also reminded me of this friend.
Takeaways
I hope you’ve found some inspiration for the holiday season. Going through the suggestions, one thing that comes to mind is techies may value non-tech gifts which get you away from screens.
And don’t forget that sometimes just spending time with family and friends may be a better gift than any single thing you could purchase.
I hope this guide is useful. If you have more suggestions, please put them in a comment!
