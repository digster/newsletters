Firms like Dragoneer, D1, and Coatue have slowed or halted their late stage private investing. (One notable exception to that has been Insight, which announced a $20 billion fund in February and has continued to make investments, sources tell me.) Tiger is doing some later stage investing but has largely shifted its focus toward Series A funding rounds. Tiger — either through the fund or its partners’ private money — has funded a bunch of seed stage investors, giving them visibility and deal flow into up-and-coming Series A deals, sources tell me. The closer a company is to going public, the more pain that it’s feeling. Even though Instacart cut its valuation, there seems to be plenty of room to fall. But from what I can tell, seed and Series A deals remain frothy. Crypto investing has been the rare bright spot over the past six months.
