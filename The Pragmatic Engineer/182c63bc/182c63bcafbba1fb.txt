The Pragmatic Engineer: Year 1
It’s getting close to a year since I announced [ https://substack.com/redirect/8e8d76d4-8fd6-42cf-8c4b-212c3af66175?r=8o54n ] that I am starting this newsletter. 30th of August will mark the first year of sending out the first article, The seniority rollercoaster [ https://substack.com/redirect/42d4207c-2be8-4a1b-90b5-6a04fb5c1d55?r=8o54n ].
Over the first year, 86 newsletter issues went out, with a total word count between 400-500,000 words. That’s a solid 5 or so books’ worth of reading, taking a length of 100,000 words - common for longer nonfiction books - as a metric. On top of this, I published 29 templates and resources [ https://substack.com/redirect/48276207-9582-4424-896d-c098a1dd58db?r=8o54n ] for software engineers and engineering managers.
With so many articles and so much reading, I organized these articles by topic. Articles marked by 🔒mean they are for paid subscribers. In the first year, 17 articles went out to free subscribers - which is about every fifth article. I’ve also opened up The perfect storm causing an insane tech hiring market [ https://substack.com/redirect/8cd6d1c9-c233-4c2a-bf4e-43b85f57d1c2?r=8o54n ] recently, which started what would become The Scoop series [ https://substack.com/redirect/b786615b-c64b-44af-bc0c-dbe6e0c900f6?r=8o54n ].
