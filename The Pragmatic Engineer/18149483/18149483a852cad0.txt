Below is a part relevant for anyone working in tech, to help understand how indicative the stream of layoffs news is for the whole market.
Data on the tech hiring market
Reading tech news, it’s the stream of reporting on layoffs which stands out. But how bad are these cuts, in context of the whole tech market? The site layoffs.fyi offers a handy comparison, starting with data from 2020. Looking at this, it seems like we’re seeing fewer layoffs than in Spring 2020, when COVID-19 struck, global lockdowns kicked off, and many businesses decided to take this drastic measure:
Looking at this chart, it feels we’re seeing a similar layoffs trend as at the beginning of the pandemic.
Could we be seeing a “COVID-19 reversed” reaction on the job market? The newsletter Robinhood Snacks reported the service sector is struggling to hire fast enough, while in tech jobs are being cut. This is the exact opposite of what happened when COVID started, where service jobs were cut rapidly and tech was hiring rapidly. From Robinhood Snacks [ https://email.mg-d0.substack.com/c/eJxFkM1uhCAUhZ9m2NUAKsKCxXQx0046m-66MghXpaNgAdP49sVx0YRALvfn3PNplWDwYZOLjwntV5u2BaSD3zhBShDQGiG01khS1awsiUBGslL1tUE2tn0AmJWdZAoroGXtJqtVst7tHVXNGyzQKJuasa6sBC9rzYD3hGOuiGIgIJcIcwir1VhwGqR30_acjLSfZ3BpH4YmOaa0xFN5PtFLPtEp_YhF8J11o_emyMX5-3_zmCMWvi43-3i_XW8_19f7x_1tFp_fOYGspJhSzLAgjNJKFGVBKFeM487onjecm1OF5-HF4CKuXUxZbZdAQdo4KlfstnPFsL_PRHbf7guvzqatBae6CcwBJh18n6jaARyEzN20KknC6qoRpOG4EfzgkM1mKpRxylBWNj53ObkElaWS1eAG6wDCH_AYlXo ]:
“A tale of two markets... Unemployment stayed at a pandemic low of 3.6%, and rebounding sectors like hospitality can't hire fast enough. The paradox: sectors that thrived mid-pandemic are slowing hiring and cutting jobs to shave costs.
We're hiring: Airlines, restaurants, hotels, and retailers are scrambling to boost headcount to meet demand — and have cut growth plans because they can’t. American Airlines has hired 16K employees this year and is still struggling to fill positions. Small businesses have been forced to slash hours and services.
We're un-hiring: As inflation and slowing demand hit tech profits, Peloton, Netflix, Carvana, and others have laid off workers (Tesla reportedly plans to as well.) Meanwhile: Microsoft, Meta, Salesforce, Twitter, Uber, Wayfair, and Snap say they’ll rein in hiring.”
The number of jobs posted on the monthly Hacker News “who is hiring” does not suggest a worrying trend. Looking at the job postings from 2012, these went steadily up until 2018, with a slowdown to 2020, and then a peak in May 2020. Zooming in to the years 2018-2022:
In May 2020, the number of jobs posted hit a bottom of 540 vacancies. In June 2021, it hit a peak of 1,041 jobs posted. In May 2022, the number of jobs posted was almost identical to in January 2021.
Based on this data source, the jobs being posted now are indeed down, the decline does not look dramatic.
The number of tech jobs posted shows no visible drop over the past month, globally.
