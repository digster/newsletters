Today, I want to share something vulnerable:

I wasn't supposed to write a book.

You see, I'm not an author. I'm not smart enough to write a book. I'm not capable of doing that.

I spent the first 30 years of my life telling myself that story.

And the story we tell ourselves has a powerful hold on our life:

  • New information is massaged to fit that story, or rejected if it doesn't
  • Thoughts, behaviors, and actions all start to fall in line with that story

The quality of your stories impacts the quality of your life:

  • If you tell yourself you aren't capable, you won't try
  • If you tell yourself you aren't worthy, you won't reach for it
  • If you tell yourself you're a static entity, you won't attempt to grow

So often, it's not the sheer difficulty of achieving something that stops you—it's the ease of continuing to tell yourself the story that you can't.

The stories you tell yourself can either hold you back or push you forward. There is no in between.

After 30 years, I finally get to rewrite a flawed story I told myself. I am an author. I am smart enough to do that. I am capable.

Now, it's your turn:

This book will help you rewrite the story you tell yourself.

It is about identifying the things that truly matter to you, then taking the actions to build your life around those things.

Not the things society tells you should matter, not the things your parents tell you should matter, not the things social media tells you should matter...but what you truly care about.

It offers a rare combination of information and action, so that you can create a real, meaningful change in your life.

Apple CEO Tim Cook said, "This book is a powerful call to action to think deeply about what lights you up—and a guide for how to build a life of meaning and purpose around those things."

No matter who you are or where you are on your life journey, this book has something for you. While the lens through which you view its ideas, strategies, and frameworks will be unique, the questions it forces you to ask are universal.

And the greatest discoveries in life come not from finding the right answers, but from asking the right questions.

It's time to rewrite the story you tell yourself.
