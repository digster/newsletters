Monday: The Archimedes Principle: The Science of Sudden Insight
On Monday, I shared a story about the ancient inventor Archimedes and the science of sudden insight.
After wrestling with a difficult problem through intense effort, the answer he was seeking surfaced during an inactive moment in the bath.
That pattern is no accident: Disciplined, structured work followed by unstructured, undirected thought is where breakthroughs are born. The brain is not silent at rest. It is actively connecting thoughts and experiences, linking distant ideas, and surfacing new connections.
The lesson: Work hard, then create space for the work to breathe.
Your next insight may already be there, waiting patiently for you to stop crowding it out.

Friday: The Pygmalion Effect: How Expectations Create Outcomes
On Friday, I explored the Pygmalion Effect: The scientific finding that expectations create reality.
Research shows that when people are believed in, they begin to internalize that belief. Small signals of confidence, patience, and trust compound over time, changing how people see themselves and what they’re capable of achieving.
That’s why your environment matters. The people you surround yourself with determine your outcomes.
Remember: Your circle creates your reality. Choose wisely.
