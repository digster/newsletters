Monday: The Domino Mindset: A Dangerous Cognitive Trap
The Domino Mindset occurs when one small negative creates a chain reaction of negative conclusions.
A tiny miss becomes the defining feature.
We all do it. A mistake becomes “I’m terrible at my job.” A skipped workout becomes “I’m undisciplined.” A stressful commute becomes “My morning is ruined.”
The question I challenged readers to ask: Are you allowing a tiny miss to become your defining feature?

Friday: The "Good Enough" Paradox: A Science-Backed Life Trap
The Region-Beta Paradox is a scientific finding that shows that intense negative experiences often resolve faster than mild ones because humans respond to alleviate the former while ignoring the latter.
This explains a common trap:
The 3/10 job, 3/10 relationship, or 3/10 life forces change. We respond to alleviate the intensely negative experience.
But the 6/10 endures. You drift. You tolerate. You wait. The peril of “good enough” is real, simply because so few people ever do anything to change it.
It takes courage to step away from good enough to find what you’re meant for.
Courage isn’t courageous because it works out. Courage is courageous because you act without knowing that it will.
