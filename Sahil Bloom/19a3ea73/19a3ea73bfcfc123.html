Monday: The Antelope Principle: How to Chase the Right Things

A lion can easily catch a field mouse—but the energy it burns in the process exceeds the calories it gains. A lion that spends its days chasing mice will eventually starve. To thrive, it must hunt antelope.

Every single day, you face the same choice:

  • Chase field mice: the small, urgent problems and opportunities that pull at your attention; or
  • Hunt antelope: the big, important pursuits that require focus and effort but actually move the needle.

Busyness can trick you into feeling productive, but motion isn’t momentum. A rocking horse moves all day and never goes anywhere.

The lion that chases mice will starve. The lion that hunts antelope will thrive.

What are you hunting today?

Friday: The Donkey Principle: How to Avoid Foolish Arguments

There’s a story I love about a Donkey and a Tiger arguing over the color of the grass.

The Donkey insisted it was blue, while the Tiger argued it was green. When they brought the matter to the Lion, the King ruled in the Donkey’s favor—and punished the Tiger with a year of silence.

Confused, the Tiger asked why he was being punished when the Lion knew the grass was green. The Lion replied, “Because someone as wise as you should never waste time proving anything to a fool.”

I call it The Donkey Principle. And its lesson is an important one.

Stop trying to reason with people who have no interest in understanding. Stop chasing rational outcomes with irrational opponents. Peace has a price, and you buy it by saying no—to drama, to noise, to pointless arguments.

Remember: If you argue with a fool, you become one.
