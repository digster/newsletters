Monday: The Hidden Power of Staying in the Game

Andy Weir is one of the most successful science fiction authors of our time, and his story is a reminder of what long, consistent effort can create.

Before The Martian made him a household name, he spent ten years posting science-heavy blogs for a small group of readers. He kept showing up, improving, and refining his craft until the right moment arrived.

Ten years of showing up. Ten years of action. Ten years of effort.

The most meaningful breakthroughs are often found on the other side of long periods of methodical, daily effort. Success requires that you show up every single day with no guarantees.

In Monday's piece, I explored the idea behind his journey and why choosing your game wisely—and staying in it long enough to get lucky—may be the closest thing we have to a universal recipe for meaningful success.
