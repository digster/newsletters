Monday: A Simple Question for True Mastery
Robert Caro is widely considered to be one of the greatest biographers of our time. His list of accolades is long and includes two Pulitzer Prizes for Biography, two National Book Awards (one for Lifetime Achievement), and many more.
But the critical acclaim is no accident.
Caro didn't set out to write "good enough" biographies. He set out to produce definitive, world-changing ones—the kind that shape our understanding of human nature, power, and the people who wield it.
His mantra—Turn every page—was a reflection of that relentless pursuit.
It’s a question you should be asking yourself: Are you turning every page?

Wednesday: You’re Always Building Your Own House
When I was a kid, my grandfather once asked me to tidy up a few bookshelves in his office. I rushed through the task, sloppily stacking the books so I could get back to whatever I was doing.
When I told him I was done, he looked at the bookshelves, smiled, and told me to sit down.
In this piece, I share the story he told me and the lasting impact it had on my life.

Friday: The Columbus Egg Principle
Breakthroughs often appear inevitable in hindsight, but that masks the struggle, ridicule, and failure absorbed by the pioneer along the way.
The best idea looks crazy—until someone does it.
True innovation is often met with ridicule at the outset. Enduring that non-belief is the cost of entry for anyone who wants to alter the status quo.
Or, as Nietzsche eloquently said:
“And those who were seen dancing were thought to be insane by those who could not hear the music.”
Dance to your music. Let everyone join in later.
