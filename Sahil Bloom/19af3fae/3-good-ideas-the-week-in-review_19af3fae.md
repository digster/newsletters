Monday: The Warrior in the Garden
This week, I shared a story about a young warrior asking his master why they train for war instead of tending the garden. The master’s response was simple:
“It is far better to be a warrior tending his garden than a gardener at war.”
Why? Because life is hard. Chaos, uncertainty, failure, struggle, pain, loss. All of those things are a natural part of being alive. They’re not good or bad. They just are. You don’t get to choose the battles that come to your doorstep. The simple truth is that you meet life’s inevitable battles at precisely the level of your preparation.
In the full piece, I shared how you can face the chaos of life with an internal calm—and how you stand ready when the battle arrives at your door.

Friday: The Clarity Curve: A Mental Model For Life
Clarity isn't a precursor to action. Action creates clarity.
The Clarity Curve shows that the early, flat, frustrating stretch is the cost of entry. But if you keep moving, something shifts. The curve bends upward. Clarity compounds.
My advice is simple: Just start moving. No one has it all figured out. The people you admire are simply the ones who had the courage to begin.
As my favorite quote says:
As you start to walk on the way, the way appears.” — Rumi
