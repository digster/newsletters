Monday: The Warrior in the Garden
---------------------------------

This week, I shared a story about a young warrior asking his
master why they train for war instead of tending the garden. The
master’s response was simple:

“It is far better to be a warrior tending his garden than a
gardener at war.”

Why? Because life is hard. Chaos, uncertainty, failure, struggle,
pain, loss. All of those things are a natural part of being
alive. They’re not good or bad. They just are. You don’t get to
choose the battles that come to your doorstep. The simple truth
is that you meet life’s inevitable battles at precisely the level
of your preparation.

In the full piece, I shared how you can face the chaos of life
with an internal calm—and how you stand ready when the battle
arrives at your door.

courage to begin.
