Monday: Amara’s Law: The Invisible Force That Shapes Your LifeIn 1978, futurist Roy Amara offered a simple reflection that later became known as Amara’s Law: We tend to overestimate the effect of a technology in the short run and underestimate the effect in the long run. The insight is about more than technology—it’s about how humans misjudge progress. We anchor on what’s visible. We think linearly. We expect immediate results. But most of life works exponentially. Today’s actions quietly stack beneath the surface for days, months, years—then suddenly, they appear. To paraphrase Ernest Hemingway, growth happens gradually, then suddenly. On Monday, I explored how Amara’s Law shows up in your habits, decisions, and life, and how learning to see the invisible compounding beneath the surface can completely change your perspective. Wednesday: How I’m Breaking My Phone Addiction In 30 DaysI have an embarrassing confession to make. I’ve developed an unhealthy addiction to my phone. The signs were clear. Constant “quick checks.” Carrying it everywhere. Letting it spill into family time. I rationalized it as necessary until one of my favorite questions cut through everything: If you were the main character in a movie of your life, what would the audience be screaming at you to do right now? The answer was obvious: Get off the damn phone. So this week, I shared exactly how I’m breaking the habit over the next 30 days. If this resonates, I'd love for you to join me. Friday: How to Thrive (A Science-Backed Approach)This week, I shared one of my favorite fables about a man, his son, and a donkey, and the painful lesson of trying to please everyone. Please all, and you will please none. I explored how this idea is grounded in real science through Self-Determination Theory, which shows that human thriving depends on autonomy, competence, and relatedness. In the story, each one erodes as the man and boy respond to the noise from the crowd. This offers a useful question for your own experience and life: Where are you surrendering your self direction to the noise from the crowd? Remember: You are not responsible for the narratives that other people have about you, your growth, or your journey. You are the author of your own story. You are at the wheel. Never let go.
