Wisdom Worth Sharing:

"That which we persist in doing becomes easier to do, not that the nature of the thing has changed but that our power to do has increased." - Ralph Waldo Emerson

Hard things don’t get easier, you get stronger. You grow. You change. You become different. And if you show up for long enough, somewhere along the way, you fall in love with the struggle. You find peace in chaos. True flow. That is the magic of life.

Monday: 3 Words to Change How You Live

This ancient Roman phrase will change the way you live: Age Quod Agis (translation: Do what you are doing). In a world addicted to multitasking, this phrase is a powerful reminder to give your full attention to the task at hand.

Energy is more important than time.

Wednesday: How to Make a Great First Impression

First impressions are important: It takes considerably more energy to change an impression than to make one—so we need to pay close attention to those early interactions, as they shape the long-term arc of the relationship.

5 tactical rules to make a great first impression: (1) Preparation is an asymmetric investment, (2) simple is much better than impressive, (3) interesting questions make you interesting, (4) lean into your pattern interrupts, and (5) embed (and execute) the thoughtful follow-up.

(read the full piece here)

Friday: The IKEA Effect

Why do hard things? Because we don’t value what’s easy—we value what we earn. The IKEA Effect shows that effort creates value and meaning. The harder you work for something, the more deeply you value it. Do hard things. Because nothing feels better than a hard-earned win.
