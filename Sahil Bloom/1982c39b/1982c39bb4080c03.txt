The Poem I Wish I Read 10 Years Ago
***********************************

Last week, a reader sent me a poem that stopped me in my
tracks...

To live content with small means.
To seek elegance rather than luxury,
and refinement rather than fashion.
To be worthy not respectable,
and wealthy not rich.
To study hard, think quietly, talk gently,
act frankly, to listen to stars, birds, babes,
and sages with open heart, to bear all cheerfully,
do all bravely, await occasions, hurry never.
In a word, to let the spiritual,
unbidden and unconscious,
grow up through the common.
This is to be my symphony.
​
- William Ellery Channing

I don’t think I’ve ever seen a better distillation of a good life
captured in so few words.

“To live content with small means”: Find your version of enough.
Never let the quest for more distract you from the beauty of
enough.

“To seek elegance rather than luxury”: Elegance is about depth.
It’s simple. Nonchalant.

“To be worthy not respectable”: Worthiness is internal, about
character. About your ability to look in the mirror and know you
did your best. You told the truth.

“Wealthy, not rich”: Rich is about money. Wealth is about
freedom, people, purpose, and health. Are you really surprised I
loved this one? I wrote a whole book (
https://click.convertkit-mail4.com/wvu37nnrx6aghkrreeef7hnv28kxxs8hwr75w/l2hehmhlzxd6mwf6/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9UeXBlcy1XZWFsdGgtVHJhbnNmb3JtYXRpdmUtR3VpZGUtRGVzaWduL2RwLzA1OTM3MjMxOFgv
) about the concept!

“To await occasions, hurry never”: To slow down in a
speed-obsessed world is the ultimate act of quiet rebellion.

“To let the spiritual…grow up through the common”: Extraordinary
things are hidden in the ordinary things that you take the time
to notice.

The beautiful final line—“This is to be my symphony”—is a
poignant reminder that you get to create the rhythm and melody of
your life.

So, perhaps the question you need to ask yourself is simple:

What will be your symphony?
