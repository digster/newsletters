Monday: The "Rules" That Hold You Back In Life
----------------------------------------------

There is a concept in cognitive science called the Illusory Truth
Effect:

It's the tendency to believe false information after consistent,
repeated exposures. In other words, if you're told something over
and over again, it takes root in your mind as a truth.

This is how suggestions slowly, silently start to feel like
rules.

If you're not careful, over time, you'll stop questioning them
and start organizing your entire life around them.

But here's the truth: Most of the "rules" shaping your life are
anything but.

This week I challenged readers to ask the question:

What rules am I following that are really just suggestions?

Friday: Chesterton's Fence: The Importance of the Origin
--------------------------------------------------------

Never remove a fence until you know why it was there in the first
place.

In our rush to criticize, it's important to first understand why
something exists in its current formтАУтАУto understand the origin.

Fences typically aren't built for no reason at all. If something
exists, there was generally a reason. Tear it down without
understanding that reason, and you may open yourself up to the
wolves it was keeping at bay.

Most discussions of Chesterton's Fence focus on change within
societies, culture, or business. It's a call-to-action to think
deeply before making sweeping change.
