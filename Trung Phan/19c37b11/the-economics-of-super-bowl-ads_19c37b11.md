SpaceX Acquires xAI
The inevitable merger of all of Elon's companies is moving apace.
Last week, Elon's space company (SpaceX) acquired Elon's frontier AI lab (xAI), —which had previously acquired Elon's social media site (X aka Twitter) — in a deal that values the combined entity at $1.25 trillion.
This caps off one of the wildest 3.5-year timelines in recent corporate history:
When Elon took Twitter private at $44B, it was a pretty clear overpay. Twitter's ad business really never had a chance against the Meta/Google/Amazon digital ad money machines and the deal was done near the COVID market top. Fidelity marked it down to $10-15B within a year.
But then Elon financial engineered a win by riding the AI wave and Twitter shareholders own SpaceX now (meanwhile, Twitter sh*tposters that are part of the creator revenue program also now technically work for SpaceX...ipso facto, I work at SpaceX).
It's the same AI wave is the rationale for the deal [per a SpaceX blog.](https://link.workweek.com/click/43992130.37138/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuc3BhY2V4LmNvbS91cGRhdGVzP2hhc2hlZF91c2VyPTBlYmVmMTJjNDI5MTE4NDU2MmRhYzA5OTY1YmNlODA4I3hhaS1qb2lucy1zcGFjZXg/6520d6df2061f24a7d071f97Bda28acf7)
Current advances in AI are dependent on large terrestrial data centers, which require immense amounts of power and cooling. Global electricity demand for AI simply cannot be met with terrestrial solutions, even in the near term, without imposing hardship on communities and the environment.
In the long term, space-based AI is obviously the only way to scale. To harness even a millionth of our Sun’s energy would require over a million times more energy than our civilization currently uses!
The only logical solution therefore is to transport these resource-intensive efforts to a location with vast power and space. I mean, space is called “space” for a reason. 😂
So, the vision is for space data centres. Maybe up to 1 million satellites, which is 100x the number that are already up there. There's a long way from here to there and Elon has a track record of overly ambitious timelines.
But the timing of everything makes a lot of sense when you consider that Anthropic and OpenAI are in talks to go public in 2026.
Retails would have shat its pants to get a taste of SpaceX on its own. A $1T valuation on $16B of revenue is expensive, except SpaceX is a monopoly provider of launching skyscrapers into space and landing them on drone barges in the middle of the ocean. Starlink is most of that revenue and has a lot more room to run...with long-term goals of funding Mars.
Now, add in an "AI data centre chip space laser beams satellite" story and SpaceX is going to raise an insane amount of money at its IPO, before OpenAI and Anthropic can get there.
All of these AI labs need fundraising. Especially with Google in its Empire Strikes Back phase, jamming Gemini down every crevice and Workspace screen it can find, while being funded by the $400B search ad machine.
OpenAI ($20B revenue, $800B valuation) and Anthropic ($10B, $350B) have found much more traction than xAI ($250-300m), which is burning $1B a month. Well, they are all burning money but OpenAI and Anthropic have clearer roads to profitability.
Matt Levine [writes](https://link.workweek.com/click/43992130.37138/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYmxvb21iZXJnLmNvbS9vcGluaW9uL25ld3NsZXR0ZXJzLzIwMjYtMDItMDMvbXVzay1zLW1vb25zaG90LW1lcmdlcj9zcm5kPXVuZGVmaW5lZCZoYXNoZWRfdXNlcj0wZWJlZjEyYzQyOTExODQ1NjJkYWMwOTk2NWJjZTgwOA/6520d6df2061f24a7d071f97B474779c7):
The combination here is not so much “use SpaceX’s revenue to fund xAI’s capital needs” as it is “use SpaceX’s fundraising ability to fund xAI’s capital needs.” There are a lot of money-hungry AI companies these days, but only one money-hungry rocket company that also does AI.
A very relevant example is Tesla buying Solar City in 2016, which many felt was Elon's car manufacturer bailing out Elon's founding investment in a solar energy firm ran by his cousins.
It's a risky play but Elon and I'm sure some SpaceX shareholders would have preferred only a focus on SpaceX. But SpaceX and xAI are both private and majority controlled by Elon (he is largest owner with 42% stake, followed by 10% for Fidelity, 10% for Founders Fund and 7% from Google)...and the $1.25 trillion valuation set mostly by his team.
Eric Berger [says of the bet:](https://link.workweek.com/click/43992130.37138/aHR0cHM6Ly9hcnN0ZWNobmljYS5jb20vYWkvMjAyNi8wMi9zcGFjZXgtYWNxdWlyZXMteGFpLXBsYW5zLTEtbWlsbGlvbi1zYXRlbGxpdGUtY29uc3RlbGxhdGlvbi10by1wb3dlci1pdC8_aGFzaGVkX3VzZXI9MGViZWYxMmM0MjkxMTg0NTYyZGFjMDk5NjViY2U4MDg/6520d6df2061f24a7d071f97Bb0cd04b2)
Musk’s plan for the merged companies is predicated on several assumptions, including that AI is not a bubble, but rather a technology that will be fully embraced in the future; that orbital data centers are cost-competitive compared to ground-based data centers; and that compute is the essential roadblock that must be solved for widespread adoption of AI by society.
If these assumptions are true, the merged SpaceX-xAI company holds a powerful position. It could potentially own a full stack of capabilities, from launch to orbital bandwidth to frontier AI models, and with Starlink Internet, it could provide AI on demand, anywhere in the world, to any mobile device.
The eventual merger with publicly-listed Tesla will be much more complicated. But Elon was on the shot clock with OpenAI and Anthropic...a the potential prize is so big, he clearly thinks it's time to go all in with SpaceX and xAI is trying to change the convo to "will space data centres be cheaper than terrestrial data centrers in 2-3 years?"
