Over the last day, I did two sets of polls comparing 16 cultural drift scenarios (detailed here [ https://substack.com/redirect/d610368b-a199-4e0e-8b97-63a215b90e21?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]) re their likelihood [ https://substack.com/redirect/cc09cc25-eb8f-43d7-9dc8-a8745d755770?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and desirability [ https://substack.com/redirect/19185705-76c1-45bc-86b4-8570231f8e9b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. Responses were unusually noisy, and desires had an especially large model fit errors. Here are best fit priorities (relative to 100 max), sorted by likelihood:
As you can see, paying parents seems the safe scenario, being 2nd highest in both likelihood and desirability. Rational culture seems the best long shot, being most desired scenario with a middle-of-pack likelihood.
Let me critique these results. On likelihood, I agree that replacement by Amish, etc. seems most likely (>30%), and malthusian AI seems pretty likely (>15%), as does something else I haven’t thought of (>5%). And a big return of war/famine/etc. also seems plausible (>5%). But the polls’ high chance  of uber capitalism seems way to high (I give <2%); most hate it. And while I see some hope of fertility DNA selection and paying parents for kids (>5% each), the poll likelihood for paying parents seems high, and they both seem unlikely to solve culture drift problems (<5%). Though it would still be great to solve just fertility. 
I’m a bit surprised to see sacred authoritarian govt rated as so likely, though I don’t especially disagree (I’d give >5%). And I’m of course disappointed to see sacred futarchy rated as the most unlikely; few think [ https://substack.com/redirect/75b2bbde-0d53-459e-87ae-13964936003d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] big social reforms can happen. Fitting with that, they rate deep multiculturalism as second most unlikely. 
Maybe the biggest surprise of this polls to me is the not-low likelihood and top desirability rating for rational culture. I agree it seems desirable, but to me this scenario seems a big ask (and thus <5%): 
A rational cultural agent would … look at their entire historical dataset of known cultures, their values, and their adaptive successes, combine that with their best theories of natural selection and the world, to estimate the culture priorities most likely to be adaptive in relevant contexts. … If such rational culture agents could coordinate with others, … they might do better to conduct a much more detailed analysis together, and then simultaneously explore many possible cultural values, in order to more quickly discover which were more adaptive. (More [ https://substack.com/redirect/43b74e1e-72d9-466a-8a2e-4fa93b4fb2c8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] more [ https://substack.com/redirect/f3c17f59-3046-4325-b0c7-85f3a66542e0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ])
Our book The Elephant in the Brain [ https://substack.com/redirect/cb8d18a8-3e93-473b-b531-dc499937f137?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and my more recent work on the sacred [ https://substack.com/redirect/71a8f4d7-1694-4711-90f9-5570bb4e3419?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], detail how deeply embedded in us are habits of not being honest and practical about our key motives. Seems really hard to change that fast. Though I have to admit that these habits are probably [ https://substack.com/redirect/29e4554c-f268-4e8d-aba4-2c450980b27a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] cultural and not DNA-based, suggesting fast changes might be possible. A generalized return seems like a smaller ask, even though yes it also seems a pretty big ask.
