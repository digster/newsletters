The following are some (lognormal fit) median estimates from some [ https://substack.com/redirect/3482325d-ca5c-4e66-8b3c-977ce1079eb9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] X [ https://substack.com/redirect/38df49e4-97e2-4172-bf68-f44cefa4ea94?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] polls [ https://substack.com/redirect/74b05c10-808a-4c39-a8e6-8b4320b742ad?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] I just ran.
First, if we knew for a fact that at least one apparently incredibly-abled UFO sighting in last century was really incredible and not from a then-current human Earth power, it would make sense to spend ~1.0% [ https://substack.com/redirect/3482325d-ca5c-4e66-8b3c-977ce1079eb9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of world income, or ~$1.2T, on researching and studying UFOs. Second, the actual median estimate chance of that fact is ~1.3x10^-5 [ https://substack.com/redirect/38df49e4-97e2-4172-bf68-f44cefa4ea94?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. So if the best amount to spend on UFOs were proportional to this chance, that would imply ~$150M/yr UFO spending. But third, median best spending on UFOs is only ~$65M/yr [ https://substack.com/redirect/74b05c10-808a-4c39-a8e6-8b4320b742ad?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and that is about twice as big as the ~$20-50M/yr that 5 LLMs estimate the world actually spends. So we are spending a factor of five less than chance proportionality advises.
I personally estimate a >1% chance, three orders of magnitude larger than the median estimate. This puts me in the top 20% of poll respondents. For this, proportionality implies >$12B/yr spending, which makes sense to me. That is, we are spending way too little on this topic.
Can we find betting odds here? I find bookies offering 20:1 [ https://substack.com/redirect/efc2f748-7313-44e5-9b12-c19181e3c420?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and 100:1 [ https://substack.com/redirect/f8c17f06-0b37-47d3-a9b0-aed9291c4398?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] odds on discovery of alien life anywhere in the next year, and one bet on 100:1 [ https://substack.com/redirect/704f67cd-11a9-45f3-b506-cccca9abac8a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] odds on finding aliens in the next decade. I also find an equal-parties 150:1 [ https://substack.com/redirect/d072261a-c5a9-436b-a769-dd7136e50432?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] odds bet on finding clear evidence of UFOs as aliens in five years, and an offer [ https://substack.com/redirect/1f90e7e0-f09f-4e12-89e2-0438e325d6c4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] at those odds for three years. These seem to me much more consistent with a >1% than a 10^-5 total chance.
I thus see great social gains from better consensus betting odds on this topic, which could come from more liquid betting markets on this, and might induce more appropriate much higher spending levels.
