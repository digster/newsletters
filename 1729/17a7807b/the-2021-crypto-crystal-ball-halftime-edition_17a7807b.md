Today we have a guest post by It's always fun to check predictions vs. results, and we're now about halfway through the year. How are these predictions holding up so far? It’s that time of year again: announcing the Annual Crystal Ball. Similar to
- Bitcoin continues to cross the chasm, deepening its institutional adoption along the “digital gold” narrative.
- Ethereum begins its institutional phase as it solidifies its role as the “settlement layer” for the new global finance stack.
- The stablecoin issuance hockey stick continues and we see early signs of traction in “stablecoin-native” financial apps/services for individuals and businesses.
- We will see at least one centralized company launch a decentralized, user-owned/operated crypto-network where the crypto-network has legitimate checks and balances over the company’s actions.
Lastly, digital art and online creator economies are gonna explode and tokenization is going to turn creative economics upside down. Culture creators will become crypto rich. Someone will tokenize and sell themselves — I’m taking bids if anyone is interested.
- The growing ecosystem of hardware wallets and nodes will continue to flourish and trend upwards. We’ll see new players and DIY projects come onto the scene. The last few years taught us this is one of the most rewarding aspects for hobbyists, many of which are eager to build their hardware wallet collection and run multiple kinds of nodes.
- Along those lines, I think we’ll see an increase in development related to the PSBT standard. It shines because it enables interoperability between air gapped devices and the demand for it is only going to grow in the coming year. 2021 is going to be a year of development that will bring a new set of features to hardware wallets/signing devices and expand the way we think about them. This will be driven by work seen in 2020 like efforts to standardize communication via QR codes as well as a proposal for a new PSBT version.
- Increased usage on the Lightning network. Any kind of bull run is going to force activity onto layer two for wins in scalability and lower fees. 2020 was a huge year for Lightning infrastructure and many pain points like liquidity management, routing, and peer selection have been addressed with new tooling. Exchanges that have integrated Lightning, folks who run nodes, and businesses that accept payment via Lightning are going to reap big rewards for their hard work.
- Education and Gen Z adoption. This is my prediction with the least amount of evidence, the one that really embodies armchair fortune telling. I believe price movements in 2021 will drive a wave of highly accessible, beginner friendly educational material. What could follow is more adoption from Gen Z. I mean, who knew 2020 would be the year all the Boomers would come onto the scene? Maximalists may or may not call any of this “true” adoption, but there are many companies well positioned to bring on a new user base with their custodial services and Bitcoin reward programs. I wouldn’t be surprised if we see Bitcoin enter pop culture in a way we haven’t seen before. Who knows? Maybe we’ll see Bitcoin pop up in a few rap videos.
Adoption of better (read: chain-avoiding/compressing like lightning and ZK-based compression) scaling technologies will be more mainstream and move past the early adopter stage.

- BTC will continue to dominate as more institutions/corporations invest/get involved.
- We’ll see more large fintechs adopting crypto — or at least BTC — as a way to expand their offerings and attract/maintain customers
- There will be continued consolidation of projects and features, particularly in DeFi as projects fight to attract users.
- Investors will continue to make predictions by extrapolating the 3-month moving average of the latest narratives. Founders will continue to build the ideas they wake up every morning thinking about.
- The thinking around governance and reporting of financials will begin to mature. We will see more projects self-reporting financials in an effort to improve transparency, accountability, and decision-making.
- As the industry matures from something that looks and feels like an R&D stage to an increasingly commercial stage, competition will heat up and look a bit more like it does for traditional companies. Projects will compete on vectors like cost, product offering, and distribution; some projects will vertically integrate while others go horizontal.
- The crypto industry will have a record year of consolidation as the M&A market heats up and fintech / banks scoop up crypto companies.
- The industry will see its first true social consumer application take off with a non-crypto audience.
- With Libra launching, we’ll see another announcement of a fiat-backed stablecoin from a tech player. There will be extreme regulatory focus / pressure on stablecoin issuance and surveillance.
- An episode of crypto-dollarization is acknowledged as playing a significant role in the collapse of a sovereign currency (which is not the Venezuelan Bolivar).
- A major stablecoin (>$1b free float) suffers a bank run and trades under 90c/dollar for an extended period of time (at least a month).
- Investors start to question the qualities of ‘governance tokens’ and begin to demand more explicitly codified tokenholder rights.
- The total supply of stablecoins exceeds $50b.
- At least a half-dozen exchanges or custodians worldwide institute regular proof of reserve protocols.
- African markets will bootstrap faster adoption of Lightning Network. A lot of micro transaction remittances are going to develop on the lightning network.
- A lot of inflow into USD-denominated Stablecoins as lot more people discover it as a way to hedge against local currency devaluation especially in the face of potential covid-driven recession in various developing economies.
- Clearer regulation — 2020 has seen massive growth and adoption. Governments will start reacting by providing a lot more framework for thinking about crypto.
- There will be at least a momentary market cap flippening between a smart contract-focused blockchain and ethereum. Ethereum will suffer some punishment for its slow development over the years as its blockchain gets even more congested.
Yes, of course we all agree that the Bitcoin system cannot be “shut down,” but if we want crypto more broadly to reach its true potential and make a difference in billions of lives, then we need to work with regulators and policy makers so it can do just that. Fortunately, through the countless hours we have spent working with regulators and policy makers, many are not afraid; they have engaged with the industry in meaningful ways and they understand the potential of the technology we are building. That said, the amount of regulatory and policy engagement we need to do in 2021 could easily rival the aggregate of our work in all years prior. We are rolling up our sleeves and welcoming the opportunity to spend time ensuring that these important constituents understand the true benefits that decentralization and crypto bring to global economies and, most importantly, to all individuals.
Meanwhile, Lightning will have a breakout year; ETH layer 2 solutions will mature and become commonplace; and I’m looking forward to at least one crypto-integrated game (and subsequent NFTs) becoming mainstream.
