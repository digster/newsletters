In this task, we want you to learn Elixir through Exercism, and will even pay you $100 in crypto for solving three Elixir problems yourself with full source code. But why Exercism and Elixir? Exercism: open-source educationExercism is a collection of programming exercises sorted by language and difficulty. The cool thing about Exercism is that it doesn’t just provide questions or answers — it provides a tool that you can use to score your source code against the correct answer. Here are some screenshots from Why is this interesting? Well, several reasons: - It’s completely
[open source](https://github.com/exercism/)and anyone can[add to](https://github.com/exercism/problem-specifications)its open question bank in a[defined format](https://github.com/exercism/problem-specifications/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md#implementing-a-completely-new-exercise) - It’s broad in scope and covers many languages, like
[PLEAC](http://pleac.sourceforge.net/) - It turns unit testing on its head. Normally programmers write many small tests to test a program, but here the programs are tests of the programmer
- It has bite-size lessons, similar to
[Brilliant](https://brilliant.org/)or[Project Euler](https://projecteuler.net/) - It allows people to self-score themselves and their progress, as a complement to higher-commitment MOOCs like Coursera and Udacity
- And it offers primitives that could be the basis for a proof-of-skill, underpinning a new digital diploma and eventually resume
We’ll go further into Exercism in later tasks, but go set up an account and try it out.
