This week, I fulfilled a childhood dream: I sold my first copies of a book I wrote.
For awhile now, I’ve been picking out some of my favorite pieces of writing — articles, tweets, essays, notes to self — and revising them, re-arranging them until I liked the way they sat together.
Once I had the pieces I wanted, it became clear what the central theme of my best writing is, so I made that theme the title: We’re Here to Renew the Sacred.
Most of the pieces in the collection are lightly reworked versions of essays I’ve already posted here or on In•Star — pieces like “[The One Essential Quality](https://substack.com/redirect/bfdcc3a2-ed89-49c3-9407-047bdaa53c6a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o),” “[Source-Tissue Festoon](https://substack.com/redirect/ca54656c-7c0d-44a4-8d05-a9e0c20407b6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o),” and “[Deep Aliveness in the Rain-Soaked Pines](https://substack.com/redirect/869b85e9-393f-4a90-adb0-090a323d230c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o).” But there are also a couple of new pieces, one of which I consider some of the best writing I’ve ever done, and which I only [shared previously](https://substack.com/redirect/ec36e72d-02be-4276-8ac3-d2c5cccc3885?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o) with a small group of people close to me.
