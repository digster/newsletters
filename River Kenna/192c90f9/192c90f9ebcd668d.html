Essay Collection & Course Discounts!

This week, I released "We're Here to Renew the Sacred," and put my Somatic Resonance and Imaginal Journeying courses on sale,

River Kenna
Oct 26
 
READ IN APP
 

Highlights:

  • I put out an essay collection, and you can get it here.

  • Somatic Resonance and Imaginal Journeying are both on sale this month, 30% off with the discount code UPEKKHA.

  • I’ll be in Asia for a couple months or so, you might be hearing less from me.

This week, I fulfilled a childhood dream: I sold my first copies of a book I wrote.

For awhile now, I’ve been picking out some of my favorite pieces of writing — articles, tweets, essays, notes to self — and revising them, re-arranging them until I liked the way they sat together.

Once I had the pieces I wanted, it became clear what the central theme of my best writing is, so I made that theme the title: We’re Here to Renew the Sacred.

Most of the pieces in the collection are lightly reworked versions of essays I’ve already posted here or on In•Star — pieces like “The One Essential Quality,” “Source-Tissue Festoon,” and “Deep Aliveness in the Rain-Soaked Pines.” But there are also a couple of new pieces, one of which I consider some of the best writing I’ve ever done, and which I only shared previously with a small group of people close to me.

If you’ve liked a couple of my essays, and would like a guided tour through some of my favorites, pick up We’re Here to Renew the Sacred. It comes in both epub and pdf; no physical copies for now, but that may change next year.

And secondly, this month felt like a good time to put my two course on embodiment and imaginal work on sale. If you’ve been curious about either (or both), October is a good time to dive in with the discount code for 30% off: UPEKKHA.

And a personal update on me: in the next week or two, I’ll be headed to India for some intensive Ayurveda treatment, and I won’t be back in Europe for a couple months, most likely.

The time zones and treatments might make it a little harder to get ahold of me for a while, but don’t hesitate to try if you have something to ask me. I’ll get back to you as soon as I’m able.
