Over and over again, I keep finding that the Path is mostly about Attention [ https://substack.com/redirect/abc67386-062a-44e5-9ee8-df8cc082a65e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Trust [ https://substack.com/redirect/dad5f895-a1ae-4b4e-88f1-fc010dceb914?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and Constancy.
There's endless language, frames, and metaphors we can use for each of those elements — here are some of my favorite pointers and ways we'll work with them:
Attention
Attention matters because it shapes everything about who you become, what you do, what's important and salient in your life. Attention is a form of worship [ https://substack.com/redirect/abc67386-062a-44e5-9ee8-df8cc082a65e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. You have to worship well, and you have to choose what to worship well. When practicing with attention like this, we can talk in terms of the gods you worship, the vow you took before you were born, your qualities of presence, the realms you inhabit... lots of ways to point at this. Some of them (gods, vows, realms...) help to point at how deeply and vastly sacred attention is. Others (modes of attending, qualities of presence, salience landscapes...) are more neutral or rationality-coded. However feels best to start working with it, here are some of the pieces most relevant to our curriculum:
Tell me what you pay attention to and I will tell you who you are.
— José Ortega y Gasset
If we turn our minds towards the good, it is impossible that little by little the whole soul will not be attracted thereto in spite of itself.
— Simone Weil
When we decide not to worship divinity, we do not stop worshipping: we merely find something else less worthy to worship.
— Iain McGilchrist, The Master and His Emissary
Your relationships, projects, media diet — everything you pay attention to shapes who you are and how your life goes. We can intentionally shape our lives and souls by intentionally attuning out attention with higher values (gods, vows, attractors, etc). 
Trust
Whether or not you rationally buy into the truth of it, it's best to act as if Reality is fundamentally trustworthy. When we can surrender to what wants to happen, life unfolds much better than you could have ever planned or controlled. When you build trust in Reality, and in the parts of your Self that sense Reality, everything starts to unlock. 
"Love is based on our capacity to trust in a reality beyond fear, to trust a timeless truth bigger than all our difficulties" — Jack Kornfield
"God's will is done, and we are the ones to do it." We are capable of sensing what wants to happen in the world, what wants to happen in our lives — and we are capable of surrendering to those Desires of Reality. (Surrender in this case being often quite active — you very often have to do something.)
There's a difference between "my ego trusts that the world will fulfill my ego's desires" on the one hand, and "I trust, from my deep Self, that Reality is unfolding exactly how it needs to" on the other.
You can trust your own perceptions, desires, and sense of Reality. You may have to adjust that trust over time, but you can always trust yourself.
"Aren’t you strong enough to walk alone? We must each of us find God by ourselves.” ― André Gide. This is only possible if you can trust both yourself and Reality.
Constancy
People tend to associate spiritual practice with "consistency." Sitting on the meditation cushion for an hour every day, taking care of the family altar, getting to church every Sunday, whatever it is. Personally, I find constancy much more helpful than consistency. 
Rather than having set habits or check-ins repeated at regular intervals, I prefer to be constantly in practice. When walking to the gym, expand my awareness and note any places where my body is constricted. While watching the rain, keep my perception centered in the heart and watch with eyes that recognize Beauty. When in the middle of a difficult conversation, stay with my deep sense of what is precious to me about this relationship. While cooking, keep centered in love and care for the people who will eat the food. So on and so forth. 
Of course you fall out of this kind of awareness over and over again, but we're not looking for perfection — we're looking for an awareness that comes back, over and over again, to what matters most. 
"The direction and constancy of the will is what really matters." — Evelyn Underhill
"You are not punished for your sins, but by them. You are not rewarded for your worship, but by it." Everything you do, you're either in the sacred stream or out of it. This can feel stressful or constricting to some people, but it can be a beautiful and motivating practice, when you find the flow.
Bringing your practice off the cushion and into daily life is often a struggle for people. Treating your practice with constancy undercuts that struggle in unbelievable ways. 
Those are the main focuses for Mysterium, and most of the practice will stem from there. Some specifics we'll also bring in are things like the following:
Find Your Gods/Vows: You very often have repeated patterns or events in your life, over and over again, which point you towards something very important at the core of you. Something that's been trying to get your attention for decades.
Embodying your Gods/Vows: Once you've figured out how your attention wants to attune to the sacred, it's a matter of embodying that. We have a lot of practices for figuring that out and practicing with it. 
Stay with What's Precious: Art, sex, cooking, driving — every human activity really: the energy you do something with affects how it goes, how it's received, how it moves in the world. If you play music by rote, it feels dead. If you have sex out of duty or boredom, the lack of care is palpable. If you cook with deep love and care, the food is more tasty and nourishing. This holds true for everything you do. So it's best to practice staying dynamically in the center of the energy that drives everything you do in life, and stay in touch with it while you act. 
Ritual, Literalizing Inner Processes: Your unconscious works in the language of experience and imagery — you can take advantage of this by bypassing language and speaking to it with experience and image. It's best to use examples maybe: I had a client who felt frozen very often. He couldn't make decisions or make moves on what he did decide. I told him to spend time every day taking an ice cube out of the freezer and watching it melt. Within a few weeks, his freeze had softened and he was doing things. — Literalizing inner processes like this has been a major practice for me over and over again.
This retreat is going to be pretty dynamically responsive — we'll flow with what people need, what people are responding to, what needs to happen. But the plans are going to flow along these lines, deepening into Attention, Trust, and Constancy.
Or, to state it a couple of other ways: we'll be cultivating deep surrender to eros. We'll be constantly practicing faith in Reality. We'll be establishing self-trust and reshaping our attention. We'll be worshipping a world that wants to be born. We'll be devoting ourselves to the unfolding of Everything.
Also, we'll just be hanging out and walking around in the woods. Doing some circling. Cooking food together. Does Casa Tilo have a nice TV? Movie night could be nice. There will be campfires, but mostly for ritual reasons (we're gonna do some yagnas, I love yagnas).
