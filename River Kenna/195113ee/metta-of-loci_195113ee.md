I’ve been doing this thing where I drop back into memories of specific places in my life — mostly my childhood.
I don’t really focus on the events that took place there, or the people, or whatever else — I just close my eyes, relax and go to the place. Then I wander around for awhile, releasing any tension or negativity from the location, and instead infuse the area with some love, some metta, some power, some agency… whatever feels good at the moment. Just let the place soak in it.
While I do this, I wander the area and take some ownership of it. In the kitchen at my childhood church, for example, I opened all the cupboards, moved some stuff around, used the stove to boil some water, took a juice out of the fridge and drank it. I opened the windows and climbed in and out of them. Danced on the countertops. Changed the colors of the walls. I generally took some time to make the place feel like mine, rather than the way it felt when I was a kid — a place that belonged to someone else, which I shouldn’t interfere with.
Once the place feels relaxed, loving, and mine, I’m pretty much done. I just take a few more minutes to bask in it and hang out. Sometimes at this point a memory will come up that took place in the space, and I’ll let it play out, maybe tinker around with it, maybe let my younger self know everything’s fine and he’s in control of all of it. Maybe invite space aliens down to abduct the mean principal — that one made my child-self laugh, that was a fun time.
But sometimes no other memory or impression shows up. Usually I just wander the place, relax the place, love the place, own the place — and then open my eyes and go about my day.
Idk, it feels like a nice practice; I’m enjoying it. Has a similar vibe to things like the Ideal Parent Figure Protocol or memory work, except it’s about the location more than the people or events.
Just wanted to share it, in case some of you want to play with it. If you play with it, please report back to me after awhile — I’ll be keeping track of effects on myself, and I’d love to hear about it from others.
