Building off my It appears to me we're opening up something — if not a new type of shaman, then at least new possibilities and importance for a type of shaman that hasn't had them before. Shamans are, primarily, for the community. A shaman tends to the wholeness of their folk. That's the job. There's any number of ways they do this, but traditionally, the job is done within their local community (or, Nowadays, that's still important and vital; we still need people tending to the wholeness of the folk around them. But as the social situation has atomized, as people have gone on the move, become more mobile, become accustomed to fewer social ties and less community connection — as modernity has developed, new possibilities (and necessities) have come up. I've thought about this a fair amount because I can feel it; my role, as I might expect from a nomadic existence like mine, doesn't seem to have a lot to do with my local surroundings. I admire people who work deeply with their local community, with the land, the patch of earth they find themselves on, and the people they find themselves surrounded by — but that doesn't seem to be my role. For me, the area of focus is less on any specific people than on a particular frequency. I have to find the frequency, live there, and broadcast from there — and My People are the ones who hear that frequency, who reverberate and resonate with it, and follow it back to me. Those are the folk whose wholeness I'm meant to tend. This has led to me having a pretty widely distributed global network of friends, allies, clients, and partners who align with me, who can hear the same frequency — but who don't necessarily share much with one another as far as location, demographics, or even the specifics of what wants to be tended to within them. I think this capacity of the shaman archetype — the ability to find wider audiences and reach the exact people that resonate with their frequency — is a pretty new capacity that's only been starting to wake up the past couple decades or so. Writing opened up a certain amount of it over the past centuries, but not enough for the archetype to really stretch its legs and get to work. There's something important here that I'm not quite expressing and not quite sure I can express yet. It's connected to the frame shift necessary for field shamanism to happen in the first place — this frame shift from the people to the frequency. A shift from the local conditions of the field over to the field itself, and the way it's resonating. There's something important and deep happening there; the field harmonics being primary, and the people you tend to being a part of that field and its resonances. Something about the way that tending to the wholeness of folks is both a method of tending to the field, and a byproduct of tending to the field. There's a unity there. A smoothness. A cleanness of the Work. This connects to a larger worldview shift wherein a given person's conscious ego is a very very small part of who they are, and arguably a fairly illusory part. This type of wholeness-tending isn't for the ego — it may naturally lead to a more appropriate and balanced ego, but the work exists for the whole, not that one small part of the wholeness. By having a focus on tending to the field through tending to the people resonating in that field, there's a magic that happens: the conscious ego of both the helper and the helped can fade into the background more. They don't matter so much in this container — and they can accept and even be excited by not mattering so much in the larger process. Which is huge. In moments of greatest resonance and wholeness, the ego is like a lighthouse keeper on a cliff, looking out at waves the size of mountains — not resenting the sea for not being about this lighthouse or its keeper, but grateful and in awe at the vastness and strength of the world around them, the forces that shape their place in it. I'm ranting, I'm on a tangent, I'm figuring this out in real time — is there something here? I can feel something I'm touching beneath words, and it matters deeply, but I'm not sure if the words are carrying what they need to carry. I might not know until I revisit this piece in a few days.
