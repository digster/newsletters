In defence of cars, sprawl and natural disasters
With careful planning, roads and cars can be an efficient means of transport even in dense cities [ https://substack.com/redirect/f5e9fecb-ce68-4ac2-943c-11144ab016f9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. For example, ring roads are often inefficient for drivers because, instead of going directly from A to B, they need to go round the urban centre they are trying to reach. But they spare city centres from traffic so they can be efficient in terms of land use. To make ring roads fast enough that they are appealing to drivers in London, we would need 44 lanes [ https://substack.com/redirect/1984dfe7-6558-49ec-ada6-d7568c239ad0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (ideally spread over multiple ring roads, not as one 44 lane monstrosity).
Why America should sprawl [ https://substack.com/redirect/9fe60f7c-5384-4a81-ad97-60037cc3a6a7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (as well as build up).
There used to be a silver lining to natural disasters – they cleared the way for new development. But today’s rules mean that rebuilding is often more constrained than original construction [ https://substack.com/redirect/b1d45c0b-942b-4209-99fb-5d5b1239a734?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
Tract was a venture-backed company built to address Britain’s housing crisis by improving the planning permission process. Its founders have published a post-mortem [ https://substack.com/redirect/8c0f7cea-d968-48a9-8a10-b435dafe56ca?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on why they are closing the business and what went wrong: the conservatism of the property market, the lack of scale in Britain, and their unforced errors.
Texas has very cheap housing by American standards, in part, due to their special Municipal Utility Districts [ https://substack.com/redirect/1b6b3853-4690-4261-abea-cb1255bdcd45?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] which allow developers to build infrastructure upfront and have future residents repay the costs over time.
National elections are rarely swayed by local infrastructure, so it is rarely a priority for national politicians. However, local elections can be decided by local conditions so devolving infrastructure decisions to mayors [ https://substack.com/redirect/1d0afe81-f43e-4ae6-ae54-07bdbb714e4b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] could mean more and better infrastructure in the UK.

How hypertumors stop whales from getting cancer, and other science
As animals get bigger and have longer life spans they have more cells which go through more cell divisions. We should expect this to mean the cell’s DNA in larger animals has more copying errors and they get more cancer. Instead we see the opposite. There are two main theories why [ https://substack.com/redirect/468c8ff0-0f1c-453a-8cfa-e81a24140156?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. First, cancerous tissues are themselves more vulnerable to getting cancer. A creature like a blue whale can survive having gram-sized tumors that grow rapidly and die. In a smaller animal, a similarly sized growth would kill them. Second, we have found evidence in elephants of genes that are protective against cancer itself. It is plausible that similar adaptations exist in all large animals.

This isn’t strictly true, but the science to make these new dire wolf-like pups is impressive [ https://substack.com/redirect/ad40f783-a322-4d00-8ca2-313f3ad13373?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] nonetheless.

Edwin Chon developed methods for plasma fractionation [ https://substack.com/redirect/b660bd03-5b69-4371-ab66-ad1ffd83f4ab?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] – extracting useful proteins like albumin from blood – and scaled it up to treat wounded soldiers in World War Two. Under the pressure of the war he built a team of chemists, engineers and technicians that iterated quickly to build something that could work on the front lines. After the war he returned to fundamental research and deeply regretted it because without the urgency of wartime, his lab lost much of its dynamism.
