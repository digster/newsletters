Books
Why Nothing Works: Who Killed Progress―and How to Bring It Back [ https://substack.com/redirect/e01b2a54-3535-46d5-ae07-1673ad56000b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is a new book from Marc J Dunkelman. He talks about the contradiction between the left’s ‘Hamiltonian’ impulse to give the state more power to enact good works and their ‘Jeffersonian’ impulse to protect citizens from whims of people in power. He argues that these contradictory impulses lead to a system of vetocracy [ https://substack.com/redirect/50c2eb70-8e6d-428a-b9d6-161d0c7cda23?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in which it is difficult to get anything done like build infrastructure, fight climate change, or have a working welfare system. If a book is too long for you, you can listen to Marc [ https://substack.com/redirect/9a107935-b14c-469d-be7e-a981157c4c92?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] summarize his thesis on Matthew Yglesias’s podcast.
