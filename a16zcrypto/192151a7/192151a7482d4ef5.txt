✨Spotlight: AI x crypto 
with Dan Boneh, Ali Yahya, Ben Fielding, Harry Grieve; Sonal Chokshi and Jason Rosenthal 
This week we’re highlighting a popular — and more relevant than ever — theme: how crypto can benefit AI, and how AI can benefit crypto. 
Building AI systems requires ever-larger deployments of computational power for training today’s largest and most powerful models. But privileged access to computing power, and the economies of scale for large data centers, gives big tech companies an advantage over startups. To level the playing field, startups and other companies need affordable access to large-scale computing power. So in this just-released (and final) talk in our Crypto Startup Accelerator [ https://substack.com/redirect/e77c9504-f758-4146-b348-06569de5eda4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] CSX Spring 2024 playlist [ https://substack.com/redirect/9b55ca09-1827-4a4a-96a0-af5a9fdc1b8a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], the cofounders of Gensyn discuss the importance of building a permissionless, decentralized, machine learning protocol upon which AI models can train… on virtually any compute device in the world. watch the talk here [ https://substack.com/redirect/a5894877-8e5b-4150-8b1d-8b800409586d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]

🔐Trend: Trusted execution environments (TEEs)
Simply put [ https://substack.com/redirect/3c773ffd-9d01-403b-acfb-e290b98e1759?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], TEEs provide an isolated environment where applications can be executed — allowing operators to run a job inside of that trusted execution environment regardless of the rest of the computing system. They are thus a powerful mechanism for designing secure systems, with many applications in cloud computing and beyond. And though they have been around for a while, TEEs have been advancing recently thanks to [ https://substack.com/redirect/f9d89dd7-08fb-49ca-be4e-bcfacb53e339?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] ongoing work by the community of people working on blockchains. Below are a couple more resources on the topic from our site and around the web:
On TEEs for blockchain applications by Ari Juels (a16z crypto research seminar presentation, 2023)
Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) are a powerful security tool, and their functionality in principle subsumes a gamut of cryptographic primitives. Cornell University’s Juels discusses the use of TEEs in blockchain infrastructure — focusing on block-building (one of the first specific applications where hardware-based TEEs took hold). He discusses leveraging TEEs to enforce fair ordering of user transactions. He also suggests an approach to addressing security concerns in TEEs, and privacy-preserving systems more generally. watch here [ https://substack.com/redirect/f4c48f31-74ba-40b4-94bc-9b5823aaa32e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] 
Confidential computing proofs: An alternative to cryptographic zero-knowledge by Mark Russinovich, Cédric Fournet, Greg Zaverucha, Josh Benaloh, Brandon Murdoch, Manuel Costa (ACM Queue, September 2024)
This article attempts to relate zero-knowledge [ https://substack.com/redirect/162f8d49-133a-4a4d-93b7-65c420f8f1e3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and confidential-computing proof concepts, which were developed by different communities under different trust assumptions. A variety of TEEs are now available to help achieve soundness and zero knowledge — but with important differences. While TEEs are not suitable for all proof applications, the article explores these tradeoffs and characteristics for proof systems. access pdf here [ https://substack.com/redirect/1544b452-45e0-4e4e-979b-5db98cfb9801?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]
