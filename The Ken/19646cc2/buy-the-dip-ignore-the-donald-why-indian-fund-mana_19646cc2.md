As I was reading the story we have for you today, one phrase was on loop in my head: fools rush in where angels fear to tread.
Public-market investors loathe uncertainty. And Donald Trump’s America has uncertainty in spades. The country’s top stock indices, the S&P 500 and the Nasdaq 100, are down sharply since Trump first announced his reciprocal tariffs.
India’s stock market, on the other hand, has recouped its losses and then some. So why did some of India’s top fund managers tell Anand, the author of the story, that there’s no time like now to buy US equities?
It’s not because they think Trump has discovered common sense. The US, for all its waning influence, is still home to world-beating companies, and their appeal to investors is in spite of Trump.
Maybe it doesn’t hurt to be a “fool”.
