Good Morning Ishan,
This is my first time reaching out to you on a Sunday. You may have already seen some of my stories by now. But today, I am writing to remind you of possibilities.
I pivoted from law to journalism—both highly demanding professions but full of possibilities. Right now, I am exploring those hopes and hazards and letting the journey unfold.
This week, The Ken, too, dug into stories of how businesses are hemming on the world of possibilities—how stakeholders in markets as diverse as grocery or ransomware are grappling with changing circumstances.
For instance, Aayush’s insightful story on Monday unravelled how a once-promising and popular company, [LBB](https://the-ken.com/story/why-10-year-old-lbb-had-to-sell-to-10-year-old-nykaa/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), sold to Nykaa. The story goes deep into the pitfalls of the content-to-commerce business model.
On Tuesday, Seetharaman wrote on how the young fast-fashion chain, [Zudio](https://the-ken.com/story/westside-makes-way-for-a-new-star-in-tatas-retail-universe-zudio/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), is surpassing the 24-year-old Westside’s footprint. It can soon become not just parent Trent’s biggest revenue driver but also the Tata Group’s most visible retail brand. Clearly, a lot is riding on Zudio.
Soumyajit and Gaurav on Wednesday told an incredible story of how the [Indian e-sports ecosystem](https://the-ken.com/story/third-times-the-charm-indian-esports-regroups-after-pubg-bgmi-bans/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter)—even after taking critical hits both in terms of viewership and revenue—is pre-emptively moving on. This is a must-read for all PUBG and BGMI fans.
On Thursday, Arundhati told the story of [Slice, Uni, and other fintechs](https://the-ken.com/story/slice-uni-cards-other-fintechs-start-over-as-rbi-says-no-to-easy-credit/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter): how their lending dreams are cut short by the RBI’s ban on credit-line-backed prepaid cards. These companies have a few options ahead of them, but they are riddled with challenges.
Maitri and Gaurav on Friday told the story of [PharmEasy](https://the-ken.com/story/a-debt-fuelled-spree-that-led-to-5-4b-pharmeasys-catch-22/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). The healthtech startup has found itself in a dicey situation after relentless acquisitions and 24 loans. It will be a hard sell to justify its valuation while raising funds to take on Tata-1mg and Reliance-backed Netmeds.
