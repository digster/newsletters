Retail is a ruthless business. Online grocery even more so. There have been many well-funded casualties in India in this space. BigBasket has not only been a survivor but has landed itself in a sweet spot as major e-commerce companies are figuring that grocery is the new recharge.
 
So, first the top-up part.
 
India’s largest online grocer, BigBasket is raising money: $280 million. The deal has been signed off by all the parties and a formal announcement is just around the corner. The e-grocer (though the business is more about grocery and less about ‘e’) has chosen Paytm-Alibaba over Amazon.
 
In doing so it has unleashed a three-way battle—Amazon, which will now ride solo; Flipkart, which has swallowed its pride and is once again getting into grocery; and BigBasket-Paytm-Alibaba combine.
 
Today Payal, Patanjali and Venkat tell us that for Paytm Mall, which has stiff competition from Flipkart and Amazon in the top-selling categories such as mobile phone and fashion, a presence in the grocery sector is a land grab. “By lining up with BigBasket, the platform can drive transactions to become the ‘top of mind’ portal for the high frequency business.”
 
Now, everyone is trying to amplify this ‘frequency’. Because unlike electronics or garments, grocery is a local play with local winners and losers.
