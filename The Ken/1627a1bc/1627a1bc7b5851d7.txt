Good Morning Ishan,

This weekend is a bit of a misnomer. I keep wondering if today is a Saturday or a Sunday, and then I realise that it is a Saturday and it gives me great joy. If you think about it, we have had two Saturdays this week. You know the best part about weekends? I don't have to squeeze myself into the insanely crowded local trains in Mumbai. 

I am using this extra Saturday to fix my Batman lamp. I made it some time ago, and let us say, it has seen better days. (I know, I know my weekends are exciting). I will also catch up on some reading. My bookmarks are piling up.

While I do that, why don't you catch up on some of our excellent stories, too?

We kicked-off the week with Shashi's piece on KYC https://the-ken.com/story/kyc-business-disruption/ (or?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Know Your Customer), three words that spook Indian businesses, ranging from telco giants to fintech startups nowadays. Either get your customer KYC’d or shut down your service; there is no escape from the KYC storm.

On Tuesday, Harveen made her debut https://the-ken.com/story/doordarshan-data-free/ and?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter wrote about how Doordarshan plans to ride on radio waves and take on the likes of Netflix and Hotstar. Really, that's their plan. A battle between DTT technology and OTT platforms. Read the story to know more what we are talking about.

We had Venkat’s piece https://the-ken.com/story/the-reliance-jio-symphony/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Wednesday, where he explores what the surprise merger between JioMusic and Saavn means for the digital music space in India, and whether we’ll see more consolidation in the days to come. Also, do you know, Venkat is not active on social media anymore?

On Thursday (our free story), Ruhi told us https://the-ken.com/story/surprise-winners-indian-states-diagnostics-race/ about?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter how pathology lab giants like Dr Lal and SRL are losing out to younger companies like Techmed Healthcare in capturing government-led opportunities in diagnostics. Can partnerships with Indian states enable small entrepreneurs to go toe-to-toe with existing market leaders?

And then we closed the week with Patanjali’s piece https://the-ken.com/story/pankaj-chaddah-zomato/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Pankaj Chaddah, the outgoing co-founder of Zomato. He is seen as the muscle behind the operations, but you don't hear from him too often because he won't talk to you. 

That's about it. See, no April Fool's joke.
