The bundling we are talking about is not something that’ll leave you with extra tees, jeans, handbags, or even grocery for that matter. Here, expensive drugs are being bundled with free care at home.
 
I know, ‘free’ in healthcare is a misnomer. But maybe once, just once, the patient is winning here.
 
Specialty drugs—those that need assisted administration and cost anywhere from Rs 10K per dose to several lakhs for a course—are the fastest growing segment in Indian pharma. And a few home healthcare companies find themselves suitably placed to be the middlemen between pharma and patients. Because the Indian drug regulation doesn’t allow drug-makers to get close to patients.
 
The margins can be as high 1000%. But those in it say even with regular <10% margins, doubling up as distributors makes a lot of business sense. The largest among these, Healthcare at Home (HCAH), promoted by the Burman family (of the Dabur group), is doing exactly what HCAH UK accidentally did in the UK more than 20 years ago. Sell drugs and related services, and improve outcomes. It expanded Pfizer’s market for just one drug by 800 million pounds.
 
Can HCAH India repeat that magic for Indian specialty drug makers? There are not more than 1500 brands for super specialty drugs in India, as against about 100,000 brands for regularly prescribed drugs.
 
The Burmans seeded HCAH in 2012 as the business for posterity. The way pharma and healthcare delivery are poised today, bundled services seem to be the future.
