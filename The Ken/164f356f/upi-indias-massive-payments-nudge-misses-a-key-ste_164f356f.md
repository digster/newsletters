Rarely do two words sound sweeter to any entrepreneur or company running a subscription business than the following: “automatic payments”.
Seamless automatic payments are at the heart of not just successful subscription companies, but practically any successful e-commerce company. Amazon's “One click ordering", Apple's iTunes and App Store, Netflix’s monthly cord-cutting fee—their growth has been made possible by seamless automatic payments. Customers sign up and hand over their payment details once, and then on, they just keep getting billed for what they consume.
Not in India though. Thanks to the Indian apex bank, RBI's strict watch over payments (with a view to protect Indian customers from fraud), online payments are often a cumbersome 2-3 step process involving repeated entries of CVV codes and 2-factor SMS codes. Even for amounts as less as few tens of Rupees.
Which is why payments and e-commerce companies were salivating at the prospect of UPI 2.0, the latest iteration of India’s digital payments protocol that is less than two years old. Because it was meant to offer automatic recurring payments.
The possibilities were endless. Monthly investments into mutual funds, automated salaries to employees, microloan payments, rentals…the use-cases were as diverse as were the startups and companies chasing them.
Alas, UPI 2.0 won’t have automatic payments. Which means there is a now a gaping void in the ambitions and goals of dozens of startups. But it still carries a new feature that could strike at the heart of Visa and Mastercard.
