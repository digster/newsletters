Good Morning Ishan,
The likes of Makemytrip, Lenskart, and Hindustan Unilever have a conundrum. Its name? Youtube.
Hundreds of millions of people watch videos on the Google-owned platform every day. That makes it a perfect match for brands that want to get the maximum eyeballs looking at their products. Want to bring in more customers? Just run an ad before a Youtube video.
The giants named above spend millions of dollars every quarter doing just that. No wonder that Youtube executives have been working hard to improve the advertiser experience, like building tools to help them target audiences more effectively. After all, over half of Google India’s revenue comes from selling digital ads.
Here’s the problem: several of these brands have discovered their ads running on videos promoting gambling, pornography, misogyny, and even extremist ideologies.
No brand wants its products and logos alongside such unsavoury content—to put it mildly. And when the stakes are this high, it’s obvious that Google is trying hard to solve this problem.
But neither AI nor human content moderators can keep up on a platform whose users upload over 500 hours of content every minute. And it’s particularly concerning in India, where “objectionable” content can easily fly under the radar if it’s in one of the country’s dozens of regional languages.
“Google loves India for the business opportunity, but it fears Indians for how hard they make it work,” a Youtube executive told Rounak, the writer of today’s dynamic story.
