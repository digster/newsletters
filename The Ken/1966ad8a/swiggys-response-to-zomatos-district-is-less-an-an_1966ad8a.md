Good Morning Ishan,
Can Swiggy swing it this time?
The odds seem even.
Pyng, I think, has a nice ring to it. Plus this latest from India’s second-largest food-delivery platform promises a neat product-market fit. Professional-market fit, if you will.
Ping and ye shall be given—white-collared experts like therapists, nutritionists, energy healers, many more. Chartered accountants too (what has this world come to?). At your doorstep. For a fee, of course.
Quite similar to what Urban Company does—connecting clients with blue-collar specialists like plumbers, carpenters, technicians, many more. The proof-of-concept is, well, well-established.
Also, Swiggy has been-there done-that. In a same-but-different avatar not so long ago. Its Minis helped small businesses set up online storefronts on the Swiggy app. From Minis to Pyng, can’t be a difficult leap, can it?
But Pyng is not Urban Company, and white-collared professionals are not blue-collared ones. Chalk and cheese. Besides, Minis didn’t go too well for Swiggy, to put it mildly. Maybe it picked lessons from the debacle.
So, what exactly is Swiggy betting on? Can it do with Pyng what it couldn’t with Minis? Can Pyng replicate the success of Urban Company? Why is Swiggy doing what seems like a random walk? Does it have a choice not to?
Gaurav unpacks many riddles in today’s [perfect Friday story](https://the-ken.com/story/swiggys-response-to-zomatos-district-is-less-an-answer-more-a-question/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter). Let’s dive in.
