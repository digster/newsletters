Good morning Ishan,

Zomato must be really attached to the name ‘Zomato Gold’ for their loyalty programme, because why else would they do this? 

Zomato Gold, which was launched in late 2017, was rebranded Zomato Pro in 2020, only to be rebranded Zomato Gold again this year. 

I don’t know about you, but this confused the hell out of me. So they’re pitching this supposedly shiny-new product—“the exclusive club for India’s top foodies”—by giving it a name they’d already given up on three years ago? If you have any idea about why they’d want to do this, let me know. 

Anyway, Zomato launched Zomato Gold (the new one) this January at an introductory offer of Rs 149 (US$1.8) for three months. According to Zomato, this definitely helped take its total member base to nearly a million in less than a month. But Zomato has since raised the price to Rs 299 (US$3.6) for some users and Rs 399 (US$4.9) for others. 

In addition to charging users more for the new Zomato Gold (Zomato Pro was priced https://startup.outlookindia.com/analysis/zomato-suspends-pro-membership-programme-users-cry-foul-news-6266 at Rs 200 (US$2.4) for three months, and Rs 750 (US$9.1) for a year), convincing restaurants to pay higher commissions and getting Gold members to order more frequently will also help the company offset the cost of running the programme. 

And Zomato seems to think it has finally cracked loyalty. 

This is chief financial officer Akshant Goyal’s explanation—given during its earnings call https://b.zmtcdn.com/investor-relations/2b511d71e0f70c5aa341cddb7cadae2d1676873314.pdf this February—for how Zomato Gold is different from Zomato Pro and the higher-tier Zomato Pro Plus: 

Zomato Gold is a very different program from Zomato Pro. Zomato Pro, essentially, had free delivery as a feature which applied to all orders whereas Zomato Gold has multiple other benefits in addition to that, and free delivery is only on certain orders.

Before Zomato Pro Plus, we had Zomato Pro program and two-three years ago, we had Zomato Gold. So, there were a lot of learnings that we had in terms of what worked and what did not work for customers, for our P&L, and for restaurants. What we wanted to do essentially was to take a step back and put all of this together into a program, which we think can now last, going forward.

One of the key highlights of the program this time is ‘on-time guarantee’, which is a very unique thing in the market right now and developing that also took some time. I don’t think of this as a call that we took and are now correcting. This was the natural path of moving forward in terms of developing a very robust membership program.

But if you ask me, discussing how, or if, Zomato Gold is better than Zomato Pro is quite pointless in light of the biggest problem Zomato is grappling with right now. 

And it’s a problem it won’t be able to fix with a loyalty programme, whatever it chooses to call it.

Why can't Zomato Gold fix Zomato's biggest problem?
