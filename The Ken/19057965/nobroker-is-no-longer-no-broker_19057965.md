Good Morning Ishan,
We have two stories ready for you today.
For Indian landlords who own high-end properties able to command higher rents, proptech unicorn Nobroker offers a more exclusive service: fees are only charged after a renter is matched with a listed property, with a guarantee that someone will become a tenant.
This is all to say that Nobroker is, in some cases, functioning exactly like a real estate broker. So far, the service is available in at least Bengaluru and Chennai, limited to clients that the platform selects.
It’s a new proposition for Nobroker, separate from its typical subscription model. The company’s postpaid plan is meant to woo a class of homeowners that are a bit wealthier—and who are more willing to pay for Nobroker’s other services, like property management, management services, home relocations, and financing. Also, this matters because there’s more new housing supply in the high-end, luxury, and ultra-lux brackets compared to three years ago.
But Nobroker needs to keep this type of service—offered only to the right set of clients—under wraps so that it doesn’t undercut its existing subscriptions.
For today’s story from India, Gaurav found out how Nobroker is testing the waters in premium rental properties—and becoming a broker itself in the process. Check out his [story](https://the-ken.com/story/nobroker-is-no-longer-no-broker/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
Meanwhile, over in Kuala Lumpur, the stock market has been on a tear.
A handful of stocks, in particular, have shot up so much in June that Bursa Malaysia, the country’s stock market, has taken notice of their “unusual activity” and sent queries to find out why this has happened.
The reason appears to be unbridled optimism stemming from major companies like Nvidia, Microsoft, and Alphabet, investing in infrastructure that will form the backbone of AI startups and services.
Interestingly, the market rally isn’t fuelled solely by domestic entities; foreign investors are also jumping on the bandwagon. The problem, as analysts who spoke to The Ken point out, is that the market may overheat.
This is likely a harbinger of events. Malaysia is still in its first steps of transforming to become a hub for AI businesses and service providers in Southeast Asia, so new developments also carry a dose of financial speculation.
