Good Morning Ishan,
Credit cards are fascinating products, and banks put a LOT of thought into their clever structuring. They find ways to get users to spend more, often enticing them to take on more credit than they can afford in a given month, ultimately pushing them towards a loan. And that’s when they swoop in to make money. Beautiful.
But what happens when credit-card users find a way to consistently one-up banks?
That’s Rounak’s fascinating story today—about credit-card wonks who are proving to be troublesome to bankers. The explosion of co-branded credit cards means there are reward points to be earned from every kind of transaction made.
And, these wonks have made it their mission to propagate hacks exploiting loopholes in credit-card reward systems on messaging apps such as Telegram, Whatsapp, and Discord.
Some even make a living out of it. One such person called it their “secret social service,” now paying him more than his day job.
What are these hacks? Who are these wonks? What can banks do about it?
