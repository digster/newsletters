Bengaluru has started to bloom with the infamously glorious Pink Poui or Tabebuia Avellanedae. The first seeds of Tabebuia were planted across the city in the 19th century in such a way that at least one variant of the flower bloomed through the first half of a year, or sometimes, a little longer.

Starting with the Tabebuia Avellanedae in January that paints the city in a dark pink shade to Tabebuia Rosea in March marking the arrival of spring, to Tabebuia Argentea that gives a golden shade sometime in June, the flowering tree encompasses various neighbourhoods and parks across the city, thus making it a beautiful sight to wake up to. I hope and wish that you all come across this sight at least once in your life.
