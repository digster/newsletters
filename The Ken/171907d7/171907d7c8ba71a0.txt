Good Morning Ishan,

The past few weeks have been all about Covid-19. The next few weeks are bound to be about Covid-19. 

So in the little space I’ve got here, I’m going to talk about something else.

How about... quotes, those little fragments of people’s thoughts that connect with us? 

Some quotes stir emotions. Others, we might just dismiss as mere platitudes (see Twitter). But the good ones are often from literature. 

I’ll just share a few good ones I’ve come across in all that I’ve read and kept track of on Goodreads. Looking back at them now, I cannot help but think of them to be foreshadowing me as a journalist.

“If you show someone something you’ve written, you give them a sharpened stake, lie down in your coffin, and say, ‘When you’re ready’.” — David Mitchell, Black Swan Green

“The books that the world calls immoral are books that show the world its own shame.” — Oscar Wilde, The Picture of Dorian Gray

“Don’t fight the system. Mock the system.” — Trevor Noah, Born a Crime

This week at The Ken, our stories had a good mix of these. Some stories enraged, some mocked but all evoked discussions and debate. 

On Monday, Arundhati laid bare https://the-ken.com/story/covid-insurance/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the inside workings of insurance during the times of Covid-19. To insurance companies, the pandemic is an opportunity. But only if they price well. Err here, and the cost might be too high for them to bear.

Pratap and I on Tuesday questioned, analysed, and explored what Aarogya Setu, the contact tracing app that the government wants us to download, does https://the-ken.com/story/fishing-with-dynamite-indias-contact-tracing-overreach/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. Some of the things we found worried us. This was our first free read for the week. Do share with all your friends who are internally debating downloading the app.

The supply chain is a beautiful thing. Like the wheels of a car, all’s well only as long as all four of them keep running in sync. On Wednesday, Abinaya threw light https://the-ken.com/story/covid-19-spurs-indian-e-commerce-to-raise-stakes-in-grocery-gamble/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on the opportunity e-commerce companies have while people sit at home all day. But it’s an opportunity they can leverage only while all the four wheels keep running.

We’ve seen that the pandemic isn’t straightforward for anyone. Not even for those for whom it presents an opportunity. Ruhi, on Thursday, wrote https://the-ken.com/story/will-india-save-its-hospitals-before-they-save-india?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter about how private hospitals in India face a precarious financial situation even while people are set to throng them. This was our second free story of the week. With 60% of all hospital beds in India in the private sector, so we better all sit up and notice.

When you think bad can’t get worse, it does. OYO was in trouble much before Covid-19, but now it might just take down https://the-ken.com/story/oyo-ritesh-agarwal-softbank-covid/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter its founder Ritesh Agarwal. On Friday, Sumanth wrote about how Agarwal’s risky $2 billion bet hangs by a thread. And this time the wall of Softbank may not be standing behind him.

That’s it from me this week. Have a great Sunday, folks. Read, rest, and ruminate. 

And most importantly, take care. Because: “It doesn’t make a damned bit of difference who wins the war to someone who’s dead.” — Joseph Heller, Catch-22

Regards,
