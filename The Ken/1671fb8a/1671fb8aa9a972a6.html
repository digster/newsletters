Good Morning Ishan,
 
Group discussions are so stressful. Honestly, I'm never quite sure how they're evaluated effectively. A controlled environment with a bunch of people shouting over each other to make their presence felt, some giving up too soon, others not knowing when to stop... yeah, stressful.
 
Why am I thinking of group discussions, or, well, groups for that matter? Because our past week though varied in subject matter has had a single, binding factor. Groups. Of all kinds. In all shapes and sizes. Resulting in very very different outcomes.
 
Take Ruhi and Roshni's Sunday piece that kickstarted this week for example. They wrote about an emerging section of Indian parents mirroring the western anti-vaccination sentiment, saying no to the needle for their kids. Problem. Certainly. (This one's a free read, folks.)
 
On Monday, Seema wrote about India's Big Pharma club, and how it's shrinking. Pharma MNCs came in bunches, realised India is not quite their cup of tea and are now rearranging their decks. What we have are layoffs, marketing alliances, and a shrewd post-consolidation wait-and-watch.
 
Tuesday saw Vandana make sense of India's growing digital mutual funds platforms. This bunch has lined up to cash in on India’s growing appetite for equity as an asset class. But without an advisory model in place, they would remain just that—a transactional medium.
 
Sumanth on Wednesday, of course, wrote about the lone wolf shaking up a larger group. Flipkart co-founder Sachin Bansal is lining up a second tryst with destiny playing in the SoftBank/Tiger Global sweepstakes, he writes. Will his entry shake up late-stage funding in India?
 
Olina's Thursday piece, also a free read, looked into a trend many seem to have adopted—decentralised sewage treatment. However, have lopsided regulations and a lack of standards already done them in?
 
The week ended with Arundhati's take on small finance banks attempting to go the big bank way. In doing so, this group has to stop depending on the microfinance model, a gift that keeps on giving. As they diversify, she writes, their biggest USP—interest rates—slowly comes under pressure.
 
That's all we had this week. We'll see you tomorrow with a fresh TKOW story. Clue: This one's in-house.
 
Have a great weekend, all. Tell us about strange groups you may have found yourselves in?
 
Regards,
