The Spotlight by Anushka Mukherjee
Podcast Producer

Good Morning Ishan,

A few months ago, a thick, heavy folder landed, with a loud thud, on the desks of my colleague Akshaya and mine.

We had a new project.

Well, okay, there were no heavy folders thudding. It was more like the culmination of a bunch of exciting conversations and meetings and riffraffs, but it really felt like this classic scene from office dramas—a mix of nervous anticipation and excitement. (Akshaya coined a term for it: ‘scited’—scared + excited.)

We were going to start working on a new podcast. A refreshing, fun, and reported podcast, exclusively for India’s young workforce. And it would be called The First Two Years. We started calling it TFTY—said like tifty—in our team.

We had so many threads of ideas, themes, and narratives. The next few weeks would be a marathon in organising these ideas and making them the foundation for a new editorial style, episode pitches, a unique score, etc. It was sciting.

One of the first items on our checklist was: commission podcast artwork for TFTY. We didn’t realise it at the time, but creating a brief for this artwork was perhaps our first exercise in really imagining the podcast. And who it would be for.

What did we imagine?

A young, ambitious, and excited person, who is just entering the workforce. A person who’s been guarded and propped up by the world of books and theories—and will now finally emerge from it and head into a new sort of life. There are so many unknowns and expectations, but also so many opportunities and milestones ahead. We all remember what it felt like, right?

Our commissioned artist, Sneha Dasgupta, took in all our words and imagery ideas and feelings and translated them into a wonderful image:

We launch with The First Two Years in two days from now. We’re pumped—and we’re hoping to reach every young person with a new job, and answer the most important questions they have about their workplace. In fact, our trailer is out now on Spotify and Apple.

Phew! It’s relieving even to be able to put this out! It’s a feeling we share with the reporters in our newsrooms, especially on the day their stories are published. There were a few of these this week. Let’s get into them.
