Good Morning Ishan,
Expectations and realities seldom align, right? We found more proof of this in a recent survey we conducted with over 600 of our readers.
Survey’s purpose: We wanted to understand what the stock-market rally has done for retail investors.
Survey’s results: Fascinating!
Survey’s expected inference: A spike in the “wealth effect” among investors. It is when spending increases more than income due to a higher value of assets.
Survey’s actual inference: Much more than the wealth effect—it’s a lifestyle revolution.
Millennials are collecting cars like we used to collect Cheetos’ Tazos. Remember? Retirees, on the other hand, are jet-setting to exotic geographies and snagging second homes in Goa. The FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement and entrepreneurial ambitions are also recurring themes in the survey.
It’s a thrilling new era in personal finance. And just like good times, one can easily be swayed into believing that the bull run will continue forever. Expectations, you see. But a sceptical group among the respondents anchors us back to some worrying realities. For instance, is a correction coming? This bunch thinks so—and with conviction.
Anand, the author of today’s story, summarises all their responses to dish out [a great read](https://the-ken.com/story/i-own-three-cars-and-two-bikes-the-wealth-effect-of-indias-stock-market-boom/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
