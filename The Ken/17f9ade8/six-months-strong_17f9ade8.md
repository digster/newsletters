Good morning Ishan,
Happy Holi!
We don’t have a story for you today. We’re taking the day off to celebrate, and I hope you are too. You may not know me, but you’ve seen my work. I’m Jonathan Ananda, Newsletters Editor at The Ken.
It’s been exactly six months since we launched our extensive range of newsletters and six months since I got my dream job. One that gives me a sneak peek at all those juicy editions our expert line-up of writers delivers every week. And I live for it.
However, my job today is to convince you to try out our newsletters. That’s it. Just hit that “Activate Trial” button. Maybe you’ll love them; maybe you won’t. But I urge you to make use of our generous trials.
So, if you want some deeply thought out fintech and personal finance insight, you should check out Arundhati and Anand’s [Ka-Ching](https://the-ken.com/ka-ching/stories/)!. They make finance sound so easy!
Or maybe you’d like a sharp, crisp take on how the world of commerce is evolving. From superstores and super apps to kirana commerce, Seetharaman’s [Trade Tricks](https://the-ken.com/trade-tricks/stories/) has you covered.
Want to keep track of what we’re doing/should do to fix this mess we’ve made of our Earth? Then Seema's [Green Margins](https://the-ken.com/green-margins/stories/) is just for you—measured, free of alarmist hyperbole, every Wednesday morning.
And then, there’s Jaspreet’s [Tokenised](https://the-ken.com/tokenised/stories/), which is about anything and everything crypto in crisp and simple language—stripped of jargon and scary words like NFT, HODL, WAGMI, etc.
Everyone wants to redefine education. Few understand it. But everyone wants to make money out of it. In [Ed Set Go](https://the-ken.com/ed-set-go/stories/), Olina explains the sector from the only point of view that matters— yours.
We have Ruhi’s [Inciting Incident](https://the-ken.com/inciting-incident/stories/), which is all about the stories. What stories? Well, the kind that brands tell. Are they working, are they not? Ruhi has the answers every Friday.
And of course, Friday evening is [Moneyball](https://the-ken.com/moneyball/stories/) time. From F1 to the IPL, Jaideep breaks down the economics of sport and the money flowing beneath it all.
To round off the week, there’s [The Nutgraf](https://the-ken.com/the-nutgraf/), which dissects and analyses one major event from the previous week—connecting the dots across the past, present and future. And it never fails to surprise.
That’s it from me! I hope I’ve convinced you to try our newsletters out. If you’re still not convinced, please do write to me about what’s stopping you. I’d love to hear your thoughts on how we can do better.
