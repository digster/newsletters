I have lots to say about today’s story, but first some stats. ‘In God we trust, and all others bring data’ to the table, and all that stuff, you know.

  • 877 satellite channels (excluding 23 of the public broadcaster) are operational in India

  • 59 companies have their applications pending for a total of 97 channels

  • 7 ministries and departments must clear an application before a broadcaster can uplink from India

  • 45 new satellite channels were permitted in 2017 to operate, against 75 in 2016

  • 0 up-linking permissions in last four months, 0 downlinking permission in last two months

The future of broadcasting in India is in limbo. And at an interesting, rather crucial, time. Companies like Star and Viacom18 are waiting with four applications each.
 
Why? Because the I&B ministry has decided to review the existing regulatory framework. And while doing so, it has declared that it won’t be considering any new TV channel applications for approval. While an official order has not been passed to keep the applications in abeyance, a decision has been made. The telecom regulator has proposed some ideas, which are not exactly what the industry wants, but it’s not something the two sides cannot discuss and resolve.
 
The question is, does the government want to resolve this issue?
 
“The ministry is being too careful about what goes on television, especially in the case of news channels. Ahead of 2019 election, the ministry won’t risk any new launches which can jeopardise things for the current government. The review of policy wouldn’t have come at a better time,” says an official.
