There is indeed something called a favourite child, no matter what parents say. The (research) jury is still out on whether it’s the earlier born or not. For India’s apex bank RBI, it’s the banks. Or at least that’s what some other kids on the block felt after the new guidelines on pre-paid instruments (PPI) came out last week.
 
The RBI has reset the button on payments while paving the way for complete interoperability between the PPIs and bank accounts.
 
Wallets, which gave millions their first brush with digital payments in India, will now have to do away with the convenience features which made them popular in the first place. Successive transactions will need to be authenticated with customer consent. And eventually, all wallet users will need to do a full customer verification (KYC), like banks.
 
For wallet providers, this may mean investment in acquiring customers all over again, never mind the millions of dollars (in discounts and cashbacks) they have already spent on this. But is it as bleak as it sounds? After all, the RBI did give them scope to become interoperable.
 
While the leading wallets will likely lose users, deep-pocketed payments providers like PhonePe, Amazon Pay and WhatsApp will lose no time in grabbing them.
 
Some believe the RBI has made the conditions to operate wallets similar to banks, the entity it is most comfortable with. Others think if wallets want scale then security and KYC are non-negotiable terms.
 
Like a grand composer, the RBI has been testing the payments notes for a while. It may not be music to all ears at this point. Arundhati brings you all that is playing out: https://the-ken.com/rbi-kicked-off-shark-fight-wallet-users/
