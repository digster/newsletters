Good Morning Ishan,
Why might it be a stretch to compare healthcare digitisation to UPI but not to Zomato? Or what will it actually take to digitise India’s healthcare system? And why on earth would a man who built a global healthtech turn his gaze to this tangled mess?
That’s Rizwan Koita for you. The creator of Citiustech, a unicorn that last saw a US$2.2 billion valuation, is now the chairman of the national board that accredits hospitals. After riding the Obamacare wave in the US, Koita is bent on transforming India’s healthcare. From rolling out new standards for electronic medical records to pushing for digital-health education, Koita is on a mission.
In [today’s exclusive interview](https://the-ken.com/story/he-founded-a-unicorn-got-bored-now-wants-to-digitise-60000-hospital/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) with Seema, Koita goes deep into these initiatives. So deep, in fact, that he goes from “Are you with me?” to pulling out books and links to “You’re asking too many questions”.
So, instead of asking you just to read the story, I am here to test how well you grasp the complexities of Koita’s mission (there may be more than one right answer).
Why does Koita think comparing healthcare digitisation to UPI is off base?
a) UPI is a breeze compared to healthcare’s tangled web
b) Healthcare data has fewer variables than a UPI transaction
c) Standardising UPI is trickier than managing health records
d) UPI’s success story doesn’t quite fit the healthcare narrative
What’s the big hurdle in digitising healthcare, according to Koita?
a) A lack of tech vendors
b) Core banking standards gone awry
c) Major foundational shifts and deployment dramas
d) Regulatory red tape that’s more of a chokehold
How’s the strategy to help small hospitals embrace digital health solutions stacking up?
a) Government tech-upgrade subsidies
b) A certification programme for little guys in software
c) A curated list of certified software and vendor matchmaking
d) Free digital health software—no strings attached
And why are India’s digital health standards so far from ideal?
a) They are too vague and impractical
b) They are out of sync with global standards
c) Small hospitals are lost in translation
d) They need a frequent update boost
