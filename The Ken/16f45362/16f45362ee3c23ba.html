Let's try something fun. How many of your household items did you buy online? Think of everything - from clothes and shoes to books and decor, from kitchenware and groceries to furniture and appliances.
 
50%? 60%? More? Definitely more in my case.
 
We have been thoroughly spoilt this decade. The explosion called e-commerce made all our wants into needs. And we need everything, and we need it cheap, and it should have been delivered yesterday.
 
You know who is to blame - Flipkart and Amazon mostly. But also Paytm Mall, Snapdeal, Urban Ladder, AJIO and ... (go on, reminisce!). 
 
In today's throwback, Durga charts the course of Indian e-tailers over the past decade. The never-ending Flipkart vs Amazon war; Walmart's waltz into the fray; Snapdeal's autosarcophagy saga; grocery grief, hyperlocal heartaches - the lot!
 
All this and we have barely scratched the surface. E-commerce is a piddly 3% of India's $650-billion retail industry. So, there's plenty of room for growth (though not quite at the staggering rate Morgan Stanley expects).
 
The next decade is going to be even more fun. But before you start exercising your fingers, let Durga's story jog your memory. It's also free, so share with anyone you know who has shopped online. https://the-ken.com/story/flipkart-amazon-snapdeal-e-commerce/  (13-minute read)
