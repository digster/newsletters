When you follow the ways successful entrepreneurs build their businesses, it doesn’t take long to realise that talent, timing, and technology are crucial elements for success. Miss any one of the three and even the sharpest idea might not take off.
 
Those three Ts don’t take into account a fourth element that is just as important—turf. 
 
Some people have skill sets and plans that can lay the foundation for the next big thing, but maybe it just isn’t the right moment to do it in their home country, or they’re expanding an existing company’s operations into a new market.
 
Indian entrepreneurs started to build new businesses in Indonesia in 2013, laying the groundwork for technical professionals to move to the Southeast Asian country as well. This created a supercharged corner in Indonesia’s startup scene, with individuals of Indian origin becoming part of senior leadership in some Indonesian companies.
 
But if this all sounds like too nice of a story about integration, it isn’t.
 
There are glass ceilings and glass walls for Indian professionals in Indonesia, and cultural frictions are aplenty. 
 
There isn’t meant to be a moral here, but perhaps the lesson is that no matter how good a plan looks on paper, no matter how well the numbers line up, the human element can still get in the way.
 
In today’s story, Nadine and Aayush explore how professionals of Indian origin found their rhythms while navigating Indonesia’s business world. 
 
These patterns are rooted in respect for the local way of building teams and inspiring team members, as well as the work that must be put in to create strong connections with local allies who will go to bat for you.
