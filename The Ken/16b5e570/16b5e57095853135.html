Good Morning Ishan,
 
It’s been just shy of a decade now since I last lived with my family. Or even in the same city. Don’t get me wrong, I visit them about twice a year, and I love them dearly… just in small, periodic doses.

In all the time I’ve lived away, I’ve had one sacrosanct rule—visit, but never host. Whereas a trip home is a return to privilege and a holiday from responsibility, hosting is a stressful affair. Keeping up appearances. Keeping people fed and occupied. Keeping away from balconies in case you succumb to the sudden urge to jump. You get my drift.

This past month, that rule was broken. I flew my siblings down to Delhi to stay with me for a month.

Big mistake. 

My siblings are both younger than me and, consequently, also believe in “doing” things. The 45-degree Delhi weather notwithstanding. Sleep optional. Food, preferably deep fried with a side of bacon and a beer for good measure. All things that destroy the new (old) domesticated me. I’m not proud of it, but I’ve made my peace with being “boring”.

I survived, older—I aged a decade last month, wiser—never again, and wearier—I’ll be catching up on sleep for the foreseeable future. But just like time, The Ken stops for no one, and so we have another set of deeply reported pieces this week.

It kicked off with student living—a concept my siblings would probably have been more at home with during their visit. Vandana wrote about how young startups are redefining the meaning of student housing, modernising the space and growing rapidly.

Next, we turned our focus to healthcare. With hospital chain takeovers the flavour of the season—we’ve written about the Manipal-Medanta deal earlier—we took a look at another chain seemingly on the block. PE firms and other hospitals chains are queuing up to buy Columbia Asia. Suraksha connected a whole bunch of dots to see who the most likely buyer is.

On Wednesday, we looked at a company you may never even have heard of—Club Factory. India was the Chinese cross-border e-commerce platform’s biggest market. Until a government crackdown led to a drop in sales. Harveen and Rozelle teamed up to tell the story of Club Factory’s plan to survive in its most important market. 

Thursday’s story was a visual breakdown of something too complex for most people (me especially) to grasp—health insurance. Ruhi, with her intimate knowledge of the space, and Prajakta, our resident infographics wizard, joined forces to explain and illustrate India’s insurance ecosystem.

That was followed by Sumanth’s latest piece on two entities he’s written about extensively—hotel aggregator OYO and mega VC investor SoftBank. Sumanth questions SoftBank’s funding of the Indian startup, asking whether we’ve reached a point where SoftBank needs OYO almost as much as OYO needs it. 

We closed the week with an interesting piece by Pranav on India’s solar ambitions. India has a 100 GW solar target to meet by 2022, with the sector dancing to Chinese manufacturers’ tune in the past. But now, as China tweaks its policies and subsidies, India is backing out. Probably not for the best either.
