Good Morning Ishan,
Earlier this year, Bajaj Finserv decided it was time to get serious about healthcare. So, its lesser-known digital-health arm went out and bought Vidal Health, India’s third-largest third-party administrator (TPA). A good idea, maybe, except for one small detail: Vidal has been shedding revenue and racking up losses.
Meanwhile, Medi Assist—the biggest TPA in town—saw this and thought it needed to get bigger. Just last week, it dropped hundreds of crores to buy Paramount Health, the fourth-largest TPA, nudging its already big market share up a notch.
And just like that, a tepid market started heating up.
In case you’re not familiar, TPAs are basically the middlemen of the insurance world. They make sure insurers don’t, you know, stiff policyholders—handling claims, managing paperwork, and wading through all the regulatory sludge.
But it’s a tough gig—low margins, lots of regulation, and declining fees. Moreover, many insurers now do the job themselves.
So why did Bajaj Finserv Health buy such a company in a market where only three out of 17 players are profitable? “Our approach is to look at the entire healthcare journey of an individual…,” Devang Mody, the CEO, tells Shivani.
Sounds like a healthcare-ecosystem utopia. Vidal brings access to millions of policyholders of retail, corporate, and government schemes; and Bajaj gets a pathway to bundle health insurance, wellness, and outpatient services.
Meanwhile, Medi Assist is playing the numbers game. More market share, more clients, more leverage.
Two strategies, one shrinking market. If you thought healthcare was getting dull, Shivani’s [got the rundown on a race](https://the-ken.com/story/bajaj-finserv-wanted-to-make-waves-in-healthcare-so-it-bought-a-wobbly-ship/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) you didn’t know you needed to know about.
