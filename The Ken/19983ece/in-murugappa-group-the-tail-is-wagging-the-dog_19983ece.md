Good Morning Ishan,
Dare House in Chennai, like Bombay House in Mumbai, has been home to vintage Indian business history since the pre-independence era. If the Tata Group with its headquarters in Bombay House has held on to its position as one of the country’s largest conglomerates, the Murugappa Group with its offices in Dare House too has held its own, in its distinctive low-profile manner.
Both have grown leaps and bounds over the decades, and now command a “governance premium” in the stock market.
But something different and spectacular has transpired in the Chennai conglomerate over the past few years. While software giant TCS has remained the unchallenged spearhead of Bombay House, Dare House has seen a huge change in its pecking order.
In CG Power, a star was born, or rather, acquired by the Murugappa Group during the pandemic. This maker of transformers and industrial motors was near-bankrupt when it was scooped up under the watch of the group’s managing director Vellayan Subbiah.
Its market-cap now towers above all of the group’s listed companies, save one, which too it may soon overtake. This despite CG Power still being a minnow in the group on metrics such as profits, compared with leading non-bank Cholamandalam Investment and fertiliser maker Coromandel International.
What gives? What really is the market betting on?
The surprise rise of the upstart—the tail wagging the dog, so to speak—has implications that go far beyond the family.
