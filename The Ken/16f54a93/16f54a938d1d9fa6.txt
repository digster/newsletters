For a generation that spent hours glued to our PCs, playing all sorts of computer games—a large part violent; remember Wolfenstein 3D, Diablo, Quake...or even Max Payne?—the concept of edtech was rather unappealing. 

Computers were a getaway from studies, after all. "Why would you do this to us?" we whined.

But that's not the attitude today's student population has towards edtech.

Right from primary school to test prep for college, edtech is everywhere. And it's starting to compete with traditional education. The past decade, if anything, has been all about experimentation with various edtech models, especially with the advent of newer technologies.

What started with Educomp and its hardware and multimedia learning modules in the first years of this decade, has now moved to likes of Byju's, Unacademy and Vedantu preferring software and taking advantage of cheap internet data.

And this new wave of edtech is minting money. While Byju's has become the first Indian unicorn in the edtech space, Vedantu has raised $42 million in a Series C round led by marquee investors Tiger Global and Westbridge Capital. Unacademy, meanwhile, has raised $50 million in a Series D round that included VC firm Sequoia, which also has a significant stake in Byju’s.

There's a lot of competition—and outside of Byju's there are no clear toppers in this class. For it's no longer a predictable hardware versus software story either. There's upskilling, online taking on offline models and offline, in turn, going online. And all the while, there's a looming threat from companies in China, the world's biggest edtech market.
