Good Morning Ishan,
There’s a gold rush underway—in silver. Unlike the shiny yellow favourite that’s obsessed over by a legion of analysts and households in India, its poor grey cousin stays largely under the radar. But that hasn’t stopped silver from staging a huge rally over the last couple of years. As if on cue, many mutual-fund houses in India have launched silver funds, especially over the past few months. Riding the wave, so to speak.
Some, though, have been betting on the underdog for much longer. Like Giva, the protagonist of today’s story. For six years now, this direct-to-customer jewellery retailer has focused on selling fine silver jewellery—the kind that’s trendy and also easy on customers’ pockets. Its more in-the-news counterparts Caratlane and Bluestone have also been selling jewellery but largely in gold and diamond. Giva seems to have found the sweet spot and is doubling down. On the other hand, Bluestone, which will list on the bourses today, seems to be feeling the IPO blues.
Yet, what got Giva so far may not take it much farther. Scale comes with its own problems. Pranathi tells you why in her sparkling [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/bluestones-ipo-shows-one-path-in-jewellery-why-giva-thinks-theres-another/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
