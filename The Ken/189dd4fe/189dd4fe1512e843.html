Good Morning Ishan,

When the facts change, minds follow. When ground realities change, people pivot – some voluntarily, while others are forced.

The many tweaks among start-ups – after the funding flood turned into a drought over the past year – have been well documented. But, what’s gone under the radar is the subtle shifts among the funders of these start-ups – the likes of Sequoia (now Peak XV Partners), Tremis, Blume, Elevation Capital, Accel, and many other storied venture capital (VC) firms.

Vanita, the author of today’s story, sets course into this relatively uncharted territory.

From being hyper-aggressive deal chasers, many of these VC firms have now calmed down considerably. The impact of this is being felt by the junior VCs and analysts – the foot-soldiers, so to speak – who do the spadework before the boss signs the cheque.

There has been a radical shift in what they do and how they do it. After grueling 16-18 hours of work daily and chasing dozens of deals during the funding heydays, analysts are now putting in far fewer hours and pursuing just a handful of deals. Isn’t that nice? Not necessarily. There are tradeoffs and some big ones at that.

Here’s how legendary tramp poet William Henry Davies might have put the predicament:

What is this life if, full of care,
We have no time to stand and stare.
Till Jun ‘22, this for junior VCs was usual fare
Not anymore, post the funding nightmare

Crazy hours, big deal FOMO; pushes and shoves
Given way to a more leisurely pace, a saner house

Is the shift seen as gold, or just as brass?
Are junior VCs, analysts glad or glum alas?

Do they miss the hustle, the learnings bright?
Or is this time to learn anew, do things right?

Working with portfolio companies gets its chance
Avoiding duds now, without the deal-making trance

Sequoia, Termis, Blume, et al – the change in plan
Vanita’s story goes deep, covers a wide span
There’s now time to stand and stare

Will they make it count? Will they dare?
