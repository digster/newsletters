It’s natural to hear Amazon’s name thrown around in connection to, say, buying a fridge online, or streaming the likes of The Family Man on Prime Video. But healthcare? Not so much.

Looks like the multinational tech company is trying to change that. Or at least dabbling in it. Either way, it doesn’t seem fully committed.

In June, Amazon partnered with Orange Health Labs in India to provide at-home diagnostic services in major Indian metros. The e-commerce giant had already launched online clinic and pharmacy services in the country.

So okay, it makes sense why Amazon is doubling down on diagnostics. It’s part of a larger push to enter healthcare in India.

But if so, the company seems strangely non-committal toward the whole thing. There’s hardly any marketing, the “diagnostics” tab is buried somewhere deep within the Amazon app, and even when you click on it, Orange Health’s site requires a separate login. Even long-term medical professionals seem to have no idea that this partnership exists.

Why all the caginess? And why not go at it alone, as Amazon has before? Why this partnership with Orange Health Labs?

Sudeshna unpacks the tech giant’s half-hearted bet on healthcare in today’s story.
