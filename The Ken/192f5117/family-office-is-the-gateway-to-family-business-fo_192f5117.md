Good Morning Ishan,
I’m sure by now you’ve heard and read enough about the new crop of wealthy people in India. So we won’t tell you more about them. Today’s story by Aakriti is about old money. And we’re talking empires worth thousands of crores.
So did you know that the number of family offices has skyrocketed from 45 to 300 in just six years? Cities like Surat, Ludhiana, and Coimbatore are becoming the new epicentres of wealth management. The family offices are transforming how the ultra-wealthy manage their fortunes—shifting from traditional industries like textiles and diamonds to high-risk, high-reward ventures. All of this, driven by a new generation of heirs.
But even as family offices multiply, Aakriti could sense an underlying tension between tradition and innovation. While these wealthy families are upbeat about modernising their investments, there is also fear: can they balance the risk of innovation with the stability of their established businesses?
The fear is reasonable. Globally, only one in three family businesses survives to the second generation. And taking a relatively risky path only adds to the odds.
So, what is the new generation doing to minimise risk while keeping it fun?
