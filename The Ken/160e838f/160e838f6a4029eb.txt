Good Morning Ishan,

A very curious story made its way into The Economic Times yesterday https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/cons-products/fmcg/lvmh-likely-to-fund-baba-ramdevs-patanjali/articleshow/62452712.cms.?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter L Catterton, a private equity fund in which one of the major investors is the French luxury conglomerate LVMH (which owns dozens of brands like Louis Vuitton, Bulgari and Moët & Chandon), publicly beseeched Patanjali Ayurved to take $500 million from it. And Patanjali, the reportedly $1.5 billion (in revenue) consumer products company born of Yoga and anchored in Ayurveda, publicly hinted that it's ready to talk to L Catterton. 

Now why L Catterton couldn’t just pick up the phone and call Patanjali is anyone’s guess, but I’m digressing.

Today’s story is about Patanjali. After streamlining his Yoga prowess to achieve consumer products success, Baba Ramdev, the founder of Patanjali, is getting into the apparel business. Yes.

For a brand built assiduously upon the planks of nationalism and self-reliance, Patanjali Paridhan, the new apparel brand, will sell through partners like FBB (by Big Bazaar), Amazon, Myntra, Flipkart and hundreds of its own stores. There will be “swadeshi” (Indian made) denim for women. And innerwear, fashion accessories, diapers, women’s sanitary needs, home textiles and even footwear. 

The revenue target for the first year is Rs 5,000 crore (~$750 million).

On a lighter note, this is the story my colleague Patanjali had been waiting to write (Ashish co-wrote it). Perhaps, was even born to write. Because how often does a Patanjali on Patanjali happen? #SorryNotSorry

But seriously, do read the story. It’s got strategy, competitors, products, pricing, etc. Competitors, large or small, absolutely nobody underestimates the juggernaut that is Patanjali today. Which explains LVMH’s newspaper entreaties, perhaps.
