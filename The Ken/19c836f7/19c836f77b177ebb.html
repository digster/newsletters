AI walks into a crowded clinic. Can it keep up with India’s doctors?

Doctors are looking for ambient AI-transcription tools that can survive the noise of the Indian consultation room. But integrating them into legacy record-keeping systems may be a daunting task

A doctor in India sees up to 10X more patients than a physician in the US. Indian doctors are warming up to AI-transcription tools, not just to tackle documentation burnout but to see more patients per OPD session. But patchy electronic medical records across Indian hospitals are tricky to work around. AI scribes from homegrown startups like Dawnbreak and Eka Care are working to bridge the gap. But data security remains a concern for their widespread adoption. [Read the full story]

For Fractal Analytics, the AI signal trumps the stock-market noise

A 25-year-old steady analytics business is transitioning to AI product-incubating services. With no real benchmark, markets will have to learn to value this unique model

Fractal has been refining its AI chops for over a decade, but as it made its public-market debut this week, the AI tag has evoked mixed sentiments. Putting real money where its long-term plans are, 6.1% of sales into R&D and the highest in its category, Fractal isn’t a short-term player. Not willing to be pigeon-holed into a service or a product company, it wants to play on both sides; such is the lure and challenge of AI. Some investors may find the IPO price expensive, but for the founder, “just the sum of the parts in Fractal” will prove it otherwise. [Read the full story]

The art of building a D2C food brand to be acquired

Marico’s Rs 225 crore deal for plant-protein brand Cosmix is yet more evidence that finding a strategic buyer is the only exit option for food startups

In 2026, Marico acquired three D2C brands in three consecutive weeks. Two of these brands, Cosmix and 4700 BC, are in the food and nutrition space. In fact, another FMCG major Hindustan Unilever has also completed its acquisition of nutrition brand Oziva in February. It marks a new wave of D2C acquisitions happening in the food space, largely thanks to the rise of quick commerce. But in spite of it all, the threshold for building a scalable, acquirable brand has climbed up. [Read the full story]

Google Gemini’s free JEE mock tests: experiment or Trojan horse?

The tech giant teamed up with Physicswallah and Careers360 to offer free test-prep tools to some of India’s most competitive exams

Google launched free interactive mock tests on Gemini for JEE Main exams from February, marking its first big move into India’s education economy. As a part of its Rs 85 crore investment in AI for education, it is also launching several other test prep content for free on its AI assistant. To do so, it has partnered with industry giants like Physicswallah and Careers360 to buy question banks. The move signals Google’s entry into India’s AI-adoption race, which was first started by OpenAI last year. [Read the full story]
