Doctors are looking for ambient AI-transcription tools that can survive the noise of the Indian consultation room. But integrating them into legacy record-keeping systems may be a daunting task
A doctor in India sees up to 10X more patients than a physician in the US. Indian doctors are warming up to AI-transcription tools, not just to tackle documentation burnout but to see more patients per OPD session. But patchy electronic medical records across Indian hospitals are tricky to work around. AI scribes from homegrown startups like Dawnbreak and Eka Care are working to bridge the gap. The tech giant teamed up with Physicswallah and Careers360 to offer free test-prep tools to some of India’s most competitive exams
Google launched free interactive mock tests on Gemini for JEE Main exams from February, marking its first big move into India’s education economy. As a part of its Rs 85 crore investment in AI for education, it is also launching several other test prep content for free on its AI assistant. To do so, it has partnered with industry giants like Physicswallah and Careers360 to buy question banks. The move signals Google’s entry into India’s AI-adoption race, which was first started by OpenAI last year.
