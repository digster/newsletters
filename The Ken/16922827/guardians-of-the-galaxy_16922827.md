Don't know about you, but I missed Steve Jobs last week. After a long time, a new phone was the talk of the town: Samsung’s Galaxy Fold. For its aesthetics, zippy features, and, of course, $1,980 price tag.
Whatever the tactical reasons behind Samsung unveiling its snazzy phones ahead of the Mobile World Congress, the launch caught people’s fancy. The Fold and the rest of Samsung's flashy lineup.
Fair to say, Samsung has awoken from its slumber and sweet dreams, ready to take on the threats that effectively dethroned it in India. The Chinese smartphone maker Xiaomi has done to Samsung what Samsung did to Nokia.
Xiaomi has even revelled in its downfall. Whether by playing up reports which call Xiaomi the market leader or by taking digs at Samsung, like when it repeatedly mocked Samsung's M Series in teasers for its upcoming smartphone.
Samsung slumped on many fronts: products, pricing, retailer engagement (where, again, they've been likened to Nokia; ouch!), online strategy. In a country where smartphones are the single largest contributor to the GMV of e-commerce platforms, Samsung never paid much attention to online sales. People estimate it contributes no more than 14% of the company’s sales, as against 70% of Xiaomi’s sales happening online. One of the biggest reasons for the Chinese company’s success in India.
But the Korean ship is turning around. It appears that Samsung intends to beat Xiaomi at its own game. Don’t expect the latter to be a sitting duck, though. Xiaomi, apart from a launch lineup of 7-8 phones this year, is wading into Samsung’s territory of consumer electronics and more.
Have a great week. We’ll hear of phones and features from Barcelona this week. Bigger screens, more cameras, better gyroscopes, more XYZ. Who said innovation is slowing!
