Since we are already talking about American elections, that's not a far leap from the title of today's story. A few weeks back, Amazon officially confirmed that it had partially bought a significant chunk of More - a chain of retail stores in India, as part of a Rs 4200 crore deal. Sumanth describes this development as 'Amazon's unlikely Manchurian Candidate in India'. He posits that this is just the beginning - Amazon has ceded ground in categories such as electronics and apparel to its competitors, so it has turned its attention to groceries and food instead. In order to pull this off, it needs candidates like More to make these categories work by owning the customer experience through aspects like real-time inventory, cashless checkout etc. 
 
We look at this event's multiple facets: How it fits Amazon's ambitions. How More really had no choice. What other players like Paytm and Flipkart are thinking right now. This is also probably just the beginning - soon other loss-making retail chains that are on the block will queue-up before Amazon, and ask if they can join as well.
 
It's almost like a twisted version of an invite-only referral program. Except all the invites are with Amazon.
