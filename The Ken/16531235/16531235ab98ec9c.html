Good Morning Ishan,
 
Much has been made about India’s financial inclusion, but the fact is, it’s still a punk. The apex bank regulates faster than the rules get followed. Politicians claim podium success before the accounts see deposits. Companies burn boatloads of investor money without figuring out a business model.
 
And if you consider the most ambitious nudge, the payments banks, pardon my French, it's one big clusterfuck.
 
What was meant to be a differentiated bank, one without the baggage of a branch-led model and having a digital makeup, has been reduced to, well, a trick-or-treat play. In the last few months, people across the country were tricked into opening zero-balance payments bank accounts. All on the back of thousands of agents running high on commissions, even multi-level marketing schemes.
 
The payments bank licence owners Paytm and Airtel have played to the policy tune, geared towards opening accounts rather than transactions. Millions of accounts were opened using electronic KYC, breaking the cardinal rule of consent. Both the companies have been chastised and penalised. Last week, the RBI prohibited Paytm from opening more bank accounts.
 
But the apex bank can’t be absolved of its massive regulatory flip-flops and botched strategy. It even pitted big banks against the new payments banks. Was the regulator framing policies depending on which side of the bed it woke up on?
 
The fact that both Paytm and Airtel circumvented regulation to find a hack to customer acquisition begs the larger question. Did policymaking render payments banks dead-on-arrival? And is this the reason why many licence holders ended up returning their licences?
 
Arundhati and Ashish have spent several days speaking to people in the field, in companies and banks to understand and narrate just how royally we screwed up the payments banks opportunity, if ever there was one. It's an absolute must-read: https://the-ken.com/story/inside-the-paytm-payments-bank-fiasco/
 
Hope you have a super start to the week.
