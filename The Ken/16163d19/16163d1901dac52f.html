Good Morning Ishan,

There’ll be a blockchain in your future, whether you like it or not.
 
Technologists think so. Even without going into the Gladwellian 10K-hour rule of acquiring expertise, some plain reading makes it obvious why so many are so bullish on blockchain. The same can’t be said of Bitcoin though.
 
Blockchain is good, Bitcoin is bad, is the general stance. Heck, even Finance Minister Arun Jaitley suggested so during his Union Budget speech last week.
 
In India, banks are the first to take a stab at enterprise blockchain.
 
On 31 January, Yes Bank, which had been testing it for a while with some help from IIT students, deployed it commercially using IBM’s Hyperledger. It has digitised financing for Bajaj Electricals and its suppliers. Yes Bank has not made a public announcement yet, but apparently, it is the second such deployment in the world.
 
And it’s not the only one. As Sidhartha’s story shows, a slew of enterprise blockchain solutions are lined up in India. Starting February, when New York’s MonetaGo is deploying a custom solution for fraud mitigation on GSTN, the Goods and Services Tax Network.
 
Then there is Axis Bank and Singapore’s XinFin, ready to go.
 
Not moving from prototype stage to commercial deployment made many question whether tokenless blockchain can compete with the current practices and software in the market.
 
Nine years after the first blockchain was built, the rubber has met the road. The commercial deployments of enterprise solutions will allow us to scrutinise the technology and see whether it delivers on the hype.
