Good Morning Ishan,
Twenty-two years ago, Nick Bostrom, a Swedish philosopher and professor, published a paper officially titled [“Ethical Issues in Advanced Artificial Intelligence”](https://nickbostrom.com/ethics/ai). You might have heard of it as the “AI and paperclips” thought experiment.
In brief, Bostrom argued that a future superintelligence, charged with something as mundane as making paperclips, could—sans guardrails—end up destroying the world and all of humanity in the process.
“This could result, to return to the earlier example, in a superintelligence whose top goal is the manufacturing of paperclips, with the consequence that it starts transforming first all of earth and then increasing portions of space into paperclip manufacturing facilities.”
Most critiques of the (deliberately ridiculous) thought experiment focus on things like superintelligence, goal design, autonomy, or guardrails.
