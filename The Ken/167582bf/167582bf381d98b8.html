We are an app nation. Indians have downloaded 36.9 billion apps in the last seven years, highest installs by any country. We also share over apps with a vengeance. China’s file sharing app, SHAREit claims to be on 400 million phones with about 200 million monthly active users in India. Phew!
 
(Fair to say we also make apps with a vengeance, from the ruling political party to engineering students. IIT-Delhi director once told me in exasperation that nearly every student in the dorm churns out apps.)
 
It’s no surprise then, how Beijing-based SHAREit, which broke out of the Lenovo group, has satiated the app hunger for mobile users in tier-2 towns and beyond. Sideloading apps became a panacea for India’s content-hungry and data-starved masses. But with Jio slashing data costs, the peer-to-peer frenzy should have died, right?
 
Wrong. It persisted. Even grew. To the extent that Google struck a deal last month, because sideloading of apps via SHAREit, bypassing its Play Store, has left it blindsided on the Next Billion users’ behaviour. The movies, videos, games they play and watch. Google’s insistence on Gmail accounts for Play Store access has also been a deterrent for users in tier-2 and 3 towns, according to Jason Wang, MD of SHAREit India.
 
Offline behaviour is very distinct in India; Google wants a front-row view of that. For users, Google-SHAREit combine is great, but is it also great for the app? SHAREit survived the Jio-hit, the question is can it survive Google?
