Good Morning Ishan,

Another beautiful January morning and I’m glad to be at work. Early. With my Aadhaar. 

Today’s story by my colleague Sumanth is quite revealing. As I was reading it, I realised how little I know about the angel funding ecosystem. The old boys club of sorts. Where if you want to raise money for your venture, you start with one fat cat. And that fat cat puts you through his other fat cat friends. Hey, I have no issues with fat cats but it is always good to have a choice. Sumanth tells me that for many years now, AngelList has been a favourite choice for many entrepreneurs in the US. AngelList launched Syndicates (read about that in the story) in the US in 2013, and the company claims to have helped more than 1,800 startups raise more than $700 million. Since then, the platform has expanded to Canada and the UK, and now, to India.

So, what happens now? In India. That’s our story for today. 

In this piece, Sumanth answers three important questions. First, will AngelList help expand the pool of angel capital available in India and fill any gaps? Second, will it help in discovery and dealflow? Finally, will it help improve the funding process and make things more efficient? Let me pick one little insight from the copy. Man, this country lacks, credible, well-known lead investors. As Parag Dhol of Inventus Venture Fund says; seed funding is about herding the cats. Someone who is only moderately known won’t be able to close the funding round. The first Rs 60 lakhs ($100,000) will come in but the rest of the money won’t. It will drag on.
