Good Morning Ishan,

Since the pandemic roiled the world early last year, a deluge of capital has poured into stock markets around the world, chasing everything from meme stocks to concept stocks. And also, occasionally, returns. 

So powerful and unprecedented has this deluge been that it's hard to single out any one trend that it is comprised of. Except one—passive investing.

Over US$15 trillion in investments last year went into passive investing, that is, into funds (mutual funds and exchange traded funds) that merely track market indices and move up or down in correlation to the indices they track.

Indices like, in India, the Nifty 50, S&P BSE Sensex or Nifty 100. For a stock to get included into one of these funds is to hit a wave that suddenly brings in a surge of investors who are merely tracking the index and couldn’t care less about any one stock within it, per se. Conversely, to be removed or under emphasised from an index means losing investors and as a result, stock value.

Like India’s oldest but second largest telecom operator, Bharti Airtel. 

In August last year, MSCI slashed Bharti's weightage in the MSCI India Index from 3.5% to 1.8% and in the MSCI Emerging Markets Index from 0.285% to 0.144%. Bharti’s shares slid 4% in response to the news and analysts estimated that between Rs 3,500 crore (US$470 million) to Rs 3,800 crore (US$510 million) would be pulled out of the stock.

Whoosh!

Why did MSCI reduce Bharti’s weightage? Who decides what stocks make up an index? Was it right for the Adani Group stocks to be added to the MSCI Index earlier this year? Should index makers be more transparent? Should they be regulated? Are they also kingmakers? 

The answer to the last question is yes. The answers to the other ones are both complex and interesting. Which is why you should absolutely read today's meticulously reported story by Seetharaman and Anand on the largely unchecked power of indices and their makers in deciding which stocks to steer billions of dollars of investor money to.

Including yours.
