Good Morning Ishan,
Remember the famous visual from the Pied Piper of Hamelin? That’s what I was reminded of while reading today’s Big Story on the behind-the-scenes of quick-commerce apps.
No, this isn’t to show employees chasing their respective company’s founders or founders chasing investors. It’s a new category altogether—brands behind quick-commerce company’s category managers, the powers-that-be behind the limited number of options on Zepto, Blinkit, Instamart, and the like.
They are the ones meeting your demands and mine instantly, all while D2C brands chase them for a moment of visibility.
And chase them, how? From taking them out for drinks every month to inviting them to office parties to sending birthday and Diwali gifts. In short, everything to “massage their ego,” as the e-commerce head of a home-care brand told Aakriti and Nuha, the writers of today’s Big Story.
All because the game has only intensified, with more brands vying for a place in instant-delivery platforms’ dark stores, which can accommodate not more than three to five brands—established or startups—per category.
Here’s when being a category manager matters, for “limited space” means enjoying unlimited power. Being the gatekeepers, they get to decide who gets visibility on their respective platforms.
But that was just the brands’ side of the story singing to the tune of category managers. There’s more to what makes the latter the most sought-after people in the 10-minute delivery space.
