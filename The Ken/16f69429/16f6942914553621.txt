Good Morning Ishan,

Last week, around Christmas, I met an old B-school batchmate for a drink.

He now works at an emerging fintech company - one that routinely makes it to lists such as ‘Hottest Startups in India’, or ‘Best workplace in Bellandur’ or ‘Top Companies whose business models are impossible to explain to your parents’. 

Anyway, after a couple of drinks, the conversation inevitably turned to work. I asked him if he thought we were in a fintech bubble, especially in consumer lending in India. 

He thought about it. And his answer surprised me.

"Yes, we are in a bubble. But not in the way you media people think. You think it’s a bubble that will expand and expand, and then suddenly, one day, it will pop. That will never happen."

Then? 

"It will expand until we can’t see the bubble anymore. And we’ll get to a point where we aren’t sure if the bubble is still expanding, if we are inside it or outside it, or if it has popped, but we just don’t know about it, while we pretend that it hasn’t. The biggest worry isn’t whether the fintech bubble will pop. It’s that we won’t know when it happens."

Maybe my friend is a genius. Maybe Old Monk rum does strange things. But when I read Arundhati’s story today, it became clear as day what he was talking about. 

Fintech in India went through a lot last year. Companies grew. Some raised billions. Others died. And nearly everyone offered a form of consumer lending. It’s a sector where the ground constantly shifted underneath your feet, with companies pivoting into each other by accident or design without any warning. It’s hard to tell anymore who is the leader, the follower, the incumbent and the disruptor. 

Luckily, that’s why we have a story today to do just that.
