Netflix and Amazon are similar in many ways, but perhaps in no clearer way than in their reticence and secrecy towards sharing operational numbers. Like Amazon, Netflix, too, uses its numbers to strategic advantage, releasing only those that build its own narrative. While subtly letting its silence over unrevealed numbers become fodder for speculation, forecasting and half-baked responses. 
 
Netflix launched in India three years ago. Its pricing is decidedly premium in a market that pundits put down as “value-driven”, charging up to Rs 800 (~$12) for a month when competitors charge something of the order for a whole year! And yet, it has only continued to double down on India by spending serious money to commission original series, acquire content and market itself. What gives it the confidence?
 
That’s what Harveen and Ashish’s story today is about. They dive deep into five numbers that help us understand the animal that is Netflix. For instance, did you know that while globally nearly two-thirds of Netflix hours are consumed on TV screens, in India that number was just about one-third in 2017. Because more Indians access it on cheaper smartphones and mobile plans than they do on TVs and broadband ones. This is what led Netflix to introduce a cut-rate (by its standards!) mobile-only plan for Rs 250 (~$3.6) last week.
 
Indian telcos must be crying themselves to bed at night because Netflix’s cheapest plan is 2.5 times their average monthly revenue per subscriber (around Rs 100 these days).
