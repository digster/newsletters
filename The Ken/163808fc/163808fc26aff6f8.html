Good Morning Ishan,
 
It is some story. Like, back in 2015, 2016, you could see dollars from SoftBank and Sequoia driving down the streets of Delhi NCR, Mumbai and Bengaluru. You know, kids on delivery bikes with the tee shirts that read ‘Grofers’. Black tee. ‘Grofers’ in orange. That’s how I remember that time. That’s how I remember Grofers. And, of course, an orange umbrella, which I got when I ordered from them. Think I still have it somewhere. And then all of a sudden, gone. I kid you not; those tees disappeared faster than your youth. So, I met Albinder Dhindsa, some time back. He is the founder of Grofers, and I asked him, why, why don’t we see them anymore?
 
And he goes: “Man, my mother says the same thing. That somehow our visibility has gone down. And I told her, how will you see them? We shut down express, and we have delivery partners now who deliver in small trucks, wearing whatever. That’s why you don’t see them anymore.” 
 
Hah! How is business though? 
 
Dhindsa: “So we are doing gross merchandise value (GMV) of about Rs 130 crore every month. I think from where we were eighteen months back, that’s not bad.”
 
Making money?
 
“Not yet.”
 
I don’t say it’s easy. Delivering food & grocery using an app to people who need them, at their convenience is a tough business. People say all sorts of things about it. It is a logistics business. It is a keep your costs under control business because it has very thin margins. People say you must buy directly from companies. People say you need to have your own private labels, if you want to make money. People say there’s no way you can make money by delivering food & grocery in under two hours. I don’t think we have heard the last word on this. Yet. 
 
Enter into all of this, D-Mart. 
 
Over the last 18 months, the RK Damani-promoted Avenue Supermarts has been discreetly experimenting with the e-retail space. Focussed primarily on food and grocery, D-Mart’s pilot is currently limited to Mumbai. It offers click and collect as well as home delivery for both food and non-food products like FMCG and personal care. That’s our story for today. One which looks at what exactly is D-Mart up to.
