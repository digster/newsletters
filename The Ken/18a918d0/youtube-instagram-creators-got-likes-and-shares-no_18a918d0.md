Good Morning Ishan,
News. Politics. Sex.
Unarguably, these are hot-button topics. Those who created content on these subjects thrived on social-media platforms such as Youtube, Instagram, and Facebook.
But as the platforms giveth, so too, they taketh away.
Content flagging, account deletion, suspension, and shadow-banning—content creators are being hit with one form of censorship or another.
These platforms have been focused on reducing scams and deceptive practices for years. And the impact of that tightening is showing up more so now. But in curbing them, these creators are left as collateral damage.
All the eight sex-ed creators, including two doctors who spoke with The Ken, say these restrictions have only increased this year, hitting them where it hurts the most—reach, discoverability, and revenue.
Shivani, in today’s story, looks at how the opaqueness of the once creator-friendly social-media platforms and the lack of human recourse are leaving creators helpless and clueless about how to go about these issues.
