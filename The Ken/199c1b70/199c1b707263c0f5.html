It’s IPO season, and the listings aren’t stopping anytime soon.

Groww is India’s largest stockbroker in terms of the number of customers. Its revenue for the year ended March 2025 was Rs 4,060 crore, with profit of Rs 1,824 crore—a seeming reversal of fortune from being Rs 805 crore in the red during the previous year.

And it’s planning India’s biggest IPO for a business of its kind in November, reportedly worth up to Rs 7,000 crore at a valuation of Rs 80,000 crore ($9 billion).

This is despite the tight regulatory squeeze that companies like Groww must contend with, such as Sebi’s limitations on futures-and-options trading, an important source of revenue for Groww.

There is a slew of numbers to plow through to make sense of the pressure that’s on Groww’s broking business—and where it’s betting big to shore up revenue.

Anand analysed Groww’s financials and teased out the key points that matter. Read his story to find out why one of the most anticipated IPOs of the quarter isn’t quite what it seems.

On another note, India’s central bank made a move that could halt the rise of private credit, a $25 billion market. For years, banks were restrained by regulation but now the RBI is unshackling them.

In this week’s Long and Short, Seetharaman G explores what this shift could mean for India’s financial landscape and how it could erode private credit’s niche.
