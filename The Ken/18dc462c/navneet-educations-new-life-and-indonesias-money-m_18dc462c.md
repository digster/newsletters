Good Morning Ishan,
Imagine the left arm seeking help from the right arm when both the arms are equally in need of life-saving drugs.
In case you missed it, BSNL’s employees’ union has asked the government to allow it to use Vodafone-Idea’s (Vi) 4G network. After all, one is fully state-owned, another has become one—by way of shareholding. BSNL has an exodus of customers to the two private telcos—Airtel and Jio—because it can’t get its delayed 4G network in order; Vi has an exodus because it can’t get its 5G network running, thanks to its deep financial crisis.
Exactly two years ago, we anticipated it, and we called spade a spade: “PMO [stakes BSNL’s future](https://the-ken.com/story/pmo-stakes-bsnls-future-on-tata-and-an-atma-nirbhar-4g-gamble/) on Tata and an aatmanirbhar 4G gamble”.
The Tata consortium wasn’t ready then; it won’t be ready until December. Taxpayers’ money being pumped in to prop up BSNL is clearly not serving the larger objective of keeping the telecom market competitive and fending off a duopoly.
But not all legacy businesses find technology upgrades herculean like BSNL does.
In her richly reported story today, Alifiya [writes](https://the-ken.com/story/an-iconic-book-publisher-once-threatened-by-edtechs-lives-to-tell-the-tale/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) about one such legacy business that has resurrected itself—65-year-old book-publishing company Navneet Education Ltd.
Some 14 members of the Gala family are running the show together at this Rs 1,644 crore (US$198 million) business, which has taken the education-technology fight to the VC-funded edtech corner where it truly began.
And Navneet’s timing couldn’t be better. Under the New Education Policy 2020, all the tech jazz that teachers are supposed to pull off can now be sourced from publishers.
Staying on with tech, the savvy millennials and Gen Z comprise over half of Indonesia’s electorate, and it’s a no-brainer that this would have been the group that political parties canvassed hard for in the recently-concluded polls there. The likely winner of the race, Prabowo Subianto, has nearly wiped off his fiery, army general past from the voters’ minds, thanks to his friendly grandpa cartoon avatar that the online troops circulated.
Popularly known as buzzers, this group of individuals manage about 2.5 million social media accounts, scripting the narrative for their employer and bypassing online regulations, [writes](https://the-ken.com/story/how-social-media-buzzers-scripted-indonesias-presidential-elections-under-meta-tiktok-xs-watch/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) Prima Wirayani from Jakarta.
