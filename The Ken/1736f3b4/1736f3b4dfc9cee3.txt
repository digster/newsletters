Good Morning Ishan,

To be upfront, deals reporting is a tad rigged. Meaning, it’s a plug by one party or the other. Which is why so many of them only happen in news columns.

Still, for anyone chronicling business, some deals look seductive even when they are not consummated. Many years ago, it was IT services company Capgemini, sold or acquired many times only in business pages. In recent times, it’s BillDesk.

The largest payments aggregator in India—it processes payments to the tune of $80 billion per year, with over 50% market share in bill payments—has been said to be acquired or sold a few times. PayU, PayPal, and American Express have been its purported buyers.

Earlier this month, a business paper reported that BillDesk was looking to sell itself entirely for $2.5 billion and at least eight investment banks have been called by the promoters and its largest investor, General Atlantic.

The 20-year-old company, which has raised (only) $160 million, plays a crucial part in the payments food chain. It’s the most eligible bachelor in the age of platforms and digital payments.

On the sell side, apart from “founder fatigue”—its three founders are in their early fifties—the business environment is getting harder. In her story today, Arundhati says the next decade of payments is likely to be a minefield for BillDesk to navigate, making a strong case for it to ride off into the sunset, exit money in hand.

Founder MN Srinivasu says getting acquired “is the last thing on their mind”. He refutes the deal news: “Ask anyone who knows us and they will tell you that news of us doing a fashion show for a host of bankers is simply not our style.”

Style or not, there’s no smoke without fire.
