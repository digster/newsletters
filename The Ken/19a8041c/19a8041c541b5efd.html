When Nuha, the author of today’s story, spoke to Zepto’s co-founder and CEO Aadit Palicha, he seemed ready to tell it like it was.

The founder of the $7 billion quick-commerce company has a market share of around 30%, second only to market leader Blinkit’s 45%. But on the way there, the company has acquired the reputation of being a cash guzzler. Especially since Zepto wants to follow in the footsteps of Dmart and play the value game—which has long been sacrificed at the altar of speed.

Its cash-burning ways were already highlighted by Swiggy co-founder and CEO Sriharsha Majety, who publicly criticised Zepto’s strategy of prioritising volume growth over profitability, particularly pinpointing the “cash burn per order.” Before that, in March, Zomato CEO Deepinder Goyal had claimed that the quick-commerce sector was burning around Rs 5,000 crore per quarter, with Zepto accounting for more than half of it.

But Palicha seemed to go out of his way to show how Zepto was growing even while keeping burn under control, and said he is all about the numbers. So much so that he has nothing but confidence in himself and his company: “My humble submission: never bet against Zepto’s execution. It has not been a winning trade for many years in a row,” he said.
