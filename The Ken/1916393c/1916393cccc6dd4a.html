The Big Story

Tanishq wrote the gold-retail playbook. Kalyan Jewellers hijacked it 

For over two decades, the jewellery retailer has dominated organised retail in the sector. Now, regional players like Kalyan Jewellers and Senco Gold are challenging its lead

Tanishq is one of the largest jewellery retailers in India holding a solid 8% market share. It’s the third most valued Tata-company stock, after TCS and Tata Motors, thanks to Tanishq’s two-decade first-mover advantage. But that’s changing as regional players like Kalyan Jewellers and Senco Gold expand pan India. That shift is evident in share price: Titan is down 9% year-to-date, while Kalyan and Senco are up over 50%. [Read the full story]

Samsung smartphone users haven’t been so angry in years 

The Korean giant is faced with complaints galore about a mysterious green line on mid-range and premium phones alike

Samsung, known as a volume-first smartphone company, switched its strategy to focus on value customers in 2022. So much so that their ultra-premium segment (Rs 45,000+) saw an 81% year-on-year growth in the first half of 2024 from the previous year. Incidentally, for the past two years, premium Samsung phone owners have been waking up to green lines across their screens. While some pay for a screen replacement, others do not go down easily. You can buy an EV in China. But can you afford to insure it? 

EV owners struggle with high premiums, while insurance providers face financial strain

In China, EV insurance costs up to 81% more than for ICE vehicles due to high accident rates, evolving designs, and complex repairs. Insurers are paying out more in claims than they collect in premiums, hurting profitability. Many EV owners in China struggle with policy renewals—some are denied coverage, while others face higher premiums or extra costs. New EV insurance rules could allow providers to set flexible premiums and introduce dynamic pricing models. [Read the full story]

Tokopedia done, it’s Traveloka’s turn in Tiktok’s giant hunt in Indonesia 

After acquiring a 75% stake in Tokopedia last year, TikTok might start nibbling at the struggling Traveloka

Traveloka, Indonesia’s leading travel site, is hit hard by fierce competition and funding woes. Ambitious IPO or SPAC merger plans, once a talent magnet, are now on shaky ground. The shadow of China’s Bytedance looms large, adding to Traveloka’s troubles. With these elements in play, fears of Bytedance swooping in to take over Traveloka are growing. [Read the full story]

Big business

LTIMindtree’s five years of promises and now a likely Nifty 50 exit

Facing a potential Nifty 50 exit, LTIMindtree’s stagnation goes beyond its merger and hints at deeper problems

An underperformer in 2024, LTIMindtree is likely to exit from the Nifty 50 index by the end of August. The company also fell short of meeting its US$5 billion revenue target in the 2024 fiscal by over US$800 million. Since the L&T takeover and its eventual merger, the company has seen some key executives exit owing to cultural differences at work. It didn’t create much wealth, nor did it preserve a people-centric work environment. Could the merger have been avoided? [Read the full story]

What does Awfis know about co-working that Wework doesn’t? 

The co-working company’s shares have soared over 70% since its May listing, thanks to the appeal of its no-frills offices. Even so, its rivals have no interest in its business model

While other flexible-workspace operators lease properties from owners, Delhi-based Awfis Space Solutions partners with them for business. It’s been able to expand much faster, albeit its portfolio of buildings is more economy than business class. The way rivals see it, this model requires executing more complex deals even while reducing margins and giving up high-profile clients. As the supply of owners willing to enter such deals shrinks, Awfis’ innovative model is becoming more of a constraint. [Read the full story]
