[You can buy an EV in China. But can you afford to insure it?](https://the-ken.com/story/you-can-buy-an-ev-in-china-but-can-you-afford-to-insure-it/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
EV owners struggle with high premiums, while insurance providers face financial strain
In China, EV insurance costs up to 81% more than for ICE vehicles due to high accident rates, evolving designs, and complex repairs. Insurers are paying out more in claims than they collect in premiums, hurting profitability. Many EV owners in China struggle with policy renewals—some are denied coverage, while others face higher premiums or extra costs. New EV insurance rules could allow providers to set flexible premiums and introduce dynamic pricing models. The co-working company’s shares have soared over 70% since its May listing, thanks to the appeal of its no-frills offices. Even so, its rivals have no interest in its business model
While other flexible-workspace operators lease properties from owners, Delhi-based Awfis Space Solutions partners with them for business. It’s been able to expand much faster, albeit its portfolio of buildings is more economy than business class. As the supply of owners willing to enter such deals shrinks, Awfis’ innovative model is becoming more of a constraint.
