Max does not plan to set up hospitals across the country like Apollo, Fortis or Narayana Health. It's going a different way
Good Morning Ishan,
 
A beautiful, I-want-an-ice-cream-right-now kind of morning here. I know, I’m early for the week, but heck, all good things should be. And that goes for today’s story as well. Written by my colleague Ruhi.
 
Rarely, and I mean this, rarely do people attempt anything contrarian. I thought long and hard about my life, and nothing stands out. I keep indulging myself that if and when I quit, and then travel the world, and then tell stories from those places, that’d be pretty awesome. But contrarian? I am not so sure. That’s the hard part, something I still haven’t been able to get to. I am sure you know this already but you are lucky if you’ve done something contrarian. I would actually use the word, special.
 
In that context, Ruhi says Max Hospital’s journey is contrarian. While others, Fortis, Narayana Healthcare, Manipal and everyone else decided to grow by expanding their footprint, setting up more hospitals, adding more beds, Max decided that’s not what it will do. Instead, it would focus on healthcare at home and diagnostic services. Sell these services to its existing hospitals and also get new users. Eighteen months in, the verticals are doing well. Max@Home began operation in June 2016, and with an investment of under Rs 2 crore ($3,01,368), it has earned a revenue of about Rs 30 crore ($4.5 million) in FY18.
 
Now. Enter. Spanner.
 
Max has come a certain distance, but how does it grow outside Delhi NCR? What will be the hospitals it can tie up with? Certainly not Fortis...Where’s the money to fund such a strategy? Worse, isn’t selling diagnostics already done by one and too many? Read Ruhi’s piece to understand all of this: https://the-ken.com/story/max-healthcares-contrarian-bet/
