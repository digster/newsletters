Good Morning Ishan,
Quick commerce didn’t just speed up deliveries—it rewired how we think about waiting. Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart turned the mundane act of ordering groceries into a 10-minute adrenaline rush. Suddenly, “instant” wasn’t just a luxury; it became the default expectation. Now, consumers want the same for everything.
Haldiram’s knows it. Lenskart knows it. Listed marketplaces like Nykaa know it.
The problem: that kind of delivery isn’t just quick—it’s expensive. Building the infra to deliver in minutes is a logistical headache that most brands simply can’t afford. And getting listed on platforms isn’t a simple solution, either.
So, what do they do? They turn to a smarter, leaner alternative: third-party dark stores.
These aren’t your usual Zepto-style dark stores stuffed with bananas and bread. They’re compact micro-warehouses run by logistics startups, such as Zippee and WareIQ, as well as listed giants like Delhivery. These players don’t promise your order will arrive before you finish blinking. Instead, they aim for a sweet spot: “same-day delivery” that’s fast enough to keep customers happy but cheap enough for brands to stay alive.
“Brands have now understood that if you’re not offering fast shipping, your D2C business will be cannibalised between quick commerce and marketplaces,” says WareIQ CEO Harsh Vaidya. (Translation: If you can’t deliver quickly, you might as well not deliver at all.)
But if brands can’t do it alone, how are third-party dark stores pulling this off? That’s the story.
Gaurav [breaks down](https://the-ken.com/story/beyond-blinkit-and-zepto-is-there-a-quick-commerce-way-ask-brands-logistics-firms-reply-quick-enough/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) how third-party dark stores are rewriting the quick-commerce playbook and what’s at stake in the emerging battle: upstarts like Zippee versus behemoths like Delhivery.
