The Holy Grail of every consumer brand is to achieve "verb" status.
You don't search online, you Google stuff. You don't photocopy documents, you take a Xerox.
In India, Swiggy is one of the few brands that has achieved "verb" status. You don't order food online, you Swiggy it.
But that was before the pandemic-induced lockdown.
Today, very few people are Swiggying anything. With a number of restrictions in place and with people wary of risks (perceived and real) around contracting the virus from food delivery staff or from the food itself, Swiggy's food delivery business has been nearly decimated.
It now desperately needs a bit of magic to get out of this hole.
Enter "Genie"—a service that lets users task Swiggy delivery executives with errands or shopping orders.
Swiggy is hoping that Genie will grant it three wishes:
First, help keep revenue and other business metrics up to compensate for the sharp dip in food delivery orders.
Second, help better utilisation of delivery executives who would otherwise have no work to do.
And finally and most importantly, help the company position itself in the minds of consumers as being more than a mere food-delivery app. This is Swiggy's greatest challenge—the hundreds of millions of dollars that it has invested to become a verb that is synonymous with food delivery in
India has overnight morphed from being its biggest strength to its biggest challenge. Its Achilles Heel. It now faces the daunting prospect of having to invest millions of dollars afresh to unwind this mental model and create a new mind-share positioning for itself.
If these challenges don't sound daunting in themselves, Swiggy might have to additionally battle another verb—Dunzo, an app that has become synonymous with running errands in urban India.
Dunzo's loose/broad positioning as a general-purpose utility app had hitherto limited its traction vis-à-vis the likes of sharply-focused apps like Swiggy and Zomato. But in the new scenario, this old weakness could emerge as a new strength.
In today's richly-reported story, Kunal Talgeri takes you through the flips and flops in this fascinating battleground.
