First Principles

Sun, 08 Sep 24

Dear Ishan, good morning. Last Saturday, I got my annual flu shot. But sadly, it was also the day I caught a bad cold (I think I got infected a day or two ago by the same virus that took out much of Bengaluru that weekend).

It was the most ironic and yet scientific of coincidences. Because, while the flu shot does increase your chances of avoiding the flu (or making the symptoms milder, at least), it does not protect you against the common cold. 

I was down and out for the better part of the week. And since I’m also a “boomer” addicted to work, I felt guilty about “losing” so many days of productivity at a time when I couldn’t afford to (is there ever a time you can?). 

I think I’m about 60–70% of the way to a full recovery. 

I’ll be back in your inbox next Sunday with a full edition. Thank you for understanding. For today, it’s just a few quick notes.

Two by Two: Private Coaching is eating away at schooling

“My hope about investment in a good education system is actually falling apart. I’ll tell you why I’m saying this.

Last year, the GST collection from the private coaching industry was Rs 5,517 crore.

And the way it’s going, and based on the projections, it’ll be Rs 12,500 crore.

On the skilling side of it, again, it is GST. And that also would be about Rs 10,000–12,000 crore.

So, now the government has an incentive to ensure that the education system is not complete so that they can earn that Rs 25,000 crore.

And that’s a very dangerous place to be in.

Which government will give up Rs 25,000 crore of GST collections? Think of it?”

That was Maheshwer Peri, the founder and CEO of Careers360, on episode 8 of Two by Two. Listen to him, Sumeet Mehta (co-founder and co-CEO of LEAD Group), Nitin Pai (co-founder and Director of The Takshashila Institution), Praveen Gopal Krishnan, and me.

P.S. After many years of trial and error and research, I’ve arrived at the conclusion that August-end/early-September is the best time to build immunity for the winter flu season. If there are doctors in the FP Community who’d like to tell us about the ideal “windows” for annual flu vaccination in India, please do write in to me. Most of the literature online is based on either hemispheres (flu vaccines are released once a year for the northern and southern hemispheres) or the US flu season. It would be good to find a reliable source for Indian conditions and flu seasons.
