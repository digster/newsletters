Zomato needs to feed the beast. Zomato’s interest in Paytm’s movie-ticketing and events business may be in preparation for the end of the Blinkit juggernaut

Good Morning Ishan,

It’s hard to keep Zomato out of the news these days, except now, the developments have nothing to do with quick commerce. 

Over the weekend, The Economic Times reported https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/technology/zomato-in-talks-to-acquire-paytms-movies-ticketing-business-deal-pegged-at-around-rs-1500-crore-sources/articleshow/111035485.cms that food-delivery and e-grocery company Zomato is in talks to buy fintech Paytm’s* movie-ticketing and events business for around Rs 2,000 crore (US$240 million). 

On Monday, both companies confirmed to the stock exchanges that these discussions were going on. Paytm has been in firefighting mode https://the-ken.com/story/rbi-issues-a-death-sentence-to-paytm-payments-bank/ for most of this year, and it stands to reason that it wouldn’t want too many distractions. “As noted in our earnings call, our focus will be on payment and financial services along with digital goods commerce, which are designed to help our merchants scale their businesses,” Paytm said in its note https://www.bseindia.com/xml-data/corpfiling/AttachLive/fa3c56a6-a32b-40e0-bf43-3412e6a2104b.pdf to the exchanges. 

Paytm began selling movie tickets in 2016, but it doesn’t have much to show in terms of market share, with Bookmyshow controlling more than three quarters https://www.medianama.com/2022/07/223-summary-cci-investigation-bookmyshow/ of the industry. 

But with live events—organising and marketing everything from concerts to treks to poetry workshops—it’s a different story. Paytm bought Insider.in in 2017 and is within striking distance https://inc42.com/buzz/paytm-in-talks-with-zomato-to-sell-movie-ticketing-biz-for-inr-1500-cr/ of Bookmyshow https://entrackr.com/2023/12/bookmyshow-posts-rs-1026-cr-income-and-rs-85-cr-profit-in-fy23/. 

Zomato runs festivals that combine food and music, and claims to have sold 190,000 tickets on its platform in the year ended March 2023. So on the face of it, Paytm Insider ties in neatly with its operations here. But the events business is also Zomato’s smallest. Truth be told, I’ve always thought of it more as a branding exercise than a legit vertical. 

But Zomato now wants to make it one. Why?
