Good Morning Ishan,

A journalist’s work is always rooted in facts. But sometimes, when we talk to sources, we tend to come across what ifs. Like last week, when I was talking to an analyst about Paytm*. 

The listed fintech has had a miserable time at the bourses recently. As of Wednesday, its stock was at an all-time low of Rs 450 (US$5.5), and its market capitalisation a little under than Rs 30,000 crore (US$3.5 billion)—lower than the company’s US$4.8 billion valuation in 2016 and far lower than the US$14.5 billion on the day it got listed. 

There were a couple of important things that happened to Paytm over the past few days that have led to this latest dip in share prices. 

The first one was inevitable. Last week, as the post-listing lock-in period for investors ended, Japanese investment giant Softbank sold https://www.reuters.com/technology/softbank-sell-215-mln-stake-indias-paytm-cnbc-tv18-2022-11-16/ a 4.7% stake in the company. Paytm’s stock fell 10% in response. 

Then, this Tuesday, the stock fell another 11%. 

Because a report from brokerage Macquarie said Jio Financial Services (JFS) is now poised to become the fifth-largest financial services player in India. And two companies in particular are expected to feel the heat with JFS’ launch—Bajaj Finance and Paytm. Bajaj’s share price has remained largely the same, given the kind of credit growth it has maintained over the years. We already know what happened to Paytm shares. 

Which brings us to that ‘what if’ my source brought up during our conversation: 

Paytm may actually be a good acquisition bet for JFS.
