Or are they a chimera? It’s tough to say. The numbers internationally look promising. Phocuswright, a travel market research platform, says that activities (in millennialspeak, “experiences”) is the third-largest market for travel companies. Surpassed only by flights and hotels, it is expected to reach $183 billion by 2020. The numbers are big enough to even excite SoftBank, which invested $225 million in Hong Kong-headquartered travel activities platform Klook, and also led the $500 million series E round in Berlin-based travel startup GetYourGuide, which focuses primarily on activities.

The market in India though is much smaller, because it's nascent and growing. A report by Google and Bain estimated that 47% of Indian travellers' budgets are spent on three categories that could be classified as activities—recreation, food and shopping. 

That isn’t stopping everyone from taking leaps of faith. Startups like Thrillophilia, Headout and Deyor Camps. Local OTAs like MakeMyTrip, Yatra and Cleartrip. International giants like Airbnb and TripAdvisor. Offline travel companies like Cox & Kings and Sita Travels. 

Everyone is on the experiences bandwagon.

Except, off take is kind of lacklustre. For a bunch of reasons, which Vandana covers in her story. Which has led market leader MakeMyTrip to try and "build a Dineout, BookMyShow and Nearbuy, all within the MMT app”, she says. Dineout offers table reservations for restaurants, BookMyShow is into event ticketing and Nearbuy is a hyperlocal deals platform for everything from food to spas and shopping.

What about the others, what are they doing?
