"Some startup sectors have been hammered by Covid-19 – travel and hospitality being the prime examples – but a surprisingly large group have, *so far,* weathered the storm," David Frankel wrote in a recent Twitter thread.

Frankel, the co-founder of seed-stage VC fund Founder Collective, may well have been talking about India's regional content and social media apps. With nothing to do in a lockdown, people have flocked to the likes of podcast player Kuku FM, and voice-based, peer-to-peer knowledge-sharing app Vokal. 

A 2X to 3X jump in users is a happy uptick in fortunes of these platforms that were birthed during India's data boom, but have lived in the shadows of larger rivals such as ShareChat and TikTok. The user growth has significantly lowered customer-acquisition costs—money that the startups are using in content creation, advertising and marketing.

So far so good.

"'So far' is not a long-term response to this crisis. If, so far, you still have customers and your burn is still manageable, count your lucky stars but don't assume those conditions will continue. If you are a startup CEO, complacency is simply never an option," continued Frankel in his tweet.

Another point to note for these startups. While they are focussed on retaining users and have enough of a cash runway to tide them over now, not all of them are thinking about monetising these users.

"Make the changes needed to survive. Survival is a necessary precondition to thriving later. Just survive. Please," says Frankel, ending his thread.
