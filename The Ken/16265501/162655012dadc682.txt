Good Morning Ishan,

Touchy subject this. Staying relevant. 

One moment you are kicking it out of the park, gathering all the accolades, and before you realise (or maybe you do), the moment has passed. Perhaps someone’s come up with something better or just that the game changed, the rules changed or the endless possibilities of change changed, and there you are in the corner. Not relevant. 

If there was a word for this, I guess Doordarshan would qualify. 

My colleague Harveen says India’s public broadcaster though has found a new way to stay relevant. Prasar Bharati, which runs All India Radio and Doordarshan—a network of 23 television channels—is betting on digital terrestrial transmission (DTT)—a technology by which consumers can receive TV channels via radiowaves, on smartphones or tablets without data consumption. The app is called TV-On-Go. Through DTT, Doordarshan offers live television via five Doordarshan channels—DD National, DD News, DD Sports, DD Bharati and Doordarshan's regional channel or DD Kisan channel—and two to three radio channels to the viewers since 2016. 

Over the past few years, DTT has primarily been limited to TV households, with a scope to expand to mobiles. What has changed now is the pace of expansion and the focus on the rural populace with access to mobile phones. Prasar Bharti says that with DTT, it is creating a new market. Afterall, not everyone can afford data. Private broadcasters watching DD believe it is barking up the wrong tree. It has numerous other problems to fix. The elephant in the room: relevant content on its channels.
