Good Morning Ishan,
 
Ans: Four—one for each of the three mobile operators, and one for good luck.
 
That’s not a joke. We’ve written extensively about the precipitous fall of competition and operators in India’s once-booming mobile telephony space. From over two dozen operators in the not too distant past, we’re counting down to the final three (private) players—Reliance Jio, Bharti Airtel and Vodafone Idea. Investors and markets expect only two of those to remain standing. (Sure, there are 1.5 sick government-run operators—BSNL and MTNL—but the government is already in the process of stripping and selling their valuable real estate assets.)
 
That would probably mean 2-3 free-standing operators. 
 
Before I get into today’s story, allow me to introduce an analogy. Say, instead of mobile operators we’re talking about pizza brands. Say, there were once over two dozen pizza brands competing for Indian consumers. And each one maintained a fleet of delivery staff to get their steaming pizzas to customers' homes within 30 minutes. What would happen after a while?
 
The companies would realise that it doesn’t make sense for everyone to maintain their own delivery staff. Instead, it would be better to spin off the delivery staff into an independent company that could also service other brands. That way, each delivery company could better utilise its staff, while each pizza company could still get their pizzas delivered without having to manage large delivery workforces.
 
How many independent delivery companies do you think could be profitable and sustainable? 3? 5?
 
Now imagine the number of pizza brands reduces to just 3. How many independent delivery companies would now be sustainable?
 
That’s exactly what’s happening in India’s mobile telephony space. The number of private operators has fallen to 3, and is possibly headed for 2. But the number of independent tower companies is growing from 2 to 3. It doesn’t make sense. If there are three pizza companies and three delivery companies, then they might as well merge and become three pizza delivery companies. Outsourcing only makes sense if there are more outsourcers than service providers.
