This quarter, Jiohotstar had one job: stream the Indian Premier League (IPL) smoothly to crores of Indians. That’s it. That’s been enough to justify its existence for a while now. But apparently, it’s not anymore. Because this IPL season, it doesn’t just want to be your go-to for cricket—it also wants to be… Youtube.

In February, the newly merged Jio–Disney giant birthed Sparks, a short-form video platform that wants to take on Youtube Shorts and Instagram Reels. And it’s gone for the jugular.

It’s poached an ex-Youtube India MD and a former Instagram product head—the very people who helped build Youtube and Instagram—so they can now unbuild them. The pitch: bring in India’s biggest digital creators—Tanmay Bhat, Zakir Khan, Ranveer Brar, Elvish Yadav—and get people to replace their Youtube doomscrolling with bingeable, episodic content. The problem: Youtube has 500 hours of content uploaded every minute. Sparks has… 45 hours in total so far. Oh, and it’s decided to remove user comments. Because if there’s one thing people love about Youtube, it’s not being able to yell at each other in the replies.

Will Sparks work where even Amazon Prime and Netflix failed? Jiohotstar—with 50 million users and a habit of brute-forcing its way into success—thinks it has cracked the code by mixing a dash of cricket, a sprinkle of Koffee with Karan, and a little bit of China.
