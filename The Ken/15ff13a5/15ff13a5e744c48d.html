Weekends are the worst, aren't they? You could sleep too much and feel guilty. You could not sleep at all and feel sick. You could do all your chores and still have lists and lists that are best left unattended. You could binge-watch some Netflix show and not know night from day.
 
You could go on a short trip and dread Monday. You could read your favourite book and regret not reading a new one. You could run out of things to do or do things you never meant to...
 
Weekends are the worst. And no one else would tell you that. But hey, you can always read this newsletter and feel purposeful. You're totally welcome.
 
Cheekiness aside, The Ken has had a good week, and if you're up for a quick flashback, do proceed.
 
We started our week with Venkat's update on the status of the upcoming Facebook Watch. Safe to say, you'll have yet another platform to stream videos on by early next year. (More weekend binging, yay?)
 
Our FREE piece this week came from Ruhi and Seema, and it looked at India's blind following of American medical standards and how it could deem over half the Indian population hypertensive overnight. Why would India do this again? Well, because we have no data of our own.
 
Wednesday saw Patanjali reporting about Future Group's future ambitions, and the man at the helm of it—Kishore Biyani. As the relentless entrepreneur launches Retail 3.0, he does what he does best—Husting.
 
Arundhati's Thursday piece was about Bengaluru's Man Friday. Dunzo, an app that can buy, carry, deliver just about anything at the touch of a button, is all the rage in the city. But can it scale beyond Bengaluru? That's the story.
 
We ended the week with Sumanth's take on Softbank's hard-nosed moves. Its $100 billion Vision Fund, which was the focus of the piece, "bears grave portents for startups and investing in India, but there could be a much bigger problem lurking". What could it be?
 
Now that you're all caught up, we'd suggest you ditch the weekend dilemma and stay guilt-free. Heaven knows we could use more of these.
 
Have a great one!
