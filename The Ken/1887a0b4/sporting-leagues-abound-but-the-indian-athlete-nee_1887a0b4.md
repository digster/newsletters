Good Morning Ishan,
For decades, the Indian government has onboarded sportspersons in various public-sector units (PSUs) under sports quota, aiming to promote sports in the country. This recruitment of footballers, hockey players, and various sportspersons serves other purposes too. From harnessing the brand image of these players to boost their own goodwill to getting good talent to represent PSUs’ own sporting teams in various domestic leagues—apart from providing monetary support to those recruited and letting them pursue the sport they love.
As private leagues have proliferated in India in recent years, recruitment of sportspersons in state-owned companies has reduced. This has put many a sportsperson, who finds it hard to make ends meet by just pursuing the sport, in a quandary. While some are giving up sports altogether to focus on their government jobs instead, others are walking a tightrope balancing both.
Vaibhav Raghunandan, who makes his debut for The Ken with today’s fascinating story, got a whiff of this phenomenon when he met V Midhun, a Kerala-based State Bank of India (SBI) employee at a football field. Midhun, a prolific player for Kerala, has led the southern state of India to lift the Santosh Trophy twice. Several others who spoke to Vaibhav confided in him that a secure government job was more important than the uncertainties of over-rated private sporting leagues. Or for that matter, the sport itself.
