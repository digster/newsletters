A not-so-quiet revolution is sweeping across the weight-loss world. The writing on the wall was clear in 2023 itself, when game-changer drugs such as Ozempic and Mounjaro became drawing-room conversations. It was also the year when global private-equity giant Carlyle undertook a curious move in India. The money-bag went contrarian by buying a majority stake in VLCC—a conventional weight-loss business that offers in its clinics solutions such as laser trimming and cool-sculpting.

Short for Vandana Luthra Curls and Curves after its eponymous founder, VLCC born in 1989 in Delhi has spread its wings across the country. But it is now up against a surge that has the capacity to swallow anything in its wake. Ask Weight Watchers, the US firm that went belly up this year due to the rise of weight-loss drugs like Ozempic.

Yet Carlyle has been doubling down on VLCC—adding both count and weight to its clinics. As many as 60 new clinics have opened since it took over, taking the total to 160. Plus the 60-odd franchisees.

Has the hard-nosed money manager lost its marbles? Or does it have an ace or two up its sleeve? Competition, co-option, or both? Nuha has the answers in today’s story. Let’s just say neither Carlyle nor VLCC is taking the imminent danger lightly.
