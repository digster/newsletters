Good Morning Ishan,
 
There was a time when a fresh orange was the perfect gift for this season. Myths and stories abound as to why this fruit was a real treat.
 
As the gifting frenzy grew over time, gizmos, gadgets, scented candles, and a zillion other things filled our minds and shopping bags as we spent December afternoons swanning around gift shops. Then, much of all that went online. From the time of crude global shipping logistics—when receiving a single orange was a luxury—to same-day deliveries, gifting in the time of e-commerce has been limitless.
 
Come 2020, it had to become contactless. No red roses, no made-to-order cakes or succulents. How about dial-a-guitarist then? A customised birthday greeting, by an artiste, over the phone. A special made-for-pandemic adaptation.
 
Pulling off personalised guitar renditions at the backend was chaotic. But Ferns N Petals, India’s largest gifting retailer, adapted well when its network of 400-odd franchises was shutting down due to Covid-19 earlier this year. Diwali in November brought the proof of the pudding; usually a non-event for the 25-year-old-company, the festive season saw Ferns N Petals’ online sales grow 50% YoY.
 
Ferns N Petals was never conceived for the digital age. Still, it pre-empted all shades of the e-commerce business, even when models like Amazon Prime or Dunzo were good 15 years away. In a plot full of Bollywood-style twists and turns, Olina brings out the gritty story of the retailer who serves 2,000 (out of 19,000) zip codes in India. It’s now focussing on its international markets in Singapore, UAE, and Saudi Arabia.
 
Ferns N Petals’ second offline-to-online move places it squarely in competition with Amazon and Dunzo on the one hand, and Swiggy and Zomato on the other. (Why fret over cakes and start franchise-bakeries when foodtech exists?)
 
But gifting is gifting; it doesn’t just involve delivery. The only personalisation in orange days was stuffing it in the toe of our stockings. Today, it’s limited only by our purses and imagination.
