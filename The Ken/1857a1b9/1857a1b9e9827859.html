Good Morning Ishan,
 
There’s a saying attributed to physicist Albert Einstein: “The definition of insanity is doing the same thing over and over again and expecting different results.” 
 
The layoff wave we experienced in Southeast Asian tech companies and the tech sector worldwide was in some way inevitable—a direct result of the previous year's exuberance and growth. 
 
Economists say that growth and contraction happen in cycles. Yet, we’d be foolish to accept this as a status quo and repeat the cycle of (over)hiring and firing without at least trying to do better.
 
In today’s interview, Dita asks Erika Dianasari Go—a partner at Alpha JWC Ventures and an expert in managing human resources—some pointed questions. The mass layoffs affected many of Alpha's portfolio companies. As a venture-capital (VC) firm, it had a front-row seat in these startups' growth and the subsequent scaling-back phase.
 
So, what are the lessons founders and VCs can take away from this downturn about building teams that last and about hiring and firing? What can they do differently next time?
