With India's national lockdown limiting access to markets, an enterprising seafood lover in my housing complex in Mumbai swung into action in April.

She found a fish vendor who could deliver to our homes and formed a WhatsApp group for the residents.

This is how it works: she gets the weekly prices for different fish—mackerel, pomfret, seer fish—from the seller and posts them on the group. I then send a message stating which fish I want and how much of it. The next person copies my message and adds their requirement. The chain continues, and the final list is sent to the vendor who delivers toward the end of the week and collects the money.

It's not seamless, but it's a good enough hack. Facebook-owned WhatsApp, which has 400 million daily active users in India, has become particularly indispensable during the lockdown. In April, Facebook expanded WhatsApp's group calling feature from four to eight people since it observed people talking for around 15 billion minutes each day on video/voice calls. 

Not surprisingly, businesses are waking up to the potential of the app in reaching customers. Except, it's not exactly how Facebook expected them to use it. WhatsApp Business, launched in 2018, allows brands to integrate the WhatsApp API and send templated messages at US$ 0.004 per message, which is three times what a telco charges for an SMS. As of 2019, 700 companies in India had API-approved accounts on WhatsApp Business. 

But, as Prasannata writes in today's story, brands are also using WhatsApp the way you and I do. For free.

Store managers at shoe brand Bata—which controls over 20% of India's footwear market—and jewellery brand Kalyan Jewellers—which runs more than 100 outlets across India—conduct virtual tours on WhatsApp for their customers and show them their catalogue. This adds that little personal touch which is missing in a regular online purchase. Once the customer chooses a product, they are directed to a payment app.

But the process is hardly friction-free and has one too many steps for it to be a smooth experience.
