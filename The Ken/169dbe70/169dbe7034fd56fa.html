Good Morning Ishan,
 
...that. 
 
Whatever it is, there’s an app for that. Looking for a cab? There’s an app for that. Want to find out the best time to go use the restroom while watching a movie so that you don’t miss anything important? There’s an app for that. Want a reminder to drink water? There are 252 apps for that, by my count, on the Play Store. 
 
A few years back, Apple even trademarked the phrase, ‘There’s an app for that’, so if we get served a legal summons thanks to this email, you’ll know why.  
 
So you can imagine my reaction when I first read Ruhi’s story. She writes about how Indian pharma companies are embracing digital therapy (basically a fancy way of saying there’s an app for that) as a way to help patients with chronic illnesses live a better life. I am not talking about stuff like an app to remind you to take a pill. This is much more evolved, like helping patients with Type-2 diabetes. 
 
I know what you are thinking. Who does this, really? Seems like a trend that exists on the fringes. Like people who use Hike Messenger. 
 
But what if I told you that India’s largest drug manufacturer by sales, Cipla, bought a stake in a three-year-old startup, Wellthy - that solves this problem, for $1.5 million dollars. And others are joining the fray. Biocon. Lupin. Sun Pharma. Ruhi thinks that a shift may be happening, because digital therapy is not just cost-effective, but also works to prevent complications from diabetes by reminding patients to exercise, eat right, and do the right things at the right time to manage their illness. And this is just the beginning.
 
But the key question is - does it even work? You can lead a horse to water, but can you make it drink? Even with 252 water drinking reminder apps?
