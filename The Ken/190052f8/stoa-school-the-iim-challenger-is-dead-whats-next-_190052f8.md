Good Morning Ishan,
The world has enough MBAs, don’t you think?
Nope. Last year, a record 330,000 students registered for the Common Admission Test (CAT), a 30% increase from the previous year.
This kind of demand should naturally rub off on alternative MBA (altMBA) schools like Stoa that emerged during the pandemic. After all, those who don’t clear the CAT need other options.
But those like Stoa weren’t just about being another B-school option if one didn’t make it to the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs). They were also about challenging the playbook of business-management education in the country. They were all about practical learning, flexibility, and cheaper fees.
Soon, the idea caught on. Stoa’s first cohort had 60 students, and by the fifth, enrollment had swelled to 130.
And now? Well, the school said it is no longer accepting students.
Nathan, the author of today’s story, delves into what happened to Stoa and examines [whether this is a Stoa problem or an altMBA problem](https://the-ken.com/story/stoa-school-the-iim-challenger-is-dead-whats-next-for-indias-altmbas/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
Private equity (PE) major Blackstone made a huge exit yesterday. It exited from the mid-sized IT company Mphasis, the only listed IT firm in India with a majority stake owned by a PE firm. Blackstone offloaded 15% of its 55% stake in the company, amounting to about US$800 million, after about eight years of investment.
Blackstone played an outsized role in Mphasis. Not only did it invest over US$3 billion in the IT company, but it also helped bring in its own clients as customers to the IT firm.
But as the PE firm now charts an exit, even a partial one, Mphasis will need to woo new investors at a time when the IT industry’s numbers are stagnating, and chatbots are expected to steal jobs and clients.
My young colleague Abhirami wrote about this very risk in an astutely observed story a week ago. [Do read it](https://the-ken.com/story/can-mphasis-attract-new-suitors-blackstone-drives-appeal-ahead-of-possible-exit/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
