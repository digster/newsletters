Aditya Birla Fashion tried every trick in the book to stay relevant, especially with Tata-owned rival Trent soaring to new heights. But finally, it was forced to sacrifice https://the-ken.com/story/whats-aditya-birla-fashion-without-brands-like-allen-solly-and-louis-philippe/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter itself in order to have a shot at redemption. 

At a market value of Rs 17,600 crore, Aditya Birla Lifestyle Brands, the demerged company that began trading on the stock exchanges in June, is worth 70% more than Aditya Birla Fashion. It’s as if retail analysts finally have something to be excited about the group. Brokers Morgan Stanley and HDFC Securities are counting on a 25–30% rise in Aditya Birla Lifestyle’s shares. 

The demerger is proving better in more ways than one. Just two weeks ago, Aditya Birla Fashion was hit https://www.cnbctv18.com/market/stocks/aditya-birla-fashion-share-price-faces-rs-14-crore-tax-demand-in-maharashtra-19795645.htm with a Rs 13.77 crore tax demand from the Maharashtra government. While not a crippling amount for a company of its size, it’s always wiser to keep your soles clean if you can.

The tax demand, though, pales in comparison to the ongoing legal drama between Bata India and Crocs.

For the last decade or so, Bata India has been fighting some version of the same case against American footwear retailer Crocs. Its latest salvo: a 14 November Supreme Court judgement upholding Crocs’ right to file a “passing off” suit against Bata India, Liberty Shoes, and others. 

Bata India is certainly acting nonchalant, as it’s aiming to open 60 exclusive outlets in India by the end of FY26. To catch up with all the drama, read Neha’s piece below.

The catalyst? 

Beginning 1 April, 2025, the government has mandated beverage makers—including the likes of Pepsi, Coca-Cola, Campa, and Parle—to use e-PET (recycled PET) bottles for at least 30% of their yearly production. In fact, the government plans to increase this threshold by 10 percentage points each year, until by 2029, companies are using recycled plastic for 60% of their volume. 

Is this environment-friendly approach helpful? Is it profit-friendly? 

Speaking of profits, here’s something interesting about Technosport:

Technosport has existed since the mid-2000s, but its rise is recent and steep. Since 2022, it has grown 40–50% every year. In 2024, it crossed https://retail.economictimes.indiatimes.com/news/apparel-fashion/apparel/technosport-crosses-rs-300-cr-revenue-mark-plans-7-ebos-this-fiscal/111757322 Rs 300+ crore in revenue and sold a staggering 25 million units of activewear—the highest volume of any brand in India, according to a senior representative at the company. Today, it moves nearly two million garments a month, competing with Nike, Adidas, Decathlon, and homegrown stars like HRX. The latter, co-owned by Hrithik Roshan, declared a Rs 1,000 crore revenue in 2024.
