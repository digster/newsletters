Going public is a big event in a company’s life. But preparing for it is no easy task.

Just ask foodtech major Swiggy, which has set its sights on a stock-market debut next year. It is seeking to raise over a billion dollars and at a valuation no less than the $10.7 billion it got in its December 2021 fund-raise.

Aayush, the author of today’s story, gets inside how the nine-year-old company is changing itself to make this shift.

From how its leadership is closely watching profitability targets to errors of any kind not being excused, Swiggy has dialled up the pressure on its employees. This means longer work hours, including on weekends.

“[We’re in] a phase where the end justifies the means,” an employee told The Ken.

Aayush reports on just what exactly are Swiggy’s means to get to the promised land: https://the-ken.com/story/ipo-bound-swiggy-turns-up-internal-pressure-in-search-for-profits/ (9-minute read)
