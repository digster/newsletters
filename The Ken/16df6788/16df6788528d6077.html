Good Morning Ishan,
 
Buckets of ink have been spilt in recent months on the travails of OYO in India, all mostly after WeWork's spectacularly botched IPO. Newsreels and newsprints are suddenly awash with red flags over OYO's financials, valuation, contracts and business model. I am half expecting someone to question why OYO is spelt in all caps.
 
But as you also know, The Ken has asked these questions (and been questioned in return) for years. Today, Jon takes our narrative forward. Or, more accurately, overseas, to Southeast Asia - a key expansion market for OYO. And a market where its antithesis awaits.
 
RedDoorz is a budget hotel aggregator with an India-born CEO and ... umm! Ok, end of OYO similarities.
 
While OYO's total funding has topped $1.5 billion, RedDoorz survived on just $25 million for four years before raising $115 million in two rounds earlier this year.
 
Some of that will go towards making Thailand RedDoorz's fifth Southeast Asian market. And this with only 70% of the Indonesia-based company's model being "set," because flexibility is "what makes the difference" in the diverse region, says founder Amit Saberwal.
 
Saberwal has previous industry experience (with online ticketer MakeMyTrip) and is nearly old enough to be the father of 25-year-old OYO founder Ritesh Agarwal.
 
RedDoorz wants to ride the region's growing hotel industry to every founder's ultimate dream—an initial public offering. That's IPO, not MPO - M for the maverick CEO of SoftBank, Masayoshi Son.
 
But, with SoftBank in its corner, OYO cannot be counted out. And there is the reinvigorated Zen Rooms. Plus, since WeWork, investors are shying away from leased-based-property business models.
