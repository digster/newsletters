Good Morning Ishan,
“I don’t even want it.”
At Microsoft’s annual Ignite conference last week, IT buyers were finally opening up about what they really thought about 365 Copilot, the company’s office productivity tool that can also provide assistance to developers. And most of them weren’t shy about saying it on the record.
Here’s [one of them](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/11/23/microsoft-faces-uphill-climb-to-win-in-ai-chatbots-with-copilot.html):
“I know a lot of customers who are like, ‘Yeah, I want 300 to go to zero,’” said Adam Mansfield of consulting firm UpperEdge, referring to Copilot licenses. Mansfield, who helps companies negotiate Microsoft deals, said those clients are saying, “I don’t even want it.”
And another:
“We just had a massive 16,000-employee company move all their mail back to Google so they can leverage more Gemini,” said Julian Hamood, founder of Microsoft partner TrustedTech.
More:
Tim Crawford, a former IT executive who now advises chief information officers, said many customers aren’t getting enough from Copilot to justify the cost.
“Am I getting $30 of value per user per month out of it?” he said. “The short answer is no, and that’s what’s been holding further adoption back.”
If you are distrustful of anecdotes, let’s take a look at what’s going on with the similarly named GitHub Copilot, which is used specifically by developers.
