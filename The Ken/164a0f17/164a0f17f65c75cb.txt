Good Morning Ishan,

God knows this; I love corporate bull. Especially the kind disguised as strategy when it's actually rubbish PR. Let me explain.

In the last decade, Bennett Coleman & Co, or The Times Group launched a slew of English movie channels. MN+, Movies Now and Movies Now HD, Romedy Now and Romedy Now HD, and MNX (earlier, Movies Now 2) as well as MNX HD. In the world of the rise and rise of streaming services, like Netflix, Amazon Prime, Hotstar and whatever other distant stars, you’ve got to ask, what exactly was The Times Group thinking?

In 2016, at the launch of Movies Now 2, senior vice president, English cluster, Vivek Srivastava said that the company is working towards a segment strategy. A movie channel for a genre, so to speak. Unlike the Indian ‘thali’ approach, where you get everything on the same plate, action, drama, romance; and supposedly that’s bad because the thali is everything and not one good thing. To quote Srivastava on the need for another channel and lord I love this bit, ‘the channel will target youth with a free-spirited mindset and rebellious personality.’ “We are getting Movies Now 2 for sexy and stylish people who will like something wild,” he was quoted as saying. 

Ah! The beautiful art of bull. 

Early this year, the Times Group put all of its movie channels on the block. 

Hat tip to my colleagues Harveen and Pooja for this most excellent graf from today’s story: Now, it’s July. Six months have passed. After months of unsuccessfully searching for a buyer, Times is going in a different direction. A source aware of the matter said that Times has begun a process of internal restructuring in recent months to raise the capital it requires. But if a property owned by the country’s leading media house couldn’t be sold after months of trying, the most obvious assumption is that there’s a problem with the portfolio. In this case, that assumption is accurate.
