Good Morning Ishan,
A few weeks ago, I was privileged to be seated in a journalism workshop with some of the finest minds in the business. There were Pulitzer winners, regional heads of large media organisations, men and women who'd covered every major event from the Haiti earthquake to the Ferguson riots. And me.
My imposter syndrome was peaking.
Then came a question that reassured me I belonged. Why did you get into journalism? I heard a little bit of myself in every response. "Speak truth to power", "Be a voice for the voiceless", "Hold power to account", the answers rang out.
The one that truly hit home, however, was simpler still—"Bear witness".
I must confess, with protests against the government's Citizenship Amendment Act and the National Register of Citizens spreading like wildfire, police crackdowns resulting in deaths, and the looming fear of disenfranchisement for many, I feel a certain helplessness.
As a business journalist who deals with billion-dollar valuations and large, faceless corporations, I have to ask myself, do I still bear witness? And despite the self-doubt that creeps in, I know for a fact that I do.
Each week I get to edit the work of a newsroom full of phenomenally talented journalists who bear witness. Who shine a light on companies and sectors in a way no one else does. With objectivity and integrity. Last week we saw more evidence of that.
