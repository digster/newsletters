Good Morning Ishan,

I’ve been very excited about the month of June. 

A close friend from college, who I haven’t met in over five years, is finally flying down to India. We came close to planning an India trip for him time and again, but given his host country’s strict visa rules, he just wasn’t able to bring things together. Then COVID hit, and we came up against a whole world of trouble.

Now that the visa issues are resolved, I’ve been surprised to see that our next biggest logistical nightmare is payments! My friend hasn’t lived in India for a long time; he doesn’t have an Indian phone number or an Indian bank account. Sure, the credit cards should work at restaurants and with Uber, but his inability to access UPI seems to be a strangely difficult problem, especially in Bangalore, where even the smallest services and shops have moved on to UPI as a mainstay. He also cannot withdraw cash or use other apps like Paypal and Apple Pay widely.

I’m probably harping on about an old topic, sounding a bit like the NRI uncle who will not stop praising the wonders of UPI. But I have never travelled abroad or had to chaperone an NRI around like this before, so the nitty-gritty of this payment problem is all very new to me!

The Indian government had dreamt of success similar to UPI’s uptake when it proposed the Unified Health Interface (UHI). The aim was to introduce a common stack that could be used to solve issues around teleconsultations, hospital bed bookings, etc. But as my colleague Anushka found out in our Thursday story, the inactivity of a powerful Bengaluru-based think tank is now coming in the way of its prompt launch. 

On Monday, Alifiya wrote about how edtech unicorn Physics Wallah is betting on an expansion from its core strengths—in engineering and medical test preparations—to become a much wider player in the ecosystem. But even as it acts on grand ambitions, its core market is at risk of getting usurped.

On Tuesday, Nadine talked about the troubles Indonesian SaaS startup Lummo, which raised nearly $130 million in venture capital, is facing as the funding environment darkens and it mulls a shutdown or a sale. And as she finds out, there may be an Indian angle to the solution.

On Wednesday, Aayush reported about how Reliance’s bet on subscription-based delivery firm Milkbasket is working out for the conglomerate’s e-commerce ambitions and what the game plan will look like in the near future.

On Friday, Arundhati wrote about a section of mid-to-upper-level management professionals who benefitted from a boom in compensation as a highly liquidated startup ecosystem fought over talent. But it is now facing difficulties getting hired because of their high pay expectations. 

That was all from us this week. Hope you had as much fun reading our offerings as we had reporting and writing them for you!
