Good Morning Ishan,
Here’s what sunrise on Cloud Gate, also known as “The Bean”, in Chicago looks like.
It’s an art installation by Anish Kapoor, a 70-year-old British-Indian sculptor who has one thing in common with billionaire Mukesh Ambani and Zerodha’s co-founder Nikhil Kamath. They’re all featured in at least one of the lists curated by Hurun, the global wealth-ranking compiler, in 2024. While Kapoor was the most successful Indian artist alive, Ambani, not surprisingly, was among the richest Indians, and Kamath became the country’s youngest donor.
Hurun India’s Rich List is so granular that it can answer which city, state, or even zodiac sign registered the highest growth in wealth this year. These lists are for those who fancy tracking India’s affluent class. And why? “Every kid wants a medal and to stand on the podium; it’s a childhood problem. No matter how old you grow, a gold medal always gets you,” a senior banker told The Ken.
With around 17 lists and counting, Hurun is elbowing past giants like Bloomberg and Forbes in tracking the riches of the world. It even has a Global 500 list, similar to Bloomberg’s Billionaire Index, and has claimed the crown as the most trusted choice for bankers and wealth managers from Forbes, which for years was synonymous with the Top 100 list.
But Nuha, the author of today’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/how-hurun-india-and-wealth-managers-turned-rich-lists-into-a-virtuous-cycle/), finds out these lists have more than meets the eye.
