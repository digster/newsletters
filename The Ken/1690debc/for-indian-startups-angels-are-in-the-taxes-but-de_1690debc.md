Never a better strategy than to drain the lake to kill all the diseased fish.
The draining strategies reached tragi-comic levels during the past few months, with dozens of startups receiving tax notices treating their investments as income. And then a few were singled out as examples, with the tax department emptying out their bank accounts surreptitiously to recover their “tax”.
There was a chorus of protests from hundreds of startups, investors and trade bodies against the “angel tax”. So, earlier this week, the government relented. By creating another set of subjective and random hoops that startups would need to jump through.
There were cheers though, from Stockholm Syndrome-affected founders and investors. Press releases were sent out congratulating the government for its sagacity.
But then there's Sumanth. The one writer you can trust to tell it like it is. And point out the Faustian bargain hidden in the details that many are missing. It's our free story for the week as well, so do share it widely.
