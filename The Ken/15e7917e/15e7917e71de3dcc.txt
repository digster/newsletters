Buy with a click, get claims with a snap. Against a stolen phone, a cab accident, flight delays or even a knee injury if you are a runner.

Anyone who’s ever had an insurance claim knows how nightmarish getting paid can get and how delectable this promise sounds. It’s a promise which insurtech is selling. And for now, investors are buying.

If the first wave of disruption in insurance was the arrival of web aggregators and call centres which threw the agent-driven market out of gear (my middle-aged agent went out of work), the second wave is about digital tools. Or as Varun Dua, CEO of digital insurer Acko, would have you believe, it is about products designed for an online market.

With Coverfox, Dua set out to upend the call-centre driven market. But did he? With Acko, a digital only product, he claims to swing a good part of the general insurance distribution to the digital channel. Will he?

Dua believes if right products are made and sold, agents are not needed. Others believe human intervention can’t be wished away. They believe in ‘phygital’—agents with tabs, apps, and what have you.

For the longest while, insurance has been sold not bought; both sides hoping there’d be no occasion to file a claim. The focus was always on customer acquisition, never on customer satisfaction. With digital, this gets inverted, to the extent that one of them calls itself Toffee Cover!

In all this, Acko’s bet appears the boldest. Ruhi’s story is about what Dua (over)sold in Coverfox and what he is planning to sell under Acko.
