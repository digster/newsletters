Here’s one conventional path for wealth to generate more wealth: Start a business. Make a boatload of cash. Invest that money. Make even more.

That sounds simple enough. But like any straightforward plan, the details trip up most people. If you’re a high-net-worth individual or family office, the venture-capital funds that seek your investments aren’t always that effective, as we learn in today’s story.

Devashish Khanna, the chief investment officer for private-equity investments of the Capri Global family office, put it this way: “Why would you want to invest your money and let it be there for 10 years in hopes that you’ll get 10X?”

This is all to say that family offices may be better off investing directly in startups and other opportunities that come along, rather than depending on venture-capital firms.

Those same VC firms counter by playing up their roles as gatekeepers to the next great chance where investors can hit the jackpot with a startup that makes it into the big leagues. Those opportunities can only be accessed by “people who are specialists in that space,” according to Sauce.VC partner Yash Dholakia.

It’s a showdown between family offices and venture capitalists.

Nuha charts this conflict in today’s story. Read it to find out how the investment managers of some of India’s wealthy families are attempting to cut out the intermediary and go their own way.
