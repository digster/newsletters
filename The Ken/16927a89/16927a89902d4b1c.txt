For many Indians, having teeth is like being alive. They’re just there. You just are. Which is why so few of us visit a dentist or purchase life insurance. Based on the research of a leading dental clinic chain's founder, only a negligible percentage of Indians see a dentist every six months for cleaning and polishing (guilty), less than 1% floss (guilty!), and less than 20% of people in urban India brush twice a day (guilty!!).

Which is why Clove Dental’s success is a great story. Since starting in 2011, it has expanded to over 300 clinics across 26 cities, in the process becoming the 8th largest dental chain globally. That they did this in a country where the majority of people go to a dentist as few as an estimated 3 times in their entire lives runs contrary to the normal VC-funded model of food deliveries, hotel rooms or e-commerce.

How did Clove Dental manage this? One reason could be that it did not raise money from VC funds. Much of the $60M it has raised thus far came from family offices. Another is its focus on more premium customers. 

Ruhi goes behind Clove Dental’s strategy in today’s story, in the process also comparing it to its nearest peer, Total Dental (100+ clinics).
