Good Morning Ishan,
We have a tech story for you today, and one of its line struck me the most: “For most investors, technology means software companies.”
Take a moment to reflect on that. How often do you feel the same? Today’s subject challenges that notion.
It’s Sedemac Mechatronics, a controls-tech leader whose technology is used by millions of Indians. Precisely, 44 million two-wheelers—especially, if they’re by the auto giants Hero Motocorp, Mahindra, or TVS—use its smart-ignition tech.
The story is also as much about its co-founder and chairman, Shashikanth Suryanarayanan, who moved from the University of California, Berkeley, to IIT Bombay 20 years ago, on a “whim” to make a difference in the country.
The proof is in the pudding. Over the years, he’s been running the private company like a listed one in some aspects. For instance, Sedemac holds investor calls regularly. After all, it’s one Indian firm competing against the Boschs and Cummins of the world.
But building a “fresh” technology and scaling it isn’t as easy as it sounds. A “compelling enough” idea can take as much as 7–8 years to be largely adopted, says the co-founder.
Suryanarayanan tells Seema why technology is an apt parallel to football, why you cannot produce a Sachin Tendulkar sitting in Barcelona, and why bringing Lionel Messi to India won’t help the country create its own football team.
