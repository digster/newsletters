Last month, Pine Labs, one of India’s largest offline payments processing companies, bought Qwikcilver, India’s largest gifting platform. For a cold $110 million in cash.

And with that, it took the fight right back to the online camp. Where Johnny-come-lately payments apps (to it, at least) like PhonePe, Paytm, Google Pay and Amazon have been slowly but surely muscling in on its offline turf. And if that means ending up stepping on the toes of online payment gateways like BillDesk, PayU, Razorpay and Instamojo, well so be it. Ambition comes with the unicorn territory, of which Pine Labs is reportedly in spitting distance of.

Arundhati weaves together multiple strands in India’s fascinatingly complex Fintech landscape to explain why Pine Labs + Qwikcilver might end up being more than the sum of their parts. Like the merging of online and offline retail into omni-channel. Or the fact that nearly 8% of the retail chain Lifestyle’s revenue comes via gift vouchers. Or how gift cards are a $175 billion market in the US, but only $2 billion in India yet. 

But will this marriage of synergistic convenience be enough for Pine Labs to beat back competitors much more deep-pocketed and ambitious? I guess you’ll have to read the story to find out. https://the-ken.com/story/qwikcilver-pine-labs-sends-itself-a-110-million-online-gift-voucher/ https://the-ken.com/story/qwikcilver-pine-labs-sends-itself-a-110-million-online-gift-voucher/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter

As an aside, it’s interesting to note that Pine Labs has raised nearly 10X the capital that Qwikcilver has ($22M) to get to 2X the revenue. Perhaps that’s why they’re a near-unicorn and the one buying Qwikcilver. While paying cash.
