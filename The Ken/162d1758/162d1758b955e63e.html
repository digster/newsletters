Good Morning Ishan,

Ask Reliance Jio, the “world’s largest startup”, as described by the chairman of its parent company. Others know it as the seemingly unstoppable player that started commercial operations in the world’s most competitive telecom market, India, only in September 2016, but it has since grown to over 160 million subscribers. Supported by over 100,000 employees. And oh, it has also spent over $30 billion in pursuit of its goal.
 
Some “startup", eh?
 
Anyhow, here’s the funny thing - Jio has no CEO. It also has no COO. Instead, it has a phalanx of senior and loyal executives that have worked with Reliance Industries, Jio’s $90 billion parent, over the years. And it has over 22 CEOs who head each of its operating areas called “circles”. And a revolving door of expats and lateral hires who come and go from and to the world's best companies.
 
Unconventional doesn’t begin to describe it. But hey, if it ain’t broke, don’t fix it right? 
 
But R Srinivasan, a professor who teaches strategy at the Indian Institute of Management in Bengaluru (IIM-B), says it’s not uncommon for a large company to exist without a CEO and there are three instances where such a situation arises. 
 
What might these be? Well, you’ll have to read Shashi’s story today to find out: https://the-ken.com/story/reliance-jio-management/
