CO-FOUNDER AND EDITOR

Good Morning Ishan,

Last week, the World Health Organization issued warnings on fake copies of the weight-loss drug from Novo Nordisk, Ozempic. Eli Lilly, which has a similar weight-loss drug Mounjaro, has already sued several entities globally for selling fake meds.

I am sure the three of them haven’t paid attention to India, the eccentric East as it were. While Ozempic’s “starting dose” costs around US$936 in the US for a month’s supply, the same is offered for Rs 8,000–1.3 lakh (US$95–1,555) in India.

As Alifiya, the author of today’s experiential and evocative story, embarked on the Ozempic hunt, she not just found 15 suppliers and pharmacists, but a whole network, through which obese people are sourcing the drug in India—the country with its own dire number of cases.

The popular supply routes are through Europe and Egypt. Alifiya found some choices:

  • Ready stock is unavailable, but the drug will be delivered to your doorstep
  • Supply route of your choice—even straight from Denmark
  • A list of users you can talk to for reference

As obesity has finally found a drug that “works on the willpower”, potential users are suspending their disbelief in the side effects or harm that may come in the wake of unsupervised treatment.

Semaglutide and its class of drugs are being termed miracle drugs also because of their effectiveness in several other indications. The industry believes they’ll break all sales charts this decade.

When Alifiya was inquiring about the drug, a chemist in Juhu, Mumbai, wanted to know if she had a prescription to buy Ozempic. “If not, there will be additional ‘doctor charges’. You won’t get a bill or warranty, but it’s guaranteed original stuff,” they said.

I don’t know which of the 15 suppliers she chose, but Alifiya has ordered Ozempic. As someone who’s been part of many crazy weight-loss waves, it’s only fitting that you learn about what’s going on in India by diving into her Big Story.
