Good Morning Ishan,
 
Would you rather be a big fish in a small pond or a small fish in a big pond?
 
If you are Ola, you might choose to do both. It has wrestled fellow Goliath Uber in the ride-hailing business to a standstill. Both literally and figuratively speaking.
 
And not just in transporting people around, but also in the food delivery business. Ola shuttered foodpanda last year, and Zomato acquired Uber Eats earlier this year. 
 
Locked in Nash equilibrium in the ride-hailing business, Ola is now doubling down on a business it started last year - cloud kitchens. It's easy to see the appeal. Cloud kitchens are cheaper and easier to set up than a full-fledged restaurant and not half as ravaged by Covid-19 pandemic.
 
On top of that, India's cloud kitchen space has 100 duck-sized horses rather than the one horse-sized duck Ola faced in its core business. As is the nature of the cloud; it hides more than it reveals. Draw the curtain, and you'll see names like Box8 and Freshmenu, which have built sizable businesses over the years. 
 
But the undisputed Goliath in the business is Rebel Foods, more popularly known by the brand name Faasos. Rebel has amassed 300-plus kitchens and close to a dozen brands. Ola Food, in comparison, has 40 kitchens and three brands. Small fish, big pond. Not for long, though. 
 
Ola, co-founded by Bhavish Aggarwal, plans to scale to 100 kitchens by the end of this year. That would leapfrog it into third place, and it has no intention of stopping there. Which is pretty amazing, considering that some fish (Swiggy, Eat.fit) in the cloud kitchen-pond are jumping out and even Rebel has taken a hit.
 
Oh, and did I mention that Uber's now-ousted founder, Travis Kalanick, has invested in Rebel Foods through his new cloud kitchen venture?
