"To win one hundred victories in one hundred battles is not the acme of skill. To subdue the enemy without fighting is the acme of skill."

~Sun Tzu, The Art of War

It's been three years since Reliance Jio ambushed the Indian telecom industry, triggering an all-out price war. Since then, the casualties have only piled up on all sides. The small victories along the way have been pyrrhic, with Jio's, Vodafone Idea's and Airtel's manoeuvres only resulting in the near decimation of the industry.

Even in the devastation, Jio seemed to win—by sheer dint of remaining the last one standing, even if on crutches. (Airtel and Vodafone Idea are firing bullets from the critical care unit)

Then, out of the blue, an unthinkable truce was announced last month. The three companies—on the same day and unanimously—said they would raise tariffs.

Only no one believes that this was an actual truce. It is more like buying time to regroup for the next battle. However, Pratap, the author of today's story, argues that peacetime = Jiotime.

His sources tell him that Jio plans to earn as much as half the industry's revenue by 2022, helped by an approximately 40% jump in average revenue per user. And this, Pratap says, is how Jio plans to win the war - "To subdue the enemy without fighting is the acme of skill."

Not quite The Art of War, but Pratap's story breaks down Jio's strategy. It's also a free story, so do share widely, while tariffs permit.
