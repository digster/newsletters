Good Morning Ishan,
I have an uneasy equation with the word ‘risk’. I associate it with a distinct lack of control, much preferring to take on things whose result I can comfortably anticipate. Yet, some of the most unexpectedly rewarding moments I have experienced lay at the end of this unpredictable word.
Applying to The Ken; my first solo trip; scuba diving; trekking in the snow; they turned out to be some of the most memorable experiences of my life despite me not knowing how they would turn out in the beginning.
Our stories this week flitted around this topic too. Whether it be about a ride-sharing firm gambling on its own electric fleet or a struggling drone industry, the stories examined the risks these companies were taking and what would come off it.

Speaking of anxiety, I am reminded of a conversation I had last week with Jon Russell, the man entrusted with helming The Ken Southeast Asia; the edition launches tomorrow. Was he nervous? Were little butterflies throwing a party in his tummy? “Three days and it begins. Exciting,” is what he said.
Since joining The Ken, I have had to dismantle several notions, but altering my perception of risk has perhaps been the most exciting. I’m hoping The Ken Southeast Asia will do the same for you.
