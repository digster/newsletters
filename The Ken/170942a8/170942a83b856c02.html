Good Morning Ishan,
 
I have an uneasy equation with the word ‘risk’. I associate it with a distinct lack of control, much preferring to take on things whose result I can comfortably anticipate. Yet, some of the most unexpectedly rewarding moments I have experienced lay at the end of this unpredictable word. 
 
Applying to The Ken; my first solo trip; scuba diving; trekking in the snow; they turned out to be some of the most memorable experiences of my life despite me not knowing how they would turn out in the beginning. 
 
Our stories this week flitted around this topic too. Whether it be about a ride-sharing firm gambling on its own electric fleet or a struggling drone industry, the stories examined the risks these companies were taking and what would come off it. 
 
Arundhati, on Monday, wrote about Czech Republic-based NBFC, Home Credit, which gives small loans to those in the ‘sub-prime’ and ‘no credit history’ categories. Its cash-rich operations in Russia and China have helped it plough money into India, she says. But would Home Credit continue to be able to take risks as it gradually sheds reliance on promoter capital? 
 
On Tuesday, Olina wrote about two-wheeler ride-sharing firm Bounce and its bet on an all-electric fleet to cut down on fuel and maintenance costs. “It’s like saying a wallet company now wants to lend,” an investor told Olina. The question is, can Bounce pull it off? Read it here.
 
Pratap wrote about India’s faltering drone industry on Wednesday. The government’s 2018 regulations on drone flying rendered almost all drone flying in India illegal, but there is a silver lining. As drone technology moves towards becoming a platform for data collection, maybe there is “still a drone goldmine for India after all”, Pratap argues. It’s our free story for the week, do share widely. 
 
On Thursday, contributors Akshay Datt and Saurabh Garg looked at the nascent podcasting industry in India through the struggles of five-year-old Indus Vox Media (IVM). The company is ahead of all competition for now. But with players like Amazon and Spotify looming and its own internal struggles, it has an uphill battle ahead. 
 
We ended the week with Balan’s story on inadequate public charging stations for electric vehicles, a primary reason for the dismal sales of EVs in India. While companies with fleets of two- and three-wheelers are building swappable batteries, the government is pushing plug-in technology for the car market, hoping to quell range anxiety, Balan writes.
 
Speaking of anxiety, I am reminded of a conversation I had last week with Jon Russell, the man entrusted with helming The Ken Southeast Asia; the edition launches tomorrow. Was he nervous? Were little butterflies throwing a party in his tummy? “Three days and it begins. Exciting,” is what he said. 
 
Since joining The Ken, I have had to dismantle several notions, but altering my perception of risk has perhaps been the most exciting. I’m hoping The Ken Southeast Asia will do the same for you. 
 
Have a good weekend!
