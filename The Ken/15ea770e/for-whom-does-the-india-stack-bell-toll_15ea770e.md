Because unprecedented changes are occurring in India in the name of digitisation and how power, privilege, and accountability are stacked in this seems amorphous.
In tech, platform is power. Think Amazon, Google, Facebook, Apple. Now try to think of one Indian entrepreneur who has built a successful platform. None would come to your mind.
That’s what iSpirt, the think tank meant to support product startups, pivoted to build in India Stack. A set of platforms that would enable governments, businesses, startups, and developers to create, provision or consume “presence-less, paperless and cashless” service delivery.
The traditional IT industry was built because the government did not understand things and stayed away. But with India Stack, the focus shifted. Its APIs slowly seeped into the government’s ‘Digital India’ objectives as officially-sanctioned ways to work with Indian consumers and citizens. Even the likes of Google and WhatsApp, which are using Unified Payments Interface for their payments services in India, have to thank iSpirt for making it all so easy.
In an attempt to “break the jinx for product entrepreneurs”, India Stack became a mandatory gateway for all businesses, and the people running it, its gatekeepers. It’s a complex, conflict-ridden journey which seems to have just begun.
Rohin breaks it all down into a riveting story, which, for want of better words, we could call the story of a new digital license raj. We hope things get better as iSpirt prepares to roll out yet another platform.
