Good Morning Ishan,
Your paycheque has gotten bigger, but that doesn’t mean you’re able to afford a nicer home. In fact, you’re probably finding it more difficult than ever to buy or rent a residence, no matter what your price point is.
Sales for homes priced above Rs 4 crore, or US$466,000, went up nearly 40% in the first nine months of 2024 compared to the same period in 2023. But these units were snapped up by investors, high-level executives, and the like—people who might even be cash purchasers. Most Indians, meanwhile, aren’t as fortunate.
More broadly, property prices in India’s top eight cities shot up by 30% between August 2022 and August 2024, growing more than twice as fast as the two years prior.
To find out more about what’s driving this steep unaffordability, Rounak took a step further and looked at housing prices in 50 cities in India. He found out that real-estate prices in half of them rose faster than the nominal per-capita income in the two years leading up to September 2024.
The average price of a residence in the Delhi and Mumbai metropolitan regions is now above 10X the average annual household income in those areas—an unprecedentedly high level, per a report by the real-estate portal Magicbricks. Prices in other major cities are climbing quickly, too. The result? Anyone who’s attempting to buy a home is getting priced out, and rental units are becoming out of reach.
Rounak covers this situation in detail in today’s [big story](https://the-ken.com/story/behind-the-luxury-drumbeating-a-housing-affordability-crisis-looms/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
