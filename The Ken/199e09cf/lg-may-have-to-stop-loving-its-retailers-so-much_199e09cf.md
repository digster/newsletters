Good Morning Ishan,
Many years ago, while touring an LG plant near Seoul in South Korea, I heard many young employees claim they’d be lifers at the electronics company. During some corporate interactions later, I noticed a reverential loyalty among many senior executives. As a reporter, I found that unusual.
I was reminded of my two-decade-old observation as I read Debanjali’s story today. She writes there’s zero attrition among LG’s key managerial personnel, and the overall employee attrition fell to 7.9% in FY25, from 9.5% a year ago.
There’s a notable stickiness about LG’s business in India. Not just among its employees but its retailers as well. With over 35,000 consumer touchpoints, LG has the largest electronic-distribution network in India, or 1.2X the next leading home-appliance and consumer-electronics player, claims its red herring prospectus.
The company has created a record by becoming the most subscribed IPO in the country. As a brand of the masses, LG has also modelled itself as an OEM that gives primacy to its retailer relationships.
“If LG’s senior management is visiting, say, Vijayawada [in Andhra Pradesh], they may or may not find time for a Reliance Digital, but will definitely visit the local stores and talk to owners,” said an Electronics Mart executive. “They’ll catch up with us over a meal and understand the problems first-hand.”
As LG lists on the stock market today, Debanjali looks at the pressures that may force the company to rejig its popular retailer-first strategy. Let’s [dive in](https://the-ken.com/story/lg-may-have-to-stop-loving-its-retailers-so-much/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
It started with a satellite dish arriving on a bullock cart. Back in 1985, that scene outside Texas Instruments’ new Bengaluru office quietly marked the birth of India’s first multinational R&D centre and opened the doors for hundreds more to follow. Four decades later, those once-humble “back offices” employ millions and look nothing like they used to. Think yoga studios, digital twins of storefronts, and world-class design.
