The average Internet user will claim to love facts, but only so long as they reinforce her own biases and beliefs. Just a few continents away we have the world’s most powerful country buffeted by wave after wave of scale-shattering climate events, led by a President who denies Climate Change.
One would think these are the times newspapers and media publishers would double down on facts. And one would be wrong. Here’s where I’ll teleport you to Ashish’s opening lines from today’s story:
"Zero. That’s the number to keep in mind. Because zero is the number of people in the fact checking department across most mainstream newsrooms in India. Today.
It is a number that isn’t talked about much. It certainly should be.”
Damn right it should be. And Ashish does. He takes you inside various fronts from the “war on fake news” to try and answer the technical, operational and business model challenges related to fact checking. Can BOOM Live, India’s first independent online myth buster, turn fact-checking (and by extension, truth) into a viable business?
