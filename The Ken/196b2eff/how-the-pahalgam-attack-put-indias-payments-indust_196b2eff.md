Good Morning Ishan,
No bank or fintech takes a call from the regulator lightly, even in the best of times. So when the Reserve Bank of India dialled up banks, payment aggregators, and fintechs in the wake of an armed attack, it was a clear signal that things were about to get serious…and intense.
In the days following the 22 April attack in Pahalgam, payments-industry leaders got a clear directive: crack the whip on risky merchants that might be engaging in terror financing or other illicit activities.
The scrutiny from the top is reaching unprecedented levels, executives from ICICI Bank, HDFC Bank, and the State Bank of India told Rounak. For example, where the RBI used to check in every three months, officials are now following up every few days to ensure that not even a single potential bad actor slips through the cracks.
That’s brought a previously low-key class of firms into the limelight. These so-called third-party service providers have been tasked with zealously investigating every aspect of merchant behaviour—from how many five-star Google reviews they have, to when they post on social media—and red-flagging them at the slightest hint of suspicious activity.
But this is no surgical strike. In the quest to weed out the bad apples, unlucky innocents are getting caught in the crossfire.
In a timely story, Rounak lays out how national security concerns have triggered a crackdown in the payments ecosystem, who benefits, and who has become collateral damage.
