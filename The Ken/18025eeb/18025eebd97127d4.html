Good Morning Ishan,
 
Empathy is the absolute first step in design thinking. I've just spent a week at a bootcamp, relearning how to view and grow any business through the design thinking lens, and what truly sticks out to me is this. Technology can go all sorts of places, but it's design—and its focus and empathy for the end-user—that gives it greater purpose.

Businesses all over the world are realising this. And the more they realise it, the more they need a product designer to understand and design for users' needs. It's why fintech unicorn CRED, through its design, makes a deliberate choice to cater only to the top 5% of India's population—credit card users. Or why Swiggy runs different screens for different users in India.

These design decisions are not dictated by some top executive and carried out by a design intern. Nope, unlike even a decade ago, product designers now have a seat at the CEO's table. In some cases, they are founders themselves. Rhode Island School of Design alums Brian Chesky and Joe Gebbia, for instance, founded Airbnb.

In India, there's now serious demand for experienced, product-focused designers. “Companies have expanded their product lines from three to 30. Design teams have doubled, even trebled in size. There’s so much demand that designers can name their price, and companies will change their norms," Vasudha Chandak, an experienced designer-turned-recruiter, tells The Ken.

But here's where demand and supply clash. As more companies vie for product designers, fewer companies can get their hands on them. There's a massive talent gap. For every 1,000 engineers, there's one designer in India, writes Olina in today's story. And an experienced product designer is even harder to get.

How are companies making do? How are specialised design schools like 10kdesigners and Ownpath rushing in to fill this gap? And most importantly, will the demand continue to look the same going forward, especially with how quickly India's startup ecosystem is evolving? Let me not give it all away; you should head over to Olina's well-researched and reported piece for more: https://the-ken.com/story/1-to-1000-the-high-stakes-hunt-for-indias-next-top-product-designers/ (12-minute read)
