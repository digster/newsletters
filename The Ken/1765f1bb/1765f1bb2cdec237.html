When digital cameras took off in the early 2000s, it left photographic film leader Fujifilm reeling. With camera film seeing a precipitous drop in demand as the world switched to the newer, more convenient tech, the company made a tough call—it would shutter most of its film manufacturing plants.
 
Instead, with the world around it changing, Fujifilm resolved to change as well. It took a long hard look at its innovations over the decades and figured out how to apply them to other fields. The vast array of chemical compounds it had developed—over 20,000 of them—soon turned it into a bonafide pharmaceutical, medical devices, and cosmetics giant. Indeed, one of the drugs which rose to prominence as a treatment for Covid symptoms in the early days of the pandemic—favipiravir—was originally developed by Fujifilm.
 
This ability to embrace disruption can often be the difference between success and failure. Fujifilm's rival Kodak, for instance, tried to stay the course in the face of disruption. Today, it is a shell of its former self.
 
Almost two decades removed from Fujifilm's bold transformation, India's government-owned thermal power giant NTPC is looking to do something similar. It understands that coal-based power is the past, and it wants to be part of India's renewables future. It will not open another coal-powered power plant; instead, it's seeking to grow its renewables portfolio 30X by 2032.
 
Unlike with Fujifilm's transformation, the future of NTPC is not so much a coin flip but a slam dunk. As Seetharaman writes in today's story, NTPC is set to bulldoze its way into the solar sector, which has traditionally been dominated by private companies.
 
It has experience in renewables having tendered clean energy projects on behalf of the government over the last decade. It also has deep pockets, access to cheaper finance than its private rivals like Adani Green and ReNew, and decades of experience cutting through bureaucratic red tape.
 
Even as it overturns the space's established pecking order, it could change things in the sector for the better. How? You'll have to read the story for that: https://the-ken.com/story/adani-renew-watch-out-ntpcs-set-to-eclipse-indias-solar-sector/ (9-minute read)
