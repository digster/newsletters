Good Morning Ishan,
My earliest and fondest memories of Godrej are of the wind blowing through my hair and the smell of soap wafting through the air. The fragrance was unmistakable, as the Mumbai local ‘BEST’ bus 373 zipped past the Godrej factory in the suburb of Vikhroli on the Eastern Express Highway—en route the trips that Appa, Amma, my brother, and I made from Chembur to Mulund and back. The soap being churned out behind those imposing gates was Cinthol, among the most enduring Godrej products and brands over the decades.
The company that made the soap, I learnt years later, was Godrej Consumer Products.
It’s perhaps best known for its soaps and household insecticides like Good Knight and Hit. Yes, a strange combo. But this fifth-biggest standalone maker of fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) in India makes and sells a lot more. That makes it a weathervane for the financial health of Indian households and, by extension, the economy, as Seetharaman points out in today’s fascinating interview with Sudhir Sitapati, chief executive of Godrej Consumer Products.
Did you know, for instance, that the company’s offerings now range from plug-in air fresheners to dog food? The choice is deliberate, as the market veteran who took charge four years back deals with a radically changed marketplace—shaped by forces as varied as smart TV and quick commerce. Selling to the top 20% of India during a consumption slump is both an opportunity and a challenge. It’s not just what it sells but also how it sells that’s changing. And given the turnaround that Sitapati has effected at Godrej Consumer, there’s a lot to pick up from this rich Q&A.
My favourite? Why did Godrej sit out of the “buy D2C brands” frenzy not-so-long ago? The answer reveals a deep understanding of the market and of the numbers behind it.
