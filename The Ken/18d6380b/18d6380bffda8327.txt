Business leaders are rushing to employ AI to unlock value, yet without the authentic touch of human emotion, even profound intelligence yields tepid results

Good Afternoon Ishan,

It’s a world where every business and professional is using artificial intelligence (AI) in one way or another. Machine learning has made insights sharper, automation has streamlined processes, and people are utilising generative AI (GenAI) for everything—from crafting CVs to content marketing. It may seem like an advantage now but soon it will be a level playing field. 

Here’s the obvious question: how can business leaders truly stand out in this AI-driven crowd?

Early signs suggest that the key lies in tapping into innate humane qualities—heartfelt emotion, genuine purpose, and a deep cultural understanding of the real world. For instance, no AI knows what it means to sip filter coffee on a Sunday morning. No AI knows what makes an investor trust an entrepreneur or the delight that customers feel when they experience sincere service. After all, the intelligence is artificial.

In his book I, Human: AI, Automation, and the Quest to Reclaim What Makes Us Unique, Tomas Chamorro-Premuzic, a professor of business psychology at University College London, co-founder of Deeper Signals, and an associate at Harvard’s Entrepreneurial Finance Lab, argues that humans instinctively respond to genuine emotions. According to him, as we are inherently wired to care about others’ feelings, tapping into our unique experiences has to be a vital element in the strategy to distinguish ourselves in the AI age.

That’s why the key to standing out lies in emotion-based storytelling.
