Good Morning Ishan,
 
I started crafting this email on Thursday evening. The initial plan was to hook you in with an interesting anecdote, narrate five snappy summaries of the stories The Ken published this week, and sign off after wishing you a great weekend. My first task was to find a perfect attention-grabbing angle.

Like any up-and-coming boomer, I searched for what’s unique about 7 May. And to my surprise, there’s a pattern.

For example, on 7 May 2000—also a Sunday—Vladimir Putin was inaugurated as the President of Russia for the first time.

Next, have you heard of Jimmy Donaldson? More popularly known as MrBeast, he is the world’s most subscribed Youtuber. He turns 25 today.

MrBeast shares his birthday with Rabindranath Tagore, the first Indian Nobel laureate.  Tagore was born in 1861.

Now, fast forward to 1946. On 7 May that year, two physicists started a radio-repair shop in a bombed-out department store in Japan. The company is now called Sony—one of the world’s largest electronics manufacturers.

Conclusion? 7 May has a way of bringing about major events and significant people that shape our world. All of these people not only broke the pattern but also accomplished—for the lack of a better phrase—the unexpected. So what’s stopping us?
