This email is my second draft. My first started with an atrociously long anecdote about going to Cafe Coffee Day as a school senior and downing fairly bitter coffee because it made us cool. Ugh. Anyhow, the main takeaway in that email monolith was this: The coffee wasn't all that great. And that CCD doesn't even exist anymore.
Why is this important? Well, because Shashidhar's story today looks at the less flattering details behind CCD's growth and profits. The numbers look good, but there's more to it than meets the eye.
CCD has proudly announced that it opened 135 new cafes in the financial year ending March 2018. However, it has also shuttered 95. And if you're just about starting to squint at your screens/furrow your brows, there's another problem the company isn't talking much about—employee anguish.
From impossible sales targets to employees using their credits cards to settle amounts to unpaid dues, there are many questions raised by employees to the homegrown coffee giant in this story. One CCD employee took his own life and left a note expressing his disappointment with the company.
And then there's this. "Managers that The Ken spoke to say that the bigger problem at CCD is that they introduce items which have no takers, and the management puts targets on these items." What kind of items? Well, burgers, biryani, wraps and rolls, along with milkshakes. (Biryani and coffee?!)
Happy Friday. I hope you get some good coffee to start your day. As you can see, I sure need some.
