Good Morning Ishan,
 
I believe you’d agree that keeping oneself motivated to follow a fitness routine at home is one of the hardest things to do. However, I have found that the lure of new fitness gear always helps me get back on the mat.  
 
This thought led me to my first mistake of the week—a sports and fitness store. The goal was to buy a few things to make my daily workouts more comfortable. Browsing through the store, I kept getting swayed by offers and discounts and, as you might have guessed, racked up a five-digit bill. Though I knew these things would make my life easier, the cost made me curb my enthusiasm.
 
It costs a tidy sum to get what you want. But sometimes, you have to rationalise your expectations and look for alternatives. That’s exactly what small startups are doing to survive the funding winter. As it’s becoming difficult for small entrepreneurs to sustain their businesses, acqui-hiring is picking up pace in India. On Friday, Aayush and Vanita explored what makes an acqui-hire click.
 
Similarly, fintech startups are now approaching the Big Four auditing firms—Deloitte, EY, PwC, and KPMG—to ensure regulatory compliance. Shashwat, on Monday, told the tale of how a necessity for the former has become a high-margin business for the latter.
 
On Tuesday, Maitri and Anushka wrote about the obstacles being faced by the Ayushman Bharat Digital Mission—the government's ~$200 million initiative to create a unified health system. State-level tenders are running into troubles, even as the National Health Authority sees a leadership churn. It’s our free read of this week, so do check it out. 
 
Unified Payments Interface (UPI), the instant real-time payments system, became popular because it made digital payments easy without charging any fee. But is it really free? Our contributor Sashwata answered the question on Wednesday here.
 
On Thursday, Soumyajit reported how a bootstrapped online gaming company Gameskraft has managed to avoid reaching for the venture capital’s penny by placing all its bets on rummy, a popular Indian card game. However, this bet is turning risky with customers’ changing preferences and the issues that plague the real-money gaming (RMG) sector.
 
That's all from us this week. Hope you and your wallet are safe from flashy discounts. See you on Monday!
