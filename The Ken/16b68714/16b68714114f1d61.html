Strange are the ways of fintech. Till just a few years ago, leading e-commerce and payments startups, punch-drunk on the Kool Aid of disruption, were writing obituaries for credit cards and bank accounts. Now, they’re busy partnering with banks to launch credit cards!
 
And they’re bringing their scale game in tow. Digital payments giant Paytm is partnering Citi; ride-sharing leader Ola is teaming with SBI Cards; and Amazon has tied up with ICICI Bank. Between just these three, they want to add around 23 million new credit cards over the next 2-3 years.
 
To put that 23 million into perspective—India’s entire credit card base is less than 48 million after nearly 3 decades of the first one being issued. While the number of debit card holders is over 800 million. 
 
A logical question would be why digital unicorns are launching credit cards when they should be spending all their energy convincing existing credit card users to dump their plastic and go all digital. An equally logical question is also why are banks, who have been at the receiving end of the digital payments disruption, partnering their former bêtes noires?
 
Because each side has something the other covets. Read Arundhati’s story to understand what that might be, and who holds the upper hand in this new marriage of convenience: https://the-ken.com/story/credit-cards-are-the-new-black-for-payments-companies/ (12-minute read)
