Over the past two decades, I have had a spotty record with invite-only apps and platforms. Back in 2004, when Gmail—the now ubiquitous email service—was launched, I chanced upon an opportunity to sign up with it when a colleague shared with me a spare invitation. While that Gmail ID is still my primary one, a range of other apps ranging from Pinterest to Clubhouse have all held my attention, but only briefly.
Companies seeking to generate buzz around their products, making them stand out in a crowded market, have all used the invite-only approach. But can a strategy that has fructified in the vibrant world of social media stick out in the more straight-jacketed banking industry?
UnionDigital—one of the six digital banks licensed to operate in the Philippines in 2021—thinks it can. It is using this 'exclusive' approach to drive its profitability, drawing in thousands of customers and boosting its loans and deposits business.
It reportedly had the biggest loan portfolio in the Philippine digital-banking industry at the end of 2022. In the first five months of UnionDigital’s operations, it already had 1.73 million customers and deposits of 9 billion pesos (US$160 million)—outperforming its peers.
And it has done so without even spending a dime on promotions, unlike its rivals Maya and GoTyme. Instead, to win customers, UnionDigital has found its niche and signed up early adopters.
For now, an invite-only model may have helped the platform create early buzz, but can UnionDigital scale up and fit its business model into the Philippines’ push for financial inclusion, a key motivation behind the digital-banking push?
