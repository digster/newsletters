Think back to the last few times you dialled out to speak with a customer service representative. There’s a good chance that the person on the other end was based in the Philippines.
But were they in Manila, as they would have been a few years ago? It’s harder to be certain about that part.
As always, Manila is a city of change. Walk through some neighbourhoods, and you’ll notice significant transformations: offices emptied and street-level stores and kiosks gone.
Business process outsourcing (BPO) operators have been gradually relocating beyond the capital region since 2020. This, in turn, is wiping out the smaller businesses where their staff spend money. Before the pandemic, around 75% of the Philippines’ IT professionals worked in Metro Manila. Now, it’s down to roughly 50%.
But this isn’t a dire situation—it’s a tale of evolution.
As Isa describes in her story today, BPO companies are relocating because of a basket of reasons. One main motivator is that their staff have stronger spending power outside of Manila, and costs for those operators are lower in other major cities. It’s an equitable situation for everyone involved.
Meanwhile, a new breed of companies—knowledge process outsourcing (KPO) services like animators, game developers, and telehealth providers—is slowly filling in the physical void left behind by BPOs that have departed from Manila.
