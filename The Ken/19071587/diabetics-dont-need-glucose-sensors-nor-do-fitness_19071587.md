Good Morning Ishan,
If you, like me, are surrounded by people who take their health very very seriously, continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) devices would have made their way into your conversations.
These devices monitor body glucose (sugar) levels in real-time. Naturally, the clear candidates for CGM devices are the 101 million diabetics and 136 million pre-diabetic population in India.
But healthtech startups like Ultrahuman, Sugar.fit, and Fitterfly breathed a new life into CGM devices. These well-funded startups popularised the idea of attaching these coin-sized sensors to one’s upper arm even among non-diabetic fitness buffs.
Even though these different Indian healthtechs tried to leverage the hype and create a business around this, they all sold CGM sensors made by Abbott, one of the largest medtech companies in the world, with a revenue of nearly US$17 billion and the dominant CGM seller in India.
If one were to use these sensors for an entire year, it would cost them a neat Rs 1.4 lakh (US$1,678).
In today’s insightful story, Shivani looks at who really gets to make the most of being those hooked to a CGM. Is it users? Is it the heathtechs? Is it Abbott?
