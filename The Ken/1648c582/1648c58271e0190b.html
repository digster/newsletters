Rarely do we, at The Ken, rush to do a story predicated on a fundraise. We sniff around but often let the funding news pass. In some cases though, we dig our heels in. Like we did with PolicyBazaar, India’s largest insurance aggregator, which raised $200 million from SoftBank late last month.
 
With its latest funding round, the company has raised a total of Rs 2370 (~$364 million) crore till date. How much do you reckon it has spent in the last 10 years in total?
 
Just 16%.
 
Why the hell then did it raise so much money? Especially when it grew 100% last year, is poised to grow 75-80% this year, and can generate 35% EBIDTA margins at 40% growth rates.
 
“We didn’t really have a need for the $200 million. Or a very clear use of those funds. In fact, our existing investors were willing to invest up to $500 million…,” says one of the co-founders.
 
PolicyBazaar’s moves thus far fly in the face of everything we understand about insurance, venture funding and unicorns.
 
Now, it wants to build an Uber of health insurance. (Yeah, we’ve heard that before, many times. Yesterday, Chelsea Clinton and Valley’s KPCB threw their weight behind Nurx, which wants to be the Uber of birth control.) PolicyBazaar wants to hire 1000 in-house doctors and get into OPD health insurance, a segment insurers in the past have stayed away from because of rampant fraud.
 
When the world is moving to small, impulse-driven insurance products like flight delay insurance or e-commerce returns insurance, the company wants to go back to selling the classic idea of insurance – as protection against death, disease and disability.
 
Founder Yashish Dahiya has the blueprint, down to the number of inquiries coming in every day. He wants 100 million subscribers.
 
With SoftBank behind it and its portfolio companies alongside it, what course PolicyBazaar charts for itself is a story we’ll closely follow.
