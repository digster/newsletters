For gamers, there’s a royale for every taste—Fortnite for those who like the cartoonish graphics, and Apex Legends for ones who like tactical squad play, for instance. For folks who play on their phones, the death match is between PUBG Mobile and Free Fire. When these two titles were banned in India last year, the studios—Krafton and Garena, respectively—behind them took serious hits.

Battlegrounds Mobile India (BGMI), the local version of PUBG Mobile, returned online in the country earlier this year, and Free Fire India is on its way. It’s a renewal for the title at a time when interest in the Free Fire franchise is dipping worldwide.

If Garena pulls off a successful launch for its localised game in India, then maybe it can reinvigorate its presence in other parts of the world too, or so the logic goes. That’s all to say that Garena has a lot riding on the smooth launch of Free Fire India.

There’s no shortage of big names attached to the title ahead of the game’s rollout—cricketer MS Dhoni, footballer Sunil Chhetri, and badminton champ Saina Nehwal. Garena has managed to drum up more than 74 million pre-registrations.

Things may sound peachy, but they aren’t. A years-long rivalry between the battle royale titles is being reignited, and players seem to enjoy BGMI well enough.
