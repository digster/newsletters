Good Morning Ishan,

Ah, February. That time of year when we will finally stop writing "2018" in our documents. When New Year's resolutions descend from their ivory towers to settle for rundown bar stools and shots of reality. That bridge between the harshness of winter and the brutality of summer (unless you're in Mumbai, like me, and scoff at the prospect of spring). A reprieve between two months that foist 31 days on us.

It's an eventful February here already, what with the budget and the coverage devoted to it demanding our attention on its very first day. Day two is eventful for me—and forgive me, really, for bringing this into the fray—because it's the first time I'm writing to you. It's eventful, not because I'm good at writing emails. God no. Quite the opposite.

Four days ago, at precisely 4:43 p.m., I addressed an email to someone named Nitin. The mail began thus: "Dear Nothing". Of course it was autocorrect. I'd hit the send button in haste, not reading through because I wanted a few seconds to look up and get some air in that crowded train. I've hit "Reply All" when I shouldn't have, not BCC'd when I should've, sent the right mail to the wrong people, sent the wrong mail to the right people. You get the gist.
