Good Morning Ishan,
 

Ah, February. That time of year when we will finally stop writing "2018" in our documents. When New Year's resolutions descend from their ivory towers to settle for rundown bar stools and shots of reality. That bridge between the harshness of winter and the brutality of summer (unless you're in Mumbai, like me, and scoff at the prospect of spring). A reprieve between two months that foist 31 days on us.
 
It's an eventful February here already, what with the budget and the coverage devoted to it demanding our attention on its very first day. Day two is eventful for me—and forgive me, really, for bringing this into the fray—because it's the first time I'm writing to you. It's eventful, not because I'm good at writing emails. God no. Quite the opposite.
 
Four days ago, at precisely 4:43 p.m., I addressed an email to someone named Nitin. The mail began thus: "Dear Nothing". Of course it was autocorrect. I'd hit the send button in haste, not reading through because I wanted a few seconds to look up and get some air in that crowded train. I've hit "Reply All" when I shouldn't have, not BCC'd when I should've, sent the right mail to the wrong people, sent the wrong mail to the right people. You get the gist.
 
I better hurry before my luck takes a time-out again. So without further ado, here's a lowdown on the great week we had: 
 
Ruhi wrote about the Danone-Drums Food deal on Monday. Drums Food wants to go from selling 100,000 to 1 million cups of Greek yoghurt daily, with the blessings of Danone Manifesto Ventures. Is this achievable? Read her story if you haven't already.
 
On the evening of 14 January, Salman almost fell off his bike thanks to an unattended Bounce scooter. That incident became the catalyst for his hawk-eyed analysis of the new blue-eyed boys in bicycle-sharing startups: e-scooters.
 
Come Wednesday, Sidhartha reminded us that tax terrorism isn't a thing of the past. Dangling carrots like "compounding fees" and prosecution targets are turning taxmen into Bogeymen of another kind.
 
Contributor Sajjad Anwar wrote the free story of the week, about opportunities gained and lost for the autonomous vehicle industry in India. Can driverless cars find pride of place in our country? Read and find out.
 
Arundhati ushered in Friday with her takedown of the all-seeing eye that's CIBIL Watch. If you've ever applied for a loan and been bombarded with unwelcome messages and emails from lenders of all kinds, you'd want to know how they know.
 
Does originality involve pushing the boundaries of an existing system, or kicking them down altogether? Saurabh Mukherjea and Anupam Gupta explore this and other hues of creative thought in the latest instalment of the Simplicity Paradigm. 
 
And last Sunday, I wrote about the people who decide the taste and texture of packaged foods. If you want to know the toil flavourists and flavour houses go through to perfect that mouthful of junk, go here.
 
Have a great February.
