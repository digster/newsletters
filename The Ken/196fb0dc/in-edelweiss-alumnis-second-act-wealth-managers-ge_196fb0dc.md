Good Morning Ishan,
One of the Warren Buffett rules of wealth creation is to pay attention to the people you have in your circle.
You are going to move in the direction of the people you associate with, says the sage of Omaha.
At the four-year-old wealth-management startup Neo Group, which manages wealth for the creamiest 0.01% of the population, the leadership is aggressively embodying this. Attract the top-notch wealth creators, as fast as possible, and give them boatloads of money. Neo is offering as much as 60% of the advisory fee as the take-home money, nearly double the share most other wealth-management firms offer.
Backed by investors like Peak XV and Japanese finserv MUFG, Neo’s approach is unusual but not unfamiliar in the VC-backed universe of startups. If money can buy speed and talent, why shy away. Some wealth-management firms are even luring away retail bankers to keep up with the industry pace.
Archishma takes a closer look at the second act of many ex-Edelweiss wealth managers at Neo, which has stolen a march on peers to boast nearly Rs 40,000 crore in assets under management.
