Good Morning Ishan,

 

 

It's 40 degrees out. Usually, I'd be cursing under my breath. Delhi has that effect; it explains the colourful Hindi we're so famous for. But today is different. I find myself appreciating the little things in this big city that make it stand out.
 
You see, I spent the last week working out of Mumbai, and in the short span of seven days, I found myself dreading the rush hour (or "death hour" as my colleagues prefer to call it), reaching out for an umbrella during sudden rains and getting drenched anyway, sweating, sweating all the time... Mumbai is a great city, don't get me wrong, but I suppose sometimes one needs to step out of their comfort zone to truly appreciate things. Interestingly, it is something we do often do as journalists here at The Ken.
 

All of us come from backgrounds often very diverse from the beats we find ourselves covering here, providing a unique, fresh lens to our stories. And even within those beats, the stories we do can be quite a departure from the journalism we've come to know. And fortunately for us, much like my Mumbai trip, it's a learning experience.

 

Like every Saturday, this email is to take you through the stories we've done, but I thought I'd take a moment for a little anecdote. Now, let's get down to the stories, shall we?

 

On Monday, Vivek made his debut addressing the recent trend of auditors of listed firms resigning abruptly. What's behind the string of exits? Interestingly, Vivek, in keeping with my out-of-comfort-zone theme, was originally a CA but stepped out of his comfort zone to pursue his passion for journalism. And I'd say he hit the nail on his first story.

 

After Vivek's piece, I wrote about Star India's shining star, the Indian Premier League (IPL). Did you know Star India made Rs 3,000 crore from the eleventh edition of IPL? The way things are going, the company may very well end up recovering its Rs 16,000+ crore investment in the tournament. Read to know how.

 

Wednesday saw this week’s FREE story from Shashidhar about Facebook’s second stab at the internet in India—Express WiFi. The social networking giant is aggregating hotspots. Or maybe advertisers.

 

On Thursday, Sumanth told us the story of SoftBank and how the company may have lost a war despite making a whopping $1.5 billion in profit from exiting Flipkart. That's right, $1.5 billion in profit is nothing compared to the bigger picture SoftBank may have inadvertently foregone. 


We took the day off on Friday to celebrate Eid and to pause and introspect. I hope you did too.
