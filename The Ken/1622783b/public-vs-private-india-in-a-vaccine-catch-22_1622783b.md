Good Morning Ishan,
Remember Robert Frost's super-deep-for-schoolchildren poem that we were taught in, well, schools? You got it; it's 'The Road Not Taken'. The last line often comes back to me at some particularly unusual junctures in life:
Two roads diverged in a wood, and I—
I took the one less travelled by,
And that has made all the difference.
The lack of closure in this poem is immense. After reading Ruhi's story today, for a moment there I wondered what the 'road not taken' for the Indian health ministry would be. In the near future.
Wait, let me back up a little. So, the ministry has a potential budget of $1.44 billion for its national vaccination programme. This means it can afford just about any vaccine producer, be it the private players or public sector undertakings (PSUs).
There are three PSUs in particular that the ministry had previously encouraged. After working towards it for a decade, these three are now global standards-compliant. Despite that, it's important to note what these private producers are capable of. For instance, two of these players (named in the story) together are capable of supplying 500 million doses of the TT (tetanus) vaccine. 500 million means they could provide for the whole world. That's right.
So, now that the government can afford these best-of-the-best private players, how practical would it be to support the PSUs?
To put this conundrum in context, India isn't alone in facing the uncertainty in vaccine manufacturing.
