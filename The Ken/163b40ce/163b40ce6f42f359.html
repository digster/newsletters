Good Morning Ishan,
 
A few weeks ago, when my colleague Arundhati pitched today's story in the editorial meeting, she was met with what was stony silence. She had just informed us that a bank was setting up an internal team to build digital fintech products. Not license. Build. Nobody was more skeptical than Rohin. I didn't blame him. Just the previous day, I had seen him trying to use netbanking on a prominent bank's website to transfer some money to Ashish. 
 
My God. What an adventure. You could have made a cartoon about it. Tintin and the Addition of the Payee. 
 
11:02 AM : Rohin lands on bank website. Website asks for login ID. He tries email address. Nope. Account number. Nope. 
 
11:36 AM : Rohin finally remembers that login ID is a random 15 digit number which was posted to his permanent address in Kerala. 
 
11:44 AM : Login ID located. Website asks for password. Luckily, Rohin has highly secure, randomly generated password saved in his password manager. 
 
11:46 AM : Rohin finds out that highly secure password has to be entered manually because website has disabled copy-paste. Login is successful. Finally.
 
11:54 AM : Logged out because Rohin clicked a link on the website twice. 
 
11:55 AM : Logs back in. Gets logged out because Rohin clicked the back button. 
 
11:56 AM : Logs back in. Gets logged out because Rohin breathed too hard on the screen. 
 
14:53 PM : Rohin spends lunch break locating the online transfer option. Website asks for secret code printed on a grid on his debit card. 
 
14:58 PM : Googles to check if driving to bank, staging a hold-up, robbing it and handing cash over to Ashish is faster than netbanking. 
 
15:32 PM : Payee finally added. Website informs that transfer can be made in 24-48 hours. Logged out.
 
15:33 PM : Opens PhonePe app. Enters Ashish's ID. Transfers amount. Ashish confirms receipt. 
 
Now, imagine a bank trying to build a fintech product. With a dedicated team. That's what ICICI bank wants to do. Arundhati spent some time trying to figure out what their plans were, what the product was, and what the team was going to build. They even have eight commandments. Will they succeed? It's too early to tell, but you can read the story here and decide for yourself: https://the-ken.com/story/icici-bank-wants-to-be-a-fintech/
