Good Morning Ishan,
In a Newtonian turn of events, the Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) advice to banks to tighten personal loan disbursements is having an equal and opposite reaction. Illegal moneylenders have seen a sharp rise in their businesses.
Growth in personal loans has been on a tear over the last year. In August, it was nearly 31% year-on-year compared to about 19% a year ago, according to the RBI. But with the banks tightening personal loan disbursements, that demand for credit is being catered to by moneylenders.
You would think customers of banks and moneylenders are two different sets of borrowers.
But when Rounak met Dadda—a moniker given to him by customers, he saw that’s not the case. Dadda runs a tiny establishment in a congested alley of Old Delhi’s GB Road. It maintains the facade of a travel agency while discreetly serving a quick fix for secured and unsecured loans worth Rs 5,000 (US$60) to Rs 1 lakh (US$1,200).
Repeat customers are even offered special interest rates that aren’t very different from what you get at non-banks.
This season, the 20-year-old shop got a new air-conditioner, thanks to the spike in borrowers. Three in 10 of Dadda’s borrowers are existing bank customers. And the ratio is only going up.
In a richly reported story, Rounak looks at what makes moneylenders tick even in the face of competition from illegal loan apps and how the banks’ actions have pushed people more into the den of moneylenders than enable wider credit access.
