Good Morning Ishan,
 
There are two stories about Shoppers Stop, one of India’s oldest and best-known apparel retailers.

The first story is about how it has turned itself around. Shoppers Stop had been facing declining growth for several years before Covid. Then came Venu Nair, former CEO of Tata Group’s Westside, who took over the reins of the retailer in November 2020. 

Since then, the company has gone on a different path. Its stock price has grown almost 4X in value—way more than its peers. Its same-store sales (a key retail metric) grew by over 16% in the three months ended December 2022 as compared to the previous year. It has rapidly expanded and created more stores since. And its contribution from its private labels is the highest it has ever been. 

The second story is about whether all this growth is sustainable.

Subrata Siddhanta, a former colleague of Nair and ex-business head of Trent’s fashion brand Zudio, thinks it won’t last, and that this is “temporary”. He attributes Shoppers Stop’s success to the fall of Future Retail, which owned Central, and had to shut its outlets when it defaulted on lease payments in 2022. Reliance Industries, which now owns Future Retail’s assets, is working to revive the stores. And there’s an argument to be made: Shoppers Stop’s gains may evaporate when that happens. 

But there’s a third story. 

It’s not about what Shoppers Stop did or how long it will last. 

It’s about how Shoppers Stop unlocked this growth. 

And if you ask me, that’s the most fascinating story of all. And that’s because Shoppers Stop didn’t get this growth by doing more—instead, it got it by doing less.  

Luckily, it’s also the story that my colleague Seetharaman writes about today.
