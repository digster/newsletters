Good Morning Ishan,
Uniqueness is attractive, isn’t it? But if you’re Onecard, a fintech trying to stand out in a jam-packed segment, it can be a mixed blessing.
You must have seen one of your favourite Youtubers advertising Onecard’s credit card. Its USP: a metal alternative to the usual plastic ones. But that’s just the tip of the fintech’s distinct business model. The one that’s helped it brave a wrecking regulatory cyclone conjured by RBI.
Unlike most co-branded alliances where non-bank partners focus on marketing, Onecard also provides banks with the entire technology needed to manage credit cards. In return, it gets a relatively larger fraction of revenue.
Gaurav, the author of today’s story, writes that Onecard took a boring route when sky-high valuations were the order of the day and rivals like Slice saw swift growth. Despite that, Onecard became a unicorn last year.
Now, the fintech is said to be striving to become a $10 billion company and emerge as one of the top credit-card issuers in India.
If only executions were as rosy as the plans. Onecard’s current partnerships with banks can only take it so far. And do lenders need it for managing their credit-card systems or even just for distribution?
Gaurav argues the fintech has much else to tap into—cities, credit-on-UPI, and more.
