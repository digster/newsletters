Good Morning Ishan,
For a country awash in all manner of mutual funds, India should be very familiar with investing based on statistical models or algorithms rather than an individual’s judgment.
Such funds are commonplace in the US. In India, however, they are still a sideshow. They account for 0.2% of the $846 billion in mutual-fund assets (excluding Quant Mutual Fund). Several fund houses have “quant” schemes, but few except Quant Mutual Fund have made a go of it.
“There are certain algorithms we’d love to use but can’t because that data does not exist in India in a comprehensive way,” said Deepak Shenoy, founder of Capitalmind, which runs funds for the rich. For instance, while the US publishes data on macro-indicators like retail sales, home sales, and employment figures, in India this is not readily available, he added.
Assessing the effectiveness of a trading strategy against historical data is a fool’s errand when you have to make do with numbers of the past couple of decades.
Interestingly, Shenoy has still chosen the quant route for his first mutual fund. Archishma unpacks the contrarian bet in [her story](https://the-ken.com/story/quant-based-funds-thrive-in-the-us-so-why-do-they-have-few-takers-in-india/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
