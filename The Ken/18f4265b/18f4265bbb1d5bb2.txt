We spent six months brainstorming, designing, prototyping, building, and testing our brand-new mobile and iPad apps. It was worth it

Good Afternoon Ishan,

I have an exciting announcement to make. 

When The Ken launched its first-ever mobile app in 2017, it was also incidentally one of my first major projects ever. I’d just joined the Engineering team a few months ago as a nervous rookie and had a minor part to play. But I witnessed first-hand the high bar that was set and achieved for everything. 

And then again in 2020, when we rolled out another major release. I was no longer a newbie. My part was more important. We had a far more ambitious set of objectives. But the bar? That remained unchanged; not lowering an inch. 

And it never will. Because right from conception, The Ken’s apps have been a critical and exclusive part of our offering to our paying subscribers. 

Minor tweaks aside, though, our last major app release was four years ago, and so many things are different now. We’re no longer a one-story-a-day publication—we do Newsletters, Podcasts, Visual Stories, Narrative Workshops. We don’t even look the same anymore, with a complete overhaul of our website design executed just a few months ago. 

It’s time now to do the same with our app. And trust me, the changes we’re bringing in are as radical. 

For the first time ever, we have an exclusively designed app for iPad. 

We’ve introduced in-app purchases, meaning you can upgrade and renew without ever leaving the app. 

And that is just the tip of the iceberg of what’s going live today. For example, we’ve even made it possible for our free users to try out the app—you can now sign in, browse through our offerings, and consume anything not behind a paywall. You can also purchase a subscription right there.
