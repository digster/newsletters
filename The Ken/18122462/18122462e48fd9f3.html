Good Morning Ishan,
 
The stock market is choppy enough to make you turn to fixed deposits, and venture capitalists are no longer handing out big cheques like they are candy. 
 
So, Indian tech companies have been forced to closely watch something they could afford to ignore all this while: the bottom line. 
 
For some of them, the problem is compounded by a crippling shortage of delivery personnel. 
 
Food-delivery major Zomato is making do with 25-35% fewer delivery staff than usual. As a result, the company has doubled its payouts to Rs 60-75 (US$0.8-1) per order. And so has quick-commerce startup Zepto. 
 
The pandemic party is definitely over for these companies. As the economy rebounds, gig workers have more options to choose from. Consequently, the relationship between the two parties is no longer the same.
 
Zomato, for instance, now allows its delivery personnel to book slots of one to three hours with a defined payout range. This not only allows its riders to decide when they want to work, but it also gives the company visibility on their availability. 
 
The balance of power has certainly shifted in favour of gig workers, even if it’s temporary. With his extensive reporting, Aayush captures this transition in today’s story: https://the-ken.com/story/how-zomato-swiggy-and-co-can-refill-their-delivery-rider-tank/ (13-minute read)
