Hello Ishan,

 

Therapy isn’t the unicorn it once used to be. And as it gets more real, regular, and normal in our lives, companies all over are adopting it as a part of their workplace perks. But it’s not simply benevolence on the part of our patrons. It’s a weapon they’re using for better product development. 

 

In this episode of Cost to Company Sneha retraces Piyush Shah’s journey of losing and then finding himself. Piyush is the co-founder of Inmobi, India’s first unicorn, and today he talks about how therapy helped “unlock” his and InMobi’s ambitions. And with this, we unravel a phenomenon that is taking over workplaces. Senior counsellors and the founder of a mental wellness start-up join us to uncover how businesses are using therapy to accelerate performance.

 

In this episode, we see:

 
  1. How Piyush hit the low. The early signs, and the stark symptoms.
  2. How he chose to take control and pull himself out of it.
  3. How start-ups are prioritising mental health not solely for the love of their people but out of love for their businesses.
  4. How therapists are funnelling feedback so businesses can become better
  5. And finally how Piyush came out on the other side, with a renewed sense of calm in one hand, and a unicorn in the other.
