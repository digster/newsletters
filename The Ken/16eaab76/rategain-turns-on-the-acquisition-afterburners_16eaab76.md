The key to winning a race—from a 100-meter sprint to a marathon—is not just speed and stamina, but also timing. An athlete has to know exactly when to accelerate. Too soon and they could run out of gas before the finish; too late and they can kiss gold goodbye.
In the startup world, one company that has turned on the afterburners is RateGain, a SaaS (software-as-a-service) company whose tentacles spread across the travel and hospitality sector.
Frustrated with seeing prices for flights and cabs and hotels change before your very eyes? Blame RateGain's dynamic pricing. Glad to see the variety of choices on travel aggregation websites. Thank RateGain's channel distribution.
In 15 years, this bootstrapped company has gradually amassed a client list that boasts of every Fortune 500 travel and hospitality company in the world. It has doubled revenue for two years now, stayed profitable even longer. This March, it aims to hit $100 million in annual recurring revenue—a coveted milestone for SaaS startups.
Surely a shining example, if ever there was one, for the restless, valuation-hungry new-age startups.
It's curious then that RateGain has ditched its steady-as-she-goes policy to buy growth and scale. It has made its first two acquisitions ever in the past year and has promised many more. It's new CEO is someone with experience in over 100 M&A deals.
"The one thing that I can say is that they are currently on steroids," is how the founder of another SaaS hospitality startup describes the sudden flurry of activity at RateGain.
