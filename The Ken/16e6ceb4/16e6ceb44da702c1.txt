What does it say about the speed of Internet culture and innovation that it already feels like years since the TikTok craze took over the world? A world that had almost resigned itself to the Facebook-Google duopoly.

What does it then say about the speed of Chinese innovation that YY, another Chinese company, is already disrupting TikTok, whose own growth is (inevitably) plateauing?

It increasingly feels like China was merely the staging ground for a whole bunch of its new-age Internet startups. But that the real battleground was India and emerging markets. As the sudden and utter rivalry between TikTok and YY demonstrates.

In today’s story, Nilesh, one of our regular contributing writers, tries to dissect the strategy being followed by YY to beat TikTok. It’s maddeningly confusing and utterly fascinating. I meant, just look at YY’s arsenal, each of which is among the top 5 in its respective category. 

- Bigo Live, for live streaming. 

- Likee, for short videos. 

- HAGO, for social gaming.

- imo, for video calls.

There’s also Noizz, a “deepfake” app that lets users morph their faces onto bodies of film and TV characters.

YY, the Nasdaq-listed Chinese company that owns all of these apps is worth $5 billion and has close to 500 million users globally. It has been in India for just two years but its already scaring the bejesus out of any company that covets a large user base in India. 

Because India is the most popular geography by downloads for YY’s apps, as per app market intelligence firm Sensor Tower, accounting for over two-thirds of Likee’s 100 million monthly active users (MAUs) and over 60 million of Bigo Live’s 200 million registered users worldwide.

If you’re TikTok, this is a multi-pronged attack. If you’re Facebook or Google, well forget about even understanding what you’re up against.
