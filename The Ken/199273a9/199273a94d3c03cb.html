If you recognise that there’s something wrong with India’s stock market, then the root of the problem might be in the mirror when you’re standing in front of it. That’s what Sanjeev Prasad, the managing director and co-head of Kotak Institutional Equities, pointed out when he spoke with The Ken about the current disconnect between fundamentals and valuations.

Prasad sounded other alarms, too. He spoke candidly about how foreign portfolio investors, private equity funds, and promoters are draining money out of the stock market even as domestic institutions plow in. And he is doubtful that India’s retail investors would make any money for the next 1–2 years if they were to invest in the public markets now.

Prasad had plenty else to tell our finance editor, Anand, in an in-depth conversation about public-market investments, covering the implications of GST 2.0, where it might make sense for you to deploy your money, and much more.

It’s an enlightening Q&A that may make you rethink your investment ethos. Read it here.
