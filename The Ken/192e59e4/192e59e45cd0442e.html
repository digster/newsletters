Larsen and Toubro and Bharat Heavy Electricals have been making a range of products on a stupefying scale over decades. In the process, they have built a complex yet efficient web of suppliers to handle their requirements.

So why would they need a six-year-old company whose claim to fame is identifying thousands of small factories that have capacity to spare?

Zetwerk, backed by investors like Accel Partners and Lightspeed Venture Partners, combined a tech startup’s penchant for aggregation (of cabs, homes, restaurants, and now factories) with a willingness to bet on an industry before the government made it desirable with billions of dollars in sops.

How does Zetwerk slice a single order it bags from a client into smaller chunks for several industrial units? Just as it’s doing with a US$300 million contract it recently won from state-run power producer NTPC for photovoltaic modules.

If Zetwerk, last valued at US$2.8 billion, is a “marketplace” for manufacturing, why does it have 11 factories of its own?

Shristi has the answers in her story.
