Good Morning Ishan,
Take a look at the chart here.
Over the past few years, FDs and government bonds have shown their predictable returns. Equities, with all their mood swings, have compounded at a much higher clip. And then there’s the bar in the middle.
That’s Reits: essentially the way investors like you and me can own slices of office parks, malls, and warehouses without buying an entire building. Their returns sit in this zone, comfortably ahead of most “safe” options.
That middle lane becomes more interesting when you think about how Indians approach real estate in the first place. For most households, property means residential property. The home you live in, the second flat you rent out while holding it for appreciation. Rental yields there hover around 2–3%. Here, the bet is that prices will rise over time.
Sometimes they do. Sometimes they don’t.
On the other hand, in a market that wasn’t exactly euphoric in 2025, listed Reits delivered close to 30% returns. Sure, that kind of performance won’t show up every year. Still!
So, if Indian households already allocate a major share of their wealth to real estate, why is so much of it concentrated in its lowest-yielding corner?
In our latest wealth story, Anand walks through the commercial-property ladder and examines where it fits in a portfolio alongside debt and equity. The chart gave you the numbers, and the story will help you decide what to do with them.
