What do you get when a semiconductor wave collides with a stock-market high?

You get a publicly traded firm, supposedly in the semiconductor-and-electronics business, bankrolling its unlisted affiliate—and seeing its stock price soar from Rs 10 to Rs 9,000 in just 10 months.

RRP Semiconductor is the listed company here, a former trading firm, while its unlisted sibling, RRP Electronics, has been making ever grandiose claims about its role in India’s semiconductor ambitions. When the unlisted company inaugurated its OSAT plant in 2024, Maharashtra’s chief minister, two deputies, and cricketing legend Sachin Tendulkar were all in attendance.

Rumours have been swirling around the trading company: that Tendulkar had invested in it, joined as an adviser, or signed on as an ambassador; that the Maharashtra government had allotted it 100 acres of land.

The company finally addressed these in a notice to the exchange, months into a rally that shows no signs of stopping. BSE responded too, issuing its own warning on 20 October, cautioning investors about the unusual price movement and placing the stock under enhanced surveillance.

The best part? Neither of the RRP companies has much experience in semiconductors or electronics.

What’s unfolding with RRP is a story of two companies wanting to make the most of a high-subsidy contest while mining investors’ fear of missing out on the next big trend.

Anand and Keshav dig into the bizarre stock-price spike of RRP Semiconductor and the big claims of RRP Electronics—and their findings are startling. Don’t miss it.
