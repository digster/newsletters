It may seem a little odd that on a day when most headlines are about India changing its constitutional position on Kashmir, 72 years after its independence, we are talking about edtech at The Ken. But you know we try to stick to our knitting.
 
It’s a massive love triangle of education: Close to 1 million students write the entrance exam every year; 23 IITs have 11.2K seats on offer; naturally, the over $40 billion-coaching industry is thriving, both in terms of stature and revenue.
 
For all the above, the notion of inexpensive, modular, bite-sized, on-demand, and functional online live-tutoring is potentially game-changing. From a class of 5-6 two years ago to an impressive 120, even aiming at 500 in the near future—online scaling is easy when offline classes max out at 30-odd pupils. Venture capital-funded Vedantu and Unacademy are mounting a good fight to the legacy test-prep business, one that churns out exam toppers year after year.
 
With its 265 million active users in India, YouTube is a natural showcase for online platforms to attract new users. But YouTube itself is teeming with live classes and recorded videos put up by independent educators, who are being trained and mentored by the video platform to increase their subscriber base. Given this, Unacademy’s recent acquisition of Wifistudy, which has over a billion views on its YouTube channel and tops the list of most subscribed-to edtech platform globally, seems like a masterstroke.
 
In this rank-eat-rank world, will online offerings democratise coaching or will they muddy the waters? How is the old guard prepping for the onslaught? Could they still have some tricks up their sleeve?
