Good Morning Ishan,
As some of my colleagues and I like to say, teamwork makes the dream work. Sure, it’s cliché, but the refrain is true. It’s a line that every business returns to because no person can carry an operation all on their own.
Entrepreneurs around the world have been relying on team members in the Philippines to stay on track. I’m referring to a class of workers who call themselves “virtual assistants”, even though the label feels like a misnomer—the work they do is as tangible as can be.
Filipino virtual assistants plug into a range of roles remotely, performing crucial office duties. But their pay is low: US$3 per hour—significantly cheaper than the minimum wage in any developed nation.
In some cases, that remuneration is being weighed down even further. There are two reasons: job-matching platforms trumpet the option of paying Filipinos next to nothing, and a supply glut of remote workers in the country leads to some people taking jobs for exploitatively low pay.
For today’s story, Katrina spoke with 10 virtual assistants in the Philippines to find out what it’s like to be in their shoes. It’s a colourful report with a close look at a set of workers who frequently appear as little more than names on Slack, Telegram, and WhatsApp but have so much to offer.
