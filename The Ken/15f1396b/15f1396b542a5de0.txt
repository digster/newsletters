Good Morning Ishan,

Once upon a time joining a startup was considered a risky move, but one with a potential pot of gold at the end of the journey: cashing out your stocks.

Cashing out usually takes place through two possible “exits” - an IPO or an acquisition by a larger (and hopefully richer) company.

In India, neither have quite worked out. The number of startup IPOs can be counted on your fingers. And as for exits, well, most acquisitions have been made by another company issuing more of its stock.

So most startup employees, across levels, slowly started discounting the value of their stocks - ESOPs. Funny money, it was.

The funniest of that money was Flipkart’s, because it was the largest of Indian unicorns and the one valued most highly (touching $15 billion). Therefore, if someone had to do something about the falling perception of ESOPs, it had to be Flipkart too.

Last week it did just that.

Flipkart put down $100 million to tell the world that its stocks weren’t all that funny. It is buying back its ESOPs from employees and former employees through a fairly open and transparent process.

This is an epochal event (even though this isn’t Flipkart’s first ESOP buyback, but the fifth).

Apart from helping Flipkart’s stock rise in value in the minds of current and potential employees, it will put pressure on a lot of its peer unicorns to offer similar buybacks too.
