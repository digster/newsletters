Vishesh Rajaram is not in the business of backing the next fintech fad or yet another derivative AI idea.

Speciale Invest, the Chennai-based venture-capital firm he co-founded, has funded a maker of 3D-printed rockets (Agnikul Cosmos), a flying-taxi startup (The ePlane Company), and a nuclear-fusion company (Anubal Fusion). Speciale’s most “consumer” bet is Ultraviolette, a manufacturer of electric motorcycles.

The firm is closing its third fund, which will be bigger than its $40 million second fund.

Rajaram is refreshingly contrarian and candid in the otherwise herd-like, boilerplate-loving world of VCs. Better than describing his chat with Seema is sharing a few excerpts from it:

I don’t think it’s possible to time it right all the time. Nor are there mental models in this. Which is why it gets hard for big VCs to play this. In my business, they always tell you the word “playbook”. What’s the playbook? The fun part is, there’s no playbook. You’ve got to figure it out, specific to every company.

There’s the Steve Jobs example which says a customer does not know what they need. But in my life, we’ve spoken only about Steve Jobs, we haven’t spoken about the second Steve Jobs yet. So, keeping him as an exception, you know your customers often tell you the truth.

Yes, [VCs] can screw up, but it also takes an insane amount of money and wrong expectations to screw up. And that insane amount of money is not available [for deep tech].
