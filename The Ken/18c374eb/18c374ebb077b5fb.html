The Kingmaker is a documentary film released in 2019 that gives viewers a glimpse into the headspace of the notoriously acquisitive Imelda Marcos. In it, her son Bongbong (AKA Marcos Jr.) was running for vice president of the Philippines in 2016.

He lost, but Imelda emphasised during one of her interviews for the film that Bongbong would eventually take his seat in Malacañan Palace, the official residence and principal workplace of the Philippines’ president. This, of course, happened last year.

One of Marcos Jr.’s agenda items was to develop the Philippines’ first sovereign wealth fund, and it’s coming together quickly.

The Maharlika Investment Fund (MIF), as it’s called, is supposed to be a major step toward developing the Philippine economy. But experts aren’t receiving it with fanfare. The fund’s formation is rushed, and it could bring about fiscal instability in the country, former central bank deputy governor Diwa Guinigundo argues.

Katrina spoke with Guinigundo to understand MIF’s shortcomings and how other public entities will be impacted now that the Marcos administration is forging ahead with the fund’s implementation.
