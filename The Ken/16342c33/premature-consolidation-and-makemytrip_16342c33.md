Roughly 2,500 years ago, Aristotle was believed to have said, “Horror vacui”, or "Nature abhors a vacuum”. He believed that true empty space or voids could not exist, because nature would rush to fill it with something else, even air.
Another 2,200 years would pass before Alexander Pope, the English poet, said, "Fools rush in where angels fear to tread.”
Cut to October 2016 and MakeMyTrip, India’s largest online travel site, acquired its largest rival Goibibo. This merger created an entity that became the market leader in the online travel category in India, spanning everything, from air travel to hotels to homestays to bus ticketing, a one-stop shop for anyone travelling in India.
The end of MakeMyTrip and Goibibo’s cut-throat battle would herald, said many industry watchers and experts, a more rational period of lower costs, higher margins and saner behaviour.
"Fate, it seems, is not without a sense of irony.” - Morpheus.
Turns out MakeMyTrip eliminated its biggest rival by merging with it, only for a newer set of rivals to rush in to fill the void. Thomas Cook, Cox & Kings, Booking.com, Expedia, Oyo, Treebo, Paytm…call them air or call them fools, but rush in they did.
And MakeMyTrip found itself in the unenviable position of having to spend more and more on marketing to earn the same "take rate" it was getting from its partners before acquiring Goibibo.
It gets worse. To fund its greater hunger for capital, it now needs even more deep-pocketed investors. Like, you know, that little-known fund with a $100 billion war chest and unbridled ambition? Surely you’ve heard of SoftBank?
And that is only the entry point into today’s story by Ashish. A “Part 1” of MakeMyTrip’s descent (or ascent, depends on where you’re standing) into whatever it takes to stay an undisputed leader in India’s travel market. Deals will be made. Partners will be booted. Backs will meet knives. No quarters shall be given.
