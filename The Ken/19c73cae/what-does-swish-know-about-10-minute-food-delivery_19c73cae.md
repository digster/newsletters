Good Morning Ishan,
For Eternal and Swiggy, food delivery is boring in the best possible way. There are no pesky competitors. It’s profitable. It’s predictable.
But the quick rise of Zepto Cafe, a service offering snacks, a couple of years ago, made them give instant food delivery a shot by setting up their own small cloud kitchens and persuading a few fast-food chains to sign up.
But ask any Eternal or Swiggy investor how seriously they take these experiments, and I bet you’d get little more than a shrug. I don’t blame them. Eternal shut down one such initiative in four months. Another is limited to 45 kitchens, mostly in the National Capital Region and Bengaluru, a year after its launch.
This makes me wonder what Swish, a 10-minute food-delivery app in Bengaluru, had in its pitch deck that it’s now reportedly raising $30–35 million in a round led by Accel and Bain Capital. This is on top of the $16 million that it has received since late 2024.
A limited menu and a low average order value are impediments to scale and profitability. But a venture capitalist told Suprita, the author of today’s story, “proximity and frequency” could come to Swish’s aid. How?
