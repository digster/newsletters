Good Morning Ishan,
 
There are very few things in the world that I like more than driving a car. I know it sounds completely silly, and you probably are wondering what sort of lunatic enjoys driving in Indian city traffic. My sort of lunatic.
 
I love driving because that is the one thing I can do with as much confidence as Salman Khan when he's shirtless or Mark Zuckerberg when he's saying things he really shouldn't be saying. It's brazen; it's brilliant.
 
Anyway, I'm not taking you for a pointless ride here. I do this most weeks. Sort of sit back and look through our week's stories, and this week, all I could think about was road signs. Maybe I miss driving too much or maybe I'm on to something here.
 
So here's what we wrote at The Ken this week; tell me if you disagree.
 
Our Monday story came from Harveen and Sarkar (as she prefers being called). They wrote about the Times Group and their one big roadblock—English-language movie channels. No one even wants to buy them. 
 
On Day 2, close on the heels of Sarkar's debut piece, Salman debuted with his MVNO saga. A free story, it charts MVNOs attempts to not fall off the Indian telecom cliff. The next sharp turn could bring it closer to IoT or take it off the map.
 
Praveen, err, I mean PGK, has a furniture fixation. I'm told the word furniture torments him now. Anyhow, his part II on the IKEA story reminded me of those pesky five-bump speed breakers that are both effective and inconvenient—much like how IKEA's entry in India probably is for existing furniture brands.
 
On Thursday, we had our contributor Olina Banerji write about the Competition Commission of India and how much more it needs to do to regulate competition. This while the EU’s Competition Commission hit Google with a record $5 billion penalty for violating antitrust rules. CCI could use a detour in its way and return with a reality check.
 
The week ended with Arundhati's story on PhonePe buying Zopper's PoS business for about $6 million. Now the fintech is looking to clear out the PoS freeway for a smoother drive.
 
Hope you enjoyed that look in the rearview mirror. Forgive me, I miss driving.
