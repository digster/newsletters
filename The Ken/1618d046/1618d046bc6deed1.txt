"I is a nice and jumbly giant! I is the only nice and jumbly Giant in Giant Country! I is THE BIG FRIENDLY GIANT!"

If you can recognise those lines straight away, I can tell you've had a great childhood. Roald Dahl's The BFG or, quite simply, The Big Friendly Giant, caught dreams and stored them in his cavernous home so he could share them with sleeping children. Functionally, not so different from what Amazon does today—shows you book titles you ought to be purchasing and reading.

With about $3 billion worth of physical books sold worldwide in just the first half of 2017 and 80% of all ebook sales coming through Kindle, Amazon is nothing short of Yeti right now. And while it sure is a Big Giant today, is it India-Friendly? That's the story our contributor Amish writes today.

"It wouldn’t be an extrapolation to argue Amazon is the single-largest marketplace for books in India today...but any hints of a possible conflict between its two roles as a marketplace for other publishers and as a publisher itself is dismissed..."

Besides, in a low-priced market like India, can Amazon dictate terms to publishers like it did in the West?

That's some of what Amish has encapsulated in his piece, but let me not give it away.
