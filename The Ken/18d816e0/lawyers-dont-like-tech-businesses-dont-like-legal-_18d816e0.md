Good Morning Ishan,
What do you get when a Harvard Law graduate, a Googler, and a seasoned product manager walk into a bar?
Spotdraft. A Gen AI-driven software-as-a-service (SaaS) startup helping lawyers from companies like Airbnb (accommodation platform), Chargebee (subscription-billing company), Phonepe (fintech), and Strava (fitness-tracking service) navigate the complex world of contracts.
The contract-signing process can take anywhere between 30 minutes and 2 hours. And it’s the bane of any lawyer’s workweek to receive a 300-page non-disclosure agreement on a Friday morning and be asked to review it, says Shashank Bijapur, Spotdraft CEO, a contract lifecycle management company.
Spotdraft got $26 million in funding from the likes of Prosus, Premji Invest, and Binny Bansal-backed 021 Capital. And since it was set up in the pre-ChatGPT era, it once trained AI models from scratch, demanding mountains of data and offering no economies of scale. Then, it made a pivot.
Abhirami has a nuanced story on how even though this GenAI is transforming a frustrating aspect of lawyers’ work, it also has to tread carefully and deal with lawyers’ tech cynicism: [https://the-ken.com/story/lawyers-dont-like-tech-businesses-dont-like-legal-teams-spotdraft-wants-to-fix-it-all/](https://the-ken.com/story/lawyers-dont-like-tech-businesses-dont-like-legal-teams-spotdraft-wants-to-fix-it-all/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (9-minute read)
