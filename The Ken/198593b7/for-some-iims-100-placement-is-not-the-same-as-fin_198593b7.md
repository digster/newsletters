Good Morning Ishan,
The Indian Institutes of Management run some of the most expensive postgraduate programmes in India, and they’ve been raising fees in recent years. More than ever, the 21 IIMs are compelled to justify the hikes. One way they do this is by showing 100% placements, even if it requires creative adjustments behind the scenes.
Sure, opt-outs aren’t out of the ordinary. Every year, there are IIM grads who aren’t counted in their school’s placement results because they go overseas, pursue higher degrees, join their family businesses, or even start their own. But some IIMs release suspiciously high opt-out figures.
IIM Amritsar, for instance, had 30 unplaced students and 48 opt-outs out of its cohort of 318 this year. Over at IIM Trichy, 26 out of 384 students opted out of MBA placements.
Atul found that there’s a pattern of students being forced to sign paperwork that withdraws them from the placement cycle. IIM Amritsar went even further in July by sending out an email to its student body, indicating that anyone who doesn’t have an employment offer by late February 2026 will be automatically “opted out” of the placement process.
This might sound like a cosmetic issue, but it’s also becoming more common for students to voluntarily pull out of the process. Atul explains why and unpacks what it now means to be an IIM grad in [today’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/for-some-iims-100-placement-is-not-the-same-as-finding-everyone-a-job/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
