Good Morning Ishan,
Every month, money is taken out of your salary for the employee provident fund. It appears on your payslip, carrying the implicit assumption that it has gone somewhere safe and official.
That assumption depends on two things: your employer actually depositing the money and the EPFO bothering to check.
In the year ended March 2024, employer arrears in India almost doubled to Rs 26,000 crore. The arrears have only risen since, say insiders. This happened while the EPFO steadily dismantled the systems meant to spot defaults. Internal systems that once flagged defaulters were scrapped, and local offices say even obvious signals like payments slowing then stopping were routinely ignored.
The fix was supposed to be technology. Essentially, big data and smarter systems. Much of that tech either doesn’t exist or work, or sits behind opaque processes that field officers can’t actually use.
Naturally, there’s a vacuum. Nearly 70% of establishments registered with EPFO are now classified as non-contributory, up from roughly half five years ago. Some may fall outside the law. Many don’t. So, employees usually find out only when they try to withdraw their money, or when their families have to.
A fire at a Goa nightclub recently made all of this impossible to ignore. Twenty workers died, but EPFO officers later admitted they would struggle to secure provident fund, pension, and insurance benefits for the families because the employer had been defaulting for years without detection.
Anand’s story today traces how a drive to end the inspector raj hollowed out enforcement without a working replacement, and how amnesty schemes with token penalties reshaped incentives. It also sits alongside our earlier reporting on EPFO’s other failings, from masked [inefficiencies](https://the-ken.com/story/the-epfo-black-box-masking-shoddy-practices-with-high-returns/) dressed up as high returns to a long-simmering crisis of claim [rejections](https://the-ken.com/story/your-provident-fund-claim-is-rejected-but-its-not-your-fault/).
