Good Morning Ishan,
A little birdie tells me that under governor Shaktikanta Das, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has morphed, slowly but surely, into the Gabbar Singh of regulators. Mortal fear is what the central bank now evokes among India’s banks, non-banks, and fintechs—much like the iconic villain from Sholay, a 1975 Bollywood flick. “So ja beta, varna Gabbar aa jaayega” (“Sleep off kid, else Gabbar will come”).
The big blows that the RBI has rendered on biggies like Paytm, IIFL, and Kotak Mahindra Bank over the past few months have only reinforced this reputation.
This comes across clearly again in today’s story by contributor Ateesh Tankha—about how the RBI may have gone too far in its squeeze on unsecured loans by fintechs. Not only did the central bank make it onerous to give out such loans, but it also initiated audits on many fintechs and recently instructed them to pare down growth in unsecured loans.
The fintechs Ateesh spoke to were categorical—they wouldn’t share for publication, even by way of disguised screenshots, sections of the audit reports that the RBI sent them. No one wants to risk crossing the regulator the wrong way.
But angst, like water, finds its own level. “It’s a waste to do business where the regulator is proud to contain credit growth and is even more proud that it will restrict someone if it smells a crisis. It doesn’t seem to need proof, mere feeling is good enough. I really want to go to the court and question what gives [RBI] the right to act with such impunity,” a senior fintech executive told Ateesh.
Ateesh digs into what’s going on to make a strong argument that the RBI’s curbs are driven more by unfounded paranoia than by justified data—with undesirable consequences. Taking a knife to the findings and assertions by the central bank, he bells the cat, “Every regulator must be held accountable.” Click [here](https://the-ken.com/story/for-fintechs-rbi-is-the-boy-who-cries-wolf/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) for a rare, spirited take against the RBI’s diktats.
From across the ocean, Isa writes about a giant—that faced near-certain death not too long ago—and its comeback. ABS-CBN, once the Philippines’ biggest TV broadcaster, faced an existential crisis in 2020. This was after it got caught in the crosshairs of Rodrigo Duterte, the then-president of the country known for his strong-arm ways. Pulled off air for being critical of Duterte, ABS-CBN saw its bustling newsroom operations screech to a halt, resulting in massive layoffs and losses.
But then, it pulled off an unlikely but spectacular pivot—transforming from a broadcaster to an entertainment powerhouse. Shrugging off the wounds of an arbitrary shutdown and charting a new course has let the media conglomerate live and tell the tale. It’s now producing the Philippines’ top music acts and blockbuster movies—that are aired on both domestic small screens and on international platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime, and Spotify.
The journey has been anything but easy for ABS-CBN. It had to make do with the little it was left with and make the most of it. Adapting to new realities and tastes, cutting deals with erstwhile competitors, and hustling to survive—the crazy run has come at a cost.
Besides, the recovery from the downfall is not complete yet, and many challenges remain. How did ABS-CBN swing its fortunes around, and can it regain its past glory?
