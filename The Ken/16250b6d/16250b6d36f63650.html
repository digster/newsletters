In the time of oversized funding, oversized valuations and oversized exit ambitions, a story of a $50-million exit may not make head-turning headlines. It certainly doesn’t look oversized. But if you look at the math, it is.
 
They say exit strategy isn’t about you, the founder; it’s about investors. But what if it is about investors who made a smart 6X returns; about founders who made a life-changing $10-12 million each; about employees, 65 (out of a total of 75) of whom shared $5 million among themselves.
 
That’s the story from Sumanth today—the bittersweet tale of an exit. It’s about the craft needed to build and exit a startup. To arrive at a success formula, if there was one. It’s also an important story, because in the Indian startup industry (emphasis: mine) today, only those seem to matter are those who have raised the most amount of money at the highest possible valuation.
 
The story is full of nuggets of wisdom—how to run a tight ship, how to expand overseas, how not to raise more than the desired money, and so much more. As much as from the exiting founder as from Sumanth himself. “One interesting rule of thumb that can be generally applied is for founders to try and choose investors whose total fund size is congruent to the size of the exit you can realistically target,” he says.
 
It’s a great Friday read. Enjoy. https://the-ken.com/story/minjar-2m-funding-50m-acquisition/
 
Have a super weekend. We’ll see you Monday.
