Good Morning Ishan,
 
Many of my colleagues were at the India Mobile Congress on Monday, arguably one of the biggest events in the region. I’m told India's minister for IT and communication, Ravishankar Prasad, had his usual self-assured air about him at the inauguration. “All the policies are in place,” he said. “Spectrum trading, sharing, and auction. E-commerce. Electronics manufacturing. Open source… On data, we are for data sovereignty.”

Barely 30 minutes later, in a conference session, all the CEOs of telecom infra companies had their usual gloomy air about them. “Telecom infrastructure is crumbling; policies are not in place,” said one.

Well, Schrödinger’s policies aside, India has a major policy pileup problem. In sector after sector—telecom, e-commerce, cloud, fintech—there are major “Big Bang” policies that are pending. Each of which individually has the ability to rewrite existing competitive landscapes, and collectively, much more. Which is why each one has pro and anti lobbying.
