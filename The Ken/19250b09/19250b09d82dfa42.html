Dear Ishan,
 
Many organisations that have strong innovation-driven cultures often also sport an Achilles’ Heel. It’s a bias against ideas and innovation that come from “the outside” or “outsiders”.

It’s called the “Not Invented Here” syndrome.

It’s when the organisation prioritises and takes pride in innovation to the extent that it ignores or disregards ideas from external sources. Us versus them. Ours versus theirs.

Eight years ago, we set up our newsroom and launched The Ken. We wanted innovation to be core to our culture and business model.

Our twin innovation engines would be our editorial newsroom and product organisation. Both staffed by talented and ambitious writers, editors, designers, developers, and podcasters.

We had a very strong “Invented Here” culture. From our storytelling style to our emails to our design to even our pricing plans, we have often done things originally and from first principles, keeping our subscribers in mind.

That is never going to change.

The core of our subscription plans in 2025 will continue to be our original, deeply reported, and meticulously edited daily business stories. Those are accompanied by our multiple weekly newsletters, each covering themes and sectors you find valuable. We are hiring more journalists to bring you an even broader scope of coverage.

In July, we launched Two by Two, our first premium podcast. Co-hosted by Rohin Dharmakumar and Praveen Gopal Krishnan, our goal is to pick the most important companies and trends that matter to you, bring in experienced and opinionated guests (ranging from B-school professors to CEOs to operators), and discuss how they play out from first principles. Our objective is to challenge our own understanding and assumptions, and in the process, hopefully yours too. We have more such podcasts planned. 

We are also launching a brand-new weekly newsletter, Long and Short, on the listed companies that matter and the funds that bet on them. Spearheaded by two of our senior editors, Seetharaman G and Anand Kalyanaraman, this will be an exclusive offering for our Premium subscribers. 

Sadly, after four years, we will be discontinuing our Southeast Asia (SEA) stories. We launched them in 2020 as a separate subscription before folding it back into our Premium subscription tier in 2022. Ultimately, we couldn’t find enough subscribers who were consistently interested in our SEA stories.

Thus, as we enter our ninth year, this is what our subscription offerings will look like.

But is that enough? We don’t think so. Neither do our subscribers.

They want us to help them form opinions on trends that are shaping the future of business. They want authentic and candid insights from people who have bucked the norm. They want insights on developing better careers and living better lives. They want to know what their peers think on various topics that matter to them too.

We know because they—you—tell us. Through comments, emails, calls, tips, and meetings, you tell us what more you’d like from us and from your subscription to The Ken. They have also collectively shaped our understanding on topics as diverse as quick commerce to parenting, through their participation in our subscriber polls and surveys.   

This is why we’re introducing a completely new axis of innovation inside The Ken: “Not Invented Here.”

We’re building a new team to help curate insights from people outside The Ken. Many of those individuals sport roles that we’re used to seeing, like founders, CEOs, executives, and investors. But also researchers, professors, regulators, coaches, authors, artists, and talented professionals across age groups. Interestingly, many of these are also our subscribers.

The products, stories, and formats that emerge from this new team might look different from our signature narrative-driven stories.

But they will all strive to the same standards as everything else we do at The Ken, and be worthy of a subscriber’s time. Of your time.

We're incredibly excited about all the new products and stories we’ll be bringing you over the next year.

Regards,
Seema Singh and Rohin Dharmakumar
Co-founders, The Ken
