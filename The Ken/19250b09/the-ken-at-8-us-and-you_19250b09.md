Many organisations that have strong innovation-driven cultures often also sport an Achilles’ Heel. It’s a bias against ideas and innovation that come from “the outside” or “outsiders”.
It’s called the “Not Invented Here” syndrome.
It’s when the organisation prioritises and takes pride in innovation to the extent that it ignores or disregards ideas from external sources. Us versus them. Ours versus theirs.
Eight years ago, we set up our newsroom and launched The Ken. We wanted innovation to be core to our culture and business model.
Our twin innovation engines would be our editorial newsroom and product organisation. Both staffed by talented and ambitious writers, editors, designers, developers, and podcasters.
We had a very strong “Invented Here” culture. From our storytelling style to our emails to our design to even our pricing plans, we have often done things originally and from first principles, keeping our subscribers in mind.
That is never going to change.
The core of our subscription plans in 2025 will continue to be our original, deeply reported, and meticulously edited daily business stories. Those are accompanied by our multiple weekly newsletters, each covering themes and sectors you find valuable. We are hiring more journalists to bring you an even broader scope of coverage.
In July, we launched [Two by Two](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/), our first premium podcast. Co-hosted by Rohin Dharmakumar and Praveen Gopal Krishnan, our goal is to pick the most important companies and trends that matter to you, bring in experienced and opinionated guests (ranging from B-school professors to CEOs to operators), and discuss how they play out from first principles. Our objective is to challenge our own understanding and assumptions, and in the process, hopefully yours too. We have more such podcasts planned.
We are also launching a brand-new weekly newsletter, [Long and Short](https://the-ken.com/long-and-short/), on the listed companies that matter and the funds that bet on them. Spearheaded by two of our senior editors, Seetharaman G and Anand Kalyanaraman, this will be an exclusive offering for our Premium subscribers.
Sadly, after four years, we will be discontinuing our Southeast Asia (SEA) stories. We launched them in 2020 as a separate subscription before folding it back into our Premium subscription tier in 2022. Ultimately, we couldn’t find enough subscribers who were consistently interested in our SEA stories.
Thus, as we enter our ninth year, this is what our subscription offerings will look like.
