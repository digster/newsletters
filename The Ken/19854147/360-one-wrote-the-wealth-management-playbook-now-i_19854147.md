Good Morning Ishan,
“Chacha Chaudhary’s brain works faster than a computer.”
Folks of a certain vintage might recall this punchline from the eponymous Indian superhero comic series—starring a quick thinker with a solution for every situation.
Karan Bhagat has a Chacha Chaudhary attribute. The CEO of 360 One Wealth and Asset Management can crunch numbers “faster than Excel”, an ex-employee told Aakriti, the author of today’s story. With such prowess, a strong nose for money, and soaring ambition, it was little surprise that Bhagat, earlier with Kotak, pioneered the standalone wealth-management business in India with co-founder Yatin Shah. Catering to the investment needs, whims and fancies of the rich and ultra-rich, this business has grown leaps and bounds to now count among India’s largest in its space. It seems to be in pole position in a market that’s riding the wealthy wave in the country. What’s not to like?
Just that cracks have been manifesting, thick and fast. Old allies like IIFL’s Nirmal Jain and Venkataraman Rajamani—who first bankrolled 360 One—have stepped back, both from the cap table and the company’s board. A marquee foreign investor has cut its holding. Why, Bhagat and Shah too have shaved off some stake. Scores of star wealth managers are calling it quits. Meanwhile, the company itself is on a buying spree, scooping up rival wealth managers and brokerages. No surprise that the 360 One stock is swerving around like a headless chicken.
What gives? Why would someone sell in a business as promising as 360 One? Why is top talent leaving? Are the acquisitions driven more by necessity than by choice? Are there power games at play?
