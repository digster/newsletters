We begin today on a sombre note. The crash of an Air India aircraft in Gujarat yesterday has left the country shaken. More than 250 lives were lost, including many children. Our thoughts are with the families of those affected.

It feels difficult to turn to anything else after news like this. And yet, the world keeps moving—often with consequences of its own.

When Donald Trump announced a truce in America’s trade war with China on Thursday, I could picture Xi Jinping quoting the unforgettable Omar Little from the TV show The Wire: “You come at the king, you best not miss.”

China didn’t just match the US tariff for tariff; it dealt a serious blow by restricting exports of rare-earth elements, including neodymium and praseodymium, crucial to electronics, electric vehicles, and weapons.

With China controlling 70% of the world’s rare-earth mining and 90% of its processing, India is learning how it feels to be collateral damage. Its fledgling EV industry has been thrown into a tizzy. Maruti Suzuki, India’s largest carmaker, has cut its EV production by 70%.

As my colleague Suprita writes in the story today, not a single rare-earth shipment has come from China since April, and automakers’ stock will run out in a month. Even if China decides to be charitable, India needs alternatives.

But how far have local companies gone with their plan B? Let’s get to the story.
