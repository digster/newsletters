India has the largest number of Facebook users globally (241 million compared to the US’ 240 million, and that was last year). A country with no privacy policy to speak of, no safeguards around how citizen data is handled, poor general awareness of digital security and hundreds of millions of people yet to get online. In short, an unexplored land filled with untold riches there for the taking. Or something like that.
 
Which is why Facebook must have been scared when the #deletefacebook trend started gaining momentum. Would it run wild through India’s increasingly prickly and permanently outraged internet? Would it arrest Facebook’s seemingly unrelenting momentum in India? Would the controversy jeopardise its cosy relationship with the government and political parties? ("Inside Facebook’s bid to become the Indian government’s default town square”)
 
Nah. The storm blew over. With hardly any effect on Facebook India. (Though India’s technology minister did sort of publicly rebuke/warn Facebook to not try anything - but it was mostly posturing)
 
What gives? What explains Facebook’s Teflon coating in India?: https://the-ken.com/story/delete-facebook-india/
