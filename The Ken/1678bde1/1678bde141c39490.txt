Good Morning Ishan,

I'm a December person. Always have been. You can't blame me for being partial, it's a month loaded with so much festivity that even a grinch like me is forced to crack a smile.

But I've noticed something about the month. It brings with it a certain complacency. As we look forward to the promise of a new start in the new year, we tend to slack on the here and now. It's an easy enough rut to fall into. I'll do better next year. Be better. Work harder. Rekindle old connections. But for now—for December—I'll just focus on the wine and cake and gifts. 

It's a trap. 

Because if you really want to start the new year on a high, you better build up some serious momentum in this one. And believe you me, we're building momentum here at The Ken. Just look at last week's stories:

Monday's story saw Vandana dive deep https://the-ken.com/story/apples-india-struggle/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter into Apple's performance in India. Or rather, non-performance. The world's most profitable leader in smartphone sales is on the ropes in the world's most attractive smartphone market.

And where Apple's seeing a slowdown in growth, the subject of Tuesday's story is seeing quite the opposite. Bengaluru-based gate management startup myGate has had a heady ascent over the last 18 months. However, writes Vivek https://the-ken.com/story/mygate-could-use-a-leg-up/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, continuing its current trajectory will not be easy.

On Wednesday, we shifted focus to medtech. In fact, we shifted focus to GE's shift in focus from urban to rural India. Most medtech majors have tried and failed to sell to tier-2, 3, and 4 towns, writes Gayathri https://the-ken.com/story/genworks/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, but GE startup GenWorks has managed to make inroads.

Next up was Vishal's piece https://the-ken.com/story/indian-regulator-mantra-big-tech/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Indian regulators. More specifically, about how they're actually doing a pretty stellar job of regulating tech heavyweights. Other countries could even learn a few lessons from what Indian regulators are doing, says Vishal. This was our free offering for the week; do read and share.

Harveen's Friday story https://the-ken.com/story/convergence-and-dth/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter took a look at the rapidly evolving Direct-To-Home space. The same companies that cut cable operators out of the broadcast equation need to evolve or risk being cut out themselves.

And before I forget, there were also our weekend pieces. Two splendid ones.

The first is part-four of our Simplicity Paradigm series. In this one, Anupam and Saurabh delve into https://the-ken.com/story/step-into-the-memory-palace/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the importance of improving one's memory. For those of you looking at self-improvement in the coming year, this series is as good a place to start as any. 

Since December is also the month of wistful nostalgia, we debuted a new series—Going Home—that will run through the month. Last Sunday saw the first piece in the series, a beautiful piece by Lathika George on life in Santa Cruz https://the-ken.com/story/lathika-george-santacruz-girl/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, Mumbai, well before the city grew into the unrelenting beast it is today.

Do check-in tomorrow for the second part in the series, if Lathika's story is anything to go by, it promises to be the perfect Sunday morning read.

On a completely unrelated note, my biggest grouse with December is that it's finally the month where I'm forced to resort to socks and shoes. I'm usually a slippers person, but between frostbite and the confines of closed-toed shoes, I'm picking the latter. If you have any strong feelings about the month, do write in and let me know; it really is a special time.

Not-so-warmly,
