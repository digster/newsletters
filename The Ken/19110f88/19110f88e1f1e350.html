Good Morning Ishan,

Bhavish Aggarwal, Ola Electric’s chief executive, is not the kind of person who’ll settle.

He wants more out of his employees, more out of consumers, more out of his investors, and more out of every fund-raise.

But when it comes to his company’s debut as a public limited company, Aggarwal’s valuation expectations from the initial public offer (IPO) seem tepid.

The electric-vehicle (EV) company’s stock-market debut is expected to be the biggest this year and is coming at a time when the bulls are running wild. Yet, its asking price for its IPO pegs its valuation at Rs 33,500 crore (US$4 billion)—a steep 25% discount to what it was in September 2023.

The Ken has accessed two valuation reports prepared at the behest of Ola Electric to facilitate equity fund-raises in 2023. Based on projections provided by Ola Electric, the company was valued a third more than what it is currently expecting to list at.

But, as Anand writes, the best laid plans of mice and men often go awry. The EV maker’s actual results in 2024 were far removed from the rosy forecasts, resulting in this valuation cut.

Why, then, is Aggarwal settling for less in this IPO? More to it than meets the eye? Also, does the seemingly “low” valuation make this an attractive buy for retail investors for whom the Rs 6,000 crore+ issue opens today?

Read Anand’s sharp story to find out. It’s a free read, so do pass this along to those hoping to invest in India’s leading maker of electric two-wheelers. Even if you are sitting this one out, read on to know what you are missing.
