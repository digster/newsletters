Good Morning Ishan,
 
The winner takes it all
The loser standing small
Beside the victory
That's her destiny
 
Beautiful, beautiful, horribly defeatist lines from ABBA right there.
 
I mean, sure, the loser stands small, but the winner absolutely doesn't take it all. One, the winner doesn't have the experience of stepping back, learning, trying something new, etc. Two, the winner will never know what a risk is, for they have nothing to lose.
 
And three, the winner doesn't always make for a great story.
 
Last week at The Ken was about those who need the attention, those who get the short end of the stick, and those who might fail, but try their luck anyway.
 
We started our week with Ruhi's carefully reported piece on the government's price cap on stents resulting in hospitals increasing the price of the procedure, and how it negatively affected the patient.
 
Tuesday saw a new set of disadvantaged. Indian Original Equipment Manufacturers. Venkat's piece [FREE story] focussed on the ingenious ways in which telecom patent holders Ericsson and Qualcomm continue to hold the throne.
 
This was followed by Sumanth's piece on Ola and its nemeses—the Devil and the Dragon. While Ola is no loser (quite the contrary), it has quite a few challenges in its way. Starting with an unhealthy association with Uber, of course.
 
Patanjali's story on budget hotel chains Treebo and FabHotels was all about keeping it ‘simple'. Except, not really. It's a tough space, and the list of losers is piling up (read: Zo Rooms and Stayzilla).
 
The week ended with Payal's Flipkart story on the company making a conscious effort to make their employees, both current and ex, happy. Of course, Flipkart's big PR win (a $100-million ESOP buyback) has put the heat on others in the Indian unicorn club, namely, Quikr, Zomato, and Ola.
 
This begs a question: How many will survive?
 
Only time will tell. You needn't worry, just enjoy your weekend for now.
