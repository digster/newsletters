In June last year, India killed TikTok—the viral short video app that India banned in June last year, because it was "prejudicial to sovereignty and integrity of India, defence of India, security of state and public order”. Killed as in, banned. Blocked. Disappeared. Offed.
Outside India though, TikTok is killing it.
Across Southeast Asia, TikTok racked up 136 million app downloads in 2020, according to an exclusive meta-analysis by The Ken based on data sourced from App Annie and Apptopia, two of the sharpest app analytics companies out there. That’s nearly double of what TikTok did in 2019.
The short video app from ByteDance wasn’t just incredibly successful at hooking Asians, it was also a canny shapeshifter. Was it competing with Facebook and Instagram for social media time? Or was it competing against Google’s YouTube for video time? Or was it spurring the category for video-editing apps?
All of the above? No damn wonder it was prejudicial to every other app out there.
App downloads are perhaps the best (or least bad) proxy to understand the success of internet companies and products. Because, for one, they’re incredibly hard to make happen. The average US user downloads a grand total of zero (net) new apps on their smartphones each month.
Had it not been for the pandemic, Southeast Asian users were headed there too, with overall 2019 app downloads lower than the preceding year. But the pandemic did happen. And it did lift the fortunes of many app-driven companies.
In today’s rich data story that melds together original graphics and sharp observations, Jum Balea, Prajakta Patil and Jon Russell partner to help decipher the shifting app tea leaves in Southeast Asia.
In video streaming, as iflix and HOOQ faded away and Netflix shimmied in, where did consumer preferences head?
Is the TikTok-inspired video editing app segment, and players like InShot, KineMaster and PicsArt for real?
