Good Morning Ishan,
Nykaa, India’s largest beauty products retailer, is a great example of staying focused on a segment and executing well on a planned roadmap. In just 7 years, it has established itself as a benchmark for what a vertical-focused commerce platform should look like.
- Spotted a large, untapped vertical market: check (the market for beauty and personal care products is worth ~$13-14 billion annually)
- Found its target customer segment and gave them more of what they wanted: check (60% of its revenue comes from repeat customers)
- Defied conventional wisdom to reach customers: check (for an e-commerce platform, it launched its first physical store after just 2 years, of which it has 39 today)
- Delivered triple-digit growth: check (150% growth the last year, and over 100% this year)
And yet—or perhaps because—Nykaa has become the prime target for a bunch of companies, large and small, online and offline. From Flipkart, Myntra and Amazon to Shoppers Stop, Health & Glow and NewU. The multi-billion dollar horizontal staples-to-smartphones e-commerce sector is now a quagmire of deep discounts and fleeting customer loyalties. Vertical commerce is where everyone sees growth and profits.
Nykaa knows this. Which is why it has rapidly iterated its offerings. From premium to mass. From online to offline. From third-party brands to private ones. And critically, from beauty products to apparel too.
Nykaa’s founder, former investment banker Falguni Nayar has till now done a great job of executing on the promises she’s made her investors. Which is why Nykaa is worth over $700 million today. Can she continue to do that?
