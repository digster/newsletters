Good Morning Ishan,
 
This might be a little late, but have you seen Hannah Gadsby’s Netflix special ‘Nanette’? If you haven’t already, I recommend it highly. It is a riveting experience across the entirety of its one hour-odd runtime.

But, among all the poignant lessons the show leaves you with, there’s one line that really resonates with me after what has been an exacting week: “I identify as tired”. At the end of yet another work week, I imagine many of you feel the same.

The weekend is good that way—it’s a time to rest. A time to recharge. But, most importantly, a time to reflect. A time to take stock. Be it a business or an individual, taking stock is important. This week’s stories at The Ken covered a wide range of businesses who, through fortune or force, are at a point where they need to do exactly that—examine the situation they’re in, how they got there, and how to move forward. 

The week began with Shashi’s report on the struggles of one of India’s oldest newspapers, The Hindu. To survive, it is willing to make tough choices.

Tuesday saw Praveen’s debut as a reporter. The first of a two-parter that looks at IKEA’s imminent entry into India, this one saw Praveen look at the companies that IKEA is looking to displace—Pepperfry and Urban Ladder. Both face an existential crisis as IKEA's arrival looms large.

While all these companies are still far from doomed, the week’s free story saw Sumanth analyse the decline of a once proud company. Hike, touted as a potential WhatsApp-beater, now looks destined for the scrap heap. There are mistakes to learn from, but is it too late?

From a unicorn that has fallen on dark days, to a unicorn that’s destined for great things—PolicyBazaar. Ruhi’s piece examines the newest Indian company to join the unicorn club, and explains how it intends to take over the healthcare insurance market.

Rounding off the week was Arundhati’s piece on Amazon Web Services (AWS). The market leader in cloud infrastructure in India, it’s facing a mini-exodus of customers. This is a first for AWS. How it reads the situation and responds to it will determine its future in the Indian market.
