Thorny topic, believe me. So, when I was joining The Ken last year, I can’t tell you the number of people who came to me with some concerned version of this: I hear you are joining The Ken. Yup. So, it will be a subscription service. Yup. Paid? Yup. Do you think people will pay? Yup. A lot of people? Yup, I guess. I don’t think so, people have enough good stuff to read already. Ohkay, I’m not sure about this, so let’s see. Repeat. Also, some conversations didn’t end with best of luck. 
 
And now lookie here, just twelve months in. Ola Share Pass. Zomato Gold. MakeMyTrip Black. MakeMyTrip Double Black. Netflix. Hotstar. Amazon Prime. And of course, Times Internet’s digital media thingie which should come out soon.  
 
My colleague Venkat says that while subscription businesses have been around for years, they’ve mostly been niche, especially in the startup ecosystem. The Tail Lovers Company. Being Juliet. Flintobox. These are just some examples. But now the big guys are coming in. 
 
A lot of it is because of Amazon Prime and Netflix. They’ve become sort of habit forming and also, right time, right place. Plus, frictionless payment. Ajith Karimpana, founder and CEO of Furlenco, says something interesting. He says that the biggest advantage of a subscription business is the predictability of revenue. “Which means you don’t have to sell all the time to survive, unlike the sales-only model. The fee the customer pays for use of the service is a guarantee of sorts.”
