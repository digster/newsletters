Good Morning Ishan,

I am a 25-day-old HR leader at The Ken. When I joined on 1 April, I was looking forward to going to work and meeting new people. But Covid-19 struck the world and everything changed. I don't think we realised just how much.

Since then, I’ve been forced to challenge many of the mental models I had accepted to be true. 

Mental Model 1: I do not like working from home. I was genuinely unproductive at home. But when I started working for The Ken, the organisation had already been working from home for a couple of weeks.

Mental Model 2: I need face-to-face interaction as an HR practitioner in order to build good relationships and do my job well. I have not been able to physically meet even one of the 40 eager and fantastic team members who make The Ken what it is.

Mental Model 3: HR is about enabling people—who indirectly or directly bring in revenues—to be more productive. Everyone at The Ken brings in revenues—from the reporters and editors, whose stories are critical to driving subscriptions, to the tech team who ensure a seamless customer experience to retain these subscribers. 

But how could I bring in revenue? We're answering that question with a bold experiment—I will be taking up a business role with real targets alongside my HR responsibilities, given my varied HR and business experience.

Mental Model 4: I write a lot on LinkedIn, where I share my stories and experiences in life. But I joined The Ken to be an HR leader, not to write. Three weeks in, here I am, writing my first email to The Ken’s discerning and engaged subscriber base. You can bet your bottom dollar that it will not be my last.

This week, our stories also touched on long-held models. Some stories spoke about models that had already been shattered. Others argued for them to be broken. 

Tuesday's story https://the-ken.com/story/covid-exit-strategy/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter by Seema, for example. ICMR's plans for the pandemic haven't been encouraging—it's not open to ideas from experts, it hasn't released enough data, it's not testing enough. Seema argues that if ICMR gets a few things in order—breaks its mental models, as it were—there's a real chance at building immunity shields. 

Kunal's story, on Monday, was of the first kind—of mental models that had to be broken in response to the pandemic. He analysed how the BPO industry—heavily dependent on humans—turned https://the-ken.com/story/bpo-india-automation/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter to automation and chatbots. This may be necessary for the industry to survive and eventually return to a path of growth.

Wednesday's story on Airbnb by Vandana chronicled https://the-ken.com/story/airbnb-trust-stress-test/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter how the company's insistence on using the same strategy it has worldwide has not worked in India. And it's probably not going to. It will now have to come up with an India-specific strategy during this time of uncertainty or perish. 

Pranav's story on Thursday really challenged https://the-ken.com/story/when-in-a-pandemic-sell-hygiene/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter our mental model of implicitly trusting services for their hygiene and how that has now taken a hit. It also showed what various businesses, particularly restaurants, are doing to rebuild trust with their consumer base.

The last story of the week, by Arundhati, was about how India’s largest stockbroking firm Zerodha is seeing unprecedented volumes https://the-ken.com/story/for-zerodha-covid-is-a-ladder/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. Markets are trending downwards but that hasn’t stopped users—in fact, many of these traders are first-timers. With a recession just around the corner, however, firms like Zerodha may have to brace themselves. 

That's it from me today. Stay safe and spend time with your family, whether in person or otherwise. And be grateful for this time. It can rebuild many bonds we may take for granted (Mental Model 5).
