184.
 
Driven by targets for collection and prosecution, there has been a rise of 184% in the number of prosecution complaints filed by the Income Tax department in India. 184% year on year.
 
I don’t know about you, but when I read this sentence, I was a bit stunned. Don’t know how else to put it. Is this the fate that small businesses have to meet to do business in India? There has certainly been no dearth of difficulties when it comes to doing business these last few years; our recent past looks turbulent, whichever way you look at it. There’s demonetisation. Then compliance for Goods & Services Tax (GST). Then, Angel Tax notices. And then prosecution notices for delays in paying TDS.
 
Businesses have stories of suffering.
 
“Sometimes, I think it is stuff like Angel Tax which gets all the limelight,” says one particular businessman, who requested not to be named because he is afraid of retribution. “I don’t blame the media because all of them have their audiences. But if you are thinking about doing business in this country, and where so many are small businesses, then it is the several other things, smaller ones which the income tax department is doing that is hurting businesses, and nobody wants to talk about it.”
 
Today’s story from my colleague Sidhartha is on them and tax terror. “Entrepreneurs and small business owners are dealing with several issues from the tax department,” says Sidhartha. “These aren’t just harmful to the free market, but could also hurt the Indian government’s ‘Make in India’ and ‘Startup India’ initiatives.”
 
Here, I’d say Stand Up India, but I see the irony.
