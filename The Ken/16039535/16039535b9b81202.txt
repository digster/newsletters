Good Morning Ishan,

Some weeks really fly by, don't they? As I look through the stories we've covered this week to write this letter out, I am surprised at all that we've scratched our heads over, poured all our morning coffees into, added, removed, added again, just to generate that one sharp story that reaches you every day.

Oh who am I kidding, that's pretty much every week at The Ken. And oddly, for a process that involves so many minds working, questioning, debating each other, it sure doesn't seem to take all that long! And so, another week flies by.

This one started with Seema's Biocon piece https://the-ken.com/behind-biocons-payday/ where?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter she talked about the 1 December US FDA approval that put a seal on the quality of biosimilars that the biotech has been developing for over a decade. The space for biosimilars is crowding, and Biocon has got itself a headstart.

Tuesday https://the-ken.com/round-one-paisabazaar-scores-bankbazaar/ had?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Arundhati pit PaisaBazaar and BankBazaar against each other. And at round one, the score reads PaisaBazaar 1: 0 BankBazaar.

Payal and Patanjali—The Ken's e-commerce alliance—wrote about Flipkart's anti-Amazon alliance on Wednesday https://the-ken.com/flipkart-first-part-deux/.?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter The piece focused on Flipkart First, and well, Amazon later? 

Towards the end of the week, we had an uncharacteristic but fascinating piece from Ashish https://the-ken.com/carnation-for-clunkers/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Carnation Auto. It gave us a glimpse into the mind of 75-year-old eternal optimist Jagdish Khattar who is holding onto the company's last vestiges.

The week ended with Sidhartha's FREE piece https://the-ken.com/facebook-anonymous-astroturfing-problem/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Facebook's astroturfing problem. Calling for an intervention, the piece looked into how easy it is for astroturfers to steal credit card details, use them for Facebook ads, return the money in due time, and stay anonymous.

Now, if you still can't read all our pieces, you probably should hit that subscribe button? Just a friendly suggestion. Honest.

Have a great weekend!
