[The death of the independent doctor](https://the-ken.com/story/the-death-of-the-independent-doctor/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
Prohibitive costs, competition from hospital chains, and the pressure of online ratings have punctured the dreams of young medics
The rising cost of medical degrees and costs to set up independent practice are leaving half of India’s doctors drowning in debt. Despite super-specialty degrees costing Rs 2–3 crore, the corporate job market demands more—a good patient base for starters. While struggling to find their footing, young doctors also have to deal with rising rents, competition from corporate clinics, and acing the social-media game. Medical careers can be rewarding, but only for those who can endure. For the others, there are side-gigs or jobs overseas. Abhishek Ganguly’s one-year-old company wants to defy the industry norm by manufacturing shoes in-house—a move that most Indian startups would shy away from
Agilitas, fresh off its Mochiko acquisition, notched up Rs 740 crore in annual revenue. And this was before it even dipped its toes into the consumer market. Mochiko manufactures shoes for international brands such as Adidas, Puma, New Balance, Skechers, Reebok, Asics, Crocs, Decathlon, Clarks, and US Polo. Agilitas also got an exclusive license to bring Italy’s Lotto to India, aiming to cater to both the premium- and the mass-market. In a departure from the sportswear playbook, the company operates a vertically integrated model, overseeing the entire lifecycle from manufacturing to retail.
