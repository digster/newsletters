I have a confession to make. I love watching ‘finfluencers’ on social media (that’s finance + influencer, for those who don’t know). I think it gives my doomscrolling a sense of purpose; an illusion of having learned something.
I’ve also noticed that people either love them or hate them. Nobody is ever neutral on this subject. I think a colleague of mine summed it up pretty well: “finfluencers are fine, but everything they say should come with an asterisk.” There is really no blanket advice that can be applied by all people when it comes to investing.
But credit where credit is due—finfluencers have definitely made investing and saving cool again.
What’s helped me personally is setting financial goals. Usually things I’d like to achieve in a year or less. Like, replace my phone, get Amma a gift, save up for a trip. So, I fall back on Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs) with short lock-in periods instead of betting on high-risk, high-return products with long lock-in periods. I think understanding your aspirations, risk appetite, and time horizons is really the key to choosing investment products that work for you.
The same goes for careers. We all have an internal riskometer when we decide which jobs to invest in. Some yield returns slowly, but can transform your life. Others offer intense bursts of growth, but don’t last very long. It depends, really.
You can see this playing out in the job market right now. Some are disillusioned with Big Tech and startups, and so they’re moving to traditional firms with sound fundamentals. Others are betting on early-stage startups, and making themselves indispensable to their growth journeys. In this week’s episode of Cost to Company, I’ve tried to faithfully capture the pulse of the times we live in and identify what the new ‘hot’ sectors for tech talent are. You don’t want to miss this one.
