Be it The Ramayana, the ancient Indian epic, or even JRR Tolkien's The Lord of the Rings, the idea of coming home has been a central theme of some of the greatest stories ever told.
"There and back again," as Tolkien so simply puts it.
It's easy to see why coming home is such a popular narrative. It's about a return to familiarity, stability, and security. And in uncertain times—and have they ever been more so—it's a comforting thought.
In 2020, the protagonist of today's story, HT Media's business daily Mint, began its own journey home. Mint started out in 2007 in partnership with the world's leading business publication, the Wall Street Journal (WSJ). That lasted a whole seven years. Now, almost seven years later, Mint is coming back to where it started, partnering with WSJ once more as part of an online subscription package.
This return to its roots comes on the back of Mint's somewhat tumultuous journey over the past few years. Sure, it quickly grew into India's second-biggest business daily. However, the paper has undergone layoffs, a change in layout—from Berliner to broadsheet—and a digital revamp to cope with the treacherous business terrain media houses are increasingly faced with. Now, with its parent HT Media slipping into losses, Mint is hoping to find succour in the partnership it began with.
Will the WSJ tie-up be the deliverance Mint craves? And can it convince users who come for the former to stay for Mint's own journalism?
