Good Morning Ishan,
India’s apex skill-development body, the National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC), is in need of an urgent intervention.
The body responsible to skill India’s millions—often seen as a solution to the country’s unemployment crisis—can’t seem to hang onto its leadership.
NSDC currently has no managing director, CEO, or chairperson. In fact, the position of chairperson—once filled by stalwarts such as former Tata Consultancy Services CEO S Ramadorai—has been vacant for over three years. Even the CEO position hasn’t been filled since 2021.
Though it started as a financial body, providing funding and loans to training institutes looking to run skill-development initiatives, with the launch of the Skill India Mission in 2015, it was designated as the implementation body for skilling schemes.
Now, NSDC’s problems are reflecting on the ground, too. Only 15% of the 16 million people trained under India’s flagship skilling scheme, the Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana, have secured job placements, according to government data. On top of this, NSDC training partners routinely get caught conducting fake training and cashing in on government money.
