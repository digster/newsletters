Good Morning Ishan,

Microsoft, Google, AWS. Big tech in India is having an HR moment.

The most visible, of course, was Intel India chief Nivruti Rai leaving the semiconductor giant in June after 29 years. She now heads Invest India, the national investment promotion and facilitation agency.

Rai was active on social media, so her departure from Intel became very public. The stuff Pratap writes about today is rarely discussed in India—how giant tech companies function and what kind of people lead them.

Microsoft India’s head for nearly seven years, Anant Maheshwari, is leaving. In a closed WhatsApp group, he shared this last week:

“Am likely to have a larger canvas to do more for India. Due to the confidentiality process, I cannot share the upcoming adventure for the next four weeks…”

The Ken has learnt there’s talk among executives at Microsoft and Google that Maheshwari is likely headed to Jio Platforms, the digital-services arm of oil-to-telecom conglomerate Reliance Industries (RIL), as its new chief executive. However, an RIL executive speaking off the record denied it.

Going by how Microsoft India grew its business under Maheshwari, he’s arguably the most successful leader the company has ever had.

At Amazon Web Services (AWS), India and South Asia leader Puneet Chandok has stepped down and is expected to step into Maheshwari’s role at Microsoft.

Google is looking for a replacement for Bikram Singh Bedi, who currently heads the cloud business in India. Bedi may move into another role.

But the hero of today’s story is Maheshwari, widely regarded for his sharpness and duly credited for taking Microsoft close to its internal goal of $5 billion in revenue from India by 2025.

“He is known more for his high IQ (intelligence quotient) than EQ (emotional quotient). He didn’t have many friends in the organisation.”

Surely, that can’t be the reason he is leaving Microsoft.
