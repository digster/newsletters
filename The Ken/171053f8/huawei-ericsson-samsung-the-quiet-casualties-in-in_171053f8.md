Even before the world was consumed by the pandemic, there was another bug circulating in India. The regulatory virus. Not novel, not contagious, but it led to sporadic health attacks in sector after sector.
Companies which had financial immunity or could buy protective gear could pivot, scramble or survive. Others saw value destruction. Telecom is a good example. Nearly hollowed out, the second order effects are now too big to hide for the telecom tech vendors.
Huawei, Ericsson and Samsung have been preparing for it for the last year or so. They have reduced their India headcount anywhere between 10-50%. For instance, Huawei’s core network division had 1,100 employees in 2019, today it’s 600. It’ll go down to 500, in all likelihood. Samsung, meanwhile, has laid off close to 1,000 (both on- and off-roll), while ZTE India has handed pink slips to 230 employees between January and March this year.
Layoffs aren’t uncommon in the telecom sector. Every time a vendor loses a circle, it prunes its staff strength. This time, say executives, it is different. Agnostic to both geography and function. From corporate offices in the National Capital Region (NCR) and Mumbai, to telecom circle offices stretching from Bihar to Tamil Nadu.
Huawei had a stellar year in FY2019. But in FY2020, reports Pratap, revenues will likely fall 35-40%. It sent back several Chinese expatriates it employed in India. “One Chinese expat is equal to two to three Indians [in terms of remuneration].”
Part of this outcome is due to the march of technology; part due to Jio’s slowing juggernaut; and part due to companies not seeing the writing on the wall. I mean, the Vodafone global CEO, until recently, thought India wasn’t ready for 4G.
On a sector that is dominated by multinationals, and hence lacking in granular reporting, Pratap has a solid story today.
