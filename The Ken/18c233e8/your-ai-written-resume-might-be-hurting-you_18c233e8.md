Good Morning Ishan,
‘Perfect imperfect’ is what came to mind when I read today’s story by Alifiya. This oxymoron can be interpreted in two ways: What is perfect is imperfect, and what is imperfect is perfect. Now, this may sound like one of Swami Nithyananda’s gems. But trust me, it’s a handy tool in arguments with the always-perfect folks.
Turns out, hiring managers are also using this tool to vet résumés in the age of artificial intelligence (AI). Perfect résumés written by ChatGPT and the AI ilk are finding their way, slowly but steadily, to the dustbin, while the imperfect yet authentic ones are getting the green flags. Delicious irony.
Actually, it’s more complicated than that. The AI-written résumé can get employment hopefuls a foot in the door only to eventually force them out. Job seekers should pay heed, especially in these tough times.
Nearly half of them are now relying on AI tools to write either their résumés, cover letters or both, according to a survey. However, HR folks are wizening up to see through the fancy words.
But is AI use always undesirable in résumés? Not really, say recruiters. The trick lies in using it smartly and efficiently without making it too much of a good thing. But how? Where should one draw the line?
