Good Morning Ishan,
What’s happening? How about a personal story today? When I was a student, one thing that scared the bejesus out of me: finding a job. I was wrapping up my graduation in IT and I sat down with a professor, a retired old man from a multinational, to talk about my career. I told him, I didn’t want to pursue a career in IT. I wanted to be a journalist. A music journalist. A rock journalist. I wanted to write on bands in India and abroad. The professor told me I could apply as an intern at one of the big publishing houses, see if they would hire me and work my way up the ladder. I spoke to a friend who was working at a national daily back then. He said an internship would involve a lot of pleading, grunt work and all of it would have very little to do with journalism. But I wanted to be in a newsroom. I wrote several emails. Nothing came of it. It was heartbreaking.
Eleven years later, when Venkat pitched the story on a weekly edit call, I couldn’t stop thinking about that evening sitting on the roof of my apartment building with a friend, listening to Nothing Else Matters by Metallica and wondering what next. A little emo, I know, but I was just 21.
That’s what this story addresses. What next. And like you can guess, a company decided to take a crack at it. Internshala is a company, which claims to be catering to 2-3 million registered students from around the country, with over 80,000 firms scouting its platform to hunt for prospective interns. It has, so far, been hustling to get students a taste of what is to come in real life and getting them internships at companies across India. It hopes that some of these internships can culminate into a job.
It is all about hope, isn’t it? Anyway, here’s Venkat’s story. Do read it.
