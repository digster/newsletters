Good Morning Ishan,
If Aadit Palicha is to be believed, Zepto doesn’t just make groceries materialise at your doorstep in a few ticks of time. It’s also going to mint India’s newest millionaires over the next five years. That’s the picture being painted by the co-founder while his company is on an ambitious recruitment drive—at a time when other firms are trimming their headcounts.
That vision isn’t conjured out of thin air. When Swiggy went public in November 2024, the net worth of 500 of its staff shot above Rs 1 crore. Zepto plans to top that in the fourth quarter this year, so Palicha is dangling the same dream in front of talented people who can join the company’s senior echelons.
The notion of building generational wealth is just one component of Zepto’s campaign to bulk up its ranks. Moving down the rungs, the company also reportedly poached several of the 1,000 delivery partners who jumped the ship from Blinkit.
Becoming a part of Zepto, however, is hardly a cakewalk. Going by employees who have shared their experiences in the quick-commerce company’s halls, some staffers are expected to join meetings late at night and grind through every weekend to complete the work that will get them stock options.
Suprita and Aakriti worked together to show us the view from the top in Zepto and also explored general sentiment within the rank and file. Today’s story captures two vastly different moods within Zepto, and the distance between them may grow as Zepto heads towards its IPO.
