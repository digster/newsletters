Good Morning Ishan,
Who knew you could find bits of Greek philosophy in India’s travel-tech landscape?
Back in the 2nd century, Plutarch raised a rather timeless question: if a ship is restored by replacing all its parts, is it the same ship? The question went on to inspire countless people, including filmmaker Anand Gandhi (who created the film Ship of Theseus) and, surprisingly enough, Aloke Bajpai, the CEO and managing director of online travel aggregator Ixigo.
Ixigo has undergone countless pivots over the years—from a meta-search engine, to an app for all things trains, to becoming a full-fledged online travel agency. It’s admittedly arrived late to the flight-and-hotel-booking party, but that hasn’t stopped it from growing faster than the broader travel-tech market for the last five quarters. Or so it claims in its latest earnings call.
Perhaps most significantly, it now has a new passenger on board its ship.
In October, tech-investment firm Prosus acquired a 15% stake in Ixigo for Rs 1,800 crore—making it the largest external investor in the company. Interestingly enough, the firm earlier held a near-40% stake in Ixigo’s rival Makemytrip, but ended up exiting in a cash-swap deal before the going got really good. It’s now hopped on board the “only travel-tech player that can challenge Makemytrip”, according to a travel-industry executive.
But as margins in train and air travel continue to be thin, and hotels present their own unique challenge, can a Prosus-backed Ixigo navigate the choppy waters ahead?
