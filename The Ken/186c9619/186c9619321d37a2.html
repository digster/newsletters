Good Morning Ishan,
 
Consider this: Company A raises $150 million in the seventh year of its existence. The fundraise, which values the company at $450 million, is one of the largest early-stage rounds in India.
 
So, it raises eyebrows.
 
But then, company A resembles company B, which in 15 years has grown to dominate its business and is among the few Indian tech startups to have gone public.
 
So, it's not surprising that investors were willing to write company A such a big cheque, right?
 
The story is not all that straightforward, though.
 
Even if company A has the body of company B, its soul is more like that of company C—a 67-year-old, state-run giant that is the very antithesis of a tech venture.
 
So, what explains the unbounded optimism of company A's investors?
 
That's the question my colleague Rounak sets out to answer in his story on Insurancedekho (company A). An offshoot of the used-car platform Cardekho, it sells insurance online like Policybazaar (company B). But most of its business comes from its 80,000 agents in over 1,300 towns—a strategy synonymous with Life Insurance Corporation of India (company C).
 
A contradiction that deserves the kind of analysis we're serving up today: https://the-ken.com/story/450m-insurancedekho-looks-like-policybazaar-operates-like-lic/ (9-minute read)
