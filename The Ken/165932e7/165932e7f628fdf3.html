Good Morning Ishan,
 
Coincidence is but a charm.
 
I am telling you this because not long back, I was on my usual, daily break. Loitering. Thinking. Middle of the day. Bright sun. Outside a popular cafe. When Saurabh Mukherjea walks out. Grizzly hair, rectangular specks, dressed in his usual, full sleeves, formal shirt and denims. Nice, lazy sway, the way he walks. You know, the kind of man who is difficult to miss in the humdrum of daily life, if you’ve seen him once.
 
Saurabh, back then, was the CEO of Ambit Capital, a leading brokerage firm in India. One of the most respected stock market commentators in the country. Contrarian, clear communicator, precise, strong views on the markets and the economy; stuff you’d associate with a man who knows what he is doing and knows very well that others know this too.
 
So, I go: “Hi.”
 
Saurabh: “Hey, Hi...How’s it going? I keep thinking about what I can do to help you guys. What you guys are doing is amazing…”
Me: “Thanks…Ha ha, I will take you up on this.”
 
Small talk only goes so far. And that was that. But a couple of months later, I reached out to Saurabh. “Thought I’ll take you up on it. Do you think you can find the time to write for The Ken.”
 
Not long after, he said yes.
 
We are very selective about people who write for The Ken. This is proof of that. For the last two months, we have been prepping towards it. Our first series. 10 stories in all. One story published on the first Saturday of every month. Written by Saurabh Mukherjea and Anupam Gupta. Which brings me to Anupam. A chartered accountant, Anupam has had a flourishing career in the stock market, as an analyst, as a consultant, and as a writer. Anupam’s wisdom on investing only matches his celebrity status on the microblogging website, Twitter.
 
Both of them have teamed up to write on the limits of the human mind.
 
A, how we can understand and appreciate this realisation. And B, how can we work around it to become better individuals. Our writers believe that in the complicated world we live in today, keeping our lives simple—through thoughts and actions—will make our life easier and happier. Spirituality and philosophy have always underscored the value of simplicity. The Simplicity Paradigm upends ideas of multi-tasking and of heroic leadership, and over ten parts, it will help us understand the implications across business and investing.
 
Over the next ten months, Saurabh and Anupam will take you through concepts in psychology, critical reasoning, economic theory and investing with a singular objective: To help you make better decisions and live a better life. Every chapter has clear takeaways for action and leadership. Further reading material. Like every piece at The Ken, the writing is sharp and approachable.
