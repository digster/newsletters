Good Morning Ishan,

When I moved to Bangalore six years ago, all I carried with me were two gigantic suitcases filled with clothes and a vague understanding that I had to find myself a place to live, but I figured things would sort themselves out. Ha! 

It was a disorganised and mildly chaotic time, but I managed to find a house to live in, only to realise that it was entirely devoid of furniture, something I forgot to factor into my 'things-adults-need' checklist. Ergo, some more disorganisation and chaos. 

Eventually, things worked out, and I am happy to report that I now have two functioning sofas. The Ken's stories this week also follow this theme of 'disorganisation', of companies trying to organise fragmented markets.  

On Monday, Olina wrote on https://the-ken.com/story/ev-local-manufacture-policy/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the roadblocks facing localisation of electric-vehicles from a 67-year-old auto industry and constant policy shifts. She argues that a lack of research in EV technology and specialised skills have hampered production and kept prices high. Enter disorganisation. 

Arundhati and Pranav's story https://the-ken.com/story/rental-economy/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Wednesday looked at India's burgeoning rental economy, as companies try to cash in on people willing to move far from home for work. Everything from furniture, electric scooters and water purifiers can now be rented, with the promise of trying to organise millennials lives.

On Thursday, Vandana looked at https://the-ken.com/story/livspace-ikea-furniture/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter another company trying to organise India's furniture market, as furniture giant IKEA looks to standardise the interior business with its investment in Livspace. 

Tuesday's story by Ruhi talked about https://the-ken.com/story/resisting-antibiotics-indias-superbug/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter India's war against superbugs—a three-year study shows we are antibiotic resistant to most bacteria. She argues that the central government needs to act fast, starting with stocking up on drugs for drug-resistant tuberculosis. This is a free read, so do share widely. 

We ended the week with Sumanth and Rohin https://the-ken.com/story/oyo-startup-kayfabe/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter rendering Shashi Tharoor's 'farrago' passe, with 'kayfabe', or more specifically OYO's art of the startup kayfabe, the world of performative entrepreneurship. Google the word while you read the story.
