A bottomless appetite for acquisitions and a gushing private-equity tap have reshaped India’s healthcare industry in recent years.

Even so, Pune’s Sahyadri Hospitals sticks out. In 2019, its founders sold the company to PE firm Everstone for Rs 1,000 crore. Three years later, Ontario Teachers’ Pension Plan bought Sahyadri for Rs 2,500 crore.

Now, Fortis Healthcare, Temasek-backed Manipal Health Enterprises, and Blackstone-owned Quality Care are in a race to buy Sahyadri for Rs 5,500–6,000 crore. That’s a six-fold jump in valuation for an unlisted healthcare chain in as many years.

Eight of Sahyadri’s 11 hospitals are in a single city (Pune). On key metrics such as occupancy and average revenue per occupied bed, Sahyadri doesn’t turn heads.

So why is it still catnip to some of the industry’s biggest names? Aakriti has the story.
