Good Morning Ishan,

This headline, or variations of it, was everywhere over the weekend: 

Blinkit more valuable than Zomato’s food-delivery business, says Goldman Sachs

But this isn’t surprising at all. 

Zomato chief executive Deepinder Goyal himself has said https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/technology/startup-mahakumbh-blinkit-will-be-bigger-than-zomato-in-a-year-deepinder-goyal/articleshow/108581415.cms?from=mdr Blinkit, a quick-commerce company Zomato acquired in June 2022, will be bigger than its mainstay in a year. 

And Blinkit, which has been trimming losses consistently, is expected to be profitable this year. 

https://the-ken.com/?post_type=trade_tricks&p=1406005?utm_source=ro_hdr&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=View in Browser https://the-ken.com/?post_type=trade_tricks&p=1406005?utm_source=ro_hdr&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=

So it stands to reason investors and analysts would want to ascribe a bigger chunk of Zomato’s market cap to Blinkit than to food delivery. 

What really caught my eye in Goldman Sachs’ report is how quickly quick commerce has gone from sidekick to protagonist in the online-grocery saga. 

In case you don’t realise how seismic this is, let me remind you that until April 2021, when Zepto began operations, there was no such thing as instant grocery delivery in India. If you wanted to buy household essentials online, you placed an order and waited for them to be shipped in a few hours or the following morning. Hence the term “slotted delivery”. You couldn’t start making tea in the hope that milk would arrive just in time. 

Everyone knew online grocers such as Bigbasket would be forced to move from next-day delivery to shipping within a few hours. But few thought delivery in 10–15 minutes would increasingly become the norm. 

Goldman Sachs estimates the total value of quick-commerce orders in the year ended March 2024 to be US$5 billion. That’s almost half the online-grocery market. In other words, 0–50% in three years flat, with most of that coming in the past year or so. 

If that isn’t startling enough, quick commerce’s market share in online grocery is likely to touch 70% in another 2–3 years. Even Bigbasket, owned by the Tata Group, gets more quick-commerce orders than those for slotted delivery.
