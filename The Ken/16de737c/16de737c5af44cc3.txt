Good Morning Ishan,

I used to be a voracious reader. Like, gobble-three-books-a-week reader, a sucker for a well-crafted story. Wordplay that made one groan, a narrative to die for, and a tale that kept me reading far beyond my bedtime — that’s what writing was all about for me. 

One of the first stories that viscerally stuck with me all my life is the excitingly pulpy The Count of Monte Cristo. I read it when I was 13 and I’ve gone back to it again and again, even devouring Jeffrey Archer’s adaptation of the novel. The story moves through so many arcs and has so many ups and downs, and at the end, when Dantes finally gets the revenge he has been plotting for so long, the 13-year-old me felt like she’d gotten her revenge too.

Frustratingly, my reading dropped off as I grew older, and I lost touch with all those stories that made me want a career associated with language and writing. But I’m getting back into the swing of things. There have been more stories and reading in my life in the last three weeks I’ve been at The Ken than this entire year. Mostly because it’s my job, but also because I’ve been slowly discovering the joy behind it, once again. 

This week saw plenty of such riveting, ups-and-downs stories at The Ken, the kind that made me forget to take edit notes and grab a bag of popcorn instead. 

Arundhati kicked things off on Monday with PhonePe’s story https://the-ken.com/story/how-phonepe-went-from-cameo-to-starring-role-in-flipkarts-show/?utm_source=daily_story%2Frecap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter of outgrowing its parent company, Flipkart. From being valued at $2 billion in 2018, the payments company is inching closer and closer to Flipkart’s own valuation--something that took it nearly a decade to achieve--in less than two years. With Walmart now in the picture, how are their equations going to change? What next for PhonePe? How much longer before someone writes this into a Bollywood script? (That bit wasn’t in the story, but we’re willing to take bets either way.)

Tuesday saw Ruhi break out the big guns https://the-ken.com/story/bmgf-vaccines-india-govt/?utm_source=daily_story%2Frecap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter with the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation. The non-profit built a vaccine market in India, helping to fund both vaccine makers and also footing part of the government’s tab, but now they need to exit. And the only way they can pull it off is if the Indian government steps in. And the only way to do that is to make friends with the government. Will they? Won’t They? Did they? 

Midweek came a palate cleanser in the form of an in-depth look https://the-ken.com/story/zomato-gold-report/?utm_source=daily_story%2Frecap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter at Zomato’s recent revenue report by our, I’ve been told, in-house allrounder Sumanth. In just two years, the former food aggregator went from a company claiming profitability to one that is losing hundreds of millions of dollars annually. Sumanth traced Zomato’s journey to find out just how, and why, this state of affairs came to be. And also concluded that Zomato probably needed better, erm, data chart makers. 

I like puns and if there was ever a story that tempted me to include as many I could fit in without being yelled at, it was Suraksha’s on Thursday. She whey-ed in https://the-ken.com/story/whey-out-protein-deficient-india-big-on-importing-supplements/?utm_source=daily_story%2Frecap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on why India, the world’s largest dairy producer, imported most of its whey, a by-product of cheese-making. Here’s this grand opportunity to make money and dairy companies are instead paying someone else to make whey while the sun shined. (Hey, here’s a whey (sorry) to make me stop making atrocious puns. Click on the story.)

Our policy writer Pratap, along with Arundhati, closed out the week with their comprehensive look https://the-ken.com/story/delayed-data-laws-hanging-e-commerce-rules-indias-policy-logjam?utm_source=daily_story%2Frecap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter at India’s policy logjam when it came to data and technology. The year is winding down and we still don’t have a firm line on e-commerce, 5G spectrum or data protection. Meanwhile, NBFC account aggregators are staring at an uncertain future. What is set to happen is a question mark no one wants to answer, but this primer might just give you an understanding about what’s going on. 

That’s it for this week. We’ll be back with more absorbing narratives in the business world (and more terrible puns if my bosses let me). 

Till then, do give me book suggestions to get back on the reading wagon. Hope you have a great weekend!
