Good Morning Ishan,

If there was one banner year for financial technology companies, it was 2018. High on growth hormones like Aadhaar, India Stack, FOMO-led easy capital, Big Tech-supplied data troves, fintechs started the year with much promise. By the time the year ended, sentiments had taken a serious turn.

In 2019, a year of general elections, a change of guard at the apex bank and regulatory reality checks, expect less irrational exuberance, more sobriety. While we’ll continue to see fintechs make strange bedfellows—foodtech get into lending, ride-hailing into prepaid cards—those with weaker business models will find it rough, starting with the lenders. (As many as 64 lending companies received over $400 million in funding during 2018.)

Arundhati, who has been closely tracking fintech this past year, says these aren’t the only lot that need to get used to a new life; India Stack and iSpirt, forces behind some of the platform tech, will also have to change tracks. Why and how? Will that affect the incumbents and the challengers?

Speaking of which, one of the largest, incumbent and challenger by turns, will be closely watched. Quoting from the story today: “It has not helped that Paytm has been distracted over the last year. Between Paytm Mall’s future as an e-commerce company, a payments bank that was caught in regulatory hurdles, and a payments company that has to face an onslaught of competition, the company has multiple fires to put out.”

With a variety of products, fintechs got the Indian market to move in 2018, learning tough lessons along the way. 2019 will see which companies learnt them best, says Arundhati.
