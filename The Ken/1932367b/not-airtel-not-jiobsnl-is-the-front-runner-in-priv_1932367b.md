Good Morning Ishan,
Have you heard of private 5G? I hadn’t until I read Seema’s eye-opener today on this transformative telecom technology and how an unlikely player holds the key to its rollout and success in India.
Private 5G is, well, private. Unlike public 5G that covers wide areas, private-5G networks are dedicated, localised, and deployed on-site for specific industrial applications. From mines to ports, private 5G’s myriad and very useful use cases are already finding much traction abroad.
Not so in India, though—even as some small but spirited players have the chops and are more than willing to get going. The spirits are willing, but the bodies are weak. Giant bodies. A few years back, India’s big telecom boys, Jio and Airtel, couldn’t stop raving about how their 5G offerings were the next best thing after sliced bread for the country’s mobile and internet consumers. Just that their multi-billion dollar investments didn’t make them much money. Until recently, when they hiked prices together (no cartel, please; we are Indian), they posted their best profit growth in a long time. Now, making this spectrum available for private-5G niche plays would be a distraction from the pursuit of profits. Not done.
Enter an eternal laggard that’s been propped up by billions in taxpayer money for as long as one remembers. It’s almost poetic—BSNL, a nearly hopeless state-run white elephant, is now the beacon of hope for many Indian product and service companies keen to roll out private 5G.
Can this elephant really dance? It’s taking baby steps, alright. But the stage is tricky and peppered with pitfalls.
Dive into the story to find out how BSNL—not Jio or Airtel—[emerged as the front-runner](https://the-ken.com/story/not-airtel-not-jio-bsnl-is-the-front-runner-in-private-5g/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) in this niche play and what it will take for it to live up to the high hopes.
