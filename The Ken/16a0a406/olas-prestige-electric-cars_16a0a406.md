One of my all-time favourite movies is The Prestige. If you haven’t watched it, you really should. It’s a masterclass in plot structure, narrative style and non-linear storytelling. In fact, if you analyse it closely, the three acts of the story are centred around three parts of a magic trick.
And then I realised—this is exactly like how car companies like Ola are changing mobility in India. There are three acts there as well.
The first act is called The Pledge. It’s when the magician shows you something ordinary—like a deck of cards, or a hat. He pretends that it’s an ordinary object, like something you see around you. But it isn’t.
When Ola launched in India, it positioned itself as a cab aggregator. Just like several others. But it wasn’t. Ride-sharing became a thing. And it soon drove down car ownership rates. It seemed everyone was using an Ola instead of buying cars.
The second act is called The Turn. It’s when the magician takes the ordinary something and makes it do something extraordinary.
Driverless cars in India is something multiple manufacturers are pursuing, but with limited success, as we wrote about earlier. Autonomous driving is the closest thing to magic right now in India.
But it isn’t enough. It needs one more thing to make it work.
Which is why every magic trick has a third act. It’s when you bring the original thing back, but in a slightly different form.
This is the hardest part of any magic trick.
It’s the part they call The Prestige.
And that’s why car companies have been trying their best to make electric work. Electric cars help Ola move from a cab company to a mobility company. And Ola’s not alone in this—around the world, ride-hailing firms from Didi Chuxing in China to Grab in Southeast Asia to Lyft and Uber (which in India competes with Ola) in the US have launched a series of EV projects and announcements. Over the years, many of these, too, have petered out. But in the past few months, all of them have been back at it.
Can Ola and Co. get it right this time around?
Our story today is about Ola’s Prestige.
