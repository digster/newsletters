There are few periods in history that you can pack into a neat little box, tie on a pretty bow, and present to people by saying, “Tada! Here is the defining theme of our times!”

And try as I might, 2024 defied categorisation.

If you browse through The Ken’s newsletters from the past year, will you see commentary on businesses that ventured into virgin territory? Absolutely, yes. Will you see companies hunkering down and pulling back? Also yes.

Innovation? Over-regulation? Disruption? Comebacks? Yes, yes, yes, and yes.

Were any of them so prominent that they became the dominant theme of the year?

No.

At The Ken, we did a few things with our suite of newsletters in 2024 that I’m quite proud of.

We launched our first-ever newsletter that focuses on India’s capital markets—Long and Short. It’s only been a couple of months since we published our first edition, and it’s already among our most-read newsletters.

We expanded our focus on our community of vastly knowledgeable subscribers. In The Nutgraf, we harnessed them to analyse one of the hottest segments in Indian business right now—quick commerce. They also helped us look at how the lustre of an erstwhile jewel for business professionals, product management, has diminished.

In First Principles, we used this collective insight to launch quests for answers to some of the toughest questions we face in life: How do we parent? What is fitness?

And most importantly, we continued doing our best to deliver incisive, thought-provoking, and consistent commentary on sectors and subjects you care about.
