Good Morning Ishan,
 
Next month, I turn 24, marking 24 years of my life in Delhi. I’ve always struggled to articulate how I feel about the capital city. It doesn’t make it easy for anyone to love it, but it also doesn’t make it easy to hate it, specifically for people like me, who’ve spent their entire lives here. Not so long ago, I think I found the perfect word to describe my relationship with the place—it’s not home, it’s not love, it’s a habit. 
 
A habit of watching the city’s landscape change quickly from that of Lutyens’ Delhi to that of Shah Jahan’s Dilli.
 
A habit of waking up one day to suddenly find that stale summer has abandoned us and the cool autumn breeze has entered the walled city. 
 
A habit of transitioning from the old and the new. A habit of being habituated to change itself. 
 
The city itself has changed so much and so often that its resilience finds its way into every Dilliwala’s spirit. The city has been broken, remoulded, broken again and rebuilt many times. But it stands. And it doesn’t resist change when it comes. This week’s stories reflect the spirit of Delhi in some ways, talking of companies that don’t shy away from rebuilding, remoulding, and redefining themselves when needed. 
 
On Monday, Jaideep wrote his debut story at The Ken about Reliance Industries. The conglomerate already owns an IPL franchise and operates the country’s top football league. But now its next big goal is to build a sports broadcasting empire. 
 
Adar Poonawalla, the CEO of the world’s largest vaccine maker Serum Institute of India, wants to use the wealth generated from the cash-rich SII to double down on financial services. On Tuesday, Arundhati delved into the challenges Poonawalla Fincorp must conquer while making this transition. 
 
Wednesday’s story focussed on a battle between Flipkart and Meesho. Flipkart was prepared to conquer the social commerce space, but it wasn’t prepared for Meesho’s entry into the e-commerce pool. Now the two giants are vying for a piece of each other’s pie, wrote Pranav and Bhumika. 
 
On Thursday, Olina took a closer look at a segment of edtech that’s often overlooked—B2B. A set of edtech companies built a strong infrastructure layer that helped them flourish during the pandemic. With physical classrooms now reopening, B2B edtech’s raison d’etre is crumbling.
 
Maitri and Anand collaborated on Friday to write about India’s second-largest pharma chain—MedPlus. Having encountered failure while trying to raise meaningful capital over its 15-year existence, it has now turned to the public markets. Will MedPlus get the love it believes it deserves?
 
We wrapped the week with our Saturday newsletter, The Nutgraf, where Praveen deep-dived into why the Freshworks’ IPO feels different. Why did this unsexy company from Chennai get the emotional reaction normally reserved for movie stars, rock musicians, and medal-winning sportspeople?  
 
I’ll leave you to enjoy your Sunday now. See you next week. We’re turning five on Monday and we have big things planned for you. I’m so excited to tell you but I must wait (!).
