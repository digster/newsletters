Hello Ishan,

 

A friend introduced me to bouldering seven years ago. He showed me how to use my limbs for power and balance while hugging a wall, how to “solve problems”—that’s bouldering parlance for visualising movements to reach the final hold—and how to fall. That quickly led me to sport climbing, so I learned how to use ropes, carabiners, quickdraws, harnesses, and other gear. A weekly outing quickly tripled in frequency. Sometimes, I’d squeeze in a fourth expedition.

 

Climbing changed how I ate and slept. It also satisfied my yen for adventure, because I’d pack up my gear, hike to new locales, and look for new cliffs to grab onto. I got to see my city from new vantage points. In between intense sessions, I’d sit, think, and clear my mind, hands covered in chalk so I couldn’t reach for my phone. I could tune out a lot of noise to be in the moment. And of course, I made great friends along the way.

 

It’s no secret that a dose of physical activity can change how we look and feel. But taking the first step isn’t always a simple matter, and it’s likely we’ll have more questions along the way. But what might be fresh discoveries and realisations for us might be past experience for others. There are expert voices out there, and tapping into their knowledge can enrich our own journey of building healthier, happier lives.


On that note, The Ken’s next live event is called “Achieving Vitality: New Visions for Health, Fitness, and Wellness”. Here’s the panel:

 

 

Nisha Millet is an Olympic swimmer and Arjuna Award winner. She’s also the director and head coach of Nisha Millet’s Swimming Academy, which has 12 centres and a team of 25 coaches in Bengaluru. The academy trained 6,000 swimmers aged 1 to 84 in 2024 alone.

 

Viren Shetty is the vice chairman of the Narayana Health hospital group, which is listed on the National Stock Exchange of India. Through Narayana Health’s new chain of clinics and health insurance arm, Viren is spearheading an effort to change the way Indians think about healthcare, shifting the focus from sickness to wellness.

 

Dr. Cyriac Abby Philips, aka The Liver Doc, is a frequently cited hepatologist and physician-scientist. He is a senior consultant at Rajagiri Hospital. But you may know him for his presence on X, Instagram, and Youtube, where he has cultivated a huge following—and a few opponents—by sharing evidence-based medical information that debunks health myths.

 

I expect the discussion to reveal interesting insights about a range of topics, like how to get great sleep, attaining mental clarity through workouts, which supplements matter, and what to keep an eye out for when we take care of aging parents and growing children. You’re welcome to submit your question for Nisha, Viren, and The Liver Doc ahead of time to help shape the discussion.

 

The panelists will be at the Bangalore International Centre on Saturday, 21 June 2025, from 10:30 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. Our team will be there, and so should you.
