Hello Ishan,

A friend introduced me to bouldering seven years ago. He showed me how to use my limbs for power and balance while hugging a wall, how to “solve problems”—that’s bouldering parlance for visualising movements to reach the final hold—and how to fall. That quickly led me to sport climbing, so I learned how to use ropes, carabiners, quickdraws, harnesses, and other gear. A weekly outing quickly tripled in frequency. Sometimes, I’d squeeze in a fourth expedition.

Climbing changed how I ate and slept. It also satisfied my yen for adventure, because I’d pack up my gear, hike to new locales, and look for new cliffs to grab onto. I got to see my city from new vantage points. In between intense sessions, I’d sit, think, and clear my mind, hands covered in chalk so I couldn’t reach for my phone. I could tune out a lot of noise to be in the moment. And of course, I made great friends along the way.

It’s no secret that a dose of physical activity can change how we look and feel. But taking the first step isn’t always a simple matter, and it’s likely we’ll have more questions along the way. But what might be fresh discoveries and realisations for us might be past experience for others. There are expert voices out there, and tapping into their knowledge can enrich our own journey of building healthier, happier lives.
