India is clearly playing smart.
The Paris Agreement in 2016 had India and 188 other countries and territories commit to reducing the alarming speed at which climate change is occurring. As per the agreement, all parties are to ensure that the global temperature won't rise by more than 2 degrees Celsius this century. Actually, 1.5, if possible.
Except, India is currently that kid at the exam hall who's confidently walking out with 30 minutes to spare while others gawk, wondering if they're writing the same test.
What do I mean? Well, one of India's promises is to ensure 40% of all of its energy capacity is from renewables...by 2030. India is currently at 37%, with a whole decade ahead of it.
So, the Paris targets are easy to meet. It's the homegrown targets that are tough. And India is focussed on those.
The country wants to up its solar capacity 8X by 2030, making it 36% of the total installed capacity. Currently, it’s at 39 gigawatts or 10% of the total.
While that's a tough target to crack, getting closer to it is no less significant. India has been building solar parks everywhere it can. And it's got every big player's attention. Right from Adani to ReNew to, now, NTPC and Coal India, who are, by name, associated with coal, not solar.
