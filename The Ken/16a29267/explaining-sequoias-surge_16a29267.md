Imagine yourself this very moment, sitting for a venture capital entrance examination.
I know what you are thinking... no such thing exists.
But on deeper thought, perhaps you are thinking, well, why not. Maybe there is merit in one. Maybe if not in the real world, then in the world of imagined possibilities, which in a way is somewhat cuddly, close to the craft of investing. And so there’s this question in front of you, which you must answer:
Q: What is common between the following companies? Byju’s, Oyo and Zomato.
If you are me: Man, they are all unicorns.
If you are you: All three have a common investor called Sequoia.
If you are Sumanth Raghavendra, the author of today’s story: Bud, Sequoia came in way too late in these companies. And that explains Sequoia’s new fund called Surge, one which they announced earlier this year.
What does Surge do, why should it exist, will it move the needle for Sequoia and the startup ecosystem in India; everything in today’s story.
