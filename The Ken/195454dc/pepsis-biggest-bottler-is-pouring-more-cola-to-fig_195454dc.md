Good Morning Ishan,
Fizz and Pop are on an afternoon stroll when the bright orange banner at the convenience store on the corner catches Fizz’s eye. It’s emblazoned with a picture of a flashy plastic bottle filled with dark, tempting cola.
The two eye the cooler as they step up to the counter. “Heard of Campa Cola?” Fizz asks Pop as she pulls out her wallet. “My investment in Varun Beverages has tanked because they’re giving it a tough fight.”
“Campa’s owned by Reliance, right? I hear it’s crazy popular, even back in my hometown,” replies Pop.
Even the shopkeeper can’t help but chime in as he hands over a bottle. “My regular customers used to buy a Pepsi every day. Now they’re buying two bottles of Campa instead.”
Fizz takes a swig of chilled cola. “I do prefer Pepsi’s glass bottles though. And is it just me, or are there a lot more Pepsi glass bottles around these days?”
“Pepsi’s been paying us and the distributors more to keep them in stock. They’ve even made their Rs 20 bottles larger,” the shopkeeper replies.
“It’s funny how it’s always been Pepsi and Coca-Cola, right?” Fizz muses as the duo walks out. “And here’s Reliance being Reliance, coming in at a lower price and shaking things up.”
“Funny, I was just reading about this in The Ken today,” says Pop, her fingers flying across her phone screen. “This writer, Aakriti, had a really detailed piece about how they’re both making Pepsi hurt. I just sent you the [link](https://the-ken.com/story/pepsis-biggest-bottler-is-pouring-more-cola-to-fight-reliances-campa/).”
Intrigued, Fizz clicks it.
