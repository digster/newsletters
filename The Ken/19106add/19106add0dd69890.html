Good Morning Ishan,

We have two stories from two countries today, and both have their hopes pinned on credit, and they can’t be more different from each other.

One is Cars24, the used car marketplace that wants to grow on the back of its non-bank arm, and the other is Albert Tinio’s Gotyme Bank.

Albert Tinio was the man in the thick of action when he saw the Philippines change from a cash-first country to a digital one, thanks to an e-wallet explosion.

The company that led that shift was Gcash, the country’s largest e-wallet, which now has 75 million users, and Tinio led the charge of Gcash in its early days of hypergrowth, which saw it become the Philippines’ first unicorn.

Now, Tinio wants to capitalise on this trend and take on the hard task of getting Filipinos to now take digital credit.

For that, Tinio has set up Gotyme Bank, where he is co-founder and co-CEO. The bank is a joint venture between the digital-banking group Tyme and the conglomerate Gokongwei Group.

In the two years since its 2022 launch, Gotyme has acquired 3.7 million users and grown its deposit base to 17.3 billion pesos, or US$300 million—the third largest among digital banks in the Philippines. While Gotyme is yet to introduce loans, Tinio has a few tricks up his sleeve to get that engine started.

He believes one of the biggest drivers that’ll help the bank get there is by staffing Gotyme with people who have scars and unfinished businesses. With such people in its fold, it is growing at a rate of 250,000–260,000 customers a month.

Read Katrina’s interview to get a ring-side view of how Tinio plans to change Filipinos’ credit habits.

Meanwhile in India, Cars24, the nine-year-old used-car marketplace, hit upon a brilliant plan to drive sales of its used cars. To set up a non-bank arm to give loans.

Typically, even though three used cars are sold for every two new cars, most loans were given only to buy new cars. Some banks believed giving loans to buy used cars was a poor and risky prospect. They didn’t trust both the car and the borrower.

So Cars24 believed a non-bank arm will not only boost sales, but it can also make a neat profit from the lending business.

It even got Bajaj Finance’s MD and financial services maven Rajeev Jain as its mentor, in addition to even partnering with the lending giant for loans.

Cars24’s non-bank grew to Rs 2,000 crore (US$244 million) in total assets under management, but it is learning the nuances of lending for used cars the hard way.

Now, five years later, it is doubling down on this business by launching a new financial-services brand called Loans24 to offer even personal loans, finds out Gaurav in today’s story.

That means it should have struck gold, right? Gaurav has a great story on this today.
