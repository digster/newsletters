Good Morning Ishan,
 
Us lot, at this company, have a good memory. Allow me to explain.
 
Feb 2010: Ankur Singla launches Wasiyat.com. In Chandigarh. A company that helps people with registering their wills.
 
August 2010: Singla moves to Delhi. Wasiyat becomes Akosha and adds other legal services as add-ons.
 
January 2011: Akosha shuts down its business and becomes a company to resolve consumer complaints. Soon Akosha launches One Direct, an offering for companies to manage consumer complaints.
 
July 2015: Akosha shuts down and becomes Helpchat, a messaging-based, intelligent personal assistant. 
 
May 2016: Helpchat shutters chat. Becomes an all-in-one app. One app for tax services, paying bills, deals and news, among others.
 
November 2016: Helpchat becomes Tapzo.
 
Sometime in early 2018: In an all-hands meeting, Singla tells his employees that Tapzo will attain break-even by March 2018. 
 
September 2018: Tapzo is dead. 
 
Earlier this month, in an email, the company told its users: “Since we launched the app three years ago, we’ve been fortunate to serve over 5 million customers. Your feedback and reviews (200,000+ on PlayStore alone) and the 4.5 rating kept us motivated and always learning. It’s been a pleasure listening to you all and continuously shipping an app update every few weeks consistently. But all good things must come to an end.”
 
Words must not be minced. Tapzo was never a good thing.
 
In today’s story, my colleague Sumanth says as much. If you are an eight-year-old startup that has raised more than $30 million in venture capital funding and believe that you have a “good thing” going, you don’t come to an end by way of a summary email to your customers. You don’t shut down your service overnight and move on. And when you do that, it is because you never had anything going for you. Vanity metrics and multiple useless pivots go only so far.

I’ll see you around. Work hard and have a great day.
