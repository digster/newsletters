What’s more disruptive than disruption?

How about disrupting disruption itself?

We’re gathered here to discuss the disruption boomerang thrown by a controversial real estate-focused startup, which is backed by SoftBank’s gargantuan Vision Fund.

If you thought WeWork, sit back down again: the focus of today’s story here at The Ken is OYO — WeWork’s South Asian cousin of sorts.

In today’s story, Sumanth unboxes the conundrum that is OYO and its increasingly uneasy relationship with hotels. Yes, the very hotels that it exists to help.

OYO is truly disruptive. There’s no doubt that it has pioneered something unique with its budget hotel vision—ask anyone who has ever traveled off the beaten path in Asia—but how did we get to the point that a hotel owner can (successfully) file charges of cheating and breach of trust against CEO Ritesh Agarwal and his lieutenants?

That is madness.

It’s complicated, of course, but OYO’s vision of standardised accommodation is challenged by the fact that the very hotel owners it serves don’t truly believe in it.

They’re in it for the booking volumes that OYO says it can drive. There’s no shame in that, but now OYO has realised that it, too, needs to make revenue. So it is battling the very hotels that allow it to call itself “the world’s third-largest hotel chain.”
