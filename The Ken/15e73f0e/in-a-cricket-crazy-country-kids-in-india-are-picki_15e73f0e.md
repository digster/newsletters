Good Morning Ishan,
Once upon a time I was young. Back then, whenever the homework and strict parent gods allowed, I would wake up early in the morning, put on my shabby, yellow cum white school keds and hit the ground. I loved playing cricket. Everyone around me loved playing cricket. The kids in the high rises. The kids in the nearby slums. The kids in the rented apartments. College kids. Men in their late twenties and early thirties who whiled away their days in ‘adda’. On the bad, rainy days, a few days every year, some people played football; they were mostly bengalis.
It will be fair to say, I’m no more young and my understanding of today’s youth isn’t particularly deep. Which is why I find Karanjeet Singh fascinating. He is 13 years old. He plays football. This is him talking: “I was just blown away by some of the skills that some of these international footballers have. I would spend at least 6-9 hours a day on YouTube, watching them, making mental notes of how to execute them, and the next day during practice, I’d try it. 99 out of 100 times I’d fail, but just trying those skills gave me the satisfaction.”
My colleague Venkat tells me, Karanjeet is not alone. There are many kids like him. Football is becoming big. In February, the international football federation, FIFA, launched a Mission XI Million programme in India, with an ambitious target of getting 11 million children into the sport. The aim: to take the game to 12,000 schools in 37 cities across the country.
