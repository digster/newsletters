Good Morning Ishan,
I was among the first 10,000 buyers of Zomato’s new subscription-based loyalty and discounts offering—Zomato Gold. I didn’t spend even a second wondering about the Rs 999 (~$15) annual fee, as a single meal at one of my favourite restaurants would get me complimentary food to recoup that “investment”. As no-brainers go, this one was right up there.
Zomato says at least 40,000 agreed with me. That’s how many subscriptions it sold before temporarily shutting down the program.
Now comes the hard part.
There is fear, bordering on despondency, among restaurant owners who are part of Zomato Gold. That’s over 1200 restaurants at last count.
They say they were promised and were expecting a premium, restricted loyalty program that would enable them to attract and retain the most sought-after diners in India. Instead, what they’re getting are bargain-hunting groups only interesting in the free stuff on offer (1+1 on food, 2+2 on drinks).
Many are already planning to withdraw from the program or implement severe restrictions on Zomato Gold customers.
One restaurant owner told us they were embarrassed because their staff was mocking them, asking why they were giving away so many free dishes. Even customers who claimed the free offers were laughing, telling the restaurant owners how the deal was too good to be true.
And now I’m wondering if I even want to land up at my favourite restaurants and claim the Zomato Gold free offers. Will I get the eye roll? Will I be put on a blacklist? Will they seat me only in dingy corners?
Zomato may have made its subscription product a little too good, but only for one side. And unless it fails to correct it quickly, it may end up with angry customers.
