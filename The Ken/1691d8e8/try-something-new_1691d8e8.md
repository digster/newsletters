Good Morning Ishan,
It's the weekend, so let's talk beer.
I was grabbing a drink with a friend yesterday. A beer; the first one Delhi has afforded me in a while since it's been rum weather for months now. I settled on Simba Wit. Beer in hand, it was time for conversation. I decided to lead with what I knew about Simba.
That it's Raipur-based. Odd, since I doubt anyone associates Chhattisgarh with beer. That its founder is a 27-year-old Sikh. That premium beer—the likes of Simba—is the lone bright spot in a dwindling Indian beer market.
To pretend that my inherent curiosity led to this wealth of information would be untrue. I owe this random trivia, and volumes more of it, to my time editing stories at The Ken. The funny thing is, if you'd asked me a year ago if I'd ever work here, I'd probably say no. I never saw myself in business journalism. Let alone The Ken. But I decided to try something new, and I can't imagine a more interesting place to work and learn.
Now, before you go thinking this is some recruitment pitch, it isn't.
