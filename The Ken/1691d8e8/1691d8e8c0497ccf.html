Good Morning Ishan,
 
It's the weekend, so let's talk beer.
 
I was grabbing a drink with a friend yesterday. A beer; the first one Delhi has afforded me in a while since it's been rum weather for months now. I settled on Simba Wit. Beer in hand, it was time for conversation. I decided to lead with what I knew about Simba.
 
That it's Raipur-based. Odd, since I doubt anyone associates Chhattisgarh with beer. That its founder is a 27-year-old Sikh. That premium beer—the likes of Simba—is the lone bright spot in a dwindling Indian beer market.
 
To pretend that my inherent curiosity led to this wealth of information would be untrue. I owe this random trivia, and volumes more of it, to my time editing stories at The Ken. The funny thing is, if you'd asked me a year ago if I'd ever work here, I'd probably say no. I never saw myself in business journalism. Let alone The Ken. But I decided to try something new, and I can't imagine a more interesting place to work and learn.
 
Now, before you go thinking this is some recruitment pitch, it isn't. Though we do have some openings here. Instead, this is about the value of trying something new. The rewards that going outside of your comfort zone can bring. Personally. Professionally. And we saw a similar trend play out across our stories this past week.
 
Like in Ashish's Oyo story on Monday. Oyo's killing the budget hotel game in India, but with SoftBank's money lining its wallet, the startup is looking at entering a lucrative new market—Southeast Asia. It's more profitable than India, by a long shot, but it won't be easy.
 
Tuesday's story was about India trying something new. The Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA)—a global ranking system—to assess higher-order, conceptual thinking. Guess where we placed last time we took it? 73 out of 74 countries. So why do it again? Because it's a step towards fixing a broken education system, Olina writes.
 
Wednesday's story was on a company so vast it required the efforts of two of my colleagues. Prajakta and Harveen teamed up to breakdown Reliance Jio into four simple charts. Charts that show just how many pies the Mukesh Ambani-led company has its fingers in. This is one company that can't seem to stop getting into new things.
 
Then there was Sumanth's story. On angel tax. And the latest developments around it. The government claims their new rules will help usher in a more functional startup ecosystem. Spoiler alert: They probably won't. In fact, they might make it worse. This was our free offering for the week, do read it if you haven't, and share widely.
 
Arundhati wrote the Friday piece, and it asks a radical question—India has Net Neutrality for internet access providers. Does it also need Net Neutrality to counter Google’s Android market share? A bold new question, for a brave new internet.
 
We ended the week with our TKOW piece. A free story, written by Pranav. It's the sort of story I live for. Random, compelling, on a topic that would ordinarily be a blind spot—India's lack of a rare disease policy and what this means for 100 million Indians left in the lurch.
 
Join us next week for more amazing stories.
