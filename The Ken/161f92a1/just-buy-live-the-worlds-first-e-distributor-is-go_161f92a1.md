Good Morning Ishan,
Research is key. I mean, how are you expected to know or remember everything. This happens to me all the time. Stuff I used to know just slips. And it doesn’t come back unless I hear or see that one crucial piece of information, and then it all comes rushing back. I’ll confess, many a time I don’t have the darndest clue; I survive that pretentious moment and research later. This is what happened with today’s story. My colleague Patanjali pitched this piece on Just Buy Live, and the whole time I was like, what the hell.
So I went online. With every tiny bit of information, things started coming back. Let me share the highlights with you.
-With 25,000 clients in 25 days, Just Buy Live could be the next big wave in mom & pop retail.
-With Rs 100 crore, Just Buy Live becomes alpha of brands.
-E-distributor Just Buy Live raises $ 100 million.
-How Just Buy Live turned its visual analytics platform into a revenue-generating tool.
-Just By Live, the world’s first e-distributor?
Hmm...impressive. FYI, all, except one, are headlines reported in the business press.
Two years in, you know what has happened? It has all come tumbling down.
I understand now that the online business to business (B2B) industry is an incredibly difficult place to be in. It is not easy being a middleman. And disruption is way more difficult than saying, let’s get these businesses online. In today’s piece, Patanjali captures the many missteps of Just Buy Live. He also points out to other companies which are trying, like the e-commerce marketplace Udaan. The copy makes for a good reality-check read.
