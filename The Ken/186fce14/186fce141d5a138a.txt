Good Morning Ishan,

India's stock market has been kind to many businesses that have gone public in recent years. 

Drugmakers are among the exceptions.

Since 2010, 17 of them have had initial public offerings (IPOs), but four have delisted. Of the rest, half are trading below their listing price. 

These stats would stop most pharma companies in their tracks. 

But Mankind Pharma has never been one to play it safe. In fact, it stirred up a hornet's nest with an ad campaign for its Manforce condoms during 2017's Navratri by telling Gujaratis to do just that. 

The 32-year-old "impish" company, as its chairman Ramesh Juneja once described it to The Ken, is weeks away from a ~$670 million flotation, and is expecting a valuation which is 10X of that. 

That would put its market cap close to Torrent Pharma's even though Mankind spends less than half on R&D—relative to revenue—as Torrent. 

Sure, Mankind has transformed itself in recent years, shifting focus from acute therapies such as anti-infectives and vitamins to higher-value drugs for diabetes and heart failure.

But the spectre of underperforming pharma stocks—including Eris Lifesciences that went public in 2017—looms large over Mankind's IPO.

So, how does it avoid what befell its peers?
