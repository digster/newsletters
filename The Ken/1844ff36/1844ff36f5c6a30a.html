Good Morning Ishan,
 
About eight years ago, I used to report on the automotive sector for a business daily, and back then, electric motorcycles were still showcased as concepts in auto expos. Now, from being mere concepts, they are finally vying for their own place in India's crowded two-wheeler market.
 
While we have seen a flurry of activity in the electric scooter segment in the last few years, electric motorcycles have taken their time. This, despite about 70% of the Indian two-wheeler market comprising bikes alone.
 
But today, all eyes are on the Bengaluru-based startup Ultraviolette and its soon-to-launch F77 electric bike. Its claimed-range of 307 km on a single charge is the highest in any electric two-wheeler in India so far.
 
It has taken the company nearly six years and US$30 million in investment to launch its first product that will compete in the premium 350cc category. And all thanks to about half of its funding coming from India’s third largest two-wheeler maker—TVS Motor Company—the startup has been able to endure these years of not having a product to sell. TVS has participated in all its six rounds of fundraising.
 
While the EV maker has been tight-lipped about the price, analysts and dealerships expect Ultraviolette’s F77 to be priced between Rs 3-3.5 lakh (US$3,600-4,200). However, its target segment is very niche: out of the 10 million motorcycles made in India, only 5% are in the 350cc category.
 
This price point means these bikes are also unlikely to qualify for subsidies—a key driver of EV sales in the country. Still, the company is deliberately entering the premium category. Even though it wants to shake up the market with its new bike, taking this long to launch means its competitors are also picking up speed with similar products.
