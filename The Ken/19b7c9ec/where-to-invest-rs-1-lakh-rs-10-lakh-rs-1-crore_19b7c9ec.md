Good Morning Ishan,
Wish you a happy New Year and a great start to 2026. Financially, too.
Before we broke for the holidays in late December, the financial sector headlines summed up the mood—Returns uncertain, investors too.
Benchmark indices touched all-time highs, but the trading volumes were the lowest in two years. The K-shaped phenomenon of the pandemic times has become a permanent feature. The economic highs and lows are experienced markedly differently by different demographics and strata.
So how does one invest in such times? Which asset class? Which instrument? Which mode? And market timing?
Over the last few years, we have curated our coverage of the markets and listed companies. This year, we bring you something new. While we’ll continue to do issue-, people-, and company-based wealth and financial stories, we’ll periodically visit the theme of personal investing.
The idea is to answer the million-rupee question: how to invest Rs 1 lakh, Rs 10 lakh, Rs 1 crore?
I grew up with little financial literacy. I wish my parents had drilled financial concepts into me, like they did multiplication tables. Even today, I don’t reach for the calculator for basic multiplication, but when it comes to investing any surplus money, the mental exercise is taxing.
In putting together today’s piece, our finance editor, Anand, has ensured that there’s a takeaway for everyone. We have an accompanying poll, which you must take to help us create a feedback loop and evolve this project.
