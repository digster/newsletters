Tesla is almost in India, but who cares for its cars?

India doesn’t have what it takes to build a Tesla car. Tesla may not have what Indians want, either

The Modi government has been trying hard, including holding a series of bilateral meetings and slashing import duty, to lure Tesla to set up shop in India. The US carmaker, meanwhile, wants to set up showrooms first. It has leased property in Mumbai and Tesla chief Elon Musk is scheduled to visit India later this year. The company is also actively hiring personnel for its showrooms and after-sales services even as its India leadership gets lean. Tesla has no immediate plans to find replacements, too. Accenture’s latest offering in the wake of AI: Survival as a Service

As GenAI deconstructs work, Accenture is busy acquiring companies to reconstruct it, aiming to beat TCS’ 10-year game of L&D

GenAI integration into workplaces of today has meant that entire roles are being scrapped for parts and rebuilt into entirely new ones. The largest consulting firm globally by headcount, Accenture, has taken this as an opportunity to reshape work—through upskilling. It plans to invest $1 billion over three years into its L&D vertical Learnvantage, under which it’s making aggressive acquisitions. Management consultancies and IT services companies are also vying for a slice of the work transformation pie. But Accenture’s size—and acquisitions—are helping it capture a sizeable chunk of it [Read the full story]

Starlink’s deal with Jio-Airtel is more optics than real partnership

For now, the narrative has helped the equity market, but the relationship can get rocky

Elon Musk-owned Starlink is six to eight months away from launching its broadband service from space in India; Amazon’s Kuiper will likely come mid-2026. The telecom regulator has unveiled its satcom recommendations and the DoT has a new checklist of stringent conditions. Satcom players are happy to comply. After years of stalling pureplay satcom, telcos have struck commercial deals with Starlink. It’s a good, easy-to-use service, but few expect Jio and Airtel to meaningfully sell Starlink terminals through their channel [Read the full story]

Tata Electronics took a painful bet on the iPhone. Luck is doing the rest

The Tata company is bleeding for scale, but its iPhone bet has turned into a geopolitical win—despite high worker churn, rising automation costs, and profit still being far off

Tata Electronics is burning cash to scale its iPhone assembly operations, absorbing losses and massive debt to position itself as a key Apple supplier. What began as a risky play for manufacturing relevance serendipitously aligned with the US–China tech decoupling. The Narasapura plant is scaling rapidly but struggling with high attrition and labour churn, even as Tata pumps money into automation to meet Apple’s quality benchmarks. In Edelweiss alumni’s second act, wealth managers get twice the cut

Neo Group, within four years of operations, already has Rs 40,000 crore worth assets under advisory—thanks to an audacious payout model, backers like Peak XV and MUFG, and poached talents

The Indian wealth-management sector is short of qualified talent and companies are racing to pocket the best advisors. In this, a four-year-old startup’s simple, yet unique hiring proposal has stood out. Neo Group is letting wealth managers keep as much as 60% of the client-advisory fee; nearly double than what its larger and older peers are offering.
