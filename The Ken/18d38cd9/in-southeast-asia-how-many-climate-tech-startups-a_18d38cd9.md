Good Morning Ishan,
There’s a series called Extrapolations on Apple TV+. It’s an anthology of loosely connected stories set in the years spanning 2037 to 2070, each episode marked by how much higher the average global temperature has climbed.
The tales are heavy-handed exposition less about climate change itself. Instead, they meander through what it means to live like a boiling frog, and two or three generations wrestling with the same big problem until it’s an even bigger problem.
Beyond the small screen, there is a crop of smart people trying to tackle climate change, each in their own way. (If you have “stave off environmental collapse” as one of your KPIs, I’d like to hear from you about your work.)
Over the past half year or so, we’ve heard repeatedly how Southeast Asia-focused investors view entrepreneurs working to mitigate the climate crisis as major targets for funding. But are they putting their money where their mouth is?
