Good Morning Ishan,

The great aggregator party is fizzling out.

Venture capitalists showered Indian startups—those that went on a shopping spree for small yet profitable online brands—with hundreds of millions of dollars.

Cash infusion. Acquisition. More cash. More acquisition. The cycle played on.

The hope was that these companies, commonly known as “roll-ups” would ride the wave of rising online sales and become the Hindustan Unilever or Procter & Gamble of the digital world.

In the post-Covid era, these e-commerce aggregators now find themselves at a crossroads.

One of them is Mensa Brands, India’s speediest unicorn. The whirlwind of acquisitions—17 companies in 14 months—for this roll-up has come to a screeching halt.

Why? What lies ahead for Mensa and other houses of brands?

Ananth Narayanan, Mensa’s founder and CEO, once mused, “The best decisions are not logical.”
