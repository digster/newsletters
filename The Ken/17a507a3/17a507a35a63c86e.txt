Good Morning Ishan,

It may well be the biggest workplace reshuffle since World War II.

After the year-long experiment with work-from-home during the pandemic, as the freeze begins to thaw, businesses are coming face-to-face with multiple challenges.

To re-envision the work week: Who’s going to commute all five days? Even better, why not work four days a week?

To re-imagine corporate culture: Remote first or remote last?

To revisit productivity: In essence never a function of the number of hours spent under supervision, it’s going to be a battle of perception vs hard data.

For employees, perhaps everything that mattered pre-pandemic may not matter to the same degree post-pandemic.

With these hypotheses in mind, our two ace writers, Arundhati and Olina, set out to understand how Indian businesses are responding to this big shift. Large companies globally have made their decisions. Those like Twitter and Shopify are remote first. Google, Facebook, Apple and Salesforce have adopted hybrid models, whereas Amazon and Netflix believe not working face-to-face is a big negative.

Indian companies haven’t made their decisions public but it’s a given that WFH is here to stay. Unsurprisingly, all kinds of companies are now adopting productivity trackers, metricising work, and ensuring that both managers and employees keep their “sense of time”—so that a 5-min break doesn’t become a 15-min break.

Some fear this would bring back into fashion HR practices that large companies have tried to move away from, like the bell curve. (It had fallen out of favour in the last few years because it forced companies to rate employees relative to how others fared.)

Counting work calories could also boomerang as employees joining in these times—the pandemic cohort, as it were—are already disengaged and weary.

In The Ken’s survey, over 56% of the respondents said they’d extended their WFH day by one to three hours. The number experiencing increased productivity in our survey isn’t very different from those with decreased productivity, but recruitment firms say the “white collar lethargy” is writ large.

In this big reshuffle, Zoho’s issues may be different from Hero Electric’s; RBL Bank may not measure like Wipro, but companies know that what they do (or not do) now will show its impact in a year or two.
