In a country obsessed with signalling, the startup-accelerator programme Y Combinator (YC) was a huge validation to founders who didn’t walk the corridors of the IITs and the IIMs.
Being "backed by YC" was signal enough.
Companies like Clear, Razorpay, Meesho, and Zepto went on to raise millions, and all graduated from the YC school of fame (Incidentally, some of the founders of these companies also went to the IITs).
But something has changed. Indian companies are not participating in YC as they used to.
The participation in the Winter 2023 cohort of YC’s three-month-long programme that brings funding and mentorship to startups dropped by a third from its previous record-breaking Winter '22 batch that hosted 400 global startups.
More importantly, only 12 Indian companies participated in the programme—a significant and consistent drop from 21 in Summer '22, 34 in Winter '22, and 41 in '21.
You see, Indian startups now have more choices at the pre-seed and post-seed stages.
Sequoia’s Surge, Accel Atoms, Singapore’s Antler, and London’s Entrepreneur First (EF) have announced India-focused pre-seed programmes.
As a result, startups don’t have to submit themselves to YC’s many conditions, which oftentimes are not startup-friendly.
