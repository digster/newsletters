Now to the story of the day. For all the beating that SoftBank has been taking for some of its hare-brained investments, there’s one in India which could prove to be a dark horse.
 
As an e-commerce baby care company, FirstCry did everything in its first 10 years which its fellow e-commerce companies did not do. In January, after SoftBank invested $400 million, valuing it at $840 million, FirstCry mustered the ambition to acquire the Indian franchisee rights of UK’s child and maternity chain Mothercare. It lost to India’s largest business group, Reliance Industries, but the spring in FirstCry’s step is unmistakable.
 
Its revenue grew 48% to $49.7M; losses narrowed by 86% to $7.7M in the last fiscal. The founder says the company is growing 100% year-on-year. With execution under its belt—several VC-funded startups in this space have folded up—FirstCry has built an enviable omni-channel retail platform. But we know that’s not enough. Now it must contend with horizontal platforms such as Amazon and Flipkart, both of whom have their own baby verticals. (Sources say Amazon’s baby and kids vertical has also been growing 100% y-o-y. And at Flipkart, the first day of the ongoing Big Billion Days sale saw pretty high growth in this segment as compared to last year.)
 
Local fights apart, FirstCry is going international; UAE and Saudi Arabia to begin with. A few acquisitions are in order. And as FirstCry figures out how to spend its SoftBank money, Alibaba-backed BabyTree, a wild success in its home country of China, is already laying down roots in India.
