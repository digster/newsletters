I have a small tradition for the last week of the year. I revisit the year, month by month, and note the most meaningful things—personal or professional. It’s like an annual report for me.
Some things rarely change. Like little to no progress on the previous new year’s resolution and a renewed commitment to making the next one count. But, broadly, 2022 has been a happy year for me. After all, the human tendency is to remember the good more often than the bad.
For me, what stood out this year was my work. Last year, I was just three months into business journalism—less confident, curious, and a bit naive. So, it’s a joy to see the stories
The Ken published across formats, and the role I played. Like the
[one](https://the-ken.com/story/sula-vineyards-116m-ipo-is-fine-wine-for-its-shareholders-not-public-market-investors/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) on Sula Vineyards that Gaurav and I did on Wednesday, a day before its IPO.
The public listing is purely an offer for sale, providing an exit to shareholders who have offloaded shares worth $116 million in the public markets. With low growth prospects, the share sale has very little to offer to public investors. As expected, the company listed flat on Thursday at Rs 361 (~$4.4) before falling to Rs ~Rs 311 ($3.8) by the end of the week.
Coming back to
The Ken, we opened the week with Sashwata’s
[story](https://the-ken.com/story/why-phonepe-wants-to-buy-zestmoney/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) on PhonePe’s $200-300 million acquisition of ZestMoney. With ZestMoney, the $12.5 billion fintech giant would get access to an NBFC license and multiple lending partners. Similarly, the deal could boost ZestMoney’s offline ambitions and help it shift its focus to Ebitda profitability.
On Tuesday, Anushka
[reported](https://the-ken.com/story/xiaomi-india-picks-up-the-pieces-after-raids-set-its-house-on-fire/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) how Xiaomi is picking up the pieces in India almost a year after the Income Tax Department and the Directorate of Enforcement (ED) together froze ~US$1 billion in the bank accounts of its local unit. Over the past year, the Chinese consumer electronics giant saw several top-level exits, closed non-core operations, and lost market share in the country. It’s going to be a long journey before it gets back on its feet.
On Thursday, Pratap
[reported](https://the-ken.com/story/aws-microsoft-may-take-the-wind-out-of-data-centre-firms-sails-with-in-house-capacity/?r_edition=2&utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) that internet giants Amazon and Microsoft are rewriting the demand-supply equations in the Indian data-centre industry with plans to build their own facilities in the country. With the biggest buyers in the town building their own facilities, domestic data-centre operators have to race against time to find new clients.
On Friday, Tushaar Talwar, a contributor, and Anand Kalyanaraman
[looked](https://the-ken.com/story/a-governance-mess-at-indias-top-power-trader-forces-state-run-shareholders-to-look-for-an-exit/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) at governance issues at PTC India. With multiple independent directors resigning, a damning forensic audit, and defiant management, it’s been
annus horribilis for PTC India Financial Services, the lending arm of India’s top power trader. And now, its state-run promoters—NTPC, NHPC, Power Grid Corporation, and Power Finance Corporation—have decided to sell their stakes.
That’s the wrap for the week. And also the year.
Behind every The Ken story, there is tonnes of research, conversations, brainstorming, and, of course, arguments. That takes a toll. As an organisation, we like to take time off during the last week of the year to recharge and come back with stronger ideas.
But we will be publishing year-enders—the best of 2022—throughout the next week.
I hope you too get a chance to take a break. Be kind to yourselves and have a lovely new year. Also, Merry Christmas!
