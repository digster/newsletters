Good Morning Ishan,
Shadowfax will list tomorrow. But going by the IPO’s modest 2.7x oversubscription last week and its near-zero premium in the grey market, the Flipkart-backed logistics-service provider’s entry on the bourses might well be a forgettable affair.
That seems odd for a company that’s clearly on a tear, with 33% annualised revenue growth over the past two years in a sector as hot as logistics in India.
Mind you, Shadowfax has worn the crown of the country’s second-largest third-party e-commerce logistics player, next only to Delhivery, for a couple of years now. And it’s going to use a good chunk of the IPO proceeds to press the growth pedal harder.
So why isn’t the market loving Shadowfax? No, it’s not about the odd name. Money doesn’t care about monikers.
The answer to the puzzle lies in timing. You see, Shadowfax is going all in and betting big—buying or leasing warehouse space with a vengeance—at a moment when its rivals are consolidating and cutting flab. But surely ambition can’t be a bad thing?
Perhaps it is, when the speed drive is powered by the need to catch up with competitors that raced far ahead on far more favourable terms, in far better days.
Being late comes at a cost, finds Priyal in her [sharp story today](https://the-ken.com/story/shadowfax-pays-todays-nosebleed-prices-for-delhiverys-old-advantage/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter). Let’s dive in.
