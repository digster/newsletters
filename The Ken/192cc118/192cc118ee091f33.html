Jiocinema and Disney+ Hotstar turn to Sensara because it can do what Big Tech still can’t

The founder of the startup that teaches machines how to ‘watch’ movies turned down acquisition offers from global tech giants—and hooked streaming giants and Hollywood’s elite in the process

Sensara uses video intelligence to make machines understand movies and TV shows, training them to think like humans when interpreting film. What started as a direct-to-television venture with the likes of Xiaomi and Airtel has swiftly evolved into a focus on tech for streaming platforms. With big-name clients already in its bag, Sensara is doing something most can’t—insert contextual ads right into the content itself. Despite a few acquisition offers, Sensara wants to “independently” climb the value chain within the media and other industries. [Read the full story]

Rapido is done just being a bike-taxi app. It’s dead set on becoming the next Ola

Its 10-month-old cab-hailing service, powered by Namma Yatri’s blueprint, is already breathing down Ola and Uber’s necks

After a decade of Ola and Uber dominating the cab-hailing market, drivers are fed up of high commissions. Rapido has seized the opportunity, aggressively wooing them with a model that offers higher earnings. Its focus on scaling supply first has helped it gain market share–mainly at the cost of Ola, who’s asleep at the wheel. The newly minted unicorn is even becoming a popular second choice for Uber drivers, sparking pushback from Uber. [Read the full story]

TV channels, beware! Youtubers Samay Raina and TVF are taking over the screen—and the couch

Content creators are making the most of 8 trillion minutes that Indians spend watching videos online. Add to it, the latter’s aversion to predictable TV content

Several Youtube channels are now offering their subscribers TV-like programming to keep them glued. This, as watching Youtube on television has become the fastest-growing category for the platform in India that has nearly 467 million active users. Aiding to that are shows created on cheaper budgets and better quality of production—letting them stand apart from regular TV programming. Youtube once let people watch videos at their convenience; now, creators are going the TV way by hyping their shows to get audiences hooked. [Read the full story]
