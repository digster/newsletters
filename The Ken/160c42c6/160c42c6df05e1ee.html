Yay, Friday is here. I know, first full working week of the year and all that restraint thing, but hey, glad we made it, right? I don’t know about you, but I think getting through a working week can only be a good thing. Anyway, let’s get on to the story of the day. The piece is from Payal. On logistics. 
 
Payal says that 2018 will see delivery turn into a strategic, competitive play for companies. Because it is the holy grail. To get a product to a consumer as soon as possible. Get it as right as possible. Get it by estimating what the consumer will need before the consumer places the order. And this is irrespective of whatever business you are in. And irrespective of whether your logistics unit is in-house or outsourced. So, food tech. Check. E-commerce. Check. Hyperlocal (grocery). Check.
 
This means investments by companies in warehousing. Technology. People. Fleet (owned or outsourced). And then, in some parts, investment in cold chain facilities. So, I’m not surprised by this perspective from Mukul Arora of SAIF Partners: “If I had to pick one sector for 2018, it would be a player who is helping unorganised transporters become technology-enabled, providing value-added services such as insurance, financing discounted fuel etc, apart from demand.”
 
You can read Payal’s piece here: https://the-ken.com/in-2018-a-lot-more-to-deliver/
 
Have a great day and an even better weekend.
