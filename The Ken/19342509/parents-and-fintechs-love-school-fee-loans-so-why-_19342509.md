Good Morning Ishan,
Ever wondered what Rs 13 lakh could buy you? An upper-end SUV, a decent stock portfolio, or a solid down payment for a home in the Rs 60–70 lakh range. Or you could also stash some gold or maybe a few other life-enhancing dreams. (Still with me? If you’re not yet curious about the oddly specific number, you should be.)
What if I told you that the same Rs 13 lakh is the price tag for enrolling a—wait for it—3-year-old in the nursery at a top-tier Mumbai school? In some metros, the cost can go as high as Rs 23 lakh in higher classes.
But when did education for children get wrapped up in consumer loans? Well, it started when parents began believing in IB and IGCSE’s teaching methods and leaving no stone unturned to meet their unbelievable fees. “Everyone aspires to send their children to the best schools. But not everyone has that kind of money to pay upfront—sometimes [they do so by] even [going] beyond their means,” said Jai Decosta, CEO of The Orchid Schools, a chain of 90 ICSE-CBSE schools spread across 25 cities.
Cue the fintechs with their no-extra-cost school-fee-financing options. It’s basically the same zero-cost-EMI model you see with consumer durables. Thanks to several of these schools and their partnerships with fintechs like Grayquest and Leo1 and banks and non-banks banks, including Avanse and IDFC, more parents are able to make that dream a reality: getting their kids into top-tier schools.
Some of these fintechs have secured partnerships with thousands of schools, including popular school chains like Euroschool, Ryan International School, and more, and already raised millions of dollars from big names like Tiger Global, Elevation Capital—and even Indian skipper Rohit Sharma.
So, if parents, schools, and fintechs all seem to be winning this game, why is there one party that stands to lose? Let’s get into [Alifiya’s richly reported story](https://the-ken.com/story/parents-and-fintechs-love-school-fee-loans-so-why-are-schools-not-all-in/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) and find out who gets the carrot—and who gets the stick.
