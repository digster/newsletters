Good Morning Ishan,
I really love Test cricket. Yes, it's a challenging time to be an Indian Test cricket fan, but when isn't it?
A few days back, somebody on a WhatsApp group raised a question: If Indian technology companies were cricketers, who would they be? The answers came quick and fast. Flipkart was obviously Dhoni. Been around for a while. Unfazed. Able to deliver at the crunch, like a big sale. Dunzo was KL Rahul—a local sensation expanding to conquer other geographies. Everyone agreed that Hike was Rohit Sharma. They both seem to have their fans, but nobody knows any of them personally.
The company that we disagreed the most on was Swiggy.
That's because Swiggy is a peculiar company that's really hard to nail down. They clearly have excellent operations, and have scaled rapidly to new heights in an area like food-delivery where everyone else has floundered. Very Dravid-esque. On the other hand, somebody said that Swiggy is successful, but appears to be one-dimensional—a bit like Murali Vijay. We didn't really get to a consensus, but we have a story.
In his story today, Salman writes about Swiggy's plans to make itself more multi-dimensional by venturing into spaces well beyond its comfort zone. Swiggy's plans are formidable—it intends to leverage its learnings and strengths in food delivery to make inroads in categories like groceries and alcohol. And milk. Yes, milk. But to do this, it has to find the right business model, strike the right deals with supply partners, and make some difficult decisions. Bolstered by its recent $210 million fund raise, Swiggy is raising the stakes and going all-in. But there's victory at the end of it. It's almost like batting in the first session of a Test match. It's risky. You make choices. But if you get through it, the rest of the day is yours for the taking.
Swiggy is walking to the crease. This is the story about how the first session may play out.
