Good Morning Ishan,
I bought an Apple Watch during the first lockdown, wore it almost every day until it conked out over a year ago, and then decided I was done with smartwatches.
I’m no fitness fanatic, nor do I go anywhere without my phone, so there was no compelling argument to own a smartwatch. What I did love about the Apple Watch was that it helped me locate my iPhone every other day. But as far as use cases go, this is even below using a smartwatch to set an alarm. And not one worth spending Rs 40,000 (US$477) on—certainly not a second time.
I guess there are several others like me as Apple Watch shipments to India more than [halved](https://www.counterpointresearch.com/insights/india-smartwatch-market-sees-record-shipments-in-2023-with-50-yoy-growth/) in 2023. But its market share is so tiny as not to deserve a mention, so this isn’t exactly reflective of the market? Correct. Overall smartphone shipments in India rose by half in 2023.
But this is where the silver lining ends.
Between January and March, smartphone shipments fell 7% from a year earlier to 9.6 million units, according to research firm IDC. In the following three months, they plunged. By 27%. And this is why the wearables industry had its first ever quarterly decline, as per data released earlier this month. Not terribly surprising, since smartwatches are the second-largest wearables category, after headphones.
Even globally, the outlook for smartwatches is anything but rosy, with a [2% decline](https://www.counterpointresearch.com/insights/global-smartwatch-shipments-market-share/#) in January–March, the latest period for which data is available. But why are the numbers for India so dismal?
