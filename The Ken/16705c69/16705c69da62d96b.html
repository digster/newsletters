Today’s story is by our editor and lead healthcare writer Seema. She’s spent the last few weeks divining the tea leaves in India’s pharmaceutical sector, and the signs aren’t looking good. Almost all the international pharma giants are in varying stages of “D”—disengagement, disillusionment, de-growth and defocusing.
 
Some, like GlaxoSmithKline, Novartis and Merck, are selling (or have sold) segments of their India businesses. Meanwhile, Pfizer, GSK, AstraZeneca, Bristol-Myers Squibb and Roche Products are letting go of employees. And almost everyone is reevaluating their India strategy.
 
Once upon a time, back in the 80s, multinationals controlled 80% of the Indian market. Today, though, it's almost the exact opposite—Indian companies control close to 80% of the $16.8 billion market. (Sadly, Indian pharma companies are bleeding too, thanks to price control, ad-hoc policy changes and the new GST).
 
Of course, the pie has grown. But not as fast as these companies had hoped. And in a world where each company must decide where to invest each additional dollar of capital, India is losing out to China.
 
Why is this happening? What does this mean for the long-term vibrancy and growth of India’s pharma and healthcare markets?
