Good Morning Ishan,
I’ve got your Friday covered.
Now listen carefully to what Lily Peng, a product manager at Google, says. About her time at the company just two years back. And let me be the first person to say this. Damn, the last two years have been crazy. Like, Little Rocket Man level crazy. But anyway, listen to Peng. “I, along with other folks, saw how machine learning was transforming our other core products. And we thought if we were able to detect dog breeds [through] images, maybe we could also help with medical images.” Wow. Now that’s a leap.
I was getting an education when I read today’s story. I’ve now learnt that AI can play a significant role in eyecare. There aren’t that many ophthalmologists and retina specialists, so the doctor to patient ratio is actually 1:10,000 in India. So if you have an algorithm which can take a retina image and diagnose those images, it can really help with early detection of blindness-related conditions. Google. LebenCare in Singapore. IBM Watson - all of them are trying to make this work.
Google, for instance, is working with Aravind Eye Care and Sankara Nethralaya. Both these hospitals are working with Google to detect diabetic retinopathy—an eye condition that is the most common cause of vision loss among diabetic people. This is our story for today. From my colleague, Venkat Ananth.
