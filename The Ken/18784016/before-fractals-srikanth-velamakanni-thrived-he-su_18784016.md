In a typically wide-ranging discussion recorded inside Fractal’s Mumbai office overlooking the sprawling Aarey forest, I asked Srikanth about a lot of topics. Like:
The sudden and rapid rise of AI, and its implications
“For the first time, at least in my life or my career, I've seen AI reach a point where it feels like artificial general intelligence. The idea that machines are smarter than human beings in every respect seems around the corner. It could be as little as three, four years.”
[…]
“Everything powerful can be used for bad purposes. Question is, can we stop? It seems impossible. The pace of scientific progress is unidirectional, and it is unceasing.”
The number of co-founders “required” for starting a business
“Three-people co-founding teams have the problem that two people can gang up and the third person feels isolated. Four-people teams can get into a deadlock.”
Investing like a venture capitalist
“We were about to do a fund raise from Khazanah in 2016. So I approached the new investors saying, look, we could make a 25% EBITDA margin in this company. That should be very easy. But I do not want to make that margin right now.
I want to spend half of that money in growing our current business to accelerate growth. And the remaining half, which is 12.5% of our revenue, I want to use that for building Fractal startups (which are now called alpha companies in Fractal parlance). We wanted to incubate them.”
The evolving roles of a CEO
“I think I'm the cultural leader of Fractal. The most important value is to know that there is a soul of Fractal and I must represent that. And that we will not do something stupid because if Srikanth is in charge, nothing stupid will happen.”
Important metrics for growth
“We also look at one more metric, which is revenue growth percentage plus profitability percentage. We add the two numbers and we call it the efficiency score. There's a rule of 40 in the world, which is like if you have 20% revenue growth and 20% profitability, you're at 40. So we look at that number and we want to be at least a minimum of 50 or 60 ideally. And some years we have done even better than that.”
On becoming a better dad via ‘feedforward’
“I believe in Marshall Goldsmith and his style of coaching. Marshall Goldsmith is one of the greatest executive coaches in the world. He says, ask everyone, all your stakeholders, how can I get better?
So I went to my daughter and asked her this question a couple of years back, saying, hey, how can I be a better dad? And she actually gave me some very good feedback. I was surprised that at eight years of age, she had things that she wanted me to get better at. She said, when you're at home, please do not use any phones. Spend more time with me, and so on.”
Multi-sensorial reading hacks
“I usually have the Kindle version and the audio version, or I use Kindle and use text to speech to read as well as listen at the same time. I read everywhere. If I'm commuting, I'm reading. If I'm in the car for 10 minutes, I'm reading. If I'm in the plane, I'm reading."
