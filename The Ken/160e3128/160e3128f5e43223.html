“Mania. Meteoric. Craze. Timebomb. Euphoria. Fixation. Fascination. Regret. FOMO. Bubble…”
 
Before you wonder what this free-flowing prose is about, these are the words used to describe the rise, fall and rise of the cryptocurrency Bitcoin lately. We’ll not get into whether you’ve made money or regret not making it when the value soared in December. The discussion on crypto can get as volatile as Bitcoin itself.
 
The thing is, crypto craze is keeping ahead of the regulation. Over the last few weeks, all top private banks (names in the story) in India have asked cryptocurrency exchanges to close accounts they hold with the banks. That, when the top exchanges have about 1 million users each, with trading volumes of about $25 million each. 
 
A source in the Union Home Ministry says the government is looking to officially crackdown on exchanges as there are concerns about people using cryptocurrencies for money laundering and tax evasion.
 
But a crackdown on exchanges through banks has led people to other channels. One currently taking a life of its own is over-the-counter (OTC) trade—paying cash to buy Bitcoins. (Like it happened in China when exchanges got banned.) OTC platforms like LocalBitcoins and Paxful where currencies like US dollar, Rupee, among others, are exchanged for Bitcoins are seeing a surge in India. 
 
Sidhartha and Arundhati say, just on Paxful, there are 300 ways in which one can pay to buy Bitcoins.
 
Now imagine, if cash for crypto becomes more mainstream, won’t the banks, RBI and the government be actually serving up a channel for black money hoarders? And it is not like banks are not aware of the consequences of withdrawing support to the exchanges.
 
Well, our two writers have an important story on how the banks, payment gateways, and exchanges are caught in a regulatory limbo. This, while investors are busy buying and selling, leaving no transaction trail.
