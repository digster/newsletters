Good Morning Ishan,
 
Do you own a vehicle? If you do, here's some advice: make sure you start it at least once a week. Even if you don't have to go anywhere, take it for a spin around your neighbourhood. Or go for a long drive on the weekend. Take it from the guy who’s now having to kickstart his brand new (six-month-old) scooter every time.

One of the best things about reading The Ken's stories is that you can almost always relate to some of them. Take Olina's story this week (my personal favourite—the fact that I edited it had no role to play… maybe).

It took me back six months ago when I was faced with a 12 km commute to work. I really wanted an electric scooter, but after about a month of deliberating, I decided it wasn't the right time. Infrastructure and cost were the main obstacles. I eventually bought a regular petrol scooter, but barely got to use it for a month before we entered a world of lockdowns.

Hero Electric, the protagonist of Olina's story and also India's largest electric vehicle maker, is trying its best to ensure that people who will look to buy a two-wheeler in a post-Covid world are more assured about going electric. With tailwinds in favour of EVs, Hero Electric is using new ownership models and B2B sales to increase its footprint.

The other stories in this week’s lineup were just as absorbing. Perhaps you will relate to some of them.

Like Seetharaman's story, which was about land ownership in India and its peculiarities. In 2008, the Indian government launched an ambitious plan to map land parcels and digitise India's land records. Twelve years later, the programme has only achieved a fraction of what it set out to do. Seetha, in this week's free-to-read story, delved into the complexities of mapping India's land, digitising the records, and what this means for various stakeholders.

Pratap wrote about Samsung as it prepares for life without Reliance Jio. Having built Jio’s 4G LTE network from the ground up, Samsung has seen its role diminish as the telco’s spending on network building is largely complete. It’s the story of Samsung’s search for its next big project in India. Is it going to hitch its trailer to BSNL or Airtel?

And finally, Pranav wrote about Chinese telecom giant Huawei's 5G future. While governments around the world ban or threaten to ban it, Huawei has tried to make itself synonymous with 5G. Through seven wonderful charts made by Prajakta, Pranav’s story documented how hard it is to envision a 5G future that doesn’t include Huawei.

We had just four stories this week as we took a publishing break on Friday on account of the Hindu festival of Ganesh Chaturthi. Did you celebrate? Did you visit your friends and family? If you plan to visit, take your vehicle. Hopefully, at least yours is in working condition.

Stay safe, wear a mask, and practice social distancing. Which story did you enjoy the most this week? Let us know.
