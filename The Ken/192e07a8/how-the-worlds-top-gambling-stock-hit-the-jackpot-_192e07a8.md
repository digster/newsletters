Good Morning Ishan,
Happy Deepawali from all of us at The Ken!
I know you’re probably enjoying the day with sweets, family, and new clothes. We are too. But we also worked extra last week to bring you a couple of stories in your inbox today, which are absolute must-reads.
Digiplus is a Philippine real-gaming company that built an empire by offering a digital version of a game that’s familiar to us—bingo. In fact, some of you are probably planning to play this today to celebrate Deepawali as part of a long-running tradition.
Well, you know who really got lucky with bingo? Digiplus. It was in the bingo business for nearly two decades, but the minute it took the game online, its business just exploded. In fact, Digiplus became the best-performing Philippine stock in 2023 and one of the world’s top gambling apps, with a market cap exceeding a billion dollars last April.
Good news, yes?
Well…yes, for a while.
Of late, things have started changing in the Philippines. There’s growing scrutiny on online gambling, partly fuelled by a ban on some of the country’s offshore gaming operators for their alleged involvement in illegal activities like human trafficking and kidnapping.
So what does the future hold for Digiplus? Can it find a way out? It’s trying to—and has carved out a couple of possible paths for itself.
But will it work?
Well, that’s our [story](https://the-ken.com/story/how-the-worlds-top-gambling-stock-hit-the-jackpot-and-landed-in-lawmakers-crosshairs/) today, from my colleague, Katrina.
In other news, my colleague, Ruhi, who heads The Ken’s learning vertical, has written a great piece on how sales is changing as a career. Based on conversations with some of India’s top sales leaders, she’s found out that tactics like cold calling, long pitches, and asking for customers’ time are slowly becoming more ineffective.
One of the people whom Ruhi spoke to, a 15-year veteran at a pharma company, said, “I would rather do what I want to do rather than what I have to do—that is what the customer is saying to us. People don’t want to listen to a pitch; they want the seller to get to the point faster.”
Salespeople are getting less and less time to make their pitches, and customers are getting increasingly impatient, armed with their research—and simply looking to close a sale.
So what can the salesperson do to change this?
Well, Ruhi has a solution [here](https://the-ken.com/learning/case-study/todays-customers-are-smart-so-salespeople-need-to-be-smarter/).
