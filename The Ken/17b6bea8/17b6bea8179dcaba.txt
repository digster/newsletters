Good Morning Ishan,

The battle between David and Goliath has served well as a metaphor for many a corporate battle. Today, though, it isn’t about either of them. Or about the battle. 

Story goes that before heading into battle with Goliath, Saul, the king of Israel, offered David a suit of armour. But David rejected it—“I cannot walk in these, for I am unused to it”. 

I thought it was apt for the week we’ve had. Too often, we tend to see businesses of the present and future through the lens used to understand ones that matured in the past. While learning from history is a useful tool, expecting others to always play by the same time-honoured rules can also hold one back. 

This week’s stories dug into how stakeholders in markets as diverse as grocery to ransomware are grappling with changing circumstances. Who are the players likely to emerge on top? Here’s a quick recap: 

On Monday, Seetharam dug into the quarterly earnings of supermarket chain DMart https://the-ken.com/story/dmarts-e-commerce-tortoise-squares-off-against-the-jiomart-hare/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter and how it differs from its deep-pocketed rivals like BigBasket and JioMart. Founded by equity investor Radhakishan Damani in 2002, the chain is known for its unwavering focus on its bottom line. It turned Ebitda positive over the past quarter and seems to be in no mood to let go of profit motives in the short or the long run. 

Replicating past success can be a tall order. On Tuesday, Maitri, in our free story of the week, unpacked why spawning the second generation of AIIMS hospitals https://the-ken.com/story/the-missing-dna-in-new-gen-aiims/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter has been such an uphill climb. In a deeply reported and layered story, she found that it may be 30 years past the approval date till six second-gen hospitals can come fully online. Meanwhile, approvals have already been handed out to 16 third-gen AIIMS. 

The current IPO market is red-hot, but how different is it from past bull runs in Indian equities? Anand, Aishwarya, and Prajakta teamed up to produce a sharp and visually rich story on how it’s a brave new world for the Indian IPO market https://the-ken.com/story/the-indian-ipo-wave-throwing-the-rulebook-out-the-window/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. Read this Wednesday piece to learn how loss-making companies are having a breakout moment on public bourses and which firms are front-running incoming regulatory changes on the role promoters play. 

Ever thought insurance could expose you to more trouble? It seems counterintuitive but as Sumanth and our contributor Anand Venkatanarayanan document in their Thursday story, ransomware attackers are coming https://the-ken.com/story/why-2021-will-be-the-year-of-ransomware-for-india/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter for the cyber-insurance honeypot to figure out who to attack next. While there are multiple factors behind the current explosion in data being held hostage for money, the story details the overlooked role of tech’s broken market, nation-states, and how 2021 could be the year of India’s troubles with ransomware. 

We capped this week with an insightful story from Olina on how the world of human resource management has changed https://the-ken.com/story/human-resources-a-brand-new-beast/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter due to the pandemic. From developing relationships across state and national borders to wrangling vaccines, HR has done it all. While they’re trying to prevent attrition at firms, the changing roles might just burn out HR professionals themselves—it's on companies now to hold them near and dear. 

In our daily newsletter, Beyond the First Order, Jaideep spoke to a sports lawyer to understand the legal implications of brands congratulating India's Olympians https://the-ken.com/bfo/indias-big-moment-marketing-moment/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on social media; Rohin laid out the subtext of an op-ed https://the-ken.com/bfo/the-meaning-of-byju-raveendrans-indian-express-op-ed/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter by the founder of edtech major Byju's; and Nithin pointed out some tell-tale signs of burnout in India's IPO market https://the-ken.com/bfo/is-indias-ipo-market-showing-signs-of-a-burnout/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter.  

In our Saturday newsletter, The Nutgraf, Praveen lays out how, after years and years, e-grocers have finally cracked the answer https://the-ken.com/the-nutgraf/why-10-minute-grocery-deliveries-are-finally-here/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter for one crucial problem. Which is why you’re being offered grocery deliveries in 10, 15, 19 minutes. 

That’s it from us for this week. Hope you have a relaxing weekend. Maybe revisit a childhood story; you never know what lesson it holds for you.
