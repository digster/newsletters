Good Morning Ishan,

The tide of seasons is turning in Delhi. Gloomy days of December have given way to bright sunny mornings, the trees are packing up their old clothes, and layers are shrinking. 

This time between winter and spring feels like a validation of the hope that things will get better. It reminds me of P.B. Shelley’s lines in Ode to the West Wind. “When winter is here, can spring be far behind?” It is super motivating and fills me with hope whenever I am feeling low. 

This hope is such a beautiful thing. Like Tim Robbins says in Shawshank Redemption, it “may be the best of things and no good thing ever dies”. And if it wasn’t for hope, I wouldn’t be here talking to you. There was a point when I wanted to leave journalism, but my hope and perseverance won. 

It can do wonders. 

I spoke to this girl for one of my stories. She ran away from her home in Uttar Pradesh, nursing a desire to become a model. She became an anchor at one of the video commerce platforms and now hopes it will be a ladder to her modelling career. This weekend, she texted me, saying she has landed an ad for a TV brand. Wow!

It is this hope, dear reader, to become the online publisher of choice for you that makes us work so hard every day for our stories. Entrepreneurship is all about hope, and our stories chronicle it tirelessly, not just this week but almost every week. 

For now, let’s take a look at this week’s assortment:

On Monday https://the-ken.com/story/flipkart-amazon-catalogue/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, Abinaya wrote about Flipkart’s secret project to take on Amazon’s expansive catalogue. While the two companies are well-matched in major categories like large appliances or mobile phones, Amazon has a sizeable lead in categories like baby care, personal care, sports and fitness equipment, etc. In October 2018, Flipkart assembled 18 teams across the country and tasked them with onboarding more products and sellers. One year on, however, the project has hit a wall.

Tuesday saw Seema writing about the ping pong of 5G telecoms. India is trying to include its regional 5G standard into the global standards, but vendors with their vested interests are throwing cold water on standards body TSDSI’s plans. Equipment vendors, including the likes of Ericsson, Nokia and Qualcomm, don’t want any additional standard to follow, claiming it makes no business case. It’s also a free https://the-ken.com/story/5g-slugfest/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter story, so do read and share widely.

My Bengaluru colleague Pranav Balakrishnan wrote https://the-ken.com/story/cruising-towards-a-debt-free-finish-line-reliance-tripped-up-by-jiofiber/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter about how JioFibre, despite a dream launch has not been able to perform as it should have. It aimed to add 36 million subscribers when the total number of wired broadband subscribers in India was 18 million. Five months after the commercial launch, Jio has realised that wired broadband is no easy business. The problems are aplenty, right from slow adoption of JioFibre to logistics and pricing issues. The story appeared on Thursday.

We had a fascinating piece by our resident startup expert Sumanth on Pine Labs. The company that is 29th on the list of unicorns in India has an interesting backstory that not many are aware of. Sumanth traces this through his investigative and analytical lens. To know what it is, read it here https://the-ken.com/story/pine-labs-unicorn-future-hoary-past/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter.

Our Wednesday story was on video commerce which I wrote, looking at how companies such as Bulbul, simsim and WMall are trying to get consumers in small towns to transact through videos. Tier-3 towns are a huge opportunity, something that not even Flipkart and Amazon have been able to tap. Will videos do the trick? They can, given what Tik Tok has done to small-town India. However, ticket size and high customer acquisition cost may hurt these players. Here’s https://the-ken.com/story/bulbul-simsim-video-e-commerce/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the story.
