Who doesn’t love a good underdog story? Archishma has one for you today.

Prudent Corporate Advisors didn’t have the backing of India’s wide-spread, powerful banks. Nor did it have the legacy and reach of NJ IndiaInvest, the country’s largest non-bank mutual-fund distributor. Yet, that didn’t stop Prudent from racing up the ranks.

Data from last week shows that it is now snapping at the heels of the mighty HDFC Bank, which ranks third in terms of commissions earned by distributing mutual funds. Prudent’s revenue and profit have raced up too.

The stock market didn’t see this coming. When Prudent got listed in 2022, the issue just about got subscribed and the stock closed its listing day below its issue price. But from then until now, the stock is up 5X, making it among the best performers in financial services.

Why, it now sports a valuation (price-to-earnings) ratio higher than that of the mutual-fund houses whose products it distributes—the likes of HDFC, Nippon, and Aditya Birla Sunlife. How did the middleman come to be valued more than its principals? Can it sustain?

Prudent seems to have got some key things right, finds Archishma. Yet, risks lurk and the company has been hedging. The twist in the underdog tale, as it were. Read the story of how Prudent got so far and whether it can go further pushing through a far more powerful force.
