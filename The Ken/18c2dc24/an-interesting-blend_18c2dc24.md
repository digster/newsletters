Good Morning Ishan,
During a trip to Dhaka, Bangladesh, in February 2017, an acquaintance asked for my thoughts on former US secretary of state Henry Kissinger. This caught me off guard—nothing in our interactions had primed it. I was aware that the question was charged.
To be frank, I didn’t have anything smart to say. I had once heard Kissinger speak, his voice sounding like sandpaper dragged against his windpipe. Otherwise, he occupied no space in my head. So, I shrugged, and the conversation rolled onto something else.
Kissinger’s death at the age of 100 made me think that he might have been the world’s leading business consultant. His process was shrouded, but it was clear that the former statesman had turned himself into a brand, with a service to braid business and public relations with politics. His media formula was to sprinkle a bit of historical intrigue into observations like the way some parties “think in centuries”.
That last bit was his take on the ruling party of China, where he had no shortage of enterprise clients. The obits coming out of the country were unsurprisingly glowing.
