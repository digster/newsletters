Kunal Shah, the founder and CEO of CRED—a reward-based credit card payments app—is unapologetic about building products for the top segment of India’s massive consumer pyramid.
One would imagine that having raised over US$600 million in venture funding puts pressure on a founder to show a potential market size that’s massive and untapped.
And yet, Shah is doing quite the opposite.
He is single-minded in his focus on catering to the top 30-odd million consumers in the world’s most populous country. Why? Because they are the ones with enough disposable income to drive the majority of most discretionary spending, he says.
CRED currently has over 11 million users, which fits the definition of “California users” [put forth](https://the-ken.com/the-nutgraf/why-india-wont-see-a-100-billion-internet-company-anytime-soon/) by Praveen Gopal Krishnan in his newsletter, The Nutgraf, back in 2021. And over the last two years, this has become painfully apparent to most Indian startups and founders.
But how did CRED and Kunal Shah arrive at this reality ahead of others?
In a wide-ranging and reflective founder conversation, Kunal Shah speaks with host Rohin Dharmakumar about:
• Actual size of the top segment of India's consumer pyramid
• Hiring “high-slope” professionals
• Why meetings inside CRED are called “shareholder meetings”
• Why the decline of working women is one of India's biggest crises
