Good Morning Ishan,

India’s population is ageing faster than ever.

Nearly six million join the group of those aged 60 and above every year. If this continues, the number of seniors in the country is expected to grow to 193 million by 2031 from 102 million in 2011.

India’s real estate companies have figured this cohort makes for a ‘silver’ opportunity, though—senior living communities.

Like most modern gated residential communities, they come with a range of amenities, including a swimming pool, clubhouse, gym, and library. Except, they are exclusively designed and built for retired senior citizens, where the service providers handle the residents’ daily chores.

Those like Columbia Pacific Communities, Ashiana Housing, The Embassy Group, all want to make the most of this. At present, India has about 20,000 such units, according to industry estimates. And over two-thirds of such properties are present in the southern cities of Chennai, Bengaluru, Coimbatore, and Hyderabad. But the country has an immediate need for about 400,000 senior living units.

Read our Chennai-based writer Narayanan’s well-reported story on what it takes to build for India’s ageing population.

Also in Chennai…

A Hindu Businessline report said US auto major Ford is looking to re-enter India. If you remember, Ford had a sprawling Chennai facility, spread across 350 acres. It had an annual production capacity of 150,000 cars and almost 340,000 engines, but it stopped operations in July 2022, after 25 years of being in the country, and having only a 2% market share.

Now, the company wants to try its luck with making and selling electric vehicles. Ford is also believed to be recruiting workers for its Chennai factory, where it once employed nearly 3,000 people.

But finding people this time around may be harder for Ford than what it was used to.
