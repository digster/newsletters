We have two stories for you today.

The first is about companies that are filing corporate-sustainability reports—specifically, the top 1,000 listed companies in India.

These are familiar names like the low-cost airline Indigo, automaker Maruti Suzuki, and drugmaker Sun Pharmaceutical Industries. On paper, they look greener, cleaner, and more responsible than ever. But flaws in the Business Responsibility and Sustainability Reporting requirements introduced by market regulator Sebi provide a lot of room to make grand claims or shrug off their accountability. So, greenwashing is rampant.

Other issues persist, too. There simply aren’t enough trained ESG professionals to handle the range of disclosures that different companies file. That means Sebi doesn’t have the resources to check these reports closely and provide feedback that would set the companies straight.

The result? ESG documents that mislead shareholders and consumers. Check out Debanjali’s debut story, featuring insights from independent researcher Sandeep Khurana, to find out how and why the system is failing investors who hold shares in India’s top companies.

Over in China, our new contributor Luna took a look at e-commerce marketplace Temu’s troubles at home. In August, 800 suppliers picketed one of the company’s offices in Guangzhou, protesting what they call unfair financial penalties that eat into their already meagre margins. It wasn’t the first time for them to commit to this type of action, but it was the biggest.

Temu’s parent company, PDD Holdings, has other woes. A disappointing second quarter and an announcement to not pay dividends had shareholders selling the stock in droves, leading to a 29% drop in PDD’s share price. The company’s founder, Colin Huang, lost over US$14 billion and his title as China’s richest person overnight.

The promise that Temu’s users can “shop like a billionaire” is catchy, but harsh policies that give customers the lowest of low prices make it difficult for suppliers to stay afloat. PDD can’t seem to keep both its customers and suppliers happy without becoming yet another e-commerce platform. Unless it breaks out of this cycle soon, it’ll lose its edge.

Read Luna’s report to find out more.
