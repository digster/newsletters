Good Morning Ishan,
 
One of the most exciting things about working for a startup is the potential for cross-pollination.
 
And The Ken is no different.
 
Ideas and feedback regularly flow up and down the totem pole. Sideways too—across the editorial and product teams. But there are companies where such collaboration is not just frowned upon but nearly prohibited.
 
Ten-year-old AlphaGrep Securities is a case in point.
 
It does not use messaging apps like Slack and relies mostly on email. Former employees The Ken spoke to are not even sure how many teams there are in the company.
 
Why so secretive, you ask? As one of India's earliest and largest proprietary high-frequency trading (HFT) firms, AlphaGrep pits its traders not just against those of its rivals but against each other. These traders buy and sell stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments in fractions of a second using algorithms.
 
But HFT feeds off volatility, which has not quite been the norm in markets in recent years. Then Covid sent them into a tizzy, giving firms like AlphaGrep a new lease of life.
 
AlphaGrep accounts for 5-7% of the total traded value on the National Stock Exchange on any given day. And the first half of 2020 was possibly the most profitable six months in AlphaGrep's history.
 
But, as Shreedhar details in today's story, this is just a reprieve for AlphaGrep. As stability returns to markets and competition intensifies— including from former employees who branched out on their own—the $14 million-revenue AlphaGrep has to diversify beyond its bread and butter. But it's not quite as straightforward.
