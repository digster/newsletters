Good Morning Ishan,
There’s a figure that caught my eye in today’s story: 15 years.
It’s roughly the span of a professional athlete’s career, the time it took to build the International Space Station, and the period it took for smartphones to be widely adopted.
It’s also the time, Cartrade, the protagonist of today’s story, took to stick to its core business—being a marketplace for new and used vehicles. This is an unusual approach in the startup world, which is often known for frequent pivots, but it eventually paid off. The company recently announced its highest-ever quarterly earnings for the three months ended March 2024 and then shattered that record in the September quarter, posting a net profit of Rs 31 crore—more than 20% higher than its previous high.
Interestingly, Cartrade’s business model is thriving amid sluggish demand for new cars. For context, while sales of passenger vehicles have plateaued, 85% of the units sold on its platforms, Carwale and Bikewale, were new.
Not just that, within months of acquiring the Indian business of OLX in 2023, it shuttered OLX’s auto segment, which rivals like Cars24 and Spinny were reportedly eyeing. And why?
