The hottest tech/startup/investment sector in India these days isn’t e-commerce, food delivery or ride-hailing. It’s digital payments. And right in the middle of that sector sits an entity like no other - NPCI.
 
"Like a mythical god with multiple faces or avatars, it is simultaneously one thing, many things and nothing. […] Depending on the time and context, NPCI is a competitor. It is a platform. It is a regulator. It is an industry association. It is a profitable non-profit. It is a rule maker. It is a judge. It is a bystander."
 
Last year, we were the first to call out the inherent contradictions and conflicts within NPCI. Our May 2017 headline was, “Rock. NPCI. Hard Place.” In the nine months since, those contradictions and conflicts have only amplified and are now becoming the foundation for a War of The Worlds - Fintech Edition set in India. In their zeal to become the default way Indians transfer money to each other, Flipkart, Google, Paytm, Facebook and Amazon are all digging in their heels.
 
The reluctant kingmaker (or kingslayer, depending on how you’re affected) is NPCI. Ostensibly a non-profit body comprised of banks. But in reality much more.
 
Read Arundhati’s deep(er) dive into the entity that is going to pop up in fintech conversations more often. It's free. Remember, you heard it from us.
