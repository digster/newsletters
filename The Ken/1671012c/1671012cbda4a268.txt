Good Morning Ishan,

Last evening, at around 5 PM IST, two notifications popped up on our Slack group at The Ken. Nearly at the same second.

One was the final draft of today's story. The other was news about Binny Bansal stepping down from Flipkart.

We will come to the former shortly. If you haven't heard about the latter, let me quickly run you through what happened. Flipkart co-founder and Group CEO Binny Bansal resigned from the company over allegations of serious personal misconduct. The resignation was accepted and 'effective immediately', said Walmart in a statement. Speculations are rife. Everyone has their theories. Honestly, at this point, we know as much as you do. But we do know one thing. Today is a very consequential day.

For the first time ever, there isn't a Bansal at Flipkart.

That may not sound as dramatic as not having a Stark at Winterfell, but it’s significant. Our story today isn’t about Binny Bansal. We will let other news outlets cover that. With single sources, breathless exclusives and quotes from insiders.

Today’s story is about the other Bansal. 

When Walmart acquired Flipkart a few months back, Sachin Bansal, the other co-founder, was reluctant to say goodbye. He saw the acquisition an opportunity for him to stick around and do greater things. He was even ready to buy more shares to signal his commitment. Walmart (and the rest of Flipkart’s investors) responded by cutting him a cheque for a billion dollars, removing his name from all their press releases and bid him goodbye.

So, Sumanth asks, what does the future hold for Sachin Bansal? What next after a billion dollars? The answer? Late-stage investing through $100 million cheques. Today’s story analyses what this will mean for the Indian ecosystem, to other investors like Tiger and Softbank, and most of all, to Sachin Bansal himself. 

Given the backdrop of the news that hit the wire last evening, I may have referred to him as the ‘other Bansal’. That’s not true. Sachin Bansal was the one who started the company, was CEO the longest, and the one who left first. He’s the first Bansal. He will always be the first Bansal.

Today’s story is about the first Bansal’s quest for a second inning.
