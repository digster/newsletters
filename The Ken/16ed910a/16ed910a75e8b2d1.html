It’s the season for travel, but spare a thought for the poor OTAs running gigantic server farms, complex pricing algorithms, massive advertising campaigns and bottomless discount offers. Like MakeMyTrip, India’s largest online travel agent (OTA).
 
Despite controlling 63% of India’s online travel market, MakeMyTrip’s NASDAQ-listed stock has fallen from an all-time high of $40.25 last June to $24.6. Because after it acquired arch-rival GoIbibo (owned by Naspers) in 2016, investors expected an end to the bleeding competition that was dragging everyone down. But despite having won that battle, MakeMyTrip is today struggling in the war. Its latest quarterly revenue fell from $198.4 million to $181 million over the preceding quarter.
 
But one investor feels that MakeMyTrip is undervalued, given the massive long-term potential that the Indian market holds and how under penetrated it currently is. That investor is China’s Ctrip, which after a share swap with Naspers in April this year, owns 49% of MakeMyTrip. And it wants to use MakeMyTrip to fight off its larger rival, Priceline (the owner of Booking Holdings), for control of Asia's travel market.
 
And MakeMyTrip itself wants to use Ctrip’s scale and experience to help it crack what it sees as the next big opportunity—outbound travel. Rajesh Magow, cofounder and CEO of MMT, pegs online penetration for outbound travel at 15%, much lower compared to the 55-60% seen in domestic travel. MakeMyTrip also wants to learn from Ctrip on how to hunt down that mythical holy grail—the “super app.”
