Good Morning Ishan,

I’m taking notes. Green flash cards. Runny ballpoint pen. 

COO of top dog media company, which owns several news channels. Straight face. Looking a bit concerned: “Inflation has touched everything in this country except the television bill. You know, price of everything has gone up, except the television bill and I am not talking about HD. We can make money only from two sources. Either advertiser pays. Or the consumer pays. Now, even OTT players are getting into the content business, where they have direct subscriptions. And now we have this Trai tariff thing, so yeah, that’s the landscape we are dealing with.”

I’ll be honest. The television business has a lot going on.

The older ways of doing business aren’t going to fly anymore. Think about just these three. One, broadcasters now must put out the maximum retail price of a channel. Two, broadcasters cannot bundle TV channels priced at above Rs 19. Earlier, they used to, to obfuscate performance, and well, because they could. Three, all distributors must now provide a pack of 100 channels, including free to air and government mandated channels.  

RS Sharma of Telecom Regulatory Authority of India says, “This is a complete rewriting of the regulatory framework in the broadcasting sector. It is a clean slate.”

My colleague Harveen is on top of this regulatory change and how it will affect the business of broadcasters and distributors. She says the changes are great for consumers. Today’s Monday read is important. It also has this current of optimism, which I like.
