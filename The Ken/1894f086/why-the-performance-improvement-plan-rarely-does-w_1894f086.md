Good Evening Ishan,
It’s Shreevar here, and I’m back with another confession.
The Hollywood film Moneyball, directed by Bennett Miller and starring Brad Pitt, is one of my favourite movies of all time.
Which is a bit of a surprise because I’m not really a fan of:
1. baseball,
2. statistics, or
3. the early-2000s.
Truth be told, though, the movie is about so much more.
Brad Pitt, as baseball coach Billy Beane, works with a bunch of ‘underperforming’ athletes to change the fate of his club. He gives inspiring monologues (written by legendary screenwriter Aaron Sorkin), negotiates with the senior management, reinvigorates his players’ morale by focusing on their strengths and hard statistics…
