Alifiya, the author of today’s story, refers to placement officers at colleges as matchmakers between students and their dream companies. This year, the matchmakers are battling the worst campus hiring in at least a decade and expect a 30% fall in recruitment.

Placement cells across colleges are usually competitive, trying to one-up each other. But as they say, unprecedented times call for unprecedented efforts. The matchmakers are now in a huddle, devising never-seen-before steps in trying to make as many unions as possible.

What steps, you ask?

A “one student, one hire” policy, sending out placement officials to meet with HRs, approaching alumni, and even asking students to make friends with seniors are just some of them.

Companies that were once regulars at placements have decided to give this season a miss. And the ones who’ve agreed to visit have more stringent policies.

For instance, one of the placement officers told Alifiya that if recruiters “ask for CGPA 7, even 6.9 won’t be accepted”.

Placements in India were always a tough nut to crack. One in every 600,000 fresh engineering graduates lands a job, per a Nasscom report. Now, the menace has infested the top-tier colleges as well.

As if getting into these premier institutions and maintaining a certain CGPA wasn’t already difficult, the end result—getting a job—now seems a task as well. And this year is not a blip on a smooth curve.
