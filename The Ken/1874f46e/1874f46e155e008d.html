For hundreds of Indian tech companies, Covid-19 was their first cataclysmic event.
 
But not for Bookmyshow.
 
Its parent company, Bigtree Entertainment, started selling movie-ticketing software just when the Dot-com bubble bust started unfolding. And a year after it diversified into selling tickets, Lehman Brothers filed for bankruptcy and unleashed financial hell on the world.
 
But even after experiencing such events, Bookmyshow couldn't have foreseen just how awful the pandemic would be for cinemas and, consequently, itself. It hardly mattered that Bookmyshow had a near-monopoly in the movie-ticketing business.
 
Hard as it may be to picture this now, no one knew if people would ever walk into a theatre again. There were much bigger things to lose sleep over. 
 
Almost 90% of Bookmyshow's revenue evaporated, and the company had to axe two-thirds of its workforce.
 
But Bookmyshow hunkered down and lived to tell the tale. 
 
People started going to the movies again, and in the year ended March 2023, its revenue was close to what it had been pre-pandemic—roughly Rs 740 crore.
 
If that comeback is one half of Aayush’s story today, the other is about Bookmyshow’s realisation that it needs to look beyond the movie-ticketing business, in which it already has a 80% market share.
