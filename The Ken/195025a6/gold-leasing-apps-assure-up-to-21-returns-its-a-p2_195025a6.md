Good Morning Ishan,
In August, India’s most feared regulator—the Reserve Bank of India (RBI)—cracked down hard upon peer-to-peer (P2P) lending platforms. The central bank had found that some of these platforms played fast and loose. So, it threw the rulebook at the industry again, and some more. The tightened regulations have sent many P2P-lending platforms into an existential crisis.
Now, what if I told you that in a parallel world, there is another set that has many parallels to P2P-lending platforms. With many practices similar to those that had the RBI fuming into action. Just that these platforms are having an unfettered, merry run without proper guardrails for their customers.
Many digital-gold platforms enable and encourage their customers to lease their holdings to jewellers—dangling the carrot of 15-21% returns. This aggressive advertising, for what is inherently a risky investment, is not the only problem. Other risks lurk beneath the shine.
So much so that some industry insiders themselves are asking for regulation. An official from the regulator agrees, though the central bank has until now maintained a studied silence on this.
An orderly, regulated market will not just protect customers and help the nascent digital-gold leasing industry grow. It will also help the country address one of its protracted problems to an extent, writes Archishma in today’s story.
