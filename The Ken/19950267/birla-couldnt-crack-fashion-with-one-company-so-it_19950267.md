Good Morning Ishan,
If you have followed this newsletter long enough, you perhaps know that my perception of Aditya Birla Group’s retail chops is uncharitable, to put it mildly.
I [wrote](https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/kumar-birla-takes-yet-another-shot-at-fixing-his-spotty-record-in-retail/) this in June 2024:
In 2007, it bought Trinethra, a two-decade-old supermarket chain that it renamed More. Just a year earlier, Reliance Industries had begun its own retail journey with Reliance Fresh. But with over 170 stores at the time of the acquisition, More had a clear edge over Reliance Fresh. And its new owners took advantage of it and quickly scaled to 800 supermarkets.
But the expansion came on the back of unrestrained borrowing and no profits. By 2016, More had accumulated nearly[ Rs 6,000 crore](https://www.livemint.com/Companies/kXM2FyZl71mjVvwATQDmtJ/Aditya-Birla-Retail-loss-widens-to-Rs650-crore-in-FY16.html) (US$717 million) in debt, which forced it to bring its store count to below 500.
Two years later, the Birlas threw in the towel and sold More to Amazon and private-equity firm Samara Capital. By now, Reliance had become not just the country’s largest grocery chain, but also its biggest fashion and electronics retailer. And Dmart, founded in 2002, had gone public and impressed investors with its unrelenting focus on the bottom line.
The grocery business wasn’t the problem. Birla was. Because More has [turned the corner](https://the-ken.com/story/why-amazon-and-samara-capital-shrank-their-supermarkets-when-dmart-kept-getting-bigger/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) since Birla sold it. If only the group’s struggles in retail were limited to the supermarket business.
In 2012, it made another ill-fated acquisition, this time from Kishore Biyani’s Future Group. It bought a majority stake in the latter’s debt-laden apparel chain Pantaloons. Three years later, it merged the group company that owned brands like Louis Philippe, Allen Solly, and Van Heusen with Pantaloons to create Aditya Birla Fashion and Retail (ABFRL).
Cut to April 2024, and the Birlas undid that very move and decided to spin off the entity with all those brands from ABFRL.
Aditya Birla Fashion tried every trick in the book to stay relevant, especially with Tata-owned rival Trent soaring to new heights. But finally, it was forced to [sacrifice](https://the-ken.com/story/whats-aditya-birla-fashion-without-brands-like-allen-solly-and-louis-philippe/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) itself in order to have a shot at redemption.
At a market value of Rs 17,600 crore, Aditya Birla Lifestyle Brands, the demerged company that began trading on the stock exchanges in June, is worth 70% more than Aditya Birla Fashion. It’s as if retail analysts finally have something to be excited about the group. Brokers Morgan Stanley and HDFC Securities are counting on a 25–30% rise in Aditya Birla Lifestyle’s shares.
It’s not hard to see why. One of the biggest problems with the erstwhile Aditya Birla Fashion was that it was throwing good money after bad. In the five years to January 2025, the company raised Rs 8,900 crore in equity, not to mention debt, and bought loss-making companies such as TCNS Clothing, which owns women’s wear brands W and Aurelia.
The lifestyle business is much less ravenous. Three in four of its stores are franchisee-owned, so expansion doesn’t cost a fortune. And it doesn’t hurt that Aditya Birla Lifestyle is more profitable than its sibling. Morgan Stanley expects its FY26 Ebitda margin, a measure of operating performance, to be 16%. Aditya Birla Fashion’s margin will not even be in the double-digits.
Aditya Birla Lifestyle also has a lesson or two to offer in squeezing profits out of its investments. Its return on capital employed, excluding goodwill, is an impressive 22%, while the same metric for Aditya Birla Fashion is negative.
There’s a lot riding on the dark horse in Aditya Birla Lifestyle’s line-up of brands. Reebok, the India rights to which the company bought in 2022, has since doubled its exclusive-store footprint and sales. Reebok could do for Aditya Birla Lifestyle what [Crocs](https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/crocs-is-2-8b-metro-brands-unlikely-secret-sauce/) has done for footwear retailer Metro Brands.
The fact that Aditya Birla Lifestyle is the more palatable of the two companies to investors isn’t going to raise eyebrows. But Aditya Birla Fashion, 60% of whose sales is from Pantaloons, is cleaning up its act too. Its bet on small-town India, which housed 20% of its stores in 2021, came a cropper. So it shut over 40 stores in 2024, or a tenth of its total network, and is now back to wooing shoppers in large cities.
Half the Pantaloons stores now have a new look, and instead of unleashing all its new designs for the season on customers at once, the retailer now teases some every two weeks.
But measuring up to Trent’s Westside, the gold standard in apparel retailing in India, is still a dream for Pantaloons. The share of own brands in its sales has inched up to 64% (75% including Aditya Birla Lifestyle brands). But for Westside, that figure is 100%.
Aditya Birla Fashion is far from being a turnaround case study at an IIM. But the ground beneath its feet has stopped shifting.
