Good Morning Ishan,
For a company obsessed with “delivering everything”, Amazon India has spent the past few years delivering… confusion. A bloated app full of half-baked experiments—Amazon Food, Amazon Pay (barely used), and a grocery business that never quite “quick comm-ed” like Zepto or Blinkit.
Enter Samir Kumar, the guy who’s been at Amazon since before Prime was even a twinkle in Jeff Bezos’ eye. He was appointed as the country head last October. Not to play with more new toys. He is here to clean the house.
Kumar’s approach, unlike his predecessor’s, is old school: betting on inputs, focusing on the fundamentals—process, inventory, pricing discipline. The results are already showing. Prime customers now account for over half of Amazon India’s business and spend twice as much as non-Prime shoppers. No wonder the company launched Amazon Now for rapid delivery in Bengaluru and Delhi.
He’s also slashing costs like moving the Bengaluru HQ to a cheaper office. Then there are the “silent layoffs.” But it’s not all bean-counting. Prime Day 2025 shattered records, with speed and loyalty at the heart of Kumar’s comeback plan.
The question is: can a 25-year Amazon veteran make India’s e-commerce giant lean, mean, and profitable? Or will the culture of “writing documents about writing documents” hold it back?
