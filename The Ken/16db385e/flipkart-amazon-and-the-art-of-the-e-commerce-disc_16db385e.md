"I'm going to make him an offer he can't refuse".
Uttered by Marlon Brando in his inimitable performance as Don Vito Corleone in
The Godfather, this dialogue resonates surprisingly well with today's story on Amazon and Flipkart.
Allow me to explain.
The line on its own, even in the context of the scene, is not particularly threatening. The intention behind it, however, absolutely was. The "him" Don Corleone refers to—the head of a Hollywood studio—wakes up the next day with the severed head of a horse in his bed.
If the message wasn't clear before, it is now—Corleone isn't asking for compliance. He demands it.
Now, I'm not saying Amazon and Flipkart are the mafia. But there are some parallels.
While their recently-concluded flagship sales tout offers you can't resist, those deals are only possible because the two platforms make sellers offers they can't refuse.
Choose whether or not to discount. Completely voluntary. Just know that your visibility will suffer if you don't.
Choose whether or not to discount. Completely optional. But good luck competing with our super-discounted private labels if you don't.
Absolutely up to you how much to discount. By the way, here's exactly how much we'd like you to discount. Or else.
Unsurprisingly, their flagship sales—Amazon's Great Indian Festival and Flipkart's Big Billion Days—were record-breaking successes replete with discounts. Both platforms understand that discounting, perhaps more than religion these days, is the opiate of the masses. And they do their best to ensure a steady supply of cut-price deals. Today's story by Abinaya is about how.
