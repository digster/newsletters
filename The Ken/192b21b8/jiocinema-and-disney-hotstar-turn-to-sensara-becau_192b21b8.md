Good Morning Ishan,
Interstellar, RRR, Mayabazar, and The Lord of the Rings series.
Not exactly a weekend binge-watch list, in case you’re wondering. It’s a business plan in movie form for Sensara—a nine-year-old company working to get machines to “watch” films.
Founder Bharat Mohan jokes that his might be the “only company where employees get paid to watch movies”. And a mini lifetime of watching movies has resulted in a technology that tech giants like Google, Microsoft, and Meta have struggled with for years.
Teaching machines to truly “understand” long-form video content—unlike what Tiktok or Instagram hosts—is very, very hard. That’s why it’s also valuable for streaming platforms, broadcasters, and advertisers alike. Even for film-production houses. Because Sensara can do something most can’t—insert ads right into the content itself.
A few years ago, when I first caught up with Mohan, he said something that stayed with me. “True deep tech causes transformation, not disruption…Which means you must work with existing players to make them more efficient, without having to perform any big act…”
In Abhirami’s story today, you’ll see a shade of that thesis. With Jiocinema, Disney+ Hotstar, and a bunch of other customers using Sensara’s tech to gain that efficiency, Mohan is comfortable with the fact that he’s building for the long term, one streaming customer at a time.
“Our video product is not known in any detail by Big Tech,” he said. As he prepares for his next move—beyond entertainment—he is also waiting to close a potential deal with a major global streamer.
In that schema, where do Interstellar, RRR, Mayabazar, or The Lord of the Rings fit in?
