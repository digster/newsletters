The person heading Meta (formerly Facebook) in any country with regulatory oversight should ideally be envisioned as a cat on a hot tin roof. A look here, a glance there, a step ahead, a move back, and a dash to the safest spot.
India, the largest democracy in the world, stands out in this regard for Meta. The country, with a 1-billion-plus population, is not only one of the largest markets for its marquee platforms like WhatsApp and Instagram, but also an imposing regulatory and political labyrinth to navigate for the world's most influential social-media company.
At the saddle, tiptoeing through landmines and striking it rich in the goldfields of a trillion-dollar economy, was Ajit Mohan, the head of Meta India, who quit earlier this month to join Snap Inc., a smaller and less controversial rival. Two other top-profile exits ensued. WhatsApp India head Abhijit Bose and Meta India's public-policy chief Rajiv Aggarwal followed Mohan's exit.
Today’s story details the eventful times of Mohan's Meta days (or Meta's Mohan days). His role in the US$5.7 billion deal with Reliance Jio. His efforts to put the India operations on a hotline with Menlo Park instead of reporting to the Asia Pacific heads. His many frustrations at how the pivot to metaverse undermined the company's goals in India. And how Meta played dodgeball with Indian lawmakers during his tenure.
In a deeply reported and richly sourced story delving into every ebb and flow of Mohan's four-year stint at Meta, Anushka details not only the man himself but also Meta's tryst with what doing business as a global social-media behemoth in India entails.
