Google and Meta are two names that spring to mind when anyone mentions digital advertising. Sure, they consistently corner the biggest chunks of the market.

But Amazon has in recent years shown them it can’t be ignored. In 2021, its share of the US digital-advertising market was 11%, impressive in a business long considered a duopoly.

In India, Amazon was even better off, with its market share set to reach 20%. But as its two main rivals have stepped on the gas since, Amazon has lost its momentum.

It’s now desperate to fix that.

It has a 400-member team to sell ads but needs to offer more than its e-commerce marketplace. Enter an ad-supported version of Amazon Prime Video, without the free- and fast-delivery benefits on shopping, and a separate app for streaming cousin Amazon MiniTV, which is only funded by commercials.
