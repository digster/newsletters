Good Morning Ishan,
“Once people know what they know, they make the unconscious assumption that they were born knowing what they know, and forget that they had to learn everything they know.”
These words by American writer James Baldwin have been stuck in my mind all week, and I’ll tell you why.
There is a common notion about growing older: learning anything, whether it is a new skill or a new language, after a certain age becomes difficult.
I disagree.
Mainly because of my grandmother. She was the most curious cat I’ve ever seen, but curiosity didn’t kill her. Instead, it made her bloom—till the very end. Which is why I truly believe in the power of curiosity and that it can easily trump the atrophy that ageing comes with. From the time we’re born right till the very end, we remain lifelong learners. We wonder, we crave, we explore, and we discover—often taking us where imagination alone can’t.
It is this very curiosity that is at the core of what drives us at The Ken, and very often, it takes us places no one has ever ventured into. See it for yourself.
On Monday, Aayush took us [behind the scenes](https://the-ken.com/story/how-a-cloud-kitchen-startup-misled-investors-and-left-unpaid-bills-in-its-wake/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) of a four-year-old company that claimed to have raised Rs 100 crore ($12.1 million) in Series A funding. Only, it didn’t. The cloud-kitchen startup, Bigspoon, is now struggling to pay its vendors and has a bunch of litigations to deal with. Besides, its kitchens had to be shut down and the equipment had to be put up for sale.
On Tuesday, Pratap [wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/airtels-b2b-party-gets-lesson-from-tata-communications-warning-from-jio/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) about Airtel’s crown jewel, its B2B segment, which brought the telco $2.2 billion in revenue last year. Turns out, the man who built the business from scratch, Ajay Chitkara, is moving on from Airtel, and CEO Gopal Vittal is taking control of the segment. But with Jio gaining ground, the company needs to gear up for a fight.
On Wednesday, Nuha, in her first [report](https://the-ken.com/story/airlines-want-pilots-pilots-want-jobs-why-are-thousands-unemployed/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) for The Ken, told us the conflict-ridden story of pilots in India. More than 10,000 pilots are jobless in the country. But airlines, instead of hiring them, have been poaching pilots from each other. The story takes a deep dive into the turbulent airline sector and the challenges faced by the freshly minted pilots.
On Thursday, Vanita gave us a [rare insight](https://the-ken.com/story/a-job-with-mckinsey-bain-or-bcg-trumps-everything-or-it-used-to/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) into the inner workings of the “Big3” strategy consulting firms—McKinsey, Bain, and BCG—and how they’re faced with a talent crisis. As they say, the future belongs to the curious!
