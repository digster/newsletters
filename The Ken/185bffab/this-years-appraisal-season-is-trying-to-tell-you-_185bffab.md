The ongoing funding crunch and slow growth has prompted edtechs such as Unacademy and Vedantu to set their teachers monthly sales targets. And not meeting them is reason enough to get teachers fired as part of the edtechs' cost-cutting exercises.
In the latest episode of Daybreak, host Snigdha tells you how teachers are being forced to [become the new sales](https://open.spotify.com/episode/5muiqdi6eDWNqYWJp1uAIt)[ force](https://open.spotify.com/episode/5muiqdi6eDWNqYWJp1uAIt) in some edtech companies.
Also, in case you missed it, here's another interesting episode from last week:
Last year, the Competition Commission of India (CCI) ruled that Google had violated competition norms in two of the four antitrust cases filed against it. In one of the cases involving Google’s dominance in the Android OS market, the CCI has asked it to pay a fine of US$161 million.
Google approached the National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) contesting CCI’s order, asking the tribunal to squash it, but the NCLAT declined. Now, Google has moved the country’s top court.
Tune in to find out what the [Android OS case](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2mGqkBu9opJ4s597FPBvJQ) is all about.
