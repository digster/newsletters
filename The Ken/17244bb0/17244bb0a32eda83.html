Good Morning Ishan,
 
...where no man has gone before.
 
This is the introductory phrase from Star Trek, the great space TV drama that spanned nearly four decades from the 60s to the 90s. It inspired a slew of movies, cartoons, spin-off serials, and also contributed significantly to the careers of William Shatner, Patrick Stewart, Leonard Nimoy, and Benedict Cumberbatch.
 
The phrase encapsulates the adventures of spacecraft USS Enterprise and its voyagers. It is also a testament to the resilience of the human spirit that pushes one to go beyond one's limits. To explore new things. To breach the barriers of intelligence and strength. And, to paraphrase the dialogue from Star Trek, to seek newer civilisations.
 
Now, while we're gingerly awakening from the throes of the lockdown and trying to understand what worlds we can explore henceforth, a bunch of folks out there are already seeking newer horizons and pushing the boundaries.  
 
Our stories at The Ken this week had a similar line running through them. They were about a group of individuals, companies, institutions, all of whom want to boldly venture into the unknown.
 
Monday brought with it news from down south in Bengaluru. An alliance of startups—led by Cure.fit, Practo and 1mg—in collaboration with think tank iSpirt are looking to create a telemedicine platform to end all telemedicine platforms. The brains behind this project are also those who built India's Covid-19 contact tracing app, Aarogya Setu. They're calling it the "UPI of healthcare". Except, as the story by Seema and Arundhati traces the development, healthcare is no payments.
 
Tuesday saw Seetharaman's debut story for The Ken making a few sharp observations about co-working companies. Their bet on enclosed private spaces and big enterprises may give them a crucial edge in weathering the Covid storm. With demand in office spaces set to decline by 15-20% in 2020, co-working spaces will need to make lasting changes to go beyond and succeed.
 
On Wednesday, Prajakta and Vandana charted the tourism industry's step-by-step crash in both India and SEA. The sector contributed 9.2% of India's GDP and 13% of SEA's, in 2018. With flights grounded and tourism forced to a stop due to restrictions across the region, the Covid crisis has reshaped the travel and tourism industry for the foreseeable future. Analysis of data from 135,000 hotels and 900 airlines revealed just how drastic the changes were.
 
Thursday brought with it another of Abinaya's deep dives, this time into food delivery giant Zomato. The company, which has been reeling from a spate of layoffs, pivots, and loss of business, seems to have identified one key area to double down on—its farm-to-fork business Hyperpure. Hyperpure is a rare window of opportunity in an otherwise bleak time, with Zomato expecting a 2-3X demand in the B2B sector due to supply chain disruptions. Rival Swiggy is breathing down its neck, but Zomato has the first-mover advantage.  
 
And as we wound down for the week, Olina told the story of the nearly 270,000 affordable private schools in India. These schools bridge a gap between quality education and access, catering to almost 79 million Indian students. They're now facing an existential threat. With the start of the academic year pushed to September, these schools will need to find new and relevant ways to brace themselves against the storm. Else perish in the pursuit.
 
That's it from me. I'm retreating to my TV to watch the new Star Trek series that's now streaming. I hope they take me on a path where no man has gone before. A path of nostalgia. A path of resilience. A path of hope.
 
Here's wishing you a Happy Ramzan. And the strength to pick our lives back up after the lockdown.
