What’s your favourite story of Independence Day from when you were young?

Perhaps it was the sweets being distributed after the morning assembly at school. Or hoisting the flag in your colony. Or watching Doordarshan’s special programs on television.

I had none. Because I grew up in the Gulf, in a country which didn’t even have an independence day of its own, and where August 15th fell bang in the middle of my summer vacation.

Even though I don’t have stories about Independence Day, I understood what it meant. I was taught the narrative of this nation—its birth, its great empires, its struggles, and its eventual freedom. That’s the power of narratives. Stories are unique. They are personal. They are true, at some point of time, for a time. Narratives are universal. They offer common ideas everyone can identify with, and evolve, and change.
