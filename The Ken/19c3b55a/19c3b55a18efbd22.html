Turtlemint’s anti-Policybazaar idea heads to the market

One’s people-powered model will be stacked up against another’s software-centric one that clocked a 160% growth in profits in the December quarter

Two opposite bets on how India buys insurance. Turtlemint is scaling by selling insurance through 600,000+ digital partners. Policybazaar is scaling by making insurance behave like e-commerce. Public markets have already validated one model. Policybazaar has shown operating leverage and profitability—making its pitch to retail investors simpler than Turtlemint’s. The latter’s IPO hinges on whether a commission-heavy model can bend the cost curve. The next stress test may hit both at once. Regulatory experiments like Bima Sugam (and emerging structures like MGAs) could push distribution costs down and reshape channels. [Read the full story]

China is the new US. How to make the most of it as an investor

India’s giant neighbour beckons with riches, restrictions, risks, and workarounds

Over the past year and a half, the Chinese stock market has delivered what many investors crave: returns. Besides outperforming the US, the default global investing destination for Indians, valuations in China still seem reasonable. But Indians keen on riding this bull market are finding their access restricted. Only two MF schemes actively invite new bets on Chinese stocks. Even if the gates were to open wider, market watchers warn against going overboard with the idea. [Read the full story]

NEET PG built this rare profitable edtech. Now the exam may change

Daily Rounds makes over Rs 300 crore in profits. But the government’s push to tweak the postgraduate medical entrance test could cast the company adrift

The controversy over allowing candidates with negative scores on the Neet PG entrance test to enrol in postgraduate medical courses has put the spotlight on the government’s plan to replace the exam. This is something the most profitable edtech company, Daily Rounds, is keeping close tabs on. The company has maintained a 45% PAT over the past five years by focussing on the niche of coaching Neet PG aspirants. But with the exam looking destined for change, the company may soon face its first major challenge. [Read the full story]

Hyperpure rode Blinkit’s coattails to the top. Until Blinkit shrugged it off

The uncoupling may have cost Hyperpure its volumes, but has brought with it a silver lining: profitability

As Blinkit has become Eternal’s primary growth driver, Hyperpure has grown with it. That was in the past, when the two were joined together at the hip, but things have changed in the last six months. Hyperpure no longer sources for Blinkit, and has chosen to separate its books to focus more on the restaurant business. But it’s easier said than done to let go of your biggest customer and still walk away the winner. [Read the full story]

In seven months, vibe-coding startup Emergent has clocked $50 million in ARR and raised $100 million from investors. Speed and capital is a must have, as this Bengaluru and San Francisco-based startup competes with global giants.
