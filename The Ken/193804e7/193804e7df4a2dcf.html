The Big Story

Tiny stocks spell big trouble for small investors

Retail investors loaded up on hundreds of micro-cap companies even as their promoters sold. There may be no greater fools left

At about 13%, retail stake in small-cap stocks has hit a 15-year high. But nearly a quarter of the 1,300-odd smallest of the small-cap stocks on the NSE have crashed over 20% since September. While retail investors now hold the chunk of the stake in many of these companies, their promoters have reduced their holdings sharply. Now, with the stock market bulls on the backfoot, retail investors in micro-caps stand to get hurt the most. [Read the full story]

India set up a new body to fix medical education. It now needs to fix that body

The National Medical Commission faces a backlash from professionals over its baffling decisions, fueled by vacant posts and poor coordination among its boards

For several months, key positions at the country’s apex body meant to regulate medical education have been lying vacant. But that’s not the only problem with the National Medical Commission. It has courted controversy over its revised MBBS curriculum and its logo among other things. Medical professionals allege the dilution of criteria to open new medical colleges has led to a drop in the quality of education. What’s even worse is that the body tasked with fixing the corruption of its predecessor, the MCI, is mired in its own issues. [Read the full story]

Big business

Why Hindustan Unilever’s ice-cream demerger is not about ice creams alone 

As the FMCG giant rethinks its empire, Surf Excel and Glow &amp; Lovely are becoming more important than Kwality Wall’s and Cornetto—a business that’s worth Rs 1,750 crore 

Hindustan Unilever (HUL), an FMCG conglomerate spanning everything from detergents to shampoos, is planning to demerge its Rs 1,750 crore ice-cream business. Consisting of brands like Kwality Wall’s, Cornetto, and Magnum, the company is the second-largest ice cream maker in the country after Amul. The decision to spin off comes at a time when new D2C ice-cream players like Hocco, Noto and others are entering the market. However, with its other brands generating far higher revenues, HUL is choosing to focus on its core businesses and not spread itself too thin. [Read the full story]

One man’s dream makes Insurancedekho and Policybazaar nervous

Debasish Panda, head of India’s insurance regulator, is bringing the fight to the marketplaces with an unexpected new rival

Insurance marketplaces Policybazaar and Insurancedekho took two distinct routes to sell insurances to India’s under-penetrated market. Now both have a common rival—IRDAI-backed Bima Sugam—threatening to eat into their marketshare. While the marketplace is set for launch in March 2025, it’s really the approaching exit of IRDAI chief Debasish Panda that’s swung the industry into action. The real dent, however, is only possible if Bima Sugam is elevated beyond just a legacy project. [Read the full story]

Stories you don’t want to miss

Vegrow takes on Ninjacart and Dehaat with two fruits. Just two

In an agritech landscape where startups try to be everything to everyone, the four-year-old startup proves that mastering pomegranates and bananas could be more profitable than juggling the entire produce

While most Indian agritechs are racing to provide it all, one of them has chosen to focus only on fruits. From a modest Rs 12 crore start in 2020, Vegrow now handles Rs 394 crore in fruit trade annually. Vegrow’s asset-light model shows how modern agritech companies can scale without heavy investments. By being a middleman, Vegrow may have created a niche for itself, but scaling with it in India’s agriculture sector will be difficult. [Read the full story]
