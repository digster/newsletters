The last week has been a blur of 14-hour days as a small team of us researched, wrote, storyboarded, illustrated and then re-researched, re-wrote, re-storyboarded and re-illustrated an ambitious journalistic effort.

Our early drafts and sketches flowed freely, because they were the raw and unfiltered expressions of a few of us. Praveen Gopal Krishnan, our most unrestrained and unpredictable narrative artist. Sharath Ravishankar, our wonderfully talented and eternally subversive graphic artist. With some journalistic perspective and research from me.

This is the result. And we’re making it free.
What is it? It’s our effort to explain the history, motives and implications behind a seemingly simple regulation to ensure a “level playing field” for India’s large, legacy news organisations.

The expansive scope and broad language of the regulation could turn the clock back on how you've been discovering and consuming news each day. Because it covers anyone: independent news websites, internationally respected publishers, news aggregators, Google News, and even Facebook or YouTube.

And The Ken.
