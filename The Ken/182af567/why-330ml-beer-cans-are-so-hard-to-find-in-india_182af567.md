Good Morning Ishan,
Today’s edition is about a strange packaging quirk, and the product in question is one that’s very close to my heart.
Beer.
I love it. And I’ll admit, I’m a bit of a snob about it. So the standard-issue lager just doesn’t cut it for me. I like to get my hands on whatever is new on the market.
But I only buy pints. (That’s 330ml in India, in case you’re unfamiliar with standard beer-talk.) The other two sizes in which it’s normally retailed—500ml and 650ml—are too big unless shared. I know some of you will disagree; a decade ago, I would have done the same. But I find 330ml to be a perfect size now.
So why in the name of all that’s deliciously chilled and foamy, is it so freaking hard to find a pint-sized can of beer?
I tried to find out. And the reasons were not quite what I expected.
