Good Morning Ishan,
It’s a great time to be a defence company.
The wars across countries and India’s heightened spending on military means it’s a gold rush for those like Tonbo Imaging that are looking to go public now.
This is the time companies could ideally raise money, invest in research, and scale manufacturing. But Tonbo, which sells night-vision, thermal, and surveillance equipment to the Indian Army, Israel, and the US military, is curiously not looking to raise any money for the company.
Tonbo has a lot going for it. Its biggest strength is its 100% in-house, owned technology, and it has a robust orderbook. Even though it made a Rs 70 crore profit from Rs 470 crore revenue in FY25, it hasn’t been a cash-rich company. Moreover, defence economics can be infrequent. Profits surge and ebb with the policy-and-politics-heavy rhythm of defence deals, regardless of geographies.
