Swiggy is at the mercy of Zomato for its IPO

The question is not whether it should be at a discount to Zomato, but by how much. Without leaving too much money on the table

Swiggy’s going public at a time when Zomato shares have trebled over the past year, thanks partly to Blinkit’s staggering rise. Zomato’s dream run has laid bare Swiggy’s struggles in food delivery and quick commerce, in both of which it has ceded ground in recent years. Unlike food delivery, quick commerce is not a two-horse race, complicating Swiggy’s prospects. Once it lists, Swiggy has to measure up to Zomato in delivering on its targets quarter after quarter. Technology

Is AI in cancer care hype or the real deal? Depends on the box hospitals want to check

Hospitals like Apollo, HCG, and Sakra are using artificial intelligence to spot more cases in India. But the typical modern AI model isn’t ready for treatment yet 

In India, six out of 10 people diagnosed with cancer today will die of this disease. Screening for early detection of cancer is needed, but its business model has a problem. AI for screening is getting ready, thanks to startups, and so is AI for diagnostics, thanks to large hospitals like Tata Memorial, but for cancer treatment, neither is the needed AI here nor does India have its own datasets to get it all right. [Read the full story here]

Southeast Asian companies devour AI investments, thanks to the US-China tiff. Is there a limit?

American and Chinese tech giants, such as Microsoft and Alibaba, are competing in Southeast Asia, where local service providers are using AI to attract young, tech-savvy users

About 27 million workers in Indonesia use artificial intelligence tools in their jobs. The country attracted AI investments worth US$7.5 billion in two years, next only to Singapore in the Southeast Asian region. With an eye on that pie, both American and Chinese tech giants are upping their presence in the market with pledges of investment, jobs, and infrastructure. While the competition between the US and China will only benefit the Southeast Asian working class, the euphoria may be short-lived. [Read the full story here]

Fintech

Your SIM card is a new weapon in Airtel and Jio’s fintech war

Airtel is banking on its deep distribution network to do what many fintechs in India would give an arm and leg for

Bharti Airtel, India’s second-largest telco, has created two unlikely entities from its stable—Airtel Finance and Airtel Payments Bank. Both financial-services arms have generated lifetime-high revenues this year. Analysts believe the profitable fintechs are Airtel’s answer to Reliance Industries’ Jio Financial Services. And Airtel has been able to pull off the unlikely bet through its most trustable trump card—SIM cards. [Read the full story here]

The green transition

The Maruti-Toyota marriage of convenience is winning against EVs

That’s despite the government subsidies and price cuts that electric vehicles enjoy in the country

Hybrid cars have seen 4X registrations than EVs in the year so far. Making them the go-to choice for car buyers is a marriage between Toyota and Maruti Suzuki. It also came with a new lease of life for Toyota; 43% of the cars it sold last year were Maruti Suzuki-built. And it’s not a one-sided win; even Maruti Suzuki’s got the makeover it needed now more than ever. [Read the full story here]

TOEFL goes all guns blazing to take India’s English-test crown from IELTS

With other English-proficiency tests like PTE and DET now in the mix, students looking to study in universities abroad have an abundance of options

Over the years, IELTS has dominated as the top English-language exam for students and aspiring immigrants alike. But with IDP’s recent acquisition posing a challenge and visa-policy changes looming, its market dominance faces a threat. Sensing an opportunity, competitors like TOEFL are vying for a substantial slice. For that, it is mirroring IELTS’s successful playbook—from influencer marketing to offering scholarships to simplifying tests. [Read the full story here]
