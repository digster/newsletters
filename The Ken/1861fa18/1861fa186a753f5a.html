Good Morning Ishan,
 
I was holidaying in the Himalayas during the last week of January. As a terminally online Zillennial, I wanted a break from social media. And it was bliss while it lasted.
 
While buying groceries from a small store near my cottage, I overheard two middle-aged men fervently discussing “this business with the Adani stocks”. It piqued my curiosity, and I ended up on Twitter. Soon, I was reading up whatever Google search threw at me with the Adani keyword to decipher the larger ramifications of Hindenburg Research’s report.
    
Then on Monday, my colleagues Anand and Seetharaman did a comprehensive analysis detailing the domino effect of the Adani stocks’ meltdown. A sustained slide could have various grave implications even for the Indian economy, considering that Adani Group is India’s largest operator of ports, airports, and clean-energy projects, they wrote.
 
On Tuesday, Gaurav wrote about IPO-bound Pine Labs’ diversification push. Over the past 12 months, the PoS solutions provider has been busy loading up companies to create multiple revenue streams. But it’s the gifting, discounts, and loyalty-card business that’s powering its growth.
 
On Wednesday, Katrina wrote about Filipino farmers who are struggling to get loans. The Philippine banks are required by law to devote 25% of their loanable funds to the agriculture sector—a mandate of the Agri-Agra Reform Credit Act of 2009. But the mandate has been ineffective, harming both farmers and lenders. 

Arundhati on Thursday looked at what’s keeping Google Pay down in India, despite the rising adoption of UPI. With resources stretched and banking tech needing a revamp, India’s number two payments app is facing multiple battles in a rapidly changing war, she wrote.
 
We ended the week with Rounak’s incisive story on a real-estate insolvency case that is threatening to set off a vicious trend for homebuyers. Delhi-based Wave Megacity Centre’s attempt to declare itself as insolvent and desert 2,300 homebuyers has set a dangerous precedent that, if left unattended, can further widen the cracks in the already shaky real-estate business, he wrote.
 
That’s all from us this week. I hope you have a great weekend. We’ll be back in your inbox Monday morning.
