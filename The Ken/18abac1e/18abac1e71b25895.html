Good Morning Ishan,

Lately, whenever I see a doctor, I love to stir the pot by asking them, “Hey doc, on a scale of ‘not a care in the world’ to ‘running for the hills,’ how AI-scared are you?” They usually quip, “Those AI engineers can only dream of replacing us”—or something to that effect; you get the gist.

Now, don’t get me wrong; I’m not totally on board with the approach. But when it comes to generative AI—ChatGPT et al.—it does make me wonder: can healthcare really benefit from a tech that sometimes just makes stuff up or “hallucinates”, gives zero hoots about privacy, and basically plays predictive text in our lives?

It doesn’t matter. Big Tech is pushing innovations anyway, whether it’s for the name or the numbers: from AWS’s AI-powered documentation tool to Microsoft’s cancer-detection model.

This is why I find it fascinating when Anand Sivaraman, the brain behind Indian eye-care company Remidio, says doctors and public-health experts should be buddy-buddy with engineers when building AI models. Today, he presents his Counterthesis to Seema: building these AI models might be easy, but proving their worth in healthcare needs more than just a rearview mirror—it needs forward-looking data.

Remidio’s devices, deployed in the US and India, are equipped with medical-grade AI. Its AI team doesn’t just sit in the corner office; it rubs shoulders with the clinical team. Divya Rao, the company’s medical director, steers the AI ship.

And here are a few of the things Remidio has discovered over the years:

  • Why the initial use of AI is not in hospitals
  • How the economics of India’s healthcare system decides AI adoption
  • How collaborations lead to government support
  • Why impact at scale precedes value at scale

If you’re even remotely interested in the AI-healthcare crossover or are just up for a darn good read, this one’s for you. It’s a firsthand account of a new-age company—backed by the mighty Sun Pharmaceutical—doing new-age things differently. It also features some examples that you simply can’t afford to miss: https://the-ken.com/story/medicos-not-tech-bros-will-bring-ai-to-healthcare/ [10-minute read]
