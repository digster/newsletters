Good Morning Ishan,
JSW is a conglomerate in a hurry. A couple of weeks back, JSW Paints—among the younger businesses of the group—made headlines by winning the war for a majority stake in Akzo Nobel India. Over the past year, the industrial house has been in the news a lot, including for its aggressive moves in EV manufacturing. JSW’s flex is clear—it’s much more than its mainstay steel sprawl.
But beyond the headlines, the group has also been doing something many of its size consider boring and not worth their while. JSW One, the B2B e-commerce platform of the group, sells construction materials—steel, cement, TMT, the likes—online. Why? You see, boring can be lucrative, even in something as fraught as the internet economy where loss is the leitmotif. JSW One’s rivals in this business Ofbusiness and Infra.Market have shown that—they are profitable and set to go public.
Oddly though, the four-year old JSW One is now charting a path different from those of its IPO-bound rivals. It’s taking the ‘marketplace’ route, selling other brands and products too in addition to those from the JSW stable. Why is JSW One walking in a direction opposite to that of the tried-and-tested competition? Does it have an ace or two up its sleeve? Can this backfire? Does the still loss-making business have a choice?
