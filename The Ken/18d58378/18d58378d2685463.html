Good Morning Ishan,

Are you a part of a large housing society, or do you know someone who is? What is your/their relationship with the Residents’ Welfare Association (RWA)? How about the main gate? Why am I bombarding you with such random questions?

Well, two startups—community management platforms backed by the likes of Tencent, Tiger Global, and Google—are currently duking it out at these housing-society gates, and sooner or later, it will affect you.

In today’s story, Arundhati says one of them is becoming increasingly competitive—offering freebies and hoping that its rival “runs out of money”—while the other is getting uncomfortable.

On one front, we have the eight-year-old Mygate, whose services (neighbourhood management) are willingly paid for by thousands of societies. On the opposing end stands Nobrokerhood, a 2018-entrant armed with 5X deeper pockets and a mission to feast on the former’s offerings. Its tactics helped it secure affiliations with 21,000 societies by the end of 2023, nipping at the heels of its competitor’s 27,000 partnerships.

But here’s the thing: RWAs that are strapped for funds, are cheering for better deals from these apps, and now have a newfound bargaining power.

How long can they keep the freebies flowing? Can both of them foot the bill to lure users away from the other? Is the ad-based model a ticking bomb? And how does it affect the market—or you?
