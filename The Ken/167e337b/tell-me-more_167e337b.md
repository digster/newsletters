Merry Christmas!
My friends have been planning a party, off and on. "Do we do biryani?" "Let's try a roast?" "Okay, a full chicken is intimidating" "Let's go back to biryani?" "Wait...should I mull some wine?" "But it's hot in Bangalore..." "Do we sangria instead?" "No, no, too much fruit."
Yeah, indecision never sat well with me. But when the subject of indecision is a friendly get-together, it does feel rather merry.
Today, other than Christmas, is also our second day of publishing what I've been calling The Ken throwbacks. Planning and executing them were processes filled with just as many questions and doubts, and that's precisely why I think our year-enders this year are great.
Today's is on interviews. You've read our interviews. We don't like violently truncating them to fit a newspaper box. They flow freely, explore hidden corners of our subjects' minds; they're often fun, explosive, even curious. I've had the pleasure of editing most of the brilliant interviews we've carried this year, and it's safe to say it was hard to pick what we'd like to feature here.
Go through the throwback piece. Read the interviews. Have a great holiday.
