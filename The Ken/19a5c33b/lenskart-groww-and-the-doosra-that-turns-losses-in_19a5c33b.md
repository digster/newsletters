Good Morning Ishan,
The change of colour in Lenskart’s bottom line is one that many companies would envy: a loss of Rs 10 crore in FY24, then a profit of Rs 297 crore in FY25. Things looked great ahead of its IPO, even though more experienced investors might have concerns about its eye-watering 250X price-to-earnings ratio.
But look closely—and shed the rose-tinted glasses—and you’ll find that there’s a sleight of hand.
A one-time gain shored up Lenskart’s bottom line. In an alternate reality where this received different treatment on the company’s income statement, Lenskart’s profit would be cut by half, meaning its shares are being sold at 500X earnings. Overlook the remaining “other income”, and it becomes clear that Lenskart is, in fact, operating at a loss.
This sort of creative accounting that pads the bottom line fuels India’s heady IPO market right now. Other players of this game include Groww, Urban Company, Boat, Physicswallah, Pine Labs, and plenty more. It’s a situation that can catch retail investors off guard—the narratives that get sold don’t necessarily reflect these companies’ real states.
In today’s story, Anand describes the deployment of these financial cosmetics as doosra, where a spinning cricket ball swerves unexpectedly and confounds the batter. Some companies are doing something similar in their published financials, using non-core, one-time incomes to prop up their statements. The result? Investors imagine a glowing picture of their future.
