Good Morning Ishan,
Apollo Hospitals has had a great year, its profits jumping nearly 30% in the second quarter of FY26. This even as Apollo 24/7, the hospital’s digital arm that includes things like virtual consultations, online pharmacies, and at-home diagnostics, has been haemorrhaging money.
Of late, though, Apollo 24/7 has managed to staunch some of the bleeding, partly due to its customer-loyalty programme called Apollo Circle.
To put it simply, a quarter of Apollo 24/7’s 40 million registered users pay an annual subscription fee of Rs 299 to access benefits such as free tele-consultations, priority doctor access, and discounted medication.
“Once you pay for a membership, you are invested in buying all your upcoming medicines and tests from Apollo,” says a healthcare-industry veteran. It’s all about building stickiness. The kind that takes a customer ordering medicines online, and sells her maintenance diagnostics—routine lab tests and scans—and sometimes, even high-cost procedures.
It seems to be working. The digital vertical’s average order value has nearly doubled in the three years to 2024.
But in a country like India where neighbourhood doctors offer both walk-in consultations and customer discounts, Apollo Circle’s USP is not all that unique. The programme has managed to reduce Apollo 24/7’s losses, but getting the vertical to break even is another ballgame.
