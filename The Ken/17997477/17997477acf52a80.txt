It feels inopportune, on this Sunday, to bring up the вҖңWednesday SocietyвҖқ. The society was a group of psychologists who would meet on Wednesday evenings at Sigmund FreudвҖҷs home to discuss their profession over tea and cakes, heralding the beginning of the entire psychoanalytic movement.

Although it is largely associated with Freud and his disciple, Carl Jung, I recently learnt about a third giant in the fieldвҖ”one who would often disagree with FreudвҖ”called Alfred Adler. His teachings are distilled in a book called The Courage to be Disliked, which argues that all your problems, with being yourself and doing what you want to do, are because you donвҖҷt have the courage to be disliked.

Being okay with being disliked grants the freedom to do whatever you truly believe in.

The concept struck me because journalists have always had to deal with it. Whatever one writes finds both lovers and haters, but neither, in any amount, is a sign of a job well done.
