Good Morning Ishan,
 
If you think you are spoilt for choice, brace for more. Facebook wants to stream videos with Facebook Watch in India, launching it anytime between January and March.
 
It’s a crowded digital video market out there, with YouTube and other streaming services like Hotstar, Netflix and Amazon Prime Video. Watch is slightly different, though.
 
The social network wants to give people a reason—apart from the alerts about where your friends are vacationing or how they baked their last cake—to return to the News Feed for exclusive content.
 
While Watch competes with YouTube, we learn it is also copying the video-sharing site in setting things up in India, which according to reports, will have nearly 300 million users on Facebook in three years, up from 240 million today.
 
In solving some of Facebook’s problems, like monetising mid-roll ads, Watch is also looked upon as a beacon of hope for content creators who are struggling with the discoverability of their content (on YouTube) and revenue generation. Blame part of it on algorithms, which as you know, is increasingly becoming the next ‘the dog ate my homework’ line on the internet.
 
“Think of the news feed as opium. The question is where is the next high? Watch fits into that category. You discover something via the feed and then move to a place with a more premium experience. Facebook is trying to offer you that,” says a CEO of a media company in Mumbai.
 
Let’s hope we don’t get mediocre dramas, commonplace on the broadcast networks.
