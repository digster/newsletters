Good Morning Ishan,
 
It was during an endless Instagram reel scroll session that I discovered how coffee came to India.
 
A pilgrim known as Baba Budan came across a unique (and delicious) drink known as ‘qahwa’ when he travelled to Mecca. Travellers weren’t allowed to take the drink outside the country but Budan swore he would take it to his home. On the way back home to Karnataka, he stuffed seven green qahwa beans in his beard and planted them.
 
We’re among the world's top ten coffee producers, and it all started with seven green beans stuffed in someone’s beard.
 
If you’re a sucker for interesting trivia like I am, here’s some from our lineup of stories this week:
 
TikTok wants to do e-commerce in Southeast Asia. That’s it. That’s the tweet.
 
And it’s counting on social commerce to do it, writes Rachel. The company has introduced a shopping venture called TikTok Shop, an app for sellers called TikTok Seller, and is testing a ‘Shop’ feed on its main app.
 
Arundhati wrote about how Stripe came to India six years ago to disrupt payment, but instead got disrupted by regulation and its own policies. Now, it’s at a crossroads where India is both an essential and challenging market.
 
What makes for a good auction? The more scarce the item on sale, the more valuable it is bound to be. Pratap and Seema wrote about India's spectrum auctions and how they are becoming more of a bake sale than an auction. It’s a brilliant story, don’t miss it.
 
Gaurav and Seetharaman wrote about Zetwerk riding India’s localisation efforts by setting up its own factory for wearables and acquiring companies in defence and oil and gas.
 
Anand and Jaspreet ended the week with an important and intriguing story about the finance influencers in India. With simple, often simplistic financial advice, finfluencers in India have charmed and blustered their way into garnering millions of followers. The clamour for regulating finfluencers is getting louder, but outlining rules for them is easier said than done.
