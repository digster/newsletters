It was during an endless Instagram reel scroll session that I discovered how coffee came to India.
A pilgrim known as Baba Budan came across a unique (and delicious) drink known as ‘qahwa’ when he travelled to Mecca. Travellers weren’t allowed to take the drink outside the country but Budan swore he would take it to his home. On the way back home to Karnataka, he stuffed seven green qahwa beans in his beard and planted them.
We’re among the world's top ten coffee producers, and it all started with seven green beans stuffed in someone’s beard.
