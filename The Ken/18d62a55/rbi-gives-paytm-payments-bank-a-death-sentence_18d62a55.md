Good Morning Ishan,
It’s not for nothing the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the country’s most feared regulator.
In a one-page press release issued on Wednesday evening, it sounded the death knell for Paytm* Payments Bank, ordering it to stop accepting new deposits in its accounts or wallets.
Effectively, the central bank has put paid to 1.6 billion UPI transactions a month, 300 million wallets, 30 million bank accounts, and 8 million Fastags.
This has far-reaching ramifications for the parent entity of payments giant Paytm, which owns 49% of Paytm Payments Bank. If you are a Paytm UPI user, you won’t be able to make transactions using it because the “@paytm” ID is issued by the payments bank. And since the money you pay Paytm’s 37 million merchants, through UPI, is routed through it too, they have every reason to look for alternatives.
Paytm has to scramble to find another bank, and it only has until 29 February.
Even the $5.8 billion-market cap payments giant’s harshest critics couldn’t have imagined a worse nightmare. But what brought this on?
My colleagues Arundhati, Rounak, and Gaurav argue in their story today that it’s possibly the RBI’s discomfort with the payments app and the payments bank being conjoined at the hip.
As a Paytm executive put it, “You are using one’s reputation to sell what the other is not allowed to sell. So, the users have no clarity and are confusing one with the other.”
