Do you love irony? I do.
irony, noun – “incongruity between the actual result of a sequence of events and the normal or expected result”
At the centre of today’s story lies KYC, or Know Your Customer. Originally (and still nominally) designed to ensure that financial institutions accurately verify their customers’ identities, it has now taken on a life of its own—as the leading scam vector—in India.
Over 70% of recorded fraudulent banking transfers are linked to KYC requirements, says a senior official at the Financial Intelligence Unit, a national agency responsible for analysing data on suspect financial transactions.
How is that possible though? An average citizen is subject to a year-round series of passive-aggressive messages and emails from their banks and other financial institutions, asking them to KYC and reKYC. The penalty if they don’t do it? Their accounts will be frozen or suspended.
This has created a perfect vector for criminals to target millions of Indians each day. Sample each of these messages below. They’re all real and received by The Ken’s staff.
- “Dear customer your HDFC account will be block. Please update your PANCARD and complete your KYC. Click here. HDFC TEAM”
- “Dear Customer your S.B.l Netbanking Account will be suspended.Today please Update your PAN Card visit the link”
- “Dear Customer, Your Paytm A/ C KYC has been Suspended. Please Re-verify your Pan_Card Video KYC follow the link and Update”
Perhaps you’re smart and sophisticated enough to not fall for them. But are your parents? Is your household help? The office security guard?
Riding upon this perfect vector of fear, India’s digital criminals are upgrading their game. Small-time gangs operate from the tri-state area of Nuh (Haryana), Mathura (Uttar Pradesh) and Bharatpur (Rajasthan). Some ambitious scammers even upskill themselves by attending cybercrime classes in Kota.
