Good Morning Ishan,
 
In creative writing, “show, don’t tell” is an old technique for developing a story’s plot through action and details. In modern writing, though, both ‘show’ and ‘tell’ have their legitimate place. As they have in business communication as well because every business, at the end of the day, is about a story.
 
But what if a business resorts to a ‘don’t-show, don’t-tell’ strategy? Don’t tell the numbers, don’t show the roadmap, don’t talk about what’s not working, etcetera. It results in a crisis. Of trust, confidence.
 
Ask the investors and analysts of Reliance Industries. In the midst of a roaring bull run, the price of the stock has dropped. From mid-September till date, RIL stock has lost 16.5%, standing in sharp contrast to the 23% gain in the benchmark BSE Sensex. The overall weak show in the past few quarters and the stock’s high valuation haven’t helped either.
 
The December quarter results on Friday showed the company had slipped on both ‘show’ and ‘tell’ counts. While many were quick to point that its oil-to-chemicals business, which accounts for 65% of the group’s revenue, led to the slump, the fact is the group’s triple strike against transparency has been the market mover.
 
RJio, the telecom arm which contributed almost all of the operating profit to its digital holding company Jio Platforms, will get more opaque hereon. Some bit of analysis can still be done on the data that the regulator will release from time to time, but it will depend on the ingenuity of the analysts. 
 
Financial services, which has significantly grown as an asset segment for the conglomerate in just one year, remains a blindspot for investors. On its much-flaunted ‘net debt free’ status, let’s just say: don’t count your chickens before they hatch.
 
India’s most valuable conglomerate is reeling from the burden of expectations post its $27 billion fundraise over the past year.
