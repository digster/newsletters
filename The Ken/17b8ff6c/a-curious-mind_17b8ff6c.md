A few days ago, I found myself looking for my first ever tweet. Look, I get it—I am no important person. But cheap thrills, right?
I was never a prolific tweeter, to begin with, yet some nine years on the platform meant I had accumulated some 700 tweets to scroll past to get to the bottom. Many tweets were merely a link to my old Facebook posts. And of course, there were the kinds that made me roll my eyes at self—you know what to do with those.
After countless deletions and many minutes later, there it was. On June 20 of 2012, I wrote: “the important thing is not to stop questioning..” I was under no illusion that my younger self may have come up with something so profound, and it wasn’t long before I figured out who actually said it—Albert Einstein, the big genius himself.
As I sat down to write this recap note, I found myself reflecting on the same lines again. Curiosity is what drives us, too, at The Ken. And it's admirable where the right questions can lead you.
