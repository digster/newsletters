Good Morning Ishan,

It’s November, and in spite of the dire air quality warnings, all roads for India’s nutraceutical sector lead to Delhi. Because that’s where India's food regulator is putting together the first cut of pre-approved ingredients that can go into health supplements.

With over hundreds of ingredients on the list, entrepreneurs and large companies alike are desperately trying to convince the regulator’s scientific panel that their ingredient is safe and belongs on the list. Being on the list means their products don’t need to apply for regulatory approval.  

(I must point out to you that Horny Goat Weed is one of the approved ingredients. It’s a herb, yeah. And yes, you guessed right, it’s used to treat libido issues.)

The Indian health supplement space is set to hit $4 billion in 2020, so there’s serious money involved. Muscle supplements for gym rats. Immunity boosters for the weak. Weight loss pills. Diabetes-control pills. Joint pain supplements.

“This is the most elaborate regulation on nutraceuticals, not just in India, even the US FDA does not have a list as exhaustive as ours,” says RK Saghavi, the head of the Indian Drug Manufacturers Association (IDMA) committee on nutraceuticals.

Read Ruhi’s story on how the new regulation is going to affect a burgeoning and fast-growing industry. With India home to one of the largest micronutrient-deficient populations (women and children are worse off) globally, this is both a pressing need and a promising opportunity.
