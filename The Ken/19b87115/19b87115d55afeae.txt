At the time of writing this, nearly 4,000 subscribers have taken the poll that my colleague posed right at the beginning of this story about the size of the surplus that is available for deploying and how subscribers plan to allocate them across asset classes.

Essentially, Anand uses this poll to demonstrate a larger point: the rules on how to invest Rs 1 lakh, Rs 10 lakh, and Rs 1 crore are quite different.

Manus gets the best of both worlds—Chinese development for a global product https://the-ken.com/columns/zero-shot/manus-gets-the-best-of-both-worlds-chinese-development-for-a-global-product/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

The new recipe for AI entrepreneurs is to build in China, relocate, then land a $2 billion acquisition

In some ways, Manus fulfilled the Indian dream—build in India, and then get acquired by an American company. Except…Manus isn’t an Indian company, it’s a Chinese one.

Brady examines Manus’s acquisition (for a cool $2 billion, by the way) by Meta to ask a deeper question about the choice facing Chinese AI startups: should they build for China, or should they build for the world?

It’s a great analysis. I recommend reading it.
