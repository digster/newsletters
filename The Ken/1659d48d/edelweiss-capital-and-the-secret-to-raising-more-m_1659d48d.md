Raising money isn’t easy. In most of our stories, the protagonists doing the raising are startup entrepreneurs. But raising money is just as hard, perhaps even more so, if you’re actually raising a fund itself.
Eight months of the year have already gone by, and Indian private equity funds haven’t even raised 50% of what they raised last year—$3.6 billion. Money just isn’t easy to come by.
Then there’s Edelweiss Financial Services. Its alternative assets fund grew its assets under management by nearly 70% year-on-year, by the first quarter of this year. Across its funds, it has raised $900 million this year, and is targeting $4 billion before the year is up. If they manage that, it would make them India’s largest alternative asset advisor.
And as Pooja points out in her story, Edelweiss’ funds business has been exploding over the last three years. But the most surprising part about
Edelweiss’ fortunes is the fact that just 1,600 wealthy individuals constituted 76% of its wealth management business. Now, the conventional wisdom around Indian HNIs (high net-worth investors) is that they aggressively chase outsized returns. But Edelweiss says they are attracting them by offering 10-12% post-tax returns annually.
Investors who had previously invested in private equity funds during the peak of 2007-08 have come to realise that returns of 30-40% are distant dreams. Having burnt their hands, investors are now cautious and are instead seeking consistent returns over the lifetime of a fund.
