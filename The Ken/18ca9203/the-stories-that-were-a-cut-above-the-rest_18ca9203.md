Good Morning Ishan,
Yesterday, we brought you the [stories](https://the-ken.com/blog/the-stories-that-kept-you-hooked-this-year/) you loved reading and gifting in 2023. But that’s just 10 of the nearly 240 long-form stories we did from India this year. There are several other features we are proud of, and for a variety of reasons: because they unearthed what our competitors had missed; because they didn’t shy away from taking a strong, counterintuitive stance; and because they bolstered analysis with on-the-ground reporting.
Here are our top picks from our India stories in the last 12 months:
Reliance’s $12 billion gift to its newest child, Jio Financial Services
When Reliance Industries chose to demerge its financial-services arm and list it, the latter had no real business to speak of. But, as Anand’s sharp story shows, Reliance put its famed financial-engineering chops to use and transferred a $12 billion investment to the young venture, whose [net worth](https://the-ken.com/story/the-secret-to-jio-financials-13x-net-worth-boost-in-the-reliance-demerger/) shot up an incredible 13X.
Sachin Bansal’s Flipkart baggage weighs Navi down
|
|
Gaurav takes a sharp look at the [predicament](https://the-ken.com/story/https-the-ken-com-story-sachin-bansals-flipkart-baggage-weighs-navi-down/) of one of India’s most celebrated entrepreneurs. Bansal, who owns almost all of the nonbank lender Navi, is in no mood to compromise on growth. But that growth requires raising money from external investors, which he is not fine with. He doesn’t want to cede control the way he did with Flipkart.
Talks like an IIM, walks like an edtech: what exactly is Masters’ Union?
A three-year-old “business school” [challenging](https://the-ken.com/story/talks-like-an-iim-walks-like-an-edtech-what-exactly-is-masters-union/) the decades-old Indian Institutes of Management? As incredible as that sounds, that’s exactly what Masters’ Union has been doing, with claims of higher salaries in campus placements. But read Alifiya’s story, and you’ll know why there’s more to those claims than meets the eye.
|
|
How an Indian IVF chain became a global giant
With increasing attention on fertility treatments, private-equity firms have backed a number of Indian in-vitro fertilisation (IVF) chains. But one of them, [Indira](https://the-ken.com/story/the-cost-of-building-a-billion-dollar-ivf-chain-in-india/) IVF Hospital, stands out from the rest. Not just because Baring Private Equity Asia’s investment in the company, valuing it at $1.1 billion, was the world’s second-largest in this space. As Arundhati and Seema point out, Indira has shaken up how IVF is done in India, taking it directly to people instead of relying on gynaecologists.
|
|
How Tata Consumer’s Sunil D’Souza put product launches on steroids
|
|
Until Sunil D’Souza joined Tata Consumer Products as its managing director and chief executive in April 2020, the company was perceived as a middling fast-moving consumer goods player. But it’s been a different story since, with [impressive](https://the-ken.com/story/how-tata-consumers-sunil-dsouza-put-product-launches-on-steroids/) growth in most key metrics. The most fascinating part is how Tata Consumer increased its new product launches 3X in as many years. I sit down with D’Souza to understand how.
Shark Tank India phenomenon triggers a wave of risky investments by retail investors
Thanks to the popularity of Shark Tank India, platforms like Tyke Invest have been able to convince retail investors to put [money](https://the-ken.com/story/shark-tank-india-phenomenon-triggers-a-wave-of-risky-investments-by-retail-investors/) into startups through campaigns resembling public offers. But since such investments do not result in shares in the company, how kosher are they really? This story, written by our contributor, Tushaar, is an eye-opener.
|
|
How Nykaa went from investor darling to tech laggard
When it went public in late 2021, Nykaa had the best listing among tech companies, with its shares debuting at an 80% premium. But a year later, its fortunes had [changed](https://the-ken.com/story/how-nykaa-went-from-investor-darling-to-tech-laggard/) for the worse, and dramatically so. In this story, I look for the answer to this simple question: why did investors suddenly sour on Nykaa?
Can Binny Bansal’s consultancy save Licious from the premium trap?
Licious was India’s first unicorn in the direct-to-consumer space. However, lately, the company has been forced to come to terms with the limits of the market for premium meat, with its revenue for the year ended March 2023 falling well short of the target. Arundhati looks at Licious’ desperate [attempts](https://the-ken.com/story/can-binny-bansals-consultancy-save-licious-from-the-premium-trap/) at a turnaround, including enlisting the help of Binny Bansal’s startup consultancy, xto10x.
|
|
Kota’s Rs 6,000 cr coaching business may never be the same again
|
|
This year has been like none other for Kota, India’s undisputed capital of entrance-test coaching. And that’s not just because of the highest number of suicides on record. Alifiya visits the city’s coaching centres and hostels to tell an evocative [story](https://the-ken.com/story/kotas-rs-6k-cr-coaching-business-may-never-be-the-same-again/) about Kota’s hour of reckoning.
Why Airtel’s Gopal Vittal prefers “hunger” to “readiness” in next-gen leaders
If Bharti Airtel is among India’s most valuable companies, it’s largely because of Vittal’s ability to steer the telco through a bruising price war with Reliance Jio Infocomm. In the story, Pratap deftly [captures](https://the-ken.com/story/why-airtels-gopal-vittal-prefers-hunger-to-readiness-in-next-gen-leaders/) how Vittal, who has been the company’s chief executive for a decade, is readying its next set of leaders.
