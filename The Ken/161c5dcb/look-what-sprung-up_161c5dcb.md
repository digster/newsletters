Good Morning Ishan,
Spring is almost upon us. And no matter how much it's romanticised in poetry or celebrated in Indian festivals, I can't help but think that's overcompensation. Because honestly, in most parts of India, spring is kinda annoying.
Think about it. It's too hot for your winter clothes, but your summer clothes could leave you sick. Turning on the fan makes it chilly, turning it off makes you sweaty. Even your pets don't know if they want to curl up in the sun or stretch out on the floor.
Spring is annoying, and in all probability, so is this rant. I'll just get down to this week's stories then? Cool.
On Monday, [Venkat wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/inside-xiaomis-india-comeback/) about Xiaomi's snakes-and-ladders-esque rise and stagnation and fall and meteoric rise, enough to displace market leader Samsung! To think it started with a word-of-mouth strategy of sorts.
By Day 3, we'd moved on from Modicare to the Indian government's love for all things blockchain (and the lack of love for all things crypto). "Is this an informed decision or mere lip service to an over-hyped technical term du jour?" [Sumanth asked](https://the-ken.com/story/indiachain-parsing-indian-governments-blockchain-fetish/) in his piece.
Thursday had [Sidhartha peer into](https://the-ken.com/story/peering-and-threat-to-net-neutrality/) peering which helps all you binge-watchers watch your favourite Netflix/Hotstar shows buffer-free. Is it too good to be true? Does it come in the way of net neutrality? These are some matters to probe.
The week closed with [Patanjali and Venkat's piece](https://the-ken.com/story/india-internet-vision/) on India's internet vision...or the lack thereof? "Messaging, search, payments, e-commerce, food tech, mobility. The list of internet sectors controlled by American or Chinese companies is increasing," they write. What's the Indian government doing about this?
That's all. See you next week!
