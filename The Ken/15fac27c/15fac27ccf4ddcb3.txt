Good Afternoon Ishan,

We are what, a year and a month old now. So, let me ask you a question. How well do you know us? And by us, I mean the people at The Ken. Sure, you like our stories and you believe in the journalism we stand for, but that said, what do you know about our writers, our developers, all of us who work together to push out one story, every weekday? I think it is important that you get to know us, because we are real people, you know. We have a life, which goes beyond The Ken, a set of values which make us, us. To put it differently, who are “the people of The Ken”?

To answer this, we decided to invert our traditional lens of storytelling (since now our writers and staff were going to be the subjects) and experiment around a bit—we turned to our subscribers' community! For a change, how about we let one of our subscribers put one of us under the lens? That would be, err, fun.

So, sometime back we reached out to Praveen Krishnan to check if he’d be interested in doing this for us. Now Praveen had done the wildly popular 2-part comic series on India’s “foodtech” space last year (here’s part 1 https://the-ken.com/illustrated-guide-indian-food-tech-part-1/ and part?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter 2 https://the-ken.com/illustrated-guide-indian-food-tech-part-2/).?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter When we pitched him, he instinctively agreed. Even better, he came up with this fascinating idea. He said he’ll write just one story from the person’s life, like a definitive story. Hmm...And so Praveen got to it, in his own fascinating storytelling style.

Our idea is that over the course of the next one year, you’ll get to know us better. Our first story from 'The People of The Ken' is on Arundhati Ramanathan. 

You can read it here: https://the-ken.com/arundhati-ramanathan/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter https://the-ken.com/arundhati-ramanathan/

Interestingly, this is also the weekend some of us at The Ken will get to know each other better. An offsite is in the offing. And in keeping with the spirit of the offsite, let's take a moment to reflect on everything you've read from us in the past week.

A large chunk of this week was about excesses. We started with Sidhartha's FREE piece https://the-ken.com/youve-got-unwanted-mail/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter SPAM—yes, those pesky emails in your inbox that often don't let you unsubscribe.

Day 2 had Venkat document https://the-ken.com/subscription-models/ the?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter influx of subscription businesses. From Ola share pass to Zomato Gold and MMT Black/Double Black—not cigarette brands, we promise—there are new subscription options mushrooming every day.

Sumanth's Wednesday piece https://the-ken.com/flipkart-zugzwang-moment/ looked?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter at the problem of capital in excess (yeah, that's a real problem). Flipkart's $4 billion warchest has put the e-commerce giant in a spot, and now it's attempting initiatives that are more likely to leave it weaker than stronger.

By Thursday we'd shifted gears, much like the company Patanjali featured https://the-ken.com/oyo-southeast-asia/ in?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter his story—Oyo. The company's attempt to make room in Southeast Asian markets could prove a challenge because the India model needn't work everywhere.

We ended the week with Arundhati's story https://the-ken.com/demonetisation-paypal-bets-india/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter PayPal and how demonetisation finally had the payments company focus on India.
