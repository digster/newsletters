Degrees of distortion: rankings reshape Indian universities https://the-ken.com/story/degrees-of-distortion-rankings-reshape-indian-universities/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

From reducing their carbon footprint to bringing in more foreign students, universities in India are reshaping themselves to rise up the global ranking ladder

Today, climbing international rankings has become an important milestone for any educational institution worth their salt. From 11 institutes that featured in the QS Rankings a decade ago to 46 now, Indian universities are desperately trying to get into the hall of fame, fuelled by government incentives and schemes. Many are willing to pay top dollar to hire consultants and purchase seats at conferences to ace the ranking metrics. In the meantime, ranking parameters are forcing universities to reengineer their models, too—from admitting foreign students to doling out incentives for research and even adding new courses to the roster.
