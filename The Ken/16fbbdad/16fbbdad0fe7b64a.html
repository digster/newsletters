Good Morning Ishan,
 
Just before I joined The Ken, I pulled out all the notes I’d made during the one semester of business journalism class we had in our journalism course. I religiously read multiple publications specialising in business news. I began to mumble terms like EBITDA and equity in my sleep. 
You see, I was terrified. 
 
Terrified that I would arrive hilariously uninformed on the first day of my new job. That I wouldn’t be able to do justice to the pieces I was supposed to be editing. 
 
I had based my journalistic career on the 24-hour news cycle in a move-fast-and-break-things way that Mark Zuckerberg would probably approve of. Diving head-first into a beat that constantly reminded me of all the essays I’d written in 12th grade and gotten a refrain of ‘economics is not your best subject’ from my teachers was, to put it mildly, nausea-inducing. 
 
But, as I found to my surprise, that’s not how The Ken does it. Our stories have jargon, but they are also painstakingly explained. We dive deep, but the reader is never taken for granted. Funnily enough, my bar for editing is actually me: would have I understood a particular chunk of text three months ago? 
 
This week was no different. We distilled hard-to-understand information into engaging reads—we even wrote about something as offbeat as poker!
 
That was our Friday story; how the growing popularity of poker as a career choice is nudging into existence a variety of courses teaching the game. Even management institutes like IIM-Kozhikode are getting into the act, wrote our contributor, Gayatri Gambhir Sarin. Professional poker players, too, think that the game can be taught as a life skill. But the semi-legal status of the game and the taboo it brings with it are obstacles players will need to overcome. 
 
On Thursday, we published what is probably my favourite story of the week—the rise of the product manager. As our co-founder and editor Seema succinctly put it in her email introducing the story, product managers specialise in being generalists. A PM is the perfect example of Jack of All Trades, and yet, she must be master of most of them, too. Olina and Shreedhar wrote how this is now a coveted career path and how the shift has made the OG PM, the project manager, nearly obsolete. 
 
Wednesday saw Pratap carefully analyse an exciting development in the telecom sector—Canadian asset management firm Brookfield Infrastructure’s acquisition of Jio Infratel. This acquisition has given Jio Infratel, and Reliance Jio by extension, the push to add another 45,000 telecom towers to its already 130,000-strong portfolio. This would also mean Jio Infratel will surpass the combined might of its older rivals, Bharti Infratel and Indus Towers. 
 
On Tuesday, The Ken’s team of Pranavs, as they are fondly called, got together to write about Lithium, India’s first and biggest all-electric cab operator. Lithium seems to have cracked the EV code, despite the problem of inadequate charging stations. It plies in a sector no one would have even thought of as a potential market—the corporate commute. Companies, however, are not all on-board. EVs are expensive in the short-term, even if the long-term payoff is worthwhile. But Lithium is still pushing ahead with its expansion plans. 
 
We opened the week with an interview. Monday was the day for The Ken’s rare but solid The Interview. This time, it was our co-founder and CEO Rohin who sat down with Big Basket’s Hari Menon and teased out the story of how the company took shape in a five-minute phone call and a subsequent meetup at a coffee shop. And in the end, it all came down to strategy, even in the face of your immediate rival getting all the big funds. 
 
That’s it from me for this week. Hope you have a relaxing Sunday and a great week ahead.
