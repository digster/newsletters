Being in the right place at the right time is great. But that’s just half the battle won. The more crucial half is doing the right thing—even if it means betting against big odds.

A punt on the stock of Indian Hotels, the country’s largest hospitality company, during the depths of Covid despair in April 2020 would have been the right thing to do. Hospitality stocks had been hammered down as if there was no tomorrow. And then, the tide turned. The stock of the company—which runs the Taj, Vivanta, Gateway, and Ginger chains, among others—has shot up 11X since then, never mind its weak show this year.

Sure, revenge-travel and then the structural surge for all things hospitality post-pandemic helped. But again, that’s just half the story. Indian Hotels, far bigger than its peers, has done far better than them.

Much of this has to do with Puneet Chhatwal, helmsman for eight years, who made the most of being at the right place at the right time. The stodgy giant with a stodgy stock is now on much firmer footing by moving nimbly and gingerly, pun intended.

In a fascinating Q&A with Seetharaman, Chhatwal offers a peek into the many things Indian Hotels did right and set right. “If you take the new hotels, Ginger is any day higher [on profitability] than Taj,” captures one of the big shifts, a deeply cultural one too.
