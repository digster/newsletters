Good Morning Ishan,
Today’s story is about how Indians are paying more for the privilege of swift service. But we’re not talking about Zepto or Blinkit here.
If you’ve ever applied for a visa to visit the US or Europe, you’ll know exactly how tedious it is to navigate the bureaucratic labyrinth of embassies and visa centres run by VFS Global.
Applying for a visa has never been fast or convenient. And things have only gotten worse in recent years—thanks to the huge backlog of pandemic-era applications and limited staff availability.
No wonder Nuha, the writer of today’s story, describes VFS as “where many travellers’ dreams go to die”.
But the picture could be quite different for those who have money to spare and no time to waste. Around 25 travel agents told Nuha they could help their clients jump the queue—for a price. Schengen-visa applicants typically wait for weeks or even months for an appointment nowadays, but an agent said he could secure one in the next two days.
High rollers pay up to Rs 50,000 to secure a date, even though an appointment is no guarantee of a visa. And travel agents have an entire bag of tricks to get them the appointment they need. Even VFS makes a pretty penny by charging for value-added services.
