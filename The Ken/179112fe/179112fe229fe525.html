Good Morning Ishan,
 
Financial experts often write about how your daily fix of coffee/latte at the local Starbucks is the biggest punishment you can dole out to your future self. That cutting back on these ‘luxury’ habits can help you achieve your goals faster.
 
The need is caffeine, which can be quickly fixed at home. But the want is to be serviced by a smiling barista in a cosy café (who’ll probably get your name wrong). And that cuts a hole in the wallet.
 
It’s called the Latte Effect.
 
This realisation—though it isn’t something I subscribe to, having spent a few years in wealth management—birthed an almost cult-like movement across the globe called FIRE. It’s an acronym for Financial Independence, Retire Early. Followers often practice extreme frugality and intense savings. There’s even a documentary about the movement called Playing With Fire. The website asks, “How Far Would You Go For Financial Freedom?”
 
By now, you’re either scratching your head wondering why I’m going on about retirement, or you’ve figured out the theme of today’s story.
 
Today, Anand delves into the world of retirement planning and saving in India and comes up with some numbers that you’re not likely to forget in a hurry.
 
Like this one: if you’re 30 and want to retire by the age of 60, you’re probably going to need upwards of Rs 10 crore saved up just for this goal. Why so much? Well, inflation’s the silent killer.
 
Or this one: a recent survey conducted in India among 3103 Indian adults aged between 26-60 years in 15 cities laid bare the fact that 89% of respondents were unprepared for retirement.
 
While traditionally much-loved (and government-run) investment options such as the Public Provident Fund (PPF), National Savings Certificate (NSC) and Senior Citizen Savings Scheme (SCSS) still offer some respite in a low interest rate environment, it’s just the foundation for the retirement tower. The government’s National Pension Scheme evolved from the realisation that to ensure a better financial future for India, products need to keep up with the times as well.
 
But without a solid social security net in India, maybe it is time for at least young India to ditch that latte and embrace FIRE?
