In the spotlight

Sachin Bansal’s loan offer: take money, let Navi peek into your bank account for years 

Lenders like Navi are taking the step of extreme borrower surveillance. This is hardwired into its DNA

Navi, along with Bajaj Finance and HDFC Bank, has emerged as a top user of account aggregators. Through this, lenders can access bank statements straight from the borrowers’ bank accounts. Navi wants access to bank statements in a way that is much more than what is recommended. The company uses and seeks data to tweak any metric that can give it growth. For Navi, a tool like this is important because it needs its collection game to be at its best. [Read the full story]

Visa slots for sale: how Indian travel agents game VFS, consulates

Using the visa-service-provider-consulate partnership, they turn the ‘unavailable’ slots for US and Schengen visas accessible

The pandemic disrupted plans for those looking to travel abroad. But now, they are back with a vengeance and it’s overloading the systems. With appointment slots hard to come by, travel agents have turned securing visa dates into a profitable business. These slots come at a steep price, with some paying up to Rs 50,000 extra for a US visa appointment. Meanwhile, the embassies and the consulates have their hands full trying to limit the waitlist. Big business

He founded a unicorn. Got bored. Now wants to digitise 60,000+ hospitals 

Rizwan Koita, who rode the Obamacare wave as Citiustech CEO, is plumbing Indian healthcare as NABH chairman. He agrees that health services options “should be like Zomato”

More than 100,000 small hospitals make up three-fourth of healthcare delivery in India. Their size and quality variability makes digitising healthcare an impossible task. Rizwan Koita believes only 25% of mom-and-pop hospitals need to give up their mom-and-pop software for digitisation to take off. To make healthcare more effective, transparent, and comparable. ‘Need a gold loan? Come through the back door’ 

Lenders are flouting every rule in their books to cater to the rising gold-loan demand. The Ken tracked down such cases deep in the country’s hinterlands from Karnal to Bulandshahr

With rising gold prices, gold-loan demand in India has also been soaring. Parallel to this growth, malpractices and violation of rules are also becoming the convention. And the Reserve Bank of India’s clampdown on IIFL Finance has only made the violators smarter. Even as the Finance Ministry has been warning lenders, there’s hardly any evidence of it working. How Rentomojo and Furlenco got buried under the weight of their own furniture 

A mix of unhappy customers and investors have rental platforms scrambling to rethink their strategy

Rental startups emerged alongside tech-focused platforms, but they faced challenges shortly after. Scaling up beyond the top Indian cities has been a pain point. Competition came not just from other players in the market, but also from organised co-living chains. As a result, Furlenco, one of the biggest brands in the space, has now shifted focus to selling furniture instead of renting. [Read the full story]

Beyond India

80K jobs and billions of dollars at stake in the Philippines’ crime-infested gambling industry clean-up 

The Philippines plans to shut its doors to foreign offshore gamblers, but replacing the lost revenue won’t be easy

Philippine Offshore Gaming Operators, or POGOs, serve foreign gamblers who play online casino games. Criminal syndicates have turned to using POGOs as fronts for human trafficking, money laundering, and kidnapping. Despite that, around 80,000 people work in the sector who claim that the job pays too well to pass up. The government hopes to shut down all POGOs by the end of 2024, so the Philippines’ casino regulator is seeking new sources of tax revenue. [Read the full story]
