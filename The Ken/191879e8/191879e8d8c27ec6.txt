80K jobs and billions of dollars at stake in the Philippines’ crime-infested gambling industry clean-up https://the-ken.com/story/80k-jobs-and-billions-of-dollars-at-stake-in-the-philippines-crime-infested-gambling-industry-clean-up/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

The Philippines plans to shut its doors to foreign offshore gamblers, but replacing the lost revenue won’t be easy

Philippine Offshore Gaming Operators, or POGOs, serve foreign gamblers who play online casino games. Criminal syndicates have turned to using POGOs as fronts for human trafficking, money laundering, and kidnapping. Despite that, around 80,000 people work in the sector who claim that the job pays too well to pass up. The government hopes to shut down all POGOs by the end of 2024, so the Philippines’ casino regulator is seeking new sources of tax revenue. [Read the full story https://the-ken.com/story/80k-jobs-and-billions-of-dollars-at-stake-in-the-philippines-crime-infested-gambling-industry-clean-up/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card]
