Good Morning Ishan,
 
Turn on the news today and chances are the conversation is a grim one. Perhaps coronavirus. With new cases popping up in India with each passing day, the only people with reasons to smile are hand sanitiser manufacturers who've seen sales skyrocket as panicked people throng pharmacies in a bid to stay safe. 
 
Then there's the ongoing aftermath of the Delhi riots, with the death toll now well past 50 and countless people in relief camps. Rains in recent days haven't helped matters.
 
Or if its business news that's your forte, Yes Bank—India's fourth-largest private bank—is on life-support.
 
The bad news seems to be coming thick and fast these days. So today, on your precious Sunday, take a break from the relentless negativity and read some of our carefully crafted, long-form stories from this past week instead. You'll be smarter for it and, hopefully, less inclined to believe we're living in the end times.
 
Our Monday story was on Curefit, the health and wellness octopus best known for its chain of gyms—Cult.fit. In it, Ruhi wrote that food was fast displacing gyms as Curefit's most important vertical.
 
On Tuesday, we had a piece from our Southeast Asia edition, co-written by Jon and Jum. It covered Gojek's stunted expansion plans in the region, where the ride-hailing company is still overly-dependent on its home base of Indonesia.
 
Then we turned our lens to Club Mahindra, the over two-decade-old travel company that has been in the doldrums for some time now. Vandana chronicled how it has struggled to keep up in an evolving travel market that's increasingly geared towards flexibility.
 
Thursday took us into the fintech space. PhonePe, the Walmart-owned payments unicorn, is doing something seemingly counter-intuitive. While its goal for years has been to kill cash, Arundhati decoded why it's now embracing cash—by turning itself into a new-age ATM.
 
Friday's piece by Shreedhar, about Microsoft-owned source code hosting platform GitHub's India ambitions, was our free read for the week. With the company ramping up its operations in the country, we tried to understand what the underlying mission is. Hint: It's closely intertwined with Microsoft's enterprise business ethos.
 
There was, of course, our weekly newsletter The Nutgraf as well, where we looked at everything from Yes Bank to Jio and more.
 
We got some more great stories lined up for this coming week, but our Indian edition won't be publishing tomorrow on account of Holi. If you still need your daily dose of The Ken, do check out our Southeast Asia edition!
