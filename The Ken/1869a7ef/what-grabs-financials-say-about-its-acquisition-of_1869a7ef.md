I have a confession to make. For someone whose job is to follow the latest developments in the consumer-tech space, I’m highly allergic to using apps.
But like the rest of the team at The Ken, I still download apps, reactivate my accounts, test out new features over the span of days, and try to form objective conclusions about them. So, yes, we order meals, groceries, book rides, and more.
Every time I wait for groceries to arrive at my doorstep, I feel like I’m missing something: the chit-chat with greengrocers who I buy fruits and vegetables from or the joy I feel when I pick out a nice slab of meat.
But that isn’t the case for folks in Kuala Lumpur, as I’ve been informed. After Grab acquired a majority stake in Jaya Grocer in January 2022, it’s been pulling out all the stops to direct users into buying their daily staples from Jaya Grocer.
Then, Grab intermingled the supermarket chain’s offline sales with its broader delivery revenue.
The result was an incredible surge: US$268 million in delivery revenue in the year ended December 2022, way up from a mere US$1 million a year ago.
This made a section of Grab’s balance sheet look fantastic when the company reported its 2022 financials on 23 February. But the move was a deft sleight of hand while the company remains in the search of new growth opportunities.
