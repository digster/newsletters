Good Morning Ishan,
As far as pivots go, this has got to be one of the most intriguing ones.
A 79-year-old IT-training company called Jetking once taught students computer hardware, networking, and digital courses. But since November 2024, it has taken to Bitcoin hoarding, and its investors seem to love that. Ever since it began loading up on Bitcoins, its stock is up 5X.
The reason is simple.
“For many Indians, directly buying Bitcoin via exchanges or exchange-traded funds is complex,” said Siddarth Bharwani, joint managing director and chief financial officer of Jetking.
That’s because India’s regulatory stance on crypto remains murky. The Reserve Bank of India has long been sceptical—first issuing warnings in 2013, then banning banks from dealing with crypto companies in 2018. That ban was overturned by the Supreme Court in 2020, but the uncertainty never quite left.
Through Jetking, investors can gain indirect exposure to the asset by just holding a stock. So Jetking holds Bitcoin, and investors hold Jetking. And the best part? Turns out you can beat the 30% crypto tax and instead sign up for equity-style tax breaks.
This should be a cue for all crypto enthusiasts to line up for Jetking’s stock; the company has some big backing—the Burman family of FMCG major Dabur and Nitish Mittersain, MD of gaming company Nazara Technologies.
But the company has now latched onto a product that’s volatile and exists in a regulatory grey area, making for a shaky business model.
