In September 2016, South African internet investor Naspers engineered the marriage of two of India’s leading payments businesses—PayU India and Citrus Pay. PayU India, a Naspers company, bought its smaller rival Citrus Pay for $130 million in cash.
It would be another month before The Ken would launch officially, so we ended up not going deeper into that story. A story of an 1100-person company that had nearly four times the revenue of its smaller 300-person rival, buying it for cold, hard cash, and then putting the acquired company’s leadership in charge of the combined entity. It didn’t make sense. Acquisitions don’t work that way. It was a story that begged to be told.
Today’s story isn’t that story. Instead, it’s about one of Citrus Pay’s original two founders, Satyen Kothari. The one whose brainchild Citrus Pay was. In conversation with Ashish. The story’s strap says it best: "The 48-year-old founder of Cube on four businesses, a fifth on its way. What exactly transpired at Citrus Payment Solutions; where from co-founder he suddenly became persona non grata. A crash course on the business of digital payments that’s increasingly looking like an ape jungle. Plus, learnings from a life half led, summed roughly thus: Stop. Think what just happened. Start again.”
