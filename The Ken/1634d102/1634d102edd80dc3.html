Trai vs Telcos, 10 May 2018.
 
Okay, I’ve not made this up, unlike my colleague Rohin who does a masterclass in made-up dictionary definitions and business situations. (You’d have read one in the email yesterday.) I’ve merely clubbed multiple court cases that India’s telecom regulator is fighting under this rubric.
 
It’s for easy recall. Because where do you find a regulator who has all of three market-moving functions to perform, and in each of which, it gets challenged? By those it is meant to regulate. 
 
We are talking Telecom Regulatory Authority of India, or Trai, which is not taking it lying down and has filed caveats in 18 High Courts. Its regulation of tariffs, the interconnection between carriers, and quality of service for consumers are all under the legal lens. It wasn’t meant to be so for the 21-year-old regulator whose recent regulations are seen as partisan, and seem to be helping one telco: Jio.
 
Trai has its counter-arguments, but can they be brushed off as Regulator’s Dilemma? Months of on-and-off confrontations are bruising the industry, but as George Orwell once wrote: “To see what is in front of one’s nose needs a constant struggle.”
 
That’s Shashi’s story for you today. Trai held an all-hands-deck meeting late in April to introspect and strategise. As a new level of convergence comes to the customer in bundled services of phone, internet and TV, a defanged regulator can neither regulate nor be a catalyst for effective competition.
