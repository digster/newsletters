Good Morning Ishan,

 

The most frustrating time for a writer is not when they have nothing to say, but when they have too much. There is never a good way to start and never a good way to end. The middle, often times, is all over the place, too. Yes, it is a short but honest admission of how my first drafts at The Ken are but let’s keep that between you and me. A lot of people work on these stories, not just the writer. It is pieced together like a jigsaw, and what you read is a labour of love. 
 
 
It may just be one story a day, but the effort is almost as much as 10. And each story is worth its weight in whatever precious metal your heart desires. This week’s stories were that and much more. In fact, in the last 20 months, The Ken has done some fabulous work. And you can access all of that if you just subscribe. 
 
 
If you still want a taster, let’s start with our FREE story of the week. Ruhi has been following the price control in medical devices saga for a year. And now she realised that they will be used as a tool in what seems to be a trade war with the US. India insists these devices need to be accessible to everyone. US MNCs are not all on board.  
 
 
Ruhi wrote one more this week. She was on a roll. This one was on Haryana being the testing ground for Modicare. This story dives deep into the struggles, the successes and the various strategic decisions that had to be taken to execute something so new. 
 
 
Also, Rohin wrote one. It is one of those things about The Ken. Even the CEO writes. This one was about Mobisy, a company that refuses to die. It is a SaaS company made up for earlier failed entrepreneurs, which has reached countries such as Nepal, Bangladesh, Tanzania and Nigeria, apart from India, that is. This is a curious one. 
 
 
Talking about refusing to die, there was one by Arundhati about P2P lenders. Peer-to-peer lenders, as a concept, is not new. And it has got its tech facelift, but something has changed: the defaults. You want to check it out. 
 
 
Sidhartha and I wrote a story, too. Yes, both of us, after our two Vakrangee adventures, got together to write about Infibeam. It is a strange company. It is the only profitable IT services/e-commerce company in the country. Can you imagine it? We couldn’t either and we realised that things don’t add up. 
 
 
This was a whirlwind, wasn’t it? I am still a little overwhelmed from the last week. I hope you are enticed enough to subscribe. It is one of the best things that happened to me. Trust me. You just have to. 
 
 
There is not much else to say. Have a good weekend. 
 
 
Warmly,
