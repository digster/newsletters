Good Morning Ishan,

The most frustrating time for a writer is not when they have nothing to say, but when they have too much. There is never a good way to start and never a good way to end. The middle, often times, is all over the place, too. Yes, it is a short but honest admission of how my first drafts at The Ken are but let’s keep that between you and me. A lot of people work on these stories, not just the writer. It is pieced together like a jigsaw, and what you read is a labour of love. 

It may just be one story a day, but the effort is almost as much as 10. And each story is worth its weight in whatever precious metal your heart desires. This week’s stories were that and much more. In fact, in the last 20 months, The Ken has done some fabulous work. And you can access all of that if you just subscribe.

This story dives deep into the struggles, the successes and the various strategic decisions that had to be taken to execute something so new. 

Also, Rohin wrote https://the-ken.com/story/mobisy-algorithmic-jugaad-company/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter one. It is one of those things about The Ken. Talking about refusing to die, there was one by Arundhati https://the-ken.com/story/peer-to-peer-lending-claims-its-first-victims/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter about P2P lenders.
