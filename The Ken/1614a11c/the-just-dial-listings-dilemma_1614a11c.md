Good Morning Ishan,
There are two well known VSS' in the Indian entrepreneurial ecosystem. Both are self-made, first-generation, canny operators. One of them even gets the “*” honorific every time we mention him in our stories. Today’s story is about the other VSS.
VSS Mani is the founder and CEO of Just Dial, one of India’s rare Internet firms that are listed. (And currently worth around $500 million). It’s doubly rare because it’s profitable too.
Alas, such are the times we’re living in that being profitable and listed while *not* sporting the tickers GOOG, FB, AMZN or BABA could be a bad thing. Especially if you’re in India.
Just Dial has 10,000 employees to collectively deliver a revenue growth of a measly 8%. Of the 105 million visitors it gets every quarter, it needs to purchase over 75% from others (cue, the Google/Facebook “tax”). It’s getting squeezed by vertical specialists like Zomato, BookMyShow and CarDekho on one end, and by biggies like Google and Facebook on the other.
But VSS Mani soldiers on. He’s tried BHAGs (big hairy audacious goals). He’s tried throwing more people at the problem. He’s tried becoming a “super app”.
No one said the life of an entrepreneur was easy, and certainly not one heading an Indian Internet business listed on stock exchanges.
Read Ashish’s deep dive into Just Dial’s dilemma to know more. Of course, you want to know more.
