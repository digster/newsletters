The original lockdown is over. We're now in lockdown limbo. That uncertain state where we know things aren't anywhere close to safe, but the country is slowly grinding its way back to normalcy... whatever that even means anymore.
The eerie silence outside our homes is more frequently broken by the noise of passing vehicles. Non-essential businesses are open, even if e-commerce platforms still function squarely in the realm of the "essential". Migrant labourers, who fought for weeks to return home, are being packed into trains and returned to faraway states in need of labour.
All the while, cases of Covid-19 continue to rise, as does the death toll from it.
For most of us, work never stopped. It merely followed us into our lockdown silos, the boundaries between the professional and personal more tenuous than ever before. But now, as exhortations to emerge from hiding for the greater good of the economy grow shriller, we are forced to make a choice.
Do we shrug off the past two months of caution, or do we stay put?
There's no easy answer to this. For me, it's about waiting for the worst to pass, however long and painful that wait may be. I'm resigned to meeting reality on reality's terms. I also understand that's a luxury. For many, getting back to the pre-Covid status quo is imperative to survival. Which camp do you fall in? I'd love to know.
