‘Healthy’ drinks make up half of Varun Beverages’ sales. Or do they? Especially when its superstar product, Sting, is part of the mix 
 By Seetharaman G https://the-ken.com/writers/seetharamang/

When Varun Beverages announced its financials for the September quarter last week, its investors had reason to rejoice. Pepsico’s second-largest franchisee outside the US may not have been able to match its growth from last year. But it had certainly left its peers in the dust. 

Overall volumes in fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) inched up just over 4% https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/cons-products/fmcg/fmcg-volume-growth-slows-amid-higher-prices-and-rains/articleshow/114663149.cms?from=mdr in the quarter. But Varun Beverages grew at an impressive 5.7%.

But that’s far from the most intriguing number in the bottler’s no-frills investor presentation https://www.bseindia.com/xml-data/corpfiling/AttachHis/22666d6a-1920-40aa-8dd8-af9f4a377021.pdf. Varun Beverages claimed that “low sugar” and “no sugar” products were responsible for half its sales volume in the first nine months of 2024. 

Has the market for healthy beverages really become so enormous? Especially when such products contributed to just a third of the company’s sales in 2022. 

Turns out, the mystery lies in how “low sugar” is defined. Varun Beverages’ annual report https://www.varunbeverages.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/VBL-AR-2023.pdf for 2023 (the bottler uses the calendar year as its reporting period) lists the products it clubs under the tag, and there are a few surprises here.

Sting, the energy drink with a 90% market share https://the-ken.com/story/red-bull-is-the-energy-drink-of-the-world-how-did-pepsico-make-sting-the-energy-drink-of-india/, has been singularly responsible for making Varun Beverages India’s most desirable FMCG stock in recent years.

Energy drinks are stealing the show among all beverages, with people visibly drawn to their appeal as stimulants. They are replacing carbonated drinks and juices. (According to the three teens, mango drinks are for kids). They’re also being used as cocktail mixers at parties. And it has become an alternative to chai at tea stalls during late-work breaks and a meal replacement for daily wagers.

So much so that Indians collectively consumed over 570 million litres of energy drinks in 2023, up about 30X from 2018, per global market-research firm Euromonitor’s data shared with The Ken.

Red Bull is the energy drink of the world. But how did Pepsico make Sting the energy drink of India?, The Ken https://the-ken.com/story/red-bull-is-the-energy-drink-of-the-world-how-did-pepsico-make-sting-the-energy-drink-of-india/

Varun Beverages’ market value has leapt 11 times to almost Rs 1.96 lakh crore (US$23.3 billion) since October 2019. The S&P BSE FMCG index, by contrast, has not even doubled during this period. 

Sting, which is to be credited with making energy drinks as mainstream in India as cola, has been called many things. But “low sugar” is definitely not one of them.

It’s not as sugary as other energy drinks—it has almost 7gm of sugar per 100ml, compared with 11gm in Red Bull—but Sting isn’t going to score brownie points with calorie-counters. The sports drink Gatorade contains 6gm of sugar for every 100ml. 

Would these quantities qualify as “low sugar”? According to the Indian food-safety regulator’s norms https://www.fssai.gov.in/upload/uploadfiles/files/Compendium_Advertising_Claims_Regulations_14_12_2022.pdf, those two words cannot be on the label of a beverage with more than 2.5gm of sugar per 100ml. This is identical to the rule in the European Union.

What is puzzling is that Varun Beverages alludes in its annual report to certain “Healthy Choice Standards”—borrowed from Pepsico—which recommend that the proportion of total sugar be less than 4.5gm for every 100ml. Pepsico’s “low sugar” drinks are well above this number. 

To be fair, the American giant doesn’t make this claim on the packaging for Sting or Gatorade. So does it really matter that Varun Beverages communicates this to its investors, instead? People who have made a killing off its shares may hardly care about the deleterious effects of saccharine carbonated drinks. 

But it doesn’t hurt Varun Beverages to use its superstar product (Sting) not merely to make its financials shine, but also to buff its own image in the anti-sugar age.
