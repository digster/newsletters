Good Morning Ishan,
Jokes and quotable quotes on lawyers are legion. Here are a couple of samples.
“Lawyer: One who defends you at the risk of your pocketbook, reputation, and life.”
“Anybody who thinks talk is cheap should get some legal advice.”
Jokes aside, litigation can indeed be quite expensive, not to mention very time-consuming with uncertain outcomes. Especially in India, where courts have a massive backlog of pending cases.
All this is often such a deterrent that many—including businesses—baulk from walking the legal route, even when they think they have a good case.
But in someone’s adversity and uncertainty lies an opportunity for others. Enter firms like Legalpay—the protagonist of today’s story. This four-year-old startup is the leader among a rare breed in India that provides third-party litigation funding. Simply put, it funds the legal expenses of businesses that seek to sue other businesses.
Nice. But what’s in it for Legalpay? It’s an interesting, intriguing arrangement. The startup’s success is tied to its clients—it earns about 20% of the awarded claims only if the client wins the case and nothing if the client loses.
Okay. But isn’t this too high-risk for Legalpay? How does it hedge? Does it fund all cases that come to it? What’s the win rate? How much does it share with investors? Is the client always best served by such arrangements? Are there conflicts of interest? More to it than meets the eye?
Shaswata gives the lowdown on all this and more in today’s insightful story.
