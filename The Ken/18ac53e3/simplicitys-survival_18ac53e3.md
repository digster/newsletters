Good Morning Ishan,
I’m a Youtube-trained cook—essentially a fledgling amateur in the kitchen. This also means I can make complex dishes that demand a lot of time and effort, but whipping up simple meals isn’t my forte.
But like the tunes in cooking videos that the platform suggests, tackling complex recipes can get rather tiring. Sometimes, I surrender to Zomato and Swiggy.
But on a recent Saturday, I craved something very basic and simple: paratha and aloo sabzi, cooked in mustard oil, spiced with nigella seeds, and jazzed up with green chilli and raw ginger. A classic Bengali delight.
The sabzi took hardly 10 minutes in the cooker: a baulking revelation for my Youtube-tinged mind. In the age of social media extravagance, I realised that simplicity is endangered.
Coming to the stories published by The Ken, Anand started the week with a pointed piece on how star fund managers are becoming an [endangered species](https://the-ken.com/story/star-fund-managers-in-india-are-an-endangered-species/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), as they are lining up at the exit sign even though the mutual-fund industry is growing by leaps and bounds.
On Wednesday, Abhirami focused her lens on [HEFA](https://the-ken.com/story/this-nbfcs-12b-loan-offer-to-higher-ed-is-leaving-its-only-customers-cold/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) (Higher Education Financing Agency). The state-run NBFC said loans up to US$12 billion to finance education institutions in India would be disbursed by 2022. But it could achieve only a third of the goal and is losing its only customer segment.
On Thursday, Elijah painted the hopes of a game-peddling Singaporean corporate—Sea Ltd’s digital entertainment unit, Garena—on our screens with the relaunch of the much loved and downloaded game, [Free Fire India](https://the-ken.com/story/why-a-tech-giant-is-more-relieved-than-happy-as-india-lifts-ban-on-its-mobile-game/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). For Friday’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/medicos-not-tech-bros-will-bring-ai-to-healthcare/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), Seema spoke to eye-care startup Remidio’s CEO Anand Sivaraman about how medical professionals and engineers should hold hands to develop near-perfect AI-augmented tools to push the boundaries of healthcare.
