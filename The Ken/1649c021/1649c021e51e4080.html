What a wonderful Sunday. Lots of special stuff happening today. The World Cup finals. Wimbledon. And our story. Not necessarily in that order.
 
Today, Rohin asks a key, central question: How are brands being built, and how do they advertise to ad-hating, cord-cutting, newspaper-eschewing users?
 
That's what our third weekend story is about. Like all our previous stories, it's a fun, easy read. It's informative as well. I learnt a lot. Sample this, for instance: 
 
"But there’s Myntra. India’s largest online fashion retailer, owned by Flipkart (in turn owned by Walmart), is where Indians buy fashion. Together with subsidiary brand Jabong, it generated sales of over $1.2 billion last year. Its home page is cluttered with global fashion brands like Guess, Calvin Klein, Marks & Spencer, Tommy Hilfiger and Desigual. And Roadster, Kook N Keech, Mast & Harbour, Dressberry, Anouk, Invictus and Ether.
 
The second lot are all owned by Myntra."
 
Fascinating, isn't it? 
 
It's not just the brands. It's also the consumers. Millennials are abandoning traditional methods of consumption as well. A study found that 84% of millennials don't trust advertising. 84%. So, how do brands try to endear and position themselves to such an audience? Rohin doesn't just stop there. He goes one level further. He postulates that the way brands have been traditionally created is changing. The old rules are being broken. It used to take a ridiculous amount of time to create brands. Not anymore. Power is moving to aggregators and platforms with data. It's a startling shift. And raises a lot of questions.
 
There are plenty of possible answers. He goes into all of them. For good measure, he uses memes to drive the point home. Lots of memes. Just read the story for the memes, if nothing else. It's free anyway.
