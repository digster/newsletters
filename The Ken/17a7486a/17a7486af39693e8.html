Good Morning Ishan,
 
Everything that needs to be said about the PNB Housing Finance-Carlyle deal has perhaps been said. The two camps remain divided, the preferential share allotment worth Rs 4,000 crore (~$537 million) has been put on hold, and the Securities Appellate Tribunal (SAT) will hear the case of the mortgage lender vs the regulator today.

But it ain’t over until the fat lady sings. In this case, even after.

In the past few weeks, the big boys of the financial world—private equity firms Carlyle, General Atlantic, and Alpha Investments, ex-HDFC Bank boss Aditya Puri and his family investment firm Salisbury Investments, and the public sector bank PNB—found themselves in a pickle. While the stock market cheered the deal, proxy advisory firms and market watchers called the deal out as unfair to small shareholders.

It appears—and that’s the dominant narrative—that the group with 85% shareholding has kept out the small investors by not going for a rights issue that is open to all.

The private investors may have misjudged the optics but did they also cut corners? Why did Carlyle not pay a control premium, a common practice when an entity acquires a majority stake in a company? Was the apparent unfair pricing driven by sheer profit motives or was it constrained by other factors?

Intriguing motivations manifest in intriguing deals, writes Anand in his story today.

The PNBHFL stock price has shot up after the deal announcement. There’s not much to complain about for the minority shareholders, some would say. But “questionable actions cannot be justified by positive stock movement”.

While the finance ministry says all’s kosher in this deal, a newspaper has reported that PNB may have voted against the preferential allotment deal in the 22 June shareholders’ meeting. If true, it would be a major volte-face from the promoter.

In a well-argued piece with fresh insights, Anand shows how a canny investor like Carlyle became both a villain and a hero. Even after the fat lady sings, there’s a case to be made for changing some rules. It’s a must-read. Let’s dive in: https://the-ken.com/story/carlyle-both-villain-and-hero-in-pnb-housing-finance-deal (10-minute read)
