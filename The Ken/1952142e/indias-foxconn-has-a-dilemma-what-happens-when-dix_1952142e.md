Good Morning Ishan,
In late 2020, when a unit of electronics contract manufacturer Dixon Technologies was chosen to receive subsidies for making smartphones, we said it was [Dixon’s chance to be India’s Foxconn](https://the-ken.com/story/dixons-chance-to-be-indias-foxconn/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
It certainly wasn’t a hyperbole. Dixon’s market value has since soared eight times to $9.8 billion, thanks largely to those production-linked subsidies. In fact, Dixon is the poster child of India’s manufacturing boom. It received Rs 200 crore from the government between April and December 2024, or nearly 3X what it got in the previous fiscal.
But what happens when the largesse tap slows to a trickle? Perhaps that’s on Dixon investors’ minds, too. The stock is down 20% this year, compared to a 5% fall in the BSE India Manufacturing index. Dixon’s plans for a $3 billion electronic-display factory hinged on yet more subsidies.
Can Dixon continue to be India’s Foxconn without a helping hand from the government?
