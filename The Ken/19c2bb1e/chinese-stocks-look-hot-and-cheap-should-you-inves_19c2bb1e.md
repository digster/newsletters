Good Morning Ishan,
The late Charlie Munger once said this: “The reason I invested in China is I could get so much more—so much better companies at so much lower prices—and I was willing to take a little political risk to get into the better companies, the lower prices.”
Berkshire Hathaway took a roughly 10% stake in BYD, the automaker, in 2008. Those shares were offloaded between 2022 and 2025, and the patient capital behind them generated profit to the tune of $7 billion. A solid score.
Meanwhile, Ray Dalio’s Bridgewater Associates launched its first onshore Chinese fund in 2018. It’s now the largest foreign hedge fund in China, managing around $7.5 billion worth of assets at the end of 2024.
Ask around. Even if impressions of China are flavoured with scepticism about its governance, there is recognition that it’s the land of (wealth-building) opportunity.
The Hang Seng, Hang Seng Tech, and Shanghai indices are up more than 50% since September 2024. “The bulls have a long runway there,” said Sumit Duseja to The Ken. He’s the CEO and co-founder of Truemind Capital, a private wealth-management company.
