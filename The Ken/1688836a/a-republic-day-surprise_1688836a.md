Good Morning Ishan,
First off, a happy Republic Day to you. Sixty-nine years of the Indian Constitution, which, if you didn’t already know, is the longest constitution of any country in the world. A hefty tome of more than 145,000 words. It’s a matter of national pride, the sort of thing they taught us in school.
Now, if you knew all that, good on you. I have a question: Did you know that if you include the constitutions of subnational units like states, India’s constitution is only the second longest? First place goes to *drumroll* the State of Alabama in the US, with well over 300,000 words—more than double the Indian one. Surprised? Clearly, they take themselves seriously in Alabama.

We also had a story by Rohin about another, shall we say, interesting company called EaseMyTrip. An online travel service that claims to be the third largest in India. It also claims to be profitable—something not even Nasdaq-listed MakeMyTrip can say. It hasn’t ever raised venture funding, and is reportedly planning an IPO that would value it at up to $1 billion. And when Rohin went digging, he found that it also sells… coal? Yes. How do you feel about ayurveda? It might not come as a surprise to you that the ancient Indian system of medicine has more than its fair share of fans around the world. But chances are you haven’t heard of Jiva—a company that operates India’s largest chain of ayurvedic clinics, as well as a teleconsultation centre. Its ultimate goal, Ruhi writes in her piece, is making ayurveda a credible alternative to modern medicine. Just how will Shah and CRED be able to justify their investors’ confidence?

A quiet startup that’s spent 10 years in different parts of the online car sales business without getting into the limelight, CarDekho raised $110 million—second among its peers only to CarTrade’s $130 million in 2016. Flush with cash, it plans to go “full stack”—from the consumer to dealer or C2D segment to fintech to classifieds.
