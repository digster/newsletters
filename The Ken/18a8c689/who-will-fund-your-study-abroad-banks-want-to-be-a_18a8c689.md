Good Morning Ishan,
Banks naturally tower over non-bank lenders in most categories of loans in India. And education financing is no different, with banks—particularly state-owned ones—controlling 80% of it.
But there’s a subset that banks have been happy to leave almost entirely to their non-bank rivals like Credila (formerly owned by HDFC) and Avanse Financial: studying abroad.
Banks, however, are starting to realise they shouldn’t have been so blasé. Overseas education accounted for more than one-third of the $11.7 billion in new student loans in the year ended March 2023.
Plus, one million Indian students are expected to leave for countries such as the US, Canada, the UK, and Australia in 2023.
Not surprisingly, the likes of Axis Bank, ICICI Bank, IDFC Bank, and Union Bank want to be their first choice.
Arundhati’s story today is about how these banks are doing that.
