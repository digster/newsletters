Hope you're holding up well. Let's not discuss the pandemic or the lockdown. There's little that has been left unsaid about either. So I thought I'd talk about something that is fundamental to what we do at The Ken.
Many of you may have devoured Israeli historian Yuval Noah Harari's supremely ambitious but eminently accessible book about our species—Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind. What fascinates me most about the book is Harari's explanation for the power of states, gods, and money: they are all mere myths that hold value only because we, collectively, decided to believe in them.
Now consider American surgeon-writer Atul Gawande's The Checklist Manifesto. He draws a straight line from the cockpit to the operating theatre to drive home the import of checklists. A surgeon needs them just as much as a pilot does.
Central to the appeal of both Harari's and Gawande's works is their ability to connect the dots. That is something we strive for in our stories and newsletters. And this week was no different.
My Delhi-based colleague Olina joined hands with our teammate in Manila, Jum, for
[an analysis](https://the-ken.com/story/india-conquered-nephroplus-begins-asian-expansion-in-the-philippines/) of NephroPlus, India's largest dialysis chain, as it embarks on expanding its footprint across Asia. The starting point for NephroPlus is its recent 51% acquisition in a Filipino dialysis chain. But it's not quite so simple, given the talent crunch and delays in reimbursement under a state-run insurance scheme in the Philippines.
The following day, we changed gears to give you something a lot more cheerful than renal diseases. A new crop of online content creators are looking beyond advertising and sponsorships to monetise their efforts, Bhumika
[wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/the-stars-are-aligning-for-indias-content-creators/) in her story. Through platforms such as Josh Skills and marketplaces like Pepper Content, they can tap into subscriptions for online classes or find clients that are willing to hire them as freelancers.
Our mid-week feature by Pranav was an extensively reported feature on three entities in India's fintech space. Quasi-government body National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) is prioritising India's real-time mobile-based payments system, Unified Payments Interface (UPI). However, this is coming at the expense of its other child, domestic card network RuPay, which was launched as an alternative to American networks Visa and Mastercard.
