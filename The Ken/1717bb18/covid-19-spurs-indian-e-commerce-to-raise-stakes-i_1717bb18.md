Even as the world was coming to terms with the 2008 global financial crisis, Rahm Emanuel—Barack Obama's chief of staff at the time—said something that still holds true.
"You never want a serious crisis to go to waste."
I know, I know, it sounds callous. Especially when applied to the current Covid-19 pandemic, which has seen nearly 2 million people infected worldwide and over 120,000 deaths. But here, in his own words, is what Emanuel meant: "It's an opportunity to do things you thought you could not do before."
For the purposes of today's story (and this email, of course), this opportunity is the e-grocery market.
See, the e-grocery game is hard. Margins are thin and inventory has a fairly short shelf life. The supply chain, therefore, is critical. Unless it's finely tuned, your margins are wiped out and, worse still, your inventory could go to waste.
These challenges were enough to scare away e-commerce giant Flipkart for years, while its rival Amazon failed multiple times before getting a handle on the space.
In the midst of the nationwide lockdown, though, the grocery category has become a saving grace. With all non-essential categories frozen, grocery has helped keep the lights on for many businesses. And as they double down on a category that is notoriously hard to crack, they have an opportunity to make their grocery operations smarter and more resilient.
This won't be easy. Already, Flipkart and Amazon are stretched thin, while Bigbasket is managing with a severely reduced inventory.
Adversity, though, is a great teacher.
