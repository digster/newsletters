Sumanth's story https://the-ken.com/story/rip-tapzo-will-the-ouroboros-rest-in-pieces/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=Email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Wednesday, the last of the Ouroboros trilogy, looked into why Tapzo shut down. Will it find redemption in a new avatar or is this the definitive end? The other free story this week, of course, was Roshni's TKOW piece https://the-ken.com/story/donation-shaming-kerala-floods/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=Email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Sunday. Really, is there a right way to donate? These are some of the questions she addresses.

Is there a solution?

Gayathri's story https://the-ken.com/story/curefit-the-high-temple/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=Email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Monday took a close look at Cure.fit and if a sum of many parts such as Mukesh Bansal's company could actually pull it off. Bansal is betting he can fit four different businesses together and assemble a healthcare empire on a scale no one's attempted anywhere in the world. His investors seem to buy it. Should we? she asks.

The last story of the week came from Ruhi https://the-ken.com/story/nephroplus-largest-dialysis-chain/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=Email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on the dialysis chain NephroPlus. The chain has gone from a handful centres in 2010 to 149 today. In doing so, it has become India’s largest network of dialysis centres. But NephroPlus isn’t done growing. Can it maintain its quality of care while expanding rapidly?
