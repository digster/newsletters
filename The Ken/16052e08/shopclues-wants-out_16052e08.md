Good Morning Ishan,
It’s December and winter is setting in all over India, though to varying degrees. From the first snowfalls of the season in the northernmost states to December rain in smoggy Delhi to 16° mornings in Bengaluru down south.
For Shopclues, the 6-year-old e-commerce platform last valued at $1.1 billion in January 2016, it's gonna be a particularly cold winter. Straddling Gurugram in India and Delaware in the US (Shopclues is headquartered in India and its parent company in the US), things are beginning to look bleak for Shopclues.
Multiple attempts to sell the company have come to nought, with the last unsuccessful one valuing it at just $400 million. Middle and senior management are starting to jump ship. ("if someone is scared, show them the door, because only believers will sit on this table. We want only those people who are pathologically optimistic,” says the CEO.)
But this story is also about the incredible shrinking space for generic or horizontal e-commerce companies in India. Other than the Flipkart-Amazon-Paytm trio that is. Armed with billions of dollars in cash and backed by leviathans like Softbank and Alibaba, there is no market segment that is safe from these three’s ambitions.
