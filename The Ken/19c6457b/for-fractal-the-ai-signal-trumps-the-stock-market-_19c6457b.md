Good Morning Ishan,
A “fractal”, in mathematics, is a self-similar object. A shape created by a simple rule that, when repeated, recreates its own structure at every scale. Zoom in and you see smaller versions of the whole. Zoom out and the pattern still holds. Repeat the rule long enough, and order turns into complexity.
Fractal Analytics may not have named itself with the stock market in mind. But as it lists today, the metaphor feels uncanny.
Zoom in on the numbers, and there’s growth trending down and margins are uneven. Zoom out, and the company says it is building for a once-in-a-generation AI shift.
Seema spoke with founder-CEO Srikanth Velamakanni, who put it like this: “We haven’t built this business to be seen from net income upwards.” Investors, he said, start at profit and work backward. He starts with a 10-year view of how AI will “power human decisions in an enterprise”.
That is a difficult pitch in a market that annualises half-year earnings and assigns multiples accordingly. But the debate is not one-sided. Seema also spoke with Venkat Viswanathan, founder-chairman of Latentview Analytics, a listed peer navigating the same AI wave. Enterprises, he cautioned, are still experimenting. “The bets that are being made have probably been a little ahead of their time…”
So what exactly is Fractal? An IT-services firm with AI as an added variable? A platform company still solving for scale? Or a business whose form changes with magnification, asking markets to value the whole before every part resembles it? At 70X earnings, at least the valuation suggests confidence.
If AI is indeed a structural shift, Fractal wants to be judged on that horizon. The question is whether public markets, famously impatient these days, are prepared to zoom out.
