Yesterday on Daybreak

When Squid Game released in September 2021, it pulled in over a billion hours of viewing within a span of a month. Not long after, a South Korean Internet service provider took Netflix to court.  It wanted Netflix to compensate it for the costs  it had to bear from increased network traffic and maintenance work due to the sudden surge in viewership.

 

For years, this tug of war between tech companies including OTT platforms and telecom companies has been going on. Telecom and internet service providers believe that content and tech companies should pay them for disproportionate traffic. But tech and content companies argue that this would violate the principles of net neutrality.

 

The debate has now reached India and telecom regulator TRAI is expected to release a consultation paper on the matter soon.
