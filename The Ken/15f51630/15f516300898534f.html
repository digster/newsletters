Good Morning Ishan,
 
I guess winter should be here soon. I’m looking forward to that bit of change. 
 
There’s some change though that has me completely flummoxed. That’s at Zivame, the Bengaluru based lingerie marketplace company, or what it is currently—an offline, single brand retail company. Which now wants to go back to becoming a marketplace. 
 
Anyway, the point is this. Zivame started out in 2011 and started from a good place. Online lingerie market in India expands choices for women, especially in smaller cities where the options are limited, and only fast-selling stock-keeping units are available. The online proposition adds sizes and choice to the process of lingerie shopping, unavailable to the larger demographic. There’s money to be made here. 
 
And clearly, Zivame was onto something. That is until it raised a lot of money in 2015, and then things started going all over the place. 
 
It started an 'experience store' in Indiranagar in Bengaluru. Then it moved away from its marketplace model to try and become India’s answer to Victoria’s Secret. And then the sales started falling, because not enough women were buying the brand. Fast forward and Zivame’s founder leaves the company. Then a top executive from one of its investors moves in. She quits and now someone else is in the saddle.
