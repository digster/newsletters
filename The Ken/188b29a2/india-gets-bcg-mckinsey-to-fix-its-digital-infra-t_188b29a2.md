Good Morning Ishan,
A whole lot of India is being built on digital platforms: from passports to goods and services tax to documents stored on Digilocker.
The entities responsible for much of the plumbing of this digital infrastructure, from email services to data centres, software applications to cloud services and security, are the nearly 50-year-old National Informatics Centre (NIC) and the decade-old Digital India Corporation (DIC).
Not everyone knows how important NIC is. A former top official from the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY) described NIC as being 20 times more significant than the state-run telco BSNL.
But NIC badly needs a revamp. It has struggled to keep up with tech, support, and cyber-security changes.
So, MeitY brought in professionals from blue-blooded consulting firms BCG and Mckinsey to assist in restructuring these organisations and also decide the fate of 4,000-plus officials who work there.
This restructuring—led by Ashwini Vaishnaw, who oversees railways, telecom, and electronics & IT ministries—is playing out with a larger context: the privatisation of India’s digital infrastructure. MeitY’s top leadership’s idea is to start sourcing the development and maintenance of critical and non-critical IT systems to the private sector, finds out Pratap, the author of today’s story.
While the value of the contracts awarded to the consulting firms may not amount to much, the message it carries through the government corridors is important to pay attention to.
