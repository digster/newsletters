Good Morning Ishan,
 
I don't think anyone enters journalism with ambitions of being on the edit desk. I certainly didn't. For me, the allure of journalism was reporting—the sexiness of uncovering hidden truths, questioning the system, and a dozen other cliches that come from watching too many movies and TV shows.
 
Some journalists, I'm sure, consciously shift to editing. It's a great move career-wise if you eventually want to head a newsroom or a media house, even. I transitioned for a reason that's far more base, though no less pertinent. Reporting is exhausting. Don't get me wrong, editing is tiring, invisible, and often thankless work. But it's also stable—the work comes to you each day.
 
Reporting, on the other hand, involves plunging headlong into the unknown, week after week. Finding a story, gathering sources, convincing them to speak, corroborating their information, parsing documents, and finally having your story scrutinised by thousands of readers, many of whom are hardwired to assume the worst about you.
 
Yeah, hard pass.
 
Every week, though, I'm amazed as our writers voluntarily enter this whirlpool, digging deep to tell some amazing stories. This week was a great example of how that hard work pays off.
 
We began the week with part 1 of Seema's two-parter on Boston Consulting Group (BCG). Having long played second-fiddle to its bigger counterpart McKinsey, BCG raised eyebrows by claiming to have pipped McKinsey in India. How did they manage that? Did they really beat McKinsey? That's what the story's about.
 
We followed that up with our free story for the week, with Maitri running the rule over the Indian government's recent policy to encourage domestic drug ingredient manufacturing. India currently relies on China for a massive chunk of its drug ingredients. Can a Rs 15,000 crore scheme fix that?
 
On Wednesday, Sumanth looked at one of India's earliest VC firms—Kalaari Capital. Kalaari finds itself in the news these days because Jio Platforms has committed to anchoring the firm's latest fund. Quite why Jio picked Kalaari, though, is a point worth pondering. In this searing story, Sumanth dissects Kalaari's fall from grace as well as its Faustian deal with Jio.
 
On Thursday, Jon shone the spotlight on an emerging trend in Southeast Asia. Already a feature of the US investment scene, Southeast Asian alumni networks are now banding together to offer ‘smart’ capital to fledgeling startups. Ironically, the trailblazer here is Uber, which exited the region in 2018.
 
We closed out the week with a fabulously reported story on how cloud majors like AWS, Google, and Microsoft are desperately wooing Indian government officials to corner India's public sector cloud market. By 2023, Pratap reports, this opportunity could be worth north of $1 billion.
 
And, of course, there's our weekly newsletter, The Nutgraf. This edition was written by Rohin, and applies a Systems Thinking lens to India's Covid vaccine rollout. It's fascinating stuff.
 
I hope you're having a good weekend. Rest up. We go again tomorrow.
 
Cheers,
