Power is a funny thing. It is a necessary part of control, which, in turn, is a necessary part of order. Without order there is no regulation. And that brings us back to power. But as Spiderman's uncle famously says in the film, with great power comes great...

Responsibility. Regulators across the board seem to be forgetting Uncle Ben's famous (but oft-misattributed) words. A lot like that class monitor in school who was your friend till the teacher decided they had the power to punish you. Terrible times. Anyhow, regulators and government agencies are using obscure legal provisions or regulations to tell companies how to conduct their business. And nobody's fooled.

You see, regulation is well and necessary, but when a regulator crosses a line to accommodate its best interests at the cost of the regulated, it's overreach. Vivek joined hands with other reporters at The Ken who supplied him snippets to put this story together - a story on overreach. Be it the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (Trai), the Income Tax Department (ITD) or various other factions of the government, all, of late, have been guilty of overreach.

From agriculture to payments, telecom to medical devices and medicines, it has almost become a reflex action for authorities to overreach with regulation whenever presented with a new or complex situation. Businesses and consumers are left to bear the brunt.

Trai has attempted to arm-twist Apple into adopting its DND app. If Apple chooses to sit this one out, it may just get chucked out of class. Some draconian class monitor this?

Also recently, in response to an RTI, the RBI decreed that e-commerce players were not allowed to collect cash through their logistics partners. What? Yes.
