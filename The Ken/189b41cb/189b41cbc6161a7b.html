Even when I read the draft of today’s story, this quote from Suumit Shah, co-founder and CEO of online-storefront provider Dukaan, stood out:

“The VCs’ point about the kind of multiples doing the rounds in today’s market is valid. But my point is that you don’t get Suumit and Subhash [Choudhary, Dukaan co-founder] in this market.”

It’s odd to see anyone referring to themselves in the third person. But if you are familiar with Shah’s unabashed Twitter persona, you know this is on-brand for him.

Dukaan has pivoted twice in its three-year existence: from wanting to digitise kiranas (mom-and-pop stores) to taking small businesses online and then, to finally being an e-commerce enabler for direct-to-consumer brands.

With the second pivot, Shah wanted Dukaan to punch well above its weight and positioned it as a rival to the $85 billion-worth Canadian tech major Shopify.

Not many took Shah seriously. Maybe it’s time that changed—and “maybe” is the operative word here. Dukaan has poached Shopify’s second-biggest client in India, cosmetics brand Wow Skin Science.

But this hasn’t yet opened the floodgates for Dukaan. The company thinks it’s worth $200 million, but investors are only willing to value it at $60 million, by Shah’s own admission. That’s less than the $72 million it was valued at in 2021.

In his sharp and well-analysed story, Gaurav goes deep into what stands between Dukaan and its dream of dethroning Shopify in India: https://the-ken.com/story/dukaans-anti-shopify-pitch-finds-one-big-taker-and-many-small-sceptics/ (10-min read)
