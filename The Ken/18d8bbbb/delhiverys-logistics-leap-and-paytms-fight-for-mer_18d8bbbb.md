Good Morning Ishan,
Today, we are serving up not one but two juicy stories. Why? Because The Ken is all about bringing you the latest in technology and business, and, frankly, these stories just couldn’t wait until next week (we checked; they’re impatient).
If you are Delhivery, you ship packages mainly for e-commerce platforms and try to operate in a way that, in ~10 years, you do well enough to get listed on the stock market. And boy, do you have a moment of glory! You become India’s logistics royalty. Soon, though, you find out that the charm of your tried-and-true strategy is fading. Giants like Amazon and Flipkart are casting shadows over your path. Your shares feel the blues.
When you do post your first quarterly profit after tax—a rather modest $1.4 million—you tell your investors not to read too much into it.
You know it’s time for some soul-searching. You are no longer content with being pigeonholed as just another delivery firm for e-commerce players; you want to become an all-purpose logistics company. With a $4-billion market cap that puts even your rivals like Blue Dart, VRL Logistics, and Allcargo Gati to shame, you’re feeling rather chuffed. You want to be different from the traditional players (ones you say have a “reverse Robinhood strategy”). You say it’s a choice.
But while your rivals might not measure up in worth, they have been playing the game of consolidating small shipments into part-truckload for long. What’s that? Read Aakriti’s fascinating take on what Delhivery’s latest results tell us about where the company is headed: [https://the-ken.com/delhivery-takes-aim-at-the-reverse-robinhoods-of-logistics/](https://the-ken.com/delhivery-takes-aim-at-the-reverse-robinhoods-of-logistics/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (8-minute read)
—
But what if you’re not Delhivery? Let’s say you’re Paytm.
With the Reserve Bank of India enforcing a rigid decree, your priorities are clear as day. You have to strike new bank partnerships while decoupling Paytm Payments Bank. But your lifeline in the crowded fintech space—your vast merchant base—is under siege. You have to convince 1.5 million merchants with Paytm Payments Bank settlement accounts to link their personal bank accounts to the Paytm app. Meanwhile, loan disbursals have come to a complete standstill (your lending partners are worried).
And when your rivals, too, start fishing in the same merchant pond, what do you do to reel in vendors? Send app notifications to merchants? Call them? Or do the old-school door-to-door visits? Naturally, there are whispers of discontent behind the scenes. Can your army of field sales executives—built over the years—save the day? Or are they fighting a losing battle?
Here’s Gaurav capturing the drama unfolding in Paytm’s corner. It’s a story of ambition, competition, and the pursuit of market dominance—and a regulator’s deadline: [https://the-ken.com/story/paytms-foot-soldiers-are-in-a-race-against-time/](https://the-ken.com/story/paytms-foot-soldiers-are-in-a-race-against-time/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (6-minute read)
