Good Morning Ishan,
It’s no secret that: a) big out-of-pocket spending on medical emergencies drives many in India into financial distress every year; b) the antidote to this malady—health insurance—is still highly underpenetrated in the country; c) so, there’s mighty scope for health-insurance players to grow their business.
Star Health Insurance, the country’s largest standalone health insurance company and the protagonist of today’s story, has been doing just that—growing its business by leaps and bounds. It has cornered nearly a third of India’s retail health insurance market. Bouncing back from the pandemic-induced losses, it turned in tidy profits since last year, aided by its growing army of agents and a vast branch network. The insurer’s stock—languishing since its flop initial public offering in late 2021—has now found a new lease of life. Nice.
But—and there’s always a but—trouble’s brewing. Star Health’s investors may finally be breaking into a smile, but another key stakeholder—the king—is unhappy, very unhappy. The customer is the king or is at least supposed to be. And there’s deep discontent in that palace, show data from the insurance regulator and ombudsman.
Star Health has the highest number of customer complaints and claims repudiation among standalone health insurers. Things have come to a stage where the insurer was recently pulled up in a state assembly and a consumer disputes redressal commission, too. This will have consequences. Not just for Star Health’s business and its stock. But beyond as well.
Kaushal, the author of today’s fine story, dives into this conflict at the heart of Star Health Insurance.
