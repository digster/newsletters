Good Morning Ishan,
Earlier this month, I received a press release. The subject line read—“India’s OTPless raises $3.5M to Revolutionize Mobile Authentication”.
My first response was a sigh; yet another startup promising a “revolution”. For some reason, I was immediately reminded of Charles Dickens’ A Tale of Two Cities. Imagine OTPless’ founders in the middle of Bastille, fighting intrusive authentication methods. Ha!
Anyway, I clicked on the mail without expecting much. I just wanted to give names to my imaginary fighters.
OTPless, I learned, is an authentication and access management software-as-a-service (SaaS) platform. The funding round is pre-Series A, led by Sidbi, India’s largest domestic investor. Launched in January 2023, the company has raised US$6.5 million so far.
But what really piqued my interest were its founders. The company is led by Bhavik Koladiya, Satyam Nathani, and Tanmay Sagar. All three were also founding members of Bharatpe, the fintech unicorn that practically camps on the line dividing popularity and notoriety.
By mid-2022, life at Bharatpe had become extremely [chaotic](https://the-ken.com/story/shaky-and-shady-bharatpes-problems-go-beyond-ashneer-grover/). A public brawl between the company’s management and its third co-founder Ashneer Grover, a string of top leadership exits, and a general lack of direction for the company were some of the highlights. And the press was unearthing more behind-the-scenes information on a daily basis.
Bhavik left the company in June 2022 after handing over the reins to its new chairman Rajnish Kumar. By then, Tanmay had already left and Satyam was a couple of months away from walking out.
I reached out to the three co-founders with a one-line question in mind: what happened after they left Bharatpe?
Bhavik was one of the two original co-founders of Bharatpe when it was first incorporated in March 2018. He was the company’s face and negotiated with investors for funding.
