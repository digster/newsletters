While financial markets have been in choppy waters over the last few weeks, the dips might just have a silver lining for India’s mutual fund industry.

Once the haunt of deep-pocketed corporates and institutional investors, mutual funds have increasingly found favour among retail buyers. And data suggests that they enjoy buying the dip.

Even beyond the immediate climate, the amount of money invested in mutual funds by retail and high net worth individuals (HNI) has been growing faster than the money invested by corporate investors. Monthly systematic investment plans (SIP)—a way to make recurring investments—for mutual funds also crossed the Rs 10,000 crore ($1.3 billion) mark last year.

As of May 2022, retail and HNI investors combined accounted for 55% of all the money invested in mutual funds, according to data from the Association of Mutual Funds in India. And while individual investors have been the engine behind the growth of mutual funds in India, the sector is also experiencing broader changes in its contours.

Exchange-traded funds (ETF) are becoming increasingly popular. Investors are picking equities over debt to invest in. States that only had a handful of investors in the last decade now have significant money invested in mutual funds.
