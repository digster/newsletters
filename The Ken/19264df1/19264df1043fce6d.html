Open the Reliance Retail website on your phone and go to the Reliance Brands page. Your thumb will begin to hurt before you reach the last of the labels that Reliance owns or has licensed in India.

From Salvatore Ferragamo and Jimmy Choo to Steve Madden and Balenciaga, Reliance is home to a number of iconic names in fashion.

No surprise, then, that two former Reliance Brands executives would float their own venture centred around global brands.

But Nitin Chhabra and Berry Singh, who founded Ace Turtle a decade ago and have so far raised close to US$58 million, didn’t begin by buying the franchise rights to brands. Instead, the duo offered them inventory-planning and order-fulfilment technology. Then, in 2021, Ace Turtle became the India licensee for Lee and Wrangler. Also part of Ace Turtle’s portfolio are khaki brand Dockers and retailer Toys “R” Us.

Chhabra and Singh want Ace Turtle to be to the middle class what Reliance Brands is to the affluent. But, interestingly, they have chosen down-on-their-luck brands to get there.

Lee and Wrangler have had a chequered history in India since their launch in 2006 and were not even sold for a few years. Dockers is not a brand that’ll resonate with a twentysomething, and Toys “R” Us filed for bankruptcy in 2017.

Ace Turtle’s strategy may have had some success. Lee and Wrangler are now available in 700 stores and have higher gross margins than the industry average. But can Ace Turtle make its brands of yesteryear count in a market dictated by fast fashion? Let’s get to Nuha’s story.
