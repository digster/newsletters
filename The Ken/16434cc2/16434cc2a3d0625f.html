Good Morning Ishan,

 

Multiples Equity is one of India’s leading home-grown private equity funds. Founded and led by Renuka Ramnath, one of the legendary cohort of women leaders that came out of ICICI Bank, it is currently raising its third fund in eight years. The size? $1 billion.
 
Now, before Ramnath founded Multiples, she spent nearly 10 years heading ICICI Ventures, another behemoth among home-grown PE funds. One of the sectors she was very bullish on back then was healthcare. So bullish, in fact, that Ramnath created a special purpose vehicle, called I-Ven, to just aggregate healthcare investments into what would become a “platform” brand.
 
At Multiples, Ramnath seemed to still retain her belief in healthcare. In 2013, she made her first healthcare investment by buying out her former fund ICICI Ventures’ stake in Karnataka-based hospital chain Vikram Hospitals. Ramnath of Multiples was buying an investment Ramnath of ICICI Ventures had made 5 years ago.
 
But then, something changed. 
 
Multiples has made no further investments in healthcare. Even while its peers are entering and exiting healthcare and hospital brands. 
 
It was, as Seema channelling her inner Arthur Conan Doyle puts it, “The curious incident of the dog that didn’t bark”. Here’s a refresher, if you’ve forgotten it.
 
Scotland Yard detective: “Is there any other point to which you would wish to draw my attention?”
Holmes: “To the curious incident of the dog in the night-time.”
Gregory: “The dog did nothing in the night-time.”
Holmes: “That was the curious incident.”
 
Why has Multiples utterly shied away from healthcare after its first and only investment? That is the dog not barking.
