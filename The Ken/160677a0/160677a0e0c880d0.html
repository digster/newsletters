And so, we begin the final two weeks of 2017. The weather may be cold, but our story today is, well, warm and sunny.
 
India is both a large country and a sunny country. And yet, less than 5% of the installed power comes from solar energy. In contrast, nearly 60% comes from burning coal.
 
The story of solar power in India will sound familiar—short-sighted policies, expensive capital, greed-driven oversupply, crashing tariffs leading to unviable businesses. But a new ray of hope could be shining on the sector, from the rooftops of factories and businesses.
 
Companies like Amplus Solar and Cleanmax are setting up and operating solar plants on the rooftops of businesses to sell energy back to them, at rates much cheaper than grid electricity. And not just small ones either. For instance, Amplus’ solar installation for Yamaha Motor Company in Delhi-NCR generates 6.3 MW of power, the largest in India.
 
The immediate potential market is the rooftops of the top 5000 companies and educational institutions in sunny India. Sensing the opportunity, investors are rushing in too. Amplus and Cleanmax have raised $150 million and $100 million respectively from investors. 
 
But regardless of the sun above, the roads ahead are dark and treacherous. Cheap imports, fickle government policies, the high cost of domestic capital and cumbersome net metering policies to feed power back to grids could still upend this space.
