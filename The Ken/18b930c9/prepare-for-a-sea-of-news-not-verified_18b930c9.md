Good Morning Ishan,
Elections no longer mean just lofty speeches, huge campaign rallies, opportunistic alliances, and disgruntled politicians. Misinformation is becoming just as integral a part of the process.
As India prepares for state and general elections, the debate around fake news will only get louder.
So, what does the Indian government intend to do about it?
Just over a couple of weeks ago, the IT ministry wrote to social-media platforms asking for an “action taken report” on “dispelling fake news”.
Now, before you conclude that India is finally getting serious about curbing misinformation, I urge you to read Pratap’s explainer today.
When you’re watching a video online, would a “News not verified” tag set off alarm bells? Because that’s the disclaimer the government wants Youtube to add to content of questionable veracity.
