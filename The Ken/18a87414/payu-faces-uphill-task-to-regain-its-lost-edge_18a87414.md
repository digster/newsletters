Good Morning Ishan,
A few evenings back, a friend humorously explained with examples “delulu”—the social-media-speak for delusional fans whose love is unrequited.
“Delulu” is what came to mind when I read this comment by Anirban Mukherjee, the chief executive of PayU India, which is among the country’s largest payment-services providers.
“The payments business is doing great, the credit business is doing great, the paytech business is doing great, and we are focusing on all three,” Mukherjee told The Ken.
Now, this appears to be a delusion on multiple fronts.
For one, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) earlier this year returned PayU India’s application for a key licence needed to run its core payments-gateway business. It’s still pending. As a result, PayU India cannot onboard new customers, growth is slowing, and it is losing ground to nimbler rivals such as Razorpay.
Next, the fintech’s overtures in the lending business have also not been too well received, at least so far. Here, too, the RBI undid what seemed a smooth, promising affair.
But wait. Not so long ago, didn’t PayU spurn rival Billdesk at the last minute after promising a $4.7 billion happily ever after?
Was parental pressure at play? What goes around comes around, and all that?
Be that as it may, PayU is at a crossroads today. It’s undergoing a massive overhaul, and Prosus—its parent—seems to be banking on leaner, more India-focused operations to pull the fintech out of its rut. But will it?
