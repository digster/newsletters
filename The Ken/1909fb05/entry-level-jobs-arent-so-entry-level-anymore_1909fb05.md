Good Morning Ishan,
Even if you’re unfamiliar with Groucho Marx’s work, you know he was the brain behind this iconic paradox: “I wouldn’t belong to any club that would have me as a member.”
That’s the first thing I thought of when I read Vanita’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/entry-level-jobs-arent-so-entry-level-anymore/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) today. That automation and the increasing adoption of artificial intelligence are chipping away at entry-level jobs is old hat. But there is an unexpected upshot: to get a gig at even the bottom of the totem pole, you need to have had a few years of employment behind you.
In other words, to get your first job, you should have already had a job.
The Ken looked at nearly 200 entry-level job postings across sectors on Linkedin and found four out of five job descriptions required candidates to have at least a year’s experience. In fact, over three in five of the listings for the software and the IT-services sector required applicants to have worked for a minimum of three years.
“Everyone is getting pushed up the food chain,” said an HR consultant. “So, in order to succeed, these entry-level folks must level up.”
It’s a tricky situation for anyone entering the workforce, especially when they are expected to do what was until recently being done by someone who has been with the company for three years.
Let’s get to the story.
