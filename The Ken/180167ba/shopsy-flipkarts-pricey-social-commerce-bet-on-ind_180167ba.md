When American retail giant Walmart bought Indian e-commerce marketplace Flipkart for US$16 billion in 2018, Amazon was the only rival in its sights.
But two more behemoths have since compounded Walmart's headache: billionaire Mukesh Ambani's Reliance Industries, which owns JioMart; the 154-year-old conglomerate Tata Group, which unveiled its super app, Tata Neu, last week.
So it made sense when Flipkart chose to look beyond this four-horse race by rolling out Shopsy to take on Meesho, the SoftBank-backed social commerce platform, in July 2021.
Unlike the 12-35% commissions it charges sellers on its flagship marketplace, Flipkart has a zero-commission model—à la Meesho–on Shopsy. That makes advertising the key revenue generator. But Flipkart has always lacked Amazon's ad chops.
This doesn't augur well for Flipkart as it plans to go ahead with its much-delayed listing next year, Soumyajit and Aayush argue in their story today.
Even as it spends millions making Shopsy attractive to India's 400 million internet users who aren't online shoppers yet, Flipkart has to get a handle on a bunch of things.
Like Shopsy's catalogue, for instance.
Nearly 95% of Shopsy's 150 million products are also available on Flipkart, while the other 5% are responsible for half of Shopsy's sales.
