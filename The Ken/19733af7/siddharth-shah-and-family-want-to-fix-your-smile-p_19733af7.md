Good Morning Ishan,
As much as entrepreneurs and VCs would like to believe that healthtech is akin to consumer tech, the fragile human health stands like a rock belying all such assumptions.
Throwing solutions of at-home convenience, e-commerce, or logistics at healthcare go only so far. And when it’s the limited market of teeth alignment—“eight out of 10 people in India have crooked teeth, but most don’t see it as a problem”—the D2C model begins to strain.
MakeO, the parent company of Toothsi and Skinnsi, founded by Arpi Mehta, Pravin Shetty, Manjul Jain, and Anirudha Kale, looks a lot like the digital-health company Pharmeasy.
The same pricing play, the same roster of investors, the same scale-up formula, and now the same impending fate of a severe markdown in valuations. Pharmeasy CEO Siddharth Shah, husband of Arpi Mehta, has been a staunch supporter, participating in multiple funding rounds of the $105 million that MakeO has raised to date from 100-odd investors.
It’s a tough business for dental aligners. MakeO alone shelled out Rs 65 crore on marketing in FY24. The problem is that the customer acquisition cost is high, but it’s a one-and-done product with no repeat customers.
In reporting for this story, Pranathi tried out some services and spoke to several players to understand why it isn’t so easy to fix smile and [why MakeO’s moves feel like a rerun of Pharmeasy](https://the-ken.com/story/siddharth-shah-and-family-want-to-fix-your-smile-pharmeasy-2-0-may-need-fixing-first/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
