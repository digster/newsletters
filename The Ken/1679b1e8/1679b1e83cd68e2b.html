I am not going to tell you what a Trojan Pig is. Instead, I am going to tell you what today’s story is all about.
 
It is, of course, about Oyo. And SoftBank. A lot of money at stake, and the aspirations of an Indian company to become the world’s largest hotel chain, while owning ridiculously little that fits the classical definition of ownership. “Our belief is that if we just keep opening 50,000 keys a month, then, by 2023, we would have opened roughly 2.5 million more rooms, which will be at least 2x of what the world’s largest hotel chain is today.” That’s Ritesh Agarwal talking. He says he is the founder of Oyo.
 
I understand Agarwal’s ambition. After all, scale is to startups, what cocaine is to addicts. 
 
And once you take money from SoftBank, you have the best dealer in town.
 
Keeping in tradition with our earlier stories on Oyo, my colleague Sumanth takes a deeper look at the company. Oyo recently raised $1 billion to fund expansion all over the globe. With this round of funding, the company is valued at $5 billion—that is more than twice that of MakeMyTrip’s current valuation of $2.4 billion. Sumanth tells this story through the lens of MakeMyTrip’s journey, plus how the SoftBank-Oyo combine dramatically alters the funding and competitive landscape of this business.
