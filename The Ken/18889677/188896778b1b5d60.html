When the idea of 10-minute delivery started becoming popular—roughly two years ago—there were many who thought this was the future of online grocery shopping.

But then, there were also many who thought Covid was here to stay.

How wrong both these groups were!

The pandemic is now a distant memory, and the punishing economics of quick commerce have left many a casualty in their wake the world over. And India is hardly immune to the trend.

Just ask Reliance-backed Dunzo, which had to shut half its warehouses, or Blinkit, which continues to be a drag on parent Zomato’s financials.

It’s tempting to conclude that the writing’s on the wall for quick commerce.

But Aadit Palicha wants to convince you otherwise.

The 22-year-old co-founder and CEO of Zepto, the startup that helped kickstart the 10-minute-delivery phenomenon in India, still sees promise where every other rival has only seen pain.

He claims that more than half its 200 warehouses, or dark stores, are already profitable, even as it spends roughly one-third of what it did every month a year ago.

But is it possible to thrive in a business whose very purpose, according to its detractors, was ephemeral?
