Good Morning Ishan,
I’m writing this while frantically preparing to travel to West Bengal for a close friend’s wedding. Ironically, I have just returned after attending this friend’s nuptials in Kerala last week. Yes, two ceremonies at opposite ends of the country.
The bride, a Bengali Hindu, and the groom, a Malayali Christian, wanted to honour both their families’ traditions.
That is why I’m packing my bags for the second time in as many weeks to fly to Kolkata and prepare to eat another round of scrumptious wedding food. And, of course, to witness the union of two great friends in holy matrimony!
But before I begin haphazardly throwing a mishmash of items into my suitcase, let me give you a recap of the stories we published at The Ken last week.
On Monday, Seetharaman wrote about billionaire Ravi Jaipuria, his investments, and his companies—Varun Beverages and Devyani International. You may not be familiar with these ventures, but you’ll recognise the brands attached to them: Pepsi and KFC. Jaipuria’s [wealth](https://the-ken.com/story/the-billionaire-who-hit-pay-dirt-in-fmcg-fast-food-hospitals-and-hotels/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) has soared 35% this year. To find out how you’ll have to read the story—if you haven’t already.
Gaurav’s story on Tuesday was about the payment-services provider PayU and its struggles. It’s still awaiting approval for a key payment aggregator licence from the Reserve Bank of India, has lost market share to rivals, and faced setbacks in the lending business on which it has now pinned its hopes. PayU’s parent company, Prosus, has initiated a massive [overhaul](https://the-ken.com/story/payu-faces-uphill-task-to-regain-its-lost-edge/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) to lend it a helping hand.
Wednesday, Arundhati’s story explored how banks, who thus far have been cautious about [education loans](https://the-ken.com/story/who-will-fund-your-study-abroad-banks-want-to-be-an-option-now/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), are now doubling down on their efforts to finance students who are going to study abroad. So students have more options and better interest rates. But the rise in aspirants for overseas education also comes with its own set of concerns.
Content creators for Youtube and Instagram now have to wade through a quagmire of community guidelines. Their reach and income are taking a hit. Shivani brought this into focus on Thursday. While topics surrounding sex education and mental health get heavily [scrutinised](https://the-ken.com/story/youtube-instagram-creators-got-likes-and-shares-now-some-only-get-restrictions/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) and flagged, creators are scrambling to find ways to dodge the murky rules against “inappropriate” content.
And finally, Pratap wrote about Bharti Airtel’s chief executive, Gopal Vittal, and his unique approach to grooming and appointing leaders. There’s a [rejig](https://the-ken.com/story/why-airtels-gopal-vittal-prefers-hunger-to-readiness-in-next-gen-leaders/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) underway at the telco, and an internal board is seeing a rapid infusion of young blood. Vittal believes that “hunger” is a better asset than “readiness”. But these changes haven’t gone down well with some.
That’s all we had for the week. I hope you enjoy many feasts this wedding season. Have a restful Sunday, and we’ll be back tomorrow.
Regards,
