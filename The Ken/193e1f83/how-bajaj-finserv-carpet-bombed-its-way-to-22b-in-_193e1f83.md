Good Morning Ishan,
In the mutual fund industry, “long term” is a common catchphrase.
Indian fund managers usually drop words like “the runway is long” , “alpha returns in the long term”, “follow the philosophy, not just the performance numbers”, and so on. Still, Bajaj Finserv Mutual Fund, the 18-month-old asset manager from the house of India’s largest non-bank, seems to be in a rush.
Today, on 20 December, the asset manager is closing its 15th new fund offer. That’s more than twice the number of funds launched by newer fund houses such as Zerodha*, Samco, or NJ. Along with speed, Bajaj is big on variety.
Last week, its parent, Bajaj Finserv, claimed that Bajaj Finserv Asset Management Company (AMC) is the fastest-growing mutual fund in the country. It has Rs 19,000 crore under management, a milestone that, say, a fund like Parag Parikh, took nearly eight years to reach.
Of course, that was a different time, and the Indian stock market was not on steroids. But there has to be something more than the buoyant markets or the dependable, yet disruptive, conglomerate brand that is providing the tailwinds. The money is flowing in the AMC much sooner than anyone had expected.
In his [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/how-bajaj-finserv-carpet-bombed-its-way-to-2-2b-in-mutual-funds/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter), Anand talks about the reasons, including a soon-to-enter formidable competitor, Jio-Blackrock.
