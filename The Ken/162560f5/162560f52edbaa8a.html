Good Morning Ishan,

As you are reading this, I’m unpacking stuff at home. Yeah, I moved into a new place, and you know how that is. It comes with its own challenges and smaller nicer things, ones that I associate with newness. There’s this one part of home which I really like, something that was planned. A wall with pictures of my mum, dad and grandpa. The grandpa pic I like; lots. He’s, I think 76 now, and the picture is a sepia from his college days. Him, at a desk, writing something, and a whiskey bottle which says 'whiskey' in big, bold letters. I don’t know, something about that just makes me smile. I sometimes feel like I’m like him and I should get a similar pic clicked, just like that. Still figuring out this part. 
 
That said, we had quite a week, didn’t we? The Facebook and Cambridge Analytica scandal made headlines in many parts of the world. I still find the whole thing scary, and if I read things right, looks like the world might soon have regulations for social media and accountability for platforms. Is that going to be a better world? I don’t know. But back in India, we kinda missed the whole Facebook outrage moment. That was Venkat’s story. Of how we missed it, why we did, and why it is important for us to have a conversation about data privacy. If you missed the piece, read it here.
 
There’s this other story which I really liked. From my colleague Sumanth. He wrote on how a B2B startup Minjar, which raised $2 million in funding, was finally acquired for $50 million. I like how perceptive Sumanth is when approaching stories like these. A set of founders build something, and someone acquires it after ten odd years; you have to be perceptive to tell a good story from there. That’s what we did. Here, if you missed it.
 
I get a lot of feedback emails, and I know that we don’t write much about IT services. It is quite good when we do. Like this piece by Sanchit on how the IT industry is using digital as a revenue buzzword. You know almost like how I use the word 'like'. That story is here.
 
Alok Prasanna Kumar, a lawyer, wrote a piece for us on how badly India’s content and freedom of speech laws need fixing. It is a very important read because this is something that will come back to us, every once in a while. And this concerns you, me, everyone. It's free; read here.
 
The last story; I really don’t need to sell this. Proof of the pudding lies in the telling, and this is a soft, delightful read from my colleague Durga. The story talks about Kudumbashree, Kerala’s poverty eradication programme which works with over 43 million women, and how it's now getting into e-commerce. To be witness to anything that changes lives of people is a humbling experience. In this case, technology. The piece is here.
 
Thanks for taking the time out to read. If you like this email, pass it to someone who’d appreciate it. 
 
I’ll get back to the house and memories.
