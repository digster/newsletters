Nothing compares with what the healthcare providers and governments are facing. Or what the informal sector is grappling with. But if anything comes close, it’s the communications infra provider. Networks are creaking under pressure.
In a country that already has the highest data usage per smartphone globally, increased online activity isn’t sustainable. Because only 2-3% of Indian subscribers access the internet through wired broadband, according to the telecom regulator. Roads are empty, but the traffic on mobile wireless networks is crazy.
Peak hours have extended to all day, every day. And since most traffic is coming from residential areas which have lower network capacity, there’s no quick-fix solution.
While many cities are witnessing 90-100% load capacity, telecom circles like Karnataka are stretching even beyond 100% capacity. This is well over the 75% capacity utilisation for which networks—and their P&L calculations— are designed and built.
Telcos and network management companies believed data usage will flatten by the end of the first week of the lockdown but that hasn’t happened. That has them worried because augmenting capacity requires resources and time. It’s quite likely that work from home and social distancing advisories will surface again.
Internet Service Providers and telcos are trying to cope, but that may not be enough, says Pratap. The virus has affected the Chinese vendors’ supply chain; there’s already 20% network quality deterioration. Will it continue?
