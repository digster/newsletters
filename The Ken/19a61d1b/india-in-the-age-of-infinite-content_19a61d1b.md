Good Morning Ishan,
I deactivated my Instagram account yesterday.
Whenever social media gets too annoying or loses relevance for me, I tend to stop using it, but always stop short of deletion. My Facebook account is still alive. I can’t remember the last time I visited it. I was one of Twitter’s (now X) earliest users. Today, I don’t even bother logging in. Somewhere in the depths of the internet, my Orkut account probably still exists.
For the past few months, I kept my Instagram active, but heavily regulated the time I spent there. Each day, I’d install it for a few minutes just to check if my friends were still alive, scroll through a few reels and then uninstall it from my phone. It wasn’t exactly a happy relationship, but it was functional.
Then the AI slop arrived.
You’ve probably seen it too. There’s the cat video where something about its whiskers feels wrong. The disaster footage with that impossibly orange sky. That explainer influencer is backlit by a slight glow that doesn’t quite match the lighting in the room. You can still catch these tells if you’re looking—for now. Yesterday, it was obvious. Today, it’s subtle. Tomorrow, it’ll be indistinguishable.
