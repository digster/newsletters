Good Morning Ishan,

 

Finally, beautiful rains are here. It is magical right now, in this part of the world. The tip tap, tip tap. The puddles. The sound that goes squish. People hurrying, clutching their belongings close. Fussing. A sea of umbrellas, going somewhere. And one-size-too-big raincoats, water dripping from the edges of the sleeves and the trousers. Everything feels fresh. Renewed.
 
In all of this, almost suddenly, wet hair is back in fashion. Rightly so.
 
You know what, so are cartoon characters. The ones from India.  
 
My colleague Harveen says that for the longest time kids’ animation in India had been dominated by foreign (dubbed) content. Such as Duck Tales, Mickey Mouse, Flintstones, etc. Meanwhile, Indian content rarely, if ever, makes it to television. Which has restricted the growth of the local animation industry. But things are changing. Just a few months back, in April, Discovery Kids launched a character called Little Singham. Since then, the channel has seen an over 300% increase in its TV ratings. The number of subscribers on its YouTube channel, which airs short clips of the show, has increased from 20,000 to 1,14,000 in the same time span.
 
Over the last 12-18 months, the Indian kids’ content segment has seen some new entrants, the most prominent being video streaming platforms like Amazon Prime, Netflix and even AltBalaji. Among traditional broadcasters, Sony Pictures Networks has joined the party, launching a dedicated kids’ TV channel in April 2017. Even the Indian government is trying to get a piece of the pie. 
 
The animation industry in India wants your kids. And they know that to get to them, they must make more of Little Singham. Harveen's story today shows that kids’ content is a huge opportunity, and some of the smartest people are betting on it.
