Good Morning Ishan,
Everyone loves backing underdogs. But for an underdog to become a winner takes grit and hustle, not just popularity.
If you agree, you’ll love today’s story about a 10-year-old Indian startup, Whatfix. It’s locked in a heated battle against Walkme—owned by German software giant SAP—for control over the market for digital-adoption platforms (DAPs). In other words, these companies help clients use the software they have “in a much better, faster, and more efficient manner,” according to Prasanna Vaidya, founder of DAP startup Gyde.
But then, the fight for the DAP market is anything but heartwarming. Just consider the fact that Walkme has taken Whatfix to court over allegations of poaching clients—even as the latter has launched four new products in the last three years in a bid to out-innovate its rival.
To be fair, the DAP market is worth around US$700 million; why wouldn’t a company want to take such an aggressive approach? Especially a challenger like Whatfix which, even with the backing of Softbank Vision Fund and Peak XV Partners (formerly Sequoia Capital India), has only one-sixth the market share and half the valuation of Walkme.
And it’s a strategy that works, after all, as Whatfix has been able to bag clients like American tech giants Microsoft and Cisco, German automaker BMW, and India’s own ICICI Bank.
Even David had to slay Goliath to earn his legendary triumph. That’s the fierce battle that Pranathi captures in today’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/this-800m-indian-saas-firm-is-winning-over-microsoft-and-cisco-against-global-rival-walkme/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
