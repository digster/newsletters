Have you ever watched the World Series of Poker (WSOP)? To fans of traditional sports, it may seem like an oddity. An absurdity, even. But the skill, decision-making, and nail-biting tension (coupled with what I genuinely believe is some of the best commentary in sports broadcasting) make for a thrilling watch.
Among the various pros that dot the WSOP circuit, I have a favourite—Daniel Negreanu. Fondly called Kid Poker, watching him convinced me that poker was not about luck but skill. See, Negreanu is a master at "reading" players—figuring out what cards they hold based on body language and their betting patterns. His track record is almost supernatural, helping him amass over $42 million in live tournament winnings alone.
Can Negreanu's genius be taught? He'd have you believe so. He's authored several tomes on poker strategy, and recently even launched a course on online teaching platform MasterClass.
In India, poker is nowhere near as popular as it is in the US. Its very legality is still up for debate, depending on which state (or court) you're in. Already, though, Indian poker players have risen to rub shoulders with the likes of Negreanu on the world stage. Today's story by our contributor, Gayatri Gambhir Sarin, deals with a number of educational courses popping up across the country that could add to the ranks of India's poker stars. Even institutes like IIM Kozhikode are getting in on the act. What does this mean for the future of Indian poker (or education)?
Oh, and if you do check out the WSOP videos on YouTube, look for Phil Helmuth. His almighty meltdowns every time the poker gods conspire to undo his best-laid plans are a great reminder that even the best poker training can only go so far.
