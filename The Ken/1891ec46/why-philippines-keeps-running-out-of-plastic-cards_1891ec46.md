Good Morning Ishan,
I have been a victim of the conflict in today’s story.
In 2014, when I renewed my driver’s license, I went through the normal renewal process: payments, validation, having my photo clicked, and then the biometrics-and-signature routine. Ultimately, the Land Transportation Office (LTO) gave me a piece of paper that did not even have my photo or signature.
The government official said they would mail the actual license card in a matter of weeks. But it never arrived. So I kept that piece of paper, which was already tattered by the end of the year. They gave me another piece of paper upon the following year’s renewal.
I eventually got a real card. But it’s quite surprising that the Philippines’ LTO is still stuck on the same problem almost a decade later.
I often wondered why they always run out of cards. Until Isa’s story finally explained why.
The bottleneck was not in the raw-material supply, not a supply-chain issue, nor a lack of workforce. It was an inefficient system that kept getting worse.
The Philippines is facing another license-card shortage this year: the sixth in this decade.
As of early June, the Philippines already has a backlog of 700,000 cards and is projected to reach 5.2 million this year. On average, the LTO issues about 20,000 licenses a day.
Filipinos have been desperate, some resorting to getting counterfeit licenses just to get a card.
