Good Morning Ishan,
Google Gemini is coming for test preps. All the venerable national-level tests: JEE Main, NEET, CAT, UPSC, and more.
Education is the new battleground for large language model makers such as Anthropic, OpenAI, and Google, as the headlines this week have shown.
To quote from Atul’s story today: “Google has paid for 250,000 questions and answers for JEE… They have also bought NEET and CAT.”
In January, Google ventured into exam prep with SATs in the US.
As in every other field, India offers huge numbers: JEE Main alone sees over 1.3 million candidates annually, and NEET is the largest exam in the country, attracting over two million aspirants each year.
Google has stated that education is among the largest use cases for Gemini in India. Even for Anthropic, educational and instructional tasks make up 12% of Claude’s use in India, according to a February report by the company.
But by paying for question sets from Physicswallah and Careers360, Google is setting a trend of buying relevant data. It’s also important signalling, on multiple fronts.
GenAI tools have helped students boost their test scores by 127%, according to a report by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) released in February. Even if this has come at the cost of a declining information retention rate.
The factory model of preparing students for engineering, medical, or civil services has raised questions about whether it’s the right aptitude or merit assessment. Now the factory, even if personalised, will run 10X faster.
