It’s streaming happy melodies in the music business. Ask Saregama, India’s century-old music label. It marshalled a cool 150 billion views—its best-ever performance—in the recent quarter ended September, mostly thanks to two Hindi tracks that went viral on Youtube.

It’s not just the music, the money is also flowing in smoothly for Saregama. Again, thanks to Youtube and its revenue-sharing model that lets the likes of Saregama be paid per user engagement. No surprise then that the music label’s investors have also been jiving with the stock soaring this year.

Youtube, the world’s and India’s favourite streaming channel, has been instrumental in effecting a paradigm shift in Saregama’s business model and led to its resurgence. Ring out the nostalgia, ring in the new. From banking on old melodies, the music label is now making fresh hits to cash in on India’s seemingly insatiable craze for music—relying big time on Youtube. The future looks rosy. Or does it?

In this seemingly sweet symbiosis lie ominous notes for Saregama. The Google-controlled Youtube clearly calls the shots in this relationship. Its algorithms can change at the drop of a hat, and with it, Saregama’s fortunes, too. It’s not that the RP Sanjiv Goenka-owned company is oblivious to this huge risk. It’s been trying to hedge its bets, but without much to show for it.

So, where does that leave Saregama, besides at the mercy of Youtube? Contributor Anirudh finds out in his story today. Let’s dive in.
