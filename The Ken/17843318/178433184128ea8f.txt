How often do you come across an Indian consumer brand that is among the world's largest in its category? You know the answer as well I do.

That's what makes the rise of the five-year-old headphones brand boAt a fascinating study.

Between October and December 2020, boAt shipped 5.4 million units, giving it a market share of 3.5%—the fifth-highest—in the wearables market. In the same period of 2019, boAt's share was a mere 0.8%.

But our story today is not about how boAt got to this point. Instead, it's about where boAt goes from here.

In February, it hired as its CEO Vivek Gambhir, a former head of the multinational FMCG company Godrej Consumer Products. A month earlier, boAt had raised $100 million from PE firm Warburg Pincus at a $300 million valuation.

Both these moves, Bhumika argues in the story, are integral to something boAt wants to do in a couple of years: an IPO. Gambhir brings with him the requisite strategic and operational chops. And the recent fundraise allows boAt to finally focus on an area it has ignored so far—building its own IP—as it moves its production partly from China to India.

But going public is not all that it's made out to be. Just ask Skullcandy, the American audio brand that went private in 2016, just five years after its IPO. As they say, boAt has to be careful what it wishes for.
