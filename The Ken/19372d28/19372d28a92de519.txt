The winning B-school team will receive a Rs 10 lakh cash prize

Good Evening Ishan, 

If you had US$1 billion, what would be your winning strategy as a quick-commerce company?

That’s the challenge that we’re posing to smart, ambitious students who will graduate in 2025 and 2026 from India’s top business schools. 

We’re thrilled to announce the inaugural edition of The Ken’s Case Competition.

Over the next two months, participating business-school students will pick one of the top five quick-commerce companies and come up with a winning strategy. We’ll publish some of the most creative and compelling solutions for The Ken’s subscriber community—for you to read, analyse, and offer your perspectives and comments. Eventually, the winning team will have to defend their solution before a diverse, accomplished panel of judges composed of leading figures in India’s business world. 

Great, innovative solutions deserve outsized rewards. That’s why this year’s competition was created in partnership with Zerodha. We’re glad to be associated with them as we take our first step to create a pioneering event that captures the imagination, skills, and creativity of tens of thousands of business-school students across India. 

We want this to be India’s biggest case competition, so we decided to have prizes that match the effort and skills that participants will have to demonstrate in order to triumph.

The winner of The Ken’s Case Competition 2024 will be awarded a cash prize of Rs. 10 lakh. 

The next four runner-ups will receive cash prizes of Rs. 2.5 lakh each.

But why are we doing this?

Case-study competitions have been around for years, even decades. Sometimes, they’re organised by student clubs at India’s major B-schools. The bigger ones are usually helmed by some of India’s most prominent companies, by both legacy conglomerates and newer startups alike. We studied all of them closely, and eventually realised that there was something missing in nearly all of them—the real world.

A business case is supposed to simulate the real world for companies, with all its choices, complexities, and challenges. As India’s first and largest subscriber-only independent business journalism publication, we have been reporting about these in depth. This also needed a radical reimagining of how case competitions work. Again, we know a little bit about this—over the last eight years, we’ve reconceived multiple products like narrative business stories, podcasts, visual stories, workshops, and many more. 

So, we asked ourselves: what would a reimagined, real world case competition look like? 

If we had to start from scratch, unburdened, what would that look like?

We designed our case to present more choices—in terms of both the subjects and the problem statement. In general, most cases limit participants to a specific company and a clear predefined objective—increase market share, or make a go-to-market strategy, or write a product spec. That’s rarely how it works in the real world. Leaders need to process much more ambiguity, not just on their way to the solution, but even when interpreting the problem itself. We wanted to mirror that, so our case has a broader objective for all participants—come up with a winning strategy for a quick-commerce company.

Which quick-commerce company? You decide. What does “winning” mean? You tell us.

We’ve introduced context about competitors and resources. Most case competitions don’t ask participants to think about the broader context around their company. Our case explicitly demands that. Participants will have to make hard calls about where they invest their resources, i.e. US$1 billion, and consider how other quick-commerce companies will react and respond to their strategy. Again, this is more like the real world.

Finally, we wanted the case competition to be of broader interest to everyone. We believe that great ideas and strategies can come from anywhere, and not just from the top business schools. In the interest of wider participation, we’re opening up the case competition to all business schools across India. Colleges that already have a campus subscription to The Ken can participate right now. For all other institutes, we’ll unlock The Ken through their libraries and administration. 

That brings us to you—the subscriber. Quick commerce is a world we interact with often. The companies that we put forward in our case are those whose products and services we use every day. 

Over the next couple of months, we’ll involveThe Ken’s community of subscribers in the case competition as well, and we’ll publish some of the best and most innovative solutions and feedback we receive.

This is what makes this case competition much more open. We expect it to become an event of intense interest and excitement for a broader community beyond India’s business-school students.

This is very exciting. I can’t wait to see how it will unfold.
