Good Morning Ishan,
Call it the swagger of a well-executed strategy or simply great timing, Lenskart’s announcement of a Rs 407-crore near-buyout of a Spanish fashion-eyewear brand fits both bills. The acquisition news dropped this week along with its draft IPO papers.
India’s largest eyewear retailer is going public, and its numbers tell a unique story of global expansion.
Out of $760 million revenue in FY25, nearly 39% came from its international business. The 15-year-old company sells in 14 countries, much less than what its fellow startups, Zomato, Oyo*, Ola tried to expand into. So far, Lenskart has succeeded where others either stumbled or failed.
Founder Peyush Bansal seems ambitious yet restrained. He believes if there was a Netflix to Disney, there has to be a Lenskart to Luxottica, the Italian behemoth that owns brands like Ray-Ban and Oakley. He’s also fashioned his business on similar lines—vertically integrated to design, manufacture, distribute, and retail eyewear.
Though he had no dearth of capital in the latter part of building out Lenskart, as Suprita reports today, Bansal has declined more cheques than he’s accepted. Even with Softbank, in 2019, it wasn’t the cheque size—what the investor is usually known for—that sealed the deal.
Then how exactly does Lenskart view its global acquisitions and partners? Suprita has [the story](https://the-ken.com/story/lenskart-succeeds-where-zomato-ola-stumbled/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
