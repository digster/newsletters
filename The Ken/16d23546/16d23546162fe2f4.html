Luckin Coffee, founded in 2017, in less than two years, has become the second-largest café chain in China, with 3,300 outlets—after Starbucks, which has nearly 4,000 stores. It’s now gunning for the number one spot, pushing aggressively with offers like delivering coffee in under 30 minutes, deep-discounting and an order-online-pick-up-at-store service. 
 
Meanwhile, in India, Chai Point, India’s largest tea café chain has grown to 141 stores after starting in 2010. Like Flipkart and Ola, which successfully staked their claim to become the “Amazon of India” and “Uber of India” respectively, Chai Point wants to become the “Luckin Coffee of India.”
 
Except, like Amazon and Uber, both of who entered India in 2013 to claim for themselves the titles of “Amazon of India” and “Uber of India”, Luckin Coffee, too, wants to enter India. And become the “Luckin Coffee of India.”
 
Not if Amuleek Singh Bijral, the co-founder and CEO of Chai Point has his way though. That’s Pranav’s forward-looking story today: https://the-ken.com/story/chai-point-india-luckin-biz/ (12-minute read)
