Good Morning Ishan,
What’s common among Mahindra’s XUV700, a BMW cruising down the Autobahn, and nearly 255 million vehicles across the globe?
They all depend on Blackberry.
That Blackberry. The wireless email gadget that once chained the corporate class to their desks. The same company that turned “sent from my Blackberry” into both a flex and a cry for help.
That Blackberry didn’t die after all. It pivoted. Today, its real-time operating system, QNX, hums beneath dashboards everywhere. It runs the digital cockpit, driver-assistance systems, and little animations that tell you your door is open. It’s the operating system of your car’s operating systems.
And it’s not stopping at cars. Last month, Blackberry even partnered with a rail-automation firm to build safety software for metro and monorail projects.
A lot of this code is being written in Hyderabad, home to Blackberry’s second-largest global engineering hub, where a hundred engineers are literally coding the brains of tomorrow’s vehicles.
