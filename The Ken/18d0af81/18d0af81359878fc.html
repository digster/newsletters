Good Morning Ishan,

We may just as well call them the class of Flipkart. And it’s not just a moniker.

Two notable companies in a space that is dominated by 600,000 distributors in India have been founded by ex-Flipkart executives and they are not just jostling with each other but competing head-on with Reliance Retail-owned Jiomart.

Disrupting the decades-old FMCG distribution system and supplying to kiranas with groceries is what Jumbotail, Udaan, and later Jiomart, aimed their tech and retail chops at. But a few hundred million dollars later, both Udaan and Jiomart are humbled.

Udaan’s GMV plunged over 40% in the year ended March 2023, and Jiomart put its B2B-grocery business on hold. Now, as The Ken has learnt, Jiomart is days away from relaunching it.

Amid this pause and recalibration, Jumbotail, also the first mover having started in late 2015, seems to have stuck to its original thesis. Which, I think, is best summarised by one of its competitors: “Udaan and Jiomart wanted to expand first and then perfect their model… Jumbotail wanted to perfect its model first.”

But it’s not just the model, it’s about execution, too. And as I was reflecting on it, the Flipkart connection came to the fore—how one team learnt from it and another did not.

Jumbotail is expanding and claims to be getting profitable, one city at a time.

Seetharaman went into the story asking the founder, Ashish Jhina, a simple question: How can the same kiranas who are averse to ordering through Udaan’s or Jiomart’s app be doing just that on Jumbotail’s?

He came away with insights that are worth your time. A third generation apple farmer and an ex-Army officer have to steer Jumbotail through the dog-eat-dog world of selling branded FMCG goods.
