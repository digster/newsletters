Good Morning Ishan,
 

Let me begin by asking you a question. 

 

Imagine you want to learn to play guitar, and you shortlist two teachers for the same.

 

The first teacher asks you not to touch the guitar for at least a few days. Why? She wants you to learn about the chords, scales, and maybe even the notes. Only once you know the gist of the fundamentals of music can you gradually begin to play.

 

But the second teacher is completely opposite of the first one. It is just a trial class, and she makes you a promise: by the end of the class, you will have learned to play Twinkle Twinkle Little Star. 

 

Which teacher would you pick?

 

I bet that most of you will pay quarterly or even annual fees to the second teacher. (Please write to me if you think otherwise.)

 

But as a Business Storytelling Trainer with The Ken, let me tell you something. 

 

It has been nearly two years since The Ken launched this learning vertical. Back then, we used to craft a curriculum where we began by explaining concepts and then introduced their application through practice sessions. 

 

But we have evolved from being the first kind of teacher to the second one. 

 

And this approach did not come organically to us. The participants of The Ken’s Narrative Workshops for Teams have taught us that learning is exciting when it is an adventurous journey—interspersed with challenges and wins. Even the modern approach to education suggests the same. 

 

The result? Fast-paced learning that is utilitarian. And fun.
