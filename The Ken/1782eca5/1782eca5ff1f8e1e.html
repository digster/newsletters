Good Morning Ishan,
 
I recently started reading the children’s classic Anne of Green Gables. Weirdly enough, I had never read it when I was actually a child, but something compelled me to pick it up now at the age of 22.

Set in the late 1800s, it follows the life of a bumbling kid called Anne Shirley, who grows up on this cosy island with a tight-knit community. Honestly, it’s one of those simple books that you can’t talk about in intelligent situations, but it’s comforting. Because it’s simple. 

In a chaotic world, I crave simplicity. That’s why I love journalists.

In a world that can never backtrack to the simplicity of the previous century, I have access to the work of people whose job is to explain things to the point of simplicity.

This week’s line-up does just that.

On Monday, we published part 2 of Seema’s story on BCG. It explores the Indian government's dependency on consulting firms like BCG and McKinsey, and whether the priorities of the former lie in short-term gains or long-term sustainability.

On Tuesday, Seetha wrote about India’s second-largest clean energy company, ReNew Power. It recently became the first major Indian company to go public through a SPAC merger. However, it has a long road ahead of itself, with intensifying competition and cratering tariffs.

Wednesday brought a story by Pranav and Olina on Ola’s ambitious plans of building a mega-factory to churn out 10 million electric scooters a year. Their reporting makes it clear that there will be a few speed bumps along the way on this rather ambitious journey.

On Thursday, Olina wrote a deeply researched story on the Indian Institutes of Management’s tussle between scaling and maintaining the franchise's brand quality. The new crop of IIMs are facing stubborn existential choices. This was our free story of the week.

Sumanth’s story on Friday unpacked the OYO-ZO Rooms fracas. In 2015, OYO was supposed to acquire its nearest rival at the time. However, the two companies ended up fighting a long and messy court battle, which seemed to have ended last week. But now, both companies have claimed victory. 
 
In our weekly newsletter, The Nutgraf, Praveen wrote about the method and madness of Bhavish Aggarwal, the CEO who took on SoftBank.
 
In our daily newsletter Beyond the First Order aka BFO, Sanjana wrote about the bind that lawmakers are in due to trademark names in a digital age; Nithin delved into the missing case(s) of government audits; Arundhati turned to a line from the classic A Tale of Two Cities to explain the growing divide between smaller companies and larger ones; Jaideep, who was a tad disappointed at not being called Suryakant by Swiggy, looked into the foodtech company's latest marketing campaign; and Anand rhymed about India's strategic oil reserves, or the lack of it. 
 
That's all from us this week. Let me know what you thought about the stories. We'll be back on Monday.
