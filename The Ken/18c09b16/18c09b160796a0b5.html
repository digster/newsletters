Good Morning Ishan,

The month has been filled with joy and excitement. The fun began when I celebrated Diwali with my family. The highlight was my nearly-year-old nephew, whom I lovingly call momo (trust me, he’s one).

Fuelled by this festive enthusiasm, I’ve been reliving my time in journalism school with a close friend since returning to Delhi. Our days have started with staring into the abyss (smog) outside my balcony. This is inevitably followed by a discussion on the frightening pollution levels in NCR.

But as the hours pass, our conversations have evolved—from the air contamination to life and everything else. Seeing a different, grown-up and better version of ourselves and acknowledging how far we have come is exciting. Yet it also brings a great sense of calmness.

Emotions have been similar at The Ken, with our website redesign launched earlier this week. It’s different, simple and certainly better. We also had a great lineup of stories to complement it.

On Monday, Nathan wrote about once-happy EV customers who now want their fuel-powered vehicles back. Why? They are exasperated due to the lack of charging stations. Charger manufacturers who had won bids to set up public charging infrastructure aren’t fulfilling the “localisation” norms. Instead, they are confused about what constitutes them. All exclusive details are here.

Zomato’s two consecutive quarters in the black this year have spurred its arch-rival, Swiggy, to have another crack at an IPO. On Tuesday, Aayush argued that with piled-on losses of $545 million, the company must deliver profits fast. It has set monthly, weekly, and daily targets and is putting pressure on employees. Result? Longer working hours, including weekends.

Wednesday was about gig workers, India’s top internet platforms, who engage with them and Fairwork India, which evaluates how “fair” this relationship is. The platforms have made a big deal out of these rankings. I tried to answer two questions: Why do these companies love top rankings? And do the rankings portray an accurate picture of the on-ground realities? The answers are telling.

In his debut story for The Ken, Narayanan wrote about the State Bank of India and how it is battling against all odds to keep its 260 million debit cards alive. At 27%, the country’s largest lender has most of India’s debit card market. But lately, it is feeling the heat—blame consumer spending patterns and new payment methods.

As a return to office beckons, most people in Bengaluru want to live near their workplaces to avoid the city’s infamous traffic and poor transportation. But the fresh reality in India’s IT capital is that skyrocketing rents are hitting the people where it hurts the most—their pockets. “While landlords are living the dream, the renters are living through a nightmare,” writes my colleague, Shaswata.

We’ll be back with excitement (and calmness) next week. Have a relaxing weekend!
