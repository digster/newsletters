Let's start the day off with a riddle.
What does a hospital in Mumbai, an American oil pipeline, and a middle-aged jeweller in Mysore have in common?
If you guessed that they were all victims of ransomware attacks—where bad actors lock access to a victim’s data and hold it hostage in return for money—well done.
These three instances show that there's no geography too removed, no business too big or small, and no sector that is safe from the scourge of ransomware. And if that doesn't make you seriously reconsider your own cybersecurity, maybe this excerpt from today's story will:
India is poised to emerge with the dubious honour of topping the list of countries hit by ransomware. A recent global study by security firm SophosLabs put India at the top of the charts, with a whopping 68% of respondents reporting that they were hit by ransomware last year. 67% of Indian organisations admitted to paying a ransom to get their data back, double the global average of 32%.
The increased digital push since the onset of the pandemic has only increased the pool of targets for ransomware operators, with many newly digitised businesses primed for an attack. Just this month, it was alleged that fintech unicorn Pine Labs had fallen victim to such an attack. When digitally-savvy businesses aren't safe, imagine the plight of firms that are just getting to grips with technology.
And given that India boasts of major digital initiatives such as the UPI and the nascent National Digital Health Mission, the stakes are higher than ever. Already, Ireland's national health service has fallen victim to a sophisticated ransomware attack.
