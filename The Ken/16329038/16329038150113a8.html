I’d say today’s story has to start with the numbers.
 
-Monthly Active Users (MAU) (global): 1.5 billion
-MAU (India): 200 million -WhatsApp Business MAU: 3 million
-WhatsApp Status Daily Active Users: 450 million -Daily message volume: 65 billion
-Daily audio and video calls: 2 billion minutes
 
I think the word I’m looking for is...staggering.
 
And when staggering meets Facebook, and Facebook meets change, a word that the company could do so much with, it makes for a riveting story.
 
For now, it is all coming down fast. It started with Brian Acton’s exit last year. He was one of the co-founders at WhatsApp. Then, a couple of days back, Jan Koum, the other co-founder quit the company. The circumstances of Koum’s departure were detailed in a Washington Post report, which hinted at clashes between the founders and Facebook, over the latter’s attempts to use personal data, weaken encryption and open WhatsApp up to more advertising from businesses. Then, only yesterday Facebook said that yes, advertising is definitely something the company is considering.
 
I’d stress on this. Koum’s departure is a definitive moment for the company. For questions on user privacy. For questions on monetising WhatsApp’s billion-plus users. Today’s piece, from my colleague Venkat gets into all of these bits. Plus, and I recommend this part highly, there is a good explainer on how ad targeting happens on mobiles. How Facebook and advertisers come after you. It is something we need to understand better. You can read the piece here: https://the-ken.com/story/facebook-whatsapp/
