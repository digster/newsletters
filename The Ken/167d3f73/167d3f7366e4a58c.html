Good Morning Ishan,
 
Some of you may have bags packed. Others may be preparing cakes, mulled wine and other delights. Some may be preparing their bodies to party hard without collapsing the next morning. Whatever it may be, the year ends in a week and everyone has plans.
 
The one question everyone asks everyone else this time of the year, "Sooo, what plans?" Well, I'm writing this to tell you that it's okay to not have any celebratory plans. Maybe your plans are to meet certain goals you couldn't this year, due to work or your daily routine; I know I sure fall in that category. No pressure.
 
Calendar years are fascinating, for they're as random as language—it helps us encapsulate life into meaningful fragments. "Oh, 2016 was when I lost a tooth!" or "In 2005, I dated a celebrity..." And so, as random as a year is, there's a lot to look back at and reflect on. Something we'll be doing over the coming week, in a most exciting way.
 
But while we're on the subject of looking back, let's get down to our last weekly recap of 2018, shall we?
 
We started out with the penultimate instalment in our Going Home series. On Sunday, poet, teacher and potter Aditi Rao wrote about her home, a demon-goddess, as she puts it. It resides in the Kullu Valley, nestled between hills and a river. Read the piece; let Rao take you through her memories of this home.
 
On Monday, we sprung into action with Harveen's piece on Times Internet and how it's taking a stab at e-commerce...via content. Through its lifestyle websites—MensXP, iDiva, Whats Hot and Indiatimes—Times Internet is building a niche, social e-commerce business.
 
Tuesday had Roshni write about cloud seeding and India. How feasible is it? Imprecise as the science is, Indian states are insisting on cloud seeding even as a scientific initiative to establish its efficacy is ongoing. This one's free; read it.
 
Vivek's piece on SmartOwner looked into this self-styled fintech which actually brokers sale and purchase of property. It also fetches returns to would-be investors. Honestly, there isn't much it doesn't do.
 
Then we had Salman write on how inventory-led B2C commerce is old hat. Instead, young, VC-backed B2B startups like Meesho and Udaan are heralding a new dawn in Indian e-commerce.
 
Finally, on Friday, Vandana wrote about UrbanClap—a startup that is aiming to become India's first home services unicorn. Realistic? "From having its unit economics questioned when it started out in 2014, UrbanClap is now India’s largest home services marketplace and has even expanded to Dubai. With a recent round of funding secured, the world is UrbanClap’s oyster," she writes.
 
That's all for this week. Come back tomorrow for our final chapter in the Going Home series. 
 
Enjoy your holidays!
