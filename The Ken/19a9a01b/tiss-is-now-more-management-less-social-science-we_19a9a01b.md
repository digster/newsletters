Good Morning Ishan,
As someone who just spent days understanding and writing about why engineering education needs an urgent rethink, Atul’s story today made me ask why the country’s premier social-science institute is being MBA-ised. This, when engineers already dominate India’s management education.
A chunk of taxpayers’ money today is being spent on an endless list of social schemes at the Centre and in states. So, one would think India needs more grounded social scientists to design, study, and evaluate the schemes and their impact on the ground.
But policymakers think engineers and MBAs can run the country.
One of the biggest experiments is afoot at the Tata Institute of Social Sciences in Mumbai. It’s a fallout of the New Education Policy 2020, after which the education ministry has been nudging, pushing, and kicking institutions to introduce more industry-aligned and market-oriented programmes.
TISS, known for pioneering social science courses like social work, has traditionally had a field-based, skill-based, and social sensitivity-based approach to teaching, even for management courses. Now it’s sawing off the socially conscious roots to make them more “managerial”, writes Atul in this fine story today.
When the motto is “the market decides the rules”, what does TISS stand for now? Let’s [get to it](https://the-ken.com/story/tiss-is-now-more-management-and-less-social-science-welcome-to-iim-lite/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
Earlier this month, Smartworks inked a lease for 8.15 lakh sq ft at Eastbridge in Vikhroli (West)—a Hiranandani project that will become India’s largest flexible office campus when it goes live in 2026. Over the past three months, the company has seen its stock climb roughly 25%, while Awfis has slipped about 4%, and Indiqube is barely above water at around 1–2%.
