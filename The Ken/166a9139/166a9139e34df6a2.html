The British TV series Black Mirror is increasingly looking more like a guide on 'how to prepare for the future' than a dystopian horror-parody.
 
If you have a gym membership, would you tell your insurer about it in return for lower premiums? Would you share your food ordering habits from Swiggy to show how you eat mostly healthy food? What about the results from your annual health check? And then, of course, the motherlode: your health band or fitness tracker?
 
All of these are increasingly passing over from the realm of fiction to reality. In India, too.
 
In today’s story, Salman looks into the coming revolution in India’s insurance market, as it transitions from a fairly standardised, static and group-based approach to a personalised and dynamic one powered by billions and trillions of data points about customers. Best part, there’s no law yet that governs what data can be collected, how it can be stored, or how it can be used to generate Black Mirror-like “scores” for each of us.
 
Salman posits that something similar to the Cambrian explosion of innovation that happened to India’s financial services sector five-six years ago is about to happen to its insurance sector. And the building blocks for this revolution will be data. Our data. Lots of data.
