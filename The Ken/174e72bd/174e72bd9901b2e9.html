Good Morning Ishan,
 
Have you ever encountered Sleepy Owl's cold-brewed coffee ads on your preferred social media platform? I get them all the time.
 
I don't even drink much coffee—yes, it's possible to be a tired copy editor who doesn't drink coffee. But something about "just add cold water" makes me want to add to cart.
 
Now, what if I told you the co-founder of the aesthetically-branded beverage company didn't want to deal with the hassle of tech requirements to run a solid digital storefront? Sleepy Owl did the smart thing. It went to the one e-commerce platform that'll help it score branding mojo.
 
Shopify.
 
Over time, the number of Indian brands flocking to Shopify as an alternative to e-commerce marketplaces (or in addition to them) has grown. Today, both newer brands such as coffee chain Blue Tokai and John Jacobs Eyewear, and relatively more established ones like fabric and fashion retailer Raymond use Shopify as a passage to the world of e-commerce.
 
The Shopify ecosystem offers businesses all the tools required to run an online store. It provides everything from data analytics and payments integration to inventory management and logistics (through tie-ups with partners such as Delhivery and BlueDart). It even helps with access to financing.
 
And Covid has really reminded businesses just how important a digital presence is. In the first six months of 2020—half of which saw the country enter a stringent Covid-induced lockdown—Shopify claims that 123% more Shopify-powered stores were created as compared to the same period the previous year. Globally, the company boasts a market capitalisation of $124.5 billion.
 
In India, though, brands are relatively slow to catch on to its value. It's tough to break the habit of playing the Amazon-Flipkart e-commerce game. That said, this really is the time for Shopify to lure Indian businesses—more so with the promise of branding—and reduce their dependence on e-commerce.
