To what extent did the axiom ‘happiness is a state of mind’ guide the naming of mid-tier IT services company Happiest Minds, I don’t know. (Never had a chance to ask the founders.) For now, though, it’s certainly making its investors very, very happy.
The stock more than doubled on listing in September and has continued its gravity- and logic-defying run. At Rs 540 apiece now, it has more than tripled till date from its IPO price. The past week has been particularly good for the stock, with gains of about 35%.
Today, the stock is among the costliest in the Indian IT space in terms of valuation.
When Ashok Soota left Mindtree to start Happiest Minds Technologies, his second IT company, in 2011, he was 68 years old. Few IT veterans have taken two companies public. Soota has, and done one better. Mindtree saw heavy oversubscription—over 100 times in its 2007 IPO, and gained 45% on the listing day.
When Happiest Minds was oversubscribed 151 times, the founders felt bad that they had to disappoint many investors who didn’t get the stock allocation. Those investors have since bought into the stock in the secondary market.
Leaving the legacy IT services companies behind, Happiest Minds is being benchmarked with global pure-play digital services such as Globant, EPAM Systems, and Endava, whose services are 100% digital. For Happiest Minds, digital comprises 97% of the overall business.
Its global "comparators" trade at an impressive 65X to triple-digit price-to-earnings. Happiest Minds is trading at about 48X its earnings.
Has the party just begun for the Indian IT company? Or has it nearly run its course?
