For years, Nokia was the undisputed leader in the Indian phone market. But in 2013, Samsung overtook it and became the new numero uno. It would then go on to become the undisputed leader for nearly five years.
 
Till, in 2018, Xiaomi overtook it.
 
The growth of Xiaomi in India is a remarkable one because it has operated in the country for just 3.5 years. It is a testament to the ambition, scale and resources of Chinese phone makers (though Samsung is still a very close #2, other Chinese players like Oppo, Vivo, Huawei, Lenovo and OnePlus have virtually taken control of the market).
 
Xiaomi came to India with a one-size-fits-all approach honed in China. But it has gradually replaced that with an India-focused strategy that has kept aggressively pushing down the lowest price segment it caters to while adding significant offline distribution too.
 
Read Venkat’s story to understand how Xiaomi managed to become #1 in India, and whether it can hold on to that position for long: https://the-ken.com/story/inside-xiaomis-india-comeback/
