Horror films can be cheesy slashers with a series of jump-scares, or they can probe deep-seated discomforts and anxieties that fester within us. They’re incredible creative outlets—and a big business in any country with its own film industry.

Consider the case of Indonesia, where superstitions and folklore are springs of inspiration for any screenwriter keen to pen the next scary tale. Indonesian horror films have become a mainstay, finding success not only domestically but also across Asia.

One of the powerhouses behind this is MD Pictures, helmed by Manoj Punjabi, a film and television producer of Sindhi descent.

By developing scary films that are sometimes sourced from viral short fiction circulated on Twitter, MD Pictures has cornered the horror genre in Indonesia, and it’s ripping and tearing to keep moviegoers’ pulse rates up.

Without giving away any spoilers, MD’s success doesn’t necessarily mean the entire film industry sees benefits. The business of movies is increasingly becoming a “winner-takes-all” game, and many other players haven’t quite figured out how to navigate a rapidly changing film sector in Indonesia.

Before you look up a new Indonesian horror flick to stream tonight, check out Michelle’s dispatch about MD Pictures. She dives into how the film production house has evolved over the years and unpacks the way it has sunk its teeth into Indonesia’s film industry.
