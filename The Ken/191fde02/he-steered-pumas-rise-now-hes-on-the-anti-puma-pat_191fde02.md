Good Morning Ishan,
Are you a sneakerhead?
Do names like Adidas, Puma, Skechers, Reebok, Asics, Crocs, and Decathlon pop up in your quest for the perfect pair?
If you answered “yes” or “maybe” to either of these questions, you’ll find today’s story particularly interesting. Even though I’m not a sneaker collector myself, I found it absolutely fascinating.
A little secret: before reading the first draft, I hadn’t heard of the protagonist of this story, and I think you might not have either. (I hope you’re as unaware as I was, so I don’t feel I overshared my ignorance.)
Pranathi’s debut story is about Agilitas, a company that’s just over a year old, which acquired Mochiko, a 16-year-old shoe manufacturer responsible for producing 70–80% of Adidas and Puma footwear.
But more intriguingly, it explores the journey of Abhishek Ganguly, Atul Bajaj, and Amit Prabhu—three industry veterans who once steered Puma’s growth and are now building Agilitas from the ground up by taking an unconventional approach in an industry known for its “extremely high barriers to entry”.
It’s where technical products, supply-chain management, brand building, and distribution need to be nailed all at once—something even Nike is yet to attempt. Who would’ve thought the upper part of a shoe alone consists of 38 separate pieces that must be meticulously sewn together?
So, how did Agilitas generate Rs 740 crore in revenue in its first year of operations and be on track for an annualised revenue of Rs 1,000 crore—even without launching its products?
