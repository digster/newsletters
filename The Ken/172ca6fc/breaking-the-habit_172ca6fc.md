Ever heard of the habit loop? It's a concept originally articulated by researchers at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. The idea is pretty straightforward: Habit formation can be broken up into three aspects—a cue, a routine, and a reward. Combine the three, and you end up with a repeating trait that plays out almost on autopilot.
Coffee. Smoking. Exercise. Reading The Ken. Whatever your habit, you can basically break it down into the cue-routine-reward dynamic.
In better times, a lot of our habits were products of our workplaces. The Ken's Delhi office, for example, is equidistant to a number of eateries as well as a gym. Some of us went through a fitness phase, some snacked liberally, some did both. All of us have a serious coffee addiction, owing to an espresso machine in our cabin and a constant supply of great coffee powder.
With the lockdown, however, those habits changed. The gym was no longer an option. Neither were eateries. For a brief, godless period, some of us even ran out of coffee. Faced with new cues, we were forced to develop new habits. Morning walks, experiments in the kitchen, and, this still haunts me, chai.
Even as the lockdown lifts, a lot of things will take a while to return to pre-Covid normalcy. Movie theatres, for example. Many may never return to the pre-Covid status quo—permanent work-from-home arrangements, perhaps. New cues, new routines, new rewards, and inevitably, new habits.
Since Covid-19 turned the world on its head, I've resorted to cooking to keep me sane. What are the habits you've developed since the pandemic changed everything? I'd love to know.
