Good Morning Ishan,

You’ve got mail. And you’ve got an invitation. A special invitation to meet the Indian Prime Minister. You’d naturally be delighted. So were 200 odd entrepreneurs last month when an email lit up their inboxes.

Many of them who attended the two-day meeting were no strangers to Delhi’s power corridors. After all, even startups have understood that as they try new things, they have to push for regulation as well as fend against regulation. But there were many ‘policy-virgin’ founders in that August gathering. 

“The general assumption is that one needs a lot of influence to get the audience of the Prime Minister, but here we were almost getting wooed,” says a founder who attended the meeting.

Still, many founders were not clear what they were actually invited for. Some were on their charm offensive with a ready wish list. In the end, it turned out to be a JFK moment for the founders: “Ask not what the country can do for you, but what you can do for the country.”

They spoke and the Prime Minister took notes.

Even though the next day, PM Modi’s quote from the event in a business paper— “There is an app for filling every gap…”—sounded more like a catchy slogan for an election campaign, the intent to engage with the ‘Champions of Change’ cannot be missed. The follow-ups have begun.

So, what happened in a room full of startup founders who had the ears of the Prime Minister and his Cabinet?
