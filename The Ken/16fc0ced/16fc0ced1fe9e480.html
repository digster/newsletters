Good Morning Ishan,
 
When is The Ken writing a story about OYO?"
 
There hasn't been a single day in the last two weeks where multiple people haven't accosted me with this question. 
 
There seemed to be a general expectation that just because The Ken was one of the earliest publications to call out issues with OYO, we would not pass up on this opportunity to indulge in some old-fashioned schadenfreude.
 
But here's the thing.
 
We get absolutely no joy in an "I told you so" story in a situation where thousands of OYO employees have lost their jobs and had their livelihoods jeopardised.
 
So while today's story is on OYO, it isn't built or based on any of our previous stories and arguments.
 
Now, if you have read any OYO story over the past two weeks, you might be tempted to believe that these layoffs have been triggered by SoftBank's recent epiphany post the WeWork imbroglio. This narrative tells you that, given SoftBank's new-found religion around sustainable growth, unit economics and profitability, OYO has, in sync with a number of other peer SoftBank-funded startups, resorted to layoffs as part of a broader "course correction" to eliminate costs and improve efficiency. All part of startup life and absolutely par for the course.
 
While this narrative of laying the blame at SoftBank's feet seems plausible, it is not the truth. Instead, it is just a convenient alibi.
 
So what really happened? And why did it happen?
 
In today's piece, join Abinaya and Savio in a richly-reported story as they piece together dozens of first-hand accounts from OYO employees and hotel partners uncovering the "what", the "why" and the "how" of OYO's retreat from its amazingly ambitious but fundamentally flawed goal. It is a story that has parallels with another ambitious red army's historic retreat from ages past.
 
Read about OYO's Long March here: https://the-ken.com/story/oyos-march-of-retreat/ (14-minute read)
