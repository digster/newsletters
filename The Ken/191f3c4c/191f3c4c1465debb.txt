There’s no business like the defence business. Ask the $400M company that has little to do with it https://the-ken.com/story/theres-no-business-like-the-defence-business-ask-the-400m-company-that-has-little-to-do-with-it/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

Unimech takes the IPO route to become a pricey Indian defence stock. Except its business, like most of its peers’, is hardly Indian or even defence

In August, precision-parts maker Unimech Aerospace and Manufacturing filed its papers for a Rs 500-crore IPO—less than a month after its first-ever funding round. The IPO and the peers it lists are symbolic of how companies are riding the “defence wave” to list at valuations of over 100X earnings. The government’s Make-in-India push has led to a huge rise in investor interest in anything “defence”. But many of them get far less revenues from India and from defence than what it seems. And Unimech is hardly alone here. The e-commerce sensation’s disgruntled vendors are jumping ship, hurting its growth. So, PDD has paused dividend payouts to shareholders

Suppliers on Temu’s platform have been unhappy for some time with the company’s policies that prioritise increasing its user base at a cost for the merchants. With merchants protesting and some even considering shifting to other platforms, Temu and PDD Holdings are faced with slow-down concerns. That’s reflective in PDD Holdings’ stock price, too, which tanked after the company missed street expectation in the June quarter. But angry merchants aren’t the only concern for Temu and PDD Holdings. The company faces fierce competition, too, from other global giants. Inside the half-truths of corporate India’s sustainability claims https://the-ken.com/story/inside-the-half-truths-of-corporate-indias-sustainability-claims/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

Sebi’s transparency drive is thwarted by companies’ knack for hiding bad news in BRSR reports, all while they navigate a shoddily built framework and no real oversight

Indigo, Sun Pharma, Maruti Suzuki, and others may be navigating gaps in the sustainability reporting framework set by the market regulator. Its foundation appears to be somewhat fragile, characterised by vague definitions and a lack of initial investment in developing skilled ESG professionals. The effectiveness of assurance, which is meant to verify sustainability claims, is limited due to its voluntary nature for most disclosures. Experts advocate for enhanced internal audits within companies, urging Sebi to improve oversight and regulatory measures. Subway franchisees in India are struggling as Everstone Capital, the fast-food chain’s private-equity owner, is aggressively undercutting their business even as pandemic woes linger

In 2021, private-equity firm Everstone Capital acquired the master franchise rights for Subway India. Back then, it was hailed as one of the “largest master franchise agreements in QSR history”. The aim was to quadruple the store count to 2,000 stores and replicate what the likes of Jubilant Foodworks have been able to do with Domino’s. But so far, the the world’s largest sandwich-eatery chain is dealing with multiple menu changes, losing its mojo, and unhappy small franchisees.
