[Inside the half-truths of corporate India’s sustainability claims](https://the-ken.com/story/inside-the-half-truths-of-corporate-indias-sustainability-claims/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
Sebi’s transparency drive is thwarted by companies’ knack for hiding bad news in BRSR reports, all while they navigate a shoddily built framework and no real oversight
Indigo, Sun Pharma, Maruti Suzuki, and others may be navigating gaps in the sustainability reporting framework set by the market regulator. Its foundation appears to be somewhat fragile, characterised by vague definitions and a lack of initial investment in developing skilled ESG professionals. The effectiveness of assurance, which is meant to verify sustainability claims, is limited due to its voluntary nature for most disclosures. Experts advocate for enhanced internal audits within companies, urging Sebi to improve oversight and regulatory measures.
