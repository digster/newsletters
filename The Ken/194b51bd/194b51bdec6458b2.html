Imagine you’re running an e-commerce brand. Every second, payments are flying in—UPI, credit cards, BNPL. And somewhere in the background, Juspay is making sure it all works. If one payment aggregator goes down, Juspay reroutes transactions instantly. No glitches, no lost sales.

Now, imagine that safety net disappearing overnight.

That’s exactly what happened when Razorpay and Cashfree—two of India’s biggest aggregators—pulled the plug on Juspay, cutting off India’s biggest payments orchestrator. Their argument: what’s the need for a middleman telling merchants where to process payments? They want merchants locked in.

The timing wasn’t random. Juspay, last year, got its payment-aggregator licence and launched its own service. Suddenly, a partner had turned into a competitor.

So now, things are escalating:

  • Large merchants handling crores in daily transactions now face the prospect of disruption to their payments infrastructure—sometimes running up to weeks or even months.
  • Juspay’s hundreds of crores of orchestration business is under fire, but it’s not backing down.

And here’s where it gets even more interesting—Razorpay and Cashfree have launched their own orchestration tools. They’re now pitching the very system they abandoned back to merchants… but on their own terms.

So, is Juspay finished? Not quite. After all, it processes $670 billion in transactions annually, is looking to raise $150 million soon, and is deeply embedded in the payments infrastructure of giants like Swiggy, Bookmyshow, and Indigo. Some merchants are resisting the push to abandon it. Others are reluctantly making the switch. And some are realising this isn’t just a Juspay vs all battle—it’s a fight for control over how money moves.
