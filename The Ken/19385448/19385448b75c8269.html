Does anyone want a laptop delivered in 15 minutes or a refrigerator in under an hour?

Wrong question.

As Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart have shown with groceries, if you can ship something instantly, you can prime consumers to want that product instantly. Always.

That has led to the creation of the hottest business in India’s internet economy.

Tata Group’s Bigbasket and Walmart-owned Flipkart were woefully late to hitch their wagon to quick commerce. But they can still have an edge over the incumbents in one category.

Croma, run by Bigbasket’s sister company, is India’s second-largest electronics retailer, with over 500 stores. And Flipkart is its biggest e-tailer of electronics.

Flipkart Minutes, the instant-delivery service, and Bigbasket are delivering gadgets and appliances in just three large cities now. But they are expanding quickly. Flipkart, for instance, wants to expand to seven other cities and quadruple its warehouse count to 400 by March.

But electronics, especially large ones, are messy in a way groceries are not. Do you set up a separate warehouse for them? How do you deal with their installation? What about exchange and financing?

Nuha has a deep dive into how Flipkart and Bigbasket are dealing with these knotty problems.
