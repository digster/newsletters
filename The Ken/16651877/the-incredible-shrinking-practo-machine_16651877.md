Practo, India’s most funded healthcare platform, is in a bit of a spot. After 10 years of being around, close to a quarter of a billion dollars in venture funding and multiple acquisitions, it’s facing an identity crisis.
Since starting out as “Turbodoc”, a cloud-hosted clinic management software for doctors, Practo has grown and acquired new arms, like an octopus driven on radioactive drugs. Hospital management. Real-time appointment booking. Diagnostic management. Wellness and preventive healthcare management. Healthcare analytics.
Being “the Google of healthcare” was its stated ambition, though perhaps being “the Amazon of healthcare” was apter an ambition. Because Practo wanted people to access everything, from health records to insurance plans, preventive care to treatment plans, diagnostics to medicines, through its app.
At the peak of its growth phase, it employed over 2000 people. Practopus was real.
Now, that’s down to 900. And it needs to raise more money. Which won’t be easy.
Because there are newer fish in the healthcare pond. Like the newly-crowned unicorn PolicyBazaar, which is launching a massive doctor network called DocPrime.
And because Practo’s operational execution story till date is looking like Swiss cheese.
