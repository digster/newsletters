Speaking of winning, I have often wondered why so many people tip-toe around the most vital question. In my brief life as a journalist, I’ve known some people to skirt. Some say they are testing the waters before actually getting to the real stuff. Laying the ground with questions like, so what do you do? Where are you at? What’s the business model like? Do you think you will be able to make money? What do you think of your competitors? And blah, blah... they go, laying the ground with more of their questions dominating the exchange. Listening to their own voice. Only they forget to ask this:
 
What is so special about what you do?
 
I think this is a question that needs to be asked more.
 
Today, it must be asked of upGrad, a company that sells courses online. The kind you pick for reskilling or becoming better at something. For which you take a break from work, and you pay well so you can land on your feet better.
 
The upGrad story is a story of sizing up an opportunity and going after it. Around four years back, a wildly successful media entrepreneur, four words that you are not going to hear often in the foreseeable future, Ronnie Screwvala, decided that he must get into the education business. His hypothesis: English-speaking population, around 200 million addressable market, generally lacking in skills, penetration of the Internet; let’s sell them courses, make them smarter, and if they lack this awareness, spend money to tell them they must get smarter. And in doing this, let’s make some money. Let’s call it upGrad—a play on the word upgrade and graduation. True story!
 
Cut to the present, Screwvala has sunk decent dough in the business, around Rs 130 crore. upGrad has sold many online courses and has come some distance. But it now wants to play in the big leagues, raise some venture money or get in strategic investors. To that end, Screwvala says: "Education is a lot about storytelling. At the core of it, that's what it is."
 
He might be right. But what he doesn’t say is, what’s so special about upGrad?
