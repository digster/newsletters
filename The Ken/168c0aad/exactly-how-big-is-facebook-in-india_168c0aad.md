Yes. That’s what Ashish wrote about today. 2500 words.
I know what you are thinking. That’s a lot of words for a seemingly simple answer. This is long-form journalism taken to the extreme. This Ashish sounds like one of those precocious roll number 1 kids who ask for an extra sheet in their final exam to answer a question like ‘What is the speed of light in a vacuum?’ or something. Why so many words? So much drama. No wonder journalists everywhere are losing their jobs.
Just tell me. One word answer.
Turns out, it isn’t. The answer is much more nuanced. And complicated. And fascinating.
The thing is, nobody really knows how big Facebook is in India. Not even me - and I spent half a decade as a Product Manager at one of the world's largest ad-networks in India. A few years back, we built an early version of a platform product which we thought would solve a key problem for advertisers. So we went to pitch to some of them. We did dozens of pitches. We pitched to large agencies, and to tiny independent resellers. In the end, when we asked for feedback, the person on the other side would enthusiastically start explaining additional requirements, features, and tools. After a few meetings, we noticed that nearly all of them were asking for the same things. They even used the same words to describe it. We never figured why - until one advertiser, to illustrate the point, opened his laptop to show us what he wanted.
He wanted Facebook.
Everyone wanted Facebook.
We knew Facebook was huge for advertisers. We had no idea it was omnipresent. It was used by the largest unicorns on the planet. It was used by a small, independent boutique store in Indiranagar. Even by a members-only swimming pool complex in a Tier 2 city. We never found out how big it was. We tried. But a huge, distributed, self-serve business model made it impossible. The only way we would know, we figured, was if the company itself disclosed it.
Facebook didn’t.
So Ashish took a shot.
