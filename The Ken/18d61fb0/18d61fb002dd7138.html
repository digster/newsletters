Today’s briefing by Brady Ng
Deputy Editor in Southeast Asia

Good Morning Ishan

One hot summer night some years ago, I stood trackside at a racecourse, watching horses race by. I struck up a conversation with the man beside me. He told me that his job involved helping energy companies ship liquefied natural gas from one continent to another.

As the jockeys and their steeds barrelled by and kicked up dirt, he described the compression that enables natural gas to be shipped across oceans and rattled off other facts like a pop-science writer. I gathered that we were speaking at a time when demand was climbing, and the job of matching tankers with supply was, by the size of his bets, rewarding—even though he mentioned that some ships were getting too old and were barely safe enough for continued usage.

Today’s story is about the biggest LNG developer in Southeast Asia and its decision to gradually shift away from burning coal for power.

Intuitively, that feels like the right, responsible move, but San Miguel Global Power is now facing financial instability because of its immense debt and negative cash flow. Worse yet, repayment will soon be due, but a bailout from its parent will add immense stress to both companies.

Writing from Manila, Katrina captured the story.
