Good Morning Ishan,
 
God’s honest truth. When Harveen first called me to discuss Miniso, I had absolutely no clue about the company. Nada. But 90% of an editor’s job is to listen, and see if the reporter can passionately and clearly talk about the subject for a straight three minutes. Harveen wouldn’t stop talking, and I kept listening. Here’s what she said: 
 
-Miniso has been in India since August 2017. Since then, the company has opened 70 stores. That’s almost four stores every month. 25 stores in Delhi alone. 
 
-Compared to Miniso, Japanese lifestyle brand Muji, which has a much larger appeal, has set up just four stores. Homegrown chain Market 99 has opened 50 stores. In 11 years. 
 
“50 stores in 11 years, Ashish.” Well, that’s Harveen. 
 
Me: Hmm…
 
-Miniso claims that in the 12 months since its launch, it has earned revenue of more than Rs 700 crore. That’s almost Rs 27 crore per store, a lot of money for a company like Miniso, and it should be taken with a pinch of salt.
 
-While Miniso comes across as a brand with Japanese fundamentals, minimal design, single colours, it is actually Chinese. And all its products are actually imported from countries like China, Thailand and Korea. 
 
That’s not all. 
 
The company is funded by Chinese Internet company Tencent and Hill House Capital, and while Miniso has been making claims in India, not all is well. As of December 2018, the company has moved a bankruptcy application against its own brand trustees in Canada alleging misuse of trademarks.
 
“Sure, but what’s the story we are telling here?” That’s me. 
 
Her: Is Miniso for real? In India. Globally?
