Good Morning Ishan,
Can a school add more than one new centre every day of the year? Like, 500 centres in one year?
It’s like a retailer adding SKUs to their inventory. Although even that requires quite a bit of work.
But the school chain Lighthouse Learning (formerly Eurokids International), a network of seven brands, tells The Ken it is scaling up exactly in this manner. Ever since the private-equity firm KKR infused US$200 million in 2019, Lighthouse has turned from a single school brand into a powerhouse with three preschool brands and five K-12 brands.
And if you think the SKU analogy is misplaced, know that the chain is now helmed by Sadashiv Nayak, former Future Retail and Future Consumer Big Bazaar chief.
PE deals in schools are few and far between in India. But whenever such deals happen, they act as a booster rocket for schools, changing their growth orbit. More professionalism, better branding, better financial discipline, and even more choices for parents as education no longer remains a scarce commodity.
Now, KKR is looking to exit Lighthouse at a US$1 billion price tag. Is that tag justified? And if getting PEs into schools is so good for the sector, why isn’t the model luring more schools? Alifiya has [some answers](https://the-ken.com/story/kkr-turned-eurokids-into-a-billion-dollar-education-giant-but-not-everyones-applauding/?utm_source=daily&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) in her story.
Riding the coattails of national fervour, or an international craze, can sometimes be a double-edged sword in business.
Since we wrote about Yotta Data Services, the new GPU cloud provider in town, [last week](https://the-ken.com/story/nvidias-ai-chips-are-about-to-flood-the-indian-market-but-who-needs-them/?utm_source=daily&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter), the company has announced an alliance with Deloitte to offer services toward building new AI applications “that unlock value from generative AI”.
Yotta’s humongous bet on procuring 20,000 H100 class of GPUs from Nvidia may have looked like a masterstroke for a brief period when the world didn’t seem to catch a break on Gen AI news and the GPUs were in short supply. But now that the Gen-AI wave has somewhat receded, the reality is revealing.
Yotta is today selling the hottest H100 GPUs at rock-bottom prices—US$2/GPU/hour. I am told this is the list price of Yotta, the actual price is much lower. An industry professional estimates “their costs are at least US$1.5 per hour assuming 13% cost of capital”.
Don’t get me wrong. I’m no tech-pessimist; nor am I in some kind of “moral panic” (as the American techno-optimist Marc Andreessen believes the AI/crypto-wary camp to be in). But in such businesses, one cannot create demand by diktat or by hype.
