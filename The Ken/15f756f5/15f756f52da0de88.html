Good Morning Ishan,
 
Delhi has a Diwali hangover. The air quality continues to be poor. And the gifting season finds a good analogy in pharmaceuticals which stare at a new law that’d penalise companies for giving gifts to doctors.
 
“Earlier, a packet of sweets to friends or neighbours made the perfect Diwali gift. Now, the sweetness is not enough; they expect something more. It’s the same with doctors. Earlier drug samples were enough, but now all kinds of demands are made. The industry has changed...,” trailed off a seasoned pharma entrepreneur in the capital yesterday.
 
The entrepreneur himself is another story for another day. For now, let’s dwell on the Uniform Code for Pharma Marketing Practices (UCPMP) which seems to be the talk of the town. Inspired by a similar law in the US, it is meant to increase transparency of financial relationships between doctors and drug makers.
 
Indian pharma companies have always relied on having a heavy field force—even a medium size company has thousands of medical reps. But once the government stops them from exchanging hospitality for prescription with doctors, the rules of the game will change. Digital marketing is the obvious route, but few understand it, and fewer still, believe in it. Alkem Laboratories appears to be the first believer.
 
From door-to-door to digital, it’s easier said than done. Still, it’s a great opportunity to shift to patient-centric, information-driven marketing and corporate branding.
