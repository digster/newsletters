Good Morning Ishan,
 
Back in May 2020, when India shut down and companies battened down their hatches, one company stood out—Reliance Jio. At the time, it received over $20 billion in funds from 12 successive investments, including from the likes of Google and Facebook.

Jio was here. It was unstoppable. And it was going to win. 

One year later… things have not quite panned out as expected. Jio’s growth is slowing—it added just 37.8 million users for the year ended March 2021. By comparison, it added 80 million users the previous year. And 100 million users the year before that.  

And it’s only going to get more difficult. 

A large chunk of Reliance Jio’s subscribers is its lower-paying and lower-earning feature phone customers who are locked into the network through JioPhones. Cheap feature phones that Jio practically gave away for free in exchange for guaranteed multi-year lock-in plans. 

Most of these users are up for renewal this year, and Jio desperately needs them to renew or upgrade their plans. And Jio needs to convince them to do this during one of India’s worst public health crises when millions of people are uncertain about their economic future.  

Then there’s the problem with Jio’s ARPUs. The telco’s closest competitor, Airtel, has added more subscribers than Jio every month since August 2020. And it’s not just the numbers—they are better subscribers too. Airtel’s average revenue per user (ARPU) has been consistently higher than Jio’s, quarter after quarter.

This is a particularly nasty thorn in its side because Jio doesn’t own its fibre and network towers—it pays a hefty rent. So Jio needs high ARPUs to justify the rent it pays.  

There’s more. The ratio of active to inactive subscribers on Jio’s network. The delays in plans to launch a 4G smartphone with Google. 

Jio needs a lot of things to go its way in 2021, or else things start to look tricky.

That’s Rohin’s story today. Quite simply, he explains that Jio actually has a K-shaped conundrum. The two arms of the K represent two segments of Jio’s users—and right now, Jio has problems across both of them.
