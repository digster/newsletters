Good Morning Ishan,

For five years now, Indore, Madhya Pradesh, has consistently been ranked as India's cleanest city. The bylanes of the city, however, hide a dirty secret—Indore serves as the home base for a widespread nexus of stock market scamsters.

While Sebi, India's market regulator, requires all investment advisors to be licensed and closely regulated, unregistered investment advisors abound in Indore. These operators use an intricate playbook of cons to part unsuspecting victims from their money.

These shady operators cold-call potential clients, offering them free stock tips to gain trust and legitimacy. They then build on this, promising their victims eye-popping, guaranteed returns on investments in penny stocks and financial instruments such as options. Victims often end up cheated out of Rs 3 lakh (~$4,000) or more.

In today's story, Jaspreet and Anand take us into this murky world. They visit registered business addresses that are, in reality, fake storefronts. They enter marketing firms that serve as the foot soldiers of these con artists. And they speak to the law enforcement officials tasked with the unenviable job of bringing these scamsters to justice.

The victims aren't just naive investors. Even registered investment advisors have suffered as a result of the Indore Inveiglers. In 2020, Sebi tightened its regulations to thwart these scamsters. However, even as this nexus has continued its illicit activities, actual investment advisors have found it harder to do business. Their case isn't helped by many unscrupulous registered investment advisors in Indore, who have been the subject of 90% of investment advisor complaints made to Sebi since 2013.
