We know that The Ken’s readers are sharp and have a keener understanding of technology than most others. So you probably know APIs as Application Programming Interfaces, the behind-the-scenes code that helps software programs and platforms to talk to and integrate with each other.
 
But there’s another API that’s the linchpin of India’s Pharma sector - Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient. APIs are the key ingredients of medicines, and the ones responsible for drug efficacy. And Indian Pharma has a China-sized API problem.
 
As Indian Pharma companies like Lupin, Advinus, Piramal, Daiichi, and Dr Reddy’s Laboratories shut down their API plants and systematically close down drug discovery efforts—some for cost arbitrage, some for lack of governmental support—the Chinese are ramping up. 
 
Nearly 25% of India’s domestic pharma sales depend on China for their APIs.
 
A mid-sized pharma company in India, like the Bakul Group, manufactures 500 tonnes of APIs in a year. In China, a mid-size company churns out 500 tonnes of API every month. And at least 500 such companies operate across 10-12 provinces in China.
 
Finally the kicker: Pharma ranks 39th on China’s export list, while for India, it’s the 5th largest.
