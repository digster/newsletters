Good Morning Ishan,
 
Social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube are often under scrutiny from the government and users for the filter bubbles or misinformation trails their algorithms create. But equally addictive and large platforms like India’s ShareChat and Moj—their combined monthly active user base of 280 million in India is bigger than Facebook-owned Instagram’s 210 million—have so far flown under the radar. 
 
Focused on a billion-plus regional language users, ShareChat and its short video sibling Moj are the veritable vernacular information highway, shaping and influencing regional public discourse. Their parent, Mohalla Tech, just raised $502 million from Tiger Global and Lightspeed Ventures to achieve a post-money valuation of US$2 billion.
 
Still, few know how this massive content factory churns out millions of posts each day.
 
In his debut story today, Munsif Vengattil, our newest writer, walks us through what is an unenviable tightrope walk for the company.
 
While Mohalla Tech itself is inspired by China’s TikTok—users' feeds are determined by algorithms, not by their social graph—its content moderation is low-tech. Done manually and to a great extent by hundreds of third-party content moderators, what people see on ShareChat and Moj is, therefore, subject to manual ‘silent discards’ and interventions.
 
Being on the right of the Indian government also has its own merits.
 
During the just concluded West Bengal elections, an edited video depicting West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee throwing a football at Prime Minister Narendra Modi’s back went viral. Content moderators were asked to remove all copies of this video from the platform. This went against the company’s own internal guidelines, which allow “public persons represented in funny way” to remain on the app.
 
One person’s humour is another’s nightmare.
 
Until now, Moj hasn’t disclosed, unlike its international counterparts, stats on its content moderation. ShareChat has been tardy, too. But they’ll soon have to get their content removal disclosures in order to comply with India’s updated social media rules, which expect all companies to file compliance reports every month.
 
Over many years, some 9,000 patents helped Facebook build and run deep algorithms that make it irresistible to advertisers. Will Mohalla Tech get so much time?  For that and more, read Munsif’s fascinating story: https://the-ken.com/story/the-cracks-in-sharechat-mojs-content-moderation-machine/ (12-minute read)
