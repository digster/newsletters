Good Morning Ishan,

As commonplace as Indians' never-ending love for gold is the way they buy them.

Very few can afford to purchase it by handing over a big wad of cash or by swiping their card. Instead, people put money monthly in a jeweller's scheme and use the corpus to buy gold at the end of a year or two.

Now imagine doing the same for a smartphone or television. That's the pitch of save-now-buy-later (SNBL) startups such as Multipl, Tortoise, and Hubble. Multipl, backed by Blume Ventures and India Infoline Ltd, has seen its user base grow 10X in the past year, but the absolute number–250,000–is nowhere close to turning heads. 

But why would you choose this when there are several startups telling you that you absolutely don't have to wait to enjoy something you want, even if you can't afford it yet? Buy now, pay later (BNPL) is centred around giving in to instant gratification, while SNBL is all about shunning that temptation.

And I don't need to tell you that the latter is a much tougher sell. 

There's also the fact that large lenders like HDFC Bank and ICICI Bank gave SNBL a shot years ago, but it didn't move the needle for them.

So how exactly do these SNBL startups plan to produce a different result?
