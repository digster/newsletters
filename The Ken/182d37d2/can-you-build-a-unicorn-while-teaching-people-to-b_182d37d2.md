Hello Ishan,
“Entrepreneurship provides you with that whole freedom to do things right.”
That’s Baskar Subramanian, Co-founder and CEO of Amagi, in the [second episode](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles/amagi-baskar-subramanian/?utm_source=launch&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=fp) of First Principles—our new podcast where Rohin Dharmakumar, CEO and co-founder of The Ken interviews CEOs to understand their mental models, how they think and how they look at the world a little differently than others.
If you haven’t heard of Amagi, it’s understandable. They sell their cloud-based software products to media businesses, TV channels and streaming platforms. Not consumers.
But you should pay attention to the company.
After starting up in 2010 in Bangalore, it raised close to $40M over the next 6-7 years and built a good revenue base too. Then one day, it decided to dump it all and pivot to a different business model.
It clicked, and how.
Last September, Amagi raised an additional $100M in venture capital. Then, in March this year, another $95M. Now, it’s rumoured to be raising another $100M.
Baskar began his career by telling his father he didn’t want a career. To Baskar, entrepreneurship was a drug. A drug so potent that he dropped out of IIT Bombay, one of India’s best engineering colleges, to build something of his own.
In today’s episode, Baskar talks about building a $1.4 billion company he envisions will outlast him and that his core job as a CEO is to just communicate the organisation's culture and values well enough to all 700 odd employees at Amagi. He answers questions about home-schooling his two kids, his outlook on success and the sheer joy of building products that can deliver value to a user.
