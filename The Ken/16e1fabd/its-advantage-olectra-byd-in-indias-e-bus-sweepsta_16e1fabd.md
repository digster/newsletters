Me: "What is Olectra? Who is Olectra?"
B: "India's biggest electric bus maker."
Me (incredulously): "Really? Anyway, why do we care about buses when everyone is making electric bikes, cars and scooters left, right and centre?"
B: "Because 41% of the budget for FAME II (the government's incentive program for EVs) is for buses."
Me (still incredulous): "Really?"
Me (struggling to ask editor-type questions): "Still, why Olectra when there are the Tatas and Leyland and others?"
B (with the calm air of one scenting victory): "Olectra, in partnership with BYD, won the most significant contracts in FAME I last year."
Me (remaining incredulous): "Really? BYD? The Warren Buffett-backed Chinese EV company?"
B (calmly): "Yup."
Me (out of questions and reallys): "Ok then, let's see what you get."
And what he got left me incredulous. It's an eye-opener on India's e-bus market. A story of how a three-year-old JV between the world's top EV company and one with zero auto expertise clawed its way to dominance. Of how, left gasping in its wake, rivals blocked its entry into the industry lobby group. Of how Indian e-bus makers are at the mercy of Chinese battery makers (solar industry redux anyone?). Of how the requirements of bidding for FAME II contracts have shifted, benefitting ...
No, I won't give away more. Suffice to say that Olectra could soon become a common noun to refer to e-buses, just like how 'Volvo' to most Indians now means any 'luxury AC bus'.
