When it comes to government schemes in India, the expectations are grand, but reality hits hard once you start talking to those tasked with making them work.

Expectation: The PM Internship scheme—to provide 10 million internships at 500 of India’s top corporates over the next five years—will address companies’ concerns that graduates don’t have the requisite skill set.

Reality: Some of the companies may not even participate in the scheme; others are realising only now that meeting the ask is a handful in itself that could even impact hiring for other roles.

Expectation: Hiring an army of interns can make companies more productive at a lower cost as the government foots the bill for 90% of their stipend.

Reality: Figuring out how to put all these interns to work calls for immense “leadership bandwidth, with dedicated teams engaged to deal with them,” says the head of talent skilling at a multinational tech firm.

Expectation: The scheme will offer opportunities for much-needed experiential training to youth from lower socio-economic backgrounds who lack the heft of a name-brand university.

Reality: Holes in the scheme design, like the application process itself, could pose pitfalls for the very applicants it targets.

Expectation: Reading this email is enough to explain why this scheme is suffering from a problem-solution mismatch.

Reality: There’s a lot more to unpack. For the details, just read Debanjali’s story today.
