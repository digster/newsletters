Good Morning Ishan,
Let’s play a little multiple-choice:
What happens when a fintech with 600 million users decides to sell insurance?
A) It dominates the market
B) It mints money
C) It sets Rs 1,000 crore on fire and makes Rs 150 crore in return
Who’s crushing it in financial services, including insurance?
A) The fintech that burned Rs 700 crore on celebrity ads
B) The one that fell from a $19 billion valuation to $5 billion, but hey, at least it kind of figured out lending and insurance
C) Trick question. They’re both struggling—just in different flavours of pain
What does a fintech with nearly 50% UPI market share need before its IPO?
A) A financial-services comeback
B) A lesson in what not to do
C) A brand-new game plan—urgently
If you picked mostly Cs, congratulations, you might have a future in fintech consulting.
Phonepe thought its payments dominance would make selling insurance a breeze. Turns out, insurance isn’t as swipeable as UPI. Now, it’s left wondering why its financial-services bet hasn’t paid off—while its rival Paytm* is busy learning some post-IPO lessons.
So, what went wrong? And what does this mean for Phonepe’s big listing dreams?
[Suprita has the answers](https://the-ken.com/story/phonepe-spent-millions-to-rival-policybazaar-its-users-couldnt-care-less/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter)—like why financial services are still just 4% of Phonepe’s revenue. And whether that 4% can pull its weight now that the government is cutting the subsidy that props up the other 96%.
*Paytm’s Vijay Shekhar Sharma is an investor in The Ken.
