Phew! Finally, Friday is here. Been a tough, long day kinda work week. So yeah, I hope you had it better. Tell me something, is it winter in your part of the world already? Here where I am, man, nothing’s happening. 
 
Okay, story time. Good news, we are in the capable hands of my colleague, Sumanth. Bad news, he’s been doing the numbers and thinking about SoftBank’s $100 billion Vision Fund, and he says it is not going to go down well. There’s a lot of money chasing a handful of companies. There’s high chance that in the next couple of years, billions will be frittered away chasing some sort of new vanity metric. Think about it, SoftBank’s entry cheque is about $250 million. How many companies need or deserve this kind of money? Not long back, under Nikesh Arora, SoftBank gave out some $100 million cheques. Housing. Snapdeal. Grofers. Oyo. 
 
For the longest time, the only news I have heard from Grofers is that they have some of that cash left in the bank. Somehow, that’s supposed to be a happy consequence. Where Grofers is in the journey of solving the grocery problem, is another story altogether. 
 
Or as Sumanth says: “Take Flipkart for instance. Until its recent SoftBank-led funding round, Flipkart was focusing on rationalising its burn rate and was targeting an IPO over the next couple of years. Now it needs to do neither.” Sumanth hints this slightly in the copy. The billions that are coming in from SoftBank into Flipkart and Paytm will bring about quite some consolidation in the business. The talk of unit level economics, which dominated much of the conversation in 2017, will be just that. Talk.
