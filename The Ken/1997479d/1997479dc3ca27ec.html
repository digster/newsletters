Reliance always has great timing.

In August, Reliance Industries’ chairman Mukesh Ambani said Jio Platforms, in which Reliance owns over two-thirds, will go public by the first half of 2026.

Earlier that month, Reliance Jio Infocomm, in which Jio Platforms owns a 100% stake, had announced a tariff hike that would lead to a nice spike in profits.

And right after the IPO announcement, market regulator Sebi eased norms for IPOs of very large companies. As per that, a minimum of 2.5% stake sale would set Jio Platforms’ issue at about Rs 30,000 crore, according to research house Motilal Oswal. That brings the company’s valuation to nearly Rs 12 lakh crore, or $135 billion—higher than rival Bharti Airtel.

In 2020, Jio Platforms had raised over Rs 1.5 lakh crore from an eclectic mix of 13 foreign investors—making it the biggest, quickest fundraise so far in India. To facilitate this, the company issued fresh shares, selling nearly 33% stake at top dollar, diluting its parent firm Reliance Industries’ ownership. Two of the investors—Facebook (Meta) and Alphabet (Google)—were strategic. The rest—ranging from global private-equity giants like Silver Lake, KKR, and Vista to sovereign wealth funds like Mubadala, ADIA, and PIF—were financial investors.

So the billion-dollar question now is what can this IPO do for these investors?
