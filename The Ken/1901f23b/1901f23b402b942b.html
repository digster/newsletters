Stoa School, the IIM challenger, is dead. What’s next for India’s altMBAs?

India’s first alternative-MBA programme has hit the pause button even as the thirst for B-school education keeps growing. Along the way, it has inspired a wave of new ventures that keep cropping up

As the return-to-work norm collides with generative AI, the future of learning stands at a pivotal moment. Demand for B-school education is at an all-time high; for young Indians, altMBA courses offer an alternative to IIMs. In this climate, Stoa School, which was the first to enter the altMBA market, is also the first to halt its operations. While the likes of Masters’ Union and Mesa School of Business opted for a post-pandemic model, Stoa has declined to navigate uncertain territory. [Read the full story here]

Who stands to gain the most from Jio Financial’s US$4.3B lease deal? Hint: Not Jio Financial 

To kickstart its business, the US$27-billion financial arm of Mukesh Ambani-led Reliance has devised a unique experiment that involves two of its siblings 

With an exalted valuation and meagre revenue to show for it, Jio Financial Services—demerged from RIL last year—needs a business kickstart and fast. The breakthrough is being sought through a Rs 36,000 crore (US$4.3 billion) buy-and-lease telecom gear deal involving Reliance Retail and Reliance Jio Infocomm. Jio Financial’s siblings and their parents, who are eyeing a listing, could gain tidily. So will parent Reliance Industries, the fountainhead of the empire. The costs and risks, however, seem tilted against Jio Financial Services. Then again, that’s the price to be paid to strike it big and quick. [Read the full story here]

BYD, Vinfast are in a race. Who will win the Southeast Asian circuit? 

While BYD made a fortune out of the 900% increase in EV sales in Southeast Asia last year, its regional rival Vinfast is upping the ante

Southeast Asia, one of the leading emerging markets for electric vehicles, is seeing an influx of Chinese brands. Companies like BYD have more than doubled their market share in the region of late, contributing to about 75% of the EV sales there. But going deeper into the region poses a challenge for Chinese players, thanks to regional rivals like Vinfast, who control the charging infrastructure. They’re left with fewer options to increase presence: build their own infra, leverage free-trade pacts, or call a truce. [Read the full story here]

It’s finance

How Paisabazaar is spoiling PB Fintech’s profitability party 

The credit marketplace has switched gears to offer more secured loans. But doing so means turning its customer-acquisition game on its head

Paisabazaar was cruising along with double-digit growth in quarterly revenue until the RBI’s interruption. The regulator sounded alarm on unsecured loans and discouraged lenders from small-ticket, collateral-free advances. As a result, Paisabazaar’s lending partners started shying away from unsecured loan leads, affecting the fintech’s growth. So, the credit marketplace has no option but to foray into secured loans. But that’s not a smooth path either. [Read the full story here]

Jupiter’s non-bank ambitions hinge on its shaky neobank origins 

The fintech’s lending play is wholly dependent on its ability to upsell loans to its existing customers

Mumbai-based fintech startup Jupiter grew its user base from half a million in 2021 to about two million in a year. It received a non-bank licence from the regulator last year, following a failed attempt in 2020. While its competitors resort to user-acquisition strategy, Jupiter wants to focus only on the creditworthy lot, by nudging them with options to borrow more. But in a bid to disrupt how people traditionally banked, the startup might end up losing its identity. [Read the full story here]

In Titan’s Caratlane shadow, Bluestone found its stride. Now, it has to step out of it 

Both fine-jewellery companies started around the same time over a decade ago, drew big-name investors, and grew fast. But a key difference made one a star while the other is trying to catch up

Bluestone, backed by Accel, is reportedly raising a pre-IPO round at a US$1 billion valuation, nearly double last year’s valuation when investors like Zerodha’s Nikhil Kamath came on board. The confidence among investors stems largely from Titan valuing Caratlane at US$2 billion when it bought founder Mithun Sacheti’s stake last August. But Bluestone has been lagging far behind CaratLane both in terms of revenue and profitability. Now, as Bluestone, also one of the first movers in the space, readies itself for a public listing, it needs to stop playing second fiddle to the market lead.
