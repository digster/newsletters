Good Morning Ishan,
I was reading Venkat’s story yesterday, and at one point, I went damn, that’s embarrassing. I haven’t watched Narcos. Still. I know, I know.
We are living in amazing times, from Donald Trump’s big nuke button to, I mean, only the other day I noticed my co-founder Rohin tweeting about some obscure show that's a hidden gem on Netflix. I wanted to make a note of it but I’m too lazy. But surely, there’s so much great content out there to consume and Indians are making the most of it. This is where Venkat’s piece starts.
Indians consume the most amount of video content in all of Asia Pacific—about 12.3 hours in a week—as per a recent study by Akamai Technologies. Our story says that 2018 is going to be much bigger. For content creators and companies who put them out, like Hotstar, Netflix and Amazon Prime. And YouTube and Facebook Watch. The jostle for good content. Yup, we will see that in 2018.
And of course, then there’s the media business. There, we should see a lot more focus on subscriptions and video. Across languages. Take this interesting data point from the story. Three of the top five YouTube channels of Network18 in the last three months, as per a report by Vidooly, are ETV Telugu (523 million views), ETV Jabardasth (345.4 million) and ETV Plus (172.1 million). That trend holds with Zee News and India Today, where Aaj Tak has been the most dominant YouTube channel. These are good numbers, but you and I should talk about which is the media company; YouTube or Network18. Not today, but someday.
