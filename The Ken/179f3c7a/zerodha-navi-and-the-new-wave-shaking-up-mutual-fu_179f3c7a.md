In the timeline of industries, the mutual fund industry is still a baby in diapers.
The first modern mutual fund in the world, which people could easily buy into and sell out of at will, was only [set up](https://www.investopedia.com/articles/mutualfund/05/mfhistory.asp) in 1924 as the Massachusetts Investors’ Trust in Boston.
The business of mutual funds was simple and the messaging straightforward. A professional would pick the ‘best’ stocks to create a portfolio. People could pay a fee and buy this portfolio. Forget how high the fees or expenses were, because the theory was that the fees were for the privilege of getting an expert who would deliver far superior returns to what you could manage as an individual.
Fast-forward to the 1970s, and there came a man who didn’t quite buy into this idea. John Bogle, who founded the Vanguard Group.
His idea was simple. Eliminate the ‘professional’, and buy a low-cost stock market index. The idea took time to catch on, but it soon disrupted the industry. World over, investors began flocking to these index funds when they realised that the ‘professionals’ were actually finding it hard to deliver a superior return.
Today, Anand writes about this baby in diapers that’s quickly growing up in India.
From managing assets worth Rs 12,30,000 crore ($169 billion) in March 2016 to Rs 32,40,000 crore ($444 billion) as of April 2021, the mutual fund industry's rise has been swift. But, it’s still an industry that’s severely under-penetrated in the country. Globally, the assets under management (AUM) to GDP ratio is nearly 70%. In India, it’s below 20%.
That opportunity is something newer players such as India’s largest broker Zerodha, and ex-Flipkart founder Sachin Bansal’s Navi are counting on. Shaking up an industry with over 40 incumbents is not going to be easy. But, in his conversations with the newbies, Anand says that all of them believe they have what it takes to disrupt the mutual funds party.
How?
They are betting on John Bogle’s innovation, passive funds, along with its more evolved and ‘sophisticated’ cousins, rules-based funds or smart-beta funds. Combine it with better technology and a smoother user experience, and they believe they have a winning model.
