On Daybreak

 

Have you noticed huge billboards in your city featuring Salman Khan dressed in Mission Impossible type of gear? The tagline says ‘upar wala sab dekh raha hai’ which translates into something around the lines of ‘Big Boss is watching you.’ It's an advertisement for CP Plus, a security camera company.
 

It does sound a bit unnerving, isn’t it?
 

But in his book called On The Move: My Journey As A Relentless Entrepreneur, Harsh Khemka,  the chairman of CP Plus, explained that uparwala meant God. He wrote about how the creative team came up with the idea that they thought would resonate with most Indians.
 

However, it is a known fact that CP Plus was also one of the main sponsors of Big Boss.

In this episode of Daybreak, Snigdha talks about these surveillance technology companies because these advertisements skip an important piece of information that you should know about.
 

For example, 80% of CP Plus’ supplies for manufacturing as well as trading activities for its parent company Aditya Infotech Ltd (AIL) come from Chinese surveillance-equipment manufacturer Dahua Technology.
 

Dahua and Hikvision—another major Chinese security-tech manufacturer are facing regulatory action in the US, UK, and Australia, and other countries, for their close links to the Chinese government.
 

In India though, these companies are flourishing. Controlling these companies is turning out to be a very tricky policy challenge for the Indian government.
