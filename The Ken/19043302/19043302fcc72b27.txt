The Big Story

Tiger Global’s downturn hits Bain India. One in every five consultants loses job https://the-ken.com/story/tiger-globals-downturn-hits-bain-india-one-in-every-five-consultants-loses-job/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

In early June, the smallest of the Big Three cut 15–20% of consulting jobs, as a crisis boiling since 2022 spilled over

Bain India went on a hiring spree in 2022 owing to expected demand. But once the business hit a slump, many of them were benched. Then, Tiger Global and its portfolio companies, which comprised a chunk of Bain’s business, had to cut down on business in 2022. Earlier in June 2024, an overhired Bain & Company’s India office laid off 150–200 people from its consulting wing. Now that Bain has “cut the excess fat”, it can afford to switch up its strategy a little. But market conditions are still bad, and it faces stiff competition. [Read the full story here https://the-ken.com/story/tiger-globals-downturn-hits-bain-india-one-in-every-five-consultants-loses-job/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card]

Inside Sugar.fit’s science-defying diabetes-reversal plan https://the-ken.com/story/inside-sugar-fits-science-defying-diabetes-reversal-plan/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

The three-year-old Cult.fit-backed startup has lured 25,000+ users for a programme that’s more about diabetes management and nothing about what it claims

Sugar.fit, a product of Cult.fit’s dream to capitalise on over 100 million diabetic Indians, was launched in 2021. Also backed by investors like Massmutual Ventures, B Capital, and Endiya Partners, Sugar.fit claims “100% reversal” of diabetes. It has managed to lure over 25,000 people with diabetes with its “reversal” claims on website, social media, and webinars. Now, users are left behind with an undelivered promise of freedom from diabetes and medications. How real is Reliance-owned Tira’s threat to Nykaa? https://the-ken.com/story/how-real-is-reliance-owned-tiras-threat-to-nykaa/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

Reliance Industries’s right of way can’t always be taken for granted

Tira has spent the last one year opening up its 12 retail stores and keeping a close eye on rival Nykaa. Unlike its 12-year-old competitor, the Reliance-owned platform started its journey in omnichannel mode. But apart from a few popular exclusive brands and its AI-led interactive in-store tools, it hasn’t created much of a buzz. Now that the platform enters its second year, can it sustain itself with only a frugal pricing mindset and more premium brands?

How Citicore created a solar powerhouse in the Philippines with a radical move https://the-ken.com/story/how-citicore-created-a-solar-powerhouse-in-the-philippines-with-a-radical-move/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

Citicore’s IPO marks the final piece in its innovative plan to recycle capital through REITs, ensuring funds flow into ongoing projects

Philippine renewable energy leader Citicore staged the biggest domestic renewable IPO in early June. Unlike conglomerate-backed rivals, Citicore leverages a strategic REIT model for funding, using IPO proceeds to fuel expansion. This involves selling solar farms to a REIT arm and leasing them back to the parent, creating a capital-recycling engine. While promising, Citicore’s long-term success relies on effectively managing inherent risks associated with the model. [Read the full story here https://the-ken.com/story/how-citicore-created-a-solar-powerhouse-in-the-philippines-with-a-radical-move/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card]

Three-year-old Shivalik Small Finance Bank is everything a fintech isn’t

Lightspeed Venture Partners, Sorin Investments invested US$11.5 million in Shivalik Small Finance Bank in May this year. This deal comes at a time when fintech funding has fallen from the highs of 2022. The investment is a sign of venture capitalists’ priorities, and it is also the easiest of the bets to make. In making this investment, venture capitalists have had a few adjustments to make. Hyundai India’s IPO pays its Korean parent way more than meets the eye https://the-ken.com/story/hyundai-indias-ipo-pays-way-more-to-its-korean-parent-than-meets-the-eye/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

India’s second largest carmaker has set the ball rolling for what could be the country’s largest public share sale. Its Korean parent is set to take home a tidy sum, and much more

South Korean auto giant Hyundai Motor Company plans to sell more than a sixth of its 100% stake in Hyundai India’s public issue. The IPO is set to be India’s largest so far. But curiously, its expected asking price is far lower than peers like Maruti Suzuki and M&M. Two major pre-IPO moves—on dividends and royalty—more than compensate for the seemingly subdued valuation in the public issue. The South Korean parent stands to win big—and not just monetarily. The Indian arm’s listing could help it break an old Korean jinx.
