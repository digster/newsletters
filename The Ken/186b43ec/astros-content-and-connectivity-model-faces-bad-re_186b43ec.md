Picture this: You're living in a Southeast Asian country with a family of four, and suddenly inflation hits, recession threatens cash flow, and your budget is squeezed. You realise it’s time to tighten the belt, but where do you start?
Food, energy, or entertainment—which area would you afford to cut back on?
My answer would be entertainment. And interestingly, it seems to be a popular choice for many households in Malaysia too.
As Malaysian consumers rebalance their spending habits due to a potential recession and increased cost of living, Astro—the leading television service provider in the country—has been getting a thinner slice of the pie in Malaysia’s household budgets. Its latest numbers show a 9% decline in revenue and an 18% fall in operating profit.
Its share price has also dropped 40% over the past 12 months on Malaysia’s Bursa exchange.
Astro is not taking this sitting down. It has revamped its content-centric business by teaming up with over-the-top media services such as Netflix and HBO Go, as well as producing original content.
But it just isn’t enough.
