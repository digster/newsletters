I’m sure you’ll have a lot to say about my colleague Aakriti’s story, too. It’s about a fascinating company. One that was often dismissed as a flash in the pan in its early days, and for two reasons: it was founded by two 19-year-olds, and they believed there was a huge market in delivering groceries in 10 minutes, which seemed laughable then.
But now, quick-commerce is on a tear and Zepto just hoovered up US$665 million, 2024’s largest fundraise in India, and got valued at US$3.6 billion. It’s reportedly in talks to raise more, which will give it a valuation of US$5 billion.
Months ago, even before there were any signs of a fundraise, any analyst or fund manager I talked to about the rise of Zomato-owned Blinkit would wax eloquent about Zepto’s “execution chops”.
Zepto was considerably smaller than Blinkit and was even behind Swiggy Instamart. But the attention Zepto got was clearly disproportionate to its size. If you want to know how well Zepto has piggybacked on Blinkit’s rise, you have to read Aakriti’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/zepto-is-a-quick-commerce-bronze-medallist-acting-like-a-gold-medallist/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
