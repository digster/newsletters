Good Morning Ishan,

At some point, you or someone you know might have faced the decision of choosing between IELTS and TOEFL for a Master’s programme abroad. Friends or education consultants probably nudged you or them towards the former English proficiency test with reasons like “It’s easier,” “Been there, aced that,” or “It’s universally accepted”.

Sure, a few might have thrown TOEFL into the mix, especially to those from the big cities. But the US-based non-profit that conducts this test is done with this second-fiddle treatment, says Alifiya in today’s story on a market that recently became interesting.

TOEFL, having managed to pocket a measly 5% of the market while IELTS has 70%, is making a move for the crown.

But there are reasons why IELTS has been the top dog. For one, its earlier connection with the British Council. Then, there is the massive network of trainers, agents, and edtechs that it built. And all this while, TOEFL was “sleeping or didn’t care,” as a study-abroad startup founder put it.

Now, though, TOEFL has caught wind of some chinks in IELTS’s armour; and suddenly, the game is on. But TOEFL’s not alone in the ring: the likes of Pearson and Duolingo are throwing punches, too, aiming to knock IELTS off its pedestal.

Read Alifiya’s fascinating story on TOEFL’s suddenly big ambitions, its stroke of luck, and how amid this showdown, students with overseas dreams might just be the real winners.

“Her” minus her

We need to talk about OpenAI’s little dance with Scarlett Johansson and her iconic voice from Her—a movie where a man falls in love with his computer. Now, if you are like me and you have watched it more times than you can count, you will know just how mesmerising Johansson’s voice as an AI assistant is.

Turns out, OpenAI had set out to bring that same magic to GPT-4o. There was only one problem: Johansson wasn’t exactly thrilled about it. She put her foot down and “declined the offer”.

But did that stop OpenAI? Nope. It went ahead with Sky—a system that “sounded just like” her (I know!!). While the company’s tech demo of the intelligent chatbot blew the minds of many last week, it soon raised all sorts of eyebrows in the process. I mean, imagine trying to replicate Samantha without Johansson’s blessing.

After some legal back-and-forth and a fair bit of drama, OpenAI finally pulled the voice from the ChatGPT lineup.

Johansson’s stand serves as a reminder that real people still matter, and even in tech and entertainment, there is no substitute for respect and consent. And maybe, just maybe, this whole ordeal will inspire a sequel to Her where Johansson’s…err Samantha’s voice finds the closure it so deserves.
