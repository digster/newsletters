Good Morning Ishan,
It's a brand new week, one filled with hope and possibilities! Or trepidation if you're in Bengaluru, because the forecast says its going to rain most days. The city's roads have mostly thrown up their hands and given up after one of the wettest Septembers in decades.
Meanwhile there's foreboding clouds elsewhere too. Price control is the cloud that's gradually covering more and more of the Indian healthcare system. From medicines to knee implants to condoms to cornonary stents, the price control shadow is falling over more and more categories.
With disastrous effect, as is to be expected.
8 months ago the government capped the prices of coronary stents - medical implants that help unclog blocked arteries. The ostensible goal was to help patients. But in reality, the opposite has happened.
Hospitals have increased prices of angioplasty procedures (through which stents are normally implanted) by up to 45%. They're renegotiating for higher rates with insurers, which in turn will inevitably push up insurance prices. Big manufacturers like Abbott have already withdrawn their more advanced stents from the Indian market, to avoid having to sell them at prices too low.
It's like the Law of Unintended Consequences ran into Free Markets at a cafe, and decided to play dominos while sipping lattes. Patients, manufacturers, hospitals, insurers...I don't think there's a single category who's been a net beneficiary in this melee.
