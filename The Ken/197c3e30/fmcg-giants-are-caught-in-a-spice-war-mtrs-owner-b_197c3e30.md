Good Morning Ishan,
Take a moment to think about your spice rack. Its contents are likely influenced by the food you ate every day with your family, reflecting where you’re from and the many memories of being at the dinner table with them. Maybe your mother or grandmother were strict about using spices from a specific brand—if you were sent out to pick up a bag but bought the wrong one, then you’d have to contend with strong words of disapproval.
But your spice rack is yours, and there are now more choices than ever before. Whereas spices was a category gleaned over by India’s packaged-food giants for years, they’ve gradually entered the space, enticed by healthy margins. ITC, Tata Consumer Products, Dabur, Wipro Consumer Care have all snapped up spice brands, expecting their products to stock pantries across India.
Today’s story covers Norwegian conglomerate Orkla’s imprint in spices. It was ahead of India’s own FMCG companies, catching onto the fact that spices offer some of the highest margins in food. Orkla bought MTR Foods in 2007, then Eastern Condiments in 2021. Its Indian unit filed for an IPO on 10 June, indicating that over 60% of its revenue flows from spices.
Selling spices requires localisation. What works in the south might not in the north. Orkla knows this better than most. Pranathi explains in [today’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/fmcg-giants-are-caught-in-a-spice-war-mtrs-owner-beat-them-to-it-years-ago/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
