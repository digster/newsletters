Act 1: Circa 2020, it’s been half a decade since Swiggy and Zomato changed the meaning of ordering food. And eateries have gone from viewing the two foodtech companies as a welcome change to their business, to grudgingly handing over the 20%-commission they charged.

Then Covid pummels restaurants into an existential crisis. They decide it’s about time they changed the balance of power between them and the food-delivery giants.

Act 2: It’s 2021, and the National Restaurant Association of India launches its #OrderDirect drive to get consumers to skip Zomato and Swiggy and order from restaurants’ websites.

Helping them in a fight few think they can win are startups like Thrive, Dotpe, and Peppo. These tiny companies provide the tech backbone to #OrderDirect, which gains momentum, or at least seems to. Dotpe raises over US$80 million in two rounds and Thrive attracts Jubilant Foodworks (the Domino’s franchisee) and Coca-Cola as investors.

Act 3: 2023 is about to draw to a close, and more than two years after going public as a loss-making company, Zomato now turns a profit. But what about the much-publicised campaign waged by restaurants to find an alternative? Is there a victor?
