Good Morning Ishan,
If you’re on top of skincare trends, chances are you’ve encountered actives. Maybe you’ve even layered them into your skincare routine.
One relatively young brand that has established itself in this space is Minimalist. Helmed by brothers Mohit and Rahul Yadav, the company has managed to stand out from upstarts and legacy brands, making great headway in just four years. In fact, Mohit told The Ken that he expects the brand to rack up Rs 360 crore, or US$43 million, in revenue for 2024, doubling 2023’s figure.
If Minimalist stays on track, it isn’t out of bounds to imagine the company as India’s next Hindustan Unilever or L’Oréal.
For today’s story, Nuha did a deep dive to figure out what makes Minimalist tick, how it gets its edge, and where it’s heading. More than that, the article is a crash course in how skincare brands function, including what happens when a new formulation is developed, and how a product lineup fits together. Check out [her report](https://the-ken.com/story/four-year-old-minimalist-does-for-skincare-what-loreal-couldnt/).
Last week, Y Combinator held its Winter 2024 Demo Day. Artificial intelligence was in the spotlight—no surprise there. More startups are developing platforms that use AI to make videos, debug code, and compose music—all the standard stuff.
But as the sage says…
It’s the plumbing that I found more interesting. For instance, it isn’t a simple matter for businesses to deploy active learners (or any kind of machine learning), even though there’s an intensifying need in some fields to do so. A major issue is data, specifically the matter of ensuring the right data goes to the right entities, and that sensitive data stays out of the wrong hands.
Startups that are solving problems like this will likely do very well in the coming years, as long as their tech actually works and they’re able to keep up with demand. It’s an exciting time for the engineers who are dreaming up solutions that make new tools easier and safer to use.
