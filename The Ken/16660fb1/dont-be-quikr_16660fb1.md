I’m back. Sooner than I would have hoped for, but I guess this is true love. It makes every day worth it. In this case, the writing. To you.
Now, I’m not going to mince words, but some companies perplex me. And not so much in terms of understanding what they do but why. Why do they do what they do? Why do they acquire companies? Why do they raise more venture capital, again and again and again? To what end. Revenue? Profit? Perhaps, even losses? Let’s give the garbage can its due. Maybe gross merchandise value. Something. Something that can be called outcome. And if we have the chance to meet for coffee sometime, I will rattle off this long list of such companies and we can spend hours. But today, let’s just focus on one. A company called Quikr.
10 years in business. 13 acquisitions. $430 million raised in venture capital. Its last known operating revenue. Rs 109 crore. As of March 2017.
Losses: Rs 301 crore.
For a moment let’s just put the loss figure aside. After all, what good is an internet business in India in the year 2018 if it isn’t making losses? Nobody wants to be the subject of sarcastic, profitable scoffs. Not in the silicon valley of India; a place called Bengaluru. But, well, Quikr. In just the last three years, in what should be read as strategy, the company has acquired five companies in the real estate business. Paid for in stock and cash. Spending close to $300 million. You’d almost believe that by acquiring these companies, from CommonFloor to HDFC Realty, Quikr is building a world-class, industry-beating, real estate vertical.
The company would have you believe as much. Quikr has claimed that its real estate vertical is doing swimmingly well. So much so that it is profitable. Standalone.
That’s where today’s story from my colleague Salman comes in. And he says, hold on a second. What’s going on here? At the time, Quikr acquired CommonFloor, in 2015, it was doing Rs 44 crore in revenue. And then just two years later, that number halved. So how is it that the real estate vertical is doing well? How is it profitable?
I understand that in the last couple of years Quikr’s classifieds business has been shrinking. It is the reason why the company has been buying companies and selling its vertical strategy. What bothers me is that its biggest vertical, real estate, is also displaying signs of slowing down, contrary to the company's claims. And now, Quikr is out to raise more money.
Think about this and enjoy your day.
