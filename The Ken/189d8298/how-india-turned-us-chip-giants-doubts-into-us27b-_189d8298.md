Good Morning Ishan,
Convincing Micron to commit to a memory chip fab in India was no less than courting a headstrong partner for marriage.
That’s how a government official described the frenetic months-long effort to get the American chipmaker to build a plant in Sanand, Gujarat. If all goes as per plans, India will roll out its first locally assembled, tested, marked, and packaged commercial-grade chip by 26 January 2024.
As you read this, Micron is shipping its first set of equipment to India, including a prefabricated plant for assembling and testing imported wafers. Concurrently, construction work for the first phase of the facility has begun.
This is Godspeed for the Indian Semiconductor Mission (ISM), which was either gathering dust or bad proposals ever since it made the US$10 billion incentive-led scheme public in December 2021.
As Pratap reports today, it took around five intense virtual meetings between top MeitY officials and Micron CEO Sanjay Mehrotra, three international trips, several hundred phone calls, and a critical policy adjustment.
These are rare concessions, never before made for any foreign company.
While businesses typically align their proposals to government norms for incentives, Micron flipped the script. The company intends to convert its first-mover disadvantage into an advantage. And why not? The deal begins to solve a two-decade chicken-and-egg conundrum India has faced with building a chip industry.
Indian officials have also rejected Vedanta’s application to set up a display fab, The Ken has learnt. The proposal of ~US$4 billion—bigger than the Micron deal—failed on all major grounds.
Which is why, despite 70% of the capex being covered by the public exchequer, Micron’s move to India, which lacks a supply chain, is seen as a calibrated risk. Indian agencies, meanwhile, are hoping Micron will help assemble an ecosystem and get others to invest in India.
It might take a while for all the ISM chips to stack up, if at all they do. But when they do, the Micron deal will go down as one where India stooped to conquer.
