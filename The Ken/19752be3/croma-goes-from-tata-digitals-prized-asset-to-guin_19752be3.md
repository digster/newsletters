Good Morning Ishan,
Elephants can’t dance. But maybe a certain elephant can?
Those were the thoughts, in general, when the Tatas birthed Tata Digital five years ago to unify the group’s digital-first consumer businesses under one roof. The elephant made some moves, some painful, some elegant, but as several reports have indicated, it’s still learning to dance.
In one of the latest gyrations, the digital marketing team of Croma—the group’s flagship asset contributing more than half of its gross merchandise value in 2024—was told to work from under Tata Digital in March. On just a two-day notice.
The idea is to streamline digital marketing across all brands, including the likes of 1mg, Cliq, Bigbasket, and Neu. Logical? Yes, but the last few years of experimentation have left Croma as a bruised lab rat for Tata Digital. After hitting profitability in FY19, the retailer reported Rs 987 crore in losses in FY24, even as its revenue crossed Rs 18,000 crore.
Now, two months into the digital-marketing transition, Tata Digital’s chief executive Naveen Tahilyani stepped down in May, leaving employees wondering who’d steer the ship.
Some believe this is like passing the parcel—Croma’s CEO is doing what Tata Digital wants him to do; the latter is doing what Tata Sons wants it to do. “They’ve bled Croma’s business, but have created nothing of value in this chaos,” said a former top management member.
Croma is big enough of a brand to withstand a few bad kicks from the dancing elephant. Debanjali’s excellently reported story today captures some steps and missteps.
