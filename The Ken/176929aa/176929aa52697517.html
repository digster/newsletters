Good Morning Ishan,
 
What happens to the grooming and wellness industry during a pandemic? It's rhetorical; we already know what happened.
 
Okay, so what happens to the software that supports the grooming and wellness industry during a pandemic? Uh uh uh, not so easy.
 
While you were trusting your partners, children, and pets with barely-Zoom-friendly haircuts at home, Zenoti, a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) startup that only focuses on aiding salons and spas… became a unicorn.
 
Just last week, the company raised $160 million in its latest funding round and became India's fifth SaaS unicorn.
 
That it took off during the pandemic year is not the lead though. Zenoti is the only vertical SaaS company—which caters to a singular industry—to have joined the unicorn club in India. The rest in the space ALL tanked. Zenoti helps salons and spas manage bookings, sales, expenses, and marketing. It's currently servicing over 1,000 brands and has a footprint of 12,000 stores across the world.
 
Across the world. The US market accounts for 60% of Zenoti’s business, with the UK contributing around 20%. Zenoti went international at just the right time. Because guess what happens when lockdowns end? People, tired of the pandemic life, make spa bookings.
 
And guess who provided software for all these digital wellness needs?
 
Sumanth's story today not only dissects Zenoti's right-place-right-time stint but also goes deep into what it took for the company to capture these markets.
