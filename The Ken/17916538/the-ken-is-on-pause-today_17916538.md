We have no story for you today. Instead, we have a sombre appeal.
India is going through an unimaginably hard time as the second wave of the coronavirus pandemic ravages through our cities, towns, and villages. The speed and scale of transmission has overwhelmed every resource we could turn to, from medicines to hospital beds to oxygen cylinders.
During other natural catastrophes, citizens and civil society organisations have actively helped provide support for those affected. But these are not normal times. With the virus still raging through India, it is advisable for most people to stay indoors to protect themselves and their families and prevent further spread of the virus.
In these trying times, the best we can do is channel our support to those organisations that are already doing the courageous and selfless job of helping others, even at risk to themselves.
Please consider donating to these organisations. That is the best we can do right now.
Also, please get yourselves and your families vaccinated whenever the opportunity opens up in your community. And do think about the people who work directly or indirectly for us—security guards, household helpers, cooks, drivers etc. Think of their families. Given that vaccines are likely to be in short supply for the near future, government-subsidised vaccines might take even longer to cover India’s population. If you have the opportunity, please consider paying for the private hospital vaccinations of at least a few other families.
Lastly, please stay safe and vigilant. The peak is yet to come and things are likely to get worse over the next few weeks before they get better. But they will get better.
