I hope you will grant me some leeway, I’m still recovering from the after effect of the 2G spectrum scam news in India. All the high and mighty convicted have been given a clean chit. Which brings me to Arundhati’s story today on the Goods & Services Tax (GST). Man, what a rollercoaster that has been. I mean, if there’s a word for being in a state of flux, then you should just say GST and get the other guy to shut up:
 
Him: I think I am in a state of flux
 
You: You mean, GST
 
Him: …
 
So yeah, there’s something new at GST. And I don’t mean this in a bad way. I think this is a fine kind of new. On 8 December, the GST Network, which runs the complicated GST system, put out a tender. In it, the Network has asked companies to bid for an accounting and billing software for small businesses. It seems, many small businesses complained to the Network about accounting issues. So, the Network has decided to put out an official software of its own, of course built and owned by a company, but endorsed by the government. If you are a small business struggling to file GST, this is absolutely great news.
 
But if you are a company which was hoping to make green from these same businesses selling them software, you just got punched in the gut. So, I’m not surprised with this quote from the story: “Software makers were designing their products to generate GSTR2 after matching of invoices, but by making that feature available in the free software, GSTN is diluting the need for software makers and also destroying their revenue potential,” says a senior executive of a Mumbai-based GST suvidha provider.
