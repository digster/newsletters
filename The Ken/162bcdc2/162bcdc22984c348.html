Nobody starts out to fail.

Thanks to the privilege of what I do, I’ve had the opportunity to meet hundreds of entrepreneurs over the years and listen to their stories. The sobering reality is, some get where they wanted, many don’t. Some like to talk about what happened, many don’t. They just shut out. Shut you out.

It is in this context that today’s story needs telling. And again, just my luck, that I have my colleague Sumanth telling it.

This is the story of MonkeyBox.

MonkeyBox is a food-tech startup founded in 2015. The startup delivered healthy vegetarian meals to thousands of school children in Bengaluru, directly delivering meal-boxes to the schools during their breakfast/lunch/snack time. It had everything going for it.

MonkeyBox served a large market that had a real need for such a product. Bengaluru alone has over 250,000 schoolchildren who could benefit from a convenient service like this that frees up parents from the hassles of wondering what food to pack for their children and worrying if the food was nutritious and tasty. If you extend the market footprint to other major cities in India where double-income parents and nuclear families are common, the potential market is easily in the millions.

The company seemed to have achieved good traction: 200,000 meals served till date to 4,500 subscribers across 120 schools serviced by 250 people working across five kitchens.

And then, on 22nd March, MonkeyBox shut shop.

Whatever happened?

That’s our story for today. It is a story with several learnings.
