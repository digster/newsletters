Good Morning Ishan,

I wonder if any of you noticed that last week's weekend newsletter never really reached you. No? This Holi bhaang business, I tell you. Annnyhow, since this week's been all about abstinence (just because), let's get past the festive and on to the focal.

We've had a fantastic week, to be honest. Our stories ranged from banks' process to media distress to data access to...a rhyme excess? Sorry, I tend to digress.

Monday saw Rohin's piece https://the-ken.com/story/banks-startups-complicated/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter banks and startups and how complicated things are for the duo. Startups say banks are okay lending hundreds of millions of dollars to iffy diamond dealers and dodgy traders, but not to them. Banks say they need to safeguard depositor money. Will the twain meet?

On Tuesday, Patanjali peered into https://the-ken.com/story/just-buy-live-collapses/ the?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter B2B e-commerce space. The lens he used? Just Buy Live—a B2B e-comm leader in a bad place. "A ghost funding round, Rs 140 crore burnt in two years and a sector that remains difficult to crack. All these, together, ravage the company," the story reads.

Midweek, we had a big media story. Shashi and Venkat https://the-ken.com/story/alls-not-well-indias-viceland/ wrote?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter about Vice and its straining relationship with the Times in India. Less than a month to launch; will it make it?

On Thursday, Sid, Arundhati and Shashi https://the-ken.com/story/data-breach-indian-companies/ put?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter their heads together to figure out just why Indian companies are so relaxed about data breaches. Thanks to Indian laws, apparently, companies needn’t bother about your details leaking out. What? Yeah. More in the copy. Read it; it's FREE.

The weekend ended with a Sumanth-Pat collab https://the-ken.com/story/tapzo-devoured-itself/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter Tapzo, an "all-in-one" mobile app company that seems to love its pivots. But it seems it might have finally run its course.

That's all. See you next week!
