Good Morning Ishan,

All of us glance.

At shoes, on a person on the street. Their presence or the lack of them. On a date, at the choice of beverage sitting opposite. At the unmistakable emptiness of a newspaper & magazine stall; gleaming covers promising a world of knowledge and escape, in passions long subdued. At people whose faces you remember, but their names you cannot. Stuck in bumper-to-bumper traffic, at people hawking a bundle of A-1 garbage bags hoping to make ends meet. At story headlines.

In 2019, more often than not, we glance at our mobile phones.

And if you believe Naveen Tewari of InMobi, the Bengaluru-based mobile advertising company, he says the time you spend doing that is worth a fortune for his company. Think millions of users. Also think millions of dollars in monetisation of time spent.

In its simplest form, Glance is an Android service. No, it is not an App. It sits over the lockscreen of your mobile phone and pushes content to your screen. No need to unlock. Glance. Swipe. Glance. Swipe. Glance. Swipe. Content that ranges from news to videos of trailers, sports updates, just about anything that can be rendered as a card which can pique your interest. In the two years that Glance has been around, it has clocked massive numbers. “In February, we crossed 26 million daily active users. And by the end of 2019, we will be somewhere between 75-100 million,” says Tewari.

Numbers in isolation are nonsense. They make sense only when compared. Tik Tok, the app by Chinese Internet company ByteDance which has taken India by storm, has daily active users of only 22 million. This means that Glance is one of India’s largest content service companies. Of course, the company isn’t stopping at that. InMobi wants to grow Glance from news curation to short videos. Fifteen seconds, at most.

By now, you should have questions. How did InMobi get here? Why did InMobi do this? What does this product mean for the company? How is InMobi planning to monetise these users?

Everything is in today’s story by my colleague Rohin. The idea. The strategy. The ingenious execution part. And InMobi’s plans going forward.
