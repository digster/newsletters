Good Morning Ishan,
Today we are celebrating Eid in India. So, Eid Mubarak everybody. Eid is also a public holiday in India, so there will be no story from The Ken today.
I don't know if you know this, but I grew up in the Gulf, and Eid was quite a big deal. Back home, Eid was seen by many of my friends as an opportunity to reflect, grow and come back stronger. This is a fantastic lesson for us at The Ken—we have to remind ourselves that it's okay to take the occasional break to reflect, even if it's for a day. We almost never do. In fact, we did it just once this year. Even on Republic Day, some of our reporters and copy-editors stretched and cancelled their long weekend plans, all because we felt that a story deserved to be published that day.
My colleagues are amazing like that. Ask anybody at The Ken, and they will tell you that writing a story here is very hard work. Especially since we publish just one article a day. There's nothing else to distract our reader. No horoscopes. No crosswords. No photos of Taimur Khan. One lesson that all of us have learnt here is that it's much harder to craft a single story that we are proud of every day than it is to publish five pieces every hour that we are indifferent about. As Rohin says: We always pick quality over quantity, depth over breadth, and substance over style.
So, breaks are good. A break lets us breathe. It lets us come back stronger. Even if it's just for a day. You should take a break too. Walk away. Pause. Reflect. Don't worry, you won't be alone—the English football team will be back home next week and doing this too.
Oh, and have some biryani. Because it's Eid, after all.
The same rules apply for biryani, by the way. Quality over quantity. Depth over breadth. Substance over style.
We will be back on Monday. Our line-up of stories for next week looks incredible. I can't wait for you to read them.
Have a great weekend.
Regards,
