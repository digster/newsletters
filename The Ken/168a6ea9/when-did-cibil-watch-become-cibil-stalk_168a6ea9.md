Good Morning Ishan,
CIBIL is TransUnion CIBIL, India’s largest credit bureau. With a market share of about 90% and over a billion credit records on 555 million Indians, it is a behemoth. But like all behemoths, it too must occasionally deal with pesky and nimble competitors. Like rival Experian, which offers its credit reports at roughly half the price of what CIBIL charges.
Nobody likes pesky and nimble competitors, especially large banks and lenders. Because competitors lure away their customers with tailored products, better rates or sometimes just better communications. With the rise of online comparison and aggregation sites, lenders can’t even sit back and enjoy the fruits of their disbursed loans, because those pesky competitors lure them away with better rates for transferring their loans.
"Incumbents of the financial world unite, you have nothing to lose but your incumbency.”
Which wasn’t CIBIL Watch initially, but in the last two years, it has become one. A CIBIL service that sends an alert to a bank or lender the moment one of its existing customers applies for a loan elsewhere. It started out as a risk mitigation tool but is today a sales maximisation tool with crazy returns.
Close to 15% of business for one of India’s largest non-banking lenders is understood to originate from CIBIL Watch. The cost of acquiring a lead (ironically their own customers) is 25% of other digital channels, while conversions are 400-500% higher. It’s crack for financial services.
Arundhati’s story goes into the rise of CIBIL Watch as a powerful sales tool for banks and large lenders. But one whose existence is a symptom of the disintermediation that’s prevalent in the space.
