Good Morning Ishan,
My colleague Suprita reported today’s story by speaking with 17 founders—12 of whose companies had a collective valuation of $1.2 billion and created over 10,000 jobs—and eight venture capitalists and executive-search firms.
He set out to answer a simple question: what does it take the founder of an abortive startup to find a job?
To a recruiter, even a failed entrepreneur should ideally score over someone who has never had to hustle to raise funds, find customers, and drive a hard bargain with vendors—all while keeping her team inspired.
A 2023 study, co-authored by researchers from Harvard University, backs up this notion. It found that unsuccessful founders land jobs with three more years of seniority than comparable non-founders.
I’m sure the founders in our story would beg to differ. “Corporates do not really know what to do with failed founders. While building [a venture], you are seen as a hero. After failure, you are either invisible or treated like a scammer,” said one such entrepreneur.
In 2025 alone, 1,800 Indian startups have shut down so far, or 4X the new ventures in the same period. Not everyone has the resolve to build again. But they still need resolve in spades in their job search.
