Good Morning Ishan,

Till a few months ago, India should not have mattered to Huawei. The country contributed a teensy-weensy 1.2% of the Chinese telecom behemoth’s $109 billion revenue.

Till December, a month before the beginning of the Chinese Year of the Pig, everything went to the, well, dogs. Huawei’s CFO and its founder’s daughter, Meng Wanzhou, was arrested in Canada based on a request from US law enforcement. As the high-stakes jostling between China, US and Canada over her trial and possible deportation ensued, Huawei found itself caught in a perfect storm of suspicion. Like dominoes, country after country aligned to the US decided to either put on hold or shut it out of 5G build-outs. US, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, Taiwan, UK, Poland, Germany, Italy. 

Which is why Huawei probably sees India in a new light now. With over 1.1 billion mobile subscribers, it is the world’s second largest telecom market. And it imports over 90% of its telecom equipment. And its three leading (or still standing) telcos are yet to make up their minds on 5G deployments.

A cumulative spend of over $100 billion over 5-7 years.

Which is why Seema likens India to the Middle Kingdom for 5G. On one side lie countries aligned to the US, increasingly closed to Huawei. On the other lies China.

The one player that could decisively shift the battle in Huawei’s favour, or away from it, is Reliance Jio. But while it is keeping its cards close to its chest, Seema’s reporting doesn’t look good for Huawei. The key vendors Jio has chosen thus far for its large 4G equipment deployments were Huawei’s rivals - Samsung and Cisco. Which way will Jio swing on 5G?
