Political risk in stocks is a very real thing. The perceived setback on 4 June could weigh heavily, especially for ‘political’ stocks

Good Morning Ishan,

What a week it’s been so far for the Indian stock market! Up ~2,500 points on Monday, down ~4,400 points on Tuesday, up again ~2,300 points on Wednesday—the bellwether BSE Sensex has been on a roller-coaster of euphoria, despair, hope. All of it driven by the exit polls (which predicted a sweep for the BJP-led NDA, only to go badly wrong) and the results of the great Indian general election of 2024 (which threw up many surprises, including a resurgent Congress-led INDIA alliance). 

The show is hardly over. In the coming days, expect more wild swings as the pulls and pushes of coalition-government formation, their outcomes, and their implications become clearer. For now, what is evident is that political risk in the stock market is a real thing, never to be taken lightly. And I am not just talking about companies and stocks considered close to the powers-that-be (we’ll come to that a bit later). 

Some stranger things first. 

This general election saw what was perhaps a first in the Indian stock market—leading lights from the government fanning market euphoria during the election campaign and in the run-up to counting day on 4 June. No less than Prime Minister Narendra Modi https://www.ndtv.com/video/pm-modi-exclusive-on-june-4-the-stock-market-programmers-will-786092, Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman https://www.livemint.com/market/stock-market-news/nirmala-sitharaman-predicts-solid-bulls-market-after-june-4-lok-sabha-poll-result-cites-s-ps-rating-upgrade-report-11717066551001.html, Home Minister Amit Shah https://www.businesstoday.in/markets/market-perspective/story/buy-before-june-4-amit-shah-says-buy-stocks-what-share-market-analysts-say-429328-2024-05-13, and Foreign Affairs Minister S Jaishankar https://www.moneycontrol.com/elections/lok-sabha-election/bharat-stocks-will-go-up-on-june-4-in-world-market-says-jaishankar-article-12731417.html publicly predicted a big bull run in the Indian stock market, on 4 June and after, on the premise that the NDA will secure a thumping majority. In the event, they were proven wrong on both counts—there was no cakewalk, and the stock market plunged on 4 June.
