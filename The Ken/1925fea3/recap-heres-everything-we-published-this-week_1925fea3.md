The “education for life” champion is not just cashing in on India’s entrepreneurship boom; it’s also bowing to pressure to showcase job placements
As Ashoka University steps into its second decade, it seeks to expand the liberal-arts approach to entrepreneurship studies. With several students opting for the entrepreneurship course, Ashoka wants to upgrade it from a “minor” to a “major” subject soon. High demand for the course comes from the university’s moves to let industry professionals teach and hire, and give more options for students to choose from. In terms of designations, the top three career choices from Ashoka are on par with other B-schools. With new-age international schools popping up in cities, age-old Indian boarding schools need to buck up to stay in the race
For several decades, legacy boarding schools like The Doon School or Mayo College remained the choice destination for India’s elite. Steeped in heritage, these schools offered not just all-round development but an intangible benefit—the power of networking. As international schools increase exponentially in metros and more foreign boarding schools set up outposts in India, elite families are now spoilt for choice. And that’s why a lot is changing at legacy boarding schools—from curriculum to crowd—as they fight to stay relevant. [Sarvam raised $54M to build AI models. Then the models ran into multilingual India](https://the-ken.com/story/sarvam-raised-54m-to-build-ai-models-then-the-models-ran-into-multilingual-india/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
The one-year-old company released a voice model in August, with a text model in development. But there’s a difference between its services—which customers love—and its models—which developers don’t
In August 2024, generative AI startup Sarvam released a suite of new multilingual products—AI agents, voice and text models, and a workbench aimed at legal professionals. Enterprise customers who used Sarvam’s services have found them up to the tasks they deployed them for. But developers have flagged issues with its voice-based models. The text model, as yet unreleased, is primarily trained on synthetic data which could lead to nonsensical answers if left untested. With the hype surrounding Sarvam and an increased competition in the space, the startup may be time pressed to solve its product issues. Pharma software firms like Evitalrx and Marg ERP, and their catalogues of over 400,000 products, are the key to relieving the country of its overcharging problem
Drug pricing varies greatly, with a huge number of SKUs and products varying across pin codes. There is no centralised database for the government and private insurers to check drug pricing for their coverage. Inflated drug bills are rampant. Pharma software companies are now becoming NHA pharmacy partners, opening doors to insights on over 150,000 offline pharmacies and the products they stock. Being an NHA partner-affiliated pharmacy can tighten scrutiny and open revenue streams for them, yet a lot depends on which drugs are being prescribed.
