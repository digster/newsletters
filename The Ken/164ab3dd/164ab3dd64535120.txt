Good Morning Ishan,

The 1995 movie The Usual Suspects was a cult classic. Its poster, even more so. It featured a hodgepodge police lineup of 5 suspects—short Kevin Pollak (“Hockney”), bearded and deadpan Stephen Baldwin (“McManus”), shifty Benicio del Toro (“Fenster”), sullen Gabriel Byrne (“Keaton”) and innocent-looking Kevin Spacey (“Verbal”).

India's organised furniture market lineup is as brilliant.

There’s one of the largest, Nilkamal, but it only makes plastic furniture. There’s one of the oldest, Godrej, so slow that it went offline on e-commerce sites for four full months because they hadn’t figured out how to implement India’s new unified tax, GST. There’s also Spacewood, the recipient, it claims, of the first foreign investment in an Indian furniture company. And Home Center, a brand with 35 stores that has steadfastly refused to sell through online marketplaces.

Then there are online sites like Urban Ladder and Pepper Fry, which we talked about last week.

And IKEA, which is about to launch operations in a few weeks.

Praveen is back with Part 2 of his story about this motley bunch. He lays out an interesting theory on Indian furniture sales, connecting disparate 

events, companies and consumer behaviour trends. Leaving us with an intriguing possibility at the end of it all. The one where everybody wins.
