Good Morning Ishan,
 
If last April was about Jio Platforms sweeping the investor world off its feet with a $21 billion fundraise, this April is about rival Bharti Airtel flexing its digital arms.
 
In the last few weeks, Bharti Airtel promoter Sunil Mittal has uncharacteristically spoken about the group’s plans to unlock value in its multiple business streams. Airtel Digital will be hived off and a step-up company will house the group's telecom and digital businesses.
 
The Ken has learnt that the restructuring plans also extend to its lucrative enterprise arm, Airtel Business, which could be spun out as well. The group is fully aware of the potential at hand—last year, it sold a 25% stake in its Nxtra data centre business valuing it, post-money, at $1.2 billion. (Earlier this month, Mastercard invested $100 million in Airtel Africa’s mobile commerce business.)
  
As it prepares its investor slide deck, Bharti is clearly inspired by Jio Platforms. Jio’s digital assets are legion, running the gamut from health and video conferencing to payments and cloud storage, along with the massive e-commerce play JioMart. Airtel Digital, on the other hand, has a smattering of digital assets, which Airtel has recently been integrating with its advertising platform.
 
Airtel Ads is the linchpin of the group’s digital makeover. The segment already contributes the bulk of Rs 100 crore in annualised earnings of Airtel Digital, which aims to garner Rs 1,000 crore in annual revenues in the near future. Net-net, Airtel Ads wants to be the fourth largest ad revenue business in India after Google, Facebook, and Amazon. A senior Bharti executive admitted that the group’s plan is to emulate Amazon’s adtech strategy and beat the likes of Indian adtech platforms like InMobi and Affle.
 
At a time when privacy and data regulations that apply to telcos and third-party ads are getting stricter, digital businesses with first party data will be super attractive in ads and e-commerce.
