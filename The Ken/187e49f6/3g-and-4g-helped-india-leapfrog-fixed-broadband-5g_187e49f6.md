If there is one verb that is routinely trotted out to explain India’s economic growth over the past two decades, it’s “leapfrog”.
- India leapfrogged into a services-driven economy past the mandatory stage of being a manufacturing-driven one
- India leapfrogged most of the world around digital payments and fintech, using the UPI infrastructure
- India leapfrogged vexing issues around identity using Aadhaar
- India leapfrogged fixed broadband by using 3G and 4G smartphones to drive wireless broadband access
I will not go into the merits of these claims (one email is woefully inadequate for that). Still, I will say that sometimes “leapfrogging” past a certain stage of technological evolution could mean you’ve only deferred a stage, not eliminated it.
For instance, fixed broadband. Sure, for a while, we can use the wireless broadband delivered by 3G or 4G smartphones as a sufficient-enough replacement for true fixed broadband. But as anyone with a good fibre broadband connection at home or office will vouch, the speed and responsiveness of those connections aren’t the same as what we get from our smartphones.
As the cliche goes, there are three certainties in life: death, taxes, and our need for faster broadband. And with less than 5% of overall broadband users being wired, I’m obviously leading to the conclusion that India needs to “un-leapfrog” and fix true broadband access.
Fortunately, two trends—one a crisis and the other an opportunity—are converging to make this happen. The crisis is that new smartphone sales have been slowing for a while. Research firm IDC said they were down 27% annually for the last quarter of 2022. Another research firm, Counterpoint Research, says they were down 19% for the first quarter of 2023. Telcos like Airtel and Jio need a way to sell more connections.
The opportunity is the launch of 5G networks, which now gives much greater bandwidth and flexibility to the likes of Airtel and Jio.
Enter 5G FWA or Fixed Wireless Access. Aka, the fixed broadband we leapfrogged over with wireless but can now un-leapfrog using wireless too.
The two global giants central to this un-leapfrogging are Qualcomm and Mediatek. They are counting on FWA for their growth as Indian telcos’ have set their sights on providing broadband access to 100 million households.
