Dear Ishan, good morning!

I have an unusual question for you. Let’s say you’re a quick-commerce company and have a $1 billion budget to put you in a winning position. What’s your move?

Two months ago, we asked some of India’s brightest and most creative minds at the country’s top business schools that question. They had to pick which quick-commerce company to roleplay as, and also define what “winning” meant for The Ken’s inaugural case competition, organised in partnership with Zerodha.

We received close to 100 submissions. Ten teams became finalists and presented their strategies to our judges. The champion went home with bragging rights and a Rs 10 lakh prize.

I’m thrilled to tell you about our latest limited-series podcast, One Billion in 10 Minutes, which is centred around our case competition’s finale. Over six episodes, you will hear the top 10 strategies—all rooted in the real world, and presented as game plans for five quick-commerce platforms that have changed the way we shop. 

My colleague and co-producer Nuha Bubere had the opportunity to chat with all 10 teams that made it to the final round. We found out about their varied interpretations of “winning” and why they chose their respective quick-commerce companies.

From leveraging a parent company’s network to becoming the AWS of rapid delivery, or from burning through the full billion-dollar war chest within a year to creating new AI-powered features, all 10 teams made hard choices to craft their strategies.

One Billion in 10 Minutes captures this blend from students hailing from India’s top business schools. In each episode, we present two teams going head-to-head, and we’ll tell you which one made it into the top five before we finally crown the winner.

Here are the six episodes: 

1. Bigbasket’s big break: The Tata edge vs. kirana tie-ups

2. Swiggy’s next offering: AI assistants vs. alcohol 

3. Blinkit’s delivery schedule: Five minutes vs. four hours

4. Zepto’s winning move: Burn $1 billion vs. acquire Pharmeasy

5. Flipkart’s new identity: Dark-store AWS vs. quick-commerce Mcdonald’s

6. Finale: The billion-dollar solution
