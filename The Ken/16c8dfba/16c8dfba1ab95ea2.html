Once spurned, twice resolved. That’s how you’d describe what GSK Velu has built in Neuberg. It came out of nowhere and became the fourth largest diagnostics chain in India in two years.
 
Okay, it did come out of entrepreneurial churn. Velu, who’s founded multiple companies, had to exit Metropolis in 2015 when his co-founder struck a deal with the PE investor Warburg Pincus, leaving Velu little choice but to sell his shares for Rs 900 crore.
 
Two years later, he founded Neuberg which is today nipping at the heels of his old company Metropolis. It’s a revenge story of the healthiest kind. Before the Metropolis saga, once I had asked him about serial entrepreneurship; how he was so prolific. He corrected me saying, “I am not a serial entrepreneur; I am a parallel entrepreneur because I haven’t exited any company.”
 
Then came the mother of all exits.
 
To put Neuberg’s growth in perspective, look at the age of the top three path labs: Dr Lal’s, 70; SRL, 24; Metropolis, 39.
 
“Being rated as possibly the second-largest startup in India just after Reliance Jio with Rs 450 crore in revenue in first year of operation and targeting Rs 1,000 crore-plus revenue within three to four years of operations…We could not have asked for anything better,” Velu told Suraksha during an interaction for this story.
 
Once bitten, twice shy, too.
 
To prevent another boardroom coup, Velu has ensured the ownership is firmly with him—99.98%. Impressively, that hasn’t stopped him from generating funds for expansion. He’s going overseas, to (almost) exactly the same places where Metropolis has a presence.
