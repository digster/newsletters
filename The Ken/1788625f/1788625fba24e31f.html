Good Morning Ishan,
 
“No one knows how much to consume or when to stop. This is dangerous. You can’t keep consuming herbal drugs blindly.”
 
This quote is from today’s story by Maitri. You’d think it was from a sceptic or disbeliever of herbal medicines, but no. It was said by Partap Chauhan, director of Faridabad-based ayurvedic products company, Jiva Ayurveda.
 
Founded in 1992, Jiva operates over 80 clinics where its hundreds of doctors treat patients using medical concepts from Ayurveda, the centuries-old Indian system of natural, mostly plant-based medicine. Chauhan himself is an ayurvedic doctor, with a bachelor's degree in Ayurveda, Medicine, and Surgery.
 
In spite of running a business whose essence is herbal medicines, Chauhan is concerned because of the sudden upsurge in Indians self-dosing themselves with what they feel are natural drugs bereft of side-effects.
 
And while for most parts they may be right, in some, they are wrong. And the organ that’s acting as the early warning signal in that area is the liver.
 
Increasing consumption of over-the-counter herbal drugs for everything from Covid immunity to skin conditions and bone health is leading to a rising number of cases of DILI. Drug-induced Liver Injury.
 
With even FMCG companies riding the herbal medicines wave, more and more people are being exposed to drugs in the harmless settings of even supermarkets. The rising consumption is doing in months to some people’s livers what used to take alcoholism and obesity years.
 
Some doctors that have tried to sound the alarm have had police complaints filed against them for criminal defamation.
 
Maitri covers the fast-growing market and the early warning signs and counsels for better vigilance and regulation. She also talks about how herbal drugs affect the livers of some people who are “Reactors” much worse than those who are “Tolerators” or “Adapters”.
