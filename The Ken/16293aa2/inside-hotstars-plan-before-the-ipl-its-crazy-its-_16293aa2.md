Good Morning Ishan,
We haven’t met. I am new here. It’s been just two days since I joined The Ken. Lovely office. Nice people. No Human Resources team. It’s perfect.
On my first day, ten minutes after I had figured out how the coffee machine worked, my colleague Venkat pinged me on Slack. Apparently, he had just finished writing a story. It was about a company called Hotstar. And a deep-dive into what they were doing before the IPL. He had chased this story for weeks, spoken to several employees and even got a peek into what their products were going to look like. All this when Hotstar was keeping a tight lid on their plans. He kept typing and typing.
“They are doing some really intense stuff, da. Very fascinating. I can’t believe how ambitious it is. Very risky. Things that nobody has ever done successfully in the past. Gamification. Distribution partnerships. And emojis. Did you know about their emojis?”
I had no idea who Venkat was, so I told him I had already linked my Aadhaar card to my bank account, so please stop messaging me again and again. Ashish had to intervene to resolve the confusion. I apologised profusely to Venkat after that. Now we are friends.
Then I read the story. He was right. It was incredible. The products they were launching were unparalleled. The deals they were striking were aggressive. I wondered what the mood is in Hotstar right now. Are they scared? Optimistic? Updating their LinkedIn profile? I tried to imagine.
Luckily, you don’t have to imagine. You can read the story and make up your mind yourself. It’s a story about a company’s bid for greatness and how it's placing a massive bet on something that may or may not work. Above all, it’s about a media company battling to build a formidable technology product that may redefine live sports forever. All before one of the most-watched sporting events in the world.
