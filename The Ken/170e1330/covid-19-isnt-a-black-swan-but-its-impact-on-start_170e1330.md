These are terrible days we’re living in. A new week brings forth new apprehensions about more Covid-19. Virtually everything we hoped would protect us against the pandemic has turned out to be false—China’s superhuman efforts to contain it, sealed borders, shuttered cities, self-quarantined populations, face masks, warm weather.
What does this mean for the hundreds of thousands of economic engines scattered around the world that increasingly power its innovation and growth—startups?
Today’s story, by Sumanth in Bangalore and Jon in Bangkok, analyses the multiple impacts of a global pandemic on Asian startups.
Some Indian startups have seen their toplines drop anywhere from 5% to 50% in a single week! In an economy that was already buffeted by slowing GDP growth, a bank collapse, social unrest, and regulatory unpredictability, Covid-19 is the catalyst for what could be a perfect storm.
Consumer startups are slowly resigning themselves to virtually writing off the next few quarters. Marketing budgets are being slashed, hiring is being frozen, and staffing matrices are being redrawn. Large-scale layoffs look inevitable, not merely possible.
B2B startups are somewhat better off, but only for now. Today’s consumer sentiment will become tomorrow’s enterprise sentiment. Sales strategies have to be reimagined for a world where travel and meetings are off the table.
And there’s funding. Following Sequoia’s lead, every major VC in India is advising firms to conserve cash and raise as much money as they can right now rather than wait, reiterating the age-old credo of “cash is king”.
