Good Morning Ishan,
Would you like the following features from your telephony operator?
“Listen”: Allows a user to silently monitor a call, such as an assistant taking notes for an executive
“Whisper”: Allows a user to speak privately to the person they are silently monitoring
“Barge”: A supervisor who is monitoring can enter the call, and the three parties can continue the conversation
“Take over”: A supervisor who is monitoring can enter the call and take over the conversation
If you think they’re mildly or overtly creepy, you would not be wrong. Because these are just some of the advertised features for Zoom’s cloud telephony service, Zoom Phone. But these features would be useful if you were an enterprise, say a call centre.
That’s exactly what Zoom is hoping for in India as it’s poised to acquire a much sought-after license for unified communications in India. This license, which seems imminent, would allow Zoom to offer cloud-based voice, video, messaging, and telephony services. It will even be able to issue 10-digit mobile numbers to enterprises and consumers.
That’s why I said Jio, Airtel and Vi won’t like it. Their telco domination is being chipped away, bit by bit.
Back to Zoom, though. Post-Covid, India had become its second-largest market by usage. But the free users didn’t translate into revenues quite so smoothly. Also, the likes of Microsoft Teams and Slack offered cheaper rates to enterprise customers.
Today, India contributes between $50 million and $70 million, a mere fraction of Zoom’s overall revenues.
This is why Zoom hopes a new telephony license will be a magic bullet. “After the licences come, and we launch Zoom Phone and other services, our revenues can go up by 30-35%,” said a Zoom executive to Pratap Vikram Singh in today’s story.
