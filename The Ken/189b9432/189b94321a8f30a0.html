We all know how important and special Make in India is for its citizens and government. But some domestic manufacturers aren’t feeling that way.

India’s largest and third-largest module makers—Waaree Energies and Vikram Solar Ltd, respectively–are preparing to manufacture in the US.

This despite the Indian government putting its back into ensuring making in India is attractive for solar modules and component manufacturers. That’s why of the $24.3 billion that companies across 14 sectors can avail through the production-linked incentive (PLI) scheme, solar manufacturing is among the top five when it comes to the largesse at stake.

These incentives, along with high tariffs, helped in keeping Chinese imports of the modules out. After all, India is keen on fuelling its energy transition through domestic manufacturing.

But when you look at what is on offer in the US, the mystery begins to unravel. In fact, it’s surprising why more solar-component makers haven’t flown there.
