Good Morning Ishan,
I don’t know if this happens to you. You are chatting with someone, talking about something, and your thoughts start racing, they are like somewhere else. This happened to me, a little while back. Durga, my colleague who has written today’s story, and I were discussing this. How Kerala’s poverty eradication programme, Kudumbashree, which works to financially and socially empower over 43 lakh women, has started its own e-commerce website. And I was telling Durga, man, this is beautiful. Like, everytime technology transcends barriers of time and distance, it is just magic. It gives people hope to do well in life. And then, racing.
I found myself thinking about the days when as newspaper reporters, we were cajoled, persuaded or threatened to file stories online. Nobody would file. This is not long back but those days were crazy. Reporters kept the best stories for the paper, online was a smelly dump yard. So much has changed today. But yeah, back to Kudumbashree. Launched late last month, you can buy all sorts of stuff from them. Clothes. Jam, chips, pickles, baked foods and beverages; personal care items like soaps and oils; jewellery; medicines and biodegradable sanitary products.
Kudumbashree’s goal is social empowerment. Through e-commerce.
Today’s story is a window into another world. To the southern part of India. Kerala.
