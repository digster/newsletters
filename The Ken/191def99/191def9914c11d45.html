Scene opens. The curtain rises.

Flipkart is sitting in a corner. It looks contemplative.

Enters Blinkit.

Blinkit: Hey everyone. Remember Grofers? I’m the same guy. I’m a quick-commerce platform. From milk and bread to chargers and t-shirts, I deliver everything in 10–15 minutes.

Audience responds with a loud cheer.

Flipkart is keenly watching. It suddenly gets up and takes centre stage.

Flipkart: So you like quick delivery? I’m launching Flipkart Quick. It’ll deliver your products in 30–90 minutes. And I’m hiring Shadowfax, a logistics provider, to take care of the last-mile delivery.

Audience has a middling response. Flipkart goes back to its corner. Slightly disappointed.

Enters Swiggy Instamart.

Swiggy Instamart: Hey! You’ve seen me deliver cooked food at your doorstep. Now I can send you the ingredients, too. Fruits, vegetables, spices—you name it and I’ll deliver in the next 10–15 minutes.

Audience nods in unanimous validation.

Zepto runs in.

Zepto: You need quick delivery and low handling charges, right? I’ve accumulated enough funding to give you both. Just download the app.

Audience gets excited. The collective sound of phones unlocking fills the air.

Flipkart gets up again. It reaches out for the mic.

Flipkart: Fine. Forget Quick and Supermart; I’ve shelved them. I’m launching Flipkart Minutes. It has everything that you find on other platforms. Not to mention exciting electronics items. And this time, the delivery will be taken care of by Ekart, our internal logistics arm.

Audience is inquisitive. They have questions.

Audience 1: So the same person who delivers my phone will now deliver groceries? Wasn’t Ekart designed to meet Flipkart’s e-commerce needs?

Audience 2: Hey. I hear Nuha’s written a detailed story that answers all our questions. Let’s head.
