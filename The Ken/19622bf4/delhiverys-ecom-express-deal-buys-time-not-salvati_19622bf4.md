Good Morning Ishan,
If Delhivery had bought Ecom Express three years ago, this email would have read something like this:
Shoppers trapped in their homes during the pandemic got so hooked to buying everything online—from groceries to bed linen to refrigerators—that it’s a habit they can’t shake off now, even if they want to.
Amazon, Flipkart, and thousands of brands eager to sell directly to consumers could never have imagined their business would soar to such heights. By acquiring Ecom at a time like this, Delhivery is putting even more distance between itself and rivals in the hottest business for any logistics company.
But Delhivery didn’t scoop up Ecom in 2022. It did so last week, in a distress deal worth Rs 1,400 crore. And nothing in the previous two paragraphs is true any longer.
Digital-first brands have learnt, much to their dismay, that there is no avoiding the brutal realities of selling through physical stores. Amazon and Flipkart are growing at rates that will make no heads turn.
So, how much further can e-commerce, which contributes 60% to Delhivery’s revenue, take the company? Especially when investors have punished Delhivery for hitching its wagon to that very business.
