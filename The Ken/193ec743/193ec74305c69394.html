The Big Story

The humble category manager is now a quick-commerce overlord

From having a rather sober existence in e-commerce, category managers are in the thick of the action in quick commerce

Category managers have evolved in their roles—from the more mechanical routine at e-commerce platforms to becoming the decision-makers in quick commerce. These managers, now responsible for stocking up the limited space in dark stores, call the shots as to who gets visibility on quick-commerce platforms. Brands are courting them aggressively; on average, at least 30,000 requests are floated a month for about 150 slots collectively on Instamart, Zepto, and Blinkit. While brands are inviting these managers over to parties and taking them out for drinks, the latter want companies to spend more on ads and marketing to stay relevant inside their platforms.

IV drips move from hospitals to spas—to ‘fix’ the elite’s hangovers and hair loss

But the medical community isn’t exactly thrilled because of the lack of regulations and scientific evidence

Originally used for medical emergencies, IV drips have transformed into a wellness trend in India. While marketed as quick fixes with near-perfect nutrient absorption, experts warn that many benefits are psychological, and improper administration can lead to severe health risks due to the lack of stringent regulations. With treatments costing up to Rs 80,000 per session, these drips cater to a wealthy clientele, raising ethical questions about expensive health solutions for  a select few while many don’t have any access to them. Despite controversies, the market continues to grow, fuelled by innovations and increasing consumer interest in quick, customisable wellness solutions. [Read the full story]

Vishal Mega Mart rides the coattails of Zudio and Dmart to a US$4.1 billion listing

The value retailer has leapfrogged most of its rivals. But its secret sauce is its ‘product mix’ which places it between Dmart’s scale and Vmart’s regional appeal

Value retailer Vishal Mega Mart is all set to be listed on the bourses, and that too at a premium. After all, institutional and retail investors oversubscribed 27x to the Rs 8,000 crore, which is completely Offer for Sale. The value retailer, which has 70% of its stores in tier 2 and smaller cities, has outpaced rivals in the same space, such as Vmart and V2 Retail. And though it pegs itself in the same league as Dmart and Trent, the Kedaara Capital-backed retailer is still much smaller in scale. [Read the full story]

Youtube is Saregama’s superpower—and its kryptonite

The US$1.1 billion company is getting more reliant on Youtube for its music business. But a shift in trend is all it’d take for it to face the music

Saregama isn’t banking on old melodies anymore but making fresh hits and relying on Youtube to cash in on India’s obsession with music. With over 2.2 trillion views in 2023 alone, Youtube commands over 50% of the country’s music-streaming market. While the platform’s revenue-sharing model allows music labels like Saregama to get paid per user engagement, for now, the former holds the power in the relationship. Meanwhile, Saregama’s diversification bets like video creation and Carvaan haven’t done quite well of late, leaving its music business at the mercy of Youtube. [Read the full story]

How Bajaj Finserv carpet-bombed its way to $2.2B in mutual funds

This asset manager from the house of India’s largest non-bank has turbocharged to become the 27th largest among India’s 45 mutual-fund houses in just a year and a half. Its pedigree precedes it

Bajaj Finserv Mutual Fund has raced ahead in the assets-under-management (AUM) league tables, leaving behind new and many old players. Making up for its lack of a track record is a strong push by distributors riding on the Bajaj brand and drawn in by high commissions. The fund house’s sponsor and associates have also contributed much in shoring up its AUM. A wide product menu and top talent has helped, too. Bajaj Finserv Mutual Fund has much going for it now, but performance will eventually matter for it to be able to break into the really big league. [Read the full story]
