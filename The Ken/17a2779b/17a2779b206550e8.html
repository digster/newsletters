Good Morning Ishan,

During times like these, when you have to remind yourself to be grateful and happy, what do you do?

I rely on two things. I put on some pumped up music at the start of my day to get the adrenaline flowing. A good song is all the superpower you need to charge you up for the day. And after winding up work, I take a walk on the terrace with my dog and get my fill of the evening sky. That, along with Bengaluru’s glorious weather, is the perfect way for me to end the day. 

Sometimes, though, I get that odd itch to sit down for a bout of reading, whether it be a good book or a story that caught my eye over the week. If some immersive Sunday reading is what you rely on to crank up your happiness meter, we have you covered. That too, with an eclectic mix of stories. 

We kicked off the week with a piece on India's largest edtech and its seemingly endless appetite for acquisitions. In its 10 years, it has bought nine companies and has three more acquisitions lined up. And it does so with great ease because Byju’s can scoop up relatively smaller companies at even 5X their top-line since it is raising money at 10X, writes Olina. The fascinating thing here is not the company’s might but the fact that smaller edtech companies want to be acquired by Byju’s.

Byju’s wasn’t the only company ramping up its acquisitions. Three-year-old hospital supplier of drugs to diapers, Akna Medical, made its fourth and most expensive buy in May this year—Vardhman Health. This also made Aknamed, as it’s called, the largest hospital supplier. Having filled up its basket, the company believes it can now woo hospitals by becoming a one-stop shop; hospitals usually have to track thousands of vendors for procurement. But as Ruhi wrote on Thursday, it will first have to upstage rival Medikabazaar, which is reigning at the top now.

On Tuesday, Seema took a deep dive into the great divide that’s brewing in telecom standards. As 5G standards for telecom were being set, in 2019, India managed to get a key feature included in it. This version is called 5Gi. It’s meant to improve the battery life of a mobile phone and enhance the signal transmission range of a base station. But India could not get its new standard accepted by the global telecom standards super-body that decides what standards vendors finally implement. Now, as India has allowed 5G trials, the telecom department is encouraging telcos to use 5Gi. But this has sent two of India’s biggest telcos into opposing camps—Jio wants it, while Airtel doesn’t. This was also our free story of the week, so please hit that share button!

If you ever wondered how easy it is for companies to borrow money, then here’s a read for you. On Wednesday, I wrote about an online debt marketplace startup called CredAvenue. It connects lenders with corporate borrowers and helps them with easy access to credit via different debt products. At a time when India's bank credit growth is at a 59-year-low, CredAvenue managed to double the debt placed on its platform to $8.8 billion. The story looks at how this company was able to break into a space in which borrowers rely on having a direct relationship with their lenders.

The typical Indian beauty shopping cart of 2021 is phenomenally different from that of the early 2010s’. On Friday, Bhumika wrote about how two homegrown skincare brands, Mamaearth and Wow Skin Science, are reaping the rewards of customers’ rapidly expanding interest in ‘natural’ and ‘chemical-free’ products. Today, both are on the verge of becoming Rs 500 crore (in revenue) brands. Though they seem like the most natural of rivals, they have taken different routes to reach where they are.
