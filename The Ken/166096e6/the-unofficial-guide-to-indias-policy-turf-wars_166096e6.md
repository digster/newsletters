RBI. UIDAI. MeitY. Trai. Niti Aayog. Ministry of Commerce. CCI. iSpirt.
Who are these fine actors, you ask? These are the eight samurais of Indian tech and fintech regulation.
Technology and fintech companies in India have never before seen such harried policy activity. The last six to seven months have seen as many as eight major policies or recommendations from most of these institutions. And each has the potential to rewrite business rules for good. With general elections to the country due next year, both Indian and foreign firms are finding themselves caught in a twilight zone where there is also policy paralysis and policy overreach.
"We don’t have a consolidated, integrated long-term strategy as a nation, what we have is five blind men touching the elephant and making rules based on what they see [because of the dispersed roles and responsibilities of different entities]," says Suhaan Mukerji, partner at law firm PLR Chambers.
The policies cover a wide swathe of themes, from data localisation to e-commerce business models to data privacy to know-your-customer rules. In many cases, there are overlapping, even competing versions. Companies are attacking each other via press releases and leaks. Lawyers and lobbying firms are laughing their way to the bank.
Arundhati and Olina have spent a few weeks digging into this shadowy power play, with an aim to truly decode what’s going on. They go deep into each of the eight samurais and lay out motivations, power equations, relationships and disruptive potential.
