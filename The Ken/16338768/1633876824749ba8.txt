Good Morning Ishan,

Today has all the making of the start of a great, hard-working, earn-your-daily-bread kind of a week. There is no other way to say this, so I’m going to get right to it. The Vakrangee rabbit hole goes deeper.

Earlier last month, we were the first to get on this company’s tail. We said that things don’t add up. Its business model, its purported deal with PC Jewellers, its share price and the open and shut Sebi investigation into the company's affairs. Nothing makes sense. I mean, if you look back from the last week of January, when it first bought equity in PC Jewellers, the share price of Vakrangee has tanked 83% from its all-time high of Rs 515 per share, losing more than Rs 40,000 crore in market cap.

But what’s bad must only become worse.

Last week, PWC, Vakrangee’s auditor resigned. In its resignation letter, PWC has said that it asked important questions twice, both times in the month of March. And since the company didn’t have satisfactory answers, PWC had little choice but to bail. My man, Pontius Pilate would be proud of the accounting firm’s action. Even as all this was happening, my colleagues, Sidhartha and Patanjali, have been working to understand the nature of bulk deals in the stock. Historically speaking. What they have found is of grave concern. Closer inspection of its trades reveals that bulk deals may have been executed by related parties. Those related to Vakrangee’s promoter.

Caught in this inexplicable mess are retail investors in the company, where both them and their shares have seen better days. Our story talks about all of this. Carefully detailing how Vakrangee’s promoter, Dinesh Nandwana is related to several individuals involved in the bulk trades.
