Good Morning Ishan,
When I was an auto reporter a decade ago, the talk of the town was how Asia Motor Works (AMW) rose to become the third-largest heavy-truck maker within a decade.
AMW was a company set up by Anirudh Bhuwalka, the nephew of Essar Group’s founder Shashi Ruia, and it was grabbing market share from incumbents like Tata Motors and Ashok Leyland in some categories of trucks.
By 2020 though, AMW lost steam and went bankrupt.
Bhuwalka is now back with Blue Energy Motors, a heavy-truck maker powered by alternative fuels like LNG, with plans to eventually go electric. And it has already raised $50 million, the latest round from Zerodha’s* co-founder Nikhil Kamath and Omnitex Industries.
In AMW style, in the five years it has been around, it has already become the largest seller of LNG trucks when compared to Ashok Leyland, Tata, and Volvo.
But it’s a big fish in a tiny, tiny pond. In FY25, of the total 950,000 commercial vehicles sold in India, only 700 were powered by LNG.
LNG sure has some advantages—it has cheaper running costs, lesser emissions, and great mileage. But the challenges are many, too. From fewer fuelling stations to higher upfront cost of the truck to lack of incentives to switch to this type of green fuel.
So why would Bhuwalka, who already has had one misadventure in this segment, relaunch a trucking venture powered by a fuel that has little going for it?
