Good Morning Ishan,

I usually look for themes for this email. What's the bigger story? What different concept or debate or subject did we explore? Did we do it differently?

This week, though, I had a quick chat with PGK. A cursory look at our stories later, we realised, well, this week was a report card. For new India, that is.

We've had a week full of stories that show a clear pattern: India is changing. And fast. From clothing to groceries to medicines, systems are evolving to suit our changing lifestyles. Some old guns survive these odds, as they should. Others are ousted, also, sometimes, as they should.

The new is here, how is the old keeping up?

Let's find out.

Harveen and Gayathri's Monday story https://the-ken.com/story/hrx-celebrity-brand-phenomenon/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on HRX and India's celebrity brand phenomenon signalled how urban India's clothing preference is quickly altering. Want to stay healthy but don't have too much time? Hrithik Roshan's clothing line has the perfect solution - athleisure. How does this affect fashion retail, in general? And other celebrity-endorsed brands, in particular?

On Tuesday https://the-ken.com/story/perhaps-the-most-ingenious-story-of-the-indian-internet-startup-ecosystem-of-this-decade/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, Ashish looked into India's 6th largest e-commerce company - Droom. A marketplace for buying and selling used vehicles in India, Droom has found a glitch in the e-commerce matrix, taking it to new heights. Though Ashish calls Droom ingenious, GMV could very well be the spoke in the used car business-buyer relationship wheel.

Shashi's story on Wednesday https://the-ken.com/story/telecom-spectrum-auctions-delayed/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter looked at how spectrum today is, well, cold cake. Its acquisition no longer priority number one in "post-spectrum" India. But if telcos aren’t interested in acquiring spectrum, what exactly are they up to? (This one's a free read.)

Thursday had https://the-ken.com/story/dmart-dalal-streets-most-precious-beat-odds/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletterSidhartha write about D-Mart, the old-school retailer. D-Mart managed to grow when the industry was collapsing, unscathed by the first wave of e-commerce. Now, as online players eye India’s brick and mortar space and partner with domestic players, what’s the retailer up to?

On Friday https://the-ken.com/story/gsts-attack-on-pharmas-weakest-link-distribution/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, Ruhi looked at how GST has changed the game for India’s drug distribution network. The supply chain is now simplifying, with e-pharmacies and private equities developing their own supply chains. Basically, the middle-men are at sea. 

As you can see, change is the only constant. (Unfortunately, not in the case of age-old sayings. Cliches are forever.)

Sometimes though, we really need change. Our Sunday story https://the-ken.com/story/memes-reddit-indianpeoplefacebook-racism/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter (also free), penned by Aria Thaker, for instance, looked at how Reddit's IndianPeopleFacebook subreddit may have gone too far with its racist undertones. Now that's a mal-effect of social media one could do without. 

That was our week. What about yours? Do write in.

Have a great weekend.
