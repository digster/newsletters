Brahmal Vasudevan, the founder and CEO of Creador, didn't pursue a career in the private equity space. On the contrary, he stumbled into it. 

An aerospace engineering grad, he met Ashish Dhawan, the man who would entice him into the world of private equity, while doing his Master's at Harvard Business School. Three years after they met, he joined Dhawan's Indian PE firm, ChrysCapital.

Vasudevan's heart, though, always remained in his home country of Malaysia. Eventually, he returned home to begin his own PE firm, Creador. His choice to base it out of Malaysia, rather than Southeast Asia's financial hub of Singapore, has ensured that over two decades into his PE stint, he still remains something of an outsider.

Outsiders tend to bring fresh perspectives, and Vasudevan is no different. Creador is unique. Unlike other Southeast Asian PEs, it focuses on India as well as its home region. In each country it is present in, it's staffed entirely by locals. And for a private equity firm, it has an unusual interest in investing in public companies.

Creador stands out not just because of its practices, but also because of the results that stem from them. Its returns are above average for the region, riding on the back of the 13 exits it has managed in its nine-year existence. Now, as it closes in on its tenth birthday, it is about to add another exit—albeit partial—to this list with the IPO of Malaysia's Mr. D.I.Y. Group.
