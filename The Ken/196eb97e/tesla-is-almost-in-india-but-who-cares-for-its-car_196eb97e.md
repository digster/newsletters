Good Morning Ishan,
When Modi met Musk in the US in February, many did a double take. On one side was the elected Prime Minister of India, the world’s largest democracy, accompanied by an entourage of officials, including the country’s foreign minister. On the other side, stood the world’s richest man—unelected yet powerful in the world’s oldest democracy—accompanied by his partner and three of their children. This odd sight highlighted the cachet the Indian government attached to the Tesla founder, also the chum-in-chief of the new US President. The way to Donald Trump’s heart, it seemed, passed through Elon Musk’s hand.
But it was more than that.
The Indian government has, for years, been wooing Tesla to manufacture its electric vehicles in India. Never mind that Musk has been playing hard to get, even cancelling a trip to India last year at the last minute. The Modi-Musk rendezvous in February was, as such and as much, an extension of the former’s long-running charm offensive. Soon after, the Indian government slashed import duty on premium EVs—the kind manufactured by Tesla—from over 100% to 15%.
Fair to say that the government has bent over backwards to get Musk to “make in India”.
But will he? India is ready for Musk, but is Musk ready for India? Does the country have what it takes to get Tesla revving? Equally, does Tesla have what it takes to make Indians happy?
