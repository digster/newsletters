Good Morning Ishan,
 
Well, it feels good to be back in my hometown. I work out of Mumbai, but my heart beats for Bangalore. Don’t get me wrong, Mumbai is a fine city in its own way, but a lot of action is happening in the old Ooru. The elections begin today, and if you’re a resident of Karnataka, I urge you all to go out and vote as per your conscience. I’m here for that actually. Personally, I feel that it is a bit of a double standard when you ask for accountability from your politicians but don’t participate in democracy. 
 
After voting, why not catch up on the stories we published this week? 
 
On Monday, Sidhartha and Patanjali teamed up once again to write about Vakrangee. After Vakrangee’s auditor PwC resigned, they untangled the complex knots of bulk deals executed by related parties. 
 
Harveen’s story on Tuesday talked about MAS Holdings, one of the largest manufacturers of lingerie, and their struggle to crack the intimate wear market in India for over 10 years. 
 
Ashish’s two-part series on Wednesday and Thursday explored MakeMyTrip’s monopolistic tendencies in the online travel agency (OTA) space. It begins with Treebo and Fab Hotels being booted out of the platform so that they could have a closer relationship with Oyo which gives access to Softbank.  
 
The second part of the story talks about how ibibo has been flooded with dubious reviews for Oyo hotels within a short period of time. Hotel owners feel that these reviews are critical in getting customers. 
 
The week ended with my story on how telecom regulator Trai is getting bogged down by a number of court orders. These orders are taking aim at its core functions of regulating interconnection, quality of service and tariffs. It’s a free read so do share widely.
