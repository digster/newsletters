Good Morning Ishan,
In its imperial march to shore up users and data, OpenAI is invoking different imagery in different countries. While launching its education tools in Greece this month, it referred to the country as the land of “Plato’s Academy to Aristotle’s Lyceum” and the “birthplace of western education”.
There was no mention of any Indian philosopher when it launched its education programme here a few weeks ago. Instead, it revealed that India was home to the largest student population on ChatGPT globally.
The American AI company has been scouting for a school partner in India for a year. But since March, when it first made a call to Arise, a network of over 1,800 private schools with 1.5 million students in the K-12 category, it has moved incredibly fast. By June, Arise was running a pilot—just weeks before the company came out with Study Mode, an interactive learning tool within ChatGPT.
Of the 500,000 free ChatGPT licenses that the company has announced to give away in India, 10,000 will go to Arise schools.
Initially, OpenAI wanted a watertight NDA and elaborate feedback from teachers and students. It isn’t getting exactly what it wanted, as Atul writes in his story today, but the company is making progress.
