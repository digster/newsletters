Tea and salt, steel and cars, insurance, airlines, hotels, IT services, and even telecom and satellite television—there isn't much the Tata Group, one of India's largest conglomerates, doesn't do.
When it comes to the digital world, however, the Tata Group is something of a stick in the mud. It has struggled to leverage its offline retail empire—consisting of hundreds of stores across grocery, electronics, and fashion—to develop a significant online presence. Even its e-commerce platform, Tata Cliq, is light years behind the likes of Amazon and Flipkart.
Somewhere in the transition from offline to online, the Tata Group always seems to come up short.
Covid, though, was a rude awakening. As digital businesses surged, Tata realised it needed to turn its superficial online presence into a super app.
Already, the Tata Group has been working behind the scenes to create synergies between its various businesses. It even launched its own food delivery app. The real marker of intent, however, is the impending acquisition of e-grocer BigBasket. Tata will reportedly pick up a 60% stake in the startup for around $1.3 billion.
The acquisition is an acknowledgement of two things—Tata realises it struggles to execute digital plays on its own, and time is running out for it to stake its claim online.
BigBasket is undoubtedly a coup for Tata. It's the country's largest online grocery startup. Covid also added rocket fuel to its growth engine. The question now is how does Tata leverage its latest acquisition to sail out of the digital doldrums? In today's story, Seetha delves into what the two seemingly disparate businesses offer one another, and how they could work together.
