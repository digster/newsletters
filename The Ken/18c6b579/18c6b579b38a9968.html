As business journalists, we always look for data to back our stories.

Data can show us how credit grows, bad loans grow in lockstep, incomes are under pressure, and savings are falling. But what about the toll this takes on indebted borrowers?

Today’s story made me realise that there are many blanks that data can’t fill—but therapists can.

“Mental health troubles arising out of debt is very much a clinical problem,” Om Prakash Singh, professor of psychiatry at West Bengal University of Health Sciences told The Ken. “It became more apparent in the last one year or so.”

To document the mental health crisis among indebted borrowers, Rounak, the author of today’s story, spoke with 13 therapists, six psychiatrists, and two online mental health service providers, including Docvita, which partners with more than 100 therapists and psychiatrists.

Peer pressure and impractical lifestyle goals are major drivers of loan-induced financial stress. However, the biggest trigger for such troubles, most therapists agreed, have been recovery agents. And Rounak takes us inside the source of this mental health affliction.
