Good Morning Ishan,
One in 10 stocks in India was valued at 100X or more of its profits in this crazy bull run, The Ken [wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/one-in-10-stocks-is-valued-at-100x-of-profits-in-this-crazy-bull-run-you-caused-this/) in July. Now, over the past month and a half, the bulls have finally felt the pull of gravity. Yet, many stocks continue to sport fancy valuations.
Like the protagonist of today’s story.
Honasa Consumer, the maker of Mamaearth beauty- and personal-care products, quotes at a dizzying 114X earnings. This, after the stock has been hammered down an eye-watering 30% over the past two trading sessions. It is now below the price at which it IPOed last year. One can almost hear desperate investors calling for mama’s hug.
But comfort is unlikely anytime soon. For starters, the stock’s valuation is still in la-la land. More importantly, Honasa’s travails—which led to the September quarter loss and the stock crash—have a long way to go.
As Aakriti, the author of today’s sharp story, finds out, Mamaearth misjudged the leap from a plucky direct-to-consumer startup to a typical FMCG company. An attempted course correction by switching from super-stockists to direct distributors has left it with dead stock, shaken investors, and distraught distributors.
Honasa’s Rs 70 crore inventory write-down, which splashed red ink, could just be the tip of the iceberg. The damage is at least four times worse, allege distributors holding crores of unsold and unclaimed goods. The pain is radiating across the Mamaearth ecosystem—except for, surprise, some early investors who sold much of their shares before the bad news hit.
