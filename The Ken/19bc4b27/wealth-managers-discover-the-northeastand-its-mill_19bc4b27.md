Good Morning Ishan,
There’s no shortage of wealth in the Northeast, and financial-services firms are finally sitting up and taking notice. In fact, GS Road in Guwahati, the commercial and shopping hub of Assam, increasingly has another identity: as the go-to address for finservs wanting to set up shop in the state.
No wonder the share of sophisticated products such as AIFs and PMS in rich investors’ portfolios is increasing. That is, compared to the primarily fixed-deposit and real-estate holdings older generations gravitated towards.
But there’s a problem. Wealth managers in the region are in for a culture shock.
Because unlike metros like Mumbai and Bengaluru, HNIs here value trust over technical chops. They don’t appreciate aggressive sales tactics, or cold calls. Instead, a good reference from a past client goes a long way.
As one CEO of a leading hospitality chain put it, “If they’re keen to work with us in spite of the fact that we’re not going to buy their high-margin products, that means they value us long term.”
Some wealth managers get this. Others spend years courting clients—often working on lesser pay than their metropolitan counterparts and almost no training—all to little avail.
