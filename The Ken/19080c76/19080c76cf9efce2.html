Good Morning Ishan,

Few outlandish things in edtech surprise us these days.

Masters’ Union, the altMBA school, would like to compare itself with the IIMs, but for all its features and bugs, it seems even more like an edtech than it was a year ago.

In fact, 15 months ago, when Alifiya first wrote about it, the headline question, literally, was: Talks like an IIM, walks like an edtech: what exactly is Masters’ Union

In July 2024, with only a third of its 199-strong batch of Post Graduate Programme (PGP) in Business and Technology Management having secured job offers just three months away from convocation, it’s fair to say that its placement record, once a source of pride, is now a millstone around its neck.

It’s not just about its reputation but survival. Everything at Masters’ Union is tied to the placement, actually. (Please tell me about one MBA school in the world that has this model.) The average CTC of any year’s cohort becomes the fees for next year’s incoming batch.

On top of it, the success of PGP decides the fate of its undergraduate courses. Perhaps the most expensive non-medical UG programmes in the country, charging Rs 40 lakh (US$48,000) for four years, it hasn’t graduated its first batch yet and how this “revenue stream” fares for Masters’ Union will depend on the PGP placements.

Already, Masters’ Union hit the brakes on its HR MasterCamp in July 2023 after four cohorts and hasn’t restarted it since.

Do you see where all this is going? I don’t think you do unless you read Alifiya’s eye-opener today.
