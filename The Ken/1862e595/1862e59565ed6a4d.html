Coca-Cola and Pepsi. Mastercard and Visa. Apple and Microsoft. We could go on and on to name more companies that have been locked in competition for decades—and maybe even pick a side.

Over in the Philippines, two telecommunications companies carve up the country to bring phone service and internet connections to people. They’re Globe Telecom and PLDT, both of which are getting close to a century old. The two companies have gone through iterations of technological advancement, and Globe, for now, has the lead with 54% market share.

The changes that Globe has undertaken did more than refresh its telecommunications offerings. It has a significant footprint in the tech sector, in a way that may be surprising to outside observers. Globe operates GCash, the Philippines’ most used e-wallet. It also owns HR-services provider Asticom and Yondu that offers IT and cloud-based services. 

Globe stays nimble by keeping startup development under the umbrella of 917Ventures, the telco's venture builder. The balance mixes startup-style speed with the stability of a telco business. This formula is working, and the telco is aiming to back another hit platform with the same impact as GCash.

For more than 14 years, through all of Globe’s changes, one figure has been the catalyst. Last week, Isa sat down with Ernest Cu, the president and CEO of Globe, for an interview.
