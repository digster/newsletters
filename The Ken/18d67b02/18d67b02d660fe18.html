Good Morning Ishan,

India’s jobs market is never short of paradoxes.

Today, as technology firms lay off thousands (or freeze hiring), jobs are raining in electronics manufacturing. Hiring is going to intensify in the next 12–24 months as the industry is likely to employ 6 million people, up from 1.2 million now.

And Tamil Nadu is the sandbox. The demand for factory workers is such that companies are even inviting candidates with education till eighth grade to job fairs. Little-known colleges entertain global companies, which visit their campuses looking to hire in bulk and notably prefer women.

Just the four major suppliers to Apple—Finland’s Salcomp, Taiwanese giants Foxconn and Pegatron, and homegrown Tata Electronics—are set to hire around 115,000 people.

It’s the second electronics-manufacturing wave in Tamil Nadu. Nearly a decade ago, at its peak, Nokia employed over 30,000 people and manufactured 10 million phones annually at its Sriperumbudur unit on the outskirts of Chennai. In 2020, Salcomp, the world’s largest charger maker, took over that plant. It employs 15,000 people and wants to double its headcount by next year.

Tamil Nadu recently beat Uttar Pradesh and Karnataka to emerge as India’s largest electronics exporter. As the state pulls out all the stops to lure global and local manufacturers, it’s also dialling up skilling programmes.

But for now, it’s a workers’ market. And as you’d expect, in the buzzing Hosur-Krishnagiri-Dharmapuri belt, home to over 20 major electric-vehicle manufacturers and several others, the usual pulls and pressures of a talent-starved market are in full display.

In his meticulously reported story, Narayanan says Tata Electronics spends over Rs 10,000 (US$120) a month per employee availing everyday benefits but charges them just a tenth of that amount. It’s also building a large housing unit close to its plant in Hosur. Just like Foxconn.

Let’s dive into how India’s top electronics hub is racing against time. With too much money and too few people: https://the-ken.com/story/indias-manufacturing-resurgence-has-a-people-problem-ask-tamil-nadu/ (10-minute read)
