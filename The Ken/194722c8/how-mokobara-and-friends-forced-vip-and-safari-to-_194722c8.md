Good Morning Ishan,
I fly often, so luggage choices matter a lot to me. I remember when my Samsonite suitcase gave out after more than two decades of rolling through airports, both wheels coming off at the same time and its side handle disintegrating into fragments. After trying a handful of replacements, I settled on an aluminium trunk last year. Every trip adds dents and scratches to its body—extra character to what feels like an indestructible container for everything I need when I’m away from home.
There’s no shortage of options for anyone who’s looking for a new set of luggage. Legacy brands still dominate department store aisles and their own outlets, but younger, VC-backed brands are increasingly capturing the attention of today’s travellers.
Mokobara, Nasher Miles, Assembly, and Uppercase are some of the newer luggage labels that have turned suitcases into lifestyle products, eating away at the business of brands you grew up with. In this Rs 10,000 crore market, we can already see some of the consequences: the stock price of VIP—which makes bags under Carlton, Skybags, and Aristocrat as well—dropped by roughly one-quarter over the past year.
The change in India’s luggage market isn’t just about fresh choices and changing consumer desires, such as products designed with pops of colour or silhouettes that stand out on conveyor belts. It’s also that more people are flying, and travel is becoming more frequent. One of the most significant shifts is that families used to share suitcases and bags, but each individual might have their own matching set now.
Luggage is one of the last consumer-product categories where internet-first, VC-fueled brands emerged. But once a few of them cemented their presence on social media, complete with aspirational photos of how you could also look great with a new set of bags and suitcases in tow, the incumbents started to feel the pain.
