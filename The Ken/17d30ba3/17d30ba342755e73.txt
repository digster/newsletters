Good Morning Ishan,

Since public memory is short, please allow me to jog it a bit.

Ever since Reliance Jio said in July 2020 that Jio Platforms had designed and developed end-to-end 5G tech, which it’d also like to export, there’s been a steady flow of news releases from the conglomerate.

Not one to be seen lagging behind, Airtel, India's second largest telco, also began its steady flow of news bytes on 5G, championing an ‘open network’ framework.

Now, when the 5G trial dates are to expire on 25 November, the two telcos, which rarely see eye to eye on anything, have asked the government for a year-long extension. The Department of Telecom (DoT) has given them an extension of six months.

This could push the 5G spectrum auction from the stated April 2022 timeline to well into 2023. And for once, telcos are in no hurry, even though the government may well be.

Despite multiple reports in the media of the ongoing 5G trials being a great success, the ground reality is less promising, as Pratap reports in the story today.

It seems, Jio has sunk the better part of three years and $60 million but has only managed to develop the core of the network and small cell radios whose performance in trials was underwhelming. Its ambitions of exporting 5G tech have also sobered.

Meanwhile, Airtel wanted to mix and match vendor tech and partnered with the IT major Tata Consultancy Services in June to incorporate the latter’s in-house 5G tech. It appears, “the tech, scheduled for trials in January, is not even in the lab” at this point.

In this story, Pratap brings to the fore what insiders always believed was going to happen.
