Pursuing medicine had the dual benefit of being able to save lives while also making money doing it.

Lately, though, doctors are questioning the financial sense in it, especially those practitioners who are just starting to launch their private practice.

Doctors make money through private practice and spend anywhere between Rs 50 lakh (US$60,000) and Rs 2 crore (US$240,000) studying for it. The income from private practice is easily 2.5X of what a doctor makes as a consultant in a corporate hospital.

But young doctors going down this path are finding themselves fighting multiple battles. From low patient turnout to a new practice to lack of affiliation with a private hospital, and the rising competition from corporate or PE-funded clinic chains.
In fact, a young doctor that Alifiya, the author of today’s story, spoke to struggles to make money even though she charges as much as Rs 3,000 for a consultation.

“If you ask me, I don’t even know why someone would want to be a doctor today… It’s just unviable,” said a 39-year-old cancer surgeon from Thane district.

Read Alifiya’s important and richly reported Big Story on how, for young doctors, it’s come down to either saving their livelihood or saving lives.
