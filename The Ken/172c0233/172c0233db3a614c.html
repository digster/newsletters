Good Morning Ishan,
 
Startups are shutting businesses and laying people off faster than you can put on a mask.
 
But in a seemingly different universe, Bengaluru-based and San Francisco-headquartered Postman, a software-as-a-service (SaaS) company offering an API platform solution, recently managed to raise $150 million at a $2 billion valuation. It was an incredible 6X jump in just a year.
 
This makes Postman only India’s fourth SaaS unicorn and, perhaps surprisingly, Bengaluru's first SaaS unicorn. It was also the fastest to get that vaunted tag, reaching the milestone in just six years. It is now the second-highest-valued VC-funded SaaS startup in India, behind only Freshworks.
 
The startup community and pundits galore are incredulous.
 
All the more so given the fact that Postman was, for the large part, an unknown entity even within startup circles outside the developer community it served.
 
Does this funding signal a new dawn for SaaS funding in India? How and why did the funding happen? What explains the nose-bleed $2 billion valuation?
 
In today's story, Sumanth attempts to answer these questions by tracing Postman's journey to date and analysing its future prospects: https://the-ken.com/story/postman-2-billion-valuation/ (14-minute read)
