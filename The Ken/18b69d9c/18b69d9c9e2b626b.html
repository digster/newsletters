There was a time when every fintech wanted to lend in some form or the other. Increasingly, every other fintech wants to be a discount broker too.

In 2020, Paytm and Groww ventured into the space pioneered by Zerodha. A couple of months ago, Paytm rival Phonepe did the same.

It’s not just fintechs that are enamoured of the business of letting individuals trade stocks by paying a smaller fee than conventional brokers. HDFC Securities, a full-service broker, and financial-services group Mirae Asset are among the recent entrants.

The fervour is understandable. Just one in four demat-account holders trades at least once a year. And 60% of stockbrokers’ active clients are with discount brokers.

But it’s not that a fat bottom line is guaranteed in this business. In fact, Zerodha, which made $350 million in profits in the year ended March 2023, is an anomaly. Groww, despite recently edging past Zerodha by active users, made just $10 million.

But every discount broker wants you to know it offers something that others don’t. Does it, though?
