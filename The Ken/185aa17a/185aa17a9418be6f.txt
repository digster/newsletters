Good Morning Ishan,

Happy 2023, and welcome back to Ed Set Go!—The Ken’s weekly edtech and education newsletter, now back after a four-month hiatus.

Our first edition of 2023 just had to touch on all the key themes this newsletter is about—essentially how big tech and big business have changed the way Indians learn and teach things. And it’s usually a struggle to find a company or development that checks all the boxes. 

But December’s news cycle didn’t disappoint at all. 

At its annual Google for India conference last month, the tech giant made its edtech intentions loud and clear with its video platform YouTube’s newest offering—Courses. As Techcrunch https://techcrunch.com/2022/12/19/youtube-to-launch-courses-in-edtech-push-in-india/?guccounter=1&guce_referrer=aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZ29vZ2xlLmNvbS8&guce_referrer_sig=AQAAANZmohzSlG4jI4AHDygrWlP44f_5N8Q9hNeOd8TSLEEtz07lVkrs2MJ0Jqsj4EIo03l3skaC1m4aMEnyG0mdyZsyU8QXDp2CErjtb5LrbSCwVNensy15TPbWIy8nJ1g4sgUbivfKToWtSZfAjgtvGAHVCpz1Z8lvdP91FCL4kYum writes, Courses will be: 

...a feature that will seek to bring structured learning experience on YouTube. Teachers will be able to publish and organize their videos and provide text reading materials and questions right on the video app. They will be allowed to offer the content for free or charge a fee…

Learners who pay up for Courses will also get an ad-free experience. 

As soon as the announcement hit social media, it set tongues wagging about the imminent death of big private edtechs in India. 

And it’s not hard to see why. YouTube’s sheer reach, at 450 million viewers, outstrips everyone else. Its vast library of learning videos dwarfs any other collection. Its army of over 2,500 creators far outnumbers the educators edtechs can hire through fancy incentives. And YouTube already has the track record of spinning out super popular edtech channels like Khan Sir, A2Motivation, and VC flavour-of-the-month Physicswallah (PW).

There’s no question YouTube has the might to snuff out all its edtech competition. But does it have the fight?

Can YouTube change India’s edtech landscape?
