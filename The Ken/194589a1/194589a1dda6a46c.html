The Big Story

Behind the luxury drumbeating, a housing-affordability crisis looms

Property prices across Indian cities shot up nearly 30% in the last two years; higher than the growth in nominal per-capita income

The luxury-housing boom seen in India over the years is driven by smaller group of uber-rich investors and buyers. Contrary to that narrative, The Ken found that the growth in household income hasn’t matched up the hike in real-estate prices in at least 25 of India’s major cities. The worry is not limited to homebuyers but even people looking to rent apartments; rentals have spiked by nearly 30% across top eight cities in the past two years. While India is not an outlier in the real-estate price run, governments around the world have taken the responsibility to ensure affordable houses to their masses. [Read the full story]

Brands once desperate for quick commerce now have a tiger by the tail

The pressure to keep up with demand on rapid-delivery platforms is pushing companies to the sidelines or, worse, causing them to opt out of the race

While quick commerce has allowed brands to acquire more customers, it has also initiated a cycle of unsustainable growth for many. The surge in demand, whether organic or platform-generated, has made it difficult for brands to keep churning out inventory. While increasing sales is in the brand’s best interest, it often comes at the expense of their long-term viability. This leaves brands with a difficult choice: continue down an unsustainable path or pivot to different channels for growth. [Read the full story]

Microsoft targets Nvidia’s AI-chip empire with an army of Indian engineers

Nvidia’s dominance in the AI market is forcing Big Techs like Microsoft to produce chips of their own. So, the software giant is changing its tack in hiring from Indian colleges

Microsoft wants to take on Nvidia’s monopoly over the AI-chip market and so, has released two chips so far; with more presumably in the offing. Using Nvidia’s GPUs brings down the margins of cloud-service providers like Microsoft Azure, Amazon Web Services, and Google Cloud Program. What sets Microsoft’s chips apart is that they’re optimised for cost, albeit losing a little performance compared to Nvidia’s offerings. Nvidia, meanwhile, is working on a plan to cut off third-party cloud-service providers and extend its dominance beyond AI workload. [Read the full story]

AI is making its way into banks. But the gates aren’t wide open

From auto loans to home loans, generative AI tools are helping buyers get faster loans. But existing biases still lurk

Indian AI startups raised US$747 million in 2024, with the number of deals rising from 78 to 121. The banking and financial sector was one of the top drivers of this growth. But this surge in AI adoption has caught the hawk-like gaze of the Reserve Bank of India, which recently set up a committee to evaluate the usage of AI in banking and financial services. While AI has certainly captured the attention of lenders, with Bajaj Finance—one of the top non banks—announcing plans to use AI across business segments, many concerns still remain. Despite the potential of AI, especially in areas like compliance and underwriting, its use in banking and finance could end up magnifying existing human biases. [Read the full story]

The mystery fund playing God and wreaking havoc on the stock market

Wild moves on weekly expiry days leave the country’s options traders scrambling and at the mercy of a mysterious player

Since April, there have been multiple instances of inexplicable moves in the stock-market indices on weekly options expiry days. Expert options traders point to a master manipulator—likely, a foreign hedge fund with deep pockets—who’s gaining at the expense of most others. The manipulator follows a pattern of “violent” and “quiet” expiries in reaping profits while other traders try to decode the former’s moves. While Sebi has curbed the exponential F&amp;O trade growth in the past, can it act now to stop the manipulator from toying with the market? [Read the full story]
