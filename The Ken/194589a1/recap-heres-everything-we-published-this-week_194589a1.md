[Behind the luxury drumbeating, a housing-affordability crisis looms](https://the-ken.com/story/behind-the-luxury-drumbeating-a-housing-affordability-crisis-looms/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
Property prices across Indian cities shot up nearly 30% in the last two years; higher than the growth in nominal per-capita income
The luxury-housing boom seen in India over the years is driven by smaller group of uber-rich investors and buyers. Contrary to that narrative, The Ken found that the growth in household income hasn’t matched up the hike in real-estate prices in at least 25 of India’s major cities. The worry is not limited to homebuyers but even people looking to rent apartments; rentals have spiked by nearly 30% across top eight cities in the past two years. While India is not an outlier in the real-estate price run, governments around the world have taken the responsibility to ensure affordable houses to their masses. The pressure to keep up with demand on rapid-delivery platforms is pushing companies to the sidelines or, worse, causing them to opt out of the race
While quick commerce has allowed brands to acquire more customers, it has also initiated a cycle of unsustainable growth for many. This leaves brands with a difficult choice: continue down an unsustainable path or pivot to different channels for growth. [AI is making its way into banks. But the gates aren’t wide open](https://the-ken.com/story/rbi-isnt-keen-on-using-ai-so-why-are-lenders-upbeat/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
From auto loans to home loans, generative AI tools are helping buyers get faster loans. But existing biases still lurk
Indian AI startups raised US$747 million in 2024, with the number of deals rising from 78 to 121. The banking and financial sector was one of the top drivers of this growth. But this surge in AI adoption has caught the hawk-like gaze of the Reserve Bank of India, which recently set up a committee to evaluate the usage of AI in banking and financial services. While AI has certainly captured the attention of lenders, with Bajaj Finance—one of the top non banks—announcing plans to use AI across business segments, many concerns still remain. Despite the potential of AI, especially in areas like compliance and underwriting, its use in banking and finance could end up magnifying existing human biases. Wild moves on weekly expiry days leave the country’s options traders scrambling and at the mercy of a mysterious player
Since April, there have been multiple instances of inexplicable moves in the stock-market indices on weekly options expiry days. Expert options traders point to a master manipulator—likely, a foreign hedge fund with deep pockets—who’s gaining at the expense of most others. The manipulator follows a pattern of “violent” and “quiet” expiries in reaping profits while other traders try to decode the former’s moves. While Sebi has curbed the exponential F&O trade growth in the past, can it act now to stop the manipulator from toying with the market?
