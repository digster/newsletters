Good Morning Ishan,

I’m a sucker for good conversations. Well, who isn’t, right? But I went and made that my career. In fact, ask any journalist here and they will tell you there is nothing quite like the high reporters get out of a great conversation. One where questions are free-flowing and answers are uninhibited. One where the other person like being challenged and takes hard questions on the chin. 

It is these kinds of exchanges that make for interesting stories at The Ken. And this past week we had a pretty solid set of stories coming out of conversations that were provocative, that were empathetic, that were straightforward and that were introspective. And all insightful. 

Take, for instance, the chat Vandana had with Ronnie Screwvala, the co-founder of edtech startup upGrad. She had some tough questions to ask https://the-ken.com/story/upgrad-struggles-to-make-its-mark/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter about why upGrad, which has been in the market to raise money for over a year now, has not been able to raise VC cash. This while other edtech startups have had VCs falling over themselves to fund the sector. 

Ruhi’s revelatory conversations with oncologists and distributors of cancer drugs led her to a heart-breaking story about how Indian cancer patients, unable to afford the high prices of cancer drugs, are now having to import them from Bangladesh https://the-ken.com/story/dhaka-indian-cancer-patients/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter because they are cheaper. This despite India being known as the world’s pharmacy. 

An honest over-the-counter chat I had with a kirana store retailer showed us the extent of Reliance Jio’s ambitions. Our Wednesday story was about the telecom operator's second pilot. This time in retail. https://the-ken.com/story/reliances-second-pilot-is-here-for-retail-this-time/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter But through the guise of the humble point of sale terminal. The conversations revealed what the storekeepers saw in the Jio PoS, what cases they would use it in and scenarios where they wouldn't touch it. 

Some stories entirely hinge on finding that one elusive person to talk with. In the case of our Thursday story, that person was Kiran Kumar Kuchi, an IIT professor. Speaking to him was how Seema was able to get to the bottom of why telecom equipment vendors like Ericsson and Nokia are worried. India was able to find its voice and added a feature to the global 5G standards https://the-ken.com/story/india-makes-its-maiden-entry-into-5g-standards-pisses-off-big-telecoms/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter, which will be rolled later this year. But the vendors don’t want to redo their products and build solutions for countries like India. It's a free read, so please share.

Sometimes stories come out not just out of conversations, but also from silent observation. Like Harveen's piece about how offline retailers are wisening up to the data play of online retailers and are venturing into surveillance tech https://the-ken.com/story/indian-retailers-ai-data/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter to bridge the data gap between themselves and online retailers. 

To end the week, Pranav and Pradip (for whom this is his first story at The Ken) teamed up to understand just how the people behind two Indian learning apps—Chimple and Amrita Learning, both finalists in multimillion-dollar XPRIZEs—believe an app can solve the problem of literacy https://the-ken.com/story/literacy-through-an-app-pipe-dream-or-secret-sauce/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. That too by getting children (and adults) to learn on their own. That was our Saturday deep-dive, and also a free.

We’ll be back on Monday with more such stories. Meanwhile, talk to us. About the weather, about coffee, about AI, and maybe how all the three should come together? We would love to chat about it.
