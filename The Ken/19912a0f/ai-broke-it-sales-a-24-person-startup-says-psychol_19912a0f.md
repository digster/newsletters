Good Morning Ishan,
Winning an IT-services deal has been mostly about one thing: shaving costs. Vendors promised 15–20% savings to potential clients, and that was enough to clinch billion-dollar contracts.
That was until generative AI blew up the playbook. Suddenly, everyone can blast out personalised emails, auto-generate proposals in seconds, and claim their workforce is “AI-ready”. Saving money isn’t special anymore.
Which is why a curious little startup has everyone’s attention.
Humantic AI—24 people, tiny by industry standards—thinks the future of IT sales isn’t just about being faster or cheaper. It’s about reading people. The company scrapes the internet—Linkedin bios, podcasts, even random Youtube interviews—and spits out a one-page “personality brief”. Maybe it’ll tell you to skip the small talk and jump straight into the numbers. Or maybe it’ll nudge you to lead with a story instead of a spreadsheet.
Basically: AI-powered mind-reading for sales. And in an industry where Wipro, Infosys, and Tech Mahindra are desperate for new growth, even a sliver of an edge matters. The company already counts McKinsey, Paypal, and AWS as clients.
But it’s also walking a tightrope: buyers don’t like the idea of being surveilled, rivals say Copilot can do the same job, and sales stacks are already bloated with disposable tools.
Abhirami’s [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/ai-broke-it-sales-a-24-person-startup-says-psychology-can-fix-it/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) looks at whether Humantic is genuinely changing how IT deals are won—or if it’s just another “AI startup” trying to sell psychology as software.
