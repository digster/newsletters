Good Morning Ishan,

Wow. It is so good to write to you again. 

I’ll be candid. I’d imagined this moment differently. This moment, when I am finally doing the writing. I’d imagined myself writing a fairly elaborate email. Asking you, how you’ve been, telling you that I’ve been away and enjoyed my break, talking about the places I’ve been, and new things I’ve picked up. People I met. But now, in this present moment, sitting on my big, reading chair, legs up and folded, to the sound of pigeons cooing, mixed with the sound of several loudspeakers calling out to believers for evening prayers, looking outside the window, to still, dusty trees in a large city, I think, I’m just happy. Happy to be writing to you as we set out to experience another year together. 

Nothing better than to start with a story about time, technological progress, the Chinese manufacturing juggernaut and all of it culminating in changing the destiny of a small town in India. 

A town called Morbi. About 63 kilometres north of Rajkot, in the state of Gujarat. 

Let Roshni take you there. She travelled to Morbi last year and says the town is almost chameleon-like. One which has gone from birthing India’s clock industry to becoming a tile hub. But life doesn’t play out in absolutes. Not everybody has come around. Local clock makers still exist, fully aware that they are racing against time. They ask Roshni curious questions; like this one, when was the last time you stepped into a clock showroom? 

I can’t quite remember. Years. Maybe a decade and more. 

Today’s piece is as much about time, as it is about hope, and the tenacity of human beings to endure when challenged with change.
