Good Morning Ishan,
Rohin is on vacation, so I am filling in for this weekend’s Zero Shot.
Disney said it will invest $1 billion into OpenAI.
It’s part of a broader deal. The highlights: Sora will be able to generate videos that include more than 200 characters from Disney, Marvel, Pixar, and Star Wars properties. “Fan-inspired short form videos” will become available on Disney+. Disney will “become a major customer of OpenAI, using its APIs to build new products, tools, and experiences”, according to the [press release](https://thewaltdisneycompany.com/disney-openai-sora-agreement/) issued by The Walt Disney Company on Thursday.
In more direct terms, anyone who uses Sora will be able to surf with Stitch, yodel with Yoda, breakdance with Buzz, and mingle with Mickey. If your prompt is cute or crazy enough, you could even make it onto a streaming platform for global distribution. (Who is meant to watch these videos generated via users’ prompts? I do not know, but that is a separate matter.)
This is an about-face for Disney, whose moat for decades has been hawkish protection over its intellectual property, with tight licensing and aggressive enforcement whenever infringement occurs. In June, the company [sued](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cg5vjqdm1ypo) Midjourney and called it a “bottomless pit of plagiarism”. Shanghai-based Minimax, one of China’s six “AI Tigers”, became the [defendant](https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/disney-universal-warner-bros-discovery-sue-chinas-minimax-copyright-infringement-2025-09-16/) in another case in September. Then, this week, Disney sent a cease-and-desist letter to Google, [alleging](https://www.axios.com/2025/12/11/disney-google-ai) that the tech giant’s “AI Services are designed to free ride off Disney’s intellectual property”.
As far as language that appears in legal documents goes, those sound like fighting words.
