Good Morning Ishan,
 
At every stage of crafting a story at The Ken, the one thing that’s always asked of the writers is, “Where are the numbers?” And for good reason. On the surface, numbers aren’t very sexy. But the stories they hide are.
 
And most of the time, the stories are those that are hidden in plain sight. You see the numbers, you connect the dots. Et voilà. At other times, though, the story is more often hidden in what the numbers aren’t telling us.
 
As writers, we are beholden to look at numbers with both eyes.
 
Numbers were the stars of this week’s stories—from India’s public sector solar energy ambitions and the chase for the #1 stockbroking slot to the reasons why telcos are both afraid of and want to work with Big Tech.
 
Without further ado, here’s a short recap of all that went down this week:
 
On Monday, Seetharaman wrote about India’s public sector energy company—the NTPC—and how it wants to grow its renewable energy capacity 30X in the next 12 years or so. This is a much-needed move for a company that produces barely 2% of its power through renewable sources. For India’s leading solar power players, though, NTPC’s ambitious numbers spell an entirely different story.
 
Arundhati, in our free story of the week on Tuesday, wrote about Upstox, currently the #2 stockbroking firm in India. Milestones are piling up; it sees nearly 300,000 new users a month and has a 2X-3X year-on-year growth. Also, $29 million of the $81 million investment into stockbroking in the last three years went to Upstox alone. But VC-fueled growth may not be enough; not if Upstox wants to close the gap with India’s largest stockbroking firm, Zerodha.
 
Speaking of Zerodha, here’s a number for you: the company’s co-founders were recently added to Forbes’ “India's 100 Richest People” list, with an estimated fortune of Rs 24,000 crore ($3.3 billion). Much of this success came from the duo deciding to do away with another seemingly insignificant number—the Rs 20 ($.30) brokerage fee. In an edition of The Interview on Thursday, Sumanth chatted with co-founder Nithin Kamath for an inside look into Zerodha’s growth and success.
 
On Wednesday, Seema and Prajakta teamed up to analyse the numbers behind some of the moves Big Tech is making in the telecom sector. Yes. Big Tech. In the telecom sector. You see, companies like Microsoft, Amazon, and Google are spending some big bucks to move into segments once considered the domain of telecom. We may just end up buying mobile data from the likes of SpaceX, the duo argued in this rich, data-driven story.
 
On Friday, Olina wrote about how India’s biggest gifting conglomerate, Ferns N Petals, is putting the petal, er, pedal, to the metal in its growth ambitions. Covid-19 may have made the business wilt, but the company hasn’t revised its target of Rs 500 crore ($68 million) in revenue. It has already set its sights in other directions—e-commerce and international markets.
 
Praveen wrapped up the week with the last edition of The Nutgraf for 2020. This week, the newsletter delved into the matter of protests. Not about who is protesting, or even what they are protesting about. But whether protesting… works.
 
That’s it for this week. I hope you have a restful weekend.
 
Happy holidays!
