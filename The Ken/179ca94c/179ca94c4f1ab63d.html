Good Morning Ishan,
 
A couple of weekends ago, after watching a chef's Instagram video urging people to order directly from restaurants, my partner and I decided to give it a shot. 
 
We wanted to have Chinese for dinner and had a restaurant in mind. So we googled it and then went to its website and placed the order. The experience was not the best, but it didn't matter to us as long as our food was packaged well and on time, which it was. 
 
The National Restaurant Association of India wants more of us to ditch Zomato and Swiggy and order from the eateries themselves. That's where restaurant ordering platforms such as Google-backed DotPe, Thrive, and Peppo come in. Similar to what Shopify does for e-commerce brands, they allow restaurants—of which they have onboarded over 12,500—to set up custom sites. They have a plethora of tie-ins with other service providers such as point of sales, food delivery, marketing, customer relationship management, and more. 
 
This is their proposition: charge a far lower commission than the 20-40% quoted by aggregators, and give restaurants access to customer data that they can use to push targeted offers. Restaurant chains have also seen average order values that are 2.5-3X what they get on food delivery platforms. 
 
Sounds great? Not entirely. You don't always know where you want to order from. Often, it takes scrolling through the endless options on Zomato and Swiggy till kingdom come before you can make up your mind. So how crucial is discoverability for restaurants? And how can DotPe, Thrive, and Peppo ensure that?
