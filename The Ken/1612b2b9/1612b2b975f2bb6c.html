What is it with e-commerce companies in India and marriages? Particularly shotgun marriages.
 
First, Flipkart "married" Myntra, thanks to the exhortations of common investors like Tiger Global. At that point in time, this was probably the largest acquisition of its kind in India. Then, Flipkart almost "married" Snapdeal—this merger would have been consummated if papa-in-law Son-san had his way but the bride ran away from the altar at the very last moment. Today's story is one of yet another marriage. Yet again featuring Flipkart as the dashing bridegroom. Only this time, this marriage is one of convenience rather than of the shotgun kind.
 
The bride in question?
 
Wildcraft.
 
Wildcraft's family had been looking for a suitable groom for a while now and they seem to have finally found one in Flipkart.
 
Stretched metaphors and unfunny PJs notwithstanding, Flipkart's purported investment in Wildcraft is a significant one in many ways. As my colleagues Patanjali Pahwa and Payal Ganguly explain in today's story, it showcases Flipkart's growing ambitions. Both within private labels (specifically in sports apparel) and in terms of moving beyond online and retailing to omnichannel and actually being involved in manufacturing products as well.
 
It is a fascinating story that unravels yet another strand of Flipkart's multi-pronged battle with Amazon.
