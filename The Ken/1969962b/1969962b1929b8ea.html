Physics Wallah’s Rs 15 lakh ‘BTech’ comes without a BTech degree

The IPO-bound edtech’s ambitious residential-tech programmes promise industry readiness but falter on degrees, transparency, and real deliveries like external internships

PW Institute of Innovation sold a dream of industry-ready BTech and BBA programmes, but left students grappling with incomplete degrees, confusing affiliations, and changing curricula. At least 30 students from the 2023 batch (from both BTech and BBA courses) dropped out citing chaos in the system and unkept promises. The haste that Physics Wallah is in has also to do with its upcoming stock-market debut. Academicians express concern over universities outsourcing regular degree courses to institutes like Physics Wallah, rather than letting them train students for skills.

Protein water, protein roti, protein kulfi: inside India’s $1.5 billion food fetish

Grand claims and the pitfalls of excess plague protein products in a country that can’t get enough of such foods

Urban consumers are increasingly turning to protein-fortified products like chips, yoghurt, and lassi over natural foods to meet their protein needs. The protein market is booming, worth $1.5 billion and growing at a compound rate of 6.5% with Delhi NCR leading the pack. A 2024 study found 70% of protein supplements in India were mislabeled, with 14% containing toxins. Protein marketing has evolved from bodybuilding imagery to a more lifestyle-oriented angle, targeting all kinds of urban consumers. [Read the full story]

How a well-timed offensive crippled Juspay’s $150 million fundraise

The standoff has cost the payments orchestrator in its latest funding round; merchant support, though, seems more enduring

Since payment aggregators refused to work with orchestrators like Juspay, the two sides have been in a who-blinks-first stare down. This came right amid Juspay’s $150 million funding round, and ended up dampening it. Merchants are caught in the crosshairs of this battle, scrambling to pick a side that does them the least damage. This fight has been simmering for a long time, simply begging for a trigger, which came in the form of Juspay’s aggregator license. [Read the full story]

Zomato, Firstcry, and others love this ‘magical’ number. You should hate it

With adjusted Ebitda, accounting again manifests as the art of the possible

India’s internet companies love reporting adjusted Ebitda, an unusual metric of profitability, than their actual net profit or loss. By their logic, adjusted Ebitda is an “additional tool” to help investors compare business operations over multiple periods. But experts believe the not-so-real bottom line actually helps no one other than these companies themselves. What’s good for the management may not be good for investors, and so, it may be time for the regulators to get into the act. [Read the full story]

*****

Inspired by Nokia, a $3B manufacturer charts a path through Trump’s tariff turmoil

Faced with a fresh wave of tariffs and trade turbulence, Zetwerk is scattering factories across borders, blending supply chains, and betting that predictability will trump price in the new manufacturing order

Zetwerk has been planting factories around the world—a strategy that suddenly looks very smart now that tariffs are back in fashion. Spreading your supply chain across borders means you’re less likely to wake up one morning and find your production costs have exploded—a trick Nokia figured out when it was still relevant. Conveniently, Zetwerk has some ex-Nokia people, like Josh Foulger, who apparently kept the old playbook and are now dusting it off. As markets start shedding real demand, manufacturers are rediscovering the old wisdom: it’s nice to build stuff near the people who want to buy it. [Read the full story]
