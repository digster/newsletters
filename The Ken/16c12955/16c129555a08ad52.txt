Good Morning Ishan,

Vanity is not something I'm particularly given to because, in truth, I have particularly little to be vain about. My eyes, however, are the one feature I've always been overly proud of. A particularly soft brown, one of three things I inherited from my father. The other two are a penchant for beer and the belly that comes along with it.

So imagine my horror when, after years of staring at brightly lit screens, the inevitable happened—my eyesight began to wane. Week after week, I sat trying to decipher the football score on a TV screen six feet away. All the while, I blamed Micromax for making terrible TVs since the alternative was too horrific to consider.

Eventually, without breathing a word to anyone, I snuck out to eyewear retailer Lenskart, signed up for a free eye checkup, and walked out with a prescription and an order for two pairs of glasses.

I've been fascinated with eyewear, eyecare, and Lenskart in particular ever since. So when Durga and I co-wrote https://the-ken.com/story/lenskart-rural-india/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=recap_newsletter&utm_campaign=email Wednesday's story on Lenskart's ambitions to conquer rural India, it had more than a shade of personal interest. Durga, too, wears glasses. She, too, bought her latest pair from Lenskart.

What I'm trying to say is that some of the most interesting stories come from a place of personal experience or interest. This week, quite a few of our stories exhibited just that.

Take the first of our stories this week. Pranav Shankar, one of our first campus hires, is a bike nut. He lives and breathes two-wheelers, and even grew up in a family involved in the business of bikes. So when he pitched his first story, of course it would be on bikes. EV ones, in this case. Together with Rohin https://the-ken.com/story/ev-motorbikes-revolt/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=recap_newsletter&utm_campaign=email, he dives deep into India's first EV motorbike and the potential of the EV motorbike space. It's richer for his personal experience, and Rohin even travelled to Delhi to experience it in person.

Tuesday's story by Suraksha https://the-ken.com/story/fiinovation-and-the-csr-funding-pot-at-the-end-of-the-rainbow/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=recap_newsletter&utm_campaign=email, similarly, is driven by her strong sense of sympathy for the underdog. In the months that I've known Suraksha, this has been one of her most defining traits. So when she got wind of a plethora of non-profits, all claiming to have been duped in their quest for CSR funding, she began digging. What she unearthed was the story of Fiinovation, an award-winning fundraising consultant firm that may not be all it seems. It's also our free story of the week, do read and share.

Not only did she pull up their inaccurate 2009 forecast for 2020, but Seema and Pranav Balakrishnan dissect https://the-ken.com/story/mckinsey-pharma-forecasting/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=recap_newsletter&utm_campaign=email why McKinsey's latest forecast looks set to continue that trend.
