Good Morning Ishan,
Self-awareness is a rare attribute. Especially in an IPO market where companies of all stripes are asking for—and getting—any valuation they can come up with. Pricing is now limited only by imagination.
Pine Labs seems to have self-awareness in spades. The fintech giant, best known for its point-of-sale payment-enabling machines, has slashed the valuation of its ongoing IPO to $2.9 billion from $5 billion in an earlier private fundraising round. It has also cut down the issue size sharply. Perhaps it doesn’t want the drubbing its arch-rival Paytm* got after asking for and getting the moon in the IPO boom in 2021. Or perhaps it doesn’t want to risk an encore of its failed 2023 IPO outing in the US.
According to founder and CEO Amrish Rau, the below-than-before valuation now is because Pine Labs believes in taking the community along and pricing the IPO in a way that makes it easier for investors to participate.
Except, investors are not participating in a big way, at least not yet. A little more than half the IPO has been subscribed, with just one more day to go.
Like the company, investors also seem aware of the challenges ahead. As Debanjali writes in today’s sharp story, the Pine Labs boat is springing leaks. The listing is a test of whether investors still believe in its moat in a world where QR codes are free, and instalment payments—the company’s USP—might soon be available on India’s favourite payment mode: the UPI. Oh, and banks, and a giant client, not to mention the competition, are now circling the business that propelled Pine Labs in the past. The 27-year-old war veteran is fighting back. Will it be good enough?
