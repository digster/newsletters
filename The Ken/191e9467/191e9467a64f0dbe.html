You may have seen the Subway commercial featuring former world chess champion Viswanathan Anand from over a year ago.

In the ad, Anand is comically drenched in sweat as he is asked to choose the bread, vegetables, and sauces for his Subway sandwich. Then a narrator chimes in, “Ordering a sub can be difficult even for a genius.” The solution: sandwiches with the ingredients chosen for you.

This is part of private-equity firm Everstone Group’s plan to put some oomph into the fast-food chain after buying its master-franchise rights in India, Bangladesh, and Sri Lanka in late 2021.

If the entire Subway menu was once customisable, only 70% is now. Customers aren’t amused; and so aren’t the store operators, with the menu being changed three times in the past six months.

That’s not all. Small franchisees still run 60% of the over 800 Subway stores in India. But Everstone is angering them, by either buying their stores out or opening new outlets around theirs.

Aakriti chronicles Everstone’s demanding quest to make a Domino’s out of Subway.
