Good Morning Ishan,

Every day, you actively sidestep fraudsters lurking in every digital corner—your email, SMS inbox, Whatsapp, and social media. You are savvy. You know all the telltale signs of scammers. Bad grammar, threats to disconnect a service, asking you to install an app, or asking to share an OTP.

And then, when you are in the middle of something, you get a call from a “buy now, pay later” (BNPL) service you use. It is from the legitimate support number of the BNPL provider, and in the call, they even say that you need to enter an OTP to secure your BNPL account from fraud. You enter the OTP. This is when irony strikes, and you become a victim of cybercrime.

In today’s story, Shivani uncovers the new way swindlers are defrauding users by targeting two systemic weaknesses.

One, fraudsters have identified that BNPL users of services such as Olamoney, Paytm Postpaid, and Mobikwik ZIP are ripe targets because of a flaw in the product design.

And two, the explosive increase in the use of OTP for various purposes, including availing gym memberships and entry into apartment complexes, has normalised revealing OTPs to strangers.

If you are a BNPL user, or know someone who uses it, or are simply curious about how cybercriminals are yet again giving everyone the slip, read Shivani’s insightful story: https://the-ken.com/story/bnpl-users-are-asked-to-pay-for-transactions-they-never-made/ [10-minute read]
