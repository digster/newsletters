“Maa, what’s in your fridge?”
I called mom. Curious. The curiosity of a man who left home years back, always promising to return and stay for long, but barely following through. Handicapped with the understanding that 10-minute phone calls, once a week, only on a holiday, are just good enough for banal updates. Where knowing enough is knowing little. But now, I had to know. About the fridge. So, I called.
She couldn’t immediately remember what’s in it. So, she walked over to it.
The fridge in our apartment in a tiny suburb of Kolkata sits at the corner where the living room meets the kitchen. LG. Grey. Two-door. A lonely, ageing, steel bottle opener stuck on it. “I have three packets of milk,” said mom. “Tomato and chilli sauce. Mustard. Vinegar. Two bottles of drinking water. Amul butter. Badam syrup and a bottle of thandai. Cadbury Dairy Milk and a box of chocolates (gifts for my nephew’s birthday last week), kulfi, lots of fresh vegetables and fruits like oranges, apples and a half-cut papaya. That’s most of it.”
No leftovers?
“None. Ice if you want to get down to details.” Well, that’s her. That’s home.
More than a thousand kilometres away, our fridge and what’s in it couldn't be more different.
Mine is a Samsung. Grey. Two-door. Some bits covered with magnets from travels to Europe, Southeast Asia and Africa holding bills and receipts. Inside: Chicken sausage, various kinds of cheese and a tub of ice cream recently bought from a supermarket. Coffee. Fresh vegetables. Lots of tomatoes, in a storage box. Some ginger, lemon, chillies. Buttermilk stored in a jar. Garlic in a box. A 2kg tub of Amul curd. Wine. Ginger ale cans. Tonic water cans. A carton of milk. A carton of soy milk. Tabasco sauce, several Pou Chong sauces for Chinese food, Jesus, some nine bottles of god only knows what kind of sauces...and leftovers from a streak of ordering in.
Quite a picture, isn’t it? Well, that’s our story for today.
