Good Morning Ishan,

Last night, I overheard A and B. Here are snippets:

A: Hey, I’m running a temperature. I need paracetamol…
B: Don’t worry. I’ll get it from the nearby Wellness Forever Medicare store.

A: Will you please get me some milk too? I’ll have a hot mug before sleeping.
B: Sure. Wellness has that too.

A: Oh great. But please walk to the nearby supermarket and grab a packet of frozen chicken nuggets. Been craving it.
B: Wellness has chicken nuggets, eggs, breads, cereals, instant noodles, and so much more.

A: You serious? What are they? A pharmacy? A grocer?
B: Well, that’s what makes them…

A: No wait. Don’t go. I’ll order everything on Zepto or Blinkit. You sit by me, please. I feel sick.
B: Okay.

While A and B may be imaginary, Wellness Forever Medicare is not. It’s a 16-year-old company with over 400 outlets in four states. And guess what? It gets roughly half its revenue—around Rs 1,500 crore (US$179 million) in the year ended March 2024—from non-pharma products. For Wellness’ competitors, like Apollo Pharmacy and Medplus Health Services, FMCG contributes less than one-fifth.

In fact, this is exactly why it stands out among its peers, even though it’s far behind them on store count. Medplus added almost 600 stores last fiscal alone.

Wellness, therefore, isn’t just any pharmacy—it has adopted a hybrid model where lifestyle meets medicine. Seetha, the author for today’s story, found that Wellness aims to replicate the success of global chains like Walgreens and Boots.

But here’s where the plot thickens: despite their innovative approach and stellar revenue per store, Wellness’ plans hit a snag.
