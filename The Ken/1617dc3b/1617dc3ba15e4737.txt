Since it's Saturday, it's a good time to take stock of supplies. You know, I experience this strange itch at the grocery store—of wanting variety. Too much of one vegetable or meat gets kind of dull, doesn't it?

So yeah, I tend to think about what I ate the past week, just to get some more variety on my plate. Curiously, that's also our goal at The Ken. To present you with stories that are all very different from one another.

And it's safe to say we managed a fair bit this past week.

The week started with Sidhartha's piece https://the-ken.com/story/yes-bank-gstn-enterprise-blockchain/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter enterprise blockchains becoming a reality in India, what with Yes Bank and GSTN opting for it. On Tuesday, we told a story that just had to be told. Ruhi's piece https://the-ken.com/story/niti-aayog-national-medical-commission-bill/ on?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter NITI Aayog writing and having its way with the National Medical Commission bill is an eye-opener. Mid-week we took stock of the Unified Payments Interface. It's been 17 months, and we thought this was a good time to analyse how the UPI payment apps stacked up against each other. According to Arundhati and Shashidhar https://the-ken.com/story/upi-payments-apps/ (the?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter newest member on our team), there are no winners.

Our week ended on a high note with Ashish's interview https://the-ken.com/story/venn-diagram-anand-sanwal-insight-overlap-high/ of?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter CB Insights' Anand Sanwal.
