Good Morning Ishan,

Since it's Saturday, it's a good time to take stock of supplies. You know, I experience this strange itch at the grocery store—of wanting variety. Too much of one vegetable or meat gets kind of dull, doesn't it?
 
So yeah, I tend to think about what I ate the past week, just to get some more variety on my plate. Curiously, that's also our goal at The Ken. To present you with stories that are all very different from one another.
 
And it's safe to say we managed a fair bit this past week.
 
The week started with Sidhartha's piece on enterprise blockchains becoming a reality in India, what with Yes Bank and GSTN opting for it. Also, Axis Bank and XinFin are not far behind. But how much can they really scale? That's the question the story addresses.
 
On Tuesday, we told a story that just had to be told. Ruhi's piece on NITI Aayog writing and having its way with the National Medical Commission bill is an eye-opener. For a bill aiming to increase the supply of doctors, this one sure has made many in the fraternity rather angry. It's a free story; do read it.
 
Mid-week we took stock of the Unified Payments Interface. It's been 17 months, and we thought this was a good time to analyse how the UPI payment apps stacked up against each other. According to Arundhati and Shashidhar (the newest member on our team), there are no winners.
 
On Thursday, Patanjali went back to December to tell a story that's relevant today—that of food tech in India. Why was December so big anyway? Have things really changed on the ground or is it just a lot of money talking?
 
Our week ended on a high note with Ashish's interview of CB Insights' Anand Sanwal. The man really likes his Venn diagrams, and chances are, you'd probably like his way of seeing the world, too. He sure has a whole lot of newsletter subscribers to attest to that.
 
Have a great weekend. See you next week.
