For most of Flipkart’s independent years, its public face was Sachin Bansal, co-founder and (the longest-serving) CEO. Till 2016, nine years after he started Flipkart, when he was asked to step down as CEO by his VC investors who had by now invested billions of dollars in the company.

Two years later, Flipkart would be sold to Walmart for $15 billion. Leaving Bansal a billionaire after he elected to sell his remaining 5.5% stake and leave the company.

But today’s story isn’t about Sachin. Yet, it is about a Bansal. Also, coincidentally a Flipkart co-founder. 

His name is Binny.

For years, he was the silent wallflower, working quietly behind the scenes. Till he became Flipkart’s CEO in 2016 after Sachin stepped down. That very year he would also buy himself a home in one of the most upmarket lanes in Bengaluru’s Koramangala suburb, one colloquially called “Billionaire’s Row”.

Today’s TKOW story is about that house. But also not about it. It's about how the house is an unlikely synecdoche to inspire a new breed of entrepreneurs.
