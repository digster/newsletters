Anyone familiar with the basics of playing chess knows that pawns are moved first to set up the defence and offence. As the game matures and the more powerful pieces mobilise, pawns are sacrificed away for the big guns to dominate and finish the game.

Apple is playing a chess game in Asia, and in some regions, it is now willing to sacrifice its pawns for the queen to dominate the field.

It happened in India three years ago; it is happening in Vietnam now.

Back when Apple was still out of reach for most Vietnamese consumers, Apple-authorised resellers (AARs) were the ones doing the leg work for Apple. And they thrived on the country’s love for the brand.

Apple shipments bucked the smartphone trend by growing 12% for the first quarter of this year, while all other rivals suffered.

The Vietnamese’s love for Apple was rewarded when it launched its official online store in May.

While Apple lovers celebrate, AARs are on the brink of losing a significant chunk of their business.

Apple’s game in Vietnam—and its collateral damage—is almost templated to its strategy in India, where it first set up an online store in 2020.

If India is any indication, Vietnam’s AARs must start gearing up for worse. Apple launched its first physical Apple Store in April this year, leaving other resellers in India scrambling.
