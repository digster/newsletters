Trust is also a large part of parenting. Let me tell you a story.

My 5-year-old keeps me on my toes. The other day he changed his dad's phone password and forgot. One day he locked me in the washroom, and ten days back, he locked the AC remote. As a working mom, I get worked up at times, but nothing like last Wednesday when I got a call from his school.

"Ma'am, your child was missing from the class for more than an hour". I almost stopped breathing.

"He and two of his friends were found playing on the see-saw behind the junior section. We found him after a lot of searching".

I heaved a sigh of relief, but this was serious. I knew he would blame the other kids. In the evening, I spoke to him. "I know you would never leave the class without informing the teacher, what happened?"

He looked at me for a second and said, "Ma'am didn't allow me to play during lunch, so I convinced Manit and Arnab to join me". I wouldn't have got that confession had he not trusted me.
