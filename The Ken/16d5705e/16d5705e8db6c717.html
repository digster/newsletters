An essential ingredient of our subscriber-publisher relationship is trust. Trust that we'll bring you one good story a day.
 
Trust is also a large part of parenting. Let me tell you a story.
 
My 5-year-old keeps me on my toes. The other day he changed his dad's phone password and forgot. One day he locked me in the washroom, and ten days back, he locked the AC remote. As a working mom, I get worked up at times, but nothing like last Wednesday when I got a call from his school.
 
"Ma'am, your child was missing from the class for more than an hour". I almost stopped breathing.
 
"He and two of his friends were found playing on the see-saw behind the junior section. We found him after a lot of searching".
 
I heaved a sigh of relief, but this was serious. I knew he would blame the other kids. In the evening, I spoke to him. "I know you would never leave the class without informing the teacher, what happened?"
 
He looked at me for a second and said, "Ma'am didn't allow me to play during lunch, so I convinced Manit and Arnab to join me". I wouldn't have got that confession had he not trusted me.
 
Parenting teaches one a lot. Including time-management. So, let's quickly get down to recapping our stories this week, shall we?
 
Our Monday started with a bang with Arundhati's story on Google Pay, an upstart within Google that has gone on to get an outsized share of the payments market. The payments regulator NPCI, however, isn't too amused. It is, therefore, contemplating a never-before-attempted move to restrict companies that run on UPI to a 33 % market share cap, both in terms of volume and value of transactions.
 
On Tuesday, we looked at Medgenome's new TB diagnostic test-SPIT SEQ and its efforts at figuring out access to a larger population. Without the approval of either ICMR or WHO, SPIT SEQ will remain limited in scope, says Suraksha in her story. It's free, so do share it widely.
 
Jon had a cracker of a story this week on Sequoia's Surge, an early-stage accelerator programme and how it's putting the focus on early-stage investment in south east Asia. A neglected region, as VCs have scaled and many firms have raised growth funds. Surge is also a tacit admission that Sequoia can do better on early-stage deals. However, investors in the region are voicing concerns that Surge graduates are pricing them out of potential investments.
 
Our contributor Nilesh Christopher, with his story on TikTok on Thursday, elaborated how the Bytedance-owned social media app is upping its brand safety game to woo advertisers. TikTok's pitch to advertisers is simple: a brand-safe environment to get new internet users excited about your brand through video ads. Read Nilesh's story to understand how Tik Tok's ad products have grown from a single product of in-feed ads in Nov-2018 to five different products now.
 
And, finally on Friday we had Seema talk about the disruptive CAR-T therapy for cancer patients which is being worked on by Immuneel, a company started by an enviable team of author Siddhartha Mukherjee, Biocon chief Kiran Mazumdar Shaw and Boston VC Kush Parmar. With $15 million in venture philanthropy from other HNIs, the founders are looking at a breakout event as early as 2020. Until now, CAR-T cell therapy has largely been geared towards the developed world. Read Seema's story to understand how Immuneel intends to change this.
 
That's all for this week. Do share your stories of trust. I'd love to read them.
