What a difference eleven years can make. 

For individuals, companies and the businesses they operate in.

Not to go on a tangent here, but eleven years back, I was idealistic, young, hardworking and not making much money. Only two of those four things have survived this passage of time. I keep telling myself this; it could be worse. Sometimes I believe it too. These are times, when I think about businesses, reading up on them, viewing them through the prism of time. I mean, take a look at telecom. In the year 2007, 200 companies in India had applied for a mobile license. Stories from back then talk about a mad scramble. How everyone wanted to get a hold of some spectrum and get into the business. Diversification some called it, for companies large and small. An under-penetrated market they said, there will be space for everyone. 

For a long time, spectrum was king. And kingmaker.

And here we are now, in the year 2018. What was once the crowd is now a pecking order. Only three players remain in the Indian telecom business. Bharti Airtel. Vodafone with Idea. Reliance Jio. And well, spectrum isn’t what it used to be. It is no longer rare. Better still, nobody seems to need it. For now. There are various reasons for it, and you’ll read about that in today’s story. From my colleague Shashidhar. 

You’ll also read why this is a bit of a spanner in the plans laid out by the telecom regulatory authority of India (Trai). Trai wants another spectrum auction, which will include airwaves for fifth-generation mobile technologies—more commonly known as 5G—which can offer data speeds of up to 100 Mbps. Trai has even recommended base prices for the same. But according to executives and analysts The Ken spoke to, the spectrum up for grabs is likely to find no takers.
