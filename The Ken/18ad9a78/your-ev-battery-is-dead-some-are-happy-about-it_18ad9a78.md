Good Morning Ishan,
Similar to how tyre manufacturers and spare-part providers benefit when car sales soar, India’s lithium-battery recyclers anticipate a bright future, thanks to the increasing adoption of electric vehicles (EVs).
EVs have graduated past their introductory phase in India. For the year ended March 2023, the country sold roughly 1.2 million EVs, a 154% rise from the previous year.
Shaswata—the author of today’s story—found proof of this swiftly rising industry boosting the country’s battery-recycling landscape.
For instance, when my colleague, Anushka, visited battery-recycling plants in Uttar Pradesh, she found that EV batteries contributed over double what they used to until last year.
Similar trends extend throughout the industry, leading to a spate of existing companies like Batx Energies, Exigo Recycling and Attero Recycling, among others, significantly expanding their operations.
However, every expansion plan has challenges. The most significant one, in this case, is the need for a proper infrastructure to collect e-waste in India. Experts, Shaswata spoke with, peg the unorganised sector’s dominance to be at 80% of the whole market.
Today’s story will take you inside a recycling plant, introduce you to the intricacies of extracting “black mass” and then zoom out to give you a complete picture of an emerging industry that sure needs your attention.
