Good Morning Ishan,
 
When you are drowning and are in dire need of a lifeline, you jolly well don't get to call the shots. You to do whatever it takes. 
But then, what is asked of you in return makes the lifeline look like a millstone around your neck. So is it worth being saved? 
 
This was the quandary in front of struggling vernacular app marketplace Indus OS last year when $1.5 billion-worth listed mobile adtech platform Affle decided to pay $2.8 million for an 8% stake in the company. 
 
The investment came with a troubling rider. 
 
All that money would have to find its way back to Affle by way of a licence fee Indus had to pay for using Affle's platform. But Indus found little use for Affle's technology and began to look for more amenable partners. Enter Walmart-owned payments startup PhonePe, which was willing to pay $60 million for a majority stake in Indus. Affle was on board. 
 
Till it wasn't. 
 
PhonePe and Affle are now engaged in what looks to be a long legal tussle. As Arundhati reports in this juicy story, Indus is not the only investee to feel stymied by Affle. Android keyboard maker Bobble AI and App Studio, a developer of apps, had some unpleasant surprises, too. 
 
For a company that has spent over $85 million on acquisitions and strategic investments since 2018 and gets half its revenue from acquisitions, this doesn't augur well. 
 
And it does even less so for entrepreneurs who don't have the option to say no to it: https://the-ken.com/story/the-kerfuffle-with-affle/ (13-minute read)
