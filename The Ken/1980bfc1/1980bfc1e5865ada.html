There’s something about magical birds and golden eggs (or feathers). These avian creatures bestow wealth on mere mortals—as long as we don’t become too greedy or impatient. The same motif appears in Aesop’s fables, Buddhist tradition, Persian folklore, and Russian myths.

Prudential has its hands on one such moneymaker, and it has been patient.

ICICI Prudential Asset Management Company is preparing for its IPO, and it will carry a valuation of Rs 1 lakh crore, or $12 billion. At a reported Rs 10,000 crore, this will be one of India’s top 10 public issues ever.

The UK-based Prudential Corporation Holdings will sell 17.6 million shares, or 10% of ICICI Prudential’s capital, after achieving a 1,000X gain on its investment from nearly three decades ago. Following the leads of Fidelity, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, and ING, it’s taking its chips off the table of India’s mutual-fund sector.

But the UK firm also has plenty of reasons to hold onto its stake. ICICI Prudential’s profit for FY25 was Rs 2,650 crore, 75% more than two years prior. It has been able to grow quickly and consistently, suggesting the stock price could keep rising after the company gets its ticker symbol.

ICICI Prudential is one of the top-performing asset managers in India’s mutual-fund industry, which makes the motive behind Prudential’s choice a mystery. Anand did some detective work and dived into ICICI Prudential’s 439-page draft IPO document. Find out what he discovered by reading today’s story.
