I’m Aditya, a Product Manager at The Ken, and I’ll be introducing this week’s episode of Cost to Company because it’s a topic I deeply relate to—The Product Manager is a Politician.
I have been in the product team of The Ken for over a year now. Here are the skills I included in my CV when I first applied:
- Software Development
- Data Analytics
Six months in, though, I realised none of these really mattered. That the single most important skill I had to acquire to be a good PM was communication. Most of my time and energy go into aligning all stakeholders around one vision, strategy, and timeline. And this is precisely what today’s episode focuses on.
In this week’s episode of Cost to Company, Sneha presents a rousing story of war and politics in Assam and, before you know it, makes it about me and every other product manager I’ve ever known.
|
|
|
Sneha talks to some brilliant product managers in this episode (leaders I personally adore)—Brijesh Bharadwaj from FamPay (builder of Dunzo Daily), Anuj Rathi from Flipkart (builder of Swiggy One), Priyanshi Poddar from CRED, and Vikram Bhat from GoodWorker. Together, all of them explain why a product manager is a politician.
Some of my favourite questions that get asked (and answered) in this episode are:
- What are the non-technical skills of a product manager?
- Are some white lies part of the job?
- How significant a role does selling the narrative to all stakeholders play?

What's coming next
In this week’s upcoming episode of First Principles, Rohin Dharmakumar, CEO and co-founder of The Ken, interviews Naveen Tewari, founder and CEO at InMobi, India’s first unicorn.
I had the opportunity to meet Naveen Tewari before this episode was recorded. Meeting the founder of a truly global company built out of Bengaluru left me grinning ear to ear.
The first thing Naveen told us was that he was just coming from lunch with InMobi employees who had been with the organisation for five years. Now, this may not seem impressive at first. But then, he told us that the number of such employees had increased so much that he could no longer host them at his home. In an age where employees switch every few months, Naveen’s InMobi is retaining talent for 5+ years!
