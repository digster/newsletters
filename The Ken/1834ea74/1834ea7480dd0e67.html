Good Morning Ishan,
 
Pop culture portrayals of journalists and journalism tend to skew pretty heavily towards individual accomplishment. Intelligent, intrepid, and alone. That’s the archetypal field reporter for you.  

And as far as tropes go, it’s not entirely untrue. Everyday journalism is collaborative, but it’s crammed to the gills with flashes of individual brilliance. 

Every so often, though, people and circumstances come together in just the right way for teamwork to work its magic—where the sum of the parts turns out to be far greater than the whole. 

It’s one of the coolest things you can see. Like this Byju’s story my excellent colleagues pulled off this Thursday. 

When I logged off on Wednesday night, the world’s most valuable edtech startup had just released its long-delayed FY21 financials to select media publications (conveniently avoiding sending it to The Ken). A few hours later, on Thursday morning, I woke up to the most detailed story on the development yet—four writers, two designers, and a desk editor had come together to create some eleventh-hour magic (along with yeoman’s service from many others). 

This story by Gaurav, Shruti, Arundhati, and Seetharaman breaks down seven essential takeaways from the financials, going far deeper than just the headline numbers. It’s a must-read, don’t miss it.  

Monday’s story on the OG Adani firm—Adani Enterprises Ltd—is a team effort too. The Adani Group may have grown into a sprawling web of interlinked subsidiaries, but as Seetharaman and Anand show, not all of them are created equal. AEL isn’t the group’s most valuable company. But it is at the centre of it all—housing most of the group’s new businesses and powering them with mammoth debt.  

On Tuesday, Shruti turned the lens on Byju’s Softbank- and Temasek-backed rival Unacademy. Its problems are different from the ones Byju’s is confronting but no less troublesome. Hit by slow growth and a funding crunch, the company has turned to its tutors to drive sales. It would be an understatement to say they aren’t thrilled. 

Arundhati’s Wednesday story, meanwhile, breaks down the intricacies and implications of one of the largest deals in the Indian banking segment—Axis Bank’s US$1.6-billion takeover of Citibank’s India consumer business. The deal is resulting in the largest migration of credit card users in the history of Indian financial services, but, as always, things are easier said than done. 

And to wrap the week off, Pratap on Friday delivered another zinger from the telecommunications space on Friday. Videoconferencing major Zoom, he writes, is poised to acquire India’s much sought-after unified communications licence. The licence will allow Zoom to offer cloud-based voice, video, messaging, and telephony services, and chip away at the domination of India’s telcos Jio, Airtel, and Vi. 

I hope you have a top Sunday reading these pieces, and stay tuned for a whole new batch of fresh stories.
