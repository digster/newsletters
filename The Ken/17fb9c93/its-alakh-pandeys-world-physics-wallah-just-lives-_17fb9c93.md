What’s the first name that comes to your mind when you think of JEE and NEET test prep in India? The brick and mortar institutes of the world like ALLEN and Narayana? Or is it new age edtechs like Unacademy and Vedantu?
Have a long enough conversation with anyone in the test prep space, and you’ll realise that the real brands behind these big institutes are the teachers they dearly hold on to. As one senior industry executive once told me, if a teacher leaves an institute, they take 30-40% of the students with them.
Few individuals come to mind as an example of this phenomenon than Alakh Pandey, dubbed Physics Wallah. The brand has garnered over six million subscribers on YouTube; its two-year-old paid app has about a million paid subscribers.
For the best part of eight years, while the bigger test prep brands were peddling discounts through their aggressive sales tactics, Physics Wallah—now rebranded to PW—offered its courses for free. Even now, the student traffic that gets funnelled to the company’s paid app pays a fraction of what Unacademy or Vedantu charge for their courses.
Things are going so well over at the bootstrapped PW that Pandey wants to change lanes to that of a venture-funded edtech. The company has earned revenues comparable to those of giants like Unacademy while spending next to nothing on marketing. And now big-name investors like Westbridge Capital and GSV Ventures are flocking to its very first venture round—PW is set to raise $100 million at a billion-dollar valuation.
But building a sustainable business would mean moving out of Pandey’s shadow. As one test prep rival put it, “Will students flock to the platform if Pandey’s not the one teaching?” Meanwhile, with VC money comes VC expectations.
