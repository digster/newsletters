Social media profiles either tell us nothing meaningful about a person, or they tell us a lot. Depends on how one reads between the lines—or words.

RBL Bank’s CEO R Subramaniakumar (RSK to his colleagues) only uses six bank-associated terms in his Twitter profile—it begins with “Techno Banker” and ends with “banking business strategist”.

Like me, if you are inclined to see it as a tautology, I’d urge you to read Rounak’s story today.

It turns out all those terms sit right on his public bio. RSK was called in by the Reserve Bank of India in June 2022 to turn around the then 79-year-old private bank. Under his leadership, RBL posted its highest-ever quarterly and annual profits in FY2023.

Counterintuitively, central to RSK’s turnaround plan are the bank’s 517 branches across the country. Earlier, customers would use them mainly for deposits and third-party services, such as insurance, mutual funds, and foreign exchange. Now, these branches have become asset-driven.

Their share in the lender’s overall income at the end of March 2023 has grown 10X, and the bank has plans of more than doubling this chunk. But not by opening more branches (which are, of course, eating into its operating profits) and unlike its competitors IDFC First Bank or Indusind Bank.

The career-PSU banker’s top-down instruction was to make RBL branch managers function like public-sector bank branch managers. Be the human touchpoint in the customers’ digital journey.

RSK met at least 1,000 employees in his first month at the bank. And though it was expected that the new CEO would mostly hire fresh talent, the veteran banker surprised everyone by promoting people internally to steady the ship.

A year later, the task before the “strategist” is far from over—RBL is yet to capitalise on what is its cheapest source of capital.
