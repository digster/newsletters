The Big Story

Why more 40-somethings are becoming ‘unwilling retirees’

With careers now lasting only 20–25 years, those in their 40s and 50s are grappling with longer job hunts, mounting stress, and financial pressure, finds The Ken’s survey

Professionals aged 40-60 are facing prolonged periods of joblessness after losing a job, with challenges in transitioning to new roles. They say they face discrimination in hiring processes and have limited opportunities for career advancement. And for employees in sectors such as pharma, retail, manufacturing, and banking, now grappling with a shorter career span, retirement is a distant dream. [Read the full story here]

The Green Transition

Xiaomi’s electric car is a risky ride in the world’s biggest EV market

The Chinese smartphone maker wants to be the heartbeat of smart homes. To that end, electric vehicles are the latest on its agenda

Xiaomi released its first electric vehicle series, the SU7, in late March, pricing it at US$30,000, and by the end of the first week of April, Xiaomi had delivered almost 1,100 cars. The SU7 was designed to fit into Xiaomi’s range of smartphones and home appliances. This is now part of a trend in China’s EV industry where carmakers and phone brands are converging. Tata Motors got big EV orders from cab companies. So, why didn’t cab companies switch to EVs?

Limited options, absent subsidies, and the persisting lack of charging infrastructure are some of the reasons

There are more takers for electric vehicles—evident from the more than a million EV registrations in the past couple of years. Cab aggregators seem keen to deploy more EVs in fleets with the likes of Uber India inking a pact with Tata Motors. But reduced government subsidies, tax benefits, and a lack of public infrastructure are proving to be roadblocks. [Read the full story here]
