With careers now lasting only 20–25 years, those in their 40s and 50s are grappling with longer job hunts, mounting stress, and financial pressure, finds The Ken’s survey

Professionals aged 40-60 are facing prolonged periods of joblessness after losing a job, with challenges in transitioning to new roles. They say they face discrimination in hiring processes and have limited opportunities for career advancement. And for employees in sectors such as pharma, retail, manufacturing, and banking, now grappling with a shorter career span, retirement is a distant dream.
