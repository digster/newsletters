Starting a modern mid-to-large size hospital in India is like having a child. It takes somewhere between 15-20 years for it to hit maturity. Till then it needs active investments, support and nurturing from its parent. The parent, though, usually gets just 5-8 years from its investors in which to multiply and return the money they bet on its child.
Raising successful children (read: hospitals) is thus a delicate balancing act between 3 factors: raising money from investors (since setting up a hospital isn’t cheap); using that capital and your experience to accelerate growth and promote maturity; rinse and repeat.
"I’m very conscientious about my investors also making money. I’ve had a track record. I’m the only guy in the country, across all business groups, to have raised 16 rounds of private equity. With 14 exits. All of them have made money,” says 46-year-old Ranjan Pai, the head of the education & healthcare focused Manipal Group, to Seema, our editor.
In an exclusive, candid and detailed "The Interview", Pai offers up a masterclass in understanding Indian hospital healthcare. Seema, who apart from being the co-founder and editor of The Ken, is also one of India’s foremost healthcare & pharma journalists, acts as a great foil to Pai’s candidness through her sharp and informed questions. Their conversation covers vast ground:
- Why is it that Manipal has never managed to acquire any Indian hospital chain over the years, in spite of wanting most?
- Is it acquiring Medanta?
- What’s the best way to value a hospital? EBITDA multiple? PAT multiple? Revenue multiple?
- What ails listed Indian hospital chains, all of which have profit margins in the single digits?
- Is universal insurance in the form of Ayushman Bharat a boon or a curse for hospitals?
