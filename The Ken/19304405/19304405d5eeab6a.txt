Rich Indians have found a new investment class. And they’re wearing their bets on their wrists 
 By Rounak Kumar Gunjan https://the-ken.com/writers/rounakthe-ken-com/

“How do you check if a VC is doing well these days?” 

“How?” 

“You just look at their wrist.” 

Good morning Ishan, welcome to another edition of Ka-Ching!. If you didn’t get the joke, don’t beat yourself up. I didn’t at first either. The two venture capitalists I met while I was reporting for my stories had to explain it to me. 

Apparently, maxed-out, wallet-killer watches are the new fancy cars of the age. 

“You can’t carry the car into a meeting, right? The watch communicates success.”

That conversation was a few days ago, and the Baader-Meinhof effect made sure I started noticing all the luxury watches around. And there were quite a few. Celebrities are one thing—Shahrukh Khan, for instance, attended the Locarno Film Festival in Switzerland this week, and it’s reported https://www.hindustantimes.com/lifestyle/fashion/shah-rukh-khan-luxurious-audemars-piguet-watch-is-worth-crores-find-out-its-price-101730600523206.html that he was sporting a Rs 4.2 crore (US$500,000) luxury watch; a limited edition Audemars Piguet. The company apparently made just 25 of these. A little while earlier, he donned https://www.hindustantimes.com/lifestyle/fashion/shah-rukh-khan-extravagant-watch-costs-crores-son-aryan-khan-brand-event-dubai-exact-price-tag-brand-name-101730102009487.html a Rs 2 crore Patek Philippe watch at an event in Dubai.

But that’s Shah Rukh Khan. 

That kind of bling is to be expected. 

But it’s not just Khan who is stacking up luxury watches. Rich Indians, it appears, have developed a keen taste for them. And it’s not just for the flash.

A new splurge

Artwork, jewellery, vintage cars, rare wines and whiskeys, rare collectibles… India’s 1% spend quite a penny on all of them. But what’s really got them hooked are luxury watches. According to a Knight Frank report released https://www.cnbctv18.com/india/indian-ultra-rich-invest-17-wealth-in-luxury-items-report-19155861.htm in February and a Deloitte report published https://www2.deloitte.com/in/en/pages/consumer-business/articles/india-emerges-as-key-destination-for-swiss-watchmakers-deloitte-report.html in July, luxury watches now rank first among all passion investments made by wealthy Indians.

Both reports say that the trend is primarily led by young folks who prefer discretionary spending on luxury experiences, fashion, and products. Added to that, of course, is the status signalling/bragging rights that come with it. The 1% club has even increasingly shifted to gifting luxury watches during festive occasions and weddings. You remember the Anant Ambani wedding, right?

A spokesperson for trade industry body Confederation of All India Traders (CAIT) says that even among multi-brand watch stores, enquiries for luxury watches have really shot up. “Earlier the trick was to keep some luxury watches on display to attract customers so that Indian, relatively cheaper, watches could be sold. Now the more expensive ones are driving sales.”

And while certainly there’s rising demand, supply has also become a lot smoother. India signed a Trade and Economic Partnership Agreement (TEPA) with Switzerland, Iceland, Norway and Liechtenstein on 10 March, and it has opened the doors a bit wider for imports from Switzerland, of which watches are a major part. With TEPA set to reduce customs duties over the next seven years, Swiss watches are expected to get far better market access. 

But do you know the other thing that’s really working in favour of luxury watches? 

Let me quote a Mumbai-based financial planner I spoke to: 

“These rich folks are not stupid, right? Not every purchase is for showing off. They also make money through them.”

A smart splurge

Not all of them do, but some luxury watches certainly command much higher prices in the secondary market. For instance, the financial planner pointed out, a Rolex Oyster Perpetual costs anywhere between Rs 7 lakh to Rs 13 lakh, depending on the customisations, but “you can easily sell these for above Rs 25 lakh.” The price of used watches can rise even more if the model is pulled out of production.

In fact, the limited edition watches that the likes of Khan, Anant Ambani, and Mark Zuckerberg have been seen flaunting sell at a premium of at least 500% on the secondary market. And each of them come with a retail price tag of more than Rs 1 crore.

According to the same Knight Frank report I quoted earlier, luxury watches have delivered compounded annual returns of 9% over the past 10 years, outperforming other passion investment classes. “While watch prices shot up 137% in this period, vintage cars rose 118% and diamonds clocked 13%,” said the report.

If you know what you are doing, the planner told me, these watches can deliver as much or more than 15% annualised returns over a 10-year period. Which could well beat the 13–14% of a Nifty or a Sensex, but clocking those kinds of gains aren’t simple. 

Not every luxury watch can be seen as an investment. For instance, Rolex’s stainless steel watches, once the rage, have very low resale value now. Similarly, Bvlgari watches, priced above Rs 5 lakh, were once considered hot property, but don’t command the same interest anymore.

So you need to be choosing the right models. 

But that’s just step one. 

Right now, the models and ranges that do command blockbuster premiums in terms of resale value are those like Patek Philippes, Richard Milles, Audemars Piguet, and of course, some models of Rolex. 

Getting your hands on one of those can be a real game of patience.
