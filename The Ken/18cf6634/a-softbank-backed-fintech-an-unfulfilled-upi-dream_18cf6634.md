Good Morning Ishan,
I have always felt that Softbank-backed Juspay, the payments tech company, missed out on a crazy big opportunity.
In 2016, when UPI was just a concept, Juspay built the payments app BHIM in three weeks to show the world what a UPI app can really do and encourage more companies to take its lead.
But with BHIM, the fintech stayed focussed on what it knew best—building the tech infrastructure. Sure, the app brought it a lot of attention and helped win clients such as Amazon, Groww, and Cred. But what if it could have been the next Paytm or Phonepe?
After reading Shaswata’s story today about Juspay doubling down on mobility, I feel the company wants a do-over.
Juspay first waded into mobility in 2021, and now, it has ride-booking apps, such as Yatri in Kochi, Yatri Sathi in Kolkata, and Namma Yatri in Bengaluru, Tumakuru, and Mysuru. It is also looking to expand to the National Capital Region and Chennai, a person close to the company told The Ken.
More importantly, the company plans to hive off its mobility business into a separate vertical.
Read today’s story to know why Juspay chose mobility and how it wants to cement its reputation in the mobility wars: [https://the-ken.com/story/a-softbank-backed-fintech-an-unfulfilled-upi-dream-and-a-new-hope-with-namma-yatri/](https://the-ken.com/story/a-softbank-backed-fintech-an-unfulfilled-upi-dream-and-a-new-hope-with-namma-yatri/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (8-minute read)
