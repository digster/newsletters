Good Morning Ishan,
 
I hope you're safe. I hope those you love are well. And if you and your inner circle have been spared the ravages of the pandemic currently choking both lungs and crematoriums, I hope you have not grown numb to the suffering of others. We're in this together, and we'll make it out together.
 
During the initial stages of the first wave of Covid, and now again during the second, I've felt disconnected from my job. Don't get me wrong—I love what I do, and couldn't ask for a better team. It just felt bizarre working on stories about billion-dollar valuations and unit economics when those around you are dropping dead or losing loved ones—either due to a once-in-a-generation virus, a broken system or both. Then again, there is our Covid coverage—some of the best in the country, though I may be biased—which brings its own rage and sadness.
 
It took a while, but I've made my peace with this seeming disconnect. For one, these stories serve as a reminder that, as apocalyptic as this all may seem, the world continues to spin. They also make for an effective distraction, however briefly, from the chaos we're engulfed in. Personally, the work serves as an anchor, keeping me grounded and (for the most part) functional.
 
I'm sure many of you have grappled with similar issues. How have you coped? What has helped you? Write in and let me know. Like I said, we're in this together.
 
With that out of the way, let's get into the stories from this past week.
 
On Monday, Bhumika and Sumanth wrote about India's first and only social commerce unicorn, Meesho. Flush with funding from SoftBank and also backed by Facebook, Meesho seemingly has the world at its feet. Its numbers, however, indicate that Meesho still has many challenges ahead of it to live up to the potential its illustrious backers see in it.
 
Tuesday saw Rohin run the rule over Reliance Jio. While the last few years saw the telco rise to a seemingly unassailable position, 2021 has not been kind to the company. Its subscriber growth has slowed to a crawl, it has a surfeit of low-value users, and its next big hope—a low-cost smartphone—stands delayed.
 
Sumanth took a scalpel to Zomato's draft red herring prospectus on Wednesday. While the company was keen to highlight that its average order value has spiked and contribution margins have turned positive, a closer look at the numbers reveals a company with falling revenue, shaky unit economics, and no clear path to profitability. What does this mean for Zomato's IPO prospects?
 
Seema and Anand teamed up on Thursday to write about a company that has dominated the news cycle for the past year—Serum Institute of India. While the pandemic may have propelled the Pune-based vaccine maker into the stratosphere, it's also in the midst of geographical, product, and tech diversification to stay ahead of the curve.
 
We ended the week with a free story from Pratap on India's dismal cybersecurity efforts. Despite the high-stakes nature of cyber attacks, India's defence against them remains a hodgepodge of bureaucracy, inadequate infrastructure, and a glaring absence of strategy. 
 
Of course, there was also our weekly newsletter, The Nutgraf. This week, Praveen wrote about the battle India and South Africa have teamed up to fight at the WTO, and why it may not do much to help the country in the short-term, at least.
 
Get vaccinated, stay safe, help others.
