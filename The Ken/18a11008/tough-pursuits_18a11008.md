Good Morning Ishan,
I’m moving to a new house for the second time in six months. The entire process of looking for a suitable place you can call home, even if it’s for a short while, can be quite frustrating. At times, it feels like uprooting oneself.
Moreover, you have to juggle so many considerations. The place must be just right, ticking all the boxes—not too hot during summer or too cold in winter. It should be well-ventilated, with just the right amount of sunlight streaming in. The location is paramount—not far from the markets and shops, yet isolated enough to live peacefully.
Iterating through multiple locations, discarding each until the perfect fit appears, is taxing.
Last week’s stories were about how companies—both big and small—are trying to find the sweet spot in pursuit of growth.
In Monday’s story, my colleague Kaushal delved into how Star Health—India’s leading standalone health insurer—has been growing despite a huge number of customer [complaints](https://the-ken.com/story/angry-customers-are-bad-for-business-just-not-yet-for-this-us4-4b-health-insurer/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter). However, this growth may not last for very long.
The Ken did not publish a story on Tuesday, 15 August, as it was India’s Independence Day and, therefore, a national holiday.
On Wednesday, Tata Consumer Products’ chief executive Sunil D’Souza was in the spotlight. In an interview with Seetharaman, he talked about how [innovation](https://the-ken.com/story/how-tata-consumers-sunil-dsouza-put-product-launches-on-steroids/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) and agility have transformed the company, which has seen 13% compounded annual revenue growth and a three-fold rise in its share price.
For Thursday’s story, my colleagues Rounak, Nathan and I plugged into the less-explored, nascent world of [electric tractors](https://the-ken.com/story/giants-and-minnows-compete-in-indias-electric-tractor-race/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) in India and the automakers gunning for the pole position. On the one hand, there are heavyweights such as the Murugappa Group and Escorts, and on the other hand are disruptors such as Autonxt and Bullwork.
On Friday, The Ken published Seema’s “[Counterthesis](https://the-ken.com/story/wait-until-you-collapse-why-this-founder-ceo-is-busting-the-cardiac-care-myth/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter)” interview with Anand Madanagopal, the founder of Cardiac Design Labs (CDL). Inverting medical conventions, Madanagopal believes early detection can help prevent conditions where “acute care” is needed, especially heart-related ailments. CDL’s devices for monitoring heart rhythm and vitals can do that at a fraction of the cost of alternative methods.
We will be back with more tomorrow. Have a great Sunday, and try to catch some rest.
