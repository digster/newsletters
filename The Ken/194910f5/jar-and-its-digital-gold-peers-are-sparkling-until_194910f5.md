Good Morning Ishan,
When Jar burst onto the scene in 2021, the app made buying gold—or the digital version of it—as easy as making a UPI payment. One could just sign up with their phone number and buy fractions of gold for as little as Rs 10.
After chugging along in its initial years, Jar appears to have reached an inflection point. A month ago, Nishchay Ag, co-founder of Jar, tweeted that the company’s annualised revenue had leapt 10X in a year. Digital-gold sales for the industry are said to be about Rs 600 crore a month, not far behind gold exchange-traded funds (ETFs).
The surge in digital gold is not surprising since gold outperformed almost every major asset class in 2021, and the government stopped issuing sovereign gold bonds this fiscal. It also helps that digital gold has been comfortable in its regulatory no-man’s-land.
If ease of use is what propelled Jar and its peers like Gullak in the first place, that may soon be under attack, as Archishma and Anand argue in their [story](https://the-ken.com/story/jar-and-its-digital-gold-peers-are-sparkling-until-regulators-come-knocking/) today.
