Good Morning Ishan,
 
As a journalist, I grew up watching the rise of a star doctor-entrepreneur. The media couldn’t have enough of Naresh Trehan, the cardiac surgeon in India’s national capital region.

So, four years ago, when I watched the rumour mill grind non-stop on his desire to sell his stakes or that the private-equity (PE) investors in Medanta Hospital needed an exit, I paid close attention, like many others, to the story.

Then 72, Trehan wanted a strategic buyer; PE investors wanted an exit at a certain valuation. A few advanced-stage talks fell through on the valuation misalignment. In 2019, Trehan and the team wanted a 24X Ebitda multiple; they didn’t get it.

Today, less than six months after Global Health (the holding company) listed on the stock market, it’s trading at a 24X Ebitda multiple. Of course, it’s been four years since the private exit talks, but the share price has appreciated 43% from its subscription price. And investors are loving it.

The hospital gets a premium because of its strong cash flows and profit margins owing to its payer mix. As much as ~63% of its revenue comes from cash receipts, and the remainder from insurance payments and government schemes. This trumps its listed rivals. Cash receipts amount to 38% of the payer mix in Max Healthcare Institute, 39% in Fortis Healthcare, and 40% in Apollo Hospitals Enterprise.

In its first earnings release last month, the company reported a remarkable December quarter in terms of revenue. That was its best performance ever, even though it’s a slow quarter for most hospitals.

A lot of things are going well for Medanta, but a lot of things are going to change for the star-doctor-led hospital chain as it branches out in smaller cities and gets back to completing its Noida hospital, a 2015 project put on the back burner.

In his story today, Gaurav Tyagi has dug up some interesting numbers and developments, including Medanta’s recent partnership with a real-estate firm in Indore.
