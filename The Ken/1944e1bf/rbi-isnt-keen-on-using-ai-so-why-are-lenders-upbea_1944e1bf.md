Good Morning Ishan,
“Wall Street job losses may top 200,000 as AI replaces roles,” screamed the sad headline of a Bloomberg story yesterday evening as I started writing this email.
Hardly a day goes by without us being reminded of the unstoppable march of artificial intelligence and the massive disruption it is bringing along with it.
The sound of the coming change is often unpleasant to hear, like the job cuts news yesterday. But AI will also usher in huge positives, we are told, across lives and businesses.
Aakriti’s story today is about AI making its way, slowly but steadily, into Indian banks with the promise of many positives. From quicker loan processing to easier compliance management to better credit monitoring and many more use cases, a host of young AI startups are now offering products and services to make life easier for both banks and their customers.
But, and there’s always a but, the RBI is watching—with hawk eyes, as usual. The country’s most feared regulator may not be too keen on the use of AI, at least not yet, in the scores of entities it oversees. Last month, a committee formed by it was tasked with “assessing the adoption of AI in financial services, identifying potential risks, and determining the compliance requirements for financial institutions.” That sounds like a lot of restrictions are coming, even if the framework goes by the liberating name of Free-AI. The RBI’s caution may well be justified—it’s been proven right many times in the past. And, to be fair, there’s much to be cautious about with AI.
So, why are lenders upbeat about AI when the regulator doesn’t seem keen? Will the positives outweigh the negatives? Can convenience trump caution? Can the twain meet?
