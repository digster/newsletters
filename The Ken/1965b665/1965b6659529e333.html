Have you ever shared your full web browser history with a colleague, friend, or family member?

How about the full contents of your personal email inbox?

Maybe the list of all your professional contacts?

You’re probably thinking that it would be outrageous to do any of that, but the fact is it’s happening right now. It’s a trade-off for convenience, and many in India are willing to pay the price.

The way your data is handled by the apps installed on your phone is not secure. Your name, address, biometric and financial details are all shockingly easy to access. This precarious situation is illustrated—literally—in today’s story.

In March, software developer Pea Bee discovered how app-based startups are bypassing Google Play restrictions to find out what other apps you have on your phone. Five software developers corroborated this for The Ken. If you’re a customer of Swiggy, for instance, its app checks whether 154 other applications are installed.

This knowledge about you and your habits is used to make decisions about how to treat you as a user. Your loan application could be turned down, or maybe you’ll receive eerily personal notifications. In all cases, it’s extremely invasive.

Abhirami worked with The Ken’s design team to show you just how exposed you are. Check out their visual story to find out how the apps you use are working against you.
