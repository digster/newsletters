If you wanted to invest in a mutual fund, where would you start? Talk to your investment advisor if you have one. Or look up a personal-finance column. Or ask that one friend who always knows where you should invest your money. Or just Google “best mutual funds”.

When I did that, among the top results were rankings on fintech platforms Groww and Paytm Money*. In November, these platforms, including Angel One, added 1.3 million systematic investment plans (SIPs)–that’s half the overall numbers.

That’s how central the fintechs have become to the $615 billion mutual-fund industry. But how they are drawing in investors is making many uncomfortable, including fund houses.

Reason? Those rankings that make decision-making so easy for investors.

Just a few months ago, brokerage Kotak Institutional Equities declared that mid- and small-cap stocks had ventured into the risky “bubble” territory.

Yet, many popular fintech platforms are nudging investors towards these bets, flaunting “best return funds”, “top-rated funds”, “curated lists”, and more, all without the proper disclaimers and disclosures.
