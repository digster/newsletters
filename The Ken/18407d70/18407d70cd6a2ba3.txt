on the episode of First Principles with Razorpay founder Harshil Mathur

Good Morning Ishan,

Happy Diwali! 🪔

Most of our team is on a break for the festival, so we aren’t publishing a podcast this time around. But we thought it’d be a nice moment to introduce something special we’ve been working on—podcast transcripts!

We launched podcasts almost three months ago, and First Principles http://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles was one of the two shows we launched with. It’s a podcast where Rohin Dharmakunar, CEO and co-founder of The Ken, sits down with a new business leader every episode to talk about the lenses they use to look at the world around us. 

Ever since we first launched the show, providing transcripts for these episodes has been a consistent request from a lot of people who prefer reading over listening. So that’s what we’re doing this Diwali—dropping the transcript for our latest episode with Harshil Mathur, co-founder and CEO of Razorpay, who joined us last week to talk about a bunch of different themes. 

From his love for coding and his early days as a rogue programmer, to the accidental journey of building Razorpay, and much more around product development and company culture. 

There’s one specific part of the episode where Harshil says, “… and usually when data and anecdotes don’t match, it’s the data that is wrong.” I’ve been thinking about that quote ever since. If you want to listen along, you can tune in on Apple Podcasts https://podcasts.apple.com/in/podcast/first-principles/id1639125773?i=1000583242391, Spotify https://open.spotify.com/episode/2Q8suTTHvwRXrfHteQMH7X?si=da52bd7bca264a94, or wherever else you get your podcasts.

Here are some themes that we touch upon in this episode.

How does Harshil define Razorpay?
	The founding of Razorpay—how the love for coding and a drive to find real solutions helped build the company 
	The breadth of what Razorpay offers customers and why it does so
	Razorpay’s principles of product development—how the company finds customers, builds solutions, and accepts failures. 
	The importance of thoughtfully, and deliberately, building company culture.
	Y Combinator’s role in Razorpay’s journey, and 
	A really fun set of short questions at the end 

All of this and more is in our transcript. You can read it here: https://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles/harshil-mathur-razorpay/ https://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles/harshil-mathur-razorpay/
