Good Morning Ishan,
Today’s story opens with Shilpa, who operates a tailoring shop in Bengaluru.
Shilpa is a success case for the lending industry. With no Cibil score and no collateral, she had been turned away by many banks when she sought credit in the past. But Kotak Mahindra Bank analysed her payment history and transaction patterns, and determined that she does, in fact, deserve a loan.
That same data has since been used to craft a hyper-personalised marketing campaign that targets Shilpa.
From the banks’ standpoint, it’s a natural extension of determining whether an applicant is loan-worthy. After all, applying AI to marketing has “zero regulatory friction, faster ROI, and abundant data” to draw from, said BG Mahesh, the CEO of industry body Sahamati.
You can expect to see advertisements—from banks like HDFC, Kotak, and even microfinance players like Chaitanya India—that not only anticipate the types of services you need, but are even packaged with messages that strongly resonate with you. If your profile suggests you’re ready to buy a vehicle, for instance, the next ad for a loan that shows up on your screen might feature an actor doing just that.
There’s a risk that this way of targeting consumers will become manipulative, and there are ramifications for ad agencies and adtech companies.
