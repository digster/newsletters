Good Morning Ishan,
 
Have you ever wanted to learn something so badly that a little part within you would never give up? Is that desire powerful enough for you to make that dream come true, regardless of your age, situation, or the city you are in?

Let’s call that little part of me Red. Red has focused on nothing but learning to play football ever since I started my full-time job in 2019. 

Initially, it was difficult to find places that would train women in their 20s to play the sport, but Red wanted what it wanted. In 2022, I stumbled upon Prakrida—a football academy for women of all ages. Red was over the moon. 

Whenever the team is short of players, the coach makes sure we still play, even if it means playing with 6-year-olds. And let me tell you, defeating those kids is no piece of cake. 

It’s been a month since I have been training, and very soon, I will be playing in the upcoming women’s football tournament. I’m kind of in disbelief, yet overjoyed at the moment.

Talking about overcoming hurdles, India’s leading fintech, Slice, finds itself trapped between banks, regulators, and products. Gaurav Noronha started the week with the story of the fintech trying to find its way through a complex path. 

On Tuesday, Arundhati wrote about Freshworks, which wants to achieve US$1 billion in recurring revenue by 2023-24. But it’s only halfway there. A tech-sector slowdown and a pursuit of profits are seeing the SaaS company painstakingly change its employee-friendly DNA.

Rounak, on Wednesday, reported how Digit is taking a two-pronged approach—one, an initial public offering and two, a foray into health insurance—to realise its ambitions of becoming a full-fledged general insurer. But, it has yet to instil confidence among the non-urban and middle-aged customers, something that its older, more traditional competitors enjoy.

On Thursday, Isa wrote about the Philippines’ BPOs moving out of the national capital region Metro Manila to far-flung cities such as Iloilo and Dumaguete. As a result, Manila’s office vacancy rates have hit record highs, impacting businesses that make their living around BPOs. 

Gaurav Tyagi ended the week with his story on the changing face of the Indian finfluencer. First came the entrepreneurs, former executives, and management graduates. Now, even fitness and beauty influencers are dispensing financial advice on social media without any checks.

Circling back to Red, it did not give up despite a hell lot of self-doubt and practical hurdles. Now, at 26, I train for football four days a week—all thanks to Red. 

That’s it from us this week. Do let me know if you love something the way I do football.
