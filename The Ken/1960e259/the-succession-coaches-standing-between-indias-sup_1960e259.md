Good Morning Ishan,
You’ve heard of this curse: the first generation starts a business, the second generation runs it, and then the third runs it into the ground.
One way to prevent that, some believe, is for the founder to hold on for dear life and maintain a grip on their business empire for as long as possible. The major drawback in that case is that heirs don’t develop the skills and experience they need to keep the business going. When it’s finally the scions’ turn to take the reins, the wheels fall off, or some members of the family feel like they have less authority than they should.
Or, in other cases, maybe the heirs just aren’t interested in taking the lead.
It’s a pressing matter for affluent families and the businesses they control. India now has 850,000 people whose net worth is over $1 million. Among them, close to 13,000 have more than $30 million in assets.
That’s where succession coaches come in. They formulate strategies to pass leadership roles down the bloodline while keeping each family member’s interests intact. The ultimate objective is to ensure their businesses endure, no matter who is at the helm.
For today’s story, Nuha spoke with close to 20 coaches and advisors who have helped nearly 1,000 of India’s wealthy families map out succession plans.
