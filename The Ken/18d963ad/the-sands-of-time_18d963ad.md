Good Morning Ishan,
Before I get started on the week that was, let me tell you about something new in the offing. The weekday morning email is how we have been introducing our long-form stories since inception. It’s a habit-forming gateway to our journalism for our subscribers. You may have already noticed the change in how the weekday email looks, in line with the redesign of our website and newsletters.
Starting tomorrow, it will read different, too. It will still be personal, punchy, and informative. But it will also be a lot more than that. Stay tuned!
Moving on, today’s recap email is special to me. It was the week when I celebrated my “almost 40” birthday.
To be honest, I’m not the reflective, philosophical type. Very little patience for it. But birthday is the only time of the year that somehow manages to put me in that mode.
Two weeks ago, a media-house representative called to tell me I had been selected for their 40 under 40 awards. They had called last year as well.
Once again, I politely declined to participate (while reminding them not to call next year as I wouldn’t qualify).
Why did I refuse? Last year, I learned that the “selected” participants had to submit a self-nomination form and pay Rs 40,000 (US$480) to register. The money wasn’t significant, but what it implied was.
A few years ago, I wouldn’t have bothered with such details and probably jumped at the opportunity. Ambition > any little voice in the head. But that was then.
Now, the voice in the head trumps anything else.
Change is constant and inevitable. People, circumstances—everything is shifting, and so are you. What’s important is how desirable the changes are.
This birthday, I spent time reflecting on these changes and the people/events which shaped them.
What do you think of change? “If nothing is broken, why fix it?” or “Perfect is the enemy of good”. Let me know your thoughts.
