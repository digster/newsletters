Good Morning Ishan,

Man, it is Thursday already. Don’t you think this week just went by? Like, sped by. 

Anyway, I’ve often thought about this: when you are looking at the big stuff, and of course, there’s always the paucity of time and some multitasking, you miss out on the small, little things. Those that can deepen your insight or give more data points for analysis. 

So, at one level, you can talk about Alibaba and Tencent and draw some pattern of the Chinese being bullish in India and investments in unicorns. But then, when someone draws your attention to the smaller companies, the small investors and what they have been doing or not doing, you suddenly go, aha, that’s interesting! I didn’t even know that thing existed. 

The idea for today’s piece came from that realisation. Also, an event that my colleague Arundhati attended last year where several Chinese investors showed up in Bengaluru. To understand India and see if there are any investment opportunities. 

So, we asked Venkat to get some reporting done on this subject. He’s come back with some interesting points. For instance, 37 Chinese investors have put their money into Indian startups since 2015. A total of 62 funding rounds. Plus, two corporate venture capital investors, Fosun and Shunwei have been fairly active in India. Others, not so much. 

Venkat’s story goes deeper into how these investors have fared and what founders think of them.
