In the spotlight

What is Shark Tank India’s draw for startups already flush with VC money?

Sony wanted its hit reality TV show to democratise entrepreneurship in India. But investors, founders, and even the sharks have something else in mind altogether

Following Shark Tank India’s success, VC-backed startups are increasingly treating the show as a stage to reach consumers. These startups don’t need the cheques cut by sharks. In fact, such deals can even mean more trouble than they’re worth for investors and founders. Meanwhile, Sony is trying to ensure that Shark Tank’s marketing opportunity doesn’t end up overshadowing its investment platform. But it’s fighting an uphill battle—one that may undermine its true appeal for founders, investors, and even the sharks. [Read the full story here]

Go Digit’s IPO is staking its future on going against the market tide

The insurtech unicorn’s upcoming stock-market listing rides on zagging into the lucrative but challenging world of group health insurance while the industry mostly zigs in retail

The insurtech unicorn is steadily shifting its focus away from its core business of motor insurance. Eyeing market share, it has chosen group health insurance to quickly grow its health insurance book. But group health business is a “bleeding segment” with even legacy general insurers going for retail health insurance. Go Digit’s aggressive underwriting to acquire group health business is likely to turn the tide against its newly achieved profitability. [Read the full story here]

The EV story

Why did Vinfast double its 2024 EV-sales target after missing the 2023 goal?

The Vietnamese carmaker’s billionaire founder is keen on pushing the EV narrative, but it’s mostly his own companies on board

Vietnamese EV maker Vinfast has fallen short of meeting its ambitious sales targets in 2023. Yet the company announced multibillion-dollar expansions with the financial backing of Vingroup and its affiliates. Vingroup, whose board is chaired by Vinfast’s CEO Pham Nhat Vuong, and its affiliates account for about 82% of Vinfast’s car sales. The company, short on cash, will see a US$2 billion infusion from its CEO. But the only way out of its many problems is like an upstream swim. [Read the full story here]

A new arena

What happens when two south Indian coaching giants take their playbook to the north?

Sri Chaitanya and Narayana are out to conquer new territories, but their winning formula begs a million tweaks

For over three decades, south Indian coaching-prep behemoths Narayana and Sri Chaitanya have capitalised on Indians’ obsession with competitive exams. They’ve done so through two distinct models—junior colleges (integrated exam and board prep for Class 11 and 12) and Techno schools (schools with advanced coaching curricula). They are now bringing this fervour to northern markets, setting ambitious growth targets for national expansion, especially post-covid. But the unfamiliar new markets are presenting some unique challenges that go beyond the expertise the group has developed in the south. [Read the full story here]

Stories you don’t want to miss

What is suddenly sending shivers down the spines of 350,000 CAs?

A young and feisty regulator that is coming for them

Since 2022, NFRA has debarred 78 auditors and has imposed Rs 16.6 crore in penalties. Earlier, during ICAI’s tenure, only 25 rogue auditors faced action in over a decade, with around 1,400 cases left pending. The current clampdown from regulatory authorities has caused fear among the auditors, leading to a spurt in resignations, which have shot up over 4X in the five years leading up to March 2022. And the problem is rooted in their scope of work. [Read the full story here]

Kreditbee told a profitable story by dishing out small loans. Now, it must look at safe loans

The lender must secure its portfolio as it gears up for its IPO, even though it already makes solid profits from its loans

Bengaluru-based Kreditbee, which dishes out unsecured consumer loans, turned profitable in the second half of FY22. Non-interest income sources (such as processing fees) accounted for a bulk of its profits till the year ended March 2023 due to its short-ticket loan expertise. Over 10 non-banks have partnered with the fintech to disburse loans through co-lending, leveraging its tech for borrower data. But with the regulator zeroing in on unsecured lenders, Kreditbee is venturing into unfamiliar territory by rejigging its loan portfolio. [Read the full story here]

Sea’s fintech arm was once just its sidekick. Now, it’s a budding star

Sea Limited’s digital financial-services arm has found a steady earnings footing by straying from its own ecosystem

Seamoney, Sea Ltd’s fintech arm, made nearly US$149 million in adjusted Ebitda in the first quarter of 2024. The company has expanded from facilitating e-commerce transactions to offering distinct financial products. Unlike fintech peers from other tech giants of the region, Seamoney has been successful in extending its services into wealth management. But to get a pie of the region’s increasingly competitive fintech space, Seamoney must embrace innovation. [Read the full story here]
