Good Morning Ishan,

I've been PMSing for a good week now, I think. And I have all these grand plans of swimming this weekend.

About six years ago, I wouldn't have been able to go for a swim during my period. But thanks to the sanitary products market finally expanding to accommodate products like tampons and cups, that's no longer a problem.

I'd usually not be droning on about me but today's story is about urban women with an income that can support sanitary choices suited to their lifestyle. And I fit the profile.

Not so long ago, menstruation for women like me (who have more-or-less comfy periods) meant a sticky, soggy, uncomfortable 3-7 days coupled with pain, anxiety, rashes, oh god whatnot. (Yes, I need to be that graphic.) And while one had the agency and the funds to buy alternatives or period care products that can help deal with these issues, there was nothing to buy in the Indian market. Hell, period has been a taboo subject (and market) for so long, no one was quite working to better it.

A lot has changed since. Enough to make period care products marketable if not popular. EcoFemme, a social business which makes washable cloth pads in Auroville, claims 44.63% growth since 2015, which was also the year it started its online store. ‘Chemical-free pads’ brand Azah, launched as recently as November 2018, claims 40-50% growth each month. And while these companies are still fairly small, they’re reaching their customers in ways the FMCGs (your Whispers and Stayfrees) aren’t. They have one-on-one channels between the buyer and the company via social media and monthly subscription programmes, eliminating the need for women to settle for a product that’s just about okay.
