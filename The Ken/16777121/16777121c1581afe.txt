…to your life. Good Morning Ishan,

As we transition to an on-demand e-commerce delivery economy, your life is laid bare at your apartment community’s security gate. Your food and grocery deliveries pass through it. Your apparel and electronics deliveries pass through it. Your workout coach and part-time cook pass through it. Your friends and guests pass through it too.

While most of its peers were focusing on providing boring community management products no one wanted to use, one clever company realised that letting people in and out of apartment communities was a big problem. And thus, a big opportunity. It’s called myGate, and if you live in Bangalore, it can be hard to miss it. In startups and tech campuses, you’ll see people remotely letting people into their communities through the day. 

But myGate says it isn’t interested in this data at all. Its founders categorically reject any possibilities of selling customer data or letting advertisers target them. “We believe in our product,” says a co-founder, “and we don’t need the clutches of advertising to support us.”

Basis this promise, they recently raised $9 million in venture capital. But, is the nondescript security gate a big enough market for VC-sized ambitions? Or is it just a wedge to something bigger? That’s Vivek’s story today.
