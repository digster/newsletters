Good Morning Ishan,
Reliance Brands, a unit of Reliance Retail, has an enviable hold over luxury fashion in India. From Balenciaga and Bottega Veneta to Paul Smith and Salvatore Ferragamo, Reliance Brands is the local licensee for more global couture labels than you can imagine.
So you’d expect Reliance to be the partner of choice for high-end watchmakers too.
But no. The retailer poised to cash in on ultra-rich Indians’ growing fascination for brands, such as Rolex, Jacob & Co, and Hublot, doesn’t have the backing of a big conglomerate like Reliance or Tata-owned Titan.
Instead, Ethos is a Chandigarh-based independent retailer that has been in the business of selling watches that cost more than entry-level cars long before it caught large retailers’ attention.
The market couldn’t be more different now.
Indians imported Swiss luxury watches worth Rs 3,000 crore in 2024—25% more than in the previous year. So, can you really fault investors for almost quadrupling Ethos’ market value to Rs 6,800 crore in the three years it has been a listed company?
