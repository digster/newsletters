When the pandemic shut down the world in 2020, Reliance Retail opened up its cap table, setting off a gold rush. Marquee investors from across the globe lined up, and money poured in at eye-watering valuations. It was an opportunity that was seemingly too good to miss.

The behemoth backed by India’s richest man went on an acquisition spree, expanding and racing at breakneck speed, leaving rivals far, far behind in size and scale. The investor hurrah continued. In 2023, Qatar Investment Authority bought a stake in the company at a $100 billion valuation, double what investors pegged the company in 2020.

The stage was set for a grand IPO at a cool $125 billion peg.

Except, over the last year or so, the tide turned, and analysts halved the giant’s valuation to $50 billion. The IPO may need rescuing, and fast.

Scale is not equal to success, after all.

For an entity from a group where “growth is life”, Reliance Retail has been struggling on multiple growth parameters. The pain is most apparent in the company’s online business.

From Jiomart to Dunzo, most of Reliance Retail’s online bets have flattered to deceive. Ajio, the company’s online fashion business competing with the likes of Myntra, was not in this list of also-rans, so far.

But it too seems to be headed that way.

A do-something, do-anything approach and shifting goalposts in the pursuit of elusive profits are taking a toll on what is perhaps the last man standing in Reliance Retail’s online empire.

That can’t be helpful for the parent’s listing plans.
