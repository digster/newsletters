Good Morning Ishan,
“Only the paranoid survive,” screams a quote plastered on the walls of Eternal’s office in Gurugram. At Hyperpure, that paranoia is steeped in people’s DNA.
Hyperpure is the B2B raw-materials company that was acquired by Eternal (then Zomato) eight years ago. It was primarily a restaurant supplier tied at the hip with Eternal’s food-delivery business. But its focus has since shifted to fresh-produce procurement for Blinkit.
Since then, Hyperpure’s fortunes, too, have grown in sync with Blinkit’s. In FY25, Blinkit contributed majorly to Hyperpure’s revenue, fuelling a 5X growth in the company’s top line since 2018. In the December quarter, Hyperpure even turned profitable, reporting an adjusted Ebitda of Rs 1 crore on a revenue of around Rs 1,000 crore.
But in the first quarter of FY26, Eternal announced that its quick-commerce arm would start untangling itself from Hyperpure to source its own inventory.
Now after years of brotherhood, Hyperpure and Blinkit are in for a dicey separation. Read Nuha’s [timely story](https://the-ken.com/story/hyperpure-rode-blinkits-coattails-to-the-top-until-blinkit-shrugged-it-off/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) on what led to this separation and what it entails for Hyperpure’s future.
