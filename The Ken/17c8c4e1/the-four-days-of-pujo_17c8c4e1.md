As a Durga, it's always an amusing time of the year for me. I start seeing parallels everywhere—and I'm about to take you through one.
As you'd know, this week marked the start of the Hindu festive season—Durgotsav being a rather prominent part of it. While Durga Pujo—as my Bengali people like to call it—ends, it also marks the beginning of a new era.
Though The Ken took the day off for Dussehra this week, our stories running up to the day spoke of ambition, promise, and… junk food. Not too far from how Durga Pujo pandals work, if you ask me.
Anand's Monday story—true to the spirit of Shoshti—unpacked an emerging new order. This one in
[Indian aviation](https://the-ken.com/story/air-india-akasa-and-the-emerging-new-order-in-indian-aviation/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), thanks to the likes of Air India and Akasa. India's airline industry is seeing some massive changes with Air India re-entering the Tata stable, Jet Airways’ phoenix act, SpiceJet running on empty, and Go First in the midst of a power tussle. Go on, you know you want to read it.
On Saptami, when crowds (pre-pandemic) would throng pandals in search of yummy food they can eat—come on, only two more days of Pujo left—Maitri wrote about how
[junk food giants](https://the-ken.com/story/how-junk-food-giants-took-on-a-toothless-fssai-and-won/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) have won against FSSAI. (Yes, that runs a bit counterintuitive to the good vs evil Pujo narrative.) India’s food safety authority wants consumers to know how much sugar, salt, and fat is in their packaged food. But industry players and consumer advocates think otherwise. Maitri, though, took to helping people out via Twitter.
