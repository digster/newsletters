Good Morning Ishan,
In a business littered with the corpses of famed rivals such as Dunzo and Swiggy Genie, Porter has lived to tell the tale. The logistics and transportation company—with its own share of near-death experiences—managed to survive, then thrive. The numbers bear witness. Porter’s daily orders and revenue grew about 15X in the past five years and in FY25, it finally did something many companies in India still struggle with. It posted profits, even if just.
Investors have been taking note and loosening their purse strings. A few months back, Porter—a decade-plus old but still unlisted—raised a neat $200 million, taking its total haul so far to over $300 million. Another $100 million could be on its way.
The company, a unicorn now, pulled off what it did through a series of reinventions, both in the business and in the org. So much so that it’s now basically two companies rolled into one, shaped by the divergent-yet-convergent philosophies of its two co-founder CEOs, first Pranav Goel and then Uttam Digga.
But if Porter thought it could rest on its laurels, it couldn’t be more wrong, finds Rahul in his sharp debut for The Ken today. A new set of deep-pocketed, formidable rivals—the likes of Delhivery, Rapido, Uber, Shadowfax—is now circling over its core competencies.
