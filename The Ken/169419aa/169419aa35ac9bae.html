Good Morning Ishan,
 
“Give us enough time and our best-supported decisions will seem wobbly, our certainties whimsical.”
 
That’s Julian Barnes in his book The Sense of an Ending. He wrote it; I have just been thinking about it. And about how some of my decisions have turned out to be, let’s just say, a little bizarre. The money I spent on that watch I was sure I wanted, the Bluetooth speaker I was going to use all the time and the dress that was going to pay for itself in six months. All gathering dust and reminding me of the importance of making calculated decisions. Till the next time I go on a little retail therapy spree, of course. 
 
As much as I appreciate my colleague Pradip for putting me onto this book, Barnes makes me wonder if my calculated decisions would be any better. With time, I may start liking my watch or develop a tolerance for loud music. After all, we all change over the years to keep up with the world around us.
