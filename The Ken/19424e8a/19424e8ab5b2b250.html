Plus, we love the worm’s-eye view as much as the big picture. Last year, Seema wrote about private-equity firms’ rapidly expanding hold over healthcare in India.

In her companion piece today, she gets down in the weeds to unpack a healthcare chain that is replacing one PE owner with another. Five years after it bought over 60% in the listed cancer-care chain Healthcare Global, CVC Capital Partners is selling its stake to KKR.

But nothing changes for Healthcare Global’s oncologist-founder BS Ajaikumar, who owns 15% of the company along with his doctors. He doesn’t believe in cancer treatment being little more than hospice or palliative care. Healthcare Global has slashed its mortality rate from 3.4% to 0.7% since 2017 while doubling the number of patients it discharges every month.

What’s more, Ajaikumar sees no reason why cancer care shouldn’t be affordable to more people. “Our back-of-the-envelope calculation shows nearly 50 crore (500 million) people can take treatment at an HCG centre and go home and sleep in their own beds,” he told Seema in an expansive chat.

Even when multi-specialty hospitals are the order of the day, Ajaikumar is wedded to his “focused factory” approach to oncology. “When a patient comes, doctors mostly go by protocols. But cancer is a complex disease. You must understand the story behind the story: why a patient does or doesn’t respond to a treatment.”

The interview is filled with fascinating insights. Don’t miss it.
