L. Frank BaumвҖҷs childrenвҖҷs novel, The Wizard of Oz, has come to mean a lot of things. But the one thing in his book that has stood out, steeping into the public consciousness in the 120 years since its publication, is the concept of the yellow brick road.

Follow a certain path, and youвҖҷre sure to succeed. ItвҖҷs an apt metaphor for IndiaвҖҷs renewable energy ambitions.

Between IndiaвҖҷs internal target to grow solar power generation 8X by 2022 and its plans of making 30% of IndiaвҖҷs vehicles electric, the country has ambitions in spades. The path ahead is clear.

The Emerald City in this overused metaphor, though, isnвҖҷt the achievement of these ambitions. Because thereвҖҷs one critical ingredient missing. Storage.

For anything in IndiaвҖҷs plans to workвҖ”whether it be solar energy or EVsвҖ”it first needs to figure out how it will store energy. With China already having cornered the lithium-ion battery market, India is scrambling to get on the manufacturing wagon.

For sure, it has earmarked an eye-watering Rs 18,100 crore ($2.4 billion) production-linked incentive (PLI) for battery manufacturing. However, the plans are hobbled, not just by the dearth of knowledge and resources, but also by the lack of an ecosystem.

вҖңItвҖҷs not going to be enough to manufacture cells in silos. If there are 10 companies making batteries, we need another 100 companies to create the full ecosystem,вҖқ says the president of a clean energy think tank.

And that may take the better part of the next decade.

India has its yellow brick road. But what not many know is that there were always two; the one Dorothy took was just the more dangerous one. How India deals with its ambitionsвҖ”whether with long-term goals or a series of bandaidsвҖ”is up to it.
