Good Morning Ishan,
 
Pratap Vikram Singh, the writer of today’s story, covers telecom for The Ken. The story he’s written today is about India's data centres, but it might as well have been about India's telecom sector.

The resemblance is uncanny.

First, there is the gold rush. From global internet giants like Amazon’s AWS and Google Cloud to UK-based Colt DCS, Singapore’s Princeton Digital Group and US data centre giant Equinix. Many of those rushing in don’t have a long history (or even a short one) of running data centres, like uber-conglomerate Adani (yup, they’re everywhere) and real estate companies like Hiranandani Group, Godrej, DLF, GMR, and Lodha. This, when major incumbents were already close to a dozen in number, with the most prominent being Singapore-based STT GDC India and NTT Netmagic, each of which controls 20-25% of the market.

Around US$9.5 billion of leveraged capital is in various stages of being announced or committed towards this gold rush, estimates a recent report.

Second, there is the pot of valuation gold on the horizon. Unlike the typical ~8X EBITDA multiples in telecom, data centre businesses are valued between 15X to 20X of EBITDA. Yummy!

Third, even though India’s prices are already pretty low and cheap (US$115-140 per kilowatt of monthly capacity compared to US$170-200 in Hong Kong), all the gold-chasing operators aren’t worried about a further glut and crash in prices. At least for now.

Pratap knows how this story ended in telecom. From 11-12 operators per state at its peak, India is now down to three private operators, only one of which makes any profits—Reliance Jio.

If you were plotting India’s data centre boom on Gartner’s overused “hype cycle” graph, where would you place it?
