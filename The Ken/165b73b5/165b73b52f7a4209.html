Good Morning Ishan,
 
This was a special week. On the personal front, on the professional front, as a citizen of this large, diverse country. For me, this was special. And for all the right reasons. 
 
On the personal front, there's a part of me that just instantly gets 100x happier when the 'ber' months—or as I would allude to them back in Delhi, the 'brr' months—come around. The weather gets more pleasant, festivities are around the corner, friends, family, whatnot. It seems rather fitting then that I began this month in my home city, where it poured, much to every Delhiite's delight. 
 
It also poured there on Thursday, or more fittingly, 6/9, as a sea of happy people walked to Jantar Mantar to commemorate the Supreme Court finally reading down Section 377. The rain, for many, washed away years of pain, becoming an ally, a symbol of freedom from an unkind colonial law.
 
Coincidentally, The Ken, in this very week, carried not one, not two, but three free stories. We started our week a day earlier than usual, with a free story, keeping in step with the spirit of the 'brr' months. 
 
On 1 September, Saturday, we launched a 10-part series we'll be adding to on the first Saturday of each month. Penned by Saurabh Mukherjea and Anupam Gupta, our first offering, titled 'Tsundoku: The burden of the unread book' looked into the deep crevices of the human mind. "It fools itself into thinking it has no limits, when the truth is anything but. Understanding those limits is the key towards leading a simpler, more productive life."
 
Then came Sunday. And with it, came our The Ken on Weekends (TKOW) offering from Roshni. She debuted with this free story on the bread wheat genome and how it has painstakingly been sequenced by a group of scientists—13 years, 200 researchers, of which 18 are Indian scientists. How did they do it?
 
On Monday, Pooja wrote about alternate assets, the new cash cow for financial corporates. Even as private equity funds struggle to raise money, financial services firms—backed by their wealth management business and distribution strength—are growing their alternative asset management aggressively. Read on.
 
I was back in Bangalore in time for Shashi's story on telecom companies bundling streaming services with data packs. I remember being hugely disappointed about buying Prime Video a month before Airtel presented it to me, gift-wrapped. Sigh. Shashi's piece talks about how this bundling drives data consumption for telcos and helps streaming platforms gain viewers. But the current arrangement isn’t sustainable. How will this relationship evolve?
 
The week had just about started and we were already four stories in. So we paused for some beer, albeit in a The Ken way. Harveen wrote about India's growing craft beer business. Simba, Bira, White Rhino... beer, beer, everywhere. But where are these businesses headed?
 
On Thursday, we offered our third free story this week. Sidhartha's piece on fantasy sport and Dream11 looked into how an industry is growing underground, fast but stealthily. Now funded by Tencent, Dream11 is more than legit, but there are no regulations in place to check its betting aspect. Do we need them? Why is regulation important? This and more in Sid's story. Go on, read it.
 
Arundhati closed the week with some time spent at the co-working spaces of WeWork. I'm just glad we got her back in office. Why? Well, in-house swimming pool and free beer for starters. But Arundhati managed to see through the layers of delectable dressing. WeWork is betting big on large corporates to be its ticket to success, she says. But companies with huge piles of cash could give it the cold shoulder. Will they?
 
On that note, I'm going to go figure my beer plans. After all, one must celebrate freedom!
 
Enjoy your weekend. If you run into me at a bar, come say hi. And do read our TKOW story tomorrow.
 
Yes, it's free.
