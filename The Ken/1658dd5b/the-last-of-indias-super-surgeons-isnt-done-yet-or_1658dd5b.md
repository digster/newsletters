By any measure you pick, Medanta, the gleaming 1250-bed super-specialty hospital in Gurugram, should not have been possible. It started in 2007, when a celebrated heart surgeon walked out of Delhi’s Escorts Hospital following a nasty breakup with the Singh brothers—Malvinder and Shivinder—the new owners who would later rename it Fortis Healthcare. He bided his time and let his revenge grow cold. Two years later, he not only drew away the best doctors from his former employer, but across most of northern India. He told them he would create a hospital in India the likes of which nobody had seen. It would be India’s Mayo or Cleveland Clinic.
The surgeon was Dr Naresh Trehan, and the hospital he set up in 2009 was Medanta. Within three months of opening, it was operating at full capacity. By the third year, it was generating 22% profit margins. Along the way, it brought on board famed private equity investors like Carlyle and Temasek.
It was the stuff of TV series, not reality.
Now, nearly 10 years after pulling off an impossibility and at the age of 72, Dr Trehan wants to do it all over again. He’s currently overseeing Medanta’s first expansion—a 1000-bed super-specialty hospital in Lucknow, the capital of India’s largest and most populous, but also one of its poorest, states, Uttar Pradesh.
Does Dr Trehan’s halo still possess the power to light the fire for another Medanta; to attract the best doctors from all around? Or is he, as the chorus of rumours in Delhi suggests, looking to exit Medanta to an acquirer? Or is he really preparing for an IPO?
Like a Kurosawa movie, all of these could be true or false, depending on whose perspective you rely on. Dr Trehan's? His colleagues and peers? Large investors? Competitors?
