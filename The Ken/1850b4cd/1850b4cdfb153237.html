2022 is perhaps the only year in recent memory where I’ve heard the term ‘winter’ being used so frequently. But now that actual winter is finally setting in, I wanted to speak to you about yet another (temperature-backed?) business phenomenon: the hiring freeze. 

 

Unlike all the dubious layoffs, there hasn’t been much brouhaha around the ongoing hiring freeze. And with good reason. Most hiring freezes are unofficial; they're never really revealed to the world. But that's not to say one hasn't gripped Indian companies right now.

 

But what if this hiring freeze is actually an opportunity for employees? Is it a faster way to grow within the ranks, since the jobs outside have dried up? Or is it just a race to the bottom?

 

Are employers taking notice of this opportunity too?

 

In this episode of Cost to Company, I speak to Vanita Bhatnagar, Staff Writer at The Ken covering careers and workplaces; Piyush, who works at a major OTT platform; and Pawan Rochwani, Lead, Community, Events and Partnerships at Pepper Content. We discuss: 

 

1. Why applying for jobs, and not just hiring, is at an all-time low.

2. How employees are going above and beyond to prove themselves at work and rise among the ranks. 

3. How companies are using this velocity to create new ways to audit performance and responsibilities. 

4. The creation of ‘accidental loyalists’ at the workplace.
