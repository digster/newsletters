Good Morning Ishan,
 
One of the morning (digital) rituals I hate but can’t get away from is deleting spam emails. At least 30-40 of them in my Gmail account before I get on with the day. I’ve made my peace with it. But not my colleague Rohin, also The Ken's resident security nerd.
 
A few months ago, he decided to go down the spam rabbit hole. It runs pretty deep.
 
First some stats:
- Of the total email volume sent worldwide in September 2017, 85% was spam
- India’s share of global spam is 8.4%
- India ranks 8th in the list of ‘the world’s worst spam haven countries’ for enabling spamming
 
The Ken tracked 400 emails for two months, maintaining a record of the sender, at what time and date we received the email, whether it had a link to opt-out or not, and if it did, then what the opt-out status was. What we found was eye-opening: even the most legit and reputed brands did not provide opt-out links; for many of those that provided it, the opt-out status was a failure. And so on…
 
In short, there is no escape from the claws of spammers.
 
In today’s story, Sidhartha, with help from Rohin, shows there is no law, and hence no remedy. But the brands spamming us have their side of the stories in place.
 
This bit from the story shows how cheap access is: “For a price of Rs 2,500 (~$40), Parkdata is offering a database which contains 50 crore (500 million) Indian email addresses and 50 crore phone numbers.”
 
The Internet gave us access to everything and everyone; now everything and everyone has got access to us. Remember the Eagles song, Hotel California? —You can check out anytime you like, but you can never leave…
