Good Morning Ishan,
I recently got into an argument with a New York-based venture capitalist who said something that implied wealthy Indians don’t know how to spend money.
More specifically, he was referring to first-generation Indian entrepreneurs who’ve made tons of money but don’t go, ahem, sailing, yacht-racing, venturing into space, or buying private islands. I think I mumbled something about cultural differences between the American and the Indian way.
Boy, was I wrong?
Today, my colleagues Seetharaman and Anand write about one such first-generation billionaire—Radhakishan Damani, the reclusive founder of Avenue Supermarts Ltd, India’s second-largest grocery retailer which is worth about $29 billion.
Since its stellar public offering in 2017, Avenue—which runs the Dmart chain of supermarkets—has had a great rally for the most part on the bourses, vaulting Damani into the billionaires club. So, the writers were curious to inquire where all he parked his money.
If it’s not listed stocks, Damani’s asset class of choice seems to be real estate. Not private islands, though. He and his family reportedly bought 24 luxury apartments in Mumbai for Rs 1,500 crore ($182 million) earlier this year. He was also said to have bought a bungalow in the city for over Rs 1,000 crore ($121 million).
Pardon me, I am getting ahead of the story.
For a man who went from being a ‘bear’ in the ‘90s to emerging as a celebrated ‘bull’ himself, Damani is someone who even the late stock-market investor and billionaire Rakesh Jhunjhunwala considered his mentor. Understandably, many retail investors obsess over Damani’s stock picks.
It’s true that Avenue accounts for 97% of the Rs 1.8 lakh crore (US$21.8 billion) equity holdings of Damani and his family. Still, about Rs 6,000 crore (US$728 million) of their investments are in companies where they own over 1%. How has Damani fared as an investor there?
In what is probably the first such analysis of Damani’s investments, Seetharaman and Anand argue his biggest bets have been nothing to write home about.
