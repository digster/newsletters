On average, over 35 startups shut down every day in India. Employees move on to their next stint, but most founders find themselves unemployed, thanks to their high pay and a leadership attitude
Over 28,000 Indian startups have shut shop in 2023 and 2024, leaving most former founders without a job. That’s because failed founders are perceived as scammers, and most job-related conversations circle back to why their previous ventures failed. The situation is not India-specific. A Yale study found people with founder experience get 43% fewer callbacks than non-founders. Things go further south when founders expect higher payscales and autonomy; hiring managers are even wary of “founders’ ego”. With an IPO around the corner and 40% of its revenue from overseas markets, Lenskart is a case study in how an Indian retailer can go global
Ahead of its nearly $1 billion IPO, Lenskart posted a profitable FY25 with revenue of Rs 6,652 crore and a profit of Rs 297 crore. Nearly 40% of the company’s total revenue now comes from its 656 stores outside India. Unlike Indian peers like Zomato and Ola that failed miserably abroad, Lenskart’s slow and steady strategy has relied on selective acquisitions, investments and joint ventures. The company’s backbone is its vertically-integrated supply chain that gives it pricing power, agility in product launches, and consistency across geographies.
