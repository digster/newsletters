Good Morning Ishan,
A fund manager I recently spoke with recalled some new-fangled terms from last year’s great Indian stock market bull run. “Sales pitches bandied gems like FMEG—fast-moving electrical goods and FMCD—fast-moving consumer durables,” he said with both laughter and exasperation. “I don’t remember the last time I changed my electrical goods. Durables are meant to be durable, no?” the veteran wondered. “How could these be like FMCG?”
Fair doubt. But “fast” sells fast. And those that roll with the tag collect more valuation, even if not moss.
Some unlikely players have got on to the game too, I learnt, after reading Sudeshna’s revelation of a story today. There’s such a thing as FMCH. Short for: fast-moving consumer healthcare. And pharma major Sanofi India is among the latest to apply this FMCG playbook to medicine cabinets, so to speak. The consumer-healthcare business it had carved out from itself in 2023 got listed late last year, and voila!—the new kid on the block now commands a valuation multiple that is almost double that of the creator.
Sanofi also has company. Many of its global peers have been walking this path. In India, too, many moons ago, Zydus Lifesciences executed a somewhat similar manoeuvre and reaped rich dividends. Speaking of dividends, the French parent to both Sanofi India and its recently-listed consumer sibling is also laughing all the way to the bank.
But, and there’s always a but, is the newborn really selling FMCH? Or is it just recategorizing old wine, oops, medicines in new bottles? Why would it do that, you ask? For many more reasons than just tidy valuations, finds out Sudeshna.
