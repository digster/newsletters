Who will blink first?

You’ve seen Mexican standoffs in movies. A bunch of armed no-gooders face each other, guns drawn, each pointing at the other. Nobody’s got either a clear advantage, or a way out. The Good, the Bad and the Ugly. Face Off. Pulp Fiction. 

Something similar is currently being played out in the high stakes automotive sector in India. The protagonists facing off include large and established vehicle makers like Mahindra, Maruti Suzuki, Hero MotoCorp and Honda, collectively facing an 18-year low in sales; ambitious young EV makers and EV-ride-sharing unicorns like Ather Energy and Ola Electric; hundreds of OEM and component makers who collectively represent $51 billion, or 2.3% of India’s GDP; and the government.

At the heart of their standoff is a curiously named government policy - FAME, or "Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric (& Hybrid) Vehicles”. FAME’s intent is to nudge the industry to adopt electric vehicle technology faster, through a combination of tariff sticks and subsidy carrots. The established players and their massive OEM and component partner networks have spent billions of dollars upgrading themselves to meet India’s stringent emission norms. Now, they bemoan, they’re being asked to junk all that and pirouette their supply chains, strategies and designs in a few years from IC (internal combustion) engines to EV ones. 

When in 2018 India sold 100,000 electric 2-wheelers versus 22 million petrol and diesel ones. That’s 0.45%.

In today’s story, Olina skilfully lays out the impossible demands at the heart of FAME, and this Mexican standoff. Few established industries adopt disruptive technological change without kicking and screaming, but the current norms run the risk of ending up as a spanner in a complex supply chain that generates billions of dollars in exports and employs 1.5 million people. While it’s commendable that the government wants to push the industry into an inevitable electric future, will the current standoff end with that?

Who will blink first?
