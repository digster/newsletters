Wow. Thursday is already here. High five.
Okay, so I am totally not embarrassed to say this. Every time I think I have a fair grip on a subject, something happens or just time happens, and I find myself catching up. And it is always with this sort of bewildering sense; like, wow, that’s a lot of new stuff in such short a time...Yes. Happened again.
Till last month when everyone was going on and on about Walmart, Flipkart and SoftBank, I told myself, yep, I have SoftBank totally figured out. Except, when Sumanth’s copy came in, and I started reading up. Crazy! Here, look at everything that has happened at the company in the last month alone and tell me if you don’t feel left behind:
* After Nikesh Arora’s exit, Masayoshi San has been looking for a successor. To that end, SoftBank announced the appointment of Katsunori Sago, a former Goldman Sachs banker as the company’s chief strategy officer.
* Rajeev Misra, CEO of SoftBank’s $100 billion Vision Fund got elevated to executive vice-president. So did SoftBank’s chief operating officer, Marcele Claure.
* SoftBank invested $2 billion into General Motors’ self-drive car division, Cruise. This is a big investment which has got a lot of people talking. Because Uber is also into self-driving cars. Plus, this now sets up the ring: SoftBank and Cruise as a challenger to Waymo (with Alphabet).
* From the Vision Fund, SoftBank invested $250 million in Cohesity, an enterprise software company in the US.
* SoftBank tied up with Abu Dhabi’s strategic investment arm Mubadala Investment Company to launch a $400 million tech fund. This fund will invest in European technology companies.
* Back in India, SoftBank decided that it is going to sell all its shares in Flipkart.
* Paytm Mall, the digital commerce company received $200 million in funding from SoftBank.
Yes. All of this. In the last month alone.
Thankfully I have someone to cover for my shortcomings. Someone who is totally on top of things. My colleague Sumanth.
In today’s story, Sumanth says that while SoftBank has exited Flipkart and there is a feeling of too soon, it is a very interesting turn of events. Both for the fund and the Indian tech landscape. There is no doubt that SoftBank’s Flipkart exit is stellar, but this is a hollow victory. Sumanth says, SoftBank might have won a battle in India but it now runs the risk of losing the war.
