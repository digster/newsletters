The US-Israeli attacks on Iran since the weekend have had a crucial, even if predictable, fallout. The Strait of Hormuz—a narrow shipping route through which West Asian countries export most of their oil and gas—is all but closed, at least for now. Iranian forces have threatened that any ship trying to pass will be fired upon. Hundreds of fuel tankers are now reportedly idling, and prices of oil and gas have spiked globally.

Asian importers, including India, will be particularly impacted. 

And among the Indian companies likely to be hurt badly is a gas utility—business-to-business, seemingly boring, mostly under the radar, but crucial to the country’s energy security. 

The stock of Petronet LNG crashed almost 5% in Monday’s trade, more than most energy stocks and benchmark market indices. More pain could be on the cards if the Strait of Hormuz crisis is not resolved soon. 

The company’s specialised operations, and on whom it depends for most of its supplies, makes it especially vulnerable.
