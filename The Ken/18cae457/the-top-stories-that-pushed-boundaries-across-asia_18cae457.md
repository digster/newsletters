Good Morning Ishan,
A couple of times a week, The Ken’s writers based beyond India join a group call. We discuss the latest developments from different countries and compare notes, trading information and ideas. What does it mean for the subsidiaries of a Singapore-based company when it gets squeezed in another country, for instance? And, generally, how do we make our stories relevant to readers across Asia or even further afield?
We tried a few new things over the past year to gauge what you, The Ken’s readers, might enjoy in terms of coverage beyond India.
Our limited-run, free-to-read [newsletter featuring deep tech companies](https://the-ken.com/sea/present-future/stories/) garnered regular feedback from a group of readers we hadn’t heard from that often before—engineering and computer science students who were close to entering the workforce, eager to figure out what opportunities there were on the cutting edge.
For our regular coverage, we took care to select topics that mattered by following the largest tech companies in Southeast Asia and did deep dives on occasionally unexpected stories that we believe reveal something about the countries where our reporters are based.
It’s the last week of the year, so we at The Ken are looking back at our work from the past 12 months, too. Here are 10 picks from the international team that highlight the range of our team’s coverage.
🇸🇬 Why a tech giant is more relieved than happy as India lifts ban on its mobile game
The no-holds-barred battle royale game Free Fire was relaunched in India as a standalone title just for the country after being banned for over a year. The release came at just [the right time](https://the-ken.com/story/why-a-tech-giant-is-more-relieved-than-happy-as-india-lifts-ban-on-its-mobile-game/)—Garena, the gaming unit of Sea Limited, has been flagging, so Indian players may be the manna that it needs to stay afloat.
|
|
🇵🇭 Virtual assistants are getting half the pay and double the workload, but Filipinos are piling in
Over the past year, we’ve noticed entrepreneurs in the US and UK singing the praises of virtual assistants based in the Philippines—almost always highlighting their low wages. So, Katrina [spoke with](https://the-ken.com/story/virtual-assistants-are-getting-half-the-pay-and-double-the-workload-but-filipinos-are-piling-in/) 10 people who work in the field to get a feel for why they do it even though wages are falling and they’re increasingly becoming replaceable.
🇮🇩 The US$7.4 billion Gojek paid for Tokopedia in 2021 comes back to haunt Goto
When Gojek acquired Tokopedia over two years ago, tech valuations were peaking. The transaction involved a hefty markup for goodwill—a premium based on the expectation of growth. The result? A US$720 million goodwill-impairment loss. That’s hardly what shareholders wanted to hear, and we [explained](https://the-ken.com/story/the-us7-4-billion-gojek-paid-for-tokopedia-in-2021-comes-back-to-haunt-goto/) how the balance sheet arithmetic played out.
|
|
🇸🇬 Grab’s loyalty programme isn’t enough to sustain its growth
One of Southeast Asia’s biggest tech companies figured out how to claw a little money back from the incentives they give to users: make it a loyalty programme where subscribers receive enough discounts to justify the fee. It made Grab’s deliveries GMV hit an all-time high, but the long-term sustainability of depending on a small set of users’ extra business [isn’t as clear](https://the-ken.com/story/grabs-loyalty-programme-isnt-enough-to-sustain-its-growth/).
🇮🇩 Indonesia built its EV battery roadmap on nickel. Why is its first local battery maker not using it?
We devoted a lot of time to following Indonesia’s development of its budding EV sector. Michelle [took a look](https://the-ken.com/story/indonesia-built-its-ev-battery-roadmap-on-nickel-why-is-its-first-local-battery-maker-not-using-it/) at the way Indonesia’s first EV battery manufacturer, ABC, is building its corner of the supply chain without tapping into the country’s massive nickel reserves.
🇵🇭 Why the Philippines keeps running out of plastic cards for driver’s licenses
Isa [set out](https://the-ken.com/story/why-the-philippines-keeps-running-out-of-plastic-cards-for-drivers-licenses/) to answer a question that had been stuck in her mind for years: why are there constant driver’s license shortages in the Philippines? She went down a rabbit hole and pieced together a holistic view of the near-monopolistic business interests and bureaucracy that created the deficit that made one type of plastic card a valued item.
|
|
🇮🇩 Ready to kick-start the climate-tech engine, SEA investors are still searching for the key—entrepreneurs
Nearly every investor we speak to in Southeast Asia circles back to climate tech at some point, but there just aren’t enough founders who are creating businesses around solving climate change. The solution, as we found out, was to develop climate-tech venture builders, and there’s [more than](https://the-ken.com/story/ready-to-kickstart-the-climate-tech-engine-sea-investors-are-still-searching-for-the-key-entrepreneurs/) one way to do it.
🇵🇭 Asia’s next gambling hub reels in casino suppliers with slot-machine craze
When we found out that providers of gaming supplies were setting up shop in Manila, we weren’t too surprised. After all, the Philippines had once eclipsed Macau briefly in terms of gross gaming revenue. In this situation, as Katrina [found out](https://the-ken.com/story/asias-next-gambling-hub-reels-in-casino-suppliers-with-slot-machine-craze/), there’s one thing that’s better than being the house: it’s supplying the moneymakers that they’ll use to keep gamblers coming back for more.
|
|
🇮🇩 Indonesia’s US$1.1B school digitisation plan ended up scoring for Google and local vendors
Education reforms are crucial to the evolution of Southeast Asia’s technology space. To make that happen, Indonesia’s education ministry went on a procurement spree for electronics and laptops—but only ones that run Google’s ChromeOS. These and other stipulations [got in the way](https://the-ken.com/story/indonesias-us1-1b-school-digitisation-plan-ended-up-scoring-for-google-and-local-vendors/) of making it easier for schoolchildren to access knowledge.
🇲🇾 Malaysia vies for pole position in Asia’s medical-tourism race
We know about Thailand as a major medical tourism destination, but other countries in Southeast Asia are trying to build their own versions, too. For Malaysia, that means focusing on oncology and cardiology to stake their corner. The country is [now trying to](https://the-ken.com/story/malaysia-vies-for-pole-position-in-asias-medical-tourism-race/) lure in patients from all over the world, but it may take years to achieve that level of recognition.
