Good Morning Ishan,

 
There are weeks that go by quickly. Blink and miss. And there are weeks where you feel every minute slowly crossing you, a bit like a deviously smirking turtle crossing your path. Make no mistake, this isn't about time not passing us by, but about time making its presence extremely known as it does.
 
This week, as you may have guessed, fell squarely in the second category. We had some stellar stories, and our founders (all four) either had a story and/or worked extensively on a story. All hands were on deck, and how.
 
But weekend comes and so does respite. And after two uncharacteristically hot days in Bengaluru, so does rain. As the city gets a breather, so do we, to recharge and come back to you with a fresh set of stories starting tomorrow.
 
In the meanwhile, join us in looking back at the stories we had this week. We've had some good ones.
 
Monday had Harveen dive right into the thick of things at Harvest TV. The brand new English news channel already has a name issue, a logo problem and an unclear shareholding pattern, and there are rumours of partisan political affiliations.
 
On Tuesday, Seema wrote about Huawei, Samsung and the 5G battle in India. With over 1.1 billion subscribers and 90% of its equipment imported, India is make-or-break territory for global 5G telecom equipment vendors. This one's a free story; don't miss it.
 
Our midweek story came from Ashish. He set out to answer what may seem like a simple question but isn't: Exactly how big is Facebook in India? For a social media company that has close to 294 million users in the country, the largest user base in the world, few seem to know the scale of Facebook’s operations here.
 
On Thursday, Sidu Ponnappa returned to contribute for us. He wrote about the crumbling IT industry and our overpopulated engineering colleges. "India’s engineering colleges churn out over 1 million graduates each year. Fewer than 1% of them can code. We need to rethink tech careers," he writes.
 
Sumanth's story on Friday was about Flock. Four years after its launch, serial entrepreneur Bhavin Turakhia’s Slack clone is going nowhere fast, he writes. With murmurs of layoffs and a lack of traction, does the company have a future at all?
 
Our Saturday TKOW story came from Roshni. She wrote about chemical ecology research in India and how it may hold answers to some of nature's biggest mysteries. This one's free, too!
 
No matter where you are, I'd say use your Sunday to recharge. We certainly are.
 
Have a great weekend.
