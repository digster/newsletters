Good Morning Ishan,
Not more than half a year ago, it looked like Ultrahuman was one of the companies that had the smart-ring sector locked down. Its 2024 revenue hit Rs 620 crore, a 6X increase from the year prior.
That position looks much less secure now. The US’s International Trade Commission made an initial determination in April, judging that patent-infringement claims made by Oura against Ultrahuman are valid. The final ruling is still pending, but Ultrahuman could face dire consequences, like being banned from importing its smart rings into the US.
Oura has been pursuing legal action against Ultrahuman since September 2023.
A final ruling that favours Oura wouldn’t merely mar Ultrahuman’s reputation. The implications on the Indian brand’s business would be huge, given that 65% of Ultrahuman’s revenue is from its customers in the US.
Another 20% of the company’s revenue is from the UK and the UAE. Depending on how Oura decides to proceed after a ruling is made in the US, it could be a life-or-death juncture for Ultrahuman.
Sudeshna visited its Bengaluru facility to find out how its smart rings are made. She also dived into the court filings to unpack the biggest challenge that Ultrahuman has faced so far.
