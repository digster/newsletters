The Big Four firms—EY, Deloitte, KPMG, and PwC—have been ramping up their recruitment of partners. They aren’t looking for professionals who excel at tax advisory or audits. Instead, they’re seeking people with deep technical know-how.
A couple of figures illustrate the change: two years ago, around 5% of the new partners hired by PwC were from companies such as IBM, Microsoft, or Amazon. Now, it’s nearly 80%. In other words, a new skill set is being acquired by the Big Four.
That change reflects how clients have different demands now. The days of consultants theorising about business strategy are in the past. Now, hands-on experience with technical implementation is what gets attention. The technical talent also brings with it a sense of functioning as an extension of the client’s operations, which is a departure from the Big Four’s relationships with customers in the past.
Debanjali looked at all of these changes and how EY, Deloitte, KPMG, and PwC are attempting to adapt to the times.
