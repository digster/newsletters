Good Morning Ishan,
Double whammy: A combination of two usually adverse forces, circumstances or effects. In other words, a one-two punch, says Merriam-Webster.
It fits the current situation at Domino’s — arguably India’s most famous pizza brand and quick service restaurant (QSR) chain — to a D, sorry, T.
One, the much-loved pizza is no longer the only or even the default show in fast-food town. Upstarts like burgers, biryanis and fried chicken have been stealing a march. Two (and worse), Domino’s is no longer the only or even the default show in pizza town. Upstart chains like La Pinoz and many independent pizzerias have also been gaining advantage.
I see first-hand evidence of this. The family has switched loyalties away from the pizza. As for me, pizza still comes out tops, but I haven’t patronised Domino’s in a long time. There are many, I daresay, better choices out there — and yes, they too deliver in 30 minutes or less.
Market size squash + Market share squeeze = Not good news for Domino’s and its listed Indian franchisee Jubilant Foodworks. The stock — an investor darling during the pandemic — is down 35% since. Peer QSRs are doing better — both on financials and in the stock market.
So, what’s Domino’s doing to break out of the funk? Can rebranding, new stores, cheaper price points and format crossovers do the trick? Or has the battle of the palate been lost for good?
