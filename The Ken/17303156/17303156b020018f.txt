Good Morning Ishan,

It’s a stunning turnabout for Doubtnut. The edtech startup that 11 months ago proclaimed to be on a “path to becoming the biggest edtech app in India”, is now being acquired by the biggest edtech company in India.

On Friday, when Entrackr broke the news of Byju’s acquiring Doubtnut in an all-cash deal, it was already a hotly discussed acquisition in the edtech circle. Meanwhile, Olina, writer of today’s story, looked at an August text message from Doubtnut co-founder Tanusree Nagori —“We don’t intend on being acquired anytime soon”— and wondered what changed in 11 months.

The deal is nearly 90% done; being brokered, according to sources, by a common investor—Sequoia.

Three-year-old Doubtnut is an app-based platform to solve doubts from grade six all the way up to competitive exams. It grew so fast that between July and August of 2019, it even crossed Byju’s in total number of daily users. But it had a problem.

In Class Clowns-How the Smartest Investors Lost Billions in Education, Columbia Business School professor Jonathan A Knee says that in the education business, the first mover advantage doesn’t come from being first—it comes from gaining scale. “More often than not, being first is charity work for whoever comes later and is able to take advantage of your generous, free market research.”

Valued at over $10 billion, Byju’s is a late comer to test-prep. It chose to buy, rather than build. And bundle it along the supplementary learning videos that it offers to learners ranging from kindergarten to those appearing for competitive exams. In the financial year ending March 2019, Doubtnut earned a little over $23,000 in revenues; in mid 2020, it is being acquired for $100M.

Aggressive?

Very, say some VCs. “It’s the Surge, India-II and China playbook premium,” says one. For Byju’s,  though, complexities may just be starting over, says Olina in this insightful story.
