Welcome to a brand new week. There’s a long-running joke about consultants: A consultant is someone who borrows your watch to tell you the time and then keeps the watch.
 
I am told the joke still riles traditional management consultants. Heck, the profession has evolved. In parts, it’s under a silent siege; in parts, it effectively co-creates (I’d hear that word a lot every time I’d interview late management guru CK Prahalad) and not necessarily looks to own the watch.
 
Our story today is about another kind of consultant. One who, of course, borrows the watch to tell the time but who also makes his own watches and lends them to the high and mighty of the consulting world, who then use them to, well, tell the time, and create new watches.
 
Apologies, if I’ve gone too far with the watch. Such is RedSeer’s story. How a little-known consulting firm with the who’s who of the consumer internet world as its client— Flipkart, Amazon, Shopclues, Big Basket, Oyo, Treebo, Ola, Practo, Swiggy, Zomato, Foodpanda, Byju’s—has positioned itself to get a seat on both sides of the table.
 
Stories abound how investor Lee Fixel, of Tiger Global and Flipkart fame, would use RedSeer for his board meetings at Flipkart. And then to work the RedSeer team to make other investment decisions in India. With this anchor client, the tiny consulting startup broke into collecting primary data about a sector which was growing in leaps and bounds, but nobody had any data. Since most businesses are still private, anything flies in the name of data.
 
Today RedSeer puts a number to most of those flying claims, though it’s another matter that they often get challenged. Perhaps, which is why Arun Kumar, 2005 graduate chemical engineer from IIT-Delhi, is all ears to know, “So, what are people saying about us?”
 
Ashish’s story today is about how Kumar, once desperate for work, has built RedSeer in a space where the landscape is increasingly looking like Goliaths vs Goliaths. Let’s dive into it: https://the-ken.com/story/how-does-redseer-know-who-kicked-ass/
 
Have a nice day.
