How has your week been? I’ve been in Bangalore, and it’s blazing hot (well, by Bangalore standards), and it’s back to Delhi tomorrow, where the extra heat makes you forget the pollution. And everyone I meet nods sagely and says, “Global warming, yeah.”
What a time to be alive.
But in all seriousness, catastrophe is right around the corner, metaphorically speaking. In the next 30 years or so—by some estimates, anyway—if we don’t cut out almost all our carbon emissions, we’re doomed.
This isn’t a problem that you or I can just solve at home. (Confession: I’ve spent far too much time calculating and basking in the glow of ultimately minute power savings from my steady shift to LEDs.) Real change, our contributor Vivek Aithal writes in today’s story, needs country-sized action.
Most of the country-sized countries, India included, have been making all the right noises at least, but in the larger scheme of things, it's little more than hot air—just what we need. And so, Vivek decided to sit down and crunch all the numbers—to a borderline-ridiculous degree—to answer the question, could India switch to 100% clean energy? How?
He came up with a way, calculating just what it would take to ditch fossil fuels entirely. How many hundreds of thousands of acres of solar panels, and wind turbines, and more. Number by number, chart by chart, he lays it out. It’s a fascinating picture.
