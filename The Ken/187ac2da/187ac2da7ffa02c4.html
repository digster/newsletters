Good Morning Ishan,
 
How are you feeling? 

I am not asking because it is polite or because I am someone who works with human resources trying to assess how settled you are in your job; I am not even using it as a conversation starter. 

I am asking because I mean it. Also, because I am sad that we are detached from having genuine, vulnerable conversations. So much so that there is a significant amount of literature online that teaches people how to steer past that question politely: just say ‘can’t complain’ or ‘I am good, thanks’. On the other end of the spectrum, there is enough management literature available that encourages managers to ask after their colleagues—in several different ways—and really listen. 

As humans, we need that emotional connection. It's a primary need that can be expressed and met organically. In an ideal world, one would imagine that we would naturally bring out our feelings at work—since most of us spend a large amount of time there. But ‘how are you feeling?’ has become a tricky question, a problem to fix. Neither the question nor the answer rolls off the tongue easily. 

With that thought in mind, let me recap the stories we wrote this week.

Google and Reliance Jio have been in a complicated relationship since 2020, when the former invested US$4.5B for a 7.7% equity in Jio Platforms to bring 4G smartphones to half a billion people. Now, their biggest collaboration has fallen flat as Jio is now forging ahead with plans for 5G smartphones, possibly without the tech giant. On Monday, Pratap wrote a detailed story on why the flagship product of the duo’s alliance, JioPhone Next, is now at the heart of growing frustration among the two companies.

On Tuesday, Anushka argued that the future of the $3 billion incentive scheme for electric-vehicle (EV) makers is uncertain. In its current state, the scheme is ambiguous and faces structural problems, causing delays in disbursements and leaving automakers high and dry. 

Similarly, what once appeared to be a success story to Indonesia, which has a strategic advantage in the EV industry because of its large nickel resources, is a problem. Its first local EV-battery manufacturer is somehow managing without the mineral, wrote Michelle on Wednesday.

From ambiguities to unforeseen situations, let's move on to the tragedy that has hit small-town short video makers. In the aftermath of the Tiktok ban in 2020 and fuelled by venture capital, several short-video platforms such as Josh, Moj, and MX Takatak—and even Instagram—normalised video-making as a career option for thousands. But off late, their earnings have halved, and they are preparing for a new reality. Soumyajit maps the journeys of a few such content creators.

Finally, Seetha ended the week with the story of a B2B e-commerce platform that is “good news” for FMCG distributors. Softbank-backed Elasticrun is not competing with the distributors but trying something new instead. 

To conclude the conversation I started at the beginning of this email, I want to say that emotion is not an afterthought or something to be skirted. It is the highest form of expression, even before thought and action. For emotion has the power to really connect people, bring them together, and create prosperity.
