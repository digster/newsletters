Good Morning Ishan,
Most of Titan Company’s brands give customers bling without the sting. One of its brands, however, goes after a more discerning clientele that is more than willing to pay an immense premium.
Zoya was built with India’s 0.01% in mind. More than that, it specifically caters to women over 40 who buy jewellery for themselves, with some of its “most sought-after” collections carrying price tags of Rs 10–20 lakh.
Its pieces are limited-edition or even one-of-ones, and they tend to have bigger rocks. The customer experience is highly exclusive, with the brand’s “relationship managers” being very hands-on: birthday dinners, safaris, and other perks are par for the course.
Fifteen years after it was established, Zoya turned profitable in FY25. More importantly, that milestone tells us something about the current state of India’s luxury-goods sector.
Zoya’s business head, Amanpreet Ahluwalia, told The Ken that the brand will open three to four more stores every year—a significant ramp-up from its current 12 stores. Meanwhile, global players like Cartier and Forevermark are also expanding their footprint in anticipation of affluent Indians splurging on gems, jewels, and general brilliance.
