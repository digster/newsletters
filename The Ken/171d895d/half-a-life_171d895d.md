It has all become way too easy. On Friday, India’s government extended the lockdown for the third time.
There was no announcement by the Prime Minister. No built-up anticipation, no clapping, banging of vessels, or stress to the electrical grid. Just a silent, if sad, acceptance that we’ve pushed the inevitable to yet another date.
With that, another empty weekend is drawing to a close in red Delhi. Another two weeks of no haircuts, packaged noodles, and diet coke; intermittent cleaning sprees; and days gone by without a sheet folded, or a cushion fluffed. I’d like to turn the page. But the biggest virus of all is infecting my brain—the fear of life being half-lived.
That’s when it helps to turn to the value of the work we do at The Ken. Our stories, our newsletters, our daily calls about how to decode what’s happening around us, for you.
Despair is only a moment. Ideas are momentum. Words are a passage.
That’s why I am so eager to tell you about the stories we did this week. It has been our attempt, from the very start of our Covid-19 coverage, to bring forward what’s next.
