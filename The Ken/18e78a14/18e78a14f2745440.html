Good Morning Ishan,

If I tell you, a CA today is losing out to an MBA on one end and a CFA on the other, I am going to get a lot of hate mails. But I’ll say it nonetheless because I have Nathan’s story to fall back upon.

Accountability for accountancy is on a decline and the demand for it to bring business acumen and effective communication to the table is on the rise.

The crown that chartered accountants held previously over all other financial certification courses in India is now up for grabs. There’s been a nearly 15% rise in the number of registrations for the Chartered Financial Analyst certification across all levels in six years to the year ending March 2024.

With the lowering of the age limit for level-1 exams, even someone as young as 19 years can enrol for CFA, and they are coming in large numbers. The total new registrations for L1 (also called NL1) saw a jump of 43% in the year ending this month.

The ICAI, the governing and accreditation body for CA, is putting up a fight and enacting changes. But the horse has left the barn: the CAs now want an “F”. And the unprecedented increase in the number of new, young investors entering the Indian stock market has ensured that the horse is racing not on one, but two tracks. Do check out Nathan’s story.

When is TB news good news?

More than 100 years after the BCG vaccine for tuberculosis hit the market, there’s hope springing for another TB vaccine.

Clinical trials for a new vaccine, MTBVAC, started in India on Sunday. Unlike the BCG vaccine which is meant for children, this new vaccine is being tested to see if it can prevent the disease in adults and adolescents. As you must be aware, India has 27% of the global disease burden.

I was witness to a frantic drug hunt when a colleague needed TB meds for the X-DR (extremely drug-resistant) variant of the disease for his family member. The newly launched drug wasn’t sold in India by Janssen then and had to be imported. Being a Delhi journalist—you know how in that city everyone knows somebody who knows somebody who knows somebody with clout—he managed to import the medicine but I’ve lived in fear of the infection since.

The vaccine is jointly developed and tested by the Spanish biotech Biofabri and Hyderabad’s Bharat Biotech. Barring a brief period of controversy around the latter’s Covid-19 vaccine, Bharat Biotech has had a long history of developing vaccines. Its R&D muscle has been strengthened during the years of collaboration with international health charities.

Some of those agencies are part of the current trial as well. I’d hope that like in old times, we’d get to see the trial data and results transparently even if this trial coincides with India’s high-decibel sloganeering on eradicating TB by 2025 (which is technically not possible).

Let science, only science, prevail.
