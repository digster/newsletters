Good Morning Ishan,
After reading Aakriti’s debut story for The Ken, I went around my house, seeking out Dabur products. The obvious ones: Dabur Red Toothpaste, Dabur Vatika, and Dabur Pudin Hara. The others: Odomos and Real Fruit Power Juice (I admit I didn’t know the first one was a Dabur brand).
The result? Almost zero. I did find a container of Chyawanprash—that thick, sticky concoction my mother insists on stuffing into my bag every winter.
The story, and then the finding. I was convinced that Dabur’s present is not as storied as its past. It is possible that my bias against all things “Ayurvedic” skewed my view.
So, I called up my mother and sent her on the same treasure hunt. Half an hour later, she responded: “We’ve got four. Amla and Vatika hair oils, Red Toothpaste, and Real Juice.”
OK. So, Dabur’s brand does have some spunk left. But it is mostly in the hearts of our folks living in non-metros. For millennials, it’s a nostalgic trip. And for Gen Z: “Dabur, what?”
Even the company’s chief executive Mohit Malhotra said, “It [above 40 age-group] is the belly of our business.”
My mother sent in another text: “We’ve also got eight Patanjali products. Just letting you know because it may be imp info.” It is—because today’s story is also about how Dabur is no longer the mass brand synonymous with Ayurvedic products.
The 139-year-old company is worth $12 billion. And it has an identity crisis.
The evidence is staggering: shares have been underperforming the Nifty FMCG Index. HUL, its bigger rival, has nearly 4X brands in the $120 million sales club.
But it is ready for a comeback, determined to distance itself from the “Ayurvedic” label and lure a whole new generation. By diving into fresh, premium categories.
Is it a tad late to the party, though? While most companies are counting their pennies amid a global slowdown and inflation, Dabur’s deciding it’s time to splurge.
It’s a fascinating story of the survival and evolution of a supercentenarian company: [https://the-ken.com/story/dabur-seeks-gen-z-approval-to-shed-boomer-image/](https://the-ken.com/story/dabur-seeks-gen-z-approval-to-shed-boomer-image/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) [10-minute read]
