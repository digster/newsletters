A term sheet is a nonbinding agreement, signalling a formal intent to invest. It outlines the terms and conditions under which such an investment will be made.

Term sheets are usually given by investors to startups as a precursor to detailed due diligence.

But an urban legend has it that there was an Indian startup, a unicorn no less, that used to issue term sheets to investors. Not only was this unusual in itself, but it was also almost bizarre that the term sheet was "exploding". In that, the investor was expected to agree to invest within a certain time frame and without requesting any additional information as part of due diligence.

There were a few investors who understandably demurred facing such a fait accompli. But there were apparently enough investors who gladly signed on the dotted line even under these "take it or leave it" terms.

While I am tempted to name the startup in question, I will, unfortunately, have to refrain from doing so as this company has a reputation of being litigious and extremely thin-skinned.
