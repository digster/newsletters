“How did you go bankrupt?” Bill asked.
“Two ways,” Mike said. “Gradually, then suddenly.”
Those 14 words from Ernest Hemingway’s The Sun Also Rises is a great illustration of how big change happens. Just like bankruptcy, nearly all big, lasting change happens very slowly, and then before you realise it, suddenly a lot seems to be happening.
Tata Group, India’s 153-year-old conglomerate, is on a path to transforming and reinventing itself by launching one of the most ambitious consumer plays we’ve ever seen. To do this, Tata has been working secretly to launch something that their Chairman, N Chandrasekaran, described as a ‘super app’...a single app that would have several products and services to meet most consumer needs.
Although Tata had telegraphed its intention to launch this super app nearly a year back, for a long time, nothing really happened.
Then recently, it exploded with activity. It took a majority stake in Bigbasket, India’s largest online grocery retailer. Then it invested in Curefit, to make a play into fitness, and pulled Mukesh Bansal, Curefit's CEO, into an executive role at Tata Digital. Bansal, if you remember, has already played a major part at two of the biggest consumer internet success stories in India—he was co-founder and CEO at Myntra and was a top executive at Flipkart. Then, Tata Group confirmed plans to take a majority stake in 1mg, the online pharmacy. And there are reports that it’s in talks with Bengaluru-headquartered concierge service Dunzo.
However, there was one question to which we still didn’t have an answer.
How will Tata actually build its superapp?
We didn’t know how all of these products and services would integrate inside the super app.
We didn’t know how Tata planned to integrate its own companies with these newer acquisitions. For example, Tata Health has a significant overlap with 1mg. How will they work together?
We didn’t know what the leadership team looked like inside Tata. Who are the major decision makers?
We didn’t know anything about the team building the super app. How many are there? What are their roles?
Until today.
Today’s story by Seetharaman has the early answers to some of these questions. In his amazing, must-read story today, we finally get a glimpse into how one of India’s oldest corporate powers is fighting to change into a new, sleek, modern one. We understand what Tata is actually doing and how it's doing it.
Be the first to subscribe and read today’s story because in the future, when people ask how Tata built its superapp, your answer will be the same as what Hemingway wrote.
Gradually, then suddenly.
