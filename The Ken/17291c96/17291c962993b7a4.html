Good Morning Ishan,
 
The first time The Ken’s newsroom started discussing the enigmatic company called Ebix was on 31 October, 2017, the day it acquired travel portal Via.com for $75 million. Just five months earlier, Ebix, a US-based provider of software services to the financial services, had spent $124 million to buy ItzCash, an Indian digital payments company. We were all quite intrigued at the deal play from a relatively unknown company.
 
The very next month, we were discussing Ebix’s flamboyant CEO Robin Raina, who said in a media interview that he expected $500 million in revenue from India “in three years or so.” Those were big numbers.
 
In less than six months, Ebix would go on to acquire a majority stake in the digital classroom business of one of India’s leading education software companies, Educomp, for just $8 million, as part of an insolvency process. Classroom education? Why on earth?
 
“Move over Batman, here comes Robin,” said the headline of a September 2018 story from a business publication. 
 
The acquisitions kept coming, culminating in a massive $340 million acquisition of Yatra, one of India’s largest travel portals, in mid-2019.
 
There is a weird dynamic that sometimes operates in newsrooms. Some subjects and stories become the object of much discussion and fascination, without them ever making the leap into being pitched, reported and written. I don’t have any definite answers as to why that happens, but one reason could be that each story and each discussion raises the bar on what new aspect was left uncovered.
 
Thankfully, a few weeks ago, Vandana decided to just go ahead and start reporting on Ebix. As part of her reporting, she sent both Ebix and Yatra detailed questions about the status of their merger.
 
Imagine our surprise when just days later, on Friday night, Yatra announced the termination of its acquisition by Ebix. Yatra’s complaint "seeks to hold Ebix accountable for breaches of its representations, warranties and covenants in the Merger Agreement and an ancillary extension agreement, and seeks substantial damages.”
 
With the merger in tatters, Yatra faces the unenviable task of rebuilding its travel business against both giant competitors like MakeMyTrip as well as giant pandemics like Covid-19. Undoing nearly a year spent as a merged entity is never easy. 
 
As for Ebix, well, read Vandana’s story: https://the-ken.com/story/ebix-yatra-and-a-broken-deal/ (12-minute read)
