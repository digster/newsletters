I’ve got a love-hate relationship with survival shows, battle royales, or any setup where the goal is to be the last man standing. While I love watching contestants approach and strategise challenges differently, I hate getting invested because my favourites rarely ever win at anything.

Luckily for me, today’s story had everything I loved about elimination-style content.

Our story today talks about Mitra Bukalapak, the online-to-offline vertical of e-commerce company Bukalapak. The business has cornered the warung segment—the small neighbourhood retailers that sell sundries and basic goods to their neighbours.

Serving over 5.1 million warungs (that’s a market share of nearly 70%), Mitra Bukalapak is the last man standing in this space, outlasting local titan Tokopedia and Jeff Bezos-backed Ula. The business has over 15,000 physical products and 42 digital products available for warung owners, with varying take rates.

Now, with competition dropping away, the number of partner users declining, and a preference for digital products emerging, Mitra Bukalapak is retooling its business to maintain leadership and grow both its profit margins and user base. Some of the moves are predictable, but a few are unexpected, even seeming counterintuitive.
