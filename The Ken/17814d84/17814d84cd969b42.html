If Bloomberg columnist Chris Bryant is to be believed, the SPAC party's over. "The SPAC boom has become the Spacpocalypse," he wrote in a column published yesterday.
 
While that may be so in the United States, the SPAC party is just starting in India. Last month, India recorded its first major SPAC merger. And while the likes of e-commerce giant Flipkart and online grocer Grofers have been in the news for possible SPAC listings, they were beaten to it by ReNew Power Ltd.
 
ReNew isn't a small company. It's India's second-largest clean energy company, with a capacity of 10 GW—behind only Adani Green Energy (14.8 GW). After a failed IPO attempt in 2018, ReNew is getting a backdoor entry to the public markets via a merger with a Nasdaq-listed SPAC, RMG Acquisition Corp II. The merged entity will be valued at $4.4 billion—more than the $4 billion valuation that investors baulked at in ReNew's failed IPO attempt nearly three years ago. 
 
While this is great for ReNew, it's now crunch time. Potential investors assessing ReNew’s prospects will stack the company up against peers like Azure Power Global, Brookfield Renewable Partners, and Clearway Energy, which are all listed on the New York Stock Exchange.
 
ReNew claims it is undervalued relative to these peers. But then it set its own valuation, which begs the question: does it believe its own hype? The road ahead won't be easy, especially in a very competitive market like India, where solar tariffs are among the lowest in the world, and there has been no let-up in regulatory arbitrariness.
