Good Morning Ishan,
Ironic, but true—artificial intelligence is turning out to be a leveller.
In the sprawling IT industry, which employs over 5 million people in India, both freshers and experienced developers are upskilling with the same AI tools. Seen objectively, there’s an upside to this.
Debanjali reports how freshers are now playing more significant roles in projects—the crunched time to billability, cost pressures, and agility work in their favour. But hang on, I’m getting ahead of myself.
Before this upside, there’s a sudden shift that millions of fresh engineering grads, including the garden variety, must withstand before they can aspire for that IT job.
As Infosys toughens entry level tests and lays off hundreds of freshers, it’s clear they need to come as pre-trained as they can get. They need to understand business and client requirements instead of basic coding, say those closely watching this space.
This is particularly tough for non-IT freshers like mechanical or civil engineers, who’re expected to upskill themselves through self-study and external certifications.
It’s showing in the data too. Infosys’ utilisation rate, including trainees, has shot up from roughly 77% in FY23 to 83% in FY25. Exclude trainees, and the increase is flatter, from 83% to 86%.
What does it mean to be a fresher in these companies in these times? [Debanjali has the story](https://the-ken.com/story/infosys-the-job-that-millions-of-indias-engineering-grads-took-for-granted-is-now-a-tough-sell/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
