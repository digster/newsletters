Good Morning Ishan,
 
Arun Kumar is one of the most mysterious, ingenious, contrarian and successful entrepreneurs I’ve come across. Over the last three decades, the founder of Strides Pharma has upended practically every management rule you can think of, and probably a few unwritten ones too.
 
Focus? His first public company, Strides Pharma Science, made over 8 times more in profit from "discontinued operations” as opposed to "continuing operations” last year. His second public company, SeQuent Scientific, made nearly 38 times more profit from discontinued operations over continuing operations.
 
Simplicity? His first public company has 51 subsidiaries. His second has 21. His third, and most recent, is also likely to follow suit.
 
Strategy? Kumar would rather not have any. Instead, he prefers his own “perspective”.
 
IRR? He doesn’t believe in it. In 2015, he raised Rs 400 crore ($57.6 million) for SeQuent within 30 minutes despite him stating publicly that the company wouldn’t make money for five years. 
 
Geographic and regulatory advantage? At a time when his peers shopped around for manufacturing locations in India like Goa, Sikkim or Baddi in Himachal Pradesh to avail tax concessions, Kumar went to Brazil, Australia and Africa.
