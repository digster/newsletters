If the mid-year big tech-platforms tango in India has been overwhelming, brace yourselves for an epic space (broadband) opera. Stars are aligning.
Earlier this month, Sunil Bharti Mittal-owned Bharti Global, invested $500 million in OneWeb, the satellite company that had filed for bankruptcy in March 2020 after burning through $3.4 billion in five years. Bharti and the UK government now own 45% each in the company that is hugely promising for space-based communication services.
The Ken learnt that until June last week, Amazon was the frontrunner to acquire OneWeb. The e-commerce company already has Project Kuiper underway to launch a few hundred satellites in the low-earth orbit.
How Bharti swooped in with the UK government isn’t clear. But what we have managed to figure out is why Bharti came back to save its 2015 investment of $25 million in OneWeb.
It’s certainly not throwing good money after bad. Though, it’s a bit mysterious why Bharti did not participate in OneWeb’s 2019 SoftBank-led $1.25 billion fundraise.
Satellite communications is not a new sector for the Bharti Group. It even sought permission from the Indian government to launch its own communications satellite five years ago.
With the OneWeb acquisition, though, many strands of tech, enterprise business imperatives, and global ambitions are converging. On Monday, Hughes Communications, which has prior business relations with both Bharti and OneWeb, said it’d invest $50 million in the latter.
The space race is heating up. In the next decade, 50K satellites will dot the low-earth orbit. Amazon via Kuiper, SpaceX via Starlink, Facebook via Athena, Google via its investment in SpaceX (and looking for value in its investments in submarine cables) are all looking up to the sky to beam broadband to the earth.
