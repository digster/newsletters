Good Morning Ishan,
Youtube turned 20 this year but it continues to change creators’ lives and livelihoods, smashing everything in between.
The simplicity with which this video-sharing platform collapses complexities is the reason creators love it. It did that with content creation, where running a micro business became a sequence of steps. It’s doing that with shopping now. From product tags to affiliate links, Youtube Shopping handles everything for brands and marketplaces.
For years, Youtube passively sat on hundreds of hours of shopping-related watch time as viewers flocked to the platform with their high-intent shopping-related searches. In the last one year alone, shopping-related watch time on Youtube grew more than 250%. But the same viewers hardly shopped there.
Youtube wants to change that by eating Instagram’s lunch. Easier desired than done.
Around 80% of people turn to Meta platforms such as Facebook and Instagram to discover new brands versus 69% for Youtube, according to a recent market study.
Debanjali’s [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/why-youtube-thinks-flipkart-nykaa-tie-ups-can-help-it-woo-instagrams-shoppers/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) has granular details on how Youtube, built for intent, is trying to close the gap with an Instagram built for impulse. Let’s dive in.
This week on Two by Two, hosts Praveen Gopal Krishnan and Rohin Dharmakumar break down Meesho’s playbook with Adarsh Menon, partner at Fireside Ventures and former head of Shopsy at Flipkart, and Ganesh Nagasekar, founder of GSN Invest.
As Meesho prepares to go public, our discussion examines its counterintuitive moves: offering zero commission when rivals charged fees, building Valmo to cut delivery costs when others chased speed, and targeting 210 million middle-class customers that Flipkart and Amazon largely wrote off. We also try to understand what the company’s next move should be: double down on logistics, try content commerce, or fix the cash-on-delivery problem that’s quietly eating into margins.
Tune in to hear why Meesho might just be building India’s answer to Walmart—or something entirely different.
