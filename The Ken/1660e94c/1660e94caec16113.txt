Good Morning Ishan,

I’m here with Tuesday love. I know it is too soon from Sunday, but I guess joblessness in this organisation has its perks.   

Now, I have special love for venture capital folks. I meet them whenever they can spare the time and cherish the conversations. About their business and whatever else they have been up to. The whatever part is almost always more work and back-breaking travel, and them being hopeful and enthusiastic in this otherwise difficult-to-make-sense-of world. So, not long back, I was in Delhi. Sitting opposite a VC with a cufflink. 

Our discussion veered to secondaries. It is one of those things that make for good conversation because for as long as I can remember, several VCs have been talking about it. And so, naturally the questions: Is there a market for it? Who is buying? Are VCs really looking to sell? Why? Isn’t the portfolio full of clunkers? Who’d buy that?

Him: There is always supply. Everyone is lined up to sell. Who doesn’t want to sell? All you have to do is look at the vintage of some of the funds here. The problem is, there are just a handful buyers and at the prices they are quoting, these conversations never go anywhere.

Well, I guess, after all the talk, we should have one VC secondary deal coming through. 

My colleague Pooja says that NewQuest Capital is set to acquire the US-based New Enterprise Associates’ (NEA) India fund. NEA had entered India in 2006, by launching NEA IndoUS Venture Partners led by Vinod Dham and Vani Kola. Kola went on to launch her own fund Kalaari Capital in 2012. Then, NEA was managed by Bala Deshpande. And then last year, Deshpande and her team quit to start their own fund. NEA has been investing in India for close to a decade and their India portfolio includes companies like Naaptol, Nova Fertility, Airworks, among others.  

Our understanding is, secondaries are a good outcome. For both VCs and portfolio companies. In all likelihood, we should see more of them in India. On the Private Equity side, there are already quite a few secondaries happening. Make the most of your day. I’ll be seeing you around.
