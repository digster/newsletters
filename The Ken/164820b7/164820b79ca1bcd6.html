Good Morning Ishan,
 
Online furniture seller Urban Ladder’s corporate office in Bangalore sits atop their first flagship company store. The store’s size is about 640 sq. m., or the size of a penalty box in a football field.
 
Later this month, IKEA will officially open its first store in India in the southern city of Hyderabad. Its size? Five football fields.
 
Thus the subject line - “IKEANAMI”.
 
Urban Ladder and Pepperfry are India’s largest online furniture sellers. They both started operations in 2012, incidentally the same year in which IKEA first applied to the Indian government for permission to set up its own stores. In the six years since, they've raised a collective total of over $300 million in venture funding. Their original hypothesis was that Indians were ready to buy furniture online.
 
Six years later, that hypothesis is still struggling for validation.
 
Of the $18-20 billion market for furniture in India, over 90% is “unorganised” (a peculiar Indian phrase for unbranded artisanal or small business products and services). And of the 10% that’s left, less than 1% is bought online.
 
"If the mountain won’t come to you, then you must go to the mountain.”
 
Which is why both Urban Ladder and Pepperfry have been concentrating most of their efforts on opening and running physical stores. There’s just a small thing though - IKEA is not just entering the ring, it's also taking its gloves off.
 
How then, asks Praveen in his first story for The Ken, did Urban Ladder and Pepperfry spend six years running to a place where they knew the IKEA bomb was going to drop? Why try to fight a battle on a turf that works to your competitor's advantage, one who has decades of experience across 24 countries, the largest supply chain in the world, and billions of dollars of capital at its disposal?
