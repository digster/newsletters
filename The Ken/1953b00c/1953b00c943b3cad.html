Let’s say you start a company selling healthy meals to fitness nuts—the kind of people who count macros and track heart rates for fun. But then you notice something: Indians might tolerate quinoa, but they love pizza. And donuts. And biryani. So, what do you do? You buy a donut chain, crank out oily rice dishes, and decide to take a swing at a pizza giant.

That’s pretty much the playbook at Curefoods, the multi-brand cloud kitchen founded by Ankit Nagori. It didn’t just pivot away from healthy food—it ran a marathon in the opposite direction. Recently, it snagged the rights to operate Krispy Kreme in south and west India. Sure, that sounds like an opportunistic sugar binge, but there’s a bigger move in the oven. Curefoods isn’t just expanding its menu—it’s trying to eat Domino’s lunch. And its not-so-secret weapon is Olio Pizza, a brand that’s adding more than six outlets a month, often setting up shop wherever you find a Domino’s.

And Curefoods isn’t some scrappy little pizza joint. It’s a cloud-kitchen operator, meaning it runs a bunch of virtual brands from the same kitchen. That sounds like a logistical headache, but Curefoods swears by its “Lego” model. The numbers suggest it’s working. Curefoods pulled in Rs 585 crore in revenue for FY24 while cutting its losses in half to Rs 173 crore. Olio Pizza alone brings in Rs 120–130 crore in annualised sales. Plus, the company has hoovered up more than 20 brands in three years.

Of course, Domino’s isn’t exactly trembling. Jubilant Foodworks, its India franchisee, still has over 2,000 outlets across the country and a standalone app with 14 million monthly active users. But Nagori isn’t losing sleep. He spent years at Flipkart, so he knows a thing or two about winning in marketplaces. And his investors seem happy to keep the ovens running.

Can Olio Pizza really out-pizza the pizza giant? Well, in a Rs 11,500 crore market where customer loyalty isn’t what it used to be, it’s at least worth a shot. Let’s get to Gaurav’s story.
