Despite being a price-sensitive market, Indians' love for Apple is growing
Good Evening Ishan,
 

An iconic street snack, a Bollywood star, and the CEO of the most powerful tech company in the world—all came together in India’s city of dreams this April. 

 

It’s been just over a month since Apple CEO Tim Cook was seen eating vada pav with Bollywood actor Madhuri Dixit, and I think many may see the occasion—the launch of Apple’s first exclusive stores in India—as a momentous day in the story of what we so love calling “an aspirational country”.
[ Photo credit: Apple ]
 

But more than that, it was also a significant moment in the story of how a premium global tech brand is trying to crack one of the most price-sensitive markets in the world.

 

“If you bury an iPhone in the ground, a customer will dig it out and leave money there”—that’s what a mobile phone retailer told my colleague Soumyajit Saha, a reporter with The Ken, late last year. 

 

Well, a few days ago, I was talking to The Ken’s audio team about my episode on Apple, and it left me with some very interesting insights into the kind of target audience the tech giant is dealing with here. On one hand, there was Rajiv, our sound engineer, who has very consciously decided not to switch to Apple because of how expensive its products are and how little value they offer to him in his work. Akshaya, the host of our other podcast, Cost To Company, had a completely different point of view. She currently uses an Android phone, but she’s been saving for months to buy an iPhone later this year. And Apple’s website, she says, has a whole section dedicated to helping people switch from Android to iOS. 

 

It is stuff like this, to put it very simply, that endears Apple to potential buyers.  

 

And one reason why Apple’s sales have been consistently rising in India since 2021. In fact, Apple sold two million iPhones in the last quarter of 2022 alone. 

 

But India has never really been a big market for high-end, premium smartphones. And the one thing we know for sure about Apple is that it is not a big believer in discounts. Which is why for close to two decades, Apple struggled with a stagnant market in India. 


And yet, since 2021, Apple has been seeing a gradual but consistent upward trend in sales. What’s changed? You could check out this Daybreak episode from last year to understand how Apple is winning India.

Still, if you compare the 6 or 7 million iPhones sold in India to the over 50 million units being sold in markets like China and USA, you may not think Apple’s India sales really justify all the effort the tech giant is putting into the country now. The huge state-of-the-art stores, Cook himself flying down to inaugurate them, making public appearances with Indian celebs…

 

What exactly is the company trying to tell Indians?
