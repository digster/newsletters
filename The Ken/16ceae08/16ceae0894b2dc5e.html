Good Morning Ishan,
 
Isn't it great when you can relate to what's expected of you? Let me explain. We all know of certain things we're good at, and sometimes, we get the opportunity to use those skills - and use them well we do.

Like when a certain colleague of mine found herself singing on stage with a band in Thailand last week. What's up, Arundhati?

Now, that's the sweet spot.

Most, though, aren't that lucky. Worse still, some find themselves dissociating from the very thing they're known for. There could be plenty of reasons for that. Some which we explored in our stories last week.

There was Sumanth's Monday story on Engineer.ai. For a startup with AI in its name, Engineer.ai has been called out for making exaggerated claims about its AI capabilities. That one's probably on them.

On Thursday, Suraksha scanned through medical equipment manufacturer Skanray's odd predicament. For a company keen on keeping things indigenous, it's still in a tough spot with the Indian government's Make in India push. Why? It's a free story, so read to find out. Oh, and don't forget to share.

Jon wrote about fashion e-commerce platform Zilingo on Tuesday. He looked at how the company was moving away from its core of a consumer-facing e-commerce platform towards seller services. Mostly because e-commerce is no good.

Beyond this running theme of dissociation, we had two great stories.

One from Vandana on PEs. On Wednesday, she wrote about how private equity funding was recoding India’s tech startup ecosystem. It’s transforming everything from company to VC behaviour, she writes.

And the other one came from Arundhati. She closed the week with her story on BharatPe and how it has seen a swift rise (in just one year) standing atop the shoulders of the consumer-focused payments apps. But payments supremacy isn’t the destination for BharatPe, merely the vehicle.

That's all for this week.

Now, have you ever found yourself doing something that made you pause mid-activity and think, "Damn, should've been doing this all my life"? Tell me; I'd love to know.

Have a great weekend!
