We live in a polarised world. And some of us are beginning to accept that it’s going to be a while before that changes. But rarely do we come across a business founder who is polarising, having divided both employees and competitors down the middle.
A maverick who plunged into a new business plan every quarter, without kicking the tyres. A medical doctor who wanted to sell everything—furniture, electronics, health insurance and diagnostics. A shrewd businessman who rents out space in his medicine warehouse. MedPlus founder Madhukar Gangadi is a study in contrast, until you see what he has accomplished (and abandoned).
Ruhi’s story today is a fascinating account of how MedPlus, India’s second largest offline pharmacy chain with 1,700 stores, is also cracking the omnichannel code. Now, that’s a term we are all too familiar with thanks to retail, where online businesses are hankering for offline sales and vice-versa. But medicine is different. MedPlus even more so. The PE-funded chain survived during a time when investors only had eyes for online pharmacies and regulations prohibited majority foreign direct investment in multi-brand retail.
Now, as e-pharmacies ready for impending regulations that should favour them, they’re also waking up to a new reality—the need to go omnichannel. Meanwhile, MedPlus’ offline competitors are trying to emulate the company's unusual strategies. Rightly so. The chain has an enviable 16-17% of revenue coming in from online sales.
Could Gangadi, often mocked for his eccentric ideas, have the last laugh? That’s Ruhi’s story today.
