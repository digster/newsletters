Good Morning Ishan,

Venture capitalists are normally a reticent lot, especially with pesky journalists. Unless they’re sure that a story on one of their companies is “positive”, they remain dark. But we’re living in the golden age of venture capital and private equity (even if that golden glow seems to be from the golden hour right before sunset), said Prajakta, the author of today’s story. How can VCs be dark?

Now, Prajakta is The Ken's data visualisation editor. She’s responsible for the informative and pithy graphics that accompany our narrative stories each day. So when she decided to analyse VCs, naturally her medium of choice was graphics.

Starting with extensive data on all the investments made by VCs in India between 2017 and 2019, via our data partners Zinnov and Traxn, Prajakta lays out various trends that have played out. Like which VCs have drastically ramped up investments in 2019, and which ones have slowed down. Or which ones have participated in the most investment deals. Or the gradual slowdown in mega (read: $500+ million) rounds. And the fast-changing leaderboard of sectors that are drawing in most investments from 2017 to 2019.
