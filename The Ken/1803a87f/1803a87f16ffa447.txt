It takes a village to take a company public. Executives, accountants, bankers, and consultants typically spend weeks putting together a prospectus document that serves as an official introduction for public market investors. 

A key section in such documents in the industry overview, which offers a 30,000-foot view of the playing field the company competes in. While firms like Crisil and Frost & Sullivan were typically recruited for such work, 12-year-old Redseer Strategy Consultants has now emerged as a favourite among internet companies eyeing public markets.

Over the last year, Redseer worked on 30 initial public offerings (IPO), of which 7 firms have already listed on markets. Paytm, Nykaa, Zomato, and Navi are all among the Indian firms Redseer has consulted for. It also worked on the IPO for Indonesian tech firm GoTo, which went public last Monday, raising US$1.1 billion in a keenly-watched listing. 

While Redseer got its start doing sundry market research for other consulting firms like McKinsey and BCG, it has now garnered quite a reputation for its sharp work. So much so, that a senior executive from Swiggy recounted an incident wherein a Redseer consultant had more accurate business metrics than the ones the executive had at his disposal.

Despite its laser sharp work and high-demand among tech companies looking to go public, some questions have also been raised about how Redseer collects data to begin with. 

In the past, the firm’s consultants have even posed as recruiters to get executives to share performance indicators with them, but for its part, Redseer says that it has fixed those loopholes and now strictly follows more kosher methods. 

In today’s insightful story, Arundhati digs into how while working on IPOs could very well be the proof in Redseer’s consulting pudding, building on the reputation it has garnered will likely be a matter of walking the fine line between accurate data and the right way of collecting it.
