Last week on First Principles

One of the recurring themes when it comes to entrepreneurship is "valuation". How much a company is "worth" is, in many ways, the tail that wags the enterprise dog in our times.
 

But almost 27 years after he started the real estate and design firm Total Environment, Kamal Sagar—its co-founder and CEO—still claims not to know its valuation. "We didn't build it with the idea of selling it, so we haven't ever looked at that option", said Sagar.
 

Total Environment has charted a distinct contrarian path for itself. At every stage of its evolution, it chose to do things that took more time, money, and effort. It built its design software. It turned apartment designs into versioned products, much like productized software.
 

In a candid and reflective conversation with host Rohin Dharmakumar, Kamal Sagar shows us an alternate view of building a company. You can listen to the full episode on Spotify or Apple Podcasts.
