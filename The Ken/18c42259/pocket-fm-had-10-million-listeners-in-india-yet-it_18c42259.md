Good Morning Ishan,
Have you ever meticulously planned a road trip? One where you scour Google Maps days in advance to ensure you take the most efficient route?
However, on D-Day, you end up on a longer, winding terrain due to some unforeseen glitch. But, hey, you don’t regret it because this unexpected path, though tiring, is more beautiful.
I was reminded of a similar incident when I read today’s piece by Anushka.
The protagonist for this story, the non-music audio-streaming platform Pocket FM, was conceptualised for the Indian market. In 2020, the company attained 10 million monthly active users (MAUs) in the country. However, it has found a more prosperous route: the US market. Pocket FM’s spicy soap operas have got Americans interested. And around 3 million MAUs is proof enough.
The company, co-founded by Rohan Nayak, Nishanth KS, and Prateek Dixit, is eyeing the unicorn tag. With its revenues set to cross Rs 1,000 crore ($120 million) in the year ending March 2024, the stage seems set for an exciting chase.
But what’s a plot without conflicts? Pocket FM is up against America’s home teams, Apple and Spotify, which have a combined market share of 70%.
The Indian market has its share of problems, too. Monetisation, for instance, has been a tough nut to crack. Diversifying the top line has been difficult, and revenue from regional languages has yet to take off.
Pocket FM, therefore, is planning for its next episode of growth. It received $16 million in debt funding from US-based Silicon Valley Bank in May 2023 and is reportedly in the advanced stages of raising another $80 million at a $800 million valuation.
