Good Morning Ishan,
As someone who writes on fintechs, the sector stopped being exciting to track sometime last year.
The banking regulator, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI), made life difficult for different kinds of players, so what’s there to write about companies that stopped dead in their tracks?
But if the regulator closes a door, it opens a window. And in the case of the US$1.5-billion-valued Slice, it just opened a French window.
Tiger Global-backed Slice had risen to fame by issuing prepaid cards loaded with a credit line, mimicking a credit card. However, the RBI banned loading prepaid instruments with credit lines in June last year, effectively scrapping Slice’s business model.
And last week, the regulator approved the marriage of Slice, which has all the money but no business model, with North East Small Finance Bank (North East SFB), a lender with a banking licence but starved for capital.
“This has changed the glass ceiling for fintechs because the regulator has acceded that a tech company can be as genuine as a pedigreed non-banking finance company,” a bank executive told The Ken.
In today’s story, Gaurav deftly looks at how this merger came to be and what this means for fintechs. There are lenders, such as Nainital Bank, Punjab & Sind Bank, and Bank of India, with bad-loan ratios ranging between 5% and 10%. Besides, many fintechs are still in search of a viable business model.
As for me, it looks like fintechs just got exciting again.
