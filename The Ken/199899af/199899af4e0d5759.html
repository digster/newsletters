Welcome to Edition #3 of the Zero Shot column. I’m Rohin, the third and final columnist in this shared series on AI and its implications and incentives. (I hope you’ve already read editions #1 and #2 written by my colleagues Praveen and Brady.)

Each of us brings our own unique perspective to this column and podcast (did you miss our first episode?). Here’s what my motivations and lenses on AI are, as mentioned on the Zero Shot page too:

“Rohin believes AI is one of the most powerful wrecking balls that shatters assumptions. It’s forcing everyone—from individuals to nation-states—to reevaluate their beliefs. As co-founder and CEO of The Ken, he’ll explore what this means for organisations, innovation, people management, and more.”

As a journalist, I like spotting connections between things, or synthesis. Thus, I like systems and systems thinking. I am more fascinated by the second, third, or fourth-order effects of events than their mere occurrence. To paraphrase a line that’s frequently attributed to the Canadian ice hockey legend Wayne Gretzky, I like to imagine “where the puck is going to be.”

I also like understanding the incentives of players involved. I love counterintuitive strategies. Most times I prefer understanding actions over words. I like contradictions. I like analogies.

I cannot promise what specific areas of AI I’ll write about each week. But I can promise that I’ll evolve my reportage and writing to the feedback I get from The Ken’s subscribers – you!

Phew! That was only because this is the first time I’m writing to you as a Zero Shot columnist. I promise I’ll cut to the chase in the future.
