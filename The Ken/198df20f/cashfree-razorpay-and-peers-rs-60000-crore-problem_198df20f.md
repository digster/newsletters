Good Morning Ishan,
For years, India’s fintechs had a not-so-secret growth hack. It wasn’t e-commerce. It wasn’t travel. It wasn’t even mutual funds.
It was rummy, poker, and fantasy cricket. Real-money gaming was the engine room powering payment aggregators and digital lenders. Cashfree and Razorpay made fat margins processing gaming deposits and withdrawals—10 to 50 basis points per transaction, compared to the measly 1–2 from, say, Flipkart. Phonepe and Easebuzz used gaming volumes to scale up. Lenders like Fibe and Navi handed out quick loans to people chasing that one last win.
You see where this is going. The government just flipped the table and walked away with the chips.
The Online Gaming Bill, passed last week, didn’t just kneecap Dream11 and MPL. It also yanked out as much as 30% of net revenue for payment processors. That’s Rs 60,000 crore in annual gaming spend—350 million UPI transactions a month—gone.
This is what makes the story bigger than gaming. A ban on “addictive” games turns out to be a ban on the quiet business model that kept India’s most celebrated fintechs flush.
And the timing is interesting. The Supreme Court was days away from ruling on whether gaming should be taxed at 28% or not at all. Instead, Parliament simply killed the sector in four days flat.
So yes, gaming is over. But the more interesting question is—what happens when the goose that laid the golden transactions for fintechs is suddenly cooked?
