Yesterday was a big day for Paytm* (One97 Communications Ltd).
It posted its first set of results after the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) [put a bullet](https://the-ken.com/story/rbi-issues-a-death-sentence-to-paytm-payments-bank/) in its payments bank for repeated non-compliance back in January, which ended up laying bare the high levels of dependence between the bank and Paytm’s other operations.
Let me catch you up on the results quickly.
If it was not an extraordinary time for the company, it would have celebrated reaching its first full year of profitability at the ebitda level (earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, and amortisation), or its annual revenue reaching close to the Rs 10,000-crore (US$1.2 billion) mark. In fact, even its quarterly revenue dipped only 3% in the quarter ended March 2024.
But its losses widened to Rs 550 crore (US$66 million) and the company is bracing for a tough June quarter as the impact of the regulatory action plays out—even if Paytm founder Vijay Shekhar Sharma* believes, as he told analysts, that “the worst is behind us”.
Quarterly revenue is expected to fall by about 35% year-on-year to Rs 1,500 crore (US$180 million) with an ebitda loss of Rs 500 crore (US$60 million) in the first quarter of the current financial year (2024-25).
| [Source: Paytm] |
Trying to limp back to growth, the company believes that things should begin looking up by the second quarter of 2025, partly because it wants to save about Rs 400–500 crore in people costs over this next year.
This estimate matches up with the layoffs happening on the ground, according to Paytm employees. “Managers have gotten a charter to do a 30% cost reduction; entire teams are disappearing around me,” one employee told me, adding, “I haven’t seen a listed company lay off as much.”
