Good Morning Ishan,
Maheshwer Peri takes no prisoners. If you don’t believe me, look him up on Twitter.
In April, the founder of education-services website Careers360 called out billionaire Anand Mahindra for distributing “WhatsApp knowledge” when the latter referenced a chart that placed India at the top of a list of countries with the most number of universities.
Two months earlier, Peri, a former publisher of the weekly Outlook, had stirred up a hornet’s nest by questioning the nearly 7X rise in the fees charged by the Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad in 15 years.
It’s hard to ignore his takes, often contrarian, on India’s education sector. “Show me any post which I put out which isn’t backed by data,” he told my colleague Alifiya, the author of today’s story. “I don’t make loose arguments.”
He has every reason not to.
Years ago, he was dragged to court by the Arindam Chaudhuri-led Indian Institute of Planning and Management, which accused Peri and Careers360 of defamation. But Peri won that battle.
For someone who has been through that, Twitter feuds in defence of his views are no big deal. And Peri, naturally, has his fair share of critics. “He runs a popular publication. For the company to offer counselling and college recommendations, isn’t it a conflict of interest? Someone should ask him about the bias,” said the director of a management institute from Mumbai.
In her sharp and engaging feature, Alifiya profiles Peri through his most contentious recent stances.
