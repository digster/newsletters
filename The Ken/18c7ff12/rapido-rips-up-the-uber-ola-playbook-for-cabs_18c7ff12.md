Good Morning Ishan,
Do you find ambition attractive? I do.
The protagonist for today’s story, Rapido, a ride-hailing platform, has been revving up on an ambitious trail—one that has never led to profitability in India.
The Bengaluru-based company, last valued at $830 million, has thrown down the gauntlet at giants Uber and Ola. That’s right, Rapido has dived into cab-hailing. In fact, it’s been testing waters in Hyderabad for the last six months. And has achieved encouraging results.
But the company is allegedly skirting verification norms. Shaswata, the author of today’s story, uncovers some answers in a conversation with Rapido’s co-founder, Pavan Guntupalli.
Meanwhile, conflicting reports emerge from drivers—some praised higher earnings, others lamented low demand. Rapido, however, claims to have cracked a unique selling proposition (USP). One that will undercut its giant competitors and attract a wave of untapped cab drivers.

Buy now, pay later, purchase a mobile or own a house,
When EMI’s start accumulating, with lenders play cat and mouse.
Status drives you to take loans, money drives them to lend,
When instalments stop materialising, they need to recovery agents send.
When the merchant offers a loan, they tap into an unsaid need,
Of how much you need to splurge- more than your neighbour, indeed!
If his kid goes to the United States, yours should go to Canada,
If your daughter’s wedding is not in millions, you are society’s anathema.
Sometimes you also borrow, from a genuine place of need,
To start your enterprise, to scale, to sustain your farm by buying seed.
But there are hardly any donors, who don’t give out of greed,
And when the chips are down, they squeeze every ounce- as you bleed.
I bought a lot of dreams upfront, I’m now paying for them with my life,
Now, all I can borrow is my therapists time- to remain sane and not die by suicide
