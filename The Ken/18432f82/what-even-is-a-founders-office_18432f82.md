We're back from our Diwali break with a fresh new episode!
The Founder’s Office is the new ‘it’ thing in the world of startup jobs. A casual search on LinkedIn throws up a metric ton of explainers, job listings, and even communities catering to either people who’ve already made it, or those aspiring to one day work in it.
The smartest and brightest minds from some of the country’s top B-Schools are embracing a whole new kind of career track—obscure, yet alluring. Thoroughbred Generalists, as they might prefer to call themselves, are picking up roles in the Founder’s Office.
But what is this really? Does it even make sense? And most importantly, why are so many bright professionals at the prime of their careers infatuated by it?
In this episode of Cost to Company, Shreevar dives into the weeds to get the answers. He speaks to folks from Ola, Klub, and Myntra to help you:
- Take a peek into the daily workings of Founder’s Offices.
- Learn about the skills it takes to make it there. And where people who do eventually end up.
- See how Founder’s Offices vary across organisations.
- Understand the concept of aspirational ambiguity, and why the Founder’s Office is the modern-day manifestation of career safety.
- Ask how people in these offices navigate loneliness, and the open waters that come with it.
