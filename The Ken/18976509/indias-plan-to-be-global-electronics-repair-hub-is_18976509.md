Good Morning Ishan,
For most Indians, getting their old phone or laptop repaired means going to a hole-in-the-wall store with a person or two tinkering with electronic components behind the counter.
But the government wants to present a very different image to the rest of the world. Think ginormous shop floors with hundreds of workers working in tandem with high-end machinery. These comprise technicians who repair your phone on behalf of brand, at authorised repair service centres or in centralised facilities.
Electronics repair services outsourcing is a $200 million business in India. But in four to six years, India wants it to be a $20 billion industry. Its reasoning is simple: “if we can make electronics for the world, why can’t we repair them too?”
So in May, India launched a pilot in Bengaluru and roped in five companies, including Lenovo and contract-manufacturing major Flex.
What’s more, India has expanded the number of e-waste items that can be recycled almost 5X to 100.
Seems picture-perfect, right? Well, “seems” is the operative word here.
Repairs at scale can be as hard to crack as manufacturing, as Anushka reports from Delhi and Shaswata and Nathan discover through their visit to two repair facilities in Bengaluru: [https://the-ken.com/indias-plan-to-be-global-electronics-repair-hub-is-ready-but-indian-firms-arent/](https://the-ken.com/indias-plan-to-be-global-electronics-repair-hub-is-ready-but-indian-firms-arent/?utm_source=story_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=story_newsletter) (8-minute read)
