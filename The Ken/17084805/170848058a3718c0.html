Good Morning Ishan,
 
Do you have a favourite podcast you tune into during your commute to work?

Do you excitedly exchange notes on said podcast with your colleagues?

Do you break into a stream of podcast consciousness each time someone asks for a recommendation?

I may not be this person, but increasingly, I know too many people who are. That podcasts are popular is one thing, but to learn that over 40 million Indians listen to podcasts monthly—and that this number could go up to over 176 million in three years—made me reassess my understanding of the medium.

Right now, there are over 800,000 active podcasts with some 54 million episodes in over 100 languages. If there were ever a problem of plenty, it'd be now, right?

Wrong. India is still picking up pace in the podcast race. And the front-runner—more of a marathon runner—IVM has been at it for five years. Recently though, the likes of Amazon and Spotify, spotting an India opportunity, have jumped in.

IVM is now staring at some heavy competition - competition it ironically also caters to. It doesn't help the company that this comes at a time when it has lost some of its talent to independent podcasting, its revenue has dipped, expenses have gone up, and partner brands are not too sure how to make things work.

That's a lot. But founder Amit Doshi says IVM is set to raise $1 million in Series A. Would that fix it all? Moreover, would the podcast industry—not a big, moneymaking industry—find workable business models? Our contributors Akshay Datt and Saurabh Garg have a rather cool story today: https://the-ken.com/story/ivm-sidelined-in-indias-podcasting-mic-drop-moment/ [13-minute read]

Once you're done reading, do suggest a few good podcasts to a podcast rookie. Aka yours truly. (Preferably something funny. Thanks.)
