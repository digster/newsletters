How does true learning happen? Does it happen through concepts that are taught to you? Or does it happen through the application of those concepts? Or does it really happen when you get curious and stumble along the way, get doubts and then try to search for answers to those doubts on your own.
 
Yes, yes I know. All three. Because learning is a continuous and complex process and because it’s not an either-or and because this is too simplistic and blah blah. I know.
 
But again, how do you really learn? Concepts? Application? Doubts? (Heard the one about three developers, a light bulb, a browser and Stack Overflow? Never mind.)
 
Well, edtech startup Doubtnut is hoping you said “Doubts”. Because it is trying to tackle the middle of India’s massively undertapped edtech opportunity pyramid by focusing exclusively on doubts. “If you have a math question, I’m 99% sure it exists in our database,” says its co-founder Tanushree Nagori.
 
It caters to students all the way from class 6 to aspiring engineers sitting for public exams, allowing them to upload pictures of questions from books and receive a video solution within minutes. Like a Google for math queries. And staying focused on this narrow but critical niche has allowed Doubtnut to go from 1,000 monthly active users in 2016 to over 7 million within three years.
 
But, but asks the super-sharp VC associate, “Is this a Rs 1000 crore opportunity?”. To which you say, good question. And then point them to Olina’s story: https://the-ken.com/story/doubts-doctrine-doubtnut-brainly/ (13-minute read)
