Good Morning Ishan,
“What’s your rent?”
If you’ve lived in Mumbai long enough, as I have, this question wouldn’t surprise you even if it came from someone you met just half an hour earlier.
This is the city’s equivalent of “why aren’t you married yet?”
Nosebleed rents relative to other big cities have always been the norm in Mumbai. But lately, such has been the spike in rents in Bengaluru that I wonder if it’s now a top conversation starter in the country’s tech capital too.
In the first nine months of 2023, rents in two key pockets in Bengaluru shot up roughly 30%–the highest among seven cities–according to a recent report.
The city’s landlords haven’t had it so good in years. Their rental income relative to the value of their property has expanded at a faster clip since pre-pandemic than in any other city.
But, needless to say, it’s a nightmare for renters.
The return-to-office policy of large employers like IT companies is an obvious factor, but in Shaswata’s well-observed story, you’ll find reasons that may not be immediately apparent.
