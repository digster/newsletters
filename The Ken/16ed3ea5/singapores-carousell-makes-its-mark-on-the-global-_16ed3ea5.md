Come winter, comes the wedding season. Like clockwork, every year, as the cold starts setting in, a bunch of couples start settling down. Even in the business world.
One such was last month's $850 million-merger of Carousell, Singapore's startup darling, with Telenor-owned rival, 701Search. The merger brings together two of the region's more prominent classifieds businesses.
Like in the real world, opposites attract. Carousell is a 7-year-old startup focused on mobile classifieds, while 13-year-old 701Search does traditional desktop web listings. The combination of Carousell's revenue growth and 701Search's profitability is seen as a perfect match, though one investor said the Singapore company was "unbelievably lucky" to pick up this deal.
You've heard that at weddings, no?
And as it invariably happens, these two families find some complicated but common connections. Likewise, these newly-weds have a family whose web of relationships include Naspers-owned OLX, one of the world's largest listings sites.
Carousell, though now part of the global classifieds elite, will forever be the plucky startup that inspires a new generation of Singaporean founders. This merger could also offer new hope to investors in Southeast Asia—that of an exit.
Speaking of winter weddings, January 2000 saw one of the most disastrous corporate mergers of all time—that of AOL and Time Warner. Six years later, and also in January, Disney and Pixar announced their merger, considered one of the most successful ever. This wheel of fortune is Jon's story today. Will this marriage will have a 'happily ever after' or succumb to the 7-year itch? Or maybe there is a different ending altogether.
