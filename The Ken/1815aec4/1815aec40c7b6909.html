Good Morning Ishan,
 
If one isn’t living under a rock, one would know that the clash of titans—telcos and tech companies—is a daily affair in India. As much as they need each other, they are fiercely competing for their business evolution and growth.
 
Talking of just one instance, the humble text message—or SMS—is at the heart of intense lobbying in New Delhi these days.
 
And one company among the giants that finds itself rather alone is Amazon.
 
Amazon spends anywhere between Rs 700-800 crore (~US$101 million) annually on text messages. While other internet companies and OTTs, like Amazon, rely on text messages for a range of functions and services including financial transactions, educational messages, etc, they aren’t paying such a steep price.
 
Because for most, these SMSes are domestic. For Amazon, telcos claim, it’s international messaging on account of its servers being hosted outside. More specifically, in Dublin, according to Pratap’s reporting. The cost difference between the two is vast – 9-12 paise vs Rs 2.
 
As many as 1.2 billion international SMSes are sent every month, and Amazon alone accounts for 20%. It’s hurting the e-tailer so much that it believes SMS charges could result in ‘substantial financial implications’ for the company.
 
At the centre of it is India’s regulatory stance that this matter is in “forbearance”;  and since there’s no “market failure”, the regulator will not interfere. Rather, it has urged the telecom department to investigate the matter.
 
For an industry that is bleeding from years of brutal price war, any opportunity to raise tariffs is gold. Telcos will grab it with both hands, and so, SMS charges are expected to go up 2X or 3X soon.
 
Other internet companies have found a temporary workaround by sewing separate commercial deals with telcos, but Amazon has stubbornly chosen to tread a different path. Along with the caller ID and call booking app TrueCaller, Amazon has been trekking to the Department of Telecom for the last 10-12 months seeking intervention.
 
In a sharply reported story today, Pratap shows why and how Amazon’s fight for SMS charges is anything but short. https://the-ken.com/story/us101-million-the-steep-sms-price-tag-amazons-fighting-in-india/ (9-minute read)
 
Have a great week ahead.
 
Warmly,
