Good Morning Ishan,
Allow me to begin this edition with a few quick questions.
Do you own a bank account?
I assume you do.
Do you regularly get your bank transaction SMSes?
You’re supposed to. The Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) rules on customer protection [state](https://www.rbi.org.in/scripts/FS_Notification.aspx?Id=11040&fn=2&Mode=0) that it’s mandatory for all lenders to send SMS alerts to users regarding banking transactions.
In fact, banks, on average, charge customers Rs 15 (~US$0.20) per quarter for these SMS alerts, according to two senior bankers.
Last question.
Have you noticed some of your bank transaction SMSes go missing?
Many have—it’s common enough that people have raised concerns on forums such as Reddit and Quora. I spoke to three of these people this week, and well, there’s a common culprit.
Truecaller.
Apparently, instead of landing in their phone’s SMS inbox, bank transaction alerts and messages are showing up on Truecaller’s business chat.
There are many problems with that.
Let me explain.
For those of you who don’t know, Truecaller is a Swedish caller-identification app with 272.6 million monthly active users in India as of March—a number [that is growing](https://www.business-standard.com/companies/results/truecaller-adds-23-3-million-users-in-india-during-first-quarter-of-cy24-124050701175_1.html).
It also has a messaging platform with a business chat feature (much like Whatsapp) that allows companies to reach out to users. Truecaller claims that only verified businesses that have the user’s contact number are allowed to use this feature. It also explicitly says that this is “not an SMS”.
