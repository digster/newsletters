Good Morning Ishan,
Arya.ag’s promise to farmers often strapped for money is simple enough: they don’t have to rush to sell their produce to a trader right after harvest. They can instead store their grain in one of the agritech’s 4,000 warehouses and take out a loan, also from Arya.ag, with the commodity as collateral.
Oiling the rusty wheels of India’s agri supply chain has allowed Arya.ag, backed by the likes of Omnivore, Stride, and Lightrock, to build a Rs 5,700 crore business over 12 years, and a profitable one at that.
Everything goes swimmingly as long as the interest farmers pay is the cost of waiting for higher prices. What happens when prices nosedive, as they have over the past year and a half, thanks to record harvests of rice in India, wheat in Russia and Australia, and maize in the US and Brazil?
Suddenly, the cultivator, who was once the ideal borrower, turns elusive, and Arya.ag’s recovery cycle lengthens.
How Arya.ag deals with this knotty situation is also a reflection of how resilient India’s farms are in the face of price shocks.
