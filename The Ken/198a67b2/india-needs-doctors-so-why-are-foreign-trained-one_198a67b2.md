Good Morning Ishan,
An estimated 25,000 students leave India each year to pursue medical training abroad. After they acquire their degrees and return home, there isn’t a clearly defined system to bring them into the fold.
The result is that these graduates become unpaid interns, they rack up debt, and their full employment is delayed at a time when the country sorely needs more doctors.
The National Medical Commission has made attempts to address the issue. A slate of initiatives is meant to make it easier for foreign graduates to assimilate into India’s medical field, but those steps haven’t moved the needle enough, The Ken discovered.
Case in point: the red tape for acquiring permanent medical registration in India is so thick that one student found it easier to deal with a war waging around her as a student in Ukraine.
Atul has the full story on the hurdles that prospective doctors encounter when they study abroad and return to practice medicine in their homeland.
