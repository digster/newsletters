Good Morning Ishan,
This week, The Ken celebrated its 7th birthday. We kicked things off by reflecting on our journey so far and the exciting road ahead.
In a world where things keep evolving, and long-form business news is at your fingertips from multiple sources, it’s essential for us to pause and reflect.
Over time, different sectors undergo different transitions. This week, The Ken’s stories delved deep to document these changes.
For instance, Nathan on Monday [looked](https://the-ken.com/story/who-would-you-trust-with-your-kids-study-abroad-dream/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) at the evolving world of study-abroad companies—the consultants and the startups. Despite the rise of tech-savvy upstarts and growing demand for overseas education, it turns out that the old, agent-based firms are still sought after. Some tech startups are even incorporating personalised services into their offerings.
Shivani’s on Tuesday [revealed](https://the-ken.com/story/bnpl-users-are-asked-to-pay-for-transactions-they-never-made/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) how ‘buy now, pay later’—or BNPL—once an innovative offering for those wanting to avail quick and flexible credit options for their purchases, is infested with scams in the absence of well-implemented regulations; and how companies finally taking ownership.
Our Wednesday, Seema and Arundhati teamed up to write a [Big Story](https://the-ken.com/story/the-cost-of-building-a-billion-dollar-ivf-chain-in-india/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) on how an Indian company offering in-vitro fertilisation (IVF) services became a global giant. With infertility becoming a pressing public-health issue affecting one in six people, private equity has recognised the potential and assigned a $1.1 billion valuation to Indira IVF—making it the world’s second-largest such bet.
On Thursday, I did a [status check](https://the-ken.com/story/four-in-10-quit-niti-aayog-in-a-year-time-to-think-or-tank/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) on Niti Aayog—examining the progress of this 8-year-old institution and the challenges it confronts. It has relied heavily on a single leader, Amitabh Kant, for guidance over the years. After his exit, the lack of robust institutional mechanisms has come to the fore.
On Friday, Pratap [dissected](https://the-ken.com/story/disney-hotstars-last-chance-at-survival-cricket-world-cup/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) a make-or-break moment for Hotstar—India’s top streaming platform that lost 21 million subscribers in a year. As it shifts from Disney’s subscription model to an ad-driven one, the 2023 Cricket World Cup could be its last dance.
That wraps up our week. We’ll be back on Monday with another set of delightful, crackling stories.
