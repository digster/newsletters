Good Morning Ishan,
I don't like dwelling on the past. Sometimes people get so hung up on what you were in the past that they forget how far you've come since then.
There's a reason why companies have quarterly evaluation. Because hey, if you were to be judged on your performance in the first quarter alone, why bother even trying the rest of the year. Right?
But if you do decide to reflect on the past, look at growth. And that too relative growth. Unless you realise how you started out in the first place, you can never know how much you've grown.
Yesterday, Pranav and Prajakta had some pertinent reflections on the digital payments systems in India in the past five years. They found that the past five years in India have been a rollercoaster ride for companies from card networks like Visa and Mastercard to Paytm and MobiKwik. And they broke it down in eight graphics. On Thursday, Vandana looked at Xiaomi's past. Particularly how it started out in 2014. Since entering India in July of that year, the Chinese smartphone maker has grown to control 29% of the Indian smartphone market, boasts of 75-80 million users, and accounts for the bulk (57%) of online smartphone sales. But it is only aspiring for more—to get India [addicted to its internet services too](http://link.the-ken.com/wf/click?upn=HQSq6eeRV1xAVaGuFOahuNCZVVXPeP-2FJpSqxhvIUDrl-2BUkz7BFcZBmah3wFL96H5jSN22HdVS6yFo9-2BnrQuWj-2BKHqeg4dT9ICTometipkVSyAHqKSDgeoV0j5UYysqh2ZqJjVu-2FxxowjpFXO6osBkupb2SJ8rmgJxBISnT-2Fhw-2BhvM2CSEhLUTs9lqVZ6LztE_S4jLOsNx-2Fv6Jev2nvow8b7P9pBCWGd8KNZfb200gkZLQNlXQHio0noZzXtsWoloZmPWeyZsh7a4QCV9ufwCUKi00jlBT0uEdvwYDMuVuqcPvDo-2BQjZ-2BH-2Fo-2Fe94Gju-2FPSeytL-2FfmtK0kHSEnjUQSd3m-2B2f5AOBXDrOjfr4G6rVf1rkHvLpKGtnS4vihaYL7x2vB4fBocP9uAnfYFNltxY78mq57Ph5QipxI6zekUMUjkqsPeeReALvPLOnuOR1VAW5lI3-2BnVsifCaiUF2zW-2FXCte6ZqSFH56llwBbd3vhLnzJo2gFzeh8BlmWdyFJLAn6iTb0by-2FsVynLH3HOZfqwG5xRtZXFyvdjP3Nasa2MJSjHt7lucr6khiueCfDZn8og-2FHIQVtytSJuXxxiAs61rEsRtmxplo2QTQwT9EF5IUS4-3D).
