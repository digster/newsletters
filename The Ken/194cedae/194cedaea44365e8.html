The first thing that strikes you in the IPO document of Dr Agarwal’s Healthcare—which makes its stock-market debut today—is its logo. An eye-catching blue-dyed DrA. Also unmissable are the initials of the eye-care hospital chain’s promoters. As if by design, the name of every promoter family member—from Amar to Ashar—starts with an A. Suffix that with an Agarwal, and it’s A riot, alright. Not to mention some cognitive confusion, even if temporary.

The real confusion and the real story, however, lie elsewhere. In the tale of two entities—no prizes for guessing, both Agarwal’s.

You see, many moons ago, about 67 years back, Dr Agarwal’s Eye Hospital was born and then listed in the mid-1990s. It was ambling along, when about a decade back came a holding company, Dr Agarwal’s Healthcare. Yes, the one that’s listing today. So, since then, the unlisted holdco, Dr Agarwal’s Healthcare, has owned the majority of the listed subsidiary and derived much of its value—at least in the early days. Now, you may wonder, why such a convoluted arrangement with an unlisted holdco when there’s already a listed subsidiary. That’s a billion-dollar question—a-billion-and-a-half dollar (Rs 12,600 crore) actually—that Seema digs into and answers in today’s story.

Let’s just say both Agarwal’s—the holdco and the subsidiary—piggybacked on each other and came out beaming. The former, to get top-dollar valuation over the years to its IPO now, and the latter, to finally get some much-delayed-and-denied investor love. After being mostly listless for much of the three decades, the stock of Dr Agarwal’s Eye Hospital soared an eye-watering 1,650% in a little over three years—before letting off quite some steam in the past three weeks. Has cognitive confusion among the As played a part in this startling spectacle? Same-same but different?

Is there more to it than meets the eye?
