Good Morning Ishan,
A 25X revenue jump in two years is impressive, sure, but also borderline unbelievable. Especially when the company in question is Bhagwati Products Ltd, an electronics-manufacturing firm that, until recently, was reckoning with rising competition and a falling order book.
But then the company ups and announces that it will grow its topline to Rs 15,000 crore in FY26, from just Rs 650 crore in FY24. In a market that’s remained largely flat during this time.
Media outlets are already calling this Micromax co-founder Rahul Sharma’s comeback. After all, Micromax holds a majority stake in Bhagwati, and Sharma has been busy talking up Bhagwati’s “locally rooted” rise.
But less talked about is the foreign factor that airlifted Bhagwati to become the fastest-growing phonemaker in the country. Last August, Huaqin Technologies, a Chinese original device manufacturer, bought a 49% stake in Bhagwati. And with this, it brought its rolodex of global clients, and more importantly, huge smartphone manufacturing orders from the likes of Oppo and Vivo.
This is also a sign of the times, where Chinese firms seeking a foothold in India must partner with Indian EMS players. That’s okay for now, given India-China’s fledgling bonhomie amid the trade war with the US. But what happens when there’s another border skirmish?
