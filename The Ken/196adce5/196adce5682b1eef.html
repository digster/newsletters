“When we started Aakash [Institute in 1988] with 12 students on a dining table, no one asked us how big the market was,” said Aakash Chaudhry to Atul, writer of today’s story.

The CEO—famous both for running a successful coaching business and then selling it to the now-infamous edtech Byju’s—is back on the education pitch to play his second innings.

In naming it Sparkl Edventure, he may have avoided the worn-out “edtech”, but the basic idea is the same. I guess, once you’ve seen value in private coaching and have made money off it, you can’t unsee it.

Instead of coaching students to crack engineering or medical tests, this time Chaudhry is focused on the next education craze in India. Especially among the well-heeled families for schools affiliated to the International Baccalaureate (IB).

Today, there are around 250 schools in India that are affiliated to IB. Not all of them are good. The single biggest reason? Not all can afford to hire good teachers.

So, many parents, who are shelling out anywhere from Rs 8 lakh to Rs 12 lakh every year from primary to middle years of schooling, aren’t happy with the teachers.

Chaudhry experienced this firsthand and thus was Sparkl born—a platform to “bridge learning gaps”, offering hourly coaching to both IB and Cambridge students.

Somehow education in India cannot extricate itself from coaching. Atul has a fine read today.
