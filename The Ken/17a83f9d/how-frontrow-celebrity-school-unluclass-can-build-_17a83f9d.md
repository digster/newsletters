Now, what if that guidance came from some of the best in the business? What if you had the chance to be taught the basics of singing by Monali Thakur? Or the art of acting by Manoj Bajpayee? Or how to kickstart your standup comedy career by Biswa Kalyan Rath? Or how to get that idea for a novel from your mind onto paper by Ruskin Bond?
That's the proposition that platforms like FrontRow, Celebrity School, and Unluclass are trading on. These startups are taking advantage of the edtech surge induced by the pandemic, bringing celebrity-taught online classes to Indians. At least four such platforms have cropped up since March 2020, and they've turned investors' heads—FrontRow is reportedly set to raise a $10-12 million Series A round at a $60 million valuation. It was launched only in November 2020. Unluclass, launched in early 2021, claims to have 300,000-400,000 monthly active users, growing 100% month-on-month.
All these platforms are modelled on the San Francisco-based MasterClass, which was launched in 2015 and has built a library of over 100 courses. The celebrities on its roster include Gordon Ramsay, Anna Wintour, James Cameron, Salman Rushdie, and Serena Williams. MasterClass recently raised $225 million at a valuation of $2.75 billion.
