Loyalty rewards, seemingly an ageless programme, is getting crazier. Companies are offering outrageous rewards for customer loyalty. Heck, it also means more fun for customers. Like this Italian restaurant in New York which gives rewards like having the customer’s name engraved on a table. Or a free Ferrari with pizza delivery, after a million purchases.
Rewards have not reached such wild levels in India, but before they became common here, there was a man who saw the opportunity, more than 10 years ago. While having been shunted to his employer’s privilege department. From that perch, Bijaei Jayaraj saw how the airline made money from his loyalty programme and how customers remained loyal.
Once the loyalty division began minting money, Jayaraj wanted it to be hived off, to be run as a separate unit. But Jet Airways was no incubator. Loyal to his idea, Jayaraj walked out to set up Loylty Rewardz. No typos. And at one point, his company handled the rewards programmes for 80% of India’s banks. It cracked a market in India which has been dominated by banks and big retailers.
Until 2015, when it got acquired by the payments gateway company Billdesk, Loylty also remained loyal to its original company blueprint. Patanjali’s story today is on a shy and dogged entrepreneur, on a small B2B company that became the biggest player in this space, on a sector which is fun on the front end, and utter boredom on the backend.
While on the bench at Jet, Jayaraj would walk up to his manager and say: ‘Hi, I played video games at work for eight hours today’. The manager would frown, shake his head and send Jayaraj away. He did it every day for a week. Finally, his manager moved Jayaraj to one of Jet’s forgotten departments, Privilege. It was Jet Airways’ loyalty programme.
