Good Morning Ishan,
 
Some weeks sail by effortlessly. You sip tea on your balcony and watch the rain. Then there are weeks where you help your friend adopt a cat, the cat absconds overnight, and you spend the next 48 hours frantically searching for her.  
 
Yes, this is far too specific an example to be fiction. But then again, real-life drama has a habit of eclipsing fiction. Most of the time, it’s nothing like whatever you’ve read or seen on TV.
 
Take our Tuesday story, for instance. Indore might be voted as the country’s cleanest city, but it also hides a dirty dirty secret—the murky world of investment advisory scams. The city is home to hundreds of unregistered ‘advisors’ conning people into investing money that they never see again. Regulators like Sebi and local law enforcement are attempting to squash the problem, but legitimate operators end up facing the brunt of imposed restrictions, write Anand and Jaspreet in this ground report.
 
On Monday, Aayush and Soumyajit wrote about how Shopsy, Flipkart’s social commerce firm, is burning through cash at what those in the industry say is an alarming rate. Not only is the market dominated by Meesho, but the rival is also moving in on direct customers in the tier-2 and further cities—something Flipkart has had an eye on for a while. It’s a steep hill for Shopsy.
 
How did the product designer suddenly become the cool kid on the block? And why are startups obsessed with them? Olina answered these questions in her story about the Indian startup ecosystem’s need for well trained UI/UX designers. While the need for good product designers has escalated, the number of good design institutes and design graduates is still disproportionately low.
 
Sometimes, drama lurks in the unlikeliest of places—like the B2B raw material procurement segment, which is increasingly becoming a financing-first play. If you have a platform that helps businesses procure raw materials, why not also have a platform that lends to these businesses for said procurement? Oxyzo, OfBusiness’ NBFC arm, became one of the first subsidiaries of an Indian unicorn to become one too. The company has managed to triple its loan book to $350 million in two years. But it still has to contend with multiple rivals, like Capital Float, CredAvenue, and Credit Saison.
 
On Wednesday, Bhumika wrote about Captain Fresh trying to be the Licious of B2B meat supply. But while Licious and FreshtoHome focus on customers, Captain Fresh is focusing on fisherfolk, sourcing intermediaries, slaughtering and processing centres, and retailers.
That’s it from us this week!
 
As for the cat, rest easy. She was successfully found lurking under my friend's apartment stairs, safely wrangled back into her carrier, and returned to the original cat parents for the time being. Here’s to hoping there’s less drama in our lives in the coming days.
 
I hope you have a restful Sunday. We’ll be back Monday, as always.
