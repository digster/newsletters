Good Morning Ishan,
If imitation is the best form of flattery, Lahori Zeera must be feeling chuffed. The sweet success of its jeera-drink—born seven years back out of a kitchen experiment in Punjab—has nudged even giants like Coca-Cola and Parle to come up with their me-toos.
Now, it’s not that jeera-based beverages were a novelty discovered by Lahori Zeera. This quintessential Indian digestion-friendly concoction with cumin as its centre has been around for ages—in homes and shops across the country. Regional favourites ruled the roost. Bindu, Sosyo, Davat, Jeeru? If you know, you know.
Lahori Zeera? Many more seem to know now. The drink has managed to shake up the status quo, and make that rare transition from a regional player to a national one. Its bottles are flying off the shelves across large parts of North India, cash registers are ringing loud, and investors are lining up. New horizons in the East and West beckon.
How did Lahori do what it did, and break the Coke-Pepsi-Parle hold? Let’s just say it followed the Ps of marketing to a T. But does it have what it takes to keep going? Or does it risk tripping over its bottle?
Pranathi has the answers in today’s perfect Friday story in the midst of a raging Indian summer. Grab your jeera and [read on](https://the-ken.com/story/how-lahori-broke-coke-pepsi-parles-hold-on-beverages/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
