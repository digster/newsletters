Good Morning Ishan,
Indian Premier League franchises Mumbai Indians and Delhi Capitals are owned by billionaires Mukesh Ambani and Sajjan Jindal (whose JSW Sports controls 50% of the latter), respectively.
But the teams couldn’t be more different.
Mumbai Indians have won a record five of the 16 IPL titles. And Delhi Capitals have just made it to the final once, losing to Mumbai.
Even so, JSW Sports has in recent years become Ambani-owned Reliance Industries’ strongest rival in Indian sports.
Besides Delhi Capitals, it owns a cricket franchise in South Africa (Pretoria Capitals), a football club (Bengaluru FC), and a kabaddi team (Haryana Steelers).
But where JSW really scores over Reliance is its track record in athletics. At the 2023 Asian Games, JSW-backed athletes won 17 of the country’s total medal tally of 107.
How JSW managed to pull this off is at the centre of my colleague Nathan’s story: [https://the-ken.com/story/how-sajjan-jindal-became-mukesh-ambanis-top-rival-in-shaping-indian-sports/](https://the-ken.com/story/how-sajjan-jindal-became-mukesh-ambanis-top-rival-in-shaping-indian-sports/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (8-min read)
