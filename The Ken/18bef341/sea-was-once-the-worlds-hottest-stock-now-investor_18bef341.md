Good Morning Ishan,
Sea Limited should have everything going for it.
The group’s three verticals—e-commerce, video games, and financial services—are lines of business that have massive customer bases. More than that, they’re segments where other companies have already cracked the code. There is no shortage of success cases to emulate, with adjustments to fit users in Southeast Asia.
Even though Sea is still the region’s biggest tech company, the company has trouble convincing investors that it’s building a sustainable operation.
Katrina took a closer look at how Sea has been doing this year. One of the first things she noticed is that all three of the company’s earnings calls were followed by harsh sell-offs.
It appears that shareholders have been ditching their shares whenever company leadership spoke. The question is why.
