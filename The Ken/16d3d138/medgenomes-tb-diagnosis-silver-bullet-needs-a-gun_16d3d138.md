India is a ticking tuberculosis time-bomb.
Heavy words for a Tuesday morning, I know. But bear with me.
All the way back in 2017, we reported on a massive shortage of the drugs required to treat multi-drug resistant (MDR) TB in India. And even those drugs the government could procure weren't reaching those most in need.
Well, it's 2019 now, and a study published in July found that India's MDR TB strains were building resistance to some of the newest antibiotics developed to fight the disease. There've only been three new antibiotics over the last half decade to fight TB, FYI.
August, though, brought good news for India's TB sufferers. A home-grown diagnostics test—SPIT SEQ by Bengaluru-based MedGenome.
Here's why it's exciting: It has a faster turnaround time for diagnosis. By a matter of 5-6 weeks. It tests TB resistance to all 13 TB drugs, as opposed to the handful of drugs other diagnostic tests check for. And it's extremely accurate.
But...
MedGenome has a real battle ahead of it. Because to get SPIT SEQ to the mass market it needs to lower the price of the test and its turnaround time. But to lower its price and turnaround time, it needs volumes, which brings us back to the mass market. It's a chicken and egg situation. And one that's especially odd for the genomics startup—because this is not where it typically plays.
To take SPIT SEQ to those beyond the large metros with the reference labs the test requires, MedGenome will need a stamp of approval from the apex medical research body—ICMR. But this is a long and arduous process, as those that have gone before it—like GeneXpert or TrueNat—can attest. And even if manages this, it will still have to convince the government to add it to the national TB programme.
Suraksha's story today is about MedGenome's path forward. A journey that's full of promise if the Bengaluru-based company can pull it off. With Sequoia in its corner, it's betting it can.
