Good Morning Ishan,
We have two stories for you today.
First, Grab had its first profitable quarter in Q4 2023 and said it would buy back US$500 million worth of class A shares, but investors aren’t happy. Its stock dived more than 10% after the earnings call on Thursday and hasn’t recovered since. Grab CEO Anthony Tan spoke last year about making “1% improvements” across the company’s business segments. The tech company has managed to reap those fruits for its transportation and delivery verticals but hasn’t cracked the code for its financial services unit yet. Sources told Katrina that this last line may only become [profitable](https://the-ken.com/story/grab-finally-hits-profitability-but-one-vertical-remains-a-headache/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) if Grab rethinks its banking strategy entirely, rather than lean on its existing customer base and ecosystem.
Then, Mohit Joshi became the CEO of Tech Mahindra, the smallest among the India-based “Big 5” IT companies. In fact, to take the position, he took a pay cut when he left his job at Infosys. It’s easy to see why—Joshi is driven, the type of leader who can turn a company around; he wants to overhaul Tech Mahindra by taking a page from the way his former employer reorganised its business structure over the past 17 years, while continuing his specialty of roping in large deals. Abhirami did a [deep dive](https://the-ken.com/story/tech-mahindras-new-ceo-wants-to-shake-things-up-the-infosys-way/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) into how this will play out for the company.
Making it into the big(ger) leagues
At their cores, both of our stories today are about shaking things up within a company. Grab hit a financial milestone, but still managed to put off its shareholders. Tech Mahindra is finding its way with a new leader at the helm, a development that had bumped up its share price.
Grab is a tough company to slice. Its offerings are hardly unique, and the quality of service is hit-or-miss. Your location matters: The experience of using Grab can be janky and frustrating in Singapore, but service can be smooth as silk in the Philippines or Thailand. To this day, shareholders who bought the company’s stock during its December 2021 SPAC merger gripe about the monumental hit their portfolios took on the first day of trading, then about their slide over the ensuing months if they held on.
It’s great to make it into the big leagues; Grab’s latest achievement is no mean feat. But to have a spot in the all-star race—and keep it—there’s a lot that still needs to be tuned up, including the relationships with people who believed the company was worth its narrative.
