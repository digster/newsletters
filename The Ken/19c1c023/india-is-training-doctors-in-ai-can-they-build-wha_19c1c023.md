The world is awash in health AI.
Earlier in January, OpenAI launched ChatGPT Health, a feature that can analyse users’ health records. Days later, its rival Anthropic launched a similar feature in Claude. Google, too, dipped its toes into this with the release of Medgemma. Between healthcare infrastructure, consumer needs, and clinical potential, AI now covers a gamut.
But while hospitals are ignoring it, the Indian government is catching the AI by its horns. Or at least it plans to.
On 21 January, the Union Health Ministry launched an AI-training programme in medical education. It aims to equip 50,000 doctors with digital skills to understand AI applications in health for diagnostics, clinical decision-making, and research.
In fact, even before the six-month programme was announced, it had signed on over 42,000 doctors.
“The government is not working in the typical way as far as AI in healthcare is concerned,” said a healthcare professional associated with the programme. “They are very systematic and aggressive.”
The private sector is also enthused.
On 30 January, when the Government Institute of Medical Sciences (GIMS) in Greater Noida, outside Delhi, hosted a “[startup OPD](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amitsingalca_gims-startup-innovation-activity-7423412488865529856-ZnqJ)”, bringing together founders and doctors to discuss innovation, several investors also wanted in. Among those eager to hear startup pitches were Ekamya Capital, Soonicorn Ventures, and Indicorn Angels, each managing Rs 100 crore+ funds. They were equally interested in how clinicians at the event evaluated those pitches, too.
“That’s the key; that’s where investors have agreed to come down…” said Rahul Singh Amritraj, who heads GIMS. “They said, ‘if your doctors can validate and help us, it becomes easy [to invest]’.”
That wasn’t the only “AI in health” discussion held in the city that day. The Ken learnt that a medical-regulatory body met with different stakeholders, including doctors and those managing compliance, to formulate regulations for AI use in healthcare.
But all of this is just scratching the surface...
