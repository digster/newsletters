[A Rs 2,500 cr scam powered by mule accounts: an illustrated guide](https://the-ken.com/story/a-rs-2500-cr-scam-powered-by-mule-accounts-an-illustrated-guide/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
A slick new “tech service” is streamlining fraud for scammers and muddying the waters for law enforcement
India’s fraud economy funnels Rs 2,500 crore monthly through “money mules” lending their bank accounts for illicit transactions. A new model, “Mule-as-a-Service”, handles recruitment and account management, letting scammers focus on growing their operations. Mules operate in layers, from low-level recruits to managers who convert funds to crypto or send them abroad. Despite AI-driven efforts, regulators recover little of the stolen funds as mule networks outpace detection.

[How Rajkot derailed Hyundai’s $3 billion IPO](https://the-ken.com/story/how-a-small-city-in-gujarat-derailed-hyundais-3-billion-ipo/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
The tier-2 city in Gujarat is not just the country’s third-largest source of retail IPO applications; its influence in the grey market decides the fate of most listings
Hyundai India’s Rs 28,000 crore IPO fizzled out despite the hype, largely due to retail investors’ reluctance. The road to Hyundai’s travails passed through a seemingly inconsequential place in the state of Gujarat—the tier-2 city of Rajkot. The city sends in the third-largest number of retail IPO applications in the country and houses its “greatest” grey market. It operates in the shadows but with the grey market flashing red, retail investors in Rajkot and beyond stayed away from India’s largest IPO.

[Zetwerk is the $3 billion supplier of factories that no one saw coming](https://the-ken.com/story/zetwerk-is-the-3-billion-supplier-of-factories-that-no-one-saw-coming/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
The six-year-old startup is a messiah to small firms with unused factories, and a reliable ally to those needing round-the-year work
Zetwerk, which began as a SaaS company in 2018, evolved into a “managed” marketplace for manufacturers in a matter of months. Its marketplace portal enlists MSMEs as suppliers and takes in orders from large companies, thus playing the role of a middleman. The model has helped Zetwerk scale up, more than doubling its annual revenue in FY23; it’s also become a base for its own manufacturing activities. What sets the model apart is its enhanced capacity to produce goods—by outsourcing work—ensuring faster turnaround time. [The age of DesignerX: why universities are racing to train India’s next design generalists](https://the-ken.com/story/the-age-of-designerx-why-universities-are-racing-to-train-indias-next-design-generalists/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
From being a sidekick to entering the mainstream, designers are no longer the underdogs in companies. Universities, too, are rising to the occasion with design-focussed degrees
Design education has taken off in India. From the dominance of a few top tier institutes, at least a dozen universities have started offering design degrees now. Unlike most old design schools that offered specialisation in disciplines in silos, the shift now is towards multi-dimensional approach where several specialities are offered under one roof.
As designers experience an unprecedented demand, with industry offering them more leadership roles, academia has responded by infusing design graduates with tech and business acumen. Recruiters though sound caution; as more universities get into the picture and designers get produced by the dozen, landing good jobs is set to get tougher in a mature job market.
