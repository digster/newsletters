Google came late to the large language model (LLM) party after OpenAI beat it to the punch in 2022 with the launch of ChatGPT.
But the search giant is trying to make up for lost time by finding the most effective way to sell its LLM, Gemini, to enterprises.
And it is relying on what it knows well. “For the longest time, Google for workplaces meant primarily email. We want to change that to Gemini,” a Google employee told Abhirami, the author of today’s story.
It helps that Gemini comes with Workspace, or with its cloud-computing service Google Cloud Platform (GCP), and the hope is that if you’re already paying for one, you’ll happily—or at least passively—end up paying for all three.
It is also driving adoption of its suite among enterprises by marketing Gemini as a “work companion”, and not as a consumer product, like ChatGPT.
But with everyone’s pricing and products converging towards each other, Google has its work cut out on that front.
