I don’t know how this happened, but I’ve been reliably told by my colleagues that blurting stuff out to your hardware devices is gonna get big. I rarely believe my colleagues, so I did my own little research. Here are some of the headlines I found in just under two minutes: Eleven jokes that show off Amazon Alexa’s sense of humour; Parenting in the age of Alexa, is it safe; 7 ways Alexa can make Halloween even spookier; Amazon Alexa devices can finally tell voices apart. Jesus, what fresh hell have we chanced upon?

Well now that it is here, we might as well talk about it.

Earlier yesterday, Amazon launched the Echo range of smart speakers in India. The first set of devices will start shipping on 31 October, by invite for Amazon Prime members. That’s about 7 million users in the country. By no means a small number.

Prepping for the launch, Amazon has been working with folks outside its ecosystem to create specific use cases for consumers. The available voice skills in India include music streaming from Saavn, booking Ola cab on Echo, sourcing recipes from Tarla Dalal, ordering food from Zomato and booking tickets on Goibibo. Of course, this doesn’t take away from the fact that Alexa can also do mundane tasks like play a song, check the weather, or simply keep your kid entertained.

My colleague Payal says that while this is how things will start, Amazon, of course, has a strategy. And a head start compared to Google and Apple in India. One it wants to exploit to understand its 7 million premium customers. What do they like? What do they want? What do they order? That’s Payal’s piece for today.
