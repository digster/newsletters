Good Morning Ishan,
It’s really not a good time, excuse the pun, to be a smartwatch brand in India. The likes of Boat and Noise, high-fliers until not so long ago, now don’t seem so smart after all. They are forced to keep a close watch on their fading fortunes—with sales crashing and profits turning into losses thick and fast.
That’s because the market for smartwatches in the country has gone from dizzy highs to depressing lows, within just one year. Shipments have crashed by half from a peak of 17 million in the September 2023 quarter. People who bought smartwatches aren’t buying anymore—they simply aren’t useful enough.
For this sorry state of affairs, smartwatch incumbents have no one to blame but themselves. Joining a race to the bottom triggered by new kids on the block like Fire-boltt and Boult turned out to be a blunder. Price cuts to ridiculous levels and a slew of launches to defend market share came at a big cost: compromises on quality and features. The biggest cost though was the flight of the customer.
Boat and Noise are now looking to turn back the clock, finds Gaurav, the author of today’s story. A return to basics and core competencies is part of the plan. But is a win even possible at this stage? At one end of the spectrum are the price-cutters and at the other are quality leaders like Apple and Samsung. Will a victory, even if it comes, be pyrrhic?
