Mukesh Ambani, the chairman of Reliance Industries, didn't say "Your margin is my opportunity". Amazon founder Jeff Bezos did.
If you're one of India's large and well-known FMCG companies though, the difference won't matter. Because with Reliance Retail acquiring most of the businesses of its second-largest retail competitor, Future Group, the FMCG firms must now deal with a behemoth whose growth ambitions will intersect with their own. And not in a pretty way.
Post the acquisition, Reliance Retail will have 2.5 times the number of grocery stores it did earlier with an annual revenue of $6.6 billion. It will not just become the largest modern retailer in India; it would also become the single largest customer for FMCG firms across multiple categories.
For most large retailers, including Reliance Retail, two metrics create the virtuous cycle of growth—prices and margins. Lower prices keep customers coming back, while high margins fund expansion and lower prices. Private labels are where this cycle is most apparent.
Consumers, on average, save 15-20% when they buy a private brand, and retailers still gain an additional margin of 10-20%.
So when a customer walks into a Reliance Retail outlet and buys, say, a 6 kg pack of Reliance's Enzo detergent powder for top-load washing machines at Rs 650 ($8), it is 60% cheaper than Procter & Gamble's Ariel and 40% cheaper than Hindustan Unilever's Surf Excel. And yet, Reliance Retail makes even more in margin.
Ariel's and Surf Excel's margin is quite literally Enzo's opportunity. Branded products in this category offer margins of 15-17% while in-house brands give 35-40%. In many cases, the same manufacturer that makes products for FMCG companies makes it for large retailers too.
If you're Hindustan Unilever, detergents contributed 22.5% of your total sales and 100% of your incremental sales growth for the year ended 2020. Ouch.
FMCG giants may think that the millions of mom-and-pop stores would act as a counterweight to Reliance Retail's dominance. Well, Reliance is already doing over 400,000 orders each day through them using JioMart, its WhatsApp-powered hyperlocal delivery platform.
