Today’s briefing by Brady Ng
Deputy Editor in Southeast Asia

Good Morning Ishan,

My friends and acquaintances have been purchasing homes. Some do this to settle down, start a family, and plant some roots. Others see it as part of immigration schemes or a way to derive income by renting out the units or listing them on Airbnb.

This has plunged us into rabbit holes, where we learned about €1 estates in rural Italy (read: abandoned homes), akiya (empty houses) in small Japanese towns, and cheap condos in Thailand.

The conclusion? You get what you pay for.

Almost everyone wants to own a spot they can call home. In Singapore, though, prospective homebuyers must contend with climbing property prices. Meanwhile, renters also need to budget for rising monthly payments.

Singapore’s property market is as hot as ever, even though the government is trying to slowly ease the pressure.

While home prices have climbed far faster than incomes, the situation has sparked interest in a type of investment vehicle that, in a way, is a step towards owning real estate.

Our new contributor, Taro, has the story. Check it out here: https://the-ken.com/story/realty-prices-are-scorching-in-singapore-yet-investors-cant-stay-away/ [7-minute read]
