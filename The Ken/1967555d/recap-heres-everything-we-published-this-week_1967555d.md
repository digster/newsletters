The data obtained is used to profile users and figure out who to send offers and issue loans to. Both Indian companies and consumers have a cavalier attitude to data, leading to a rising number of data-security breaches. This breached data is subsequently sold to apps on the grey market, and again used by these apps to target you. One of the three companies globally to show cell therapy results for dry AMD, an eye disorder affecting nearly 200 million globally, Bengaluru’s Eyestem is also eyeing a disruptive price
Eyestem has created a stem-cell therapy that’s improving vision in early studies for legally blind patients of dry AMD, a disorder affecting 5 million in India. Cell therapies are obscenely priced, it’s just the nature and logistics of the medication. Having spent just $10 million to get this far, Eyestem is aiming to price it under $10,000 when it enters the Indian market next year. Global pharma is queuing up to partner; Indian billionaires want to help enhance the access, too. Will the Bengaluru startup live up to its promise?
