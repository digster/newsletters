Using Swiggy, Zepto, or Cred? They have access to at least 150 apps on your phone

It’s a dire time to be an Indian consumer, with apps trafficking other apps’ data from users’ phones and engaging in under-the-table deals

In March, a software developer who goes by the alias Pea Bee discovered how different apps obtain access to other apps on your phone. The data obtained is used to profile users and figure out who to send offers and issue loans to. Both Indian companies and consumers have a cavalier attitude to data, leading to a rising number of data-security breaches. This breached data is subsequently sold to apps on the grey market, and again used by these apps to target you. [Read the full story]

How a 14-member biotech spent just $10M on a product global pharma wants to lap up

One of the three companies globally to show cell therapy results for dry AMD, an eye disorder affecting nearly 200 million globally, Bengaluru’s Eyestem is also eyeing a disruptive price

Eyestem has created a stem-cell therapy that’s improving vision in early studies for legally blind patients of dry AMD, a disorder affecting 5 million in India. Cell therapies are obscenely priced, it’s just the nature and logistics of the medication. Having spent just $10 million to get this far, Eyestem is aiming to price it under $10,000 when it enters the Indian market next year. Global pharma is queuing up to partner; Indian billionaires want to help enhance the access, too. Will the Bengaluru startup live up to its promise? [Read the full story]

Angel One got what it wished for. That’s the problem

It ditched the full-service suit for a discount-broking hoodie. Now, with the markets turning, it might need to borrow a tie from HDFC and ICICI banks

Angel One rode the retail boom of 2020–24 straight into the top tier of Indian brokerages. It had smartly reinvented itself as a discount broker just before the party started. Then Sebi crashed the party in late 2024, causing F&O volumes to drop by 40% with new rules. Now Angel wants to hedge its bets—serving both the everyday trader and the high-net-worth elite. [Read the full story]

When more Youtube is not better Youtube for brands

Advertisers are paying Youtube to play ads for their target audience. But loopholes in the platform’s checks are leaking them to undesirable ones, too

Brands have a basic business need—they want to advertise on a platform with the maximum eyeballs. In India, that’s undisputedly Youtube. Naturally, every brand wants to get to their target audience through the channel. But what happens when Youtube is unable to place these ads only for the target audience? Two distinct winners emerge. But there’s only one endurer. [Read the full story]

*******

Swiggy’s response to Zomato’s District is less an answer, more a question

The foodtech giant’s bid to diversify beyond sluggish horizons in food delivery and quick commerce is caught between a rock and a hard place

With their food delivery business stagnating and quick commerce burning cash, Swiggy and Zomato are looking for their next big bet. Swiggy has launched Pyng—a marketplace connecting consumers to skilled professionals. But it’s not such a new venture after all. Pyng is the evolution of Swiggy’s not-so-successful brand-storefront platform Minis, tweaked to address the latter’s shortcomings. It’s also taken a leaf out of Urban Company’s book. But a similar approach may not work when operating in a different category. [Read the full story]
