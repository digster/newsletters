Good Morning Ishan,
 

In case you missed Ashish’s blog and The Ken’s tweets from earlier in the week, this is probably the first you’ve ever heard of me. I’m the latest addition to the team at The Ken, and probably their strangest one yet. 

Apart from just being a physical oddity, I’ve spent my time in journalism doing things vastly different from the stuff The Ken does. I’ve scripted satire shows, managed a features team, and, in a moment of spectacularly poor judgement, played the role of a morbidly depressed Manmohan Singh. I’m telling you that last part in the spirit of full disclosure, please, for the love of all that is good, do not look it up.

But, like with every great Hollywood sports story, the misfit eventually has his moment in the sun, and I’d like to think that’s sort of the case with how I came to be a part of the incredible team here. 

Even before my first day, I was casually stalking my new colleagues on Twitter (Don’t judge, we all do it). Apart from finding out Rohin has an unhealthy obsession with mangoes, I came across a tweet from one of the founders stating The Ken didn’t believe in carrying a knee-jerk reaction to the Flipkart-Walmart acquisition. When I punched in on Monday, I realised why. 

Sumanth’s piece on the deal, the week’s first, was markedly different from everything I’d read prior. Without raining on the Indian e-commerce parade, he carefully peels away the hype to reveal Walmart’s ultimate motivation in splashing the cash. What’s more, there wasn’t a single Walkart/Flipmart joke. You’re welcome.

Tuesday’s story went in an altogether different direction, with Pat's story on how Paperboat, the healthy fruit drink brand, was far from a vision of health. Backed into a corner, Pat analyses Paperboat’s options.

With the Delhi summer taking its toll on those of us karmically punished to work here, it was little wonder that Ruhi’s piece this week focused on something that helps one beat the heat—yoghurt. For fans of yoghurt, in all its delicious forms, this piece brings good news.

Amidst all of these, we also had a two-parter (part I and part II) from Arundhati that suddenly makes journalism seem like a pretty good career choice. IT professionals with decades of experience are suddenly finding themselves out in the cold as tech companies look to younger, fresher talent instead. For those of you whose parents unsuccessfully tried to push you into IT, it's worth subscribing to The Ken for this one alone. Sure, the first part is free, but if you want the full satisfaction of that “I told you so” moment, you might want to subscribe for part two.


Sorry about how long this letter was, I promise our long form is usually confined to the articles. But you only get one first email, and I figured I’d make it count. Have a great weekend!

Regards,
Ranjan Crasta
