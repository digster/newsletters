Good Morning Ishan,
Group chat: “Ebitda BFFs only”
(a totally fictional conversation… unless it isn’t)
Zomato (sorry, Eternal):
Q4 results are in, folks. Adjusted Ebitda at Rs 165 crore. Down 15% year-on-year, but hey—still headline material.
Swiggy:
Oof. Rough quarter?
Eternal:
Nahhh, just some “accelerated store expansion” in quick commerce. Totally normal.
Anyway, we kept the really bad number out of the spotlight.
Firstcry:
Oh?
Eternal:
Net profit is down 78%. But who’s checking the bottom of the line when adjusted Ebitda is the bottom line, amirite?
Paytm*:
Preach. Always lead with the adjusted. Net profit is for GAAP nerds.
Swiggy:
I rode the same wave in December. Adjusted loss: Rs 490 crore. Real loss? Sshhh… Rs 799 crore. I call it “profit vibes”.
Firstcry:
I lost Rs 15 crore, but adjusted my way to Rs 139 crore in “profit”. Best quarter ever, baby.
TBO Tek:
We can always show the bridge from net to adjusted buried somewhere in the annexure. Transparency-ish. Wink!
Policybazaar:
It’s not a lie if it’s in a footnote.
Eternal:
Exactly. It’s not adjusting the truth. It’s adjusting the narrative.
SEBI (typing…)
***
You see, adjusted Ebitda is a beautiful thing. It takes regular Ebitda—which already ignores interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortisation (aka most things that make a business hard)—and just keeps going.
Stock-based comp? Not real.
Salaries after an M&A? Just a phase.
Rent? Only matters if you believe in buildings.
Reality? Entirely optional.
It’s all in service of telling the story investors want to hear. Are you ready to explore the alternate reality of internet-era accounting?
