Good Morning Ishan,
 
Confession time—I've never really aspired to own a car. As friends turned eighteen and made a beeline for driving tests and auto dealerships, I dreamt of days spent in the backseat—headphones on, book out.
 
Blame it on growing up in Bangalore's perma-jammed traffic. Blame it on rising fuel prices. Blame it on the convenience of cab aggregators like Ola and Uber.
 
At the end of the day, I'm one of those millennials who's ruining the auto sector, as Indian Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman so eloquently put it.
 
Whether or not that blame truly belongs at my doorstep is anyone's guess, but what's indisputable is the desperate state of India's automobile space. With India's economy slowing, automobile sales in the country have seen their worst fall in over two decades.
 
But while new cars are gathering dust in showrooms, used cars still have plenty of takers. In fact, India's used car market is bigger than the new car market. And growing steadily, too. Which is why despite the doom and gloom in the space, the four major used car startups—Cars24, CarDekho, Spinny, and Cash My Car—have managed to raise $620 million in total in the last three months alone.
