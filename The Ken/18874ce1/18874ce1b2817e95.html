An industry mothballed for long is seeing a sudden revival in West Bengal.

After the ban on single-use plastic in countries across the world, global retailing giants, such as Walmart, Ikea, and Tesco, are rushing to Kolkata-based jute mills, seeking sustainable alternatives for shopping bags.

This demand has led to a surge in exports of jute. India exported jute bags worth over Rs 990 crore (US$120 million) for the year ended March 2023, growing by more than 50% from the year ended March 2021, according to the National Jute Board.

This means farmers who had once given up on jute farming are suddenly taking to it again; even the number of exporters entering the business has gone up.

Interestingly, even though jute is a lot costlier, companies are adopting the golden fibre over other alternatives like cotton given the huge amount of water such crops require.

But while Indian jute makers are making the most of this opportunity, neighbour Bangladesh also has its sights set on the same opportunity.
