“Go to where the puck is going, not where it has been,” is one of the most overused quotes in sports and business. Perhaps because it is so succinct and powerful. Spoken by the father of Canadian ice hockey player Wayne Gretzky, Walter Gretzky, it recommends anticipating and preparing for the future, instead of merely reacting to the present.
 
Ignore the events. Spot the patterns. Understand the behaviour.
 
As economies the world over begin to fathom the deep destruction of businesses and livelihoods as the second-order fallout from Covid-19 lockdowns, it is important to look beyond the current job losses. And instead, focus our efforts on where job creation is likely to take off first.
 
Economist and contributor Ritika Mankar studies the numbers for India and tries to divine where the jobs puck will be. Will it be the “Super Seven” sectors, which in 2019 added 95,000 more new jobs over the previous year? Will it be primarily in younger metro cities like Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, and Ahmedabad, which accounted for nearly 80% of new jobs? Will it be in tech, long the bellwether sector for jobs growth?
 
Please do read and share Ritika’s story, not the least of all because it's also free: https://the-ken.com/story/post-covid-jobs-market/ [8-minute read]
 
It’s disappointing to note that economists like Ritika are forced to rely on largely private estimates of job growth from sources like companies (Teamlease, Naukri) or B-school recruitments. Because the last big-picture government report on India’s employment scenario was a July 2017-June 2018 labour force survey (PLFS).
