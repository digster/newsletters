Good Morning Ishan,
 
I have a confession to make. It's really difficult to write a good wrap-up email that goes out on Independence Day. The stakes are too high. Saying anything about the largest democracy in the world, on her birthday, means striking just the right tone of poignancy, hope, and nostalgia. 
 
But a minute away from August 15, I'm not going to attempt anything like that. Instead, I'm just going to talk to you about a fleeting moment of happiness that ballooned inside me last night. The first night, in one-and-a-half years, that I felt free from the fear that has gripped the entire world, and put all of us in a state of social, economic, and emotional deep freeze. Some of that fear thawed yesterday. And when I looked up, I saw the buildings around me light up in the tri-colour. Purely coincidental. Slightly poetic. 
 
It strikes me now, as I write this, that the absence of fear itself was freeing. It's a feeling that's worth revisiting I think, especially with more tough times ahead for the country. Freedom is fuel, and we use it liberally when we're trying to bring you the best, boldest stories from the business world. This last week was no different. 
 
On Tuesday, Arundhati broke down 13-year-old PolicyBazaar's plan to raise money from the public markets. PolicyBazaar revolutionised the online insurance aggregation market. But in order to raise more money, PolicyBazaar will have to enter a new arena of play: selling insurance offline. 
 
On Monday, Pratap took us into the chaotic world of Vi, which is on the verge of shutting down. And if it does, a third of India's telco triad will disintegrate, leaving in place a duopoly between Jio and Airtel. Vi has limited options for survival. Either the government does a Satyam-style bailout for the company, or Vi hikes its tariffs. Either way, the telco war is going to get ugly before things settle down, says Pratap, in our free read for the week.
 
On Thursday, Munsif wrote about a brewing concern within the Disney+ Hotstar camp. What happens when their exclusive contract to air the IPL ends in 2022? Almost 50% of Disney+ Hotstar's ad revenue in India comes from the IPL season. But the streaming service is prepping a plan B with a revamped subscription strategy and a focus on original content.
 
Our Wednesday story was a candid and reflective interview with Prajit Nanu, the chief executive and co-founder of Nium, Southeast Asia's first B2B fintech unicorn. Nanu opened up to Jon about the company’s behind-the-scenes struggle to raise growth capital after the pandemic, including when $60 million in promised funding just vanished once Covid made landfall. A raw, rare interview about business model pivots and the faith of investors.
 
We rounded the week off with a story about storytellers. I teamed up with Bhumika to write about India's largest regional language self-publishing platform Pratilipi, and its relationship with its latest funder, the South Korean gaming giant Krafton. On the surface, the interest is hard to decipher. The key, we argue, lies in Pratilipi's aggressive hunt for literary gold, and characters that form the basis for a uniquely Indian cinematic universe.
 
Our Saturday newsletter The Nutgraf was on its first break in two years this week. Praveen has promised us a stellar edition next week to make it up. #nopressure
 
In our daily newsletter, Beyond the First Order, Pranav peeled back the layers of a surprise announcement by Amazon on ending a joint venture in India; Seetharaman wrote about why Zomato's decision to not engage in quarterly calls with investors could impact valuations; and in light of the report by the IPCC, Nithin examined how since the real polluters haven't been paying up, the burden is falling on the emerging economies.

Happy Independence Day.
