…then indeed, it must be an interesting investor. I’m talking about Peepul Capital, the enigmatic private equity fund run by Sandeep Reddy. Across three funds, Peepul has raised over $700 million. And while it calls itself a “growth-oriented private equity” fund (about as vanilla as car models being called “movement-oriented combustion engines”), its observed behaviour is quite unique.
You see, in many cases, Peepul Capital behaves like a venture capital (VC) company when it makes the investment, and a buyout fund once it’s the major investor.
Take Brandis, which in 2012 was a one-year-old innerwear and activewear company founded by Nischal Puri, the former marketing head of Jockey India. Peepul Capital came flying in to invest $13.5 million. Very VC! (Because VCs like spotting interesting startups early on to invest in.)
For an 85% stake in the young company! So un-VC, but so buyout! (Buyout funds acquire controlling stakes in mature but undervalued companies.)
Coming in early to invest in fledgeling startups only to take a controlling stake in them is… inexplicable. Anyhow, Brandis’ founder resigned, forfeited his shares, and started a new company.
In a meticulously reported story today, Seema peels the mystique around Peepul Capital. Why are so many of its investments in trouble—Medall, Unibic, Cura Healthcare, Maiyas, Oriental Cuisines? Why do so many of its founders not see eye to eye with it? When will it see some decent exits on its investments?
