Good Morning Ishan,
 
Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman once asserted that overconfidence is a powerful source of all illusions, primarily determined by the quality and coherence of the story you can construct, not by its validity.
 
Today’s story allows you, the reader, to judge the confidence and capabilities of a relatively new kid on the electric-mobility block.
 
The company in question is four-year-old Blusmart, an electric-cab-hailing service not many of you outside Delhi-NCR or Bengaluru would have heard of. The protagonist is its rather iconoclastic co-founder Punit Goyal. The characters at the receiving end of his perceived challenge are ride-hailing giants Uber and Ola.
 
So, what would you say about the mindset of startup founders who only want to target “the learned, Ivy League-educated Indians” with the ability to pay a premium to travel in EVs for a better future?
 
That the gentry, which rides these electric cabs, are discerning tree-hugging nature lovers gasconading their monumental efforts in reducing their carbon footprints on a transatlantic flight from New Delhi to Newark.
 
That even executives at competing firms ditch their company’s decrepit cabs to enjoy the premium feel of a Blusmart EV and then thank the founder for introducing some competition.
 
That companies like Ola that plan to challenge Blusmart are likely to fail, given their failed attempt to launch an electric-taxi service in Nagpur a few years ago.
 
Some chutzpah for sure. But is there ballast behind Blusmart’s confidence? Shaswata finds out: https://the-ken.com/story/blusmart-wants-ride-hailing-glory-by-saying-no-to-uber-olas-scale/ (8-minute read)
