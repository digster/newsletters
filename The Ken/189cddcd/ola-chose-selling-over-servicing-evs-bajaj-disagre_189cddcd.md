Good Morning Ishan,
I can’t think of a market leader in India as divisive as Ola Electric.
The Bhavish Aggarwal-led, Softbank-backed company controls 40% of the electric two-wheeler industry. But for every Ola cheerleader on social media, you’ll find a trenchant critic of its scooters and, increasingly, its after-sales service.
Since it sold its first scooter in 2021, Ola has set up 200 service centres. But it has opened 600 experience centres in the same period.
Ola’s strategy was decidedly to sell its way to the top of the market and figure out the service problem later.
That clearly hasn’t worked out all that well for Ola.
So it’s not surprising that one of its challengers, Bajaj Auto, has done the exact opposite, even though its electric scooter, Chetak, is far from a customer favourite yet. It has pressured its dealers to open service centres, too.
That’s certainly great news for anyone looking to buy a Chetak, but not for the dealers themselves. For one, servicing EVs is just not worth their while the way fixing petrol-powered two-wheelers is.
