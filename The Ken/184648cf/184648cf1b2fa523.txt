Is it a culture shock? Is it poor timing? Is it lousy judgement?

These are just some of the questions that come up every time a startup is shunned abroad, even after tasting much success in India.

And these firms are from varied sectors. Foodtech startup Zomato, hospitality chain Oyo, and ride-hailing company Ola have all had to scale back from new geographies despite being flush with cash.

The protagonist of today’s homecoming story, though, is Urban Company (UC). And the storyteller is Aayush, who tries to find answers to why the home-services marketplace pulled out of its second international market.

The company’s Australia journey started three years ago, immediately after a $75 million funding. The move also offered the company a pedestal for its US dreams. And now, the five international markets, including the UAE, Singapore, and Saudi Arabia, contribute 10-11% of UC’s revenues.

But Australia has been an underperforming market. It made Rs 29 crore (~$3.5 million) in losses on revenues of Rs 6 crore ($0.7 million) in the year ended March 2022. And the fundamental reason? A missing product-market fit due to a lack of service partners.

Unlike India, Australia’s labour is either temporary and amateur, or professionals who don’t need to work with UC. For comparison: UC has managed to be a dominant player in the UAE—a “very India-like” market.

Now, UC’s focus is changing from accelerated growth to cash conservation. At the same time, the company is reevaluating its international strategy—pausing expansion plans in Egypt, Oman, and Southeast Asia. But even back home, it’s making several cost-cutting measures. And while UC has enough runway in the bank, profitability is still some distance away.
