Two months into 2025, and those who bought a gym subscription at the turn of the year must have already dealt with buyer’s remorse.

I’m positive that quite a neat chunk of those wannabe health nuts hoped to get started with group classes like power yoga and dance fitness.

Cult.fit, the $1.5 billion-worth company that has grown to be synonymous with gyms in cities, pitched these sessions as gateway workouts to those otherwise at peace with their fatty livers and love handles. Rivals like Physique 57 followed suit.

Many may have signed up for a fun, community-led way to burn calories, but they have little to show for all that jumping and stretching but sprains and tears. There has been a 50% spike in “Cult injuries” since before the pandemic, three physiotherapists told Pranathi, the author of today’s story.

She takes a close look at the incentives that draw users to these classes and force trainers to keep the pressure on them.

Between Youtube and streaming companies, which do you think will become the new television?

That was the question Two by Two hosts Praveen Gopal Krishnan and Rohin Dharmakumar posed to our guests this week.

Youtube TV has surpassed mobile as the primary device for Youtube viewing in the US. The company wants to press this advantage further and bring more attention to itself from linear television and streaming companies. How is the bigger picture shaping up in India?

To discuss, we invited as guests Swati Mohan, the ex-head of marketing at Netflix India and an independent advisor to many consumer tech companies, and Vanita Kohli-Khandekar, an India-based media specialist and a contributing editor at The Business Standard writing about media and the business of media.
