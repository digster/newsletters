Good Morning Ishan,
Multinational stent makers have found a way to cement their place in Indian patients’ hearts. Especially after a regulatory upset.
In 2017, India’s pharma pricing body capped the price of cardiac stents—both Indian and foreign—in response to rising costs. While hospitals simply raised the overall cost of treatment in response, the sales of multinational stent makers such as Abbott, Medtronic, and Boston Scientific were hit hard.
Between 2017 and 2023, foreign stent makers saw their market volume fall from 70% to 40%; while domestic players’ market volume doubled to 60%.
This should have been the Indian stent makers’ market to seize. After all, cardiovascular diseases are the leading cause of death in India, responsible for about 31% of all deaths. Not just that, since 2020, there’s been a 13% rise in heart attacks among adults under 45.
But it’s the international stent makers who are resurrecting their interest in India’s fast-growing cardiac market, even after the setback. For starters, it’s about what patients want. More importantly, though, these companies have figured out that the way to Indians’ hearts is through not just stents, but also other cardiac products.
