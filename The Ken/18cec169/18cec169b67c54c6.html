Good Morning Ishan,

Unless you’ve been living under a rock, you’d know that the word of the month in India is Ayodhya. The upcoming inauguration of the Ram temple in the city on 22 January has kept newsrooms and family WhatsApp groups busy for weeks now.

The frenzy is hard to miss. The preps, who’s invited, who’s not, the programme details, etc. Going by all the buzz, Ayodhya and its economy are set to be transformed in the coming years, with investments worth billions expected to pour in.

The stock market, never to be left behind, has got in on the action, too.

Aakriti, the author of today’s story, uncovered a phenomenon that’s largely gone under the radar. Turns out,  ‘Ayodhya’ stocks are all the rage among some investors and a few investment advisors.

From giant hotel chains to small tent makers putting money into Ayodhya, some of these stocks have been on a tear. One such stock—Apollo Sindoori Hotels—surged 20% to hit the upper circuit just yesterday. Call it faith, sentiment or opportunism, the money mountain has been moving.

But—and there’s always a but—many market experts aren’t convinced. They urge caution, pointing out that fundamentals eventually outweigh sentiment in the stock market roulette.
