Good Morning Ishan,
The everywhere, everything CEO—that’s Kalyan Krishnamurthy, chief executive of Flipkart, India’s largest e-commerce platform.
Its parent, Walmart, is on a roll—it has out-Amazoned Amazon, cracked grocery delivery, has a thriving ad business, and is a stock-market darling with a valuation multiple that’s higher than Apple’s. Naturally, more than six years after acquiring Flipkart, the retail giant is growing impatient with the Indian e-tailer’s forever-arriving profitability.
Krishnamurthy, who’s been a wartime CEO for a while, today looks more like a general who’s running out of lieutenants on the battlefield.
As things stand, Flipkart has been getting punched from all sides. Meesho took tier-2 cities while no one was looking. Amazon never budged from tier-1. Then came the quick-commerce land grab. Zepto. Blinkit. Instamart. Flipkart was not even in the same conversation.
Some of this, several executives Nuha spoke with believe, was self-inflicted, including the travel segment that came with the Cleartrip acquisition in 2021.
Though Krishnamurthy has a “jolly” side, most of Flipkart’s 30–35 senior vice-presidents don’t get to see it. The pressure build-up is causing the inner circle to flee. And many are not willing to suspend their disbelief that the war is nearing its end.
To quote from the story: “If Flipkart were a person, how would you describe it? ‘Impatient, distracted, and confused’.”
