Good Morning Ishan,
 
Spot the difference between these two statements:

1. Our hope is to create more access to better, cheaper, and open financial services.

2. The mission is towards providing a safe, efficient, accessible, inclusive, interoperable payment systems.
 
Got it? No?

The first is from the whitepaper introducing Libra, Facebook’s recently proposed global digital currency. 
 
The second is part of a note from the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) introducing the Unified Payments Interface.
 
Read the two sentences again. The choice of words used is noteworthy. Better vs. Safe. Cheaper vs. Efficient. Open vs. Interoperable. Hope vs. Mission. Two routes to change the way payments go digital. And sooner or later, they will clash in India. What happens then?
 
That’s our story. 
 
Today, Arundhati writes the definitive, forward looking story about the future of Libra in India. Facebook has plans to create a global digital currency to take over the world, including India, its biggest market if you consider WhatsApp as well. Enough has been written about Libra, about how it makes sense in emerging markets where financial systems aren’t that robust or established, and about how it will change everything. 
 
However, it’s not clear if India meets that description. 
 
India has a pretty robust, vibrant and arguably egalitarian digital payments sector of its own. All built on the back of UPI—a ubiquitous, fast and interoperable payments interface open to anyone. If Facebook intends to beat UPI, it’s got its work cut out. If Zuckerberg intends to win, he needs to convince the person who opened up India’s FinTech sector to world-class innovation, disruption and competition in equal measures. 
 
How will that work out? Can Libra and UPI work together? What happens then? Or will they always run counter to each other?
