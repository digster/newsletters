Since ancient Greece, barbershops have been more than just about grooming yourself. It's a place where you shoot the breeze and catch up on the latest scuttlebutt.
 
But the pandemic put paid, at least temporarily, to this wonderful tradition. Barbershops and their snazzier cousins, salons, were shuttered for months. Even now, as they have reopened in most parts of the country, customers are staying away. Walk-ins at Naturals, India's largest salon chain with nearly 700 outlets in 92 cities, are only at 40% of pre-Covid levels.
 
There's an unusual competitor to Naturals that is faring much better—the Tiger Global and Accel Partners-backed home services marketplace Urban Company. Valued at $933 million, it has seen its at-home beauty service business return to 85% of what it was before the pandemic.
 
Inviting a stranger into your home at a time like this may not be the best idea. But if you want a facial or a waxing session, this is a better option than going to a salon and coming in contact with multiple strangers.
 
And that is Urban Company's edge over Naturals and the Hindustan Unilever-owned Lakmé Salon. And pricing. Urban Company offers threading for as low as Rs 19 ($0.26), while salons charge anywhere between 2X and 5X of that.
 
The salon chains are not giving up, though, as Shruti notes in her story today. They are doing everything they can to ease customers' anxiety over hygiene, including offering five-minute hair treatments. How do you feel about going to a salon?
