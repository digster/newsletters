Good Morning Ishan,

As a Big Tech reporter, I can say with some degree of confidence that almost every story about Big Tech (Alphabet, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon et al.) ends up being a David vs Goliath story. The market capitalisations of these companies are worth the gross domestic product (GDP) of some nations. And yet they have to fight off heat, whether competitive or regulatory, continuously.

In India, it's more of the latter. Especially since Google Play Store plays gatekeeper to some 3.5 million apps.

It has its own set of rules on what apps to allow. Moreover, when it comes to financial services apps, it has laid down conditions that are relevant in the US but not in India. Consequently, Indian lenders are torn between the Indian regulator's guidelines and the pseudo regulator’s ultimatums.

But now, all that is set to change with the government cracking down on illegal loan apps with its allowlist for digital-lending apps.

The Indian finance ministry has asked the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) to create the list, and has entrusted the ministry of electronics and information technology (MeitY) to implement it. As much as this is about weeding out illegal lenders, this is also an attempt at taking back some of the gatekeeping power Google holds.

But as Sashwata writes in today's story, things aren't as black and white.
