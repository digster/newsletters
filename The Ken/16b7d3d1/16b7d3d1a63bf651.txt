Good Morning Ishan,

Like almost every newsroom out there, there are certain cliched expressions we, at The Ken, also like to indulge in. And with a cliche as relevant as the one I'm writing to talk about today, we can't really be blamed for the indulgence.

Peeling back the onion. That's the one.

In fact, we like it enough to have had a whole range of image suggestions involving onions over the past 2+ years. And they're not all that good-looking. Trust me.

So, why the obsession? Because without peeling back the onion, without digging deep to pull out the real story behind the obvious outer layer, journalism becomes a bit of a disservice. And often, the removed layers tell a completely different story.

Our past week stands testimony.

Olina wrote about https://the-ken.com/story/grey-zone-rapidos-shortcut-bike-taxi/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the bike-taxi company Rapido and its rapid growth in the business on Monday. On paper it's great. India's largest bike-taxi business. Aiming to do 1 million rides a day, and soon. Except, lose that shiny layer and you see that the business isn't entirely legal.

On the face of it, payments companies such as Paytm and Amazon Pay are directly competing with credit cards. But Arundhati looked beyond https://the-ken.com/story/credit-cards-are-the-new-black-for-payments-companies/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter the obvious in her Tuesday story and wrote about how credit cards today are, if anything, more of a lifeline to these companies.

Did you read Abinaya's Flipkart-Namdhari's story https://the-ken.com/story/namdharis-fresh-flipkart-grocery/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Thursday? While the possible deal itself has been in the news, she went a step further to dig out Flipkart's ambitions with this Bangalore-centred grocery chain.

Our free subscriber story this week, Suraksha's piece on https://the-ken.com/story/biomedical-waste-india-ticking-bomb-thriving-market/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter India's biomedical waste problem assessed how a potential biohazard crisis could actually mean serious business. One man's waste...?

Harveen closed the week https://the-ken.com/story/bytedance-factory-in-india/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter with a story on the one Chinese company that's swept over India—ByteDance, which runs TikTok, Helo and Vigo. Is it the really "world's most valuable startup"? Read on.

That's all we had this week. Hope you have a well-rested weekend.
