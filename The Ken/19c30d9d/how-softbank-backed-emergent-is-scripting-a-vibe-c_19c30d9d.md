Good Morning Ishan,
Around this time last year, the term “vibe coding” emerged. One of OpenAI’s founding members, Andrej Karpathy, coined it on X (formerly Twitter) and remarked, “It’s not too bad for throwaway weekend projects, but still quite amusing.”
A year later, on 3 February 2026, he graduated it to “agentic engineering”.
That’s how far vibe coding has come in just 12 months. Well, 12 months is a long time in AI.
“It feels like a week is a month, and a month is a year,” Mukund Jha, co-founder of Emergent, the seven-month-old vibe-coding startup from Bengaluru (and San Francisco), told Arundhati.
In January, the company raised $70 million from investors such as Softbank and Khosla Ventures, bringing its total capital raise to $100 million—just enough to compete. With 60 employees and $50 million in annualised run rate (ARR), Emergent is racing to capture a pie of the $4 billion market.
Along with standalone startups, frontier model makers are also vying for this space.
From “English is the new programming language” to “English + vibe” to “English + vibe + whisper”, to who-knows-what: the world of software is on a path that few understand.
Investors are backing it, influencers are promoting it, and users are giving it a try. Emergent has no time to check out any other vibes. Ram Viswanathan Commented Interesting take by Rohin, but I’d argue that AI PCs aren’t the ‘new Chromebooks’—they are actually the antithesis of the Chromebook philosophy. Chromebooks were built on the idea that the ‘local’ machine doesn’t matter because the Cloud is the engine. AI PCs (with dedicated NPUs) are a pivot back to the Edge. For those of us driving enterprise AI adoption and conducting business value assessments for tools like M365 Copilot, the hardware shift isn’t just rhetoric; it’s about latency and data sovereignty. Moving AI processing from the cloud to the device (the NPU) allows for ‘Always-on’ assistance without the privacy risks or subscription costs associated with sending every keystroke to a server. If Chromebooks were about ‘Cloud First,’ AI PCs are about ‘Local Intelligence First.’ It’s a hardware evolution that actually gives power back to the device, rather than just making it a window into the cloud. |
