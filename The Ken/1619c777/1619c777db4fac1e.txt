Good Morning Ishan,

Till some years ago, I used to worry about my smartphone’s “battery life”. I’d track it, figure out hacks to improve it and generally get all OCPD about it. Till I learnt to let go of it completely one day.

Because, I finally figured, the trick lay in understanding that you could charge your smartphone as often as you got a chance. Of which there were plenty—while driving, while working, while at home. And even when not near an electrical outlet, with a portable source of power that is today ubiquitous: the power bank.

Mumbai-headquartered ION Energy is building one those—a power bank. Not just any power bank though, but the O '+ve’ of power banks: the universal one.

From today’s story by Seema: "Now, think of a battery you can use in your two-wheeler, car, carry home as a Powerwall, take to work or pack as a compact box for when you go camping. A universal battery pack so to say. Like today, we use power banks for electronic devices.”

26-year old Akhil Aryan, ION Energy’s founder, is building modular battery packs that can range from 5 volts all the way up to 1000 volts. He’s calling his (as yet unreleased) product “Udyr”. ION wants to rent them out via a SaaS-like model. 

“I have a vehicle, I drive 20 km; you have a vehicle, you drive 200 km. But we both go to the gas station to buy gas, not own the gas station,” says Aryan.

It’s an ambitious vision, no doubt. But one fraught with massive risks as well.
