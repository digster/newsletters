Good Morning Ishan,

“They” = India, where on top of anaemic car ownership estimated at between 2.2-3%, new car sales were down 7% in December 2018 over the preceding year.

“They” = China, where annual car sales fell for the first time in 20 years.

“They” = millennials, 'cos as everyone knows, they despise ownership and love avocado toast.

And yet, just last month CarDekho, a car sales portal, raised $110 million in venture funding from a group of investors including the likes of Sequoia Capital and Google Capital. So, with apologies to Mark Twain, cars are new anti-land*. What’s going on? If nobody’s buying new cars, how on earth is the used cars market so hot?

Well, that’s today’s story by Vandana. How a company started by two brothers in the garage of their home bided its time, acquired its rivals, and kept an eye on cash flows to build India’s second largest auto player. With this new $110 million round, they want to increase their commission per car to 6-7% of a used car’s price? How?
