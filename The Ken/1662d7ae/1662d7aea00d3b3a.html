Welcome to ‘O hushed October morning mild’. Whether you like Elon Musk or not, especially for all that has happened at Tesla in the recent months, you’d agree that the passionate and cocksure entrepreneur keeps electric vehicles in public consciousness.
 
(In case you missed the Saturday night high drama, the US Securities and Exchange Commission and Musk came to a settlement. Musk stays on as the CEO, steps down as the chairman and pays $20 million in fine for, well, a set of tweets.)
 
California and Musk bring some dazzle and drama no doubt, but they are not going to bring any significant shift to EVs globally. If anything, it could be led by China, with technology backed by solid government support. In India, which has bumbled—from an electric mobility plan in 2013 to a FAME-1 policy in 2015, and to an about-turn in 2018 when the idea of even bringing out a policy was dropped—two-wheelers could be the harbingers, not the cars on which everyone had pinned their hopes.
 
The humble two-wheeler is poised for an electric disruption. At least half a dozen manufacturers have forged a second attempt with new ones like Ather and Tork Energy showing how true collaboration can happen on streets. Of the 23 million two-wheelers estimated to be sold in India in 2018, EVs will make a modest 75K. But that number is going to change. Charging grids and battery swapping stations, years in the making, are finally for all to see and experience. 
 
As one CEO puts it: “It’s important that buyers see them, or else how will they decide if they want one.”
