Too many males, too many engineers, too many freshers. That’s a typical MBA class in most top-rated Indian B-schools. Homogenous. With little to no experience, and harder to stand out to recruiters. Yet B-school placement headlines scream at you every year around this time. As they did last month.
In the B-School game, you live and die by the salaries and placements your graduates manage. For students, uncertain how a costly spell of education will pan out, getting an offer in hand on Day-0 or Day-1 is all that matters. The sole aim is to leave the campus with your nose up in the air, buoyed by a fat CTC, even though it’s common knowledge that many fresh hires leave their job inside the first year.
Have B-schools become glorified placement agencies or are they centres of learning? Do students want high-priced credentials or skill development? Neither is able to answer that.
The global rallying cry to reinvent management education after the Lehman Brothers crisis only briefly touched India. A few B-schools nursed their guilt, held camps, went to slums and hospitals, reiterated they had a wider role to play in society. But it was transient. Even ISB, which came into being breaking many rules, succumbed to it. The tail is wagging the dog.
Everyone is risk averse. Students, because they have loans to repay. Colleges, because they have courses to fill & placements to ‘monetise’. Companies, because they have hiring targets per campus. Worse still, placements are handled by alumni. Now you see why everyone is spinning their wheels.
