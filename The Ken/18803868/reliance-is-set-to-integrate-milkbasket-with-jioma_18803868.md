Good Morning Ishan,
Did you have your morning tea/coffee yet? If you live in a metro city, especially in one of the large, isolated apartment complexes, there’s a decent possibility you got the ingredients delivered by an e-grocery player and probably from either BBdaily or Milkbasket.
This domination has been difficult to attain. Razor-thin margins. High perishability. High customer expectations and demands. E-grocery is an intensive, complicated business. And even after existing in India for over a decade, it is still taking shape.
The clearest example of this is the quick-commerce boom led by the likes of Swiggy, forcing established players such as Bigbasket to cut their delivery times and launch new products. But three years on, the venture-capital-funded disruption seems to be subsiding, and delivery timelines are normalising as the cash burn is becoming unbearable.
In the meantime, subscription-based next-day-delivery services like BBdaily and Milkbasket have been quietly at work, rationalising costs and improving efficiencies. Milkbasket has especially boomed, thanks to the parent Reliance’s large-format-retail-store network.
As the next-day-delivery pioneers come back to dominate their old territory, their fortunes remain closely tied to the most important conglomerates in the country. If Milkbasket seeks to further Reliance’s grocery ambitions, Bigbasket is now a crown jewel of the salt-to-software behemoth Tata Group’s digital arm.
But how is Milkbasket doing this exactly? What are the things that are going extremely well for it, and where may the pitfalls lie?
