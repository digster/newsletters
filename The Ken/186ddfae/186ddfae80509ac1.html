Good Morning Ishan,
 
“We are in India, return on investment (ROI) is the end goal for everyone.”
 
Coming from the founder of a B-school, this sounds surprising and yet, not surprising.
 
If you are setting up an institution, other metrics will precede ROI. But if you are running a B-school like an edtech, then it’s a legit goal.
 
Three-year-old Masters’ Union (MU) is the new B-school in town, and the one thing it can’t stop talking about is its placement numbers. Again, not surprising.
 
As we have written at The Ken earlier, the Indian Institutes of Management (IIMs) made placements and salaries into a formidable moat over the decades. And now MU is using the same playbook on IIMs it wishes to compete against.
 
Loud on its website sits the claim that the average domestic CTC of the 2022 cohort was ~Rs 33 lakh (~$40,000), the same as the mean domestic CTC students of IIMA’s flagship programme received that year.
 
Secondly, MU got its placements audited—a practice followed by select B-schools. And that too by the same firm that audits IIMA’s placements. It’s another matter that, unlike IIMA, it hasn’t shared the full audit report but only its own analysis of the numbers.
 
This show-some and hide-some approach is raising eyebrows, and for obvious reasons.
 
But MU is undeterred in its approach. Between April and December 2022, it spent Rs 7.8 crore (~$950,000) on salaries, and almost the same amount, Rs 7.1 crore (~$864,000), on business promotion.
 
An edtech or a B-school? You ask. If it looks like a duck, swims like a duck, and quacks like a duck, it probably is a duck.
 
Founder Pratham Mittal knows a few tricks of the education trade. The son of Ashok Mittal, a member of the Rajya Sabha and the chancellor of Lovely Professional University in Punjab, junior Mittal prefers to distance himself from the family-run university.
 
For now, he is ramping up cohort numbers at MU; in the medium term, though, he’s set his sight on the global B-school rankings.
 
“Do you know nearly 60% of the weightage in FT rankings is linked to pay and placements?” Pratham asked Alifiya, author of today’s story. It’s a riveting read.
