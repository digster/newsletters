Cloudtail, a company backed by Infosys founder Narayana Murthy, was once a one-stop-shop on Amazon India for millions of people to buy anything under the sun. From sarees to sunscreens, mobile phones to safety pins, and pet food to protein powders, Cloudtail had it all.
Then in 2016-17, the Indian government decided to curtail Cloudtail’s dominance and that of others like it. A few years later, Amazon, which had a stake in Cloudtail, exited it as the government prohibited platforms from owning stakes in their biggest sellers. Soon, the Competition Commission of India joined the crackdown. Facing intense pressure, Cloudtail eventually decided to close and was dissolved in May 2022.
It sold its massive inventories of apparel, electronics, and other goods to some firms that would replace the behemoth as aggregators on the e-commerce platform. Did the end of Cloudtail mean the dawn of a new era? Not really.
Over the past few months, sellers have realised that Cloudtail was a more benevolent aggregator to hawk their wares. The new blokes on the block seem to be no match for the convenience, efficiency, and business empathy offered by Cloudtail.
With Cloudtail gone, sellers are abandoning Amazon to sell on smaller platforms, opening their own websites, and even going back to selling to brick-and-mortar stores. In today’s story, Soumyajit unravels the causes behind the simmering discontent and its consequences for Amazon in India.
