Good Morning Ishan,
 
Few things are more bizarre than valuations these days, be it public or private companies. Adani Total Gas perhaps ranks the highest on the bizarreness scale, and it's not just because of its 1000% share price gains in one year.
 
As an investment advisor Giriraj Daga tweeted last week, Adani Total Gas market cap is greater than the combined value of Gujarat Gas + IGL + Petronet + GSPL + MGL. The five companies’ combined profit of Rs 7,674 crore is much higher than Rs 436 crore of Adani Total.
 
The Midas touch has brushed off other stocks in the Adani stable as well—there are five decacorns, valued at over $10 billion. Yet there’s little analyst coverage on most of these stocks, though some of those analysts pointed us towards the shareholding of the Adani companies. There’s a pattern—a certain type of investor looms large, a certain type is conspicuous by their absence.
 
Apart from that, however, is the riveting story of how Adani Total Gas, has emerged as the largest private sector player in the city gas distribution (CGD)—the next public utility arena after LPG that the government wants universal coverage of. The company is expected to go all guns blazing in the upcoming 11th round of CGD auction in 44 areas.
 
City gas distribution is one of the best businesses to be in. Government policies are supremely favourable. You get cheap gas, you have no control on the selling price, margins are great, and there’s volume growth thanks to the sky-high competing fuel prices. Local producers like ONGC and Oil India may bleed—Rs 7,000 crore annually, according to one estimate—but CGD players can laugh their way to the bank.
 
But if you live by government policy, you can also die by government policy. Just saying.
