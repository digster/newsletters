Good Morning Ishan,

It’s entirely likely that you may not have heard of XA Network. I hadn’t too.

The group began in 2018 as a bunch of Google alumni and executives who were interested in making angel investments in interesting startups. (XA stands for Xoogler Angels and Xoogler stands for an ex-Googler). Today it comprises 86 executives from some of the world’s best-known tech giants like Facebook, Twitter, Netflix, Microsoft, and of course, Google. But it is still largely under the radar.

It doesn’t even invest a lot, with the average deal size being around US$100,000.

And yet, experienced founders of some of the hottest Southeast Asian early-stage startups are figuring out how to get XA Network on their cap table. There’s also a group comprising former Uber executives that’s hotly sought after.

Why? For their connections and expertise. Unlike most venture capital funds, these groups are populated with sharp and seasoned professionals who are really good at solving the real-life problems that early-stage startups face. Like hiring, marketing, productising ideas, and building rapidly from scratch.

Jon’s story today is about the emergence of tech alumni networks as a new catalyst in the Southeast Asian entrepreneurial ecosystem. And with more IPOs expected from the region, expect this trend to only strengthen in the coming years.

XA Network declined to respond to our questions when Jon reached out to them. Perhaps they’d rather keep things under the radar. It’s understandable—sources say that XA may be planning to enlarge the network and go full-time.
