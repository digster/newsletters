By last evening, my mailbox had more than 40 PR emails and quotes from executives wanting to share their opinion, soundbites, to be precise, on the Budget. I am sure by the time you get to The Ken morning dose, you’ve had your share of news, views and entertainment on the FY18-19 budget. (Some news channels had called in stand-up comedians; can’t say whether it was for genuine comic relief or pushing TRPs on Budget day.)
 
We, very humbly, decided to stick to our knitting. Today’s story is about how artificial intelligence could be transforming the legal industry where law firms have traditionally had a pyramid structure, which, now, tech is evolving into a diamond.
 
In principle, as the bottom of that pyramid is being automated, nobody wants to be left behind; everyone wants to own the tech. Today, big tech is offering platforms (yes, Watson included), but a bunch of nimble folks believe platforms will go the ERP way. Get commoditised.
 
Then where lies the value in India?
 
In looking at the cases disposed under the IT Act and discovering new ways of efficiency improvement? Or analysing orders of the Competition Commission of India and figuring ways of predicting outcomes? Or even replicating Uber in how it solved the information asymmetry among drivers and disrupted the supply story? Though Indian court judgements are in the public domain, they are spread across 20-odd websites. While lawyers pay for a commercial database, there isn’t a good way to find case law for citizens. Can AI bring in the consolidation that is waiting to happen in ease-of-access?
 
That’s Virat Markandeya's (our new contributor) story. Let’s get on to it: https://the-ken.com/ai-changing-legal-services/
 
Have a great weekend. We’ll see you Monday.
