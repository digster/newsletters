When Amazon finally docked on Indian shores in 2013, not everyone was convinced it had what it took to crack the local market. Already, six-year-old Flipkart had built up a head of steam and a formidable set of backers, including Tiger Global. India's regulatory landscape was also seen as (and, indeed, has proven to be) messy, with naysayers wondering whether Amazon would be able or willing to navigate it.
In the nearly eight years since, Amazon has more than put those doubts to rest. It is arguably the stronger half of India's e-commerce duopoly, save for a few categories such as fashion and mobile phones, where it is also gaining ground.
Somewhere along the way, though, Flipkart stopped being Amazon's arch-nemesis. Or rather, it became just one among many.
In grocery, for instance, Amazon launched Amazon Fresh—a direct competitor to e-grocers like Bigbasket and Grofers. It began offering flight bookings, jostling for elbow room with the dozens of online travel agents like MakeMyTrip and EaseMyTrip.
But Amazon's willingness to fight on multiple fronts didn't end there. It has ventured into the e-pharmacy business, edtech, payments, and even foodtech.
