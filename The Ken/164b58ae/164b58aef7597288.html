Have you played this game called Six Degrees of Kevin Bacon?
 
It's easy. The game says that the shortest path from any random actor to the great Kevin Bacon will always be less than six. The degrees of separation is the Bacon number. Let's say you pick Tom Cruise. Tom Cruise was in the 1992 movie A Few Good Men with Kevin Bacon—giving him a Bacon number of one. You can do it with anyone. Take the most talented actor ever—by whom, of course, I mean Raveena Tandon. Raveena has a Bacon number of four. She was in the 1994 niche classic Zamane Se Kya Darna with Gulshan Grover who was in Desires of the Heart with Desiree Markella, who was in the 2007 movie Death Sentence with...Kevin Bacon. 
 
Easy no? 
 
There's a similar game in startup circles in India these days. It's called Six Degrees of Walmart. Last week, when PhonePe acquired Zopper, a  point of sale (PoS) company for $6 million, that connection got a lot shorter. Let's try. Walmart has a majority stake in Flipkart, which acquired PhonePe, which then acquired Zopper. Walmart number of three. 
 
Today's story is about why the acquisition happened and how it helps PhonePe's ambitions. Arundhati posits that PhonePe is on its way to building a formidable platform for transactions, and she lays out how it's all part of a three-tiered plan. Sameer Nigam, the CEO of PhonePe, weighs in as well. Incidentally, apart from the number, there are synergies from Walmart all the way to Zopper, and it runs through PhonePe. Walmart sells to retailers. Retailers can now manage their inventory through Zopper. The loop is complete. 
 
That's one theory. There's more.
