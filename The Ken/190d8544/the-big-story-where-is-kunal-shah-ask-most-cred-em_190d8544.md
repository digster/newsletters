Good Morning Ishan,
Startups exude different kinds of energy at various stages of their life.
So what works in running a company in its early stages may not work for it in its later stages. And if anyone were to know this well it would be Kunal Shah, the chief executive and founder of the fintech Cred.
Shah is known not only for being a sharp entrepreneur but also for his pithy observations on management styles, building companies, and life itself. In The Ken‘s First Principles [podcast](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles/kunal-shah-cred/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) in 2023, speaking of his management style at Cred, Shah said, “I don’t need to solve [the] motivation problem.”
That’s because Cred’s nearly 950 employees are a group of self-motivated and aware individuals who don’t need any push to carry out their work. And this lets Shah focus on the bigger picture.
This also meant Cred’s workforce could go wide. They could work across as many as eight different departments and move from one launch to another, getting the job done.
Now, the US$6.4 billion-valued company is changing tack from launching new products and features to “maintaining and chiselling” what’s already been built. But its employees’ skill set is not meant for this mission. So they are leaving.
On the other hand, there is another set of employees at Cred who need more direction from Shah on how to do their job as Cred’s daily active users have slowed—it’s remained in the 3–3.5 million range since March 2023. They are also leaving the company.
Rounak, the author of today’s story, has a deeply reported long form for which he spoke to over 25 former and current Cred employees. He has pieced together the predicament that Kunal Shah faces as the company sees never-seen-before attrition levels.
