The public sector undertaking wants to bet on the major food aggregators with an extensive network, but food delivery in trains isn’t as simple

Good Morning Ishan,

Train journeys are surely exciting, but one can’t say the same about the food served on it.

Unless you are carrying your own, food served on trains can be underwhelming, often unhealthy. And at a time when food delivery companies have changed our relationship with the number of options, the meal options on the train just don’t cut it.

The Indian Railway Catering and Tourism Corporation (IRCTC)—the catering arm of the Indian Railways—wants to change that.

IRCTC recently partnered with the foodtech giant Swiggy to provide pre-ordered meals to passengers through IRCTC’s e-catering services. It got into a similar partnership with Zomato last year. Catering is a very important line of business for IRCTC as it makes for 41.5% of IRCTC’s total revenue of Rs 3,662 crore (US$441 million) in the 2023 financial year.

As for the food aggregators, if 20 million people travel by Indian trains every day, the catering arm gets 1.8–2 million meal orders daily. Though that’s only 10% of the people ordering on trains, it’s still a significant scale for the food delivery companies to eat into.

But delivering food to a person on a train is not that simple. Shivani has a rich and vividly-told story on why both IRCTC and the food aggregators want to go to such lengths.

The path for a Tiktok comeback in India

Bytedance’s Tiktok has a long and uncertain road in the US.

Last week, the House in the US passed a bill that could force Bytedance to sell Tiktok to a non-Chinese owner or risk having the app banned.

Bytedance has been under pressure in the US since 2020; it has been trying to allay US’ national security concerns about sharing 170 million Americans’ data with Beijing. Now it’s a big *IF* that this bill will be passed in the US senate and a sale will materialise.

Even if it does, any of the big tech companies who buy Tiktok, will have to pay more than US$80 billion, according to reports.

And if anyone is spending that much, they would not want to miss India.

In 2020, the central government banned Tiktok in India, along with several other well-known Chinese apps after Indian soldiers died over a clash in the India-China border. Back then, it had the highest number of users at 200 million.

But now, if Tiktok’s American-owned, things could change.

Clearly, we have not seen the last of #Peakcringe.
