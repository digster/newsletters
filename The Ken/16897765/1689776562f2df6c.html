Good Morning Ishan,
 
If the average British person spends about five months of their life talking about the weather, I’m betting an average Bangalorean will spend no less time talking about the traffic.
 
You are either chronically early, or pathologically late. You may plan and time your commute, and yet find every hour is rush hour. The construction of mass transit has been in transit for the past two decades. The city can’t add enough or more roads for the thousands of new cars that hit the road every day.
 
Given all this, you’d assume the city is a crucible for urban mobility experimentation. That people would take to bicycle sharing as they took to cabs or three-wheeler-hailing. But after millions of dollars and multiple pilots, several entrepreneurs across India are waking up to the product-fit reality: Urban Indians just don’t want to use bicycles.
 
There are about 15K cycles on the roads from at least a dozen startups—Mobike, Mobycy, Yulu and others. They all came because the sharing of cabs, autos and bikes had their drawbacks. 1. You don’t get to drive the vehicle independently and stop where you like. 2. They don’t promote the use of existing public transport like buses and metros.
 
When we first wrote about bicycle-sharing in August and asked if China was to blame for the speculative mood, the signs of stress were apparent. In less than six months, most of the dozen companies are ditching cycles. But for what?
 
In Salman’s story, you’ll read all that’s going on. One thing stands out though. Our mobility disruptors kept the urban transport authorities out of the loop. While it makes sense to go unregulated and test the market, having authorities by your side, as Dallas in the US has shown, helps with smart regulations as you ride along. Karnataka’s official laments that “it was started in isolation”.
 
If bicycles have to change the urban mobility landscape, there’s a role for everyone, even the neighbourhood. Salman bumped into an unattended Bounce scooter left on the dimly-lit main HAL road a week ago. The scooter is yet to be removed. Really.
