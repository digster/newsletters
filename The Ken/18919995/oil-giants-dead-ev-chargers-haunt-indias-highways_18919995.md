Good Morning Ishan,
Imagine driving a car powered by petrol or diesel on a highway anywhere in India. When it’s time to refill the tank, you quickly look for the nearest fuel pump on Google Maps, pull into it, and you’re back on the road in no time.
But it’s different when you have an electric car. If you’re lucky, you’ll find a charging station that’ll get the job done in time for you to finish a cup of coffee. If you’re not, you’ll have to order some snacks and maybe a dessert too.
That’s still okay next to the nightmare of finding a charger that doesn’t work at all. And state-owned Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd (BPCL) has quite a few of them.
BPCL is no ordinary entity. It is India’s second-largest fuel retailer and only trails Tata Power when it comes to the network of chargers. And BPCL is worth US$9.6 billion.
The company wants to have 2,500 chargers by March 2024. And it already has 600, most of them on highways.
But 60% of them are dead.
Yes, you read that right: 60%. That’s hardly reassuring when BPCL’s largest shareholder, the Indian government, is dangling the subsidy carrot to get people to switch to electric vehicles.
It’s not unusual for chargers to be down. At any given time, roughly 20% of India’s public chargers are unavailable, similar to what you see in developed countries. So why is BPCL worse off?
I’ll let you read our contributor and EV enthusiast Priyans Murarka’s informative story to get to the bottom of this.
