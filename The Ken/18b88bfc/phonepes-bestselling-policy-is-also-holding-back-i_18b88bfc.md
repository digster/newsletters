Good Morning Ishan,
Comedy as a clutter-cutter can be a cool tool. Ask payments giant Phonepe. Its funny ads during the ongoing Cricket World Cup screenings seem to be getting the job done. Of getting Phonepe noticed in a crowded insurance distribution market, where it’s up against biggies like Policybazaar. Phonepe’s ad-blitz claims that the monthly premium payment option on its platform makes health insurance ‘affordable’.
But this ‘affordable’ insurance pitch comes at a tidy cost—the fintech has paid Rs 150 crore for the advertising rights. Small change for the Walmart-run giant? Maybe. But quite significant considering that Phonepe Insurance Broking Services—the entity housing the insurance business—posted revenue of just Rs 32 crore and a loss of Rs 427 crore in the year ended March 2023. Much of this loss was due to promotional spend, which continues unabated. There’s been little success to show in the three years since the company entered the insurance broking business.
So, why is Phonepe—the numero uno in the payments space in India—pouring money into and taking a big punt on health insurance distribution? Is it more out of compulsion than choice? Is it driven by the need to compensate for a low-margin business that’s become a drag? Is it to bulk up and justify Phonepe’s top-dollar valuation? All of the above? Does the big splash now guarantee success or will the ghosts of the past come back to haunt?
