When Reliance Jio was launched back in 2016, it basically obliterated the pre-existing telecom industry, and rebuilt it from scratch. Free voice calls, dirt-cheap data, and a pure 4G network ushered a data-starved nation into perpetual connectivity.

The same fate could now await wealth management—courtesy of Jio Blackrock Investment Advisers, a 50:50 joint venture between Jio Financial Services and Blackrock.

It’s USP? Personalised investment advice at just Rs 350 a year. And by personalised, the company means goal-based plans and daily nudges through Blackrock’s quintessential Aladdin technology.

In a country where only a tiny fraction of the 120 million or so investors interact with Sebi-registered advisers, this price-warrior, robo-advisory sounds like the democratisation of a once-elite industry.

But wait, there’s a catch.

Jio Blackrock Investment Advisers’ unabashed affinity for its own, in-house funds is a version of what Bollywood is often accused of: nepotism. In fact, if your investment limit is below Rs 1 lakh, expect exclusively Jio Blackrock schemes—despite there being several long-tested, better-performing alternatives.

Tack on Jio’s captive userbase, many of whom may be first-time investors with little understanding of “conflict of interest”, and it begs the question: is this democratised advice or a low-cost funnel for a young AMC?
