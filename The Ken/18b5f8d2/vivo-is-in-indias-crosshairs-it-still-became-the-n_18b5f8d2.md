Good Morning Ishan,
It took smartphone maker Vivo a decade to know what it feels like to be number one.
In the second quarter that ended this June, Vivo inched past Samsung’s share of 15% in India’s smartphone market, securing over 18% of the pie, according to market research firm International Data Corporation (IDC).
Its top status comes at a time when it has been navigating regulatory waters as a Chinese brand. Since July 2022, the company has been the subject of multiple raids and investigations by the Enforcement Directorate, leading to a freeze on its accounts.
Not just that, it saw this growth while most other leading brands saw their sales fall.
What gave it a leg up this time were the various initiatives it took over the years—from improving its relationship with offline retailers to launching multiple products in a year.
But smartphone wars can be fierce. A new product launch here and another incentive plan there can level the playing field again.
