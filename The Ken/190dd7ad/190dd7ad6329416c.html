Good Morning Ishan,

A classroom with just a handful of students. No age-based division of classes. Open-ended assessments where children can submit Instagram posts or podcasts to prove their understanding. Sounds pretty out there, right?

But that’s exactly the kind of learning model being pushed by educators like Lina Ashar, founder of Kangaroo Kids and Billabong High International School.

Innovative, hyper-personalised micro-schools like these are catching on in the post-Covid era. Parents saw for themselves that the pedagogy being employed in schools wasn’t meeting their childrens’ needs. And that’s exactly why this new market has emerged, propagated by the likes of Comini Learning, Agile Shaala, and Ashar’s Dreamtime Learning.

And, of course, there’s a business angle to it, as micro-schools’ economics are quite lucrative. They have several other revenue streams – like licensing out their methodology and operating in a capital-light mode. That means one of these institutions can break even in one-third the time it takes for a full-scale K-12 school.

“I am an educationist, but I understand I need to build a business with solid numbers for it to grow,” Ashar told The Ken.

In today’s free story, Alifiya takes us through the A-to-Z of the micro-school model and what the trend means for all parties involved. It’s a free read.
