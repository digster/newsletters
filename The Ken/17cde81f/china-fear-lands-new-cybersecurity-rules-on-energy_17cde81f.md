Last October, smack in the middle of The Ken's fourth-anniversary celebrations (and well into our remote-working jaunt), our Mumbai bureau disappeared for over six hours. It took us a while to figure out that they hadn't just upped and left. Most of the city had had an unprecedented—and unexplained—power outage.
The Indian government attributed the outage to human error, a glitch in the operations. "The way the system is designed, one has to physically pull down a lever," one official told The Ken.
That wasn't the full story, though. The team has to pull that lever if specific markers in the power utilities' operating systems give out red alerts. That's what likely happened in Mumbai.
But how did those markers get triggered? By malware, of course. Specifically, embedded malware and software backdoors that could have granted access to hackers and other unsavoury elements. Or through unpatched internet-connected and air-gapped equipment that power utilities often use.
So far, not much has been done to address this. In fact, cybersecurity at Indian power utilities is notoriously abysmal, writes Pratap in today's story.
There are roughly 300 power utilities, large and small, from both the country's public and private sectors. And between 2017 and 2020, the number of Indian electricity utilities certified annually ranged between 0 and 1.
This was also the time that border conflicts erupted between India and China, giving officials in the Ministry of Power sleepless nights—Chinese equipment is near-ubiquitous among Indian power companies. "The ministry feared that if there were trojans embedded in the equipment in the grid, China might activate them at an opportune time," said an official who's worked closely with the ministry. In fact, in May last year, US authorities seized a massive transformer imported from China right when it arrived at the Houston port and took it to a lab to test for threats.
Naturally, the Indian government decided it was time for some action. And so, for the first time ever, a year after the Mumbai outage, it issued extensive cybersecurity guidelines for the power sector.
