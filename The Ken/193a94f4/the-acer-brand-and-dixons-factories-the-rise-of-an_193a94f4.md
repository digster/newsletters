India’s manufacturing sector is worth a handsome US$155 billion, but any new entrant that wants to build an assembly line needs prohibitively immense capital. Is there, then, a way to keep assets light while still producing new products?
The answer is trademark licensing—paying royalties to use a brand name and becoming responsible for product R&D, sales, and after-sales service. This isn’t a new concept, but one company is applying it to tap into the existing manufacturing expertise in India and give international brands new paths into the market.
Indkal partners with Acer and Black+Decker to create products like TV sets, refrigerators, and air conditioners, then outsources the manufacturing of those products to better-known firms like Dixon, Zetwerk, and Amber Electronics, which have more than 50 years of collective experience.
Meanwhile, Acer and Black+Decker get to plant deeper roots and shore up their presence in new product categories in India. Indkal announced the launch of Acer-branded smartphones earlier this year and plans to create a broader range of handsets for the Taiwanese firm.
This works so well that Indkal’s revenue went from Rs 61 crore in FY22 to Rs 260 crore in FY23. And it recently managed to raise Rs 300 crore, or US$36 million, from a venture-capital firm registered in Mauritius.
