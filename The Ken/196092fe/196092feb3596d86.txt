Lenskart built its empire on franchisees. Now it’s battling them in courts https://the-ken.com/story/lenskart-built-its-empire-on-franchisees-now-its-battling-them-in-courts/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

The eyewear giant faces legal challenges from franchise owners accusing it of unfair practices, financial fraud, and withholding data, with multiple FIRs and ongoing court battles

Lenskart is facing mounting backlash from franchise owners who accuse the company of unfair practices, including opening company-run stores near their outlets. Franchise owners in Karnataka also filed an FIR accusing Lenskart of financial fraud, citing revenue discrepancies and misuse of accounting software. While the Economic Offences Wing launched a probe, Lenskart secured a stay from the Karnataka High Court in January. Besides FIRs in Indore, Mysuru, and Dharwad, the legal battle spans the Karnataka High Court as well as the Delhi International Arbitration Centre. How AI is creating a rift at McKinsey, Bain, and BCG https://the-ken.com/story/bcg-and-mckinsey-sell-speed-as-ai-shakes-up-consulting-so-why-arent-consultants-buying-it/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

Instead of making consultants’ lives easier, generative AI has led to shorter deadlines and vanishing creativity

Clients’ increasing access to AI tools is transforming the way consulting firms operate. Apart from moving towards an implementation-based model, the Big 3—McKinsey, BCG and Bain—are now incorporating AI in a major chunk of their projects. Consultants at the juniormost level are encouraged to use AI for research, strategy and ideation. Albeit on considerably shortened timelines. Thoughts on AI vary across the firms’ hierarchy—while managers and partners love it, consultants who are closer to the ground view things differently. UPI can be forever or free—not both https://the-ken.com/story/upi-can-be-forever-or-free-not-both/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

A recent subsidy cut threatens the zero-fee model that made UPI ubiquitous, forcing banks, fintechs, merchants, and regulators to rethink who should foot the bill

UPI recorded over 18 billion transactions in March, the highest ever since its 2016 launch, aided by subsidies and a zero MDR policy. But the incentives for FY25 have been cut by more than half to Rs 1,500 crore from the previous year. While a modified policy allows payment-service providers to charge merchants for certain transactions, it still doesn’t recover the total costs they incur to process them. While more cuts in subsidy may be in store, reintroducing merchant discount rates will go a long way in keeping the UPI ecosystem vibrant and healthy. How Lahori broke Coke-Pepsi-Parle’s hold on beverages https://the-ken.com/story/how-lahori-broke-coke-pepsi-parles-hold-on-beverages/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

Though this jeera-flavoured drink has established itself in the north, it’s facing headwinds in other parts of the country

Priced at just 10 rupees, Lahori Zeera’s affordability makes it accessible to a wider audience, particularly in a price-sensitive market like India. Aiming for a pan-India presence, the company is investing in additional manufacturing facilities and strengthening its distribution network. Established players like Coca-Cola and Parle are entering the jeera soda market due to Lahori’s rapid growth. The company faces logistical challenges due to the perishable nature of its products, and the need for wider distribution across a vast country like India.
