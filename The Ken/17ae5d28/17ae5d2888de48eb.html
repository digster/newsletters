If you look at the history of the relationship between news publishers and Big Tech companies, a pattern emerges.

And it is this—one party wins, and the other loses.

When digital media exploded and news got distributed through Facebook and Google, the companies swept up all the ad-dollars, and little was left for the news publishers. Soon, when Facebook’s platform was used to influence elections and transmit hate-speech, the world’s news media dug deep, investigated, and published several damning stories. Google got into a tussle with Spain’s media outlets, and chose to shut down Google News instead of paying a licence fee to publishers—which is what the law demanded. Germany. USA. Australia. All places where Google fought news publishers.

And there has always been one winner and one loser.

For nearly a decade, across most countries in the world, Google and news publishers have been locked in a zero-sum game.

Until now.

Quite recently, Google and India’s news publishers did something remarkable. They’ve figured out a way to work with each other, and both of them stand to benefit from the relationship. Google has managed to do this by learning from its missteps with Australia’s news publishers, and by transferring what it learned there to India. Google did this by launching a new product called Google News Showcase with 30 news publishers in India. The list is impressive. HT Media. The Hindu. Indian Express Group. NDTV. And many more.

Munsif’s story today is about how and why this happened. He spoke to multiple media executives across several leading media publications, and got a picture of what happened behind the scenes. He reports why some publishers received hefty multi-million payouts from Google, how publishers used their leverage on Google after observing what happened in Australia, and why Google still stands to gain from this deal. And like all our best stories, he goes deeper and analyses what will happen next, and who stands to gain in the long-term.

It’s a fantastic story, and chronicles a turning point in the global history of a decade long tussle between news publishers and Google.

You should read it if you want to understand how history was made.

It’s here: https://the-ken.com/story/inside-google-news-showcase/ (13-minute read)
