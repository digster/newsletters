Good Morning Ishan,
What’s common among Reliance Industries, Rajesh Exports, and Ola Cell Technologies? If you drew a blank, fret not—the riddle is a Category A head-scratcher. I mean, what in the world could be common among an oil-to-retail behemoth controlled by India’s richest man, a jewellery company with a chequered past, and an entity whose founder gets into Twitter duels with comedians?
Most of us at The Ken didn’t know either. Until Suprita, the author of today’s important story, enlightened us.
Turns out, three years back, this motley trio won bids to produce advanced chemistry cells (ACC)—the kind used in electric vehicles—under the production-linked incentive (PLI) scheme. The government of India—to make the country an aatmanirbhar battery-manufacturing powerhouse—earmarked over Rs 18,000 crore as a subsidy to make the dream a success. But that dream has remained just that, with only Rs 24 crore paid out against Rs 2,700 crore that should have been given so far. That’s because Reliance Industries and Rajesh Exports have precious little to show so far, while Ola Cell Technologies has taken just baby steps—despite the 2026 deadline fast approaching. To sum up, India’s Rs 18,000 crore battery plan is stuck at Rs 24 crore.
What’s behind this massive overpromise and measly underdelivery? Suboptimal player selection? Scheme design flaw? Dominant neighbour blues syndrome? Slouching tiger, hidden dragon? All of the above?
Is there hope still? Who’s trying to revive the dream?
