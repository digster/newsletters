Good Morning Ishan,

Let’s start this Monday with Google. And connect the dots.  

Now, it has been a crazy year for e-commerce so far. Needless to say, we’ve been on top of it. The Flipkart-Walmart bit. The Amazon and its offline ecosystem bit with More. The Paytm Mall part. Even, the offline retail bit, with the likes of D-Mart and Future group. You’d almost wonder, who’s next, and in comes Google. With Shopping. 

For a company whose mission is to “to organise the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful”, for a while now, Google has been thinking about Amazon. And a possibility if Amazon could soon become the destination. For commerce. For instance, when someone is searching for a noise cancelling headphone. Or clothing. Or succulents. More and more there is no reason to start these searches/transactions with Google. 

My colleagues Olina and Arundhati say that today about 80% of all online search volume and revenue come from commerce-related searches. For a company whose bread and butter is search, this is one meal that Google cannot afford to miss. If users don’t search for products on Google, and instead make Amazon or Flipkart their first destination, it threatens Google’s very existence.

So in the interest of self-preservation comes Shopping. 

It has been around for a while. In more than 35 countries, where Google has been with big retailers like Walmart, Costco, Target, Home Depot and, most recently, Nike. China’s second largest online retailer JD.com also announced plans to open an online storefront on Google’s shopping platform in the US. 

And now, India.

There are other, important things today’s piece talks about. Is this the start of an anti-Amazon alliance? Is there more to Google’s connect-the-dots strategy? Do you see a world where everything starts at Google? I’m not sure. I think we need to ask Alexa.
