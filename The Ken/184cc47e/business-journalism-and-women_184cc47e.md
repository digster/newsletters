Dear Ishan,
There is a problem in the world of business. One that we're all aware of, but something we still have a long way to go to solve.
According to the Tata AIA survey on financial awareness among women:
- Only 15% of Indian unicorns have women co-founders.
- More than 56% of women are not allowed to make financial decisions, despite being the family's breadwinners.
The Ken, too, suffers from a symptom of this problem. Only 20% of our subscribers are women.
And it’s all related. The fewer the women who read business articles, the fewer the women who develop an interest in business. This means fewer women participate in business decisions, and only a handful ever reach the top.
Despite having access to the best education, business and finance intimidated me as a kid. It didn’t come to me naturally. To make matters worse, no one around me explained their importance. And for a while, I embodied the stereotypical girl who avoided business news because that was not meant for me. Now, I am a marketing manager with a graduate degree in English Literature, on my way to pursuing an MBA, and I’ve realised just how wrong I was.
My story isn't an isolated one. It may be your story, too. Women's exclusion is a systemic problem and a very difficult one to solve. But then again, no problems worth solving are simple.
Six years ago, The Ken started with a simple mission—to make business journalism relevant and accessible to all. And over time, we found our newsroom filled with women writers as well. But the numbers told us what we’d known for a while: the number of women in business (and our subscriber base) was falling short.
And we cannot consider ourselves successful in our mission if only one out of every five of our subscribers is a woman.
So the women at The Ken and I stopped and asked ourselves, "What can we do to correct this?"
And it’s why we have come to you today; to ask for your help.
You will know that we rarely offer discounts. But because this is important to us, we are introducing an exclusive 50% discount for women readers during December.

And if you know women who you think would benefit from this, please forward this email to them.
Help us solve this problem. Help us make our little world more equal.
