Good Morning Ishan,

"Here's to the ones who dream/

Foolish as they may seem"

All of us have dreams that we want to live out. Some of us have the resources and support systems to chase them, and some of us let go of those dreams to adjust to that oft-unsavoury thing called reality.

But for those lucky few who get to live out that coveted dream, well, guess what they do?

They dream bigger.

Last week's stories at The Ken touched upon this need to follow a bigger dream, and the many places this journey can take an individual or a company.

There was Abinaya's Monday story https://the-ken.com/story/amazon-india-fashion-unfashionably-late/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on Amazon. A giant like Amazon has one dream it hasn't quite lived out yet—that of being a fashion destination, especially in India. With Flipkart's own fashion segment doing well, and with its Myntra-Jabong acquisitions, Amazon's dream needs an awakening to fight reality.

They told you that one mustn't drink and drive. But what they didn't tell you is that dreaming and driving could get you pretty lost. Olina's story on Tuesday https://the-ken.com/story/destination-unknown-zoomcars-many-turns/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter captured Zoomcar's many turns in achieving its car rental dream, and how that's veered the company off course.

Sumanth, on Wednesday, https://the-ken.com/story/saas-startups-100m-arr/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter wrote about the Indian SaaS startup playing field. Despite the recent rise of Indian SaaS unicorns, India’s SaaS ecosystem accounts for just 1% of the global pie. Can the new breed of Indian SaaS players change this, or is it still a distant dream?

Sometimes, trying to live out a dream can get tricky. All that "go live out your dream" positivity could also lead one to bite off more than they can chew. That's what happened with Quess, says Arundhati in her Friday piece https://the-ken.com/story/quess-corp-acquisition-execution/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. India’s largest staffing firm is an acquisition machine. But with its stock plummeting in recent months, the markets seem to think Quess needs a rethink.

Roshni's story this week, https://the-ken.com/story/india-and-sleep/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter though, was literally about dreams (or the lack thereof). She wrote about how India, as a country, just doesn't sleep enough. Besides, Indians often romanticise not sleeping. Startups focused on ‘sleep tech’ see an opportunity here. It's our weekly free story, so share widely.

That's all for this week. Thanks for staying with me and my overused metaphor.

Have a great Sunday. 

And, *cough* dream on!
