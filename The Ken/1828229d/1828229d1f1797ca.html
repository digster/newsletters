What happens when salaries are made public? 

 

In February this year, when demand for high-skilled software engineers and developers was at its height in India, two startup founders published the salaries of their new hires online. It got a lot of attention. Many were surprised and even shocked at the lengths that companies were willing to go to attract talent to their firms. 

 

Six months later, in today’s pilot episode of our brand new podcast, Cost to Company, we tell you what happened next. 

 

It’s a story about what led to this experiment, what publishing a salary does to a business, and more profound implications around pay transparency and pay equity in India’s biggest companies.

 

You should listen to it on our website, Apple Podcasts, or Spotify. If you wish to add it to your favourite player, here is the RSS Link. I think you’ll love it. Unfortunately, it’s not on our mobile app yet, but we’re working on getting it there soon. Sorry about that. 

 

But now that you are here, let me tell you a bit about the podcast, why we started it, and what you can expect going forward.

Cost to Company is a weekly podcast about how careers are changing in India. We live in a time of accelerated hiring, rapid layoffs, and shaky career ladders. So many questions about navigating your way through modern workplaces, how to get ahead, and what’s really going on. And nobody seems to be talking about this. 

 

Every Tuesday, we try to find answers. And to do this, we turn to the people experiencing this first-hand. This includes CEOs, executives, leaders…but most importantly, you — subscribers of The Ken. 

 

This podcast is hosted by my excellent colleagues, Sneha Vakharia and Shreevar Chhotaria. I’m sure you’ll get to know Sneha and Shreevar better as you keep listening to Cost of Company every week, but you can read more about them here and here.
