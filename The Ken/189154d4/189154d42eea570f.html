Good Morning Ishan,
 

If I got a rupee everytime my colleagues and clients asked “but what is the problem?”, I would have earned a trip to the Maldives by now. But, instead, I’ve come to realise that identifying problems is as much my job as it is to teach storytelling as a trainer with The Ken. And the more I’ve tried to answer that question, the more it tends to show up—in all kinds of conversations. 

 

Say I’m designing a new curriculum for organisations on ‘Differentiating with Narratives’. My co-trainer goes: but what problem is this curriculum solving for a data analysis team in a tech startup?

 

Or when I was customising the curriculum for a team of mid-career professionals from various departments at a newly listed company. The learning and development head of this company: but what is the universal problem (and what are the individual problems) you are solving with this customisation? 

 

Or even that time when I was adding more practical exercises and reducing theory in our curriculum. 

 

My CEO: …. 

 

You get the drift. 

 

The question has surrounded me wherever I go these past couple of years. I started out as a storytelling trainer for organisations, and now, I see problems and solutions everywhere. And this is in very large part due to the participants in our narrative workshops, whose incisive questioning has taught me to see the world from their perspective even as I was trying to make them see the world from mine. 

 

But it has been a fun two years, to this exact day, since The Ken launched the Narrative Workshops vertical to enable teams within organisations to learn what we practise every day. And now, we’ve asked and been asked—“but what’s the problem”—enough times that we’ve been able to design a highly effective curriculum for the solution. 

 

In fact, this month, 90% of participants from four different narrative workshop sessions told us that we have been successful at solving the prescribed problems. Hurrah for us, and hurrah for you!

 

If you have a business problem and you think storytelling is the solution, please apply here to enroll in the next batch of narrative workshops.

 

I would also recommend that you check out this blog post. It is a compilation of all the problems that we have unearthed over these past two years. Some are counterintuitive, others plain shocking, and the rest are predictable business problems, but ones you wouldn’t imagine would have storytelling as a solution.

 

Narratives are a solution for companies. But what is the problem?

 

Hope you have a wonderful Sunday. 

 

Warm regards,
Ruhi Kandhari

Head, Learning Vertical

The Ken
