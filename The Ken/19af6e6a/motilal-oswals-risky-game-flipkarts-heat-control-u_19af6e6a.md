[Why are companies running towards Uttar Pradesh—and not away from it?](https://the-ken.com/story/uttar-pradesh-repelled-companies-for-decades-it-now-makes-half-of-indias-smartphones/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
After decades of poor literacy, high crime rates, and shaky politics, the rise of UP as an electronics-manufacturing hub is riddled by an imbalance
Uttar Pradesh’s low human-development standards, high crime rates, and political instability made it a difficult zone for large electronics manufacturers to set up shop. But over the last few years, the state’s western belt—Noida, Greater Noida, and Yamuna Expressway—has seen immense development. The state’s policy push helped. But it’s left out the still-underdeveloped interior regions. Heavy dependence on incentives also means the growth is limited and not rooted in the state’s fabric. Perplexity’s AI drills, Google’s real-time translation, and India’s own Airlearn and Multibhashi are reshaping the market Duolingo now must defend
Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Google are wandering into language-learning, posing competition to incumbents like Duolingo, Preply, and Multibhashi. India remains Duolingo’s fifth-largest user base but one of its weakest revenue engines. AI threatens with the question: why pay for language learning when AI can teach, translate, and converse for free? Everybody in the language-learning space is adapting: adding AI summaries, feedback, and tutors. And the Indian market has splintered into four camps—gamified apps, hybrid tutor platforms, AI chatbots, and old-school classrooms.
