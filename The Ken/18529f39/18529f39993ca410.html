Good Morning Ishan,

Six months may be a blip for most businesses building for the long term, but not for Adani Green Energy Ltd. It’s a company in a hurry. 

In May, Adani Green operationalised India’s first hybrid (solar + wind) power plant in Rajasthan; by early December, it became the world’s largest hybrid power generation company. The plant already has 25-year power purchase agreements with the Solar Energy Corporation of India (SECI), a state-owned entity dedicated to developing solar energy in the country. 

Hybrid power generation has been gaining traction—in India, and at Adani Green. 

In October, the company placed a repeat order with Suzlon Energy, India’s largest (and long-beleaguered) wind turbine maker and operations and maintenance services (OMS) company. In the same month, it also acquired three special purpose vehicles (SPV) of the Inox Group.

All this would make one think that Adani Green is working with a plan. And as it turns out, India’s Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) already had a plan which nobody, at least publicly, knew about. 

Here’s what the Suzlon leadership told analysts over an earnings call in November:

…you see the focus has shifted over the last 12 months, though it was not publicly announced by anybody. So, over the last 12 months, the pure solar bids of SECI, which used to be done [periodically?] and on the base of SECI bids have not
come out. So, the government has realized that the future is hybrid, round the clock and peak power.

Suzlon’s management was right. Because in the same month, SECI floated a large 1.2 GW connected wind-solar hybrid projects tender, bids for which will become public later this week. 

If the future is hybrid, and if the Adani Group believes in owning the complete value chain, could Suzlon, reduced to a penny stock for some years now, find a new home? 

I have a hypothesis.

Will Adani give Suzlon a new home?
