Good Morning Ishan,
 
Growing up, I used to really look forward to Easter Sunday. Setting aside its religious significance, what really excited me was the television premiere of all the films released during the Christmas break.
 
When I was growing up in the early 2000s, there were no OTT platforms like Netflix or Hotstar. It was the golden age of cable television. However, on 360 of the 365 days, they would replay the same set of old movies, with the sporadic cricket matches serving as a welcome relief from the monotony.
 
During festivals such as Onam, Vishu, Easter, Eid, and Christmas, cable channels would premiere movies released during the previous holiday break. Christmas movies would air on TV on Easter, Easter movies on Onam, and Onam movies during Christmas. Easter was all the more special because, in India, April-May is the summer break. No school, no exams, and no homework.
 
Oh, the joy! Sitting at home with no homework and binge-watching new films all day. What a pure cinematic feast! Ah, those were the days! (Alexa, please play some nostalgic music in the background.)

I understand that this has been an unusually long Sunday email. But now that we have come this far, let me throw in an Easter joke while we're at it.
 
One day, a mother was on her way home from the town patisserie with some Easter eggs for her children. As she was driving, she suddenly saw a bunny dart across the road in front of her car. 

Baam! She slammed on the brakes. 

Nervously, she stepped out of the car to see if the bunny was ok. 

There lay the lifeless bunny. Tears started rolling from the mother’s eyes. She didn’t know what to do. 

Just then, a man in another car drove up and stepped out. Looking at the crying mother and the dead bunny, he swiftly returned to his car, brought a tiny shiny bottle with him, and sprayed it on the bunny. 

And lo! The bunny immediately stood up, twitched for a second and hopped away. 

The bewildered mother asked the man, "What was that spray?" 

He turned the bottle around so she could read the label. It said: "Hair spray. Restores dead hair."
