Necessity makes strange bedfellows. How else would you explain this:
 
The telecom department calls for a big consultation on spectrum policy. Doesn’t invite the three key telcos— Bharti Airtel, Reliance Jio, and Vi—but finds it fit to invite Big Tech, including Apple, Facebook, Google, chipmaker Qualcomm, and Korean giant Samsung.
 
What do you think happened in that January meeting?
 
The livid telcos gate-crashed the video meeting. They don’t want a certain spectrum band to be delicensed.
 
Here’s how the interests converge and collide. The telecom department is tasked with rolling out the new PM-WANI (Prime Minister WiFi Access Network Initiative) with a soft launch, done by the Prime Minister himself, as soon as the programme can enlist 100,000 public WiFi hotspots. The scheme aims to create 1.5 million WiFi hotspots across the country by the end of 2021—up 4X from the current ~500,000. Small businesses are expected to buy bandwidth from telcos and ISPs and resell to mom-and-pop stores, cafes and the like. These, much like the public coin-operated telephones of yore, will serve the end user. Importantly, resellers are exempt from the 8% licensing fee.
 
But everyone, including the government and Google-Facebook, which have had their respective WiFi programmes in India, know that spectrum alone will not make the hotspots successful. Google pulled the plug on its WiFi project, called RailWire, in 2020 after talks with RailTel, the Indian Railway's telecom arm, hit a wall. (The story explains the math.)
 
Facebook, meanwhile, has been slow and steady with monetisation. In providing software for the hotspots and ad exchange integration for free users, it gains through access to rich, albeit anonymised, user behavioural data. The Ken has learned that Facebook is in talks with an up and coming WiFi provider as well as RailTel to integrate its ad monetisation solution with their WiFi services.
 
Though an investment in Jio makes access a given for Facebook and Google, the telco’s struggle to become a bonafide broadband player could still leave the tech giants open to PM-WANI.
