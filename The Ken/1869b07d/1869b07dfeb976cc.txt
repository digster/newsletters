The pandemic not only changed the way we work, but it also changed the prominence of teams within companies. 

One such team is the corporate development, or the 'Corp Dev', function. 

Corp Devs in companies have traditionally played a largely passive role—typically, the leadership, with some help, evaluates incoming offers from businesses, investors or investment bankers.

But now, well-recognised businesses, such as Bewakoof.com, Rivigo, Limeroad and ZestMoney have all been involved in distress sale talks in the last six months. 

Startups created by the pandemic finding themselves at a loss are seeking out Corp Devs, presenting more opportunities for the team to play a crucial role. "Corporate development teams are looking out like hawks for the best possible match," said a corp dev executive to Aayush, the author of today's story.

These teams have also matured, and processes have started to set in—a trend indicative of an established function.
