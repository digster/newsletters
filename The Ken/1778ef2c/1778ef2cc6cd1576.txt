Good Morning Ishan,

“I am not as composed or calm as Indu (Bhushan) is. I am a little bit of a temperamental character, so be ready for all sorts of things.”

Probably said half in jest at the farewell of the outgoing CEO, this is quite a statement from an incoming chief. It shook the National Health Authority (NHA) employees and associates late last month, and it’s going to shake up the healthcare system in the coming months as the new CEO revs up the engine of the world’s largest insurance scheme—Ayushman Bharat or PM-JAY.

An IAS officer and a trained health economist, Indu Bhushan served as the first CEO of the NHA to execute the Prime Minister’s 2018 promise to insure 500 million Indians. Since Bhushan did a good job, his term extension was a given. Except on 27 January, three days before his term expiry, he was shown the door. The news came as a “bolt from the blue” for the NHA officials who’ve enjoyed a high-energy, startup-like atmosphere at their central Delhi office.

Even the Union health minister Harsh Vardhan was caught off guard and admitted his own surprise at the suddenness of the decision while speaking at Bhushan’s farewell—an intimate event held on 29 January. In the same address, Vardhan termed Bhushan’s exit a “deep loss” to the NHA.

The new CEO, RS Sharma, has had a storied career—first as the chief of UIDAI, which gave unique IDs to a billion-plus people, then as the telecom regulator in whose regime telco RJio unleashed a price war. Known to have the PM’s ear and seen as the “Aadhaar” man, Sharma has a magic ID for everything—PM-JAY, CoWIN (Covid vaccination), and National Digital Health Mission, the latter aimed at creating unique health IDs for Indian citizens.

Maitri reports that plans are afoot to assign a unique ID number to each PM-JAY beneficiary with Aadhaar linkage, which will pave the way for implementation of NDHM. The problem is: PM-JAY and NDHM are categorised differently under the Aadhaar Act, 2016. Still, the idea is to make health IDs indispensable for the people, “just in the way Aadhaar was made indispensable.”

Told you, “be ready for all sorts of things”. In this masterfully reported story, Maitri gives us a glimpse of what’s to come.
