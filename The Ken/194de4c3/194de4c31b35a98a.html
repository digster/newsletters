IPL will be back soon. But one rivalry has already started.

Act 1:

(Boardroom. Hindustan Unilever (HUL) execs gather.)

HUL: This season, we go big. Rs 100 crore on ads. Maximum reach. No wasted impressions.
Marketing VP: Disney+ Hotstar has the biggest audience. No-brainer. Sign the contract.
(Disney+ Hotstar execs nod. Contract signed. Applause.)
HUL: The perfect ad strategy.

(Curtain falls.)

Act 2:

(Living room. A cricket fan watches IPL.)

Commentator: “And that’s a glorious cover drive from Virat Kohli! That’s an over.”
TV: “Dove. For soft, glowing skin.”
(Five minutes later…) “That’s an over.”
TV: “Dove. For soft, glowing skin.”
(Five minutes later…) “That’s an over.”

(Fan twitches toward the remote.)

Act 3:

(HUL HQ. Execs on war footing.)

HUL: Why are the same people seeing the same ad 150 times a week? That’s not ‘reach’!
Marketing VP: We ran diagnostics. Impressions hit targets, but they’re looping to the same users.
HUL: That’s like selling 100,000 tickets to a 10,000-seat stadium—then making them sit through the match 10 times.

Act 4:

(A former ad-tech exec speaks to The Ken.)

Ex ad-tech exec: Classic “impressions vs. reach” trick. Platforms promise 3 million impressions. But if only 1 million users show up? They just… repeat ads.
Another exec: The contract says three views per user. But “average” or “maximum”?

(Silence. HUL scans the contract… gasps! Curtain falls.)

Act 5:

(Disney+ Hotstar’s office. HUL storms in.)

HUL: You’re milking ad spend and annoying users.
Disney+ Hotstar: Unique users are hard to track! Cookies don’t sync with device IDs. The tech isn’t precise!
HUL: So fix it.
Disney+ Hotstar: Hahaha. No.
HUL: Then we’ll pull out of IPL ads.
(Long silence.)
HUL: …Okay, no, we won’t.
Disney+ Hotstar: Right. Because the IPL is too big to ignore.
HUL: But you’ll fix the frequency problem?
Disney+ Hotstar: Oh, for sure. Absolutely. (winks at audience)
(The fan groans as another Dove ad plays.)
