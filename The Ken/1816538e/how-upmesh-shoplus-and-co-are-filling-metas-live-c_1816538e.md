Fruits. Crystals. Kidswear.
These are some of the goods e-commerce sellers are peddling on Facebook and Instagram.
There’s an inexplicable thrill in buying something off a live-stream session, right? The seller shows the product upfront, you decide you want it, and then place an order. Similar to live auctioning. No wonder it became a popular pastime during the Covid-19 pandemic.
In fact, more than three million Southeast Asians watched over 55,000 live broadcasts related to shopping on Instagram over a period of 90 days earlier this year. On Facebook, over 160,000 live broadcasts related to shopping were streamed during the same period.
However, as a merchant, tracking orders from a live stream is still a rather manual process. In Southeast Asia, the live-commerce industry hasn’t successfully married social and commerce. Orders, payments, and logistics are yet to be integrated on Facebook and Instagram Live.
This is where live-commerce enabler startups like Upmesh and Shoplus are seeing an opportunity to come in and automate this process.
Upmesh, for one, allows buyers to comment using a code, which would then launch a Facebook Messenger conversation to proceed with the transaction. The data is tracked on an online dashboard provided by Upmesh.
But this business model works because Meta is reluctant to roll out a live-streaming solution of its own. That could change in the future, though. To remain relevant in the space, live-commerce enablers need to figure out a competitive advantage and rely less on Meta’s platforms.
