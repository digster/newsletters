Good Morning Ishan,

All business schools worth their salt are reinventing. As cries for reimagining capitalism get shriller, how MBA courses are taught and what they teach are under serious introspection. At such a time, when the oldest and the most prestigious B-school in India regresses, unable to manage a leadership crisis that has become a public spat, the story needs a telling.

In December, the faculty body at the Indian Institute of Management Calcutta wrote a blistering letter to the Ministry of Education. Signed by 75% of the faculty, the letter lists the charges against the director and seeks the ministryвҖҷs intervention. The faculty believe the new director is undermining the democratic structure of the 40-year-old institute. So much so that the faculty have registered a different body to challenge the draft regulations from the directorвҖҷs office.

ItвҖҷs ironical that the IIM Act of 2017, which was meant to grant the much-awaited autonomy to B-schools, ended up concentrating power in the Board of Governors, now an eager conspirator in exalting the directorвҖҷs position.

The consequences are unsavoury. The dip in recruitment means IIM-CвҖҷs faculty strength has dropped from 92 in 2018 to below 80 in 2020. Annual spends on research are down to $9,500, from a high of $81,000 in 2018. The institute is functioning in a regulatory no manвҖҷs land as far as rules and procedures go.

AACSB, a global accreditation that IIM-C got in 2014, is under review as well.

In contrast, the two peers, IIM-A and IIM-B, have moved in the opposite directionвҖ”to вҖңde-directoriseвҖқ decision making.

It all began in November 2017. The director of the institute, Saibal Chattopadhyay, was replaced by Anju Seth, a professor of management from the US-based Virginia Tech. Various measures that followed either made little sense or were squarely aimed at cost-cutting. The schoolвҖҷs internet network was changed from Airtel to the cheaper BSNL. Library access to international journals, critical to research, was also abruptly cut.

Placements, one of the hallmarks of IIMs, are holding up for now.
