How many video streaming platforms do you subscribe to? 

I have five—two for movies and series, two largely for sports, and one just because it's part of a good bundle with music streaming and cloud storage.

And still, there are times when I can't find what I want to watch on these subscriptions and wish I had more. Five subscriptions aren't much when you consider how there are more than 40 over-the-top (OTT) streaming platforms in India. Not all 40, of course, would have content you'd pay to watch. But then, in a country as large and diverse as India, with so many different languages and dialects, maybe 40 isn't even enough! 

Today's story is about one of these 40 platforms—a rather large player at that. One with reportedly 17 million subscribers, having captured 64% of Indian households with broadband by the end of 2020. And this platform, Amazon Prime Video, is now trying to become the mother of all OTT platforms in India.

Amazon is weeks away from launching Prime Video Channels India, a marketplace for OTT platforms. Built within the Prime Video platform, it'll allow users to purchase subscriptions and watch content from third-party players. Amazon has already signed up Indian and international partners like Voot Select, Eros Now, Lionsgate Play, Mubi, and Hoichoi. It wants to become a one-stop-shop for all your OTT needs.

Prime Video Channels launched in the United States in 2015, before going to countries like the United Kingdom, Japan and Germany. It's estimated to have raked in $3.6 billion in revenue in 2020. And Amazon, which has done many things right since launching Prime Video in India in 2016, believes now is the right time to become a super aggregator in the country.

There's just one problem, though. Amazon has not been able to convince its main streaming rivals—like Netflix, Disney+ Hotstar, and ZEE5—to come onboard. And there's no reason why they should, considering the deal on offer.
