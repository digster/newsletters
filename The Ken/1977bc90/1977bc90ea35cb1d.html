Karan Bajaj, the guy who built Whitehat Jr by promising Silicon Valley jobs to 12-year-olds, is back. And this time, he’s got a startup for cancer patients.

Yes, that Karan Bajaj. The one who said he was done with startups after selling Whitehat Jr to Byju’s for $300 million in cash. The one who scaled an edtech rocketship on the backs of outlandish claims and barely-trained teachers. The one whose last venture left a lot of scorched earth—and a 10.6X return for Owl Ventures.

Now he’s running a company called Complement 1, which offers personalised lifestyle coaching for cancer patients. Think: diet, exercise, mental health—delivered through an app by live Indian coaches. It has clinical trials, top oncologists from MSK and AICR, and $16 million in VC money. It also has a business model that’s still…loading.

Bajaj believes he’s learned from the past. And sure, there are differences. This isn’t a D2C blitz. It’s B2B. It’s regulated. It’s in the US. But when the founder who once pitched coding to kindergartners says his cancer app can cut healthcare costs by 30%, you start to wonder: is this evolution, or déjà vu?

In today’s story, Arundhati digs deep into the science, the coaching, the venture math, and the shadow of Whitehat Jr that just won’t go away. Read it here.
