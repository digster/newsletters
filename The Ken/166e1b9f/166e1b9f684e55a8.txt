“Bhavish actually believes that the (food delivery) market is a food-first market.”

When I read that line in today’s story, I had to stop, back up and read it again. Because, I mean, of course food delivery is gonna be a "food-first" market. That’s the point of, you know, food delivery. 

But Ola CEO Bhavish Aggarwal’s comment wasn’t a tautology. Because he was referring to Foodpanda India, the food delivery service that he bought from Delivery Hero in 2017 in a fire sale. Which Delivery Hero bought from Rocket Internet in 2016 in sort of a fire sale. Before which Foodpanda India itself bought competitors like TastyKhana and JustEat India in 2014 and 2015 respectively. At its peak, it claimed to be operating in 150 Indian cities. Today it's down to 50.

If there was a CDO equivalent for food delivery companies, Foodpanda India would pretty much be it. 

Anyhow, cut to 2017 and Ola bought Foodpanda with an aim to take a second shot at food delivery. (Its first attempt, Ola Cafe, had bombed.) It promised to set aside $200 million to grow the Foodpanda business. It even brought in the McKinsey suit army to turn things around.

This is where Salman’s story today takes you inside. Inside Bhavish and Ola’s attempts to resurrect Foodpanda into the giant it once was. But in an environment where it has to contend with three large and well-funded competitors - Swiggy, Zomato and UberEats.

Is there place for a fourth player? What will its USP be? Why will customers choose it?

The answer lies in Bhavish’s answer of being “food-first”. Read the story to understand more about it: https://the-ken.com/story/foodpanda-india/ https://the-ken.com/story/foodpanda-india/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter
