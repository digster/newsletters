“I love seeing people grow, and the transformation that they go through in achieving their full potential. That's what drives me. Seeing that change, being able to be a participant, or a catalyst sometimes, to see people transform.”
And what drives him as an entrepreneur?
“As an entrepreneur, what drives me is doing things that most people think will not work out. I think that's what most good entrepreneurs are built of. Their fuel is all sorts of mockery, all sorts of disbelief. It's a negative motive. A negative fuel. But it works out well, because I like proving and doing things contrary to what the world is doing.”
How is working at CRED?
“If you ask anybody at CRED what the journey has been, they will say, exciting, but painful. Because transformation is painful. You have to know yourself first, then change yourself, and then excel. That journey is painful for most people.”
What is CRED?
“CRED is a place. It's not a verb, it's not a thing to do. It's not an app. CRED is a gated community within a city. That's the best way to think about it.”
What’s holding back India’s growth story?
“We need more dual-income households in order to start creating more jobs. In India, urban female participation in labour has dropped to under 10%. Today, 90% plus of all financial products are being used by men. We don't realise that we are probably in the bottom 10 for female labour participation in the world.
I was talking to a BPO founder in Bombay, and he said there was a time where 80-85% of the employees were women. And now that person has seen the transformation to becoming a largely male BPO.”
Why is paying it forward so important for him?
“I remember once going to a petrol pump having literally five rupees. At that time, the price for a litre used to be like 26-27 rupees. And I told the guy that I just needed enough fuel to give my scooter for servicing. In reality, I had five rupees to go make a delivery and was going to get some money over there. And I remember that the guy knew that I was lying, and that I had no more money than five rupees to pay for the fuel. And I know for a fact that he put more than five rupees of fuel, for sure. And there was, and there is, no way I can thank him in life.
And I think a lot of times we forget that behind all these successful people and successful entrepreneurs, thousands of people have made small contributions to really make them successful. I can't go and find these individuals in life to thank them, so the only way I see is by paying it forward.”
What did he lose by being forced to earn a living while still a teenager?
“I think you lose what youth stands for. Having fun, doing interesting things, travelling, all of that.”
Why do Indian venture capitalists and angel investors ghost entrepreneurs?
“You will see venture capitalists or angel investors finding it very hard to say no to a founder versus ghosting them. As a society we can't take an explicit ‘no’. I have seen founders who get abusive with you. Because you said no.
We take rejection personally, not realising that entrepreneurship is everyday rejection. Like non-stop. So as a society, what did we do? We say, ‘let's stay in touch’.”
How does he handle setbacks?
“I came from a place where I remember literally there were days I did not have money to eat. So, what else can go wrong? I know that it will never be as bad as it was. No matter how bad my life turns out to be now. So what setback do I need to worry about?”
What are his top four modes of communication?
“WhatsApp, WhatsApp, Slack, and in-person. I think 80% of our company is running on WhatsApp.”
But WhatsApp doesn’t allow for knowledge creation and sharing?
“When a new person walks in, they're just clueless about how to make sense of things. They almost feel that there are months that they need to just understand the context of the organisation. So one of the things that I want to do from this year is to kind of put together playbooks on how things are done, what and why we do certain things, or also make a repository of decisions made.
So one of the things I'm trying to do, for at least my own self and directs who report to me, is to document the decisions we took. So that people can look back in time and say ‘okay, these are the series of decisions made for the company, and how did they turn out to be?’. We're trying to do that. As of now, this is very hard.”
Should marriage and dating sites display credit scores?
“Why are credit scores not important in marriages? It's an interesting thing to think about, because in China and Japan, dating apps have credit scores, where both parties have to reveal their credit scores to be able to match.”
How does he ensure culture and values aren’t just posters?
“At CRED, (annual) appraisals are done on values and not on performance.”
Why is talent alone not enough?
“You need talented and motivated people. Just being talented is not good enough. Motivation comes from what is unfinished business for people. For example, we have maybe 75-80 ex-founders in the company.”
“I think people follow slope. So, if you go back in time and see how much of a slope they had to climb to get where they are, and the other person had to climb only this much, you will know that their trajectory will be this much. A lot of times people start from a lot of privilege and continue to not have a lot of slope. Do they hang around with high-slope people or do they find comfort in hanging around with low-slope people?”
How does he instil an owner’s mentality within an organisation?
“So most of our internal meetings are called shareholder meetings. They're not called review meetings or employee meetings, they're called shareholder meetings. So when you come into the meeting, you behave like a shareholder.
There are so many people who have hired their own bosses, because as a shareholder, they felt that the company needs somebody much better.”
Why does he find it hard to read books?
“I've reached a point where now when I read books, I get insanely anxious. I'm going to want to implement a lot of things that I learned almost instantly, without really having the time to really synthesise the information. And that's not always good for teams that are there."
