Narayana Health’s Viren Shetty on learning ‘how much insurance is about selling and how little about health’

Eighteen months, 75,000 policies, and some course corrections later, Narayana One Health is sticking to its long game of changing the insurance market

In the health-insurance industry, where policies have always been treated as commodities, Narayana One Health arrived as a disruptor. Targeting the middle, their affordable plan that combines insurance with healthcare, is aimed at the 50% that has money but not enough to buy traditional coverage. But a year-and-a-half in, not everything is going according to plan. Though they know healthcare, they are learning that selling insurance is a whole other ball game. [Read the full story]

Shadowfax pays today’s nosebleed prices for Delhivery’s old advantage

As the logistics firm plans to pour IPO money into new warehouses and long leases, incumbents are squeezing returns from assets they locked in when land was cheaper, and rents were lower

Shadowfax’s IPO pitch hinges on buying physical closeness with larger hubs, tighter networks, and faster turnaround, in an industry where geography still beats algorithms. That bet is getting expensive fast, with rental costs and long-term lease commitments ballooning just as warehousing turns into a landlord’s market. The timing shows up in contrast: incumbents are optimising and sweating older, cheaper assets while Shadowfax is locking itself into today’s cost structure to win scale. Investors are being asked to bet that expensive proximity today will pay off in tomorrow’s margins. [Read the full story]

Maruti’s E Vitara is here (almost) but feels strangely alien

The 26 January launch of the carmaker’s first and only EV has been pushed once again as it hitches its hopes to government subsidies

Having shipped 13,000 made-in-India E Vitaras to 28 countries so far, Maruti Suzuki is yet to reveal its India pricing and start taking bookings domestically. The carmaker wants to replicate MG Windsor’s sales strategy on a bigger scale, but it’s waiting for the PM e-drive scheme to incorporate subsidies for electric four wheelers as well. All things considered, Maruti’s first born-electric EV may be dated compared to its peers, despite offering a decent range and battery capacity. Since 2019, the company’s market share has fallen from 50% to 40%. The E Vitara is part of the carmaker’s larger strategy to regain lost ground. [Read the full story]
