Good Morning Ishan,
Spills, thrills, and iconic moments await when the Indian men’s cricket team gets its World Cup campaign rolling against the mighty Australian team in Chennai on Sunday.
It’s the first time the sport’s showpiece tournament is taking place in India since MS Dhoni walloped a memorable six against Sri Lanka in 2011 to win the cricket-obsessed nation’s first World Cup in nearly 30 years.
Expectations are high, the excitement is fever pitch. The month-and-a-half-long cricket bonanza is expected to draw 800 million viewers in India and generate up to Rs 2,200 crore (~$264 million) in ad revenues.
On the face of it, the signs clearly bode well for Disney+ Hotstar as India’s beleaguered but largest streaming platform faces its moment of reckoning.
It’s somewhat ironic that I’m using the title of a Netflix-hit sports documentary on basketball legend Michael Jordan to tell the tale of Disney+Hotstar’s Last Dance in India.
Losing the digital rights to the prized Indian Premier League cricket series and dropping content from popular entertainment studio HBO has cost Disney+ Hotstar at least 21 million subscribers in just a year.
Now, the streaming platform is also nearing the end of its International Cricket Council (ICC) rights cycle.
Parent firm Walt Disney is reportedly mulling a sell-off or a joint venture for the Indian television and the streaming business.
Moreover, the lack of a coherent strategy—one that today’s story indicates is more reactive—and a flood of senior leadership shuffles have done little to help Disney+ Hotstar’s cause.
However, undeterred by its current woes and unshackled from its parent company’s Wall Street dynamics, Disney+ Hotstar’s current head honchos are determined to get matters back on the profitability track or, at the very least, go out in a blaze of glory.
