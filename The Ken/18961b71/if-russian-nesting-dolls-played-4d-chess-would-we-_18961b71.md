Good Morning Ishan,
On Friday evening, I watched Tom Cruise perform impossible stunts in Mission Impossible: Dead Reckoning. And today, you’re going to read the corporate equivalent of that. Absolutely edge-of-the-seat stuff. Mystery. Danger. Destruction. Subterfuge. It’s all there in what’s happening with Pharmeasy, soon-to-be-formerly the highest-valued Indian healthcare startup, last valued at $5.6 billion.
For the last few weeks, newspapers and websites have been agog with the news about Pharmeasy’s reported 90% fall in valuation as it tries to desperately raise money to pay off earlier loans.
A 90% fall in valuation? More than what meets the eye.
A bunch of existing investors who banded together to offer Pharmeasy the money to repay its loan, so it doesn’t lose its crown jewel, Thyrocare (acquired for $553 million in 2021)? The team has more interests in common than you’d think.
Could an IPO still happen, after all of this? Yes, but perhaps not of Pharmeasy.
How long can Thyrocare continue to pump its own lifeblood (read: cash dividends) to sustain its parent? Not for long.
Seema Singh and Anand Kalyanaraman deftly explore each dark and twisted alley in this ongoing corporate caper, and leave us with the best available assessment yet of Pharmeasy’s future. A Monday morning corporate action-spy thriller, if you will.
