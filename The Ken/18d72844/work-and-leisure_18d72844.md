Good Morning Ishan,
I’m writing this from my hometown Kolkata, after having some delicious prawn curry and fried fish cutlets. Good food, no chores, just the right amount of winter chill, and the city’s leisurely laze have made me forget about the mad rush of Bengaluru, where I’ve migrated for work.
Like me, millions of other young people are moving out in droves to other cities within India or abroad, in search of better work opportunities.
On Friday, Narayanan [wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/indias-manufacturing-resurgence-has-a-people-problem-ask-tamil-nadu/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) about one such opportunity—a job boom in the electronics-manufacturing segment in Tamil Nadu. It has created an unprecedented demand for workers. So much so that companies are even hiring eighth graders.
On Thursday, Arundhati, Rounak, and Gaurav took a [deep dive](https://the-ken.com/story/rbi-issues-a-death-sentence-to-paytm-payments-bank/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) into why the RBI ordered Paytm Payments Bank to stop accepting new deposits in its accounts or wallets.
That same day, Katrina [wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/how-san-miguel-corporations-energy-arm-became-its-problem-child/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) about how the San Miguel Corporation, an industrial behemoth in the Philippines, pinned its hopes and money on LNG, and how that’s taking its energy arm to financial extinction.
On Wednesday, I zoomed in on what Ola Electric is doing in the run-up to its IPO. From cutting costs in manufacturing to firings, the company is [trying](https://the-ken.com/story/what-is-ola-electrics-top-priority-if-it-is-not-e-scooters/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) to do all it can, when it should have instead focussed on improving its scooters and services.
Yesterday, in the latest edition of the [Nutgraf](https://the-ken.com/the-nutgraf/krutrim-is-whatever-ola-wants-it-to-be/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), Praveen asked an important question: What is Krutrim, the AI startup founded by Ola’s Bhavish Aggarwal? And answered that very question in a very curious and practical manner: what it means for the founder’s business ambitions?
