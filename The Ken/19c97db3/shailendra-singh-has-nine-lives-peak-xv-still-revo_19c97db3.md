Good Morning Ishan,
Earlier this month, when three partners broke away from Peak XV, our immediate journalistic instinct was: how will the VC firm survive this blow? But Shailendra Singh had been here before.
Just two weeks later, when the firm announced its $1.3 billion fund close amid high-profile churn, our immediate reaction, again, was why did the partners leave on the eve of this big event?
Arundhati set out to answer these.
Turns out clashes snowballed at the last minute, apologies were tendered, resignation letters were allegedly retracted, and yet Peak XV splintered the way it did. A lot of it had to do with the timing.
For years, the Indian VC world was about theoretical wealth. Partners invested, and then waited for years, and hoped to see big payoffs. Since there wasn’t much liquidity, there wasn’t much to quarrel over either. With Groww’s bumper exit, one particular partner at Peak, Ashish Agarwal, demanded a lot more than was customary.
Singh has led the firm with a certain philosophy. He’s worked hard and is probably working harder now that the firm has to oversee more than $10 billion across 450 companies, with only seven partners. The bench has thinned; Singh is rebuilding. But how did he land here?
