Good Morning Ishan,
Few businesses have been as immune to the transformative power of VC dollars as selling beds, sofas, and bookshelves. Edtech, for instance, soared before it plummeted. But furniture retail has always flatlined.
Pepperfry and Urban Ladder, which Reliance Retail bought for a song in 2020, have been at it for at least 13 years with sales that are nothing to write home about. Even Ikea, despite its global renown, has been far from a resounding success in India.
So it came as a surprise to me when Premji Invest, hardly a garden-variety investor, recently wrote Wooden Street a Rs 350 crore cheque. Perhaps, furniture retail in general, has turned the corner. Or Wooden Street has learnt to avoid its rivals’ pitfalls.
How much can you customise before it becomes unviable? How do you choose between the flexibility that comes with relying on multiple manufacturers and the control in-house production provides?
The answers to these questions undergird Premji Invest’s bet on Wooden Street, Pranathi argues in her [story](https://the-ken.com/story/wooden-streets-furniture-play-that-pepperfry-and-urban-ladder-missed/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) today.

As placement season heats up in India’s premier B-schools, this week’s episode of Two by Two breaks down how placement cells in these institutes function and what needs to change to ensure better outcomes for all students.
Our guest for the episode is Professor Varun Nagaraj, Dean and Professor of Information Management & Analytics at SP Jain Institute of Management and Research (SPJIMR), Mumbai. And over a nearly hour-and-a-half discussion, the professor and hosts Rohin Dharmakumar and Praveen Gopal Krishnan discuss how placements have evolved since their MBA days, their misgivings about the current system, and what institutes have to get better at.
