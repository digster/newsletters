Good Morning Ishan,
In April 2023, Bigbasket co-founder and CEO Hari Menon had no interest in getting into 10-minute grocery delivery. “Consumers never wanted quick delivery,” he had said dismissively.
And yet—open the Bigbasket app today to order some milk or veggies; that’s exactly what you see by default.
“We have stopped using the term ‘e-grocer’ now,” a senior executive told Nuha, the writer of today’s story. The slotted-delivery business that was once the Tata-owned company’s bread and butter has been relegated to just an additional option at checkout.
And yet—after dominating the online grocery market for years, Bigbasket’s market share in quick commerce is in the single digits.
The thing is, Bigbasket is fighting its own DNA. It’s late to a game where speed is everything, and it’s hard to take such a U-turn after years of being a pragmatic, profitably-run business. Still, that’s the direction the Tatas reportedly want Menon to take.
And yet—being sensible is kind of the Tata way.
While Blinkit, Instamart, and Zepto burn through cash chasing speed and discounts, Bigbasket won’t play by those rules. So, Menon’s job now is to offer the same convenience—without compromising the Bigbasket way.
And yet—are the Tatas really all-in on this quick-delivery thing?
Bigbasket’s biggest battle isn’t just with its competitors. It’s with its own identity, writes Nuha. Let’s get to [the story](https://the-ken.com/story/hari-menon-has-to-save-bigbasket-from-itself/).
