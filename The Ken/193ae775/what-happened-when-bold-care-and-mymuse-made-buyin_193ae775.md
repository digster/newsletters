Good Morning Ishan,
Today’s story explores a subject that many Indians find sensitive. Here’s a hint:
No, this is not an Indian soap opera but a viral commercial for a sexual-wellness brand, Bold Care, featuring American adult film star Johnny Sins (middle) and Bollywood actor and the company’s co-founder Ranveer Singh (right). The brand’s revenue recently crossed Rs 100 crore (US$11 million) in annualised revenue, a milestone it had set for 2024, its sixth year in business.
But what is sexual wellness, you ask? It is the ability to experience pleasure, intimacy, and satisfaction in sexual relationships while also maintaining physical and emotional health with products like long-lasting condoms, safe lubricants, Viagra and pure shilajit, couples massagers, and more.
Bold Care’s competitors, like Mymuse, are also proving that different marketing tactics can be just as lucrative. It pulled off a Rs 1.2 crore haul during a 24-hour flash sale on its platform. But what really fetched windfall gains was a three-day Single’s Day campaign—on Blinkit. The result? A 3X surge in revenue with its best-selling Groove massage wand flying off the shelves at 6X its usual rate, Sahil Gupta, the company’s founder, told Sudeshna, the author of today’s story.
Even Bold Care now accounts for one-fifth of the sales in Blinkit’s sexual-wellness category, with 23 other brands in the mix. Instamart and Nykaa are in on the action, too.
But how come they struck gold in an industry with giants like Durex and Manforce and with social stigmas attached to anything to do with sexual well-being?
