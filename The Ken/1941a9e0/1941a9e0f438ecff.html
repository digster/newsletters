2024 will be etched in podcast history as the year of the churn. From the lines between video and audio blurring with the mainstreaming of video podcasts, to the surge in AI-generated content, podcasting is undergoing a remarkable transformation.

Here at The Ken’s studio, we steered clear of both—call us purists! Even so, it’s been a year full of experiments, learnings, milestones, and reckoning. We launched two brilliant podcasts, hit pause on a popular one, and closed the chapter on another. Meanwhile, our flagship podcast, Daybreak, crossed 1 million cumulative downloads.

Let’s take a closer look at our progress and achievements in 2024.

Your daily fix: Daybreak

In April, we turned Daybreak, our flagship podcast, into a daily. Three months later, we hit two milestones!

After more than 280 episodes, Daybreak crossed 100,000 monthly downloads and one million cumulative downloads. As an independent, subscriber-driven newsroom, this was huge for us. When we started Daybreak more than two years ago, the idea was to tell interesting business stories—stories about ideas, people, money, power, triumphs, and failures. We wanted to give you everything you would need to develop your own perspective and form your own opinions.

Daybreak is chronicling a pivotal time in the Indian startup ecosystem—the near-collapse of edtech, the rise of fintech companies and the way they manoeuvre through ever-fluid regulations, the fascinating evolution of quick-commerce, and so much more.

My co-host Rahel and I recorded an episode on what 2024 was like for our podcast. You can listen to it here: “Daybreak 2024 — Episodes that Soared and Stumbled.”

One of a kind: Two by Two

In July, we launched Two by Two as The Ken’s and India’s first premium business podcast. The idea is simple: discuss and break down the most important business stories happening in India, visualised as a 2×2 matrix of players, stakes, and winners. These discussions are particularly valuable because our guests speak their mind and bring their own counterintuitive perspectives to the table. Every week, they join hosts Rohin Dharmakumar and Praveen Gopal Krishnan, and our guests range from professors from India’s top B-Schools to CEOs and founders of companies that you know well.

Our inaugural episode was “Will Flipkart become Phonepe before Phonepe becomes Flipkart?”

These two companies are among India’s biggest startups. Each is the largest in its respective sector, and Flipkart previously acquired Phonepe, then spun it off.

The surprise was that our guests—Perfios CEO Srikanth Rajagopalan and R. Srinivasan, professor of strategy at IIM Bangalore—disagreed with our hosts about the discussion’s premise. What followed was an intense debate about the whys and hows of Flipkart and Phonepe venturing into each other’s territories.

Two by Two is a spiritual successor to one of our most popular podcasts, First Principles, which, throughout two seasons and 40 episodes, gained a cult following for candid conversations with India’s best-known entrepreneurs and leaders, and fresh insights on how we can apply first-principles thinking to not only see the world differently but also remake it in our own vision.

Two by Two is a big bet for us. Paying a subscription fee to listen to a podcast is unheard of in the Indian podcasting space. It’s still early days, but subscribers have shared their excitement for Two by Two, and we’re ready to carry it forward.

The career pulse: The First Two Years

In March, we launched The First Two Years, or TFTY as we prefer to call it in the newsroom (rhymes with “nifty”). It was a new podcast to help young professionals navigate the early years of their career. Six months later, we made the hard decision to release its final episode.

TFTY host Akshaya Chandrasekaran described this podcast as a “friend that will ask—and answer—the most important questions about learning to succeed at work.”

How do you negotiate your first salary? Can you manage your manager? Should you fight for your next promotion? Should you make friends with your colleagues? When is the right time to walk away from a job you hate?

In TFTY’s 30 episodes, Akshaya spoke with founders, best-selling authors, organisational psychologists, HR consultants, and leaders from around the world to locate the answers to those questions.

Going by the sheer volume of messages and comments we received for almost every episode, TFTY managed to strike a chord. In fact, in November, it even made it to Spotify’s list of top new podcasts for the year.

So, why did we decide to pull the plug? For starters, Akshaya decided to leave The Ken and start a new chapter of her life. We also realised that our initial hypothesis for TFTY may have been flawed. Picking professionals aged 18–25 as our target audience helped us sharpen our topics, but we wanted to do more.

Our CEO, Rohin Dharmakumar, put it nicely: “We are going back to the drawing board and remaking plans for a new careers podcast. One that will walk the fine line of being appealing to many more career professionals, managers, leaders, coaches, and founders. One that will still have the sharp point of view The Ken’s podcasts are best known for.”
