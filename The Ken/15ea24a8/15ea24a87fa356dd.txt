Good Morning Ishan,

Let’s start at the beginning. Because public memory, at best, is the long-term memory of a squirrel. 

When 30 executives and founders broke away from the IT industry body Nasscom four years ago and formed iSpirt, a think tank to help and grow product startups, the underdogs finally had a voice of their own. 

iSpirt did yeomen service for the first few years. It helped Indian product startups go global and sell local. The energy and missionary zeal of the founders and volunteers were infectious, spreading across the country. I even had a selfish reason to be happy. I was also writing for IEEE Spectrum those days and my editors in New York were somewhat frustrated. They thought I wasn’t looking hard enough for good ‘tech’ (read: breakthrough tech) stories in Indian IT. With iSpirt, I thought it’d be a one-stop hunting ground for such stuff. 

But before any such thing happened, iSpirt shifted shape. And joined itself at the hip with India’s unique digital identity project Aadhaar. From a champion of Product Nation, iSpirt has become a pusher of India Stack, a collection of technologies built atop Aadhaar. From a representative body taking policy proposals to the government, it has become a policy implementer, even a powerful gatekeeper as some allege. From a general leading a war against the 'digital colonization' of India by the Big Tech, iSpirt has ended up offering the digital platform to the Big Tech. From a credible voice of the underdogs to an entity that has tried silencing its critics—it’s a metamorphosis that is hard to understand.

That is Rohin’s story for you. He doesn’t come to it from an iconoclastic standpoint, even though he has actively campaigned for Net Neutrality and critiques Aadhaar on social media in his personal capacity. This two-part story is the result of weeks of reporting.

When iSpirt broke away, we joked in the newsroom that Nasscom needed iSpirt more than it needed the larger, irrelevant-looking body. Today one wonders if iSpirt, the product think tank, needs itself. R.I.P irony 2017.
