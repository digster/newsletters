India’s ‘other Warren Buffett’ won’t stop selling while everyone’s still buying https://the-ken.com/story/indias-other-warren-buffett-wont-stop-selling-while-everyones-still-buying/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card 

Pulak Prasad-run Nalanda Capital considers itself a “permanent owner” of its portfolio companies. And so, the marquee investor’s numerous recent exits may be a canary in the mine

Singapore-based Nalanda Capital, with an enviable reputation in the Indian stock market, has been selling partial stakes in many of its portfolio companies. The Pulak Prasad-led fund house with a Rs 50,000-crore portfolio has a philosophy of never selling on valuation. It seems to be making an exception now. Nalanda Capital draws lessons from evolutionary biology in its investment approach: “avoid big risks, buy quality at a fair price, and don’t be lazy, be very lazy”. It has been anything but lazy in its “sell” decisions in recent times—potentially flashing warning signs in a runaway bull market that has seen valuations soar. A Peak XV-b​​acked school declares war on IIT coaching—by hiring 200 IITians https://the-ken.com/story/200-iitians-hired-in-two-years-a-kedaara-backed-school-declares-war-on-iit-coaching/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card

Orchids, a K-12 school chain, is not alone in losing students to test-prep centres after tenth grade. But it has an unusual fix

An indifferent rote-based learning in schools gave rise to India’s tuition republic—a Rs 58,000-crore after-school coaching industry. Coaching centres have flaunted ‘star’ faculty and a conceptual learning approach to do a one-better in traditional schools. But Orchids The International School has had enough. In 2 years, it has hired 200 IITians. The star teachers are not just bringing hands-on learning to classes but are tasked with helping retain students after class 10. For a school struggling to carve its niche and arrest student attrition in higher classes, this could be a make-or-break experiment. And IITians are just the beginning of the school’s ambitious un-coaching plans.
