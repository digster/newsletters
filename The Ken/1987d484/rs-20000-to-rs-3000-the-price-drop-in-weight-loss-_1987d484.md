Good Morning Ishan,
Last year, we wrote about India’s underground obsession with [Ozempic](https://the-ken.com/story/ozempic-is-unapproved-and-unreliable-in-india-but-ive-still-ordered-the-miracle-weight-loss-drug/)—a “miracle” weight-loss drug that wasn’t even approved here yet. People were buying it, smuggling it, injecting it.
Everything’s changed now.
India is no longer just whispering about semaglutide—the molecule inside GLP-1 drugs like Ozempic and Wegovy. It wants the real thing. Legally. Cheaply.
That’s because in March 2026, semaglutide goes off patent. Prices of the Rs 20,000-a-month injections are expected to crash by 85%. And when that happens, a drug reserved for the rich will suddenly be within reach for millions. This is based on market research shared exclusively with The Ken.
Naturally, the pharma world is losing its mind.
More than a dozen Indian companies—from Cipla and Zydus to Dr Reddy’s and Sun Pharma—are racing to build manufacturing capacity. Investors are throwing money at anyone who can spell “peptide”.
There’s just a teeny problem: this stuff is hard to make. Semaglutide is a squiggly, complex peptide that needs pristine bioreactors, cold chains, and years of expertise. India still imports 70% of its pharma raw materials from China. Oh, and while everyone’s investing in injectables, a cheaper-to-make version is already catching investor attention.
