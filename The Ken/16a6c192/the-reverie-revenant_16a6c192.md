Today’s story is about return; not particularly from a business trip but more like from being dead.
Now, you can’t put "The Revenant" in the headline and not go back to Alejandro Gonzalez Innarritu’s cinematic masterpiece in 2016. The Revenant is a gritty, cold movie. I loved most parts of it. But the best is what happened after. When instead of the big bear, who, by the way, acted really well; Leonardo DiCaprio, the star of the movie won the Academy Award for Best Actor. The man’s first. Ever.
Bear: 0; DiCaprio: 1
After reading today’s piece, I believe Arvind Pani has done a DiCaprio.
Pani started Reverie, a local language technology solutions company in 2009. In the last ten years, he went through the motions, building the company, adding new lines of revenue, raising capital and getting more customers. But my colleague Sumanth says that in many ways than one, Reverie was stuck. Its revenue just wouldn’t shoot up however much the company tried, its business didn’t grow 5X or 10X, its investors lost faith and in all of this Reverie just hung around, unsure of the outcome.
And then came Reliance. A few weeks back, the company acquired Reverie for $37 million.
It is an outcome that perhaps no one saw coming.
Sumanth though says that Reliance, with its Jio phones, wants local language tech badly. And so Reverie just fits snug, at the right time. As if it had been waiting, all these years, for just this outcome.
