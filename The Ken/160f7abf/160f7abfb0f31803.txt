See, the thing with the idea of PressCoin, the subject of today’s story is not that it is too good to be true. Those too-good-to-be-true stories of cryptocurrency and blockchain, those you can catch. They have a certain stench to them. They stink of bad intentions. It is the ideas like PressCoin, which have this veneer of sanctity and higher purpose, that makes them more difficult to get to. But that’s where we come in.

PressCoin calls itself a cryptocurrency for an independent press. It believes in the higher purpose that journalism is suffering from—the twin plagues, if you can call it that, of fake news and compromised ownership. So, using blockchain technology, PressCoin wants to build an entire system that supports a diverse array of independent media companies. And to support this with tools. To do this, PressCoin is doing an initial coin offering (ICO) of its token, NEWS. As you are reading this, PressCoin is hoping to raise $100 million. To participate, the minimum purchase amount for an INR transfer is Rs 50,000, and $1,000 for USD.

The problems are many. To start with, what PressCoin is selling. First, it said that its token NEWS is an asset-backed or an equity token. Then when The Ken reached out to them and said, hey, do you guys have permissions from the US SEC, from the authorities in India, PressCoin said they didn’t need any. And even as Sidhartha, my colleague, was reporting this piece, PressCoin started changing things in its white paper listed on its website.
