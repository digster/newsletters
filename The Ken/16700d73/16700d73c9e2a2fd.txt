Good Morning Ishan,

12 days. That’s how long it has been since I last wrote to you. 

In this time, I’ve had a quick break to the hills. There, I found the time to read. Take long, deep breaths. Think through life decisions. Stare at the night sky full of stars. Learnt that I suck at jogging on hilly terrain. Stretched my arms wide, riding pillion on a scooter, breezing through mountain roads. Enjoyed spending time with friends I love. Made new friends. Had corn candy for the first time in my life. And learnt that pirating Netflix shows and selling it to a clientele of school kids is a thriving business.

Through all of this, I still missed you. So I am glad to be back. To the one thing I absolutely love.

I understand it has been a festive weekend, but I think today’s story is important. Written by my colleagues Ruhi and Roshni. It picks up from something that has become a hot potato in the West. The growing voices of parents who are anti-vaccine. Such is the complexity of this issue that is now almost drowned in part science, part hoax, part fake news, part strong beliefs, part religion, part conspiracy. And in all parts, a gigantic mess. One that must be understood better.

It is only natural that what’s going on in the West must spread East.

While there isn’t any nationwide data or a study to suggest vaccine resistance, our reporters were able to locate quite a few parents in India who believe their children don’t need vaccines to be healthy. Some say it causes autism. I’ll leave you with this bit from the story: “Home birthing, homeschooling, extended breastfeeding, clean eating and natural medicine are five of six lifestyle choices Hemant and Sangeeta Chhabra made over the course of 30-odd years. The sixth was to not vaccinate their three children…”

To this, an exasperated Dr Ajit Gajendragadkar says, “Too many people read shit on the internet but don’t use common sense.”
