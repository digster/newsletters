If you’re working in Corporate India, then you can prompt yourself to success if you’re willing to put in the time, effort, and creativity. At least, that’s the message that’s being put across by business leaders across the country.

Employers are increasingly demanding their staff to use artificial intelligence to make themselves more efficient and innovative. This doesn’t always come with a well-defined plan, but employees are expected to make the leap and take their performance to new heights.

There is a “new language” of work that involves a layer of unlearning, according to one source for today’s story. It favours younger, less experienced team members who tend to be more open about using new tools or charting new ways of working that more senior colleagues might not be able to imagine.

There are downsides though. Forced adoption of AI-assisted methodologies and solutions could make every task look like a nail, with employees wielding AI as a hammer.

The Ken polled nearly 500 people who span entry-level to senior positions. We wanted to find out how often AI assistance is a requirement in India’s workplaces, what the expectations are, and how this is changing the way employees work.

For today’s story, Arundhati made sense of the responses to showcase how the transformation is unfolding in companies such as Razorpay, Phonepe, Zerodha*, and more. Read it to find out what it’s like to be in the thick of it.
