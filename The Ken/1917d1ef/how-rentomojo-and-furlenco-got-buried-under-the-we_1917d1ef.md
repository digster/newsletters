Good Morning Ishan,
I remember that when startups like Rentomojo and Furlenco burst on the scene about 10 years ago, I was excited by the idea of them.
In the era of pay-as-you-go, the idea of paying for furniture and appliances for just as much as you use them seemed revolutionary and also sensible. Who wanted the burden of upfront costs and the hassle of getting rid of them when you moved jobs or cities?
But that was until the broken products began to pile up. This ended up being a business convenient for consumers but not so convenient for the companies.
Rentomojo and Furlenco, which pioneered the rental model in India, have found that the math of this business model was just not adding up. Refurbishment costs alone can eat up to 30% of total revenue, or 3-4 months’ rent per product.
Moreover, servicing these customers has turned out to be a reality check for the companies.
On the one hand, their customers are less likely to care for something they don’t own—it is common to find cigarette burns and food spills on furniture. And on the other, consumers want to rent something that looks new.
It doesn’t help that VCs have also gone cold on this. In what ended up being a down round, Rentomojo’s latest bid to raise US$25 million didn’t attract any of its existing backers.
