Good Morning Ishan,
 
How has the new year been for you? For me, it started with marriage. Mercifully, not my own. As my mother once said to an overbearing aunt―the sort you see twice a decade and who's always surprised you've aged―no woman deserves that sort of suffering. A wise woman, my mother. Sadly, I only inherited her waistline. 

But back to the wedding.
 
As I stood in attendance, trying to contemplate why anyone would willingly wear suspenders in the 21st century, I realised something. Five days into 2019, my cousin's year was already defined. Even if aliens landed tomorrow, he would forever remember 2019 as the year he got hitched.
 
But for me and, I dare say, you, our years are still to be defined. Circumstances in existence will play their part, but unseen variables will determine its ultimate course. The subjects of this week's stories are also on the cusp of some big developments.
 
Take Seema's week-opener, for example. Seema wrote about Birac, the Biotechnology Industry Research Assistance Council, which has brought Rs 1870 crore to the tech commercialisation table. The question now is whether it can be nimble enough to succeed in the next phase of its mission.

Tuesday saw Vivek turn the spotlight on the latest threat to Startup India—the Angel Tax. At the fag end of 2018, India's income tax department fired off a bullet that looks set to hurt fledgeling startups. Ones that still haven't turned a profit.

On Wednesday, we had Ruhi, who looked at India's healthcare situation. In their quest to revamp India's medical education space, the government has scrapped the Medical Council of India. Now, the interim body it instated is hoping to help bridge India's shortfall of specialist doctors. But not all good intentions make for great solutions.

Avinash Luthria, our first contributor of 2019, wrote Thursday's piece. In it, he discussed an oft-overlooked aspect of the mutual funds industry—AMC fees, distributor commissions, and the need to shift to direct plans.

We ended the week with a look at a fascinating retail chain. One that claims Japanese heritage but is more Chinese than it would like to admit. Miniso. The company has boasted some big numbers despite only being around since 2017. Harveen dives deep to see if the numbers add up.

There was also our first TKOW pieces of 2019. Saturday saw the fifth installation of Saurabh Mukherjea and Anupam Gupta's Simplicity Paradigm series. This one delves into the benefits of a spiritual approach to simplicity.

And on Sunday, Roshni worked her storytelling magic on the unlikeliest of subjects—Morbi, a small town in Gujarat. Morbi was once India's clock manufacturing hub. But in today's world, as our dependence on clocks wanes, so too have Morbi's fortunes.
