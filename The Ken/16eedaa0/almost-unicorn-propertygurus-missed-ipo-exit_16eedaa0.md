Imagine if WeWork were a giant, gargantuan butterfly. And it flapped its massive wings atop the Nasdaq building in New York, before crashing promptly to the ground. That butterfly’s effect would ripple through the world’s IPO markets, before finally arriving in faraway Australia a few weeks later. And result in the surprising cancellation of an IPO Asia desperately needed—PropertyGuru’s.
Southeast Asia’s largest property classifieds company, PropertyGuru, canned its much-awaited IPO on 23rd October, exactly two days before its shares were set to trade on the Australian Securities Exchange (ASX).
The IPO would have helped PropertyGuru raise $256 million, in a region starved of them. And if anyone had the pedigree to pull off a successful IPO, it was PropertyGuru. Backed by global blue-blooded private equity investors like TPG Capital and KKR, it raised $125 million in 2015 and then $144 million last year, at a valuation close to the magical one billion dollars. To make things even better, it then declared itself profitable and cash-flow positive.
A unicorn backed by TPG and KKR that also generated profits? Bring out the chequebooks already, right?
But fate had different plans, as Kay reports in her detailed story on the cascading series of unfortunate events that befell PropertyGuru. The profits looked questionable. A set of competitors decided to merge and become more formidable. The company’s cash is running out. ASX, the exchange where it wanted to list, is having some existential crises of its own.
And of course, the IPO got canned.
