Good Morning Ishan,
 
The duck-sized horses eliminated, the two horse-sized ducks left in India's food delivery industry were galloping/waddling along merrily, burning money by the truckload.
 
No more. Zomato and Swiggy now face a new enemy in the shape of a dinosaur-sized elephant called Amazon. Two weeks ago, Amazon launched its food delivery operations in Bengaluru, Swiggy's backyard.
 
It has started on the slow and steady path. It dog-fooded the service with employees in Bangalore, before piloting the service in only four pin codes in the city, with a mere 100 restaurants signed on.
 
But that's how it always starts, a slow and steady pace before becoming a juggernaut. There are already signs it will steamroll Swi-mato. It charges restaurants a commission of about 10-12%, less than half of what Swiggy and Zomato charge.
 
That's enough reason for them to shift uncomfortably in their perches. "Amazon's entry is nothing less than an existential threat for us," a senior Zomato executive said.
 
But hold that order. This is Amazon. What is it doing in the food business, which has single-handedly made a mockery of the 'economies of scale' theory? Practically no company globally is making a profit on food delivery, and Amazon certainly is not out to eradicate world hunger. So what gives?
 
It already sells everything you could possibly want, including groceries. So why get into the business of mere delivery? That too of a product people order more regularly than anything els... Oh! And Swi-mato have allied business as well-selling groceries to restaurants, which the dinosaur-sized elephant can do as well.
