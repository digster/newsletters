Speaking of reading though, we might have some material to help you get started. Namely, The Ken's week that was.
 
We started this week with a fresh free story from Sidhartha, which grew with every bit of reporting he got in. Sid's (as we call him) story scrutinised the apparently reformist PressCoin's NEWS—cryptocurrency for journalism—and showed how its claims may not be particularly accurate.
 
On Tuesday, Ruhi wrote about Healthcare at Home, which offers home healthcare services, in addition to delivering specialty drugs. The story looked at how high margins on expensive drugs could, in turn, help make bedside care affordable.
 
Day 3 had Venkat telling the OTT video story in India. And how OTT (as used colloquially) it is. Now Alibaba wants to join the bandwagon with a video service powered by Paytm and UCWeb, and with technology from its native OTT app in China, Youku Todou.
 
We had Sidu Ponnappa, a first-time contributor, write for us on Thursday. Sidu's story looked into the changing ways of growing IT and IT-enabled services businesses. With the advent of software scaling horizontally, automation, small teams—so small that Jeff Bezos called them 'Two Pizza Teams'—today's businesses are no longer Project Manager driven.
 
The week ended with Venkat again. He wrote a piece on messenger app Hike, which took a closer look at the $1 billion unicorn's recent announcement on going back to telecom. Piggybacking on Airtel, Hike is now in search of new users, a different demographic.
 
Hope you have a great weekend. If you're yet to subscribe to us, maybe it's time you struck that off your weekend to-do list!
