Good Morning Ishan,
Indian startups are full of loud founders. The podcast regulars. Twitter’s gyaan dispensers. The ones who can turn a funding round into a TED Talk.
Sriharsha Majety has been… not one of them. Calm in a crisis, quick to give autonomy, reluctant to micromanage—he was Swiggy’s Mr Nice Guy. The founder who would rather ask you how you were than grill you on last week’s metrics.
Not anymore.
Over the past year, the 39-year-old co-founder has recast himself—and by extension, the company’s leadership culture. What triggered the change? A bruising year in Swiggy’s quick-commerce arm, Instamart, where rivals Blinkit and Zepto closed the gap and, in some places, pulled ahead. For a man used to playing the long game, the market-share loss hit like a jolt of caffeine.
The pivot at the Rs 99,800 crore market-cap company has been deliberate. Majety began attending weekly reviews personally, poring over order-level data, quizzing senior leaders, and pulling in Flipkart alumni to speed up decision-making. He gave the green light for Instamart’s footprint to nearly triple in months—but tied those bets to hard performance goals. The new mantra: trust, but verify.
Inside Swiggy, the shift is palpable. Meetings are tighter, conversations blunter, patience thinner. Some veterans have thrived in the sharper atmosphere; others have left. Majety himself called it a “mind shift” before the IPO—a willingness to be “rude if it means getting results”.
Nuha’s fascinating profile on him today goes behind closed doors to show how a soft-spoken engineer-turned-CEO is grappling with the demands of scale. You’ll see the Instamart war rooms, hear from executives navigating the new pace, and watch as Swiggy’s culture evolves from consensus-driven to output-driven.
