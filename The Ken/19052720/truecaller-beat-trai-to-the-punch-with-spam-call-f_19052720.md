Good Morning Ishan,
Death and taxes aren’t the only certainties in life. At least in India. There’s also that great leveller—spam and scam calls. The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (Trai) was supposed to put a stop to them a long time ago. It tried, hemmed and hawed, and then, like many powers-that-be, did precious little. “No decision is, in itself, a decision,” a wise person once said.
But like nature, India’s desperate telecom users abhorred the vacuum. Enter a knight in shining armour—from the far-off cold climes of Sweden, no less. Truecaller, it called itself. It filled the breach and how. Today, the call-screening app has—believe it or faint—273 million monthly active users in India. That’s nearly a quarter of the country’s telecom user base. This makes it “an arbiter of calls”, as Rounak, the author of today’s sharp story, aptly puts it. Truecaller is the real Trai, some say. Ouch!
Make no mistake, Truecaller is making the most of its dominance in the country—making money off both people tired of spam calls and businesses who want to reach out to more users. India has become as important to Truecaller as Truecaller has become to India. A chunk of the company’s revenue now comes from the country. It’s calling the shots on the calls.
And that’s making many uncomfortable, especially given the company’s not-so-nice past on data privacy.
So, a hustle is now underway in India’s telecom power corridors to seize back the initiative. A new government has been recently anointed, after all, and results have to be shown in quick time.
What’s at stake? Let’s just say, a lot. Will Trai finally find its true calling and deliver this time around with its Calling Name Presentation (CNAP) service? Or will it be another round of Trai and Trai till you succeed? Truecaller will be watching and listening with bated breath.
