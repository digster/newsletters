An imaginary conversation that never happened at Google

Rohin

April 2020. Mountain View, California. Senior Google executives are sniggering at the press release https://about.fb.com/news/2020/04/facebook-invests-in-jio/ put out by arch-rival Facebook, announcing its US$5.7 billion investment into Jio Platforms, a six-month-old entity owned by India’s largest private conglomerate, Reliance Industries. 

“Haha, are they really touting the fact that they’re now the largest minority investor in Jio Platforms?” says the senior Indian-born executive. “I mean, come on. They’re actually gonna pretend this was a typical venture-style investment? 

“It would appear so.”

“How many users do they have again in India?”

“Nearly 300 million for Facebook alone.”

*whistles*

“They have even more on WhatsApp. 400 million. And another 160-odd million on Instagram.”

May 2020

“I don’t believe it! Jio has raised over $10 billion in just over a month! Did nobody tell them there’s a freaking pandemic around??”

“They’re fundraising over Zoom, apparently. And it’s Jio Platforms, not Jio.”

“Should we be worried?”

“No, I think we’re cool. I mean, Android alone practically powers India’s mobile sector. Last I checked, we had a 91% market share.”

“Good.”

June 2020

“Boss, it’s time. You need to take a call.”

“Yeah, I know. Make the draft..”

“The draft press release announcing our investment?”

“What else?”

“What do I say in it?”

“What did Facebook say in its release?”

“That its ‘a new collaboration to accelerate India’s digital transformation.’”

“Makes sense, hmmm. Let’s stick close to that. How about ‘investing in India’s digital future?’”

“I guess.”

“What else did Facebook say? What were their *makes air quotes* ‘key messages’?”

“Ummm…India’s explosive internet growth…businesses coming online…digital economy…and that they absolutely love how special India is for them.”

“Okay. Let’s say all of that, but add more stuff about our products. I mean, I get it, Facebook is a three-horse show. But we aren’t! Let’s talk local languages, payments, AI, internet access…get that list we made before my last visit.”

“Dunzo!”

July 2020

“Did you hear? Intel and Qualcomm are investing too. The announcements are going to hit any day.”

“Yeah, I heard. 15th is the Reliance annual general meeting. The news is gonna drop then. But I wanna get ahead of it.”

“How?”

“Get the draft out. Tell me, how much are we spending each year in India? I mean, legit expenses.”

“Yeah, I get it. In 2018 we *makes air quotes* ‘spent’ about US$1.2 billion. But last year that dropped to around US$400 million. https://entrackr.com/2019/10/despite-55-decline-in-revenue-google-india-profit-rose-by-16-in-fy19/”

“This was because we started moving revenues to Singapore, right?”

“Right. And the new accounting rules.”

“So, over the next five years, we might spend, what, US$4-5 billion in India?”

“Easily.”

“Let’s add the Jio investment to it. And call it the ‘Google for India Digitization Fund.’”

“And let’s announce it a couple of days ahead of Reliance’s shareholder meeting.”

“So, we’re gonna do two press releases?”

“Yeah. If we’re gonna invest US$4.5 billion in Jio, we might as well own the narrative.”

An edtech to end all edtechs​

Olina

A slight madness has descended on the Indian edtech space. A funding and acquisition fuge, brought on by Covid-19. As one investor told me in early April, “it’s the best time to pick up cheap equity.”

“Byju’s is in advanced stages of talks to acquire Doubtnut, a two-year-old education learning app https://techcrunch.com/2020/06/26/indian-edtech-giant-byjus-in-talks-to-acquire-doubtnut-for-more-than-125m/”

“Vedantu invests $2 million in doubt solving app Instasolv https://www.livemint.com/companies/start-ups/vedantu-invests-2-million-in-doubt-solving-app-instasolv-11592916355320.html”

“Unacademy acquires PrepLadder for $50 million https://www.livemint.com/companies/start-ups/unacademy-acquires-prepladder-for-50-million-11594105908291.html”

“Unacademy picks up majority stake in Mastree for $5 million https://www.bloombergquint.com/business/startup-news-unacademy-picks-up-51-stake-in-mastree-for-dollar5-million#:~:text=Edutech%20platform%20Unacademy%20has%20picked,the%20K%2D12%20learning%20space.”

“Byju's raises funding from BOND, valuation rises to $10.5 bn https://www.livemint.com/companies/start-ups/byju-s-raises-funding-from-mary-meeker-s-vc-firm-valuation-rises-to-10-5-bn-11593162078797.html”

Notice anything? The three biggest edtechs in India—by valuation and money raised—are locked in a battle for supremacy. Each, through acquisitions, mergers and giant punts, wants to be the “Everything Store” of edtech. This means stacking up K12, test-prep, K-3, coding, STEM, one-to-one classes….the list is endless. But the recent activity in the edtech space will show that the B-V-U triumvirate is going after each slice with methodical madness. 

That was until yesterday.

On Wednesday, we watched, mouths agape, Reliance Industries’ ambitious scope of digital products. The one that stood out most to me was a Jio Glass.
