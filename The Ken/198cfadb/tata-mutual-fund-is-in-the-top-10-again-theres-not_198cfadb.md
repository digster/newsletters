Good Morning Ishan,
Imagine being mired in quicksand, trying desperately to get to safety for what feels like ages. Then, when you do succeed and begin to count your blessings, you realise you’re actually standing on unstable ground.
Tata Mutual Fund, founded in the mid-1990s and relegated to obscurity for years, has finally found its way back into India’s top 10 mutual-fund firms by assets under management.
Seven years under a new CEO and after a string of exits, some things have clearly gone right for Tata Mutual Fund.
But with nearly Rs 2.16 lakh crore in assets, the fund house is Lilliputian next to rivals that are as old. SBI Mutual Fund, the market leader, is five times as large.
That’s not even the real problem, though, if you ask Anand, the author of today’s story. Its feisty competitors have put their fund managers front and centre, or talked up their teams. Tata Mutual Fund has done neither, according to an industry expert. Also, its mix of assets hurts where it really matters.
