Private equity and Indian pharma are old buddies with storied alliances.

When Henry Kravis, co-founder of the private equity giant KKR, said https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/industry/banking/finance/kkr-could-deploy-20-billion-in-india-over-the-next-decade-says-founder-henry-kravis/articleshow/128393218.cms?from=mdr last week that his firm could deploy as much as $20 billion over the next decade, its latest successful exit must have been fresh on his mind. 

In January, Torrent Pharmaceuticals completed its buyout of a 46.4% stake in JB Chemicals from KKR for nearly Rs 12,000 crore. The PE firm made over 3.5X returns from investing in what was then a staid contract development and manufacturing (CDMO) business, a little over five years ago. Today, thanks to geopolitical gymnastics, this is a hunting ground for PE firms looking to deploy large sums in a relatively safe drug manufacturing base. (We wrote about this growing PE interest when Anthem Bio made a stellar stock market debut https://the-ken.com/story/anthem-bios-rs-3395-crore-ipo-is-riding-the-crazy-run-that-cohance-started/ last July.) 

The December quarter results show that Torrent has acquired an efficient business from KKR. At Rs 1,065 crore in revenue, the rate of growth has fallen over the last two quarters, but the operating profit is trending upward. Torrent itself reported nearly 18% revenue growth to Rs 3,251 crore in the same quarter, with net profit growing even better, by over 26% to Rs 635 crore. 

With this acquisition, Torrent has vaulted to become the number three pharma company by market cap. Since the deal announcement in June, its share price has appreciated 34%, significantly outperforming the Nifty Pharma index’s ~8%.

As generics continue to face pricing pressure in most markets, the Indian stock market is looking for a differentiated play. And Torrent’s generics-only story has a new chapter—JB’s CDMO business. 

“There’s certainly a scarcity premium attached to somebody doing a differentiated business today…The other obvious aspect is that there is so much public money now available in equity markets that people are willing to take risks [by ascribing high valuation],” said the chief executive of a mid-size generics pharma company in Bengaluru...
