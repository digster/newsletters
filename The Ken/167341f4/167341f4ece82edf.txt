Good Morning Ishan,

It’s a story that’s almost unbelievable. India, a nation of 1.3 billion people woefully underserved with healthcare, is on the verge of an exodus of its best hospital chains out of the country. Even as one out of every four Indian deaths happens due to lack of medical attention, its best run hospitals are deciding to focus their efforts on expanding in Africa, the Middle East, East Asia, Bangladesh, and even the Cayman Islands.

It’s like fate conjured up a perfectly sequenced series of Black Swan events, each one delivering a body blow to an innovative, world-class sector. 

Demonetisation followed by the introduction of GST. The shift from direct cash payments by patients to insurance settlements, including by the government. Many of the latter payments take months to materialise.

The dwindling of the medical tourism market. Aggressive price capping by the government and regulators.

Ruhi brings a deeply-reported but disturbing story of India’s world-class hospital chains who are deciding against expanding further in India, and instead, looking abroad.
