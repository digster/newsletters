Welcome to the 2025 edition of The Ken’s annual gifting guide, created 100% through subscribers (including The Ken’s staff, who are all subscribers too).

This year’s guide is based on two different sets of subscriber recommendations—the Coffee Survey https://the-ken.com/newsletter/first-principles/21-grams-the-first-principles-coffee-survey/ (31 August, 314 responses) and the Gifting Survey https://the-ken.com/newsletter/first-principles/why-we-gift-and-what-we-gift/ (28 September, 255 responses).

There are no ads, no sponsors, no affiliate links. There are also no links to Amazon, Flipkart, or other major e-commerce platforms. In some cases, we couldn’t find local retailers who offer the item, hence some dollar prices.

The text inside quotes under each recommendation comes from subscribers. The Ken’s comments follow those in most cases. To keep things simple, we’ve combined multiple recommendations into one in many places (for example, automatic wrist watches, semi-automatic espresso machines). 

There are a few new categories we’ve added this year—“Learning & Knowledge”, “Causes”, “Coffee”, “Pets”, “Personal Gear” and “Apple” (what can we say? A lot of subscribers love to gift and receive Apple products).

We’ve made it easier to see last year’s list by simply clicking a button. You can also upvote individual items.

We know we’re a bit late this year. But we’re hoping to make this a list that gets updated throughout the year from now on. 

Lastly, remember there is no right list or wrong list. You may disagree with some of the recommendations, or you may be angry at some of the missing ones. Help us make it better by telling us, so that we can make this list into our list.

A new chapter for FP Books 

Hi Ishan, Tanim here again.

Last week, I promised you there’s something new that’s cooking for the books section. Well, I’m thrilled to announce a whole new revamp starting next week!

A blend of the old and the new—designed to cover more ground and involve you, the reader, far more than we did earlier.

I’ll begin with one of my favourite quotes from Maya Angelou: 

“Lift up your faces, you have a piercing need

For this bright morning dawning for you

The horizon leans forward

Offering you space to place new steps of change.”

I’ve always seen this as a call to embrace new beginnings. Personally, I’ve realised one area where I need a new start is my reading. For too long, my consistency has wavered—I’ve found myself reading more about books than reading the books themselves. 

To answer that “piercing need” for change, I’ve decided to get more intentional and set up a reading list. A simple tool that provides some structure, while still allowing the freedom to explore a range of diverse topics. 

If you, too, are looking to bring more structure to your reading, we invite you to join us. 

The process is simple: each month, just pick a book that resonates with the theme of each upcoming edition we’ve listed below, read it, and share your thoughts with our community here https://theken.typeform.com/FPBooks?typeform-source=the-ken.com. (When you enter the book title, please mention the theme in brackets!) Or just write to fp@the-ken.com mailto:fp@the-ken.com. 

16 November – Mythology 💫

14 December – Christmas 🎄

11 January – Reinventing yourself 🤸

8 February – Numbers in the title 🔢

8 March – Gender 🌈

5 April – Money 💰

But that’s not all we have in store for you. 

The world of books and reading always brings up fascinating questions. Every month, we will tackle one of these questions and share diverse perspectives from our reader community. Better yet, if you have a reading- or book-related question of your own, we would love to hear it. Please mail them to us at fp@the-ken.com mailto:fp@the-ken.com. 

Finally, we are thrilled to introduce “The FP Monthly Pick”. A single, carefully chosen book we believe is truly worth your time. In a world of endless choices, consider this our curated recommendation for reads that promise to be insightful, compelling, and fun. 

Your recommendations, however, will continue to be the heart of this community. We welcome your everyday discoveries and will feature the best of them each month. To help our community choose wisely, though, we ask that you share in detail why a book is worth our readers’ precious time. After all, the best way to champion a book is to share your personal experience with it—what captivated you about it and why it resonated. You can submit those recommendations here https://theken.typeform.com/FPBooks?typeform-source=the-ken.com. 

Let’s build a more intentional reading life, one month and one book at a time—together!

Flight and freedom

Hello Ishan, welcome to another edition of First Principles Silent Sunday. It’s Jonathan here, and I wish you all a very happy, colourful, and joy-filled Deepavali! 

We have two pictures from the avian world today that I absolutely loved, so that’s how we’re going to kick off this week’s Sunday album. You can submit your own photos for the FP photos section using this link https://theken.typeform.com/fpsilentsundays. 

From Chet Kamat, of his home garden in Nandi Hills, near Bengaluru: 

“For a month in August this year, I kept watch over a Tickell’s Blue Flycatcher nesting in the hollow of a teak root bar stool in my outdoor bar in our garden. The wait was long, but the vigil was rewarded—with strong new chicks and unforgettable moments. One morning, the nest could no longer contain them. The hatchlings spilled out—onto a sun-warmed boulder—hesitant at first, but quickly curious. Their wings were unsteady, their balance unsure, yet their eyes were already lit with discovery. All the while, the mother stayed close, proud and protective, still slipping food into eager beaks. 

From bar stool hollow to open garden, it was a passage in miniature: the shift from safety to self, captured in frames I’ll always treasure.”

Nandi Hills Home (Chet Kamat)
