Good Morning Ishan,
 
My colleague Roshni always writes fascinating stories about topics that initially make me sceptical. A few months back it was succulents. Today it’s about the rise of Korean beauty products in India. 
 
Just like last time, before writing this email, I asked her what the big fuss was about. 
 
“Basically, Korean beauty products have a different philosophy. Unlike other products, it works on perfecting your skin instead of curtaining blemishes with makeup. Think acne-prevention, anti-ageing, reversing UV damage and, yes, even skin-whitening.”
 
But Roshni, I asked. Isn't this another artificial capitalist construct to sell us something we didn’t even know we needed? And everyone is buying because everybody else is. Isn't this Amazon Echo all over again?  
 
“No, PGK. It’s very interesting. They have a ten step skincare program. They use all kinds of ingredients: Pomegranate. Egg white. Coffee. Goji berry. Gold. Platinum. Volcanic ash. Seaweed. Snail mucin...”
 
Wait. Did you say snail mucin?
 
“Yes. It’s a core ingredient. What do you use to exfoliate your skin?”
 
I don’t know. Lifebuoy soap?
 
“K-beauty is becoming big in India. They are top-sellers on massive websites like Nykaa. In fact, skincare sales on the website went up by 15% the year it was launched on their platform. It’s getting very popular.”
 
Yes, but many Indians don’t know how to use the internet, Roshni. Just see how many people congratulate others on their work anniversary on LinkedIn... 
 
“You know what’s the most fascinating part of my story? Sheet masks.”
 
That’s revolting, Roshni. 
 
“S-H-E-E-T masks. They are these white sheet fabrics, soaked in serums or essences and meant to be left on the face for 15-30 minutes. They are available from Rs 90 to Rs 6,000 a pop.”
 
Really?
 
“Yes. And you know how K-beauty got this popular in India?”
 
No... 
 
“On black market WhatsApp groups. From a corner in North-East India.”
 
What?
 
“Yes. Just read my story.” 
 
I did. I learnt a lot. My mind was blown.
