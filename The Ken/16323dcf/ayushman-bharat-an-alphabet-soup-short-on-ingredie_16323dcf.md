While I am as excited as anybody else about Modicare, I can’t wrap my head around the alphabet soup of acronyms that seems to be coming out of Delhi every other day. (My colleague Durga calls it a North Indian classroom of Hindu kids – Ayushman, Disha, Rohini, …)
Anyhow, Wednesday was celebrated as Ayushman Bharat Diwas, whatever that means. We, at The Ken, are glad that we have this story today because we’ve been trying to understand for some time how exactly this grand scheme is going to be implemented. And the government doesn’t have time, officials in Delhi agree.
The think tank Niti Aayog is the nerve centre, and virtually everyone in the healthcare industry is queuing up before it to get a piece of the $1.54 billion pie. Medtronic to Abbott to Microsoft, startups to insurers, World Bank to WHO, hospitals to the Big Four auditors.
Often their conversation goes something like this: “We ask them ‘How do you plan to execute this scheme?’. The reply is: ‘We have no idea, just yet. Why don’t you help us out?’”
Ironically, the government has stalled the process of building the largest health data hub, the very platform that it needs in order to monitor the flow of money in Modicare. Sources tell Ruhi that the government wants to outsource it to someone else, in spite of having closed the tender and identified a builder earlier. The most likely candidate is the most-funded health tech startup in India, Practo.
There’s another name floating around, Health Stack, from the stable of India Stack which built the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) and wishes to be a repository of technologies atop Aadhaar. But what is Health Stack? Everyone seems to have heard of it but no one seems to know what it is. Reminds me of Kevin Spacey’s character in the movie The Usual Suspects—Keyser Söze.
Have a good day.
