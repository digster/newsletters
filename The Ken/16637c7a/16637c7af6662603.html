Last week, India’s Supreme Court held as “unconstitutional” the use of Aadhaar by private companies. It’s a body blow to many sectors where companies have been using Aadhaar-based eKYC (electronic Know Your Customer), but it’s particularly bleak for India Stack.  
 
On the face of it, India Stack is a set of APIs whose architecture is designed by think tank iSpirt and other private companies and then given to different government entities to run. But over a short span, this architecture, neither government-owned nor government-run, has become mandatory for India’s digital economy.
 
Now, based on multiple conversations with lawyers, policy experts and entrepreneurs, it’s clear that all parts of the Stack that touch Aadhaar are now endangered—in payments, smart cities, health, storage, drone and many others.
 
Many sources believe that the government will bring in an enabling legislation that’d allow private companies to be able to access Aadhaar data. But lawyers say that’s “a blunder and a wishful reading of the judgement”.
 
Then what is the correct reading? How will it impact businesses, especially VC-funded startups as they stare at legal and regulatory risks if they misread the constitutional judgement of the apex court?
 
Rohin’s story today lays down the ramifications. Even if the government does manage to bring in legislation to allow the use of Aadhaar, its scope will be severely curtailed.
 
If Aadhaar was India Stack’s version of a digital passport, then many businesses face the imminent threat of deportation. On to the story here; in the spirit of our anniversary, it's free: https://the-ken.com/story/aadhaar-and-the-gradual-collapse-of-india-stack/
