Good Morning Ishan,

“This university is different from other universities in one critical way. We only pray to one god. The employer.”

How’s that description for a university?

Catchy, no?

That’s Manish Sabharwal, co-founder of TeamLease Services, India’s largest staffing company. Famous for pithy quotes and one-liners, especially on India’s broken education system that shuffles students from schools to colleges to a job market that they’re largely unprepared for. “Prepare, not repair,” is what he set out to do in 2013 by setting up India’s first skilling university.

In a country, where 63% of employers feel no job seekers meet the ‘required skills’ or 4 out 10 formally trained young Indians are unemployed, it’s obvious that a vast number of students entering the workforce every year need more skills than degrees. But India is a degree-obsessed country. Vocation is for the poor, education for the rich. And somehow that dichotomy has been hard to bridge.

TLSU is trying. For the 63 million formal and informal Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprise companies —the TLSU-anointed God—its graduates are the real devotees. The MSMEs employ nearly 100 million people. Also because from his perch in industry and policy-making,  Sabharwal has had a ringside view of the skills gap, scope and opportunity.

Does that mean he’s got it right? If yes, it’d mean a wage premium for the TLSU graduates. At 200,000 students, it’s the fastest growing university, one that has inspired many state governments to start their own skills universities.

Creating a brand new category of graduates confuses the rigid social hierarchies of degrees versus diplomas. It also begs the question: did TeamLease really need a university campus to do this? Does it run the risk of becoming yet another centralised facility? asks Olina in her story today.

A few months ago, when we met Sabharwal in Bengaluru, he boasted that TLSU, with theory-light but practice-heavy curriculum and pedagogy, is a university of the future. Olina travelled to Vadodara to check it out.
