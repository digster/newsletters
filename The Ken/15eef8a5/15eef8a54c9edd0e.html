“It doesn’t rain on the moon, so the rovers and landers will not have to worry about it,” said one of the Google Lunar XPRIZE judges yesterday in Bangalore as heavy rains lashed the asbestos rooftop of TeamIndus’ mission control center.
 
It may not rain on the lunar surface, but space is a pretty demanding environment.
 
Yesterday TeamIndus crossed the first step towards the $30-million Prize by demonstrating the ‘readiness’ of its spacecraft and payload to the visiting GLXP judges. There are nine more steps to go before it gets the green light to launch its spacecraft using Isro’s rocket.
 
Then there’s getting the launch windows right. Because March is also when India will be launching its own moon mission, Chandrayaan-2, from the same Isro launch facility, Sriharikota. It’s not known if Isro’s deep space network would support two lunar missions simultaneously.
 
Getting all of those steps is only half of the challenge for TeamIndus. The other is raising an additional $30-40 million to fund itself, most of it via a Series B round that its currently in the middle of, and some via direct public contributions.
 
You might remember our stories from late July when we asked if TeamIndus still had a chance of meeting its prize deadline, 28 December 2017. Within days of our story, GLXP extended the deadline to March 2018.
 
Yesterday's review could be a deciding factor in TeamIndus' fund raise. Sources say two big Indian conglomerates, the Tatas and Wipro, are waiting in the wings to fund it.
