Just yesterday, The Ken put up a post you don't see too often these days—we announced the hiring of two new reporters. We had a similar post last week as well. Meanwhile, we're still hiring for our already amazing Southeast Asia team.
At the moment, hirings, especially in the Indian media, are a rarity. Mass layoffs, on the other hand, are all too common. From digital portals to old-school newspapers, each day brings news of more journalists left jobless.
The media isn't alone, of course. Be it startups or traditional businesses, the economic downturn caused by the pandemic has led companies to shed jobs to stay afloat. A fifth of India's salaried jobs—nearly 19 million of them—have been wiped out since April.
There's similar bad news on the Southeast Asian front. A report estimated that 25 million jobs would be lost across the region's six major economies.
It would be tempting to say that, at the moment, there are no jobs. For recruitment firms, this should be a sucker punch. Their entire business is based on filling available positions.
But according to Shreedhar's story today, some recruiters are a happy lot. One firm in today's story claims 2020 will be its best-ever year. Another says it saw more users than ever before. Even their commissions are shooting up.
