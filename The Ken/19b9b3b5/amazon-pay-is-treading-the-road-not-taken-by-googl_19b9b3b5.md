A weekly newsletter about how finance is getting supercharged by tech in India, and how you can make money work for you.
Good Morning Ishan,
This week, Amazon Pay, the 10th-largest digital payments app, said it will allow its users to open digital fixed deposit accounts offered by some banks and non-banks.
It said users can open fixed deposits (FD) starting from Rs 1,000 on Amazon Pay, with its partner institutions offering interest rates up to 8% per annum. All partners provide an additional 0.5% interest for senior citizens, with Shriram Finance, which is one of its partners, offering up to 0.5% additional interest for women investors.
| Amazon Pay FD: An overview |
The fintech Stable Money had confidently rolled this product out in 2022. Then other digital platforms such as Super.money, Tata Neu, Wint Wealth, and Grip Invest followed suit.
In fact, last year, the Flipkart-backed Super.money, which has [grown](https://the-ken.com/story/can-kalyan-krishnamurthys-ideas-man-build-another-phonepe-for-flipkart/) to become the fifth-largest UPI app, said that on day one of the product launch, an FD was opened every two minutes.
The main draw for customers to open digital FDs with these platforms is that it removes the need to open a savings bank account with the bank before opening an FD with the same bank.
The bigger draw is the interest rate. Most platforms partner with small finance banks like Utkarsh, Slice, and Shivalik for this product since they offer higher interest rates on FDs than other banks. For these banks that have partnered with these platforms, the response has been encouraging. A small finance bank executive said about 5% of deposits for their bank come from digital FDs. And this is without going aggressive.
This partnership also comes at a time when FDs have been looking for a second lease of life.
As per the latest RBI monthly bulletin, bank deposits held by households fell by about 9% to Rs 12.54 lakh crore in FY25, reversing the growth seen over the previous two years, The Hindu Businessline[ reported](https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/data-stories/data-focus/households-reduce-investments-in-fixed-deposits-insurance-and-small-savings/article70459727.ece). This is reflected in the nature of household savings too. The share of bank deposits in overall financial savings fell from 40.9 per cent in FY21 to 35.2 per cent in FY25.
Amazon Pay launching this feature must give Google Pay a fair bit of heartburn, especially since the second-largest payments app launched digital FDs as an industry first in 2021, only to roll it back soon after. Google Pay had partnered with Equitas Small Finance Bank and bank infrastructure provider Setu for this.
But the RBI had questions about the partnership. The launch had come at a time when the regulator, in its 2021–22 annual report, said it had concerns that Big Tech’s entry into banking and financial services could bring systemic risks. The regulator had then [looked](https://www.ndtvprofit.com/business/equitas-google-pay-partnership-for-deposits-fizzles-out-as-rbi-raises-questions) into the partnership to assess if it would allow Google a backdoor entry into banking.
Those in Google Pay may be stumped by this development. Sources told The Ken that Google Pay did what Amazon Pay is now doing. It left all the heavy lifting of KYC, disbursal, etc., to the bank and was simply a source to generate leads.
In any case, it also doesn’t seem like any of these entities, banks or platforms, explicitly asked the RBI for its approval to roll out this feature, since it appears to be a rather straightforward product.
In a 2023 interview with The Ken, Stable Money co-founder Saurabh Jain [said](https://the-ken.com/story/this-fintech-aims-to-sail-where-google-pay-sank/) as much:
As of now, there is nothing specific by the RBI [on] allowing or disallowing the distribution of bank FDs by fintech platforms. There are regulations on how the FDs should be sold, the KYC norms, etc., and we are following them.
But the fact that the largest payments app, Phonepe, which distributes multiple financial products, has still not launched this feature means that the dust might not have fully settled.
We will know how the RBI really feels about it only when this feature grows really big. Or if Google Pay launches it again.
