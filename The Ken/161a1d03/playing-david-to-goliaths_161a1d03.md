Good Morning Ishan,
Oh, what a roller-coaster of a week. We pretty much lined up all the Goliaths and played David, writing pieces (and not hurling stones as the original David did) prodding, probing, questioning, and even commending, their contribution.
There was Google. Amazon. WhatsApp. Matrimony. In that very order. We ended the week on a forward-looking note—a much-needed breather from all the giant-critiquing.
On Monday, there was [Venkat and Shashidhar's](https://the-ken.com/story/google-cci/) piece on the Competition Commission of India (CCI) giving Google a rap on its knuckles. As we see it, CCI's order fining Google for the abuse of its dominant position in search could just be the start of many more regulations to follow.
Day 2 had our [contributor Amish](https://the-ken.com/story/amazon-set-occupy-indian-publishing-throne/) talk about Amazon's place in Indian publishing. Safe to say, it's making its way to the throne. Amazon is dominating the market in sales, has bettered its distribution, and has Kindle on its team. Can anything go wrong? Read to know.
Midweek, [Arundhati spent](https://the-ken.com/story/facebook-tests-waters-walled-payments-garden-india/) a lot of time on WhatsApp's new payments feature. It's a joy to use, she says. But it seems rather opposed to interoperability, a central feature of UPI. And the otherwise strict NPCI seems, well, pretty chill about it. It's a free story; read it!
Thursday saw [Shashidhar's solo debut](https://the-ken.com/story/matrimony-indian-wedding/)—a piece on Matrimony.com. The matchmaking company has decided to go all out and offer a bunch of other services as well. As Shashi so eloquently puts it, "It starts once you are matched. It ends once you are exhausted."
The week ended with [Seema's story](https://the-ken.com/story/ion-energys-race-build-universal-battery-pack/) on batteries. That's right. Mumbai-based ION Energy may have a universal solution to batteries—one size fits all! But how far and how soon can it change user and industry behaviour? Seema asks.
Do read. Do share. Do subscribe. You know the drill by now.
Have a great weekend! See you next week.
