Chinese telecom equipment giant Huawei is the centrepiece of the US-China trade war that is, in reality, the new Cold War. The US believes that Huawei’s telecom equipment cannot be trusted because the company has deep links to the Chinese communist party and army. And that any country that uses Huawei’s equipment extensively risks having its critical infrastructure compromised because, well, everything runs online today.
Last month [Seema reported](http://link.the-ken.com/wf/click?upn=HQSq6eeRV1xAVaGuFOahuNCZVVXPeP-2FJpSqxhvIUDrmXzHfpZnmI03-2BUU5QyNMDkNR651A4uw12SoUwjr8bb9xp8pALK4TH9UIFHcI4B4l-2B-2BhyGgZNx5iIbX1aUpIiYJ4VZw14o-2BGwylyzhU4tQDew-3D-3D_S4jLOsNx-2Fv6Jev2nvow8b7P9pBCWGd8KNZfb200gkZLQNlXQHio0noZzXtsWoloZmPWeyZsh7a4QCV9ufwCUKi00jlBT0uEdvwYDMuVuqcN7F8iiTN4jQGN4vA7s0pDPJKf2O1dvIPpUNTxLNTfgm7-2BtQTk6aGV0pSFZuCaKHaRBBCcWgH6eufPon5Xi4FWJrcnQxZcOR5wG5hfKTMqfOFq3-2BwXifG4rVh9yy6Lq2bgxZXPXydAmq4-2FYkAHlTOkuAK7zynzrDvD2kZeqrOgC30T4r-2BxNKI-2BoxqapNpF2ue-2FA1GoroObWHYTgXjtc-2FQjR-2Bv8yTZ-2FtELNJ-2FQgBZubRyuQFC4BIPRw-2BRQ5pq4Ly-2BmzCwtyufKIVea3DOvzvsS-2BzUhMbfBIyVQuZHj38tYdZN7eiXSEAW-2FZuS8tRwzSrWEk-3D) on how India is one of the most critical markets for Huawei and its peers. Because it has over 1.1 billion telecom subscribers, $100 billion worth of impending 5G rollouts over a 5-7 year period, and most importantly, imports close to 97% of its telecom equipment (that alone was worth $21 billion last year).
And while India has, up till now, resisted US pressures to ban Huawei from 5G rollouts, what exactly is it doing to protect itself from any risks of compromised equipment? A senior official from CDOT (Centre for Development of Telematics), an R&D organisation under the Department of Telecom, and two other officers from DoT-promoted bodies confirmed that vulnerabilities have been discovered in India’s telecom hardware.
The centrepiece of India's strategy was a 1 April 2019 deadline to introduce a woefully belated mandatory testing and certification program for all telecom equipment, right down to mobile phones. It will miss it.
For reasons that range from intense lobbying by equipment makers and their telco customers; gross understaffing of the labs that are meant to do the testing; a lack of security standards to hold equipment makers up to; and an abysmal lack of coordination between various regulatory bodies.
In today’s story, Vandana lists down the litany of interconnected woes that stare India’s new government (it will have one by 23 May) when it comes to solving India’s telecom imports problem. Just last August its apex telecom regulator brought out a paper aiming to bring down 97% telecom imports to net-zero by 2022. That goal, too, will be missed. It's free.
