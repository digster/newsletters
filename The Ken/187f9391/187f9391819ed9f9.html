Good Morning Ishan,
 
Physics Wallah, or PW, is one of the rare edtechs that is profitable. It is expecting its revenues to grow manifold in the times to come. However, it is changing its DNA in this stage of hyper-growth.
 
From focusing solely on NEET-JEE, it is now looking to expand its universe. It has established Vidyapeeth—offline centres with 650 tech-integrated classrooms. It is entering other test-prep segments—from banking to defence to even the civil services. At a time when other edtechs are actively firing people, it is hiring. Aggressively.
 
Over the past year, it has acquired test-prep platforms such as Preponline and NCERT-based-book publisher Altis Vortex. It has formed a joint venture with Utkarsh Classes & Edutech Pvt. Ltd, one of the leading edtechs in the civil-services-and-government-exam-prep market.
 
Founded as a Youtube channel, it now has millions of subscribers and 40 different channels.
 
But beneath this story of glory, things are falling apart internally for Alakh Pandey-led edtech. There is mutiny in the ranks, teachers are quitting and posting their ordeal on social media, and founders are being forced to firefight to assuage concerns that all is not well with their organisation.
 
In today’s story, Alifiya speaks to the PW founders and trawls through Youtube videos to find the real story behind the tales of glory. Read it here: https://the-ken.com/story/indias-youngest-edtech-unicorn-physics-wallah-is-making-an-audacious-gamble/ (10-minute read)
