Good Morning Ishan,

Mid-week is here. And so am I. With beer. 

Now, I am a beer person. And IMHO, right now is the best time to be alive in this country. Minus, your politics. 

Not kidding. The Kingfisher days are well settled in London; like all despicable things of the past are wont to be, once you’ve made peace with them. And the present and the future beckons with intoxication. Just look at what’s on offer, almost everything in the last three years. There’s Witlinger. Then, there’s Bira, of course. There’s White Rhino from New Delhi. There’s White Owl from Mumbai. And now comes, well, Simba. From Chhattisgarh.

In under two years, Simba has grown from Chhattisgarh to Assam to Goa to last month, Delhi and Gurugram. What Simba is—also known as Sona Beverages—and what it does and how it got here is everything you’ll read in today’s story. Written by my colleague Harveen. 

You will also read a lot more. About the beer business. Because these are certainly very interesting times. More and more companies want to enter the bottled craft beer business. There’s news that United Breweries, India’s largest beer maker, which makes beers like Kingfisher Strong, Kingfisher and Heineken is also exploring a craft beer. (The definition of craft beer is a technicality, but still.) “In the past few months, 21 home-grown brands have approached us to retail their beer. The consumer is evolving and there is certainly a demand, but we hope it does not become overkill.” This is from Rahul Singh, the founder and chief executive officer of beer chain The Beer Cafe, which has 35 outlets across 12 cities in the country.

I am with Rahul. Except, I am not complaining. 

I would if I had to make a business out of it and make money. For myself and my investors. 

That’s our story for today.
