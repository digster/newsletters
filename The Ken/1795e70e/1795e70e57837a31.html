Good Morning Ishan,
 
The first time I saw them, they were quite surreal. Adverts in Bengaluru for a service that wasn’t even available in India.
 
Why was Indonesian ride-hailing service Gojek hiring in India? On the face of it, it was baffling.
 
It turned out to be a heck of a growth hack. 
 
Gojek looked to India for developers, which are hard to find in numbers in Southeast Asia. But hiring was just half of it. It made at least three acquisitions that we know of on Indian soil to build a world-class engineering team.
 
That team helped Gojek weather the competition of Uber and Grab, and it grew Gojek to a valuation of US$10 billion and a regional business covering five countries. 
 
None of this would have been possible without its unprecedented move to develop engineering chops in India.
 
In today’s fascinating story, Pranav looks at how Gojek meshed these acquisitions successfully—often a tricky business—and built a thriving culture for a business that served customers located over 8,000 km away.
 
But—and you knew this was coming—times have changed…
 
Gojek has lost key senior executives in India, including its CTO Ajey Gore. And the immediate future of its India-based team remains unclear as Gojek puts the finishing touches to a merger with fellow Indonesian digital giant Tokopedia. 
 
Will the strategy change? What comes next?
