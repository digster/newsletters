Good Morning Ishan,
 
Billionaire Mukesh Ambani is particularly good at yoking his company Reliance Industries' interests to India's. 
 
At the company's annual general meeting in July, he dedicated its telecom arm Reliance Jio's efforts to build a 5G network from scratch to Prime Minister Narendra Modi's calls for a self-reliant India. He said the network was being developed "using 100% homegrown technologies and solutions". 
 
Sunil Mittal, Ambani's arch-rival in telecom, does not have that luxury. For one, Mittal's Bharti Airtel–India's second-largest telco, after Jio–does not have access to oodles of money like Jio. Still, Mittal is making the best of the hand he has been dealt. 
 
By choosing a contrarian approach to Ambani's. 
 
Instead of building every part of its 5G network, Bharti is using open-source telecom architecture to source components from different vendors and then get a systems integrator to bring them together into a high-quality white-label product. This will help reduce its dependence on its traditional European suppliers Nokia and Ericsson.
 
The difference in scale between the two telcos' goals is illustrated by the fact Bharti has less than 100 engineers at work on its 5G project, while Jio has 600-1,000. And even if Bharti's game plan is cheaper than Reliance's, it will still require significant investments. And neither company's 5G solution is anywhere close to deployment. Even once it's done, maintenance will be a nightmare as it involves coordinating between multiple vendors.
