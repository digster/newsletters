Over the weekend I told you we see a clear and long-term opportunity to build The Ken into an Asian equal, one day, to respected publications like The Wall Street Journal (founded in 1889) and Financial Times (founded in 1888). One that reports from and writes for Asia first and foremost. One that can be around 100 years from now.
With your support, The Ken has gone in four years from one country, one daily story, and a few hundred subscribers to six countries, multiple subscription products, and over 25,000 subscribers.
Our subscribers have funded our existence, validated our beliefs and fueled our ambitions. But most importantly, shaped our journalism, whether it be the sectors we cover; the questions we ask; or the way we write our stories.
And yet, we don’t really know who most of our subscribers are, because we don’t like profiling them in order to sell their attention and intention to advertisers. We don’t even know the most basic demographic details about them like their age, gender or interests.
But we still know that we’ve got a problem. A bubble problem.
In spite of being a business publication and a subscription one at that, we’ve managed quite well to find subscribers who found our journalism valuable enough to pay for it.
We did this without ever having to spend money on marketing because our first subscribers told their friends, who told their colleagues, who told their college mates, who told their friends and so on.
While this helped a fledgling business journalism startup like us to grow to over 25,000 subscribers, it also silently transmitted a set of characteristics across most of our subscribers.
They were most likely to be men. Having studied at India’s best known colleges. Working in or running companies in tech and finance. And living in cities like Bangalore, Delhi and Mumbai where most of the largest or most funded companies are.
How do we know this if we don’t track it? Because we’ve met, spoken, emailed and messaged with thousands of our subscribers over the last 4 years. We may despise secretly tracking our subscribers, but we absolutely love communicating directly with them.
This is our subscriber community. Help us grow it.
This is also our bubble. Help us break it.
