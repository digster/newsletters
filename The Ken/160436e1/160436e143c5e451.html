Good Morning Ishan,
 
I don’t know what else to call it. Perhaps you have a better word for it, but the way I see it, this is absolutely brazen on Indian pharmaceutical companies' part. Earlier last month, the US Food & Drugs Administration (FDA) issued a warning letter to Lupin after inspecting the company’s drug manufacturing facilities in Goa and Indore. In a nutshell, this is what the FDA said: “These repeated failures at multiple sites demonstrate that your company’s oversight and control over the manufacture of drugs is inadequate. You should immediately and comprehensively assess your company’s global manufacturing operations to ensure that systems and processes, and ultimately, the products manufactured, conform to FDA requirements at all your sites.”
 
Brazen. Because this continues to happen. Again and again. With a whole host of Indian pharma companies. 
 
Despite what the companies might tell its shareholders, the cost of getting a rap from the FDA is detrimental to business. Here, just a few questions must be asked. A. Why can’t the companies get their act together? How difficult can it be? B. Where are they failing? C. How can they fix this? Now, it is easy to feed into the stereotype that Indian businesses like to cut corners, and that doesn’t fly when you are building a global business. Maybe. But there has to be something more to it. So, what is it? That’s Ruhi’s piece for today.
