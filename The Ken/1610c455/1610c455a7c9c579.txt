Good Morning Ishan,

Memory is a funny thing. So, the moment Hike’s press release hit my inbox on Wednesday, I was like, no, no, no, something has changed...hang on, did you ask what’s Hike? Valued at $1 billion plus, it is India’s answer to WhatsApp, built by Kavin Bharti Mittal, the son of Sunil Bharti Mittal, founder of Airtel, one of India’s largest telecom companies. Right. Now, back to my memory story. So, I circled back to the earlier press releases. Here’s what I found:

Way back, in 2016, this is how Hike identified itself: “Hike is simplifying how people connect and interact with content and services on mobile. Hike believes that messaging will do for mobile what the browser did for the desktop times a 100.”

Now, in 2018: “Hike is building products with the aim of simplifying the internet to bring India online.”

Ah, the sweet sound of purposeful altruism.

Anyway, today’s piece is not about good intentions. It is about what Hike has launched. Something called Total. An app which will allow first-time smartphone users to “use the internet without data”. Hike has partnered with three telecom companies (Airtel, Aircel and BSNL) and two of the major low-end smartphone manufacturers (Karbonn and Intex) for Total’s rollout. The company said it has patented a technology on Universal Supplementary Service Data (USSD) to do this. It has also discovered that Re 1 sachet internet packs will get the next billion users in India online. 

In today’s piece, we’ve asked and answered just a few questions. Is this new? Is this useful? Is it innovative? Is it all PR bluster?
