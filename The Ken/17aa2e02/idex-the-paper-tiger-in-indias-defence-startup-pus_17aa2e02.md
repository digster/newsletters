With rapid advances in technologies such as counter-drone and underwater surveillance systems, India cannot solely rely on its stodgy defence contractors, including the state-owned Bharat Electronics Ltd (BEL) and Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd (HAL).
That was the idea behind Innovations for Defence Excellence (iDEX), a government initiative unveiled in 2018. Through iDEX and its offshoot, the Defence India Startup Challenge (DISC), the objective was to tap India's entrepreneurial vigour to meet its military challenges.
But the reality of iDEX is out of sync with its intent.
There have been 84 winners under DISC, but not one has a prototype yet. This is partly because only 10% of the $200,000 conditional grant given to a winner is disbursed upfront when even the entire amount makes little difference in such a capital-intensive business.
