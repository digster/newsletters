Good Morning Ishan,

Even after a considerable number of years in journalism, I've kept away from one subject—insurance. Health insurance in particular. I guess when you are young, you have other things to occupy your learning curve (and wallet). Over time, I got around it. But I’ll admit, it was tough to tell interesting stories in insurance.

Not anymore. E-commerce, fintech, digital health and perhaps every other new economy company wants to enter insurance. Traditional insurers are not batting eyelids to partner with the new entrants to distribute or create new products.

In this mix, you throw the mammoth government scheme to insure 500 million under Ayushman Bharat. The insurance pot is significantly stirred. Many insurers are not keen to join the government party, but third-party administrators (TPAs) still have an important role to play in administering claims, settlement, etc. And yet, their business is under a flux. Especially because the public TPA is all set to become the largest Indian TPA.

Ruhi gets to the heart of this through the story of a unique, diversified TPA—Medi Assist, which probably has the largest pool of patient data in India. (A goldmine you’d think given how everyone wants to get into OPD insurance.) Still, the largest private TPA, by a considerable distance, has knocked all investor doors, from Premji Invest to Ant Financial, to raise money or get acquired. Multiple sources have confirmed. And with each failed deal, the TPA has dropped its valuation.

Given the circumstances Medi Assist finds itself in, does it jump at the next offer on the table or does it hold out for what it thinks it’s worth? More importantly, what is a fair price for the beleaguered behemoth?
