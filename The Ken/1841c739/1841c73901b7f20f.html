Good Morning Ishan,
 
After years of trying and failing, when Manipal Hospitals finally broke the acquisition jinx, it went on a shopping spree. The group bought three healthcare companies last year and is close to sealing its fourth deal with the Emami Group for AMRI Hospitals. 
 
For all this, Manipal Health Enterprises Pvt. Ltd (MHEPL) would end up spending ~$500 million. That’s the price the group is paying for a hospital-chain stature that would impress public investors when the company goes to the markets next year. 
 
Three years ago, in an unusually candid interview, MHEPL chairman Ranjan Pai told me that he was not a fan of expensive acquisitions because “if you buy expensive, you will have to take the hit for somebody else”.
 
Still, looking at his acquisitions in the last two years, it’s evident he’s paid a pretty penny. For the two less expensive buys—Vikram and Columbia Asia hospitals—MHEPL paid premiums of over 100% of the net assets acquired. 
 
Even for AMRI, which is still losing money on its operations, MHEPL has gone to great lengths to facilitate the deal. In June, it passed resolutions to allot non-convertible debentures worth Rs 2,000 crore ($243 million) to refinance the loans availed by AMRI Hospitals, which interestingly owed ~Rs 1,350 crore ($164 million) to its promoters.
 
Details of the Manipal-AMRI deal are not public, but hints of its advanced stage are divulged all over the recent regulatory filings, says Gaurav, who has been poring over the data.
 
As the private-equity (PE) major KKR is reportedly looking to take a 48% stake in MHEPL, likely in a private round before the initial public offering (IPO), I am reminded of what Pai told me in 2019:
 
Look, I don’t have the luxury [of being reckless or irresponsible]. I’m very conscientious about my investors also making money. I’ve had a track record. I’m the only guy in the country, across all business groups, to have raised 16 rounds of private equity. With 14 exits.
 
It’s anybody’s guess why both PE firms and Pai would want to maintain that track record.
