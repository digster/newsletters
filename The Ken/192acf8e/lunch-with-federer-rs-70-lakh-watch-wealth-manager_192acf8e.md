Good Morning Ishan,
A 3:00 a.m. call to book the penthouse at a seven-star hotel in Dubai.
A seat at Berkshire Hathaway’s annual shareholder meeting to meet Warren Buffet.
Lunch with Roger Federer.
These are some of the demands made by India’s uber-rich. The cost is of no concern. It’s up to luxury lifestyle-management firms like Redberyl, Indulge Global, and Quintessentially to make them happen.
As affluent Indians seek products and experiences that are extravagant or exclusive (often both), wealth managers are partnering with these firms to differentiate themselves. It’s no longer enough to merely carry out investment activities on behalf of the well-heeled. To stand out, wealth-management firms such as True Beacon and ASK Wealth need to cater to the whims and fancies of their clients. Think of sourcing concert tickets to see Coldplay or Diljit Dosanjh or getting their hands on an ultra-rare Birkin bag.
India’s concierge market serves both individuals and companies and is estimated to be worth Rs 500 crore, or US$59 million. Its rise over the past few years mirrors an annual 15-20% increase in the assets under management in the wealth-management sector—and the fact that affluent clients have built closer relationships with those handling their money.
As concierge services gain traction, wealth managers that work with them now have a way to edge out the competition. After all, for their clients, what’s the point of making immense amounts of money if you can’t spend it with style?
