Here in India, Diwali and the long holidays continue. But hey, we promised you a story on Friday, so here we go.
Now, I have started seeing them more often. At traffic signals around 8:30 AM, jostling. On flyovers, huffing and puffing, a large bag on their shoulders. Sometimes, they raise one hand in the air to suggest they are about to do something and the world must take notice. Of course, I’m talking about cyclists; those going to work.
I’m bringing this up because last week, Zoomcar, the car rental company in India said it is getting into the cycle sharing business. It is calling it PEDL.
Within a year, Zoom-car will put out 10, 000 cycles in Bengaluru, Chennai and Kolkata. The company’s rationale is that for trips less than 5 km, cycling is the best mode of transport. So when given the opportunity, at a small price, thousands of people will cycle.
To me, this idea has all the trappings of, let’s try something different, just because we can.
My colleague Patanjali looked into Zoomcar’s plans, the rationale and how far this idea can travel.
