Good Morning Ishan,
A regulatory slap from the banking regulator earlier this month did not come as a ‘surprise’ to many Axis Bank employees.
We may find employees’ reaction surprising, but for a bank known for its cutthroat competitive environment and aggressive sales targets, such lapses were part of the game. After all, modern banking in its market-share acquisition drive often resembles the old arcade game of Pac-Man—players eat as many dots as possible before being eaten up themselves.
Midway through last year, when Axis Bank was closing its acquisition of Citibank’s India business, we wrote about why Citibank’s employees would be key for the former to make its US$1.6 billion buy of the foreign bank work. Rounak had argued Axis Bank’s culture was “coming in the way”.
A year later, he writes Axis Bank is succeeding in scrubbing off its “high-pressure workplace” tag.
The CEO, Amitabh Chaudhry, whose own high-pressure initial tenure was only focused on turning around the business, is finally paying attention to the “culture.” Culture eats strategy for breakfast, and all that jazz aside, the CEO, as we learnt, “wanted Axis Bank to imbibe some of Citibank’s qualities”.
To the extent that when his peers expressed surprise in one interaction, he quipped, “Oh, come on, guys! I have a soft side too”.
The numbers speak for themselves: consolidated profits have increased 5X since 2019, and the once-notorious employee churn is now looking better than the industry average.
Rounak has a fine [read](https://the-ken.com/story/amitabh-chaudhry-launches-charm-offensive-at-axis-bank-and-employees-are-buying-it/) today on how Amitabh Chaudhry is going about from being a bad cop to a good cop in smoothing the rough edges.
