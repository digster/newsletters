Did you know that a study by UK investment firm MMC Ventures found that sprinkling just one form of techno-alphabetical pixie dust on funding pitch decks helps startups attract up to 50% more funding than those who don’t? The magic pixie dust is called “AI”.
AI-enabled healthcare. AI-powered hiring. AI-driven robots. AI-connected governments. Anything becomes more magical with AI pixie dust.
Including, well, the development of software itself. Which was how Engineer.ai managed to raise over $30 million from blue-blooded investors like DeepCore (a SoftBank AI-focused fund) and Lakestar (an early investor in Facebook and Airbnb). It figured the best place to be during an AI gold rush was to sell AI shovels. This it does by offering customers the ability to “Make software as easy as ordering pizza”, by using AI, of course.
Picking up from a WSJ investigation into the notable lack of AI in Engineer.ai’s offerings, Sumanth digs deeper in today’s story. He goes into its patent pending (pending because Engineer.ai claims it will be applying for patents in 2-3 weeks) software building menu, which allows customers to replicate sites or apps like Flipkart, Uber, WeChat or Amazon. He looks into its financials. He looks into its customer roster. He looks into its leadership team.
