Good Morning Ishan,
Today’s story looks at a company whose IPO is expected to take place in the coming months, in iffy market territory.
Tata Capital is reportedly heading toward a Rs 17,000 crore ($2 billion) offering, with a tidy $11 billion valuation. If all goes to plan, it will be one of India’s largest IPOs to date.
It isn’t hard to see why the firm has much going for it. Tata Capital is among the five largest non-banking financial companies in the country, with steady profits and a loan book worth Rs 1.8 lakh crore as of September 2024. Besides, Tata is a household brand. What’s not to like?
The answer is a “disastrous” merger with Tata Motors Finance, as one financial-sector analyst described to The Ken. “A below-average Tata Motors Finance will turn an above-average Tata Capital into an average business overall.”
Other analysts who track Tata Capital share a similar assessment.
Our finance editor Anand writes that Tata Capital is in a race against time. It is on track to complete the merger with Tata Motors Finance and is bound by regulations to go public within the next six months. In that process, it risks losing lustre with investors.
