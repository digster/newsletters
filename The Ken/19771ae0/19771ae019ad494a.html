Is Jane Street the all-powerful hidden hand in India’s stock market?

Sebi is probing the US-based hedge fund’s trades over the past three years

Jane Street allegedly gamed the system by moving index prices after taking massive options positions, upending how markets are supposed to work. The ultra-secretive hedge fund is now under Sebi’s scrutiny for potentially rigging India’s derivatives market. While retail traders were deep in the red, Jane Street is said to have raked in up to $3 billion in India, making more here than its global peers. [Read the full story]

Croma goes from Tata Digital’s prized asset to guinea pig

With several U-turns in strategy, the electronics retailer is a victim of the internet company’s endless quest for relevance

In a surprise move, Croma’s digital marketing team has been told to directly work under Tata Digital in an effort to streamline business processes. Croma has been Tata Digital’s favourite lab rat over the last four years, and constant flip-flops have plunged the firm into losses. The problem lies with a leadership team that doesn’t understand offline retail, so Croma has been trying a range of tactics, hoping one move sticks. [Read the full story]

Big business

TVS waits, Tata localises, Mahindra hedges—how Indian automakers are dodging a magnet meltdown

Unless China clears shipments soon, EV production could stall

Indian EV makers could be forced to halt production by 15 July, industry insiders warn. Even if China gives rare earth export immediate clearance today, the paperwork, port procedures, and shipping could take up to a month. In parallel, automakers are exploring magnetless motors that are smaller and more energy-efficient, hoping to reduce reliance on rare earths. [Read the full story]

Reliance Trends, Pantaloons, and the shopping spree that never came

Humbled by store closures and layoffs, fashion retailers are trying new ways to draw shoppers back

India’s largest retailer Reliance Retail closed around 2,200 stores in FY25. Other chains followed suit. Value retailers like Zudio and Style Up are outperforming their peers in the mid-to-premium segment. There is a slump because consumers have stayed away from post-Covid revenge shopping, which retailers thought would bring them fortunes. Now, these companies are experimenting with a number of different store concepts to attract new foot traffic. [Read the full story]

Sebi-registered advisors are an endangered species. So who guides retail investors?

Licensed investment guides mostly cater to the rich. Fintechs like ET Money and Value Research may have a solution for those who aren’t served

Sebi-registered investment advisors are declining in number, even after the regulator made it possible to conduct business more freely. That’s because complying with all regulations requires advisors to shell out big bucks, forcing them to limit their clientele to the wealthy. Most retail investors either invest in direct plans without proper guidance, or buy a regular plan—both lead to lacklustre returns. Fintechs now offer automated advice for direct plans, but can they plug all the gaps? [Read the full story]
