Good Morning Ishan,

There’s no better validation for a top B-school graduate than being chosen by one of the “MBB”: McKinsey, Bain, and Boston Consulting Group.

This assertion would have been hard to contest a few years ago.

Not anymore.

At the top three Indian Institutes of Management, one in every four graduates chose to work in strategy consulting this year. Certainly impressive.

Until I tell you that five years ago, one in every three did so.

Sure, strategy consultancies are not hiring the way they used to, thanks to belt-tightening by their clients.

But there’s a perceptible shift in what consultancies mean to young employees.

“We give advice, but we don’t have to live with the consequences of that advice. To make it happen is a different ball game altogether,” according to someone who left McKinsey to work as a product manager for a technology company.
