The Philippines’ 132-year-old conglomerate San Miguel Corporation (SMC) has interests—and a strong position—in industries ranging from food to infrastructure to beer and real estate. The sprawling business employs over 46,000 people and on its own, contributes around 5% of the Philippines’ GDP.
But, there is one sector where SMC hasn’t had its way—banking. As Katrina writes, SMC-backed Bank of Commerce (BankCom) has been a small player in the market. Operating first as a payroll bank, before scaling up, the entity is just the 15th largest in its business. For an enterprise the scale of SMC, being an also ran wouldn’t do. Just in the last year, SMC aggressively added heft to its banking operations, infusing 5.5 billion pesos (US$98 million) and adding more deals from within the parent network.
But simply funnelling deals won’t be enough to satiate SMC’s ambitions. A new universal banking license may give the bank the fillip it needs to scale up its business, but will it be enough to become a table-topper?
The mandarins at SMC have been busy dealing with a break up in the power business, thanks to differences with the country’s Energy Regulation Commission. Now, they will need to focus their energy on fixing the conglomerate’s banking business and ensuring BankCom grows into a player of note.
