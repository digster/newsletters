“Mutant bicycle.” The phrase caught my attention from Olina’s story today. I meant, how could it not?!
 
She uses it to refer to the hybrid bike called “Miracle”, from Yulu, formerly a bicycle-sharing startup. It’s hybrid because while it looks like a cross between a BMX bicycle and a moped, it has a battery pack and intelligent tracking device attached to it. Which makes it really an “e-bike”.
 
Rival Bounce, soon-to-be-formerly a scooter-sharing startup, is a few weeks away from launching an e-scooter of its own. 
 
The two are set to battle each other for dominance over the next iteration of India’s last-mile travel market—e-bikes and e-scooters. I say “next iteration” because the last iteration was led by bicycles. Companies like Yulu, Ofo, Zoomcar (with its PEDL bikes), Mobike and Mobicy burnt through millions of venture capital dollars flooding cities like Bangalore and Pune (connected, progressive and temperate all-year weather) with blue, green and yellow bicycles. But their capital and dreams were sucked dry because of theft, vandalism and widespread loss of their bicycles. 
 
So everyone "pivoted” to scooters and e-scooters. Which brings me back to today’s story. Olina goes deep into the business models and plans of Yulu and Bounce, both in their second avatars. 
 
Yulu designed its “mutant bicycle” from scratch. “Miracle” has been custom-made by China’s second largest electric vehicle manufacturer, shipped over in parts and assembled by a local partner. Its battery is sourced from a small start-up in Gurugram. 
 
In contrast, Bounce is sourcing existing electric form factors from Ampere (India) and Okinawa (South Korea). One offers docked bikes, while the other dockless.
 
Which one will succeed? Could both? That’s what Olina’s story is about: https://the-ken.com/story/bounce-vs-yulu-e-scooters-or-hybrid-cycles-whatll-make-it-in-india/
