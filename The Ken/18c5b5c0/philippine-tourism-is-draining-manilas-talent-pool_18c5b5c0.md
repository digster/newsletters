Good Morning Ishan,
Taking one’s career in a completely different direction can be scary. After all, it’s not easy to casually explain things like dreams, passions, and aspirations to people.
That was my experience, too, when I left my job as a lawyer eight years ago to become a journalist. A handful of my former colleagues asked me if I’d lost my mind. My parents questioned my decision. I get questioned to this day by fellow journalists, employers, and new friends every time they learn about the choice I made.
Lately, I have learned that I’m not the only one who took the roller coaster of a career shift.
In the Philippines, for instance, some lawyers, auditors, and government officials have decided to quit their comfortable office jobs in Metro Manila and moved to tourism destinations, working as baristas or surf instructors. Some of them have even built their own cafes and bakeries. They are now living the life and pursuing the career they wanted.
But the reason for this shift finds roots in the country’s capital. Long working hours with stagnated wages, limited opportunities, and poor living conditions were among their reasons for leaving their jobs in Metro Manila. Now, their exodus has triggered some changes throughout the country.
Isa, who travelled to the beautiful surf town of San Juan in the Philippines for this story, will be our guide in helping us understand this phenomenon taking shape in the country’s labour market.
