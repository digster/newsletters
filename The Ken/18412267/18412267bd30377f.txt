Blitzscaling is a specific set of practices for igniting and managing dizzying growth; an accelerated path to the stage in a startup's life cycle where the most value is created.

The concept prioritises speed over efficiency. Powered by venture capital, most startups have blitzscaled to capture the market over the past decade.

Even in Indonesia, venture capital served as a booster for new tech. Venture capitalists (VCs) handed out big cheques quicker than banks and big funds, among other investors, helping the country create consumer-technology giants like GoTo, which is now listed on the IDX.  

But now, the market is awash with apps, and most of them don’t bring anything new to the table, says Martyn Terpilowski. Once a hedge-fund manager in Tokyo and Hong Kong, he is now an entrepreneur in Indonesia. 

He believes that the country’s venture-capital blitz has stalled. Anyone can make an app now, and VCs are willing to throw money at any of them, he tells Nadine in today's Counterthesis.

VCs are no longer investing in any “real” innovation, says Terpilowski, and as a result, Indonesia is struggling to create its next big tech player.

Instead of creating an ecosystem that encourages startups to focus on long-term returns, VCs are backing firms chasing the same limited opportunities for short-term gains, giving rise to “zombie companies” that have no future, he adds. 

Once a booster for tech startups, VCs may have become a barrier to their growth, suggests Terpilowski.
