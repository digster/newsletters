Fashion is in a weird place right now. Trousers have been traded for jeans. Jeans have been traded for cargo pants. And almost everything has been traded for pyjamas.

While we sit at home, looking out our windows and balconies at stores finally opening up post-lockdown, fashion remains stuck. There's no worse time for the industry.

In the West, it's a bloodbath. RTW Retailwinds—which operates the New York & Company clothing chain—announced that it will close most, if not all, its ~400 retail locations. The US’ oldest department store chain, Lord & Taylor, which had an outsized focus on fashion, has filed for bankruptcy. It plans to liquidate all 38 of its stores.

Indian retailers could do better as they reopen stores. Or at least they're making an attempt. About 80% of the stores of Aditya Birla Fashion—most famous for its Pantaloons chain—75% of V-Mart’s stores, and 74 of Shoppers Stop’s 88 department stores have now reopened.

But an open store doesn't mean business as usual. Sales for Aditya Birla Fashion and Shoppers Stop were down by 84% and 93% in the last quarter. Seasons have changed in the time they stayed shut, with summer wear piling up. They need to rid themselves of that. With the 'ber' (brr in colder places) months here, they need to be able to sell winter wear, but how?

Even if they did find a way to get around the fashion season problem, the only fashion season that's left at this point is pandemic season. That's right, there's only so much festival attire you're going to buy if you're not going out to celebrate the upcoming festival season, right?

Maybe they need to start blinging up pyjamas? Read Prasannata's story on the fashion industry's pandemic meltdown: https://the-ken.com/story/covid-changes-business-models-for-fashion-retailers/ (13-minute read)

Have a good weekend. Let me know of good sites to buy comfy pyjamas. Note: I'm a large person.
