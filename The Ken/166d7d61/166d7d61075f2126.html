Good Morning Ishan,
 
We're entering that dreaded time of the year, at least for my kith and kin up north. Delhi is already choking, with low-visibility and unbreathable air. And Diwali is just around the corner.
 
It's sad, really. I remember growing up in a much more breathable, green city. Diwali, at our home, stopped being about crackers very early. I was 7. 
 
My school had been carrying out dedicated anti-cracker drives, educating us. And then, we got our first puppy. 
 
On Diwali night that year, she ran out of the house, too scared to understand the madness. My folks searched for hours. They found her dirty, wet, shivering and whimpering behind a bush in a nearby park. And while we were never big on noisy crackers to start with, that year, everything stopped.
 
Cut to today, all we do is light small earthen diyas and decorate our balconies with lights. But even standing out long enough to light them has left me coughing and gasping for air that's...just not there.
 
This may sound like a very bleak outlook towards a wonderful festival. It's not. It's a lament about not being able to enjoy that wonderful festival. 
 
Gayathri's piece on Thursday shone a spotlight on the problem—when it comes to curbing pollution, we continue to get it wrong. Shell, the UN and the USA spent millions on cookstoves—$1 billion in 10 years to distribute 100 million clean cookstoves, grow a marketplace, and protect millions of lives from air pollution. But are cookstoves the solution at all?
 
We also had some other excellent stories this week. Olina's week-opener on Sunday (#TKOW) looked into India's tipping culture vis-a-vis the new trend of digital tipping. Is it an easy transition?
 
Another piece I really liked was Arundhati's Wednesday piece on how fintechs are not too happy with the RBI's regulation. Fintechs say the RBI is slow, opaque and corpulent. That’s ammunition for a government that wants to clip its wings.
 
Ruhi's Tuesday piece [free story] on Ayushman Bharat was another winner. Except it put a question mark on Ayushman Bharat itself being a winner, as it's widely touted to be. While undeniably grand a program, it is facing roadblocks with hospitals and insurers. And that's not all.
 
We also had a piece from Vandana on Monday, who wrote about Info Edge—a master of all trades, by the looks of it. It’s an internet platform. It’s a holding company. It’s a VC fund. It’s also a stock market darling. And the only non-VC firm with two unicorn investments. But these mask deeper problems.
 
The last piece of the week by Vivek looked carefully into the living accommodation provider Nestaway. The company is the market leader in India's organised rentals space. But with less than 1% market share, its leadership is still pyrrhic. Besides, Oyo wants to dethrone it. Can it make it?
 
We had a good week. We hope you have a good one, too.
 
Stay safe, and if you're in polluted northern regions, keep that mask handy. It's not uncool.
 
Have a good weekend, all.
