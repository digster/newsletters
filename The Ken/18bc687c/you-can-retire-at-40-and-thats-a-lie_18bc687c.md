Good Morning Ishan,
Diwali Greetings from The Ken.
Until I became a co-founder seven years ago, I harboured a FIRE dream like millions in this country and elsewhere.
But in hindsight, I would say it was like living in a fool’s paradise. Financial Independence, Retire Early (FIRE)—a fashionable acronym that has lately become popular thanks to the so-called ‘finfluencers’—and a profession like journalism don’t go hand in hand.
I’ve stopped chasing FIRE, but I am sure others haven’t.
So, how realistic is that chase?
The corpus needed to retire early is smaller than that needed to retire later. It’s counter-intuitive, if not baffling.
Anand, our resident personal finance expert, went out reporting and collecting wisdom from financial planners to put together a corpus calculus that also solves the above conundrum—a 40-year-old wanting to retire at 45 will need a smaller corpus than what she’d require to retire at 60. And yet, he writes, it’s harder to reach the former goal.
Finance is complicated. But human nature is even more so. I mean, how many of us can practice extreme frugality and delay gratification to save, and save hard?
During this festival of light, which is also associated with celebration of wealth, Anand’s story has insights one can use and decide for oneself—FIRE or not to FIRE.
