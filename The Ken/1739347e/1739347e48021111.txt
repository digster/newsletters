I know, I know. Another story about Jio Platforms. 

At one level, it seems like that’s all everyone is talking about, but can you blame them? Billions of dollars have poured into Jio from luminaries like Google, Facebook and a clutch of PE investors from across the world. Then there’s a partnership with Microsoft. And then there are unconfirmed reports of Amazon investing into Jio’s retail arm. 

Normally, when companies are flush with money, either because of a surge in their stock price or through venture capital, they choose to expand. At this point, they look at where they are headed and ask themselves one question. It’s a question called “Build versus Buy”. This thinking is something like this: Do we spend the time and effort to build something new, or do we acquire an existing player? 

But Jio isn’t a normal company. 

Jio’s capital is rivaled only by its ambition. It doesn’t want to be India’s telco leader, or the provider of 5G technology to the world, or be a formidable OEM, or India’s largest retail player, or the owner of the top consumer-facing apps in the country. It wants to be all of them. And then there’s the fact that while Jio is valued as a tech-company, it hasn’t yet demonstrated its ability to build software from scratch that can disrupt existing players. 

My colleague, Pranav, instead thinks that the Jio’s choice isn’t Build vs Buy but Build vs Buy vs Aggregate. This is a decision Jio will have to make for every category. Payments. Retail. Grocery. Communication. Content and OTT streaming. 

That’s what today’s story is about. Jio’s decisions, and how it hopes to continue what got it this far. What to build? What to buy? What to aggregate? And how it can be both the jack of all trades and the master of India’s digital economy.
