If you haven’t heard of Samay Raina yet… no, you haven’t been living under a rock. But maybe, just maybe, you haven’t been spending enough time on India’s most-binged-on streaming channel. You are also missing out on a big shift underway there.

Indians spent a cool 8 trillion minutes watching videos online in just the first half of 2024—that’s a third more than the time they spent doing that in the entire year before. Over 90% of this was on just one platform—Youtube. Safe to say that the Google-owned platform is riding a tsunami of viewership in India.

Youtube has much going for it. For starters, it’s still mostly free to watch, unlike its rivals. That gets in hordes of subscribers. Also, unlike other streaming giants, it keeps its content creators quite happy—by sharing a chunk of its mighty ad revenues.

This flywheel has been spinning even faster in India over the past few months. Thanks to big-ticket tweaks epitomised by Raina’s offering—India’s Got Latent—that’s been the country’s top trending show for several weeks now, within hours of being uploaded. Several Youtube channels, such as Raina’s and TVF, are now offering their subscribers TV-like programming—with lower budgets and better quality.

As Nathan, the author of today’s story writes, Youtube is no longer satisfied offering snackable content. It’s poised to lay out the full spread and pose a real threat to TV channels. Click here to know how and what this means for the streaming industry in India.
