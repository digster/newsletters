Good Morning Ishan,
The French have an old saying, hopefully just-a-joke: “The customer is king. You know what we do to kings.”
Now, Counter Cyclical Investments doesn’t go so far as to decapitate its monarchs. But it does read its customers the riot act. Consider some gems from the ‘who should invest with us’ long-list of this little-known PMS fund from Nagpur—the tier-2 city in central India famous for its oranges.
“Those who won’t disturb us by calling us for reassurance everytime the market falls.”
“If you are prone to panic during market crashes, then panic before investing in our PMS because selling small caps during market crashes will be a nightmare for us and a disaster for our investors.”
“We don’t have any provision for a relationship manager. Once you have taken an informed decision to invest in our PMS, please avoid calling us. Those who are looking for regular correspondence and active interaction, may please stay away.”
Now, if you think moneyed investors—the kind who have to put in at least Rs 50 lakh to be a part of Counter Cyclical—would bristle and walk away in the face of such insolence, you would be as right as Donald Trump in the tariff wars. Counter Cyclical’s assets under management have shot up over 10X in the past three years, and its customer count has grown by leaps and bounds.
Why? The only scheme—a small-cap one —run by the six-year-old fund house is a chart-topper with five year annualised returns of, believe-it-or-not, 78%. As we know, investors love nothing more than returns. Pride can wait.
Amidst the Trump-chaos, when many experts advise staying away from them, Counter Cyclical is doubling down not just on small-caps but also their tinier cousins.
How does this upstart do what it does? Do its quirks go beyond how it treats its kings? Archishma finds out in her revelatory [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/this-tiny-pms-firm-makes-millions-where-large-rivals-fear-to-tread/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
