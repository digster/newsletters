Good Morning Ishan,
 
As journalists, our curiosities are particularly piqued by startups choosing to be in stealth mode nearly seven years after their founding. Or, even after raising an impressive $100 million.
 
Playing pseudo Sherlock Holmes is fun when the startup’s competitors draw a blank or when the selective information that the startup chooses to drop via PPTs scattered over the internet don’t quite add up.
 
Absolute is one such startup.
 
In May, this agritech announced that it mopped up $100 million from investors like Sequoia Capital, Tiger Global, and Alpha Wave Global. It was a significant jump from the $3.2 million it had raised two years ago.
 
And the valuation multiple? An easy-to-calculate 10X, to $500 million, and in just one year.
 
Absolute is now worth as much as DeHaat—also a Sequoia investee—even if the two companies’ toplines can’t see eye-to-eye—Absolute grossed Rs 28 crore ($3.6 million) in revenue in FY2021 when DeHaat’s stood at Rs 360 crore ($46 million).
 
I am sure by now you are equally curious about Absolute’s secret farm ingredient. So are many in the industry.
 
To quote from the story today:  “It’s the Loch Ness monster of Indian agritech…it’s a ‘black box’…the seven-year-old company’s fundraise ‘came out of left field’.”
 
Absolute describes itself as a “pathbreaking plant biotech company” that is building the “world’s largest precision farm network”. But a former employee says, “It’s all about procurement”.
 
A company that was inspired to solve India’s food security problem but set up vertical farms, and finally landed up in food trading, Absolute’s about-turn is nearly absolute. The Ken visited some farms in Haryana to understand what exactly its “farm operating system” is.
 
Startups choose to be in stealth mode to keep their technology under wraps, prepare for a grand debut, or simply avoid distraction. I’ll leave you to read the story and judge for yourself why Absolute has chosen this label.
