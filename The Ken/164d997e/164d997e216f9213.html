Good Morning Ishan,
 
Happy Friday to you.
 
Now, I must not mince words. IMHO, the biggest lie ever perpetrated by advertising agencies in India is that spraying a deodorant makes a man irresistible to women. You’ve seen multiple variants of those advertisements on television and are none the wiser for it. Truth be told, I’m the living, breathing example of how that’s absolutely, ridiculously untrue. Every bit of the salary spent there, gas. 
 
That said, women, I guess you need to be sounded off. Because the Indian deodorant manufacturers have finally found you. After years of selling confidence masked as deodorants to men, they believe you are the next sunrise business. You have joined the workforce. You have the money, and all put together, your business is worth at least Rs 2,000 crore and counting.
 
One of the many deodorants coming your way is called Desire. A fragrance composed of 1,000 Turkish roses. 
 
The man behind it? Narendra K Daga, the founder of Kolkata-based McNROE Consumer Products.
 
It is a fascinating story. That of Daga and McNROE. 
 
Named after yesteryear’s American tennis legend John Patrick McEnroe, it is a privately-held family business, started in 1986. In the year ended March 2018, McNROE claims to have made Rs 408 crore in revenue. The company takes after Daga himself. The 60-year-old perfumer is a traditionalist. A man of humble roots. Slightly reserved. No flashy clothes. No branded accessories. McNROE started life as a talcum powder manufacturing company, under the brand Heaven’s Garden. After several fortunate and unfortunate events, Daga found success with deodorants like Wild Stone and Secret Temptation. And now, he wants to double down and grow his company to take on Vini Cosmetics, the market leader in this business.

I loved this one thing Daga says in the piece about his life: “I’m a lucky man.” Now if only I could say the same.
