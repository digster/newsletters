Good Morning Ishan,
 
Professional writers would attest—sometimes you don’t choose stories, they choose you.
 
SigTuple’s story today is one such case. In February, an entrepreneur in Delhi requested me to look at the many twists and turns taking place at this five-year-old company. He gave me a tip on their AI device, but somehow, I never dug into it.
 
In May, Edd Gent, a Bengaluru-based freelance sci-tech writer, sent us a pitch on how “hype hubris and endless pivot” had turned SigTuple into a diagnostics chain. We got interested because the startup genuinely held a lot of promise and had raised over $40M in VC funding. And those who follow medtech counted on it to fix parts of the broken diagnostics sector.
 
The company’s goal was to slash the cost of medical testing and solve India’s chronic shortage of pathologists by creating an affordable device that could automate the visual analysis of medical samples. But, in 2019, things took a puzzling turn when the company launched a chain of diagnostics centres across India designed to be “the Apple store for diagnostic labs”. A spree of mergers, acquisitions and expansions had converted this B2B AI company into a “SigTuple group of companies” with interests in everything from home sample collection to corporate wellness programs and lab equipment distribution.
 
That’s way too much distraction for a deep tech company. AI is notoriously fragile. When Edd spoke with Israel’s Sight Diagnostics, which has commercialised an AI-powered device for the same kind of blood analysis as SigTuple, the CEO said it took them 10 years to develop their product.
 
Patience, as we often notice, is not the virtue of investors (or entrepreneurs) in India. SigTuple tried to change its DNA—from a deep tech company to everything-diagnostics.
 
It was coincidental that a day after Edd sent a ‘long’ list of questions to the company, the new CEO posted a ‘long’ note on LinkedIn, disclosing, among other things, that his two co-founders, ex-CEO and COO, had moved on. The note, however, doesn’t take away from the rigorously reported story which goes deep into the tech that the company still hopes to develop: https://the-ken.com/story/ai-startup-sigtuple-cash-burning-laboratory-chain/ (13-minute read)
