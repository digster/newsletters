For middle-class Indonesians, it’s quite common to seek medical treatment overseas. Especially when it comes to serious illnesses. Local hospitals often lack specialist doctors and top-notch treatment methods. In general, trust in the local healthcare system in Indonesia is low.

Indonesians then turn into “medical tourists”: they fly to other countries, often for extended periods of time to seek treatment. It can require multiple visits over many years.

Luckily, they don’t have to look far. Neighbours like Malaysia, Thailand, and Singapore are specialising in accommodating medical tourists from around the world. India is a major destination, too. It’s a big business opportunity for hospitals.

For Indonesians, so far, Malaysia has been the place to go. Similar culture and language makes it easier to adapt. But more importantly, the small country (34 million vs Indonesia’s 273 million inhabitants) has a much more mature healthcare industry than Indonesia. It’s also home to Asia’s largest healthcare group, IHH Healthcare.

Malaysia now wants to further expand its medical-tourism facilities even as Indonesia is taking steps to improve the quality of care at home.
