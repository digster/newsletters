If you’ve been following Reliance’s moves to create a financial-services giant, let me remind you that the shareholder voting on its demerger will take place tomorrow.
As I wonder if it can pull off a Jio Infocomm story again, I’m reminded of what I learnt about its telecom strategy from one of the insiders. Before it rolled out 4G services in 2016, Reliance Industries Ltd (RIL) knew exactly how long it needed to keep the price war going to decimate its competition, thanks to modelling exercises. In retrospect, the planning was immaculate.
Then in 2020, when RIL created Jio Platforms to raise $15.2 billion in private investments, its corporate structuring was a masterstroke—in one fell swoop, RIL cleared a ton of debt on its books. It was financial engineering at its best.
In 2023, once again, the two enviable traits of the conglomerate are on display when it’s at the cusp of creating Jio Financial Services Ltd (JFSL). This company will operate in the lending business, digital broking, insurance, payments, mutual funds, etc.
Phew!
It’s a gigantic deal. At the heart of which are ‘treasury shares’—RIL’s shares that RIL itself owns and that came about way back in 2002.
As Anand writes in his clear-eyed analysis today, various finserv businesses across RIL and its group companies are being demerged and transferred to non-bank Reliance Strategic Investments Ltd (RSIL). The RIL subsidiary runs a small security-trading business at present.
But after the demerger, RSIL will no longer be a subsidiary of RIL; it’ll be renamed JFSL and issue its own shares. Some analysts are already assigning top-dollar valuations—up to ~$18 billion—to the yet-to-be-listed JFSL.
In early 2021,
The Ken [wrote](https://the-ken.com/story/reliances-dont-show-dont-tell-takes-a-toll/) about the big plans afoot at RIL for its financial-services unit.
Cut to now, RIL seems to have made a virtue out of necessity—by shifting an investment worth ~Rs 1 lakh crore (~$12 billion) to the fledgling JFSL while also ring-fencing this investment from a looming regulatory threat.
