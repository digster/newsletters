It’s Shreevar here, and this is the first time I’m speaking to you via email at The Ken. You’ve probably heard me rambling through my microphone before. One such instance was the second episode of Cost to Company: ‘Who Gets Laid Off First?’

So much has changed since then, and yet, so little. Tanay Pratap, one of the guests on the episode, had mentioned that he was expecting the layoff season to continue for the next “12 to 18 months at least.” I didn’t want to believe him, but I guess we all know that he’s probably right. 

The past couple of months have seen international tech giants like Meta, Twitter, Stripe, and more recently, Amazon lay off thousands of employees. Indian shores haven’t been spared either. Unacademy, Udaan, Zomato, Byju’s have let people go too.

But there’s a nuance (or the lack thereof) with which these layoffs are communicated. The words that they use. The words that they don’t use. The message. The medium. Was it a leaked blog post? Was it a Slack message? Was it a long email where the founder claims that 5% of his heart had been laid off? Behind the unfortunate times, there’s a spectacle going on behind the scenes. And between the lines. 

In this special edition of Cost to Company, Sneha and I speak to Karthik Srinivasan—Communications Strategy consultant, LinkedIn maverick, Ex-Flipkart and Ogilvy, Beast of Traal (no seriously, that’s his Twitter handle). We discuss: 

1. Which companies aced their layoff messaging, and which didn’t. 

2. Why Airbnb’s 2020 lay off template is being rinsed and repeated by most tech companies.

3. The importance of the founder’s personality + public perception while communicating layoffs. 

4. Whether layoffs can be a nifty time to talk about brand values.

5. The different stakeholders you have to address (sometimes subliminally) while communicating the bad news.
