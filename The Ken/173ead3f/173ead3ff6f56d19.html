Good Morning Ishan,
 
The celebration that accompanies a startup securing funding is something to behold.
 
Founders take to LinkedIn, delivering the entrepreneurial equivalent of an Oscar acceptance speech. Team members outdo one another with mythical tales of the managers who made this possible. Journalists, meanwhile, are inundated with requests to cover the story of how the company persevered and beat the odds. The resultant articles are inevitably accompanied by a waist-up photo of the founders, arms crossed to show they mean business.
 
The whole performance is understandable. Funding is massive. It is validation. It is hope. Most importantly, it is survival. So, when a startup raises funding and doesn't so much as whisper about it, it should raise curiosity, if not eyebrows.
 
Earlier this month, healthcare startup Practo raised its second-biggest funding round—just under $32 million. It was Practo's first fundraise since 2016. Big news, right?
 
Practo still hasn't announced it.
 
Perhaps Practo's founders are simply over the thrill of a successful funding round. This is, after all, the same company that raised $115 million across three funding rounds in just over a year back in 2015-16, raising its valuation to $660 million. Of course, a $32 million round would seem like a drop in the bucket—this is a startup that made it to a prestigious global list of 50 future unicorns.
