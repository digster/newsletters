Good Morning Ishan,
 
When I first joined The Ken, I was still trying to find the best possible beat for myself. A journalist without a good beat is an oarsman without a strong oar. But luckily for me, 2016, the year The Ken started, was a landmark year for fintech. India decided to ban 86% of its currency, and a new digital payments system arrived on the scene. And I didn’t have to look any further. I tentatively dipped my toes into the quietly swirling world of fintech and got sucked into this twister of a phenomenon. And now fintech has me. 
 
Fintech has been a snakes and ladders game, where regulators and the government cast the dice and fortunes rise and, just as easily, crash. And the cycle continues. 
 
In a short span of three years, payments has had an accelerated evolution, making it hard to keep track of how we even got to where we are today. Payments' success is crucial for the growth of internet-based companies, but more importantly, it is the only way we can remove the inefficiencies of a cash-based economy. Today’s story, a graphical narrative, stemmed from a typical Monday morning chat on Slack. Pranav, the co-author of today’s piece had some very real questions. What happened to IMPS after UPI? Did wallets ever recover? He then pulled up RBI data and remarked: A fledgling payments system actually shot ahead of cards and wallets! But how are multi-billion dollar enterprises Visa and Mastercard holding up then? And what about payment gateways? 
 
Prajakta, our graphic designer and data visualiser had one simple observation to all his questions. Wouldn’t it be great to have a poster or a pin-up of sorts that answers all of these questions in an easy to consume fashion? 
 
That conversation got the two started on mapping this very complex landscape in 8 charts, and what we now have is a graphical narrative that can serve as a ready reckoner. It's a free read, so do share it widely.
