Happy Thursday to you. Yeah, I know the feels. Same. Can’t wait.
You know what else can’t wait? The subject of Kunal Shah. In the last three months, from among the people I’ve met, not a meeting goes by where the other person doesn’t have a question about Shah. Or about his startup, CRED. About the wondrous, jealousy-inducing $30 million in seed funding it raised. Few people have seen that kind of money, let alone raised it in seed capital. So a conversation around it is mostly awkward; in part, incredulous, in part, wow, isn’t it great that second-time founders in India are raising so much money? Look at Kunal Shah. Look at Mukesh Bansal...
At this precise moment, I say, hmmm…
But if you are Sumanth, you say, no, look at the math. A round of this size implies that CRED has a post-money valuation in the range of $100-125 million. The base expectation of a venture capital investor is that a startup investment gives an exit multiple of at least 3-4X. Which means that CRED is already looking at an exit that needs to be around $500 million. Half a unicorn. A demicorn/hemicorn?
It is right then that I remember this famous venture capital saying:
“Smoking venture capital-made business plans is injurious to your health” — Anon
But I also remember this. Kunal Shah is too sharp for this. He is a man who is aware; one who, if you were to meet him, will eventually tell you he is aware.
If you were to meet him, you’d be surprised by the number of languages Shah can speak. Much of it starts from what you’d like to hear, and then a quick pick of something you just said—one word in your sentence and then close analysis of the word, followed by analysis of the thought, the world, life and existence, and God, and Naval Ravikant, and why nobody writes about Paytm, and why FreeCharge didn’t get as much media love as it should have, and how travel is the source of all human energy and knowledge, and how Silicon Valley is so great and we are so far behind. A tornado of thoughts and ideas. Oh, and he's also a prolific angel investor.
Thank you for reading.
