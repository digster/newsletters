There is a moral burden that you carry as a state-affiliated investment company. You’re not there just to make money. Your raison d'être is much larger: to help shape a more resilient future for your country.

Such a company must always be on the lookout for innovations that address the nation's biggest challenges today and tomorrow. For Singapore’s Temasek, that mandate includes looking out for the city-state’s food security. 

Singapore has limited land to build enough farms to supply food for its 5 million people. One of the most densely populated countries in the world, the city-state relies heavily on imports, which leaves it vulnerable to food shortages when the global supply-chain experiences shock.

No wonder Temasek is keen on supporting the growth of alternative meat. Its investments in the industry include cell-grown crustacean provider Shiok Meats, oat-milk producer Oatside, and plant-based meat-alt Impossible Foods. In terms of the size of its funding, its investee, Next Gen Foods, stands out.

In February 2021, Next Gen Foods raised a US$10 million seed round, the world’s largest for a plant-based foodtech startup, as we’ve mentioned in a previous story https://the-ken.com/story/how-next-gen-growthwell-and-co-can-become-asias-impossible-foods/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter. In a year, it managed to bag US$130 million. Surely, there must be a strong thesis behind Temasek’s support. 

Perhaps it’s because the company is trying to answer the more fundamental questions about people’s consumption habits. Its chicken substitute called Tindle, for one, is meant to appeal to chefs.

That’s a strong starting point, and the company has introduced the product in multiple markets—Kuala Lumpur, London, Amsterdam, and San Francisco, among others. Lately, sales of alternative meat have stagnated, and its high price point has deterred many of its consumers.

Will the current market conditions shake Temasek’s faith in the role the alt-meat industry has to play in the future of Singapore’s food security?
