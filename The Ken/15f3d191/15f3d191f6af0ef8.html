Digital wallets, Ponzi schemes, Google Indic and cycle-sharing

Good morning Ishan,

 

What's the weekend looking like for you? Did you get a long Diwali break? Are you up in the mountains or at the nearest beach? Did you go home? Wherever you are, we hope you enjoyed the festival, preferably away from all the pollution.

 

If you’ve been on social media like me, you’ve probably seen many ask: Why would Delhi burst crackers after a blanket ban? Here’s a thought: Cities all over India continue to choke. The problem is larger than Delhi and cannot be dealt with a last-minute ban.

 

Sometimes, in order to understand what's happening around us, we need to look at the larger picture. In such situations, addressing the macro is the only way forward. Be it with India's post-Diwali pollution problem, or any of our stories at The Ken this week.

 

We started with Arundhati's analysis of the RBI guidelines that made wallets unsexy. Except, the larger picture is that all those wallet users are now up for grabs, and her story explores what becomes of them.

 

Tuesday saw our debutant Sidhartha's FREE PIECE on crypto Ponzi schemes. Around 500,000 people have entrusted their money with fraudsters. Wonder what happens next?

 

This was followed by Venkat's report on Google's attempt at linguistic diversification in India—Google Indic. Interestingly, the projected user base for non-English languages by 2021 stands at a whopping 536 million.

 

Yesterday, Patanjali told you where PEDL—Zoomcar's cycle-sharing initative—is at. No points for guessing why India can do with fewer cars and more cycle lanes!

 

It's no coincidence that big companies are realising the value of reaching out to the masses, or that RBI is putting checks in place for the same. Because the more they focus on the individual, the larger the pool gets. Even for conniving crypto conmen.

 

On that note, we leave you to enjoy your weekend. And maybe, just maybe, to subscribe to The Ken.

 

See you on Monday!

 

Cheers,
