Good Morning Ishan,
Unless you are a close observer of the stock market, Ashish Kacholia is a name that is unlikely to mean much to you.
Sure, he is no Rakesh Jhunjhunwala. But ask anyone who trades in small-cap and mid-cap stocks, and they will tell you Kacholia is among the top weathervanes.
His stakes of over 1% in 48 companies are worth Rs 2,750 crore.
Among his biggest bets, and certainly the most intriguing, is Zaggle Prepaid Ocean Services. Zaggle offers companies such as Pepsico, ITC, and Wipro a platform to manage their reimbursements, rewards, and vendor invoicing.
Zaggle, with a market value of Rs 5,500 crore, is intriguing, not because it has bought or invested in five companies since September. What caught my colleague Debanjali’s attention is the path the company has chosen to find a toehold in the US.
It’s not more acquisitions. Or acquihires. Or even strategic investments.
