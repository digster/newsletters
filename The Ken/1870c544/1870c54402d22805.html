Good Morning Ishan,
 

I'm never sure what to make of people who talk about themselves the way Viresh Joshi did during a virtual chat with equity brokers in 2021.

The then chief trader of Axis Mutual Fund referred to himself as a "tornado" that could sweep away those in his path, a broker told The Ken.

Joshi clearly wasn't big on self-effacement.
 
He is among those recently barred from India's capital markets by watchdog Sebi for illegally trading stocks for personal gains ahead of large orders placed by Axis Mutual Fund, India's seventh-largest asset-management company (AMC).
 
Sebi has been playing whack-a-mole with the practice of front-running in AMCs. “It’s rampant in the industry—an open secret,” said an ex-fund manager. “But Joshi took it to another level, and he is paying the price."
 
For today's story, contributor Andrew Peretti, a former equity trader in Mumbai, joined forces with my colleague Anand and spoke with a range of people, including former colleagues, brokers who worked with Joshi—and the man himself.
