A few IIMs have been offering undergraduate education since 2024.

These new programmes have the heftiest undergraduate price tags in India, except for those that lead to a medical degree. A fresher who joins IIM Kozhikode to pursue a bachelors in management studies, for instance, can expect to pay at least Rs 7 lakh per year—on top of accommodation and other expenses.

These offerings are the result of plans that were years in the making, and a necessity at a time when there’s a hole in IIMs’ cash flow. There was a steep dive in the Indian government’s funding for these institutions—around 50% since 2022—so they needed to fill that gap.

Even so, there are enough takers lured by the IIM brand. Even if the schools are not making any promises regarding job placements for now, the students are banking on their alma mater’s name recognition. Maybe a BMS from one of the IIMs could have just as much cachet as an MBA, the logic goes.
