History shows that to sustainably gain control of a situation, it is important to forge the right alliances. Be it the power blocs during World Wars, or how your maternal uncle's second cousin's NRI nephew is a great match for you because he's "good for the family business". But let me get to the point before I digress.
So, Ola, too, has a hunger to expand and gain control, especially in a market with Uber. And this hunger has made it buy Foodpanda. Within seven months of Uber serving UberEats on a plate, Ola has pulled a potential trump card with its acquisition of the foodtech company.
But Patanjali, who talks about this acquisition at length in his piece today, has a slightly less obvious observation to make. Which is that not every move of Ola's is Uber-driven.
"Ola is not mirroring Uber anymore. The company is gravitating towards an on-demand logistics model, much like fellow SoftBank-funded company Grab or Indonesia’s Go-Jek. It will be fair to say that Ola has been dropping a trail of breadcrumbs over the last two years. And it is important to follow them. In 2015, Ola launched two products: Cafe and Store. Ola Cafe was its food delivery platform and store was a hyperlocal grocery player. Neither of them worked and Ola shut them down in 2016. But now, it has returned to those ideas. Well, at least one of them. It will not be surprising if Ola takes the acqui-hire route for a grocery company too," he writes.
