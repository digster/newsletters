Good Morning Ishan,

If you've been on Indian Twitter long enough this past week, there were two bits of news that made people go "whoa! what?", incidentally, on the very same day. One, of JNU student protesters and journalists getting roughed up and locked up by Delhi Police. And the other, of Reliance Jio integrating its music business with US-based OTT music service Saavn.
 
Just to be clear, we're talking about the latter here. The Saavn-Reliance Industries (RIL) deal which would see RIL acquire an 81.7% stake in Saavn India in exchange for the transfer of its music business, interestingly, chooses to maintain the Saavn brand.
 
Two weeks ago, Venkat had written about the now Tencent-backed Gaana and how it looked like it had one-upped slow and steady Saavn. Well, you know what they say about the slow and steady.
 
With this merger, Saavn has become the #1 music streaming service in India, pipping Gaana in its wake. And according to App Annie, Saavn and Jio Music should have a combined user base of 33 million—that's way ahead of runner-up Airtel's Wynk which has 21 million.
 
RIL's acquisition game doesn't stop here though. Nope, this just about scratches the surface. Curious? Read Venkat's piece: https://the-ken.com/story/the-reliance-jio-symphony/
 
Have a great day!
