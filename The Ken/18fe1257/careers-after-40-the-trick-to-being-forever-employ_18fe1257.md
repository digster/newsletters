Good Morning Ishan,
If you’re in your 40s or 50s, you may be staring down the barrel of an early retirement. Even if you’re younger, you’re not far behind. In April, we wrote a story based on an exclusive survey on career longevity, revealing that most 40-somethings are now [unwilling retirees](https://the-ken.com/story/why-more-40-somethings-are-becoming-unwilling-retirees/)—careers cut short at just half the traditional 40-45 years. Ouch!
Sure, retirement sounds lovely—leisurely sips of coffee on winter mornings, no more marathon meetings after reaching the office in the scorching Indian summers, and blissful ignorance of the email refresh button. But hitting it at an early age, especially with longer lifespans and rising inflation, cannot be so ideal (despite what influencers tell you).
So, many of you asked—What’s the game plan here? What happens when our well-planned career and retirement strategy goes out the window? Vanita came up with the goods by asking the big question: is forever employability even realistic or just a pipe dream?
One of the answers, she discovered, might be to become everything, everywhere, all at once. But what else?
Here’s a teaser of what you’ll find in today’s story:
- A 38-year-old “fractional” CMO’s career path from big pharma to a boutique marketing firm founder and finally seeing his profession as more of a mosaic than a ladder. A masterclass in non-linear success.
- A 53-year-old freelance consultant’s journey via 15 companies and myriad roles. A lesson in reinvention, flexibility, and adaptation.
- Insights from a new-age platform on how it helps those who stay relevant past the age of 50 find jobs.
- From board positions to independent directorships and other “viable” options.
- How companies who don’t use the older workforce will be stuck in brutal competition for a shrinking pool of overworked middle-aged talent.
- And more…
[Miss the story at your own risk.](https://the-ken.com/story/careers-after-40-the-trick-to-being-forever-employable/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter)
Ola Electric is playing a risky game of cost-cutting in its bid for profitability ahead of its IPO. Bhavish Aggarwal, the supremo, may channel Elon Musk on X, but behind the scenes, he’s slashing expenses like a samurai in a fruit market.
The Ken wrote about how the EV maker is using [fewer components](https://the-ken.com/story/what-is-ola-electrics-top-priority-if-it-is-not-e-scooters/) in its scooters and thinking of in-house design. Great! But now, reports suggest it is about to fire about 500 of its nearly 3,500 employees. These layoffs raise a question: is it a prudent financial move or a frantic scramble to spruce up the books before going public?
Despite holding half the market share in May, Ola Electric needs to tread carefully here. Because a disgruntled workforce is the last thing it needs right now. As for potential investors, sure, they love a company with a clear path to profitability, but they also value transparency and integrity
