Good Morning Ishan,

Summer is here in Delhi and we have all packed away our sweatshirts and jackets. So imagine my surprise when I get to work and I see my colleague, Harveen, wearing a sweatshirt and sketching.

I laughed at her and I was, like, Harveen, one, it is burning in Delhi, what’s with the sweatshirt? Two, does your job description also include illustrating stories? It turns out The Ken lost the annual negotiations in our co-working space to set the air conditioner temperature. It is now at a constant 18 degrees. Brrr.

A few minutes later, I wished I had one of my sweatshirts. I wish I had her foresight.

Talking about foresight, I realised that sketching is something Harveen uses to de-stress. And she picked it up when she was in school during her summer vacation. If I had taken those classes seriously, maybe I would have more than cake to de-stress.

How do you de-stress? I would recommend reading. And maybe read some of our stories, which we published through the week.The week started with Shashidhar’s piece on Amazon Pay.

The e-commerce company is making its second attempt at payments. And it has started to get other merchants on board. All of this leads back to getting people to shop on Amazon some more. It is interesting to see the strategy it has taken.

Next, Sidhartha told us (in his FREE story) that demonetisation is here again. No, don’t run to the ATM. He is talking cryptocurrency. He wrote this after RBI’s announcement prohibited banks and financial institutions from dealing in virtual currencies. It shut all legit modes of trading the Rupee for cryptocurrencies.

On Wednesday, yours truly wrote about medical tourism. Usually, hospitals invite tourists to occupy only the vacant beds. You knew that. But this story medECUBE’s founder Dilpreet Brar created Fortis’ highest grosser. Can she recreate it as a medical tourism facilitator?

Thursday saw Patanjali and Venkat team up again. This one was about Ola’s back up plan. Now, you know about the buzz in the market is that Uber and Ola are all set to merge. But what if the merger fails? Ola has a Plan B. In involves going to the UK. That’s their story.

The week ended with Sumanth’s analysis of why MonkeyBox, a startup that packed tiffin boxes for school children failed? It was a star in Bengaluru but its light faded. There is happiness in failure here and that’s what this story is about. A must read.

That's all for this week. Have a good weekend.
