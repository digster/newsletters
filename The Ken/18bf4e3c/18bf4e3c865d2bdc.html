Today’s story is full of ironies.

It is about gig workers—those who may never know that this is about them.

It is about internet companies—those who are supposed to provide for their “partners” but end up providing more for their users.

It is about Fairwork India—a nonprofit that evaluates the working conditions of such “partners”. It is the only one that tracks the gig economy and ranks platforms based on fair pay, conditions, contracts, management, and representation. But its assessment may not entirely be fair.

The report recently revealed that Bigbasket, Flipkart, and Urban Company were the only digital companies in 2023 to ensure that their gig workers receive the local minimum wage. But here’s the hitch: the results were based on interviews with only 359 gig workers from four cities. This is against a total gig workforce of 10–15 million.

The biggest irony of all: internet companies employing gig workers want to be part of the annual ranking. But how big a deal should it really be?

Shivani’s sharp story will help you arrive at the answer: https://the-ken.com/story/bigbasket-on-top-ola-at-bottom-is-fairwork-indias-gig-economy-ranking-fair/ (9-minute read)
