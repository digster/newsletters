Good Morning Ishan,
 
Not many of you know this, but Ashish was one of India’s best automotive beat writers till he found his second true calling, the writer of “complicated”, investigative business features.
 
Today, he’s back - if only temporarily - to his first calling. On the surface, his story is on Carnation Auto, originally launched in 2008 as a network of multi-brand car dealerships, where people could buy or service any make of vehicle under one roof. But nearly a decade later, Carnation is on its last few breaths. From 2300 people, it is down to 30. Debts have piled up to impossible levels. Accumulated losses run into hundreds of crores. And practically all possible pivots have been tried out.
 
But the story is also about the tenacious 75-year-old Jagdish Khattar, Carnation’s founder. As the head of Maruti Suzuki, Khattar was once one of two blue-eyed boys of Indian auto (the other one being Hyundai’s BVR Subbu). He took the company’s revenues from $1.5 billion to $3.4 billion between 2000 and 2008.
 
"In that time, Khattar became the most recognisable face of Indian auto. Simply put, the industry loved him back, many times over. So maybe it is only fitting that in the end, the automobile must consume him.”
 
Read Ashish’s measured and non-judgemental story about Carnation, Khattar and the senseless melee of players all chasing an elusive and low-trust market of older car owners.
