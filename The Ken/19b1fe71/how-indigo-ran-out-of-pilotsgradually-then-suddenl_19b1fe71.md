Good Morning Ishan,
The recent Indigo fiasco was a long time coming.
News of 12–24 hour wait times, massive pile-ups of luggage at major hubs, and skyrocketing fares made everyone—frequent flyer or not—sit up and take notice.
The immediate cause of it was the new flight duty time limitations that kicked into action in November. They basically rejigged the rules to allow pilots more rest time. Stuff like giving pilots 48 hours of rest and restricting night-duty timings, among others.
But the real reason for the crisis was more systemic.
Despite getting nearly two years to prepare for this change, Indigo was content to let sleeping dogs lie. It failed to increase its pilot strength, even as competitors like Air India doubled theirs. On top of this, it reduced pilots’ leave days and salaries, all while maintaining the same, tired refrain: that the airline had no pilot shortage. In fact, it claimed it had a 30% excess.
The result was nearly 4,000 cancelled flights, not to mention the DCGA coming down hard on the airline, asking it to cut 10% of its scheduled flights till March.
