Good Morning Ishan,

 

Remember college? Just yesterday I caught myself telling PGK about college, about pulling all-nighters and then being up at 8 AM only to make sure your friends were also up for their respective classes... Yeah, that's definitely not how my body clock functions anymore.
 
But this conversation was sparked by a little nugget of information he shared with me. Our student readers love to read us at 1 AM. That's right.
 
We have two awesome B-schools—namely the Indian School of Business, Hyderabad and Harvard Business School—who have signed up with us through the campus subscription programme. And the option is open to the rest of you studying or teaching at colleges. Details here: https://the-ken.com/blog/if-you-unlock-it-they-will-come/
 
This excites me, I'll be honest. As a journalist, I've worked in fairly diverse, divergent places. But the student subscribers kick it up a notch, because now, we're putting out journalism also to supplement education. And while that is a social responsibility for all of journalism, we're certainly a lot more answerable. That, truly, is exciting.
 
Speaking of journalism, here's all that we had this week.
 
On Monday, Seema wrote about Multiples Equity and its founder Renuka Ramnath. Multiples' maiden hospital investment isn't in a good place, and as Ramnath's first fund nears its end, is an exit on the cards?
 
Harveen, on Tuesday, looked at what your kids are watching these days. Indian kids’ entertainment is moving towards more local characters like Little Singham. But that could pose a few challenges.
 
We had a contribution from Olina Banerjee about India's water woes on Wednesday. A free story, it looks at what makes India focus on supply instead of controlling demand for water. Go on, you should read this one.
 
Shashi took a quick look into the (very near) future in his Thursday piece on eSIMs, the Apple Watch Series 3, and the immediate reality of Internet of Things. "These eSIMs might open the floodgates for IoT adoption in the country," he writes.
 
The week closed with Ashish's interview with Jatin Singh, the weatherman. Founder of Skymet, Singh talks about Indian monsoons, agriculture and all that he can do to help. But isn't able to.
 
That's all for this week. Time to buy yourself an annual subscription? Give it a think, c'mon.
 
Enjoy your weekend!
