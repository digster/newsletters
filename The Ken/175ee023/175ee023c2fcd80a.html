Good Morning Ishan,
 
There are always two roads. Remember Robert Frost's poem The Road Not Taken? I often revisit it in my head.

"Two roads diverged in a wood, and I—
I took the one less travelled by,
And that has made all the difference."

Now, I'm not sure which one is less travelled by, but our stories this week looked at companies that chose either of the two roads to get to their end goal. For some, it may have been a case of getting out of a sticky situation; for others, it may have been an opportune moment to profit. The reasons differed, but the decision to get to an end goal by hook or by crook says a lot about a company.

We started our week with Maitri's story on Fortis Healthcare. Fortis, which is among India's largest private healthcare chains, wants to get rid of its popular branding. Wait, why? Because of its association with dubious promoters. It now wants to call itself Parkway. There's a hook to cover up the crook.

On Tuesday, Pratap looked into the guys that are busy outing every company out there. Cyble, a two-year-old cyber threat intelligence firm, has made a name for itself by naming massive startups like RedDoorz and Bigbasket for data breaches. But how does it find these breaches? And more importantly, why do the victims need to subscribe to Cyble to get real help? It's a free story; don't miss this one.

Midweek, we switched gears with Bhumika's story on the crumbling co-living industry. After all, who wants to share space during Covid? Bhumika, though, says occupancy levels are slowly recovering, but co-living can no longer function exactly as it did before. It's an industry switching up to cater to a whole new, cautious world.

Thursday saw Seetharaman write about a company that somehow found the hook to climb back up even though no one expected it to. Nature's Basket 2.0 under Spencer's Retail's umbrella has managed to turn itself around in 2020—a year when everyone else crashed and burned. It's secret recipe? Good old gourmet retail—something Spencer's is now ready to scale on. It can't be easy, though.

The final story this past week came from Olina and Pranav. The duo spent days getting the inner workings of Lithium, India's largest all-electric cab fleet operator. Lithium has seen one too many leadership changes, and with every iteration, it has burned some serious fuel. Irony, amirite. It even saw an exodus of top executives in the past year. Read to know what went down.

Praveen came back from his vacay this week and dived straight into our Saturday newsletter, The Nutgraf. This week, the edition featured two major media companies—BuzzFeed and HuffPost. One acquired the other in an all-stock deal, but there’s a Monty Hall problem to deal with. I’ll let you read and find out.

That was our week. Hope you're having a solid weekend. November is almost over; got any December plans in motion?

Cheers,
