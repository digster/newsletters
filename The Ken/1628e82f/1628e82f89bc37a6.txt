Good Morning Ishan,

I’m not in love with my bank. I think I last visited them during the demonetisation drive in late 2016; I ended up arguing with the manager when he asked me to fill lengthy forms for a couple of grand I was depositing. I’ve declined to have a Relationship Manager for some years now because I realised they only sold (or tried to sell) lemons during financial closing.

But, today, they have my sympathy. High on NPAs, low on public trust and squeezed in the middle by fintechs. Even by Point of Sale terminal aggregators. One of them just raised $82 million, getting itself valued at nearly $1 billion. Really. Is this PoS space so strategic?

During the digital payments drive and note ban of 2016, in just three months, banks hooked merchants up with 1 million more PoS terminals, taking the total to 3 million. But in a country with 50 million merchants, that leaves a huge headroom for growth. Then there are 800 million debit cards, only half of which are in active use. Converting those users to adopt payments can bulk up transactions.

Still, banks are struggling. The government gave banks a 5.5 million target to reach by Sept 2017. Having missed that, banks now look to PoS aggregators for expansion. The opportunities to ride on each other’s strengths are ample, but they look deceptively simple. In this three-legged race, who is going to be the ‘PoS Boss’?
