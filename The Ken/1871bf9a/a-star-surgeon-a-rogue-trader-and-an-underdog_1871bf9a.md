Tomorrow is World Theatre Day. This reminds me of my first-ever experience on stage—or rather, the lead-up to it.
I was in 5th standard when I had my first brush with acting. I studied in a missionary school in Kolkata, where "theatre" meant teachers picking a chapter out of the Bible and making us enact it in a play on stage. It was a ticket to fame. Participants were addressed as their characters for almost a month after the performance. It felt nice to be recognised among 1,200 students.
So, when one of my teachers picked me and two of my friends as actors who’d be on stage the longest, I was elated. She didn’t tell us about the roles but asked us to come prepared the next day. I spent hours in front of the mirror, trying to perfect my expressions and dialogue delivery.
I reached the school the next day, thinking I would be the next Shah Rukh Khan. The teacher walked in with a script, looked at me, and said: “Rounak, you’ll be on stage throughout. You’re tall. You’ll be the tree around which Adam and Eve will act.”
And there I was, standing in brown trousers and a green tee, with my arms stretched wide to look like branches of a tree on the final day. But hey, don’t feel bad. Thanks to my stellar performance, I went on to play a lamp post, giraffe, and other critical characters in my short stint as an actor.
