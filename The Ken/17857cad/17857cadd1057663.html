In world football, India is often referred to as the 'sleeping tiger'. No Indians play in any of the world's top leagues, and India has never played in the sport's ultimate competition, the FIFA World Cup. It seems implausible that a country of over one billion suffers from such a paucity of talent in the world's most popular sport.

The Tata Group is to India's FMCG space what India is to world football. One of India's largest conglomerates—with interests in everything from automobiles to apparel and steel to salt—Tata never cracked the FMCG space the way a company of its size and reputation should have done.

And while it built a few category leaders like Tata Tea and Tata Salt, it lags far behind FMCG majors such as ITC and Hindustan Unilever. Until very recently, though, much like India in football, Tata seemed content with being an also-ran.

In the last few years, however, Tata seems to have finally woken up. Realising that the sands were shifting under its feet, it instituted a raft of changes in its FMCG business.

In 2019, it merged its Tata Beverages and the food side of its Tata Chemicals businesses into a single consumer goods enterprise. It changed its FMCG leadership, opting for sector veterans over company loyalists. It even revamped its distribution practices. Its most recent signal of intent was the ~$21.5 million acquisition of healthy cereal and snacks brand Soulfull.

Tata is definitely shaking off its decades-long FMCG slumber, but will it be enough for it to regain lost ground on its rivals?
