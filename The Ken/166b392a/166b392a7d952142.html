Good Morning Ishan,
 
And just like that, October is slipping away. As I look forward to festive lights—Diwali, Christmas, New Year, they're all coming soon—I can't help but wonder where the year went. But that's everything, isn't it? Everything has a moment of inception, when it starts; then progression, when it's built; then there's high-drama, emotion, conflict, big changes; and finally, it ends.
 
Everything.
 
A day has the same cycle. So does a week, a year, and well, all that our stories mapped over the last six days.
 
Monday brought us the story of a new beginning. Google is adding shopping in India, and Olina and Arundhati's story looked at how the company's search for relevance has taken it to a "Shopping" tab on its search engine. More e-commerce eh?
 
The day before that, on TKOW we had a free story on ANI by Harveen and Pranav. It was all about how one builds something, and in ANI's case, with stupendous success. The family-run media company has established a virtual monopoly over delivering news video feeds to TV channels in India. How? Read the story.
 
Gayathri's piece on Tuesday looking into the conflict that looms over the production and sale of syringes in India. The syringe-makers have split into two factions: Indian and foreign companies; and both are battling for market share.
 
Our Thursday piece by Salman on health insurers looked into a very real threat. The data of more than 450 million lives in India is up for grabs. Personal health data is invaluable to insurers, and they’re out to get it.
 
Harveen's Friday piece was an important reminder about how even what we consider most unchanging eventually changes. Winter is shrinking; it's down to two months from five, and this means that winter wear is quickly going out of fashion.
 
And then there was our story about a definite end. Ashish wrote about Internet Freedom Foundation on Wednesday and how it no longer has the robust board of trustees it once did. About how friends went their separate ways. "The public versions of why they broke up are platitudes. Reams of it. The more private reason is simpler. Almost basal to human beings; a heady mix of self-righteousness conflated with disagreements," he writes. It's free; give it a read.
 
That's all we had this week. Looking forward to the fresh start that comes with tomorrow's story; do check it out.
 
Have a great weekend, all.
