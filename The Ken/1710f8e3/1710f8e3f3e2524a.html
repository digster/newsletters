Xiaomi has a very particular way of operating in India.

It enters established category, blitzes incumbents with its feature-packed, cut-price offerings, and laughs all the way to the bank.

Take smartphones. It entered in 2014. Just over half a decade later, it sells more smartphones in the country than any other company. Incumbents like Samsung, once seemingly infallible, are left in its dust.

Or smart TVs. Where Xiaomi sauntered into a club dominated by the likes of Sony, Samsung, and other majors, and now controls 27% of the market.

Its fashionably late strategy has served the Chinese electronics giant well. So when one hears that it is trying to play pioneer in a nascent sector, one takes pause. Especially when that space is smart appliances, and you consider that broadband penetration in urban India is still at a woeful 10%, while 5G is a distant dream.
 
So what gives? Is Xiaomi really changing its stripes and leading from the front? Or is it carefully stacking its hand and waiting for its moment to strike?
