Good Morning Ishan,
 
A few weeks back, I met someone at Google India. For lunch. This was one of those fulfilling, chat about everything under the sun kind of meetings. You know, the only good kind that’s there. In the world of agenda driven everything. So, we spoke. About this and that. Till we finally ended up talking about Facebook and WhatsApp; the privacy disaster that’s Cambridge Analytica and proliferation of fake news on WhatsApp. 
 
Me: Why don’t you do something about that?
 
Him: Us? Really?
 
Me: Yeah. You are Google. I am sure you can figure something out. 
 
Him: Well, I have been here a long time and I can tell you that Google is a lot of things. But social, huh, no. We have never been able to get social, and I don’t think we will. We are a company of engineers. Ask us to build a product? Sure. But social, it is just not us. 
 
This is true. It is not like Google didn’t try. Remember Google+...Circles? Sparks? That’s a tech disaster for the history books. I mean, when Google+ was being built, it was supposed to be the company’s answer to Facebook. The internet is littered with the corpses of social network companies that dubbed themselves as “Anti-Facebook”. And what about Facebook? Unstoppable. Facebook has more than two billion monthly active users. Five years ago, it only had a billion. In India alone, Facebook has more than 270 million users, the highest globally. Despite its scale, the company’s user count grew at 17% last year—growing as fast or faster than any year since 2012. WhatsApp? Well, hello. It is everywhere.
 
All of which brings me to today’s story and two individuals: Anand Mahindra and Baba Ramdev. Two successful Indian businessmen. Both great salesmen. Both with the unique ability to please the masses; except one toiled at Harvard and the other just twisted himself—in unfathomable, human and not so human ways. And coincidence is but a charm; both with the same idea. The idea of a social network for India. Which can be an alternative to Facebook and WhatsApp. 
 
One calls it Peepul. Of, By and For...Yes, that. The other called it Kimbho, walked into a privacy disaster and then twisted expectedly, looking in the other direction.
