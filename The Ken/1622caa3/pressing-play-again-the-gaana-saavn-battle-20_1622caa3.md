Good Morning Ishan,
Friday and all is fine but what are your thoughts on 'money makes the world go round'?
I mean, if someone spoke of music streaming last year, I would be like, let’s see when it becomes big. Let’s not waste our time on hipsters who swear by some music streaming service claiming its the best and all that. Hipsters like one of my co-founders and you know who. But then, suddenly, boom. Gaana, owned by Times Internet, has received millions of dollars in funding from Tencent. Amazon Music has launched. Saavn is looking to raise money so that it can stay in the game. Spotify is launching its streaming service. An app by telecom company Bharti called Wynk says it has millions of listeners. There’s Reliance Jio in the fray. There’s Apple Music.
There is no other way to say this. This is music to a reporter’s ears. In this case, my colleague Venkat's.
He says that India’s digital music industry is expected to reach Rs 3100 crore ($478 million) in revenue by 2020. The hypothesis is that the new Indian internet user is a media-first user, keen to guzzle data by consuming video, and indeed, music, which these companies hope makes for an attractive touch point. Each one of them wants to reach the 100 million active users mark. The two companies which you must watch out for are Gaana and Saavn. Gaana has the money and is the challenger. Saavn has years of experience in the business and must defend its turf.
What both of them are plotting plus where the music streaming business is headed in India is today’s story.
