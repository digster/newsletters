Good Morning Ishan,
Undergoing an identity crisis can be difficult, isn’t it?
The protagonist of today’s piece, Tata Digital, the digital arm of Tata Group, is experiencing something similar.
On one end is Mukesh Bansal, the architect behind Cult.fit and Myntra. He was hired by Tata Digital in 2021 to steer Tata Neu, the company’s super app. Bansal brought with him an agile, startup-like energy but was faced with a cultural mismatch at the newest venture of the over 150-year-old corporate house.
What ensued was a series of exits. In January 2023, Bansal stepped down, and many of his key hires followed suit.
“Internally, we used to call ourselves a startup. Maybe we still do,” one of the employees of Tata Digital told Aayush, the author of today’s story. “But, it’s nothing like one.”
Apart from the exits, Tata Digital also seems to be taking on too many tasks all at once. For instance, it’s planning to enter the ultra-competitive online travel-aggregator segment, build a wedding services business, and venture into beauty and cosmetics through the group’s apparel retailer, Trent.
Aayush digs deep and dishes out a meaty story on whether Tata Digital will emerge from this soul-searching with a redefined identity and a winning strategy: [https://the-ken.com/story/tata-digitals-identity-crisis-wants-to-be-a-startup-works-like-an-it-company/](https://the-ken.com/story/tata-digitals-identity-crisis-wants-to-be-a-startup-works-like-an-it-company/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (10-minute read)
