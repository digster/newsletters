No, not for toddlers, but this could soon replace the traditional 'birds and bees' talk. Dating, matchmaking, friending, whateveryoumaycallit, is now in the internet age. And therefore on an app.

The sidelong glances of yore (yes, it's that outdated) have been replaced with swiping left and right. Secret hand-written messages are now texts and sexts. A picture in a wallet is now the DP on some dating app. 

"But how popular are these apps in India," you ask? Last year, Tinder became the highest-paid-user app in India, unseating Netflix. Let that sink in.

Still, the traditional matrimony sites rule the revenue roost in India, if only by virtue of having been around longer. But the staggering rate at which Indians are matchmaking online (OkCupid has a 15X growth rate) means Cupid is slowly training his financial bow.

Still, after a great first date with India, it has to be an even better second date if these apps don't want to end up being ghosted for the third. So it is only natural that some dating-only apps such as TrulyMadly are trying to push users towards the altar.

Then there is the question of safety of the fairer sex - as big a worry online as it is offline. While India's offline sex ratio is skewed male, the balance is even more lopsided online - a 1:5 average. That, though, is an opportunity for some. Like Bumble, a women-focussed app that has shot up the ranking since its launch last December.

All in all, the online world of love is just as confusing as the offline world (some things never change). So, Pranav and Ruhi set out to map the land using data.
