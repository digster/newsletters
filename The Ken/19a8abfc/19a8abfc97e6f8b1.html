India’s e-bus dream is finally coming to life. The power grid can’t keep up

India plans to put 50,000 electric buses on the road by 2030, but lags in upgrading grids to sustainably power them. The result is an ad-hoc arrangement: buses plug into the same 11kV distribution lines meant for homes and corner shops. The price Havells paid to become a household name

Keeping dealers and customers happy hurts the AC maker’s profitability. An imminent energy-rating change and a weak summer fused it out

Havells reported a 5% increase in revenue in the September quarter, but its AC segment, Lloyd, saw an 18% decline from the year ago. Lloyd’s profitability has been under pressure for some time now, thanks to the brand’s soft corner for dealers and customers. Since Havells acquired Lloyd, the total capital employed in the brand stands at over Rs 3,000 crore. Yet, it has remained the third-largest AC player in India—the same position it held before the acquisition. But its first major hurdle is to get over its huge pile of unused inventory, which could soon be branded not saleable. [Read the full story]

Why Prosus boarded Ixigo’s Ship of Theseus

The Dutch investor exited Makemytrip before the latter’s stellar post-Covid rally. It’s now hitching its travel-tech hopes to a latecomer

Ixigo, a Rs 10,000-odd crore midcap company, saw its first cash infusion in October, 16 months after it listed on the bourses. Dutch tech-investment firm Prosus bought 15% stake in it, making it its first investment in a listed Indian firm and its second time in a travel-tech organisation. The marriage of the two comes at a time when Ixigo is at an inflection point of entering hotels, just like other online travel aggregators.
