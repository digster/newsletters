Nykaa is doing all it can to protect its turf. But not from Reliance Retail’s Tira 
 By Seetharaman G https://the-ken.com/writers/seetharamang/

4 min read

Good Morning Ishan,

A year ago, it looked like beauty-and-fashion e-tailer Nykaa was faced with its most formidable challenge in years: the launch of Reliance Retail’s Tira. Neither Flipkart nor Amazon, India’s two largest e-commerce companies, had been able to make a dent in Nykaa’s dominance in personal-care products. But here was India’s largest retailer looking to do an encore of its fashion portal, Ajio, in beauty. 

Lately, though, Nykaa has been busy figuring out how to fend off an entirely different kind of rival. Instant-delivery apps such as Zomato-owned Blinkit, Zepto, and Bigbasket have chosen beauty to establish their beachhead beyond grocery. 

They stock 4,000–6,000 stock-keeping units (SKUs) in categories such as colour cosmetics, skincare, haircare, and bath and body—or a mere 5–8% of Nykaa’s range—according to a recent research note by Kotak Institutional Equities, a broker. 

But look closer, and you’ll realise that the fight is not so lopsided. Take nail polish, for instance. Nykaa offers 475 items to choose from, and Blinkit has 130. And Bigbasket lists over 60 compact SKUs, which is one-fifth the variety on Nykaa. 

You may remember a piece https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/the-tyranny-of-choice-in-online-shopping-is-no-longer-a-given/ I wrote a couple of months ago where I made the case that quick commerce, with its limited choice in non-grocery segments, may finally deliver us from the decision paralysis so common in online shopping.

I was quick to dismiss Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart’s diversification into non-grocery products. But now, I love that they barely give me any choices in several items and have no reviews, which only reinforced the stasis I found myself in when I used to be a frequent Amazon user.  

I have purchased a bunch of things on quick-commerce apps, from a toiletry bag to shorts to tennis balls for my toddler. It’s not necessarily low-value items, though. Next on my list is carry-on luggage, which could cost Rs 8,000–10,000 (US$95–120). Once again, “good enough” is just fine by me. I’m not looking for the most-bought bag or the best-reviewed one.

But consumers tend to be more discerning with cosmetics than with, say, socks or sunglasses. They wouldn’t want to trust just about any brand with your skin or hair. And they may be partial to the mascara of one company while being vehemently opposed to using its concealer. 

What matters more than the options on quick-commerce apps is the fact that one can lay their hands on the product of their choice in just minutes. 

Nykaa knows it can’t take this lightly.

“So in FY21, on average, our order to delivery timelines are close to four days. Today, we have reduced that drastically, whereby the average order to delivery timeline on our platform across all 19,000 pincodes across the length and breadth of India is now 2.2 days.”

Anchit Nayar, Executive Director and CEO, Beauty E-Commerce, Nykaa, at the company’s earnings call https://www.bseindia.com/xml-data/corpfiling/AttachHis/9058bc0c-c8a8-4a06-b4c8-87b0c534b0d5.pdf for the April–June quarter results

As of June, one in every four orders placed on Nykaa was delivered the same day or the following day. By this month, the e-tailer expects that number to rise to one in two orders. Not surprisingly, Nykaa’s cost of storing, packing, and shipping beauty products, relative to their price, is the highest it has been in nearly two years. 

Nykaa’s response is a no-brainer—beauty brought in over 90% of Nykaa’s revenue in the June quarter. Nykaa’s parent FSN E-Commerce Ventures’ shares are up by a fifth this year, but the S&P BSE Midcap Index has risen by a third. FSN is down almost 50% from the day of its listing in late 2021. 

In June, I wrote https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/the-humbling-of-nykaa/ this about Nykaa’s fashion business:

Nykaa’s prospects in fashion seem even dimmer. Even if the market for fashion is five times as big as beauty. Nykaa’s fashion vertical should be growing at a much higher rate than beauty, considering it’s only a tenth of the latter by revenue. But in the year ended March, they both expanded at roughly 20%. On other metrics—such as active users and orders—fashion trails beauty. 

These are hardly the signs of a fashion giant in the making that investors were hoping for.

Which leaves Nykaa no choice but to fiercely guard its mainstay.
