Good Morning Ishan,
 
I was in Barcelona for a week this August when a forest fire almost engulfed a train. When I returned to Germany the next week, thousands of dead fish were found washed ashore along the Oder river on the German-Polish border. 
 
Hearing of such extreme events within a month makes me feel like we’re living through apocalyptic times. It’s a terrifying thought, but it’s also an everyday reality. Extreme weather events, floods, heatwaves, and droughts are becoming more and more commonplace. 
 
A recent report said that a quarter of the global workforce is vulnerable to climate change. It puts the onus on businesses to shake things up and move us towards a sustainable future. Top players have again pushed for renewable solutions in an open letter ahead of the 27th Conference of Parties (COP27). 
 
Renewable energy-based products are the need of the hour as automobile is one of the biggest carbon-emitting industries. As a result, investments in electric-vehicle makers are mounting.   
 
We also opened our week with the story of an Indian startup that is shaking things up with its new F77 electric bike. Shruti delved into six-year-old Ultraviolette that is set to launch its first product with a range of 307 km on a single charge—the highest in any electric two-wheeler in India. But its rivals are also upping the ante. 
 
Soumyajit, on Tuesday, reported that a new war is brewing in the Indian OTT space, with Viacom 18 winning the streaming rights for the Indian Premier League (IPL). Viacom18’s sister firm Reliance Jio pulled out of an important bundling contract with Hotstar in October, ending a fairytale Reliance-Disney collaboration.
 
On Wednesday, Nadine brought us the story of Blibli—Indonesia’s third tech giant to list on the local bourse. It has raised US$510 million, despite tech stocks underperforming on the local stock exchange. But the IPO proceeds can’t carry the loss-making Blibli for more than six months. 
 
The Tamil Nadu government is rolling out an unprecedented public-health programme called Makkalai Thedi Maruthuvam. It has been working informally with Google for a few years to create a population health registry (PHR). Pratap sheds light on their work together and tells us why the state must spell out data-sharing and privacy-related provisions for the PHR to succeed. 
 
Aayush and Gaurav on Friday told us why Urban Company withdrew from its second international market, Australia, just three years after starting the operations there. The home-services marketplace’s focus is now shifting from accelerated growth to cash conservation before it reconsiders its IPO plans. 
 
That’s all for this week. I’d be really happy to hear your ideas on tackling climate change and what businesses can do to help us move forward.
