I was in Barcelona for a week this August when a forest fire almost engulfed a train. When I returned to Germany the next week, thousands of dead fish were found washed ashore along the Oder river on the German-Polish border.
Hearing of such extreme events within a month makes me feel like we’re living through apocalyptic times. It’s a terrifying thought, but it’s also an everyday reality. Extreme weather events, floods, heatwaves, and droughts are becoming more and more commonplace.
A recent report said that a quarter of the global workforce is
[vulnerable](https://the-ken.com/greenmargins/microsoft-lends-its-green-creds-to-linkedin/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) to climate change. It puts the onus on businesses to shake things up and move us towards a sustainable future. Top players have again
[pushed](https://www.wsj.com/articles/corporate-executives-call-for-action-based-approach-at-cop27-11667854336?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) for renewable solutions in an open letter ahead of the 27th Conference of Parties (COP27).
Renewable energy-based products are the need of the hour as automobile is one of the biggest carbon-emitting industries. As a result, investments in electric-vehicle makers are mounting.

[F77 electric bike](https://the-ken.com/story/tvs-backed-ultraviolette-set-to-kick-start-e-bike-race-as-bigger-rivals-loom/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter).
