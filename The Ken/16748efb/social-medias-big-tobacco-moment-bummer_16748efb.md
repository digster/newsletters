"Behaviors of Users Modified, and Made into an Empire for Rent”. BUMMER.
That’s quite a term, I know, but it’s the one at the heart of one of the sparsest, clearest and starkest books I’ve read in recent times. It’s called "Ten Arguments for Deleting Your Social Media Accounts Right Now”, which, I know, sounds like a BuzzFeed title. It’s anything but. Written by one of Silicon Valley’s earliest visionaries and scientists, Jaron Lanier, it makes the undeniable argument that social media, as it exists today, is toxic. And the best way to respond to that is to quit it.
This Sunday, Pranav and Ashish delve into the new avant-garde of tech: social media quitters. Deleting your social media accounts is the new cutting the (cable TV) cord. Or is it the new quitting cigarettes?
I quit TV years ago. Then quit Facebook in 2013, when Edward Snowden showed us the surveillance foundations underneath the global consumer internet. Never signed up on Instagram.
Then, after reading Lanier’s book, I deactivated Twitter. After a 15-day break, I unfollowed all the 2800+ folks I followed and now use it very sparingly.
My last frontier is quitting WhatsApp.
But this isn’t about me. It’s about you. And your sanity.
