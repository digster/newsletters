If you have been listening, man, everyone has been talking about Dropbox. The software as a service (SaaS) company which said that it is headed for an Initial Public Offering (IPO). And just for good measure, it threw in that it has crossed $1 billion in revenue. Jesus, that’s a lot of revenue. So impressive is the number that my colleague Sumanth actually got inspired by The Social Network while introducing it.
 
“A billion dollars in valuation isn't cool.”
You know what's cool? 
“A billion dollars in revenue.”
 
A Unicorn…by revenue...not by valuation.
 
Dropbox. A Revenue Unicorn.
 
In today’s story, he has picked several important bits from Dropbox’s S-1. Like the long, hard road for tech startups to get to an IPO. The scale for Freemium. The continuous hunt for gross margins to build a viable business. This and much more. Sumanth’s point is simple. The Dropbox IPO is a watershed moment for SaaS companies in India, and also, the rest of the world. Specifically for Indian SaaS companies, this is the time to learn and use it to build a credible, viable business. 
 
The piece reads well. Here: https://the-ken.com/story/dropbox-files-unpacking-ipo-filing-billion-dollar-saas-startup/
