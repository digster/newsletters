Good Morning Ishan,
For at least 20 years, Tanishq has been the undisputed king of the Indian jewellery-retail market—a gem in the Tata group’s crown. It has it all: market dominance, dazzling brand recognition, and a storied legacy to boot.
But now, the throne of the brand owned by the US$35.7 billion Titan Company is wobbling.
Regional challengers like West Bengal’s Senco Gold and Kerala’s Kalyan Jewellers and Malabar Gold & Diamonds are aiming for the national spotlight. The market is whispering of the upheaval: Titan’s stock is down 9% this year, while Kalyan and Senco have surged more than 50%.
These rivals are cleverly turning Tanishq’s own tactics against it, chipping away at its once-unassailable lead with the very strategies that once gave it an edge.
Shoppers, too, are flocking to these rivals for things Tanishq didn’t anticipate. For one, Tanishq’s grand stores and high-end branding are starting to look a bit tired compared to the sleek, agile rival brands.
On the revenue front, Tanishq’s 9% growth rate in Q1 of FY25 might seem respectable, but it’s dwarfed by Kalyan Jewellers’ about 24% rise—albeit at different bases. Malabar Gold is not far behind, adding further squeeze. Meanwhile, sales figures for most of them are now similar, and the action has also moved from the metros to the heartlands, where Tanishq’s grip is slipping.
Now, Tanishq’s market share in this fragmented industry and Titan’s status as the third most valuable Tata stock are at stake. Can it keep its crown?
As an analyst put it: “The pie is growing bigger, but not as fast as the number of players entering. As they say in the markets, ‘a rising tide lifts all boats’.”
