Good Morning Ishan,
As a Gen Zer, I’m used to hearing critiques of our generation from everyone—from bosses to random uncles to columnists online. The refrain is often something like, we don’t know how to work hard; we’re too demanding; we quit at the drop of a hat etc etc. In response, I usually crack a self-deprecating joke and chalk it all up to generational differences.
But, as Arundhati writes in today’s story, there’s a general ambitionless-ness infecting the Indian workforce across age-groups in 2025. So yaa, it’s not just Gen Zers, it’s employees across one to 20 years of experience. This is especially true for IT and new-age tech employees, who’ve borne the brunt of rampant layoffs, AI disruption, and slowing growth over the years.
As one TCS executive with 13 years of experience put it, she sees no point in being ambitious when the company itself is on a budget freeze—bringing nothing and no one new to the table. Another 38-year-old product manager at a ride-hailing company admits that he “just doesn’t care anymore”, especially considering he’ll have to pull off miracles to grow in his career.
The Ken surveyed about 600 employees across companies such as TCS, ITC, Nykaa, Accenture, Google, Amazon, among others to understand how ambitious they are about their careers in 2025. The results were revelatory.
