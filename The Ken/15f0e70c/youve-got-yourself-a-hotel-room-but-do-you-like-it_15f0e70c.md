Good Morning Ishan,
God came to me in my dreams and told me coffee is the way to secure a pass to heaven. That and some uncharitable stuff about green tea.
Now, there’s a certain dread I have when I’m travelling for work, staying in hotels and trying to make coffee first thing in the morning. A while back, I was stunned to note the presence of coffee, the other useless ingredients and the absence of an electric kettle. To top it, the phone wasn’t working. Or somehow I couldn’t figure out which number would work, 9, 0, 3; no luck. So off I went, all the way down to the reception and got myself the kettle, with a face and voice of avoidable irritation.
Speaking of irritation, man, I struggle with the television like nobody’s business. Those TV and set-top box remotes...how do you make them work? This is why this quote from today’s story amuses me no end. The quote is from Vaibhav Aggarwal, the founder and CEO of FabHotels: “You know, budget hotels is a very simple space,” he says. “All that travellers need is a good experience. And that’s what you have to give them.”
Full marks for brevity and clarity of purpose.
The question though, is, how do you get there? And once you do get there, are you going to make any money? Think about it like this. Oyo Rooms and its millions of dollars are chasing this. Same for FabHotels. Same for Treebo.
Between the three of them, they have raised $700 million in funding. A little over Rs 4000 crore. On top of that, there are the aggregators. Let me throw in something more. In India, about 75% of the bookings of budget hotels come through aggregators. It changes to 50% when it comes to starred hotel chains.
So, who has got the upper hand? What exactly is the business model for FabHotels and Treebo? That’s the story from my colleague Patanjali. (This is not a typo, the dude’s name is actually Patanjali).
