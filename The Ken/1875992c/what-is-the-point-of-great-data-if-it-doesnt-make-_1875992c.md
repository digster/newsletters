Today, on account of Good Friday, we aren’t publishing our regular news stories. We are, however, sharing the[ story](https://the-ken.com/blog/the-missing-link-between-data-and-growth-is-a-narrative/) of how professionals from three different organisations who participated in our Narrative Workshops—FMCG giant ITC, data analysis startup Prescience Decision Solutions, and humanitarian nutrition think tank Sight and Life—went on to apply data storytelling skills to improve their and their organisations’ performance.
Having a eureka moment is easy. The real challenge is persuading people to act on the insight.
This is the story of every data professional in high-growth, ambitious companies. Analysing mountains of data gives you answers—like what went wrong with a particular business decision. Or where they should be investing in the future. The skills and technology needed to look for the proverbial needle in the haystack is available. Abundantly.
But dozens of founders, senior management executives, and data professionals tell us that what isn’t abundant is the ability to persuade living, breathing human beings. They could be investors, customers, or talent you want to recruit—all of them can choose not to trust even carefully-mined, diligently-finessed, and carefully-presented data analyses.
Unless you weave it into a compelling narrative that helps them make a decision.
