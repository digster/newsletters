What caught my attention was the idea of вҖҳTethered CatsвҖҷ. Let me tell you a short story. Once upon a time, a Taoist teacher and his disciples lived in a monastery in the Chinese hills. Every evening when the monks meditated, a stray cat would mewl, scratch, and annoy them. One day, the irritated monk told his followers to put a collar on the cat and tether it on the far side of the monastery. This worked for a while.

One day, the teacher died. But the monks continued to tie up the cat. Years passed. Eventually, the cat died too. The monks went down to the village, found a replacement for the cat, and started tying it up each evening.

Two centuries later, religious scholars would write books on the importance of tying up a cat before evening meditations.
