Good Morning Ishan,
 
I recently started reading Hitting Against the Spin by Ben Jones and Nathan Leamon. It’s a fascinating book that uses data to illustrate the inner workings of modern cricket. Full of charts, graphs, and diagrams, it is a masterclass on visual storytelling.
 
What caught my attention was the idea of ‘Tethered Cats’. Let me tell you a short story. Once upon a time, a Taoist teacher and his disciples lived in a monastery in the Chinese hills. Every evening when the monks meditated, a stray cat would mewl, scratch, and annoy them. One day, the irritated monk told his followers to put a collar on the cat and tether it on the far side of the monastery. This worked for a while.
 
One day, the teacher died. But the monks continued to tie up the cat. Years passed. Eventually, the cat died too. The monks went down to the village, found a replacement for the cat, and started tying it up each evening.
 
Two centuries later, religious scholars would write books on the importance of tying up a cat before evening meditations.
 
There are many Tethered Cats in cricket. I would say the world of business is also full of habitual actions that have long outlived the conditions that created them. On Thursday, Vanita wrote about one such Tethered Cat: employment contracts. Extensive notice periods, cruel non-compete clauses, and enforced in-person office hours have become conventions that companies are still clinging to. But things are slowly changing.
 
On Friday, Anand and Prajakta wrote about India's real estate sector and a slice of population that's traditionally not known to engage with it—millennials. With work-from-home becoming the norm, people under the age of 35 have been strongly considering buying a home. However, the recent RBI rate hike and increasing inflation might change things yet again. On Tuesday, Seetharaman and Pratap wrote about Absolute. The playbook to agritech success seems to help farmers with one or a combination of: sourcing inputs, getting financing, or selling their produce. Instead, Absolute is focussing on R&D investments. They have recently raised $100 million, just 2.5 years after raising their seed round.
 
Jaspreet, on Monday, wrote about RBI’s new regulation: card tokenisation. More specifically, how and why the RBI extended the deadline by three months.
 
Rachel opened her account with The Ken on Wednesday with her story on Zalora, a Southeast Asian fashion retailer. To improve upon its bottom line, the company expanded into India, tying up Flipkart’s Myntra/Jabong. But expanding into India is far from easy.
