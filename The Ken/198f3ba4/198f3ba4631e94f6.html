It may seem like India’s four-wheeler EV market is small.

After all, just four out of every 100 cars sold are EVs—but it is scaling quickly: from 4,210 EVs sold in 2020 to 158,000 in 2024, per a Niti Aayog report. And local incumbents like Tata Motors and Mahindra already own more than half the market, thanks to their existing and extensive dealer and after-sales network.

Any new player that has to take share from them will have to go head-to-head with that scale on every front. Vinfast, Vietnam’s largest automaker, believes it has what it takes to make a dent in India’s EV car sales.

It has committed $2 billion to the country, including a $500 million plant in Tamil Nadu capable of producing 150,000 cars per year. The first two models to roll out of there will be the VF6 and VF7 SUVs.

But Vinfast wants to change the way you buy cars, starting with where and how.

Cracking India is important in more ways than one for the carmaker. It has already burned billions trying to break into the US and European markets. And with Prime Minister Narendra Modi highlighting India’s aim to export EVs to over 100 countries, for foreign automakers like Vinfast, India is both a growth market and a potential export hub.
