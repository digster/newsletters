I think the space and timing of a conversation make a world of difference. For instance, this week, I ended up with the chattiest Uber driver in all of Bangalore. During the two-hour ride to the airport (I know!), I went through a mountain of his life events—his education, his marriage, his ambition of owning a fleet of cabs, and bits of conversations he carried from other passengers.
As his life unfolded while we zoomed through the twilight bokeh of oncoming traffic, the weight of his entirely separate but equally vibrant existence started to weigh on me. I think there's even a word for that feeling—sonder.
As a reporter, it's essential that we step into other people's shoes. Of course, that is extremely difficult, and honestly, I am still terrible at it. But we keep trying.
This Thursday, Aayush's story on the
[delivery personnel crunch](https://the-ken.com/story/how-zomato-swiggy-and-co-can-refill-their-delivery-rider-tank/?r_edition=2&utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) portrayed the tribulations and opportunities facing the country’s 15 million gig workers, most of who work in the hyperlocal delivery industry. The story also decoded the pain points delivery companies will need to work on to tide over this crunch.
But the hyperlocal economy is not the only turmoiled sector we wrote about this week. On Monday, Pratap and Seema scored a scoop on
[Amazon’s possible investment](https://the-ken.com/story/why-amazon-is-vis-likely-financial-saviour/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) into the ailing telecom sector through Vi, which sent the telco's stock soaring.
On Tuesday, my story looked into fantasy sports giant
[Dream11’s venture capital dreams](https://the-ken.com/story/the-emperor-buys-clothes-dream11s-us250m-moat-with-dream-capital/?r_edition=2&utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). Its parent company Dream Sports is trying to use investments in the sporting and adjacent industries to build a conglomerate and de-risk its revenue streams.
On Wednesday, Arundhati turned the spotlight on the
[catch-up game Indian banks have been playing](https://the-ken.com/story/indias-banks-got-schooled-on-digital-payments-now-they-want-to-even-the-score/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) with popular digital payments apps like PhonePe and Google Pay, as they look to regain supremacy in merchant payment transactions.
Jaspreet ended the week with a story on
[PhonePe’s finserv ambitions](https://the-ken.com/story/one-playbook-two-strategies-phonepes-finserv-ambitions-come-from-paytm-stock/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), as it tries to emulate the Paytm diversification playbook of building out a profitable wealth management vertical. And although the ploy left Paytm in a soup of sorts, PhonePe has a few tricks up its sleeve.
That's all from us this week. As for me, I just started on a tiny mid-year vacation that will see me hop across a bunch of places, so I am looking forward to lots of impromptu conversations with complete strangers.
I hope you get to have one soon, too.
