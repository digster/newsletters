Soybean meal is the main protein component of chicken feed, and thus crucial to raising healthy broiler birds. In Indonesia’s chicken industry, however, soybean is one of the main obstacles to fully unlocking the poultry segment’s growth potential.
It takes a lot to grow chickens and sell their meat, but in terms of costs, chicken feed accounts for 65% of the total expenses of raising chickens to maturity. Soybean meal makes up a bulk of that feed.
This is where the obstacle comes in. Indonesia does not grow enough soybean, partly due to its climate and partly due to restrictions on genetically modified crops in the country. Currently, Indonesia imports up to 95% of its soybean needs from countries such as the USA or Brazil.
While Indonesia is easing regulations around GMO commercialisation, which will allow the country to grow more soybeans, it is going to take years for the benefits to show.
But chickens need to eat and Indonesians need chicken. As it stands, local entrepreneurs and poultry industry leaders are trying to find solutions to the chicken-feed problem and to optimise chicken growth.
