Good Morning Ishan,
 
American politician Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (also known as AOC) recently spoke about the importance of paying for news to her 6.5 million followers on Instagram. Ocasio-Cortez is the youngest woman to ever serve in the United States Congress. She’s also a woman of colour, serving as an inspiration to many.
 
As an organisation that solely relies on subscriptions, her message resonated with us. People want to be well-informed now more than ever and they’re cognizant of the news sources that they trust.
 
A major part of this rests on our ability to bring you in-depth and well-reported stories, ones that dissect business models and analyse the moves made in the industry. This week, however, was a little different. We covered not only interesting businesses but also the people who form the backbones of these businesses. A company is only as good as its people, right?
 
We had Aneesh Reddy, CEO of Capillary Technologies. In one of my favourite formats called The Interview, our co-founder Sumanth sat down with Aneesh to chat about Capillary's choices, decisions, successes, and failures. The 12-year-old company has more than US$100 million in funding, around 1,000+ employees, and an ARR of close to US$50 million. It is an intensely candid and self-reflective interview, so you don’t want to miss this one!
 
Next up, we had Brahmal Vasudevan, the founder and CEO of Malaysia-based private equity firm Creador. To date, it has over US$1.5 billion under its management; half of its exits have come from the Indian market, with the rest from Malaysia and Indonesia. My colleague Kay from Malaysia pieced together Vasudevan's approach and Creador's strategy to provide a lucid insight into what makes the firm tick.
 
Last, but definitely not the least, we had Reliance Industries’ chairman Mukesh Ambani. What’s he after now, you ask? Well, Reliance Jio wants all of India’s 300 million 2G user base to itself. But there’s a catch. Reliance Jio does not have 2G technology; it boasts of a network that is entirely 4G. And hence Mukesh Ambani wants a “2G-mukt (free)” India. And patience is not a strategy Jio is known for.
 
We also had two industry specials this week, deep-dives into possibly two of the worst-hit industries this year—Fashion and Auto. The $55 billion Indian auto industry saw the worst decline in sales in two decades, in 2019. And the lockdown drove the sector deeper into a sinkhole. Automakers and dealers had to find immediate digital fixes to get back on the road. Kunal chronicled the digital transformation underway in a sector that contributes 7% to India's GDP.
 
Meanwhile, in the world of fashion, there is just chaos. About 80% of the stores have now reopened. But an open store doesn’t mean business as usual. Seasons have changed in the time they stayed shut and summer wear piled up. Not only do they need to rid themselves of that, but they also have to sell winter wear now. But sadly, the only fashion season that's left at this point is the pandemic season.
 
We also covered a range of topics in this week's edition of The Nutgraf, 'Chain Reaction': India's GDP growth, Vodafone Idea's debt woes, AT&T putting its ad-tech division up for sale, and more. It's a must-read.
 
That’s all from me. I hope you enjoyed some of the stories this week at The Ken. Do show some love by sharing them with your friends and family or on social media.
