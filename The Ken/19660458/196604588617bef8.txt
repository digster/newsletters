Tata Elxsi gives the market something it desperately wants—hope. This time, it promises to do better in revenue and margins, courtesy of a few new deals

Quick quiz. A listed company posts its lowest quarterly profit in at least three years. It misses analysts’ expectations big time to hand out a “negative earnings surprise”. It is in the midst of major uncertainty: AI on one side and Trump’s tantrums on the other.

How should the stock of this company react?

It should tank, you say?

Well, in normal times, that’s the right answer. But it’s anything but normal now.

The Tata Elxsi stock shot up a neat 10% on Monday—the first trading day after it announced March quarter results—and rose another 2% or so on Tuesday. That’s about a 13% gain in two sessions. Never mind its profit dropped 13% over the same period a year ago, the second consecutive quarter of decline.

What gives?

The engineering, research and development specialist from the Tatas pulled a rabbit from its hat. Three, actually. And it took the fight to the market bears, waiting to bleed it some more.

The stock at its peak in 2022 had crossed the Rs 10,000 mark and hit a market capitalisation of about Rs 73,000 crore ($8.5 billion). Last August, the stock hovered around the Rs 9,000 mark, only to crash by nearly 50% until a few days back.

In Tata Elxsi, the market had lost something crucial: hope.

The stock consistently got crazy high valuations in the past—over 100X price-to-earnings at one point. But then, the company did not deliver on its promise of huge growth as a niche engineering player. Its profit stagnated or declined over the past two years.

Punishment, inevitable, eventually followed for the stock. Until Monday, when it took off.

At play again is hope. From three key announcements—two big deal wins in segments that Tata Elxsi has been struggling with, and an entry into a new category that many investors love.

First, the company has signed a three-year $100 million product engineering consolidation deal with a marquee player in the media and communication segment—the largest in its history. (For context, the deal size is almost a quarter of Tata Elxsi’s annual revenue for the year ended March 2025.)

Next, a €50 million multiyear software-engineering deal with a European company in the automotive segment. And finally, it announced entry into the aerospace and defence vertical, inking agreements with the likes of Hindustan Aeronautics and other players.

Plus, there’s another $10 million deal with a global broadcaster.

“These deal wins are commendable in this environment. It’s short-term good news,” said Pareekh Jain, chief executive of IT consultancy EIIR Trend.

Tata Elxsi expects these wins and the new vertical to contribute to its revenue and margin soon.

“Margins would improve with the growth in business. I think one of the key reasons for the margin decline has been the revenue degrowth. So very clearly, our focus is to bring back the revenue growth which will automatically bring back the margin growth,” said Manoj Raghavan, CEO of Tata Elxsi in the company’s March quarter earnings call.

“So, we’re really hoping that we will be able to deliver better performance [in FY26] than [in] FY25,” he added.

The market seems to be sharing the hope and optimism, at least for now.

But not everyone is convinced about things turning around. Research house Morgan Stanley downgraded the stock, noting: “Despite some deal wins, the lack of supportive macro tailwinds and continued softness in key segments could weigh on the stock.” The threat of US tariffs in particular remains an overhang on the automotive segment, the largest contributor to Tata Elxsi’s revenue. About a third of the company’s revenue comes from the Americas.

Other research houses like JP Morgan, too, have their doubts.

Management aims for better fiscal 2026 growth than in financial year 2025, but JPMorgan expects otherwise due to persistent macro headwinds.

— Tata Elxsi’s Q4 Review: Brokerages See Limited Upside Amid Macro Uncertainty, NDTV Profit https://www.ndtvprofit.com/quarterly-earnings/tata-elxsis-q4-review-brokerages-see-limited-upside-amid-macro-uncertainty

Both brokerages have cut their stock price targets, citing overvaluation. The stock still trades at a princely 44X earnings, higher than most IT-service companies and its engineering-services peers.

That takes me to the story I wrote https://the-ken.com/story/tata-tech-just-merge-with-tata-elxsi/ in January about why Tata Technologies should merge with Tata Elxsi.

The time is ripe for the listed, niche engineering entities within the Tata Group to join and spare their shareholders the woes.

The business and stock fortunes of both these engineering-service companies were dwindling, and their investors were left in the lurch, I wrote then. Their overlapping exposure to the beleaguered auto industry and the potential to churn out a bigger entity with varied, complementary offerings made a strong case for a merger.

Tata Technologies is set to declare its March quarter results this Friday. And while the stock has been perking up over the past few days, much will depend on whether it manages to get out of the rut and grow profit. Or at least, make big-ticket sentiment-boosting announcements like Tata Elxsi.

In any case, the case for the merger stays.

A stronger, bigger, single Tata engineering-services company will likely weather the AI-Trump storm better, both as a business and on the bourses.
