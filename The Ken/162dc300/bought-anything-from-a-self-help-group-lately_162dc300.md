Good Morning Ishan,
I'm not sure how many of you read my only story (so far) on The Ken. (I edit copies here, okay.) I'm usually found sulking around the office, hunched over my laptop, looking for that one perfect word, squinting at things... basically, confusing the hell out of everyone. Anyhow, on this one occasion—incidentally, it marked exactly six months for me at the company—I wrote about Kerala's poverty eradication mission Kudumbashree and how it was embracing e-commerce. And I'm pretty sure I beamed at everyone, one full day.
Many of you who did read it had kind things to say (thank you!), and one of you, namely Sudarshan Rangarajan, had much to add. So Sud (as Ashish has been calling him) DM-ed Ashish on Twitter, telling him much about how Self-Help Groups (SHGs) have increasingly been adopting this sort of thing. And how that may not go down all so splendidly. Ashish put him in touch with me, and that resulted in this follow-up to the Kudumbashree story.
Sud postulates that SHGs still have a long way to go, at least when it comes to e-commerce. He breaks the problem down into five points: Teamwork. Consistency. Income generation. Technology. Quality.
"Flipkarts and Amazons have separate departments with clear responsibilities for dealing with vendors/HR/corporate governance/finance, etc. Besides, they deal with entities who are registered. Now, with Kudumbashree, for example, where social growth and profit go hand in hand, the lack of such checks and balances could prove tricky," he writes..
Thursday is here, Friday can't be far behind.
