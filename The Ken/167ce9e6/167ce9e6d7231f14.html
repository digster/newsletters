And with every passing second, every service listed on the page, I realise that Amazon isn’t kidding when it says that the company has serious ambitions to be in the Home Services business. Despite initial hiccups, for a good three years, the company has been at it. Tweaking its model, figuring out which services work and which don’t, how it can marry goods sold on its website with services; it recently decided that it will hire home-cleaning service staff by itself, instead of depending on third-party contractors who’ve proved to be unreliable. The experiment is limited to Seattle, but Amazon isn’t giving up. It is working on the problem.

Words must not be minced. Nobody fights Big Tech. 

The few who do, do so in the hope of being bought or finding a niche and hoping that Big Tech doesn’t bother.

This is both the biggest opportunity and the biggest problem for UrbanClap.

Yep. UrbanClap, the local services company from India. Stuck in a business that’s seen four years of froth and the stench of rotten startups, UrbanClap continues to survive and soldier on. In FY18, its revenue went up by 225% to reach Rs 53.37 crore ($7.27 million), while losses came down by 14% and now stand at Rs 57 crore ($7.79 million) as opposed to Rs 66.7 crore ($9.1 million) in FY17. A while back, the company raised $50 million, at a valuation close to $500 million.

How UrbanClap got here against the odds and what it hopes to do next is today’s piece. From my colleague Vandana. She says the company is optimistic and will be focussing on four areas: beauty, home repair, large home projects and B2B. As more people move online, every household will need about 20 beauty services and 10 home services in a year. As of now, the overall services market volume stands at 500 million transactions.
 
With just 5 million currently happening through UrbanClap, the company believes it has a chance.
