Good Morning Ishan,
What’s in a name? Ask India’s mutual funds who at once are in the best of times and the worst of them. It’s boom time in their market, with assets under management of Rs 75 lakh crore and counting. But they also face intense pressure with nearly 50 players and counting, and a regulator who keeps them on a tight leash. Differentiation, more important than ever, is becoming tougher than ever.
So, every trick in the book is being used—including packaging the same old stocks in exciting-sounding schemes. This old wine in shiny bottles is best exemplified by the ‘innovation funds’ that have proliferated over the past couple of years. HDFC Mutual Fund is the latest to join this bandwagon.
Just how innovative are the stocks in innovation funds? Do old banks make the cut? What about some of India’s biggest corporate names? Are these funds any different from their plain-vanilla large-cap or flexi-cap counterparts? Do they deliver on returns? Just how effective are the portfolios of ‘business cycle’ funds—another favourite in the shape-shifting world of hazily-named mutual-fund schemes?
Archishma has sharp answers to these pertinent questions in [today’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/mutual-funds-shiny-new-bet-is-their-innovation-fund-but-whats-inside-it/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter). Let’s dive in.
