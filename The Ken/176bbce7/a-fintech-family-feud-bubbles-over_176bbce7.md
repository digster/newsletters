This is a scene out of a typical potboiler. The old, irascible patriarch is reluctantly watching the eldest sibling march towards the throne. There's been no contest, no worthy opponent to stand in the way. But all that's about to change.
The patriarch, Reserve Bank of India (RBI), just threw a regulatory spanner in India's fintech kingdom. The National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI), which runs the now-ubiquitous payment system UPI, will now have not one, but three potential factions to battle with in 2021.
NPCI's clear path to the crown—cornering two billion digital transactions a month—will now be muddied with strife and competition. From New Umbrella Entities (NUE)—groups of banks/fintechs that will come up with payment solutions to rival the quasi-governmental UPI.
These warring NUEs could come in all sizes. And like typical younger siblings, RBI has given them a much longer leash. One faction, led by banks, has over 40% of India's bank accounts under its belt. The other, a potential international collaboration, could even take the solution overseas.
Amidst it all, NPCI is having an existential crisis. In true karmic fashion, writes Arundhati, what UPI did to first-gen payment solutions like Visa and Mastercard, have come back to bite NPCI.
