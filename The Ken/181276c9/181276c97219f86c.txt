It's no secret that PhonePe, which is the largest digital payments app, wants to become a full-stack financial services company.

The Walmart-owned payments app has been accumulating regulatory licences. It has a licence to distribute mutual funds, and has applied for a stockbroking licence, a lending licence,  and most recently, an asset management company licence. Last month, it ventured into wealth management by acquiring WealthDesk, a marketplace for investment bundles, and OpenQ, a registered investment advisory (RIA) firm, for a total of $75 million.

If PhonePe’s moves sound similar, it’s because the company is following a playbook that has already been tried and tested. By Paytm. The payments behemoth that went public late last year.

Paytm’s journey from payments app to financial services company hasn’t been smooth. Even though Paytm has nearly 71 million monthly users, it is only the 12th largest stock broker in India.

Now PhonePe hasn’t made a big splash with financial services either. Finserv-related transactions accounted for 0.05% of all transactions made on the app as of the first quarter of 2022.

But, PhonePe trying to make changes to the Paytm playbook in an attempt to succeed.

At the crux of this playbook is cross-selling financial products, trying to convince users that come on the apps to use their payments services to invest in stocks, the most lucrative money-making opportunity in the wealth management vertical.

I was talking to a fintech executive about this cross-selling strategy recently when he said he was reminded of yebhi dot com.

In 2013, India's railway ticketing platform IRCTC had a brainwave. Since it had about 1.2 million daily visitors coming online to buy tickets, it decided to sell its users other things besides railway tickets. It even found a partner in Yebhi to manage this e-commerce portal.

Just a year later, however, Yebhi was struggling to stay afloat. The IRCTC partnership fizzled out. The realisation was that people came to IRCTC to buy tickets and not fashion.

Cross-selling was hard back in 2013. And it’s still hard in 2022.

Can PhonePe effectively break the cross-sell jinx and go where Paytm has not been able to yet?
