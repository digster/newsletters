Away from the political, pollution and perception battles that we hear about every day, there’s a seemingly small patent battle that is being fought in India right now.
 
I call it small because it’s really about David challenging the Goliath, Panacea Biotec and two other mid-size companies taking on Pfizer. It’s about overturning a patent that India already granted in August, despite a pre-grant opposition. Importantly, it’s also a patent that was overturned in Europe and is currently disputed in the US and South Korea. Not surprising, because most governments are burdened with public health expenditures and are taking a relook at IP laws.
 
So why India didn't look at this IP hard and long before granting a patent to Pfizer’s expensive pneumonia vaccine is a little puzzling.
 
Nearly 5% of all deaths in India are related to pneumonia. Under the new immunisation programme, 26 million children born every year are promised this vaccine. Until 2021, it’ll be funded by international aid, but after that, India will have to fund it itself. That’s when this decision will come to bite it because Pfizer’s product is the most expensive vaccine in its programme and the patent will prevent three cheaper, almost-ready versions from entering the market.
 
Pharma IP continues to be at the centre of global trade rules. But in India, there’s a troubling trend—of accepting donations and running skeletal public health programmes. We’ve seen this in two TB drugs earlier. Now it’s pneumonia.
 
This bit from the story is illustrative: “It is a neat strategy that Pfizer has adopted. First, offer the government vaccine at fairly low rates, then get a patent to monopolise the market for ten long years. When the government is thinking of universal immunisation, the cost of this patent is huge, right when we are on the anvil of introducing the product.”
 
It’s a tricky issue, with no easy answers. Ruhi’s story today puts a lot of these questions into perspective even as this legal battle only buys more time for Pfizer. Read it here: https://the-ken.com/pfizer-vaccination-program/
