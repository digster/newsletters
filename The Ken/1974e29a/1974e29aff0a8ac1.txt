Hello Ishan, how are you this fine Sunday morning? 

This year’s summer feels like the shortest we’ve had in a long time. In Bengaluru, the heat has only been a hesitant acquaintance these past few days, which means that my wife and I have resumed our habit of taking early evening walks around our neighborhood lake whenever we get the chance. 

It’s hard to explain the comfort of having a little piece of (mostly) unspoiled nature just a little walk away when you have to live the rest of your life surrounded by artifice. I’ve always been a city boy, and until I moved here, have exclusively lived in localities where nature could be had only at a premium. The wide open spaces, the scent of freshwater, the soaring kites and paddling ducks are all novelties that still haven’t worn off—three years and counting. 

This particular week, we managed to get some pictures and videos of a few friends that we share the lake with. (And do forgive the quality of the images; we had to make do with the inadequate phone camera we had).

1. New Visions for Health, Fitness, and Wellness

Nature is nourishing, but it’s only part of what keeps us in good health—in all the many ways you can define that phrase. 

The Ken’s Beyond the First Order event series is back this month with a topic that should be of interest to all of you—“Achieving Vitality: New Visions for Health, Fitness, and Wellness”. And we have lined up a highly qualified and diverse panel of speakers whose knowledge we can tap to enrich our own journey of building healthier, happier lives.

Nisha Millet is an Olympic swimmer and Arjuna Award winner. She’s also the director and head coach of Nisha Millet’s Swimming Academy, which has 12 centres and a team of 25 coaches in Bengaluru. The academy trained 6,000 swimmers aged 1 to 84 in 2024 alone. Viren Shetty is the vice chairman of the Narayana Health hospital group, which is listed on the National Stock Exchange of India. Through Narayana Health’s new chain of clinics and health insurance arm, Viren is spearheading an effort to change the way Indians think about healthcare, shifting the focus from sickness to wellness. Dr. Cyriac Abby Philips, aka The Liver Doc, is a frequently cited hepatologist and physician-scientist. He is a senior consultant at Rajagiri Hospital. But you may know him for his presence on X, Instagram, and Youtube, where he has cultivated a huge following—and a few opponents—by sharing evidence-based medical information that debunks health myths.

If you are in Bengaluru on 21 June, do join us. 2. Audiobooks—Closing the loop

Hello Ishan, it’s Hari here with this week’s books section. I’m sure long-time readers and contributors have this link saved in their favourites by now, but if you are new here, here’s where you can send us your own book recommendations: https://theken.typeform.com/FPBooks https://theken.typeform.com/FPBooks

Back in April https://the-ken.com/newsletter/first-principles/habits-streaks-and-goodharts-law/, we featured a couple of beautifully narrated audiobooks sent in by multiple readers, which I said I would save up for listening to later. Well, I’ve been taking a run at The Rise and Fall of the Third Reich https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/767171.The_Rise_and_Fall_of_the_Third_Reich—written by William L Shirer and read by Grover Gardner—on my morning walks for a while now. It’ll be sometime yet before I finish it (it’s 57 hours long; the book is more than 1,100 pages), but I already think it’s such a great unpacking of history.

1. The Spy and The Traitor https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/37542581-the-spy-and-the-traitor?ref=nav_sb_ss_1_21, written and read by Ben Macintyre—from Pradeep Kumaresan

“This is an absolutely fantastic thriller which takes place during the cold war. The author himself narrates the book in a very engaging way. Note: The second half of the book is so thrilling that you might find it difficult to stop listening!”

2. Can’t Hurt Me https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/41721428-can-t-hurt-me?ref=nav_sb_ss_1_13 written and read by David Goggins—from Manoj Radhakrishna

“The audiobook is powerful. Especially because of the unique format with the commentary between chapters by David Goggins himself.”

3. No Filter https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/50772888-no-filter?ref=nav_sb_ss_1_6 written by Sarah Frier, and read Sarah Frier and Megan Tusing—from Pradeep Kumaresan

“This is a very revealing story about how the conundrums social media platforms face. And about the incentives of the influencer economy. A fantastic listen. Narrator does a good job here too.”

4. The Sialkot Saga https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/28986040-the-sialkot-saga?from_search=true&from_srp=true&qid=AYPLcs2el2&rank=1 written by Ashwin Sanghi and read by Sagar Arya—from Remo George Joseph

“It’s a gripping fictional tale set in a sea of true historical events. The historical details are thoroughly researched. There are books that kill your time but also make you feel smarter in the process. This is one of them. Also, the narrator in the Audible version did an excellent job. He uses a different voice for different characters.”
