Good Morning Ishan,

Has your first week of February started well? If you're located in colder regions, this is probably a good time to start basking in the sun. Well, mostly because it's finally out.
 
Here, in Bengaluru, it's easy to lose track of seasons, and that, honestly, is quite disorienting. Especially for yours truly, who's a byproduct of harsher conditions. But hey, no one's complaining.
 
Anyhow, as you get started with the second month of this new year (seems awfully old already, doesn't it?), we hope you pause a bit this Saturday and indulge us. Read us.
 
We started this week with Ruhi's piece on digital insurance in India and whether these insurers could replicate their Chinese counterpart ZhongAn's success. For context, ZhongAn has sold seven billion policies and raised $1.5 billion in an IPO, with a valuation of $13 billion.
 
On Tuesday, Payal wrote about the slowing down of Bessemer Venture Partners in India. After an aggressive stint between 2014-16, the storied Silicon Valley venture capital firm is now using the time to think through how it wants to play in the Indian ecosystem.
 
Wednesday saw Ashish's Just Dial story. This one delved into how the listings enterprise has been at a crossroads. "Today, the fate of the company hangs in a precipitous balance," writes Ashish. Curious?
 
On Thursday, and more significantly, Union Budget day, Arundhati wrote about "Angel Tax" and how startups are being repeatedly notified by the IT department. Now is when the government really needs to STAND UP for these startups. This one's a FREE story; check it out.
 
The week closed with a contributor piece from Virat Markandeya who wrote about the changing face of legal systems in India with the advent of AI. While the old structure was an ancient pyramid, the new one could shine like a diamond. Our law firms just need to adapt.
 
Hope you enjoyed reading these. Have a great weekend!
