Imagine you go to your GP for a bacterial infection, but she has no way of knowing which antibiotic might be best for you. So, in the absence of any data, she might prescribe the newest or most expensive one, when perhaps a simple course of erythromycin might have sufficed. That’s how many cancer treatments end up, especially since making a wrong choice could take away the only window of successful treatment and recovery from a patient.
 
Cancer is a serious and silent killer, even though modern medicines and treatments are increasing the odds of human beings surviving a diagnosis. It is also under-diagnosed, remaining under the radar of detection till it's often quite late. Treatment is expensive, painful and time-consuming. Thus, even with newer, more expensive and more powerful drugs arriving on the scene, there’s no foolproof way for a doctor to predict which medicine might be best for each patient.
 
Mitra Biotech has spent the last 10 years patiently creating a platform that, in layman’s terms, creates the equivalent of an antibiotic sensitivity test for cancer treatments. In just a few days, it can analyse and map a tissue sample against a set of known treatments, and rank them in order of efficacy (The final decision must still be made by the doctor).
 
“We have the potential to become the number one company in the world in therapy selection,” says Mallikarjun Sundaram, one of the co-founders of Mitra.
 
Read Seema’s deep dive into Mitra’s frugal (it spent, over 10 years, what a typical tech startup raises and burns in a couple of years—$36 million) decade leading up to its flip to the US. 
 
“We are in no hurry. The half-life of such products is very long. The glucose tests developed 50 years ago are still around,” says Pradip Majumder, the other co-founder.
