You may have seen this quote show up on your Linkedin and X feeds: “Good design is like a refrigerator—when it works, no one notices, but when it doesn’t, it sure stinks.”

That’s a quip by Irene Au, a design partner at Khosla Ventures. She wrote it in 2016, but it’s a line that has everything to do with today’s story.

India needs designers—badly. For every 1,000 engineering graduates in the country, there is only one designer. The consequence is that teams can build the guts of products and platforms, but they might not be created with a pleasant user experience in mind.

At least a dozen schools are trying to address that shortcoming. Institutions like O.P. Jindal University in Haryana, Flame University in Maharashtra, Shiv Nadar University in Uttar Pradesh, and others have started their own design schools. The goal is to train the next wave of designers who mesh well with tech teams, can shape the strategy behind a product, and advocate for sustainable practices.

Walk through Ahmedabad’s Anant National University, and you’ll get a sense of what that means. Meals in the campus canteen come with biodegradable flatware. The trash bins are made from cardboard. There are plots of organic farms here and there.

“Designers don’t just beautify things. They can create solutions. Say, using design to mitigate the challenges of climate change or create self-sustaining structures, incorporating design elements that help reduce carbon footprint,” Anunaya Chaubey, the provost of Ahmedabad’s Anant National University, told The Ken.

More importantly, almost every business needs them.

In today’s story, Alifiya wrote about the way design as a field is changing and what India’s schools are doing to cultivate the next wave of design talent. Check it out.
