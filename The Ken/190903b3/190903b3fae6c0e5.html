Good Morning Ishan,

Loss-making Flipkart is on a profitability drive, but it’s the brands present on the e-commerce giant’s platform that are feeling the heat.

Among all the categories, fashion is in the hot seat: it’s the second largest vertical after electronics and it’s the one that is struggling to grow. Flipkart is now demanding higher fees from the likes of Spykar, Puma, Adidas, and Nike to sell on the platform, and that’s resulting in a face-off between Flipkart and the brands, each wanting to protect their end of the business.

“It’s like paying more rent. The landlord will ask for an increase year-on-year but the tenant would want it to remain as stable as possible or even have a downward trajectory,” a former e-commerce executive told Nuha, the author of the story.

Flipkart is enlisting the help of its fashion arm Myntra to drive a hard bargain. Flipkart and Myntra combined have a 45–50% market share in fashion e-commerce, and the e-commerce giant, which acquired the e-tailer in 2014, has started using the latter as “bait” to maintain its 27% market share lead in fashion as opposed to Myntra’s 22%.

Are the brands able to resist? How are they fighting back? Who holds greater sway? Nuha’s story captures the nuances of these changing dynamics unfolding between brands and Flipkart.
