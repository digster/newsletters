The only coffee my mother cares for, and in fact craves, is scalding, viscous filter coffee. Just before she was wheeled in for her coronary-bypass surgery a few months ago, she asked for a quick cup, leaving the nurse dumbstruck.

She couldn’t have had a better introduction to the idea of a coffee shop.

I took her to Café Coffee Day in Mumbai, maybe 15 years ago. And the barista suggested she get a cold coffee. “Coffee, cold? And you want me to pay for it?” I wish I could describe her incredulity. Or her disgust.

I guess her reaction wouldn’t be so strong if she knew that most people walking into a café tend to order an iced drink. Two-thirds of Starbucks’ beverage sales in the US are cold.

So it was no surprise when quick-commerce firm Zepto chose Vietnamese iced coffee as the flagship drink of its 10-minute coffee-delivery service in 2022. The simple idea behind Zepto Cafe was to make coffee affordable in a country where it had, for years, been sold as a premium beverage.

By putting its coffee machines next to groceries in its tiny warehouses, Zepto struck at the heart of what made the café business unprofitable in India: rent. Not long ago, Zepto Cafe was in 1,000 dark stores across cities.

But even without the hassle of real estate, Zepto can’t seem to escape the coffee jinx. Roughly 100 Zepto warehouses have stopped selling coffee. What gives? Pranathi has the perfect Friday read.
