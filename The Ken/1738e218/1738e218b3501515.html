Good Morning Ishan,
 
If 'C' is the most dreaded alphabet right now, then 'E' is the most promising.
 
Nothing encapsulates this more than edtech right now. There are more than 4,000 edtech companies in India, of all stripes and shades. Not only have the national lockdowns given them an influx of students, but they have also led to a flood of funds. And a flurry of acquisitions.
 
Just between January and March, Indian edtechs raised over $700 million in venture funding; a 7X jump from the same period last year. Byju's, for example, not only held a funding round that gave it decacorn status (a valuation of more than $10 billion), but it also bought growth.
 
That sort of activity has attracted another C. A 'C' that is not all that welcome in India in the current environment.
 
China.
 
VCs like Shunwei Capital and Fosun International Ltd; tech companies like Tencent Holdings Ltd, YY Inc, and ByteDance Ltd; edtech firms like TAL Education Group and Yuanfudao have all set their sights (and wallets) firmly on the perennial promise of the Indian market.
 
And why not, given the similarities? Education in India and China is structured around seminal exams—entrance exams, final exams, mid-terms. For classes between as-soon-as-you-can-read to just-before-you-get-a-job. And then, Tiger Moms of the US have nothing on the Dragon Parents of Asia.
 
All in all, very promising times for Chinese VCs and edtechs, who already have an established playbook back home that can be replicated in India. And all with the promise of a quicker path to profitability. "Edtechs will have free cash flows at least three to four years before e-commerce or content apps can monetise," says one investor.
