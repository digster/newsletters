When a company is faced with an existential crisis, it can do one of two things.

Either go back to what is familiar and low-risk, and live to fight another day.

Or throw caution to the wind, and use the crisis to, hopefully, remake itself.

Streaming giant Disney+ Hotstar has been grappling with this since it lost the rights for the Indian Premier League (IPL) to Reliance Industries-backed Viacom18 in June 2022.

For many, there's nothing worth watching on Hotstar besides the IPL, whose latest season begins today. (Please don't say HBO shows because I'm sure you know today's the last day you can access them on Hotstar.)

So, how does it make up for the 30 million viewers it would get on average every day when the IPL was on?

It no longer has that one thing that gave it an edge over Netflix and Amazon Prime Video.

So, which of the two paths mentioned above do you think Hotstar has chosen?
