And much has changed since we published our first story
Dear Ishan,
 

If you are new to The Ken, a warm welcome to you. If you’ve been reading and listening to us for a while, a big thank you for coming along on this journey. 

 

On this day seven years ago, we went to our potential subscribers with a simple promise: we would publish one original, analytical, and deeply reported longform story every weekday that the readers paid for, not advertisers. 

 

Over the years, we delivered on that promise even as we continued to build on it, adding longform stories from Southeast Asia, seven weekly newsletters, and two podcasts. 

 

This is how we built India’s first subscription-only business-news publication. 

 

Now, over 45,000 paying subscribers and 500,000 registered subscribers later, we want to renew our promise. 

 

But why? 

 

Much has changed since we published our first story. 

 

There were 11 Indian startups valued at US$1 billion or more then. Now, there are over 110. 

 

Listed tech companies were almost unheard of in India in 2016. In 2023, they are among the most closely tracked stocks. 

 

Back then, there were very clear distinctions between, say, fintech and financial services, and e-commerce and brick-and-mortar retail. Now, the lines between them are blurred, if they exist at all.

 

When we launched, workplaces were not very different from how they had been five years earlier. Now, in a post-pandemic world, they have been transformed. 

 

Finally, we were a one-of-a-kind news organisation then. Now, we have a handful of competitors that have followed in our footsteps. 

 

These are compelling reasons for us to not just tell extensively reported and skillfully narrated stories, but also be quick to report on big developments, with sharp, actionable insights. 

 

We will continue to chronicle the biggest disruptors in India’s startup ecosystem and internet economy, and dig deep into large publicly listed tech and consumer companies. And we will also report more frequently on the most consequential shifts in careers, workplaces, and the complex world of investing. 

 

In addition, twice a month, we will publish a “Big Story”, which will stand out from our other stories in scope, design, execution, and impact. 

 

Finally, your consistent feedback—in the comments section, through email, and on social media—has been invaluable to our growth. So we will find better ways to engage with you, including periodic subscriber surveys.  

 

Here’s to another seven years of impactful journalism and storytelling!

Regards, 

Seema Singh and Rohin Dharmakumar
Co-founders, The Ken
