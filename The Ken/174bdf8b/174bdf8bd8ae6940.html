WorkIndia is hardly the kind of job portal you and I are used to.
 
For one, it lists vacancies under four qualifications: graduate, 12th pass, 10th pass, and below 10th pass. And among the positions on offer are security guard, peon, delivery boy, and driver.
 
WorkIndia is the anti-LinkedIn.
 
The six-year-old company is a matchmaker between India's humongous blue-collar workforce and its small and medium-sized enterprises. WorkIndia has 15 million jobseekers and 900,000 employers registered on its platform. The service is free for the former, while employers are charged Rs 50 (US$0.7) for every call they get from an applicant.
 
WorkIndia and its peers, such as Workex and Aasaanjobs, are riding the low-cost mobile telephony wave unleashed by the launch of billionaire Mukesh Ambani's Reliance Jio in 2016. Most blue-collar applicants may not have a computer, but they are much likelier to own a smartphone. That's why WorkIndia offers its mobile app in nine Indian languages.
 
But it's not all smooth sailing for WorkIndia, as Olina reports in her perceptive story today. The company has no control over whether an applicant attends a scheduled interview. Workex and Aasaanjobs, on the other hand, have solved this by taking care of the entire hiring process instead of just providing leads.
 
The story has particular resonance at a time when India is grappling with an unemployment crisis made much worse by the Covid-19 pandemic: https://the-ken.com/story/workindias-match-blue-collar-hire/ (14-minute read)
