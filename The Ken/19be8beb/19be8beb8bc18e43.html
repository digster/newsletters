“A return-to-roots movement.”

Such a phrase almost always has political connotations in India. But I’m not surprised that a Cognizant Technology Solutions manager used it to describe the Nasdaq-listed IT company’s plans to list in India.

There has been a melding of companies’ financial aspirations and the supposed imminence of the country’s greatness over the past decade. “Atmanirbhar Bharat” is a case in point.

For Cognizant, though, the considerations are fairly practical.

Public-market investors fearful of the impact of artificial intelligence have lately shunned Cognizant’s peers in India, such as Tata Consultancy Services, Infosys, and Wipro. In the US, even without the long shadow of AI, Cognizant wouldn’t be terribly exciting to a stock market used to Nvidia, Microsoft, and Alphabet.

That explains why Cognizant, worth $40 billion, trades at 19 times trailing earnings, while HCL Tech and Infosys trade at 26 and 23, respectively.

The real question, though, is not what multiple Cognizant can dream of, according to Debanjali, but whether it can get past the messiness of dual listings in India. Ask Standard Chartered.
