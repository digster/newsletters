“If you think research is expensive, try disease.”

The words of the late Mary Lasker, noted health activist who believed that research funding was the best way to promote public health, particularly resonate now.

In April, the Covid National Task Force in India had five subgroups—clinical research, diagnostics, epidemiology, operations research, and vaccines or drug R&D. While all other groups continue to function, the one for vaccines was disbanded in just three weeks.

Today, when vaccine trials are progressing, the one group that could have held everything together under supervision is sorely missing. Result? India’s Covid-19 vaccine development has bordered on chaotic.

But first, the good news. There’s an overwhelming response from people volunteering to enrol. At the All India Institute of Medical Sciences in Delhi, the principal investigator says they needed about 100 volunteers for Phase II, but they received some 4,500 emails in just three days. One hopes that companies and investigators continue to get volunteers they require because Phase III will need tens of thousands of healthy volunteers. (Don’t ask who’s going to fund all that.)

At some trial sites, like in rural Maharashtra where Serum Institute of India is testing the AstraZeneca-Oxford Institute vaccine, even getting 100 healthy volunteers is proving difficult. Because villages are already rife with infectious cases. Now, imagine what sort of coordination, funding, and trial inspection would be required when Bharat Biotech and Zydus take their vaccines into Phase III in the next few weeks.

Surprise checks and inspections at vaccine sites have been next to none. Enforcement has been paltry, say regulatory officials.

Noted vaccine scientist Gagandeep Kang’s worry is of a different sort—conflict of interest among companies that are conducting clinical trials. “If I have a definition [of a] clinical outcome, I want the test to be done in a reliable lab. I don’t want the lab making the vaccine to also be the lab that tests if the vaccine works or not.”
