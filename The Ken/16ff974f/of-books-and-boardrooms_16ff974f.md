As someone who is technically a professional writer—yes, that’s right Mr Nicholls, bet you didn’t see that coming based on my English exams—I’m occasionally asked if I will ever write a book.
Work on a single story for a year? Goodness no.
I have incredible respect for anyone who can stick to it and deliver a book; the ability truly does elude me. But my respect for you cranks up a few notches if you’re writing a book while you work a full-time job.
That’s why I jumped at the chance to be the person to write today’s email to you, dear reader. When I heard today’s story was about how CEOs and top Indian executives business are writing books—works of fiction as well as the management texts you’d expect—I had to be among the first to read it.
Today's story goes into how some of these books—such as An Extreme Love of Coffee by Harish Bhat of Tata, The Making of Hero by Hero MotoCorp founder Sunil Kant Munjal, and Excellence Has No Borders by Dr BS Ajai Kumar of cancer care provider HealthCare Global—come into being.
I still don’t have plans to write a book, but one particular line resonated strongly with me.
“It is important to stay away from activity traps when one writes,” said T.N. Hari, who is the head of HR at e-grocery firm Bigbasket and co-author of Saying No To Jugaad.
Now that we all can relate to, I'm sure.
