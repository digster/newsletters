Often when we set out to write recap emails, the first question that pops into our head is—is there something common among our stories this week?
We don’t set out to thematically organise our stories. More often than not, it happens organically. Some weeks, it’s startups on a tear; others, it’s companies struggling to get their acts together. There have been stories revolving around high stakes, big bets, and Sisyphean tasks—all in the same week.
We’ve also had some truly serendipitous weeks where all stories followed a singular theme, like the one in the middle of May when we wrote about stories steeped in history and tradition. Even The Nutgraf, our Saturday newsletter, stuck to the theme unwittingly that week. (And no, I manage the pipeline of upcoming stories in this office and I promise that wasn’t intentional.)
But I don’t want to draw your attention to themes and patterns this week. Though, if you look hard enough, there’s undoubtedly one or two hiding under the bonnet. Instead, I want to talk about the people and the companies, the cast of characters in this week’s arresting drama in the world of business and tech.
First up, we have
[The Carlyle Group](https://the-ken.com/story/carlyle-both-villain-and-hero-in-pnb-housing-finance-deal/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). On Monday, Anand very nearly translated the script of Kamal Hassan's iconic Tamil movie,
Nayakan, while analysing the role of the private equity firm in PNB Housing Finance’s recent move to raise funds. Can you be someone’s hero and villain at the same time?
Starring in our free story this Friday was the
[Russian vaccine, Sputnik V](https://the-ken.com/story/vaccine-sputnik-v-india/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), which was meant to save the day in light of the Covishield and Covaxin shortage. But delayed imports and a struggle to manufacture domestically have put paid to the vaccine’s potential in India, wrote Maitri.
Download an app to just order pizza or download an app to order everything under the sun,
including pizza? Logic says it should be the second option, and yet, the
[app of pizza chain Domino’s](https://the-ken.com/story/how-dominos-defied-the-might-of-zomato-and-swiggy/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) is the third player in this foodtech drama. It has been downloaded a massive 57 million times so far, wrote Seetharaman on Wednesday as he explored the company’s journey to retain an edge over India's foodtech duopoly—Zomato and Swiggy.
Thursday saw Bhumika dive into the world of
[celebrity-led online learning platforms](https://the-ken.com/story/how-frontrow-celebrity-school-unluclass-can-build-indias-masterclass/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). If Masterclass is the Hollywood A-lister in this lineup, India’s FrontRow is the homegrown Bollywood star. And it’s not the only one—in the last 15 months, at least four other similar platforms have cropped up.
