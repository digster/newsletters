Good Morning Ishan,

India’s competition law practice just turned super competitive.

Nisha Kaur Uberoi, the country’s foremost antitrust lawyer who built Trilegal’s formidable and market-leading competition practice, is moving lock, stock, and barrel—well, almost—to J. Sagar and Associates (JSA).

The legal fraternity is stunned, even though such partner moves in law firms are not uncommon.

But Uberoi is no average law firm partner. A senior Supreme Court advocate described her as “the gold standard of competition law”.

In 2016, when news first broke about her move from Cyril Amarchand Mangaldas (CAM) to AZB & Partners, LegallyIndia, a digital news publication tailored for professionals in the legal industry, crashed due to high traffic.

Later, when she moved to Trilegal, she brought along her clients. “My clients had moved from CAM to Trilegal,” Uberoi claimed. It’s no different this time around. Her clients will be moving with her to JSA, she told The Ken.

Why would someone leave Trilegal, a top-tier law firm whose antitrust practice is ranked by the likes of Asialaw as far better than JSA?

While Uberoi cites JSA’s more “equitable partnership model” as the primary reason for moving, the fact is that JSA is aggressively poaching. The firm just lured away Iqbal Khan, Shardul Amarchand Mangaldas’ rainmaker, and his 20-member team.

Typically, in competition law matters, corporate teams often pass the ball to the competition team when merger deals come knocking. So, with Khan joining JSA’s corporate team, Uberoi’s new playground is set to get a lot busier, writes Vanita in her story today. She unpacks all the action in antitrust practice.
