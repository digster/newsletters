Even as corporate India faces a skill deficiency in talent, they seem confused as to what to make of the 10 million youths expected to join them as interns in the next five years
The pilot project for the PM Internship scheme starts on 2 December and 125,000 interns are expected to start work with the top 500 companies. But corporate India is concerned about onboarding interns in such numbers, potentially throwing off hiring plans for other roles. Corporates also doubt if they’re equipped with enough resources to train and retain interns, and also hire again from the pool in a year. Applicants begin registering themselves from 12 October; but the scheme lacks a sector-specific approach, potentially creating a problem-solution mismatch.
