Good Morning Ishan,
 
No one could have prepared enough for COVID-19. It’s bigger than anything most of us have seen before.
 
On a lighter note though, when one of India’s largest digital health apps started throwing up beer stats when users searched for Corona—beer drinkers would know this brand—it was excusable. I am told the chatbot has since been fed all the relevant information. And they better do a good job of it because if there ever was a time to make a case for digital health, it’s now.
 
It’s surprising then to see the PolicyBazaar group, India’s largest insurance aggregator, selling a majority stake in DocPrime, the virtual doctor consultation platform it launched in late 2018. A good portion of $200 million that the group, including the financial marketplace PaisaBazaar, had just raised from SoftBank was meant to go into DocPrime. Among other things, to boost the two existing platforms’ sales.
 
The founders are all but exiting DocPrime even though they built it to be one of the largest teleconsultation providers in the country with a huge network of clinics and diagnostic labs. In January, DocPrime even launched an exclusive outpatient health insurance product with Apollo Munich.
 
What happened since? The conservative, return on investment-focused group couldn’t bear burning $1 million with next to no revenues? Or did the near-failures of early movers like Practo and Lybrate spook them? (At one point in 2018, the group had even evaluated Practo for a buyout.) Or is DocPrime being sent to a new owner to don a new avatar, with a new business model under its belt?
 
Or did the group not see Corona coming?
 
It’s all of the above and more.
 
Ruhi has an exclusive, cracker of a story today. On a group which is “relieved” at shedding a majority stake and yet retaining a significant minority stake in DocPrime.
