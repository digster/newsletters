Good Morning Ishan,

Today’s story is about an exciting topic—hacking.

Specifically, it’s not about hacking per se, but it’s a story about where hacking has reached right now. Hackers are no longer just breaking in and defacing websites. They’re now in the business of stealing data from companies… only to sell it back to them for a considerable ransom. 

Perhaps you already know this is happening. But what you probably don’t know is how it happens. In fact, if you think about it, hackers have gotten as sophisticated as the companies they target. 

Let me explain. 

Historically, the development of software has gone through three broad stages. First, there was the lone, independent coder who would build things to solve specific problems, which would then extend into a product. Think Bill Gates of Microsoft, or Steve Wozniak of Apple. 

Then teams of like-minded individuals—with specific competencies and skills—were formed, all of them working together. Finally, in the last stage, a lot of software became services, which could be purchased off the shelf and integrated, leaving the coders to work on specific problems. 

Hacking has gone through the exact same journey. 

In Pratap’s story today, you’ll read about how hackers have attacked, targeted companies, and held their personal data to ransom. In most instances, they’ve cut off access to a company’s data and demanded a ransom for it. If the company didn’t comply, the hackers threatened to leak it online. You’ll understand how hackers have gone from lone rangers, to organised teams, to actual quasi-companies that build and sell modules of code that can be used to target any company. Ransom as a service. 

And India is on top of the list of worst-hit countries. 

It’s not just run-of-the-mill corporates that have been hit, either; ransomware attackers are now looking at bigger targets. Cancer care chain HealthCare Global, automobile dealership Navnit Group, IT firm Nucleus Software, pharma company Aurobindo Pharma have all been attacked by ransomware recently. Even the Indian government’s highways agency wasn’t spared. 

You’ll read about how these companies have responded. Some have caved and paid up. Others have fought back. And many others are figuring out how to protect themselves. 

It’s an amazing story. I’m sure you’ll love it.
