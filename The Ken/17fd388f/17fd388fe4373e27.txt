Imagine you’re scrolling through your favourite e-commerce app. Just window shopping for some earpods, books, those shoes you’ve been eyeing for a while or a backpack for your next trek. Once you’re done adding items to your wishlist, it’s time to head back to the web series you were watching. No, no, you don’t need to leave your shopping app. Just head back to the homepage and tap a button.

Welcome to Amazon miniTV. 

If you don't believe me, just check out the top menu on the Amazon India app, under the search bar. Next to shortcuts for categories like mobiles, fashion, and electronics, you'll find a button called miniTV, with an icon that somewhat resembles YouTube's. If you've missed it so far, you're not alone, especially if you have an iPhone. 

Launched in India in May 2021 (December 2021, if you have an iPhone), miniTV is Amazon's advertising-based video-on-demand (AVOD) service. It has free, snackable content like comedy sketches, mini-series, and short films, sourced from digital entertainment companies like The Viral Fever, Pocket Aces, and Only Much Louder. 

I know what you're thinking. Doesn't Amazon already have a video streaming service? Yes, and a rather successful one at that. Prime Video, launched in 2016, was among the top five most downloaded online video apps in India in 2021.

So, why launch another streaming service? Amazon has its reasons.

And why put it in the e-commerce app? Amazon has its reasons for that too.

How is it faring so far? Amazon says it has received “strong positive feedback” from viewers. But no other details.
