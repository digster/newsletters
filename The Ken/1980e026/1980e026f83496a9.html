Hello Ishan,

 

I am terrified. I’m excited. I’m also invigorated. 

 

Here’s what I’ve been up to for the past two weeks: I’ve been in touch with builders, designers, engineers, CEOs, founders, and a range of other people who are immersed in product design and creation. It’s clear that the rumbles about how artificial intelligence is changing everything we know about those processes is no great exaggeration. All of it is true.

 

And it goes much further than that.

 

I’ve seen how business teams are shipping code, eager to turn ideas into functional products without their colleagues’ help. Engineers are coding faster than ever with the assistance of co-pilots. Anyone willing to pay a couple hundred dollars a year can have a useful “intern” shadowing them 24/7. As long as you have a plan and some creativity, experimentation quickly becomes a new work paradigm.

 

This got me thinking—what’s really happening inside organisations? The change isn’t just about new tools, processes, or products. It goes much deeper, and is remaking power dynamics and org charts. Which teams are gaining influence, and who’s losing ground? Who has the CEO’s ear now? Who’s becoming the bottleneck? 

 

Across product, design, engineering, and business, people aren’t just collaborating. They’re competing, sometimes fiercely, to make others irrelevant.

 

That means job roles are being compressed into one. Timelines are shorter than ever. Every bar is being raised, and they’ll only keep going higher.

 

Maybe you are also terrified. Or excited. Or invigorated.

 

This exploration isn’t merely a personal interest. I’m doing this to lay the groundwork for a great discussion at The Ken’s next live event in Bengaluru. And we’re bringing together four founders and leaders from across generations: some who built products and organisations the “old” way and are now figuring out what the new rules are, and others who come with no baggage of the past, unburdened by how things used to be.

 

Here’s who will join us on stage:

 

 

We’ll hear firsthand accounts of the real, messy, and exhilarating transformations taking place in India’s startups and organisations. We’ll also find out who’s holding out and refusing AI, who’s embracing it fully, and how everyone is coping with the changes to their roles, identity, and value. We’ll see how young, AI-native startups are building products and what their org charts look like. Finally, expect live demos to take place alongside the discussion—you’ll get to see how cutting-edge practitioners build out their concepts at breakneck speed.

 

Join us at the Bangalore International Centre on Saturday, 26 July 2025. The event will run from 10:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m.

 

And for those who aren’t in Bengaluru, we have you covered. This event will be live streamed, so you can be part of it no matter where you are.

 

Here’s where you can find out more about the event and secure your tickets: https://the-ken.com/event/how-ai-is-breaking-and-remaking-the-way-products-are-built/ 

 

I’ll see you there.

 

Best Regards,
