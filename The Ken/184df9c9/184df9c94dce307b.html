Good Morning Ishan,
 
Indonesian tech giant GoTo’s Series A shares lock-up period expired late last week, and what happened next was not a good look for the company. Apparently, lots of investors holding the pre-IPO locked-up shares were rushing to sell, with not enough takers. Bloomberg called GoTo “the worst large-scale tech IPO in the world” as it lost 61% in value since its debut. Ouch!
 
GoTo certainly isn’t the only tech stock getting battered of late. A correction was expected and, perhaps, overdue: GoTo was still trading at a higher multiple than most peers. Listed on the Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX), its market cap had, until recently, soared far above Nasdaq-listed rival Grab’s. Now, GoTo’s market cap is at ~US$10 billion, below Grab’s ~US$12 billion. GoTo could drop even further, according to analysts, before it finds a value the current market will accept. 
 
What’s notable about last week’s events is that GoTo may be losing grip on its own narrative. It had successfully engineered an IPO at a time when market sentiment was already cooling, and employed multiple mechanisms to shield itself from an uncontrolled price volatility. Then again, prior to the lock-up ending, it tried to broker share sales via a secondary offering, rather than an open-market mechanism, to have more control over the price.
 
But this time, GoTo’s plan didn’t pan out. Yunindita’s story today—a double take on last week’s news—zooms in on this event, asking what GoTo wanted to achieve, and what it means now that it didn’t. 
 
It’s definitely going to be a rough ride for the company going forward. Here’s the story: https://the-ken.com/sea/story/after-secondary-offering-fails-goto-finds-itself-at-the-mercy-of-market-forces/ (9-minute read)
