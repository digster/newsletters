Before sitting down to write this, I checked the calendar only to realise that Christmas is almost here. The year is ending. It's puzzling. Wasn't it October just a few days ago? If I were in school, I'd definitely be the kid mistakenly writing the year as 2021 for the first few months of 2022. And then scratching the 1s and replacing them with the 2s. Were you that kid too?
But we will talk about new beginnings and rewriting some other time. Today is about recaps.
There's something about endings that make you sit back and go on a nostalgia-induced walk down the memory lane.
At times, this year has felt like a million days put together into one. At others, it has seemed like an athlete running a sprint who couldn't wait to reach the finish line.
But mostly, it has been a year unlike any other, marked by grief that washed over families during the second wave of the pandemic and also the hope that vaccines brought. At the same time, it has been a year of bull markets, unicorns becoming more real than myths, and fundraisings that defied balance sheets.
In such a year, it's no surprise that we've had one hell of a week bringing you stories about battles, bumps, disruptions and more.
Arundhati began the week with a stellar story on Flipkart founder Sachin Bansal's three-year-old venture
[Navi Finserv](https://the-ken.com/story/home-loan-sachin-bansal-navi/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). The company is going head-to-head with banks to offer the lowest home loan rates in the market—all without being a bank itself.
On Tuesday, Seetharaman shifted gears and dived deep into the world of the
[Indian Premier League](https://the-ken.com/story/why-being-a-loser-is-just-fine-for-ipl-teams-bottom-line/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) (IPL). One would think the cricket teams would have to win to be a profitable franchise or command a sponsorship premium. But in the glitzy tournament we’ve all grown to love and hate, that’s not exactly the case.
Moving beyond the Ola Electrics and Athers of the electric vehicles industry in India, on Wednesday, I mapped the bumpy ride of recently listed auto components maker
[Sona Comstar](https://the-ken.com/story/tesla-supplier-sona-comstars-domestic-ev-title-needs-a-two-wheeler-assist/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). The company wants to balance its global ambitions with a better shot at the home turf, but it’s not going to be an easy task, even for a company with a $6 billion market cap.

The company provides "one-click" collateral-free loans, but in its way are well-backed and deeply-networked incumbents.
