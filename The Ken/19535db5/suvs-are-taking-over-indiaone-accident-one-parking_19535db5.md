Good Morning Ishan,
Muscular. Imposing. Aggressive.
No, those aren’t words meant to describe a gym rat with steroids running through his system. They’re the colourful adjectives bandied about by sales reps in India’s dealerships when you’re perusing new SUVs.
As one senior executive at Tata Motors told The Ken, “Cars are now being sold as fortresses. A protector for your family. And for that, they must be large, bulky, and heavy-duty.”
Whether it’s their perceived safety for passengers, higher ground clearance, or symbolism for having made it, SUVs are more popular in India than ever.
But more people driving larger cars means less space for everyone—in parking lots, on public roads, and everywhere else. Their weight also leads to more damage to any surface their tyres roll on. The worst part is that SUVs are particularly lethal for anyone involved in a collision with them.
Even so, the appetite for bigness is getting even bigger.
For today’s story, Rounak combed through 25 years of car-sales data, spoke to eight automobile industry executives, and touched base with government officials, police officers, as well as urban planners.
