Good Morning Ishan,
 
Today’s story is about a company called xto10x. You probably haven’t heard much about it. I’ll explain.
 
But first, let me tell you a quick story about another company. In the late 90s, Jason Fried, a web designer in Chicago, started a company called 37signals with a few friends that designed websites. You know how it works. Find a client. Talk to them. Understand what they want. Make a website. Next.
 
Now xto10x doesn’t design websites, but just like 37signals, it offers a service. Founded by Binny Bansal, Flipkart’s co-founder and former CEO, along with some former colleagues, xto10x (read as “ten to ten X”) is a Bengaluru-based company that helps growth-stage companies solve problems that come with scale.
 
xto10x believes that nearly all companies at a certain stage face a common set of problems. Org structures. Goal setting. Culture. So they work with the founders and help solve them. And charge them a fee. Part cash. Part equity. Pretty neat.
 
It says it’s less consultancy, and more startup school. xto10x’s list of clients include some familiar names - Razorpay. Meesho. Cleartax.
 
But the longer that xto10x stares into the abyss, the abyss stares back. There’s only so much scale that xto10x can unlock with this approach. So xto10x asked that famous question that have previously sunk a thousand startups - “Can we productise this?”
 
That’s where xto10x finds itself today. Building software. An org-wide goal-setting and performance management tool. Another to record and manage the Net Promoter Score of employees. Internal surveys. Things like that.
 
xto10x is about the most treacherous leap of all - a service company becoming a product company.
 
But it has been done before. Remember 37signals? In 2004, it built an application to help companies manage tasks, goals and other stuff in-house. Then after a decade of doing some really strange things—like ignoring revenue, building less and turning down over a 100 VCs—things that xto10x would probably not advise you to do, it finally built a SaaS company, and changed the name. Today, Basecamp is worth $100 billion.
 
But I’ll let Arundhati and Sumanth tell you where xto10x will go from here. You can read their story here: https://the-ken.com/story/startup-school-to-saas-binny-bansals-xto10x/ (14-minute read)
