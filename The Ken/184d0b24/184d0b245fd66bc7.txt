Good Morning Ishan,

When Reliance Industries Ltd decides to enter a new business, expect a whole bunch of feathers in that industry to be ruffled. The latest to get a taste of that is Udaan, a leading B2B e-commerce player.

Until a few months ago, Udaan was the toast of the town. But the tide suddenly seems to be turning against it. Headlines now document hundreds of job cuts at the five-year-old company, scaling down of its operations, and funding woes.

Undoubtedly, Reliance’s strategic moves—the acquisition of German B2B player Metro Cash & Carry and the stake sale to Meta, which helped it quickly onboard 30 million WhatsApp-using retailers—have played their part. But, Udaan’s soaring ambitions also seem to be hit by burning cash too fast in a bid to scale up while ignoring the unit economics of the business.

Now, Udaan is going through a much-needed transformation under chief executive Vaibhav Gupta. The Lightspeed-backed startup is bringing the shutters down on new initiatives, reorganising its supply chain, and restructuring its high-revenue but loss-making consumables business that it refers to as 'Pacman'.

In doing this, it is presenting a template on what other startups in the same boat (and there are many like this) could do to sail through.

In a deep-dive, Aayush and Gaurav meticulously detail the troubles brewing at Udaan, and how as a late-stage startup it is now aiming to break even rather than maintain its pole position in the market.
