Good evening Ishan,
 

A little over a year ago, we’d announced The Ken’s Narrative Workshops — a hands-on program for leaders, founders and executives at ambitious, high-growth organizations who seek to unlock the power of narrative storytelling to drive business outcomes. 

 

I’ll be honest. At the time, we created this program because In the nearly six years that The Ken has been around, we kept getting one question from subscribers : 
 

Do you teach storytelling? I want to write stories like you do. 

 

Storytelling is a great skill, but we believe what makes it incredibly more valuable is if it can be used the right way to achieve tangible results for your work and career. This is why our program focused on two key principles:

 

First, narrative storytelling needs to be connected to what companies care about — this includes hiring, product launches, execution cadence, customer engagement, sales and even employee retention. 

 

In other words, narrative storytelling needs to help your business. 

 

Second, successful narratives are deeply connected to the truth. This includes learning how to report by asking the right questions to stakeholders, how to use data effectively, and how to really understand what your audience and customers thinks, feels and wants. 

 

The right way to do it is by leveraging the principles of journalism.

 

Today, 100+ leaders at over a dozen organisations, including listed multinationals like General Mills, ITC and LatentView Analytics; ambitious startups like Jocata Financial Services and Prescience Decision Solutions, and I’m proud to say that many of them have already reported they are seeing the impact that powerful, journalistic storytelling has had in their organisations.

A couple of months back, we published a case study of how LatentView Analytics used data storytelling to fast-track sales. 

 

Today, we are publishing another case study to share the impact the program had on an international organisation called Swissnex in India. 

 

Swissnex is an organisation unlike any that you may have seen or heard of. It’s special. It works with startups, corporates, and universities in India and Switzerland making sense of some of the biggest issues of our times, like better healthcare, sustainable use of resources and digital transformation. It’s a really special organisation — filled with talented people, trying to tell stories that connect audiences across two nations on opposite sides of the world, who seemingly have little in common.

 

In August last year, they enrolled in The Ken’s Narrative Thinking Program and by October they had completed over 20 hours of training.

 

And in January, the venture had a milestone moment when the Swiss Ambassador to India announced that he wanted to accelerate ties with India, create more trade and investment opportunities, in particular, establish an Indo-Swiss innovation platform to be spearheaded by Swissnex in India. “This is a significant success,” said Jonas Brunschwig, CEO, “as the narrative and the messaging we could craft whilst persuading him (President, Swiss National Science Foundation) was inspired by the (narrative thinking) program.”

 

Today’s story is about how Swissnex built a stronger identity that has a deeper relationship with the audience.
