Today, we are publishing another case study to share the impact the program had on an international organisation called Swissnex in India.
Swissnex is an organisation unlike any that you may have seen or heard of. It’s special. It works with startups, corporates, and universities in India and Switzerland making sense of some of the biggest issues of our times, like better healthcare, sustainable use of resources and digital transformation. It’s a really special organisation — filled with talented people, trying to tell stories that connect audiences across two nations on opposite sides of the world, who seemingly have little in common.
In August last year, they enrolled in The Ken’s Narrative Thinking Program and by October they had completed over 20 hours of training.
And in January, the venture had a milestone moment when the Swiss Ambassador to India announced that he wanted to accelerate ties with India, create more trade and investment opportunities, in particular, establish an Indo-Swiss innovation platform to be spearheaded by Swissnex in India. “This is a significant success,” said Jonas Brunschwig, CEO, “as the narrative and the messaging we could craft whilst persuading him (President, Swiss National Science Foundation) was inspired by the (narrative thinking) program.”
Today’s story is about how Swissnex built a stronger identity that has a deeper relationship with the audience.
