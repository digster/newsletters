Get regular physical checkups. Go for health counselling. Keep your vaccinations up to date. Most of us understand the importance of preventive care, even if we don’t always practice it in our daily lives.

But the backers of India’s healthcare providers believe in the better angels of our nature. They also believe firms that offer preventive care will be the next big thing in healthcare. After all, if you don’t get sick, you won’t need costly medical treatment, and there’s a business model to be built around that. Big cheques are being written to back that conviction.

Pune-based Loop Health is one firm that offers health-assurance services, as they’re called. It currently sells third-party insurance products with add-ons such as free eye checkups and Cult.fit membership discounts. Take advantage of those perks, the logic goes, and there will be fewer claims from customers.

It’s a concept that the company claims to have attracted 1,200 corporate clients and $40 million in investments from the likes of Elevation Capital. That has also forced some of its insurance partners to sit up and notice its actions.

Yet something is about to change. Loop wants to become an insurer so that it has more control over the products that are offered to its 850,000 end users. The company believes the wellness layer that it offers leads to an advantage as it breaks out of being a mere intermediary.

Sudeshna explores Loop’s transition and explains why it matters to you in today’s story. Read it to find out more.
