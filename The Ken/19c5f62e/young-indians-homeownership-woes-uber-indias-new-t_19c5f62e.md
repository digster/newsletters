Maharashtra Deputy CM Ajit Pawar’s death in the Baramati crash brings fresh attention to a fast-growing sector, where accidents expose risk, but rarely dent demand
Non-scheduled aviation operators or private charters account for the second-highest number of air accidents in India. Yet, politicians and the ultra-rich rely on them, thanks to their advantage of speed and access over commercial flights. But the crash of Learjet 45, a private jet operated by VSR Ventures, which killed a top politician, has laid bare the reality of this industry. In the gap between operators’ race to meet urgency and the regulator’s years-long probes, accountability weakens.
