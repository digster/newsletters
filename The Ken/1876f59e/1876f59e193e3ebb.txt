Good Morning Ishan,

It seems like SBM Bank India can’t catch a break. 

Earlier this week, on 31 March, corporate credit card-holders of thousands of startups found their cards blocked—with very little notice https://www.livemint.com/companies/news/sbm-blocks-fintech-partnered-credit-cards-on-a-short-notice-11680500084775.html. These startup executives held cards issued by SBM Bank India in partnership with fintechs such as Kodo, Happay, Razorpay, Karbon, and EnKash. 

For the impacted startups, this was no small crisis. These corporate credit cards were their operational lifeline—that’s how they made purchases to keep their servers up, paid for Google ads, and other critical infrastructure. The last time something like this happened was when the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) turned off the ability to make automatic recurring payments.

I spoke to an executive working with a fintech that issues corporate credit cards and what they say about the situation sounds a lot like chaos:

We are now fielding 500-600 calls a day from customers. This time it’s more dire, because the ability to make all transactions are hit, not just recurring ones.

You might think that, this time, it’s only customers of SBM Bank who are affected, and so the impact wouldn’t be that widespread. But you’d be wrong, because SBM Bank was the one lender that most fintechs partnered with. It works with over 40 of them. 

And it’s not the first time that SBM Bank India is finding itself in the middle of a mess of such proportions. It was only in January this year that the RBI banned https://m.economictimes.com/tech/startups/multiple-fintechs-impacted-with-rbis-order-against-sbm-india/articleshow/97383099.cms the bank from processing any foreign exchange remittances under the (so-called) Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS). Which meant SBM supported fintechs like Niyo and IndMoney had to halt operations. 

And last year, SBM lost its fastest growing piece of business in India when the RBI directed it to stop https://the-ken.com/story/slice-uni-cards-other-fintechs-start-over-as-rbi-says-no-to-easy-credit/ issuing credit line-backed prepaid payment instrument (PPI) cards, which wiped out the business models of fintechs such as Slice and Uni Cards.

This time around, it seems like SBM was acting on its own without any regulatory diktat (at least publicly), but those in the industry say that the RBI has been running audits on the bank and that this was most likely due to pressure from the top.

Whatever the reason, it’s a new day, with a fresh set of fintechs and their users waking up to their businesses getting hammered—courtesy SBM Bank India.
