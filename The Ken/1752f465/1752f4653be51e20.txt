A minor side effect of being a journalist in a newsroom full of really sharp and experienced business reporters connected via Slack is this: few big stories really surprise you. Barring gossip and unrevealed facts, virtually every significant trend, company or story has often been posted or discussed.

Finding a major story that hasn’t been reported and told well is, to a journalist, the equivalent of finding a species of animal or a nearby constellation that the world hasn’t discovered yet. It doesn’t happen all that often for the same reason new animal species and nearby constellations aren’t discovered often—people are constantly looking. And if they were easy to find, they’d have already been found.

Today’s story by Seema, our editor, is about India’s largest single-strategy private equity fund, PremjiInvest. It started in 2006 with an initial investment of $163 million largely in public equities. The investment was entirely from the personal wealth of billionaire-turned-philanthropist Azim Premji, which in turn was derived from his stake in the company he founded, IT services giant Wipro.

Then 61, Premji had a few quirky and old-fashioned thoughts when he hired a 33-year old research analyst to run the new fund. It would focus on building a long-term portfolio. Nothing too speculative. Only in companies with high governance standards. And in ethical entrepreneurs.

It walked away from investing in India’s hottest edtech unicorn, Byju’s, which has helped its investors multiply their money every six to 12 months. Why? Byju’s aggressive sales model didn’t pass PremjiInvest’s internal forensics checks.

With all those filters and bars, it would be surprising if PremjiInvest made enough money to even cover the cost of inflation or to pay the salaries of its staff.

Instead, it has consistently been able to generate annual returns of over 20%. And today, the fund has assets of over $5 billion. Including stakes in companies like Moderna, the US biotech that’s a frontrunner for making a Covid-19 vaccine.
