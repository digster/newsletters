Good Morning Ishan,
Bajaj Finance is easier to define by what it is than by what it is not.
Known for: Being India’s largest non-banking financial company that finances smartphones, air conditioners, shoe racks, and gas stoves. Essentially, a consumer-durables lender (80% of its new customers come for these loans).
Not known for (yet): Being a bank-like entity handing out long-term, secured loans.
But hold on; Bajaj Finance is attempting a wild leap. From what it’s known for to what it’s not known for. In the last three quarters, this non-bank has dabbled in tractor financing, kicked off a microfinance pilot, and even hopped on the new-vehicle loans bandwagon. Rounak, the author of today’s story, discovers that the company is eyeing the uncharted territories of commercial-vehicle financing and agri-financing.
“Why the diversification now,” you ask? Because it can and because it has to.
It can: The $50 billion company performed strongly in the quarter ended December 2023. Its assets under management grew higher than IDFC First and Indusind; new loans flooded in, and the customer count breached 80 million.
It has to: India’s banking regulator has been throwing many a curveball, putting Bajaj Finance’s favourite consumer-lending toys on the restricted list. Even the co-branded credit card got a one-year extension instead of the usual two. And, meanwhile, the once-smooth consumer-credit market has turned choppy.
And then, there’s a major challenge—mostly of Bajaj Finance’s own making—that’s not letting it make that jump.
