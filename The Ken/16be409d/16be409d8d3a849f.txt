Good Morning Ishan,

Normally, I don’t even read stories about OYO published in Indian media publications. Why not, you ask? It’s simple. If you have read one story, you have read them all. That’s because most stories about OYO have a common template. It looks like this: 

OYO to invest <insert dollar amount> over <insert years> in <insert country>

It’s like a mad lib. Don’t believe me? Just google and check. You will find stories about China, https://yourstory.com/2019/06/oyo-crosses-5-lakh-room-inventory-china Vietnam, https://skift.com/2019/07/02/oyo-to-invest-50-million-in-vietnam-to-grow-to-20000-hotel-rooms-by-2021/ USA, https://www.zeebiz.com/small-business/news-oyo-to-invest-300-mn-in-us-promises-to-bring-hospitality-experience-at-price-point-never-imagined-102897 South Asia... https://www.livemint.com/companies/start-ups/oyo-to-invest-rs-1-400-crore-in-india-south-asia-in-2019-1552385845515.htmland Kerala https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/info-tech/oyo-to-invest-100-cr-to-ramp-up-business-in-kerala/article28159066.ece. And that’s just in the last three weeks. The best part is this also makes for a great party game. Call some friends over. $60 million in Peru. $2 million in Antarctica. $83 million in Wakanda. Try it out. Lots of fun.

However, in a shocking departure from tradition, there were two stories about OYO published last week that didn’t fit this template. One was that OYO was ‘looking to IPO’ for the sum of $18 billion ‘two or three years down the line’. The other was that Ritesh Agarwal, OYO’s founder, is reportedly in talks to buy back OYO shares to the value of $1.5 billion.

Huh. 

What does this mean? 

Sumanth and Rohin, in our story today, give us the answer. In one word, they call it a ‘kayfabe’ - which translates to ‘the willing suspension of disbelief that is used to create aspects such as feuds and gimmicks in a manner similar to other forms of fictional entertainment’. 

Our story today is about why OYO and Ritesh Agarwal are doing what they are doing. It’s about breaking down the intricacies of fundraising, buyouts, and debt financing to explain why a company’s CEO would raise and borrow at the same time. All in simple, everyday language. It’s a story that has everything - including the identity of OYO’s new investor. 

Personally, I think it’s one of our best stories. I learnt a lot. You will too.
