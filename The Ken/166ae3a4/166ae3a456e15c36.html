This is back in school. In a tiny suburb of Kolkata. Specifically, the half pant days, which means before class 6. Our school had the switch from half pant to trousers after class 5.
 
Now that I think about it, I don’t know why, and I can’t remember if I have ever thought about it so deeply. But half pant. In winter. This is the 90s we are talking about, and boy, I do remember feeling chilly. And so, on one of those early winter mornings, I’m on my way to school, just about stepping outside the house, when the Bengali landlord uncle stops me.
 
Him: Why have you put powder all over yourself? 
 
Me: What? 
 
Him, now pointing at my face and legs. Me, embarrassed, looking at my legs and seeing it is all white. Aghast, I avoid the uncle and start walking away furiously. I remember this phase of school as long and embarrassing. Until I finally figured out the culprit. Parachute coconut oil. That damn oil would turn hard and white in winter, and the whole family would wrestle with that blue bottle. But back then, that’s all we had. And we didn’t know better. So, every day. Bath. Parachute. White. School. 
 
Precious winter memories. And if I may indulge you further, precious winter days. Which my colleague Harveen says you must care about deeply. 
 
Winter is not how it used to be. The temperatures are higher. The duration of the season is shorter. Which means that winter wear, a category which once heralded the change in season and fashion, is suffering. Over the last two years, winter wear sales in India are estimated to have fallen by at least 10-12%, as per the Clothing Manufacturers Association of India.
 
Of course, India isn’t alone. It is a global phenomena. 
 
This change means that companies in the business of winter wear are suffering. And here’s the clincher. 2015 was the fifth warmest year since the early 1900s and the first one when distributors of Ludhiana-based apparel brand Monte Carlo saw a drop in sales of woollen sweaters. A first in the company’s 34-year history.
