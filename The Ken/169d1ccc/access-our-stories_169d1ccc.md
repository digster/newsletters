Good Morning Ishan,
The past week has been nothing short of a blur. But what a colourful blur it has been.
Our stories have tackled different, challenging subjects, with some kickass storytelling, if I may say so myself. So when PGK asked a pointed question about my experience of The Ken as a reader reading on the app, I paused. As a member of the editorial team, sometimes it becomes hard to look beyond the quality of our stories and appreciate the quality of the product as a whole.
The answer came to me. Access.
The best thing about reading on The Ken app, in my experience, is knowing that, as a paid subscriber, I have access. It was an interesting revelation. A revelation that stayed with me long enough to view our individual stories this week through that lens. Interestingly, quite a few of them had to do with that privilege.
Look at Suraksha's piece on oxytocin, for instance. One patient activist group and three pharmaceutical companies at loggerheads with the government over this critical drug—one that ensures the safety of birthing women. The government has given a public sector company sole domain over oxytocin manufacturing. Essentially, for many mothers in need, this is a classic case of "Access Denied".
