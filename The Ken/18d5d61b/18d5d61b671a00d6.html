Good Morning Ishan,

You gotta hand it to Ola supremo Bhavish Aggarwal.

It takes a certain chutzpah to expect and get “unicorn” status for a just-born business. Last week, Krutrim, the AI startup only recently co-founded by Aggarwal, raised money at a cool $1 billion valuation. Many jaws fell to the floor.

But the feat pales in comparison to last month’s stunner—a $7–8 billion valuation reportedly being eyed for IPO-bound Ola Electric. Never mind that the electric two-wheeler maker is still deep in the red with a loss of about Rs 1,500 crore ($180 million) last year. Losses deter losers. Aggarwal, a man in a hurry, wants to go long and fast.

Ola bikes also go long and fast, claims the company. But the buyers who placed their trust are now grappling with much heartburn and range anxiety.

When the rubber hits the road, the actual distance covered on a single charge is nearly 40% less than what Ola Electric advertises, finds Shaswata after speaking with multiple owners for today’s story.

That, too, might have been no deterrent had Ola Electric remained the only real show in EV two-wheeler town. But worthy competitors like TVS and Ather are snapping at its heels. And to boot, the chasm between their claims and realities is much smaller than that of the market leader.

And range is not the only worry on the minds of Ola’s riders.

Meanwhile, the company is busy fine-tuning its operations and slashing costs in all manner in the run-up to the IPO.

Can Ola Electric ride over the weak spots? Can its proposed public listing overcome public perception?
