Microsoft chief Satya Nadella was in town yesterday and made some big-ticket announcements on making India AI-first, training 10 million people, etc. The tech giant is also on a major catch-up spree these days.

If its artificial-intelligence assistant, Copilot, is struggling to catch up with ChatGPT in the consumer space (37 million vs 433 million downloads in the 18 months to December), its hardware unit is catching up with Nvidia’s chips.

The AI boom has been a gust of wind under its intelligent cloud services—this business grew 40% between the 2022 and 2024 financial years, compared to other business divisions that grew 13%. This wind will continue to blow, but Microsoft, like AWS and Google, is worried about its margins being eaten up by Nvidia.

A regular non-AI workload programme run on Azure’s CPUs brings in “margins of about 65% on average” but when it runs AI models, like say, Meta’s Llama on Azure, the “margins drop to 15–20%”.

That explains why the Redmond giant is running out of time to fix its leaky business. Since one in five of the world’s chip-design talent comes from India, Microsoft hurriedly showed up on campuses this year with a laundry list for hardware engineers.

As Abhirami reports in her story, this hiring is special (Nadella also hinted at their ambition on Tuesday). In Microsoft India, this team is just three reporting levels below the country head—making it a relatively high-level team compared to most others in the organisation.

At the upcoming GPU tech conference in San Jose in March, AI-chip maker Nvidia is likely to announce the launch of its serverless API, Nvidia Cloud Functions, The Ken has learnt. That is, Nvidia’s GPU users will no longer have to go through a third party’s middle layer of software—such as Azure’s development environment—to access cloud functions.

This presents an opportunity for data-centre-service providers such as India’s Yotta and the Japanese NTT and a setback for cloud-service providers like Azure and AWS that derive quite a bit of their revenues from providing this layer.

With India’s chip environment already activated, Microsoft’s entry will be watched by campuses and customers alike. Let’s get to Abhirami’s story.
