This is the first email I’m writing to you after joining The Ken as the Head of Products. Coming out of a whirlwind of product launches in October, as I sit down to write the weekly recap email, I’m reminded again of why I love The Ken so much.
Apart from the wonderful writing and the journalistic depth, one of the main reasons I started reading The Ken is that every single story made me more curious. If building a mental model is like solving a giant jigsaw puzzle, The Ken always gives me the missing pieces, helps me rearrange the existing pieces, and sometimes pushes me to throw out the entire board and start from scratch again.
So, let’s have a look at last week’s puzzle board.
We began the week with Seetharaman’s story on Reliance’s ambitious bet on
[solar power](https://the-ken.com/story/reliances-future-proof-wager-against-chinas-solar-might/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), going in-depth into the strategic and geopolitical implications for the company after it made a series of investments earlier this month.
Then on Tuesday, Anand wrote about
[ITC Infotech](https://the-ken.com/story/itc-infotech-the-hidden-it-gem-that-could-make-itcs-stock-shine/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter), how the company has orchestrated a turnaround success story, and what it means for the parent conglomerate. If it plays its cards right, ITC Infotech could become for ITC what L&T Infotech has become for L&T.
In the world of entrepreneurship (and product management), there is always opportunity wherever there is friction. Jaspreet’s story on Wednesday is about companies that are grabbing this opportunity by building financial infrastructure through their APIs, in an industry that is notoriously full of friction. They may even
[reimagine banking](https://the-ken.com/story/bankings-new-bridge-builders-are-fashioning-a-more-level-playing-field/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter).
On Thursday, Aayush wrote his debut story for
The Ken on
[Shadowfax](https://the-ken.com/story/too-many-cooks-in-shadowfaxs-hyperlocal-delivery-kitchen/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) and the uber-competitive space of hyperlocal delivery. Read the story to discover how Shadowfax is navigating the space, where its current biggest clients are quickly becoming its biggest competitors.
On Friday, Olina pulled back the curtain on
[Reliance’s edtech ambitions](https://the-ken.com/story/reliance-jiofy-edtech-embibe/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) with her report on the impending relaunch of Embibe, the little-known edtech startup that Reliance acquired in 2018. An interesting nugget in the story is that Reliance, with all its distribution might, plans to launch the product through schools rather than going direct-to-consumer.
