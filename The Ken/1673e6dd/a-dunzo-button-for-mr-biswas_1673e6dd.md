Kabeer Biswas, founder and CEO of Dunzo, the surprise sleeper hit app of 2018, clearly has a love and hate relationship with Amazon. Just like most of his on-demand or e-commerce peers. They all want to simultaneously defeat Amazon and be Amazon.
Till last year, Dunzo was Bangalore’s best-kept secret. Passed on exclusively through word of mouth by early-adopters, Uber-users and startup folks. No matter how ridiculous or mundane a task you had in the real world, Dunzo would politely do it for you.
Then, last year, an investment led by Google gave it enough muscle to start cranking up the growth levers. In one year, it went from 65,000 monthly transactions to nearly 1 million. From one city to six. From cute hipster fulfilment service to an instant delivery layer for local commerce. And though you could hear the collective groans when it decided to suspend alcohol deliveries (regulatory uncertainty), it instead decided to try and move people around, too (only in Gurugram for now).
