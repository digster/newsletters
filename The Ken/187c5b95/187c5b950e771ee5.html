Good Morning Ishan,
 
What do a female bodybuilder, a small-town vlogger with an army of online bhakts, and a fitness influencer belting out workout tips while boasting about his riches have in common? 
 
Two things primarily. They have millions of followers. And they are not known for their expertise in investing.
 
Yet, to their dedicated followers, their investment advice is an epiphany of sorts. A blind faith that following their advice is the quickest way to get rich.  
 
This is the phenomenon of social-media figures with huge followings—many of whom The Ken’s readers may not have heard of—peddling investment advice and promoting platforms that could potentially lead to losses for many financially naive Indians. After all, just 27% of Indians are considered financially literate. 
 
This is probably what prompted finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman at a recent event in Bengaluru to note: “Social influencers, finfluencers are all out there, but a very strong sense of caution is required in each one of us to make sure we do double checking, counterchecking, and don’t go as a flock into something because somebody else has gone there.”
  
In today’s story, Gaurav Tyagi digs into the world of such social-media influencers, infiltrates Telegram networks, and tracks their investment-advisory tactics to reveal the underbelly of this ecosystem.
