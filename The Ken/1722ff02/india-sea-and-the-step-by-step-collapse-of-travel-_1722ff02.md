After being cooped up for weeks, the mere thought of travel can perk up many of us these days. I’ve begun telling myself that if 2020 is about indoors, 2021 will be about outdoors. About wide open spaces, offsites... (Note to my colleagues.)
The pandemic has pounded the travel and tourism industry. All global destinations have restrictions in place; local air travel is grounded. Yet sections of the industry are already looking ahead.
The short-term trends:
1. More road trips, which will adversely affect the airlines but not the hotels
2. Rise in room rates: well, data shows average daily rates in India are actually back to the levels they were in January. They have even risen in Indonesia.
But before we looked ahead, we decided to partner with RateGain to look at what just happened. The travel tech company works with a multitude of OTAs and hotels, including every Fortune 500 travel and hospitality company.
RateGain culled and analysed data from its platform for more than 135,000 hotels that use its platform across India, China, Singapore, Thailand, Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. It also shared data on more than 900 airlines in partnership with OAG, an aviation data intelligence company.
The data is divided into two periods—January through the first week of May this year and the same period last year, with an equal number of samples taken for both periods. Prajakta and Vandana have parsed this data into several charts that tell us a Covid impact story of their own.
All countries have been affected, to varying degrees. But all could take hope from China, where flight volumes are picking up.
Strangely, Thailand, which is estimated to take the biggest hit in the region owing to a tourism-driven economy, also stands to be in good stead for recovery.
