On 13 November, edtech Physicswallah’s IPO closed fully subscribed—a strong start for a company aiming for a $3.2-billion valuation.

But more interestingly, even post listing, founders Alakh Pandey and Prateek Boob will manage to hold on to 72% of the company—an almost unbelievable feat in India’s consumer-internet landscape. For comparison, Deepinder Goyal owned just 5.5% at the time of Zomato’s IPO.

How did Physicswallah manage to stay so tightly held?

For starters, its first institutional fundraise in 2022—$100 million from the likes of Westbridge and Lightspeed—was straightaway at near-unicorn valuation. This, coupled with the freeze in late-stage edtech funding after the Byju’s fiasco, meant that capital simply stopped being available.

During this time, the company ran a tight ship—without a single salesperson for the first seven years, almost no marketing spend, and a tidy profit on a modest revenue.

But then around FY24, something shifted. Marketing spend shot up, acquisitions started piling up, and offline investments like the Physicswallah Institute of Innovation kicked in. A lot of the IPO proceeds will go into similar ambitions.

That said, the personality that is Pandey has remained the heart and soul of the enterprise. As Physicswallah enters its most capital-intensive phase yet, will the cult of Pandey—not to mention his highly concentrated ownership—prove an asset or a liability?

Suprita’s story today dives into some of these questions.
