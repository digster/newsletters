Good Morning Ishan,
Once in a while, I hear my colleagues in Bengaluru talk about their water woes.
It’s a situation that I’ve experienced while working in West Asia and North Africa, so I can sympathise. But for this to be a persistent problem in South Asia’s premier IT hub just feels… wrong.
Today’s story looks at an attempt by the Bangalore Water Supply and Sewerage Board, or BWSSB, to fix the problem. They’re working with private tankers to bring water directly to residents across the city, which sounds good on paper.
And just like Apple said in 2009, “There’s an app for that.”
But hauling water on behalf of BWSSB is hardly a good line of business. It has “very minimal margins” and is “not even profitable” at times, according to a tanker operator who spoke to The Ken. Besides, payments drag on for months. So why bother working for the board at all?
Mutasim has the details about how the BWSSB hands contracts to operators that it previously called “the tanker mafia”, and how the initiative is leading to more problems in Bengaluru’s water supply than there were before. Even if you don’t live in the city, today’s story is well worth your time. [Check it out](https://the-ken.com/story/bengalurus-fix-for-the-water-tanker-mafia-yet-more-tankers-what-could-go-wrong/).
