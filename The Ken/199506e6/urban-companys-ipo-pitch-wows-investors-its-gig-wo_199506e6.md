Good Morning Ishan,
Urban Company’s IPO has already made headlines for being the “most subscribed offer this year”. Subscribed over 100X at a frothy valuation of Rs 15,000 crore, it’s way ahead of, say, Swiggy, whose IPO last year was subscribed just 3.5X.
For a home-services platform hinged on gig-workers—many of whom have protested against the company in the last few years—the heightened investor interest seems surprising. After all, it’s a messy business.
Think 55,000 plumbers, electricians, and beauticians sprawled across 47 cities, subjected to low pay, lack of benefits, and arbitrary deactivations. Seems like a powder keg more than an investment. Uncertain government policy hardly helps.
But it’s these very gig workers driving the company’s latest pivot.
Urban Company is expanding from services into products, and its gig workers, from after-sales support to salespeople. Central to the shift is the company’s in-house brand Native—selling water purifiers, smart locks for doors, and, before long, ACs and refrigerators—whose revenue has grown 30X in two years. Investor interest in Urban Company’s IPO isn’t just a punt on the company. It’s a punt on this model.
Then again, is it really so easy to go from cheap after-sales service to making and selling highly technical appliances?
