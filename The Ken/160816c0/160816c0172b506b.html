Good Morning Ishan,

As expressed in an earlier letter, December to some means home. At least to me, it does. So here I am, back home, in a city that understands winter, albeit with a fair bit of lung-choking smoke. I speak of Delhi, in case you're wondering.
 
As the year rapidly ends, I hope you too find what you're looking for. It could be a new job, a pet, conquering a fear, climbing new heights, being home, not being home, travelling, reading, reading a lot more... Could be anything, really.
 
But while you still figure out all that plus your last-minute Christmas (oh we know you have plans already) and New Year's plans, how about a Saturday morning flashback of all that we've written for you this week; our last week of production in 2017? Stay with me.
 
We started the 2017's penultimate week with a sunny story from Seema about commercial rooftop solar, specifically Amplus and Cleanmax. They're striking gold, Seema says. Read to know why.
 
Tuesday saw Venkat's piece on a company that thrives on helping interns find their way in a befuddling job market. Intershala, as it's called, went from being a humble hobby blog to a space that entertains over 15,000 internship applications a day!
 
Midweek, our newsroom put their sharp minds together to focus on Ola's acquisition of Foodpanda. The result? Patanjali's nuanced (and yet, intriguing) report on the same.
 
Gayathri Vaidyanathan, a first-time contributor to The Ken, wrote our Thursday piece on the generics price-fixing saga in the US. Gayathri's FREE piece focuses on the events that transpired between a few companies in the US generics market that may or may not amount to price-fixing 101. Trust me; you want to read this one.
 
And we closed with the BHIM of stories. Arundhati's exclusive on businesses getting a GST version of BHIM ended our year on a high. It looked at the government's attempt to cushion companies while filing returns. Enough to provide free accounting software!
 
Now, next week we'll be emailing you the Best of 2017, authored by our writers, highlighting all the fascinating things that happened this year. These will be free-for-all reads, and if you like them, you must share them. After that, we'll be back in production, writing to you while you drink your morning cuppa, starting 2 January. Hope you look forward to our stories in 2018! We know we do.
 
Have a great weekend. Merry Christmas!
 
Cheers,
