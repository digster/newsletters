Good Morning Ishan,

Growing up as an Asian child, my summers overflowed with extracurricular activities: ballet, piano, swimming, singing, social sports, art, cooking, pottery—the list was endless. Some passions endured, while others distilled into valuable life lessons.

Take my singing class, for example. My teacher and I delved into the nuances of performing “Edelweiss” from The Sound of Music—learning to modulate voices, shift tones, and project with precision. Countless sessions were dedicated to mastering the established rules for this seemingly simple song. Once I grasped the conventional approach, my teacher surprised me with an unexpected directive: “You’ve learned the rules; now it’s time to break them.”

In singing, as in numerous disciplines, mastering techniques grants the freedom to bend or break rules to enhance one’s craft. Apparently, there’s an art to breaking rules.

Singing itself didn’t resonate with me in the long run, but the underlying lesson about embracing rule-breaking—or challenging expectations—has woven itself into my art and in the fabric of our stories for this week.

In India, EV charger manufacturers are skirting ambiguous schemes to get subsidies. On Monday, Nathan brought a story with exclusive access to documents on the scheme. The documents uncovered these manufacturers’ failure to comply with the 50% localisation requirement in production, raising concerns about adherence to the subsidy scheme.

Meanwhile, on the New York Stock Exchange, Singapore-headquartered Sea Limited’s third-quarter earnings call established a recurring pattern: a substantial market sell-off following each call in 2023. My curiosity as to why investors are fleeing the stock after the leadership spoke led me to Tuesday’s story.

Companies though aren’t just defined by bottom lines, they’re also defined by how they treat their workers. This is what Fairwork India’s ranking of internet companies is aiming to do. Shivani’s deep dive on Wednesday posts a bold question: Is the ranking of companies’ work conditions reliable?

In the Philippines, venture capitalists are shifting interests. Isa tells the emerging story of how previous heavy investment in B2C in the country transitioned to a focus on pouring capital into the B2B space.

Lastly, in Friday’s StraitUp edition, the Southeast Asia team looked at the Philippines’ recent step forward in nuclear energy and how the digital banking space is heating up in Indonesia.

That’s it for this week. Hope you folks enjoyed our stories, as we’ll be back with more cutting-edge insights on Monday.
