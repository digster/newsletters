Good Morning Ishan,
 
December brings with it a lot of introspection. That introspection often results in insight. And that insight often gives us clarity into all that was right, and all that wasn't.
 
And quickly, we realise how to solve the things that aren't right, the solutions that have been staring at us all along, and even those pesky ones we've tried hard to ignore. After all, the years are passing, and one's got to fix things and make the most of what one's got, right?
 
Well, the companies and agencies The Ken covered this week are no different. We started the week with Ruhi's story on the FDA's solution for slacking Indian pharma—various warning letters. And some more.
 
Tuesday saw Paytm's solution for a sticky situation. As Arundhati puts it, Paytm is trying to turn a bad investment into a better one by acquiring Nearbuy and merging it with Little. She calls it Paytm Lemonade.
 
Payal narrated the final chapter of her ESOP's Fables on Wednesday. Her piece charted the good-to-have, the must-have and the strictly forbidden map in Esop-scape.
 
Thursday had Patanjali ask Shopclues how it plans to survive. The answer? By selling itself. But wait, this was worded so eloquently that you must hear it from CEO Sanjay Sethi himself. "Look, Shopclues is like my daughter. I will marry her off to the best man. That man can be an IPO, that man can be Paytm, that man can be Flipkart or even Snapdeal." You know you want to read the rest of it. Go on then; it's free.
 
The week ended with yet another long-term solution. Sumanth's story on Sequoia—arguably the world’s most prestigious VC firm—was about how, despite its stature, it hasn't done so well in India. Instead, it has found a new, unlikely avenue.
 
If anything, this week gave us some fresh perspective on how things can be fixed, changed, refurbished, or even, sold. Those are some solid life goals I'd say.
 
Hope you have a great weekend! And do subscribe.
