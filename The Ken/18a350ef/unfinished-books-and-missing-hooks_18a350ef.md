Good Morning Ishan,
Oops, I did it again. Second time in three months. What rotten luck!
Have you ever enthusiastically read the first few chapters of a book, only to never pick it up again shortly after?
In my case, the first one to fall was Dostoevsky’s classic The Brothers Karamazov three months ago. More recently, Malcolm Gladwell’s Blink–a book persuasively recommended by my best friend–has been gathering dust on my shelf.
With this, the seeds of self-doubt were planted. After all, these were well-regarded books written by two great writers. The problem must then lie with me, right?
While I partly agree with that, I’ve figured out what actually went wrong.
I started reading both books with great expectations, waiting to be blown away right from the first line. It didn’t happen. One chapter down, two chapters stressfully down, and my patience began wearing thin. And because it wasn’t a work email, I had no incentive to finish it.
However, I reckon it has happened to you too. Expectations or not, sometimes, some things just don’t have…erm…what do they call it?
The Hook!
