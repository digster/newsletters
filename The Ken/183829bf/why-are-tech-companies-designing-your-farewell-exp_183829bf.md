An engineering company I once worked for sent me a letter 45 days after my last working day saying, “we’re terminating your employment as a disciplinary action for being a no-show for 45 days.”
Cut to 8 years later—the last company I worked for had me sit down with almost every person in leadership relevant to me and thoughtfully tried to dissect why I was leaving and what could’ve been better.
How we join and leave companies has drastically changed over the last 10 years. While pictures of swanky onboarding kits flood your LinkedIn feeds, there is a softer, more purposeful, and far more significant phenomenon taking place. That of offboarding well.

A wonderful subscriber of The Ken, Rohan Jaikishen, has done my job and summed up the best insights from this episode on his [blog](https://medium.com/@rohanjaikishen/jackies-journaling-day-66-first-principles-podcast-feat-naveen-tewari-58a28675e658).
- The opportunity for Glance: convert 1 billion screens into smart surfaces. This is so powerful and empowering. The opportunity of screens converting into smart surfaces is large, especially with 5G and IOT on the anvil to engulf ways of living and working.
- Survivor vs Innovator: These days, most start-ups call themselves innovators. Innovation is par for the course to operate in today’s business environment. However, surviving (in fact thriving) for 15 years plus, going through the share of ups and downs, is what makes the InMobi story so inspirational.
- Building something sustainably requires time: Rapid blitz scaling may come with survival challenges in the long run.
