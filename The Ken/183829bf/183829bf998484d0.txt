An engineering company I once worked for sent me a letter 45 days after my last working day saying, “we’re terminating your employment as a disciplinary action for being a no-show for 45 days.” 

Cut to 8 years later—the last company I worked for had me sit down with almost every person in leadership relevant to me and thoughtfully tried to dissect why I was leaving and what could’ve been better. 

How we join and leave companies has drastically changed over the last 10 years. While pictures of swanky onboarding kits flood your LinkedIn feeds, there is a softer, more purposeful, and far more significant phenomenon taking place. That of offboarding well.
