As far as smartphones are concerned, Google is the King. That’s because it’s number one in the world in three areas: Destination, Network and Distribution. The most visited website globally. The biggest ad network ever built. The largest mobile operating system. This is what makes it Lord of the Seven Kingdoms, Protector of...you get it. 
 
But India is a little different. Google is powerful, but its influence has limits. Why? Because the world’s largest mobile operating system, Android, has a peculiar feature. 
 
Google’s Android works only on smartphones. 
 
And India has around 400 million feature phones. 
 
Sure, you might say, but it’s a matter of time. Smartphones will phase out feature phones in India eventually. 
 
Haha. So cute. Did you also think Ned Stark was the hero GoT?
 
Say hello to Jio. The King in The North. With 300 million users. A significant chunk of whom use Jio’s feature phone to access the internet. With WhatsApp, YouTube. Facebook. And 4G. It’s as good as a smartphone.
 
Woah woah woah. A feature phone that supports YouTube? 
 
Yup. All thanks to this little company that made it possible for Jio - called KaiOS Technologies, who built the operating system on the Jio Phone. KaiOS is a remarkable company. With two prominent investors. 
 
Jio, which invested $7 million.
 
And Google, with $22 million. 
 
Jio needs KaiOS to keep its feature phone relevant in India. Google needs KaiOS to get to its next billion users. And KaiOS, well, it needs to figure its way out. What are these three companies doing? Will they forge an alliance? Or wait for the right opportunity at a wedding? How does it all end?
