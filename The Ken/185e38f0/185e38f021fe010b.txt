We understand so little about corporate coaching. What it is, what it does, how it is practiced, who practices it.

There are a number of reasons for this. First, it's expensive to become a corporate coach, which means there are very few executive coaches on the market. This drives up the price of the service.

This, in turn, means that access to an executive coach is denied to all but the most financially successful and autonomous. Most of the most financially successful and autonomous people in the world are men. 

Third, there's almost no good way to find an effective executive coach, except through word of mouth. Recommendations are shared, and anecdotes swapped, only through networks of wildly successful and affluent men. Which means the rest of us -- even those who could afford the price tag -- aren't clued into what an executive coach does. Or what a good executive coach is. Or who to go to. The incumbent networks keep us out of the loop.
