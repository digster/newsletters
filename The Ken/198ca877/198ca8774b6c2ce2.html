“The US and China have never treated India as their equal partner. The UAE does.”

That line, from a former diplomat in today’s story, explains quite a lot.

Because while Washington is busy slapping tariffs and Beijing is busy withholding rare earths, Dubai has been busy making deals. It took just 88 days for India and the UAE to sign their big trade pact in 2022. Since then, bilateral trade has surged past $100 billion.

And Indian companies have noticed. Not content with just selling toothpaste or buses into the Gulf, they’re now building the stuff there. Himalaya Wellness is setting up a 225,000 sq ft pharma plant in Dubai to spit out 3 billion tablets a year. Jindal Saw is dropping $105 million on a new pipe factory. Omega Seiki is turning the city into its EV-and-drone hub. Even Tata, Dabur, and Lenskart already have serious operations across the UAE free-trade zones.

Why? Zero corporate tax. Customs clearance in hours, not days. Sovereign-wealth funds that actually cut cheques. Yes, wages in Dubai are higher than in India—but the rest more than makes up for it.

Is this brain drain, then? Not quite. Most of these companies are keeping their biggest factories in India. Dubai is less an escape hatch than a launchpad to Europe, Africa, and the Middle East.
