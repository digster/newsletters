Good Morning Ishan,
Zomato started delivering food. Info Edge wrote the first cheque.
Zomato became a unicorn. Info Edge hung on.
Zomato went public. Info Edge hung on.
Blinkit blinked into existence. Info Edge hung on.
Zomato’s name became Eternal. Info Edge said, “Nice.” And hung on.
Eternal’s profits fell 80%. Zepto knocked. Swiggy knocked. Tata knocked. Amazon and Flipkart knocked. Info Edge watched the traffic—and, yes, still hung on.
This is Info Edge: part classifieds business, part tech investor, full-time believer in its portfolio companies. It’s been riding shotgun with Zomato and Policybazaar for over a decade—never sold a share. “Strategic, not opportunistic,” it likes to say.
But when Zomato’s food delivery is slowing and the entire quick-commerce market looks like a money pit with a stopwatch, one has to wonder: how many exit gates were missed?
Info Edge’s core business, Naukri, isn’t exactly coasting either. IT hiring is cooling, AI is knocking on the door, and the rest of its classifieds empire—real estate, education, matrimony—barely moves the needle on investor excitement.
Sure, Policybazaar is a bright spot. It’s profitable. Investors are finally warming to it. And that makes the eternal question even more pointed: if you had to sell, wouldn’t you sell the one sweating to stay ahead?
But Info Edge won’t blink. At least not yet. (Rs 720 crore invested in Zomato and Policybazaar more than 10 years ago is now worth over 50X, just saying!)
