I feel bad for this person. But then, you know: what’s bad must only get worse. The complaints for AppsDaily go on for 62 pages. 
 
After having raised $30 million in funding from the likes of Kalaari Capital, Ru Net, Qualcomm Ventures, Zodius Capital and being in business for almost nine years, AppsDaily has gone under. One of the reasons AppsDaily’s fall stands out is because it was among the first to try the Online 2 Offline (O2O) strategy to sell apps and services. And sure, it was successful for a while. A lot of other startups followed in its footsteps. The likes of PepperFry and Lenskart perfected it, but AppsDaily could not. Where did it all come apart?
 
Four reasons. A. The rise of e-commerce. B. Cheap data. C. Humongous number of claims and more so, fraudulent claims. D. Pivot to phone repair.
 
AppsDaily just could not keep up, as the world around us changed. That’s my colleague Patanjali’s piece for today. You can read it here: https://the-ken.com/appsdaily-shuts/
