Good Morning Ishan,

Getting fit is hard. The problem lies not in starting out but in maintaining it. Who knows this better than Bengaluru-based Healthify, which claims to be the “world’s largest AI and fitness” company with “40 million users”?

Even if we take “40 million” with a bucket of salt—its monthly active users averaged 2.7 million in 2024, according to Sensor Tower—its free user base has always been sizeable, thanks to performance marketing. But the digital fitness company has found it hard to maintain the paying user base. Or even the rate of addition of free users.

As if a subscription business isn’t hard enough. You add fitness to it, sprinkle some AI tools, and you have a health recipe that the Indian palate rarely likes. It’s not a DIY nation.

Healthify has been trimming its Indian workforce over the last several months, with the latest considerable cull hitting its sales team. Having run a 10+ year race in India, Healthify, which counts Khosla Ventures—founder Vinod Khosla lives and breathes AI—as one of its investors, has embarked on a sprint (or a marathon?) to the US market.

The Americans may be more DIY-friendly, but the US is a competitive market where Healthify is not the first or even the second mover. But its investors believe that’s the market to be in if its AI-based plans have to earn money.

How and why Healthify has chosen this financial fitness regimen for itself is Shivani’s story today.
