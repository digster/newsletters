You would think that a company that placed its bets on 'Make in India' years before the phrase came into vogue would be raking in the moolah right about now.

Not quite so with Skanray.

The medical equipment maker started in 2011 and has been laser-focused on indigenously manufacturing devices such as X-ray machines, ventilators and patient monitors.

It started with a bang in 2012, with the headline-grabbing acquisition of Larsen & Toubro's medical technology business. A business whose revenue was 20x Skanray's at the time. That was a statement of intent if ever there was one.

Its revenue surged, obviously. And rocketed as Skanray acquired a couple of other companies through 2013 and 2014. 

That's where the good news ends. Skanray's revenue has stagnated in comparison since, while its profits have evaporated. Ask founder Vishwaprasad Alva why, and his blame list spans everything from rising imports of devices to a bad banking system for credit.

"India is the toughest place to do business. We are lonely here. We don't have another company of similar nature. Where is the question of comparing?" Alva asks.

He's not wrong. Not complete in his assessment, but not wrong.

"We don't see any competition from Skanray anymore in the market. Operationally, they aren't doing good. Revenue is one indicator - they manage to sell products by lowering prices. But if we talk about profits, there are hardly any," says an executive of a rival company.

A scathing assessment by any standards.

So what happened to what was once destined to be the poster-boy for 'Make (medical equipment) in India'?
