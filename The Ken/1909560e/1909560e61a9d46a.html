Good Morning Ishan,

India’s spread of equity culture has been phenomenal of late. In recent years, the number of investors in the country has trebled to 95 million, with a little less than half of them being under 30 years of age.

Unsurprisingly, the current crazy bull run in the Indian stock market is driven more by domestic investors than by any kind of foreign capital.

Consider this: one in every 10 stocks in the BSE 500 now sports a price-to-earnings of over 100X. This is far higher than the 27X that the index trades at.

Market watchers globally are spellbound. But compared to the US market—another bull run story which is driven by tech stocks and AI optimism—India’s is a broad-based story. Heck, even staid public sector stocks are on fire.

Take, for instance, the state-controlled defence company Mazagon Dock Shipbuilders. A name that for many of us rings familiar not because of business fundamentals but because of old Bollywood movies, has seen its stock quadruple over the past year. Its market cap is above Rs 1.12 lakh crore (US$13 billion). Its profit? Less than Rs 2,000 crore.

People believe it’s the “gush of money” that is not letting the market fall, or even making every minor fall in the market look like an opportunity to buy. Apparently, “buying the dip and more has become a favourite game in India,” a market expert told Anand, the author of today’s story.

Rich with data, insights, and a perspective that runs contra to the perception that the good times will continue to roll, Anand has a sharp story today. It’s free, so read and share widely.
