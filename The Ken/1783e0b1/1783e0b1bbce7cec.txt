What does it take to become a successful fintech startup in India, arguably the world’s most innovative and fast-growing fintech market?

An original idea? Nope. Everyone eventually ends up lending, so why not just cut to the chase at the get-go.

Backing by well-known VC firms? Nope. Doesn’t make any difference. The VCs know it too.

Adequate capital so you have the balance sheet strength to be able to offer loans to your customers? Nope. Just partner with one of the hundreds of non-banking lenders who already hold licences from India’s central bank, but don’t have much of a business (or business sense).

Great branding and advertising, so that your product will stand out in a market filled with hundreds of competitors? Nope. Nobody cares for your brand. Customers only care for the speed and ease with which they can borrow money from you.

A salesforce? Nope. Just publish your app on Google’s Play Store and let its distribution algorithms do the hard work. Some paid marketing to push your app can’t hurt, though.

A great product? Ah. Now we’re talking.

Nope. You know why? Because you can just buy it. That’s right. Go buy a couple of SDKs here, buy another set of APIs there, smash them together with a generic user interface that you bought too. And voila! You are a fintech lending app.

Continuing with her dispatches from the frontiers of Indian fintech, Arundhati explains how easy it is for anyone to spin up a fintech lending app thanks to “FaaS” (Fintech as a Service) companies.

You get one. She gets one. Everyone gets one!

This isn’t all bad. Because FaaS allows faster and more democratised innovation that isn’t restricted to just a few companies that happened to get lucky in the funding or traction lottery.
