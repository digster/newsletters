Read the transcript of any recent earnings call. There’s a good chance that artificial intelligence is mentioned in some way. For most companies, the context is adoption—specifically that company staff will become more productive, products will be developed faster and released sooner, and all of that will translate to a juicier bottom line.

After all, if you’re a software engineer and there’s an opportunity to do more just by becoming fluent in a new tool, what’s not to love?

Coding assistance comes in many forms. Github Copilot, Langchain, and Codewhisperer are commonly found within bullpens across India, the result of vendor bundling and upper management signing company-wide contracts. Some engineers aren’t happy with this because it leads to subtle errors such as hallucinated APIs, outdated library usage, and patterns that don’t fit the existing architecture.

That means code generated by these assistants comes with a “verification tax”, as engineers call it. While the process used to be “write code, then review”, it now has extra steps: “generate, validate, rewrite, review”.

The age-old conflict between engineers and managers has a new front. One side sees the mandate to use specific AI tools as an adjustment in workflows to justify a contract. The other sees it as a chance to track AI adoption and talk it up during investor calls.

Mrunmayee spoke with people on both sides of the dispute to discover how it’s playing out. Read her story today to get the details.
