By most measures, electric vehicles are not particularly complex products. Their software might be complex, but EVs are mechanically simpler than cars with engines that burn gasoline.
Even so, the chokepoint is one that you’re familiar with—roughly 90% of the world’s rare earths are processed in China. Consequently, 94% of the magnets that are crucial to EV motors are made in the country, and Beijing isn’t shy about leveraging its control over a much-needed resource.
For automakers anywhere, including India, there is immense upside to weaning off China’s supply. There are efforts to do this—startups such as Chara Technologies and Aditya Avartan Technologies are developing motors for EVs that don’t use rare-earth magnets.
That might sound like manna from heaven, but surprisingly, Indian legacy vehicle manufacturers aren’t particularly receptive to the idea. They seem perfectly fine with staying the course and depending on China.
Something doesn’t quite add up.
