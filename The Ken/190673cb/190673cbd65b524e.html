The Big Story

Ozempic is unapproved and unreliable in India. But I’ve still ordered the ‘miracle’ weight-loss drug

“I am willing to take the risk and give this drug a shot, for I’ve tried and tested almost all anti-obesity means myself—and even failed at some. As a first step, I am banking on other Ozempic users’ experience”

India is home to the largest population of diabetic patients and has the third largest populace of obese people. Yet the latest drugs released worldwide in the anti-obesity market are unlicensed here. Diabetes drugs—semaglutide and tirzepatide—can control cravings and create satiety helping obese people stick to diets for longer and get unbelievable results. Not interested in merely watching from the sidelines, Indians are ignoring risks to get their hands on the injectables—leading to the brisk business of grey market pharmacies and slimming clinics. Nearing 100 kg with comorbidities like Type-2 diabetes, hypertension, and hypothyroidism and having avoided bariatric surgery, I, too, have ordered a box after over 12 weight-loss attempts failed. Also in the spotlight…

Nobroker is no longer no broker 

The US$1 billion proptech is secretly trying to entice wealthy customers with its new “postpaid” model—a leaf it ripped right out of the brokers’ playbook

Demand for high-end living is on the rise, and Nobroker’s subscription model doesn’t suffice to grab a piece of the pie. While it has disrupted the mid-range market, elite offline brokers still have a stranglehold in the premium segment. So, Nobroker introduced a postpaid plan to entice wealthy property owners, both for immediate financial gain and future cross-selling. But scaling it comes with challenges unique to the sector—those that the company has yet to learn to crack. [Read the full story here]

Zepto is a quick-commerce bronze medallist acting like a gold medallist

Zepto, despite being overshadowed by Blinkit’s rapid rise, has secured its biggest funding so far and has been hobnobbing with investors and analysts

Zepto recently secured its biggest funding round yet at a US$3.6 billion valuation, mainly from its existing investors, such as Glade Brook Capital and Nexus Venture Partners. It now plans to raise another round, aiming for a US$5 billion valuation, once again from “external” top-tier VCs. Zepto is the third-largest player in the quick-commerce market, after Blinkit and Instamart. And some believe it’s a once-in-a-cycle opportunity that Zepto is capitalising on. Other deep dives…

Exit, but without big cheques: VCs warn India’s startup founders 

To prioritise corporate governance and financial management, VCs like Peak XV, Matrix, and Accel are exercising a lot more oversight, introspection, and prudence while signing deals with Indian startup founders

With the funding environment turning, VCs are firmly in the driver’s seat. And nowhere is this more obvious than the term sheets they are setting. There is a heightened focus on information rights, inspections, thresholds for reserved matters, monitoring related party transactions, board constitution, anti-dilution provisions and reporting. They also include the right to consult senior management (other than founders) and conduct inspections or special audits. These clauses are sparking deeper discussions and setting clear expectations after investment rounds. [Read the full story here]

Truecaller beat Trai to the punch with spam-call fix 

While businesses want to reach more users, people are tired of spam calls. As Truecaller takes advantage of the opportunity, Trai is planning to launch a clone of the former’s caller ID feature on 15 July

India proudly boasts of its digital public goods infrastructure—the likes of Aadhaar and UPI. But the seemingly unstoppable spam-and-scam-call menace throws light on a vital, yet ignored public good—telecom. Telecom regulator, Trai, has no concrete solution to the scores of unsolicited communication, including frauds, unlike Truecaller, a private entity. The Swedish company has taken part of Trai’s responsibility and is making money from both users and businesses. Can Trai match up to Truecaller’s offerings? [Read the full story here]

Small-caps in Malaysia soar on Microsoft, Nvidia, Google’s AI bets. Too soon? 

Analysts sound caution at the small-cap rally as Big Tech investments are in the early stages and infrastructure is still budding

Notion Vtec, a Malaysian small-cap precision-parts manufacturer, was served with a Bursa Malaysia query over its soaring stock price this month. The company’s stock shot up about 8X year-to-date, fuelled by substantial investor interest, both domestically and internationally. Other small-cap companies linked to Malaysia’s AI sector, too, are witnessing significant stock-price jumps. With escalating interest, Malaysia must meet high market expectations to sustain the momentum. [Read the full story here]
