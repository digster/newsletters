Good Morning Ishan,
There’s no shortage of organic food labels in India. For cold-pressed oil alone, there are at least 10 companies whose products are on Swiggy’s Instamart, including small and mid-sized brands like Natureland Organics, 24 Mantra, and Pro Nature, as well as options under major FMCG companies like Tata’s Simply Better.
It’s those smaller brands that have spent years or even decades educating consumers about organic food and building networks of farmers who use the right cultivation methods. Yet, FMCG majors are now reaping the biggest benefits. Tata Group, for instance, bought Organic India for Rs 1,900 crore (US$225 million) in early 2024.
There’s a compelling reason for that. Walk into any mid-tier supermarket and you’ll find organic options for nearly every food item.
Organic food might not be part of everyone’s diet, but more people are stocking their pantries and fridges with these items—5 to 6 million Indian households, or up to 4X pre-pandemic levels, according to 24 Mantra founder Rajashekar Reddy Seelam.
After decades of brands building trust among consumers, the organic food space is maturing. The biggest winners, however, aren’t the companies who were the first to shape this space.
