Thanks to the pandemic, the $460 billion grocery retail market is where all the action is right now. Even the Tata Group, which runs the struggling Star supermarket chain, wants a bigger piece of it. The steel-to-software conglomerate is reportedly in advanced talks to buy ~80% of the country's e-grocer unicorn BigBasket for around $1.3 billion.

India's online grocery market may still be nascent, but competition is growing fiercer by the day. Earlier this year during the lockdown, millions of Indians ordered groceries online for the first time.

And they're continuing to do so even after the restrictions have been lifted.

BigBasket saw an 84% jump in the number of new customers and 50% higher retention rates over pre-Covid levels. 64% of the people who shopped on rival Grofers since the lockdown were first-time customers.

Reliance Retail plans to tap into this seven million-strong network of neighbourhood stores to deliver your onions, rice, and sugar.
