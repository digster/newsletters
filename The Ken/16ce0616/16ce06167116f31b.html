Good Morning Ishan,
 
In all honesty, Arundhati, the writer of today’s story on the rise of QR code payments company BharatPe, first pitched a QR code story on July 18, 2017. Her story pitch was rejected* because some of us (mea culpa) felt that there wasn’t much action happening with QR codes in India. 
 
Fast forward 2 years and boy, was I wrong and was Arundhati right. If PhonePe (now owned by Flipkart, itself owned by Walmart) was the first fintech company to ride to runaway success on the rails of UPI, India’s open payments format, then BharatPe (no relation) is the first to repeat that feat by riding on the rails of QR codes. In just one year, it has acquired over 1.5M merchants and claims to process over 21M payment transactions a month. 
 
On the surface, BharatPe’s business model seems deceptively simple. Raise venture capital ($67.8M thus far) to hire more feet-on-street; then send them to small shops and merchants so they can paste a QR code sticker on the counter. But peel the obvious layers and it becomes clear that helping merchants accept all sorts of digital payments through a single QR code was just BharatPe’s Trojan Horse. Its real game is to offer all sorts of financial products to merchants. Like working capital loans, credit cards and insurance.
 
“I’m probably the dumbest guy [among the founders of payments companies],” says Ashneer Grover, the CEO and co-founder of BharatPe. “But I’m the dumb guy who figured the one thing others were reluctant to do.”
