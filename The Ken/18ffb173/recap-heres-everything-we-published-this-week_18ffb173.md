In a world where new companies and many new jobs are constantly emerging, unwilling retirees prove that they are far more capable than just passing the baton of knowledge
Increasing life expectancy and soaring living costs are ending the dreams of retiring comfortably between the ages of 58 and 62. Professionals aged 40–60 are discovering new career paths to stay employable even after their retirement. Employees are considering a career that involves making leaps sideways and across rungs rather than a straight climb upward. But connecting senior professionals with meaningful opportunities isn’t that simple. It takes a whole village—of companies and new-age platforms. [What do health insurers have against pregnancies?](https://the-ken.com/story/what-do-health-insurers-have-against-pregnancies/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
And here’s how you can make sure you don’t pay the price for that
For many young couples, pregnancy is their only major medical expense. So, they expect to claim the maximum amount from their insurers. For insurers, the high probability of claims associated with maternity often leads to its exclusion from regular health insurance policies. This perpetual conflict is compelling many young couples to rely on their corporate health insurance for maternity coverage. Customers are now banking on strategic rider covers and add-ons that insurers offer helping reduce the waiting periods. The catch though is higher premiums. Arzooo’s bid to shake up electronics retail crumbles amid audits, leadership shifts, and a six-month disappearance
Former Flipkart executives starting B2B firms is not new, and neither are their struggles with a viable business model and profitability. But Arzooo is an outlier. The company, valued at US$400 million with revenues north of Rs 1,000 crore (US$120 million), and miniscule losses in the year ended March 2022 is on the verge of shutting down. The trouble for Arzooo began with a series of audits, which led the IT department to their doorstep and caused the business to shut for four months. As it tries to revive business, Arzoo is yet to pay off its staff, sellers, and third-party partners for months.
