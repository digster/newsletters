Careers after 40: the trick to being ‘forever employable’

In a world where new companies and many new jobs are constantly emerging, unwilling retirees prove that they are far more capable than just passing the baton of knowledge

Increasing life expectancy and soaring living costs are ending the dreams of retiring comfortably between the ages of 58 and 62. Professionals aged 40–60 are discovering new career paths to stay employable even after their retirement. Employees are considering a career that involves making leaps sideways and across rungs rather than a straight climb upward. But connecting senior professionals with meaningful opportunities isn’t that simple. It takes a whole village—of companies and new-age platforms. [Read the full story here]

What do health insurers have against pregnancies?

And here’s how you can make sure you don’t pay the price for that

For many young couples, pregnancy is their only major medical expense. So, they expect to claim the maximum amount from their insurers. For insurers, the high probability of claims associated with maternity often leads to its exclusion from regular health insurance policies. This perpetual conflict is compelling many young couples to rely on their corporate health insurance for maternity coverage. Customers are now banking on strategic rider covers and add-ons that insurers offer helping reduce the waiting periods. The catch though is higher premiums. [Read the full story here]

The Philippines puts together 20% of the world’s chips. Now, it must rethink its role in the global supply chain

Companies like EMS are trying to become chip producers—a time-consuming and costly move that could pay off handsomely

Electronics are the Philippines’ top export, with its semiconductor sector specialising in assembly, packaging, and testing. Exports reached US$45 billion in 2023, but rising material costs are eating into profits and automation is reducing assembly orders. Local companies are trying to move up the supply chain and start manufacturing their own chips. While one chip exporter has started to taste success, an industry-wide transformation will take nearly a decade. [Read the full story here]

The study-abroad business is red-hot. So why is Leverage Edu anything but?

The US$150 million startup needs both strict quality control to hold on to existing business and new markets to reduce its vulnerability, as its markets raise their entry barrier

By 2025, 2.1 million Indian students are expected to go abroad, double since 2019. It’s an opportunity which Leverage Edu hopes to make the most of, as the study-abroad company rapidly expands. But amidst an immigration crackdown from countries like Australia, Canada, and the UK, it’s failing to make headway in key markets. Even as the company switches strategy to fight external factors, it’s parallelly struggling to get its own house in order. [Read the full story here]

Flipkart thought it could make Cleartrip fly the e-commerce way

But the Walmart-owned giant’s hypergrowth playbook hasn’t quite helped Cleartrip take off, despite a booming travel industry

While the likes of Makemytrip and Easemytrip have been minting money from the post-pandemic travel boom, Cleartrip’s losses have only multiplied. Flipkart acquired the online travel agency in a distress sale in 2021 and deployed its tried-and-tested e-commerce playbook to turn it around. That meant bringing in the best and the brightest, fixating on growth metrics and investing heavily in discounts and advertising. But Flipkart has discovered that it can’t do without patience in the travel industry, where margins are slim and experience is key. [Read the full story here]

Why did two Flipkart alums’ US$400M startup go MIA?

Arzooo’s bid to shake up electronics retail crumbles amid audits, leadership shifts, and a six-month disappearance

Former Flipkart executives starting B2B firms is not new, and neither are their struggles with a viable business model and profitability. But Arzooo is an outlier. The company, valued at US$400 million with revenues north of Rs 1,000 crore (US$120 million), and miniscule losses in the year ended March 2022 is on the verge of shutting down. The trouble for Arzooo began with a series of audits, which led the IT department to their doorstep and caused the business to shut for four months. As it tries to revive business, Arzoo is yet to pay off its staff, sellers, and third-party partners for months. [Read the full story here]
