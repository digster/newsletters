Good Morning Ishan,
You know that surprise you would feel if A24—the studio known for smart, beautifully crafted indie films—suddenly announced its next project was Transformers 12: Rise of the Recycling Bin? Or Fast & Furious 12: Vin Diesel Drives an EV?
That’s the energy around Parag Parikh Mutual Fund right now. The one fund house investors treated like the indie darling of finance has decided it’s time to… launch a large-cap fund. The most formulaic, studio-mandated, franchise-sequel of the mutual-fund universe.
This is the same fund house that built a decade-long reputation on not doing what everyone else does. It insisted on simplicity, resisted asset-gathering temptations, and swore off anything that looked like index-hugging. And yet here it is, rolling out a Nifty 100-tracking product and insisting it’s somehow “different”.
It gets better. Or worse, depending on who’s looking. Its flagship flexicap scheme is currently parked on a mountain of cash because valuations are “too high”. Apparently, those same valuations are totally fine for the new large-cap scheme.
So, industry folks are whispering the obvious: Parag Parikh boxed itself into a corner with its purist philosophy, money kept flooding in, and now it needs an escape hatch. This “active-but-also-mostly-passive” hybrid looks like one.
Anand’s [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/parag-parikh-mf-the-rebel-that-finally-conformed/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) unpacks the contradictions, strategic gymnastics, and slow transformation of a fund house that once prided itself on being the A24 of asset management—contrarian and allergic to clichés—until the irrational market forced it into action-movie territory.
