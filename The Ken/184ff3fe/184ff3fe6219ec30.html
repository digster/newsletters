Good Morning Ishan,
 
Do you believe in ghosts? I don’t. But I recently learnt “everyone in Indonesia believes in ghosts”.
 
In late November, The Ken Southeast Asia team had an offsite in Bali. One evening, while returning from a short trip to Ubud, our youngest colleague at the steering wheel, tired and sleepy, requested, “Everyone, please tell ghost stories”. The team obliged heartily.
 
It started with Indonesia. Then a story from the Philippines followed. I saw that Hong Kong was no less. In fact, when one of our colleagues landed there the first time, he was given a host of do’s and don’ts to ward off evil spirits at his place. As amused as I was, I didn’t have a ghost story to share.
 
That night at the Airbnb villa, as I resumed reading Pico Iyer’s Autumn Light, page 37, I kid you not, read like this:
​
​"What makes the air feel thronged is the presence of household deities and ghosts, the spirits that for my neighbours inhabit every last desk or box of chocolates. Nothing essential ever seems to die in Japan, so the land is saturated with dead ancestors, river gods, the heavenly bodies…"
 
Though I was still ambivalent about my belief in ghosts, I have to admit I slept with all the lights on that night.
 
As for this week, though, we had a bunch of real-world, exciting stories:
 
On Monday, Gaurav and Anand wrote about the well-known but little-understood (faux) process of startup valuation. How the fair value of a company grows 8,000% in 17 months or 150% in three is neither art nor science; it’s a mutually agreed upon deal. A category of professionals called “valuers”, some 5,000+ registered ones in India, has been a party to the recent wild run.
 
Tuesday was movie time. Well, not really. Contributor Anirudh Somani wrote about how the rise of OTT platforms, coupled with high ticket and popcorn prices (that’s one reason for me for sure), is why people are not thronging multiplexes. It’s reflecting in all sorts of numbers for the industry.
 
If you’ve been an advocate of plastic recycling, then you must read this Wednesday piece from Isa. She had a Counterthesis from the convenor of the No Other Year for Plastics Initiative in the Philippines who says even if a brand made a valiant attempt to make 100% recyclable packaging, it goes to waste.
 
On Thursday, Arundhati wrote about an unusual practice at the newly listed non-bank Five Star Finance. This TPG-Matrix-Sequoia-backed company has had the best bad-loan rates, mysteriously next only to giant banks like HDFC. Read the story to understand why the unbelievable run at Five Star can’t last long.
 
We ended the week trying to understand yet another puzzling behaviour in the business world. The spate of layoffs in some of the biggest startups in India has been brutal and non-trivial. And it’d make anyone think hiring top talent is easier now. Vanita writes "Layoffs, layoffs everywhere but not much talent to hire" to explain exactly why.
 
That’s all for this week. I’m tempted to say please write in with your ghost stories but I’m going to resist. You are welcome to write about other stories, though.
 
Have a great Sunday.
 
Warmly,
