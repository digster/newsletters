Hello Ishan,

 

The Ken turns six today! And for the first time, we’re not running a month-long campaign to celebrate it as we’ve done in the first five years. Because, even as we have a lot to celebrate, thanks to your support, we are also poised at a critical, albeit exciting juncture in our evolution. 

We are deep in the process of changing what The Ken is.

This is an ambitious order. In many ways we’re going back to the drawing board and revisiting many of the assumptions and hypotheses we made in early 2016 in the runup to our launch. Some of those have held up remarkably and continue to guide our thinking. Others, we will need to change or perhaps even let go. 

But without your feedback and support, this won’t be half as fun. That is why I request five minutes of your time with this email. 

When The Ken launched in October 2016, this was our singular value proposition: every day we would email our premium subscribers one deeply-reported, longform business story from India. Nothing more, nothing less.

I apologise for the multiple bold accents, but each of those words represented a cornerstone for us. 

None of which exist today. 

Let’s start with “one”. No matter your subscription plan, today you have access to not just one, but multiple pieces and forms of journalism from us on any given day, including weekends. Strike one.

Then there’s “India”. We launched our Southeast Asia offering in 2020 and today we have a team of six incredible journalists reporting from across the region each day and bringing us must-read stories of entrepreneurship and business. Strike two.

“Longform” business stories too are no longer the only thing we offer our subscribers. We also offer original infographics, newsletters and podcasts, whose numbers are only set to increase. And there are newer products in the offing. Strike three.

And from just one “premium” plan when we started, we now offer subscribers multiple ways to subscribe. From richer bundles like “Borderless” and “Echelon” to the ability to subscribe to just newsletters. Strike four.

And this brings me to the most important word: “Email”.

When we started, it made sense for us to email our subscribers every time we had a new story. Because it was just once a day. 

It no longer does now. In fact, it hasn’t for a while. Strike five. 

And yet, our primary mode of engagement with many of our subscribers – including possibly you, Ishan - continues to be email. We email you about a new story. We email you our newsletters. We even email you our new podcast episodes. 

These are incredibly hard but also immensely rewarding challenges for us to solve. But this is also where all of us – we, as The Ken, and you, as subscribers – need to arrive at a new compact. 

About what The Ken is.

Our ambition today is to become the business subscription you rely on to understand and appreciate business, tech, and careers across India and Southeast Asia. And the one that makes you happier and smarter in the process.

How we do this isn’t fixed, or even known. We’ll keep evolving. Across forms, mediums, and channels. Today it might be longform stories, newsletters, and podcasts. Tomorrow it might include in-person events, online discussions, subscriber communities, and more.

Ironically perhaps, because The Ken now produces more journalism than our subscribers can count, we need to make ourselves less about the quantity of things we offer you. As a publication, we are coming to terms with the fact that you will not - cannot - consume every piece of journalism we create. And that’s okay.

Here we need your help, support and most importantly, feedback. Subscriber feedback is the magic flywheel that spins at the core of organisations like us. It’s what allows us to innovate, build and course-correct faster and better than others.

If you’ve read me till now, I thank you. I’d also love to request an additional 10 minutes of your time to send us your feedback as a subscriber on The Ken’s evolution. How are you perceiving our evolution? What do you want us to do more (or less) of? How should we engage more frequently, and yet, more mindfully with you? What areas do we need to improve upon?

Our ambition is to build on this to become an organization that will be around decades from now too.
