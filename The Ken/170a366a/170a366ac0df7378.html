Like OYO, Club Mahindra pioneered a then-niche, now-mainstream innovation in the hospitality industry in India. Along with the likes of Sterling, Club Mahindra introduced Indians to the concept of vacation timeshares. Why pay for expensive hotel rooms year on year when you can instead pay an upfront fee and get assured and inflation-free access to a large number of hotels for seven days a year for the next twenty-five years?

And like OYO, Club Mahindra has been often accused of overpromising and under-delivering; social media is full of complaints from disgruntled Club Mahindra customers airing their grievances in public.

Over the last few years, numerous subscribers have written to The Ken asking us to do a deep-dive on Club Mahindra. So we did.

In today's richly reported story, Vandana explores Club Mahindra across all facets—customer experience, business fundamentals, stock market performance—and discovers a company that is nothing like OYO. She lays out a nuanced narrative that covers the company's journey to date and a prognosis for the future.
