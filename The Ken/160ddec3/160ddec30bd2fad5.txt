Money can help you do the pivot dance. But money can’t buy you a business model. Call me a sceptic but that’s how I look at new age businesses ambling their way to something that sticks, and that’s how I look at Voonik. I mean, the company’s been around since 2013 and has raised millions and spent millions. But today, all of that background and baggage, or whatever else you might want to call it, is at a fickle moment of hope. The hope that once the company goes offline, in its search for customers, business and margins, somehow Voonik would have found its true calling. 

Maybe. Maybe not. If you ask me, I think the odds are stacked against Voonik. But my reporters, Payal and Patanjali think otherwise. Payal says Sujayath Ali, the founder of Voonik, is a guy with nerves of steel and hustle. So much hustle that when the reporters spoke to sources, almost everyone got back saying that if there’s anyone who can successfully chart out the online + offline play, it is Sujayath. 

You know, I’ve said this before. Offline is the new hail mary for stiff targets, on which the last funding round was raised. Fail that and the future is uncertain. It is that period of limbo where you don’t know if you are going to get more money, get acquired or fade into irrelevance. After four pivots, Voonik believes the company can find relevance selling to customers in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities.
