Good Morning Ishan,
In the Philippines, the question “kape tayo?”, or do you want to grab a coffee, is likely to be met with a vigorous nod of agreement. The country’s residents guzzle 3.6 kg coffee per capita, almost double that of the second placed Vietnam in Southeast Asia. And this number is rising. However, the Philippines produces just a small portion of the coffee Filipinos drink and relies on imported beans to cater to this burgeoning demand, which is rising 14% every year.
In today’s story, Isa, a self-confessed retired coffee connoisseur, takes us behind the scenes of the ongoing coffee revival in the country. Coffee growers have smartened up their practices—they are learning new flavour notes, adopting regenerative growing practices, and new-age packing techniques to not just stop a slide that began in early 2010s but revitalise the coffee industry.
While production has spiked between 2019 and 2022, dive into this story to understand how growers plan for sustainable growth. They have, for example, devised a scheme called Pick Red for higher value products, which cost thrice as much as regular cherries and are quickly picked up by artisanal roasters.
Coffee growers won’t find it easy. The industry doesn’t have its own lobby like sugar, coconut, or rice sectors, so the farmers are reliant on their own associations to define prices, labour costs, wages, and delivery routes.
