Have you heard of Sisyphus? He was a cruel Greek king notorious for his deception and trickery. He angered the Gods no end and even managed to cheat death a couple of times.

If not of Sisyphus, you have undoubtedly heard of his task. The Gods punished Sisyphus for his aggrandising ways by condemning him to roll an immense boulder up a mountain. But, when he neared the peak, the boulder would roll all the way down, and Sisyphus would start over. And over. And over. Locked in an everlasting and futile task.

In the startup world, this peak is called profitability. Founders and CEOs are tasked with pushing their business to this peak. The mountain is Everest for some and a hillock for other companies. But, climb they must. Some reach the summit and hang on. Others roll back down and start again.

Sumanth's story today is about one such company. 

Exactly two years back, food tech company Zomato became profitable. "Yes, throughout the 24 countries where we operate, and across all our businesses, we are starting to make money," founder and CEO Deepinder Goyal had said.

Today, Zomato's boulder is sliding back down the mountain. 

In its latest annual report, Zomato not only reported a loss, but a loss that had exploded nearly 25X year-on-year.

And it isn't clear if its boulder is still descending or if it has reached the foot of the mountain.

The question isn't how, but why Zomato has ended up in a situation where it is losing hundreds of millions of dollars per year. Sumanth traces the journey over these two years. And, like in the Sisyphean tasks of other startups of late, SoftBank features here too.
