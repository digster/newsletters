Good Morning Ishan,
Keeping a poker face may not be a feat in itself, but playing poker (and winning) has many life skills associated with it. At least those who play the game say so.
In the world of venture investing, this means playing it cool and building up your cash prize. One of the reasons why Girish Mathrubootham* has taken to playing poker these days, as he makes the move from being a hands-on founder of Freshworks to a full-time VC. “The savviest of investors in the Valley play poker,” he told Arundhati, the writer of today’s story.
Earlier in September, the 50-year-old entrepreneur said he’d be leaving Freshworks, the $3.7 billion company he built over 15 years. His second life begins as a VC at Together Fund with his partner, Manav Garg. The two founders have had successful exits, and they want to lean into their SaaS learnings.
If poker is a practice in probabilistic thinking, Mathrubootham is practising the right art because at Freshworks, and many of the SaaS startups he’s backed personally as well as through the fund, most were caught off-guard when AI struck in full force just a couple of years ago.
The speed of technology, scale-up, and capital raise in AI is nothing like what SaaS guys have seen or experienced.
But Mathrubootham and Garg believe they are well-placed to create a $100 billion AI-focused firm out of India. Read Arundhati’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/can-girish-mathrubootham-spot-the-next-girish-mathrubootham/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) to gauge the hand they are displaying here.
Tiny text that makes you squint. Confusing math to decipher what it all means. Figuring out consumer labels is a struggle. But now, platforms like Natfirst and Unbox Health—represented respectively by their VP of growth and marketing, Munaf Kapadia, and founder, Arjun Anjaria—are making it easier to decode what’s really in our food.
They join hosts Praveen Gopal Krishnan and Rohin Dharmakumar for a deep dive into what it means to be a “conscious consumer” in India in this week’s Two by Two.
