Let’s play a little game.

Step 1️: Open your go-to payments app.

Step 2: Check Transaction History. Count the familiar names, those you know personally. Then, count the strangers you’ve exchanged money with.

Step 3: Grab a calculator (or use your math skills). Work out the percentage of those unfamiliar folks among your total transactions.

Now, what if I told you that the probability of you being indirectly impacted by a cyber scam is as high as the percentage you just calculated? Even if you are not a scammer or a direct victim.

Wild, right? Allow me to explain.

In Digital India, cash is a rarity. UPI, or Unified Payments Interface—India’s real-time e-payments network—has largely replaced it for at least 300 million of us. Why shouldn’t it? The interface is seamless, and its official aura convinces us that every transaction within this system is infallible.

However, here’s the hard truth: Unlike cash, the money that finds its way into your account comes with more strings attached to it than you might know.

A transaction can unwittingly land your bank account in the crosshairs of law-enforcement agencies’ and your bank’s efforts to recover funds pilfered from cyber-fraud victims.

Still, baffled? Wondering why your account would ever get frozen? After all, you haven’t committed any crime. Well, it’s the money trail unravelling itself down multiple levels from a scammer’s hideout.

Think of it as a spillover effect, a ripple that extends beyond its initial impact.

In India, this drama is still unfolding. And Arundhati, the author of today’s tale, goes further into this labyrinth—and tries to uncover what happens after the dust settles.
