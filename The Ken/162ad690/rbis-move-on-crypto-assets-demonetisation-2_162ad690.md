Good Morning Ishan,
Sometimes just making sense of a policy decision can send a reporter down a rabbit hole. That’s what happened with Sidhartha as he began figuring out on Friday why the Reserve Bank of India, in one fell swoop, cut off the crypto industry from India’s formal payment network.
It’s been just three days, and already alternate routes are being used and explored by the 5 million-strong community of traders, investors and crypto exchange operators. Some in the crypto-community are calling it a second demonetisation of sorts.
Think about it, before India has even made up its mind on whether crypto asses are going to be legal or illegal, or if it’s legal, then how it is to be regulated, the RBI has caused chaos in the market. It feels particularly punitive given that a government task force is yet to give its recommendations on how to regulate this sector.
The Bank is citing concerns of ‘consumer protection, market integrity and money laundering’ for its drastic move. But to curb money laundering, a formal channel needs to be put in place for safe transactions. Such restrictions, as mentioned in our [earlier story](https://the-ken.com/story/indian-banks-crypto-crackdown/), will leave traders with no choice but to conduct deals over-the-counter with cash, which would be untraceable.
There are scams in every industry. An outright ban to tame the irrational exuberance of crypto assets doesn’t sound pragmatic and isn’t going down well with many. Some have gotten into flight mode, some others into fight mode, and a few others are cashing in on the ‘demo’.
Could there have been no other way to weed out the bad actors? Because it may well turn out that the RBI is powerless in curbing this tech.
