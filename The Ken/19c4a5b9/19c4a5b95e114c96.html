The Anthropic-induced tech rout last Tuesday cut short IT services company Cognizant’s rally with an 11% dip, returning the stock to its early December levels. Yet the US-listed company remained unfazed in its year-end and fourth-quarter earnings call, having killed two birds with one stone. It entered the “Winner’s Circle” two years ahead of target with 28 large deals in hand, while simultaneously surpassing Accenture and HCLTech in revenue growth.

I wasn’t surprised by the strong numbers, having dug deeply into their financials for a recent story in which I wrote about their listing plans in India. But when the briefing morphed into an AI keynote, I zoned out. A major chunk of CEO Ravi Kumar’s 15-minute opening remarks was devoted to AI, from building IP and rethinking linear staffing, to partnering with AI companies. If that wasn’t enough, the rest of the hour-long earnings call followed the same pattern, with AI remaining the central focus.
