India is in the grip of an economic slowdown. A proper one. People aren't buying Rs 5 Parle biscuits or cars worth lakhs of rupees. Nor are they buying anything in between. Except for ...
... smartphones. No, scratch that. Premium smartphones! While the overall growth in phone sales slowed in the April-June quarter in India, premium smartphones sales surged 33%.
33%! In a country notorious for deals and bargains. How? Why?
"Fundamentally, as consumers spend 6-7 years using a smartphone, at some stage, they start looking beyond the basic smartphone experience: better gaming, better imaging, more storage space, aesthetics, and the like. In India, a large part—almost 60%—of the market is sub-Rs 10,000 ($139). So the potential to upgrade is very high," says an official at a Chinese smartphone maker.
Apple, OnePlus and Samsung are the usual suspects, controlling 85% of the market.
Still, willing to go 'premium' does not mean willing to splurge. Not in India, not in this environment. Hitting the sweet spot of a premium price is critical. Apple was languishing until price cuts propelled it back to growth. OnePlus is raising rates, but off a lower base.
Then there's perception, a key currency in the business of 'premium' anything. There is a reason Apple has those fancy stores and spent time in its recent keynote touting the renovation of its iconic store on 5th Avenue. Meanwhile, OnePlus has moved from online-only to 1,500 'offline touchpoints.'
And they have competition. Serious competition. The likes of Xiaomi, Oppo and Vivo, after decimating Indian phone makers, are upgrading to the premium category. There are other variables: Apple's Make In India; the government easing regulations; Samsung the sleeping giant.
