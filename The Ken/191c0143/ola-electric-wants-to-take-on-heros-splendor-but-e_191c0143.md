Good Morning Ishan,
Most motorcycles are happy to serve as mere conveyances for the daily grind. But Bhavish Aggarwal, the Ola supremo, isn’t just playing the long game—he’s in a frantic sprint. His latest endeavour, the Roadster lineup of electric motorbikes, is proof.
Aggarwal’s ambitions are twofold. First, he’s gunning for the commuter-bike market, which remains firmly in the hands of loyalists of 125cc ICE models like Hero Motocorp’s Splendor. Second, he’s set an audacious target: every new bike and scooter in India must be electric by next year.
Launched on India’s 78th Independence Day, as is the Ola ritual, the Roadster lineup rode the coattails of Ola Electric’s recent stock-market debut. “Since the launch, we have received several thousand orders, with our Roadster X [priced at Rs 74,999] seeing the most traction,” an Ola Electric employee tells Nathan.
But despite the initial hype, Ola Electric is not merely entering a competitive market—it’s heading into a battleground where incumbents like Revolt Motors and Tork Motors are already struggling for traction.
In a bid to meet its ambitious delivery timeline, Ola has managed to develop its electric bikes in just 12–18 months, a feat that typically takes 36 months for rivals like TVS. However, this breakneck pace brings its own perils. After all, last year’s Ola S1 Pro fire incident set the company back Rs 10–12 crore in PR damage control, The Ken has learnt.
Besides, the Roadster’s design, some say, is not exactly what electric-scooter enthusiasts have been lining up for.
As Ola Electric speeds down this path, Nathan’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/ola-electric-wants-to-take-on-heros-splendor-but-e-bikes-are-not-e-scooters/) investigates whether the Roadster has the chops to mirror the success of the company’s scooters.
