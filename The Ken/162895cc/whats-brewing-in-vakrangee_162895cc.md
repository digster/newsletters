Good Morning Ishan,
Ever walked into "a supermarket for financial inclusion services"? That's how Patanjali and Sidhartha describe their Company of Interest today—the curiously named Vakrangee.
Vakrangee—named after company owner Dinesh Nandwana's kuladevi or family deity—offers its services through 40,000 humble kendras or outlets across India. It has white label ATMs, helps people open and run bank accounts, gives out money to those enrolled in rural employment schemes, enables people to buy insurance, and, in some cases, it helps customers connect to Amazon (which is now eyeing rural India).
But what makes Vakrangee odd is that this BSE-listed company is doing almost too well for its context. In FY17, Vakrangee had a topline of Rs 4,000 crore (~$615 million) with a peak market cap of Rs 50,000 crore (~$7.7 billion). It has continued this growth. In January, it bought 2 million shares of another listed entity, PC Jeweller Ltd for Rs 112 crore (~$17 million). Why is that odd? Because before this, all it had invested in three years was in mutual funds. Just about Rs 2 crore (~$307,600) at that.
Besides, thanks to its work with government enrollments, the many Aadhaar contracts had its stock prices go up. Of course, Amazon as a logistics partner helped its case as well.
Interestingly, Sebi had started an investigation against Vakrangee for share price manipulation. However, it cleared Vakrangee in its short-lived enquiry. Almost all too soon.
"Vakrangee is a curious company. It does many unique things. It makes money off financial inclusion; it was a penny stock just three years ago and is now trading in triple digits; its market cap has been steadily increasing but yet few analysts want to talk about it," the duo write. So instead, today, they chose to talk about it.
