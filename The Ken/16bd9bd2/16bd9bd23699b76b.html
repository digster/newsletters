Good Morning Ishan,
 
I'm a millennial.

I'm a millennial who moved cities, rented an apartment on her own and tried to transport a hand-me-down car from another city, only to realise selling is more economical.

The thought of owning things in Bangalore is daunting to me. What if I need to suddenly move back (not-young parents in Delhi) or feel the need to upgrade? Commitment to my cats comes a lot easier to me than objects, which I see as transient and pointlessly bulky.

Solution? Renting. Rented bed, rented refrigerator, maybe a rented car in the offing. Rental companies such as Furlenco and Rentomojo don't just give me new/freshly-repurposed goods, but they also promise to do yearly checks, repairs; and were I to, say, move cities, they can just take my stuff back here and reissue me what I like there. 

Now, give me one good reason why a millennial like me wouldn't want to sign up for a 2-year rental that's sub-2K per month?

That's Arundhati's story today. When I first moved here, I barely knew about the option. Quickly, I learned that rented furniture and appliances were the new normal; and more the tenants in one house, more economical it gets.

(Btw, I still don't get the point to phone rentals though. I could just EMI and own - it's not like it takes much to lug it around. Just yesterday, Rentomojo texted me about renting "the latest Samsung S10+ at Rs 2999/month".)

Renting has been common in the West for a bit now. For instance, 10-year old clothing and home décor rental company Rent the Runway hit a $1 billion in valuation. But in India, rental companies in all have raised only $426.82 million funding so far. This nearly seven-year-old phenomenon is still in the shadows of the larger shared economy. Roughly, only about a million people today rent, according to those in the industry. 

But we're also a young, ever-moving, ever-job changing population, aren't we? Read Arundhati's story to understand how consumer behaviour is shifting towards rental: https://the-ken.com/story/rental-economy/ (13-minute read)
