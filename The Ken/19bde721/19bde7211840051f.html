Sun Pharmaceutical is making headlines. India’s largest pharma company is reportedly looking to snap up US-based Organon in a mega $10 billion deal. If it goes through, the acquisition would be Indian pharma’s largest cross-border buy yet. It’s got “big” written all over it.

But away from all this limelight, there’s an almost-unknown, much smaller pharma player also getting a lot of attention—and money—from those who know about it. This unlisted company’s valuation has jumped 15X in 10 years, 5X in five years, and 2X over 18 months, way higher than the pharma index. That’s thanks to half-a-dozen marquee private-equity players—from Peak XV to Chryscapital—falling over each other to get a slice of it.

But why? Well, the moneybags hope to make a tidy buck when the company goes public, possibly in a year or two. It’s among the last unlisted pharma plays in India on the listing path, and a high-potential one at that, its investors think.

I had never heard of La Renon before reading Sudeshna’s eye-opener today. It sounds all French but is actually based in Gujarat, where many of India’s pharma biggies such as Sun Pharma and Zydus are also registered. But La Renon’s playbook is nothing like these biggies. It’s all-in on what the big guys won’t touch, making what no one else is and selling where it works best. Let’s just say, it is riding on the complicated and the chronic. But have the moneybags gone overboard on their bets?
