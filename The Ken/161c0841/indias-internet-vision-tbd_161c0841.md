Good Morning Ishan,
Country after country, sector after sector, the internet is rewriting the rules of business. From Amazon, Facebook, Google and Uber in the US to Alibaba, Tencent and Didi in China, the internet is enabling the rise and growth of massive businesses at a pace we never thought possible. And India is the battleground where Chinese and American companies are battling it out for control over the largest unserved market globally.
What are the rules of engagement? Err, nothing specific. Because India doesn’t seem to have any sort of coherent and long-term vision for what it wants from its internet sector. Which means it's free for all and largely bereft of regulatory or government oversight.
There’s nothing wrong with that, mind you. It’s great if India’s government has decided that it wants to adopt a laissez-faire policy towards its internet and does not mind who ends up with the largest market shares, provided it's all done above the board and to the benefit of its citizens. But I don’t think they’ve decided that (or if they have, they haven’t articulated it).
Instead, sector after sector is being won over by Chinese or American players or their capital. Where does this go?
Venkat and Patanjali set out to determine who in the government was responsible for how India’s internet sector will evolve. (Spoiler alert: there were many, and none).
