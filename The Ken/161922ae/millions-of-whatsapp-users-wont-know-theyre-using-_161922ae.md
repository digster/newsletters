Good Morning Ishan,
Today's subject is a riff on [a 2015 Quartz story](https://qz.com/333313/milliions-of-facebook-users-have-no-idea-theyre-using-the-internet/) that went, "Millions of Facebook users have no idea they’re using the internet”. (Tl;dr: millions of Facebook users in developing countries confuse Facebook for the Internet, which is all too fine for Facebook, but not so good for competitors).
Our story is a contrarian analysis by Arundhati on WhatsApp’s almost universally acclaimed beta launch of payments in India. She posits that Facebook has been able to make payments so seamless and magical because it has been allowed to hide, nay, obfuscate the fact that it's riding upon an open payments protocol - UPI.
It’s a provocative take, but one that I’m siding with. The regulator NPCI seems to have carved out loopholes and rolled out red carpets for Facebook (and Google), at the cost of a level playing field and by compromising the core promise of UPI - interoperability.
It is entirely conceivable to think of tens of millions of WhatsApp users in India who have no idea they’re making UPI-based payments to other users or businesses. From their POV, they’ll be making WhatsApp payments.
Read today’s story (it's free) to understand why that may mean the gradual co-option of UPI from an open, interoperable protocol to an invisible and pseudo-proprietary one. The winners will be Facebook and Google. The losers? Well.
