Good Morning Ishan,

Good stories take time. They also take a lot of effort. Planning. Chasing. Telling. 

We’ve been on this story for a long time now. By we, I mean, my colleague Patanjali. I know; parents hath no fury like naming a child Patanjali. But, it is almost the end of the week, a long weekend and... I’m digressing. Our initial line of inquiry was; who exactly is Pankaj? Very little is known about him but when you speak to people at Zomato, they talk. A lot. About the role he played at Zomato. How he is shy, and doesn’t talk ever. And even as we were chasing, on 1 March, exactly 30 days back, Pankaj quit the company. In his farewell email, he said: “...After 10 years of building Zomato, I have decided to hang my boots. I have been thinking of moving for some time now…”

The way stories get told today, a sprinkle here, a sprinkle there and done. 

But we kept at it. The result is this story. 

In a world where founders are celebrated, sometimes even raised to demigod status, Pankaj is an outlier. It is that unique part of being a co-founder. A co-founder is the other guy. Someone who gets a lot of work done, away from media glare. This is where our story comes in.
