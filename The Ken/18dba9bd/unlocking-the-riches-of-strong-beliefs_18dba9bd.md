Good Morning Ishan,
On 1 January 2023, I hopped on the resolutions bandwagon and decided it was time to lose some weight. But for the next three months, I did… nothing.
I couldn’t settle on a weight-loss regimen. A few friends suggested cutting sugar from my diet; others recommended drinking black coffee or green tea. And before I knew it, fitness influencers dominated my social media feeds. I was overwhelmed and indecisive.
Soon, three months became six, and then a year. By the end of 2023, I had gained 10 kgs, and only I was to blame.
However, I recently drew inspiration from an unexpected source: our [Narrative Thinking Programme](https://the-ken.com/blog/building-differentiating-narratives-workshop/?utm_source=recap&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). No, it’s not a weight-loss course but rather a comprehensive leadership workshop on effective business storytelling conducted by The Ken’s finest.
Last week, I attended one of these sessions led by my colleague Ruhi. She emphasised that powerful stories are created only when authors believe in their messaging. That’s because genuine convictions push them past the inevitable trials and tribulations of storytelling.
And then it struck me. The key reason behind my indecisiveness was that I didn’t truly believe that I could shed weight. The workshop made me realise how strong beliefs can inspire consistent action, which leads to discipline and, sooner or later, desired results.
Since then, I have—finally—taken my first significant steps towards achieving my weight loss goals: avoiding sugar and junk food. As I inculcate a daily workout routine, I’m confident my failed 2023 resolution will become a reality this year.
I’ll be waiting to hear from you about your strong beliefs (not necessarily about weight loss, though I would love to hear about it) and know how you formed them.
