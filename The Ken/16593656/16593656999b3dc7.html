Good Morning Ishan,
 
It's been three weeks here at The Ken for me, and they've been rather eventful—to say the least. Finding my way around the system, getting used to a Mac (yes, we're snobbish that way), editing, rushing off to do a story (got my first byline too).
 
What has stood out most though is the sheer amount of time and effort put into each and every story that we put out. Right from ideas through the actual process of reporting and writing to editing and getting the story out there, no stone is left unturned. We pride ourselves on our stories, and boy did we have a great line-up this past week. 
 
The news of the week, naturally, was Berkshire Hathaway, the US-based company headed by legendary investor Warren Buffett, investing in Paytm's parent company One97 Communications. Arundhati took a long hard look at how this could play out for both companies in a lovely piece on Tuesday. 
 
Speaking of investments, a new war has been brewing in Indian e-commerce—a battle royale between Alibaba, Amazon and Google. For the longest time, these giant conglomerates were happy to stick to their own swim lanes, but no longer. Vivek's story dives deep into the details.
 
We had another great story this week by our editor Seema, on the various goings-on at Medanta, India's most famous super-specialty hospital. Medanta's founder, Dr Naresh Trehan, is looking to expand for the first time in the 10 years since Medanta's inception. But speculation is rife that he's looking to get out of the business altogether. Where does the truth lie? Read the piece to find out.
 
Then we had another health story, this one from Ruhi, on the state of bariatric surgery—a procedure used to help people tackle obesity, diabetes, hypertension and more. One company, Johnson & Johnson, has been pushing hard, Ruhi writes, to get rid of the 'cosmetic surgery' tag that's associated with the procedure. It's been an uphill slog and there's still a distance to go.
 
And don't miss out on our Sunday read (we like to do things a little differently on the weekends). Ranjan and I talked to veteran news anchor Rajdeep Sardesai about the state of television news today. Spoiler: he does not paint a pretty picture of TV news. Do read and share, it's free.
 
Which brings me to our regular free story of the week. Our contributor, Srinivas Kodali, pokes holes in the government's grand plans for transforming public transport. Policy think tank Niti Aayog is betting big on so-called Intelligent Transport Systems (with a pinch of AI thrown in), but the hurdles are legion, Srinivas writes. Read. Share. You know the drill.
 
That's all for now, watch out for our next weekend read tomorrow (also free). It's a cracker.
