Good Morning Ishan,
 
Don't you just love Navaratri? It's my favourite time of the year. Nine days, all celebrated in a different way, depending on which part of India you are from. Pandal hopping in Bengal. Dandiya in Gujarat. In the South, it's Golu - the making and display of small dolls and figurines. No strict rituals. No public inconveniences. Just be happy and celebrate your victories. Do it however you want to. Best. 
 
Speaking of quiet victories, today's story is about a company named Mettl. Mettl is an online skill-assessment company and has a SaaS product to help corporates manage hiring, training and recruitment at scale through online tests. I know what you are thinking. There are tons of companies like this. Yes, that’s true. But what makes Mettl a little different from them is that it was acquired by the global giant Mercer for a cool $40 million last week. All cash.
 
When I asked Naveen Tewari, the CEO of InMobi, and one of Mettl’s early-stage investors about what made Mettl different, he said, "They built this business profitably and kept their focus on that core metric." 
 
Focused on profit? An Indian tech-company?
 
Chances are you probably haven't even heard of Mettl. The company's Wikipedia page is 167 words long. That's about half the length of this email. Also, in the context of exits, $40 million does not even sound like much. As Sumanth notes, "That's how much Flipkart burns in a single month." So what makes this acquisition special? Several things. I could tell you about how Mettl raised just a tenth of that amount in its entire eight years of existence. Or about how it’s unusual for SaaS companies in India to exit like how Mettl did. Or about how this happened to be one of those rare situations where everybody won - early-stage investors, late-stage, founders, everyone. 
 
I could. But I will let Sumanth, who understands SaaS businesses and the economics of venture funding better than most people, explain it better.
