Talking of timing, let’s get to our story today which is bang on time to illustrate how 2018 and beyond could see consumer brands occupy the space and stand stable, exactly where e-commerce stumbled. How these young companies are driven not by capital burns but by loyal, discerning customers; not by gross merchandise value but by repeats.
The scrappy startups by first-time entrepreneurs (and not the MNCs or family businesses which have dominated so far) in food and personal care are innovating. Products go beyond hipster items like Greek yoghurts, almond milk, artisanal coffees. Add to which, the proliferating supermarket stores with aisle displays that ensure targeted brand building without burning capital on TV or print ads.
Finally, there are decent exits which have eluded the e-commerce folk.
Rohin’s story is a riveting account of how new consumer brands are being built in India today. He has some of the most active VCs and entrepreneurs spill the beans on why “suddenly now there’s a gold rush”.
