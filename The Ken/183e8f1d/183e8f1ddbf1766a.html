Good Morning Ishan,
 
It's embarrassing to admit, but the truth needs to be told. 
 
I've been writing for a living for 13 years, but I can never seem to make my posts go viral in the LinkedIn universe. And when I see people with barely any writing experience amass impressions and followers, I do wonder how they manage that. 
 
Turns out, behind viral LinkedIn posts there are silent ghostwriters. For $500 a month, a ghostwriter in India can turn in three LinkedIn posts every week. The posts could be about the company’s product, the person’s journey, or a take on the latest industry trend.
 
There are dozens of agencies in India that help early-stage startup founders, established entrepreneurs, and ‘thought leaders’ build and manage their personal brand on the professional-networking site by tapping into the growing network of ghostwriters.
 
The idea caught on when the likes of Ankur Warikoo, founder and former chief executive officer of hyperlocal online platform Nearbuy; Kunal Shah, founder of fintech CRED; Aman Gupta, co-founder of consumer electronics company boAt; and Vineeta Singh, co-founder of luxury beauty brand SUGAR Cosmetics began gaining popularity on the platform.
 
They posted regularly, engaged massively, and gathered hundreds of thousands of followers—even more than their companies. 
 
In order to replicate their success, many other founders and CXOs are now loosening their purse strings to hire ghostwriters to write content on their behalf.  
   
It's not just about personal branding. Engagement on LinkedIn also helps in hiring people and influencing the purchase decisions of potential buyers. 
 
But, it’s not all that easy. Writing for LinkedIn is very different, says one ghostwriter. 
 
“It’s a tricky industry as you need to get inside someone else’s mind and write LinkedIn posts as them, not for them," says another ghostwriter.
