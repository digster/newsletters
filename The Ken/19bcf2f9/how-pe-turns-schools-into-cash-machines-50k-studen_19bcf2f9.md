As one of the first PE-backed edu-infra platforms, Elevate Campuses, prepares to go public, its draft papers offer direct insight into how private-equity firms squeeze money out of schools
One of the first PE-backed edu-infra platforms, Elevate Campuses, which owns St Michaels School in Hyderabad, is preparing to go public. Its draft papers reveal that St Michaels is paying 65% of its revenue, and a whopping 83% of its revenue surplus, as rent. That’s because the landlord and the tenant in St Michaels’ case are one and the same entity, owned by private-equity firm Hillhouse. Across the world, private-equity firms are turning private schools into global empires with sticky revenues and rich valuations.
