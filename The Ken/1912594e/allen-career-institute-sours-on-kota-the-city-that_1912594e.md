Good Morning Ishan,
It’s the first time in 36 years that the coaching giant, Allen Career Institute, has seen a fall in its admission numbers. And it’s a significant dip—nearly 35%, or a loss of over 45,000 students in the coaching city of Kota.
Loss of students means loss of business. As the BITS Pilani vice-chancellor wrote in a column on Sunday, Indian education institutions fall into two categories—those that depend on the government for 90% of their sustenance and those that rely on student tuition for 90% of their survival.
Allen falls in the second category. Especially now, when it has an external investor in Bodhi Tree Systems, which paid US$600 million for a third of the company two years ago.
Allen’s first a business and it’s reacting like a business—revising salaries and introducing variable pay for 4,000+ faculty and staff in its history. Still, some 600 teachers wrote a letter to the founder last month asking the institute to reconsider salary revision.
The Kota-headquartered company is now moving out of that city. It has set up 200 centres outside Kota; many don’t have the requisite numbers to be sustainable yet. “Transformations take time and will have some resistance as well,” CEO Nitin Kukreja told The Ken.
Alifiya, the author of today’s story, visited Kota in September 2023 and [found](https://the-ken.com/story/kotas-rs-6k-cr-coaching-business-may-never-be-the-same-again/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) that the city’s Rs 6,000 crore coaching business was bound to take a hit in admissions. The Kota phenomenon was on a decline, she said.
While she has been proven right, it’s also true that the Indian test-prep business has witnessed a hurricane called Byju’s, which decimated many businesses in its wake. Alifiya’s report today is a fine read on [how Allen is trying to cope](https://the-ken.com/story/allen-career-institute-sours-on-kota-the-city-that-made-it-a-test-prep-behemoth/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
