For years, India's business media made it look like the country was going to be Apple's next big production hub.
 
At the time, that couldn't have been further from the truth, as Apple had little reason to look beyond China.
 
But now, its compulsion to find alternatives to a China-dependent supply chain is forcing Apple to finally give India the attention worthy of all those hyperbolic headlines.
 
It's not a coincidence that this is happening at a time when India is providing incentives to encourage local manufacturing in more than a dozen sectors.
 
The likes of Dixon Technologies, which makes smartphones and washing machines for top brands, have cashed in on the government's push.
 
But there's a little-known Indian company that is not happy with just being a contract manufacturer. Video Voice Data Network Technologies doesn't have many peers, for it designs and makes a range of electronics, from industrial CCTVs and IoT gadgets to devices used in telecom and auto.
 
Among VVDN's clients are semiconductor majors Qualcomm and Intel, and wearables brand Boat. And its revenue in the year ending March 2023 is expected to be ~$220 million—a 6X jump over its pre-Covid top line.
 
Nearly one-third of VVDN's 10,000 employees are engineers—a proportion one would be hard-pressed to find in the Indian electronics-manufacturing ecosystem.
 
Small wonder, then, Motilal Oswal Private Equity decided to put $35 million in the company in 2019. The Ken has learnt that the investor is part of another $100 million infusion into VVDN in the coming weeks.
 
But there's a lot that stands between finding backers who believe in your vision and blazing a trail, as Pratap reports in today's story: https://the-ken.com/story/motilal-oswal-backed-vvdn-is-closing-in-on-a-100m-round-and-tech-design-giant-status/ (8-minute read)
