I’m sure you’re up to speed with the recent Bookmyshow-Coldplay ticketing row. For those who aren’t, the platform recently sold tickets to the India leg of the UK pop band’s world tour, that’s scheduled in early 2025. An event so big that the demand for tickets was 60X the number of available seats. Naturally, it attracted high instances of ticket resells through black-market channels and caught Bookmyshow, the event’s organiser, in a negative light.

Today’s story, however, is not about this row, but about this very growing demand of Indians seeking such experiences like live events and concerts. India, touted to have the highest per-capita income growth in the world, is moving towards the top of Maslow’s hierarchy of needs. Here, more of its population is indulging in creative activities, moving beyond just fulfilling the basic needs.

For context, India’s overall live-events-and-ticketing market is flourishing at 15–18% a year.

And look who else is here to tap into this trend: foodtech Zomato, with its Rs 2,000-crore acquisition of fintech giant Paytm’s* ticketing arm, Insider. It’s tech versus resilience, as Zomato’s deal has finally put it squarely against Bookmyshow, writes Gaurav, the author of today’s story.

As if joining the party wasn’t enough, it’s also, well, throwing more of these parties (read: experiences). Next month, it’s bringing British pop star Dua Lipa, and hosting tickets for the India leg of Punjabi singer Diljit Dosanjh’s tour later this year.

As a former Insider executive put it, “Bookmyshow has become an elephant, too big to turn around in its own room.”
