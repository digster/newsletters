Good Morning Ishan,
Alcohol’s reputation for bringing out the laughs is second to none. Among my favourites is this Urdu couplet by the 19th century poet, Mirza Ghalib.
“Waiz! Teri duaon mein asar ho to masjid ko hila kay dikha
Nahin to do ghoont pee, aur masjid ko hilta dekh”
Translation:
“Preacher! If your prayers are potent, make the masjid sway
Else, drink a couple of sips, and see the masjid sway”
But alcohol and its ecosystem in India are no laughing matter, finds Aakriti in today’s story. More and more people in the country are helping themselves to bottles and pegs—trading up not just in quantity but also in quality. So much so that India is an outlier: while global alcohol consumption has been going down, the country’s intake has nearly doubled since the pandemic.
Even as the drink and its dynamics vary across consumers—from rural to urban, and from the rich to the poor—the market is seeing interesting evolutions and convergences. Meanwhile, alco-bev stocks such as Radico Khaitan, Tilaknagar Industries, and Allied Blenders have been in high spirits, and foreign makers, previously hard to get, are now making a beeline for the country. Oh, and state governments are milking their cash cow and raking it in.
Why and how is India drinking more, even as the world sobers up? Are systemic issues at play? What does this mean for alco-bev makers and their consumers? Can the country really hold its drink?
