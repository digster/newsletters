Exclusive: Amazon’s game plan for festival sales
Savio
Imagine a company telling stores not to sell its product? Imagine doing this for a newly-launched product and when there is pent-up demand. Imagine a smartphone company doing this… just when it is about to launch a mid-range phone… in India… as the festive season nears.
|
Cellphone retailers across the country have reached out to OnePlus to allow them to sell its products online, days after the Chinese smartphone brand reportedly sent an email asking retailers to stop all online sales.
[...]
Apparently, OnePlus had allowed all retailers to sell online when the pandemic started, but last week asked them to stop it. OnePlus competes closely with Apple's iPhone and Samsung in the Indian market.
|
OnePlus asks retailers to stop all online sales; retailers condemn the move. [Economic Times](https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/hardware/oneplus-asks-retailers-to-stop-all-online-sales-retailers-condemn-the-move/articleshow/77239559.cms?utm_source=ETTopNews&utm_medium=HP&utm_campaign=TN&utm_content=23)
|
|
|
Context: OnePlus—the market leader in the premium market (over Rs 30,000 or US$400 segment)—is set to launch the ‘Nord’ just weeks after the launch of the flagships OnePlus 8 and OnePlus 8 Pro. The OnePlus Nord, however, is at least Rs 15,000 (US$200) cheaper than the most affordable phone in the OnePlus 8 series.
The Nord is OnePlus’s shot at that mid-range market, where the volumes lie. And where Xiaomi is the undisputed overall market leader, followed by (with the order switching with each research firm) Samsung, Vivo, Realme and Oppo.
What makes it even stranger is that the OnePlus Nord is [NOT](https://gadgets.ndtv.com/mobiles/news/oneplus-nord-pre-order-india-begins-amazon-price-rs-27999-specifications-sale-august-4-2270293) up for pre-order on its own website. Isn’t the volume game enhanced with reach - sell through as many channels as possible?
So where do you buy a OnePlus Nord?
|
|
|
Ah!
But why would OnePlus restrict sales to Amazon?
Is it because of the anti-China sentiment in India? (Chinese smartphone makers have [lost market share](https://www.ndtv.com/business/chinese-smartphones-market-share-slipped-in-india-in-june-quarter-says-research-2268278) in India in the April-June quarter.)
But the Indian government has mandated e-commerce companies, including Amazon, to clearly name the country of origin on each product. So that doesn’t help.
Then what?
|
Amazon India is busy preparing for its annual Prime Day sale. Usually a July event, this year, the e-commerce giant’s big sale day is going to take place in the first week of August, courtesy a Covid-induced delay. And to push the envelope, one of Amazon India’s two top sellers, Appario Retail, is securing some exclusivity.
Appario, which operates in the electronics and accessories category for popular brands like BoAt and Sennheiser, shot an email to handpicked third-party suppliers on 10 June. It sought to lay the groundwork for product launches exclusive to Appario, demanding that sellers exclusively retail these products through Appario for at least 30 days from the launch date.
|
Exclusivity high on Amazon’s wishlist, [The Ken](https://the-ken.com/story/amazon-exclusivity-wishlist/)
|
|
|
Exclusivity has become all the more important now because there isn’t much difference in discounts—once the primary driver of online sales. Which is why OnePlus has been exclusive on Amazon since 2014.
But hasn’t India explicitly barred e-commerce platforms from exclusivity agreements since February 2019? Sure, but there’s always a workaround.
|
Both sellers—Appario and Cloudtail—are joint ventures of Amazon Asia Pacific Holdings with Ashok Patni Group and Infosys co-founder Narayana Murthy’s Catamaran Ventures, respectively. Neither, however, is considered an Amazon group entity as Amazon Asia Pacific Holdings controls less than 25% of each company. Not only does this make them eligible to hold inventory and sell on the site, they are also seemingly exempt from the exclusivity rules.
|
