Good Morning Ishan,
 
There is nothing quite like my colleague Sumanth’s understanding of subjects. I mean, read this bit: 
 
“One might be tempted to wave this development away as inconsequential; so what if one lone VC firm in India decided to head East? Normally, it wouldn’t matter, but in this case, it does, as the name of this VC firm is Sequoia Capital (India). With a corpus of over $3 billion and a portfolio of nearly 150 startups, Sequoia Capital is India’s biggest VC by some distance. But in the recent past, Sequoia has adopted a sharp and decisive focus in Southeast Asia along two important dimensions. First, the firm has expanded its portfolio in Singapore and Indonesia to more than 15 startups.”
 
You know what, I read this story yesterday and I had a really busy day. Purely for non-work and leadership motivation reasons. So I was like, what! I mean, Sumanth goes again and again:
 
“A small transacting population implies shallow markets further poisoned by VC-fuelled deep-discounting battles to win market share.”
 
Bam. “Singapore might seem a small market consisting of less than six million residents but 3 million tourists from Indonesia visited the island nation last year. If you add Thailand and Vietnam to the mix (apparently one million tourists from these nations visited Singapore last year), it is possible to visualise Singapore as the entry point for a single largely homogenous market of more than half a billion people. A recent report by Google claims that with 330 million internet users, Southeast Asia now has the third largest number of internet users in the world, bigger than the whole population of the United States.”
 
You have to read this piece. It is here: https://the-ken.com/sequoias-singapore-sling/
