Good Morning Ishan,
Have you noticed that e-buses across cities, such as Bengaluru, Delhi, Mumbai, and Pune, are starting to stall? Bengaluru, in particular, which runs over 1,500 e-buses, has logged nearly 14,000 breakdowns since these buses hit the road around 2020.
The reasons are varied, ranging from batteries losing capacity to software protections getting jumpy to vehicles that simply refuse to restart after charging. A lot of this has to do with the hasty rollout that failed to build and test them for India’s potholed roads and high temperatures.
But more alarming than the breakdowns is the convoluted system in place to fix them.
The moment a public electric bus in India breaks down, accountability splinters across several agencies. That’s because the buses are procured by a central agency, run by state transport units, while fines for downtime and violations are levied on OEMs themselves.
No one is really incentivised to fix what’s broken.
To make matters worse, the government has already rolled out over Rs 4,000 crore worth of subsidies to manufacture nearly 14,000 new e-buses—all in the same convoluted way.
The result? State transport units will keep losing routes and revenue, OEMs will keep absorbing penalties, and public trust in electric buses will keep eroding, writes Mrunmayee in today’s deeply-reported [story](https://the-ken.com/story/14000-stalled-e-buses-how-many-agencies-does-it-take-to-fix-them/).
