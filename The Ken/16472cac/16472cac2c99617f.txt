Good Morning Ishan,

Our newsroom is changing, growing, bringing in more fresh, bright faces with unique, new ideas. This week alone, if you chanced upon our Twitter updates, you'd know we have two new members on the team.

And as an ex-colleague and dear friend reminded me, change is good, give it a chance.

Sometimes, that's all it takes. A shift in perspective. And what better way to perceive a change than to first analyse it?

That's a large chunk of what our writers did this past week. They focused on rethought priorities, on quiet experiments, on generational shifts, and on the demand for change, the demand for a better future.

Arundhati opened the week https://the-ken.com/story/inside-the-race-to-modernise-kirana-stores/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter with her piece on startups trying to modernise kirana stores. They are using tech to transform the humble kirana store and, in the process, attract the attention of FMCGs.

Ruhi and Anushka https://the-ken.com/story/the-indian-nurse-demanding-her-due-and-finally-being-heard/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter (her debut) teamed up on Tuesday to tell a moving story of struggle. Indian nurses have long been ignored for the work they put in. But now they're demanding their due, and the chorus for change is growing louder. This one's free.

Midweek, Sidhartha wrote about https://the-ken.com/story/ripple-plan-to-disrupt-swift-cross-border-remittances/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter payments tech company Ripple and its two-pronged approach to challenging incumbents. While its crypto token XRP is yet to change the game, Ripple could be a game-changer in cross-border payments.

Thursday saw Vivek talk about https://the-ken.com/story/loophole-games/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter what has not changed i.e. how corporate structures thwart e-commerce regulations. "As the number of capricious e-commerce regulations in India grows, so do the number of Amazon, Flipkart and Paytm subsidiaries," he writes.

On Friday, we circled back https://the-ken.com/story/finding-the-kodak-moment/?utm_campaign=The%20Ken&utm_source=Email&utm_medium=Newsletter to this week's theme with Harveen's copy on Kodak. The iconic camera brand is looking at sales through a rose-tinted lens. So much so that it has announced many new (some would say bizarre) products—TVs, refrigerators, etc.—just to regain consumers' attention.

That's all for this week. Hit the red button; it won't blow anything up. Promise.

Have a great weekend!
