You know the story about the Trojan Horse. Greeks. Troy. War. Horse. Hide soldiers. Boom. Surgical strike. Very thrilling. But also very fictional.
 
Most historians agree that horses were involved. They just can’t agree how. I have read many theories. A popular one goes that it was a case of impersonation. The Greek army got their hands on the Trojan cavalry’s armour and uniform and rode up to the gate, which was lowered to let them in. Another theory is the Greeks broke the gates using a wooden siege engine, which was covered with wet horse hides as a retardant against flaming arrows. Then there's my favourite. Apparently there's a reference somewhere which claims that Troy managed to piss off the Greek god, Poseidon, who broke the city’s walls in revenge. Poseidon was the God of Horses...and earthquakes.
 
So make of it what you will.
 
Today’s story by Arundhati, is about Reliance Jio’s strategy to disrupt the retail business in India...through PoS (Point of Sale) machines. She calls it a Trojan Horse and constructs a case, told through multiple insiders, about Reliance’s ambitious plans to take control of an entire industry using those little devices. It seemed fanciful to me, but three sections caught my attention.
 
“A highly-placed Jio executive said that the company plans to use its existing infrastructure of 5,000 (and growing) Jio Points—customer sales & service touch points—to also distribute PoS devices.”
 
Impersonation.
 
“If you are keeping count, Jio’s PoS is the linchpin for six other business opportunities addressing a market worth $1.8 trillion. And the first to feel the heat from this creation of new energy in the ecosystem are going to be the PoS companies.”
 
Siege machines.
 
“The investment laid out for this retail play is similar to the size of its telco investment, added the Jio executive. With bombast (because Reliance is known to adopt shock-and-awe tactics to take on competitors) we are talking Rs 2,50,000 crore ($35.4 billion). Even without, the investment is likely to be a big number for the simple reason that Reliance is trying to create a trillion-dollar retail opportunity for itself, starting with something as elementary as a PoS terminal.”
 
Earthquake.
