Good Morning Ishan,
 
The best way to understand Mobile Premier League (MPL) is to see it as a game.

No, not the kind of game(s) millions of people are purportedly playing daily on this app.

MPL is a game of performative entrepreneurship. A game of VC kayfabe.

Don't see this as a pejorative—there is nothing good or bad about this game. Those constructs don't even apply here. Instead, it is cold, ruthless VC calculus at play—something that operates at a different plane relative to narrow judgemental concepts of right or wrong.

It goes something like this.

Cherry-pick a set of founders who have a certain disposition and pedigree—real, imagined or contrived. In this case, the founders founded and exited a previous startup called Creo that was backed by the same investor. It doesn't matter if this exit was orchestrated by the same investor—if anything, that makes it better as it builds both street cred for the founders as well as mutual familiarity between the founders and the investor.

After a respectable cool-off period, back these founders again for a new venture that is designed for one thing and one thing alone—scale. Scale that is driven by one thing and one thing alone—capital.

Use that capital to hire an expensive brand ambassador like Virat Kohli and splurge on TV ads even if you have only raised your seed round (because more capital is not just likely, it is all but guaranteed).

But shouldn't these marketing spends be backed by a product that is useful/valuable? Not particularly. Even though MPL is purportedly a gaming app, it doesn't develop any game itself —it has simply used its capital to purchase clones of other popular games off-the-shelf and cobbled them together into a "platform" that promises cash rewards to "players". These cash rewards are enough to attract people to come to the app. Contrary to what one might be tempted to believe, MPL is not a gaming company. It is a company that is buying time and attention of users at scale, and the games that it offers on its platform are merely vehicles to channelise these user actions.

Next, use these engagement metrics to attract even more capital—so much so that from launching an app that sounds suspiciously like the Indian Premier League (IPL), you become a jersey sponsor for not one but two IPL teams. Rinse and repeat with the Indian national men's cricket team.

What next? It doesn't take a crystal ball for one to predict with absolute certainty that MPL will continue to scale aggressively and raise progressively larger funding rounds until it finally exits as an acquisition target for one of the behemoths or offers a lucrative secondary exit to its investors at a fat internal rate of return.

Exemplary VC kayfabe. Capital in. Capital out.

It is probably not a coincidence that the legal name of MPL is "Galactus Funware Technology Pvt. Ltd". For the uninitiated, in the world of Disney/Marvel, Galactus is a celestial being, a "devourer of worlds"—literally eating up planets on a daily basis. Scale, anyone? Also, Galactus is an immortal whose actions are not bound by the narrow considerations of good and evil—he exists only to sate his insatiable appetite for energy. Fun.
