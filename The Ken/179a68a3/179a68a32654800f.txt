Good Morning Ishan,

Wipro, the grand old daddy of Indian IT Services, had, over the years, become just the slow old daddy of Indian IT. 

From being a strong number two after TCS, it fell behind rivals Infosys, Cognizant, and HCL Technologies. Its 10-year compounded growth rate was in the single digits while those of its rivals were in the double digits. With 73% of the company held by the founder and promoter Premji family, CEOs never had a clear hand to shake things up. Naturally, its stock stagnated too.

Thus, when it appointed a new CEO last July, bang in the middle of the first Covid wave, many thought the new leader had terrible luck to be taking over a slow-moving organisation in the middle of a fast-moving pandemic. Especially a European CEO who came after decades at higher-end IT consulting companies like Arthur Anderson and Capgemini. What would he know about the frugal, margin-driven Indian way of IT?

Well, Thierry Delaporte sprung quite the surprise on everyone.

With tacit support from the founder family, Delaporte shook things up. He made expensive acquisitions, like the US$1.45 billion Capco one. He dismantled and remade Wipro’s management structures and teams, letting go of some of the old guard while others left on their own. He also infused some much-needed aggression inside Wipro. 

The results speak for themselves. The grand old daddy is reinvigorated and gaining strength. Wipro’s stock has nearly tripled in the last year. Its most recent quarterly earnings were its best in a decade. Analysts are even talking about the possibility of it giving HCL Technologies a run for third place. 

In a well-rounded and sharp story, Anand goes inside Wipro’s makeover and asks an important question: is Thierry Delaporte good, lucky or both? And can his streak continue?
