Everyone's busy doing math right now, by force if not choice.
"Is the novel coronavirus spreading at a faster or slower rate?" "How badly has my business been hit by the lockdown?" "How long before I have to step out to buy groceries?" "How much should I sip to make my alcohol stash last?"
India's insurance industry is also running numbers to see if and how to profit through Covid-19-specific policies. There is strong demand. It took Digit, the new kid on the general insurance block, just three weeks to sell out its Covid-specific health insurance policy. And this was even before the nationwide 21-day lockdown started on 24 March.
Demand alone, though, doesn't make for good, or even smart, business. Take Star Health, one of India's largest standalone health insurers. It has paid out Rs 75,000 on average per approved Covid-19 claim so far, well above its average payout of Rs 41,000 last year.
No wonder a senior executive at one insurer says, "The claims department is in a state of paranoia." And doing math.
How fast is the disease spreading? How many infected people will need hospitalisation? How much will the hospital bill total? And after all that, how to price a policy so that it can sell and make money?
There are other unknowns in the equation. Firstly, Covid-19 is unprecedented, which makes using existing models tricky. And second, insurers are racing against time. "We can't sell Covid-specific coverage after 2-3 months," says one insurance executive.
