Good Morning Ishan,

Today’s story turns the spotlight on a rather unglamorous, lesser-known facet of the shiny, growing e-commerce business in India—reverse shipments or reverse logistics. The part where customers return things they order from the likes of Myntra, Ajio, Flipkart, and Amazon.

The bulk (two-thirds or so) of reverse shipments are handled by the same entities that handle forward shipments—third-party logistics providers such as Delhivery, Bluedart, and Shadowfax.

Reverse shipment seems straightforward. It is anything but.

Interesting moves—with shades of game theory—play out among the triad of e-commerce platforms, customers, and logistics providers.

Customers love the flexibility of returns, far too much in some cases. Then, there’s the small matter of reverse shipments being way more lucrative—up to 50% higher margins—than forward shipments for logistics providers. And bearing the brunt of all this largesse are e-commerce platforms.

Let’s just say, they didn’t like being a patsy. So, some like Ajio and Myntra have tightened the screws on returns.

Is that really helping though? Can it help at all with old habits dying hard? Dive in to find out. (Also don’t miss the cool illustration by my colleague Kavipriya, reflecting some of the complexity in the game.)

Thrust into the spotlight

Like reverse shipments, Santiago Martin, too, was mostly in the shadows. Until the spotlight was thrust upon him last week.

The “lottery king” has now become the talk of the town. His business Future Gaming was shown to be the largest contributor—a cool Rs 1,368 crore (US$165 million)—to electoral bonds, as per data finally released by the State Bank of India, after the tongue-lashing by the apex court.

But what I find more intriguing about Future Gaming and some others on the donors’ list is that they donated many times their annual profits.

Did these entities dip into their accumulated reserves to contribute so liberally? That’s touching and must have called for deep conviction in political ideologies.

Or are their published profit numbers to be taken with quite a bit of salt?

Interestingly, Martin’s family members are said to have spread their affiliations across the political spectrum. Hedge for the edge? The house always wins? Life is a lottery? Take your pick.
