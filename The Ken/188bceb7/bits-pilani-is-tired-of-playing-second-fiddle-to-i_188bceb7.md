Good Morning Ishan,
You’ll need a minute to process this number. As of last year, Harvard University’s endowment—made up of donations and investments made with that corpus—was worth almost $51 billion.
That’s more than the state-owned behemoth Life Insurance Corporation of India’s market value.
Harvard’s engineering-and-applied-sciences school alone had an $1.8 billion endowment. But the university has been getting contributions from alumni for four centuries.
Now, consider the Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi (IIT Delhi), one of the country’s best engineering schools. It only set up an endowment four years ago and has since received commitments for over $60 million. But even with the IIT brand, drumming up big donations requires some serious effort.
The academic who got that endowment rolling is now doing something similar at a rival institution that has had to settle for second best for decades.
V Ramgopal Rao, a former director of IIT Delhi who is now group vice-chancellor of BITS Pilani, wants to put an end to that once and for all.
He wants students and faculty to choose BITS over the top IITs.
As simple as that statement sounds, it’s just as easy for someone to dismiss it as wishful thinking. Rao has to make many pieces of a complex puzzle fall into place. From establishing a $100 million endowment to doubling the PhD intake to a 20X rise in grants for faculty members keen on founding companies.
