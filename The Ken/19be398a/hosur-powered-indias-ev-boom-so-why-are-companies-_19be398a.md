“It was a small city, much like any other tier-2 town. A lot of historical monuments. Many colleges.” That’s how a senior official of Toyota Kirloskar Motor described Sambhajinagar to The Ken, recalling a fact-finding visit made around two years ago. “But step outside the main city area, and you’d be blown away by the industrial setup.”
Fast forward to the present. Toyota Kirloskar, Ather Energy, JSW Green Mobility, Uno Minda, and more have all set up operations in the city, to manufacture electric vehicles or their components.
Sambhajinagar has all the right ingredients for an EV manufacturing hub—cheap land, low energy costs, skilled labour, and automotive-component vendors that have good track records spanning generations.
In fact, Sambhajinagar’s AURIC zone has managed to pull in more than Rs 71,000 crore in committed investments between 2023 and 2025, all from a range of manufacturers that are part of the EV industry. A Maharashtra Industrial Township official told The Ken that this translates to 62,000 new jobs.
Mrunmayee takes you across state lines in today’s story, unpacking the way India’s map for EV manufacturing is stretching north, beyond the Hosur-Sriperumbudur corridor.
