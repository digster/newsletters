During my days as a young journalist, I was briefly enamoured by the cult of Gurmeet Ram Rahim Singh Insaan, the head of Dera Sacha Sauda. I have had the unique experience of meeting him in person and watching him perform his greatest hit, You Are My Love Charger, to an audience of five lakh devotees in Gurgaon. (If you haven't heard the song or watched the video, you probably should https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q48tagwurUw).

The Dera Sacha Sauda is a religious cult based out of Sirsa, Haryana, and it's distinctive for a number of reasons. 

First, the cult attracts people from lower castes, who discard their last names and take up the name Insaan. 

Second, the Dera sees itself as an antidote to the drug menace in Punjab. If you're an addict, the Dera hosts an annual event called the Roohani Jam, where followers drink Rooh Afza and are told they'll be cured of their addictions. 

Third, the Dera has released multiple full-length feature films with Gurmeet Ram Rahim Singh Insaan as the chief protagonist and Dera followers as extras. Those films are the most prolific and effective tools of his propaganda machine.

Gurmeet Ram Rahim Singh Insaan was convicted of rape in 2017. As of today, he has been convicted of two murders and two rapes. But journalists have reported that the real numbers are much higher. You would think that this would have some impact on his appeal. But by no measure does that appear to have happened. When the conviction was announced, many of his followers began to riot. And Dera Sacha Sauda gatherings, and its political power, continue to grow unabated.

And that's the thing about faith. It's remarkably resilient, even in the face of evidence. It's a nifty survival mechanism of the brain, evolved for us to live happier and with hope. It is belief in a thing, in spite of.

This week's Cost to Company podcast is also about faith—sticky, resilient, undeterred—but in a different thing. We interview Ramalingam S, former head of marketing at CoinDCX, Prajwal Simha, formerly of Coinbase, and Mannan Amrolliwala, a former member of the founding team at crypto startup Pillow, and ask them about their faith in crypto.

We explore:

1. The origins of their faith and why they came to the cryptocurrency industry.

2. What they found working in the belief-based industry.

3. Whether they still believe, and what they believe in.
