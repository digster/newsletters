Good Morning Ishan,
 
The empire Kishore Biyani built is staggering.
 
As the founder and CEO of Future Group, Biyani oversees a pan-India retail network, spanning over 1,500 stores across 500 cities and towns. Annually, the company claims its stores serve upwards of 350 million customers.
 
What Biyani hasn't been able to do, though, is translate this brick and mortar retail success online. Indeed, like Sisyphus—the mythical Greek character—Biyani seems doomed to keep rolling his e-commerce boulder to the top of the hill, only to see it fall back down again.
 
His first attempt was in 2007. Yes, the same year Binny and Sachin were getting Flipkart off the ground. Not only did it not pan out, but it also burnt a $40-something million hole in Biyani's pocket.
 
In 2018, as Flipkart was being sold to Walmart for $16 billion, Biyani was still trying to figure out e-commerce. He was on his fourth big try. That attempt, Retail 3.0, as Biyani called it, is currently reeling; between 350-400 of its employees were laid off in January.
 
But even before Retail 3.0 has been buried, Biyani is putting his shoulder behind his e-commerce boulder once more. And Future Group is also betting the house on it.
 
It is assembling all its might—from groceries to apparel to home decor—behind a single banner. This time, the boulder will make it to the top of the hill and stay there. And well... it better. Because, as Prasannata writes in today's story, there may not be a next time. Future Group has $1.7 billion of debt on its books, with lenders looking for their pound of flesh.
 
This is where the Sisyphus analogy gets stronger. Sisyphus was punished for twice cheating death. Biyani's problem is debt, not death. He already survived it once, in 2012, when he sold two companies to manage Future Group's $1.2 billion mountain of debt. Eight years later, he needs another great escape.
