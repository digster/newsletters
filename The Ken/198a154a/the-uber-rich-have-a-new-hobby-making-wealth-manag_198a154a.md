Good Morning Ishan,
There’s no business in India like the wealth-management business. Or so it seems. In a country of staggering income and wealth inequalities, the toniest parts are seeing a boom like never before. The count of the rich and super-rich has zoomed since the pandemic. And so has the legion of wealth managers catering to the money matters, whims, and fancies of those with deep pockets.
In 2024, the number of relationship managers working in India’s private-banking and wealth-management sectors soared past 10,000. The year also saw the combined assets handled by India’s top 30 wealth managers cross $800 billion, a cool 20% jump from the year before. The future seems swell, with some projecting that the market will double by 2029.
So, wealth managers must be popping the champagne, right? Well, not quite. The growing pot of riches that sparked off a gold rush is turning elusive for many. Let’s just say, a classic demand-supply mismatch is playing out. The likes of 360 One, Nuvama, and other biggies promoted by ICICI, Kotak, and Barclays now have company, a lot of it. And India’s richest—sensing their opportunity in a buyer’s market—are bargain-hunting for advice and making wealth managers sweat.
The upshot? Many desperate wealth managers are now resorting to some of the oldest, even if unsavoury, moves in the dog-eat-dog competition book.
Aakriti, the author of [today’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/indias-ultra-rich-have-a-new-hobby-making-wealth-managers-sweat/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter), unearths and unpacks the irony of India’s wealth managers scrambling in a booming wealth-management market.
On another note, Sebi’s crackdown on Jane Street is no doubt seen in many quarters as a feather in its cap, even drawing some global commendation. The swift rise of India’s capital markets have certainly forced the watchdog to get better at sniffing out suspicious behaviour—at least the number of Sebi investigations has nearly doubled in the past decade.
But numbers can only take you so far.
For a regulator, perception is at least as important.
