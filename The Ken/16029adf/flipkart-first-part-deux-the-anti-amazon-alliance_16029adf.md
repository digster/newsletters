Good Morning Ishan,
How do organisations assess brand loyalty? Through customer purchases and repeat visits, right?
Wrong. Loyalty is more emotional than rational, especially in retail, says a global survey from Capgemini [released](https://www.capgemini.com/resources/loyalty-deciphered/) yesterday. We stumbled upon the survey results just as we were wrapping up today’s story which is all about driving loyalty and building a walled garden for customers.
Cutting to the chase, Flipkart is close to launching a subscription platform, Flipkart First. The e-commerce’s second shot at subscription—Flipkart First launched and shut down in 2014—is also an anti-Amazon alliance of sorts. But unlike Amazon Prime, where the company controls the experience tightly by offering its own products, Flipkart First is a loose federation of services, from MakeMyTrip, Gaana, Hotstar, and others. Clearly, there are many moving parts.
How is that going to impact its ambition of building a super app, one like WeChat (now that Tencent is an investor)? As the Capgemini survey shows, and as many of us know, discounts and deals only get you bargain hunters. Flipkart needs to configure its premium value proposition for subscribers. Not just mirror Amazon as best as it can. And this time around, it has money in the bank, senior employees to build this product, and more importantly, lessons from the first failed attempt.
Patanjali and Payal analyse how Flipkart is likely going about it.
