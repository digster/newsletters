Some day we’ll hopefully look back at the times we’re living in as the peak of unsustainability. We consumed too much. We wasted too much. We spent too much. We borrowed too much.

And one result of living in a world awash with liquidity, guilt and desire is the incredible rise of “greening”. If we spent our way into the unsustainable present, we’ll need to spend our way to get to the hopefully sustainable future. $1.4 trillion is India’s projected cost to finance green energy tech over the next two decades.

Green housing? That’s another $1.25 trillion by 2030.

But what is green housing? They’re apartments and homes that try to eliminate negative impacts on climate and environment through their design, construction or operation. They do this through the efficient use of energy, water and other resources, waste reduction, higher use of renewable energy, and use of non-toxic materials.

If you’re thinking that doesn’t come cheap, then you’re right. They’re roughly 12% more expensive than un-green homes. That’s why you see them adopted by premium builders like Godrej and Total Environment. But to nudge more people to buy these homes, banks like SBI, IIFL Home Finance and HDFC are offering discounts on their loans.
