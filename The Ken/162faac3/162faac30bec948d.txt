With general elections in about 12 months, the run-up to Karnataka assembly polls gives us a sneak peek into what India’s digital election campaigns would probably look like

Good Morning Ishan,

If you are in Karnataka, you know that elections are just around the corner. Cars with ‘On election duty’ stickers, police patrolling, posters, party flags and all that jazz of Indian elections are noticeable. What is not so visible, but will soon be, is the WhatsApp onslaught.

It isn’t surprising. The 2017 elections in the northern state of Uttar Pradesh, where the Bharatiya Janata Party won comprehensively, was the watershed moment for WhatsApp as a tool for political communication. But if BJP deployed 9,000 WhatsApp groups then, it is deploying 23,000 now in Karnataka. Congress is not too far behind.

Political parties say there’s something both “constant and instant” about WhatsApp. Facebook, on the other hand, is being used as an amplifier. Candidates are spending $15-$22 daily on advertising on their respective FB pages. Parties and candidates have even found a hack around the Election Commission expenditure limit in Assembly elections. In all this, those with a large FB following are earning ‘rents’.

Venkat gives you a glimpse of what’s going on in the name of digital campaigns. Actually, it’s more than a glimpse. Which is why when political parties say technology has made their life easy, it’s time to ask how far is too far when it comes to profiling and microtargeting voters. How responsible will the platforms be? In using tech, is Karnataka election a prelude to 2019 General Elections?
