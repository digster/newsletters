I know what you are going to say. You had it coming, Ashish. What did you expect? So, let me just get it straight out. About a month back, I bought a nice, big, smart television. I deserve a smart TV and I’m going to have it. 

Enter, today’s story. 

From the government to private broadcasters, everyone wants to know what you watch on television and when you watch it. The set-top boxes installed in our houses are privy to all subscriber data—what we watch, when we watch it and for how long. What wasn’t there till now in India, and what is being increasingly adopted, is this decade-old technology called ‘return path data’ (RPD) which can send real-time viewership data back to our DTH and cable operators. 

Tata Sky is doing it. Barc is doing it. Dish TV is doing it. And early last week, the government of India said, hold my beer. According to a document, the information and broadcasting (I&B) ministry had proposed installation of chips in the new set-top boxes being deployed by DTH operators, which can give data about channels watched and their duration, in a letter sent to the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (Trai).

What happens now is what I call a dystopian future, a deadly concoction of capitalism and a surveillance state. You must read Harveen’s story to find out why.
