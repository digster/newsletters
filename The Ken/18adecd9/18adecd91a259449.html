Good Morning Ishan,

Earlier this week, we had a story on the hiring spree at Jio Financial Services. There was a quote by a former Jiomoney executive that was quite telling.

“Reliance always wants to be the market leader in whichever space they enter. I have never been in a discussion where they said, ‘Let’s aim to be in the top three.’”

Which is why it’s hard to accept that the Mukesh Ambani-owned conglomerate would even consider beating a retreat.

But that’s exactly what’s happening with Jiomart B2B. Over the past three years, Jiomart has been trying to disrupt the relationship between fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) companies and kiranas by wedging itself in the middle as an alternative to distributors.

Jiomart B2B even managed to clock Rs 950 crore ($114 million) in monthly revenue.

However, it’s now giving up. Well, kind of.

My colleague Aayush has learnt that Jiomart B2B has halted operations since August. And its headcount has been slashed from over 1,500 to 250-300.

Reliance is still not entirely out of B2B, though, and it has its acquisition of Metro Cash & Carry India, a wholesale chain, to thank for that.

So, what is it about FMCG distribution that was too hot to handle even for a behemoth like Reliance? And can it be any different with Metro?
