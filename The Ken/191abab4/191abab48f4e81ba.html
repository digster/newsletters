The Big Story

Why Reliance Retail will be stock-market pundits’ most perplexing company

It is so sprawling that every listed Indian retailer is a rival. Even when none of them has a fraction of its complexity

Reliance Retail Ventures (RRVL) is the most prized part of Reliance Industries, with analysts pegging its valuation at over US$100 billion. But they have struggled to make sense of the company given Reliance’s scant disclosures on RRVL’s different businesses. Even with granular data after its IPO, RRVL will pose a challenge to investors with its motley collection of retail and FMCG ventures. And so will some of the 20-odd acquisitions RRVL has made since the pandemic. [Read the full story]

Jindal Law School shook up legal studies like ISB did management. Copycats now want in on the action 

Jindal Global Law School’s high-fee model for international exposure was a commercial hit. Now, a fresh crop of law schools, led by ex-Jindalites, want to raise the bar

Jindal Global Law School (JGLS) broke the law degree price ceiling 15 years ago. Now, new schools are pushing the limits even further. Most new schools are following JGLS’s playbook, with some even poaching its former faculty. Ivy League faculty, interdisciplinary curriculum, research culture, and foreign immersions are some of the common traits of these new schools. Premiumisation promises big returns for universities, but how many students land those high-paying jobs? [Read the full story]

Health fintechs pave a new cashless path. Destination: Rs 48,000 crore. Target: India’s ‘missing middle’

Matchmaking between cash-strapped hospitals and the booming insurance market, startups like Gmoney and Digisparsh are chasing the 10 million patients seeking  reimbursements

The number of people covered by health insurance has increased more than 2.5X over the past decade. And recent government scheme budgets and cashless claims indicate a burgeoning US$5.7 billion market for cashless transactions. But 85% of health facilities in India are small-to-mid-sized hospitals, which frequently encounter months of delays for cashless claims settlements from insurers. So, a new wave of health fintechs is emerging to address the cashless claims issue for hospitals. But non-bank competitors may soon begin to take notice. [Read the full story]

A cab driver’s rage against Uber, Amazon, and Zomato’s machine is redefining India’s trade unions

When job markets are characterised by increasing uncertainty, Shaik Salauddin and his unions are fixing fellow workers’ pressing problems 

For the longest, IT employees saw joining labour unions as going against their employer, thus risking their careers. That’s now changing, thanks to scores of workers’ unions stepping up to support laid-off professionals and gig workers. Trade unions have also steered away from protests and are now actively engaging in dialogue with the government. Shaik Salauddin’s IFAT is now pushing state governments to recognise social security benefits for gig and platform workers. [Read the full story]

Indonesia’s clean energy sector pins hopes on new regime as ‘ambivalent’ incumbents exit

A new regime and shifting public mood are firing up investor excitement for Indonesia’s clean energy, but intrinsic systemic issues cannot be ignored

Indonesia is experiencing a surge in enthusiasm for green startups and renewable energy. Solar-generated electricity costs 800 rupiah/kWh, 20% less than fossil fuel power in the country. Green energy enthusiasts and investors are hopeful that a regime change in October will lead to positive changes. However, the country’s cheap petrol complicates the transition, making it harder for people to switch to alternative energy sources. [Read the full story]

Meesho goes from Ecom Express’ dream client to feared rival

India’s second-largest logistics player is hurting after putting all its eggs in one basket

Ecom Express’ revenues have stagnated after its biggest customer Meesho took logistics in-house. The company hasn’t been able to turn its warehousing and fulfilment business into major revenue drivers either. That’s in stark contrast to its biggest rival Delhivery, which has diversified both its customer mix as well as services. These strategic limitations, which could weigh on the company’s upcoming IPO, stem from a lack of decisive leadership. [Read the full story]
