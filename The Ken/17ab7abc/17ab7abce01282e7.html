Good Morning Ishan,
 
Reading a typical feature on The Ken takes as long as listening to BB King's Lucille or Bob Dylan's Desolation Row. 

But unlike with either of these sublime songs, which one can never get enough of, most of you will never revisit a Ken story. Even if it blew your mind. And if you didn't like it, there’s no way you're going to give it another chance. 

It's different for a song you didn't care for when you first heard it. You might chance upon it again on Spotify or in a movie, and you could very well change your mind. 

While we look forward to you sharing our stories with others, we know we can't expect you to read them again. That's why we spend days, sometimes weeks, making sure the ten minutes you spare for us are worth it (maybe with Dylan playing in the background). We may not hit it out of the park every time, but we certainly try. 

Much of the discussion on Zomato’s $1.2 billion IPO has been about what the profitability-focussed public market investors mean for the foodtech major. But in his piece on Friday, Sumanth made a case for how the celebrated startup's listing could change India’s equity markets. 

On Thursday, Pranav and Olina teamed up to find out what electric vehicle maker Tesla’s initial hires in India tell us about its immediate priorities here. 

A day earlier, we had a deep dive by Pratap on how the government may be shooting itself in the foot with the inadequacies of its much-hyped Innovations for Defence Excellence (iDEX) initiative. This was our free story of the week, so do share it widely. 

On Tuesday, contributor Rachel Chia set out to understand what startups Next Gen and Growthwell need to do to become Singapore's answers to American plant-based meat makers Impossible Foods and Beyond Meat. 

Right at the top of the week was a revealing look at Amazon’s growing might in the digital advertising business in India. Up against deeply entrenched incumbents Google and Facebook, Amazon is on course to clock $500 million in ad sales in the year ending March 2022, Munsif uncovered in his story. 

In our weekday newsletter, Beyond the First Order, Anand delved into how a beleaguered PNB Housing Finance is facing pushback from an unexpected quarter; Rohin wrote about startups' new retention hack for top executives: the co-founder tag. 

In our Saturday newsletter, The Nutgraf, Praveen ran the rule over what seems to be a Unified Payments Interface equivalent for Indian e-commerce. 

That's a wrap from us this week. Don't let the Monday blues spoil your Sunday.
