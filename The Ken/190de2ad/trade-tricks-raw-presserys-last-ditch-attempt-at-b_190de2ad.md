Good Morning Ishan,
When I got on a call with Anju Srivastava, the founder of Wingreens Farms, on a recent Monday morning, I jumped straight to the point.
Why was Raw Pressery—a company Wingreens acquired in 2021—entering a category that was the very opposite of what the brand has stood for all this time? I was talking about Raw’s new energy drink line, for which a formal launch announcement hasn’t been made yet.
Energy drinks, after all, are widely associated with unhealthy levels of sugar and caffeine, and a brand image that screams “machismo”. Raw Pressery, founded by Anuj Rakyan in 2014, is on the very opposite end of the spectrum: a clean-label brand, selling beverages you are more likely to get at your local juice-wala, minus the dust and the flies. Before it was acquired by Wingreens, the company had managed to capture a good-sized chunk of the market, and raise significant funding from marquee investors such as Peak XV Partners (formerly Sequoia) and DSG Consumer Partners.
Srivastava was not defiant. Instead, she asked me why I thought energy drinks were unhealthy. They had high amounts of caffeine and sugar, which people tended to over consume, I answered. Srivastava, an advertising professional-turned-entrepreneur (she launched Wingreens in 2008) had a response to each of these points.
The caffeine content in energy drinks, she pointed out, is the same as in a cup of regular coffee. As for the sugar content, the trouble came from refined sugar and artificial sweeteners, she said, but Raw Pressery’s new energy drinks used “khandsari sugar” and “natural sweeteners”, which made them less harmful and slightly healthier.
But is an energy drink range actually better for the consumer, or is it just better for the bottom line?
In 2023, Indian consumers consumed a massive 571.6 million litres of energy drinks, up about 30X from 2018, data from global market-research firm Euromonitor indicates. In fact, the growth that bottler Varun Beverages has been seeing has largely, if not single-handedly, been pulled off by its energy drink [Sting](https://the-ken.com/story/red-bull-is-the-energy-drink-of-the-world-how-did-pepsico-make-sting-the-energy-drink-of-india/).
So is this what Wingreens is trying to replicate with Raw Pressery?
