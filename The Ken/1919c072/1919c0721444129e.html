There’s nothing like a dependable client in business. Ask Ecom Express, India’s second-largest third-party logistics company, which filed for IPO for the second time this month.

Just when the company needed its oldest and largest client, Meesho, to keep its business steady, the e-commerce marketplace is turning out to be a formidable rival.

Founded three years after Ecom Express, Meesho grew with its logistics partner and sent a significant share of shipments to it. According to Ecom Express’ recent IPO filings, over half of the Rs 2,600 crore (US$310 million) operational revenue it registered in fiscal year 2024 came from one unnamed customer. At least four people have confirmed to The Ken that it was Meesho.

That stable revenue source is thinning for Ecom Express as Meesho’s in-house logistics arm, Valmo, now gets a bigger share of the shipments. In fact, Valmo’s growth has shaken the e-commerce-logistics industry to the extent that Delhivery CEO Sahil Barua acknowledged it in the latest earnings call.

The writing was on the wall, though. While Delhivery, having expanded into full-service logistics, deflected the Valmo impact, Ecom Express tied itself in knots to keep and woo Meesho.

Meesho claims Valmo has helped reduce its logistics cost by nearly 10%. So it’s obvious it’ll try to emulate what captive logistics companies ATS and Ekart do for Amazon and Flipkart, respectively.
