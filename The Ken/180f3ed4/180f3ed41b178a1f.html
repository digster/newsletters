Good Morning Ishan,

What's in a name? Really? Quite clearly, The Bard hadn't foreseen the power of names in the Indian stock market.
 
Consider the protagonist of today's story—RattanIndia Enterprises.
 
Last April, this little-known company, an offshoot of the Indiabulls Group conglomerate, changed its suffix from 'Infrastructure' to 'Enterprises'. And voila! After languishing for years, the penny stock shot up 14X in four months. Market punters live and die for such days.
 
And while it has let off some steam since then, the stock is still priced 8X higher than what it was last April. Such has been its rise that RattanIndia Enterprises now finds a place in the MSCI India Small Cap Index. That's yet another booster for the rocket.
 
The rebranding was meant to reflect RattanIndia's new avatar—from a boring backer of a thermal power producer to a cool investment company for new-age businesses like e-commerce, fintech, drones, and, of course, EVs. 
 
The right buttons may have been pressed, but something's not quite right, writes Seetharaman in today's story. For starters, the company's market cap of Rs 5,800 crore (US$750 million) is way out of sync with its financials—the top line is near-absent, and the bottom line is in the red. Also, institutional investors have kept away, at least so far. What gives? Will things change?
