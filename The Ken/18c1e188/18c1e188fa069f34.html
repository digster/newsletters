Good Morning Ishan,

I’m excited.

Why, you ask?

A most-likely financial blockbuster is about to drop in a while from now.

What’s that, you ask?

Tata Technologies is making its stock-market debut, and experts told Anand—the author of today’s story—that it might list at a premium of over 80%. After all, the ~Rs 3,000 crore (US$360 million) issue, which closed on 24 November, saw 7.36 million applications.

However, the IPO is surprising to many. Tata Technologies is abundantly cash-rich. “Tata Technologies has adequate financial resources for the growth plans. As of now, incremental capital isn’t required to finance any growth strategies,” a company spokesperson told The Ken.

And so, there were no fresh shares on offer.

An obvious question follows: why the IPO extravaganza?

Take a hint: N Chandrasekaran, chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of all Tata Group firms, has been driving consolidation across the conglomerate. He wants the group to “simplify, synergise, and scale”.

The IPO might just be a precursor to one such synergy. Versions of such mergers have been tried before. But this time, the plot looks more favourable. Both protagonists are in the same line of business with significant overlaps.

So, what exactly is brewing?
