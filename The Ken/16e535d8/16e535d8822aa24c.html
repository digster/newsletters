Good Morning Ishan,
 
For most of us, there is this one "old school" fixation that is hard to break away from, despite the advent of online alternatives. For me, that's shopping at stores. 
 
I know, this might sound antiquated to a whole bunch of youngsters, but the feeling of spotting and holding what you can buy is so instantly fulfilling; it acts as therapy for me whenever I am extremely stressed out. Even when I am not, there's an unexplained gratification that offline shopping gives me. Looking at the poorly-dressed mannequins, make-up coated aunties, sassy teenagers, kids running amok, would mortify many a people, but not me. Perhaps because I love observing people and things around—the new brands and stores that crop up, new designs on display, products, their variants—sometimes these are fodder for stories.
 
But people who know me would tell you that my love for shopping goes beyond journalism, so much so that during our recent offsite in Phuket, Thailand—where all my colleagues were busy exploring the nightlife—all I cared for was shopping.
 
However, surrounded by obligations, responsibilities and deadlines, as we all are these days, I agree there is little time. And, that's why I have started moving, albeit slowly, to online. Groceries have been the first to go. Change is hard, I must say. 
 
To think most people today find partners on apps?
