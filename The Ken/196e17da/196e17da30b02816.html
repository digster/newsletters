Inside the legal drama that may exile Ultrahuman from the US

Oura came out swinging last September—and by April, it had a US court upholding its claim that Ultrahuman copied its ring tech

Oura went public with its gripes in September 2023, accusing Ultrahuman of ripping off its smart-ring tech. That set the tone. Soon, Oura started picking off other ring-makers like Ringconn and Circular. This April, the fight got fiercer: a US judge ruled that Ultrahuman did, in fact, infringe Oura’s patent—and, for good measure, may have tried to fudge the evidence. Now Ultrahuman is banking on a post-grant review to save it. If that fails, it could lose its biggest market, one that generated Rs 400 crore in revenue last fiscal. [Read the full story]

Deep dives

Smallcase bet on DIY investors. Then DIY investors moved on

The investment platform hasn’t quite lived up to its initial hype—plagued by high fees, sub-par returns and stiff competition from mutual funds

Smallcase’s bet to ride the DIY investing wave is now facing multiple challenges. The ever-dependable mutual fund has encroached on Smallcase’s USP of offering thematic strategies over the last few years. The company’s mounting losses do not help its case either, even as it looks to cut costs. In an effort to diversify, Smallcase has entered into a joint venture with Zerodha to start an asset management company. [Read the full story]

*****

Infosys: the job that millions of India’s engineering grads took for granted is now a tough sell

More skills, same pay, and barely any job guarantee—the IT services giant is raising the bar for freshers

Infosys trainees are in for a rough ride, as qualifying tests for 2024-25 become tougher due to higher “business standards”. This is an IT sector-wide phenomenon—service companies want trainees who come with higher skillsets, and faster billability. Freshers’ salaries, however, have remained stagnant for almost a decade. Companies use these lower salaries to maintain margins amidst business slowdown, as they face intense competition from other service firms, GCCs, and automation. [Read the full story]

Cricbuzz may give Dream11 real-money gamers. But Times Internet is the real winner

Dream Sports’ $50 million deal with Times Internet gives its core business a chance to survive

Investing in Cricbuzz and Willow TV allows Dream Sports to unlock strategic marketing and cross-promotion opportunities in its core fantasy-sports business. In particular, the company is betting on Willow TV as a way to unlock the US market at a time when India’s stringent tax policies have put a damper on its growth. Then again buying into Cricbuzz at a time when the media industry’s future looks shaky isn’t exactly a clear win for Dream Sports. But it’s certainly something for Times Internet to cheer about at a time when the giant’s own business hasn’t exactly been on fire. [Read the full story]

*****

Is it time for Info Edge to cut its Zomato stake?

There’s perhaps a case for the classifieds company and tech investor being just what it said it doesn’t want to be: opportunistic

Zomato, now renamed Eternal, is dealing with a slowing food-delivery business and bruising competition in quick commerce. Investors once euphoric about its prospects are now wary, and Info Edge may do well to pare its 12.4% stake in the company. Info Edge’s mainstay, job portal Naukri, is vulnerable to the potential impact of AI on jobs, especially in the IT sector. And the company’s other classifieds verticals—real estate, matchmaking, and education—have failed to enthuse investors. [Read the full story]
