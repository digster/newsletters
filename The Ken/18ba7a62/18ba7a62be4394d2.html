When consumer internet meets healthcare, a healthtech company is born in India. We’ve seen many, right?

However, discounting cannot be the differentiator. It’s healthcare, after all.

Any extent of mouth-watering pricing—Rs 199, Rs 299, Rs 399, and so on—or a dizzying range of tests for an equally dizzying array of parameters can only go so far.

But Healthians started eight years ago to challenge the status quo—do preventive screening and tests for chronic diseases, avoid doctors, keep costs low, and spread healthiness.

Having cricketer Yuvraj Singh, who is also a cancer survivor, as both an investor and brand ambassador also caught everyone’s attention. Like consumer goods and services, Healthians’ offerings came with “discount” coupons.

Then came another cricketer on the pitch, MS Dhoni. Advocating “Doctor First” in an ad campaign for the roll-up diagnostics chain Neuberg, the Dhoni ad was “issued in public interest”. As if to counter the discounting message and say: don’t DIY in diagnostics.

It’s interesting how competition is playing out between conventional and digital diagnostics companies in India. The industry saw a boom in the financial year ended March 2022, which, lab owners tell me, will level off by 2024 and reveal who has the sustaining power.

Healthians, which has raised over $85 million so far and claims to be the fifth-largest diagnostics company, is making a sharp turn.

Like in retail, e-diagnostics companies have learnt that the road to profitability and sustainability goes through the offline channel. From having no labs a few years ago, Healthians today has 22. It will likely have collection centres, too.

Healthians started out to be a low-cost, low-priced business model but ended up as a high-cost, low-priced service that is trying hard to break free of the latter, writes Nuha in her story today: https://the-ken.com/story/healthians-ignored-doctors-in-diagnostics-and-lost-any-chance-at-disruption/ (10-minute read)
