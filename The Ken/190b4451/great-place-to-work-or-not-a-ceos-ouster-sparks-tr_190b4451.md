Good Morning Ishan,
Irony died a thousand deaths
One late night in late May
A CEO was summarily ousted
A founder rammed through his way
Big deal? So what?
You might just want to ask
But wait until you hear
This company’s name and task
An arbiter of workplace culture
Great Place to Work India, it’s called
What and how it happened there
Jaws fell, left many appalled
Three years into her CEO role
Yeshasvini Ramaswamy had done well
Client count tripled, profits and valuation doubled
Great going, the numbers would tell
But then, the old jungle saying
Numbers never say it all
Intrigue wafts through the air
Prasenjit Bhattacharya had plotted her fall
What made Prasenjit rage?
He’d brought Yeshasvini in
Gave her a sweet deal and a stake
What now did he consider sin?
Untangling of a spider’s web?
A Rs 300-crore sellout deal?
Was the founder’s favourite child hurt?
What sparked this bitter feel?
It all cuts a sorry picture
Corporate-culture judge now in the dock
“Diversity”, “equity”, “inclusion”
At GPTW, all take a long walk
Behind the all-ok facade
Its employees are on edge,
Marquee clients feel wronged
Will this drive a wedge?
Will hell now have no fury
Like a woman scorned?
