With a resolve as weak as mine, I have little choice but to look for a fix for my tech obsession in, well, tech.
I rely on my iPhone to keep tabs on the time I spend on Slack, Netflix, and news apps. Now, I'm considering springing for Freedom, a paid tool that will block these distractions when I have to write my stories. With coffee shop sounds from around the globe to boot.
Yet another service to add to my growing list of digital subscriptions. Partly because it's easier than ever to get one.
Freedom, for instance, uses Chargebee to manage payments made by its over one million users. Chargebee is a nine-year-old company that helps B2C SaaS outfits like Freedom and their B2B counterparts such as Freshworks handle their subscription billing.
Headquartered in Chennai, Chargebee recently raised $55 million from its existing investors, including Insight Partners and Tiger Global, to take its total funding beyond $100 million.
Chargebee could very well have become redundant. After all, its core proposition is offered as a feature on payments platforms, such as the $36 billion-worth Stripe—Silicon Valley's most valuable startup. But Chargebee managed to make a case for subscription billing as a standalone product while also working with the likes of Stripe. A case strong enough for it to process $3 billion of billings each year.
