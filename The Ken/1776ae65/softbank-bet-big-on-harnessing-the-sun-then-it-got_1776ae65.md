In 2015, SoftBank announced plans to set up a solar energy venture in India.
The news must have felt like the falling blade of a guillotine, seemingly guaranteed to separate heads from bodies in India's fledgeling solar sector.
The Japanese conglomerate was still years removed from its $100 billion tech investment vehicle, the Vision Fund, but it had already made its mark globally, including India. Just the year prior, it had splashed nearly $1 billion on three Indian startups—Snapdeal, Ola, and Housing.com.
Its new innings promised even bigger things. India's total solar capacity was 3GW. SB Energy—a joint venture between SoftBank, Taiwanese electronics manufacturer Foxconn, and Sunil Mittal's Bharti Enterprises—would invest $20 billion to build a 20GW portfolio.
In 2020, just over five years on from that announcement, India became home to the world's largest solar power developer.
It wasn't SB Energy.
Rather than tower over India's solar sector, leaving the competition to wither in its shade, SB Energy has unravelled spectacularly. Plummeting tariffs, regulatory issues, as well as problems of its own making saw the company stagnate, even as the likes of Goldman Sachs-backed ReNew Power and Adani Green Energy Ltd became veritable solar titans.
Things have gotten so bad for SoftBank that it now wants to get out of India altogether. Two people aware of the developments confirmed to The Ken that SoftBank has sold its 80% stake in the joint venture. The news comes barely two years after Foxconn exited the JV.
