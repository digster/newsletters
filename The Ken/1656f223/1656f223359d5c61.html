Good Morning Ishan,
 
Do you watch TV news? I try my best not to. But sometimes I slip up and turn the idiot box on to see what its inhabitants are discussing. Five minutes later, startled by the combination of screaming and bright, flashing graphics, I retreat into a dark room, clutching a bewildered cat to my chest, rocking back and forth while muttering “Never again, never again”.  Somehow, despite the trauma, the cycle repeats. I chalk it down to a previous job where tracking India’s primetime lunacy was a KRA. PTSD is real. In my case, self-inflicted as well.

I was speaking to a senior TV anchor of some repute earlier this week, and he confessed that he, too, found the current television news status quo unbearable. Strong sentiments, especially coming from a man who played a key role in the evolution of the medium. But it would be misleading to say this malaise has not crept into print and even digital news. Sensationalism sells. Opinion trumps actual news. And the difference between true and false is a matter of taste.

With this being the case, we at The Ken work really hard to maintain a sanctuary for you, our reader. One where facts stand tallest and reportage isn’t divorced from intelligent analysis. By those yardsticks, this past week has been a good one.

Take, for instance, this week’s free story from Gayathri. In it, she talks about statins—drugs that reduce cholesterol levels in the blood. Indians are especially prone to heart disease, making statins particularly important. However, some doctors have proposed new thresholds that could see low-risk patients medicated at great cost and little benefit. All of this comes even as a new, expensive drug is set to be released in the Indian market.

Olina and Salman teamed up this week, putting together a rather interesting story. They looked at the various voice interfacing tools being developed to unlock the internet for India’s vernacular language speakers. It’s a forward-looking story, and one with serious implications for India’s e-commerce platforms.

We also had Sumanth running the rule over OYO Rooms. The SoftBank-backed hospitality startup is reportedly set to raise up to $1 billion in funding at a valuation of $4 billion. Good news for the company, undoubtedly. But will the good days keep rolling?

Then there was Pooja and Sidhartha’s story on why India’s largest renewable power company, ReNew, decided to shelve its grand plans for an IPO.

And, before I forget, you must read our TKOW article from last Sunday. In it, contributors Anisha and Aysha take a look at that most ubiquitous of household appliances—the refrigerator. The contents that lie within, they argue, tell us a lot about who we are.
