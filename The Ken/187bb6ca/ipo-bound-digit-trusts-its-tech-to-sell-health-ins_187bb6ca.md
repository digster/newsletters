What's confusing? Buying insurance. What's even more confusing? Buying health insurance.
The jargons and T&C can be mind-boggling. Co-pay, pre-existing illness, waiting period, expense caps, deductibles, exclusions, add-ons, top-ups, cashless, floaters, loading...it goes on and on. Health insurers want your business but want to cover their backs too. Most folks need health coverage but often find their claims truncated. Much hand-wringing happens.
But in confusion and frustration lies opportunity—for those looking to make a mark in a highly competitive market dominated by well-entrenched players.
That brings me to the protagonist of Rounak's story today—Digit General Insurance. Founded in 2016, this digital full-stack insurtech has covered a good distance—it has a valuation of $4 billion (courtesy of Sequoia Capital and others), is profitable (surprise, surprise), and is now bound for an IPO. Much of this has been on the back of Digit's motor insurance business, where it has used tech to good effect to get to a respectable market share.
But what got it here may not get it there. To become a full-fledged general insurer, Digit is now upping its game in health insurance—a much bigger and underpenetrated market with better margins to boot.
It's trying to set itself apart by keeping it simple for buyers and doing away with restrictions that get their goat. Plus, it's deploying tech (of course) and doubling down on marketing. But is this all enough to move the needle? Not quite, says Rounak. A crucial piece of the health insurance jigsaw is still missing.
