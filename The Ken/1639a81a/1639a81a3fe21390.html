“No way,” I said. “That does not happen.”

 

“You want to bet?” asked the girl. “You will be surprised how many times this happens. So, let’s order a whiskey and a glass of wine. I can bet the whiskey will come to you and the wine to me.”

 

I don’t think so.

 

“Dude, you have no idea. There’s this general concept that wine is supposed to be a woman’s drink and men drink whiskey. I can prove this. Like, right now. You want to try?”

 

Man, it is too early in the day to get a drink, but I hear what you are saying.

 

And just like that, we start talking about stereotypes. The world, our world, abundant with them. Journalists are penniless drunks. Depressed, raging maniacs. Those who can’t do, teach. Doctors have bad handwriting; worse lifestyles. Nobody gives a credit card to a lawyer. A Muslim name must always be at the receiving end of terrorism jokes. A single woman in her 30s. Men are awesome at driving cars. Women suck. Fat people. People with tattoos. You start thinking about it, and boy, the stereotype life digs such a nice hole for itself. And strange how it seeps, ever so silently, into our daily lives. One moment you are your smug self. Next, in the hole.

 

The good thing about life, you never stop learning. And I can say pretty much the same about stories from The Ken.
