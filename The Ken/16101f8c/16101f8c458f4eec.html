Yup. Now the Chinese want in. 
 
After Amazon, Netflix, Hotstar, Balaji and others, it is now Alibaba's turn. My colleague Venkat reports, that for the last many months, senior officials from Alibaba have been scoping the video content market in India. And by the looks of it, they’ve liked what they’ve seen. So, very shortly, Alibaba will be launching its own over-the-top (OTT) service. It is likely to be powered by two of Alibaba's key divisions in India: Paytm, the Indian payment company where it is the majority stakeholder, and UCWeb, a subsidiary of Alibaba's media and entertainment business. 
 
My heart goes out to content creators. A mighty lot rests on your already content shoulders. But having said that, also, congratulations. 
 
Okay, so I’m not saying this in jest. The OTT market in India is now thoroughly overcrowded, with more than 30 different players across languages and geographies. There’s more purpose in a morning fish market than in this room, but hey, for now, no one is complaining. Venkat says we are sitting on the cusp of a content explosion, especially video, with cheap data rates; and everyone wants to take a shot at it. He says short video content, or anything less than 20 minutes in length, has become a phenomenon in China. Some reports estimate the average daily views for the format at 15 billion. 
 
The piece has a lot more. On Tencent entering the country with its gaming business and Jinri Toutiao, the $20 billion valued Chinese news aggregator which has also been slowly ramping up in India. You can read the piece here: https://the-ken.com/alibaba-ott-india/
 
Be woke and have a great day.
