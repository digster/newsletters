For HDFC Bank, it’s Flipkart over Apple. The throughput that happens during annual e-commerce sales is something that banks can’t afford to ignore 
 By Arundhati Ramanathan https://the-ken.com/writers/arundhati/

Good Morning Ishan,

E-commerce companies Flipkart and Amazon live and die for their annual sale events, the period between October and December being when they make most of their topline. And preparations for it begin right after the last sale ends. 

This week, Flipkart announced the dates for the 2024 edition of its annual sale, Big Billion Days, and this time around, the e-commerce giant is partnering with India’s top private bank. HDFC Bank card users (the bank has 20 million of them) will get a straight 10% discount on their purchases. 

Signing on a large banking partner is a big deal for these sales, because it’s those credit cards that do all the talking. And such a partnership is equally important for both the likes of Flipkart and the bank. 

For the bank, this means signing on more credit-card users who want to make use of those discounts, and a good chance to fire up some inactive card users. It also means that there’s more spending through those cards, something the e-commerce companies definitely want. As a result, these events have become the largest spending programme for banks in the country. 

“If you are a bank and you don’t take part in this, your credit-card market share does not move,” said a credit card executive.

Reunion

HDFC Bank is coming back as a partner with Flipkart after a hiatus of a few years. And it is able to do that because it recently cut its long-running partnership with Apple. 

In July, The Ken wrote about how the bank was calling off the expensive agreement under which HDFC bank customers got exclusive cashbacks on Apple products.

“HDFC Bank was spending about 1.5–2 times more on offers for Apple products than that for other brands,” said a credit-card executive at a large issuer who has studied how Apple works.

HDFC Bank and Apple’s exclusive partnership, once the envy of lenders, is over for now, The Ken https://the-ken.com/story/hdfc-bank-and-apples-exclusive-partnership-once-the-envy-of-lenders-is-over-for-now/

Two months after this story was published, HDFC Bank’s Parag Rao, group head for Payments, consumer finance, and other businesses, also admitted this. 

“We worked with Apple for five years. We built a good brand relationship with Apple. We took a pause because we reviewed the entire nature of the partnership. We monitor cost to income and sometimes the cost-to-income measure does not work out for a particular partnership. We have taken a temporary break.” 

We have taken a temporary break from our partnership with Apple: HDFC Bank, Business Standard https://www.business-standard.com/finance/news/we-have-taken-temporary-break-from-our-partnership-with-apple-hdfc-bank-124090100217_1.html

The partnership with Apple would have surely helped the bank get access to premium customers, but the thing with financing sale events like Big Billion Days is that they cover a far wider audience, which includes these premium customers.  

The Ken found that during the course of its partnership, HDFC Bank may have spent almost Rs 400–500 crore (US$48–60 million) a year in financing Apple purchases. And during these annual e-commerce sale events, banks can spend anywhere from Rs 180–250 crore (US$22–30 million) on just the first wave of the sale alone, lasting six or seven days, according to a credit card executive who has partnered with these e-commerce players. 

One can argue that these events are just for a few days, while deals like those with Apple last the entire year. But the throughput that happens during these sales is something that banks can’t afford to ignore. For many retailers, especially in the jewelry and consumer durables segment, anywhere from 40–65% of annual sales happen during this festive period, the credit card executive added. 

HDFC Bank, though, is coming back to Flipkart at a time when e-commerce growth is slowing https://economictimes.indiatimes.com/tech/technology/ecommerce-carts-half-full-as-mass-market-crowds-thin/articleshow/108923525.cms?from=mdr, and this is best felt in the amount of effective discounts being offered. 

“The effective rate of discount offered used to be 8–8.5% about three–four years ago, but now discounts have fallen to 7–7.5%,” the executive noted. 

What is also a strike for both e-commerce companies and banks this time around is that two of the biggest festivals in terms of consumer spending—Diwali and Durga Puja–are clustered around the same month, October. If they had been spaced across two months, then there would have been two pay cheques for people to spend out of, the executive pointed out. 

In any case, HDFC Bank is likely hoping the sale will put the shine back on its share of credit-card spends, which had fallen https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/money-and-banking/top-4-credit-card-issuers-lose-market-share-in-fy24/article68128902.ece as of March 2024, as shown in this chart compiled by Hindu BusinessLine https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/money-and-banking/top-4-credit-card-issuers-lose-market-share-in-fy24/article68128902.ece.

What else caught our eye

Moneyview turns unicorn, acquires payday loan app Jify

The lending fintech’s valuation now sits https://m.economictimes.com/tech/startups/fintech-lending-platform-moneyview-acquires-fintech-startup-jify-attains-unicorn-status/articleshow/113299253.cms at US$1.2 billion after it raised US$4.6 million from Accel India and Nexus Venture Partners in its latest funding round, according to a report by The Economic Times. Moneyview has also acquired Jify, a cash-advance app for employees, through a share swap agreement with Accel and Nexus Venture Partners, which are both existing investors in the payday loan platform.

Sebi to enable T+2 bonus share trading from October

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (Sebi) will cut down https://www.livemint.com/market/bonus-share-issue-sebi-to-fast-track-trading-process-under-t-2-timeline-from-october-1-11726493058566.html the time it takes for bonus shares to be credited from the existing two-week timeline to two days from the record date (T+2), starting 1 October. This will bring the trading of bonus shares in line with regular equity shares, where T+2 settlement has been the norm for a while now.

Indian Bank raises US$600 million via long-term infrastructure bonds

Public-sector lender Indian Bank has raised https://www.thehindu.com/business/indian-bank-raises-5000-cr-through-issuance-of-bonds/article68642006.ece Rs 5,000 crore (US$600 million) through the issuance of long-term infrastructure bonds. The bond issue was oversubscribed nearly 3X, with the bank receiving 88 bids amounting to Rs 13,680 crore (US$1.6 billion).

Mahindra Finance expands mortgage business

The board of Mahindra and Mahindra Financial Services has decided https://www.business-standard.com/markets/capital-market-news/mahindra-finance-hits-52-week-high-after-board-oks-to-expand-moartgage-business-124091300924_1.html to expand its mortgage business, with plans to enter into housing finance, home renovation loans, and lease rental discounting. The company’s stock price hit a 52-week high on Friday following the announcement.
