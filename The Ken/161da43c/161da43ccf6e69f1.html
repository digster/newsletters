Good Morning Ishan,

A few years ago, Cipla’s then chairman YK Hamied received a call in the middle of our meeting in Mumbai. It was a pharma company wanting to get acquired. Hamied told me he got one such call every week. The spate of pharma consolidation has intensified since. (My sense is it will continue without big fish getting bigger.)
 
Now, hospital group Max Healthcare’s CXO tells Ruhi he gets one acquisition offer every fortnight. As many as 175 small hospitals have been put up for sale in just one year. Distressed small hospitals and impatient investors of medium (and large) hospitals are causing smaller ones to be swallowed whole by the big fish like never before. More beds mean better valuation. Well, TPG Capital, an investor in Manipal Hospitals, continues to chase a Fortis buyout even as the latter has a complicated ownership drama going on.
 
Even those that have gone public in the last three months gained scale by gobbling up smaller hospitals. For instance, Aster DM built one and acquired seven. (In a high-touch sector like hospitals though, sometimes you wonder if growth for the sake of growth—cancerous is the word—makes any sense.)
 
Anyhow, Ruhi’s story today captures the current dynamics: What is driving the fire sale? Who stands to benefit? What would the market look like in a few years? On to it here: https://the-ken.com/story/buying-building-indian-hospital-giants-get-bigger/
 
Have a nice day.

P.S. Personally, I am distraught at the new trend. Last week, I underwent an eye surgery (post-Lasik complication). I shopped around for the best price, researched for the best doctor and settled for a lesser-known single-doctor owned eye care centre. Insurance was pushing for a cashless transaction at any of the big hospitals but they were all ~30% more expensive, consumerist and couldn’t have cared less about my sundry niggling doubts. So much for economies of scale in big hospitals!
