Good Morning Ishan,
 
I doubt it's the general consensus, but with each passing year, birthdays become less and less of an occasion to me. Sure, I'm still on the right (?) side of thirty, but the wide-eyed wonder that accompanied birthdays when I was a child feels like a feeling from a different lifetime altogether. I guess there's something about age that has a jading effect, blunting the thrill that was once associated with such landmarks.
 
Curiously, for the first time in a while, I felt that thrill again this past week. The Ken turned two, and while I've only been a part of this journey for under half a year, it really got my blood flowing. Probably because, unlike birthdays, which have become dull and formulaic, everything at The Ken is still in its nascency. It is still dynamic. We're learning, experimenting, and evolving. 
 
It is an exciting time to be here, and the two-year mark was a great time to marvel at all that has been accomplished in such a short time. I confess I was one of the sceptics who thought The Ken was doomed to failure. I couldn't be happier to say that I couldn't have been more wrong.
 
Still, this week was no time to get caught up in the moment. We have a lot of new passengers on board this ride, thanks to our anniversary referral offer. So we were back at the grindstone, sharpening our story-telling skills to remind both new readers and old why we deserve to become your daily reading habit.
 
Our week started on Sunday, with the latest The Ken on Weekends (TKOW) offering. Sumanth and Pranav looked at Patanjali goods and realised they were marketing doppelgangers of products from other FMCGs. It's a free story, so do read and share it widely.
 
On Monday, India's tryst with electric mobility was under the scanner. If India's hoping for an electric vehicle revolution, Olina writes, it will be driven by two-wheelers, not four.
 
Tuesday's piece was an interesting one. Counter-intuitive, even. We examined Alphabet's venture capital arm CapitalG's investment in fintech lender Aye Finance. Why was a tech-centric VC investing in a fintech that follows a brick and mortar model? Arundhati has the answer.
 
On Wednesday, we turned two. A fitting occasion for our CEO Rohin to wear his journalist hat and examine the recently delivered Aadhaar verdict and what it means for India Stack. 
 
That's another thing about The Ken, by the way. We all write. CEO, editor, deskie, reporter, it doesn't matter. Yes, we're different like that.
 
That was followed up by a piece on Amazon's acquisition of retail chain More. Why would Amazon want a failing physical retailer? Sumanth shines a light on Amazon's plans.
 
Last week, we ended on beer. This week wasn't quite as heady. But it was certainly important. Pooja looked at the long-awaited IPO from the National Stock Exchange (NSE). Will it ever come, and what does the future hold for those betting on India's largest bourse?
 
That wraps it up for this week; I think it was a great one. To those who've just joined us, welcome aboard. To those who've been with us for a while, thank you for your faith. I hope to see you all at our third anniversary, and many more after.
 
Have a great weekend!
 
Cheers,
