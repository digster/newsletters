Do you live in Bengaluru, Mumbai, or Delhi? If you do, maybe you're in one of those pin codes where foodtech giant Swiggy has chosen to pilot its newest venture. In which case, you'll see this:
After concierge service Genie and grocery e-tailer Instamart, Minis is Swiggy's third big bet beyond food delivery since 2019.
Housed within the Swiggy app, Minis is an online storefront for small brands in baked goods, pet care, and jewellery, among others. Since May, Minis has onboarded 6,000 such brands.
On the one hand, Swiggy is taking aim at Instagram, which has been tapped by local brands to sell their wares. On the other, Swiggy wants to be an alternative to e-store providers like Shopify and Dukaan.
But unlike them, Swiggy doesn't charge brands to have their own page on Minis. What's more, Swiggy is dangling its 20-25 million monthly active users in front of young brands struggling to be discovered.
It's not all hunky-dory for Minis, though. For instance, a savoury-snacks brand that is on Minis has to spend a third of its order value on logistics.
