If startups were animals, Grofers would be a cat. A cat with nine lives. Except, lives would be pivots.

It’s a company we’ve covered before. Back when they were a hyperlocal marketplace, like Instacart. Then when they pivoted to an inventory-led model, like FreshDirect. They’ve competed with and discussed merging with Bigbasket, India’s largest e-grocer, in equal measure.

During all of this thick and thin, the one thing Grofers has stuck with is the groceries space. Now, in its latest pivot, Grofers wants to copy Aldi, the giant discount supermarket chain with over 10,000 stores across 20 countries. By catering strictly to “value-conscious customers.” Grofers calls them “motorcycle families."

With the tailwinds provided by a $60M funding round led by SoftBank, will this be the pivot that Grofers needed? Vandana reports in her story today.
