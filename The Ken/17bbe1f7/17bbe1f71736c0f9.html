"The tagline is - It's more fun in the Philippines. 
 
But I don't like that. 
 
A better term is - There's more love in the Philippines."
 
That's how popular YouTuber Nas Daily summed up the Philippines in one minute back in February 2017. Fast-forward to August 2021; the Philippines isn't showing him much love. But I'll get to that shortly.
 
Nas Daily or Nuseir Yassin is an Israel-born influencer living between Dubai and Singapore—and now running an academy in the island nation. His creator-focused venture Nas Academy is inviting funds from the likes of Lightspeed Ventures. US$11 million, to be exact.
 
Before he turned towards education, though, Yassin was focused on going around the world making one-minute videos that tell you a little something about different cultures, languages, and peoples. His channel, Nas Daily, has more than 40 million followers, including 20 million on its main Facebook page and over four million on YouTube. Yassin, at 29, claims to be worth US$5 million—a figure he breaks down in this blog.
 
Yassin's plan is to use Nas Academy to get influencers to teach aspiring influencers the ABCs of becoming, well, successful influencers. To do this, he's got a bunch of young, well-known entrepreneurs onboard, including India's Ritesh Agarwal, founder of hospitality company OYO. 
 
He's slowly building an edtech without traditional educators. And he's starting with what he knows best—the creator economy.
 
“We're trying to decentralise education. If you want to become a university, you don't need a building, tables, and chairs. And you don't need to suffer with marketing. You can build your own university on our marketplace,” he tells The Ken. 
 
Nas Academy's one true differentiator comes from the Nas Daily ethos of cultural plurality—to cater to non-English speaking people and in local languages. But this is where the academy recently stumbled in the Philippines, losing almost 600,000 fans and some influencer educators on the platform.
 
How's Yassin turning this bad publicity around, given that being localised is hugely important in the face of competition? Hear it from Nas Daily himself in Jon and Kay's piece today: https://the-ken.com/story/influencer-101-inside-nas-dailys-digital-harvard-promise/ (13-minute read)
