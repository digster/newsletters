"Alexa, what is Amazon Fashion's market share in India?
 
"Sorry, I'm not sure."
 
Not sure or too embarrassed to say? The triumvirate of Flipkart, Myntra and Jabong command 70% of the online fashion market. Amazon is a blip in the rear-view mirror with 21%.
 
Amazon has steadfastly disputed it is second best. In fact, it claims to be the largest, fastest-growing fashion e-tailer in India, based on site traffic, customer acquisition and other such metrics.
 
But fashion is not metrics. It is mood, personal, aspirational. Perhaps that's why it gets lost in Amazon's vast, commoditised, reviews-and-ratings-based one-size-fits-all website. Myntra and Jabong, meanwhile, offer a more touch-and-feel sort of experience.
 
"Prime works for day-to-day, private labels and low-cost fashion. You are not willing to waste time for a basic t-shirt, so you might buy a 3-pack of shirts. So you shop on Amazon, you know delivery will be good. But spending time researching for fashionable clothing is not something people do on Amazon,” says one analyst.
 
And fashion is a market that Amazon cannot afford to lose. Mobiles and accessories command nearly half of all online sales now. But that lead is threatened. By 2021, fashion will overtake electronics, according to one study.
 
Amazon has tried everything—brand partnerships, private labels, offline partnerships, advertising. Not that it has not worked. It's just that its arch nemesis does it better, and stays in front.
