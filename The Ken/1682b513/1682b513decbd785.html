Angel Tax is stupid. Its very idea, archaic. Its implementation, anarchic. Its objective, dull, and so badly thought through that it is only worse than a millennial's plan to do laundry every weekend. There are better things that belong in a garbage can than talk of an angel tax in a tax office. But here we are…
 
...a crying shame of a step that had no business being put in action, but here we are, discussing it, because someone thought, my, my; wouldn’t this be clever! 
 
Using a legal provision meant to catch illicit money laundered by inflating share prices of companies when they are sold, income tax officers across India have been issuing notices to thousands of entrepreneurs over the past year. All of them have to answer one question: why is someone funding your losses? Why are your investors buying your equity shares at a premium when the business isn’t doing well? 
 
These are strange times. Those are worse questions.
 
Many entrepreneurs, over the last 3-4 months, have taken to social media to voice their anguish. There has been panic that the government—which had, in April 2018, promised not to take coercive action and impose the dreaded ‘angel tax’ on startups—is out to get them. 
 
Thoughts?
 
“I was like, how can this happen,” says Govindraj Ethiraj, founder of Ping Network, a digital video network. “This flies against every and the basic tenets of free-market principles we took for granted.”
