"It’s an Internet platform. It’s a holding company. It’s a VC firm. It’s all of them!"
 
Welcome to a brand new week, the last one of October. Today we’re talking about Info Edge, simultaneously the granddaddy of India’s internet and the poster child of its stock markets. And boy, Info Edge is killing it.
 
Its stock is up 46% over the year, and 14% in just the last three months. The cash on its books has multiplied 4X since 2014. Its operating margin is 33%, a curious oddity in an era where losses are seen as a sign of growth (“we’re investing for the future”).
 
And did I tell you it has significant stakes in two of India’s hottest “unicorns”? 13.6% in insurance aggregator Policybazaar and 27.68% in food platform Zomato.
 
Info Edge seems to be having its many cakes and eating them too. 
 
But times of great prosperity, wealth and happiness often hide deeper issues that are still only incipient. It takes a truly innovative and visionary leader to prepare for them. Is Info Edge that leader? There’s no reason it can’t be.
 
It has the cash. It has the leadership. It has the brand. It has tech platforms. 
 
While Naukri, Info Edge’s cash cow and the undisputed leader in India's job search market, is currently ruling the roost, it's on a weak wicket when it comes to certain structural changes.
 
And can Info Edge pull off another multi-bagger startup investment in a market where angels, VCs and corporate investors are now in perpetual spray-and-pray mode?
 
That’s Vandana’s story today. I can’t think of a better way to start the week. https://the-ken.com/story/info-edge-various-faces/
