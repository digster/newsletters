Good Morning Ishan,

Growing up, I loved proverbs. The brevity and exactness of a good proverb can often help others mentally visualise the “big picture” behind a story instantly. My colleagues at The Ken know (and humour) me as someone who’s always got an opportune proverb in Hindi, Malayalam or English for most situations.

I tell you this because the first thing that came to mind after reading Arundhati’s story on PhonePe and its parent Flipkart and its parent Walmart was, "एक म्यान दो तलवार” That’s a Hindi proverb which translates to 'two swords rarely fit into one scabbard'. 

One of the two swords I’m talking about is Flipkart, India’s leading e-commerce website, which was acquired by Walmart in May 2018 for over $16 billion. The other one is PhonePe, a nascent digital payments app Flipkart itself “acquihired” back in 2016 for $120 million in stock. $120 million was less than 1% of Flipkart’s valuation back then.

Today, that acquihire has grown to become a second sun within the Flipkart solar system, with Walmart reportedly planning to spin it off into an independent entity worth approximately $10 billion. And awkwardly, it is beginning to even dwarf its parent Flipkart on many metrics. PhonePe has 65 million monthly users versus Flipkart’s 10 million. Or $100 billion in annualised transactions processed, versus Flipkart’s expected $17.6 billion gross merchandise value.

As a result, PhonePe founder Sameer Nigam is today the only real equal to Flipkart CEO Kalyan Krishnamurthy. “Sameer and Kalyan have separate meetings with the board during their quarterly meetings with their own separate strategies, which they don’t have to share with each other,” said an executive. 

The investor deck https://cdn.corporate.walmart.com/5d/11/4968b4d745159149c8e8b0295a3f/walmart-flipkart-ir-presentation.pdf published by Walmart in May 2018, right after acquiring Flipkart, had a slide titled "Experienced and Committed Management Team with Strong Market and eCommerce Expertise” (slide 12). There were 4 people listed. Only two remain. 

Kalyan Krishnamurthy and Sameer Nigam.
