"Let’s say you are the founder and CEO of a startup and you have now been at it for four years. The company is doing great, you’ve raised several rounds of financing, you have a product in the market that is solving a real problem, you have a bunch of customers, you have a growing team, and things are stressful but largely great.”
These are the opening lines from a recent, sharp post by venture capitalist Fred Wilson, titled, "What Happens When A Founder Is Fully Vested?”. But they could just as easily describe the remarkable events that led to today’s story by Arundhati.
A few weeks ago, Facebook-owned WhatsApp announced the appointment of its first India head (and the first head of a country WhatsApp unit outside of the US), Abhijit Bose. By all accounts, Bose is a capable, experienced and sharp executive, having spent time in both the US and India in both executive and entrepreneurial roles. With a salary package that is estimated to range between $2.5-3.5 million (including Facebook stock) and an existing user base of over 200 million Indians, it’s a role that many would kill for.
Or as Bose puts it, “When WhatsApp says let’s just meet, you meet.”
But here’s the thing—Bose was the co-founder and CEO of a high-profile tech startup that had raised over $50 million in funding from really sharp VCs, Ezetap. And the news of his appointment took his co-founders, employees and investors by surprise. Because it’s not often that the co-founder and CEO of a growing, VC-funded company decides to suddenly leave for a better-salaried opportunity.
Ezetap’s largest investor is Chamath Palihapitiya-led Social Capital, which owns an estimated 30% stake. For Palihapitiya, Ezetap was the first shiny bet on India and the burgeoning payments market. He saw it as the coming together of the capabilities of three superstar businesses—Payment gateway Stripe, cloud behemoth Amazon Web Services and Google’s operating software Android.
Bose’s sudden exit places Ezetap in a precarious position, as it must now convince newer investors, employees and customers that its founder-CEO left it simply for a better job.
