Good Morning Ishan,
 
You make $50 million worth lemonade, of course. If you’re Paytm*, that is.
 
In July 2015, TCFKAZ (The Company Formerly Known As Zovi) raised a massive $50 million funding round to build a hyperlocal deals platform for restaurant meals, movie tickets, spa visits etc. The company was called Little App, and the lead investor was Paytm.
 
A month later, Groupon sold a controlling interest in its India business to venture capital firm Sequoia and its local management. In return, Sequoia would invest close to $20 million in the new business called Nearbuy. Like Little App, Nearbuy was a hyperlocal deals service.
 
Last week, it turned out the two companies were being merged and assimilated by TITHMCP (The Incredible Transaction Hungry Machine Called Paytm).
 
Why would Paytm, despite its hunger for e-commerce transactions at scale, buy two overlapping deals startups? Especially when its own deals transactions are estimated to be nearly 10 times that of both Little App and Nearbuy put together?
 
One word: lemonade.
 
Through a clever sleight of hand, Paytm has not only salvaged its investment in Little App but also ended up owning a majority stake in two of the largest deals startups in India.
