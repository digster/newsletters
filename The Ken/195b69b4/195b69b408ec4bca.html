If you have lived in Mumbai for a while, chances are you have had Apsara Ice Creams’ guava ice cream with chilli salt.

Apsara is next only to Naturals among popular ice-cream chains in the city, with 57 stores in Mumbai and an even 100 across the country. But Kiran Shah, the son of one of Apsara’s founders, knew that if he wanted to build an ice-cream brand his way, he had to do it from scratch.

So, in 2022, he launched Go Zero—ice creams sweetened with stevia instead of sugar. If Apsara is for the masses, Go Zero, which recently raised Rs 30 crore in venture capital, is squarely aimed at calorie counters.

Shah and the heir to the Medimix fortune, who launched a luxury Ayurvedic brand, are among a growing group of scions choosing to strike out on their own, interestingly, in the same business that is the source of their family’s wealth.

They have broken with convention while also tapping the best of what the older brands have to offer, as Aakriti writes in her story today.
