Good Morning Ishan,

Last year, Kunal Bahl and Rohit Bansal were nominated in the “Midas touch” category in one of the many awards handed out like candy by Indian news organisations. This happened around the IPO of Mamaearth, a company they had backed since 2017.

More recently, the founders of Snapdeal were celebrated for their “200X” exit from Urban Company. Then there’s the imminent listing of Unicommerce, an e-commerce SaaS company in which Bahl and Bansal are promoters.

It’s quite tempting to wax eloquent about the duo’s investing chops, even when all the headlines are about a few of the 270 companies they have bankrolled.

A senior executive at Titan Capital, Bahl and Bansal’s firm, said last year that four in 10 startups it backs manage a Series A fundraise within two years. But that’s no different than the norm.

With Titan launching a US$30 million “Winners Fund” and raising money from external investors for the first time, this question is more relevant than ever: are Bahl and Bansal special in a way their peers are not? Let’s get to Aakriti’s story to find out.
