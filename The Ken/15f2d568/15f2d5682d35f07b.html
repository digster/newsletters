While I was reading my colleague Venkat’s story, I couldn’t help but feel a little embarrassed about how I’ve lost touch with Hindi. I have never been great with languages, but Hindi I knew. To read. Write. Talk. Over last decade or so though, I've been completely out of touch. And it has reached bizarre proportions; so I get quite a few forwards or messages in the family WhatsApp groups which are in Hindi. I almost never bother to read them. I don’t know why but I’m like, I don’t want to make that effort. And I think I’ve faltered reading full paragraphs in Hindi before, so yeah, that doesn't help.  
 
Needless to say, India is changing, as far as languages and the Internet are concerned. To quote from Venkat’s story today: Nearly 99% of Indian language users in India (also identified as non-English users), access the internet from their mobile phones. A Google-KPMG report further underscores the dramatic growth in India’s language internet user base, which increased 5X from 42 million in 2011 to 234 million in 2016. In contrast, India’s English internet user base stood at 175 million in 2016. There’s more to come; the language user base is expected to grow to a number as precise as 536 million in 2021.
 
This is an opportunity. And companies want to be in on it. Companies like Amazon, Alibaba and more. But on top of that list is Google. Of all the companies, Google is seriously working towards reaching out to this second wave of Internet users. That’s Venkat’s story for today.
