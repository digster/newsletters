I’m old enough to have used mobile phones that relied on the first generation of SIM, back when it actually made sense to call them “SIM cards” (that’s the 90’s). You know why? They were literally the size of a credit card!
 
Then, they started shrinking. SIM cards —> Mini-SIMs —> Micro-SIMs —> Nano-SIMs. At just 2% the size of SIM cards, nano-SIMs were about the smallest we could get.
 
Naturally, the logical progression was to make them disappear altogether. And so they did. In 2013, the cardless SIM - the eSIM - was introduced. Apple adopted it into its iPads the very next year.
 
Then, in September last year, Apple decided to embed them into the latest generation of its smartwatches, the Apple Watch Series 3. 
 
Hell broke loose in Indian telecom last month. Because in April, Apple formally launched the Apple Watch Series 3 in India, in partnership with telcos Airtel and Reliance Jio.
 
Jio complained to Indian regulators that Airtel’s implementation of eSIMs was a threat to India’s national security. Airtel said Jio’s allegations deserve to be completely ignored and that it should publicly apologise for making them.
 
(Those two need to get a room.)
 
But behind the scenes, both of them were lobbying with regulators to use Apple Watch’s eSIMs as a tool to grow the IoT (Internet of Things) era. eSIMs allow for more seamless machine-to-machine (M2M) data communications, sans cumbersome SIMs. 
 
An era where “smart” ACs, washing machines, TVs and fridges will be able to communicate with each other, and perhaps us, too. 
 
I’m trivialising things, but Shashi isn’t. His story today looks at the wedge that the Apple Watch’s eSIM has driven into Indian telecom regulations and the potential market opportunities that lie with its widening. Intelligent VoIP calls. Automated IVRs. Intelligent logistics tracking. Driverless cars. Smart cities. Flying chariots.
 
IoT could be a $9 billion opportunity within India in two years. Read Shashi’s story to understand how - https://the-ken.com/story/apple-watch-esims-iot-m2m/
 
I would like to end with a famous quote. "Do not try and miniaturize the SIM, that's impossible. Instead, only try to realize the truth...there is no SIM."
