Do you know the story of the Ugly Duckling? A quick recap, if you don’t—it is a fairy tale about a duckling that is mercilessly teased by other farm animals because it does not look like its siblings. It is considered ugly. One day, it sees a flock of swans and, much to its delight, realises it was a swan all along. The gorgeous bird then takes flight with the rest of its new family.
Last year was when Indian SaaS (software as a service) companies shed their “ugly duckling” tag and stepped into the limelight that hitherto was reserved for their more feted Indian peers.
Or as Sumanth, the author of today’s story, puts it: For Indian SaaS startups, 2019 was Annus Mirabilis–a “year of miracles”. A perfect storm of ingredients, along all dimensions—markets, capital, strategy, macro-trends—provided a platform for Indian SaaS companies to thrive like never before.
A recent study shows that 2,300 B2B/SaaS startups mushroomed in India in the last five years. Beyond behemoths like Freshworks, Zoho and Druva, there are nearly two dozen SaaS companies that have scaled beyond $10 million in ARR (annual recurring revenue).
Despite that, the combined revenue of all Indian SaaS startups is under 1% of the global pie. But it’s the start of a new year—a time of hope, optimism. The 1% then, rather than a dot, represents room for growth.
Will 2020 be that year of banner growth? Will the swans take flight? Sumanth takes a look at what this year has in store for India’s SaaS companies.
