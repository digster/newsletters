Good Morning Ishan,

I can’t remember ever going to museums. Even a museum. Not in the first 18 years of my life.

I know how that sounds, so I called mum to be doubly sure. Do you remember taking me to a museum? Anything, with family? Anyone? Nope, she said. Can’t remember a single visit. All that this mum and son duo could recollect is countless visits to temples and Victoria Memorial in what used to be Calcutta. And is now a blue and white charade called Kolkata. But, we were always outside. In the lush, green grounds. Perfectly timed between 5-7PM, when it's neither too hot nor too dark. Seated on a colourful bed sheet. Food and sandwiches strewn around, wrapped in newsprint. And a frisbee. Yeah, we had that. I remember it being red…

Now you can imagine with such upbringing, I overcompensated for it when I moved out of home.

I’ve been to some museums and then some more. But I can’t remember talking about this. Visits to museums rarely come up in chats. It isn’t bar talk. It isn’t work talk. And those are the two kinds of talks I do a lot. Which is why, when my colleague Roshni started talking about a gold tiffin box, studded with diamonds, worth a few pretty crores, one which had been stolen from Hyderabad’s Nizam Museum, I was all ears.   

“It is not just that. The story gets more interesting. The thieves were eating in the gold tiffin.” That’s Roshni. “And they were living it up in a luxury hotel.” It is a most fascinating story. How the Nizam’s Museum was robbed by Mohammad Ghouse Pasha and Mohammad Mubeen. How they stole the artefacts and travelled to Mumbai to try and sell them, and then failed remarkably. Except this lone heist story opens a Pandora's box, of the ridiculous state of affairs of India’s museums. 

And that ridiculousness starts with security. 

India has no official guidelines regulating the security of our cultural heritage. When museums are woefully understaffed or have untrained guards, it’s a cakewalk for anyone interested in taking a piece. The Indian Museum, 204 years old, India’s oldest museum, is mixed up in all sorts of controversies. From corruption to an alleged kidnapping. 

That’s our story for today. A piece on the state of Indian museums, and why they need our urgent attention.
