Good Morning Ishan,
Passive funds were finally poised for their big moment. And upstart fund houses like Navi, Zerodha, and Groww thought they were perfectly placed to shake up the market.
After all, the case for passive investing seemed airtight. Over the five years leading up to June 2024, a whopping 88% of large-cap active funds and nearly half of mid- and small-cap active funds underperformed their benchmarks. “Why would investors keep paying fat fees to fund managers who couldn’t even beat the market?” goes the logic. Fair question.
If passive funds in the US could surpass active funds in assets under management for the first time ever last year, why couldn’t the same happen in India?
But the passive-investing revolution hasn’t gone as planned.
Besides, it’s not the would-be disruptors who are winning the game but legacy fund houses like ICICI Prudential, Kotak, HDFC, and Nippon. Largely thanks to the preferences of institutional investors and mutual-fund distributors.
Forget that, passive funds themselves haven’t exactly taken off. Their share of the mutual-fund industry was a lacklustre 16% in 2024—and that was actually a drop from the previous year. No wonder many new passive fund launches have struggled to raise even a few hundred crores.
Archishma’s story today is packed with numbers that explain what’s happening—and reporting that deciphers why. [Click here to read on](https://the-ken.com/story/zerodha-navi-wanted-a-passive-investing-revolution-nope-said-older-fund-houses/).
When the Bajaj Group bought out partner Allianz SE’s 26% stake in its insurance business, it put a spotlight on Bajaj Holdings—the corporate parent who has acquired a fifth of the insurers. The company is truly a Matryoshka doll containing several dolls within. It’s the listed holding company of Bajaj Finserv, itself a holding company of Bajaj Finance, which in turn is the promoter of recently-listed Bajaj Housing Finance. Such holding companies are usually valued lower than the combined market value of their subsidiaries—at a conglomerate-discount if you will. But Bajaj Holdings has bucked the trend, outpacing three major companies under it in the past year and five years.
In this week’s Long and Short, Seetharaman explains why that is.
