Good Morning Ishan,

I am old enough to remember that if you were a young, fresh-out-of-college kid looking for a decent place to stay in Bangalore, there are two distinct eras - pre-Nestaway and post-Nestaway. Back in 2006, when I had just moved here, my experience with house-hunting was like this: 

1. Search for a real-estate agent in your neighbourhood. 

2. Meet real-estate agent in his office, which is actually an office cum xerox shop cum internet cafe cum unisex hair salon.

3. Ask to see a house in your preferred locality. Be taken on a bike halfway to Hyderabad, or as the agent insists on calling it - ‘Outer Koramangala’. 

4. Enter the prospective house, which lies somewhere between ground and first floor - depending on the weather, via a ladder that had ambitions of becoming a staircase someday. But remain optimistic because house appears to have a spacious shoe-rack. 

5. Find out that the shoe-rack was actually the living room. 

In 2015, Nestaway, a Bangalore-based startup looked at this situation, quickly figured out that it was broken, and decided to solve it. By working with house owners directly and by building a trustworthy brand, it offered a solution to thousands of people seeking reliable accommodation without having to spend an exorbitant amount of time, money or both. 

It worked. While others tried and failed, in a few short years, it managed to taste success. Fast-forward to 2018, Nestaway is now in eight cities, has 1200 employees, clocks an annual revenue of over Rs 25 crore, and has diversified to providing accommodation to families as well. Investors were buoyed by its success and pumped $45 million into the company last year. The mandate was clear - go and win the market. 

But now there’s a new contender. As my colleague, Vivek writes, "Seeing the scope of scale in the shared rentals, Oyo, which raised nearly $1 billion from Softbank in September, is stepping up to the plate, with its investment into a vertical called Oyo Living. Could the entry of a well-capitalised company in this space upset Nestaway’s plans?"

Amarendra Sahu, the CEO of Nestaway, is unhassled. He has a target. He even has a magic number. Today’s story is about Nestaway’s path to that magic number, its significance, and what lies ahead for it to get there.
