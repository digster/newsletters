There’s a joke about a gambler who is new to slot machines. They ask an attendant to explain how the machines work. The gambler is shown the seat, where the money is to be put, and the button to spin the reels. Then, the gambler asks, “So where does the money come out?”

The attendant dutifully replies, “The ATM.”

Even though the odds are against them, slot players have their own rituals and grammar. It’s something that I never really spent time trying to delve into. That’s why I found today’s story by Katrina to be fascinating.

She tracks the way casino suppliers are taking the Philippines more seriously, specifically by looking at how the slot machines have evolved.

For companies that develop these machines, it has become worthwhile to customise them for Filipinos and offer bigger jackpots—there’s immense demand and it’s paying off to rework the economics behind this mode of gambling.

Katrina’s report is peppered with factoids that demonstrate the popularity of slot machines in the Philippines, such as how they outnumber ATMs in the country.

And soon, the Philippines may give Macau a run for its money by becoming a shiny destination for international visitors who want to try their luck on casino floors.
