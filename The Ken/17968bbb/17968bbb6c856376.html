Good Morning Ishan,
 
Earlier this year, when traders on Reddit decided to take video game retailer GameStop on the mother of all bull runs, it was seen as the YOLO bet of a lifetime. Over just two weeks, the stock surged over 1,500%, reaching a peak of $483. 
 
The worry during the initial stages of the GameStop buying frenzy was that the stock would eventually crash, wiping out many investors who were late to the party. The crash eventually came, with GameStop stock falling to as little as $44 mere weeks after its stratospheric highs.
 
The #GameStopToTheMoon saga, however, seems like positively prudent investing compared to the world's latest YOLO bet, Dogecoin. Since the start of 2021, Dogecoin's value has risen over 150X, taking a cryptocurrency with next to no commercial applications and shaky foundations to a valuation of around $90 billion. 
 
The Dogecoin rally shares a protagonist with the GameStop saga—Tesla CEO Elon Musk. Musk tweeted in support of the GameStop short squeeze and has repeatedly fanned the flames of the Dogecoin frenzy. With Musk's cult following extending to India, too, the value of Dogecoin transactions on just 8 May—the day before Musk was scheduled to appear on Saturday Night Live—is estimated to have crossed Rs 2,000 crore ($272 million) across the country's crypto trading platforms.
