I hope you had a great time this Diwali. I returned home this year after missing about three year's worth of celebrations and found myself smiling at the little things that accompany the festive season.
As a household of frantic cleaners, we often spend our off-time dusting the peskiest of corners, and that continued till the eleventh hour this time around as well. While I usually detest loud noises and especially bright colours, I made an exception to sit outside a little and soak in some of the fireworks.
Though I would urge us all to cut down our love for pyrotechnics, that’s a story for another day, and for now I was just happy to see people stepping out again and enjoying themselves. Given the year and a half we’ve had, it was endearing to see people around me smile a little—albeit cautiously and underneath masks—in the hopes that the worst might just be behind us.
On that note, let’s look at some of the stories we featured this past week at The Ken. All of them, in a way, speak about new beginnings—forced, bumpy or opportunistic—but beginnings nonetheless.
On Monday, Munsif wrote about why short video app Roposo is [pivoting](https://the-ken.com/story/roposo-plays-a-live-commerce-game-in-inmobis-playground/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) to live commerce, and how that resets the calculus for its stakeholders. While InMobi and Roposo are pinning their hopes on the synergy between live commerce content and the lock screen service Glance, scaling the offering could prove tricky as competitors enter the fray.
While cybersecurity has really been on the back-burner at private and public firms involved in India’s electricity infrastructure, recent worries about sabotage by Chinese actors have shaken the regulators into action. On Tuesday, Pratap wrote a [detailed account](https://the-ken.com/story/china-fear-new-cybersecurity-rules-india-energy-utilities/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) of why new rules won’t be enough to get defences up to speed at power utilities, and will instead require fiscal support and better testing infrastructure.
Wednesday was all about Nothing (will admit, this has been a fun pun we’ve been making internally). Pranav wrote about how OnePlus founder Carl Pei’s earbud brand has fast [captured market share](https://the-ken.com/story/money-for-nothing-the-indian-piece-in-carl-peis-consumer-tech-puzzle/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) in India, and while it has started building an ecosystem of devices, acing that effort might just require it to build a smartphone.
We capped this week off with how new beginnings can get bumpy if your planners are lost in transit. On Friday, Bhumika [wrote about](https://the-ken.com/story/the-design-flaws-in-cash-rich-homelane-livspaces-interiors-promise/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) how interior design startups HomeLane and Livspace have fumbled their promise to customers even though they seem to be getting a lot of love from investors. With limited skilled labor to go around and competition heating up, the two early movers might have limited time to get their own houses in order.
Even though I’d love to take you through all the topics we covered in our newly launched [newsletters](https://the-ken.com/newsletters/), I’ll recommend you spend some of your downtime this weekend discovering them, if you haven’t yet given them a look. Just as a tease, this week’s line-up covered everything from how MakeMyTrip is trying to get around IRCTC’s monopoly on railway tickets to why Unilever’s Lifebuoy is doubling down on a simple idea—hand-washing is good hygiene, and they’re here to help with that.
With that, I bid you adieu. I hope you got to relax a little over the weekend, reply to the deluge of Diwali wishes flooding your inbox, and most importantly, soak in some of the smaller, joyful moments that make festivals special.
We’ll catch you on Monday to kick-off another exciting line-up of stories and newsletters.
Take care.
