Good Morning Ishan,
Duolingo vs Perplexity vs Unacademy vs language tutors vs smart glasses.
There’s a new language war playing out in India, the land of many languages, where the absolute number of (potential) users on any app can change a company’s profile.
In a banner year when Duolingo’s revenue is expected to cross $1 billion in 2025, the language-learning app faces the messiest competition in its fifth-largest market.
On one side are AI companies like Perplexity, OpenAI, and Google that never cared about language learning but are serving the purpose well. On the other side are Indian companies building culturally tuned, result-oriented models that go beyond gamified learning.
Even the struggling edtech, Unacademy, is testing a dog in this fight with Airlearn.
Older companies like Multibhashi, which pushes real-world use, and Preply, which blends one-on-one tutors with AI feedback loops, hope that humans in their AI loop will keep their magic on.
Carrie Wang, senior director and head of global growth at Duolingo’s english-test vertical, told The Ken that Duolingo sees “the AI shift as something as fundamental as the web-to-mobile shift”.
With Apple’s Airpods and Meta’s glasses inching toward real-time translation, the shift goes beyond the form factor, where learners will eventually reflect on whether they get a cognitive kick or a commoditised machine output.
