Good Morning Ishan,
When a company operating two Yamaha showrooms in Delhi with eight employees gets bids worth Rs 4,800 crore (US$572 million) for its Rs 12 crore IPO, you know the stock market is well and truly off the rails.
Just as baffling is Nalanda Capital partially selling its stake in 21 companies in the June quarter.
Why should an institutional investor wanting to make money in such a go-go market be such a big deal? It shouldn’t, except Nalanda is no ordinary name. With assets under management of Rs 50,000 crore, the Singapore-based fund house is one of India’s largest equity investors.
Nalanda has only invested in 39 stocks—including online classifieds company Info Edge, innerwear maker Page Industries, and electrical and home-appliances maker Havells—since its inception in 2007, and its market-beating returns have made it a sought-after money manager.
Plus, it certainly helps that Nalanda has a founder that a casual stock market observer may not immediately recognise, but who has an ardent following among fund managers.
Pulak Prasad, a former private-equity executive, has drawn comparisons with American investor Warren Buffett for his philosophy of being a “permanent owner” of companies it puts money in.
So what does the change in Prasad’s strategy tell us about the unprecedented bull run? Anand has an [informative analysis](https://the-ken.com/story/indias-other-warren-buffett-wont-stop-selling-while-everyones-still-buying/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
