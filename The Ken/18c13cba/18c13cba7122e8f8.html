Many times, clarity on what you want to build comes from not just belief in what you want to do but also conviction in what you don’t want to do. It helps build a good counterthesis.

For this type of clarity, you should meet Amit Lakhotia, who built three sizeable businesses, including Paytm’s* wallet business.

When this founder and chief executive decided to build Park+ in 2019, the access-control system that allows for entry and parking of vehicles, his list of things he did not want to do went like this:

He did not want to spend on acquiring and retaining users

He did not want an app to be the be-all and end-all of a digital journey

He did not want an error rate of even 0.01%

He did not want a DIY business

The biggest takeaway from his earlier experiences was that “somebody shouldn’t come and disrupt your business overnight”. So, it was important to build an internet business with strong offline and hardware elements.

That’s how in Gurugram, “every third” residential building with at least 200 flats and “every fourth or fifth” such complex in Mumbai uses its control system. To do that and more, Park+ has raised about $53 million from investors such as Peak XV Partners (formerly Sequoia Capital India & SEA) and Matrix Partners. It was valued at $340 million in January.

Seetharaman speaks to Lakhotia and lays out how he is going against the grain in a business, which people think is rather easy to build. Read here: https://the-ken.com/story/park-founder-once-built-paytm-wallet-why-does-he-now-avoid-the-purely-digital-path/ (7-minute read)
