Good Morning Ishan,
Clogs and mules became all the rage in 2020 or so, and they’ve only become more of a cultural phenomenon since then.
In India, Birkenstock, often referred to as Birks, has become part of the footwear rotation for those willing to shell out a little more for their special cork footbeds. Ever since launching in December 2019, the brand’s sales have doubled each year. By March 2024, its annual sales were estimated to be in the Rs 190–200 crore (US$23-24 million) range.
That performance—and the brand’s more recent acclaim—tells us the brand isn’t just capitalising on consumer preferences for comfort. For some buyers, Birks are a fashion statement.
In fact, the idea that Birks feel extra good under your soles is challenged by some. As Sahib Aggarwal, founder of Zen Barefoot, told The Ken, “Birkenstock is not particularly comfortable… It is a matter of brand play. For most people, I would assume it is more about associating with the brand that is cooler than the actual product.”
But maybe that only applies before they’re broken in. In any case, it has hardly stopped Birkenstock from slipping onto the feet of well-heeled Indians. Lookalikes are aplenty; they’re priced at Rs 1,000–3,500. But the real thing, with a price tag of Rs 4,000–19,000, got off on the right foot with its target customers and now commands a loyal following.
Considering that Crocs is 6X the size of Birkenstock in India, the German brand still has room to grow.
