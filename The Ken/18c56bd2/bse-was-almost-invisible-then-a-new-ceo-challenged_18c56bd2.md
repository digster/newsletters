Good Morning Ishan,
Who doesn’t love a good underdog story?
But what we are serving up today is a fascinating twist on that.
BSE, formerly the Bombay Stock Exchange, was a near-monopoly for a century—until the birth of the National Stock Exchange of India (NSE) nearly 20 years ago.
Since then, there’s been a role reversal. NSE dwarfs the erstwhile giant, with a 93% market share in the kind of stock trades most of us are used to and almost 100% in derivatives (futures and options).
Until recently, that is.
In November, BSE’s market share in derivatives rose to 4%. That’s a really huge deal for a company that failed repeatedly at challenging NSE’s dominance.
What’s more, the person making this happen spent two decades at NSE and was key to building its derivatives business. Since Sundararaman Ramamurthy joined BSE as its chief executive in January, its shares are up 350%.
As far as turnarounds go, this is right up there, and you’ll agree with me after reading Aakriti’s timely story.
