In 2013, when India’s best known and most successful of entrepreneur couples, Meena Ganesh and K.Ganesh, acquired home healthcare startup Portea from its founders, the entrepreneurial, VC and healthcare ecosystems all took note. Because the power couple’s reputation preceded them. Portea was Ganesh and Meena’s fifth venture, after notable successes in diverse sectors capped with the runaway hit TutorVista, an online tutoring company acquired by Pearson for over $150M*.
Investors and entrepreneurs spoke with awe about the Ganeshs' uncanny ability to seed, build, scale and exit companies in “hot” spaces. And not only was the $2 billion home healthcare market ripe for disruption, Portea wanted to expand the pie as well. “70% of visits to the clinics are unnecessary in India and we plan to disrupt this space,” said one of Portea’s founders after selling his company to the Ganeshs.
Sure enough, by September of 2015, Portea had raised $37.5 million in venture funding with a simple playbook: set up call centres, train people to take calls and requests for healthcare services, match demand with in-house or outsourced supply, and scale up before your competitors could blink.
Venture money chases growth. But often, growth chases venture money. Portea, too, utilised its cash pile to acquire multiple companies in the healthcare space, which allowed it to report 2.5X growth in 2016 and operations in 24 cities. Which enabled it to raise another $26 million in November 2017.
But there are perils in growing inorganically in untested, nascent and high-touch markets like home healthcare. The early signs might even appear trifling. A missed diagnostic report here, a delayed payment alert here, an unprepared nurse there. Home healthcare is not a space where you can “move fast and break things”.
Which brings me to Portea today. It faces a reality check. A moment of reckoning. Its largest customer, Manipal Hospital, is dissatisfied with its quality and customer focus. Its account books are so circular and littered with buyouts, write-downs and transfers that even experts couldn’t help us decode them. Portea, the company, is now a mere “brand”, while operations are performed by a holding company. It is virtually impossible to compare Portea’s growth journey from its financials, because last year’s apples are this year’s oranges.
