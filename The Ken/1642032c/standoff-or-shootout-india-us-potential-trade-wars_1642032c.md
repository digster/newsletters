Remember the celebratory atmosphere that accompanied the government’s decision to cap prices of stents and knee implants? Those sure were heady times. Affordability, accessibility, and other such buzzwords were sprinkled liberally across glowing pieces that praised the government’s initiative. Indeed, all of it was probably well-deserved; affordable healthcare is a fantastic idea.
If only things were that simple though. Instead, as the adage goes, the road to hell is paved with good intentions.
Thanks to the globalised economy we are enmeshed in, the government’s decision upset medtech companies in the US. Now, Uncle Sam is looking to retaliate on their behalf. Yes, we’re on the verge of a trade war with the world’s most powerful economy.
But trade wars are more about brinksmanship than an actual battle. More a standoff than a shootout. The Indian government seems to have understood this. It has set in place a Plan B, if you will, to both de-escalate the situation and also further its agenda of making healthcare more affordable. How is it going to achieve these two seemingly contrarian ends?
