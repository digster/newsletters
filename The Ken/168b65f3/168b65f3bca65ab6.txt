Title: A Harvest of Controversies

Actors: Kapil Sibal (of Indian National Congress fame. Also read: the opposition), Barkha Dutt (veteran television journalist looking for a gig to get back to network TV), Karan Thapar (another famous veteran television journalist, ready to get back in the game after a book), Bharatiya Janata Party (the party in power in India), Veecon Media and Broadcasting (a relatively unknown private company in Delhi that has launched a television news channel called Harvest), Harvest Television Network Private Limited (a very stupefied company, more commonly known as Harvest TV, a Kerala-based Christian devotional TV network) and the Information and Broadcasting ministry.

Guest appearances: Media licences. Promila, Kapil Sibal’s better half and a majority shareholder in a certain company called Analog Media Private Limited.

Plot synopsis: Harvest TV’s launch was pegged as an important one in the world of television news media. A political news channel rumoured to be backed by some politicians from the Indian National Congress, it launched just ahead of the upcoming general elections. It also marks the television comebacks of veteran journalists like Barkha Dutt and Karan Thapar. But after only a week of its existence, Harvest TV already finds itself knee-deep in controversy. It has received multiple legal notices and government complaints. There is a name issue, a logo problem, an unclear shareholding pattern, and an “alleged” association with Congress leader Kapil Sibal. Who really owns Harvest TV? And what the hell is going on?

Running time: 8 minutes
