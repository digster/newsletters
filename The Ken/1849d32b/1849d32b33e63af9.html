Good Morning Ishan,
 
The venture capital (VC) industry has been drawing flak for backing companies such as FTX and allowing the crypto exchanges’ financial missteps to fly under the radar.
 
But the heat of the critique is not drying up funding for VC firms—at least in Southeast Asia, not yet. Instead, Southeast Asia-focused VC firms have raised record amounts of money recently (potentially amounting to US$4 billion), to be deployed in the region in the coming years. Some VCs are even describing the soured mood as a “new golden age” for tech.
 
And it’s true, if you’re a VC.
 
Dita’s story today describes how the pace of funding has slowed in the region; despite the arsenal of dry powder, conditions for founders have toughened. If 2021 was a founders’ market for raising money, this year is not. The tables have clearly turned as they find themselves making compromises on valuation, and digging deeper to unearth the metrics that would catch the attention of VCs.
 
But deals are still happening. And so begins a new funding cycle.
