Good Morning Ishan,
 
An audit reckoning.
 
That’s what our story in June called Byju’s delay in declaring its financial results.
 
Byju’s had denied it then, and its auditor Deloitte chose not to comment. When we obtained the corporate filings yesterday—Byju’s sent it to select publications and avoided The Ken—we found ourselves vindicated. Revenue recognition indeed was at the heart of the story.
 
I’ll quote the auditor verbatim lest it gets lost in translation: Deloitte said that Byju's revenues from streaming services (the online courses it sells), which was previously recognised fully on commencement of contract, has been adjusted to be recognised rateably over the period of the contract. Along with that, the interest paid to loan partners on behalf of customers in respect of loans granted directly to customers have been reclassified from finance cost and adjusted against revenues, since these payments are in the nature of payments to customers.
 
Thanks to the above auditing changes, the $22 billion edtech company saw its revenue grow only 4%. Don’t forget, it was a year when Byju’s made head-spinning acquisitions and garnered head-scratching valuations. 
 
When we had asked the company in June about the filings delay, the spokesperson blamed a lack of audit bandwidth—after all, the company had seen a 5X scale in size.
 
Well, that size largely reflects in its losses—15X.
 
In our story today, Gaurav, Shruti, Arundhati, and Seetharaman team up to bring you seven things you must know about how the world’s largest edtech performed in the year ended March 2021. If a brand new ‘fee’ surfaces this year—contributing 14% to its revenue—an older ‘fee’ finds itself in a new place. 
 
As one founder of a global edtech said: “Revenue recognition is part of CEO training in all global companies…That’s because the CEO and the CFO can collude and engage in intellectual dishonesty.”
 
Still, thanks to Deloitte, we got to see the Diet-Coke version of Byju’s financials this year. We’ll get to the grand projections for the year ending in March 2022 when the audited results emerge; for now, read the FY21 story.
