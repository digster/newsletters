Good Morning Ishan,
You know that feeling when the entrées and mains fill you up, but you’re still drooling over the dessert menu? That’s Zomato right now.
On 22 October, the food-and-grocery delivery giant announced plans to raise up to Rs 8,500 crore (US$1 billion)—after over three years of having raised an almost equal sum as a fresh issue. This, when Zomato, with a market cap of Rs 2.3 lakh crore, ranks among the 40 most valuable listed companies in India. As of last month, it was already sitting on Rs 10,800 crore in cash.
Interestingly, it’s using qualified institutional placement (QIP)—a route often taken by capex-heavy sectors such as infrastructure, real estate, energy, and financial services. Well, Zomato, with its quick-commerce dreams, needs another growth engine in Blinkit. “If they don’t expand quick-commerce beyond the top 8–10 cities, the business will plateau like food delivery,” per the founder of an investment advisor.
Deepinder Goyal, the company’s chief executive and founder, calls it a move meant to ensure a “level playing field with competitors who continue to raise additional capital” and to “strengthen its balance sheet”. Indeed, it counts Swiggy, which is gearing up for its own public listing at nearly half this amount, as its nemesis in the food-delivery space, and Zepto, which raised a third of that sum in the last four months, in the quick-commerce category.
