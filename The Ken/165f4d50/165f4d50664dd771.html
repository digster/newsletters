Good Morning Ishan,
 
Something is happening in the retail industry in India.
 
That's a tall statement to make. But it's true. For nearly a decade, while other sectors were getting upended by India's great technology revolution, the retail sector shuffled along like a line at the checkout counter at a Reliance Fresh store. Slowly. Leisurely. Credit card machine not working. Next.
 
Very suddenly, all that changed. Earlier this year, Walmart acquired Flipkart. Then IKEA entered India. Closely followed by Amazon purchasing a stake in Shoppers Shop. And then, just yesterday, while we were filing our story, news broke that Amazon and Samara Capital were acquiring Aditya Birla group's More, India's fourth largest supermarket chain, at a valuation of Rs 4200 crore. All of this happened in the last six months.
 
Like I said, something is happening in the retail industry in India. 
 
At The Ken, we have been tracking developments in this sector for a while now. Today's story is another step in that direction. Interestingly, our story isn't about online players, or international behemoths, but on a very special Indian retail company called D-Mart.
 
If you aren't familiar with D-Mart, where do I begin? If D-Mart were a person, it would probably be a rockstar. A retail chain of nearly 160 supermarkets in several metros, it's a company that subverts all previously-held assumptions about retail firms. As Sidhartha writes in his story today, 'While the rest have struggled to stay afloat, D-Mart has only grown. Phenomenally. In the last six years, the company’s revenue has grown at a compounded annual growth rate (CAGR) of 38% and its profits have grown at a rate of 54%'. This hasn't gone unnoticed. Last year, when the company issued an IPO, it opened at nearly double its offer price. Since then, D-Mart's stock price has rallied by over 400%. Rockstar.
 
Our story today is about several things. What makes D-Mart unique? What gives this company its unique competitive advantage? And most crucially, with all the action happening in retail right now, what lies ahead for D-Mart? Can it sustain the edge? For how long?
 
Sidhartha has some interesting answers. With lots of beautiful, insightful graphs. You can read our story here: https://the-ken.com/story/dmart-dalal-streets-most-precious-beat-odds/
