Good Morning Ishan,
It’s a crowded local train—a swarming mob boards from Khar. Within seconds, the seemingly full bogey further accommodates the new crowd. And most, almost robotically, reach out for their phones to catch a glimpse of the ongoing cricket match score.
Passenger 1 (peeping into the other’s phone): Cricbuzz? Log into Cricbuzz, no? Let’s see the score.
Passenger 2 (adjusting his pinkie to hold the over-burdened handle above his head): Hmm, okay.
Logs into ESPNCricinfo.
Passenger 1: No, no, Cricbuzz. It has nearly double the viewership compared with Cricinfo.
Passenger 2 (in a dry tone): Hmm, okay.
Logs into Cricbuzz.
Passenger 1 (excitedly): Did you know Dream Sports just bought a minority stake in Cricbuzz and Willow TV for $50 million? I think it’s a great move.
Passenger 2: Hmm, okay.
Dhoni hits a four.
Passenger 3 (peeping in): By the way, Dream Sports owns Dream11. And Willow TV reaches over 5 million cricket enthusiasts in the US and Canada. It’s a match made in heaven. Can you tilt the phone slightly? I can’t see properly.
Passenger 2: Hmm, okay.
Passenger 1: Do you want to know why it’s a great investment but chances are it can be a difficult one to execute?
Passenger 2: Hmm, okay.
Passenger 3: Hang on. Head to The Ken for this. Gaurav has a [fantastically analysed story](https://the-ken.com/story/cricbuzz-may-give-dream11-real-money-gamers-but-times-internet-is-the-real-winner/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) about this.
Passenger 2 (looks up and stares at both of them): Couldn’t you begin with this information?
Logs out of Cricbuzz. Logs into The Ken and auto-deboards at Santa Cruz. Thanks to the swarming mob.
