Good Morning Ishan,

Since late 2016, the Indian telecom story has been full of cliffhangers. To the extent that people are no longer surprised by outsized outcomes—in losses, customers, investments, or plain bloodshed. It’s part of the game. 

The sangfroid may not last long, though. If Reliance Jio steamrolled competition in 4G retail services with a price war, it’s adopting a slightly different approach for the enterprise business.

Worth nearly $3 billion, the market is split evenly between large enterprises and MSMEs. Like retail, taking enterprise to a scale where “all stakeholders have a stake in ensuring your success”, is how a former competitor describes Jio’s enterprise entry. Since hiring Prateek Pashine, the former head of the enterprise business at Tata Teleservices, in 2018, Jio has built a 1,000-member team around him.

With a well-fiberised network and a business arrangement with Microsoft to embed Azure into Jio offerings, the telco plans to sell sachet services to MSMEs. While large businesses want to migrate their applications from their own servers onto the cloud, MSMEs are looking to adopt software and applications off of the cloud.

Where Jio will face stiff competition, however, is with large enterprises. Tata Communications Ltd (TCL) rules the segment with 40% of the market. It counts AWS, Apple, Accenture and IBM as its premier customers. Sources tell Pratap, the author of today’s story, that Jio has tried to bag a contract from Apple in the past.

Spooked, TCL has since been focused on getting its house in order. It is also looking for more synergies with Tata Consultancy Services. The two arms of Tata Sons, which previously competed against each other for domestic contracts, are now bidding for projects together. Bharti Airtel has also moved to steady its ship.

Jio may well get a backdoor entry into this segment if it buys enterprise assets of the bankrupt Reliance Communications. As Pratap’s well-sourced story today indicates, cliffhanger moments are here to stay.
