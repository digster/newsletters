Good Morning Ishan,
 
On the weekends, I like to unwind with movies, either new releases that I haven’t had the time for or good old classics that only seem to get better with each viewing. This weekend, I revisited The Truman Show, the immensely watchable Jim Carrey starrer about an insurance salesman who is leading a quiet, peaceful life on Seahaven island. Or so he believes, until he realizes he is the star of the greatest live show on earth, and that his entire life being watched by millions. The discovery forces him to reevaluate everything he thought he knew.
 
Most of our stories this week revolved around change, of established companies trying to grapple with change and coming out on top.
 
We began the week with Rohin’s story on SoftBank, post the WeWork saga. Even as WeWork’s $65 billion valuation plummeted to $10 billion, scrutiny turned to SoftBank and the fact that implied valuations based on SoftBank’s funding are ephemeral at best. Rohin looked at SoftBank’s Indian bets to see who would be most affected by its implosion.
 
Bajaj Finance might be one of the biggest players on the non-banking finance company playground, but it has struggled to crack the emergency care market even as smaller startups look to disrupt its way ahead, Ruhi wrote in our story on Tuesday.
 
On Wednesday, Olina and Vandana wrote about coaching behemoth Aakash Education’s plan to replicate its success online. Aakash has to contend with a changing landscape that has the likes of Unacademy, Vedantu and Toppr. Or as its CEO Aakash Chaudhry candidly said, “It's such a complex time. I'm taking new decisions everyday without really knowing how they’ll pan out.” This is our free read for the week, do share widely.
 
On Thursday, Sumanth looked at "disruptive startup" OYO's business model, which has recently faced the ire of several groups of hotel owners who are affiliated with it. They accuse OYO of employing "gimmicks and arm-twisting tactics" which has led to huge losses for hotel owners and has, in turn, disrupted the hotel industry. Certainly a change from the kind of disruptor OYO aspires to be.
 
We ended the week with my story on Tata Group’s e-commerce venture, Tata CLiQ, as it tries to change the way e-commerce is conducted and fulfilled. CLiQ wants to take advantage of its physical footprint by eschewing deep discounts and focusing on a niche market with curated products.
 
That's all for this week. If you are a fellow fan of the movies, let me know your favourite, must-watch films! Have a good weekend.
