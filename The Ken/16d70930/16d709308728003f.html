As much as PGK hates the word 'phygital'—and I've got to agree it isn't for the aesthete—, I must say the convenience of buying exactly what you want from a nearby story, guided by your smartphone, is rather swell.

This is what Tata is managing through Tata CLiQ, its e-commerce site. And while all e-commerce players are attempting their various phygital models and omnichannel plays (retailing across online and offline channels), Tata has the advantage of having its own offline brands. Without the need to tie-up. *cough* Amazon *cough*

There’s a flipside though. The biggest draw of e-commerce, thanks to the big players, is discounts. And CLiQ wants to stay niche and steer clear of deep-discounting.

Not just that, it also dreams of becoming profitable. And soon.

The first question, of course, is: Is that even possible? But the second question, which is the more interesting one, is: If possible, could CLiQ's model become a playbook for small-scale e-commerce success?
