Good Morning Ishan,

I know the question you all have in mind. Yes, I have watched Avengers, and no, I am not here to give any spoilers. In fact, I wiped out the memory of what I saw the moment I walked out of the theatre. Not just because I want my relationships to last with my co-workers and friends, but also because a part of me wanted to forget that this could be the end. 

I have always disliked endings. Last night with a friend who is moving out of town, last slice of that delicious pizza from my favourite restaurant, last day at a job I have worked for half a decade, saying goodbye to the family even when I know that I will be back in a few months. No matter how much we romanticise these moments, the pain is real.

As much as I detest it, things do end. The sun sets on days both good and bad. And the next morning, we have to wake up and find a way to live with some unfamiliar and uncomfortable realities. Sometimes, we try to move on and make way for a new present to take over and other times, we fight to hold on to things because we don’t know who we are without the familiar past. 

Oddly enough, it seems like Uber Technologies has experienced both in India. Uber, under its controversial founder Travis Kalanick, was here to win the market in 2016—but lately, the company driven by a new chief executive officer seems to be playing it safe. In fact, Ashish and Pradip say that Uber might be retreating from India. Read about India's diminishing importance in Uber's scheme of things here https://the-ken.com/story/ubers-retreat-from-india/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. 

If you ask me personally, I wouldn’t want to live in a world where there is just Ola, and no Uber but then, how long am I going to live with Indian cities running out of groundwater. While we seem to have found a solution in smart water meters that link price and consumption, Olina says this solution comes with a price, a huge one at that https://the-ken.com/story/mad-max-2020-smart-water-meters-solution/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. 

But it isn’t just the groundwater where the clock is ticking—India needs to switch to clean energy and (along with the rest of the world) cut CO2 emissions by 85% by 2050 to avoid disaster. Our contributor, Vivek Aithal, says that the current energy policy is inadequate when you account for truly liveable standards of power consumption in the future. Read his graphical narrative here https://the-ken.com/story/india-green-energy-charts/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter on what it really takes to go green. It’s a free read.

Meanwhile, the Indian healthcare system is dealing with its own endings and realities. On the one hand are the standalone in vitro fertilisation centres reeling from the impact of a bill banning commercial surrogacy that was passed by the Lok Sabha in 2018. However, Ruhi says that this might be a moment in the sun for institutionally funded chains that are moving on to other fertility treatments and rising from surrogacy’s ashes https://the-ken.com/story/surrogacys-fertile-ivf-chains/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter. 

On the other hand, Healthcare Global Enterprises (HCG), India’s largest private chain of cancer hospitals, is fighting for its future against the National Pharmaceutical Pricing Authority (NPPA), which capped the trade margin of 42 cancer drugs at 30% in February. Read Suraksha’s story here https://the-ken.com/story/hcg-is-fighting-the-govt-to-keep-cancer-drugs-costly/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter to know why HCG wants to keep cancer drugs costly. It’s also a free read, share it with your friends and family.

While all these companies and organisations are dealing with their fate as they know best, there is one company that is readying itself to fight Google. Very few in the past have done so, and fewer have lived to tell the tale. But Arundhati tells us that Reliance Jio is erecting a platform complete with an operating system, app store, apps and an ad network, much like Google’s Android, in the race to the next billion internet users. Read her story https://the-ken.com/story/jio-google-reach-next-billion-users-via-kaios/?utm_source=recap_sunday&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter to know about Google and Jio's three-legged race.

That was all from us this week.
