Good Morning Ishan,
 
I enjoy festivities. I think they are awesome. But with the festival that just passed us by that one half of my family indulges in, to delightful levels, I can be a slight sceptic. And this despite my namesake.
 
See, Durga Puja, all the fun, food, fanfare and feminism aside, is not exactly what it seems. Good vs evil is great. But what is good and what is evil also depends on who gets to tell its history. Same goes for Dussehra.
 
Don't get me wrong, it's a great time for revelry, for family and for that delectable dose of Bengali nostalgia, but it does remind one that multiple narratives are important - a theme our stories this week seemed to reflect.
 
Sumanth's piece on Mettl is a perfect example. A SaaS startup operating in the HR technology space, Mettl was recently acquired for by Mercer, a subsidiary of professional services firm Marsh and McLennan. For $40 million. Doesn't sound like much, right? Wait till you read the piece.
 
Jio has always been known for going big. It started its operations exclusively on smartphones, but Shashi's story tells us that it now wants to tap into the feature phone customer base. What does this mean for incumbents? Well, quite a bit.
 
Ruhi's piece looked at how health insurance is booming. But wait, India's largest TPA, Medi Assist, is not doing so well. Turns out the rules of the game have changed. But why? Read to know.
 
Our TKOW piece by Roshni also stayed close to the theme. Cacti love, or #succulove, is fast becoming the go-to decor piece among urban Indian millennials. And yet, a collective demand may pose a problem, she says.
 
Then there was Arundhati's piece on Alexa and its skills. On the face of it, the Amazon voice assistant is making all the right noises, but look closer and you begin to see how being tethered to voice skills can be tricky. 
 
Our free story this week came from Harveen. Turns out, the government wants public broadcaster Prasar Bharati to have its fill of the sports broadcast cake through Doordarshan. And make private channels foot the bill. Oof.
 
That was all for this week. As always, watch out for Sunday. But if you love the news business, then possibly, more than usual, I'd recommend you to log back in tomorrow. 
 
Have a great weekend, all.
