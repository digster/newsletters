As a recent NBA fan, I've been fascinated by the stories of athletes and teams who overcome odds to achieve greatness. One such story I came across was on Netflix's The Playbook, which told the story of the 2008 Boston Celtics through the eyes of the team's coach and former player Doc Rivers. Rivers took a struggling team, with one of the worst records in NBA, to becoming league champions in the course of a year, thanks to his adoption of the African philosophy of Ubuntu, often translated as "I am because we are". The philosophy played a key role in the Celtics coming together as a team.
The world of sports and entrepreneurship share interesting parallels. While each sport or company is unique, the tight boundary conditions and rules create a playground that simplifies the complex probabilities of skill, hard work, talent, teamwork—and luck, all essential elements for success.
