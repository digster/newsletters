The regulator has singled out four non-banks for charging “usurious interest rates, frivolous penalties” even as others are playing by a similar book

In the latest in RBI’s squeeze on non-banks, it has barred four of them lending activities on the grounds of charging “usurious interest rates and frivolous penalties”. But the regulator hasn’t defined any usury ceiling for lenders. Also, if the profile of customers were similar, big banks and non-banks would charge a similar rate. Non-banks charge high interest rates because they lend to riskier customers with poor credit history, have to borrow from banks at high rates, and have higher related expenses. Introducing a usury cap, limiting cost of funds for non-banks, and rationalising processing fees will help drive financial inclusion more cheaply and responsibly. Will the RBI bite these bullets?
