John Doerr, the famed venture capitalist, is such an evangelist for the objectives and key results (OKR) goal-setting system that he claims to have even used it to make sure he had dinner with his family often enough. 

Doerr—who refined and championed OKR after working with its creator, Intel's Andy Grove—introduced it to Google in 1999. The framework has since been a go-to for Netflix, Twitter, and LinkedIn, among others. 

Over the years, it has really caught on among Indian tech companies too. Razorpay, CRED, PhysicsWallah, Freshworks, and Lenskart have all used OKR.

But here's the truth: eight out of ten companies that adopt OKR fail to make it work, according to Sienam Ahuja, founder of consulting firm OKR Edge. 

For one, companies try to link OKR to performance appraisal, which it's not meant for. And OKR, given its focus on lofty goals and the specific results needed to achieve them, tends to discount business-as-usual functions and the people handling them. 

But OKR still scores over its alternatives, so the likes of mobile-adtech InMobi and cosmetics e-tailer Purplle are tweaking it in more ways than one.
