Good Morning Ishan,
 
It’s the last day of November, there’s something in the air, and I think it’s Kevin Bacon! 
 
The last 15 days have seen a flurry of senior exec appointments in some of the biggest names in India’s tech ecosystems. It’s the perfect time to play Six Degrees of Kevin Bacon all over again.
 
Kaushik Anand left CapitalG, formerly known as Google Capital, to join as-yet-unlaunched A91 Partners. A91 Partners is basically the Ocean’s Thirteen of India’s VC ecosystem (Ocean’s Eleven was Westbridge Partners, Ocean’s Twelve was Sequoia India, and A91 Partners is Sequoia 2.0) - an ensemble cast of former Sequoia colleagues come together for a new $~300 million sequel fund. The cast includes former Sequoia partners VT Bharadwaj, Abhay Pandey and Gautam Mago. Mago was the one at Sequoia who invested in hotel room aggregator Oyo.—>
 
—> Oyo is where Aditya Ghosh, the former CEO of India’s leading airline, Indigo, has ended up at after a 6-month break. He will head Oyo’s India and South Asia businesses and is being seen by many as the adult supervision for Oyo’s 24-year old founder and CEO, Ritesh Agarwal. Supervision installed by Oyo’s largest shareholder, SoftBank.—>
 
—> SoftBank itself gets a new head for its India operations, Sumer Juneja. SoftBank’s biggest bets in the Indian tech ecosystem today are Oyo, ride-hailing service Ola and payments platform Paytm. —>
 
—> Paytm’s key competitor for digital payments in India will be Facebook’s WhatsApp, which gets a new country head in the face of Abhijit Bose, formerly the co-founder and CEO of Ezetap, a B2B digital payments platform that had raised over $50 million in VC funding, including from Chamath Palihapitiya’s Social Capital.
 
2019 is general elections in India and all large tech companies, especially foreign ones, are wary about getting caught on the wrong side of the government or regulators.
