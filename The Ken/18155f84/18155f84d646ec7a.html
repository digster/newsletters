Good Morning Ishan,
 
How was your week? I hope it was better than the one I had. I’m writing to you after a week that was nothing short of a stand-up comedy set. Long story short: it involved two mess-ups I caused because I thought we were still in 2021, one broken pair of glasses, and one extremely overpriced plate of veg fajitas.
 
But it wasn’t all bad. At the end of it all, Ma made aloo parathas (flatbread stuffed with potatoes) with mango pickle and life was good. And I realised just how comforting aloo paratha is to me, as a meal. So, randomly, I began to ask my friends and colleagues about their comfort foods.
 
I was pleasantly amused at the answers I got, which ranged from moong dal khichdi (a dish made of rice and lentils) to biryani with aloo, sattu ka puri (deep-fried flatbread made of lentil flour) to red packet of Lays’ chips and dal chawal to koshimbir (a type of salad) with rotis.
 
These answers left me more curious. Which is precisely why I pose this question to you today. To satiate my curiosity about other people’s comfort foods. So do me a favour and write back to me with your comfort food?
 
Now that you've indulged my curiosity, let me quickly dive into a recap of last week's stories.
 
On Monday, Anand and Maitri wrote about the company that’s a front-runner in the race to acquire a minority stake in Metropolis Healthcare: Flipkart. They explore if Flipkart can do for Metropolis what PharmEasy did for Thyrocare in India’s increasingly competitive diagnostics space.
 
Shruti covered the Indian Energy Exchange and its aim to make the most of a growing demand for ‘green power’ and renewable energy certificates to boost the next growth phase.
 
Kay did a deep dive into the future of Zilingo, after its co-founder and former CEO Ankiti Bose got suspended due to alleged accounting malpractice. In its fight to survive, its software-as-a-service business could become a key part of the company.
 
Metro Cash & Carry is also similarly struggling to survive; the B2B wholesale company has a range of suitors, from Thailand’s CP Group and Reliance Retail to Amazon and Swiggy. But B2B wholesaling is a difficult business—despite everyone’s interest in the company, Metro Cash & Carry only makes sense as a minor cog in the online-grocery wheel, write Aayush and Seetharaman.
 
Seema closed the week with an interview with Rajashekar Reddy Seelam, the co-founder of organic food brand 24 Mantra. Its IPO may be stuck, but its growth timing couldn’t be better—organic farming is in the news. The company’s 18-year-old bet on the sector may yet pay off.
 
That’s it from me today. See you tomorrow with more stories!
 
Oh, and don’t forget to write back to me with your comfort foods. I’m truly and genuinely curious to find out different foods we have associated comfort with!
 
Until we speak again,
