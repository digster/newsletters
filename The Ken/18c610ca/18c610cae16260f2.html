With the festive season in India ending soon, bargain-hunting customers can be forgiven for taking a moment to catch their breath. Personally, I was looking forward to taking a break from obsessively checking sneakers on e-commerce platforms.

But India’s leading fashion e-tailer, Myntra, isn’t done yet.

Since July, the company has been creating back-to-back category-led sales events to generate more revenue from its monthly active users, which could be upwards of 50 million when discounts rain heavily.

This strategy, however, isn’t yielding the expected results. A few years ago, Myntra’s biggest sale events led to 5–6X the turnover than the other days; now, it’s just 2X.

This begs the question: At what point do discounts turn from a special offering into an expectation for customers? If Myntra’s sales strategy is anything to go by, that could have already happened.

The Mukesh Bansal-founded company has around 22 sale days a month, up from about 11 two years ago.

Amid this, the Flipkart-owned firm has been missing its revenue targets. Even sales of its superstar private-label brands like Roadster and HRX have declined.

While this can be explained by Myntra’s efforts to reposition itself as a mass-market player focused on tier-2 and -3 cities, that isn’t the complete picture.
