The pandemic has laid bare many things. The shortcomings in our healthcare delivery systems; the glaring inequalities between the privileged and the less fortunate; the generally warped priorities of elected officials the world over.

But it has also highlighted something truly remarkableвЂ”the sheer resourcefulness of the human race when its back is up against the wall. The Covid-19 vaccines, already being deployed in various countries, are proof of this. Developed in record time, they could save countless lives from the dreaded virus.

It isn't just about inventing something new. It's also about looking at what you already have and figuring out how it could prove useful in this fight. That is how Qure.aiвЂ”a Mumbai-based startupвЂ”added a new tool to the anti-Covid effort.

Four-year-old Qure's artificial intelligence technology specialises in scanning chest X-rays to detect nascent cases of Tuberculosis (TB). Trained on a repository of over 3.5 million chest scans from around the globe, it's able to throw up readings in just around a minute, with a high degree of accuracy.

With Covid also attacking the lungs, Qure decided to go from just recognising TB to trying to detect Covid cases from chest X-rays. According to the company, its algorithm detected up to 90% of Covid cases. An Italian study pegged its accuracy at almost 80%.

Already, it has been deployed at 15 sites across the country, adding another weapon to India's Covid detection arsenal. Maitri details this transition in today's story, our free read of the week.
