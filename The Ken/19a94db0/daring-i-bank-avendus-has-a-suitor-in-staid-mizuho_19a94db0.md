Good Morning Ishan,
Every investment bank has assets: clients, mandates, relationships, and Excel models. But Avendus has something more: a personality. A forceful, argumentative, fight-for-every-inch personality one can’t really put on a balance sheet but which definitely shows up in the valuation. Paired with a disciplined strategy of sticking with founders long-term, being picky about mandates, and doing unusually deep research, it helped Avendus advise 3X more private deals than the next seven competitors combined.
Which makes its latest development…fascinating.
Avendus is in the “final stages” of a deal with Japan’s Mizuho—a bank so large and so methodical that if you asked it to describe chaos, it would probably schedule a pre-meeting, align on the definition, and then decide not to proceed because the term felt insufficiently precise.
This, of course, raises a simple question: can a bank built on instinct and risk-taking survive being owned by one built on consensus and choreography? The answer is not simple at all.
Inside Avendus, the deal has stirred real concerns. Six senior executives were so uneasy they considered leaving. Will Mizuho’s famously deliberate style mute what made Avendus special? What happens when a business engineered for speed suddenly reports to an owner engineered for patience?
And yet, a Japanese owner might be exactly what Avendus needs.
KKR’s decade-long timer is up. A $115 million investment turning into roughly $700 million doesn’t wait for alignment. An IPO would have punished the firm, and private buyers weren’t exactly lining up to pay top dollar. Mizuho, meanwhile, brings deep relationships across Reliance, Adani, and Tata groups.
Valli’s engrossing [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/daring-i-bank-avendus-has-a-suitor-in-staid-mizuho-what-comes-next/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) is about a bank that spent 20 years asserting its way to market leadership, now being adopted by an institution that whispers its way across continents. It’s more than just a culture clash.
