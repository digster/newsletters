All consumers and most business owners will agree that competition is good, even essential. One company can learn from its rival's mistakes and strong points, course correct and plough onwards.

Keeping tabs on the competition, then, is a given. To know what they are doing right, or wrong, to anticipate their next move, etc. With the right data and proper insights, a company can find ways to play fair, yet give itself a potent edge.

Abinaya's story today is about just that. About how Flipkart, India's No.1 homegrown e-commerce portal, used reams and reams of data in 2019 to boost the number of products and merchants on its website. All to better compete with its top rival, Amazon India.

Only this data, in the hands of a special Flipkart team, wasn't on products it had on its website, but on products that it didn't. More specifically, this data was on specific products and sellers that were on Amazon, but not on Flipkart. Data on products in categories in which Amazon had a more extensive assortment, such as baby products and home improvement tools.

Nothing unique or illegal here. We just agreed that keeping a close tab on rivals is par for the course, and even important.

No, today's story is about how the goals of these teams of contract employees—internally called Feet on Street—became more and more Amazon-focussed through 2019. Of how, in the hunt to match Amazon's top products, the data reached extremely granular levels like 'adjustable safety-harnesses for babies up to 1.5 meters elastic wire - Blue.'

And how at the end of last year, these teams ended up creating a new compliance headache for Flipkart, and forced its parent, Walmart, to get involved.
