Good Morning Ishan,

Are you one of those green-fingered people? Do you have a way with plants? Good for you. 

My relationship with garden plants is like a scene from an Anurag Kashyap movie. Two people meet. A long pregnant pause follows. Then, suddenly and very violently, one of them dies. I have killed plants because I have watered them too much. I have killed plants because I have watered them too little. Once, one of my money plants, probably deciding that enough was enough, leapt off my balcony, and fell faster than the rupee-dollar exchange rate, shattering the windscreen of a neighbour’s car below. I quit my apartment complex WhatsApp group immediately and told myself - never again. 

Plants and I don’t get along. 

That’s when I found out about succulents. My colleague Roshni told me about them. They are cacti-like plants that can grow anywhere. You don’t even need to water them. You can put them in a corner, ignore them, and they will make your house look pretty. 

But that’s why I buy books from Amazon, I told her. 

This is different, she insisted. People are going crazy for succulents of late. Especially in India. There are meetings and associations, where cactophiles gather and discuss rare succulents. The rarest ones cost between Rs 20,000 to Rs 70,000. Everyone wants one. They are even smuggling rings for succulents in North America. 

No way. 

Yes way, said Roshni. I attended these events. I even went to meet a guy who calls himself the Cactus King. He wasn’t around because he was attending a succulent conference in China, but I met his assistant. Mind-blowing stuff. 

Is this for real, or is it a temporary fad - like vaping, Candy Crush Saga or the promises to bring back black money? 

No, no, she said. It’s taking off. Online. Offline. Everyone is buying succulents. Demand is sky-high. So all sorts of shady stuff is happening. People are even importing them. Regulatory-workarounds are being done. It’s very complicated. 

But why? Why the sudden surge in popularity? 

Simple. Instagram, she said. 

No way. 

Will you stop asking me questions and just read my story, she said. 

I did. It was amazing. My mind was blown.

Me, I am off to buy a succulent. We will try not to kill each other.
