Good Morning Ishan,
If Ola, Uber, and Rapido could take a ride together, the conversation might go something like this…
Ola: So… Rapido, huh? Heard you’ve been stirring up trouble in our territory. You know we’ve been dominating cab-hailing for years, right?
Rapido: Oh, you mean that dominance that’s down to 60–70% from over 90% just a few years ago? Yeah, I’m helping with that dip.
Uber: Pfft, 60–70% is still huge. You’re what, just taking some crumbs in four cities?
Rapido: Crumbs? Try 10–20% market share—largely from Bengaluru, Delhi, Mumbai, Hyderabad—and nearly a third of my US$1.2 billion gross bookings are already from cabs.
Ola: Cabs? Weren’t you just a bike-taxi service?
Rapido: Yep, started there. Now, I’ve…err…borrowed Namma Yatri’s subscription-based model for cabs. No hefty commissions for drivers like your 25–30%. That’s why your drivers are “dual-apping” with me—better deal, better money.
Uber: (chuckling) Oh, we’ll just penalise them for that. Problem solved.
Rapido: Sure, but it doesn’t solve your other problem: 30–40% fare hikes and longer wait times. Riders and drivers aren’t exactly thrilled with you right now.
Ola: (grumbling) Fine, but we’ve still got the bigger fleet… for now.
Rapido: Maybe, but you can’t afford to ignore the fact that I’m growing fast.
Ola: But. But. EVs… AI… innovation…
Rapido: You do that. Meanwhile, I’m already valued at over $1.1 billion, filling the gaps, and eating up your market share. What’s that old saying? “The bigger they are…”
Uber: (sighs) Okay, maybe we’re feeling a little heat. But can you keep it up? We’ve got scale and a premium user base.
Rapido: Maybe, but you know how it goes in this business—whoever gets the drivers wins. And I’m bringing more to the table with my lean, commission-free model.
—
