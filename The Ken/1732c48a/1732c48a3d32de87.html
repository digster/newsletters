If you scanned the list of startups that raised money in the past three to four months, I’m pretty sure you wouldn’t expect a news aggregator to feature on the list.
 
Not if you consider that it’s the rare user that is willing to pay publishers for news, leave alone a news aggregator. Or if you consider that Facebook told Australian regulators that news content is easily replaceable and had little commercial value. Or that Bytedance shut down its global news aggregation app, TopBuzz.
 
Now, what if I tell you this Indian news aggregator raised not one, but two rounds of funding, totalling nearly $60 million, since April?
 
If the subject line was not a giveaway, or if you still haven’t guessed, this company is Dailyhunt, a 13-year-old company.
 
Dailyhunt’s premise is simple—aggregate news from multiple sources in multiple languages to build a massive and loyal reader-base that it would use to draw advertisers.
 
Its secret sauce was regional language readers, those in the tier-2 and tier-3 cities. Users that a Google or a Facebook couldn’t reach. It worked like a charm then, and it is working like a charm now.
 
At least on the user front. Dailyhunt boasts 250 million monthly active users, who spend 24 minutes each day on its app, up from 14-15 minutes in 2017. And it has the capital.
 
But it’s the other two cogs in its business model—publishers and advertisers—that are not buying the promise.
 
Pranav’s story today is about the changed world that Dailyhunt is operating in; a world that has seen news aggregators like UC News and Newsdog sidelined due to the ban on Chinese apps; a world where Bytedance as an investor is a double-edged sword.
