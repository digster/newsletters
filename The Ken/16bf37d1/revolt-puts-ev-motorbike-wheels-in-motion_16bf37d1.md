Good Morning Ishan,
Once consumers have driven electric, they will never go back — Chetan Maini, SUN Mobility
We spoil the petrol vehicle experience for you —Tarun Mehta, Ather Energy
Two icons of EVs in India, separated by (almost) a generation, batting for it on the same ground—experience—on the same day this Saturday, in the same [newspaper](https://www.livemint.com/companies/people/once-consumers-have-driven-electric-they-ll-never-go-back-chetan-maini-1562950283364.html), in two different reports.
It isn’t surprising then why Rahul Sharma, one of the founders of smartphone maker Micromax, is counting on the user experience ensemble that his new venture, the EV motorbike Revolt, offers. To the extent that he has turned the noiselessness of EVs, their universal selling point, upside down. Why not. Bikes are synonymous with their signature sound. And India’s first all-electric motorbike lets you download a new bike tone every day.
Sharma’ phone to EV bike journey is seamless.
“The current auto industry is like a feature phone. The customer relationship ends with the sale. But the future will be like a smartphone, where the customer relationship startswith the sale,” says Sharma. The rubber will meet the road later this month when RV 400 goes on sale.
Today, Pranav (Shankar) and Rohin examine how Revolt is perched on the revved-up market of electric 2-wheelers, where the government is unrelenting in phasing out non-EV 2-wheelers by 2025. From 1% of the market, EVs will have to make up 100% in six years. Will the ICE bike makers, incurring higher costs due to higher emission standards, hasten the crossover? Most likely, yes. That’ll markedly change the game because they’ll come with plants, networks and cash, something EV disruptors may not have.
So far, Revolt is funded entirely through Sharma’s $70 million investment. He has ambitious plans; he wants the biggest piece of the pie. Consumers will eventually move away from the emotional benefits of owning an EV; economics and experience will be the differentiators. Bikes dominate the 2-wheeler market in India. Will Revolt jolt the others?
