Good Morning Ishan,

Let me start with a (potentially unpopular) disclaimer. I get cricket. I do not get the Indian Premier League (IPL). But I'm clearly in some minuscule minority here; because as far as I can tell from today's story, this IPL sure is Star India's golden goose.

Allow me to explain myself though. Capsule cricket. Bling jerseys. Branded teams. You'd agree it's a bit far from the real deal? (Who knew I could be a purist.) And yet, IPL has made it past Season 11, and its popularity is only increasing.

Star took a chance on it this year. It went all out. IPL matches were aired in regional languages, and in not one but four—Tamil, Telugu, Bengali and Kannada. Not just that. Star also aired IPL across 10 television channels, compared to five last year. And it looks like Star is reaping rewards.

For starters, it set a new record of 10 million concurrent viewers watching the final match between Chennai Super Kings and Sunrisers Hyderabad on the online video-streaming platform Hotstar. Besides, the ad revenues look rather pretty, too. In a townhall, Uday Shankar, chairman and CEO of Star India said that the company earned somewhere between Rs 1,700 - 1,800 crore ($252-266.8 million) in ad revenue from IPL. This is Rs 500 crore ($74.1 million) more than last year, just short of its Rs 2,000-crore target ($296.5 million).

All in all, Star is estimated to have earned somewhere around Rs 3,000 crore ($444.75 million) from IPL alone—an increase of 35% from the Rs 2,200 crore ($326.15 million) in the previous year. Isn't that crazy? I think that's crazy. Especially because Star wasn't in the best place. It made a total loss of Rs 1,002 crore ($148.5 million) in the financial year ending March 2016.

Harveen's story today breaks down Star's post-IPL numbers. And going by what she says, it sure looks like the times they are a-changin' for Star.
