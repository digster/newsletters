Symbiosis is just one of the fascinating aspects of the beauty of life on earth. Two different organisms, each with entirely different objectives and often from different species, decide to enter into a long-term relationship because doing so often improves their individual and collective odds of survival and propagation. 

Like clownfish that make the tentacles of sea anemones their homes, each protecting the other against their respective predators. Or fig trees that let wasps live inside their fruit, in return for the wasps pollinating them and helping them propagate.

Not all fig trees though. 

The strangler fig starts out by depositing its seeds in the crooks or branches of other mature trees in rainforests. Once the seeds germinate, the fig sends down its roots to the ground and starts sucking away water and nutrients from the soil that could have gone to its host tree. It then starts growing a thicker canopy and starts sucking away the sunlight meant for its host. As it grows stronger, its roots encircle the host tree and squeeze it. Till the host dies and the strangler fig is all that remains around a hollow trunk that was once its host.

I digressed, for effect.

Truth be told, the symbiotic relationship between the world’s most fearsome e-commerce retailer, Amazon, and one of India’s earliest department store chains, Shoppers Stop, seems more win-win than win-die. 

The partnership isn’t new - it was announced last year. But I would argue that it's only now that we’re in a position to understand why it matters. Because, as Vivek argues in today’s piece, both Amazon and Shoppers Stop seem to be in this relationship via rebound than first love.

Amazon hasn’t had much luck with the fashion vertical, where it continues to be the equivalent of a country simpleton when compared to the (now Walmart-owned) Flipkart trifecta led by Myntra (Myntra, Flipkart Fashion, Jabong). And Shoppers Stop squandered away its early lead, expanded over aggressively, and ended up picking the wrong horse for its own e-commerce gambit - Snapdeal.

But Vivek argues that Shoppers Stop is important because it marks the beginning of Amazon’s next phase in India.
