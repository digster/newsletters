And if media is crowded, spare a thought for those who inhabit the cut-throat edtech space.
A boom sector ever since the pandemic began, edtechs have mushroomed for every use case imaginable. The glut of competition has forced companies to constantly add new bells and whistles at every turn in order to hold the attention of students and investors alike.
It is this cycle of perform or perish that seems to have landed Unacademy-owned PrepLadder in trouble. The medical test-prep app stands accused of plagiarism for a new feature it launched in July. PrepLadder's accuser, US-based Medical Joyworks, claims that both the content and design of its 11-year-old app, Prognosis, have been copied by PrepLadder. The company has also approached Indian courts to take PrepLadder to task, and a protracted court battle appears inevitable.
In today's exhaustive investigation, Olina lays bare the facts of the case. The allegations are egregious. The evidence is seemingly glaring. Edtech executives, however, are quick to remind us that all this is surprisingly common. As the saying goes, good artists borrow, great artists... sometimes become product managers.
