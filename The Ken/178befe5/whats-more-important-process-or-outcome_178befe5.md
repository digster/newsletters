When the lockdown was first imposed in March last year, I reluctantly began yoga classes via Zoom. I’d never enjoyed it much, compared to kickboxing or dance fitness classes at the gym; yoga was too slow for my taste.
But circumstances demanded that I choose. Lazing around all day in front of the laptop or learning something new that did not need much? Over the next few weeks, much to my surprise, I ended up befriending the yoga mat.
It was hard in the beginning. According to my yoga teacher, I was so concerned about the outcome of my workout (a good handstand photo for my Instagram page), that what I was doing was, in fact, not yoga. “It’s all about the process,” she would tell me repeatedly.
Over time, her esoteric jargon—“trust the floor”, “accept where you are in your journey”, “surrender to the flow”—began to sound less and less weird. The process, as she had promised, slowly, almost imperceptibly gave way to outcomes. No, I’ve still not managed a photogenic handstand.
