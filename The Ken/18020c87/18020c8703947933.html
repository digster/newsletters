Good Morning Ishan,
 
Thrice a week, without fail, our neighbourhood fishmonger turns up at our doorstep at 7:30AM on the dot. Her wares are whatever she caught that day—one day it could be the plump, glistening vanjaram (seer fish), the next a handful of prawns. Every visit is a surprise. 
 
My fishmonger is a bit of an anomaly. Most of her peers end up selling their catch to local suppliers, who then sell them to major wholesale markets across the country. Slightly larger operations like those with a boat, for instance, sell their entire day’s catch to exporters. A typical exporter buys around 3-5 tonnes from such small suppliers every day. 
 
It’s a messy, inconsistent, and largely unorganised process. 
 
For companies that aim to bring some semblance of order to the space, such as Licious and its peers FreshToHome and Tendercuts, there’s a goldmine waiting to be tapped. However, they’re all focussed on the end-user, customers like you and me, who might buy a kilo of fish on a good day. 
 
Even as they build their farm-to-fork empires brick by brick, a dark horse in the space is challenging the notion that D2C is the best path to meat and seafood success—Captain Fresh.
 
The three-year-old company built its business by solving for the seafood supply chain, building a digital layer connecting fisherfolk, collection centres, and retailers. It wants to ensure that the seafood it sources reaches the end buyer as quickly and efficiently as possible—eliminating the unpredictability of sourcing from local markets. 
 
This value proposition has helped Captain Fresh sign up more than 2,500 clients, including the likes of e-grocer BigBasket and food delivery giant Swiggy’s grocery arm Instamart. The company hit a valuation of $500 million recently, on the back of a $50 million fundraise, co-led by existing investors Tiger Global and Prosus Ventures. 
 
While the B2B approach allows Captain Fresh to leverage scale to drive costs down, dragging India's seafood space out of the dark ages is a mammoth task, writes Bhumika: https://the-ken.com/story/captain-freshs-b2b-recipe-for-indias-unorganised-fish-and-seafood-market/ (9-minute read)
