Remember Sisyphus?
The Green mythological king is quite the favourite metaphor to many of us at The Ken. I'm quite partial to it myself. We've often slipped the king's name in to describe certain companies, policymakers, whatnot.
But today, I can say with certainty, we're all looking a bit like Sisyphus.
The king of Corinth was punished by (the very problematic) Zeus to forever roll a boulder up a hill in the depths of Hades, no less. Why? Because he had twice cheated death (and other less trivial matters).
Covid-19 feels a lot like that boulder. The closer a country is to finding a solution and vaccinating its population, the faster it hits rock-bottom. I mean, look at the UK now.
Today's story, the first in our week-long year-end series, looks into India's Sisyphean task of ramping up testing, clearing vaccines, and developing alternatives.
While we wait and watch (with some good signs to keep the spirits up), let's just hope that this isn't that pesky "God of the sky" at play, shall we?
