Hello Ishan,

 

It’s Shreevar again. I’m back this week with another episode of Cost to Company, our podcast centred on careers and workplaces. 

 

But what if I wasn’t here…

 

What if I decided to take a breather and head to Vietnam for a sabbatical. Would it look cool on my CV? Would my colleagues want to come along too? Would I still have my job after six months?

 

See, breaks always seem tempting. But in a job market that is as brittle as the one right now, does a career break even make sense?

 

In this episode, I speak to Vatsal Sanghvi (Consultant, Omidyar Network India*; former Category Manager, Flipkart) and Issac John (former Head of Marketing, Discovery India; former Marketing Head, Puma India) on how they bounced back from their sabbaticals. Two people with starkly different experiences, but oddly similar outlooks.

 

These were some of the things we explored in this episode:

 

1. Why there’s no good time to take a career break.

2. How Flipkart baked sabbaticals into employee benefits… for its own benefit.

3. Navigating through the choppy waters of an ‘unproductive’ career break.

4. Why finding mentors is essential during (and after) a sabbatical.

5. How prorated + vested sabbaticals can work in organisations.
