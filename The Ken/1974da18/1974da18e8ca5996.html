Siddharth Shah and family want to fix your smile. Pharmeasy 2.0 may need fixing first

MakeO’s aligners promise perfect smiles at a fraction of the usual price—but behind the Instagram gloss lie surprise charges, FDA claims, and a $270 million valuation in freefall

MakeO, offering teeth aligners and at-home dermatology treatments, appears to be treading a familiar path—one laid by Pharmeasy, the e-pharmacy firm that shelved its IPO plans. Its valuation has reportedly halved within six months of raising funds at Rs 2,231 crore, echoing Pharmeasy’s plunge from $5.6 billion. The two companies share investors, and a playbook. MakeO raised its largest round in April 2022, just as PharmEasy was preparing its ill-fated IPO. In India, cosmetic dentistry is a tough sell: though many have misaligned teeth, few see it as worth paying to fix. [Read the full story]

Loop Health is a broker that talks like a wellness company and wants to walk like a health insurer

Tired of relying on insurers, the company has ambitions of becoming one itself. For now, though, it relies on a workaround to keep selling health insurance and wellness services together

Investors are writing big cheques for a new healthcare model in India—one where prevention takes precedence. Loop Health, a seven-year-old corporate insurance broker, has also been acting as a health assurer. But the regulator doesn’t allow brokers to double as wellness-service providers. Loop has a workaround, though. Health assurance is still nascent in India, and Loop wants to capitalise on its edge; it just needs a license. [Read the full story]

The inside story of how Ecom Express went from IPO to fire sale in months

The company with Rs 2,500 crore in annual revenue on the cusp of being a unicorn was sold to Delhivery for a paltry Rs 1,400 crore—a slanted deal by any standard

Citing how he and other minority shareholders weren’t informed of Delhivery’s acquisition of Ecom Express, Ecom’s board chairman resigned during the May 28 EGM. Being a distress sale, the deal triggered a waterfall mechanism, which left minority shareholders and esop holders in the lurch.In the last few years, Ecom Express has faced significant headwinds, including its largest customer walking away and two co-founders passing away. The uncomfortable question remains, why would a company with Rs 2,500 crore in revenue, Rs 300 crore in cash, and an upcoming IPO—essentially on the cusp of becoming a unicorn—sell itself at a steep discount? [Read the full story]

How BSE is using AI to sniff out rotten IPOs

India’s exchanges, drowning in draft prospectuses, have deputised AI as their first reader. Speed is up; what else can they do?

BSE is using AI agents to scan through the hundreds of SME IPO filings it gets every month. The focus on SME IPOs is because they’re relatively less regulated, and has seen a boom in the past few years. Scanning through documents may be low-hanging fruits; the future may see agents recording and monitoring calls, among other use cases. With exchanges gearing to welcome more DRHPs in the coming months, more tasks may be in store for the AI mill. [Read the full story]

Why is Genus up 300% when India’s smart-meter rollout is stuck at 5%?

India’s target of 250 million smart meters by 2025 has been plagued by slow execution, delays in subsidy disbursals, and a general lack of will on the part of discoms 

Genus has had a dream run in the last couple years in terms of its revenue, order book, and share price. All thanks to the government’s ambitious 250-million-smart-meters-by-2025 target. The booming demand for smart meters even drew the interest of other electronics manufacturing services companies like Kaynes. Yet, with the deadline looming, smart meter installations are nowhere near complete, instead hovering at around 5% of target levels. For companies like Genus, their overreliance on government agencies and subsidies—replete with their erratic timelines and delays—may prove unsustainable in the long run. [Read the full story]
