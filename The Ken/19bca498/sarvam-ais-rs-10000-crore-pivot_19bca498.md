Good Morning Ishan,
A lot happened in AI this week, but well, nothing surprised me more than this.
The Tamil Nadu government on Tuesday signed an MoU with the Bengaluru-based Sarvam AI to establish India’s first full-stack Sovereign AI Park—a project that will see an investment of around ₹10,000 crore over a five-year period.
The project includes setting up data centres to exclusively host data of the government.
The project will generate 1,000 high-skilled, deep-tech jobs and represents a foundational step in building a robust and inclusive AI ecosystem rooted in public interest.
Tamil Nadu, Sarvam AI to set up India’s first sovereign AI park with ₹10,000 Cr investment, [The Hindu](https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/news/national/tamil-nadu-and-sarvam-al-to-establish-indias-first-sovereign-al-park-with-investment-of-10000-crore-in-five-years-and-create-1000-jobs/article70504386.ece)
I did a double-take when I read this in the morning. Until now, I was under the impression that Sarvam was an Indian AI startup doing nerdy things like building language models and conducting research. And here they were, signing MoUs with governments to set up physical infrastructure to provide jobs, like they were Foxconn, Apple, or NVIDIA.
Also, Rs 10,000 crore is what… a billion dollars?
Last I checked, Sarvam AI had raised about $40 million in Series A funding, so this is really perplexing. I even went back to check if the dollar–rupee exchange rate had plunged further without me noticing.
So where’s the money coming from?
The story was quite sketchy about this seemingly important detail. There’s just one mention of it, which is a quote from a Tamil Nadu Minister, TRB Raja, who declared, “Sarvam will bring in the investment for the project while the government will provide land and necessary support.”
I guess that means Sarvam AI apparently can raise a billion dollars—but only to build infrastructure, not to fund its research, applications, or products.
So, what exactly is Sarvam AI?
And that answer isn’t a straightforward one…
