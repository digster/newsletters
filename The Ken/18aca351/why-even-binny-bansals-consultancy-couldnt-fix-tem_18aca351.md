Good Morning Ishan,
“In fond memory of all those who died waiting for the bell to ring” is a phrase scribbled across countless college desks. It’s safe to assume that a few of these carvings would’ve been chiselled, sometimes with bare nails, during lectures on economics—a science that many consider dreary, desolate, and dismal.
But that’s not entirely true, methinks.
Many moons ago, in my 12th standard economics class, I learned something very interesting—pricing strategies—and understood that some businesses adopt “premium pricing”. That is, they set higher-than-market prices, which gives their product or service an aura of superiority and high quality.
Now, that’s what came to mind when I read about Licious, the online meat-delivery startup and the protagonist of today’s story.
You see, Licious’ meats are priced significantly higher than that of the competition. But, this premium pricing for high quality hasn’t quite helped the company—its revenue has been stagnating and the room to grow has shrunk after the pandemic-induced highs.
Maybe, just maybe, Licious should have adopted “price skimming”, a strategy that involves first setting a high price for its product or service, and then gradually lowering it until it is at the market average. The trouble is—the startup didn’t, or rather, couldn’t.
So, it did what many businesses caught in a funk do: appoint a consultant to suggest solutions. Licious went with Flipkart-fame Binny Bansal’s xto10x.
A former Licious employee told The Ken, “Week on week, we were asked for different data cohorts. They helped us structure the data very well, but we couldn’t find answers to the growth problem. I could see that they were adding more value in helping [founders] Abhay [Hanjura] and Vivek [Gupta] ask better questions.”
Costs reduced but the problems at Licious worsened and have taken on another dimension in the form of an exodus of talent in key positions.
However, Licious has something going for it—cash in the bank thanks to a $150 million fund-raise in 2022.
Will this be enough to pull it out of the rut?
