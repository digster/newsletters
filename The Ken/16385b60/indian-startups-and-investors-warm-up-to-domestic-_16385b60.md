Beautiful Tuesday morning in this part of the world. So, let’s get straight to it. Vaping. Yes, vaping. I don’t know about you but vaping has been following me everywhere.
I met this founder dude in New Delhi last week, and he was like, dude, I’m into vaping. And then, he stood right there, vaping. Drawing on some green stuff with a hideous device and blowing some white something. I asked him if it is cheap; he said it depends. I then met my former colleague and one of the best copy editors in the country, and he said he is trying to quit smoking. Needless to say, I asked, are you vaping? He fell for it. “Aren’t they banned?” he replied. I’m like no, everybody seems to be doing it. He shook his head disapprovingly.
I got back home, got down to Netflix, and again, vaping! David Letterman was talking about it with Tina Fey. The New Yorker just did a piece on it...man, it seems to me that vaping is the new cool. I am yet to scour the Internet for my hypothesis but vaping has been top-of-mind stuff for some weeks now. Speaking of which, today’s story. Both brand new and top-of-mind. From my colleague Sidhartha.
Just weeks back, two venture capital-backed startups listed on the National Stock Exchange (NSE) Emerge platform. While the exchange is old, these VC-backed companies are the first of their kind to list. How significant is it? Take for instance E2E. Its IPO got oversubscribed 70X, and against an issue price of Rs 57/share ($0.84), the stock listed at Rs 85/share ($1.25) on 15 May—a 56.5% listing gain. This hasn’t gone unnoticed. The chatter is that many VCs and startup founders are looking at these platforms as an alternative route for exits and raising capital.
