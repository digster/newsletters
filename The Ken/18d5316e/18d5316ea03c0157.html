Good Morning Ishan,

There’s no reason for you to read anything on “back-office” work in India. For years, global banks and tech majors have had centres in India taking advantage of cheap IT talent.

What could possibly be new?

Well, what you think of as back offices, or global capability centres (GCCs), is far from the reality these days.

According to one estimate, the 1,600 GCCs in India were home to 5,000 global roles in 2022. That number’s set to quadruple by the end of the decade. Here’s what’s even more interesting: over one in five of those roles are to do with engineering—a higher proportion than IT. And operations are not far behind.

So Lalit Ahuja is not being hyperbolic when he says that in the not-too-distant future, the CEO of a global company could be based in India. “Because most companies are built for running from anywhere—with the business being anywhere. If you can run Flipkart, why can’t you run Walmart or Amazon from here?”

Ahuja can claim to know a thing or two about GCCs. As founder and CEO of ANSR, he has set up 125 GCCs for everyone, from Victoria’s Secret to Delta Air Lines.

From establishing these centres through joint ventures to offering them on subscription, ANSR has come a long way. Seema caught up with Ahuja and his son Vikram, who runs a workforce-management-software unit of ANSR, to discuss how ANSR measures productivity, why it needs to open a centre in a new country every four to five months, and much more.
