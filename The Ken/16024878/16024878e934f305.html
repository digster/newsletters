We compare things often. Between stuff that’s irrelevant, and by that I mean apples and oranges, India and China, coffee and green tea (you know this is totally worthless), and many other exciting little, big things. And then there’s the good kind of comparison, one that helps us understand something better. It adds something new to our knowledge, of why something is happening. Arundhati’s story today, on PaisaBazaar and BankBazaar, falls in this relevant category.
 
It is as much a story of two companies jostling with each other for leadership as it is of how companies can adopt different strategies of solving the same problem. Irrespective of how long they have been in business; a theme that has played out time and again in corporate history. 
 
So, BankBazaar has been in business for about nine years and raised a total funding of $110 million. PaisaBazaar—a part of PolicyBazaar—has been in business for about three years; it was started in 2014. PaisaBazaar processes nearly Rs 250-crore worth personal loans every month, according to its CEO Naveen Kukreja. BankBazaar reportedly processes about Rs 100-crore worth personal loans a month. BankBazaar, on its part, neither confirmed nor denied this number. It did say though that it is ahead of the competition. It didn’t say by how much. 
 
At its core, both businesses are about generating leads for banks. But this story is also about how far can you stretch the leads business. When does it become imminent for a transaction to actually take place? When does that add more value than just leads? You can read Arundhati’s piece here: https://the-ken.com/round-one-paisabazaar-scores-bankbazaar/
 
Did you know that only about 55% of folks who are recognised as creditworthy and whose loan applications are passed on to the bank actually end up getting loans? Now I know what happened.
