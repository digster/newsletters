One in every three smartphones sold in India last year came from the BBK stable. Not BB’s, or BlackBerry as some may know it, but BBK’s.

BBK Electronics (Bu Bu Gao Electronics in Chinese) has already toppled Huawei to capture the No. 1 spot in China and is nearly there in India, the world’s fastest growing smartphone market. In just five years, BBK has zoomed up to the No. 2 spot (30% market share), displacing Samsung and reining in market leader Xiaomi (31.4%).

BBK, if you don’t already know or haven’t guessed by now, is the parent company of Oppo, Vivo, Realme and OnePlus—the quartet that has decimated all competition. All while BBK choses to stay in the shadows, happy to let the spotlight shine on the Fantastic Four (a moniker I invented) and let the world think of them as different entities competing against each other.

One would not not be wrong on both counts: the Fantastic Four do operate independently, have different marketing strategies and even cannibalise the other’s market. But, BBK and the Fantastic Four are mum on the rationale behind this puzzling, but undeniably successful, strategy.

The same strategy that has helped cement the demise of MILK (an industry moniker, not mine) - India’s quartet of Micromax, Intex, Lava and Karbonn. A quartet whose combined market share is now under 3%, while Realme alone has 7% since its launch last May.

It's a strategy that involves the age-old plan of piling marketing money onto brands. But also one that involves the spunk of taking on all comers at all levels - OnePlus is premium, while Oppo and Vivo make mid-range phones and Realme is challenging Xiaomi’s domination of the budget segment.

There have been missteps, for sure. And humongous losses. But with smartphone shipments down globally, the lure of the booming Indian smartphone market is worth the struggle.
