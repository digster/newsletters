Good Morning Ishan,
If you happen to be in any of the almost 100 constituencies that went to the polls on Monday, I hope you managed to cast your vote despite the debilitating heat. My turn is next week.
I had something else planned for this edition, but then I realised Zomato would announce its financials for the quarter and year ended March 2024 yesterday. It’s impossible not to weigh in on a company whose numbers have made even the most optimistic projections look fairly conservative.
It’s no surprise that quick commerce once again took centre stage. Zomato subsidiary Blinkit’s gross order value (GOV) doubled year-on-year in the March quarter to over Rs 4,000 crore (US$480 million). Zomato’s food-delivery business grew by a more modest 28% to Rs 8,440 crore (US$1 billion).
Blinkit’s expansion frenzy isn’t stopping anytime soon, if Zomato’s [letter](https://b.zmtcdn.com/investor-relations/475179f950da18319c26051c3cfc7e5c_1715593232.pdf) to its shareholders is any indication.
In Q4FY24, we added 75 net new stores taking our total store count to 526. For comparison, this is more than the number of stores we added in the three preceding quarters cumulatively.
Then came the kicker.
At this point, we are aiming to get to 1,000 stores by the end of FY25 🤞.
So Blinkit is going to double its store count by March. This is bonkers! (The crossed-fingers emoji is Zomato’s, by the way.) Just last month, brokerage Goldman Sachs said that a quick-commerce company in India can serve its entire addressable market (which, in its opinion, is the top 50 cities) with just 900-1,000 warehouses.
Blinkit’s target for as many dark stores in under a year, with hundreds more to come, reiterates what I said earlier about projections. No one has any idea how…
