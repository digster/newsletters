Good Morning Ishan,
A lifetime ago, to pay for college, I took on as many part-time jobs as my course load allowed. One of my jobs involved showing up at a store, folding clothes, managing the cash register, and generally keeping myself entertained during four-hour shifts that seemed to last forever.
One day, my colleagues and I were told that the clothing chain we worked for was going to open a new location just a 10-minute drive away, and it would stock $1 million worth of merchandise—nearly four times what we kept on our racks. We all had the same question on our minds: how long did we have before our location would be wound down?
I was reminded of that moment when I read today’s story, which covers the rapid expansion that Indian retailers underwent in anticipation of post-Covid shopping sprees. Those expectations are falling short.
Reliance Retail, for example, opened almost seven stores a day across fashion, groceries, and electronics in FY22, and nine stores each day in FY23. Now, that has gone back to 7 new stores a day, plus the retailer has shuttered 2,200 stores in FY25. Others such as Arvind Fashion, Aditya Birla Fashion & Retail, and Shoppers Stop are in the same boat. Every chain pursued aggressive growth in presence, but they have all lost the wind in their sails.
This has led to a range of experiments, from assuming space in outlet malls to trying out main-street locations. Retailers are trying to come up with ways to keep consumers interested in strolling through their shops and spending money. [Aakriti has the details in today’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/reliance-trends-pantaloons-and-the-shopping-spree-that-never-came/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
