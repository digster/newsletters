Hello Ishan,
We don’t have a story for you today on account of Ganesh Chaturthi. Instead, we have a [case study](https://the-ken.com/blog/latentview-analytics-data-shines-when-married-to-an-authentic-story/). But I’ll get to that shortly.
I am Ruhi. I used to write stories for The Ken, but in January this year, I decided to start teaching. So I started leading The Ken's Narrative Thinking vertical. It felt like a natural progression. I had pitched, reported for, and written over 100 stories. Every story taught me something about storytelling, feedback, and interviews, among other things. The document named ‘Learnings’ in my Google Drive, the books on my desk, and the lists of sources were all growing. It felt like it was the right time to start a new chapter.
Over this year, I have learned the importance of learning. I knew, in theory, that great teachers learn from their students. Now, I realise that listening to students is the most important quality of a teacher. This is where the [case study](https://the-ken.com/blog/latentview-analytics-data-shines-when-married-to-an-authentic-story/) I mentioned comes in. This case study captures the experience of some of our students from a company that enrolled in The Ken’s Narrative Thinking programme.
The emerging leaders from the data analytics firm LatentView that went public recently, taught us the fundamental purpose of storytelling. Over Zoom calls, surveys, and private sessions, the participants taught us that stories connect people who speak different languages. They made us see how hard it is for a data professional who speaks technology to convince customers who speak business.
