What about healthcare? 
 
According to a survey by the Association for Democratic Reform, an NGO concerned with improving governance and strengthening democracy by working towards electoral and political reforms, healthcare is a key issue in the upcoming General Elections. Voters in five states—Assam, Uttarakhand, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh & Mizoram—see better hospitals and healthcare as the second most important issue for them.
 
So, on that count, how did the incumbent BJP-led National Democratic Alliance do? That's what today's piece is about. According to Ruhi and Suraksha, today's co-authors, a whole range of initiatives and policies fell by the wayside. Only two really took flight—Ayushman Bharat and the price control of essential drugs and devices. Both seemingly complementary. But, as it turns out, not quite so.
 
In fact, they say, these two policies (and the vastly different ideologies that drive them) have caused problems for most stakeholders in the Indian healthcare ecosystem. Hospitals, drug manufacturers, insurers, device manufacturers, and, of course, patients. How? That's what you'll find out in today's story.
