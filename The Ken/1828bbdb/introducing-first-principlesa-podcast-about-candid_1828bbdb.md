You aren’t completely wrong. This is yet another podcast with business leaders, but it’s also unlike anything you may have listened to.
They say that if we want to get the right answers, there must be someone asking the right questions. Ideally, someone who understands the shoes that leaders are in, and someone who has closely observed businesses in India.
Every fortnight, Rohin talks to CEOs and leaders to understand how they think, how they make decisions, and what they do to succeed. Every fortnight, through these conversations, we dissect the lenses through which entrepreneurs and business leaders look at work and life. Lenses that help them look at things differently than others do.
You should listen to the episode on our [website](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/first-principles/dunzo-first-principles/?ajs_event=fp_home_banner_user_click_on_cta), [Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/in/podcast/first-principles/id1639125773?i=1000575665400), or [Spotify](https://open.spotify.com/episode/2x27rr1s1aZqkI8L1NOVku?si=3lYpzedSQAWo-56x-rZa4g&nd=1). If you wish to add it to your favourite player, here is the [RSS link](https://feeds.transistor.fm/first-principles-261f2954-4e6b-4315-973d-17d02efc10f6). Podcasts aren’t a feature on our app yet, but we’re working on getting them there soon.
And with this, our podcast bundle has two titles—Cost to Company, our podcast about careers and workplaces that you can listen to [here](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/cost-to-company/your-salary-is-not-private/?ajs_event=ctc_home_banner_user_click_on_cta), and First Principles.
We are taking audio very seriously, and it is only a matter of time before you hear about our other titles. I can’t wait for you to check them all out.
All our podcasts are free to listen to. For now. But they will soon move behind a paywall and only remain available for our subscribers. We’ll also launch some new plans, so keep an eye out for that.
As it always is with new pursuits, we’re bound to make mistakes, but we’re determined to iterate on top of them and improve.
And for that, we count on you, our subscribers, readers, and now listeners, to let us know what you think was good, and more importantly, what was bad.
