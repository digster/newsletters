Hello Ishan,

Kabeer Biswas, CEO and co-founder of Dunzo, has a hobby. 

Here’s how he describes it to Rohin, CEO and co-founder of The Ken, in the first episode of our brand-new podcast, First Principles.  

“One the funnest moments that I have nowadays is, in the morning hours, usually on a cycle route, I’ll go stop at one of Dunzo’s warehouses, and as orders start pouring in, I’ll just stand over there and look at a shopping cart, and just try and figure out what shopping mission this was.

Like… what need would have been created for somebody to buy twelve packets of milk and four packets of Marie biscuit?”

Kabeer spent nearly eight years figuring out shopping missions for millions of Indians. Today, everyone knows about Dunzo thanks to their national ad campaigns. But for many years, Dunzo was Bengaluru’s best kept secret. It started off as a WhatsApp group where you could ask for anything—whether that was a cup of coffee or getting your rental agreement done—and poof, it would be done. Just like magic. And most of that magic was done by Kabeer himself, who, by his estimate, personally completed 10,000 tasks. 

That was in 2015. 

Today, Dunzo is a ~$800 million company. 

In today’s episode, Kabeer talks about this journey. He talks about how he works, about trust, about how Dunzo makes mistakes and learns from them, feedback loops… and even how becoming a parent has changed him as a leader and a person. 

He also talks about his first principles.
