Good Morning Ishan,
 
The last week for me has been absolutely hectic because I was moving house. The move happened last Saturday, and it’s Saturday once more. How time flies.
 
Packing is an exhausting process, but it’s also an interesting one. The idea that you can literally fit your entire life away into a dozen cardboard boxes in the space of just a few hours, and start afresh somewhere new is liberating.
 
The entire act of packing, I realised, is very similar to putting together a story. Bear with me. First, you survey the field. Take stock of all that lies in front of you. The sheer scale of this is often terrifying. You retreat, contemplate, gather up the strength, and finally get to work. Once you’re knee-deep in the story (or the packing), it’s a bit of a blur. You start methodically putting things away. There’s a structure. There’s order. This is practically art, you think. Then you’re done. 
 
Except, you realise, there’s a whole bunch of little stuff you forgot to include (in the story or boxes). And now everything’s all tied up. At that point, it’s a matter of priority. What is essential, and what absolutely isn’t. 
 
My packing, I admit, was largely a disaster. I’m just thankful all my cats made it to the new house without anyone being mauled or blinded. But the way we package stories at The Ken is the exact opposite. The level of planning, diligence, effort and intervention that goes into each story is something I’m truly proud of. This last week has been no different.
 
Let’s begin with the Sunday story, since that’s something you may have missed. Last Sunday, Durga took on an interesting concept—empathy gaming. These are games that essentially put you in someone else’s shoes, hopefully raising empathy and awareness in the process. Durga went beyond the feel-good surface to discuss the ethical implications of such games. Our Sunday offerings are also free, so do check this one out.
 
On Monday, two members of our Mumbai bureau—Sid and Pooja—teamed up to deliver a real doozy. Their interest piqued by LIC’s imminent takeover of IDBI Bank, the two looked into LIC’s investments over the years. Spoiler alert, things aren’t as they should be.
 
Tuesday saw Gayathri make her debut as a full-time member of our team. In her piece, she takes a look at the Bayer-Monsanto merger and what it means for the Indian seed market.
 
Wednesday, a free story, was Vivek’s piece on a phenomenon he likes to call a Neo-Licence Raj. When the regulator is trigger-happy, he asks, who will regulate the regulator?
 
Then came Shashi’s piece on Jio’s wired broadband offering—Gigafiber. Jio wants to disrupt the wired broadband segment with Gigafiber, but Shashi isn’t so sure it will be that easy.
 
Finally, to wrap up the week, we had Colin’s piece on India’s bike-sharing startups. A lot of people are bullish on them, but are they truly destined for success? Read the piece and decide for yourself.
 
As for my move? I’m currently still living out of boxes. Let’s hope that isn’t the case by the time next Saturday comes around. I hope you've subscribed by then. You're missing out.
