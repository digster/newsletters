“Grown-ups like numbers. When you tell them about a new friend, they never ask questions about what really matters. They never ask: ‘What does his voice sound like?’ ‘What games does he like best?’ ‘Does he collect butterflies?’ They ask: ‘How old is he?’ ‘How many brothers does he have?’ ‘How much does he weigh?’ ‘How much money does he have?’”
These lines are from the French writer Antoine de Saint-Exupéry’s timeless masterpiece, The Little Prince—a story I found myself re-reading just as Daybreak completed [100 episodes](https://open.spotify.com/show/2N3NVn7d3z1N2KQiyBNVNQ?si=e1be09ea0d834c4b) earlier this week.
While I’m all aboard with the profound wisdom these words weave through Exupéry's enchanting tale, I can’t dismiss the power of numerical markers—the ones that serve as milestones, guiding our way.
When Daybreak launched in December 2022, the destination was clear: bring The Ken’s pioneering business-news reportage to life, free from the news-cycle frenzy. And we’ve stood by this commitment—bringing you one powerful business story that really matters—three times a week for over 30 weeks now.
From our extensive coverage of [Byju’s troubled fall](https://open.spotify.com/episode/7KCL5jSPPcuHk3N2mjLljE?si=6409766c4d6f4258) to illuminating the [Ikea and Walmart-led resurrection](https://open.spotify.com/episode/4S1BYWcyuDSFO7xse9tVxJ?si=660272d0bfc4425e) of India’s fading jute industry, we have been dedicated to giving you an unfiltered insight into India’s dynamic corporate landscape.
