I got stuck at zero. Or zeros, if I have to be specific.

Now, there’s history to this. Not long back, whenever my pop would be pissed at me, he would thunder, “You have become a zero. Full zero.” For reasons best known to parents dealing with incompetent kids, that’s supposed to be some version of a reprimand. I gave my pop many such occasions. Three out of hundred in mathematics. “Full zero.” His prized, shiny, winding alarm clock used as an experiment to own tens of meticulous, spinning tops. Ah! Beautiful, little things. “Full zero.” Well, that was fun. Sadly, the zeros in my colleague Sidhartha’s story are not.

As of date, India’s banking sector’s non-performing assets stands at Rs. 8400000000000.

Needless to say, this has led to measures and counter-measures from the Reserve Bank of India, only leading to a serious liquidity crunch in an already credit-starved economy. And the ones it has impacted the most are micro, small and medium enterprises (MSMEs). A bit of a saving grace is Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDs).

Originally proposed in 2008 by then-RBI governor Raghuram Rajan, TReDS was set up with the aim to ensure that MSMEs receive timely payments at a low cost of funding. They are digital platforms where MSMEs can get finance by auctioning off their trade receivables. For the first time in India, these exchanges have created a platform where MSMEs (sellers), large corporates (buyers) and banks (financiers) can communicate with each other in real-time, settling their invoice financing in a matter of a day or two. MSMEs don’t have to chase after banks for finance, and they don’t have to present any collateral either.

This is Sidhartha’s story for today. Fair to say, TReDs is yet to make its mark, but it is a step in a good direction.
