Good Morning Ishan,
Kimbho? It means ‘What’s up’ in Sanskrit. We were all introduced to this word when Ramdev, the founder of Patanjali (not me), decided to launch his messaging app. It was then violently wrenched out of the internet when security flaws came to light. You’ve read that in the papers.
I came to learn of these flaws late at night. I couldn’t sleep. I had turned off the lights; the only sound was the quiet humming of the AC as I scrolled through my Twitter feed. People were angry, they wanted to say their piece on Donald Trump, a sting done by CobraPost, football and Israel and pollution. It went on and on. And for someone who often struggles with sleep, this wasn’t helping. Usually, the reason I can’t sleep is I am anxious about the next day. Maybe it is a story or maybe it a big meeting. Sometimes it is just big decisions.
Big decisions. It was the theme of this week. To be honest, I hadn’t realised it was the theme until I reached this sentence. Now, you haven’t subscribed and I think you must because it is a big decision and there is a lot that happened this week that you have missed.
NPCI made a big decision when it decided to give about $75 million as cashback to vendors who accepted BHIM [payments](https://the-ken.com/story/bhim-cashbacks-digital-payments/). If a mobile wallet company did it, we would say, ah, VC money funding discounts. I’ll have me some of those. But here, the tax-paying citizen is the VC. And BHIM is not public goods that it needs to be subsidised. Shashi wrote about it.
Sticking to the payments theme, Arundhati says that ICICI Bank has decided it won’t [buy technology anymore](https://the-ken.com/story/icici-bank-wants-to-be-a-fintech/). It wants to build it. Just like a fintech company. Whatever is happening at the board level aside, this is an interesting move by the bank.
I am careful not to listen to the radio whenever I feel I can’t sleep. There is a reason: way too many ads, and that’s the problem with FM in India. [Streaming killed the radio star](https://the-ken.com/story/fm-radio-midlife-crisis/). But what can radio do? Harveen tells you the story.
Talking of killing, e-pharmacies almost died. Red tape, bad business models and discounts were bleeding them. But the sector, Ruhi says, has settled down. And a winner is about to [emerge](https://the-ken.com/story/deaths-door-to-doorstep-delivery-e-pharmacies-are-here-to-stay/), it did things a bit differently. (This one is free, you don’t need to pay for it. Just log in.)
And finally, time for some self-promotion (and one of the reasons I couldn’t sleep); I had a story out. I met the founder of a small B2B company in Mumbai called [Loylty Rewardz](https://the-ken.com/story/loylty-rewardz-perfect-exit/). The CEO of this company built it, raised funding and then sold it all in seven years. And he did it all in a very niche sector of loyalty points. You should subscribe to read this one at least.
And before I do my usual salutations. ICYMI, The Ken is [profitable](https://the-ken.com/blog/the-ken-is-profitable/) now. It brought us great joy to announce it. Thank you for being part of the journey. We value you.
Until next time,
Patanjali Pahwa
