And avoid simple mistakes that are hurting your chances

Good evening Ishan, 

I’ve never been a fan of being stuck with 73 other sweaty people packed in a room together. Back when I was studying in an all-boys school in Kolkata though, it was my daily routine. 

The crowd at our canteen comprised mostly pre-pubescent boys, fresh off trigonometry and first crushes, trying to get their plates of chowmein or chole served with a side of rubber bhature during our short lunch break.

The place was small; the temperatures high. But the one person who ruled the roost was a moustachioed man affectionately known as ‘Chief’.

He was the head cook, the cashier, the messiah. And the one who dispensed lunch. So we tried our best to woo him. Some tried 1) banging the grimey lunch tables to attract his attention, others 2) shouting his name with rising intensity, while most 3) just waited for the crowd to clear. 

None of this really worked, to be honest. The real trick was: you had to lock eyes with him for at least three seconds, briefly but clearly mouth your order, and leave a smile. Not too wide, but not too weak either. 

And there you’d have it. Lunch. 

The reason I’m taking you through this oily memory lane is because it isn’t too different from what’s happening in the current labour market. Everyone wants their plate of chowmein. Everyone wants to woo their ‘Chief’. And almost everyone is making mistakes. 

You’ll want to get your notepads out for this episode of Cost to Company, where I spoke to three people—Ria Shroff Desai (People and Culture Lead, Blume Ventures), Udayan Walvekar (Co-founder and CEO, GrowthX) and Bhakti Jhaveri Dhanak (Associate Vice President - HR, CIIE. CO)—who told me what these mistakes are in today's ‘employer’s market’ and how best you can avoid them. 

It’s a great episode you wouldn’t want to miss.
