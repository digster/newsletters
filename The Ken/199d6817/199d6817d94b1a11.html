Lenskart turned its ‘1+1’ offer into a potent weapon—and its customers into sceptics

Lenskart’s Rs 1,000 glasses are a product of plastic lenses and acetate frames, moulded together in a vertically-integrated business—but the pitfall of lower prices is lower quality

Lenskart, India’s largest eyewear platform valued at $6.1 billion, is preparing for a Rs 7,500-8,000 crore IPO in November. Its managed to produce spectacles under Rs 1,000, courtesy of its vertically integrated manufacturing, use of cheaper materials, and lack of middlemen—enjoying 60% margins. But this has resulted in a slew of customer complaints regarding incorrect prescriptions, flimsy frames, and rashes from the acetate. The company has led an industry-wide shift to cheaper glasses, turning eyewear from medical necessity to fashion accessory. [Read the full story]
