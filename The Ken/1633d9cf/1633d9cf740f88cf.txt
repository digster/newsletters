Good Morning Ishan,

A long time ago, when I was an idealistic, dewy-eyed and a lesser-handsome version of what I am right now, I was a Product Manager at a fashion e-commerce company. A week after I joined, the Category Manager of the women's lingerie division took me along for a meeting with one of our vendors. I figured I would do what most Product Managers did when they were in an unfamiliar setting. I would nod along and occasionally interrupt the conversation with a question like 'Yes, but can we do a deep-dive into that?' with a searching, sceptical look. And watch everyone else fumble for answers.

Fortunately, the meeting went well. The deal was struck. We shook hands, and the vendor suggested that we start off with listing a couple of bras for sale, and see how we fared. I thought I had misunderstood. A couple? Surely we could handle more. The vendor looked at me as though I was an idiot.

"Do you know how many variants you will need for a single bra?", he asked me.

I was young back then, and bras weren't really my specialisation.

Over two hundred, he said.

I must have looked confused. He explained. You see, for every bra style, we needed to stock four cup sizes from A to D and four band sizes from 28 to 36. That was the bare minimum. And then four types: with underwire, without underwire, strapless and push-up. Oh, and four colours, at least. Four times four times four times four.

That's two hundred and fifty-six variants. For a single bra.

I only went for meetings with vendors who sold belts after that.

That’s the day I learned that the women’s lingerie space was complicated. But I will let my colleague, Harveen tell you more about that.
