Good Morning Ishan,
You know my thoughts on money but who am I kidding? Rs 16,347.5 crore. That is a lot of money. Even for Star India. That the company was willing to bet so much on cricket, in the year 2017, especially on the Indian Premier League (IPL) caught a whole lot of people by surprise. Equally surprising is that Sony Pictures Networks (SPN) did not place a global bid. Can you believe this? Only last year, it acquired the Ten Sports Network from Zee Entertainment for $385 million.
For a moment, if you keep the numbers aside, the bottomline is this. Starting 2017, if you have to watch some of the best cricket the world has to offer, you’ll have to watch it on Star. As my colleague Venkat tells me, how about you consider this for a rights portfolio: ICC events till 2023 (World Cup, World T20, Women’s World Cup, Champions Trophy), all international and domestic matches played in India (till March 2018) and now, the IPL. All of this, mind you, on one network with broadcast capabilities of around 11-13 channels, and growing.
Venkat’s copy is sharp and covers a lot of ground. Like what this win means for Star? More importantly, winning the bid was the easy part. Making money and recouping the investment, that’s where the difficult part starts. Starting today. So how is Star going to do this? I am already telling you a lot. Let me add just a little bit more. Venkat reached out to Uday Shankar, the CEO of Star India for perspective. Fun fact: Uday used to be a journalist. Late yesterday, he got back to Venkat with a fairly tongue in cheek reply. I found it amusing.
