Good Morning Ishan,

How was your week? Mine wasn’t as productive or fulfilling since I was sick for most of it.

Being an Editorial Designer at The Ken has its perks, including the opportunity to read our stories before they are published. It is riveting to watch a first draft turn into a polished, final one. But this week, while on leave, I read our stories the way you all did—every morning when they appeared on the website.

I will admit it was a great week full of delightful surprises as I discovered a new story each day.

We opened the week with Soumyajit writing about Zepto’s persevering belief in the quick-commerce model. Why does Aadit Palicha, CEO and co-founder of Zepto, see no reason to turn away from delivering groceries at high speed?

On Tuesday, Shashwata dug into Uber’s biggest cab supplier, Everest Fleet. The company provides around 9,000 cabs to the ride-hailer, but could it meet the high demand for EVs when the anticipated “electrification of cabs” happens?

For our Wednesday story, Katrina discussed Filipino virtual assistants (VAs). Wages are declining, and task-automation tools are taking away the need to employ some workers, but VAs are determined to stay in the sector. Why?

On Thursday, Aayush explored ID Fresh, the brand most well-known for its dosa and idly batter. The company, founded by PC Musthafa and his cousins, now intends to transition from a family-run business to something much bigger. But as Aayush writes, “the challenge lies in keeping the old and the new guard in harmony”.

A few years ago, some banks made the audacious choice to give free access to airport lounges to sell more credit cards. The scheme has now become too successful, and banks are reaping the costs of what they once sowed. Arundhati’s story on Friday examined the changes that banks now have to make, but how are they going to break it to their customers?

I hope you enjoyed our stories this week as much as I did. Have a great weekend!

Regards,
