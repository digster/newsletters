Good Morning Ishan,
 
If you're an edtech startup in India right now, you're in a strange place.
 
On one hand, it could be the best of times...
 
The pandemic boost for the industry has accelerated the growth of companies like Byju's and Unacademy by several years. These edtech leaders have raised huge funding rounds on the back of this traction. And they have put the money to good use by staking out new markets, as well as making big acquisitions like WhiteHat Jr.
 
On the other hand, it could be the worst of times...
 
The rising edtech tide does not raise all boats. Spare a thought for the fledgling edtech startups that are now facing the daunting prospect of competing with behemoths like Byju's. What are their chances to carve a niche for themselves in such a competitive market?
 
And, then, there are the likes of Vedantu...
 
The Bengaluru-based edtech company was an early pioneer in the online tutoring space. With nearly $200 million in funding, it is neither too small to be snuffed out by competitors, nor is it yet big enough to compete toe-to-toe with leaders like Byju's and Unacademy. The two unicorns have far bigger treasure chests and resources in their armoury.
 
Vedantu is currently in the uncomfortable 'middle space' in the market. From here, it could still go on to become a leader, or it could get squeezed out from both sides and end up as a has-been.
 
In today's richly reported story, Olina dives deep to parse Vedantu's current reality and future possibilities—its strengths as well as its challenges.
