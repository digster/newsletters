Hello Ishan,
I’m Tanishka, part of the marketing team at The Ken. I just came back from a week-long vacation.
The Ken has an unlimited leaves policy—something that many startups have adopted in the recent past. This means we get to decide when and why we take our leaves, and how many days we take off. There are no rules. Which… as we know by now, is a double-edged sword.
Unlike many, I didn’t have to frantically calculate my leave balance before I left for my vacation. But like many, the thought of leaving work for a week still made me anxious. Thankfully, my teammates are wonderful people who picked up my work and made it look effortless.
Today’s episode of Cost to Company is about exactly this dilemma. Multiple studies have shown that employees working with unlimited leaves tend to take less of them. But why? Sneha finds out.
|
|
We dive into the topic head first, right to where the tension is—our relationship with work, and rest. Sneha talks to business leaders, HR professionals, people consultants, and employees themselves to ask:
- Who decides how many leaves in a year is normal?
- Why do we have unlimited leaves in the first place?
- Why are people taking fewer leaves when they’re allowed to take more?
- What is our relationship with rest?
I never knew I needed those questions answered. But I did. And that makes this episode really special.
