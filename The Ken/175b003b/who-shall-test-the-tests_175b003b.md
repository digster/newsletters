Good Morning Ishan,
Yesterday, news broke that one of the leading Covid-19 vaccine candidates, made by Pfizer and BioNTech, had demonstrated 90% effectiveness in trials.
Every week brings some good (or not bad) news about the various vaccines racing towards approval. It's tempting to thus wonder, are we on the cusp of defeating the novel coronavirus? Are we at least past the peak of infections?
The answers to that are “No" and "We don’t know.”
What we do know though is that tests were important to controlling the spread of the pandemic. As Seema Singh, our editor, writes in today’s story about the lessons India has learned after administering 118 million Covid tests, “Testing was vital in February; it is vital in November.”
They will be vital in February 2021. And November 2021.
After an initial shortage of tests, India saw an explosion of tests. Over 330 were approved by regulators. New ones are awaiting approval. Even newer ones are under development, including ones that can survive the real-world conditions of India’s smaller towns and villages (standard PCR tests require low temperatures for storage and administration).
It looks like a great opportunity for India to formulate forward-looking rules around tests that are not just built for India’s reality, but are also more accurate.
But are we doing this?
