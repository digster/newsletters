Good Morning Ishan,
We don’t have a story from India today on account of the country’s 78th Independence Day.
But we do have a breezy feature from Indonesia. It’s about the quandary Traveloka, the top online travel agency in Southeast Asia’s largest economy, finds itself in. Its Indian counterpart, Makemytrip, has never had it so good. With business and leisure travel growing at an impressive clip, Makemytrip’s shares are trading at an all-time high.
The mood at Traveloka, on the other hand, must be anything but euphoric. A company that has raised US$1 billion from investors such as Singapore’s GIC and Qatar Investment Authority now has to contend with airlines doubling down on selling tickets directly and the effects of TikTok parent Bytedance’s Tokopedia acquisition last year that stirred up issues within Traveloka.
Even worse, travel agents are increasingly hawking tickets on Tiktok, which has 125 million monthly active users in Indonesia.
Where does this leave Traveloka? That’s our contributor Jeff Hutton’s [story](https://the-ken.com/story/tokopedia-done-its-travelokas-turn-in-tiktoks-giant-hunt-in-indonesia/).
Swiggy pioneered food delivery in India. That got copied. It popularised quick commerce. Same thing again. And right now, as my colleague Praveen observed in his weekly newsletter, The Nutgraf, particularly about Swiggy Instamart in his massive survey-led edition on quick commerce apps—Swiggy is good at everything but great at nothing.
This narrative seems to be reflecting throughout Swiggy as we discussed how Swiggy went from being a disruptive and innovative company to a timid company. And with an IPO on the cards, Swiggy needs a show of confidence. What needs to change?
