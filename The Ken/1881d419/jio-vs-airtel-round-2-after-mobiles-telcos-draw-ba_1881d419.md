Good Morning Ishan,
Just when we thought telecom stories were getting boring—no epic price wars, no high-decibel brand promotions, no new territories to conquer—comes round #2.
The mobile market is saturating. More and more mobile users are using two SIM cards. Unsurprisingly, Reliance Jio and Bharti Airtel are shifting their focus to fixed broadband.
In March, Jio launched a new Rs 198 (US$2.5) plan with unlimited data at 10Mbps to coincide with the 16th edition of the cricket tournament Indian Premier League. With this, Jio undercut its own previous minimum price of Rs 399 (~$5) to introduce JioCinema as the IPL host.
Jio blew the price-war bugle. Field reports say that “students, hostels, and shop owners are opting for this plan”, but there’s no way the company will make any money on this tariff.
Barely two weeks later, Airtel responded with a Rs 219 (US$2.7) plan.
Home broadband is the new battleground.
Historically, telcos have targeted retail customers and lucrative enterprise clients. However, the Covid-19 pandemic introduced a fresh cohort: home-internet subscribers. India already has around 32 million such users.
For telcos, these home subscribers offer a potential average revenue per user (Arpu) of Rs 1,000 (~US$12), a sizable jump from the retail Arpu, which has stayed low at <Rs 200 (US$2.5).
At the centre of this Arpu calculation lies the shift—part underway, part likely to manifest soon—among 120 million viewers who pay for television content. The telcos anticipate that Indian consumers will eventually opt for bundled plans from a single service provider instead of spending separately on pay TV, fixed broadband, and OTT.
Telecom executives believe victory in this battle depends on four primary factors—the “three Ps” and “one C”.
Pratap has a sharp story today. In contrast to the 2016 4G battle between Jio and others, in which Airtel survived but lost its numero-uno title, this time, says an Airtel executive: “The telco will not let the same happen with the home broadband… It will be a battle worth watching.”
