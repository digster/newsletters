On a day when India’s largest carmaker, Maruti Suzuki, played down its impending electric vehicle, the E Vitara, global EV pioneer Tesla signalled a downsizing of its own ambitions by stopping production of two models.

The comparison ends there.

On its earnings call on Wednesday, Maruti disclosed that it has exported 13,000 units of the E Vitara to 28 countries across Europe. That’s a great feat. But the car is still not available for Indian consumers.

The auto giant has been flirting with E Vitara’s India launch for almost a year now, making and reneging on one commitment after another. After the vehicle was unveiled at the Auto Expo in January 2025, its launch was scheduled for March that year. Then it became September. Then December. And finally, it was pushed into the new year.

When Suprita, author of today’s story, visited showrooms in Bengaluru, he found the vehicle standing tall but not ready for sale. It’s quite unlike how other automakers showcase new models and take bookings.

“As per our demand, if the PM e-drive scheme incorporates entry-level cars costing up to Rs 15 lakh, Suzuki could go the extra mile to reduce the price by Rs 1 lakh,” a source close to the development told The Ken. And that’s the ongoing wait.

It’s a mysterious wait, given that since 2019, Maruti’s market share has fallen from 50% to 40%, and E Vitara is part of its broader strategy to regain lost ground. Suprita has an insightful story today.
