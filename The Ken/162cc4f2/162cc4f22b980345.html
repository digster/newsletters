Good Morning Ishan,

It’s not by design, but once again we start the week with a fintech story. And why not. It’s incredible what next-gen consumer finance companies are solving for and what money matter data can tell about a person.

Much good has come out of the proliferation of digital payments options. Yet, there is a, well, wallet-sized hole in this ecosystem, especially with the Reserve Bank of India treating two-factor authentication as the sacred cow that can’t be sacrificed.

What seems to be filling the hole, for now, at least, is the single-click wonder—Pay Later. No One Time Pin, no m-pin, no CVV, no mother’s birth date and first boss’ name, no pet’s name, no biometrics, and, of course, no Aadhaar number.

Increasingly, merchants in travel ticketing, food delivery, utilities, and elsewhere include this product in their payment options, many others are getting into Pay Later themselves, including Ola and Flipkart. Some see it as a payment option, others see it as a credit fix as this comes with a handsome weekly credit limit.

By now you know that Arundhati likes everything payments. Today she takes a good, hard look at this convenient payment option which is yet to come under the regulator’s lens. The instrument is ripe for misuse by defaulters as it is ready for solving merchants’ problems.

Let’s dive into the story, here: https://the-ken.com/pay-later-the-next-wallet/
