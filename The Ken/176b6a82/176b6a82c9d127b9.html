Good Morning Ishan,
 
India's telecom sector over the past decade has been a bottomless wellspring of drama. 

There have been rocky marriages—like the one between Vodafone and Idea, now called Vi.

Bloodbaths, like the one Jio sparked when it entered the sector with its dirt-cheap data tariffs, causing a wave of extinction.

Allegations of favouritism against the sector's regulatory body, Trai.

And crucial court case after crucial court case, each one seeing telcos' fortunes swing like a pendulum.

Indeed, if the last decade was the golden decade of television, India's telecom story over the same period could slot right alongside some of the best shows in recent times.

The year gone by, though, marked an end to many plotlines. Vi, as it turns out, will most likely survive—though for how long is anybody's guess. The tenure of RS Sharma, the sometimes controversial Trai chief, is officially over. And the price war that marred the last five years is finally at an end.

As Pratap points out in today's year-ender, it feels like a season finale. And with a new season comes new plotlines, subplots, and characters. From the new Trai chief, PD Vaghela, to the all-important 5G spectrum auctions, and India's looming satellite policy, Pratap gives us a glimpse into what the year ahead holds for Indian telecom.
