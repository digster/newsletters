In our story today, Pranav and Pradip ask a simple question: Can you teach basic English and Maths through an app?
It’s not as weird as it sounds. There’s an app for everything. To search for a job. To find a date. To send you good morning messages. To remind you to drink water. Until you finally watch Marie Kondo and then decide to delete everything except Dunzo and Google Maps. Joy.
It seems like it’s obvious. Learning English through an app must be easy. But it isn’t.
That’s where Elon Musk comes in. Hopefully minus his Twitter account.
You see, Elon Musk has funded a program called the Global Learning XPRIZE - which is an open global challenge to build software to teach children basic reading, writing and arithmetic in 15 months.
The value of the prize? $15 million.
So far, five teams are in the finals - and one of them is an Indian company called Chimple. There’s another team - AmritaCREATE, which was a finalist in another XPRIZE, for promoting adult literacy. Two Indian teams. Finalists at two competitions to promote literacy. There’s some deep irony here, but I am going to steer clear of it.
But there’s no reason you should do the same. Our story today is about what these companies are trying, what they are after, and most importantly - how they expect their technology will work. Technology has made inroads into learning. But that’s usually much more advanced forms of learning, like MOOCs, or courses, or even entrance exams. But for literacy? To teach you how to read? Seems like a tough ask.
Our story is about what it takes. And if these companies have it in them. It's a free read, so do share it widely.
