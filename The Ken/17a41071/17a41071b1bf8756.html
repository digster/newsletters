Good Morning Ishan,
 
We began this week with a story on how the 153-year-old Tata Group, which thrummed with the prospects of a super app for over a year, is finally putting the jigsaw puzzle together. It’s likely that you were left with more questions than answers after reading it.
 
Today’s story adds one more piece to the jigsaw. It’s the most revealing.
 
To back up a bit, three years ago, the group wrote off nearly $4 billion on its telecom business. With Reliance Jio unleashing a price war, the consumer mobile business of Tata Teleservices Ltd (TTSL) was sold to Bharti Airtel (which was willing to buy the entire business but the two parties couldn’t agree on the valuation).
 
The remaining enterprise business has been a lumbering, loss-making entity which the group could neither merge with Tata Communications, a thriving enterprise business, nor sell it off. Like a dutiful parent, Tata Sons, the holding company, has been infusing capital in Tata Tele—around Rs 46,000 crore as of June 2019.
 
Since the end of May, the listed subsidiary of Tate Tele, TTML, saw its share price triple. The markets knew more than the most—the Tatas are scripting an unlikely turnaround for their telecom business. Their plan is to service small and medium enterprises, a segment that Jio, Airtel, and Amazon Web Services are hawkishly eyeing.
 
It’s all part of a massive restructuring that's been in the works since earlier this year. Tata Tele has been renamed, it’s revenue target has almost doubled, and could soon be housed in a new umbrella entity—a new e-marketplace, Tata Business Hub.
 
How Tata Tele fits into Tata Digital’s game plan, its potential role in the group's upcoming super app, and how it could be used to turbocharge Tata's SME ambitions is our story from Pratap and Anand today. After Bharti Airtel, it’s the Tatas who want to emulate Jio Platforms’ super structure. Phew!
