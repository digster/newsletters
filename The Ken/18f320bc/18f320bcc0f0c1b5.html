Good Morning Ishan,

Does size have anything to do with spunk?

If you asked Ajit Dayal, he’d say no. The mutual-fund house he founded in 2006, Quantum, is the 35th largest among 44 players, with US$270 million worth of assets under management.

But few rivals have stuck to their guns like Quantum has—on everything from how fund returns are measured to how distributors are compensated. Most importantly, Quantum and Dayal have no qualms about facing off with large companies when they see something that’s beyond the pale.

Like ICICI Bank’s decision to delist its broking subsidiary, ICICI Securities, and make it a wholly-owned subsidiary. The move has been widely slammed as being unfair to every shareholder but ICICI Bank.

But Quantum is the only mutual-fund house to publicly chastise ICICI Bank for pursuing the delisting by “fraudulent means”. Quantum even threatened to take the lender to court.

But what good is being on the straight and narrow, though, when investors can choose from several funds that offer better returns than Quantum? Do read Anand’s sharp story.

Trent’s growth is frankly unreal

I’ve heard analysts and rivals tell me repeatedly over the past six months that Tata Group’s apparel retailer Trent’s dream run will not continue for too long. The basis for their argument: there are only so many stores Trent can add for its hugely successful value format, Zudio.

But a quick look at its numbers for the March quarter, and you know there’s no reason to believe the Zudio juggernaut is going to stop anytime soon. Trent added 85 stores to take the total Zudio footprint to almost 550 outlets.

Trent grew its top line by 50% and its profits by 400% in the quarter. That made analysts further move up the target for Trent’s share price, which hit yet another high after the results. The stock has trebled in value just over the past year and is now worth US$19 billion.

It’s safe to say no Tata company has got investors this excited in years.
