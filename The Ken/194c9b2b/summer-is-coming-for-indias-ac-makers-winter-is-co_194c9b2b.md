Good Morning Ishan,
For many households, a new air conditioner (or a few) is the answer to summers that are becoming hotter and longer. Some figures from last year tell us what this looks like for home-appliance brands: a 50–100% bump in revenue in the first three months of 2024 for Voltas, Blue Star, Amber Enterprises, and PG Electroplast.
Those in the industry expect demand to remain strong this year, so much so that PG Electroplast Managing Director (Finance) Vishal Gupta told The Ken that there might be a shortage of air conditioners as soon as mid-April.
The main reason for that shortfall is Indian manufacturers’ reliance on imported components. At present, 60% of any air conditioner’s parts are supplied by overseas sources.
This includes the compressor—the piece that makes it possible for a unit to cool air. It’s the most expensive component in an air conditioner, and even though there are four manufacturers that make it in India, they only have an annual capacity of 6–8 million when the Indian market’s current demand is 10–12 million.
The Indian government’s production-linked incentive for white goods was launched in 2021 to address situations precisely like this one and motivate industrialists to move their supply chains into India, yet the problem persists.
In today’s story, Shristi looks at how India’s air-conditioner manufacturing sector has fallen behind and what suppliers are trying to do to remedy the situation.
