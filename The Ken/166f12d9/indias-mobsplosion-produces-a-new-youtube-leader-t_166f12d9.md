Welcome back from a mid-week Diwali filled with lights, sweets, family and love!
The “mobsplosion” caused by the “Jio Effect” caused a five-fold rise in India’s mobile data usage, making it the world’s largest user base. And the resulting waves of mobile usage have started creating ripples so powerful they’re being felt around the world.
One ripple will be felt by Felix Arvid Ulf Kjellberg, aka PewDiePie, the world’s most popular YouTuber. The Swede has held YouTube’s top position since late 2013 - an eternity in the Internet world. He’s amassed over 69 million subscribers and over 19 billion views.
But sometime this month, PewDiePie will be dethroned by India’s leading music label, T-Series. Like a sprinter suddenly releasing a burst of energy reserves during final laps, T-Series has gone from 30 million subscribers at the beginning of 2018 to over 68 million even as we speak.
The rocket fuel for that spectacular acceleration was the byproduct of Reliance Jio’s entry and disruption of Indian telecom.
Mobsplosion.
But as Shashi tells us in his story today, T-Series may end up on the podium only to realise that PewDiePie still has the last laugh. And the gold trophy. Because all those millions of subscribers and billions of views are worth only a fraction since they’re from India.
Nonetheless, T-Series and its peers are realising that YouTube is becoming a significant source of royalty revenues. But can it be a sustainable one?
