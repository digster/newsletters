The journeys of Myntra and PhonePe within Flipkart couldn't be more different.

When the e-commerce major acquired fashion e-tailer Myntra in 2014, it was a coup. Myntra, a market leader, was key to giving Flipkart a leg-up on rival Amazon in fashion.

PhonePe, on the other hand, was a shot in the dark. When Flipkart acquired it in 2016, it didn't have a final product to speak of. Crucially, UPI—the real-time payments system it was meant to piggyback on—hadn't even launched.

Myntra was undoubtedly the favourite child. And, indeed, this remained true even when Walmart came knocking and bought Flipkart last year. At the time, the global retail titan ascribed a value of $6 billion to Myntra and $2 billion to PhonePe.

It's been just over a year since, and my, how things have changed.

PhonePe—now valued at close to $10 billion—is largely autonomous. It no longer needs to toe the Flipkart line, instead allowed to put its interests ahead of its parent company's. If reports are to be believed, it may soon be hived off.

Myntra, on the other hand, is facing an existential crisis. Before Walmart's entry, it had what it called 'Mission Mars'. A project to reach profitability by 2019. That now stands scrapped. Not because it didn't show promise, but because Walmart shifted the goalposts. From profitability to revenue generation. And to do this, it is pushing Myntra to alter the very identity that made it a market leader.

Growth has slowed, internal targets have been missed, and the distinction between Myntra and its mass-market cousin Flipkart has blurred.
