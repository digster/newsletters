Would you bet on a horse no one's betting on?

Let me back up a little. Bangalore-based Vardhman Health Specialties (the horse) had been looking for a taker for a while. It apparently knocked on many hospital doors—rumoured to be big names, major hospital chains—but none answered.

Then came the barely-three-year-old Aknamed. Last month, it scooped up Vardhman—not out of pity, but to strengthen its team—to become the largest supplier to hospitals in India. Today's story is about this unusual horse bettor.

Vardhman, which helps Aknamed add an oncology portfolio in its offering to hospitals, is #3 in its carefully curated list of drug distributor acquisitions. It has Delhi-based Impex Healthcare, which deals in specialty drugs for immunotherapy and critical care; and Mumbai-based Shreeji Distributors Pharma for generics. These two have contributed over Rs 100 crore to Aknamed's revenue, and that's a number Aknamed wants to raise to Rs 5,000 crore in a few years.

All three acquisitions (and its existing market) combined have given Aknamed over 750 hospitals across India. Not a small number.

The supplier plans to pause at horse #3 and focus on scaling. After all, it's staring at tough competition, especially when selling to hospitals—a hill two e-pharmacies almost died on.

Then there's MedikaBazaar. Now, this is a prized horse. Technically, what Aknamed is to offline play, MedikaBazaar is to online. Except, the Gurgram-based digital platform for medical products—running since 2013—has many years on Aknamed. And many clients, too.

What's Aknamed game plan to wrest some of that market share? To scale.

"You have to consolidate in this market, acquire scale in the future," said Naresh Chowhan, CEO of Vardhman Health, as quoted in today's story. But how you crack that scale?
