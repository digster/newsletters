Good Morning Ishan,
Question 1: Why did the obvious escape me? You know, strolling through cosmetic stores or scrolling through D2C apps selling skincare products, it was right there in front of me.
Q2: A reader tossed out an intriguing comparison: 5G versus AR/VR. Similar, right? they said. Wrong. Why exactly, though?
Q3: A company demerged a chunk of its business. It saw its stock soar. Does it happen often?
Q4: A regulatory U-turn caught everyone’s attention. A state-run heavyweight may have had a hand in it. But how do you make anyone care about regulatory manoeuvres?
Q5: Green stocks are taking a nosedive globally, sure. But does it rattle the cages of massive energy giants? Even if they’re killing it elsewhere?
Recently, I found myself absentmindedly sitting on a hillock and asking questions (not the ones above). While catching a short break from the deluge of emails, Slack pings, and mind-numbing online browsing, I was lost in a haze—resigned to the view. Rather than fretting about my situation, I decided to embrace it. I didn’t have a phone. Yet, I had a notepad where I started jotting down every mundane detail around me—from a man sweltering in a coat to a woman sporting a kaleidoscope of hair colours. Why? I kept asking myself—channelling my inner Ed Ruscha (the famous painter who found art in the banalities of everyday life).
I often wonder (like above) how my writer colleagues at The Ken find gems in the seemingly mundane news cycles, much like Ruscha’s knack for finding charm in American gas stations and apartment blocks.
Answer 1: Nuha’s sharp eye [caught something intriguing](https://the-ken.com/story/four-year-old-minimalist-does-for-skincare-what-loreal-couldnt/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)—cosmetic companies like Lakme are suddenly plastering chemical names all over their products. What gives? Turns out, it’s a story involving industry disruptors like Minimalist and The Ordinary.
A2: Seema hit the nail on the head with her response to the reader’s comparison between 5G and AR/VR. Sure, they’re both tech, but [the investment landscape tells a different story](https://the-ken.com/story/your-5g-phone-is-bleeding-airtel-and-jio-dry-is-3g-history-repeating-itself/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card). It’s these subtle insights that add spice to an already delectable story on a tech once seen as utopian.
A3: Seetha pulled back the curtain on Aditya Birla Fashion and Retail’s demerger strategy, revealing a move that’s raising eyebrows among investors. Seems like there’s [more to this spinoff than meets the eye](https://the-ken.com/story/whats-aditya-birla-fashion-without-brands-like-allen-solly-and-louis-philippe/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card).
A4: Anand and Narayanan posed the question: “LIC or IRDAI, will the real insurance regulator please stand up?” That got the gears turning. A headline from the editorial—“How life insurers stop at nothing to get you to renew a terrible policy”—was the hook that reeled readers in. Admit it, [you wanted to click on that one](https://the-ken.com/story/how-life-insurers-stop-at-nothing-to-get-you-to-renew-a-terrible-policy/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card).
A5: The CFO of a company with a US$2.7 billion market cap [explained in response to Katrina’s inquiry](https://the-ken.com/story/a-2-7b-philippine-energy-giants-blue-chip-status-is-at-risk/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card): “We do believe we have a very good story to tell. It’s just a pity that the market doesn’t seem to recognise that.”
