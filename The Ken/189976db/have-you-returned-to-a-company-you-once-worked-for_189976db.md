What’s the one thing common to all our careers? It is that they never really take a linear path. Often, careers are a messy journey of exploration, punctuated by purple patches, liminal periods, U-turns, and straight lines.
And today, more than ever, your career path could be circuitous.
Like returning to an employer you left earlier.
It’s a very interesting decision, and one way to reinvent your career. But it is a choice that is relatively undiscussed.
On Cost to Company, The Ken’s podcast on careers and workplaces, we hope to offer useful principles and best practices that can guide you through such career changes. But to do this well requires your contribution. As a host, I love featuring The Ken community on our podcast; it is your stories and experiences that help us shape our episodes in meaningful ways.
For one of our next episodes, we want to delve deeper into the story of employees who rejoined a company they once left. Especially in the past two-three years.
So if you are someone, or know of someone, who recently went back to a former employer, please take this survey. And trust me, it will take only about 5 minutes to complete.
[Here](https://theken.typeform.com/to/kpYebGW4) is the survey.
If you are interested in receiving the results, please submit your email address at the end of the survey.
