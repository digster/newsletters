During last week’s festivities, my newlywed friends hosted our annual holiday party in their new apartment. The vibe was fun: great food, exquisite wine, Christmas music playing on their turntable, and interesting games.
One of the games we played was word-guessing via association. Say, if the word on the card is 'kitchen', the player can drop clues like 'place', 'cook', 'food', 'bake', etc., for others to guess.
If the word was 'advertising', I would naturally associate it with media-centric spaces, such as newspapers, television, and social-media platforms like YouTube and Instagram. I wouldn’t think of a superapp like Grab.
So, when I learnt in Rahul’s story that Grab has ventured into advertising, I imagined it would be groundbreaking. However, four years after GrabAds, it still hasn't.
Advertisements usually thrive under one crucial condition: they must be visible. In terms of traffic, Grab definitely has that down, with 34.5 million app users and 4 million merchants.
But in reality, Grab’s ad business is struggling. Its revenue target is reportedly behind by at least 50%, and the company has been cutting commissions to reduce costs.
Grab has tried various models to find the right balance for its new venture, yet yields have been patchy.
