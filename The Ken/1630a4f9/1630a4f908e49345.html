Good Morning Ishan,
 
You've heard of the seven deadly sins. Greed. Lust. Gluttony. Pride. Wrath. Envy. Sloth. I studied literature, so these seven were the kind of suspect friends Snow White probably had in that home with seven little people. Or perhaps, she was the suspect one. Who knows, eh?
 
I'm not writing this to tell you about these sins - to be fair, they're fairly self-descriptive. Nope. It's just that I was going through this week's stories to write to you and I realised something. We largely covered five not-so-deadly coping mechanisms over our five stories. Let's have a look; see if you agree with me?
 
The week started with Arundhati's piece on MasterCard and Visa and their post-demonetisation POA (Plan Of Action). The two card networks suffer from what Arundhati aptly describes as FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out), especially with UPI in the game. Their plan ahead (and all they've put in place) makes the story. 
 
FOMO was followed by JOMO (Joy Of Missing Out). That's right. Max Healthcare doesn't bother itself with the norm and does what it thinks is best for it. Ruhi's story on Tuesday was about Max choosing to opt for at-home healthcare—a contrarian bet.
 
On Wednesday, Venkat put out our FREE story of the week. The Karnataka elections are going WhatsApp-first, and the BJP and the Congress, for once, are headed the same way. We'll call this one "when you can't beat 'em, join 'em". "With general elections in about 12 months, [this] gives us a sneak peek into what India’s digital election campaigns would probably look like," Venkat writes.
 
Airtel and India were a team. Africa was the unwelcome third-wheel. Today, Africa keeps Airtel's balance sheet in check, for India is looking Jio, talking Jio. Shashidhar's Thursday piece termed Africa Airtel's "masterstroke of foresight". Airtel has much to cope with at this juncture, but what it has is a bankable friend. One it now recognises.
 
Sumanth and Patanjali closed the week with Snapdeal and how it's finally providing an exit for VC Kalaari Capital, its first institutional investor. The duo retraced Kalaari's Snapdeal investment, right from inception. Honestly, this one's my favourite mechanism. To let go.
 
I hope you see what I see. And I hope you have a great weekend that helps you cope with the coming week.
