Amidst a sea of pleas from individuals looking for ICU beds, ventilator beds, home ICU care, and of course, oxygen, last week, a new set of alarming posts began popping up on social media. Hospitals begging for oxygen.
While some got oxygen in the nick of time, some others weren't so lucky. Hospitals across the country have reported deaths due to a lack of oxygen.
India's demand for Liquid Medical Oxygen (LMO) has gone from 700 tonnes per day (TPD) pre-pandemic to 2,800 TPD last year to 8,000 TPD now.
Of this, Delhi, the national capital, alone needs 700 TPD, but it's unable to arrange even 500. Maharashtra, meanwhile, needs as much as 1,550 TPD of oxygen for its Covid patients. After a week of mobilising its trains, the Ministry of Railways was able to ferry just over 450 metric tonnes of oxygen, all of which went to Delhi, Maharashtra, and Uttar Pradesh.
And most of this LMO goes to the big hospitals. What happens to the smaller hospitals? What happens to the other states? What happens to the smaller hospitals in the other states?
The first problem is procurement. If these hospitals are able to procure LMO, then they have to pay through their noses for transportation. And when hospitals need to pay huge sums for essentials, what would the patients' predicament be?
In today's free story, Maitri unpacks India's oxygen scarcity, the help slowly coming in from big industries and other countries, and how and why it may still not be enough: [https://the-ken.com/story/no-room-to-breathe-indias-oxygen-scarcity-isnt-just-about-production/](https://the-ken.com/story/no-room-to-breathe-indias-oxygen-scarcity-isnt-just-about-production/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) (12-minute read)
