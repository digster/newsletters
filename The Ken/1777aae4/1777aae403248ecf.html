Good Morning Ishan,
 
By now, you would know that we have a fondness for the dramatic here at The Ken. A charismatic founder being forced out of her creation. A down-and-out company meticulously plotting a comeback. A market leader flaming out spectacularly.
  
We keep reminding ourselves that we are, first and foremost, storytellers. But we are equally mindful that each one of us is something else, too. And I found just the right word for it in the book Factfulness: Ten Reasons We're Wrong About the World–and Why Things Are Better Than You Think. 
 
"Possibilist."
 
This is how the authors define it: "Someone who neither hopes without reason, nor fears without reason, someone who constantly resists the overdramatic worldview."
 
How is it connected to what we do? Even our juiciest stories don't pass muster if not built on a foundation of painstaking reporting, unassailable data, and informed analysis. And that quite holds true for the past week's line-up. 
 
We couldn't have asked for a better start than Pranav's extensively reported, detail-rich feature on India's leading B2B e-commerce platform, Udaan. It's a tale of how the company bounced back from a near-death experience at the start of the lockdown and pulled off a $280 million fundraise last month at a $3 billion valuation. 
 
On Tuesday, we returned with a fascinating story on another unicorn. Biocon Biologics, a unit of Asia's largest biotech, has seen its valuation rise from $3 billion to $4.1 billion in just a year. But, as Seema writes in the piece, the hard part comes now for the company, as it tries to justify its worth by tapping bigger markets and increasing margins.
 
A day later, Pratap examined India's ambitious push to set up 1.5 million WiFi hotspots by the end of the year, up from 500,000 now. Tech giants like Google and Facebook are key to that plan. But will they play ball, not having had much success in the past? It's a free story, so feel free to share it widely. 
 
Thursday saw us doing a post-mortem of Japanese conglomerate SoftBank Group Corp's clean energy ambitions in India. In 2015, its high-profile CEO Masayoshi Son said SoftBank would lead a $20 billion investment to build solar farms in India. But now, humbled by the complexities of an ultra-competitive sector, I report, SoftBank is on its way out of India. 
 
Then came Bhumika's insightful take on the latest iteration of cloud kitchens in India. Businesses like Kitchens@ and Kitchens Centre are offering white-label cloud kitchens to restaurants that don't want to spend on setting up their own kitchens. But even as they help eateries scale, the new cloud kitchen players will find it hard to scale themselves. 
 
We rounded off the week with yet another edition of our weekly newsletter, The Nutgraf. Shunning the theatrics of headlines, Praveen distils the three factors responsible for the ballooning of the most worrying number in India's budget: the fiscal deficit. 
 
Oh, by the way, we also put out the second edition of our podcast, Unofficial Sources, this week. If you're curious to know why two of India's hottest tech companies, Flipkart and Zomato, want to go public, you should give it a listen on our website, app, or wherever you get your podcasts.
