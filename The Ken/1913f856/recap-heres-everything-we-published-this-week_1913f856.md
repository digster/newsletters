[Allen Career Institute sours on Kota, the city that made it a test-prep behemoth](https://the-ken.com/story/allen-career-institute-sours-on-kota-the-city-that-made-it-a-test-prep-behemoth/?utm_source=weekly_email&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=weekly_recap_card)
With students wanting to move out of Kota’s pressure-cooker model, the test-prep giant finds itself being one, at least for its 4,000-strong staff
Coaching giant Allen Career Institute has never seen its enrolments drop in Kota as much as in 2024—by over 30%. With Kota’s reputation hit by frequent student suicides and the nearby town Sikar attracting more coaching institutes, Allen’s stronghold seems to be hit. That translated into reduced pay for staff. The company also hired people in other cities, in a possible move to shift its base out of Kota. Now, it’s banking on the expansion and its bets in the digital space to balance the scales, even as the industry says it’s eating its own business.

What makes Delhi startups resilient, and what happens to Bengaluru?
For a long time, most people agreed that if you wanted to start a tech company, the “default” place to do it was Bengaluru. It had everything that young startups wanted—a vibrant city, access to tech talent, VCs, and even great weather. For over a decade, some of the most notable startups emerged from Bengaluru. Everyone believed this was an advantage, i.e., the ecosystem made these companies stronger, resilient, and more innovative.But of late… we aren’t so sure about that. Even though most people in Bengaluru believe that they are doing exciting, innovative work, it’s the startups from another city that are really succeeding as businesses. And that’s Delhi.
