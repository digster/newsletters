Good Morning Ishan,

If imitation is the sincerest form of flattery, Elasticrun has arrived. 

You might not have heard about it so far. But this Softbank-backed B2B e-commerce platform—worth $1.4 billion (yes, a unicorn)—is being emulated by its much bigger, more famous, and significantly more loss-making rival, Udaan. 

Elasticrun, it seems, has managed to crack an elusive code—hyper-scale without upsetting incumbents. Which, in this case, are distributors of FMCG products. 

Indeed, Sandeep Deshmukh, co-founder and chief executive of the seven-year-old company, says, "I'm good news for distributors and brands. We increase FMCG companies' direct-distribution network rather than cannibalise it." 

Now, that's a striking counter to the thesis of players like Udaan and Jiomart eating into the business of distributors and angering them. And Elasticrun does this by going counter-intuitive—seeking its fortune in low-density Bharat instead of crowded India. Unlike its bigger rivals, it operates in the country's villages (as many as 120,000) and not in the cities.

But wait, isn't this a logistical nightmare on multiple fronts—transport, warehousing, people, et al.? Not really, says Deshmukh. The distribution challenges in the hinterlands are what make rural areas a more natural destination than cities, he explains to Seetharaman, the author of today's story.
