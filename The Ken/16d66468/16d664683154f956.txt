If there is one sector besides healthcare that is recession-proof, it has to be education. It's a years-long commitment once you sign on the dotted line.

However, nothing is tech-proof — not even education. As Aakash Education Services Limited (AESL), a 30-year-old institute and a leader in the offline test prep market, will testify. With edtechs like Unacademy and Vedantu stealing away not just students but also talent from offline coaching, it's hardly a choice.

After six years of dabbling in digital in some shape or form, online courses still account for only 3% of AESL's revenue. The aim is to boost that to 25% in the next four years, CEO Aakash Chaudhry says. 

Easier said than done, though, and Chaudhry knows that. "It's such a complex time. I'm taking new decisions every day without really knowing how they'll pan out."

How would he? On one hand AESL has to play catch up with well-funded startups and on the other, contend with a slowdown in sign-ups up for prep courses. On top of that, its own brand and legacy are both a boon and a bane.

It does have an ace up its sleeve though — a $150 million infusion from Blackstone Capital last year. In fact, AESL chose the PE giant's funds over those it could have raised from a planned IPO. Not that it needed the money, Chaudhry insists.

What it could do with some of, though, is Blackstone's input as the 30-year-old AESL heaves itself onto a new course. However, is a $150 million seatbelt enough?
