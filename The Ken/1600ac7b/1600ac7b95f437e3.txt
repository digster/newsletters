Good Morning Ishan,

Ridiculously low bid prices. Financially anaemic bidders. Renegotiated power purchase agreements…

Commercially speaking, solar energy in India isn’t looking very bright. Solar power developers say it is tough to make money at the current prices.

Well, we had the prime minister at the Global Entrepreneurship Summit in Hyderabad on Tuesday take pride in the fact that “solar energy generation has increased over 80% in the last year”.

Indeed it has, and yet a lot of things in solar are not adding up, including the recent announcement of more auctions by February. Earlier this month, the chief executive of Acme Solar, which made waves in May for bidding at the record lowest price, expressed regret at his company’s aggressive bidding.

To cut a long story short, the question that everyone asked of solar a few years ago—is it sustainable—is worth asking once again.

Solar module prices have begun rising; there’s a talk now of an import duty; tariffs are in free fall; PPAs are witnessing record renegotiations; power producers are engaged in massive financial engineering; more auctions are around the corner. Is anyone thinking even five years ahead?

So, what’s the hurry? Election promises. Even though one government agency doesn’t know what the other is doing. On its part, the private sector has taken all the risk. But to what avail?
