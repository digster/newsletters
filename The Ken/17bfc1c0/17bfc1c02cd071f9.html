Good Morning Ishan,
 
I have spent the better part of the last year and a half in my bedroom. Usually, I can be found in the corner, at my study desk, working. Before Covid hit, I was in the habit of walking 10,000 steps daily. The pedometer strapped to my wrist ensured I didn't falter. Walking from the office to the metro station, crossing the road to grab a cup of afternoon tea, and hopping across to have meetings with sources were all commonplace. But during the pandemic, my step count on some days reduced to less than 1,000. As habits changed, walking 50 metres to the neighbourhood park became a challenge. 
 
Last month, I decided to turn things around. I felt a need to break away from my urban routine, and embraced it. On a whim, I signed up for a six-day trek in the remote hills of Kashmir. What lay ahead was an arduous 50-kilometre route made up of steep ascents and descents, rocky in most parts, slippery in others. The trek wasn't easy—over the course of four days, we ascended from 7,000 feet to the summit, which stood at 13,000 feet. My pedometer clocked an average of 15,000 steps each day.

Standing atop the summit, I spied the lake we had camped at the previous night. It sparkled like a giant blue Mediterranean sapphire, studded with tiny emerald-like mini islands at its heart. On the other side of the mountain was a path that led to the erstwhile Silk Route, leading deep into Gilgit Baltistan and adorned with its own share of glistening lakes.
 
That experience held valuable lessons for me, especially with regard to what we do here at The Ken. Each of our staffers climbs mountains every day—reporting, editing, designing—eventually chiselling out a sharply told story for our readers. Some days are easy, others hard. When we are tired, we pause for a moment or two and breathe. But the beauty is in moving. So let’s walk together through the week gone by. 
 
On Monday, Sumanth narrated the story of Girish Mathrubootham, founder of software-as-a-service (SaaS) company Freshworks. Well on it’s way to an IPO now, the story chronicles how Mathrubootham built a solid value proposition for an Indian firm.
 
Next up, Munsif had an exclusive scoop. In it, he revealed that Amazon India is building a truly touchless payment system based on Alexa that can support everything from UPI to credit cards. It’s a concrete step towards monetisation of the voice assistant, and would be a first for the company worldwide. Crucially, the project is based out of India.
 
On Wednesday, Pratap did a deep dive on why the latest JioPhone has been delayed, what that means for telco leader Reliance Jio, and how the likes of Bharti Airtel are protecting themselves from Jio's latest attempt at disruption. It was also the free story of the week, so do share it widely. 
 
Thursday saw Jaspreet take a look at investment platform smallcase. The company has enjoyed the gains of being the first-mover in the bundled investing space—it has over 3.5 million users and has raised $62 million in funding so far. But with others like WealthDesk and ICICI showing up to eat the cake, smallcase could have a price war on its hands
 
Our final story for the week saw Janane reporting on the hottest pet-care startup in town—Heads Up For Tails. Last month, riding on the back of a generational shift in pet parenting, Heads Up For Tails bagged a $37 million funding round, arguably the biggest in India's pet care space. The omnichannel player has ambitious retail plans—250 stores in the next three years—but so do rivals like Just Dogs and Glenands. Also, don’t miss the guest appearance by Mort, one of The Ken's many cats.
 
Then, of course, there was The Nutgraf, a weekly newsletter from Praveen. This week's edition draws parallels between the Indian government’s relief to debt-ridden telcos and the Zamindari system propagated by the British way back in the 18th century.
 
That's all for this week. When are you taking that long overdue break? If you have any thoughts, write in.
