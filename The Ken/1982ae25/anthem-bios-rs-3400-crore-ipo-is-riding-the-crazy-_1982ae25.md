Good Morning Ishan,
Anthem Biosciences lists on the bourses today with a near Rs 3,400 crore IPO. And a perfectly timed pitch.
It’s priced above Biocon’s Syngene, the first mover in India’s contract-biotech playbook, and just below Cohance, the PE-fuelled giant stitched together by Advent. In other words, Anthem has placed itself right in the middle of biotech’s ongoing identity crisis: science or scale, R&D or real estate, upside or exit?
This moment of market madness began when Cohance sparked a rerating frenzy, pushing biotech valuations into the stratosphere. That frenzy, stoked by geopolitical shifts and weight-loss drug FOMO, has made India’s contract development and manufacturing orgs, or CDMOs, the hottest new picks in pharma.
Enter Ajay Bhardwaj. Years ago, he stormed out of Biocon and built Anthem as a biotech twin with more fermenters. Now, he’s ringing the market bell.
Yet, for all the biologics jargon, this business is still what one CEO called “a glorified rental model”. You rent your factory, follow a manufacturer’s recipe, and hope your margins hold up long enough for PE investors to cash out. Still, Bhardwaj has played this well. Why else would Anthem confidently price itself at 70X earnings?
Seema’s story today is a [cracking read](https://the-ken.com/story/anthem-bios-rs-3395-crore-ipo-is-riding-the-crazy-run-that-cohance-started/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter)—it tracks the hysteria, the geopolitics, the PE machinations, and one founder’s well-timed return to the game.
