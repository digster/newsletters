Good Morning Ishan,

The good lord knows I know nothing about almonds. You, the discerning reader, might appreciate that I have never been to California either.

I have never bought almonds. 

I have never gifted them, in those horribly, distasteful looking transparent, plastic boxes, carefully compartmentalised with other “dry fruits”.

I have seen close friends and distant family consume them in questionable quantities and judged them. 

I have only consumed almonds when forced. Force is not equal to hunger.

So you can imagine then, that I silently chuckled and then launched into serious Internet research when my colleague Roshni called almonds “brain food”. Who knew that there is a country hungry for brain food in general, and California almonds in particular. The answer is India, of course. Data from Roshni’s almond story today makes for sober reading. Figures by the Almond Board of California (ABC) show that the United States accounted for 82% of imported almonds in India in 2018. And in the 2017-2018 crop year (an almond crop year is from August to July), shipments to India touched 100,000 tonnes, up 19.7% from the previous crop year’s 83,500 tonnes.

India is now on its way to becoming the world’s largest importer of American almonds.

To quote a writer who has recently recovered from a tragic season of national elections: Things weren’t always like this.

Back in the day, almonds were grown in Iran and Kashmir. Both geographies have seen better days, and while Asian cultivation of almonds has fallen, California has stepped up its brain food game. Roshni says ABC has the resources, the bees, the water and clever endorsement by Bollywood celebrity Karisma Kapoor to sell its almonds. Today’s story is a delightful read of what happens when countries get capitalism right.
