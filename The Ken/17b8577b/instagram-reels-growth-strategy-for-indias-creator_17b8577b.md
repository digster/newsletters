In the months leading up to Instagram's official launch in October 2010, founders Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger picked its first users very carefully. They courted people who were good photographers and designers, with a high number of Twitter followers.
As Bloomberg journalist Sarah Frier writes in her book No Filter: The Inside Story of Instagram, "Those first users would help set the right artistic tone, creating good content for everyone else to look at, in what was essentially the first-ever Instagram influencer campaign, years before that would become a concept."
Eleven years and an acquisition by Facebook later, influencer campaigns are run of the mill at Instagram and other social media companies in today's creator era. More than 200 million Instagram users have over 50,000 followers, and they all make a life out of digital validation.
A key product in this creator economy is Instagram's short-video platform, Reels. Inspired by TikTok, Reels has profited from an incredible stroke of luck in India. It was launched in the country in July 2020, around the same time when the Indian government banned TikTok and dozens of other Chinese apps.
Since then, Reels has battled several TikTok clones, but it's the clear leader. Reels users from India are uploading nearly six million short-form videos on average every day, compared to 2.5 million per day at its nearest competitor, Moj, which was also launched in July 2020. Reels, of course, had a substantial pedestal to build from—Instagram and Facebook's estimated 210 million and 340 million Indian users, respectively.
