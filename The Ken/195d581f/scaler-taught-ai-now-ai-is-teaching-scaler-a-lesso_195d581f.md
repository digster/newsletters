Good Morning Ishan,
Abhimanyu Saxena and Anshuman Singh couldn’t have chosen a better time when they decided to change tack with their interview-prep startup in 2019.
Online education was about to have its moment in the sun, thanks to the pandemic, and enterprising techies had begun to realise the importance of artificial intelligence in their careers.
Scaler Academy, backed by Peak XV Partners and Tiger Global, combined the two and offered AI courses, among others. For a while, there was no looking back. In the two years to FY24, its revenue soared 6X to Rs 384 crore.
But now, as Atul writes in his story today, “AI isn’t just eating into its graduates’ jobs—it’s coming for Scaler’s own job as a tutor.” Companies are replacing entry-level developer jobs with AI—the exact ones Scaler trained people for.
Even as rivals like Upgrad sold courses across management, marketing, law, and data science, Saxena and Singh resolutely stuck to Scaler’s tech-only USP.
But what is its pitch in a world where AI is not just what it teaches, but also its most formidable competitor? Read Atul’s story [here](https://the-ken.com/story/scaler-taught-ai-now-ai-is-teaching-scaler-a-lesson/).
[Should you invest the first two years of your career doing strategy consulting?](https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/should-you-invest-the-first-two-years-of-your-career-doing-strategy-consulting/)
Even now, many might answer “yes” to that question. But there are a lot more factors at play than before. In episode 35 of Two by Two, our guests had a lot to share about their days as consultants and how that career is changing now. But is a career in consulting today as promising as it used to be a decade or two ago? Are the consulting companies serving the best companies out there?
Throw in the impact AI can have on a consultant’s work today. And that raises a few questions. Will consulting companies go back to their original vision of becoming CEO advisors? Will students continue to chase consulting firms as a lucrative and promising career option? And so on…
Joining hosts Praveen Gopal Krishnan and Rohin Dharmakumar are Rahul Chaudhary, co-founder of Treebo and Pragya Batra, co-founder of Quirksmith, both of whom have previously donned the hats of consultants.
