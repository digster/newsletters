India is the world’s second most populous country. Its economy is the 6th largest. That means, even stuff that flies under the radar (or falls between the cracks) of its financial regulatory systems are pretty big.

For instance, over half a million Indians are estimated to have collectively invested over Rs 4,000 crore (~$600 million) in a multitude of cryptocurrency Ponzi schemes. Over 50 at last count.

As the two apex regulators, central bank RBI and securities regulator SEBI, reportedly squabble over who needs to regulate cryptocurrencies, conmen are making merry.

It's easy. 1. Combine “crypto”, “coin”, “bit” and “value” into making random sentences. 2. Make outlandish statements about the imminent rise in valuation of your virtual currency. 3. Harness buyers into the age-old multi-level marketing (MLM) pyramid selling model, so each one who gets suckered has an incentive to recoup his money by suckering a few more. 4. Decamp the country before getting caught. 

Sidhartha, our most recent staff writer, opens his account with a reported story on how shysters have latched on to cryptocurrencies. It’s also our weekly free read.
