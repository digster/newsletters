That fundamentals in life—healthy eating, regular exercise, restful sleep—are important is a no-brainer. It ultimately comes down to whether we are doing them right.
Most famous figures, from basketball legend Michael Jordan and Google co-founder Larry Bird to Mahatma Gandhi, have stressed the importance of fundamentals at one point or another. And the message is always the same—get the basics right and you will succeed. A strong foundation is everything. Once you crack these fundamentals, it becomes a way of life.
This week at The Ken, we explored the fundamentals of some of the largest businesses and personalities in India.
My personal favourite this week was the story of Abhijit Mishra, who is doing an important job of digging into the fundamentals of law and finance through Public Interest Litigations. He uses PILs as a way to hold institutions like the RBI and big companies like Google accountable. Courts are already wary of PIL litigants and their motives, and big institutions aren’t used to being questioned. Taking them on has already cost Mishra. In Sumanth's
[interview with](https://the-ken.com/story/the-interview-kunal-shah/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) the founder and CEO of credit card payments startup CRED, Kunal Shah, we tried to understand the fundamentals of CRED and its business model. We got a peek into the mind of a person who could have taken the easy way out and hung up his boots to be on the beach somewhere but chose to get back in the game.
In our story on Friday, Rohin
[dug into](https://the-ken.com/story/the-unbearable-heaviness-of-being-jio-platforms/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) the fundamentals of Jio Platform’s valuation and the company’s way forward. It was a wonderfully nuanced story about where Jio Platforms stands in comparison to FAANG (Facebook, Apple, Amazon, Netflix, and Google), the group of tech powerhouses with which comparisons will eventually arise.
Prasannata, on Wednesday, dove into the fundamentals of e-commerce and the difficulty of shifting from a retail to a direct-to-customer model for a niche company like
[Epigamia](https://the-ken.com/story/epigamia-pours-into-digital/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). The greek yoghurt maker must deal with the problems of catering to disaggregated demand as well as address last-mile delivery issues.
On Monday, Seetharaman
[wrote about](https://the-ken.com/story/nisaba-godrej-consumer-products/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) the return of the family at one of India’s best-known FMCG brands, Godrej. In some ways, it's a fundamental shift in strategy after 10+ years of professionals from outside the family running the organisation. Nisaba Godrej is taking the company’s reigns not only during a pandemic but also at a time when the company is up against a host of well-established competitors.
