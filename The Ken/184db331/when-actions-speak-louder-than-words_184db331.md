This weekend I decided to attend an Urdu language literary festival in New Delhi. I have been surrounded by art, music, literary discussions, poetry recitals, masterclasses, and even food in the past two days.
Based on the Perso-Arabic script, Urdu became a significant part of India due to several invasions in the past. It is an Eighth Schedule language in India, and the Constitution recognises its status and cultural heritage.
But earlier this year, a media article
[explored](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/is-urdu-dying-where-it-was-born/articleshow/89326549.cms) if the language was dying slowly even in Uttar Pradesh, where it was supposedly born. So, it’s heartening to see a festival aiming to celebrate such a historical language.
