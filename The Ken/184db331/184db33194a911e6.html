Good Morning Ishan,
 
This weekend I decided to attend an Urdu language literary festival in New Delhi. I have been surrounded by art, music, literary discussions, poetry recitals, masterclasses, and even food in the past two days.

Based on the Perso-Arabic script, Urdu became a significant part of India due to several invasions in the past. It is an Eighth Schedule language in India, and the Constitution recognises its status and cultural heritage.

But earlier this year, a media article explored if the language was dying slowly even in Uttar Pradesh, where it was supposedly born. So, it’s heartening to see a festival aiming to celebrate such a historical language.

Meanwhile, even we at The Ken explored some bull runs, great influences, and high ambitions in these uncertain times as a general theme.

On Monday, Anand unpacked Adani Enterprises' upcoming further public offering (FPO). The company’s announcement is a well-timed, strategic move with the stock price at a record high. But new investors could baulk at the stock's sky-high valuation, driven by the huge rally in the past two-and-a-half years.

Pratap on Tuesday laid out the stark differences among stakeholders on key provisions of the Draft Telecom Bill. This impacts broad areas of India's expanding digital economy, and the story looks at whether the Bill could sail through to Parliament in spite of them. He says not. Several changes in the draft—primarily the ones on OTT services and the telecom regulator—are likely to be unveiled soon.

On Wednesday, Shruti narrated an exciting saga of certain companies bucking larger downturns by cashing in on the trends. 'Edfin' companies—the ones that provide counselling and financing help to students to study abroad—are bringing home the big bucks by tapping into the increasing trend. Firms like Leap and Leverage Edu are raising money and enlarging their workforce to meet the demand.

Rounak on Thursday delved into how Piramal Finance has had to rethink its bet on housing loans due to the pandemic. It pivoted to tying up with more fintech firms: a road to more customers but marred by fintech's own funding crunch and regulatory challenges.

Finally, on Friday, Aayush and Gaurav took the B2B e-commerce platform Udaan as a case in point to show how a changing environment is making high-growth startups rethink their strategies. The company has laid off ~500 employees over the past six months and plans to sack more while it puts its house in order by streamlining its operations.
