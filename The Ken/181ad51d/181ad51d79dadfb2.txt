Zalora is the "patient zero" of the internet economy in Southeast Asia.

It was one of the first Rocket Internet ventures in this region, founded over a decade ago. Before that, venture-backed startup building was pretty much unheard of. Zalora shaped and moulded many people who went on to become prominent entrepreneurs. For example, Nadiem Makarim. He had his start as a Zalora co-founder in Indonesia and went on to build Gojek—which is now part of GoTo, Indonesia's largest internet company.

Zalora undoubtedly played a role as a catalyst for Southeast Asia's tech sector. But what has Zalora itself really achieved?

It is one online shopping destination among many in Southeast Asia; by far not the biggest one. It is still posting losses (albeit narrowing ones.) It has expanded into, but also sold off or consolidated operations, in multiple geographies. It has had frequent management changes.

It seems that, now, under CEO Gunjan Soni, Zalora is making one last effort to stabilise the business and turn profits. Part of this pivot is an expansion into India, where it is partnering with e-commerce site Myntra.

India is obviously a huge market, and Soni knows it well. She was Myntra's CMO prior to joining Zalora as Group CEO. But will things really be that different in India for Zalora?

Some of its old problems, like internal culture clashes, still plague the firm.
