Growing disenchantment with India’s top retirement-funds manager means a big opportunity for its closest competitor—the National Pension Scheme

Delivered to Ishan, Subscriber to The Ken.

Fri, 29 Mar 2024

VIEW IN BROWSER
The Spotlight by Arundhati Ramanathan
Deputy Editor

Good Morning Ishan,

Earlier this month, my colleague Anand wrote a story on the alarming claims crisis that besieged the Employees’ Provident Fund Organisation (EPFO).

In that story, we asked our readers a question on why they made an EPFO contribution. About 30% of those who took the poll said they didn’t know why they made the contribution—just that it gets deducted from their payslips.

It’s almost as if the EPFO has no rival. But there is. In fact, a better one: The National Pension System (NPS), a market-linked retirement and pension scheme.

It was established in 2004, almost more than 50 years after the EPFO. Yet its assets under management are already about Rs 11.5 lakh crore (US$137.9 billion), roughly half that of the EPFO.

Over 15,000 corporates, including oil-to-media conglomerate Reliance Industries and IT services giant Infosys, are now part of the NPS, The Ken has learnt.

Can there be an en masse transition from EPF to NPS? Read Anand’s insightful sequel on the EPFO crisis to find out.

Is India’s unemployment a skills problem or a jobs problem?

It’s not exactly surprising that the 2024 India Employment Report found that 83% of the unemployed in India are youth.

Still, the scale of it makes you wince.

The International Labour Organisation (ILO), which published the report, said that the first thing the country needs to do to fix this as part of its five-point plan is promoting job creation.

I was trawling through research to see why this is a jobs problem first and not a skill-related one and stumbled on this 2017 report by researchers from the World Bank group. They found that for every 100 people offered vocational training, fewer than 3 will find a job they would not have otherwise.

Now, if it’s a jobs problem first, the challenge is not just about creating millions of jobs but the right ones as well. TN Ninan, chairman of Business Standard, recently argued that the answer could lie in creating farm-related jobs. This means those not on the farm but off it if enterprises look to add more value to crops farmed.

With the report dropping during peak election season, promises will be made, and the fix to this vexed issue could seem closer than ever before—at least until the India Employment Report 2025 report drops again.

My experience in this process indicates that it is always helpful if you can make a personal visit to the concerned EPFO to follow up on your claim. There are case workers who work on each claim, and if you can identify and work with the case worker assigned to your claim, the process can be expedited.
