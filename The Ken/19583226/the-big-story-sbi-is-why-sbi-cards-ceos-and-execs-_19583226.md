Good Morning Ishan,
In Mickey 17, a recently released dystopian romp, Bong Joon-ho (of Parasite fame) sends Robert Pattinson (of Twilight fame) on a dangerous mission. Every time he dies, they print a new version of him and send him back out. Same job, same problems—just a new face. Well, sort of!
Which, come to think of it, is exactly how SBI Cards works.
Every two years, the State Bank of India ships in a fresh CEO to run India’s only listed credit-card company. These bosses arrive with zero credit-card experience, big ambitions that often don’t match reality, and smaller paycheques than the people they manage. By the time they figure things out, their term is up—and the cycle starts again.
It’s going… badly.
The current CEO roasts his staff for earning more than him.
His predecessors wasted crores on a buy-now-pay-later project that never launched.
The company just slipped from second to third place in market share while HDFC and ICICI pulled ahead.
Meanwhile, senior executives—sick of the chaos—are leaving for better jobs. Those who stick around joke that if you see a car speeding toward SBI Cards’ office in the morning, it’s probably someone trying to avoid losing a day’s pay for being 30 minutes late.
And now, on 1 April, a new boss—Salila Pande—is about to step into the mess. Will she just be another “expendable” in the loop—or will she be SBI Cards’ Mickey 18 and finally break the cycle?
It only gets weirder from here—and Rounak’s got the [Big Story](https://the-ken.com/story/sbi-is-why-sbi-cards-ceos-and-execs-cant-stand-each-other/).
