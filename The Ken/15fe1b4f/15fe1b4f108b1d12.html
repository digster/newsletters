Kishore Biyani’s Future Group calls it Retail 3.0. I won’t tell you what 1.0 and 2.0 are; for that, you must read the story. What I’ll tell you is this bit.  
 
So, I went back to the good old forgotten years of retail. Just to understand what the conversation was back then, on Indians looking to buy stuff and the business model around it. Rummaging through old articles, I chanced upon this bit, straight from Pantaloon Retail India Limited’s Annual Report in 2003: “India is different. And so we thought we would be better off trying to create an Indian model of retailing. We believe that we have been able to capture the imagination of Indian customers. We have emerged as a fast-growing retailer with three formats of retailing…” That’s Pantaloons. Big Bazaar and Food Bazaar. “Retail is organised to the extent of 85% in the USA, and in India, it is just at 2%. So everybody is surprised at Pantaloon not aping any western model. The answer to this is very simple. We believe that India is a market that is distinctly different from others.”
 
So, 14 years hence, is Retail 3.0 different? Indian? 
 
Here’s what Future Group wants to do. The company plans to set up a store every 2km and plans on 1,100 stores by FY18 and then to grow beyond. These members get a 10% discount on the total bill and first access to certain products. You can buy stuff from the app. Home Delivery. Pick up. Or somehow create a predictive shopping list so that you can enjoy the true technological advancements of the year 2017.
