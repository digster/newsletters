Good Morning Ishan,
 
To translate a popular Hindi adage, the poet manages to reach even where the sun doesn’t.
 
I was reminded of this when I learnt how JioFiber is laying fibre out in the boondocks of Uttar Pradesh. Literally going into the interiors of this northern state, fully aware there might not be any return on investment. But then, when Jio is in “project expansion” mode, nothing comes in its way. Not sunlight, not ROI.
 
But for all you know, JioFiber may get a reality check sooner than it had imagined. We did say just before its commercial rollout last year that JioFiber would be a slow starter. Pranav Balakrishnan writes in the story today that it’s much slower than the company had anticipated.
 
In August, chairman Mukesh Ambani had said JioFiber would add 36M subscribers, when the total wired broadband in India was just 18M. There was no timeframe given, but Jio needs to top up at least 300,000 customers/month to reach that number in the next decade. It added 123,000 customers in October, but only 43,000 in November. December and January numbers are not out yet but going by our reporting in some states, it isn’t a roaring success.
 
Executives tell Pranav the company will soon revise its numbers down. That may not be good news for the hived off fibre company which is trying to raise private money. At this stage in Indian telecom, fibre is the most crucial differentiator, and Jio has more than anyone else. Ultimately telcos will share fibre, but Jio’s hopes of holding out on its own for long may be short-lived.
