Good Morning Ishan,
 
Back in 2015, the common trivia question among friends gathered at the local watering hole was, “Hey, do you know what the 91 in Bira 91 stands for?”
 
The name was different, the taste was different, and soon Bira was the rage, synonymous with craft beer. If Kingfisher was the King of Good Times in the mass market, Bira was the King of the Craft Times. As it says on its website, Bira evokes a sense of familiarity to the Punjabi word for “elder brother.” 
 
But somewhere along the way, the stories went flat. The elder brother’s success has spawned roughly 50 brands, all jostling for leadership in a space that is worth just 2% of the overall beer market in India.
 
Combine that with problems brewing its ale, running short of money, squabbles among management and, of course, India’s famous regulatory hoops, and it starts becoming clear exactly why Bira was way off its target of breaking even last year. Strong sales were not translating into profit because costs went up. Then the sales started to lose their fizz.
 
It was then that Bira, to avoid a bust, went Boom.
 
Last month, the premium brand launched Boom, a strong brew with which it entered the mass-market segment, the very space it had religiously abstained from for four years. The elder brother had shifted tables, to sit with the big boys, or rather to brawl with them for space in a segment that commands 90% of the beer market. 
 
Bira, as the website says, also evokes a sense of familiarity to the Bengali word meaning “the brave one”. You sure require some spunk to take on the likes of Kingfisher and Carlsberg. Bira is looking to raise more money to boost production and marketing. It is also taking a bit of a gamble by releasing…
