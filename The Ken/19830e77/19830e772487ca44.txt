Episode #1: Pitches, pickleball, and the new rules of networking

Good morning Ishan,

I’m Rahel, one of The Ken’s podcasters. I’m writing to let you know we’re launching a new audio show that covers the latest developments in India’s workplaces and careers. 

It’s called 90,000 Hours.

We did the math. An average person spends that amount of time at work from their early 20s until they retire. That’s more time than you would spend with your family, partner, or closest friends. (Unless you work with them.)

It made me wonder: what are India’s brightest minds doing with all these hours? And what makes that time truly count?

I posed those and other questions to founders, CXOs, and employees at different stages of their careers. In each episode, I will share their insights.

You’ll find out how startups are meaningfully integrating AI into the workplace, how leadership is evolving as careers shorten, and whether the American Dream is still alive for Indians. In our first episode—out today—I chat with founder-turned-VC Arjun Vaidya and others about why networking feels broken, and how pickleball might be the fix.
