Are we done with May yet? It’s been one long, terribly hot summer—temperatures began climbing in March—and there seems to be no end in sight.
At The Ken, we’ve had multiple cases of heat-related illnesses. The blistering heat wave got to us, and now we’re trying to inject coconut water and watermelon juice into our veins in an attempt to cool off.
I am kidding.
We may not be done with May yet, but June—and the famed Indian monsoon—isn’t far away. Just gotta hold on to our hats (and umbrellas and sun scarves) for a little while longer.
While we load up on summer sundaes, here’s a recap of all that The Ken published this week:
On Monday, Shruti, in our free story of the week, dived deep into the [extraordinary influence of RMI](https://the-ken.com/story/us-think-tank-rmis-swift-rise-to-become-niti-aayogs-go-to-cleantech-advisor/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) on India’s clean energy and e-mobility policies. The US-based think tank has co-authored nearly 70% of Niti Aayog’s research reports on the subject.
On Tuesday, Seetharaman wrote about the 8X stock surge that this little-known company called [RattanIndia Enterprises](https://the-ken.com/story/8x-stock-surge-negligible-revenue-the-curious-case-of-rattanindia-enterprises/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) has been seeing. But get this: its market cap is a mind-boggling 1,975X its annualised revenue for the year ended March 2022.
On Wednesday, Sheila wrote about [Singapore’s love affair with cryptocurrency](https://the-ken.com/story/when-crypto-crashes-what-happens-to-crypto-payments/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). An increasing number of luxury category merchants are accepting crypto payments. But given the volatility of cryptocurrencies today, there's only so much a payments infrastructure can do.
On Thursday, Maitri focussed on India’s ambitious [distribution plan for fortified rice](https://the-ken.com/story/the-weak-supply-chain-links-in-indias-us350m-year-rice-fortification-plan/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter). The country wants to fortify all the 35 million metric tonnes of rice it distributes to the public by 2024, with a goal to reduce anaemia levels. But implementing its ideas on the ground has turned into a veritable supply-chain nightmare.
On Friday, Vanita wrote her first story for The Ken on why [India’s maternity law](https://the-ken.com/story/indias-maternity-law-needs-to-stop-being-an-outlier/?utm_source=recap_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=recap_newsletter) needs to stop being an outlier. The country’s labour force participation has gone from almost 60% in the 90s to under 40% this year. The maternity law is widening this gap further.
