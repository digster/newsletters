Good Morning Ishan,
Last Sunday, I got my hair cut from the one place in Delhi where time stops. One of the smallest and the oldest salons in the city. Its uniqueness lies in the sound of the flute. It enters the ear and shocks the coils of memory. Suddenly I am eight. It is Sunday, nothing is rushed and the tea is still served with two Parle G biscuits.
I have associated this salon with its owner, a Chinese woman who cuts hair like she is painting a watercolour. With love and patience. As luck would have it, the owner, who is now in her 90s, decides to pay a visit. She does the trick as she always has, pulls a candy out of thin air and offers it to a five-year-old getting a haircut next to me.
As I read each of our stories this week, I often thought about their conversation. Surprisingly, they had a lot in common—magic tricks, favourite colours and flavours of ice cream. I thought how the very young and the very old connect in trying to survive, to enjoy themselves and to adapt in an ever-changing world.
