Good Morning Ishan,
As Ola Consumer’s market share and valuation keep falling, its investors are getting jittery. And fair enough. The company has gone from shiny market leader to a trailing laggard in just four years.
Some investors went so far as to approach a rival about a possible merger in October last year, according to a long-term VC partner at Ola.
But there have been mixed feelings all around. Founder Bhavish Aggarwal is intent on maintaining the “status quo”; some investors like Softbank have settled for divesting their stake in Ola Electric; while others are still mulling over the “drag-along” clause in their shareholder agreements.
Safe to say, no one’s on the same page.
In fact, four years ago, Softbank had approached Aggarwal with a similar idea: a proposed Ola-Uber merger. Aggarwal’s response was to buy up more shares in the parent company, ANI Technologies, to consolidate his voting rights.
In some ways, it’s a classic case of favourite-child syndrome. In the last few years, Aggarwal has poured all his time, energy, and money into Ola Electric, relegating Ola Consumer to the shadows—without even a CEO to its name. But ask him to let the ride-hailing company go, and he’s vehemently reluctant. Investors are at their wits’ end.
