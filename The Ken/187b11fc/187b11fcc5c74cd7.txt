Good Morning Ishan,

A challenger to an incumbent in any business always earns that title long before it ends up knocking the latter from its perch, if at all.

A challenger is, ultimately, one who shows early signs of shaking up the existing order.

Remember how Zepto's 10-minute grocery service had many wondering about the future of giants like Bigbasket? Even though Zepto ultimately flattered to deceive.

In financial services, though, there's a key variable that should give any ambitious upstart pause: the omnipotent regulator.

Slice knows this better than anyone else now. Until a year ago, the Tiger Global-backed fintech positioned itself as a "credit-card challenger". It was issuing 300,000 credit-loaded wallet-like cards a month, more than the credit cards issued by some of the country's top banks.

Then the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) swung a sledgehammer on Slice's business. In June 2022, it banned credit lines on the kind of cards Slice issued.

But the RBI wasn't done with its blows.

In April, it allowed payments on the Unified Payments Interface financed by credit lines, but only banks could offer this credit. That meant Slice would have to partner with banks, the rules for which the RBI had already made quite stringent.

Slice is really feeling the squeeze, as Gaurav writes in today's well-reported story. Whatever it's trying to do in response to the apex bank's actions—including launching a co-branded credit card—is like putting Band-Aid on a bullet wound.
