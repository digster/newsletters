Good Morning Ishan,
 
Probably you’ve been there. Wine tasting events, which are part education, part entertainment. Many years ago, when I enrolled for a week-long wine tasting workshop, I thought it’d be pure fun.
 
It was, and yet it wasn’t. In blind tastings, I couldn’t tell a Chardonnay from a Sauvignon, a French wine from a Californian wine. I think I didn’t have the vocabulary to describe what I was tasting. (In any case, journalists didn’t drink expensive wine those days.)
 
More notably, the wine snobbery at the summer workshop at that prestigious American management school wore me down. I quit the third day.
 
Why am I recounting that? Because millennials today have reinvented wine consumption—from its description to packaging to retail. Here’s how a bunch of millennial women described three unidentified red wines at the monthly The Wine Rack event in Mumbai last month:
 
“Number one was like a bad boyfriend.”
 
“The first is like a good date. Two is philosophical. Three is a
friend.”
 
“The second one was mysterious, but opened up slowly.”
 
“Really? It left a bad taste in my mouth. Like my ex [peals of laughter].”
 
“I’d say wine number three is fun, flirty, and forgiving…”
 
No baggage, no care for the wine pedagogy. No surprise also that Indian winemakers, pushed to unlearn, are happily bending to this demographic. Business after all is good for this alcobev which accounts for just 1% of all alcoholic beverages sales. 
 
Sonal Holland, India’s only Master of Wine — the highest qualification in the wine world — has been producing the India Wine Insider report and its 2018 edition reveals that over half of India’s wine drinkers are millennial.
 
If tasting or buying wine, remember “BLIC’. Short for Balance, Length, Intensity, and Complexity. Also an acronym, says Roshni in her story today, applicable to India’s wine market, and its quest to capture the attention of the under-38 drinkers. People who are drinking more by the bottle; at home than at restaurants.
 
Quoting Holland: “The world of wine is so diverse. If you were to drink a new wine every day for life, you’d never drink the same wine twice. That also means being constantly surprised.” 
 
Roshni’s story will surprise you, for sure. Enjoy: https://the-ken.com/story/india-millennials-wine/ (15-minute read)
 
Have a great weekend.
