The OTT wars in India are not any less entertaining than shows on these platforms.

Between Disney+ Hotstar and JioCinema butting heads and Amazon Prime Video and Netflix slugging it out, the competition among OTT streaming platforms is popcorn-worthy.

Adding more masala to this mix now is Sony Liv. Sony released an important number recently. Its global subscriber base has surged to 33.3 million from about 18 million at the start of 2022, majority of which came from India.

“A year ago, we wouldn’t even track Liv but now they suddenly seem impossible to miss…,” a top OTT executive told The Ken.

Sony Liv’s strategy of focusing on distribution and the type of content has especially unlocked advantages for the streaming company.

What has also got others to sit up and take notice of is the long-pending merger of Zee5 and Sony Liv, which is expected to be completed by the first half of the year ending in March 2024. Sony and Zee would own 92 TV channels, cornering an estimated 27% viewership in the Indian TV market, surpassing reigning leader Disney Star, which has about 19% share.
