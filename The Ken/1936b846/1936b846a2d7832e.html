India’s insurance sector loves Debasish Panda. As the chief of the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority, or IRDAI, he was proactive in hearing out industry requests and forming feasible action plans that were agreeable to all sides. Panda will leave his post in March. Before that happens, he’s taking a final shot at leaving a legacy.

That mark is Bima Sugam, an IRDAI initiative that will be an online marketplace for insurance products and services. It’s meant to go live by March 2025—nearly two years later than planned—and will be the first platform of its kind. Even though Bima Sugam will function like Policybazaar and Insurancedekho, the key difference is it’ll be run by the regulator.

Given Panda’s imminent departure, there’s been a rush to enlist insurance bigwigs to run the platform. Prasun Sikdar, who was previously chief executive of Manipalcigna, and Bharat Panchal, an executive with three decades of experience in the industry, joined those ranks this month.

That recruitment and other moves are coming together. At a closed-door meeting between IRDAI and representatives of the insurance industry held in mid-November, Panda said Bima Sugam is nearly ready to be rolled out. Private insurers are now working on new integrations to link up with the platform, according to executives who spoke to The Ken.

This is all meant to make insurance plans easier to understand and create a smoother experience when purchasing coverage. But for the likes of Policybazaar and Insurancedekho, Bima Sugam will also be a direct competitor.
