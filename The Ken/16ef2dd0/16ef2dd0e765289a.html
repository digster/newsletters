Scout. Carry. Serve. Loomo. EffiBot.
 
Some are a giveaway, but, if you haven't guessed it yet, these are names of autonomous delivery robots. Or robots that will deliver packages and food and whatnot to your doorstep, solving one of the top pain points in logistics - last-mile delivery.
 
However, it'll be some time before these bots ring your doorbell (or whatever). They are all in trials and tests, and once done, there will undoubtedly be a 1,000 regulatory hoops to jump through.
 
In the meanwhile, we'll have to make do with humans on a bike. And e-commerce firms will have to think of other ways to cut last-mile costs, which can account for roughly 60% of total delivery costs.
 
One such not-so-successful experiment that online sellers have done is partnering with kirana stores, or local mom-and-pop shops, for deliveries. Something that Amazon, Flipkart and online grocer Grofers are dabbling in.
 
The newest entrant is Shadowfax, not an online seller, but a startup that specialises in last-mile delivery. Shadowfax is whom e-commerce companies turn to when they are flooded with orders, or for deliveries at inconvenient hours or to inconvenient places-all money-draining exercises.
 
But, if Shadowfax helps e-commerce firms cut expenses, how does Shadowfax control costs? For starters, its Uber-like model of delivery agents choosing when they want to work maximises utilisation. Its employee referral program also helps them lower 'delivery agent-acquisition' costs.
 
And now, co-founder Abhishek Bansal calls its plan to partner with kirana stores a "gamechanger," with the potential to cut last-mile costs by 25%. It also made enough sense for customer-cum-rival Flipkart to lead a $60 million funding round in Shadowfax last week.
 
In today's story, Abinaya details how Shadowfax plans to succeed where better-funded e-commerce firms, including Flipkart, have struggled. It's also a fascinating example of Indian jugaad—an innovative way to solve problems using limited resources.  https://the-ken.com/story/shadowfax-key-to-delivery/ (12-minute read)
