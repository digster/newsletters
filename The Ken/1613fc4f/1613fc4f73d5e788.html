Ever since the Indian insurance regulator laid down guidelines on insurance e-commerce last March, insurtech has been in overdrive. While insurance brokers have made a steady beeline for Flipkart and Amazon discussion tables, a few (three, to be precise) online-only insurance companies have made swift moves.
 
In scoping out the opportunity to bundle insurance and commerce on the internet, all are looking east. To China’s ZhongAn, whose unique model has not been replicated anywhere in the world. Because no insurance seller has exclusive access to hundreds of millions of consumers. Yes, you guessed it right. Alibaba and monopolistic traits go hand-in-hand.
 
Digit, Acko and Toffee are trying out three different strategies. (Don’t ask me why it’s named Toffee.)
 
In the next few weeks, Digit will launch a few products. And if newspaper reports are to be believed, Acko might get acquired by Amazon. Now, why would a company, so early in its life, want to get tied at the hip with a tech behemoth, putting the biggest trump card in such associations, consumer data, at risk, and at one party’s mercy?
 
And risky it is. In its IPO filing, ZhongAn said, "However, our continuous cooperative arrangements with our shareholders and related parties in the future are subject to certain risks…"
 
Digital insurance products are likely to flood the market in the next few months. The insurance industry, in general, was pretty active last year, collecting $20.4 billion in premiums in FY17.   
 
Probably the most regulated business sector globally, insurance has an unsaid rule—an insurance company should outlast human lifetime easy. Does it hold today? When people are insuring for delayed flights and phone thefts?
 
Ruhi has a detailed account of what’s cooking on this front in India. She even tries to answer if ZhongAn's model is worth replicating. On to it here: https://the-ken.com/digital-insurers-replicate-zhongan/
