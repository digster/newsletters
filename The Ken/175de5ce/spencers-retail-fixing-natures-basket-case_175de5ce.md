Have you all been buying fancy ingredients for pandemic cooking? You know, for those aesthetic Dalgona-brewing lockdown-chef Insta stories? That would explain some of it.
Eighteen months ago, if someone had said Spencer's Retail buying gourmet grocery chain Nature's Basket would actually work out, that brazen someone would've been scoffed at. After all, Godrej sold the chain after suffering some Rs 350 crore in losses over a decade. In May 2019, when Spencer's chose to make this surprising buy at Rs 300 crore—an amount so close to the losses, it seemed like dark humour—Nature's Basket was making just about that much in revenue.
Today, though, Spencer's bet seems to have paid off handsomely. So much so that Nature’s Basket has posted positive Ebitda for three straight quarters. Its gross margins increased to 26.5% in the quarter ended September 2020 from 17.6% in the same period in 2019. Loss-making outlets have been shut, the store layout has been tweaked, and Nature's Basket is back to having an identity.
Its own niche, gourmet identity at that. Spencer's is now making another risky bet—scaling Nature's Basket as a gourmet retailer. That comes with a whole host of challenges, starting with world food.
