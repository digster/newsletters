Deeply negative cash flows and continued sales pressure raise serious questions about the sustainability of the electric two-wheeler maker

However, it now wants to turn its fortunes around by selling 25,000 units a month. But overpromising and underdelivering isn’t a new game for Ola. India’s top general insurer is ‘too big to fail’. It’s also too bad at making money

New India Assurance’s premiums are rising, but so are its problems. The math isn’t adding up

The public-sector insurer now collects more premiums than ever before—but loses even more through poor underwriting. It leans heavily on investment income to plug these gaps, burning over half its returns just to stay afloat. New India’s domestic business is bleeding. Its foreign operations, however, run like a… real insurer. How come? [Read the full story]

Why Fortis and Manipal are duking it out for this hospital in Pune

The nearly 30-year-old Sahyadri Hospitals has seen three owners change hands so far. Yet, it’s profitable, trusted among patients, and is a steal for new bidders

PE-VC firms have shown rigorous interest in Indian healthcare, pouring in nearly $7.4 billion since 2020. After national operators, that money is now being targeted at regional chains like Pune-based Sahyadri Hospitals that’s out on the block now. Temasek-backed Manipal Hospitals, Blackstone-backed Quality Care, and publicly listed Fortis Healthcare are reportedly after the chain, valuing it at nearly Rs 6,000 crore. But what makes Sahyadri—which has already been twice through arbitrages from financial investors—so attractive? [Read the full story]
