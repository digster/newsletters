Or did a side pick Swiggy? That’s a good question.
 
The side I’m referring to here is Flipkart (backed by Softbank and Tencent), which is set to invest $50 million in Swiggy. The other “sides” are Amazon and Paytm* (backed by Alibaba and, interestingly, Softbank again).
 
Now, why is India’s largest e-commerce company investing in India’s largest food delivery company? Conversely, why is Swiggy, which is growing like gangbusters (over 4 million orders each month) and which has raised over $150 million till date, raising money from another e-commerce company instead of from VC investors?
 
Those are good questions, too.
 
Here are two reasons—strategic synergies and deep pockets. A Flipkart + Swiggy combo makes eminent sense for both sides. One side gets frequency; the other gets predictability. As for deep pockets, well, any meaningfully large and successful e-commerce startup in India must now choose one of three sides—Flipkart, Amazon or Paytm. 
 
There are more reasons, too. But for those, you’ll need to read today’s story by Patanjali and Payal. You can get a sense of the consolidation games that are going to play out in 2018 if you read between the lines of their story.
