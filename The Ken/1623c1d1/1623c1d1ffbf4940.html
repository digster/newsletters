Nope. A Silicon Valley company hasn’t invented a way to wash your clothes digitally (though there are numerous cryptocurrencies that will clean your wallet, digitally).
 
Instead, the headline refers to the increasingly common practice of large IT services companies classifying more and more of their client work as “digital”. Cloud? Digital. Social? Digital. Mobile, analytics? Digital. IoT, CRM, Supply Chain? Digital. Servers and workstations? Digital. AI, ML, AR, VR? Why, digital, of course.
 
TCS claims 22.1% of its revenue is digital. Wipro says it made over $500 million in digital revenue the largest quarter. Accenture says over 50% of its revenue was digital last year.
 
But Sanchit Vir Gogia, the founder of Greyhound Research, says a lot of this is just "smart financial realignment of existing revenue”. Or in layman’s terms: old wine in new bottles.
 
Today’s article is by Sanchit, who pokes holes in this runaway “digital washing” trend and says the true digital work is the one that’s “transformative”. Whether to business, technology or talent.
