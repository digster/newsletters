Good Morning Ishan,
 
In the UK, where I’m from, November is all about light and darkness. The clocks move back, and winter comes knocking. The short days begin with darkness and the return home after work, or school, is typically dark, too. No more long, lazy, sunshine-filled nights. No more summer breeze. 
 
Instead, there’s a nip in the air, a chill that can only be chased by a cup of hot chocolate and a warm blanket—something to illuminate the daunting prospect of cold, snowy days ahead. 
 
Speaking of daunting, this is also my first weekly recap email. Don’t get me wrong, writing stories for The Ken is tough. And it is something that I relish and find rewarding. But this email also recaps an entire week of work. Oof.
 
So for this week’s email (and for me), we’re going to be channelling a British November. We'll be talking about stories that shone a light on issues that would have otherwise gotten lost in the cold.
 
Our week kicked off with Arundhati’s story on why a bank is taking on Amazon and co. We know e-commerce companies have their eyes on fintech—and one leg in payments already—but this story is an unlikely reverse. HDFC is jumping into e-commerce. And potentially more.
 
Tuesday saw Pratap take a fiery baton to one of the darker questions looming in India. What happens to Vodafone Idea if it can’t pay the Rs 1,31,000 crore, or $18.23 billion, it owes? Read the story for the full range of answers, of course, but the summary is bad news for all—not just Vodafone and consumers, but its rivals, too.
 
Wednesday took us to Southeast Asia. I teamed up with Ka Kay Lum, one of our recent hires, to dissect a topic that has intrigued me for some time: the crazy rise of the region’s VCs.
 
20X is the kind of growth top startups post. However, venture capital firms like this Singapore-based duo, Golden Gate Ventures and Jungle Ventures, have jumped from debut funds of $10 million debuts to third funds over $200 million each. More money is coming in, but it isn’t clear whether the exits that generate returns will come from.
 
Our shining lantern theme continued into Thursday as Suraksha skillfully profiled Tata Health, a division of Tata that few people know exists. Let alone that it accounts for 11% of Tata Industries Limited’s turnover. After a four-year trial period of numerous pilots, Tata Health is preparing for a full consumer launch. Suraksha’s timely story chronicles what to expect, looking at what Tata has abandoned on its way.
 
Finally, the week closed with Friday’s story from Seema—our editor—who picked up the torch to cast light on Ashoka University, a higher education project aimed at reimagining liberal arts… with a cool $100 million in backing. 
 
It’s an intense story that explores the lost art of critical thinking, Ashoka’s expansion plan, and its potential impact on society. One reader called it “the story I didn’t know I needed... that is great journalism”, and that says it all.
 
This week’s stories exemplified our desire to chronicle narratives that others can’t, or simply won’t, tell; and to tell them in a way that is accessible to many. Much like providing light to those who are otherwise in darkness in the approaching winter.
 
Best,
