Good Morning Ishan,
 
Sometimes, the news that we cover in our daily newsletter Beyond the First Order becomes a fully fleshed-out, reported story that one of our reporters dive into. 
 
In July, when India’s capital market regulator Sebi published yet another circular with yet another mandate, I wrote about it in the newsletter. Here are the introductory paragraphs from that piece titled “Mutual fund platforms get a little too mutual?”
 
“If you invest in mutual funds in India, what’s your go-to option for executing a transaction? 
 
  • Physical forms?
  • Directly on the fund house’s website?
  • Free online platforms like Groww, Kuvera, Zerodha?
  • Camsonline?
  • BSE Star?
  • MF Utility (MFU)?
 
Investors are spoilt for choice.
 
Now, if the capital markets regulator Sebi has its way, there could be one more. 
 
On Monday evening [26 July], Sebi published a circular asking mutual fund companies and registrar and transfer agents (RTAs)—who process and maintain records of transactions—to band together and set up yet another ‘platform’ to be a one-stop solution for mutual fund related affairs.”
 
Today, Anand takes it further in his story and says that Sebi’s diktat has mostly been met with the question of—why have another platform when so many exist already? 
 
But there does seem to be a method to this madness. Take, for instance, when an investor wants to undertake non-financial transactions such as a change in address or nominee. Often, it’s a cumbersome process of dealing with each of the two RTAs, or individually reaching out to each mutual fund where one has investments in, or even filling out physical requests for the change. 
 
A Bengaluru-based investor, who conducts all his MF transactions online through the Paytm Money platform, had to submit physical forms at the RTA offices to get his bank account mandate changed at the back-end. 
 
Running from pillar to post for such a simple matter is never fun. So it only makes sense to introduce a single interoperable platform and make people’s lives easier. 
 
And since RTAs are almost a duopoly, it does get difficult for other stakeholders like asset management companies and even MFU to get services and data from them. The new platform could solve that problem as well.
 
But, is this a threat to the platforms like MFU that I listed at the start of this email, or is there an opportunity that is waiting to be unlocked? That’s Anand’s story today: https://the-ken.com/story/the-method-to-sebis-mutual-fund-platform-madness (9-minute read)
