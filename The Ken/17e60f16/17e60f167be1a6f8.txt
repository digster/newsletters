My favourite opening line in literature is from Richard Yates' novel Easter Parade: "Neither of the Grimes sisters would have a happy life, and looking back, it always seemed that the trouble began with their parents' divorce."

This is an incredible sentence, not just because itвЂ™s beautiful in and of itself. It stands out because Yates doesn't waste a second to tell you what the book is all about, which is very uncharacteristic of a work of fiction, particularly a novel. If 'story' is all you care for, there is no reason for you to read any further. Viewed journalistically, the line tells you the "what" and "why" of the Grimes sisters' doomed lives.

But it derives its power from making you very curious about the "how".
