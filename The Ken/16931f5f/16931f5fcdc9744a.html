Good Morning Ishan,
 
At some point, unicorn numbers stopped making sense. Growth rates in the thousands, funding rounds in the billions, valuations in the dozens of billions. Meh.
 
India’s hotel rooms aggregator Oyo has raised over a billion dollars in VC funding. It claims a “run rate” of $1.8 billion. And a valuation of $5 billion. I know, right? Billions this, billions that.
 
$1.8 TRILLION. 
 
Oyo wants to be “the number one hotel chain in the world”. And the size of the global opportunity is $1.8 TRILLION, plebs. Billions are for dweebs.
 
But after our calculators ran out of screen space and Excel spreadsheets out of memory, we decided to think small instead of big. Sumanth and Ashish went back to the smallest cog in Oyo’s gargantuan wheel that keeps turning and chewing up billions and trillions (reminds me of the giant cities that roam the world in Mortal Engines, eating up other cities)—the humble, independent hotel. Oyo promises a 16X (that’s 1500%) growth in profits for hotels that sign up for its platform. A no-brainer if there ever was one.
 
How easy is it to deliver that kind of growth? How does the unit economics work?
 
For today’s story, Sumanth and Ashish teamed up and spoke to multiple hotel owners who have tied up with Oyo. They had a lot to say. On the pulls and pressures of doing business with Oyo. Understandably, most owners were reluctant to share real numbers as that could be construed as confidential business data.  
 
Till they finally found one entrepreneur who was ready to talk. Not just talk, but open his books for The Ken to take a look at.
 
The result is a fascinating peeling of the giant Oyonion. Sorry, onion. Read the story here: https://the-ken.com/story/peeling-the-oyo-onion-one-hotel-at-a-time/
