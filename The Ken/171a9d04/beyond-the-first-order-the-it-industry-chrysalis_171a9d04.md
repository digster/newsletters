A mere 24 words that—if followed up on by TCS and its peers in the Indian IT industry—could transform the ecosystem created around a sector that employs 4.36 million. What if working from home is the new normal for 75% of them? Or 60%? Or even 40%?
Tech offices are real estate hoarders, typically clustered in massive IT parks in pockets of a city. Electronic City in Bengaluru is one; another is HITEC city in Hyderabad. What happens to commercial real estate developers like Embassy, RMZ or DLF when fewer people are needed per office? Already, overall (not just IT) office leasing this year is [expected](https://link.the-ken.com/ls/click?upn=sy6ExvMQ-2BqIqW2Qsf-2FVG5rTGl7Vf-2BRh64r543aPmvHN-2BAjGzhSxmRPBumOJzGc958HxZ0HC2H0l00rmfR1TTSjXHUrNWQTnhPl7JvMMbq3RHjlS0iWEbFW3QVqLtifdi4JAAJImGUMdMaBSybKDKQyjBYnUOwBLBYtPGnn7AN76kvxaaHNdLcieBYSEwL9QGeCXc_dmDs81p1OM5gBGYvHfsMFvgp9PcMB6iZkwowmLx-2FVhwlHXbL5m6csiptGuneRwktW27c6ZwIBsjV67BZcfkOlPppVavmb9GVjXF4V-2FTqNpWn8uXmMrMxpgkdQfKVNAlFLEWfO0QDtPNXLYew6TFY7oiSXfirv5Bfd1BunXfDn9xAywjMpYaVeTCOvzPVYH3LMlTHJb-2FoW7vKgiltNm7ErtZlzuHoP-2B6JFRBAPPyvr-2BAf6ns3dblMi7HeiwSBhbAdsZDtZ-2BKE2pnSOAwVvTPW7gD-2F9dtNyyZEQn6i0n3WrIyhji5Vxuj9YQFpn1CdCBM6cKGbQbCqWhUnzWYCTgELWwGABeDSJ6H7EnfWTR93LIgssolwq8SuUgqRftf9agPA2Z9NzR69HGbzQBJrC62YR2x7cgErcDEc-2FMwvzTOhGYY-3D) to be lower than 2019.
What happens to the PG (paying guest) accommodations, hostels, co-living and other residential real estate projects that have mushroomed around IT parks? Co-living firms like Zolostays and Stanza are already [realigning](https://link.the-ken.com/uni/ls/click?upn=sy6ExvMQ-2BqIqW2Qsf-2FVG5sc84ge4M35NkSHIl0HH-2B5COqcVrGR-2FuIaE2vn7-2FI8RG33eb4AAJ58NQaBpkKb6VoZDOqLDb-2BAqkOOt2lHCgxQU-3D9Z3A_dmDs81p1OM5gBGYvHfsMFvgp9PcMB6iZkwowmLx-2FVhwlHXbL5m6csiptGuneRwktW27c6ZwIBsjV67BZcfkOlPppVavmb9GVjXF4V-2FTqNpWn8uXmMrMxpgkdQfKVNAlFLEWfO0QDtPNXLYew6TFY7oiSXfirv5Bfd1BunXfDn9xAywjMpYaVeTCOvzPVYH3LMlTHJb-2FoW7vKgiltNm7ErnX2p7bNEnuV3WVnbuW-2F2MCVDApEzFxZ40FLGmOtcnlFiBSP0cvI3jdiAQJeiLNxMbyhz-2F3E6D0CIiI02PyNYiIh7RN2LxYV48MV3Qp0BgJtQ-2FI2lUcmEroHsammr-2BtyurWNoadhjOK-2FLkkoiZUr7TOM8Y5K0rY8XGlmTpkz1TBqH-2FOTTd-2F9lXoHnuJe0WfE-2BhSvcd7Zeby55-2FCDGTW0R6I-3D) their business models as occupancy rates halve.
What happens to the food ecosystem? To caterers supplying meals to these campuses. To the restaurants—McDonalds, Starbucks, etc.—that have sprung up in these campuses. To the migrant workers who run the roadside tea stalls, must they re-migrate?

There’s this simple yet powerful concept in systems thinking called emergence.
It means that sometimes when things come together, something new and unexpected happens. Something that wasn’t evident from its individual elements. It’s biological as much as social. Like a caterpillar becoming a butterfly.
I wonder if that’s what will happen with the IT industry ecosystem.

Vaccines are the answer. But are they also a problem?
Rohin
Dozens of teams across countries have been working round the clock for the last few months to develop a vaccine to protect human beings against Covid-19. In India, Serum Institute, the world’s largest vaccine maker, is starting trials on a variation of the BCG vaccine that was first invented in 1919.
Serum Institute’s current BCG vaccine, which it sells in over 100 countries, is largely administered to newborn babies. But if its tests on high-risk adults in India turn out favourably, it expects to increase vaccine production.
