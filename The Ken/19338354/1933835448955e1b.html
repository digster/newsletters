Jiocinema + Hotstar is the final nail in the coffin for edgy content

Shrinking budgets, fewer projects, and a fixation on bankable “stars” over innovative stories—the new reality of Netflix, Amazon Prime, and other OTT platforms

The Indian OTT space is facing an unprecedented slowdown, with over half of the 116 shows that were commissioned in 2021 being snubbed. Be it budget cuts or reduced future projects, it seems the OTT space, once seen as a “paradise” for storytellers, is losing it all. A major reason for this slowdown is the consolidation of large streaming platforms—the latest being the creation of Jiostar.com by combining Jiocinema and Disney+ Hotstar. While actors face pushback for not being reliable faces, storytellers are also on the receiving end of these platforms and, sometimes, also want to play it safe. [Read the full story]

How the stock market is fuelling India’s grandest-ever wedding season

The country will host nearly 140,000 weddings a day in the next 30 days. But not all of them will end like a fairy tale

Rs 6 lakh crore—that’s how much Indians are spending on weddings in 2024. But there’s more to the story. The frenzy is being fuelled mostly by a group of stock-market winners and real-estate investors. All thanks to the unprecedented rally on the Indian bourses and the resilient real-estate demand, despite supply shortage and towering prices in the NCR. Yet, lurking in the background is an army of debt-collection agents awaiting an anti-climax. [Read the full story]

The cutting edge

Water is the new air: the race to cool India’s data centres with pricey new tech

Three foreign firms—Vertiv, Schneider Electric, and Stulz—are set to capture a slice of India’s Rs 10,000 crore data-centre boom as liquid cooling becomes essential to handle AI’s power surge

India’s data-centre capacity is set to double to 1.8 GW, pushing data-centre providers and companies to upgrade from air to liquid cooling to manage the load. Plus, the government’s Rs 10,000 crore GPU tender has triggered several companies, including the likes of Reliance Industries, to enter the data-centre race. That presents a prime opportunity for companies to be early entrants to the nascent Indian liquid-cooling market. Yet, while liquid cooling promises superior efficiency, high costs and potential risks to servers are making data-centre providers cautious. [Read the full story]

Airtel and Jio won’t touch this 5G goldmine. So why is BSNL stepping in?

While giants chase consumer profits, the state-run telco is eyeing the niche they’re keen to overlook. With government spectrum and a startup-friendly approach, it could lead a private-5G course—if only it can dodge red tape and markups

India’s private 5G seems to be picking a surprising leader as Jio and Airtel bow out. Busy with monetising their mobility network and having paid billions of dollars for 5G spectrum, the niche play of private 5G is not for big telcos. But product and services companies, who have the tech, expertise, and will, are either going overseas or pinning their hopes on BSNL. BSNL is opening up its 5G spectrum reserves, but it’s got work to do. [Read the full story]

How Hurun India and wealth managers turned ‘rich lists’ into a virtuous cycle

Hurun wants to go where Forbes and Bloomberg couldn’t

Hurun started curating rich lists in India over a decade ago, sensing an opportunity, just like it did in China. The aim was to go beyond Forbes and capture the stories of more than just the top 100 wealthy individuals. This led to the expansion of Hurun, and with it came the sponsorships with leading banks and wealth management firms. But it’s not just Hurun’s extensive research on wealth creation that’s attracting these firms. [Read the full story]
