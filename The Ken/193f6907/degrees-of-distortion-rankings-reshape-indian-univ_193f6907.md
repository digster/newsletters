Good Morning Ishan,
I remember how dizzying it was to figure out which universities to apply to. Some friends sent their forms and essays to their parents’ alma maters. Others heard of specific programmes that interested them. Others yet went by rankings, especially when it came to institutions they had never visited and knew little about.
University rankings give students and parents a starting point when they’re new to the application process. For the universities themselves, these lists matter a lot, too.
When Himachal Pradesh’s Shoolini University climbed 200 places in the 2025 edition of the QS World University Rankings from the previous year, its administrators went from being unable to schedule a meeting with even an assistant professor of an institution in the United Kingdom to being hosted by that school’s dean, according to vice-chancellor Atul Khosla.
Shoolini University is one of 46 Indian institutions that made it into the QS World Rankings of over 1,500 schools. A decade ago, there were only 11 on the list.
Students, parents, and India’s government officials view a high position on lists like this one as a mark of excellence. There are other real-world consequences, too, like research funding being tied to school rankings. To ensure applications and grant money keep flowing in, Indian universities are doing all they can to first be included and then move up the ranks.
The problem is some of these rankings are flawed, with an emphasis on pageantry and perception rather than academic innovation. A crop of consulting firms, as well as the ranking agencies themselves, now offer services to help universities boost their chances at moving up. In that process, universities are changing their admissions policies, managerial schemes that affect their carbon footprint, and plenty more.
In today’s story, Alifiya looks at the way different types of rankings are now a focus of Indian universities. Check out her [coverage](https://the-ken.com/story/degrees-of-distortion-rankings-reshape-indian-universities/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).
