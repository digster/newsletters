Good Morning Ishan,
 
This is a pandemic. Not a productivity contest. 
 
You would’ve seen this all over the internet lately. The pressure to be super productive with all the extra time we have on our hands from not commuting to work is very real. According to the internet, if you’re not spending your day acing work, getting all your chores done promptly, and baking enough to feed an army every day, you’re probably failing. 
 
And like everybody else, we regularly have issues with productivity at The Ken too. Things just don’t work out some days. Meetings get delayed, stories don’t meet deadlines, and the dishes in the sink pile up. The running joke at the office is that the kitchen sink magically multiplies our dirty dishes. There can be no other explanation. Nuh uh. 
 
However, this week was extra special, you know? We not only made pancakes, but we made pancakes with maple syrup, chocolate, bananas, strawberries, and Nutella! 
 
What I mean is that we not only put out some of our best stories, but these were stories that were richly-reported investigative pieces. Stories that gave us the highest traffic on our website in the history of The Ken. 
 
If you don’t know what the fuss is all about, let me give you a glimpse of our stories this week. 
 
I’ll start with stories by Arundhati and Sumanth. Both of them cover a topic that has been buzzing in the fintech ecosystem for a while now—lending. We even asked our audience on Twitter what they thought. Take a look at the results.
On Friday, Arundhati wrote about iSprit’s latest project—Sahay, a lending app aimed at thousands of MSMEs. Sahay could do for the adoption of fintech lending what payments app Bhim did for the adoption of digital payments in 2016. But ironically, Sahay was called off because business, especially MSMEs’ cash flows, have been hit. This story of how it was built is more important than why its launch was canned and that is why it is a must-read. This was also one of our free reads for the week, so do share the story widely. 
 
On Wednesday, we wrote about CRED, one of India’s most storied and envied startups. They have two new products—a quick credit line facility called Stash, and RentPay, which allows members to pay monthly rent with their credit cards. But in a post-pandemic world, CRED will probably need to ramp these up in a far more meaningful way to occupy and own mind-share for consumers of these offerings. If you are a startup fanatic, you will love this story by Sumanth. 
 
Our blockbuster story of the week was by Pratap on Reliance Jio’s 5G tech development. Unlike 4G, which was built with Samsung, Jio is attempting to DIY its 5G open interface network. And also become a global telecoms giant in the process. Now that Trai has redefined Make in India—it has a new category where one could ‘design in India, manufacture abroad’— Jio’s plans would qualify for incentives under the scheme. Can it pull off a 100% in-house developed network? 
 
Fake certifications. Favouritism in tenders. Shell companies. Missed deadlines. Our Tuesday story had it all. Shreedhar’s story exposed the favouritism and a lack of due diligence in the government’s attempts to address India’s ventilator shortfall. AgVa, the firm that won the tender put out by state-owned HLL Lifecare, has more problems than one. This story will take you on a roller-coaster ride and have you riveted throughout. This is also a free read, so please do share the story. 
 
If you’ve been ‘Dunzoing’ essentials through this lockdown, now you can ‘Swiggy’ them too. With Genie, Swiggy’s new concierge service that lets users task delivery executives with errands and shopping orders. In our Monday story, Kunal talks about how Swiggy could evolve from just food-delivery to a brand that provides unparalleled convenience to urban consumers.  
 
That’s it for our satisfyingly productive week. It may have been an ideal week for us, but I know all we did was set a higher bar for ourselves. And we promise to keep pushing it higher and higher. 
 
Stay safe. 
 
Warmly,
