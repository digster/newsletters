A bonus-shares announcement played to the gallery, platitudes were mouthed, and a lot was said. A lot was left unsaid, too.

It was an anti-climax yesterday for those awaiting breaking IPO-news at the annual general meeting jamboree of Reliance Industries. Studied silence seems to be the strategy—at least for now—on the much-anticipated public listings of the retail and digital businesses of India’s most-valued company.

When they happen, they are expected to be record-breaking big—in keeping with Reliance’s tradition. And they are expected to happen sooner than later. Billions have been raised at multi-billion-dollar valuations from the who’s who of foreign investors, many of whom will be itching for an exit.

The IPO ball is almost certainly rolling, especially inside Retail Retail Ventures Limited (RRVL)—the giant tent under which all and sundry of the Reliance retail empire reside. Events of recent past—a minority shareholding squeeze, a capex cut, and a workforce slash—are giveaways to what’s coming. Cutting the flab, as it were, for the big, fat wedding.

But just how much can RRVL command? US$100 billion as recent deals suggest? Much more? Much less? Solving this valuation puzzle is not going to be easy for stock-market pundits, to put it mildly. RRVL is a riddle, wrapped in a mystery, inside an enigma.

As Seetharaman, the author of today’s superbly analysed Big Story puts it eloquently, “A company so sprawling that every listed Indian retailer is a rival. Even when none of them has a fraction of its complexity.”

From silent shifts to scant disclosures to motley acquisitions, RRVL will be one tough nut to crack.

Is it a retail bird, is it an FMCG plane?

This hydra-headed entity is all that and more.

Here’s how it will give analysts much pain.
