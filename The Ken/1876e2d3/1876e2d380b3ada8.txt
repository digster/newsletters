Good Morning Ishan,

Picture yourself sunbathing on the deck of a hired yacht off the Greek coast. With a glass of champagne in hand, as you soak up the Aegean sun, your portfolio manager from Mumbai calls and asks for permission to invest Rs 10 crore (over a million dollars) from your account in some stocks. You instruct the manager to do anything she likes as long as you make a neat 30% return over the next year. The cruise continues. 

If you are one of those likely to be on that hired yacht, you will want to read today’s story. If you are someone who aspires to be on that yacht someday, you will not want to miss today’s story. If you are neither, you will definitely be interested in what’s brewing.  

For India’s fat cats who don’t know what to do with their billions, an army of wealth managers known as portfolio management services (PMS) are at their beck and call. 

But a series of events over the past few months has caused turmoil in this community of portfolio managers. Used to little regulatory meddling and high flexibility in their operations, they are being exposed to new rules by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (Sebi). 

Managers consider the new benchmarking norms most irksome. The way Sebi tightened the screws using a fledgling industry association representing just a handful of big PMS firms is equally intriguing. 

Will the regulator now limit the fees these managers charge their clients? Will these developments hurt smaller managers and drive the business of investing the wealth of the affluent to larger PMS firms?
