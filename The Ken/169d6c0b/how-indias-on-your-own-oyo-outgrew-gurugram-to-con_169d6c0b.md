Good Morning Ishan,
I have a problem with big numbers, so I always try and make them smaller. Easy to understand.
From today’s copy: 3,40,000.
India’s OYO says it has 3,40,000 rooms in inventory in China. It also says that it got here pretty quickly. The company started operations in China only by mid-2018.
It is a tall claim. When it launched, it claimed 11,000 rooms. So, from June 2018 to March 2019, we are basically talking some 270 days. Do the math and what OYO is claiming is that it added close to 1,220 rooms every day. At 50 rooms per hotel, we are basically talking almost 25 budget hotels added every day. Note: These aren’t big cities, so 50 rooms per hotel is a lot of rooms for a budget hotel.
Wow!
It is only fair that The Ken asked Huizhong Wu to take a deeper look into this. A business correspondent based in China, Wu has written for The Caravan, CNN, Wired and Al Jazeera, among other publications. She went poking around with questions. Are the numbers being bandied about for real? How did OYO get here? Can it do better? Who is competing with?
