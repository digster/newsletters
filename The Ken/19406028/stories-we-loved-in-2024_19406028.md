Good Morning Ishan,
Every story we do matters a great deal to us. But some end up being more special than the rest. Because we had to overcome serious hurdles in reporting them, or they resonated with readers in a way we didn’t anticipate, or we spotted them before our rivals could, or simply because we loved writing them.
So, here are some of our favourite stories of the year:
Ozempic is unapproved and unreliable in India. But I’ve still ordered the miracle drug
Marrying a deeply personal account with extensive reportage, Alifiya [navigated the complex world of weight loss](https://the-ken.com/story/ozempic-is-unapproved-and-unreliable-in-india-but-ive-still-ordered-the-miracle-weight-loss-drug/) and people’s desperation to get their hands on drugs like Ozempic that haven’t been approved yet in India.
Stoa, the IIM challenger, is dead. What’s next for India’s altMBAs?
Nathan landed [this scoop](https://the-ken.com/story/stoa-school-the-iim-challenger-is-dead-whats-next-for-indias-altmbas/) months ahead of the actual announcement. After four years, 15 cohorts, and 1,500 students, Stoa School, a newfangled alternative to traditional business schools, called it quits. What led to this, and where does this leave challengers like Stoa?
Zepto and Blinkit threaten kiranas in a way that Bigbazaar and Amazon never did
We were among the first to make the case that the rise of quick commerce was undoubtedly detrimental to mom-and-pop grocers. Aakriti marshalled convincing data and reported widely to put [this story](https://the-ken.com/story/blinkit-and-instamart-threaten-kiranas-in-ways-that-bigbazaar-and-amazon-never-could/) together.
A Rs 2,500 cr scam powered by mule accounts: an illustrated guide
Rounak teamed up with Aishwarya, one of our designers to tell a [strikingly visual story](https://the-ken.com/story/a-rs-2500-cr-scam-powered-by-mule-accounts-an-illustrated-guide/) of the alarming rise of mule accounts. Potential scammers can even choose “mule-as-a-service” to streamline their operations.
Tata Zudio makes a no-brainer entry into beauty products. Here’s why ‘anyone’ can
The beauty business in India has, over the years, drawn several brands—the latest being Zudio which offers deodorants at about Rs 100. The category’s growth, though, is partly attributed to a set of contract manufacturers who’ve practically lowered the entry barrier for new brands, as Sudeshna and Seema point out in [this story](https://the-ken.com/story/tata-zudio-makes-a-no-brainer-entry-into-beauty-products-heres-why-anyone-can/?searchTerm=zudio).
How Rajkot derailed Hyundai’s $3 billion IPO
Rajkot [punches way above its weight in the stock market](https://the-ken.com/story/how-a-small-city-in-gujarat-derailed-hyundais-3-billion-ipo/). Not only does it consistently rank third among cities with the most IPO applications, but it also determines the grey-market premium that can make or mar a listing, as was evident in India’s largest-ever public issue.
He founded a unicorn. Got bored. Now wants to digitise 60,000+ hospitals
Imagine a patient to first go to a hospital in Jalandhar and then a year later visit one in Mumbai, only for the latter to have seamless access to his electronic medical records from the first hospital. Rizwan Koita, a healthtech entrepreneur who is now chair of National Accreditation Board for Hospitals and Healthcare Providers, [laid out his vision](https://the-ken.com/story/he-founded-a-unicorn-got-bored-now-wants-to-digitise-60000-hospital/) for true interoperability between hospitals.
Pharmeasy’s mis-selling puts its 3.9 million customers’ health at risk
[Shivani’s investigation](https://the-ken.com/story/pharmeasys-hard-sell-puts-its-3-9-million-customers-health-at-risk/) blew the lid off the unsavoury practices at healthtech platform Pharmeasy. Its doctors are happy to prescribe supplements even if the customer has no real need for them, and it adds products to the cart without the buyer even choosing them. The implications are worrying.
Cheap products, fast shipping, and variety. Temu has buyers hooked to the cart
If there’s one e-tailer that out-Amazoned Amazon, it’s China’s PDD Holdings-owned Temu. With laughably cheap products, global shipping, and an unending selection, Temu is all the rage in the US and several other countries. But [Zhou set out to answer](https://the-ken.com/story/cheap-products-fast-shipping-and-variety-temu-has-buyers-hooked-to-the-cart/) this simple question: who pays the price for Temu’s rise?
Amazon and Samara Capital turn the Dmart model on its head to fix their supermarkets
Dmart is the gold standard in grocery retail in India, and central to its success are stores spread over 40,000 sq ft. There are enough corporate misfires to show that neighbourhood supermarkets, which are one-tenth that size, just don’t make sense. But I learnt, to my surprise, Amazon and private-equity firm Samara Capital [deliberately chose the latter](https://the-ken.com/story/why-amazon-and-samara-capital-shrank-their-supermarkets-when-dmart-kept-getting-bigger/) for their More chain of stores and still made it work.
CMOs are a dying breed. Nykaa, Nestlé India, and others are living proof
Do chief marketing officers really matter today? Companies are trading them for channel-specific specialists, leaving marketers to juggle tactics without a unifying strategy. [Debanjali’s story](https://the-ken.com/story/cmos-are-a-dying-breed-nykaa-nestle-india-and-others-are-living-proof/) couldn’t be more relevant.
Bengaluru’s EV battery-making hopes are draining fast—without ever being fully charged
Electronics maker Foxconn is setting up its new factory—possibly the second-largest plant outside China—near Bengaluru airport. Yet, electric-vehicle battery manufacturers, a bunch of whom have made the city their home, are struggling to make progress. [Shristi finds its roots buried](https://the-ken.com/story/bengalurus-ev-battery-making-hopes-are-draining-fast-without-ever-being-fully-charged/) under bureaucratic red tape and sluggish incentives.
