Salal, a village in India's state of Jammu and Kashmir with a population of 10,000, may seem similar to many other villages in the northern part of the country. But the there's much more to the village than meets the eye. Located on the banks of the pristine Chenab River and surrounded by the steep Himalayan mountain range, Salal recently made news for the discovery of nearly six million tonnes of Lithium, a crucial ingredient required to power India's ambitious clean energy plans.
 
If mined successfully, these deposits could significantly aid India's efforts to reduce its dependence on fossil fuels and transition towards clean energy. However, the region's geopolitical situation is volatile, and residents are concerned about being exploited. Moreover, the environmental costs of mining are substantial.
 
The pursuit of renewable energy is not unique to India. Indonesia has emerged as a key player in the electric vehicle (EV) industry due to its ownership of over a fifth of the world's nickel reserves and a third of the global supply of a key material used in battery development.
 
Nonetheless, ABC, a company known for producing kitchen condiments such as ketchup, syrup, and snacks, is placing its bets on LFP, an emerging battery standard made up of Lithium, Iron, and Phosphates. LFP batteries are considered safer, more environmentally friendly, and have longer lifespans than nickel-based batteries. The new technology has also been endorsed by EV giants such as China's BYD, and the US's Tesla, and Ford.
