Good Morning Ishan,
Two billion dollars. That’s roughly how much Peak XV is expecting to reap as returns from the impending IPOs of Groww, Pine Labs, and Meesho before the end of 2025.
It’s a step up from the firm’s past performance, and a departure from typically poor venture-capital exits in India. From the outside, things look bright for Peak XV.
But the view from within has a dimmer tint. Earlier this year, two of Peak XV’s senior general partners, Shailesh Lakhani and Abheek Anand, left the team. They’re among nearly 20 people who have departed the storied venture-capital firm. These exits cut through investors, marketing, legal, public policy, and operations.
Peak XV can’t stem the bleeding, and the exodus of senior-level personnel also means losing sets of founder relationships that stretch back more than a decade. “In the VC business, relationships are everything. That is what wins deals,” as one source told The Ken.
Arundhati explains what’s going on with Peak XV in [today’s exclusive](https://the-ken.com/story/peak-xvs-post-sequoia-reinvention-comes-at-a-price/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter).

Dairy producer Milky Mist has filed draft papers for a roughly Rs 2,000 crore IPO. The Tamil Nadu-based company has inverted the model championed by its rivals, which have used milk as the gateway to the dairy market. Milky Mist, on the other hand, saw no reason to sell milk and instead built a lucrative business around value-added products like paneer and cheese.
