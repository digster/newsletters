Good Morning Ishan,
A year back, The Ken had [written](https://the-ken.com/story/ansrs-ahuja-duo-on-why-everybody-from-victorias-secret-to-google-will-do-pretty-much-the-same-thing-in-india/) about the idea of a “second headquarters” in India gaining steam, thanks to technology upending every business. These global capability centres (GCCs) set up by foreign companies to provide a myriad of services, mostly tech, to the parent and group entities have evolved massively. From merely cost-arbitrage models for back-office work, they are now skills-arbitrage engines for high-end strategic services and solutions. More control, more agility, and a grip on whatever the new technology is—the benefits of GCCs are many. Cost savings are just the cherry on top.
All manner of businesses now flock to India to set up GCCs. But the surge is especially strong among one of its earliest adopters—banks. More than a third of all banking GCCs in India have been set up in the last seven years, and the numbers keep adding. This, plus their scale-up in the value chain, is having a profound effect on tech talent dynamics at these earlier staid places, writes Debanjali in today’s well-researched story.
Once a no-go at worst or a slow-go at best due to the dead-end nature of the work, banks are now hot and happening attractions for many engineers. The march of AI is only boosting their charm. Of course, it also helps that GCCs pay much more than IT firms that, for long, were the first port of call for banks.
Where did all this leave IT companies? Are Indian banks keeping pace with the paradigm shift that GCCs have engineered for tech talent? What’s next for banking GCCs?
