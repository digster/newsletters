Look, it’s a hotel chain! It’s an aggregator! It’s a marketplace!
 
It’s OYO.
 
After having entered, exited and profited from Flipkart over a few months, SoftBank needed a new blue-eyed favourite in India. And that now clearly appears to be hospitality startup OYO. 
 
Reports say OYO is raising a new investment round of nearly $1 billion, valuing itself over $4 billion (Q: What’s better than reaching a billion dollars in valuation? Raising a billion dollars! Unicorn square, baby.) And for the right reason, if we go by SoftBank founder Masayoshi Son’s observations. He says OYO will add 10 times the number of rooms by the end of the year that India’s largest hotel chain Taj Hotels has (15,000 vs. 15,000). And that it is growing 10 times faster than the world’s largest hotel chain, Marriott (81,000 rooms vs. 8,000 rooms added in the last three months). OYO founder and CEO Ritesh Agarwal says he has a 90% share of India’s market for “branded rooms”.
 
But, asks Sumanth, how does OYO make money? Does it actually make money?
 
To answer his own questions, he analyses OYO’s market positioning, its business model, its revenue agreements with hotel owners, its China entry, and even its ubiquitous brand. He doesn’t come away impressed with what he finds.
 
But then, if you have SoftBank backing you, do all of those even matter? I’d say not. But what do you think?
