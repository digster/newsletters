Good Morning Ishan,
“Don’t look for the needle in the haystack. Just buy the haystack,” advised the famous Jack Bogle—the man behind the world’s second-biggest asset manager Vanguard—some half a century ago in the US.
In 2023, Mr. Groww, the spiffy Indian discount broker, is practically itching to lead you, dear equity investor, on an Indian haystack adventure. And he’s supersizing that haystack to a level rarely done in the country.
Just the other day, Mr. Groww rolled out the Nifty Total Market Index Fund. He is offering investors a piece of the pie that consists of 750 stocks—a move that channels the spirit of Vanguard’s Total Stock Market Index Fund in the US.
The backdrop of Mr. Groww’s decision is compelling:
-
India’s mutual fund sector, with more players than you can shake a stick at, is booming.
-
Mr. Groww, with a wallet-popping valuation of $3 billion, seems to be on a rocket ship ride. The sponsor of his mutual-fund arm, Nextbillion Technology, reported a 3X growth in revenue and a 10X surge in profit in 2023.
-
Passive funds are suddenly the life of the party.
There’s a problem, though: Mr. Groww is taking a dip in uncharted waters. His decision to track micro-cap stocks—the top 250 from a pool of about 4,000—is unconventional, to say the least. He only has one fellow traveller in this micro-cap expedition: Mr. Motilal Oswal.
This also means he’s zigzagging away from passive-fund adults like SBI, Nippon, and ICICI Prudential and even thumbing his nose at the newcomers Navi and Zerodha.
But there are some forces at play:
-
Despite Mr. Groww’s impressive run, his discount broking rivals Zerodha, Angel One, and ICICI Securities are way ahead in the profits race.
-
Mirae Asset, HDFC Securities, and Phonepe have also crashed the broking party. Mr. Groww must diversify his offerings, and quickly.
Can I ask if you would like to pose this question to Mr. Groww? “Does the market have an appetite for such a total-market fund?”
Today, Anand chats with a fund manager, a distributor, and other industry experts to find out. Spoiler alert: There are some sceptics.
