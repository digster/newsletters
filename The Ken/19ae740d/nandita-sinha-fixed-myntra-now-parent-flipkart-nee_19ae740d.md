Good Morning Ishan,
The CEO position at Myntra seems cursed. Co-founder Mukesh Bansal left just about a year after the company was acquired by Flipkart. Ananth Narayanan and Amar Nagaram followed and barely lasted three years.
In 2022, Nandita Sinha, former VP at Flipkart, took over the accursed throne. And for all the juju associated with the position, she managed to do something unprecedented.
While Flipkart has been drowning in losses of thousands of crores, Sinha has led Myntra to profitability. In FY25, the company posted a profit of Rs 550 crore on a revenue of Rs 6,000 crore. No small feat.
There was a lot that Sinha got right. She increased the share of external brands on the platform, doubled down on new initiatives—whether for Gen Z-focused fashion or quick 30–90 minute deliveries—and actually paid attention to things like analytics and marketing.
Not everyone is impressed, though. Some executives point out that she got a well-functioning company handed to her on a platter. All she had to do was not mess up.
In any case, Sinha has now been handed the reins to Flipkart Fashion too. Will she be able to repeat her Myntra trick? Or will her detractors have the last laugh?
