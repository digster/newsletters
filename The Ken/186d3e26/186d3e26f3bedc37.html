Good Morning Ishan,
 
This week I reread the Irish playwright Samuel Beckett’s famous two-act play Waiting for Godot. It was one of those weeks that went perfectly with the long meandering aimless conversations between Didi and Gogo that take place under a leafless tree while they are waiting for Godot. They talk; they reminisce, and they ponder on life. But mostly they wait. For one or two moments, there is hope that Godot will arrive. The story ends. He never does. 

Several critics have interpreted this story in many different ways, exploring some basic questions: who is Godot? Is he even real? Then, some even go on to examine extremes: is the story a political allegory, a psychological metaphor, or an existential question about the meaning of life itself? The Wikipedia page took me down a rabbit hole of interpretations that consumed a considerable amount of my time. 

By the end of this frustrating quest, I concluded that Beckett just wanted to play some cryptic mind games with our control-, speed-, and clarity-obsessed generation. And he may have well succeeded—he brought me back all the joy and frustration of being and not doing, existing and not striving, and asking but never knowing. 

On that note, I will summarise the wonderful stories my colleagues wrote this week. And, in the end, I will share what inspired Beckett—who dismissed every critic—to write this. 

On Monday, we wrote about the largest insurance marketplace in India, PolicyBazaar. It went public over a year ago but its stock reached an all-time low a quarter ago. Recently, its share price has staged a turnaround at surging at over 67% since November. It hopes to be profitable soon. You can read the story of success, along with all the challenges it is yet to face, here.

Then follow it up with the story of its competitor—Insurancedekho—we published on Friday. While it looks like the savvy insurtech PolicyBazaar, it behaves like the state-run insurer Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC). And it's big because it raised $150 million—the largest funding in a series A round by any Indian insurtech—at a valuation of $450 million in February. However, the picture is not straightforward. 

After you have had your fill of insurance, check out another comeback story of ITC, once known as the Imperial Tobacco Company of India, written by Anand. Even though ITC is the second largest FMCG company in India, investors did not like to be associated with it—the reason being its identity is synonymous with cigarettes. But something has changed. Now, cigarettes are at the heart of ITC’s stock rallying 80% in one year. 

Finally, end this Sunday’s reading with the story of a new-age defence manufacturing company $55 million-worth Tonbo Imaging. It has to face issues such as the need for liquidity and search for investors and customers. And it's not just a story of one Indian defence company but of what it means to be any defence company in India.

As I promised in the beginning, let me share what Beckett himself felt about the play. He too was tired of interpretations that he thought were a source of ‘endless misunderstanding’. "Why people have to complicate a thing so simple I can't make out…It's all symbiosis,” he'd once shared. 

You see, the play for him was never about Godot but about Didi and Gogo chatting under a leafless tree. That was all there was to it.

Have a nice Sunday.
