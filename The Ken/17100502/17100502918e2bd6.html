Good Morning Ishan,
 
A 2011 Steven Soderberg movie is seeing a sudden uptick in popularity. 
 
From languishing at #270, it is now the second-most-watched Warner Bros. film among its catalogue titles. It is also #7 on iTunes and is quickly rising to find a place in Google Play’s list of Top 20 films. According to Business Insider, it’s also highly sought after on piracy websites. 
 
If you’ve been on any of the streaming sites lately, you’d know I’m talking about Contagion, the sci-fi thriller about a mysterious virus that spreads all over the world, killing millions. 
 
Sounds familiar. 
 
The movie has much to say on the topic of a pandemic, and it is not just related to the virus. Fake news. Disinformation. Social media. Panic buying. Social distancing. Rioting. The question of who gets the vaccine first. “Stop touching your face!” says one character in the film, eerily echoing the advice that has been given out in the wake of the Covid-19 pandemic. 
 
This week at The Ken, we debated how to cover the disease. Should we just talk about how it’s impacting the businesses? Should we go beyond that and talk about the disease itself? We had a survey too, asking our readers what they wanted us to do. 
 
In the end, all our carefully planned strategy was tossed out the window as things changed day-by-day; sometimes hour-by-hour. Our lineup changed every day, keeping our reporters, editors, and graphic designers up at all hours, working on this story and that, working to bring you the latest and up to date information. 
 
And much like Contagion, our stories this week covered aspects of the pandemic beyond the disease itself. 
 
Thursday saw a very relevant and on-time story by Seema about two Indian companies that are working on testing solutions for Covid-19. Both are Bengaluru-based and both companies are uniquely poised to expand the scope of diagnoses during the current pandemic. This was also our free story of the week, so you can share it widely. 
 
We opened the week with Sumanth and Jon giving us an overview of how investors, VCs, and companies see the fallout of the Covid-19 play out. Because, yes, the fallout has begun. In fact, Sequoia is already calling it a ‘Black Swan’ event. 
 
Olina dived into the muddled marketplace model of Unacademy, one of India’s premier online test-prep companies, on Wednesday. With schools and colleges shutting down, edtech has become more relevant and popular than ever. And Unacademy Plus, its subscription model, is close to hitting 100,000 users. What will the future bring for the company? 
 
Tuesday saw Ruhi write about insurance aggregator PolicyBazaar’s move to hand over the reins of DocPrime, its virtual doctor consultation platform, to a new investor. The move was announced after SoftBank invested in the company, so what gives? 
 
On Friday, Rohin sat down with Swiggy’s co-founder Sriharsha Majety for an edition of The Interview. The company is working on several fronts to mitigate the impact of Covid-19. Majety tells us that and much more in this intensive chat.
 
That’s it from me. I hope this week’s stories gave you enjoyment and insight in these stark times. 
 
Have you watched Contagion? Or its older brother Outbreak, starring Dustin Hoffman and Cuba Gooding Jr.? Let me know how you found these movies. 
 
Oh, and feel free to tell us what you’d like to see covered on the Covid-19 pandemic next. 
 
Stay safe. Wash your hands. And stop touching your face.
