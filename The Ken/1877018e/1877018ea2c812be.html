Did you know more than 75 per cent of adults in India do not understand basic financial concepts. It's worse with women.   

 

But in the last two years or so, partly because of the pandemic, there has been a dramatic increase in retail investments in the Indian stock market. 

 

But still, it is safe to say that a large section of our country’s population is financially illiterate.

 

So, if you wonder why you keep seeing ads on Youtube and other social media platforms offering investment advice, saying invest your money on here or there to grow it by two times or four times, you now know why it is not surprising.

 

There is a dire need for making the world of investments and finances more accessible.

 

And here is where ‘finfluencers’ come in. As ridiculous as that name sounds, these influencers or financial influencers, break down the mind-numbing financial jargon that usually leaves most people scratching their heads. Their videos are simple, they speak in a mix of different regional languages and English. In fact, one could even argue that we need more folks like them.

 

But there is another side to this story.


Many of these influencers operate in a grey area. They are not registered with SEBI and they depend on advertisements and sponsorships to make money. Many of them also tend to offer misleading advice to innocent investors.

SEBI has been receiving many complaints for a while now. But regulating them is a bit of a catch 22 situation.
