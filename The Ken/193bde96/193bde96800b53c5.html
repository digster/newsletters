What if a business school taught automobile design? No, that’s not a hypothetical question. An Indian management institute has set the wheels in motion for the first batch of its unexpected programme.

It’s not any B-school, though. It was India’s first when it opened its campus in Jamshedpur in 1949. And it has consistently ranked among India’s best B-schools.

But XLRI is shaking things up at its four-year-old campus near the national capital with its school for automobile design. A third campus, in Amaravati in southern India, will offer graduate programmes in liberal arts and law.

The Indian Institutes of Management have rapidly expanded their footprint across the country, and the Indian School of Business, with its one-year MBA that requires applicants to have worked for at least two years, reimagined management education in India.

XLRI, on the other hand, hasn’t changed much in all these years. Is it then making up for lost time with a whirlwind of activity? Or, is it finally setting the agenda for rivals?
