A merger with the automaker’s finance arm adds Rs 40,000 crore to Tata Capital’s book—but will dent its financials

Tata Capital is gearing up to make its stock-market debut soon and is awaiting NCLT’s nod to merge Tata Motors Finance with itself. Ironically, the merger, expected to be greenlit by 31 March, stands to hurt Tata Capital’s otherwise impressive financial performance. That’s because Tata Motors Finance is exposed to high-risk borrowers, and so, has lower margins; thanks to its “captive” status. If at all it was up to Tata Capital, analysts believe the company wouldn’t have opted to either merge or list, for it was in its sweet spot.
