The rise of Blinkit and Zepto may have queered the pitch for the one-of-its-kind Indian drugstore chain

With a mere 400 stores, Wellness is far behind Apollo Pharmacy and Medplus Health and is restricted to just four states. But unlike its peers, Wellness gets half its revenue from non-pharma products and has for years served as a quasi-supermarket in parts of Mumbai. Ola Electric’s IPO, which opens today, pegs the electric two-wheeler leader’s valuation at US$4 billion—25% less than what it was less than a year ago. After more than 40 years in the chip industry, Raja Manickam has founded a company and devised a business model which, he believes, is the only way to scale semicon products in India

A fab in India is a few years away but P Raja Manickam of iVP Semiconductor is buying wafers and making chips. But not in the conventional way. The world is his design house and Taiwan, his foundry. Projecting US$70–100 million in revenues in the first 3–4 years, it’s the fastest route for iVP to market and scale its bet. Maybe like Morris Chang, who founded TSMC at 55, Manickam is old enough, and knows the industry well enough, to ride the chip disaggregation wave. The Ken spoke to Tinio about his strategy leading a digital bank. His tactics are unconventional but notable, as Gotyme is on track to reach 5 million users within two years of its launch.
