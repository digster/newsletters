Good Morning Ishan,

If a good laugh and a long sleep are the best cures in the doctor’s book, Nuha’s perfect Friday story today offers both—or rather, the ironies of a long sleep inducing a good laugh.

Buying a mattress in India these days is anything but a dream, she finds out. Folks just looking for something nice and comfy to sleep on—like the good old days—are subject to a battery of questions and postures.

“I was made aware that my thinking was outdated and I had to buy a mattress that was not only comfortable but would also do wonders for my body and had features that could put my sagging cotton mattress to shame,” confessed a confused buyer who hunted in vain for weeks for the ideal match.

The buzzword in the tech-driven mattress world now is “orthopaedic” with the mind-boggling array of choices only compounding the confusion and inducing insecurity among buyers.

Leading the mattress-complexity brigade are the new-age direct-to-consumer brands like Wakefit and The Sleep Company. Late to the race but not to be left behind are the traditional players like Duroflex. And with good reason—it’s a US$2.5 billion opportunity lying out there, nothing to be slept over.

But then the wake-up call: is this really what the doctor’s book prescribed? Click here to find out.

Heated up?

Hopes of quick interest rate cuts—globally and in India—are quickly melting away. The US Fed again kept its rates unchanged this week due to a “lack of further progress” on lowering inflation.

Back home, the Reserve Bank of India, too, continued its “hold” operation on rates last month. Inflation is again a key concern everywhere, thanks to high commodity prices. And the relentless heat wave across India won’t help—food prices could rise further.

So, interest rate cuts seem unlikely anytime soon. Then why is the stock market behaving like it is and going up, up, and away? The heat has got to it, I daresay.
