October is always a special month at The Ken. This year was no different. We published some blockbuster stories over the course of the last three weeks.  
 
While we won’t be publishing a story today on account of the Hindu festival of Dussehra, do take a look at some of our best stories this month.
 
Rage against the machine: behind Byju’s swift silencing of dissent: On one side is edtech decacorn Byju’s. On the other are two individuals who challenged the prowess of this $11 billion company and its latest acquisition, WhiteHat Jr. Read our story about the continuous silencing of dissent, multiple social media takedowns, and a system where terms like “copyright infringement” and “defamation” are used interchangeably.
 
SBI’s one app to rule them all: The State Bank of India pegs its YONO app at a valuation of $40 billion, a number that experts shake their heads at. India’s most valuable startup, Paytm, is valued at $16 billion. But YONO isn’t just any banking app. You can not only transfer money, but also buy insurance and credit cards, and even shop online through the app. Does India’s fintech space have a new entrant in the form of one of India’s oldest banks?
 
Ola’s uneasy transition into an everything company: Ola began its life as a ride-hailing app. Fast forward 10 years and it is no longer just a ride-hailing app. It has a financial services arm, a cloud kitchen business, as well an electric vehicle business. Getting to this point wasn’t easy. And it’ll only get tougher for Ola in its journey to become an everything company.
 
Ethics? Profits? Scale? How the $5 billion PremjiInvest pulled off all three: This 15-year-old Bengaluru-based investment firm had some specific rules in place before it began investing. Nothing too speculative. A high premium on governance. Top-notch entrepreneurial ethics. As a result, it has now built up an enviable portfolio while also managing some neat exits in the process. Here’s the story of how PremjiInvest pulled off the perfect alchemist's recipe for prudent investing.
