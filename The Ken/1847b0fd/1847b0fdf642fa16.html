Hello Ishan,

 

Eventually, it all comes down to dopamine. A nifty little hormone that makes achievers, doers, and builders out of us. A taste of it gives us the satisfaction of accomplishment and makes us want it more. Which in turn motivates us to do more. And a virtuous cycle sets in.

 

The smarter among us already know this. They also know that productivity hacking is, in many ways, dopamine hacking. Making sure we get just the right amount of dopamine, at the right intervals, in predictable ways.

 

But how do we plan our day to give us the dopamine we need, and to keep it coming so we can keep going? Do we design our lives so that we're rewarded for the time spent working? Or do we design our lives so that we're rewarded for the tasks we get done? What should we track—time or tasks? Which gives us the dopamine we need to get through a day?

 

In this episode of Cost to Company, Shreevar Chhotaria talks to some of our best and brightest productivity enthusiasts about how they plan their day, their work, and their hits of dopamine. And the complicated and fascinating ways in which tasks intersect with time. There are no easy answers. Only nifty, incremental processes.

Shreevar interviews a lawyer, a founder, and a director of product management. And he finds out: 

 

1. Why having a to-do list may lead to the ‘illusion of control’

2. How productivity is turning into an art project

3. How personal productivity has team-wide ramifications

4. How the calendar became the default ‘super-app’ at Swiggy

5. What happens when you go too far with time chunking?
