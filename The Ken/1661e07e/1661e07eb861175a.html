Good Morning Ishan,
 
“Full stack developer” used to be a pretty common career aspiration (or LinkedIn self-description) in Bangalore as recently as a few years ago. There were also “full stack growth hackers”. And “full stack employees”.
 
Nobody knew what they were “full” of, although many had good guesses. Anyhow, the phrase fell out of fashion. 
 
Now Beer Cafe, a five-year-old chain of cafes that serves, well, beer, wants to go full stack. In five years it grew to 40 outlets across a handful of India’s top cities, powered by venture capital and a rising preference for beer in a country that has historically preferred “strong” alcoholic drinks.
 
But that journey didn’t come without its share of hard knocks. Onerous regulations, disastrous nationwide policy shocks (demonetisation), court judgements against alcohol sales, a schizophrenic new unified tax law and high rentals.
 
“We have first-world rents and third-world sales,” says Rahul Singh, the founder of Beer Cafe. “At least 25% of my revenue, if not more, goes into the rental expense, which ideally should be a single-digit percentage.”
 
Thus, just 40 cafes into his vision of being “like a Cafe Coffee Day for beer”, (Cafe Coffee Day has over 1700 outlets in India) Singh wants to pivot. Not away from beer cafes, but towards making his own brand of beer - Indie18.
 
By doing so, he hopes to earn 50% of his revenue from the new beer brand by 2020. And eventually, overtake Beer Cafe’s total revenue. Unless Singh can convince every patron at a Beer Cafe to drink a few more bottles of his own beer, he will compete with the other craft beer brands that he sells. Like Bira, White Rhino, Simba, Arbor.
 
There will be fireworks. Or damp squibs. To find out which, read Harveen’s story: https://the-ken.com/story/the-beer-cafe-indie18/
