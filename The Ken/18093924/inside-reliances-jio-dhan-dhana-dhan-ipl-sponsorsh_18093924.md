Good Evening Ishan,
I’ll admit it. I’m not big on the Indian Premier League (IPL), or T20 cricket in general. I’m one of those boring people who prefer their cricket over five days and in all-whites. And as a ’90s kid, I can’t say no to some good old one-day cricket either.
But that doesn’t mean I don’t watch the IPL. It’s usually on in the background on mute while I’m working in the evening. But more than the cricket, I’m fascinated by the advertisements and all the madness that goes on in the IPL sponsorship world during these two months.
Which is why, till last week, I was surprised that I hadn’t seen a single Jio ad. For the last four years, the telecom company had bombarded the IPL broadcast with multiple ads featuring cricketers and Bollywood celebrities, all dancing to that annoying earworm of a jingle—“Jio Dhan Dhana Dhan”. Love it or hate it, it’s catchy as hell. So catchy that I was actually missing it this season.
And I say I was missing it, because last week, the Jio ads started popping up. They’re a lot shorter this season—just 20-second spots—which actually makes the jingle less annoying. But the formula is the same: three cricketers from each IPL franchise doing the signature dance steps—some of them so visibly uncomfortable that it’s hilarious—in tune with the “Jio Dhan Dhana Dhan” jingle.
What Jio has achieved over the last five years, whether you like the campaign or not, is a tremendous brand recall. If you’ve watched the IPL since 2017, you know what I’m talking about.
And behind it all is a rather unique sponsorship strategy, which is what this week’s edition is about.
