Good Morning Ishan,

What if I told you that the Indian Premier League (IPL) has decided to host Test matches instead of T20s? Or that Apple will be going back to launching its vintage desktop soon? Surprised? Don’t worry, none of that is happening. But I was similarly bewildered when I first heard about Anand’s counterthesis interview.

It’s about Stable Money, a fintech firm founded in 2022 that wants to build a marketplace for fixed deposits (FDs). It recently announced raising $5 million. And in just about 20 days since its launch, Stable Money claims to have over 20,000 customers who have invested more than Rs 10 crore (around $1.2 million) through its platform.

But aren’t fintechs the unofficial flag-bearers of everything cool, and FDs are, well, quite the opposite?

Saurabh Jain, co-founder of Stable Money, finds FDs to be the best products for building a business for the masses. His confidence is backed by the fact that roughly a third of India’s household savings are invested in FDs. Reasons, you ask? They’re insured, safe, and currently offer very lucrative interest rates.

But don’t banks already have a well-established network for selling FDs? Why would they partner with Stable Money and spend on commissions?

Assuming banks agree to collaborate, for how long can the fintech ride the bull run in interest rates? What happens once they eventually stabilise?

Also, isn’t the model easily replicable? How is Stable Money different from any other direct-selling agent?

And didn’t Google Pay, in September 2021, allow its users to open FDs with Equitas Small Finance Bank only to be wrapped in regulatory troubles later?

Relax! Anand’s interview with Jain answers all of the above questions and so much more. Let’s head over to this super interesting read: https://the-ken.com/story/this-fintech-aims-to-sail-where-google-pay-sank/ [8-minute read]
