Good Morning Ishan,
 
So far, 2021 has either been blink-and-you'll-miss-it or a drag worse than 2020, depending on who you ask. I fall in the first category—it feels like I just celebrated the new year, hoping that it will turn out better than the previous one. And now nearly half the year has sped past. 
 
Much of it has been spent online trying to absorb the reality of the situation that we’re currently in. Desperate calls for oxygen supplies, ICU beds, and life-saving medicines have become as routine as the morning cup of coffee, and you can’t help but wonder: will it ever come to a stop? 
It may not. But it's okay for you to pause.
 
Linger an extra 10 minutes over your morning cuppa. Read that article you bookmarked but haven’t found the time to. Stay in bed for that half-hour in the morning after you wake as you’ve always wanted to. Play another round of fetch with your pet. Stop. Blink. Take a deep breath. It’s okay. The world outside will stagger on, but if you can afford a moment, take it. 
 
This week at The Ken, we paused as well, celebrating the festival of Eid. Someone even ordered paneer biriyani.  
 
Here’s a short recap of how the rest of our week went. 
 
Seetharaman wrote about, I’ll be honest, a bit of a boring subject. Who wants to read about construction materials at 8 am on a Monday? But Infra.market, a five-year-old construction materials supplier, became the first unicorn from the dusty, unglamorous world of concrete and stones. The topic may not be fascinating, but how the company managed a $1 billion post-money valuation from $170 million certainly is.
 
Olina and Maitri teamed up on Tuesday to look into the world of Covid-19 testing. The virulent second wave of the pandemic has caught India unawares, and with RT-PCR tests in short supply, quicker, cheaper, and more mobile alternatives could help India grapple with an inevitable third wave. That is, if they’re able to crack the mass market. 
 
Very often, we only know what the PR teams and skin-deep media interviews tell us about a company. Pranav, on Wednesday, went into the muscle and bone of ride-hailing firm Gojek to bring us a story of a different kind. How did this Indonesian company work with a small, spunky engineering team in the faraway land of India, going from a $100 million company to $10 billion? 
 
We never thought we'd write 2,000 words on a cryptocurrency developed as a joke in response to a meme, but here we are. Thanks to Elon Musk. (I didn’t think I’d ever string such a sentence together, either). On Friday, Sumanth gave us his take on the 150X rise in Dogecoin’s value, pushing a valuation of nearly $90 billion. If the price of a currency is determined by what one person tweets whimsically, what is the real value of that currency? 
 
At what point is it a good time to think ahead? Praveen tackled this fundamental question in The Nutgraf this week. World over, countries have seen a third and even a fourth wave of the deadly Covid-19 pandemic. India is still only on its second. How well are we prepared for it? 
 
That’s it from us this week. My moment of pause this week was my morning coffee, as you may have guessed. I’ve even begun waking up earlier in an attempt to enjoy a bit of time not punctuated with loud meows demanding breakfast (I have two cats). 
 
I hope you are staying safe. Mask up, vaccinate if you are able to, and hunker down. This too shall pass.
