Good Morning Ishan,
Let’s say someone walks into your office with a killer portfolio. They can code. They can problem-solve. They have actually done the work. But they dropped out of college. So you say no, and hire the person with a 4.0 GPA and zero practical skills. Because the system says degrees matter.
This sounds insane. And yet, it’s exactly how corporate India hires. Every single day. Because no one has time. Drowning in resumes, companies mostly outsource judgment to software. Applicant Tracking Systems—Oracle’s Taleo and SAP’s Successfactors—filter applications not by merit, but by manageability. Bias gets automated.
As a result, India has built an expensive machine that rewards credentials and ignores competence. Ashoka University charges Rs 40 lakh for an undergrad degree. IIMs are pricing BBAs like MBAs. Meanwhile, entry-level IT salaries haven’t moved in a decade. But sure, your hire went to the “right” school.
Then everyone acts surprised when there’s a “skills gap”. And companies are paying dearly for it. A self-imposed tax on productivity.
Sure, some firms are breaking the cycle. Hire for skill. Train for context. Care about mindset, not just college name. But just as the old system starts to creak, AI is about to blow it wide open.
Himesh’s [story today](https://the-ken.com/story/why-should-your-degree-get-you-a-job-anymore/?utm_source=daily_story&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=daily_newsletter) is about the credential bubble: how companies are burning real money to uphold a broken hiring system.
