Introducing The Ken’s first live subscriber event

Dear Ishan, hello. In just over six months, The Ken will be celebrating its ninth birthday. But in all these years there’s one thing we haven’t done, despite it being the mainstay for most media organisations and startups to earn revenue: live events.

In our early years, we consciously resisted the “lure” of events. Because we were afraid that if we started making good money from events before proving our worth to subscribers, we might end up becoming an events-driven organisation instead of a subscriptions-driven one.

Once that moment had passed, we started thinking about what kind of events we would be excited to put together for our subscribers. We did not want our events to become mere networking meets or avenues for sponsors to spout safe talking points via “fireside chats”.

We wanted to create events that we’d love to attend ourselves. Events where the people, discussions, and experiences were valuable enough to be part of in person. Events that would compel you to buy a ticket.

Well, 8.5 years after we started The Ken, I just bought my first ticket to an event from The Ken. Heh.

If you’re in Bengaluru, I invite you to join us at the Bangalore International Centre at 5.00 p.m. this Monday, the 24th of March.

My colleague Praveen Gopal Krishnan (also my co-host on the Two by Two podcast) and I are interviewing two accomplished, candid, and often contrarian guests: Nithin Kamath, CEO of Zerodha, and Deepak Shenoy, CEO of Capitalmind.

The title of our discussion? “We are in a bear market. What if it lasts two years?”

I know, it’s a provocative one. You’re thinking whether that kind of rout is realistic or probable, right? That’s not the point.

Instead, the point is that we’ve been long overdue for a significant market correction. One that we’re already in the midst of. And given all that’s taking place all over the world, from North America to the European Union to India to China, there isn’t an “easy” or “quick” way out.

So, our selfish motivation behind this event is to learn how to upgrade or reset our thinking, mental models, and decision-making constructs to assume a medium-term bad case: that stock markets continue to fall and stay subdued for at least the next 24 months.

How do Nithin and Deepak approach this possibility? What are the obvious first-order effects of such an eventuality, then  the second-, third-, or fourth-order impacts?

That’s why we’re calling our new event series “Beyond the First Order”. Some of you will remember that was also the name of our pandemic-era daily newsletter focused on systems thinking and hidden connections.

We’re pricing the event at Rs 1,999. If you’re an existing paid subscriber of The Ken, you get a straight 50% off as a gesture of thanks from all of us. 

You can buy the tickets here: https://the-ken.com/event/beyond-the-first-order/ #

I know most of you are not based in Bengaluru, so I’m unlikely to see you on Monday. But I’d still love to get your best questions for the event. We have an exclusive Q&A session at the end, moderated by our colleague Rahel Phillipose, the co-host of our Daybreak podcast. We want to pick the most original and thought-provoking questions sent by our subscribers and present them to the guests.

You can send us your questions via this Typeform: https://theken.typeform.com/to/jpBZ90A2 https://theken.typeform.com/to/jpBZ90A2

Thank you for your wonderful support and attention. We have the privilege to embark on these ambitious bets every now and then because we can count on your curiosity, intelligence, and interest. Believe me, I can’t thank you enough.

Regards,

Rohin Dharmakumar
