If you’re in Bengaluru, I invite you to join us at the Bangalore International Centre at 5.00 p.m. this Monday, the 24th of March.
My colleague Praveen Gopal Krishnan (also my co-host on the Two by Two podcast) and I are interviewing two accomplished, candid, and often contrarian guests: Nithin Kamath, CEO of Zerodha, and Deepak Shenoy, CEO of Capitalmind.
The title of our discussion? “We are in a bear market. What if it lasts two years?”
I know, it’s a provocative one. You’re thinking whether that kind of rout is realistic or probable, right? That’s not the point.
Instead, the point is that we’ve been long overdue for a significant market correction. One that we’re already in the midst of. And given all that’s taking place all over the world, from North America to the European Union to India to China, there isn’t an “easy” or “quick” way out.
So, our selfish motivation behind this event is to learn how to upgrade or reset our thinking, mental models, and decision-making constructs to assume a medium-term bad case: that stock markets continue to fall and stay subdued for at least the next 24 months.
How do Nithin and Deepak approach this possibility? What are the obvious first-order effects of such an eventuality, then the second-, third-, or fourth-order impacts?
That’s why we’re calling our new event series “Beyond the First Order”. Some of you will remember that was also the name of our pandemic-era daily newsletter focused on systems thinking and hidden connections.
