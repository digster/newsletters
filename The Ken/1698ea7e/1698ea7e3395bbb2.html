Strange how some of the most pertinent questions often go unasked.
 
Not long back, my colleague Rohin, Rozelle, the writer of today’s story, and I found ourselves in a glass cabin discussing just these simple questions. Why in the name of God did Amazon acquire a minority stake in Shoppers Stop? What was in it for Shoppers Stop? What were the two hoping to achieve? Did they get anywhere? Great, if yes, but if not, then whatever happened?
 
That’s where Rozelle started reporting.
 
Allow me to let you in on what she found. Shoppers Stop's original choice for the deal wasn’t Amazon. Around the time it was in talks with the company, it also tried to talk to Chinese e-commerce giant Alibaba. But either it couldn’t get through, or Alibaba wasn’t interested. So it settled on Amazon instead.
 
Shoppers Stop had a lot riding on this partnership. After a string of failures over the last decade, this was supposed to work. Deliver focus and strategic objectives, like a strong presence online. Shoppers Stop understood fashion retailing like no other. Amazon knows how to run a marketplace.
 
But nope, things didn’t go as planned.
