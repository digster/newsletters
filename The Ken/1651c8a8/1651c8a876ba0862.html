Of the many melodramatic ways in which one can describe this, let’s stick to that epic scene in Jurassic Park. A few people trapped in a car, scared. A glass of water on the dashboard. Ripples forming in it as something huge approaches. Unseen. Its arrival a terrifying inevitability. Terrifying, because it may just spell the end for the people in the car.
 
In today’s story, the car is India’s wired broadband space; the trapped passengers are incumbents like Airtel, ACT, Hathway, Spectra and others. And the looming behemoth? Reliance Jio.
 
With Gigafiber launching next week, Jio gets into what, in telecom speak, is a triple play. Effectively, this means Jio versus everyone—cable
television operators, internet service providers (ISPs) and DTH services. Share prices of many of these companies fell when Jio announced its wired broadband plan on 5 July. 
 
But is it going to be an action replay of the brutal wireless cellular competition? Will Jio explode onto the scene? After all, wired broadband is a street fight that requires a company to go to each neighbourhood, city and state. It’s not like mobile where with 100,000 towers, you pretty much have 85% of the population covered.
 
Jio is aggressively running beta trials; it wants to get the pricing right. Other service providers are being reactive as well as proactive. In this superbly reported story, Shashi digs deep into how Jio and the rest, the ISPs, DTH and multi-system operators, are slicing and dicing their prices and offerings. The existential threat may be overblown for now but trapped inside the car, operators know it’s no escapist thrill ride.
