Good Morning Ishan,

It’s not often that you meet an investor who, in a matter of minutes, convinces you that the sector he is investing in is his "life’s work”. I mean, we at The Ken are the typical dyed-in-the-wool sceptical lot. But Mark Kahn, the Canadian-American co-founder of Omnivore Partners, was different. 

A few months ago, Mark came to say Hello to us and ended up giving us a peek into the “Bharat Darshan” (India tour, to quote him) he had as an executive vice-president of Godrej Agrovet, a large diversified agri-business in India. He quit Godrej to start Omnivore, an agritech-only fund, in 2011. A self-proclaimed life-long Indophile, Mark says Indian agriculture is that “impossible problem” which he wants to spend his life solving. 

Omnivore’s just-closed second fund of Rs 680 crore ($95 million) may be a drop in the ocean toward solving that problem, yet he sees agritech at an inflection point. India’s low average landholding obscures the fact that 20.4 million farmers are classified as having landholdings that are semi-medium, medium and large. They represent 57.20% of the total land under cultivation. These 20.4 million farmers, who control more than half of India’s cultivable land, have a more commercial orientation, selling their surplus produce into domestic and export value chains.

Agritech investments grew 13X in the last six years. Of course, it grew from a small base, but the growth stimulants continue as the sector has been one of the beneficiaries of urban B2C reaching a funding saturation in 2015.

We prevailed upon Mark to break it all down for us. Unsurprisingly, F2C deals were the highest in number; B2B marketplaces and farmer platforms saw a jump, too, with 56 and 44 deals respectively. Rural fintech, precision tech and agri SaaS lie in between. Ninjacart, last funded by Tiger Global, leads the pack, but Prajakta’s charts will tell you how Indian agritech is developing a long tail with network effects kicking in as well.

What we have today from Mark, his colleague Shruti and our writer Sharath, is a definitive current account of the agri-tech VC investment in India.
