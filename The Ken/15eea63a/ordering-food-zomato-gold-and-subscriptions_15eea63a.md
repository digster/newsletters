Good Morning Ishan,
In my three decades+ of existence, this has got to be a record of sorts. The number of subscriptions I’ve bought now. There’s of course, The Ken; alright, don’t snigger already. I bought it with my hard earned money, and without any discount.
Then there’s The New York Times, Fortune magazine, Business Standard + WSJ, Hotstar, Amazon Prime, Netflix and Apple music. Now imagine my plight when my colleague Patanjali told me that this list is only going to increase. Enter Zomato Gold.
Ordering food is habit-forming. I am sure Zomato and the others know this and want to build on it. Patanjali says that with Gold, subscribers will get exclusive deals with restaurants and pubs. Such a platform already exists in Dubai and Portugal. But, let’s understand the context: “Recently, Swiggy (food delivery company) successfully raised money from investors, including Naspers. The company is valued at around $350-$400 million (around Rs 2,300 crore). Sprinkle that with the emergence of UberEATS, with its seemingly unlimited treasure chest, which has set up shop in Delhi NCR (Zomato’s backyard) and Mumbai. Then toss it up with Google’s plans to start listing restaurants. Zomato needs to roll up its sleeves and retain the customers it has acquired through years of marketing.”
The company hopes that Gold will be of some help there.
