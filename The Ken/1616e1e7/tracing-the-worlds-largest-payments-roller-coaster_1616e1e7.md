Good Morning Ishan,
In September 2016, India introduced a brand new digital payments protocol called “UPI”, short for Unified Payments Interface. It was meant to be easy-to-use, near-free, instantaneous and applicable to all possible kinds of scenarios (peers, merchants, online, offline…the works).
And what a roller-coaster it would turn out to be. In just 17 months, UPI processed 431 million transactions adding up to nearly $8 billion. Arundhati and Shashidhar (our newest staff writer) trace the evolution and growth of a protocol that has upended sectors and flummoxed players.
For much of 2017, it looked as though Flipkart’s PhonePe was the undisputed UPI leader. Till BHIM, a competitor created by NPCI (ironically the creator of the UPI too! But stranger things are known to have happened in India) emerged on the scene, and things started looking like a 2-horse race. Till Google threw its hat in the ring with Tez, a brand new payments app, and serious money as ‘cashbacks'. Only for Paytm* to come in and open up its money spigots too. Next up is Facebook.
The result is a rollicking, unpredictable and utterly gamified protocol that’s growing like [marbled crayfish](https://www.nytimes.com/2018/02/05/science/mutant-crayfish-clones-europe.html) on crack. Nobody knows who’s gonna win. Or what happens if the cashbacks and incentives stop. Or why average transaction values are dropping like bitcoin prices.
But hey, YOLO! The UPI story is a fascinating one that shows how Indian fintech is arguably one of the most competitive, innovative and brutal ones in the world.
