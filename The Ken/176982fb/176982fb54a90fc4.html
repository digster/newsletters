Most Viral Story 2020
This piece by Olina was shared hundreds of times on not just Twitter or LinkedIn but also…Reddit. No one was talking about it then, but now there are memes about it and even dedicated parody Twitter handles. It was a story crying out to be told, and we were the first to tell it.
 
In it, Olina details how two individuals from two different walks of life challenged the prowess of edtech giants Byju’s and WhiteHat Jr. One of them, Pradeep Poonia, had YouTube videos criticising WhiteHat Jr taken down. The other, Aniruddha Malpani, was booted out of LinkedIn.
 
Poonia and Malpani claim the edtech companies are targeting all posts critical of them, using third-party enforcers and abusing platform safeguards to remove posts they deem negative.
 
It is one thing to exercise such power over media agencies, but entirely another to get social media platforms of this size and repute to do so.

Most Engaging Story 2020
This lockdown has introduced (and broken) a lot of habits, hasn’t it?
 
One of the biggest habits to go, of course, is the routine of having to wake up in the morning, get ready, and head to work.
 
Hygiene, meanwhile, is a habit that has gained steam and even evolved. If you weren’t washing your hands regularly before, the pandemic has changed that aspect of your life forever.
 
In this story, Seetharaman maps the fluid landscape of habits to understand what their evolution means for different segments of the economy.
 
There are obviously businesses that have been badly hurt—think multiplexes and restaurants. But there are also gainers, for whom the pandemic has done what no amount of marketing dollars could. Like makers of robotic vacuum cleaners and dishwashers, for instance. It’s a fascinating look at how our world is changing, and how these changes have cascading effects.
