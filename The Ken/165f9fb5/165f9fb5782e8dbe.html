Can’t believe I just wrote a Friday email. Happy weekend and all, and another Friday is here. Wonder if you also feel that the pace of life is kind of continually speeding up?
 
Visionary physicist Geoffrey West even has a name for it: ‘the superlinear dynamics of social networks’. Because of this, he says, businesses are born and die more often, commerce is transacted more rapidly, diseases spread faster. And more: people even walk faster. Phew! 
 
One industry that is facing this, after almost 30 years of onerous existence, is pharma distribution. The effective speeding up of time here is primarily due to the year-old goods and services tax. The pharmaceutical supply chain in India is chaotic at best. A handful of players control the entire chain in the US. In India, it takes 80,000 distributors along with over 8,00,000 retailers.
 
But in just 15 months, nearly two-thirds of small pharma distributors have shut shop. There’s frenetic activity going on: pharma companies are dropping C&Fs (carrying and forwarding agents), shutting warehouses, online pharmacies are building their own distribution...
 
Ruhi’s story today gives an overview of how things are changing for good. The incentives for med reps, collusion with distributors and gifts for doctors are some way from being eradicated, but the benefits for patients are already showing through. More in the story here: https://the-ken.com/story/gsts-attack-on-pharmas-weakest-link-distribution/
 
Don’t let life’s accelerating pace et al spoil your weekend. Have a relaxed one.
