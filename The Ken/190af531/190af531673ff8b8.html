How Birkenstock became the Bata for India’s elite

In just five years, Birkenstock—once labelled as “ugly”—has become one of the fastest-growing footwear brands in India, with FY2024 sales reaching Rs 200 crore

Birkenstock has seen rapid growth since launching in India in 2019. By FY2024, the company reached sales of around Rs 200 crores, with a total of 37 stores as of June 2024. India and other Asia Pacific markets are now essential to the brand’s growth. And despite competition from low-cost knockoffs, Birkenstock plans to maintain and expand its footprint in India. Learning and careers

Looking for your first job? The recruiter would like to see your work experience 

Caught between dwindling jobs and companies wanting more experience even in entry-level jobs, fresh graduates entering the workforce find that they need to level up

Companies bruised by recession have slashed entry-level jobs and automated these roles to stay lean. Simultaneously, firms like Accenture and Deloitte, with higher expectations, are raising new graduates’ entry bar. This has forced freshers to acquire far more skills than traditionally needed, to meet the bar set for the job. And by changing job descriptions and hiring fewer beginners, companies themselves are undergoing foundational changes. [Read the full story]

Big business

Incred Finance saw a saviour in KKR, but itself became a hero post-merger 

The retail non-bank became one of the few unicorn non-banks in India since it merged with KKR’s Indian credit arm in 2022. This was a rare and one-of-a-kind deal that few can pull off. We now have the results to see

Incred Finance has more than doubled its profits in the financial year 2024 from the year-ago period. Fuelling this growth was KKR’s Indian credit firm, KIFS, which Incred merged with in 2022. It plans a public listing within the next two years after turning into a unicorn-valued company. But the windfall can only take Incred and KKR so far, especially when the latter is yet to recoup its own returns. [Read the full story]

Flipkart’s desperation for profits is squeezing top fashion brands 

The Walmart-owned e-commerce platform wants its second-largest category to be operationally profitable in the next two months. To achieve this, it is betting on higher margins and moving further away from its inventory model

In a bid to be profitable, e-commerce platform Flipkart has resorted to asking for more billing margins from its fashion brands. The brands aren’t keen on dancing to Flipkart’s tune, and yet, they cannot disregard the reach provided by the platform. The Walmart-owned entity has left no stone unturned; it’s even leveraging Myntra’s name to its advantage. Now, both brands and the business-to-business vertical of the marketplace are working to reach a middle ground. [Read the full story]

Tokopedia’s takeover by Tiktok is a win-win for all. Except for its employees 

As the acquisition’s potential unfolds, 450 job cuts in June and the integration of Bytedance employees have Tokopedia employees on edge

Tokopedia, having shed hundreds of employees in June, is now streamlining its organisational structure. Uncertainty looms for Tokopedia’s remaining staff amid ongoing restructuring, from project continuity to potential leadership changes. Working with colleagues from the Beijing-based company carries added weight, each interaction likely subject to critical evaluation. Expanding ShopTokopedia’s services is still a task, with only 7 million sellers onboarded so far, while Tokopedia boasts twice that number of merchants. [Read the full story]
