But what do you make of Unacademy's third key decision: venturing into software as a service (SaaS)?
Yes, you read that right. And few could have seen this coming.
The $3.4 billion startup has pitched its newest offering, Cohesive, to a range of companies, my colleague Shashwat found out in his first story for The Ken. These include fintech Slice, live-commerce and short-video platform Trello, and, wait for it, Unacademy's rival BYJU's.
In simple terms, Cohesive is meant to help companies that see periodic spikes in usage, such as e-commerce and food-delivery services, test and review their apps before they are launched or updated.
It's understandable that Unacademy wants a piece of the SaaS pie. Several SaaS startups have something that eludes their edtech counterparts: profits.
But SaaS executives think Unacademy is looking at best-case revenue of just $500,000 a year from Cohesive—less than 1% of its topline.
Also, why should any potential customer care for Cohesive? “Unacademy hasn’t produced results in its core business—edtech. Will it do so for SaaS? I have my reservations,” says someone whose company Unacademy pitched to.
