Good Morning Ishan,
 
Have you ever tried hiring someone?
 
I haven't, but numerous industry executives I've talked to over my (rather short) career have described it as an atrocious process. In India, where multiple reports have repeatedly pointed out the lack of employability among young graduates, hiring can get extremely tricky.
 
Of course, there are ways around it. Like The Ken, you can hope and pray that a former breaking news reporter (me in January) will be able to do long-form stories (me now). 
 
Getting funding, like a job, has become equally tricky, with over 11,000 startups closing shop across the country over the last couple of years and the rest needing funds up to $10-15 billion to get through the next six months. 
 
Lately, the idea of acqui-hiring has got to founders of many startups grappling with the question of shutting down or finding a better deal for themselves and their teams. 
 
Acqui-hiring happens when a larger company buys out a smaller one primarily for its people. This can work out well for both as the benefits outweigh the cons.  
 
Yet, taboos persist. It’s seen as the last resort for failing businesses. Plus, there is the challenge of finding the right fit. And like with everything else, there are losers and winners here.
