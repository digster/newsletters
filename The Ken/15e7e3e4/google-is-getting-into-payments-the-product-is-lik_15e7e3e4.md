Good Morning Ishan,
I find it quite fascinating. There are so many people wanting to get into payments.
Last time so many people wanted to do the same thing was when my teacher or any relative would ask, Son, what do you want to do in life? Pat would come my reply: I want to become a pilot. Now, I know that you are a discerning reader but place your trust in this anecdotal data. I can assure you, that a whole lot, substantial lot of kids in India wanted to become pilots. Engineer never came to my mind. Back then, I didn’t know what an engineer does. I still don’t. This includes my colleague, Rohin.
Anyway, back to Google. How hot is India’s payment space? This hot: Google, tick. WhatsApp, tick. Truecaller, tick. Myriad wallet companies like Paytm and Mobikwik, tick. Flipkart, tick. Of course, banks, a whole lot of them, tick.
My colleague Venkat Ananth tells me that Google will be launching its payment product, likely to be called Tez early next week. The company has also partnered with several large private banks in India, plus wallet companies like Paytm and MobiKwik. I can see why someone like Google is excited about India. Other than its home market, the US, Google’s payment piece hasn’t really made much headway in other countries. Of course, we present a significant opportunity. According to figures released by the Reserve Bank of India, the country saw nearly a billion digital transactions in July 2017 (859.2 million to be precise). Also, India’s digital payments industry is likely to grow to $500 billion by 2020. These are good numbers.
Maybe, there’s room for everyone. Maybe, not.
