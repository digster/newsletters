Good Morning Ishan,
 
Remember that time, not long ago, when we obsessively sanitised packages (I did) and washed vegetables with store-bought 'cleaners' (I didn't)? They may have seemed necessary and effective then but, in hindsight, were plain ridiculous. 
 
You can put the rush to buy 'immunity boosters' during the pandemic in the same bucket. From vitamin and zinc tablets to multi-mineral gummies to herbal concoctions, nutraceuticals and health supplements were supercharged during the Covid-19 outbreak. 
 
The market for such products in India was pegged at $4-5 billion in 2020. But come 2025, it could be worth ~4X of that. 
 
That's not the only reason a key regulator has turned its attention to nutraceutical and health-supplement products. In March, the Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) wrote to its state units to take action against products which exceed the recommended intake of essential nutrients being sold freely on e-commerce platforms.
 
Some products breach that limit by 1.5X to 450X. Even if that's not always worrisome, it has become hard for the FSSAI to ignore the growing use of nutraceuticals and health supplements, which are often bought without prescriptions.
 
Interestingly, the consumer-rights body that made the FSSAI sit up and take notice is led by former officials at the regulatory body. 
 
Alifiya spoke with them, doctors, and nutritionists, among others, to isolate the signal from the noise on nutraceuticals and health supplements. Let's dive in: https://the-ken.com/story/how-healthy-are-health-supplements-indias-food-regulator-wants-to-find-out/ (9-minute read)
