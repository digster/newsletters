The future of work is… whatever you don’t want to do
India’s unemployment crisis is fueling a booming gig economy, where startups—big and small—turn even the smallest chores into business opportunities. Venture capitalists have poured $20 billion into gig-driven startups, betting on an endless supply of underpaid workers to keep their business models afloat. From kneading dough to standing in line, tech apps are transforming mundane tasks into paid gigs—because there’s always someone willing to work for less. As stable jobs vanish, millions of young Indians are turning to odd jobs, while the government’s plan to issue ID cards quietly makes this gig economy official. It got off the ground as a health-first cloud-kitchen brand—and then embraced pizzas, donuts, and dine-in restaurants to grow
When Curefoods acquired the franchise of Krispy Kreme in December 2024, it might have looked like an unusual move given its flagship brand Eatfit’s focus on healthy food. Pepsi and Coca-Cola had been the undisputed kings in India’s aerated-drinks market. Until Reliance-backed Campa Cola re-entered with its palm-sized bottles at half the price

Reliance, Rajesh Exports, and Ola have little to show in terms of production. And India may not have all that it needs for an advanced battery industry
