Good Morning Ishan,
Warren Buffett, who recently stepped down as CEO of Berkshire Hathaway, has long been a role model for legions of investors and market players across the world. In its September quarter results presentation, Motilal Oswal Financial Services too credited the Omaha-based investment holding company as its inspiration.
It went beyond platitude though.
The Mumbai-headquartered diversified finserv group also runs a formidable investment holding operation—treasury, as it calls it. Now, its size and scope is far smaller than Berkshire Hathaway’s. But it is still formidable enough for Motilal Oswal to anoint it Engine No.2, with Engine No.1 being its operating businesses such as wealth management, asset management, capital market, and housing finance.
This Engine No.2 sets Motilal Oswal apart from its peers, who either do not run a treasury or do not deem it big enough to warrant a separate disclosure. In contrast, Motilal Oswal considers treasury a “strong backbone” whenever the core operating businesses need capital. And yes, it takes big external bets too, like the recent Rs 400 crore one in Zepto.
The upshot? The treasury book has ballooned to eye-popping numbers and accounted for as much as half the company’s profit in some past years. But like most good things in life and business, this sword is double-edged too. In her deep-dive today, Aakriti finds out how Motilal Oswal did what it did, and why its treasured superpower risks being its kryptonite.
