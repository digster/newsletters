Good Morning Ishan,
Flipkart lost out when Phonepe went its own way in 2022, so the e-commerce company is trying a do-over with a new fintech subsidiary, Super.money.
So far, Flipkart has devoted US$20 million to bring Super.money up to par, spearheaded by Prakash Sikaria—“the 0-to-1 man for Kalyan [Krishnamurthy]”—who came up with Flipkart Video, the company’s ad business, and reselling platform Shopsy, according to a former Flipkart executive who spoke to The Ken.
Super.money is credit-first—think of personal loans and payments using credit cards. It’s run as a standalone unit, in its own location away from Flipkart’s office in Bengaluru. Super.money launched its beta in late June. In the month that followed, it roped in 10 million users by offering 5% cashback. A former Flipkart executive said the platform is on track to hitting 25 million users by the end of September.
The way resources are being poured into Super.money has ruffled feathers within Flipkart, which has its own fintech services. Some senior managers who are part of that Flipkart division, led by senior vice-president Dheeraj Aneja, have quit. As a former executive put it, “You don’t have money to power your own burn, but you are now investing in this?”
Super.money is looking to raise even more money from investors other than Flipkart. Its objective is to become one of the top five apps in India, but it will be difficult to peel users away from India’s 70+ payment apps—especially the top two platforms, Phonepe and Google Pay.
Flipkart’s newest subsidiary will need to develop superpowers to hit that mark. In today’s [story](//the-ken.com/story/can-kalyan-krishnamurthys-ideas-man-build-another-phonepe-for-flipkart/), Arundhati presents the lay of the land and how Super.money fits into India’s cutthroat fintech space.
