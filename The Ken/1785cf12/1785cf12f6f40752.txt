Insurance administration is probably a thankless job. Day in and day out, someone sits behind the counter and tells those with loved ones in the hospital that only so much money has been sanctioned, and could you please pay the rest of the bill so we can discharge the patient?

For those on the other side of the counter, though, it doesn’t matter who’s working out the nitty-gritty of insurance paperwork. As long as it gets done, no one—not the policyholder, not the hospital, not even the insurance company itself—bothers about the details.

And that, in a nutshell, defines the existence of one of the biggest and most important cogs in India’s sprawling healthcare system—the third-party administrators (TPAs).  India’s TPAs processed about $2 billion worth of claims for the year ended March 2020. That’s about two-thirds of the total amount claimed by Indian policyholders that year. The rest was processed in-house by private insurers.

Despite their importance, growth in the sector is slow; at around 10-12%, it’s growing at just around half the pace of the health insurance sector. In addition, more and more private insurers are opting to process claims in-house instead of contracting out to TPAs. Many TPAs also get the vast majority of their business from the public sector, which is a notoriously low-margin channel despite the scale it offers.

All this paints a sorry picture for investors. India’s biggest TPA, Medi Assist, for instance, began looking to either raise funds or sell back in 2017. It’s still looking.

Bengaluru-based Vidal Health, though, seems to have taken notes. It knew what it had to offer wasn’t enough, not after the insurance regulator ruled that policyholders could pick and choose which TPA to use. It needed scale, it needed revenue streams other than the government, it needed ways to leverage its tech. And perhaps most importantly, it needed to attract investors.

On 9 March, it acquired Chennai-based Vipul Medcorp for an undisclosed amount, making the combined entity into one of the top three TPAs in the country. But is that enough to weather the impending consolidation in the space?
