The first was the third value.

1. The Third Value

"Doing things" is the value I consider important enough to be in the top three for both adults and children. You may know it through fancier versions, such as "taking initiative" or "bias for action". 

Allow me to backtrack, starting with the most foundational of values for me: curiosity.

None of us, regardless of our age or experience, have a full view of the world with all its complexities and wonders. It is curiosity that drives us to know more of it. To explore. To hypothesise. To understand. 

As we do, our understanding of the world shifts. And with it, our perspective too. From making sense of the world given to us, we begin to form beliefs that emanate from within us. Our locus of control shifts from external to internal. It is belief that fosters in us the urge to create. 

And the most logical outcome for a belief is an action, for me. Actions are where beliefs are put to test. 

Don't like the way your work role is structured? Want to pick up the electric guitar? Fancy your chances as an artist? Not impressed with your Chess.com score? Wonder if that brilliant business concept you came up with is going to succeed? Agonised about the cruelty inherent in raising meat for food?

Do something about it. 

Everyone has ideas and beliefs. Only a fraction do anything about them. 

This is why "doing things" is a value I try to inculcate in not just my son, but also myself. It doesn't have the pithy of a value like, say, resilience, but action is what truly defines us. 

Perhaps most importantly, actions are what enable a lifelong feedback loop to guide us. It is only when we do things that we realise what we truly enjoy versus what we only thought we would enjoy. 

And as both parents, managers or leaders, our job is to provide the space for beliefs to emerge and actions to take place. It is for us to question, to nudge, and to support. 

Thank you to the subscriber for forcing me to articulate this. And for giving me the idea to ask all of you.
