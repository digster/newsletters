Bhavna Hiremath on gender pay inequality
“Claudia Goldin is an American economic historian and a professor of Economics at Harvard University. She has won the Sveriges Riksbank Prize in Economic Sciences in Memory of Alfred Nobel this year. Her research on the inequity between men and women's pay and employment rate is intriguing because it comes at a time when we think we know all about the historical reasons and the current challenges of gender inequity in employment and pay. The research throws a very different light on this topic by taking us back 200 years and weaving the facts on the timeline, thus bringing us to the present situation. The reasons are now clearer than ever and this should only enable the governments and private enterprises to look at the history and plan the next steps to deal with this long term inequity.... There's no hurriedness, rather a deeper rumination. Which in itself feels relaxing, like loosening the tense brain muscles from the week's events. I'd love for this community to be addressing what it means to cold-start—the privilege of it, the loneliness of it, the cognitive load of it....
