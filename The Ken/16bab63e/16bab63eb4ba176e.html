Among SaaS (software-as-a-service) startups, the $1M ARR (annual recurring revenue) figure is considered somewhat like turning 18. That’s when you go from being an adolescent to an adult. Like with young adults, for $1M+ ARR SaaS startups too, from there on the world is a wide open field. It’s their oyster. 

Going from $1M in ARR to $10M is hard. And from there to $100M, way harder. There are only two Indian SaaS companies that have crossed $100M in ARR. And incredibly, one of them managed to go from $1M to $100M in just over five years, placing it among an elite list of global startups who have achieved such “hockey stick” growth. The company is Chennai-headquartered Freshworks, founded by Girish Mathrubootham in 2011. In just eight years, Mathrubootham has taken Freshworks from being a company created out of a casual comment on HackerNews to being worth $1.5 billion. 

How did he do it? By understanding his two real competitive strengths and by chasing a “red ocean” market strategy instead of a “blue ocean” one. But will those hold up as he aims for an IPO and $1 billion in ARR? That’s Sumanth’s story today: https://the-ken.com/story/freshworks-zero-100-million/ (10-minute read)
