One has to credit the startup culture for popularising the 10X or 100X term, even though it has proven self-destructing for many. Not every business can or must grow at breakneck speed, right?

After 35 years, one of India’s most successful test-prep institutes (for JEE-NEET) is ready to swallow this growth pill. It isn’t surprising why.

In mid-2022, promoters of Allen Career Institute divested a 36% stake for US$600 million from Bodhi Tree Systems— an investment platform by James Murdoch, founder and CEO of Lupa Systems, and Uday Shankar, former chief of Walt Disney Asia Pacific.

Some 10 months later, a new CEO has taken charge, and a steady stream of professionals has begun assuming control of different parts of the business. The Maheshwari family’s ambition is 100X growth. It wants to go from training 250,000 students in 2022 to 25 million in the next five years.

The storied town of Kota, the coaching powerhouse with 100+ institutes, saw a fierce battle of wits, funds, and survival last year. Well-funded edtechs like Physicswallah and Unacademy brought some unexpected but welcome free publicity to the offline centres.

In a well-reported story today, Alifiya writes how the stakes went up a notch during that warfare, but if anyone thought Allen’s dominance was in danger, then the Maheshwari family proved them wrong.

It has taken the fight back to the edtech’s turf. And how? Allen Ace, Allen Global, Allen Next, and more. 100X or not, it’s certainly aiming for 10X.
