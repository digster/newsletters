Good Morning Ishan,
 
I know it is early in the morning, but could you do me a small favour? If you come across life, tell her to slow down. Pause, every once in a while. Reflect. And read.
 
“Because morale was so low, I went around meeting our people and giving them hope. I began to watch movies in which the underdog won, the sort of stories where guerrillas defeated a big army. Everywhere I went, I told my people that I knew that we were not expected to win. But, I would tell them, if we can win against the odds, then we make history.”
 
That’s Sunil Bharti Mittal, founder and chairman of Bharti Enterprises.
 
If you’ve been following the turbulent shifts in the fortunes of the telecom businesses, by now you’d know that with the arrival of Reliance Jio and merger of Idea and Vodafone, Bharti Airtel is in a tough spot. Relegated to No.2, from No.1. And so, you’d almost assume that this is Mittal speaking about today's state of affairs.
 
You know what; you’d be wrong. His quote is from 2002. Seventeen years back, to the time when the Ambanis and BSNL got into the telecom business. There was widespread fear back then that both new players would undercut the GSM operators, and so Airtel’s days were numbered. The chapter on Mittal from Vir Sanghvi’s book, Men of Steel, makes for fantastic business history reading.
 
We are now in 2019. Bharti Airtel is still here. Still fighting the good fight.
 
My colleagues Rohin and Vandana say that the current setback has only liberated the company. Both say that the shock of losing the No.1 position in India has rid the company of its arrogance, passivity and organisational myopia. In fact, investors no longer want Airtel to behave like an incumbent, defending its market share. They expect Airtel to fight back, one tough decision after another, and create a sustainable business.
 
Airtel’s strategy is premiumisation. Going after 450 million customers from 1 billion, those that contribute the bulk of the industry’s revenues.
Today’s piece (the second of two parts) is perhaps your first window into Airtel’s fight back plan. It is both timely and deeply reported. You can read it here: https://the-ken.com/story/airtels-fightback-plan/
