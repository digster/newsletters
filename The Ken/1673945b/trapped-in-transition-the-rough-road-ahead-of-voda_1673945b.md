In telecom, size matters. Which is why, country after country has made the case that fewer, larger operators are best for a healthy market. Bharti Airtel chairman Sunil Mittal endorsed this when he called the Vodafone-Idea merger “a perfect match” at a Davos meeting, months before the two telcos officially announced their merger plans.
Vodafone Idea's maiden quarterly results, which came out last week, and bits of news trickling in ever since raise the question: At what price has this size come? Reportedly, Vodafone Idea is seeking deferral of the Rs 900 crore spectrum-linked payment to the government it needs to make by March 2019.
As dire a situation as this may signal, the Vodafone Idea top management on Wednesday put up a confident front before their first post-merger analysts meet. Contrary to everyone’s belief that the fresh promoter capital infusion of Rs 25,000 crore ($3.47 billion) would lead to an uglier price war in India, Vodafone Idea doesn’t see the tariff war going on. Nor does it see the tariffs sliding further.
Really? They think the upstart’s (Reliance Jio) offers are not sustainable?
All the calculations that Vodafone Idea must have had done on the spectrum synergies, pricing, network integration, digital offerings, better customer experience…have gone awry. The merger that was meant to shake up the market, has shaken the merged entity itself. Even Mr Mittal got it wrong on the merger and its various benefits.
The upshot for the management though is that in a post-integration company, at least employee challenges are not there. Thank god for small mercies, you’d say, once you read Shashi’s story today on where Vodafone Idea Ltd has landed itself and what lies ahead. A nervy battle, to say the least.
