Investors have fallen out of love with Ola’s ride-hailing business. But its founder’s reluctance to let go and a host of internal troubles are stopping them from breaking up
Barring a few, investors in Ola Consumer have been quietly lobbying for the firm’s buyout, while Aggarwal continues to push for a delayed IPO. No ideal offer is on the table yet. Some of the investors, including Softbank, Tiger Global, and Z47, prefer to wait and watch to avoid a distress sale. Meanwhile, Ola is facing a significant leadership vacuum and rapidly losing ground to Uber and Rapido in terms of monthly active users. Its cash burn has also worsened. Bhavish Aggarwal has also doubled down on Ola Electric and Krutrim, making Ola Consumer seem abandoned.
