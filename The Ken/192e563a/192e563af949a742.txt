Happy Deepavali! Here's Two by Two's journey so far 
 By Rohin Dharmakumar https://the-ken.com/writers/rohin/

6 min read

Good Morning Ishan,

Happy Deepavali and Karnataka Rajyotsava to all those celebrating. We didn’t have a regular episode of Two by Two yesterday given the festivities—the Two by Two team is taking a short break this week—but we’ll be back to regular programming next week onwards with a whole new line-up. 

Until then, if you wish to, you can check out some of the 15 episodes we’ve released over these past four months. Our first two episodes are still available in full for anyone who’d like to give them a listen—no subscription required—as are four other episodes which we’ve unlocked for a limited time. 

In fact, if you are a Free or Basic plan subscriber, I’d suggest checking out the episode we unlocked this Monday https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/netflixs-last-growth-market/. It’s a sprawling, insightful discussion where my co-host Praveen Gopal Krishnan and I debate what Netflix’s strategy should be in India with our guests: Kunj Sanghvi, Content and Creative Head of Kuku FM, and Nishad Kenkre, Operating Partner at Verlinvest. 

Of course, if you are a Premium subscriber (or choose to upgrade to The Ken’s Premium plan https://the-ken.com/pricing/?utm_source=newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=twobytwo or get a monthly subscription https://podcasts.apple.com/in/podcast/two-by-two/id1757938645 on Apple Podcasts) you can listen to all our episodes in full. 

#1: Will Flipkart become Phonepe before Phonepe becomes Flipkart?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/will-flipkart-become-phonepe-before-phonepe-becomes-flipkart/Walmart-owned entities seem to be roving into each other’s territories in search of growing their customer base and revenue.

#2: Is Zepto a gold medalist or a bronze medalist?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/is-zepto-a-gold-medalist-or-a-bronze-medalist/Zepto is not the biggest quick commerce player in the market, yet they’re the talk of the town.

#3: Swiggy needs to reclaim its past glory
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/swiggy-needs-to-reclaim-its-past-glory/The Bengaluru-based startup pioneered both food delivery and quick commerce in India, but competitors have pulled ahead in the race.

#4: Google Pay: Big. Successful. Vulnerable
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/google-pay-big-successful-vulnerable/How long before UPI businesses come to attack the UPI product?

#5: Why couldn’t Stripe become the Stripe of India?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/why-couldnt-stripe-become-the-stripe-of-india/It’s the world’s most powerful fintech, and yet it failed to capitalise on the world’s second-largest startup market–India.

#6: Netflix’s last growth market
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/netflixs-last-growth-market/Today, Netflix is in its strongest position ever. But how Netflix does tomorrow depends on whether it can succeed and scale in India–its last growth market.

#7: Ather Energy was a pioneer. Can it also be a leader?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/ather-energy-was-a-pioneer-can-it-also-be-a-leader/The co-founder and CEO of Ather Energy discusses the IPO-bound company’s strategy with the director of one of India’s most prestigious management institutes.

#8: Health insurance in India is ripe for disruption
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/health-insurance-is-ripe-for-disruption/From the surface, health insurance is one of the brightest spots in India’s insurance story, growing at nearly 20% annually. But that number doesn’t tell the whole story.

#9: Private coaching is eating away at schooling
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/private-coaching-is-eating-away-at-schooling/Twelve years of schooling is losing out to private coaching as entry into India’s colleges gets increasingly centralized via entrance exams.

#10: How will Ola and Uber avoid “death by a thousand cuts”?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/how-will-ola-and-uber-avoid-death-by-a-thousand-cuts/The ride-hailing giants conquered city after city using a disruptive model and technology, and now the same thing threatens to happen to them.

#11: Why has all the excitement and disruption gone out of startups?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/why-has-all-the-excitement-and-disruption-gone-out-of-startups/A decade back, they were the answer in response to a boring and unexciting job. Today, a job at a startup seems to have become just that.

#12: Delhi pricked the Bengaluru bubble
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/delhi-pricked-the-bangalore-bubble/Have Delhi startups pulled ahead in the race and claimed the startup capital title from Bengaluru?

#13: Do we even need Product Managers?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/do-we-even-need-product-managers/If you are a product manager, especially in India, you’re probably going through a crisis of faith and existence.

#14: Is the golden era of the (software) engineer over?
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/is-the-golden-era-of-the-software-engineer-over/In an age where AI chatbots are capable of writing code and serving as a programmer, what will engineers do?

#15: The relentless rise of the government as a competitor
 https://the-ken.com/podcasts/two-by-two/the-relentless-rise-of-the-government-as-a-competitor/What happens when the government plays the role of regulator, policymaker and competitor?

And that’s how far we’ve gotten in Two by Two’s journey. 

We’d also like to take this opportunity to ask you what you feel would make for good future Two by Two episodes, and to suggest guests we should consider inviting for them. Essentially, people who know what they’re talking about and aren’t afraid to speak their minds. 

You can write to us at twobytwo@the-ken.com mailto:twobytwo@the-ken.com. We’ll see you next week with a new episode!

Regards,

Rohin Dharmakumar
