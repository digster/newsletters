Today's article deals with a tale as old as time itself. In fact, it's a story that deals with the passage of time, and the subsequent challenges that it brings. It's a story of the strong growing old, and eventually being challenged by younger, hungrier competitors, all jostling for supremacy. Survival of the fittest – it's something we see all the time in nature. And, as the case of Bessemer Venture Partners (BVP) India shows, in business as well.
 
The oldest venture fund in the US, BVP's India division has been a force to be reckoned with in the past. With a sterling track record on exits in the past 14-odd years, the fund seemed poised for big things as recently as 2016. However, things have been on a downward slide since then. A series of different factors have seen BVP India go from top dogs to fighting for scraps. 
 
And now, things for the once-thriving fund have now slowed down, markedly, with BVP halting their investments in India. Is this the end of BVP's India connection? What brought about this dire state of affairs? And what lessons can other VC funds learn from BVP's initial success and subsequent condition?
