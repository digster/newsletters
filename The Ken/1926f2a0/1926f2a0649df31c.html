You’re at a family reunion, and your cousins are trading tales of how they’ve bounced back from their latest crises. One cousin is bragging about raising millions through equity deals, while another has just cashed in with some big institutional investors. And then there’s you—having borrowed a hefty sum because you’ve got… big plans.

At least, that’s what you tell everyone.

This, dear reader, is the world of Adani Total Gas, where once upon a time—okay, in January 2023—it was the pride of the family. Market cap: Rs 4.3 lakh crore. P/E ratio: 900X! Then came Hindenburg, crashing the party and sending stocks tumbling across the board, Adani Total Gas included.

Cut to 2024: while most family members have been charming investors, Adani Total Gas decided to secure a US$375 million debt package—the largest in the city gas sector (which supplies natural gas to urban consumers). Slick. Except it had been talking about its low debt levels until not long ago.

Why did it suddenly double down on debt? Well, when your stock is down 80% from pre-Hindenburg heights, you don’t have much choice left.

Besides, this one isn’t a typical Adani family member; it has one more promotor—French giant TotalEnergies—sharing an equal stake.

Sure, Adani Total Gas would like to be India’s largest city gas distributor. But it’s only delivered a fraction of its ambitious promises. Translation: it needs cash—and fast. And with debt piling up, profits lagging, and millions of customers waiting for their gas fix, the clock is ticking. Will debt help turn the tide for the company’s beleaguered investors?

You don’t want to miss Anand’s story today. (Oh! By the way, a mysterious name has emerged on the shareholder list, while another is conspicuously absent.)
