In the times we live in, a startup turning profitable is more headline-worthy than them becoming a unicorn.

So when online marketplace Meesho declared that it had turned profitable, it caught our attention.

On 29 December, Meesho said it recorded a profit in the quarter ended September. This came after a turbulent year of layoffs and valuation swings. So when all eyes were on the 77% revenue growth, a key venture was overlooked: Meesho Mall.

Meesho started as a social-commerce platform for resellers before pivoting to become a marketplace full of unbranded products. For a time, it even flirted with grocery deliveries. But throughout, the Bengaluru-based company was clear about who its audience is: the growing base of online shoppers in India’s smaller cities and towns.

But with Mall, it’s shaking things up for itself.

Meesho Mall features products across 6–7 categories from over 500 brands, like Mamaearth and Himalaya (personal care), Boat (electronic gadgets), and Paragon (footwear)—not premium labels but a mix of well-known and direct-to-consumer companies. This is a stark contrast to the generic products with descriptive names such as “Black Printed Sneakers for Men” and “Round Neck Puff Half Sleeves Polyester Blend Top” that occupy most space on the platform.

Branded goods are meant to keep the profit machine humming—from higher commissions to better ads. But winning a brand’s love is no mean feat.
