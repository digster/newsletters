As sure as night follows day, sobering downturns follow market frenzies. This is as true for the hiring bazaar as it is for the stock exchanges.

Booms and busts often have memorable markers. In the go-go days of the late 1920s, even shoeshine boys on Wall Street were said to be giving stock tips—before the Great Depression crashed the party.

About a century later, in 2021, the great Indian startup hiring boom was epitomised by fintech Bharatpe giving out BMW bikes to tech talent—before the funding crunch brought along with it a screeching jobs slowdown.

The hiring scene has since changed, and how. The change in scenery has become more pronounced in recent months.

From rolling out fast and furious in 2021, jobs are now taking their own sweet time to reach their seekers. It’s an employers’ market, all right, and they are spoilt for choice.

Ergo: job seekers are being put through the grinder—they now have to face far more rounds of screening than before, undergo a battery of tests, and endure long delays before they land an offer letter, if at all they do.

“To be honest, it felt like a trial,” said a 30-year-old senior business analyst who finally got an offer from a consulting firm, a full 61 days after the process started. That’s double the average time it took job seekers in 2022 and thrice that in 2017.

The pain is felt across job profiles and levels; some have it worse than others.

While job seekers bear the brunt, the pinch is felt across the ecosystem—from hiring agencies to ironically, employers, too. There are some silver linings, though.
