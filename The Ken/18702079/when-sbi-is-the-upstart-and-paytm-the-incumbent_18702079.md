In 2019, when Paytm* began providing its merchants with a plain-looking "sound box" that loudly announced the notification of a UPI payment, people didn't pay much attention.
But after seeing its success for Paytm, a thousand competitors entered the fray. In the last year, Paytm's rivals PhonePe, BharatPe, and Google Pay have all started issuing this device. Now, India's largest bank, the State Bank of India, also wants to lay more focus on this device, according to Rounak, the author of today's story.
All of them are using the soundbox with one goal in mind: to retain the merchant. But for SBI, the stakes are much higher. It needs more merchants to not only use it for payments but also to maintain more balances in their SBI current accounts.
Balances in current accounts are a very inexpensive source of funds for banks as they do not have to pay any interest rates. However, SBI is facing some issues in tapping that source. SBI's Current Account Savings Account ratio recently decreased by 176 basis points (bps) to 44.48%—the lowest in four years.
*Paytm founder Vijay Shekhar Sharma is an investor in The Ken.
