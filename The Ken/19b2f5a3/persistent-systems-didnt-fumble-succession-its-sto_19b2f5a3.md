Good Morning Ishan,
Sometimes messy, sometimes public, the leadership transition in Indian IT has often been storied. Not the least because it’s also one of the most closely watched industries.
Infosys, Wipro, Mindtree, Tech Mahindra…all have had their share of agonising momentum loss. It’s also why TCS almost never hires outsiders in leadership roles. In Pune, Persistent Systems had a brief brush with leadership blues, but it’s been a steady run since 2020, under CEO Sandeep Kalra.
Founder Anand Deshpande tells The Ken that when mulling leadership transition, he sought advice from founders who had succeeded—and failed—at succession, distilling three rules: stay busy outside the firm, maintain a direct channel with the CEO, and avoid all other internal contact.
Deshpande stuck to those rules. (And he must be secretly pleased with himself for doing so.)
A December 2025 study by Motilal Oswal ranked Persistent among India’s 10 fastest wealth creators over five years—the only IT-services firm on the list.
Persistent’s valuation, at around 60 times earnings, now tops mid-tier peers LTIMindtree (38) and Coforge (55) and dwarfs larger rivals like TCS and Infosys, which trade in the low 20s.

A total of 70 teams attacked healthcare, 24 went after fintech, 14 picked education, and 12 targeted consulting.
The pattern? These are sectors where knowledge, judgment, and service delivery are the chokepoints. Places where humans are expensive and where information asymmetry exists. Where the experience is broken.
