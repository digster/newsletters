Other stories include:
Crash Testing GPT-4 [ https://substack.com/redirect/7ab8bc01-9b61-4b45-86d4-ce4c009f2bf7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]: Before releasing GPT-4, OpenAI sent a preliminary version to the Alignment Research Center to test it for unsafe capabilities; the detail that made the news [ https://substack.com/redirect/b28e01ce-4fa2-4b54-8e07-631bd0589020?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] was how the AI managed to hire a gig worker to solve CAPTCHAs for it by pretending to be a blind person. Asterisk interviews Beth Barnes, leader of the team that ran those tests.
What We Get Wrong About AI And China [ https://substack.com/redirect/8911a62a-18d9-4bee-b07f-27eff8cde6ea?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]: Professor Jeffrey Ding discusses the Chinese AI situation. If I’m understanding right, China is 1-2 years behind the US, but that this number underplays the size of the gap, and if the US stopped innovating today, China wouldn’t necessarily push ahead in 3 years. Today’s Marginal Revolution links [ https://substack.com/redirect/aea63680-9590-42e0-b4da-612579cd4b48?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] included a claim [ https://substack.com/redirect/cf640863-987c-4fc2-86c2-cddf04e9bceb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] that a new Chinese model beats GPT-4; I’m very skeptical and waiting to hear more.
The Transistor Cliff [ https://substack.com/redirect/ffff4a52-6876-417b-9838-35b418aa7121?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]: Sarah Constantin on the future of microchips. Most predictions about the future of AI center around the idea that lower compute costs → bigger training runs → smarter models. But how sure are we that we can keep decreasing compute costs indefinitely? Will we reach physical limits or memory bottlenecks? What if we do?
A Debate About AI And Explosive Growth [ https://substack.com/redirect/560c57be-52d1-40fe-be53-a39cc2de6e8a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]: Tamay Besiroglu vs. Matt Clancy. Will AI be just another invention that is probably good for the economy but leaves GDP trajectories overall unchanged? Or will it create a technoeconomic singularity leading to “impossibly” fast economic growth? A good followup for my recent Davidson On Takeoff Speeds [ https://substack.com/redirect/856f0af4-75cf-4af5-9f2c-a96ceea39ede?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. I don’t think they emphasized enough the claim that the natural trajectory of growth is a hyperbola reaching infinity in the 2030s, we’ve only deviated from that natural curve since ~1960 or so [ https://substack.com/redirect/86973eb0-d5b4-486d-9608-85eef348faa4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and that we’re just debating whether AI will restore the natural curve rather than whether it will do some bizarre unprecedented thing that we should have a high prior against.
Plus superforecaster Jonathan Mann on whether AI will take tech jobs [ https://substack.com/redirect/aa0863ac-c755-428d-9dcc-934eaed139f8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Kelsey Piper on the different camps within AI safety [ https://substack.com/redirect/082bbb9a-ca6b-42bc-9103-285f22d5cfdb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Michael Gordin on how long until Armageddon [ https://substack.com/redirect/e292e4de-263e-48d2-bb75-13381fe0da53?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (surprisingly not AI related!), Robert Long on what the history of debating animal intelligence tells us about AI intelligence [ https://substack.com/redirect/0947554d-66e1-4d13-8da2-e103d6985720?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Avital Balwit on the technical aspects of regulating AI compute [ https://substack.com/redirect/0f83a2fe-f301-487c-a029-45c2724d9047?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Carl Robichaud on how we (sort of) succeeded at nuclear non-proliferation [ https://substack.com/redirect/559dd24f-5cc8-45cd-a356-a817944e9cdb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and Jamie Wahls’ short story about chatbot romance [ https://substack.com/redirect/915f3b18-e22b-40ed-9e16-3c36e3789f0a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
