Tell us about yourself.
This feels like a very existential question? My name is Andrea Hernandez, I currently lead a cult that worships sexy pantry items and sultry snax called Snaxshot, I'm pretty sure I was an iguana in a past life as I do not tolerate cold weather and need copious amounts of sunshine. My favorite food is anything savory, I love making my own ceviche, am a huge fan of martinis and castelvetrano olives which I can snack on at all times during the day, a friend to all dogs, and am also an Aries, which I'm sure will have everyone responding immediately with -- that makes sense.
Describe your media diet. 
Podcast-- Otherworld (real people telling paranormal stories)  
Dieline (fun weekly commentary on design news) 
Really love the roundups of memes via Taylor Lorenz 
Really nerdy journalistic papers I come across through research 
Arena, Instagram, and Pinterest for curating imagery 
What’s the last great book you read?
Just re-read Ficciones by Borges [ https://substack.com/redirect/57e871c2-6c5f-407b-bb12-1d78ca3d66e6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], a compilation of his best short stories.
Before that, the last one I consumed in one day was Cultish.
What are you reading now?
Currently not reading anything in particular at the moment and don't feel guilty or obligated to pretend I am, let's normalize not having to post about the 100 books we read a year, and be able to live our lives presently.
What’s your reading strategy when you pick up a print copy of your favorite publication?
Enjoy, lol? People have reading strategies? It feels very much like Americana culture, as a Latin American, I think of reading as a pleasure and indulgence, no need to strategize.
Who should everyone be reading that they’re not?
Alicia Kennedy [ https://substack.com/redirect/6b4aebad-18c9-47b9-b33c-afc0ed6141ab?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Bettina Makalintal [ https://substack.com/redirect/bcbc4c74-b940-4874-b290-c802e6b92c90?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Emily Sundberg [ https://substack.com/redirect/90e75cf6-c103-4f3c-af72-3038a2d6b7c2?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and Taylor Lorenz [ https://substack.com/redirect/2c06f9bf-e64d-46ae-a137-00bd57f73707?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] for sure but also if you want to mind trip go for Latin American authors that dove into magical realism, my favorite is Borges of course, combining science, math, philosophy, the concepts of infinity, time, etc wrapped in airs of mystery, thrill and humanity.  
What is the best non-famous app you love on your phone? 
Delli [ https://substack.com/redirect/3baab09b-5392-4cce-ac0d-5e77067fdfdc?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] by Simon Beckerman (founder of Depop) it's still relatively new.
Plane or train?
Trains, even though we don't have trains in my country/city but I love to take trains in the Northeast, flying is a scientific marvel but every little turbulence sends me into an anxiety spiral
What is one place everyone should visit? 
Wadi Rum, the vast desert felt like everything and nothingness in one, plus you'll never see the sky as beautiful as in the pitch darkness there, the sunsets are filled with colors you've probably never experienced either.
Tell us the story of a rabbit hole you fell deep into. 
I love a good rabbit hole--this piece [ https://substack.com/redirect/b4a38d29-0dce-40a3-967b-0123cf312a6b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on psychedelics in the old testament, scientists discovered that plants in the Sinai desert have the same psychedelic properties as Ayahuasca, taking apart scriptures of Moses' time crossing the desert and trying to justify it as a psychedelic trip.
Most recently too, going down magic and the ring of Solomon [ https://substack.com/redirect/9165f6cb-63bf-4f34-821e-3876e43dde40?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Duncan Trussell has many good episodes in relation to this, [ https://substack.com/redirect/43df08ed-c0fb-4fb9-81f1-d036487ff4c9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] learning that Dr. Doolittle took inspiration from tales of King Solomon as a demon slayer and being able to talk to animals, etc.
