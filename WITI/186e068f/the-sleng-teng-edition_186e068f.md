I was pulling the thread a bit on musical “backbones” and found a delightful and unlikely example rooted in the preset rhythm pattern programmed into the Casiotone MT-40, released in 1981. According to One of the programmers working on the project, a new graduate, Hiroko Okuda, was more familiar with popular styles of the moment and programmed a pre-set that would live in infamy in Jamaican dancehall history.
