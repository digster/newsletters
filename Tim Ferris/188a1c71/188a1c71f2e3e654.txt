Word of the week
----------------

Callipygian, “having well-shaped buttocks.” From Ancient Greek:
κάλλος (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/e5u4vlw0vkt7hp7pv6rim/x0hph6hwpo9lgxb5/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWt0aW9uYXJ5Lm9yZy93aWtpLyVDRSVCQSVDRSVBQyVDRSVCQiVDRSVCQiVDRSVCRiVDRiU4MiNBbmNpZW50X0dyZWVr
) (kállos, “beauty”) + πυγή (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/e5u4vlw0vkt7hp7pv6rim/dpheh0h0ov8m3dtm/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWt0aW9uYXJ5Lm9yZy93aWtpLyVDRiU4MCVDRiU4NSVDRSVCMyVDRSVBRSNBbmNpZW50X0dyZWVr
) (pugḗ, “buttocks”). To easily remember it, think of this word:
calligraphy, “the art of beautiful writing, elegant penmanship,”
1610s, from Latinized form of Greek kaligraphia, from kállos
“beauty” + graphein “to write.”
