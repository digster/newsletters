Movie I’m absolutely loving

​KPop Demon Hunters. I’m late to the party, but better late than never. Now, I’m gonzo. I’ve been force-feeding myself all of the related YouTube videos I can find. This animated action-musical is everywhere and has become Netflix’s most popular film of all time. But I didn’t know any of this until last night. I showed up for my annual full-body MRI (e.g., Biograph or Prenuvo) yesterday, and the technician said, “For your viewing, I see you requested KPop Demon Hunters,” to which I replied, “What the hell is that?” Then I realized that I’d been punked by my assistant. Nice one. I decided to roll with it, and I’m so glad that I did. It has a 99% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes. As soon as I finished it, I thought, “Oh, I could easily watch this again tomorrow.” The animation is truly spectacular, and the music is the most earworm-rich that I’ve heard in years.
​
But the real magic is story, story, and story. This one is elegantly architected and beautifully polished. If you want to dig under the hood, consider starting with The Writer’s Journey or Save the Cat! Goes to the Movies.
​
Short description: “K-pop superstars Rumi (Arden Cho), Mira (May Hong), and Zoey (Ji-young Yoo) use their secret identities as demon hunters to protect their fans from growing, supernatural danger. Together they face their biggest threat: an irresistible rival boy band of demons in disguise.” Yes, it sounds like the last thing I’d ever want to watch, and yet… I’m a true believer.
​
It’s co-directed and co-written by Maggie Kang and Chris Appelhans. For the screenplay, Maggie and Chris were joined by the writing duo Danya Jimenez and Hannah McMechan, with the original story coming from Maggie. Watch the trailer here.
​
This film was seven or so years in the making, and it paid off. Here are just a few impressive stats:
​
• It has become Netflix’s most popular film ever with 266M+ views.

• Four songs from the soundtrack simultaneously hit the Billboard Hot 100 Top 10—a record.

• The song “Your Idol” by the fictional (read that again) Saja Boys reached #1 on Spotify’s US chart, the highest placement ever for a K-pop boy band, surpassing (real) #1 K-pop boy band BTS.

• The KPop Demon Hunters cast, collectively, amassed more than 2.09 billion streams worldwide on Spotify, averaging ~30 million daily streams on 13 tracks.
​
I had so many questions after watching this. Who made this?!? Why wasn’t it in theaters? How and why and when and who?!?
​
So, if you’re curious about that side of things, here’s one piece about the whole enchilada: “The Tragedy of ‘KPop Demon Hunters.’”
​
To be clear, I don’t think it’s a tragedy. Sony made a justifiable decision based on the circumstances and information they had at the time. So criticizing their decision is somewhat like critiquing a top poker player for folding when they ran all the math and probabilities correctly but ended up with a one-in-a-million flip thereafter that would’ve been a windfall. Judge people by processes and not outcomes. Still… damn, Netflix. Lady Luck has smiled upon you, and nicely done.
