Mental model I’m using again
----------------------------

Via negativa: improving by removing. This principle is widely
applied in the “Elimination” section of The 4-Hour Workweek (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/zlumlo2ql9snhk3x965sxiw3gxg00t6h687/dpheh0heqr2r8qim/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS80LUhvdXItV29ya3dlZWstQW55d2hlcmUtRXhwYW5kZWQtVXBkYXRlZC9kcC9CMDAzMUtONlQ4Lw==
), which is effectively 25% of the book. Here’s the basic
heuristic, courtesy of Nassim Taleb (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/zlumlo2ql9snhk3x965sxiw3gxg00t6h687/e0hph7h7k5d5q0s8/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueW91dHViZS5jb20vd2F0Y2g_dj0xOFp4Q3hOMlpNbw==
): “In action, it is a recipe for what to avoid, what not to
do—subtraction, not addition.” (Worthwhile short read: “Via
Negativa: The Process of Making Good Decisions By Eliminating Bad
Ones (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/zlumlo2ql9snhk3x965sxiw3gxg00t6h687/7qh7h8h903x3w9hz/aHR0cHM6Ly9jb2ZmZWVhbmRqdW5rLmNvbS92aWEtbmVnYXRpdmEv
