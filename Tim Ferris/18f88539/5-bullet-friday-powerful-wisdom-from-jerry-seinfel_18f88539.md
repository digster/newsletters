Article I’m reading
“[Can psychedelics treat people with a severe brain injury?](https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9ho58zo4t0iw973555fn/7qh7h8hodx8mw7bz/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubmF0aW9uYWxnZW9ncmFwaGljLmNvbS9wcmVtaXVtL2FydGljbGUvc2V2ZXJlLWJyYWluLWluanVyeS11bnByZWNlZGVudGVkLXRoZXJhcHktcHN5Y2hlZGVsaWM=)” by [Jonathan Moens](https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9ho58zo4t0iw973555fn/owhkhqhrx0n7gmsv/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9qb25hdGhhbl9tb2Vucz9sYW5nPWVu) for National Geographic. ([Alternate link](https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9ho58zo4t0iw973555fn/z2hghnh35m78pqap/aHR0cHM6Ly9hcmNoaXZlLmlzL0wzWTRm).) This is a deeply researched and deeply moving piece. It underscores some of the hopes and gaps related to treating “disorders of consciousness.” Coverage of psychedelics tends to overstate how much we understand, and this is a good counterbalance with open questions. Here is an excerpt: At the heart of this scientific initiative is the notion of brain “complexity.” In neuroscience, complexity is a measure of how diverse and unpredictable brain activity is. Brain activity that is uniform and fixed is described as having low complexity. Activity that is scattered, unpredictable, and rapidly changing from one state to the next is considered to be highly complex. Over the past two decades, a series of experiments have shown that brains of people who are awake and healthy tend to be higher in this complexity measure than those of people who are in dreamless sleep, sedated, or have a disorder of consciousness. The findings have led scientists to hypothesize that complexity acts as a reliable indicator for how conscious someone is, making it a particularly powerful tool for assessing the conscious level of individuals who can’t communicate but who might still be aware, as with patients with disorders of consciousness. For a deep dive on the potential application of psychedelic compounds to traumatic brain injury (TBI), I suggest [my conversation with Stanford’s Dr. Nolan Williams](https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9ho58zo4t0iw973555fn/p8heh9hzrpdlgqsq/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDI0LzAxLzA1L25vbGFuLXdpbGxpYW1zLz91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJmJnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQjY29udGVudA==) that covers new science related to ibogaine and GDNF as well as applications of direct brain stimulation.
