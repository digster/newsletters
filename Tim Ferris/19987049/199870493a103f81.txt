Study I’m tracking
------------------

"Some people struggle after psychedelics. A new study wants to
help. (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/o8u50wr80xfqh6w9zo0hvhq4m9rrrioh75d/x0hph6hew5oxm2h5/aHR0cHM6Ly9tYXBzLm9yZy9uZXdzL2J1bGxldGluL3Bvc3QtcHN5Y2hlZGVsaWMtY2hhbGxlbmdlcy1zdHVkeS8=
)" If you have experienced difficulties after taking a
psychedelic, you may be eligible to participate in a study
sponsored by Emory University. Click here to learn more (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/o8u50wr80xfqh6w9zo0hvhq4m9rrrioh75d/6qheh8hl7mz04eto/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucHN5Y2hlZGVsaWNjaGFsbGVuZ2VzLm9yZy8=
). Adverse events and challenges are underreported, and it’s
incredibly important to study them. They’re not as rare as you
might think, and given the power of the tools, we need to find
better and better ways to mitigate risk and treat people when
they are destabilized. This isn’t for “bad trips,” per se; it’s
about the after-effects. As explained on the website:
​
“‘Bad trips’ are important to study, but our focus is more
specific. We are interested in people who have had distress or
impairment, or have sought help from a family member, friend, or
professional, after taking a psychedelic. What we are focused on
here is the impact: difficulties in the days, weeks, or months
after the psychedelic experience is over.”
