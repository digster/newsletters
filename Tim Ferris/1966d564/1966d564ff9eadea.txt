Audiobook I’ve fallen in love with
----------------------------------

​When We Cease to Understand the World (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h25ggmlbpipnkk733hr/g3hnh5hmlr68r8br/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYXVkaWJsZS5jb20vcGQvV2hlbi1XZS1DZWFzZS10by1VbmRlcnN0YW5kLXRoZS1Xb3JsZC1BdWRpb2Jvb2svQjA5Rk02SjlGSw==
) by Benjamín Labatut (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h25ggmlbpipnkk733hr/9qhzhnhd60zv0ou9/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQmVuamFtJUMzJUFEbl9MYWJhdHV0
). This is probably my favorite book of the last two years. The
prose is gorgeous, and it reads as if your favorite biography and
a fever dream had a love child. I’ve never encountered such a
wellspring of rich historical accuracy and mind-bending
imagination. It was first recommended to me by an iconic
filmmaker, and I pushed it off, thinking to myself, “If I can’t
separate the fact from the fiction, what’s the point?”
​
I was perhaps missing the point. First, I think the separation is
pretty obvious. Second, you should read this book for the pure
joy of it.
​
It was selected as one of The New York Times Book Review’s 10
Best Books of 2021 and shortlisted for the 2021 International
Booker Prize and the 2021 National Book Award for Translated
Literature. Here’s the description: “A fictional examination of
the lives of real-life scientists and thinkers whose discoveries
resulted in moral consequences beyond their imagining. When We
Cease to Understand the World is a book about the complicated
links between scientific and mathematical discovery, madness, and
destruction.”
