Pitch letter I’m enjoying and studying
--------------------------------------

​Steve Albini’s letter to Nirvana (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/75u0qzo2qkh8h6m9eg8sniwzr9r66fn/qvh8h7hdvwzn65bl/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9OaXJ2YW5hL3N0YXR1cy8xNzg4MzYzMTAxMDY4NTA5MjIzPw==
). This is the four-page letter audio engineer Steve Albini (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/75u0qzo2qkh8h6m9eg8sniwzr9r66fn/g3hnh5hmn9rpz8ir/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvU3RldmVfQWxiaW5p
) sent to Nirvana, pitching himself to produce their album In
Utero. Hat tip to Alex Danco (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/75u0qzo2qkh8h6m9eg8sniwzr9r66fn/9qhzhnhdxw092ou9/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9BbGV4X0RhbmNv
) on the Dialectic (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/75u0qzo2qkh8h6m9eg8sniwzr9r66fn/3ohphkh3rm0zdpir/aHR0cHM6Ly9qYWNrc29uZGFobC5jb20vZGlhbGVjdGljL2FsZXgtZGFuY28=
) podcast.​
​
​Early in the letter, Steve writes: ​
​
​I’m only interested in working on records that legitimately
reflect the band’s own perception of their music and existence.
If you will commit yourselves to that as a tenet of the recording
methodology, then I will bust my ass for you. I’ll work circles
around you. I’ll rap your head in with a ratchet…
​
I have worked on hundreds of records (some great, some good, some
horrible, a lot in the courtyard), and I have seen a direct
correlation between the quality of the end result and the mood of
the band throughout the process. If the record takes a long time,
and everyone gets bummed and scrutinizes every step, then the
recordings bear little resemblance to the live band, and the end
result is seldom flattering. Making punk records is definitely a
case where more “work” does not imply a better end result.
Clearly you have learned this yourselves and appreciate the
logic.
​
​And his famous closer: “If a record takes more than a week to
make, somebody’s fucking up.”
