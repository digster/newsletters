Speech I’m reading
------------------

“Personal Renewal (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhnnnoezt3i8rw5000tg/dpheh0h03nx6n5im/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGJzLm9yZy9qb2huZ2FyZG5lci9zZWN0aW9ucy93cml0aW5nc19zcGVlY2hfMS5odG1s
)” by John Gardner (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhnnnoezt3i8rw5000tg/e0hph7h0xozvo5f8/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGJzLm9yZy9qb2huZ2FyZG5lci9zZWN0aW9ucy93cml0aW5ncy5odG1s
), delivered to McKinsey & Company on November 10, 1990. One of
my favorite paragraphs is this one early on:

Not long ago, I read a splendid article on barnacles. I don’t
want to give the wrong impression of the focus of my reading
interests. Sometimes days go by without my reading about
barnacles, much less remembering what I read. But this article
had an unforgettable opening paragraph. “The barnacle,” the
author explained “is confronted with an existential decision
about where it’s going to live. Once it decides … it spends the
rest of its life with its head cemented to a rock.” End of quote.
For a good many of us, it comes to that.
