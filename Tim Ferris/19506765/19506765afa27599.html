Speech I’m reading

“Personal Renewal” by John Gardner, delivered to McKinsey & Company on November 10, 1990. One of my favorite paragraphs is this one early on:

Not long ago, I read a splendid article on barnacles. I don’t want to give the wrong impression of the focus of my reading interests. Sometimes days go by without my reading about barnacles, much less remembering what I read. But this article had an unforgettable opening paragraph. “The barnacle,” the author explained “is confronted with an existential decision about where it’s going to live. Once it decides … it spends the rest of its life with its head cemented to a rock.” End of quote. For a good many of us, it comes to that.

 

Podcast episode I’m listening to

“Michael O’Leary, CEO of Ryanair” (Apple Podcasts, Spotify) on the In Good Company podcast with Nicolai Tangen. Nicolai is the inimitable CEO of Norges Bank Investment Management (NBIM), and he has interviewed guests ranging from Jensen Huang to Magnus Carlsen. I’ve been fascinated by O’Leary since I read Ryanair: How a Small Irish Airline Conquered Europe in 2007. Listen to 10–15 minutes of this and you’ll see what I mean.
