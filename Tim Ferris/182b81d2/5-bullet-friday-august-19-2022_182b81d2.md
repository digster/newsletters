Ursula was one of a kind. It’s almost impossible to overstate how far outside of the box she was for her generation. Much of what we take for granted now was simply unheard of when she was crafting worlds. Here is a brief snippet [from Ursula’s Wikipedia page](https://click.fourhourmail.com/mvu9xermxzs5hgqwe3gs3/m2h7h5hoplgkdgum/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvVXJzdWxhX0suX0xlX0d1aW4=) (bolding and link are mine):
Le Guin’s writing was enormously influential in the field of speculative fiction and has been the subject of intense critical attention. She received numerous accolades, including eight Hugos, six Nebulas, and twenty-two Locus Awards. … Le Guin influenced many other authors, including Booker Prize winner Salman Rushdie, David Mitchell, [Neil Gaiman](https://click.fourhourmail.com/mvu9xermxzs5hgqwe3gs3/dpheh0hqg24zlocm/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE5LzAzLzI4L25laWwtZ2FpbWFuLz91dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX21lZGl1bT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9jYW1wYWlnbj01YmY=), and Iain Banks. After her death in 2018, critic John Clute wrote that Le Guin had “presided over American science fiction for nearly half a century,” while author Michael Chabon referred to her as the “greatest American writer of her generation.”

“[The First-Ever Quantified Self Notes (Plus: LSD as Cognitive Enhancer?)](https://click.fourhourmail.com/mvu9xermxzs5hgqwe3gs3/qvh8h7h85l7g9ecl/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDEzLzA0LzAzL3RoZS1maXJzdC1ldmVyLXF1YW50aWZpZWQtc2VsZi1ub3Rlcy1wbHVzLWxzZC1hcy1jb2duaXRpdmUtZW5oYW5jZXIvP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZg==)” This is a fun slice of history.
