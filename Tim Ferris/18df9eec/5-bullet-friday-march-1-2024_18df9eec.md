From the sub-heading: “For decades, Chip Wilson has been facing a form of muscular dystrophy. Now the notoriously unfiltered billionaire is spending $100 million to cure it.”
Sometimes, the ultra-rich can produce breakthroughs in medicine, if they’re willing to spend big and take risks. Chip is willing to do both. Survival is one hell of a motivator. Here’s one excerpt from the piece: “If you’re going to be in a foxhole, being in one with Chip Wilson feels a little less suffocating,” says Craig Kelley, a 53-year-old FSHD patient. Kelley, who has difficulty going up stairs and can’t take off a T-shirt without bracing himself, guesses the odds of a cure have “tripled” since Wilson got involved. “I hope anybody with any kind of long-term disability can have a bit of a game changer like Chip.” There are also some incredible examples of non-rich people doing the seemingly impossible to save family members.
