What I’m listening to
---------------------

“Song of the Cedars (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/d0uv8kxd86s0hownnr3c9izp25244cl/9qhzhnhd6elnwrt9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueW91dHViZS5jb20vd2F0Y2g_YWJfY2hhbm5lbD1Db3Ntb1NoZWxkcmFrZSZ2PUhIX3RpbXAyUzNF
).” Past podcast guest Giuliana Furci (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/d0uv8kxd86s0hownnr3c9izp25244cl/n2hohvhvxlkeq9u6/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIxLzA3LzI5L2dpdWxpYW5hLWZ1cmNpLz91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJmJnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQjY29udGVudA==
) shared this song with me. Beyond being a lovely and calming
listen, as The Guardian (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/d0uv8kxd86s0hownnr3c9izp25244cl/48hvhehmp7nkl4ix/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cudGhlZ3VhcmRpYW4uY29tL3dvcmxkLzIwMjQvb2N0LzI1L2xlZ2FsLWJpZC1mb3ItZWN1YWRvci1mb3Jlc3QtdG8tYmUtcmVjb2duaXNlZC1hcy1zb25nLWNvLWNyZWF0b3I=
) explains, there is more to the story:
​
​A forest in Ecuador could be recognised as the co-creator of a
song under a groundbreaking legal proposal. A petition is to be
submitted to Ecuador’s copyright office to recognise the Los
Cedros cloud forest as the co-creator of the composition Song of
the Cedars. The action by the More Than Human Life (Moth) project
is the first legal attempt to recognise an ecosystem’s moral
authorship of a work of art. The song contains melodies of
echo-locating bats, howler monkeys, rustling leaves and even a
subterranean recording of the soil taken from the spot where a
new species of fungus was collected and described.
​
I highly recommend the listen. Headphones are ideal.
