Sheila Heen is a New York Times best-selling author, founder of [Triad Consulting Group](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hk7kvehx/g3hnh5he32e0m4hr/aHR0cDovL3RyaWFkY29uc3VsdGluZ2dyb3VwLmNvbS8=), and a deputy director of the [Harvard Negotiation Project](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hk7kvehx/9qhzhnhgp8ged5i9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucG9uLmhhcnZhcmQuZWR1L2NhdGVnb3J5L3Jlc2VhcmNoX3Byb2plY3RzL2hhcnZhcmQtbmVnb3RpYXRpb24tcHJvamVjdC8=) at Harvard Law School, where she has been a member of the faculty for 25 years. Sheila specializes in particularly difficult negotiations, where emotions run high and relationships become strained. She often works with executive teams, helping them to resolve conflict, repair professional relationships, and make sound decisions together. In the public sector, she has provided training for the New England Organ Bank, the Singapore Supreme Court, the Obama White House, and theologians struggling with disagreement over the nature of truth and God.
Sheila is co-author of the New York Times bestsellers [Difficult Conversations: How to Discuss What Matters Most](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hk7kvehx/n2hohvhn34nlv4f6/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9EaWZmaWN1bHQtQ29udmVyc2F0aW9ucy1EaXNjdXNzLVdoYXQtTWF0dGVycy9kcC8wMTQzMTE4NDQ3Lw==) and [Thanks for the Feedback: The Science and Art of Receiving Feedback Well (even when it’s off base, unfair, poorly delivered, and, frankly, you’re not in the mood)](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hk7kvehx/48hvheh0r307mocx/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9UaGFua3MtRmVlZGJhY2stU2NpZW5jZS1SZWNlaXZpbmctV2VsbC9kcC8wMTQzMTI3MTM2Lw==). She has written for the Harvard Business Review and the New York Times—as a guest expert and contributor to the “Modern Love” column—and she has appeared on NPR, Fox News, CNBC’s Power Lunch, and shows as diverse as Oprah and The G. Gordon Liddy Show. She has spoken at the Global Leadership Summit, the Nordic Business Forum, the Smithsonian, Apple, Google, and Microsoft. Sheila is a graduate of Occidental College and Harvard Law School. She is schooled in negotiation daily by her three children.
