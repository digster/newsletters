5-Bullet Friday

Hi All!

Here is your weekly dose of “5-Bullet Friday,” a list of what I’m pondering and exploring.

What I’m watching —
Comedians in Cars Getting Coffee, directed and hosted by Jerry Seinfeld (@JerrySeinfeld). This saved me when I had COVID and isolated for ten days. At 17–20 minutes apiece, I would watch one episode first thing in the morning with my own coffee, I would watch more episodes whenever I needed to lie down, and I would watch another one or two before bed. There are 80+ episodes, so you can snack on them for a nice long while. Favorite episodes so far include Bob Einstein (both of his eps), Larry David, Julia Louis-Dreyfus, Ricky Gervais, George Wallace, and Robert Klein. For more Jerry Seinfeld, check out my interview with him here.

Japanese concept I’m thinking about —
Mono no aware. Thanks to WV for introducing me to this! From the Wikipedia entry: “Mono no aware (物の哀れ, もののあわれ), literally ‘the pathos of things,’ and also translated as ‘an empathy toward things,’ or ‘a sensitivity to ephemera,’ is a Japanese term for the awareness of impermanence (無常, mujō), or transience of things, and both a transient gentle sadness (or wistfulness) at their passing as well as a longer, deeper gentle sadness about this state being the reality of life.”

Poem I’m rereading —
“Wild Geese” by Mary Oliver. Take a few minutes and read this again. It’s worth the reminder. And if that’s too highbrow for ya, I’m also reading Pornhub Insights’ “2021 Year in Review,” which shares analytics like unusual search density in each US state. Louisiana is my favorite.

Fun test I’m sharing with friends —
Food Choice Test. This is somewhat ridiculous, but that’s part of what makes it fun. Description: “Food has always been tied to class. Silvia Bellezza and Jonah Berger at the University of Pennsylvania now believe they have a way to identify a person’s social class based on how they feel about certain foods. The results are not always what you would expect. What do your food choices say about your social class? For each of the following dishes, indicate your feelings toward it below.” How did I do? I ended up at 85.71% lower middle class. Fuck, yeah, ’merica! I win!
