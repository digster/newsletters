Japanese concept I’m thinking about —
[Mono no aware](https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkhkpgp3hz/9qhzhnhg9d709qb9/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvTW9ub19ub19hd2FyZQ==). Thanks to WV for introducing me to this! From [the Wikipedia entry](https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkhkpgp3hz/9qhzhnhg9d709qb9/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvTW9ub19ub19hd2FyZQ==): “Mono no aware (物の哀れ, もののあわれ), literally ‘the pathos of things,’ and also translated as ‘an empathy toward things,’ or ‘a sensitivity to ephemera,’ is a Japanese term for the awareness of impermanence (無常, mujō), or transience of things, and both a transient gentle sadness (or wistfulness) at their passing as well as a longer, deeper gentle sadness about this state being the reality of life.”

Fun test I’m sharing with friends —
[Food Choice Test](https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkhkpgp3hz/wnh2hghrgqzng3b7/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaWRybGFicy5jb20vZm9vZC1jaG9pY2UvdGVzdC5waHA=). This is somewhat ridiculous, but that’s part of what makes it fun. Description: “Food has always been tied to class. Silvia Bellezza and Jonah Berger at the University of Pennsylvania now believe they have a way to identify a person’s social class based on how they feel about certain foods. The results are not always what you would expect. What do your food choices say about your social class? For each of the following dishes, indicate your feelings toward it below.” How did I do? I ended up at 85.71% lower middle class. Fuck, yeah, ’merica! I win!
