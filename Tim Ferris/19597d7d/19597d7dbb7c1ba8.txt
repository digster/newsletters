https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36foh8gr5xlb0iglnk444hg/n2hohvhvw4gdl0u6/aHR0cHM6Ly9wdWJzLmFjcy5vcmcvZG9pLzEwLjEwMjEvYWNzZm9vZHNjaXRlY2guNGMwMTAzMA==

where they stay trapped.” Not all tea is created equal, so click

​PlasticList — Data on plastic chemicals in food (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36foh8gr5xlb0iglnk444hg/vqh3hrho2645q6ag/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGxhc3RpY2xpc3Qub3JnL3JlcG9ydA==
). A disparate group of researchers united by a common interest
(to find and share how many plastic chemicals are found in our
food) compiled this remarkable list. From the website:
​
​We detected plastic chemicals in 86% of the foods we tested. At
least one of the 18 chemicals was found in every baby food,
prenatal supplement, human breast milk, yogurt, and ice cream
product that we tested, to name only a few categories. We also
found plastic chemicals in all the products we tested from
Starbucks, Gerber, Chobani, Straus, Celsius, Blue Bottle, RXBAR,
Coca-Cola, Tartine, and Ghirardelli.
​
Since we launched PlasticList, we’ve been heartened to have quite
a few food companies reach out and ask for help interpreting
their results and tracking down and eliminating their
contamination. We’ve had calls with a bunch of them. We are happy
to report that no food company wants this stuff in their food and
they are all eager to figure out what’s going on and how to
remove it.
​
​Though the website observes that “the existence of a chemical in
a food doesn’t necessarily imply a safety issue,” an awareness of
