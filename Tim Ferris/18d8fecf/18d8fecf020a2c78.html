Blog post I’m excited to share

​“You Don’t Need More How-To Advice — You Need a Beautiful and Painful Reckoning.” If you like it, please leave a comment on the blog post. Here is the opener:
​
​For most of us, the how-to books on our shelves represent a growing to-do list, not advice we’ve followed.
​
Several of the better-known tech CEOs in San Francisco have asked me at different times for an identical favor: an index card with bullet-point instructions for losing abdominal fat. Each of them made it clear: “Just tell me exactly what to do and I’ll do it.”
​
I gave them all of the necessary tactical advice on one 3×5 card, knowing in advance what the outcome would be. The success rate was impressive… 0%.
​​
​People suck at following advice. Even the most effective people in the world are often terrible. There are at least two reasons:
​
​1. Most people have an insufficient reason for action. The pain isn’t painful enough. It’s a nice-to-have, not a must-have. There has been no “Harajuku Moment.”
​
​2. There are no reminders. No consistent tracking = no awareness = no behavioral change. Consistent tracking, even if you have no knowledge of fat-loss or exercise, will often beat advice from world-class trainers.
​
But what is this all-important “Harajuku Moment”? And how can you use it?...

Movie I’m watching over and over again

​American Fiction (Amazon, Apple TV). American Fiction is probably my favorite movie of the last 1–2 years. I’ve become obsessed with it, and you’ll laugh your ass off. I caught it last week at the theaters on a whim, and I’ve already watched it a second time to try and deconstruct the magic. It’s a comedic Trojan Horse of epic proportions. Don’t trust the streaming descriptions; American Fiction is about much more. Every character is flawed in their own beautiful, human way, and the writing hits a home run. Ambiguities are left in so that the movie becomes your own Rorschach test. Huge kudos to everyone involved, including 2024 Oscar nominees Cord Jefferson (Best Adapted Screenplay) and Jeffrey Wright (Best Actor). Thanks to Orion Pictures and MGM Studios for having the stones to back this marvel. You can now also watch it at home. It currently has a 95% audience score on Rotten Tomatoes, and you can find the trailer here.
​
Trust me and watch the trailer.
