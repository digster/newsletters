Book I’m savoring
[Masterpieces of Fantasy Art](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25boh2494v8u0ix2pw666/9qhzhnhppw28xzf9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9NYXN0ZXJwaWVjZXMtRmFudGFzeS1BcnQtRGlhbi1IYW5zb24vZHAvMzgzNjU5MzYyOQ==) by [Dian Hanson](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25boh2494v8u0ix2pw666/3ohphkh77md8rzsr/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvRGlhbl9IYW5zb24=). This is a sumptuous dive back into the most gorgeous art I met through Heavy Metal magazine, fantasy nerdom, and everything in between. I think of it as a buffet of creative inspiration, as the styles vary widely. From the official description: “Fantasy art, that colorful blend of myth, muscle and sexy maidens, took off in 1923 with the launch of Weird Tales magazine, was reinvigorated in the 1960s with The Lord of the Rings, Conan the Barbarian paperbacks with Frank Frazetta covers, and the late ’60s emergence of fantasy psychedelia. This book features original paintings, contextualized by preparatory sketches, sculptures, calendars, magazines, and paperback books, for an immersive dive into this dynamic, fanciful genre.”
