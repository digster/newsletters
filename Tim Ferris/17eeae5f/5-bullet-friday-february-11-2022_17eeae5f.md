Mask I’m wearing —
[Seirus Innovation MagneMask](https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhp87zpa3/8ghqhohgk73z57ak/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9kcC9CMDBVS0lHQUoyP3BzYz0xJnNtaWQ9QTgwVk9VUTU3RUZNOSZyZWZfPWNoa190eXBfaW1nVG9EcA==). I first came across this in Antarctica, where a fellow visitor to [Union Glacier Camp](https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhp87zpa3/l2hehmhowd4ekgc6/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvVW5pb25fR2xhY2llcl9DYW1w) was sporting one. This ended up solving a long-standing problem for me. The conundrum is as follows: If I don’t wear a [balaclava](https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhp87zpa3/m2h7h5howl9voksm/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQmFsYWNsYXZhXyhjbG90aGluZyk=) in very cold weather, my face and neck freeze. However, if I do wear a balaclava, the portion covering my nose and mouth gets wet and gross and often freezes over. This Seirus balaclava-like mask fixes this entirely, as a section can be flipped up or down with ease using a magnetic release. This has been an absolute godsend for skiing and other winter sports.
