https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h28gxoouqhpd5w733hrhv8r/58hvh7hg76g8z0h6/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9sb2NhbHZjP2xhbmc9ZW4=
). Mike Maples Jr. (@m2jr (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h28gxoouqhpd5w733hrhv8r/25h2hoh3dm3ze0a3/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9tMmpyP2xhbmc9ZW4=
)) is the man who first taught me how to angel invest in startups
(
https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h28gxoouqhpd5w733hrhv8r/qvh8h7hdnkd500bl/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy9wb3J0Zm9saW8v
). He’s one of my favorite people in Silicon Valley and a
co-founding partner at Floodgate (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9h28gxoouqhpd5w733hrhv8r/g3hnh5hmpqm5kzsr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZmxvb2RnYXRlLmNvbS8=
). He has been on the Forbes Midas List eight times in the last
decade, was named a “Rising Star” by Fortune, and was profiled by
Harvard Business School for his lifetime contributions to
entrepreneurship.
​
In particular, I loved his exploration of inflection points as
well as his questions and checklists for assessing startups.
These apply equally to founding or investing in a tech company.
In fact, many of his frameworks can be applied to investing in
public tech stocks.
​
Here’s the book description: “Startups like Twitter, Twitch, and
Lyft had achieved extraordinary success despite their disregard
for ‘best practices.’ In contrast, other startups deemed highly
promising often failed, even when they seemed to do everything
right. Seeking answers, Maples and coauthor Peter Ziebelman set
out to discover the hidden forces that drive extraordinary
startup success. Pattern-breaking success, they reveal, demands a
different mindset and actions to harness developments others miss
or that may, at first, seem crazy.”

reveals the societal cost.”
