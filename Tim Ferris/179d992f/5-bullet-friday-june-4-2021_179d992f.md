Short documentary I’m watching —
[Magical Death](https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9hw355qc8/reh8hohq55gogkt2/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueW91dHViZS5jb20vd2F0Y2g_dj10eFQwb1drTWpKTQ==). It’s a common fiction that indigenous use of psychedelics is entirely focused on healing. In reality, while healing is one common and legitimate use, psychedelic plants have also been weaponized for warfare for centuries, if not millenia. Stated uses include night-vision enhancement, attempts at divination of enemy locations, and “remote attacks.” This video shows an example of the last. Whether or not you believe such things are possible, it demonstrates that human nature—warts and all—is cross-cultural. Description: “A documentary film by anthropologist [Napoleon Chagnon](https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9hw355qc8/08hwh9hm773p3vtl/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvTmFwb2xlb25fQ2hhZ25vbg==) that explores the role of the shaman within the [Yanomamo](https://click.fourhourmail.com/p9uwl9zvl6f9hw355qc8/8ghqhohg55m7mdtk/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvWWFub21hbWk=) culture, as well as the close relationship shamanism shares with politics within their society.” Plant medicine does not automagically mean peaceful or harmonious ever after. Humans love power.
