Down-to-earth and perfectly realistic in scale, this is not an over-the-top Blade Runner tale. It is not science fiction.” Before committing too hard to a near-term dystopian future, however, it’s a good idea to read the predictions in [Market Wizards: Interviews with Top Traders](https://click.fourhourmail.com/n4udw372wpivh8vk33xc5/25h2hoh28pkww9c3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9ncC9wcm9kdWN0LzAxMzU1NjA5MzQ_cmVmXz1kYnNfbV9tbmdfcnd0X2NhbHdfdGhjdl8wJnN0b3JlVHlwZT1lYm9va3M=), first published in 1989. [Drawing on the Right Side of the Brain](https://click.fourhourmail.com/n4udw372wpivh8vk33xc5/qvh8h7hr4m9ppptl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9EcmF3aW5nLVJpZ2h0LVNpZGUtQnJhaW4tRGVmaW5pdGl2ZS9kcC8xNTg1NDI5MjAxLw==) by [Betty Edwards](https://click.fourhourmail.com/n4udw372wpivh8vk33xc5/g3hnh5h3l84ww5cr/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQmV0dHlfRWR3YXJkcw==). This isn’t just for “artists.” This book is for anyone who’d like to be more creative and see the world with fresh eyes. Try the upside-down drawing exercise once, and it might just blow your mind. As Betty puts it in the Introduction: “Many of my readers have intuitively understood that this book is not only about learning to draw, and it is certainly not about Art with a capital A. The true subject is perception. Yes, the lessons have helped many people attain the basic ability to draw, and that is a main purpose of the book. But the larger underlying purpose was always to … teach readers how to see in new ways, with hopes that they would discover how to transfer perceptual skills to thinking and problem solving.”
[Ed Catmull](https://click.fourhourmail.com/n4udw372wpivh8vk33xc5/9qhzhnhp6vorrvu9/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE0LzA4LzEyL2VkLWNhdG11bGwvP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZg==), co-founder of Pixar, said practically the same thing in [our hour-long conversation](https://click.fourhourmail.com/n4udw372wpivh8vk33xc5/9qhzhnhp6vorrvu9/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE0LzA4LzEyL2VkLWNhdG11bGwvP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZg==) together. Learning how to make basic ‘art’ isn’t frivolous—it teaches you, first and foremost, how to *see* the life around you much more clearly. Learning even primitive drawing, which you can do in a few afternoons, will fundamentally change how you see the world. As Betty writes: “My students’ most frequent comment after learning to draw is ‘Life seems much richer now that I am seeing more.’”
I’m skeptical of the neuroscience conclusions in the book, but the metaphors and exercises are rock solid.
