What I’m watching (short) —
Fox Calls. I am doing some field identification in forests nearby, and I found this video quite helpful for distinguishing fox from coyote cubs and other creatures. Here is the description, slightly edited for clarity: “In this video, you will learn a few different fox calls and sounds and what they mean. Foxes can make more than 40 calls and sounds: contact calls, interaction calls, etc. Foxes are normally quiet animals, and their calls are mostly heard during the mating season when foxes are calling out to each other and fighting over territory. Fox cubs are very quiet and only make sounds when they are playing, fighting, or nursing. Most of the time, fox calls are mistaken for the call of another animal.”
