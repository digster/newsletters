​“Billionaire No More: Patagonia Founder Gives Away the Company (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/e0hph7hk3gppzzt8/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubnl0aW1lcy5jb20vMjAyMi8wOS8xNC9jbGltYXRlL3BhdGFnb25pYS1jbGltYXRlLXBoaWxhbnRocm9weS1jaG91aW5hcmQuaHRtbA==

https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/owhkhqh4mvkkz3cv/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MZXQtUGVvcGxlLVN1cmZpbmctRWR1Y2F0aW9uLUJ1c2luZXNzbWFuLUluY2x1ZGluZy9kcC8wMTQzMTA5Njc3Lw==

Patagonia is not terribly surprising coming from Mr. Chouinard.

the 1960s, Mr. Chouinard lived out of his car and ate damaged
cans of cat food that he bought for five cents apiece. Even
today, he wears raggedy old clothes, drives a beat up Subaru and
splits his time between modest homes in Ventura and Jackson, Wyo.
Mr. Chouinard does not own a computer or a cellphone.”

Blog posts I’m reading
----------------------

“The NFT Mint ‘Sweet Spot’: Data on Early Decisions (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/p8heh9h9w033v2cq/aHR0cHM6Ly9hMTZ6LmNvbS8yMDIyLzAzLzMxL25mdC1taW50LWRhdGEtZWFybHktZGVjaXNpb25zLw==
)” by Daren Matsuoka (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/6qheh8hp8gkkq6io/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9kYXJlbm1hdHN1b2thP2xhbmc9ZW4=
). I’m a fan of Daren’s analysis in the NFT space. This is a
great primer on some fundamental mint decisions, and the matrices
are very thought-provoking. It tackles many good questions,
points out some important negative correlations, and also raises
a lot of good questions. This was published on 3/31/22 (the date
is somewhat hidden in the URL and at the very bottom of the
piece), and I hope Daren posts a further exploration of potential
confounders (e.g., What’s the likelihood that 10x returns for
projects with 0.5–1.0 ETH mint prices are partially or wholly a
byproduct of many more projects launching in that range?) and
updated market/landscape commentary. It seems really challenging
to isolate variables in this space. What follows are a few
wishlist questions, and I think the first one is the pink
elephant in the room: How do project outcomes appear to be
affected by the price/USD value of ETH at any given point in
time? What new patterns can you decipher using the overall larger
data set now available? How much do you think projects can bank
on secondary royalties in the current conditions of a bear
market, and what trends can you parse related to secondaries
using a larger and more current data set? How might the piece be
updated given some of the post-publication successes like
Moonbirds (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/kkhmh6h80qdd73cl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubW9vbmJpcmRzLnh5ei8=
), which set new records (plus had a 10x return on ETH for a
period of months) with a 2.5 ETH mint price? If I could put in
another blog post request, I would love to see something like
“Testing NFT Dogma: Exceptions That Prove The Rule?,” wherein you
can look at lots of questionable “rules” that get repeated often
on NFT Twitter and elsewhere. For instance, “You absolutely must
have a Discord” used to be gospel, but then mfers (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/58hvh7h5d8vve9i6/aHR0cHM6Ly9tZmVycy5hcnQv
), Goblintown (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/25h2hoh7pzvv57f3/aHR0cHM6Ly9vcGVuc2VhLmlvL2NvbGxlY3Rpb24vZ29ibGludG93bnd0Zg==
), and others showed (or did they?) that successful projects can
do without them, although “successful” can be squirrely and needs
to be narrowly defined. “You can’t set mint price above ___ or
you’ll squash secondary volume” is another that seems to have
counterexamples. Sartoshi has a dogma starter list towards the
bottom of this page (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/qvh8h7h8m577xrbl/aHR0cHM6Ly9taXJyb3IueHl6L3NhcnRvc2hpLmV0aC9RdWtqdEwxMDc2LTFTRW9OSnVxeWMteDRVdDJ2OF9Ub2NLa3N6by1TX25V
), and despite the debate about his departure from NFT land,
there are lessons to be learned. And even if the data support one
of these consensus maxims, are there a handful of projects that
have proven to be exceptions? Those could be worth studying, as
could ultra-hyped projects whose teams “did the right things” but
ended up failing, regardless. So many questions!

Related: “Introducing OpenRarity (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/g3hnh5he85ddvmar/aHR0cHM6Ly9taXJyb3IueHl6L29wZW5yYXJpdHkuZXRoLy1SOFpBNUtDTWdxdHN1ZXlTbHJ1QWhCNzdZQlg2ZlNuU19kVC04Y2xaUFE=
).” I suggest reading this page (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/9qhzhnhgvmqq7zs9/aHR0cHM6Ly9yYXJpdHl0b29scy5tZWRpdW0uY29tL3JhbmtpbmctcmFyaXR5LXVuZGVyc3RhbmRpbmctcmFyaXR5LWNhbGN1bGF0aW9uLW1ldGhvZHMtODZjZWFlYjliOThj
) first to get a simple, illustrated walk-through of the problem
(
https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhqx6g6rb5/9qhzhnhgvmqq7zs9/aHR0cHM6Ly9yYXJpdHl0b29scy5tZWRpdW0uY29tL3JhbmtpbmctcmFyaXR5LXVuZGVyc3RhbmRpbmctcmFyaXR5LWNhbGN1bGF0aW9uLW1ldGhvZHMtODZjZWFlYjliOThj
), then the short FAQ here (
