Here is your weekly dose of “5-Bullet Friday,” a list of what I’m pondering and exploring.
Venue and experiment that I’ve enjoyed —
[MoFE](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/58hvh7h5m7qdx3h6/aHR0cHM6Ly9tb2ZlLmNvLw==). This was described to me as “a guided lucid dream,” but here’s the official description: “MoFE is a venue in Brooklyn, NY, for immersive storytelling that combines Virtual Reality and Ambisonic sound design. MoFE productions are seated, ticketed shows that run 70 minutes and are limited to 16 guests.” I was quite surprised how much I enjoyed not only several of the VR segments but also the audio-only sections. Be sure to check out the feathers and library in the front waiting room.

[The Artists’ Prison](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/25h2hoh72d6p96h3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9BcnRpc3RzLVByaXNvbi1FdmUtV29vZC9kcC8wOTk4ODYxNjE4L3JlZj1zcl8xXzE_ZGNoaWxkPTEma2V5d29yZHM9VGhlK0FydGlzdHMlRTIlODAlOTkrUHJpc29uLiZxaWQ9MTYzMDI2OTk4NyZzcj04LTE=) by [Alexandra Grant](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/qvh8h7h8rnzmwwul/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQWxleGFuZHJhX0dyYW50) ([@alexandragrantstudio](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/g3hnh5he3pr89wbr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9hbGV4YW5kcmFncmFudHN0dWRpby8=)) and [Eve Wood](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/9qhzhnhgp90vwmf9/aHR0cDovL3d3dy5ldmV3b29kLm5ldC8=) ([@evewoodstudio](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/3ohphkhq7z05m5tr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9ldmV3b29kc3R1ZGlvLz9obD1lbg==)). Here’s the description, edited for length: “Imagined through the heavily redacted testimony of the prison’s warden, written by [Alexandra Grant](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/qvh8h7h8rnzmwwul/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQWxleGFuZHJhX0dyYW50), and powerfully allusive images by [Eve Wood](https://click.fourhourmail.com/r8ug29rn25bohgdpmzb0/9qhzhnhgp90vwmf9/aHR0cDovL3d3dy5ldmV3b29kLm5ldC8=), the prison is a brutal, Kafkaesque landscape where creativity can be a criminal offence and sentences range from the allegorical to the downright absurd. In The Artists’ Prison, the act of creating becomes a strangely erotic condemnation, as well as a means of punishment and transformation. It is in these very transformations—sometimes dubious, sometimes oddly sentimental—that the book’s critical edge is sharpest.”
