Medical headset I’m investigating closely

​Cognito Therapeutics. The team at Cognito is developing a novel, non-invasive, wearable medical device aimed at slowing the progression of Alzheimer’s disease by using light and sound stimulation to restore brain-wave activity. They are focusing specifically on gamma waves, which are critical for memory, learning, and healthy brain function. In Alzheimer’s patients, gamma waves are altered, indicating impairment in normal brain function. Related: “Evidence that gamma rhythm stimulation can treat neurological disorders is emerging” from MIT News.

 

TED Talk I’m revisiting

“Trial, error, and the God complex,” a TED talk by Tim Harford. Official description: “Economics writer Tim Harford studies complex systems—and finds a surprising link among the successful ones: they were built through trial and error. In this sparkling talk from TEDGlobal 2011, he asks us to embrace our randomness and start making better mistakes.” This talk takes a little time to warm up, and it might seem meandering in the middle, but I found the payoff and message to be really helpful. Related: Tim Harford’s podcast Cautionary Tales, “telling true stories about mistakes and what we should learn from them.”
