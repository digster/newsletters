) (The New York Times). Click here (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/08hwh9h22g9oq9ul/aHR0cHM6Ly9hcmNoaXZlLmlzL1hOanJXI3NlbGVjdGlvbi02MzcuMC02NDEuMTE=

​
​Mr. Whitney, 76, is a scientific unicorn with potential to
provide answers about one of the world’s most devastating
diseases. He has a rare genetic mutation that essentially
guaranteed he would develop Alzheimer’s disease in his late 40s
or early 50s and would likely die within a decade.
​
His mother and nine of her 13 siblings developed Alzheimer’s and
died in the prime of their lives. So did his oldest brother, and
other relatives going back generations. It is the largest family
in the United States known to have an Alzheimer’s-causing
mutation.
​
“Nobody in history had ever dodged that bullet,” Mr. Whitney
said.
​
But somehow, he has done just that. Something has shielded him
from his genetic destiny, allowing him to escape Alzheimer’s for
at least 25 years longer than anyone expected.

What I’m grieving
-----------------

This past week, a friend of mine killed himself. He left a family
behind, and the people who knew him are still reeling. No one in
my close circle saw it coming.
​
Yesterday was World Mental Health Day. If you are going through a
really tough time, please know this: you are not alone, and you
are not uniquely flawed. The two blog posts below describe how I
got through the two toughest experiences of my life, narrowly
dodging suicide in the process. It’s not easy reading, but I hope
it helps someone.
​
​Some Practical Thoughts on Suicide (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/8ghqhohooq20k3hk/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE1LzA1LzA2L2hvdy10by1jb21taXQtc3VpY2lkZS8_dXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZiZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0
) — This post has saved some lives, and I should’ve written it
sooner. Read the comments.
​
​My Healing Journey After Childhood Abuse (Includes Extensive
Resource List) (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/vqh3hrhooevx83ug/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIwLzA5LzE0L2hvdy10by1oZWFsLXRyYXVtYS8_dXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZiZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0
) — This includes two very different but complementary
approaches.
​
If you or someone you know is struggling or in crisis, help is
available. Call or text 988 (the US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) or
chat 988Lifeline.org (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/l2hehmhll6n0wzc6/aHR0cDovL3d3dy45ODhsaWZlbGluZS5vcmcv
). Crisis Text Line (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/dpheh0hee8o4rqum/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY3Jpc2lzdGV4dGxpbmUub3JnLw==
): text “HOME” to 741741 to connect with a crisis counselor for
free, 24/7 support. If you’re outside of the US, please click
here for a list of international suicide hotlines (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl6o8z7srh8dxm699bmh8ed/e0hph7h77qlp50b8/aHR0cDovL3d3dy5zdWljaWRlLm9yZy9pbnRlcm5hdGlvbmFsLXN1aWNpZGUtaG90bGluZXMuaHRtbA==
).
​
​And last but certainly not least: call your family and closest
friends this weekend. Check in, tell them you love them, and make
a pact that none of you will suffer in silence.
