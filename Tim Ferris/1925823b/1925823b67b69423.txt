***************
5-Bullet Friday
***************

 

The latest from author and investor Tim Ferriss (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/m2h7h5h868n857bm/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8=
)

 

 

Hi All!

 
Here is your weekly dose of 5-Bullet Friday, a list of what I’m
pondering and exploring.

 
This edition features some of my favorite outdoor gear, which I
used on a recent weeklong trip over the Continental Divide in
Colorado. I tested a lot, and below is what made the cut. Quite a
few extra bullets in this one!

 
I have zero affiliation with any of these brands or products
(aside from one that I’ll note), and no one paid to be included.
In fact, none of them even know I wrote this.

 
For additional fun, I asked ace outdoorsman and creative Mark
Seacat (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/dpheh0h0907067sm/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS93ZXN0c2VhY2F0Lw==
) for his top picks after decades of trial and error. Thanks,
Mark!

 
And here we go…

 

------------------------
Headlamp that won’t quit
------------------------

​PEAX Backcountry Duo (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/e0hph7h030n0vka8/aHR0cHM6Ly9zaG9wLmdvaHVudC5jb20vcHJvZHVjdHMvcGVheC1lcXVpcG1lbnQtYmFja2NvdW50cnktZHVvLWhlYWRsYW1w
). My weaker headlamp wasn’t doing the job on tricky terrain or
during cooking prep, so Mark was kind enough to loan me his
backup PEAX. I’ve since ordered my own, and this will be
essential packing in the future.
​
Weighing in at just 4.8 ounces with battery and described by one
customer as “hands down the best on the market,” PEAX touts this
as “the brightest, most durable … headlamp ever designed.”
Breakthrough lumen output armored in a machined aluminum housing;
3600 mAh rechargeable battery with run times up to 69 hrs; and
180-degree adjustability so you never have to worry about putting
your headlamp on upside down. It also comes with a red-light
mode.

 

----------------------------
Sticks for saving your knees
----------------------------

I used the Leki Khumbu Lite telescoping trekking poles (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/7qh7h8ho2o4oloiz/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuc3RvbmVnbGFjaWVyLmNvbS9wcm9kdWN0cy9sZWtpLWtodW1idS1saXRlP19wb3M9MSZfc2lkPTFhOGM1MDkyMCZfc3M9cg==
) daily. Nearly everyone in our six-person group, including the
pros, used trekking poles, which help you climb and also save
your knees on the descent.
​
You might also consider the ultra-light Cross Trail 3 Carbon
trekking poles (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/owhkhqhrmr8r9whv/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucmVpLmNvbS9wcm9kdWN0LzI0NDUzMi9sZWtpLWNyb3NzLXRyYWlsLTMtY2FyYm9uLXRyZWtraW5nLXBvbGVzLXBhaXI=
) for improved stability and endurance. They’ve been
discontinued, but there might be a few left in stock.

 

-----------------------------
Tent to keep you warm and dry
-----------------------------

​The Allak from Hilleberg (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/z2hghnh323d36pup/aHR0cHM6Ly9oaWxsZWJlcmcuY29tL2VuZy9wcm9kdWN0cy90ZW50L0FsbGFr
). This one is from Mark. I slept under multiple tarps held up by
trekking poles on my last trip, which was a fun creative exercise
but a little breezy for 12,000 feet. From the description of the
Allak: “Remarkably light yet impressively strong, Allak models
are well-suited for anything from summer paddling to winter ski
mountaineering and from year-round backpacking to all-season peak
bagging. They are easily light enough for mobile journeys, when
you move your camp every day, and their two-entrance construction
allows for superb ventilation options in warm weather.”

 

--------------------------
Footwear that saved my ass
--------------------------

The Crispi Briksdal MTN (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/p8heh9hzwz7z6xuq/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY3Jpc3BpdXMuY29tL2JyaWtzZGFsLW10bg==
) has been helping me weather long days of carrying heavy loads
on questionable footing. Below are other strong recommendations
from Mark, all from La Sportiva:
​
The Boulder X (

snow but not staying overnight, check out Trango Tech GTX (

https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/25h2hoh2p2o29wi3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cub3V0ZG9vcmdlYXJsYWIuY29tL3Jldmlld3MvY2xpbWJpbmcvbW91bnRhaW5lZXJpbmctYm9vdC13b21lbnMvbGEtc3BvcnRpdmEtc3BhbnRpaw==

G-Summit (

extremely warm at high altitudes.

Gloves for early and late season
--------------------------------

​Sitka’s Ascent Glove (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/9qhzhnhpvp2pwoc9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuc2l0a2FnZWFyLmNvbS9wcm9kdWN0cy9hc2NlbnQtZ2xvdmUvb3B0aWZhZGUtb3Blbi1jb3VudHJ5P3E9YXNjZW50JTIwZ2xvdmU=

https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/3ohphkh757d7mphr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuc2l0a2FnZWFyLmNvbS9wcm9kdWN0cy9tZXJpbm8tMzMwLWdsb3ZlL29wdGlmYWRlLWVsZXZhdGVkLWlpP3E9bWVyaW5vJTIwMzMw

warmth, and the recycled synthetic lining wicks sweat fast to
prevent your hands from getting cold and clammy.”

Compact, powerful air pump for fast inflating
---------------------------------------------

​Flextail’s Max Pump High Speed 2-in-1 Rechargeable Air Pump (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmh0ddn4wh3i80onv00cg/n2hohvh393o3qlf6/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZmxleHRhaWwuY29tL3Byb2R1Y3RzL21heC1wdW1wLTM=
). Mark yawned and used this while I strained my lungs and cheeks
to inflate my sleeping pad. From the description: “Max Pump 3
combines powerful performance with lightweight portability,
weighing just 122 g (4.3 oz., or roughly the weight of a bar of
soap). With an integrated, powerful LED light, Max Pump 3 serves
as both a pump and a light source, perfect for easy carrying
during outdoor activities and travel, ideal for camping, hiking,
fishing, and more. It delivers a powerful 5 kPa pressure (.725
psi) and a high airflow rate of 500 L/min (132 gpm).”
