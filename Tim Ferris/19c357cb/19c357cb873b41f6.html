Documentary I’m watching

​Chaos: The Manson Murders. This is based on a book, CHAOS: Charles Manson, the CIA, and the Secret History of the Sixties, by Tom O’Neill and Dan Piepenbring, which has been recommended to me by several close friends. The “chaos” in both titles refers to Operation CHAOS, a domestic spying program by the CIA.
​
I was going to skip this documentary, as I’ve seen prior docs on Manson and find the murders themselves horribly tragic but also uninteresting. I rarely watch true crime, as I come across enough darkness without consuming it as entertainment.
​
But then I learned it was made by the iconic Errol Morris (more below). Next, I realized that it focused on everything before the murders. Not only that, the archival footage and audio are incredible.
​
I’d known of the almost-certain overlap between Manson and the CIA’s MKUltra project, which focused on mind control, but I didn’t realize just how deep that rabbit hole goes.
​
From the description: “In August 1969, Charles Manson’s followers killed seven people on his orders. Why? Explore a conspiracy of mind control, CIA experiments, and murder.” It is directed by Errol Morris of The Fog of War fame and father of pharmacologist extraordinaire Hamilton Morris. You can watch the trailer here.
​
Caveat: the reviews on Rotten Tomatoes are not good. I was a bit surprised by this, but I think it’s because I have read a lot on MKUltra, given the crucial element of psychedelics, and most people have no context. If you’re seeing it for the first time, it will sound like the ravings of an insane conspiracy theorist. Unfortunately for some of the innocents who were involved, it was very much real. Truth can indeed be stranger than fiction.
