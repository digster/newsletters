Book I’m revisiting
-------------------

​The Lessons of History (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/mvu9xermxzs5hr7zl2t3/owhkhqh4xo3rdpsv/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MZXNzb25zLUhpc3RvcnktV2lsbC1EdXJhbnQvZHAvMTQzOTE0OTk1WA==
) by Will and Ariel Durant. This short and powerful book was
first recommended to me by investor Ray Dalio (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/mvu9xermxzs5hr7zl2t3/z2hghnho5w93x6fp/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE3LzA5LzEzL3JheS1kYWxpby8_dXRtX3NvdXJjZT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJm
). At fewer than 150 pages, it can be digested in a few sittings
and offers an impressive overview of human nature and the rise
and fall of civilizations. The prose is also beautiful. Here’s
the official book description: “A concise survey of the culture
and civilization of mankind, The Lessons of History is the result
of a lifetime of research from Pulitzer Prize–winning historians
Will and Ariel Durant. With their accessible compendium of
philosophy and social progress, the Durants take us on a journey
through history, exploring the possibilities and limitations of
humanity over time.”
