Passage and verse I’m pondering: Mary Oliver meets Kanye West —

“It is six A.M., and I am working. I am absentminded, reckless,
heedless of social obligations, etc. It is as it must be. The
tire goes flat, the tooth falls out, there will be a hundred
meals without mustard. The poem gets written. I have wrestled
with the angel and I am stained with light and I have no shame.
Neither do I have guilt. My responsibility is not to the
ordinary, or the timely. It does not include mustard, or teeth.
It does not extend to the lost button, or the beans in the pot.

My loyalty is to the inner vision, whenever and howsoever it may
arrive. If I have a meeting with you at three o’clock, rejoice if
I am late. Rejoice even more if I do not arrive at all.

There is no other way work of artistic worth can be done. And the
occasional success, to the striver, is worth everything.

The most regretful people on earth are those who felt the call to
creative work, who felt their own creative power restive and
uprising, and gave to it neither power nor time.”

— Mary Oliver (
