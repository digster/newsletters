Culinary trick I’ll be testing

​How to “Dry Age” a Steak in Two Hours. “Dry-aged steak is famously tender and flavorful, but often too expensive for home cooks to attempt. In this episode of Techniquely, Lan Lam explains how dry-aging works and shows us how to achieve the flavor of dry-aging at home in as little as 2 hours with the use of shio koji, a fermented Japanese paste.” Chef David Chang of Momofuku fame also swears by koji.​
​
​Shio koji (塩麹) is a traditional Japanese seasoning made by fermenting a mixture of koji (rice inoculated with Aspergillus oryzae), salt, and water. It’s prized for its ability to tenderize meat, enhance umami, and balance saltiness with subtle sweetness—often referred to as a “natural MSG.”
