Unusual paper I’m revisiting
----------------------------

“Impact of Long-Term Fasting on Skeletal Muscle: Structure,
Energy Metabolism and Function Using 31P/1H MRS and MRI (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/e5u4vlw0vkt7hl7wpmgcvb86gl222ilhk40/e0hph7h702lg9ni8/aHR0cHM6Ly9vbmxpbmVsaWJyYXJ5LndpbGV5LmNvbS9kb2kvMTAuMTAwMi9qY3NtLjEzNzcz
).” From the conclusions section: “Long-term fasting [250
kcal/day] did not alter muscle metabolism or performance, nor
induced structural or inflammatory changes. The decrease in
muscle volume is minor when accounting for glycogen and water
depletion during fasting. Fat is relocated to the intracellular
compartment of myocytes. Both anaerobic and aerobic metabolic
pathways remain unchanged after 12 days of fasting in both sexes
and older subjects. This suggests that human muscles, like those
in animals, have evolved to withstand seasonal food shortages and
endure long fasting periods.”
