Wine I’m enjoying

​Domaine Tempier’s Bandol Rosé. Despite all the anti-alcohol zeitgeist, I still enjoy sipping a glass of wine while cooking on the weekend. This rosé is bone-dry, savory, and nothing like sweet, grocery-store pink wine. Domaine Tempier is a family estate in Bandol on France’s Provence coast. In the early twentieth century, Lucien Peyraud and his wife Lulu built the domaine’s reputation by betting on Mourvèdre, a harder to grow grape. Fun fact for the foodies: Lulu became a mentor to legendary chef Alice Waters of Chez Panisse fame.

Rabbit hole I’m exploring

People who have acquired savant syndrome, whereby they exhibit savant skills after central nervous system (CNS) injury or disease, as distinguished from a person born with Autism Spectrum Disorder or other developmental disability.
​
Here is a partial list of documented cases—some controversial—from the Wikipedia entry on the broader savant syndrome:

• Alonzo Clemons, American acquired savant sculptor
​• Tony Cicoria, American acquired savant pianist and medical doctor
​• Derek Amato, American acquired savant composer and pianist
​• Patrick Fagerberg, American acquired savant artist, inventor, and former lawyer
​• Orlando Serrell, American acquired savant
​• Tommy McHugh, British acquired savant artist and poet
