What I’m revisiting amidst present-day craziness
------------------------------------------------

“50 ways to be ridiculously generous—and feel ridiculously good.
(
https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36fohvg377ru0/vqh3hrhnzxqvn4ig/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueW91Y2FuZ2V0aXRkb25lLmNvbS9wb3N0LzUwLXdheXM=
)” by Alexandra Franzen (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36fohvg377ru0/l2hehmho80dnovu6/aHR0cDovL3d3dy5hbGV4YW5kcmFmcmFuemVuLmNvbS8=
). This is a great list, which I found recommended on the website
of fellow Austinite Professor Raj Raghunathan (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36fohvg377ru0/m2h7h5hodglqo7im/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaGFwcHlzbWFydHMuY29tLw==
), author of If You’re So Smart, Why Aren’t You Happy? (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/27u53lgo36fohvg377ru0/dpheh0hql42oq7bm/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9Zb3VyZS1TbWFydC1XaHktQXJlbnQtSGFwcHkvZHAvMTEwMTk4MDczNw==
). If you feel overwhelmed or underjoyed, try this: give an extra
$10 or $20 to the barista next time you buy coffee. Give $5 of
that to the barista and pay for the next person or next few
people behind you. Simply walk out after the drive-by karma bomb.
The afterglow is incredible and can last hours. Paradoxically,
the fastest path to feeling better is often this type of indirect
route, and it makes me think of this quote often attributed to
Henry David Thoreau: “Happiness is like a butterfly: the more you
chase it, the more it will evade you, but if you notice the other
things around you, it will gently come and sit on your shoulder.”
