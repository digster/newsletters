Hi All!

 
Here is your weekly dose of 5-Bullet Friday, a list of what I’m
pondering and exploring.

 
To help you kickstart 2025, this edition contains some of my
favorite life advice that I found (or revisited) in 2024.

 
Have a merry and safe New Year’s Eve, everyone!

Advice I’m revisiting
---------------------

“A message for the New Year (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/25h2hoh2qwd09ls3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9wL0MxbTB2T25MU0QwLz9obD1lbg==
)” from Brandon Stanton (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/qvh8h7hrlpn2wzcl/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE4LzA2LzE4L2JyYW5kb24tc3RhbnRvbi1odW1hbnMtb2YtbmV3LXlvcmsvP3V0bV9jYW1wYWlnbj01YmYmdXRtX21lZGl1bT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdA==
), creator of Humans of New York (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/g3hnh5h30wpo99sr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaHVtYW5zb2ZuZXd5b3JrLmNvbS8=
). This relates to something he and I have texted about often:
attention. How should you think about protecting your own
attention in 2025? If you don’t have attention, the time and
money you have matter little. Attention is the mother resource.
Brandon’s post was first written earlier this year, but it’s more
relevant every day.

“Why You Should Seek More Awe in the New Year (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/48hvhehr78gql4ux/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDI0LzAxLzI0L2F3ZS8_dXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZiZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX3NvdXJjZT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0I2NvbnRlbnQ=
)” by Ashley Stimpson (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/wnh2hghwl6gv70t7/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYXNobGV5c3RpbXBzb24uY29tLw==
). Every time I’ve done a past-year review (PYR) (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/reh8hoh0ozxvdos2/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIxLzEyLzI3L3Bhc3QteWVhci1yZXZpZXcvP3V0bV9jYW1wYWlnbj01YmYmdXRtX21lZGl1bT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCNjb250ZW50
), a pattern emerges: peak positive emotional experiences are
correlated to awe at least 70% of the time. For at least the past
3–5 years, this has been so consistent that I often determine
what big blocks to schedule in the new year based on potential
for awe. The payoffs include time dilation (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/08hwh9hdprzwe4tl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9yZWVsL0MyY2RoRFFPN19TLz9pZ3NoPU1UazRNM2xoYzJkaVpqVmpOQSUzRCUzRA==
) and, more broadly, traversing the miraculous canvas of full
human experience. Friends have asked me why I do silent retreats
in nature, why I love ski touring, why I hunt once in a blue
moon, or why I am deeply interested in psychedelic science and
psychedelic-assisted therapies (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/8ghqhohl7n9pd4bk/aHR0cHM6Ly9zYWlzZWlmb3VuZGF0aW9uLm9yZy8=
). If I had to sum it all up in one word, it would be: Awe. But
what exactly is “awe,” and how can we embrace more of it? I
haven’t found a better article exploring these topics than Ashley
Stimpson’s “Awestruck (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/vqh3hrhpqrg3r2fg/aHR0cHM6Ly9odWIuamh1LmVkdS9tYWdhemluZS8yMDIzL3dpbnRlci9zY2llbmNlLW9mLWF3ZS1wc3ljaGVkZWxpY3Mv
),” featured in Johns Hopkins Magazine (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/vqh3hrhpqrg3r2fg/aHR0cHM6Ly9odWIuamh1LmVkdS9tYWdhemluZS8yMDIzL3dpbnRlci9zY2llbmNlLW9mLWF3ZS1wc3ljaGVkZWxpY3Mv
), so I asked for permission to publish on my blog, which was
graciously granted. I hope you find it as thought-provoking as I
did.

 
“Masters of Love (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/l2hehmh3d7rv70a6/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cudGhlYXRsYW50aWMuY29tL2hlYWx0aC9hcmNoaXZlLzIwMTQvMDYvaGFwcGlseS1ldmVyLWFmdGVyLzM3MjU3My8=
).” (The Atlantic). Alternate link (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/lmuzwpd0wosmhn5wnoeh3i8lmg400cg/m2h7h5h8lze0zqim/aHR0cHM6Ly9hcmNoaXZlLmlzLzIwMjQuMDMuMTItMDcyNzQxL2h0dHBzOi8vd3d3LnRoZWF0bGFudGljLmNvbS9oZWFsdGgvYXJjaGl2ZS8yMDE0LzA2L2hhcHBpbHktZXZlci1hZnRlci8zNzI1NzMv
). This is the best article on courtship, love, and dating that
I’ve read in years. It’s not only well-researched and full of
memorable insights, it’s very actionable and gives labels to
concepts you can use. Don’t let the subheadline throw you; it’s
terrible. “Science says lasting relationships come down to—you
guessed it—kindness and generosity” would make me think, “Yeah,
duh. I’ll skip this one.” But the article is much more
interesting and helpful than the subheadline implies.
