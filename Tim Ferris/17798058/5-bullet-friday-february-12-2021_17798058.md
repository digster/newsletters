What I’m reading (short) —
[Ketamine for Depression: What the Treatment Reveals About the Brain](https://click.fourhourmail.com/xmuzxlgnxos6h2g7z8u4/x0hph6hnzd886wt5/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbGVtZW50YWwubWVkaXVtLmNvbS9rZXRhbWluZS1pcy1yZXZlYWxpbmctYS1uZXctdW5kZXJzdGFuZGluZy1vZi1kZXByZXNzaW9uLWFuZC10aGUtYnJhaW4tM2ZjZDYxZTM0NmIx) by [Lauren Tanabe](https://click.fourhourmail.com/xmuzxlgnxos6h2g7z8u4/6qheh8hpw6vvglto/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubHRhbmFiZS5jb20v) ([@lauren_tanabe](https://click.fourhourmail.com/xmuzxlgnxos6h2g7z8u4/58hvh7h50opp87s6/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9sYXVyZW5fdGFuYWJl)). This is one of the best pieces on ketamine therapy that I have read. It covered some familiar ground for me, but it also surfaced several possibilities and combinations that I’d never considered. One such combination is ketamine plus rapamycin, an immunosuppressive drug widely used for organ transplants, which also happens to have profound life-extension implications in many species:
“He [Chadi Abdallah, MD] and his colleagues recently published a study that found that giving rapamycin, an anti-inflammatory drug, to people prior to intravenous ketamine, prolonged the antidepressant effects; at two weeks, remission rates were higher in the pre-treatment group. Rapamycin may have protected new connections by reducing inflammation, but it does other things too that could potentially explain the findings. For example, it can increase autophagy, ‘the process through which cells remove toxic materials and dead elements in tissues,’ says Abdallah. In other words, it helps to clear the neurons of any junk, which may also help to preserve new synapses.”

Poem I’m pondering… which is more inspiring than the title implies —
When Death Comes
When death comes like the hungry bear in autumn; when death comes and takes all the bright coins from his purse
to buy me, and snaps the purse shut; when death comes like the measle-pox;
when death comes like an iceberg between the shoulder blades,
I want to step through the door full of curiosity, wondering: what is it going to be like, that cottage of darkness?
And therefore I look upon everything as a brotherhood and a sisterhood, and I look upon time as no more than an idea, and I consider eternity as another possibility,
and I think of each life as a flower, as common as a field daisy, and as singular,
and each name a comfortable music in the mouth, tending, as all music does, toward silence,
and each body a lion of courage, and something precious to the earth.
When it’s over, I want to say: all my life I was a bride married to amazement. I was the bridegroom, taking the world into my arms.
When it’s over, I don’t want to wonder if I have made of my life something particular, and real. I don’t want to find myself sighing and frightened, or full of argument.
I don’t want to end up simply having visited this world.
— [Mary Oliver ](https://click.fourhourmail.com/xmuzxlgnxos6h2g7z8u4/08hwh9hmxw005pal/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvTWFyeV9PbGl2ZXI=)
