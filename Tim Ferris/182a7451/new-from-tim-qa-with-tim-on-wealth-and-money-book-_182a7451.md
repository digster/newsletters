I began diving into the Wild West of NFTs a few years ago. For me, NFTs were a convenient, muggle-friendly onramp for learning about new technologies. It was also an excuse to [break out the pencils](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/p8heh9h90wm94ouq/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9wL0J3cExUVWVnZEQwLz9obD1lbg==) and screw around again. I’d let my drawing languish for years, and, no matter what people said, JPEG mania was inspiring.
After a few months of exploration, I felt confident, and still feel confident, in a few things:
-
[Web3 and blockchain will change the world in incredible ways](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/x0hph6hn6vone6a5/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIxLzEwLzI4L2NocmlzLWRpeG9uLW5hdmFsLXJhdmlrYW50Lz91dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX21lZGl1bT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9jYW1wYWlnbj13ZWVrbHktcm91bmR1cC1uZnQtcG9saWN5LXFh). No more governments!) and severely under-appreciated (It’s just another
[Dutch tulip craze](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/6qheh8hpg8zpl8ho/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvVHVsaXBfbWFuaWE=)! Right-click and it’s game over!). - It’s the greatest creator, resurrector, and accelerant of artists that I’ve ever seen. Graphic artist and musician friends of mine, jaded after years of barely making ends meet, touring to pay mortgages, etc., are not just excited again (no small thing) but also making good money. TBD if
[DALL·E 2](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/kkhmh6h8q0r8nktl/aHR0cHM6Ly9vcGVuYWkuY29tL2RhbGwtZS0yLw==)and related tech helps or hurts them; I suspect it will be a lot of both. - Legions of brilliant people are dedicating most of their waking lives to it
[¹](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/58hvh7h58dq5g6h6/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIyLzA4LzA5L25mdC1pbnNpZGVyLXRyYWRpbmctcG9saWN5LyNmb290bm90ZQ==). This is perhaps the most compelling case for being long web3. - Last but not least, most of web3 is still an overwhelming garbage fire of junk, grift, and bad behavior. It’s fucking ridiculous. This is the most common case cited for being short web3.
For me, this all started off as a safari to a new Weird Wide Web, a sight-seeing tour of novelty. I do this with [a lot of new tech](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/25h2hoh7zp673qt3/aHR0cHM6Ly9hbmdlbC5jby9wL3RpbQ==), and I was a casual spectator. Then [I began doing little experiments](https://click.fourhourmail.com/k0u3wp9vw7b6hd56qlofx/qvh8h7h85mz8d3al/aHR0cHM6Ly9vcGVuc2VhLmlvL2Fzc2V0cy9wcm9vZi1jb2xsZWN0aXZlLWdyYWlscz9zZWFyY2glNUJzb3J0QXNjZW5kaW5nJTVEPXRydWUmc2VhcmNoJTVCc29ydEJ5JTVEPVBSSUNFJnNlYXJjaCU1QnN0cmluZ1RyYWl0cyU1RCU1QjAlNUQlNUJuYW1lJTVEPUFydGlzdCZzZWFyY2glNUJzdHJpbmdUcmFpdHMlNUQlNUIwJTVEJTVCdmFsdWVzJTVEJTVCMCU1RD1UaW0lMjBGZXJyaXNzJnNlYXJjaCU1QnRvZ2dsZXMlNUQlNUIwJTVEPUJVWV9OT1c=) and considering bigger projects of my own. That’s when I got more serious about research, especially risks and pitfalls.
Some of the unsavory bits I noticed were clearly unethical and often illegal, like deliberate fraud. There are bad actors, to be sure. That said, I think the majority of folks are normally good actors, and these same people can slip up and do stupid things under certain conditions. Temptation and accidents run rampant in a world of ambiguity. Much of the mess appeared—and still appears—to be a byproduct of unclear rules, lack of agreements, or misaligned incentives.
I often ask myself “What positive constraints can we apply here?” I apply this to myself for creative projects, and I apply it in many aspects of my professional and personal life. This question also led directly to the document in this post.
