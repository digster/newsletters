Tech stuff I’m tracking
-----------------------

“This is a weird comparison, but Zcash reminds me a lot of
BitTorrent… (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkh2xn8x9crh4qd8onns7hegz/p8heh9h48qvlp8iq/aHR0cHM6Ly94LmNvbS9ob3NzZWViL3N0YXR1cy8yMDA5MzM1MjM5MzUzNDc1MTU3
)” by Haseeb Qureshi (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkh2xn8x9crh4qd8onns7hegz/x0hph6hexqgl4kt5/aHR0cHM6Ly9oYXNlZWJxLmNvbS9hYm91dC8=
). I think the nature of privacy, and how much we value it, will
change fundamentally in the next 2–3 years, as the realities of
being fully exposed become more obvious. For several years, I’ve
been tracking privacy-reclaiming technologies, and this is one
that I’m paying attention to.
​
How would you like your online purchases, precise physical
location, voting and dating preferences, health or
health-adjacent information (e.g., wearables), and more to be
correlated and made public? What if insurers and potential
employers could purchase this information, even if done
indirectly to skirt regulation? What if script kiddies worldwide
could scrape most of it from publicly available data and piece it
together with AI in an afternoon? What if they could also ingest
all of your social media posts at the same time? This is a lot
closer than people realize. In fact, most of it’s already here.
​
There are an estimated 4,000+ data brokers in the US alone. They
already offer precise geolocation, political preferences,
demographic details, real-time tracking, and even military status
or employment (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkh2xn8x9crh4qd8onns7hegz/6qheh8hl09qd3zco/aHR0cHM6Ly90ZWNocG9saWN5LnNhbmZvcmQuZHVrZS5lZHUvd3AtY29udGVudC91cGxvYWRzLzIwMjEvMDgvRGF0YS1Ccm9rZXJzLWFuZC1TZW5zaXRpdmUtRGF0YS1vbi1VUy1JbmRpdmlkdWFscy1TaGVybWFuLTIwMjEucGRmP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y2hhdGdwdC5jb20=
). Major data brokers may have ~1,500 data points on single
individuals, sometimes including cell phone data, credit card
transactions, SSN, date of birth, and even driving data (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/38u8kd42kehkh2xn8x9crh4qd8onns7hegz/kkhmh6hn4x73owbl/aHR0cHM6Ly9hcG5ld3MuY29tL2FydGljbGUvZnRjLWdtLWRyaXZpbmctZGF0YS1pbnN1cmVycy1hNTU1YWJiNTZhMGQ1ZjMxYWZhOWI3M2MzZWI0ODI4Nw==
). There’s currently no comprehensive federal right for
individuals to see, correct, or opt out of data being compiled
about them. Think I’m exaggerating? I fact-checked this bullet
with a few LLMs:
​
Claude Opus 4.5: “The claims check out. The piece is well-sourced
on the data broker landscape.”
​
Gemini 3: “This text is a blend of accurate industry data,
specific historical quotes, and a slightly outdated statistic
that actually understates the current scale of the data broker
industry.”
​
And this was all before AI. I’ll save quantum computing
considerations for another time.
