Type of arbitration I’m learning about
--------------------------------------

“Baseball arbitration (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9h9ve376hehv8dxngmhnhpq8/g3hnh5hmr36m82sr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubWRycy5jb20vYmFzZWJhbGwtYXJiaXRyYXRpb24taXNudC1qdXN0LWZvci1iYXNlYmFsbC8=
).” Pretty cool idea.
​
A summary I found helpful:
​
​“Baseball arbitration” is named for its origin in Major League
Baseball (MLB), where it was introduced in 1974 to resolve salary
disputes for eligible players. It is called this because it
specifically applies to the “final offer” format, where a panel
must choose between the team’s salary offer and the player’s
request.
​
Key details about baseball arbitration:
​
​• Origin: Introduced in 1974 in the MLB to prevent holdouts.
​​
• Final Offer Mechanism: Unlike traditional arbitration, the
panel cannot “split the baby (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9h9ve376hehv8dxngmhnhpq8/9qhzhnhd0pzdv7a9/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvSnVkZ2VtZW50X29mX1NvbG9tb24=
)” or create a compromise number; they must choose one of the two
submitted figures.
​​
• Process: Both sides submit a proposed salary, and a
three-person panel selects the more reasonable offer.
​​
• Goal: The structure incentivizes both parties to submit
reasonable numbers to avoid appearing unreasonable, often leading
to a settlement before the hearing.
​
Due to this distinct “winner-takes-all” approach, it is often
referred to in legal and ADR (Alternative Dispute Resolution)
circles as “final offer arbitration” or “baseball arbitration.”​
​
Also, from this page (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/0vum69236ps9h9ve376hehv8dxngmhnhpq8/3ohphkh30743e6sr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubWRycy5jb20vYmFzZWJhbGwtYXJiaXRyYXRpb24taXNudC1qdXN0LWZvci1iYXNlYmFsbC8jOn46dGV4dD1UbyUyMHNvbHZlJTIwdGhlJTIwcHJvYmxlbSUyMG9mLGl0JTIwZGVlbXMlMjBhJTIwcmVhc29uYWJsZSUyMGZpZ3VyZS4=
):
​
​To my mind, the most significant advantage of baseball
arbitration derives from its unique feature. Since the arbitrator
is directed to choose the more reasonable of two figures, it
forces each side to be reasonable, at least by their own
reckoning. Unlike conventional arbitration, where arbitrators may
be tempted to “split the baby,” thus driving the parties to
extreme positions, baseball arbitration does just the opposite.
​
...
​
On the basis of my (limited) experience, I have long believed
that baseball arbitration has much to recommend it as a
cost-efficient way to effect final resolution.
