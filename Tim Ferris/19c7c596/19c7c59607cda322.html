Type of arbitration IвҖҷm learning about

вҖңBaseball arbitration.вҖқ Pretty cool idea.
вҖӢ
A summary I found helpful:
вҖӢ
вҖӢвҖңBaseball arbitrationвҖқ is named for its origin in Major League Baseball (MLB), where it was introduced in 1974 to resolve salary disputes for eligible players. It is called this because it specifically applies to the вҖңfinal offerвҖқ format, where a panel must choose between the teamвҖҷs salary offer and the playerвҖҷs request.
вҖӢ
Key details about baseball arbitration:
вҖӢ
вҖӢвҖў Origin: Introduced in 1974 in the MLB to prevent holdouts.
вҖӢвҖӢ
вҖў Final Offer Mechanism: Unlike traditional arbitration, the panel cannot вҖңsplit the babyвҖқ or create a compromise number; they must choose one of the two submitted figures.
вҖӢвҖӢ
вҖў Process: Both sides submit a proposed salary, and a three-person panel selects the more reasonable offer.
вҖӢвҖӢ
вҖў Goal: The structure incentivizes both parties to submit reasonable numbers to avoid appearing unreasonable, often leading to a settlement before the hearing.
вҖӢ
Due to this distinct вҖңwinner-takes-allвҖқ approach, it is often referred to in legal and ADR (Alternative Dispute Resolution) circles as вҖңfinal offer arbitrationвҖқ or вҖңbaseball arbitration.вҖқвҖӢ
вҖӢ
Also, from this page:
вҖӢ
вҖӢTo my mind, the most significant advantage of baseball arbitration derives from its unique feature. Since the arbitrator is directed to choose the more reasonable of two figures, it forces each side to be reasonable, at least by their own reckoning. Unlike conventional arbitration, where arbitrators may be tempted to вҖңsplit the baby,вҖқ thus driving the parties to extreme positions, baseball arbitration does just the opposite.
вҖӢ
