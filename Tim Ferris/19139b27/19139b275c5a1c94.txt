Question I’m asking myself frequently
-------------------------------------

“How do you feel when you wake up and when you get into bed at
night, and how easily do you fall asleep?” The time in bed in the
morning and at night tells you all you need to know. It’s not
purely intellectual reasoning. It’s not a pro-and-con list. It’s
not a spreadsheet. It’s not a Venn diagram. How do you feel? Are
you even aware of how you’re feeling? How much energy have you
spent blocking out certain feelings because you don’t want to
feel certain things?
​
To borrow from Tara Brach (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/75u0qzo2qkh8hknmo8xuniw642666tn/25h2hoh2w4x0zxh3/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDE1LzA3LzMxL3RhcmEtYnJhY2gvP3V0bV9jYW1wYWlnbj01YmYmdXRtX21lZGl1bT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCNjb250ZW50
): “There was a wise old sage who said, ‘There’s really only one
question worth considering, and that is: What are you unwilling
to feel?’”
​
Do you wake up with a sense of foreboding and anxiety and a
desire to stay in bed? When you go to bed, are you full of
anxiety and worries and preoccupation about what happened, or
what could happen the next day? I use this question as a systems
check-in for identifying things I should stop, lessen, or double
down on.
