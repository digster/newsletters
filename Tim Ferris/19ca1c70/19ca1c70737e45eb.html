Scientific study I’m reading

Some of you might recall my mention of the potential liver toxicity risks of 1,3-butanediol-based ketone supplements 6–12 months ago. 1,3-butanediol—sometimes abbreviated as 1,3-BD, BDO, or 1,3-BDO—is found in the majority of the most popular ketone supplements.
​
Even the monoester I’ve enjoyed refers to BHB bound to 1,3-BD. While I still occasionally consume it, I have strongly dialed back the dosages and frequency. I treat it more like a shot of moonshine and less like a nutritional supplement.
​
A new study was just published February 19th that further supports exercising caution: “Disparate Hepatic [Liver] Mitochondrial and Inflammatory Effects of Ketone Supplements.”
​
As always, assume there is never a biological free lunch (read post here) and consider cycling off of your non-critical supplements periodically. All that said, speak to your doctor before changing any medications. Be safe out there!

 

X thread I’m reading

“Going Founder Mode On Cancer.” After surgery, radiation, and brutal chemo failed to stop his cancer’s recurrence, co-founder of GitLab Sid Sijbrandij did what he knew best. In Silicon Valley’s parlance, he went “founder mode,” the term coined by Paul Graham to describe entrepreneurs who go deep and fully hands-on into a problem. Sid assembled a team, built a system of maximal diagnostics using cutting-edge, single-cell sequencing, and pursued 10+ experimental therapies in parallel, which included a radioligand treatment in Germany that most patients would never find. His cancer is now undetectable.

 

Passage I’m pondering

“I have always admired a man like the late Fred Fuller Shedd, who had a gift for stating an old truth in a new and picturesque way. While editor of the Philadelphia Bulletin and addressing a college graduating class, he asked: ‘How many of you have ever sawed wood? Let’s see your hands.’ Most of them had. Then he inquired: ‘How many of you have ever sawed sawdust?’ No hands went up. ‘Of course, you can’t saw sawdust!’ Mr. Shedd exclaimed. ‘It’s already sawed! And it’s the same with the past. When you start worrying about things that are over and done with, you’re merely trying to saw sawdust.’”​
— How to Stop Worrying and Start Living by Dale Carnegie
