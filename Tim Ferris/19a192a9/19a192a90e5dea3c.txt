What is on my mind
------------------

“Advice for Young Men Who Are Feeling Lost (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/owhkhqhwwe62zxcv/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDI1LzEwLzI0L2FkdmljZS1mb3IteW91bmctbWVuLz91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJmJnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQ=
).” This is a blog post with writing from Scott Galloway’s
upcoming book, Notes on Being a Man (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/z2hghnheezkqgnap/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9Ob3Rlcy1CZWluZy1NYW4tU2NvdHQtR2FsbG93YXkvZHAvMTY2ODA4NDM1WC8=
). I heard Scott present a few weeks ago, and it hit very close
to home. We are sitting on a tinderbox and need to address the
elephant in the room: young men need help.
​
In high school, I won the lottery by chancing upon one coach
whose influence saved me from the fates of many of my male
friends: jail, overdoses, DUI deaths, and more. Ever since, I’ve
searched for ways that we might nudge young men (including 20s,
30s) towards optimism and better lives. Left unaddressed, the
potential for violence and societal disaster is also high.
Testosterone and aggression will go somewhere, so best to channel
it.
​
I’m hoping Scott’s book will act as a virtual mentor for young
men who are feeling lost, stuck, angry, or despondent about the
future. Here is the sample chapter (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/owhkhqhwwe62zxcv/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDI1LzEwLzI0L2FkdmljZS1mb3IteW91bmctbWVuLz91dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJmJnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fc291cmNlPWNvbnZlcnRraXQ=
).

What I’m reading and experimenting with
---------------------------------------

“The history of Neurospora crassa in fermented foods (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/p8heh9h44e2nv6aq/aHR0cHM6Ly9saW5rLnNwcmluZ2VyLmNvbS9hcnRpY2xlLzEwLjEwMDcvczQ0MTg3LTAyNS0wMDU0Ny04
).” Yes, this is a weird one, and it’s actually a fascinating
read. Too busy to read? Take a peek at the below macros from the
linked article, which are hard to believe:

​
The above is on a dryweight basis, but even per 100 hydrated
grams, Neurospora crassa contains:
​
More fiber than oats (fungal polysaccharides, including
substantial β-glucans).
More protein than eggs.
More iron and zinc than beef.
More potassium than bananas.
Not to mention spermidine and other goodies with longevity
implications (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/x0hph6heemp5g0h5/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYmV0dGVybWVhdC5jby9sb25nZXZpdHk=
).
​
But let me back up.​
​
​Humans need protein. And while I am an investor in companies
like Maui Nui Venison (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/6qheh8hll45mqgio/aHR0cHM6Ly9tYXVpbnVpdmVuaXNvbi5jb20vbHAvdGlt
), which sells the most nutritionally dense meat you can buy in
the US, it’s ultra-premium. I eat it every week, but it doesn’t
scale to billions of people. And our current solution—factory
farming—is an ethical and ecological disaster. So what to do?
I’ve been tracking The Better Meat Co (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/kkhmh6hnnp5k70fl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYmV0dGVybWVhdC5jby8=
) (BMC) for perhaps two years, and I recently invested. Their
“protein brewery” mycoprotein, Rhiza, was recently selected as
one of TIME’s Best Inventions of 2025 (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/58hvh7hggwnreru6/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW1lLmNvbS9jb2xsZWN0aW9ucy9iZXN0LWludmVudGlvbnMtMjAyNS83MzE4MzMxL2JldHRlci1tZWF0LWNvLXJoaXphLw==
) and is FDA GRAS (“Generally Recognized As Safe”). The
production is innovative, and their approach is super novel: the
production of N. Crassa biomass for itself, not as a fermenting
agent. But what first caught my attention was that N. Crassa has
been used by humans for centuries.
​
​Sidebar—Better Meat Co. is actually B2B and doesn’t sell to
individual consumers, so I’m not selling you anything. I’m just
excited and consuming it myself. I’ll tell you later how I
wrangled a few bags for subscribers, but this isn’t a sales page.
Now back to this long-ass bullet…
​
​I’ve tried most plant-based proteins and “alt meats,” and they
tend to be incomplete, highly processed and full of crap, hard to
digest, or cost-prohibitive. Rhiza’s PDCAAS (Protein
Digestibility-Corrected Amino Acid Score) is 0.92 on a scale of
0–1, or roughly on par with chicken/fish/beef. Rhiza ferments in
hours—less than 2.5% of the time it takes to produce chicken—and
is incredibly cost-effective. For my first-ever chowdown, I
cooked a heaping pan-full and ate it in 10 minutes to push the GI
envelope. No issues whatsoever, and it actually sauteed up and
tasted something like taco meat.
​
So what’s the B2B business model? Meat companies use Rhiza
granules to enhance their meats (better nutrition, better
texture), and baked goods companies use the Rhiza flour to add
protein and fiber to their products (nutrition bars, tortillas,
crusts, etc.). If you’re a food company, you can contact them
here (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/25h2hoh33x8k5mc3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYmV0dGVybWVhdC5jby9jb250YWN0
).
​
​But how can you personally try some? It will come as a dry
powder in a vacuum-sealed bag and look shady as f*ck. I’m
currently mixing it into my shakes. If I can hit my fiber numbers
without spiking glucose, and if the spermidine gives me a little
extra autophagy, I’m double thrilled. I asked the CEO, Paul
Shapiro, if he could mail out 20 of these one-pound bags to 5BF
subscribers, and he agreed. For Better Meat Co. to find you, you
just need to put up a post tagging them on LinkedIn (here’s their
profile) (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hl698rlirh8dxm699bmh8ed/qvh8h7hdd349xlcl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cubGlua2VkaW4uY29tL2NvbXBhbnkvYmV0dGVybWVhdGNvLw==
) by 5 p.m. PT this Sunday, Oct 26.
​
There is no other way to get this stuff as an individual
consumer. BMC will randomly pick 20 folks by EOD Tuesday and
direct-message the winners to get you your bags o’ mycelial
delight.
