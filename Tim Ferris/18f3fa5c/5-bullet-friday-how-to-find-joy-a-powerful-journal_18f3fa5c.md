What I’m reading
[David Mamet’s Writing Memo](https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hd5rexpt8i5qor999cm/e0hph7h0m7dn9qt8/aHR0cHM6Ly9zY3JlZW5jcmFmdC5vcmcvd3AtY29udGVudC91cGxvYWRzLzIwMTgvMDMvRGF2aWQtTWFtZXQtTWVtby5wZGY=). Even if you don’t give a shit about writing, you’ll enjoy reading this outraged and brilliant five-page memo from [David Mamet](https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hd5rexpt8i5qor999cm/7qh7h8hod9x4vzbz/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvRGF2aWRfTWFtZXQ=). He curses like a sailor and imparts the wisdom of a sage. David won a Pulitzer Prize and received Tony nominations for his plays Glengarry Glen Ross and Speed-the-Plow. Here are two excerpts from his excellent memo to the writing staff of the TV show The Unit. I preserved the CAPS of his writing for the second part for proper impact: Question: What is drama? Drama, again, is the quest of the hero to overcome those things which prevent him from achieving a specific, acute goal. So: We, the writers, must ask ourselves of every scene these three questions. 1) Who wants what 2) What happens if [they] don’t get it? 3) Why now? [...] YES BUT YES BUT YES BUT, YOU SAY: WHAT ABOUT THE NECESSITY OF WRITING IN ALL THAT “INFORMATION?” AND I RESPOND “FIGURE IT OUT” ANY DICKHEAD WITH A BLUESUIT CAN BE (AND IS) TAUGHT TO SAY “MAKE IT CLEARER”, AND “I WANT TO KNOW MORE ABOUT HIM”.
WHEN YOU’VE MADE IT SO CLEAR THAT EVEN THIS BLUESUITED PENGUIN IS HAPPY, BOTH YOU AND HE OR SHE WILL BE OUT OF A JOB. If you are pressed for time and simply want to skim the lessons, read [9 Brilliant Lessons from David Mamet’s Writing Staff Memo](https://click.fourhourmail.com/gku6dv5rd2s5hd5rexpt8i5qor999cm/owhkhqhrxw0835fv/aHR0cHM6Ly9zY3JlZW5jcmFmdC5vcmcvYmxvZy9uaW5lLWJyaWxsaWFudC1sZXNzb25zLWZyb20tZGF2aWQtbWFtZXRzLXdyaXRpbmctc3RhZmYtbWVtby8=). But truthfully, don’t be so lazy that you rob yourself. His full memo is the way to go.
