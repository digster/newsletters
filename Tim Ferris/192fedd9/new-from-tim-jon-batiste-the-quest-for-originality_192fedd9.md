The film follows Batiste’s journey, starting in early 2022, as he receives 11 Grammy nominations for his studio album [We Are](https://click.fourhourmail.com/o8u50wr80xfqhkeqzvpugiq3egrrrto/wnh2hghwemp7x9h7/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9XRS1BUkUtRGVsdXhlLUpvbi1CYXRpc3RlL2RwL0IwOUNMQlJGUEIv). As a composer, Batiste scored Jason Reitman’s [Saturday Night](https://click.fourhourmail.com/o8u50wr80xfqhkeqzvpugiq3egrrrto/08hwh9hd3n5eg9tl/aHR0cHM6Ly95b3V0dS5iZS9pWjlPX3RsNU5waz9zaT1QMDZhZE5iRkNiLUF3US13), now in theaters. The film depicts the chaotic 90 minutes before Saturday Night Live’s first broadcast in 1975, underscored by Batiste’s blending of jazz, classical, and contemporary elements. He composed and produced the music live on set, capturing the intensity of the show’s debut. Batiste appears in the film as Billy Preston, the show’s first musical guest.
Additionally, Batiste composed and performed music for the Disney/Pixar film [Soul](https://click.fourhourmail.com/o8u50wr80xfqhkeqzvpugiq3egrrrto/8ghqhohlmwvdq3hk/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuZGlzbmV5cGx1cy5jb20vYnJvd3NlL2VudGl0eS0wMmEwMGQ0NC00NjA3LTQ4NjYtODYyZi05ZDI2MGE4ZGJkY2E=), for which he won an Academy Award for Best Original Score alongside Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross.
