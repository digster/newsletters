Nassim Nicholas Taleb ([@nntaleb](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/58hvh7hmzml973s6/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9ubnRhbGVi)) spent 21 years as a risk-taker (quantitative trader) before becoming a researcher in philosophical, mathematical, and (mostly) practical problems with probability.
Taleb is the author of a multivolume essay, the [Incerto](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/25h2hoh2e2nod6a3/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9JbmNlcnRvLUZvb2xlZC1SYW5kb21uZXNzLVByb2NydXN0ZXMtQW50aWZyYWdpbGUvZHAvMDU5MzI0MzY1WC8=) ([The Black Swan](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/qvh8h7hr0rq6nwhl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9CbGFjay1Td2FuLUltcHJvYmFibGUtUm9idXN0bmVzcy1GcmFnaWxpdHkvZHAvMDgxMjk3MzgxWC8=), [Fooled by Randomness](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/g3hnh5h3k3xzpwur/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9Gb29sZWQtUmFuZG9tbmVzcy1IaWRkZW4tTWFya2V0cy1JbmNlcnRvL2RwLzA4MTI5NzUyMTkv), [Antifragile](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/9qhzhnhplpn29mf9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9BbnRpZnJhZ2lsZS1UaGluZ3MtVGhhdC1EaXNvcmRlci1JbmNlcnRvL2RwLzA4MTI5Nzk2ODAv), [The Bed of Procrustes](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/3ohphkh7n7vdz5tr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9CZWQtUHJvY3J1c3Rlcy1QaGlsb3NvcGhpY2FsLVByYWN0aWNhbC1BcGhvcmlzbXMvZHAvMDgxMjk4MjQwMS8=), and [Skin in the Game](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/n2hohvh3k3eorzt6/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9Ta2luLUdhbWUtSGlkZGVuLUFzeW1tZXRyaWVzLURhaWx5L2RwLzA0MjUyODQ2NDYv)), covering broad facets of uncertainty. His work has been published into 49 languages.
In addition to his trader life, Taleb has also written, as a backup of the Incerto, more than 70 technical and scholarly papers in mathematical statistics, genetics, quantitative finance, statistical physics, medicine, philosophy, ethics, economics, and international affairs around the notion of risk and probability (grouped in the [Technical Incerto](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/48hvhehrnrk2gxcx/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9TdGF0aXN0aWNhbC1Db25zZXF1ZW5jZXMtRmF0LVRhaWxzLVByZWFzeW1wdG90aWNzL2RwL0IwQ0Q4WDVZQzUv)).
Taleb is currently Distinguished Professor of Risk Engineering at NYU’s Tandon School of Engineering (retired). His current focus is on the properties of systems that can handle disorder (“antifragile”).

Scott Patterson ([@pattersonscott](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/wnh2hghw3w8ogla7/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9wYXR0ZXJzb25zY290dD9sYW5nPWVu)) is aninvestigative reporter for The Wall Street Journal, currently based in Washington DC, working on climate and energy policy. His new book is [Chaos Kings: How Wall Street Traders Make Billions in the New Age of Crisis](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/reh8hoh0e0n5xpu2/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9DaGFvcy1LaW5ncy1TdHJlZXQtVHJhZGVycy1CaWxsaW9ucy9kcC8xOTgyMTc5OTM3Lw==), a profile of the rise of “black-swan traders,” such as Nassim Taleb and Mark Spitznagel, as well as a survey of the many perils the world faces today—and how we might fix them.
Scott has covered everything from Berkshire Hathaway to stock exchanges to high-speed traders to the financial regulators. His first book, [The Quants](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/08hwh9hdkdq7z8tl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9RdWFudHMtV2hpenplcy1Db25xdWVyZWQtU3RyZWV0LURlc3Ryb3llZC9kcC8wMzA3NDUzMzgzLw==), describes the rise of mathematical finance and delves into its role in the 2008 financial blowup. [Dark Pools](https://click.fourhourmail.com/92u58xp28qfnhq9g2rka5i027kd33/8ghqhohlxlk5kesk/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9EYXJrLVBvb2xzLU1hY2hpbmUtVHJhZGVycy1SaWdnaW5nL2RwLzAzMDc4ODcxODkv), his second book, tells how computer traders took control of the US stock market, starting from the birth of computer trading in the 1980s to the explosion of high-frequency trading in the late 2000s.
