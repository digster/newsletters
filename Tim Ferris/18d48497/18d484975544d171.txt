Essay I’m reading
-----------------

“The Acceleration of Addictiveness (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/e0hph7h07r3o4zt8/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGF1bGdyYWhhbS5jb20vYWRkaWN0aW9uLmh0bWw=
)” by Paul Graham (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/owhkhqhrwqmd53hv/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cucGF1bGdyYWhhbS5jb20v
) (@paulg (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/z2hghnh3el2x04up/aHR0cHM6Ly90d2l0dGVyLmNvbS9wYXVsZz9sYW5nPWVu
)). This was strongly recommended to me by Matt Mullenweg (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/kkhmh6hlnx0v43bl/aHR0cDovL21hLnR0
), who was recently on the podcast (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/58hvh7hmg6d2l9a6/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIzLzEyLzI5L21hdHQtbXVsbGVud2VnLTIvP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZiNjb250ZW50
). To give you a taste, here are two excerpts that I highlighted
for myself:
​
​One sense of “normal” is statistically normal: what everyone
else does. The other is the sense we mean when we talk about the
normal operating range of a piece of machinery: what works best.
​
These two senses are already quite far apart. Already someone
trying to live well would seem eccentrically abstemious in most
of the US. That phenomenon is only going to become more
pronounced. You can probably take it as a rule of thumb from now
on that if people don’t think you’re weird, you’re living badly.
​
​[...]
​​
People commonly use the word “procrastination” to describe what
they do on the Internet. It seems to me too mild to describe
what’s happening as merely not-doing-work. We don’t call it
procrastination when someone gets drunk instead of working.
​
​Note that the second snippet is from one of his footnotes, which
I always read.

Book I’m reading
----------------

​The Lincoln Highway (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/08hwh9hd2l6rqrtl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MaW5jb2xuLUhpZ2h3YXktTm92ZWwtQW1vci1Ub3dsZXMvZHAvMDczNTIyMjM1NS8=
) by Amor Towles (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/l2hehmh3lxm7w8u6/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQW1vcl9Ub3dsZXM=
). A good friend and bestselling author told me that he
considered this the greatest American novel of the last 20 years.
I haven’t read enough classics to know if that’s accurate, but
it’s one hell of a read, the prose is gorgeous, and you’ll laugh
out loud many times.
​
The official book description makes it sound darker than it is.
Certainly, parts of the book showcase questionable characters and
questionable actions, but this doesn’t detract from the pervasive
humor and beauty of the writing, which are pleasures unto
themselves. From Amazon (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/08hwh9hd2l6rqrtl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MaW5jb2xuLUhpZ2h3YXktTm92ZWwtQW1vci1Ub3dsZXMvZHAvMDczNTIyMjM1NS8=
): “In June, 1954, eighteen-year-old Emmett Watson is driven home
to Nebraska by the warden of the work farm where he has just
served a year for involuntary manslaughter. His mother long gone,
his father recently deceased, and the family farm foreclosed upon
by the bank, Emmett’s intention is to pick up his eight-year-old
brother and head west, where they can start their lives anew. But
when the warden drives away, Emmett discovers that two friends
from the work farm have hidden themselves in the trunk of the
warden’s car. Together, they have hatched an altogether different
plan for Emmett’s future.”
