One sense of “normal” is statistically normal: what everyone else does. The other is the sense we mean when we talk about the normal operating range of a piece of machinery: what works best.
These two senses are already quite far apart. Already someone trying to live well would seem eccentrically abstemious in most of the US. That phenomenon is only going to become more pronounced. You can probably take it as a rule of thumb from now on that if people don’t think you’re weird, you’re living badly.

“[Why You Should Seek More Awe in the New Year](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/g3hnh5h3mq8wxmhr/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDI0LzAxLzI0L2F3ZS8_dXRtX3NvdXJjZT1jb252ZXJ0a2l0JnV0bV9tZWRpdW09Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fY2FtcGFpZ249NWJmI2NvbnRlbnQ=)” by [Ashley Stimpson](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/9qhzhnhpd5vrnza9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYXNobGV5c3RpbXBzb24uY29tLw==). Every time I’ve done a [past-year review (PYR)](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/3ohphkh73p5gvztr/aHR0cHM6Ly90aW0uYmxvZy8yMDIxLzEyLzI3L3Bhc3QteWVhci1yZXZpZXcvP3V0bV9zb3VyY2U9Y29udmVydGtpdCZ1dG1fbWVkaXVtPWNvbnZlcnRraXQmdXRtX2NhbXBhaWduPTViZiNjb250ZW50), a pattern emerges: peak positive emotional experiences are correlated to awe at least 70% of the time. For at least the past 3–5 years, this has been so consistent that I often determine what big blocks to schedule in the new year based on potential for awe. The payoffs include [time dilation](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/48hvhehrmxd8kwtx/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuaW5zdGFncmFtLmNvbS9yZWVsL0MyY2RoRFFPN19TLz9pZ3NoPU1UazRNM2xoYzJkaVpqVmpOQSUzRCUzRA==) and, more broadly, traversing the miraculous canvas of full human experience. Friends have asked me why I do silent retreats in nature, why I love ski touring, why I hunt once in a blue moon, or why I am deeply interested in psychedelic science and [psychedelic-assisted therapies](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/wnh2hghwq0268nb7/aHR0cHM6Ly9zYWlzZWlmb3VuZGF0aW9uLm9yZy8=). If I had to sum it all up in one word, it would be: Awe. But what exactly is “awe,” and how can we embrace more of it? I haven’t found a better article exploring these topics than [Ashley Stimpson](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/9qhzhnhpd5vrnza9/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYXNobGV5c3RpbXBzb24uY29tLw==)’s “[Awestruck](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/reh8hoh0m6lznds2/aHR0cHM6Ly9odWIuamh1LmVkdS9tYWdhemluZS8yMDIzL3dpbnRlci9zY2llbmNlLW9mLWF3ZS1wc3ljaGVkZWxpY3Mv),” featured in [Johns Hopkins Magazine](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/reh8hoh0m6lznds2/aHR0cHM6Ly9odWIuamh1LmVkdS9tYWdhemluZS8yMDIzL3dpbnRlci9zY2llbmNlLW9mLWF3ZS1wc3ljaGVkZWxpY3Mv), so I asked for permission to publish on my blog, which was graciously granted. [The Lincoln Highway](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/08hwh9hd2l6rqrtl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MaW5jb2xuLUhpZ2h3YXktTm92ZWwtQW1vci1Ub3dsZXMvZHAvMDczNTIyMjM1NS8=) by [Amor Towles](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/l2hehmh3lxm7w8u6/aHR0cHM6Ly9lbi53aWtpcGVkaWEub3JnL3dpa2kvQW1vcl9Ub3dsZXM=). A good friend and bestselling author told me that he considered this the greatest American novel of the last 20 years. I haven’t read enough classics to know if that’s accurate, but it’s one hell of a read, the prose is gorgeous, and you’ll laugh out loud many times.
The official book description makes it sound darker than it is. Certainly, parts of the book showcase questionable characters and questionable actions, but this doesn’t detract from the pervasive humor and beauty of the writing, which are pleasures unto themselves. From [Amazon](https://click.fourhourmail.com/v8unvm27v9srhxlq5xgizizxl28ll/08hwh9hd2l6rqrtl/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9MaW5jb2xuLUhpZ2h3YXktTm92ZWwtQW1vci1Ub3dsZXMvZHAvMDczNTIyMjM1NS8=): “In June, 1954, eighteen-year-old Emmett Watson is driven home to Nebraska by the warden of the work farm where he has just served a year for involuntary manslaughter. His mother long gone, his father recently deceased, and the family farm foreclosed upon by the bank, Emmett’s intention is to pick up his eight-year-old brother and head west, where they can start their lives anew. But when the warden drives away, Emmett discovers that two friends from the work farm have hidden themselves in the trunk of the warden’s car. Together, they have hatched an altogether different plan for Emmett’s future.”
