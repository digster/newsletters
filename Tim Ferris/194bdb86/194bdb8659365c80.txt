Book highlight I’m pondering
----------------------------

“Half the students billed the company 15 cents per minute
($9/hour) for their time, while the others billed the company
$1.50/minute ($90/hour). Afterward, students who billed the
company at the higher rate reported feeling more pressed for
time—even though they had completed the same tasks for the same
amount of time as students who billed at the lower rate. In other
words, making students’ time worth a lot of money was all it took
to turn them into stressed-out, time-squeezed consultants.”

From Happy Money: The Science of Happier Spending (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/d0uv8kxd86s0ho4vpk0c9izwxq244tl/reh8hoh0832nppu2/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuYW1hem9uLmNvbS9IYXBweS1Nb25leS1TY2llbmNlLUhhcHBpZXItU3BlbmRpbmcvZHAvMTQ1MTY2NTA3NS8=
) by Elizabeth Dunn and Michael Norton

description: “Roadrunner: A Film About Anthony Bourdain is an

lived his life unabashedly became a world-renowned cultural
icon.” Find the trailer here (
https://click.fourhourmail.com/d0uv8kxd86s0ho4vpk0c9izwxq244tl/m2h7h5h8rqgwr9fm/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cueW91dHViZS5jb20vd2F0Y2g_dj1paEVFandSbGdoUQ==

the dark, and this film raises important questions for me. For
example: How can you emulate certain characteristics of tortured
outliers without inadvertently absorbing beliefs and behaviors
that contributed to their pain? Is it even possible? Tony’s story
is inspiring, incredible, and tragic, and this film does an
admirable job of capturing all three.
