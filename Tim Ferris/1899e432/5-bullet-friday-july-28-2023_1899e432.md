[Sort by unread messages in iMessage](https://click.fourhourmail.com/wvumzkq3z7sgh5xv2mvhoix/qvh8h7hrvx29gqfl/aHR0cHM6Ly9zdXBwb3J0LmFwcGxlLmNvbS9lbi11cy9ndWlkZS9pcGhvbmUvaXBoZTliNDhiODllL2lvcw==). This has reduced my device anxiety tremendously. I get a lot of texts but will often lose unread messages several scrolls down and forget about them, disappointing a friend or losing a fun opportunity in the process. Last week, I was hanging out with a bunch of tech-savvy friends, and I noticed that one of them had an “Unread Messages” folder in iMessage. WTF?! I’d been begging Apple for this feature for years but had never seen it. He suggested it was because I hadn’t updated my iOS, but all of us were up-to-date, and he was the only one with this amazing folder on his phone. After some detective work, my friend Matt figured out how to implement it (Settings > Messages, then scroll down to Message Filtering, then turn on Filter Unknown Senders. In the Messages conversation list, tap Filters, then tap Unread Messages), and you can find [the step-by-step instructions here](https://click.fourhourmail.com/wvumzkq3z7sgh5xv2mvhoix/qvh8h7hrvx29gqfl/aHR0cHM6Ly9zdXBwb3J0LmFwcGxlLmNvbS9lbi11cy9ndWlkZS9pcGhvbmUvaXBoZTliNDhiODllL2lvcw==). Thanks, Apple! I’d also love to request the ability to archive messages in iMessage, but being able to sort by unread is at least half the battle. Special thanks to JZ and Matt!
P.S. iOS 17 has [a slew of new iMessage features](https://click.fourhourmail.com/wvumzkq3z7sgh5xv2mvhoix/g3hnh5h3nvo47gtr/aHR0cHM6Ly93d3cuY25ldC5jb20vdGVjaC9zZXJ2aWNlcy1hbmQtc29mdHdhcmUvYXBwbGVzLW5ldy1pb3MtMTctbWVzc2FnZXMtZmVhdHVyZXMtaGVscC1pdC1jYXRjaC11cC10by1jaGF0LWFwcHMv) coming in September, and one I am excited about is automatic transcription of voice memos.
