2. A prosecutor from the same office recommended filing a criminal complaint to quickly get Republicans prior to an indictment — so why couldn’t they have done that for SBF?
Second, was it really impossible for prosecutors to file a criminal “complaint” to bring SBF quickly into custody, after which they’d get a full grand jury indictment? Because that’s exactly what this former prosecutor from the same office that prosecuted SBF recommended [ https://substack.com/redirect/970212f2-ae45-4a3b-bcdd-a7d96b96502f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] as a strategy when they wanted to prosecute Republicans: the use of a criminal [ https://substack.com/redirect/0e49d42f-0a19-4820-8f25-cfb1a0d6c319?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] complaint [ https://substack.com/redirect/d9c33e8f-f698-41b2-8926-1c9e2ce2ac5f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to make an arrest quickly.
A criminal complaint, by contrast, is frequently used when prosecutors need to make an arrest quickly. This may happen, for example, when federal agents learn that a crime is about to occur, or has just occurred, and must act immediately.  In this situation, prosecutors do not have the time to go through the grand jury process.  Instead, prosecutors file a written document called a criminal complaint, together with an affidavit signed by an agent familiar with the case.  A judge or magistrate judge will review the complaint and affidavit, and issue an arrest warrant if he or she finds probable cause. Once an arrest is made on a criminal complaint, federal law requires that the defendant must be charged by an indictment (or by a criminal information, if it’s a misdemeanor case or the defendant agrees to waive indictment) within 30 days.
I mean, I’m not a lawyer, but this makes sense. Are we to imagine that the government has intentionally tied its hands for weeks in every such situation? Or can they move fast on other white collar cases when they want to, such as when it’s time to get Trump [ https://substack.com/redirect/316d0d66-0026-42be-8ab0-1cd4dd3204cf?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]?

WHAT HAVE WE LEARNED?
OK, that was long, but someone had to write it. Now the question is: what did we learn [ https://substack.com/redirect/b0ffe507-4982-420d-be09-2841fdcbc12a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]? I actually think there are a few takehomes.
We learned that Crypto Twitter broke the case wide open, exposing [ https://substack.com/redirect/470b7a92-95f0-4787-bf8f-d6d75897e097?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Bankman-Fried in the first place, causing [ https://substack.com/redirect/f0cae12d-cfed-4783-86c7-849b765589c7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] a massive liability for the Democrat Party, and ultimately compelling the regime to action on their #2 donor [ https://substack.com/redirect/7f80363a-c260-48e1-af32-2e21ec41ee14?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
We learned that contemporaneous accounts acknowledged [ https://substack.com/redirect/75535d1b-7b2e-4ff7-8879-656e13fa8d51?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] that citizen journalism had outperformed legacy media on this case, and indeed that the New York Times and Congress seemed to be involved in covering [ https://substack.com/redirect/07a4672f-c093-410d-9d6d-21e78b72f7a6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] up the crime.
We learned that regulators are incapable of distinguishing good projects from bad; they were contemplating [ https://substack.com/redirect/aaecca12-cb9b-47a5-8d31-c81166de5db7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] no [ https://substack.com/redirect/ddba00c0-d804-4de1-b474-56f0bfcb92d0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] action [ https://substack.com/redirect/3d7be8b3-c185-4b6e-8862-99c14dbee850?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on SBF, while taking no action on approving [ https://substack.com/redirect/1b1c44a1-8a06-4039-9410-f1fa7efbbad6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Bitcoin ETFs.
And we learned that no legacy journalist, politician, regulator, or policeman flagged SBF before his stolen money swung elections [ https://substack.com/redirect/7f80363a-c260-48e1-af32-2e21ec41ee14?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] across the country, elections that will never be unwound. The investors who put money into FTX lost their shirts, but the politicians who took money out of FTX will keep their seats.
Look, I get why people want to claim that the “system worked.” After years of abolishing the police, after years of rampant crime in blue cities and completely [ https://substack.com/redirect/8b3cd89d-1842-45fa-8d7f-03a7e3233de9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] politicized [ https://substack.com/redirect/771a8bfd-64be-494a-953f-d01df71fe9ce?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] law [ https://substack.com/redirect/1a14b66e-1489-46c7-816f-fdfd834dfa08?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] enforcement [ https://substack.com/redirect/7a8c133d-7a57-41b1-9eb3-e89332c18a4e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]…it’s refreshing to see one case that seems to get the right result.
But don’t take your eye off the ball. The only reason we’re here is that the Network succeeded [ https://substack.com/redirect/2ffd4d5c-2561-49a6-90b2-c4827f7a62da?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] where the State failed. No legacy journalist, politician, regulator, or policeman figured out that SBF was a criminal. But the Internet did.
