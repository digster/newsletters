That’s Gavin Newsom: former mayor of San Francisco, current governor of California, and possible future President of the United States of America if Biden [ https://substack.com/redirect/bc198193-02f9-4591-8621-c87f05df4ce2?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is [ https://substack.com/redirect/179af568-5fa9-48cd-9973-d2fb414b957a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] firmly [ https://substack.com/redirect/d41445ad-fcbe-42ea-b6d3-12aceb120589?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] escorted [ https://substack.com/redirect/779d8b9f-7d58-4957-8eba-531f50906bbf?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] off the stage.
As we’ll detail, Newsom recently pulled off an under-reported diplomatic coup by not only getting a rare in-person [ https://substack.com/redirect/f04594d8-02fd-41b6-935d-facb02b908a8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] meeting with Xi Jinping, not only getting Chinese state-run media to sing his praises, but apparently getting Xi to come out in person to the Nov 11-17 APEC Summit [ https://substack.com/redirect/fe67be51-f0b4-4108-87c4-505985176043?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in his home city of San Francisco, California.
In advance of Xi’s visit, Newsom just held up a hand [ https://substack.com/redirect/65a149b5-1510-4187-be83-2278832798af?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and rolled up SF’s seemingly permanent homeless encampments. He spent the political capital to do this, overriding all the Democrats paid by the city [ https://substack.com/redirect/fe69b8a9-654f-437f-95f6-3fe54bf8da0a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to feed drugs to addicts [ https://substack.com/redirect/9caec99c-89a5-4b66-a527-6fdb99751ba5?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. Of course, Newsom could have done this at any time to the homeless industrial complex [ https://substack.com/redirect/2e205712-5f23-49d1-abe9-41c3878b0dd5?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], but why now? If Newsom pulls it off, we may soon see rapprochement between the farthest left [ https://substack.com/redirect/21af2d66-480d-470a-a1a7-772e2a6a6335?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] party in the world (the ultra-woke American Democrats) and the farthest right [ https://substack.com/redirect/21af2d66-480d-470a-a1a7-772e2a6a6335?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] party in the world (the ultra-nationalist Chinese Communists). But you gotta admit, it is suggestive.
So, this is a speculative post about (a) why a Democrat/Communist pact is possible and (b) what the implications would be for each faction if it goes through. It’s just a scenario, of course, but there are enough signals in this direction for it to be worth contemplating.
Why a Democrat/Communist Pact is Possible
First, a Brief History
To understand where we might go, we need to understand where we’ve already been. From 1972 [ https://substack.com/redirect/94a8ad66-dfa6-4c63-94e3-12912402ee1b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (when Nixon went to China) and 1978 [ https://substack.com/redirect/53398b67-f423-4177-84ff-90e13546cf4b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] (when Deng turned China capitalist), until 2015 (when Trump escalated [ https://substack.com/redirect/301b8ea3-4f24-48bb-9b16-82364cb91b4d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] with China), the US and China were basically at peace.
Neither Democrat nor Republican leadership cared much about low-wage jobs being sent to China over this period, because (a) these were initially at the bottom of the value chain, (b) environmental and labor regulations made them infeasible in the US anyway, (c) victory in the Cold War meant China would eventually convert to democracy, (d) the rightist value of free trade and the leftist value of cosmopolitanism meant that it’d be both expensive and immoral to favor Americans, and finally and most importantly (e) China was perceived as a submissive third-world sweatshop that wasn’t capable of posing a real threat to the global hyperpower.
As a consequence, the American public didn’t care about China until 2020 [ https://substack.com/redirect/bc99d283-7ec2-4ef3-90dc-a8104353a494?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], when media coverage suddenly shifted sentiment.
A more detailed timeline lets us see why this shift happened in 2020.
1991: When Krauthammer writes “The Unipolar Moment [ https://substack.com/redirect/ca47754c-8872-4a39-b199-84cf2b5b7f70?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]”, China gets only the briefest of mentions and isn’t taken seriously as a threat.
2000-2010s: the US continued ignoring [ https://substack.com/redirect/6407953f-3421-437b-8f8a-cfa9aed032f8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] China to fight in the Middle East. During this period, the US actually bombed [ https://substack.com/redirect/0bacb825-a266-48c1-af70-505111326f0d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Uighurs in Afghanistan and put Chinese Uighur [ https://substack.com/redirect/f399a4be-9570-4ff3-8e60-bccc1c0faa8f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] militants [ https://substack.com/redirect/383abee7-2571-4443-8a30-c35774891ab6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in Guantanamo [ https://substack.com/redirect/18f53d99-3fda-4db5-9146-02db6b5852f4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
2011: After the financial crisis, NYT even proposed selling [ https://substack.com/redirect/9864ee59-eadd-47cb-8f75-3d6b0163c7fa?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Taiwan to China for $1T in debt forgiveness, signaling how little the US cared.
2015: Trump saw an opportunity with blue collar Republicans harmed by offshoring, pushed China to the forefront (“China, China, China [ https://substack.com/redirect/4be42bc2-e770-4b8a-a962-8883689967db?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]”), and was mocked as a racist for doing so. During the Trump years, Russia [ https://substack.com/redirect/67201695-7abc-47ca-8876-60e3d1532e4e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] was the primary antagonist rather than China.
2019: Obama releases American Factory [ https://substack.com/redirect/2a7ecf85-41dc-4a3b-971f-2e636fbaff83?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], a movie promoting peace and trade with China as an implicit rebuke to Trumpism. But in the same year, China defeats what they saw as US [ https://substack.com/redirect/bcdb19ef-3e9e-4690-97e0-28f9c9acb6b0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]-backed [ https://substack.com/redirect/f257b517-658d-42f4-866e-6e083f53d6b1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] democracy activists [ https://substack.com/redirect/12595139-4be3-4a65-a4ff-e2d94e300ce2?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in Hong Kong [ https://substack.com/redirect/00f05aa8-edff-4fa9-887f-63a79796c4bb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], escalating negative sentiment within China towards the US and vice versa.
Mid 2020: This is the flip. Once COVID goes pandemic, the US establishment flips to copying China while also attacking China. This happened most obviously on lockdowns, but also on speech controls [ https://substack.com/redirect/dcfc1524-e0a2-4c51-b782-fda0966df1f0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], “industrial [ https://substack.com/redirect/7c953623-1367-4eeb-82ac-95f9427cc76d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] policy [ https://substack.com/redirect/73ce968f-f366-4b6a-9bdf-668fbe1088ad?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]”, and even mimetic rivalry [ https://substack.com/redirect/8bd00edc-1f40-444f-81a4-1c8bf48efb0c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on Taiwan.
2021-2022
After the election, the regime completely u-turns on China, adopting many Trumpian positions [ https://substack.com/redirect/2eb924c3-ed06-4d51-be2b-e95faa148f2e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], reversing Obama-era trade policies, and attempting to stop China’s rise [ https://substack.com/redirect/8b81751c-58b1-4e08-9449-07c784b89b21?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
They also allowed people to speak about the lab leak theory — before the election it was politically useful to blame Trump alone, but after the election it was politically useful [ https://substack.com/redirect/1c21787d-ea08-4a84-98db-a33eda31446c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to acknowledge China may have been responsible. 
All of this anti-China turn was wrapped in cynically patriotic rhetoric — just a few months after tearing down George Washington [ https://substack.com/redirect/37f263b1-19d7-4459-b7e8-1a7e4c9e3dca?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and declaring America systemically racist [ https://substack.com/redirect/d4f8a78e-2a30-4535-8747-9872651d4510?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Democrats suddenly repositioned [ https://substack.com/redirect/599df8e6-a9fd-4c71-9af9-b10c705d8248?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] themselves as super-patriots to the right of Republicans on China.

One might argue a Democrat/Communist pact doesn’t make any ideological sense. As detailed in this chapter on The Global Flippening [ https://substack.com/redirect/21af2d66-480d-470a-a1a7-772e2a6a6335?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], American Democrats are the farthest left party in the world on the cultural axis, while the Chinese Communists are the farthest right. We’re talking about ultra-wokes flying the progress flag [ https://substack.com/redirect/4678ef07-ccd4-426f-9dc3-07334a478aac?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] on the White House and Han nationalists purging “sissy men [ https://substack.com/redirect/fc33a106-48dc-4a2a-962a-62f590ec6d46?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]” from public life. How could they possibly get along?
But if you remember 1972 [ https://substack.com/redirect/94a8ad66-dfa6-4c63-94e3-12912402ee1b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Nixon’s Republicans were on the right and Mao’s ChiComs were on the left. And if you go back to 1939 [ https://substack.com/redirect/a4763c90-fe5a-431e-a942-081a9137f2bf?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], Hitler’s Nazis were on the far right while Stalin’s Soviets were on the far left.
These pairs of regimes didn’t like each other, but their deals focused on mutual enemies. The secret part [ https://substack.com/redirect/96beb310-d431-4133-a1d1-933fc4ce383f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of the Molotov-Ribbentrop pact allowed the Soviets and Nazis to carve up Poland between themselves in 1939, while the secret terms of the Shanghai Communique [ https://substack.com/redirect/38d76e97-60dc-40d0-84fb-6e610369fe80?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] allowed the Americans and Chinese to team up on the Soviets after 1972.
While one of those deals did blow up in years as the Nazis and Soviets began fighting with each other, the other one lasted for decades as the Chinese and Americans continued trading with each other.
Moreover, the secret part of Molotov-Ribbentrop where they agree to divide up Eastern Europe [ https://substack.com/redirect/57b39fd0-cc94-4e09-9c56-59fdc3999c4b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] wasn’t fully revealed till after the collapse of the USSR in 1992 [ https://substack.com/redirect/eb95b76d-26e9-4c06-b6d5-a3a46d508799?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. The secret part of the Shanghai Communique where Nixon agreed to concede on Taiwan [ https://substack.com/redirect/7b4af534-f2f0-4e04-b015-7f9d95e0c8b7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] wasn’t revealed till 2003 [ https://substack.com/redirect/b296d60f-f637-4541-aae3-9cc02551e7b8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
So we might not know all the details of what Newsom and Xi are discussing for a while. But we can make an educated guess by asking a related question, which is: what are the shared interests and mutual enemies of both Chinese Communists and American Democrats?
A Shared Interest in the Most Powerful State
The shared interest part is easy: Democrats and Communists both love the state, and the Democrat base is more friendly to China than the Republican base. So a new G2 [ https://substack.com/redirect/1a780d66-885c-4fb1-a8b7-08aa372f9c8b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] under Newsom/Xi has enough Democrat support from both elites and base to reshape the “international community.”
Democrats love the state
If Republicans want to be part of the most powerful nation, Democrats want to be part of the most powerful state.
That is: Democrats are wired differently from Republicans. While Republicans want to be part of a tight-knit community that organically scales from baseball and hot dogs to aircraft carriers and world domination, Democrats are fine with simply becoming senior administrators of a multi-cultural, multi-ethnic, and even multi-national global empire.
For example, Republicans complain that the US is focusing more on the Russo-Ukrainian border than the Texas-Mexico border. But that’s because Republicans naively think of the US as a country populated by people with shared culture. Democrats by contrast think of the US as an empire, governed by enlightened bureaucrats, and recognize that the actual border of democracy is indeed in Ukraine. For a Democrat, the internal migration from Mexico to Texas is of no more moral concern than the internal migration from New Mexico to Texas. All of these are just provinces of the American Empire. And if you expand the borders of the empire, sure, you may dilute [ https://substack.com/redirect/17f2eccc-7295-4ffd-bc51-31984d7bafd1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] the founding nation, which would anger Republicans — but you expand the reach of the state, which would favor Democrats.
So, while Republicans would find the idea alien, it’s not that big a deal for Democrats  to work out a power-sharing deal with Communists to manage a global empire. Again, that’s been part of the playbook since [ https://substack.com/redirect/6e03de79-13de-4b23-9294-42972fa6667d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] FDR [ https://substack.com/redirect/33414b13-8038-4eb4-871b-77605540e9e9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and [ https://substack.com/redirect/e6f4e1a7-d509-4014-b8dd-ea2112910d66?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Ted [ https://substack.com/redirect/bb7a2885-ad0a-43ab-ab1e-8559bb100806?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] Kennedy [ https://substack.com/redirect/f26fbd1b-028a-4e9f-8948-25f011530ff1?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
Young Democrats less negative on China
Also, if the Republican base leans Russia, the Democrat base likes China more.
Some Republicans have a soft spot for Russia, which they see as surviving communism to rebuild the Russian Orthodox Church. Democrats by contrast hate Russians [ https://substack.com/redirect/eb391b92-eacf-46a1-bbaf-61e658a9a06e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], because (a) they’re mostly white Orthodox Christians, (b) they blame them for Russiagate [ https://substack.com/redirect/a6e8edf4-c5d8-468e-bcbf-e9be21806ecb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and (c) they refuse to submit to the American state.
These sympathies are reversed when it comes to China. Republicans now see the Chinese as the most threatening outgroup; while not acknowledged, China is what unenlightened generations would have called the “Yellow Peril.” But the TikTok Democrat has much more sympathy for China [ https://substack.com/redirect/ea9905f4-c064-4597-a536-2b13180056e6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. After all, the CCP is non-white, non-Western, and calls themselves communists…so can they really be that bad? Or so the logic goes.
So, while the Republican base would hate the idea, it’s also not that big a deal for the Democrat base to go along with Democrat elites on China detente. Basically: young Dems like TikTok, don’t want to fight a war, and think that because China calls themselves “communist” that the CCP is on the left. Wokes for Chinese nationalism is like LGBTQ for Hamas [ https://substack.com/redirect/5bdde9ba-43f2-4782-b922-0162b21483d8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], an easily papered-over paradox.
A Mutual Enemy in Decentralized Networks and Independent Nations
We’ve spoken of shared interests. But who’d be the mutual enemies of any Democrat/Communist detente?
Chinese liberals. The CCP doesn’t like Chinese liberals. They’ll want American Democrats to stop funding perceived color revolutions like Hong Kong, and to give them a free hand in their sphere of influence like the US has historically given other regimes. The US is already looking the other way on activists like Joshua Wong [ https://substack.com/redirect/a5791f8d-e2a2-4ef3-8bb3-92ef0efaa3d4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], so this could plausibly happen.
American conservatives. US Democrats don’t like American conservatives. They’ll probably want Chinese Communists to give them more control over TikTok, to ship them goods to reduce inflation, and to buy Treasuries to maybe kick the can for a bit longer, all of which could boost them against Republicans. Personally, I’m skeptical as to whether even a Democrat/Communist pact to keep buying Treasuries could forestall the level of economic reckoning [ https://substack.com/redirect/0bc148ba-9086-4f49-9142-1a89d33f7401?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to come…but maybe we see both a crash and a pact, wherein a relatively functional Communist regime ships hard goods to help Democrats maintain domestic power. It may not be too different from how the Chinese support dysfunctional [ https://substack.com/redirect/ea2718fc-fd41-413a-bcb2-e0edef6edd34?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] regimes [ https://substack.com/redirect/f8cd650a-16fe-4550-aa80-bf63ccbc34bd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in Africa.
The Internet. Both Democrats and Communists hate an uncensored, uncontrolled internet. They want to regulate AI, regulate crypto, regulate everything. And neither of them likes their troublesome domestic tech CEOs, whether Jack Ma [ https://substack.com/redirect/770fd028-7a50-4494-94f3-0e6ec049c775?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] or Elon Musk [ https://substack.com/redirect/a5650bb2-b1dc-42d1-9bd8-a9b70354868f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
Other groups would have more mixed outcomes.
Europe. They’re both neutral/positive towards Western Europe, which has basically taken itself off the map anyway by deindustrializing [ https://substack.com/redirect/73a98de1-16ca-4e08-ab35-3c84e9cdf39a?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] itself in the fight against Russia.
India. On India, while Democrats have been making noises of support and the Chinese have recently been polite, they could both flip on a dime to neutral/negative from neutral/positive if Democrats and Communists align with each other. However, there’s too much economic growth [ https://substack.com/redirect/45798f4e-e7f2-423e-b61a-0cd65d1938fc?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in India to flip to fully negative.
Finally, Democrats and Communists would need to settle conflict zones.
Taiwan. There might be some kind of grudging trade on Taiwan, where the US and China agree on the status quo. If China achieves a cultural victory [ https://substack.com/redirect/6feff6cb-7f93-4a10-aba3-a4781fc36d4c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in Taiwan, by getting the less-US-aligned [ https://substack.com/redirect/a4e33a17-b59a-4481-a1fb-6a2a3e484341?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] parties to win, maybe they also agree to keep shipping chips. Israel. Neither of them is particularly fond of the Israelis.
