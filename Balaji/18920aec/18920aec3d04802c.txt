I’m not really interested in the endless primary battle between Trump and DeSantis for the Republican nomination. But I am interested in this recent salvo by Trump-supporter Laura Loomer — mainly because I anticipate many more things like it, where group A blames group B for an economic losses caused by the Fed.
Check it out [ https://substack.com/redirect/b3c5172b-cdb4-4e65-a6f2-c96298e935f4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]:
Loomer is accusing Florida’s state government, and by extension DeSantis, of somehow being derelict in the management of these insurance companies. And the thing is, it’s quite likely that many insurance companies are indeed underwater.
But if so, that’s the fault of the Fed, not Florida.
Why? Well, one of the insurance companies named below is American Coastal. And Weiss Ratings reports [ https://substack.com/redirect/5235f013-1456-4a41-a5b7-18b4460039cb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] they put 77% of their portfolio into safe, investment-grade bonds. Problem is, thanks to the Fed, bonds are no longer safe.
See, many financial institutions bought [ https://substack.com/redirect/c793ae91-d86e-4987-921c-7391c7ef168c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] supposedly safe bonds from the government in 2021. But then that same government did a massive surprise devaluation [ https://substack.com/redirect/8ae9a517-f4dd-4086-9743-2749b67df04c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of all of the bonds it had just sold — screwing over its biggest customers, from banks to insurance companies, causing the largest losses in bond history [ https://substack.com/redirect/7ed4abe4-cf4c-45f6-b736-6581d136de40?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], and saddling institutions what Stanford researchers estimate to be literally trillions [ https://substack.com/redirect/4c290769-bc5a-4e36-8cac-b01abde46523?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in unrealized losses.
These bond losses remain “unrealized” so long as people don’t come looking for their money. But when they do — like during a bank run when banks must pay out, or during hurricane season when insurance companies must fork over cash — then bad assets are sold and losses become “realized.”
And then we all “realize” that the poor dumb institutions that bought US government bonds in 2021 are actually insolvent — unless the Fed prints to paper over a bond crisis the Fed itself caused. Which it will do, over and over again, until the crisis devalues the dollar itself.
Anyway, this is what happened in March 2023 when five huge banks died [ https://substack.com/redirect/5de05240-fde5-44f6-bae4-f3a8711899b8?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in quick succession, followed by a quick print called BTFP [ https://substack.com/redirect/a4f9e854-a420-48ec-96ef-a139dece54b9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to cover up the Fed’s failure. Bond losses are why Roubini [ https://substack.com/redirect/28e4a728-df40-4edf-a0b8-ea782314e2b6?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is saying “most” banks are insolvent and why Bank of America alone has $100B in unrealized losses [ https://substack.com/redirect/9e100fba-126d-4608-94eb-fd069351afcd?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. A similar phenomenon in Europe is why Germany’s central bank apparently has negative [ https://substack.com/redirect/909c9b9d-02fc-4598-a289-d80cd3c3259f?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] equity [ https://substack.com/redirect/53f0edcc-0a59-4bb7-b3ca-f84e3e0095c7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and pensioners [ https://substack.com/redirect/848e0170-76ce-4028-bac3-839ac35d1417?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] face massive losses [ https://substack.com/redirect/488e0357-5a2b-4e49-9b46-c63f35faab90?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ].
And this is all just the tip of the iceberg.
Jerome Powell and Janet Yellen are bond villains. They destroyed every institution that trusted their words in 2021. They said inflation was transitory and that they’d keep rates low even as late as November 2021 [ https://substack.com/redirect/4f9c2151-a38f-49a1-9392-7a10309abac9?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. They sold billions in bonds on that false pretense. Then the Fed executed a surprise rate hike [ https://substack.com/redirect/5e9e1bd0-0d36-4d3f-8b43-da95ba85219d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], devaluing all the bonds the government had just sold, and turning Treasuries into the new toxic waste.
I discussed all this here [ https://substack.com/redirect/a5567bdb-d087-4055-9522-7721f69d37b3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and here [ https://substack.com/redirect/c801ea29-6fe7-4c93-8ccf-9872a5e0e796?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], even mentioning insurance companies as bust [ https://substack.com/redirect/b3c5ae2e-972c-4136-8742-be6ff7efc1e2?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] months ago. The thing to understand is that this financial hurricane won’t be limited to Florida.
FAQ
Why didn’t the institutions hedge?
I hear this question [ https://substack.com/redirect/8df5a5ab-3515-4877-a692-0f71eb51391b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] a lot. I’m a little surprised that I hear it, because if you know enough to mention hedging duration risk…then you should know that the situation of the last few years was impossible to hedge. But here’s [ https://substack.com/redirect/a2dd5803-c1de-4e8b-bd31-7b7ad704afca?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] a reasonable version of the question:
The short answer
The short answer is that if one bank has an issue it’s a bank problem, but when hundreds of banks have an issue it’s a central bank problem. And the Fed and FDIC and others have known for a while that literally hundreds of institutions have problems — which should tell you that it wasn’t trivial to “manage duration risk” of this magnitude.
The long answer
“Nobody hedged.” And why would they? Because hedging is expensive, and in 2021 the Fed kept saying inflation was transitory, as explained in the clip below [ https://substack.com/redirect/5a58a1ea-dcae-4f8a-9a38-8c25a7cdb50b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] from actual bankers.
Thing is, hedging is like buying an insurance policy. And you might well buy car insurance. But do you go through life buying personal insurance policies for plane crashes? You don’t, because (a) you don’t consider that a probable event and (b) the cost isn’t worth the benefit and (c) you’d be dead anyway.
In more detail:
Hedging isn’t free. Many people seem to think hedging is magic, and somehow delivers a risk-free return. But hedging is actually expensive and gives up scarce margin.
Hedging isn’t possible for extreme outcomes. For example, you can hedge [ https://substack.com/redirect/41ed4f5e-f19d-4b1c-933a-a2c2342ce4c0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] a move to say 1.1% from 1%, but not 1% to 5% in a year. Something so dramatic changes the fundamental nature of the asset class.
Hedging gets combinatorial. You can hedge bonds on many different parameters. Interest rates are just one parameter. Another might be the probability of sovereign default. A third could be inflation. A fourth might be exchange rate risk if you’re working from a foreign jurisdiction. And so on and so forth, and that’s just for bonds. It quickly gets combinatorial and you can’t hedge every possibility without giving up all your margin.
Hedging didn’t seem necessary in 2021. Back then, we’d all just had 10+ years of low/zero rates and a strong public commitment from the Fed to keep them low indefinitely [ https://substack.com/redirect/17addafa-d572-4fdf-9fbc-7d545e41c7c4?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], all the way through November 2021.
Hedging was low status. More than that: the Treasury and Fed said inflation was a conspiracy theory [ https://substack.com/redirect/40598589-369c-48ae-8425-800c028f2c8b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in 2021, so it was low status to hedge!
Hedging just shifts the risk to someone else. It doesn’t fix the systemic devaluation that Fed and Treasury caused. You’re just playing hot potato. Someone is stuck holding these horrible bonds.
Hedging is impossible if you’ve marked the devalued bond as hold-to-maturity. This is technical, but bankers that were surprised by the Fed’s rate hikes in 2022 tried surprising their depositors and investors by hiding their losses in their balance sheets with what I call “hide-to-maturity” [ https://substack.com/redirect/1bcf97bd-f82c-42f4-b741-dbceae3f8911?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] accounting, essentially pretending that the loss didn’t happen. But once they did this, they couldn’t easily “hedge” the asset as it was no longer available for sale.
Hedging is impossible if the rulebook makes you buy bonds. The entire Western financial system is premised on long-term US government bonds, especially Treasuries, as having the “risk-free rate of return” and being the safest asset around. And many rulebooks encourage (or require) financial institutions to buy these so-called high quality liquid assets [ https://substack.com/redirect/b808b0b6-088e-433a-8dc7-c0aff8d512fe?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. So they were told to buy massive amounts of assets that the Fed then devalued.
Hedging deals with duration risk, not devaluation policy. Rates aren’t an organic market phenomenon, they’re set directly by the Fed. So even the term “duration risk” isn’t right — it’s really devaluation policy. Remember, rate hikes weren’t a lightning bolt from the blue that no one knew about, they were a surprise attack [ https://substack.com/redirect/40598589-369c-48ae-8425-800c028f2c8b?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] from the Fed. Look at Powell’s own comments from 2012 on page 193 of this pdf [ https://substack.com/redirect/5df5b89d-ff92-49c2-ad0f-5faedc608a33?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] — he knew rate hikes would cause massive losses.
Put all that together. Most banks nowadays are run by risk-averse committees of NPCs. You think that in mid-2021 they were going to spend a big chunk of their margin hedging a massive inflationary risk, a risk that hadn’t happened for 10 years, a risk that the Treasury and Fed said vociferously wouldn’t happen? No, all these lemmings were led off a cliff because they followed Fed and Treasury.
In short: all the mid-2023 commentary about hedging duration risk is 20/20 hindsight. It’s like asking five years from now “why didn’t you hedge debasement risk”, as if “everyone knew” that de-dollarization is happening and that you should hedge into gold, Bitcoin, or a non-US-controlled fiat currency.
Inflation wasn’t an establishment-approved thing to hedge in 2021, and de-dollarization isn’t an establishment-approved thing to hedge in 2023.
The visual answer
Powell often talks about a “soft landing”, which implies he’s the pilot of the economy. Hedging is like fastening your seatbelt against expected turbulence. And that’s a good practice much of the time. But if the pilot is crashing the plane, which is what Powell is doing, then no amount of seatbelt fastening will save you.
