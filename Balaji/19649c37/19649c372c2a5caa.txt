Global meritocracy is finally here.
We’re launching a new fellowship for founders and creators, open to anyone from any country. You can apply online [ https://substack.com/redirect/7569e79c-c514-4025-8df3-930bce1ac8af?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] in ten minutes. If accepted, you will receive $100k in funding for a new or existing venture. Our only requirement is that you relocate to the Network School campus near Singapore with other fellows for one year to lock in.
If that already sounds amazing, go to ns.com/apply [ https://substack.com/redirect/f63c6abc-ca92-42c6-b6be-7165f1137c0e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. Then read on for details on who should (and should not!) apply, what the process is like, and why we’re launching this.
(1) Who should apply
You should apply if you’re a global tech founder, AI-first creator, or community builder.
Our ideal founder recognizes that the Internet is rising, that Asia is rising, and that technology has now [ https://substack.com/redirect/875b7db8-54f9-47f1-a4fd-cf8def234b8c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] fully [ https://substack.com/redirect/ba9d998e-9bb3-4fa0-97f6-f19122fb9f9c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] decentralized out of Silicon Valley. We welcome applicants from any country, so long as you have English language fluency. As for the specific area, what you’re working on matters less than how intensely you’re working on it. Some of our founders will be early in their careers (like recent high school grads or newly minted PhDs), while others will have more experience (like open source developers, research scientists, early stage startup alumni, or engineering professors). But we’re flexible, so long as you’re technical.
Our AI-first creator category is newer and deserves a bit more explanation. In short, we believe 20th century media is over. And we think every niche filled by the formerly mainstream media — Hollywood, newspapers, magazines, broadcast television, and the like — is up for grabs as never before, with production shifting from traditional venues to the global Internet. So, we’re looking for filmmakers, artists, and storytellers who want to make tasteful use of AI to hyperdeflate [ https://substack.com/redirect/9f9b3b2b-ab76-44bc-adba-76e63f66a131?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] production costs and write new stories for the 21st century. We’re open to “traditional” creators too, but we think the economic leverage from AI will be substantial, so we want creators who think about AI as an integral part of their workflow.
Our community builder category is likewise new and also merits explanation. Briefly, we think the third kind of “thing” after tech companies and cryptocurrencies will be startup societies. That means intentional communities, pop-up cities, and network states, where digital tribes assemble offline in person. I’ve written about this at length in The Network State [ https://substack.com/redirect/cfdb846f-7a0a-4935-a2e1-c34345e62050?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], but see this video [ https://substack.com/redirect/7d7a5146-adfb-4803-9e49-f353eaaaf1ee?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] and dashboard [ https://substack.com/redirect/b04485d8-6d5c-4855-8b48-4e7a630ca10c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] to see why we we think startup societies will be a megatrend.
About you
OK, so that’s the type of person we’re looking for professionally — a founder or creator. But what are you like personally?
You’re Internet First [ https://substack.com/redirect/0689bfb2-7845-4da8-9a3a-ee8707f90c0c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ], in that you broadly believe in the open web, decentralization, cryptocurrency, and encryption.
You’re classically liberal, centrist, or otherwise politically pragmatic — rather than ultranationalist or socialist.
You’d be in tech even if markets crashed for a decade, so long as you had time to study, learn, and explore.
You’re excited to relocate to our solarpunk campus [ https://substack.com/redirect/dc252313-9186-4bf4-89a0-23df5b2baae0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] near Singapore for a year to level up among others of like mind.
And you’re likely what we call dark talent [ https://substack.com/redirect/aea81b61-f919-4fea-9ca7-6125525b299c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] — undiscovered greatness from the middle of nowhere, whether that’s India or Indiana, Eastern Europe or the Middle East — who just needs a shot to prove yourself. And this is your shot.
So: you should apply for the Network School Fellowship if you’re looking for a sunny, green, and calm environment to build your dream amidst a community of builders. We’ve created a peaceful place with minimal distraction where funding, coffee, food, gym, office space, laundry, cleaning, and all the rest is provided so you can focus on work.
Now, let’s focus on the application.
(2) How to apply
Because we’re open to the whole world, we expect many to apply. So, we have a simple multi-stage process for obtaining a Network School Fellowship.
Form. First, fill out the form at ns.com/apply [ https://substack.com/redirect/f2684e66-670a-4d80-b8db-081a95ef5f2d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ]. We’ll review to see if you have either potential (as demonstrated by test scores and academic accomplishment) or results (as demonstrated by tech portfolio or startup traction). We’ll also administer a short screening test and ask for references.
Zoom. For those we deem competitive in the first round, we’ll do a structured video interview where we get to know you and ask a number of interactive questions. We seek people with high integrity and high agency, a combination that’s hard to assess through forms alone.
Room. Finally, we ask candidates who make it to the third round to fly out to our Network School campus near Singapore for a in-person, proctored, pencil and paper objective examination similar to the IIT JEE, the GaoKao, or the SAT. This offline component allows us to AI-proof the online talent search, thereby confirming your skills. During your trip you’ll also meet other Network Schoolers, see the facilities, and determine if you’re ready to build.
Boom. Then, if you’re accepted, you’ll relocate to Network School for one year to build. We’ll either fund your existing company or set up a new one. Existing companies will be backed in their current jurisdictions, while new companies will be incorporated by default in Singapore. 
So that’s the idea: a global startup draft, like the NBA draft, where we source talent from around the world and colocate it in person. We’re starting with 100 global founders in our first batch, and plan to scale up over time if things go well. 
But why are we doing this?
(3) Why we’re doing this
We believe the old path for finding global tech talent — which went through Stanford, Silicon Valley, San Francisco, California, and ultimately America — is ending. So we’re going to need a new path, based on pure global meritocracy. And we think the Network School Fellowship could be a starting point for this new path.
To motivate this, let’s talk about (a) what the path for tech talent used to look like, (b) why it’s now broken, and (c) how we plan to replace it.
The old path for tech talent
Let’s break this up into the American and global paths.
The American path once started with a smart young kid in the middle of (say) Indiana like Scott McNealy. He’d take the SAT, get a great score, attend a top engineering school at a reasonable price, come to Stanford for his graduate degree, found a Silicon Valley tech company, scale it up, and eventually go public.
The global path once started with a smart young kid in the middle of (say) India like Vinod Khosla. He’d take the IIT-JEE, get a great score, attend a top engineering school at a reasonable price, come to Stanford for his graduate degree, found a Silicon Valley tech company, scale it up, go public, and eventually become a US citizen.
Now, if you know anything about those two men, you’d know that they cofounded a little multibillion dollar company called Sun Microsystems. That is: they came from very different places, but thanks to the magic of global meritocracy, thanks to international capitalism, they could build something great together that pushed tech forward and benefited the world.
The old path for tech talent is broken
Yet their story is much less possible in today’s US. I won’t rehash every headline, but today’s Scott McNealy might well be blocked from matriculating by DEI, and today’s Vinod Khosla might well be blocked from immigrating on an H-1B. And that’s really just the start of it. Every single piece of the old path for tech talent is now either fully broken or under assault. To review:

Silicon Valley. That path is now closing.
Fortunately, we still have the Internet.
The new path for tech talent
Remember, there’s no silicon being mined out of the hills of Silicon Valley. There’s no natural resource keeping tech there. So in theory, given that you just need a laptop to build things on the Internet, we should be able to materialize the global tech community anywhere. But in practice, someone needs to actually do that.
And constructive proofs [ https://substack.com/redirect/bd0df8f7-1a06-4d87-b768-f188fc997402?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] are always more satisfying than existence proofs. So, rather than simply assert that a better path exists, we must build it. And from a practical standpoint, we must also recognize that we can’t replace the old path for global tech talent in one fell swoop. After all, literally millions [ https://substack.com/redirect/7f3c5fc3-e2e4-4818-abe4-15f2c2940f26?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] traveled that path, and will continue to do so for some time, even if it’s breaking. So we’ll have to start small, with perhaps just one hundred builders, to prove a new path for tech talent.
And thus the Network School Fellowship.
In a sense, our fellowship just does the “obvious” thing. It starts with the observation that tech has already [ https://substack.com/redirect/7e8c4eff-6d67-4e0d-ba52-895824f0151e?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] decentralized out of Silicon Valley, and it gives a new coordinating point for global tech talent to assemble.
We put the first node of the Network School in the new Singapore-Johor Special Economic Zone [ https://substack.com/redirect/0d27a263-3a16-4f19-a98c-1310051cb9ed?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] because (a) thousands [ https://substack.com/redirect/193dc844-f21c-484c-a1fb-92946a5cbf61?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of millionaires have moved to Singapore, (b) >50% [ https://substack.com/redirect/39ec6d59-464d-4a52-9cd5-7e55853fabb3?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of the Earth’s population is within a short plane flight, (c) ~50%  [ https://substack.com/redirect/18f010bf-fa9c-49d2-bca7-5bdeb6221ee7?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] of GDP is now within Asia, (d) crypto [ https://substack.com/redirect/2afb7bb5-4226-40db-93e0-60f94578fbc0?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] has already decentralized to Asia, (e) AI [ https://substack.com/redirect/ba9d998e-9bb3-4fa0-97f6-f19122fb9f9c?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] is in the middle of similarly decentralizing, and (f) the zone has plenty of [ https://substack.com/redirect/8f985b91-d2b3-4f96-ae50-7a4f400a912d?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] visas [ https://substack.com/redirect/a83a143a-4e94-4b92-9725-1cb1af0303fb?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] for global talent and a stated goal of recruiting 20,000 [ https://substack.com/redirect/825c1a03-5a18-49b3-8fdc-2166b9f14012?j=eyJ1IjoiOG81NG4ifQ.6oeetudtJEh-zO1onUJvtadWEdbodFIw0h4xbugrh1o ] skilled workers.
