Luckily, Google has the "huge advantage" of TPUs – something which is seemingly becoming more clear [https://spyglass.org/r/52b3e487?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] by the day [https://spyglass.org/r/0ad14d16?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. It’s not quite logical to me." That's a bit self-serving, of course. But that doesn't mean it's wrong [https://spyglass.org/r/253a4f13?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]! And there will be more calls and pressure [https://spyglass.org/r/7f06c09e?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] when the third Knives Out is released, which would obviously [https://spyglass.org/r/c29df011?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] be a huge theatrical event because Netflix has helped make it a big franchise. BTW, those first bids from Paramount [https://spyglass.org/r/6087b46c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], Comcast, and Netflix are now in [https://spyglass.org/r/85419c84?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] David Zaslav's hands... [Bloomberg [https://spyglass.org/r/704c9908?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] 🔒]

📲 Perplexity's Mobile AI Browser [https://spyglass.org/r/ca8afb48?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – For all the talk [https://spyglass.org/r/319c8d5c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] and hype [https://spyglass.org/r/cd7876c4?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] about the "AI Browser Wars" [https://spyglass.org/r/d5468fd3?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], I've been surprised that none of the major players have tried to immediately go after the green field: mobile. I get that it's harder to do – in particular on iOS which remains locked-down when it comes to browser technology, which is undoubtedly part of the reason why Perplexity is starting with Android. Still, as someone now fully sold on using an AI browser, I find it weird/frustrating to use a mobile browser and have no such AI options to do what I regularly do on the desktop now. Instead I have to use a separate app, like an animal. There might even be a real opportunity [https://spyglass.org/r/809dcc68?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] on iOS as Apple should add AI functionality to Safari in about 15 years. I still regularly use The Browser Company's (clever [https://spyglass.org/r/aa42931a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]) Arc mobile app [https://spyglass.org/r/660a4bf5?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], even though they've abandoned ship [https://spyglass.org/r/8029eead?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] on Arc for the more AI-centric Dia. [TechCrunch [https://spyglass.org/r/e4f5be01?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]]

📺 Big Streamers Bet Big On 'Pause Ads' [https://spyglass.org/r/2c131abd?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – As much as I generally dislike [https://spyglass.org/r/fcd891e2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] advertising – certainly, when it's say, crammed into your TV UI [https://spyglass.org/r/e08e2af6?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], or certainly your refrigerator [https://spyglass.org/r/3ba1fa05?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]! – I think I'm actually okay with this. Right now when you pause content, it's just frozen as a static image. Some people obviously pause to try to catch something, but most of the time it's just to do something else. So I'm okay with using this otherwise dead space – especially if the ad is actually relevant to something you just saw on-screen. You can imagine a world in which some consumers may even pause on purpose to see if they can buy something they're seeing. This may sound like the definition of dystopia to any filmmaker, but you could see this being a thing if it worked well enough. Certainly for Amazon TVs! And in general, I'd be more in favor of this type of ad if you got something back in exchange for seeing it, such as a discount [https://spyglass.org/r/0afc81c1?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] on the streaming service. Sort of like the lock screen ads on Kindles. Or even that free TV set [https://spyglass.org/r/2d75adff?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] that has the ad bar at the bottom. This all sounds like a nightmare to me [https://spyglass.org/r/a4b4f44a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], but I get it. And I don't think it has to be all bad. [Variety [https://spyglass.org/r/fdfd60bb?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]]

💸 Sam Altman's 'Rough Vibes' Memo [https://spyglass.org/r/0158af19?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – Three interesting elements here. First, that Altman felt the need to write this just ahead of Google's release of Gemini 3. Clearly, he knew it was going to be good. And second, that's a problem for OpenAI because if nothing else, it highlights a fear [https://spyglass.org/r/fe18aba6?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] that many investors and watchers have that Google's sheer size and scale – and most importantly, profits – will allow them to ride ChatGPT's first-mover advantage [https://spyglass.org/r/28e040bf?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] into the ground. This is obviously why OpenAI is trying to move heaven and earth to create their own infrastructure [https://spyglass.org/r/188d73a9?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – but do they have enough time before the bubble bursts [https://spyglass.org/r/acc4949a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]? That's the million dollar question [https://spyglass.org/r/63b2f50e?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Actually, the trillion dollar [https://spyglass.org/r/fb7f3637?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] one. Perhaps [https://spyglass.org/r/d31da917?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] several trillion. Finally, the specific notion that Google has in some way overcome the pre-training hurdles that the other LLM makers have run into is interesting... Google itself is playing this up [https://spyglass.org/r/de86ffc8?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – is it something specific about TPUs (versus NVIDIA's GPUs) or something else in the process? That's also a several trillion dollar question [https://spyglass.org/r/fda3b20d?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]!
