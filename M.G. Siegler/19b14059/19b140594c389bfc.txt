Hard to believe we're only a week removed [https://spyglass.org/r/ce5cd296?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] from Netflix announcing the acquisition of Warner Bros. Feels like a lot has happened since then, because it has. Notably, a move to go hostile by Paramount Skydance, with David Ellison pitching WBD shareholders directly [https://spyglass.org/r/5fce3d42?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], via "lengthy letters". He's clearly big mad. BOLD mad, no less.

A couple [https://spyglass.org/r/af1a95f0?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] fun tick-tocks [https://spyglass.org/r/188ec7b1?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] reveal why: they clearly thought they had this deal in the bag – I mean, they got Jared Kushner's fund involved, when Netflix swooped in, leading David Zaslav to ghost Ellison. Ouch. WBD was clearly worried about the foreign money components [https://spyglass.org/r/54b6543f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] of the financing as well as the financing itself. It certainly can't help that Oracle's stock is down nearly 35% in the past few months – and nearly 15% this week alone – given that Larry Ellison is backstopping the deal.

Unless Paramount pulls out, it feels like this is going to go on for months. As such, the deal may not actually close for years. History – both Barry Diller and Sumner Redstone back in the day battling for Paramount (more on that at the bottom of this newsletter), and more recently, Disney and Comcast battling for Fox [https://spyglass.org/r/a0f28690?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – would seem to indicate Netflix is still the likely victor, but it will probably cost them a lot more money to get it done. But history also didn't have this particular administration in power so... Who knows.

One big signal coming in January [https://spyglass.org/r/1e600158?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] will be when Comcast spins off Versant (their TV channel cruft [https://spyglass.org/r/9d12e861?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]) and we'll see how that's valued, which will dictate how WBD's channels (not part of the Netflix deal but part of the Paramount deal) should be valued [https://spyglass.org/r/5de2c48f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c].

Anyway, I've gotten two takes out of this fiasco so far – that Hollywood is silly to fear Netflix here [https://spyglass.org/r/4bd14039?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], relative to the alternative at least! And wondering if Netflix truly has found religion with movie theaters [https://spyglass.org/r/a3c47397?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], or if they're just saying that to try to woo Hollywood and seal this deal – and I look forward to months more of good fodder in the form of popcorn.

💰 Anthropic is Broadcom's $10B Mystery Partner – to Buy TPUs [https://spyglass.org/r/0db95799?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – In last quarter's earnings, Broadcom disclosed [https://spyglass.org/r/5f8daf65?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] a new $10B deal with a partner they refused to name. Oh, and apparently $11B more on top of this.
