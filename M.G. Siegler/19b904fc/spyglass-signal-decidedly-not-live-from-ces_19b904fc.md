I Wrote...I Note...📝 [Satya Nadella Blogs](https://spyglass.org/r/b9fe3016?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) – To me, the content is less interesting – AI is gonna be big in 2026, you heard it here first! Though I do appreciate the "scaffolding vs. substitute" & "slop vs. sophistication" framing. And, of course, referencing the "bicycles for the mind" without mentioning you-know-who... – than the meta aspect of Nadella deciding to blog. Presumably, this is an attempt to establish some sort of "thought leadership" position as Microsoft navigates the extremely competitive AI waters. He perhaps even learned a thing or two from his friend Sam Altman, who [oscillates between](https://spyglass.org/r/b4837ab1?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) his own personal blog and OpenAI's official blog – [or even other ones](https://spyglass.org/r/ba702674?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) – seemingly on a whim. To that end, Nadella's post isn't a blog on an official Microsoft site or even Microsoft-owned LinkedIn, where many such thought leader-y posts are born, but instead on "snscratchpad.com" – there wasn't a better URL available?! That perhaps led it to fly under the radar, with [Techmeme just posting it now](https://spyglass.org/r/554bcf2f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c), even though Nadella [posted this a week ago](https://spyglass.org/r/94451d9c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c). Maybe this was a new year's resolution to write more, which I can certainly appreciate! We'll see how long he keeps it up... [[SN Scratchpad](https://spyglass.org/r/92330118?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)] 📱 [OpenAI Latest App Store Attempt](https://spyglass.org/r/9fbd2dd3?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) – Rolfe Winkler tried to use some of the initial apps in [ChatGPT’s latest attempt](https://spyglass.org/r/923ebf39?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) to re-create the magic of the App Store. The result? Predictably not great. OpenAI says it’s early, which it is, but the real issue is how much easier/better it is to use the partners' services via their actual apps and/or websites. The App Store worked because it took advantage of a new device and form factor in the form of the iPhone. OpenAI’s hope here is to leverage the fact that people [already have ChatGPT open](https://spyglass.org/r/46a76cc5?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c), but these other apps really are just a click away, be it in another tab or another app. Which is to say, maybe this strategy works when OpenAI [has their own device](https://spyglass.org/r/7c331d2c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) (if that device [is compelling enough](https://spyglass.org/r/61ed775a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)), but then again, Amazon has been trying that for years too [without much success](https://spyglass.org/r/1c9953a4?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)… I can see some business use cases here, but consumer use cases are going to remain a challenge unless OpenAI can figure out something truly novel (perhaps by using ChatGPT’s knowledge about you?)… [[WSJ](https://spyglass.org/r/97fb805a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) 🔒] 📽️ [Hollywood Had A Slightly Better 2025](https://spyglass.org/r/d94a2c73?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) – $8.9B in ticket sales (in the US and Canada) was 2% better than 2024 — but such numbers are still down over 20% versus the pre-pandemic levels. And that’s before you take inflation into account. This naturally brings up the "is this the new normal?" question, which it probably is unless Hollywood actually does something to change the equation here. [Netflix could help](https://spyglass.org/r/418d71de?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c), but the industry [will do everything in their power](https://spyglass.org/r/957a3591?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) to [stop that from happening](https://spyglass.org/r/7c524126?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) so… Still, one of [my predictions for the year](https://spyglass.org/r/dfaf54e2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) is that the 2026 box office will be up pretty significantly, sequentially. And it may even touch those pre-pandemic numbers (again, before inflation). The slate looks that strong with franchises galore ([not that those helped much this year](https://spyglass.org/r/5061abda?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)). But it will likely be a mirage. Real change — reel change? — [remains needed](https://spyglass.org/r/ded01e80?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c). [[NYT](https://spyglass.org/r/413333df?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)] 🥽 [Apple Cuts Vision Pro Production & Marketing](https://spyglass.org/r/9caac0b4?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) – Many in Apple's orbit seem to be quick to call this latest report a "nothingburger" – mainly in that it's nothing new (IDC's market report notes that Luxshare halted production [last year](https://spyglass.org/r/10c3beb0?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)), but I would just note that the company did ship an updated version of the device since then (also [IDC's sales estimates do keep falling](https://spyglass.org/r/630bbdf9?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) below even their muted expectations). Sure, it was a small spec upgrade, but many Apple updates are and they still manage to move needles year to year – that does not seem to be the case with Vision Pro. IDC believes they shipped just 45k units in the holiday quarter; Apple sells something like 600k iPhones and about 60k Macs a day. Still, I think that small update was probably [the right strategy](https://spyglass.org/r/7c6ecbeb?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c) with the product already in market (they should have [launched it as a dev kit](https://spyglass.org/r/7f06686d?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c), not a full-on consumer product in its current state, but we're way past that now...). Rather than seeing them try to do a lighter/slightly cheaper Vision Pro right now, I'd rather see them meet Meta in the market for Smart Glasses ([which is clearly happening](https://spyglass.org/r/cfd4fdd7?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c)). And build up from there. There's just still no real market for headsets yet – [Meta dominates it](https://spyglass.org/r/a8b5d362?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c), but even they're [seemingly pulling back](https://spyglass.org/r/1cf5cf70?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c).
