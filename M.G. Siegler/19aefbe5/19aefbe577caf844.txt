Earlier today, on the back of the reporting [https://spyglass.org/r/b5810bd9?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] that Netflix was closing in on a deal with Warner Bros Discovery, I wrote up some thoughts [https://spyglass.org/r/4f5dac0a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. It's obviously a massive deal with ramifications both for entertainment and tech. Just a few hours later, Netflix confirmed [https://spyglass.org/r/e2db3b81?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the $82.7B cash-and-stock deal. Mind you, that's just for the Warner Bros studio and HBO Max streaming service, the cable channel assets will still be spun-out ahead of this deal closing. So yeah, it's a big price. And certainly a deal unlike any Netflix has done before.

While investors clearly dislike the deal (Netflix's stock has been down between 3% and 5% on a day the overall market is up), it could make quite a bit of sense – if it actually gets completed. Netflix is going to have a hell of a fight on their hands on a few fronts there. Including with Hollywood in general, which clearly does not want to lose another studio, let alone such a historic one, to the hands of tech. But I also don't think it will end up being a bad thing for that industry in the long run. Which is just an impossible thing to hear right now, but it's an industry on the verge of everything being thrown into disarray with AI.

For now, I'll just tout my predictions from last year [https://spyglass.org/r/cd0de4c6?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], both that WBD would be acquired, but also that Netflix would eventually backtrack on their theatrical release stance [https://spyglass.org/r/1b610703?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Book look good right now. But again, there's a long way to go here. Get the popcorn ready.

So you have to do this kind of work outside of the Valley, in Paris." Hypnotized to the point of spending $15B or so [https://spyglass.org/r/e3f0ecb0?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] to reboot your AI work with offering billion-dollar comp packages [https://spyglass.org/r/6db882e2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], perhaps? 🇪🇺 The EU Strikes Back [https://spyglass.org/r/73bffed4?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – While it has seemed [https://spyglass.org/r/9a3dc310?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] like regulators in Europe were taking their foot off the gas a bit [https://spyglass.org/r/1d49d68c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] with both their own regime change, as well as the situation with the Trump administration, they're clearly still finding new and interesting ways to go after Big Tech. Note that this has nothing to do with the DMA and is simply a probe for now – the EU loves to launch probes [https://spyglass.org/r/09be2844?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. I Quote...

> "We as a company try to manage as responsibly as we can. And then I think there are some players who are YOLO-ing."

– Dario Amodei speaking at [https://spyglass.org/r/1d5c2f0f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the New York Times DealBook Summit [https://spyglass.org/r/ecd247d9?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] about some unnamed competitors, at least one of which is pretty clearly the place he used to work: OpenAI. This is basically the point I was making in writing "AGI or Bust" [https://spyglass.org/r/d363fe06?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Great risk, great reward, and all that. But Anthropic's more prudent approach [https://spyglass.org/r/f313cde9?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] could pay off depending on [https://spyglass.org/r/dfc93321?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the markets...

In the same talk [https://spyglass.org/r/133748bc?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], Amodei also said he thinks scaling (LLMs) will get us to AGI eventually (i.e. he doesn't think we need new models [https://spyglass.org/r/45972425?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – which makes sense for him to say that since, at least as far as anyone knows, Anthropic isn't working on other models). And yes, he also had a fun quote about how they don't do "Code Reds" [https://spyglass.org/r/60e57ef7?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – like that other company [https://spyglass.org/r/420f7ed2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] he dare not name.
