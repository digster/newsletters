I Note...

📹 TikTok Signs Away US Entity [https://spyglass.org/r/773878fe?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – Technically, the deal still isn't done. It's set to close on January 22, 2026 and presumably China (or the Trump administration) could still kill it in some way. But presumably TikTok wouldn't have signed without China's blessing – or assurances China would give the final sign-off? I mean, who knows, this whole situation has been such a farce [https://spyglass.org/r/6bb6f7ae?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Including the "around $14B" valuation [https://spyglass.org/r/b68e9228?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – I often joke [https://spyglass.org/r/ed7825ca?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] about big deals being less than AI seed rounds these days, but this one truly is! It's also a little odd that an Abu Dhabi-based fund is one of the key backers to ensure American control of the entity? It's also still not clear which "new investors" own the mysterious other 5% – is it the Murdochs? The US Government? [https://spyglass.org/r/616a7405?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] Oracle is clearly the lead here, so it will be interesting if that in some way gets roped into the Netflix/Warner Bros/Paramount love triangle [https://spyglass.org/r/22ec99fe?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – does the ownership of the father mean anything for the ownership of the son? What if the father is backstopping said ownership? With shares in said buyer of TikTok? It's yet another wrinkle [https://spyglass.org/r/a5f8da2f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] that could be in Netflix's favor here... But the main question remains: is TikTok really still going to remain the hot consumer product [https://spyglass.org/r/e1608214?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] when owned by a nearly 50 year old tech company (one in turbulent waters given their AI reinvention efforts, no less) and private equity funds? Where's 'TikTok 2' [https://spyglass.org/r/af81963f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]? [Axios [https://spyglass.org/r/76cd5dd7?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]]

🚙 Waymo Worth Way More Than $100B [https://spyglass.org/r/1e8623ca?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – I mean, at least in my own estimation [https://spyglass.org/r/270f5ae1?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], just based on our current market environment (Tesla, Uber, etc) and not to mention [https://spyglass.org/r/a087f015?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] that AI startups are closing in on such valuations with little to show for it – certainly not traction and/or revenue like Waymo has! Then again, Alphabet is getting others to validate the valuation, which is undoubtedly part of the very reason for the raise (as Google could clearly just fund this themselves). And they sort of have the burden of showing actual revenue – $350M ARR [https://spyglass.org/r/8e3993c6?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – versus even Tesla, which still has a very much in beta self-driving taxi program [https://spyglass.org/r/1f8c9ea0?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Still, there's a whole [https://spyglass.org/r/0cecb41a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] future-of-transportation/magical experience narrative that they could spin here [https://spyglass.org/r/68d80fcb?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] (and would [https://spyglass.org/r/7029d362?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], were Elon Musk running the company, you imagine – though he would also say, as he often does, that Waymo's approach isn't scalable in the way Tesla's will be) where $100B is an absolute steal. For now, maybe they're just fine to fly relatively low-key under the lidar. [Bloomberg [https://spyglass.org/r/ef7f8562?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] 🔒]

💰 OpenAI Worth Way More Than $800B [https://spyglass.org/r/4eacb800?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – By which I mean they're apparently targeting an $830B valuation in a new would-be fundraise. That's a curiously precise number for a famously imprecise business, unless you consider that they really perhaps just want that number to be higher than $800B – the number at which SpaceX is now valued [https://spyglass.org/r/b1f64364?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], of course. And when earlier reports suggested [https://spyglass.org/r/62629808?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the valuation could be a mere $750B – the old pre versus post switcheroo? – that simply couldn't stand. So who is investing? I mean, who is left? Amazon, for one, it seems [https://spyglass.org/r/ef5ca384?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Undoubtedly more sovereign wealth funds for another. Would Apple revisit [https://spyglass.org/r/68b08760?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]? Would Google dare [https://spyglass.org/r/3034b6bc?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]?! Nothing can be ruled out. Good on Disney for squeezing into the old $500B valuation [https://spyglass.org/r/de8c8dae?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – with warrants for more, no less. 🎶When you wish upon a paper gain...🎶 Speaking of, all of the SoftBank money from the last [https://spyglass.org/r/fa6a5e6c?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] fundraise isn't even in the bank yet. This is true perpetual fundraising. And clearly necessary! But it will also undoubtedly push up the targeted IPO valuation (as I predicted a whole month ago [https://spyglass.org/r/030195af?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]). Maybe OpenAI could buy Waymo with this fresh $100B? [WSJ [https://spyglass.org/r/68fdb6a4?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] 🔒]

⚽️ FIFA's New Netflix Game [https://spyglass.org/r/dbcddef2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – There's been a bunch of Netflix gaming news in the past several weeks that's now completely buried under the weight of Warner Bros [https://spyglass.org/r/17f3416f?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. But the initiative will clearly be key if Netflix wants to become the "everything app" for entertainment [https://spyglass.org/r/a526e5fd?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – and the first $1T media company [https://spyglass.org/r/54ef606e?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – which is why they keep trying to pivot their way into [https://spyglass.org/r/fb59c4ea?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the right model/experience in gaming. FIFA is an interesting play here because it was a beloved franchise (though not exactly the best brand! [https://spyglass.org/r/b833cd92?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]) that vanished over a licensing dispute with EA (which obviously kept going with new 'EA Sports FC' branding). The timing of the return, of course, is good with the World Cup next year. But can an unproven developer (that Netflix is partnering with) really nail this on the first shot? (They're also co-developing the first new James Bond game [https://spyglass.org/r/9892879e?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] in forever due in March.) If nothing else, the price is right (free, for Netflix members). [BBC [https://spyglass.org/r/a610bee5?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]]

💰 Yann LeCun is Raising Money [https://spyglass.org/r/3ad81aff?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – Two key tidbits: 1) the company is called Advanced Machine Intelligence Labs (AMI Labs) – doesn't get more generic than that – 2) they're targeting raising €500M at a €3B valuation. That almost seems quaint and shows some restraint in this day and age! Still, it presumably will make LeCun a billionaire, or close to it, on paper. Sort of interesting/fun to think about in the context of what Zuck has been dishing out to AI talent [https://spyglass.org/r/82680f63?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] and what he clearly wasn't dishing out to LeCun. This report reiterates (as LeCun has stated [https://spyglass.org/r/e09d0a94?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]) that Meta won't be invested in the lab but there is still some sort of "partnership", which continues to sound like PR [https://spyglass.org/r/a804f03a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] for both sides post-breakup [https://spyglass.org/r/ab0c516a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. Also, LeCun has brought on a CEO named LeBrun, that's just LeFun. [FT [https://spyglass.org/r/7a9ff367?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] 🔒]

🎬 Warner Bros Discovery Rejects Paramount's Takeover Bid [https://spyglass.org/r/4036ada7?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] – No surprise, but the two most interesting tidbits are how WBD felt misled by Paramount as their bids kept morphing to make them more attractive, and how they don't believe Paramount is actually good for the money – even though it's being backstopped by Larry Ellison. They don't trust the revocable trust (where Ellison's Oracle shares reside) element. It can't help that those shares are in a free-fall as investor concerns mount over Oracle's AI spend and the debt required... Paramount hasn't raised the offer [https://spyglass.org/r/b69a8ff2?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] thus far and instead seems intent on taking it to the shareholders for a vote. And while some of those investors [https://spyglass.org/r/6396c6fe?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] are open to Paramount's offer, it feels like there are an increasing number of things starting to work against it – beyond the Oracle concerns, the YouTube/Oscars deal may oddly help Netflix's regulatory case [https://spyglass.org/r/18424b75?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c], the fact that hedge funds are now circling [https://spyglass.org/r/e15c7a46?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] the WBD TV networks (so there's clearly outside interest in those), Jared Kushner oddly dropping out [https://spyglass.org/r/99c81ac7?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c] of the consortium may not matter monetarily but seems like it will optically for you-know-who, etc. It's worth listening to RedBird Capital's Gerry Cardinale for the counter-arguments on Matt Belloni's podcast The Town [https://spyglass.org/r/69df8800?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]. He truly believes it will come down to how those TV networks are valued, and that the WBD/Netflix would be implicitly wildly overvaluing them (versus their offer). It is interesting that no one is talking about the potential Netflix stock upside in this deal... Feels like they should [https://spyglass.org/r/1ec89c9a?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]? All eyes turn to January 8, the deadline for investor tenders... [NYT [https://spyglass.org/r/bc8b7bf5?m=1ed23d55-8119-4b10-b903-392c96d1198c]]
